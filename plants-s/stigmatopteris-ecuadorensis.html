Stigmatopteris ecuadorensis

Stigmatopteris ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian stigmatopteris, is a species of fern native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help it spread in moist, shaded environments. Its sori, which contain the spores, are arranged in a unique linear pattern along the undersides of the fronds.

This fern is of particular interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution.
