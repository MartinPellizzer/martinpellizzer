Salaxis flexuosa var. cognata

Salaxis flexuosa var. cognata, commonly known as the curly salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and biodiversity.
