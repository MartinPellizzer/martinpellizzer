Senecio vallecaucanus var. corralensis

Senecio vallecaucanus var. corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils, and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
