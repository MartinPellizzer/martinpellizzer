Silene andarabica

Silene andarabica, commonly known as the Andalusian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Silene andarabica is often found in dry habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and wastelands.
