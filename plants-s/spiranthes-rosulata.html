Spiranthes rosulata

Spiranthes rosulata, commonly known as the Rosy Ladies' Tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Spiranthes rosulata is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing a unique symbiotic relationship.
