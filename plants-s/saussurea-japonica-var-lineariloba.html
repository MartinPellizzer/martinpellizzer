Saussurea japonica var. lineariloba

Saussurea japonica var. lineariloba, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow or narrow-leaved saussurea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
