Silene morganae

Silene morganae, commonly known as the Morgana's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene morganae is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
