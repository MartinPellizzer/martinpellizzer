Salacia vahliana

Salacia vahliana, commonly known as the Vahli's salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Salacia vahliana is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
