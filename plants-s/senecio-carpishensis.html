Senecio carpishensis

Senecio carpishensis, commonly known as the Cape Town groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers.

Senecio carpishensis is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.
