Senecio icoglossus var. montanus

Senecio icoglossus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is an annual or biennial species, producing small, herbaceous stems that often form dense clusters.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
