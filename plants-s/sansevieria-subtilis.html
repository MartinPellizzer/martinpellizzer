Sansevieria subtilis

Sansevieria subtilis, commonly known as the snake plant, is a succulent perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with green and yellow or white stripes. The plant is adapted to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its ability to purify the air by removing toxins such as formaldehyde and benzene is a notable botanical trait.

Sansevieria subtilis is also known for its resilience, surviving in a wide range of environmental conditions.
