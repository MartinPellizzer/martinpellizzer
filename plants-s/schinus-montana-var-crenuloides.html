Schinus montana var. crenuloides

Schinus montana var. crenuloides, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
