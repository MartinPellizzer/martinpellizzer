Smilax liukiuensis

Smilax liukiuensis, commonly known as the Okinawan arrowhead, is a climbing shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Smilax liukiuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local traditions.
