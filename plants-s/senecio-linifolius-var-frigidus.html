Senecio linifolius var. frigidus

Senecio linifolius var. frigidus, commonly known as the cold-flower senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variant is considered a rare and specialized species, adapted to the extreme conditions of its native range.
