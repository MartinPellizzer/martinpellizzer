Senecio leyboldi

Senecio leyboldi, commonly known as the Leybold's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio leyboldi produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
