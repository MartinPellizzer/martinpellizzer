Sasa ovatoelliptica

Sasa ovatoelliptica, commonly known as the broad-elliptic bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color, growing in dense clusters. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
