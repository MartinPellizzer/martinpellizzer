Seriphidium turanicum

Seriphidium turanicum, commonly known as the Turanian tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Seriphidium turanicum is often found in steppe and desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
