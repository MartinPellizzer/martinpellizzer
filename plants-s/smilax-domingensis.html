Smilax domingensis

Smilax domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed edge.

Smilax domingensis is often found in humid, lowland areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
