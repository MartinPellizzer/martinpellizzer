Sasa omokoensis var. hirsuta

Sasa omokoensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa omokoensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its foliage is narrow and linear, with a distinctive silvery or grayish hue due to the dense pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
