Syzygium subamplexicaule

Syzygium subamplexicaule, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often broader at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Syzygium subamplexicaule is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
