Scutellaria glecomoides

Scutellaria glecomoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular blue or purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, rich soils.

Scutellaria glecomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
