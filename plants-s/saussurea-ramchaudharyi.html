Saussurea ramchaudharyi

Saussurea ramchaudharyi, commonly known as the Nepal saffron or Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles saffron threads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, with the latter being tubular and arranged in a central cluster.

The botanical significance of Saussurea ramchaudharyi lies in its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
