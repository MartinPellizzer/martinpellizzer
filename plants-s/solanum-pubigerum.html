Solanum pubigerum

Solanum pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Solanum pubigerum contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

However, like many members of its family, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
