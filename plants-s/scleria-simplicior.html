Scleria simplicior

Scleria simplicior, commonly known as the simple spearplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Scleria simplicior typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense colonies in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable moist environments.
