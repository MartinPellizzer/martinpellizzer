Sarcomphalus chloroxylon

Sarcomphalus chloroxylon, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is an important component of the region's biodiversity.
