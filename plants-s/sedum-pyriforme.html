Sedum pyriforme

Sedum pyriforme, commonly known as the diamond plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, diamond-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
