Senna wurdackii

Senna wurdackii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Senna wurdackii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
