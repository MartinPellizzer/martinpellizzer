Syzygium turbinatum

Syzygium turbinatum, commonly known as the turban bush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are white or pink, and they emit a sweet fragrance, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
