Sciaphila yakushimensis var. okinawensis

Sciaphila yakushimensis var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa shade plant, is a rare and unique species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in forest understories where it receives filtered sunlight. Its flowers, though small, are usually white or pale pink, adding a subtle elegance to its appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and endangered species requiring careful conservation efforts.
