Sarcostemma ovatum

Sarcostemma ovatum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in some regions.
