Syzygium rosaceum

Syzygium rosaceum, commonly known as the rose apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits with a pinkish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
