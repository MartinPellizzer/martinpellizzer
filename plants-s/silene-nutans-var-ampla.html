Silene nutans var. ampla

Silene nutans var. ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Silene nutans var. ampla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.
