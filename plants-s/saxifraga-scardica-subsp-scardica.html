Saxifraga scardica subsp. scardica

Saxifraga scardica subsp.

scardica, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
