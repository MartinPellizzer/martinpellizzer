Strychnos calantha

Strychnos calantha, commonly known as the red-eyed cycad, is a member of the family Strychnaceae.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants. The plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellowish in color.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
