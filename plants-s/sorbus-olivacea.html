Sorbus olivacea

Sorbus olivacea, commonly known as the olive whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish pome that matures in autumn.

Sorbus olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
