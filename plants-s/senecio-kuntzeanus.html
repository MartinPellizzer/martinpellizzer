Senecio kuntzeanus

Senecio kuntzeanus, commonly known as the Kuntze's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Its succulent leaves and compact growth habit make it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
