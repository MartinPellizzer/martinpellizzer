Strophanthus thierryanus

Strophanthus thierryanus, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb or trail along the ground.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many plants with toxic properties.
