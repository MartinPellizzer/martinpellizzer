Senecio cariensis

Senecio cariensis, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flower heads.

Senecio cariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
