Sorbus aria var. obtusifolia

Sorbus aria var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often slightly toothed and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
