Spergula fimbriata var. pulvinata

Spergula fimbriata var. pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence is a spike-like structure with bracts that are often fringed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
