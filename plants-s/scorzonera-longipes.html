Scorzonera longipes

Scorzonera longipes, commonly known as the longipes scorzonera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. Scorzonera longipes is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
