Stylidium piliferum var. bicolor

Stylidium piliferum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored spurred orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a distinctive appearance with two contrasting colors on its petals and sepals, typically greenish-white and reddish-brown. The plant produces a single, erect flowering stem that bears a few showy flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This species is noted for its unique spurred structure and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.
