Sida mexicana

Sida mexicana, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sida mexicana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
