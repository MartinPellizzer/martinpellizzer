Schoenus bifidus var. jamesonianus

Schoenus bifidus var. jamesonianus, commonly known as the Jameson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
