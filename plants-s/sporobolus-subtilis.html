Sporobolus subtilis

Sporobolus subtilis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
