Strychnos spinosa subsp. volkensii

Strychnos spinosa subsp.

volkensii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually pubescent underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn red when ripe. The fruit contains several seeds surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp, making it an important food source for various animals.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
