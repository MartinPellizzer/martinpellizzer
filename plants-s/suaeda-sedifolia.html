Suaeda sedifolia

Suaeda sedifolia, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor and nutritional value.

Suaeda sedifolia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert landscapes.
