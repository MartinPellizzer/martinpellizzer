Solanum devernicascens

Solanum devernicascens, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Solanum devernicascens thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a plant to be handled with care.
