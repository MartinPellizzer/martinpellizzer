Smilax platycentron f. emarginata

Smilax platycentron f. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a climbing or trailing vine with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves that often have a wavy or notched margin, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a notable species within the Smilacaceae family.
