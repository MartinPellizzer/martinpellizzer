Serapias × timbali

Serapias × timbali, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant that combines the characteristics of its parent species, Serapias and Timbali.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in rocky and sunny environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects, a trait known as mimicry. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit, fibrous roots, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This hybrid is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
