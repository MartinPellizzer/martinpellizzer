Senecio lanuginosus

Senecio lanuginosus, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens.
