Senegalia bahiensis

Senegalia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and the plant is often used for forage and soil stabilization in degraded lands.
