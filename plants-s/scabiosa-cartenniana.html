Scabiosa cartenniana

Scabiosa cartenniana, commonly known as the sweet William or the rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Scabiosa cartenniana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
