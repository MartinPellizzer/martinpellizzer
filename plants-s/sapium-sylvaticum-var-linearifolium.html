Sapium sylvaticum var. linearifolium

Sapium sylvaticum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically 5–10 cm long and 1–2 cm wide. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins during the spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
