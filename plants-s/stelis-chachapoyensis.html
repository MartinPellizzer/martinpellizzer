Stelis chachapoyensis

Stelis chachapoyensis, commonly known as the Chachapoyan orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habit.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
