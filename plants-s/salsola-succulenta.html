Salsola succulenta

Salsola succulenta, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds allow it to colonize a variety of habitats.

Salsola succulenta has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
