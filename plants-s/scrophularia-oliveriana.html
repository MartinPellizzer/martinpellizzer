Scrophularia oliveriana

Scrophularia oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native regions.
