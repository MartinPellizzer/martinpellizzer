Schizophragma viburnoides

Schizophragma viburnoides, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically medium green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black and are an important food source for birds.

Schizophragma viburnoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
