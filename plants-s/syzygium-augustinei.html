Syzygium augustinei

Syzygium augustinei, commonly known as the Australian apple or the satinash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic oils and often fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, round, red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Syzygium augustinei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
