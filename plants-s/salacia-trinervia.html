Salacia trinervia

Salacia trinervia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and distinctive reddish color.

Salacia trinervia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
