Serapias × semicolumnae

Serapias × semicolumnae, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's ear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias acaulis and Serapias semicolumnaris.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
