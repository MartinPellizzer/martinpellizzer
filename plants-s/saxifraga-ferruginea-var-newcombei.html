Saxifraga ferruginea var. newcombei

Saxifraga ferruginea var. newcombei, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are often covered in a rusty-red or orange-brown bloom, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive leaf coloration and is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows.
