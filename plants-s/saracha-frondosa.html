Saracha frondosa

Saracha frondosa, commonly known as the featherleaf fern, is a striking plant native to the temperate rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Saracha frondosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
