Saxifraga sarmentosa var. tricolor-superba

Saxifraga sarmentosa var. tricolor-superba, commonly known as the Three-color Saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trifoliate leaves that display a striking combination of red, green, and white. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
