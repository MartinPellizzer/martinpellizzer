Syzygium pulgarense

Syzygium pulgarense, commonly known as the Java apple or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is not recommended for indoor cultivation without proper knowledge.
