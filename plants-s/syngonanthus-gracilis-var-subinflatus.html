Syngonanthus gracilis var. subinflatus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. subinflatus, commonly known as the slender-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis. It is native to parts of South America, particularly in the Amazon rainforest.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
