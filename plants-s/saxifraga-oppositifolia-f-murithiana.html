Saxifraga oppositifolia f. murithiana

Saxifraga oppositifolia f. murithiana, commonly known as the Alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and showy, pink or magenta flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas and tundra environments, where it blooms during the short summer season.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
