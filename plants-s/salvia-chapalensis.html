Salvia chapalensis

Salvia chapalensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
