Salvia carranzae

Salvia carranzae, commonly known as Carranza's sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
