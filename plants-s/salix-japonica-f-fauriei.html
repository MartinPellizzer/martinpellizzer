Salix japonica f. fauriei

Salix japonica f. fauriei, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.
