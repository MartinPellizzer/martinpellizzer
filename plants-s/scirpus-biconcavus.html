Scirpus biconcavus

Scirpus biconcavus, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flowering spikes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its hollow stems and fibrous roots contribute to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus biconcavus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
