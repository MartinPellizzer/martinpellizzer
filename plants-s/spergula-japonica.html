Spergula japonica

Spergula japonica, commonly known as Japanese spikerush, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color.
