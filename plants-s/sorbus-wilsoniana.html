Sorbus wilsoniana

Sorbus wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, persistent berries that provide food for wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
