Sida surinamensis

Sida surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant has fibrous roots and is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
