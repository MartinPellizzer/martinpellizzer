Syngonanthus linearis

Syngonanthus linearis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, narrow leaves and produces white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its berries.

Syngonanthus linearis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where its host trees are abundant.
