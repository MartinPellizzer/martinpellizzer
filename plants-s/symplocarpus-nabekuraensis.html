Symplocarpus nabekuraensis

Symplocarpus nabekuraensis, commonly known as the Japanese bunchberry, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Symplocarpus nabekuraensis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact size and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
