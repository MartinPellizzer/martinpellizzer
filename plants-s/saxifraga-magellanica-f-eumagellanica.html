Saxifraga magellanica f. eumagellanica

Saxifraga magellanica f. eumagellanica, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
