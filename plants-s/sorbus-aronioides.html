Sorbus aronioides

Sorbus aronioides, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
