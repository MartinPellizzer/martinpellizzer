Sisymbrium austriacum

Sisymbrium austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in Europe and parts of Asia. Its life cycle involves germinating on the roots of host plants such as grasses and legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecological systems by influencing host plant growth and nutrient cycling.
