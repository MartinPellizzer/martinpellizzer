Sorocephalus phylicoides

Sorocephalus phylicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Sorocephalus phylicoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
