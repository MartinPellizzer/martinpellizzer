Solanum congense

Solanum congense, commonly known as the Congo nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Botanically, it is characterized by its glandular hairs and the presence of alkaloids in its foliage and fruits.
