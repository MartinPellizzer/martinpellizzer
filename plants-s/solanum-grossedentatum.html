Solanum grossedentatum

Solanum grossedentatum, commonly known as the toothed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive toothed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum grossedentatum is often found in humid, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
