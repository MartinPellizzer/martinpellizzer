Stylidium diademum

Stylidium diademum, commonly known as the diadem mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered together. The plant relies on a network of roots to attach to its host tree, from which it derives some of its nutrients. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important component of its native ecosystem.
