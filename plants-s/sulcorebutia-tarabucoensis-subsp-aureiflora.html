Sulcorebutia tarabucoensis subsp. aureiflora

Sulcorebutia tarabucoensis subsp.

aureiflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered sun rose, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
