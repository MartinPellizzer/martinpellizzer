Scutellaria pumila

Scutellaria pumila, commonly known as small blue skullcap, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it often grows in moist, wooded areas. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Scutellaria pumila is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of its genus.
