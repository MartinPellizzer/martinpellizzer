Siphonia kunthiana

Siphonia kunthiana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. Siphonia kunthiana is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
