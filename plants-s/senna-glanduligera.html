Senna glanduligera

Senna glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, glandular structures along the veins. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its medicinal properties. Its seeds contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their laxative effects.

Senna glanduligera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
