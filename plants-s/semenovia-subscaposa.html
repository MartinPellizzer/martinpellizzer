Semenovia subscaposa

Semenovia subscaposa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-arid environments.
