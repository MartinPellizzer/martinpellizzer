Schefflera apiculata

Schefflera apiculata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Schefflera apiculata is known for its adaptability and ease of care, though it can be toxic if ingested.
