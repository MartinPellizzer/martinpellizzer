Scilla andria

Scilla andria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Scilla andria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
