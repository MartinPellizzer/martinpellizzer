Syzygium myrtoides

Syzygium myrtoides, commonly known as the common satinash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Syzygium myrtoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
