Saxifraga paniculata var. jabalambrensis

Saxifraga paniculata var. jabalambrensis, commonly known as the Jabal Amman saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Jordan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It has dark green, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is part of the Saxifragaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
