Streptanthus barnebyi

Streptanthus barnebyi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada streptanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

It is a rare and endemic species, with limited distribution and facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
