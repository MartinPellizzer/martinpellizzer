Spiraea × billiardii var. macrothyrsa

Spiraea × billiardii var. macrothyrsa, commonly known as the large-thorned spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. Its compact form and ornamental value make it suitable for borders, hedges, or container gardens.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in a variety of garden settings.
