Sarcanthus fallax

Sarcanthus fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming habit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
