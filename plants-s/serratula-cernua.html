Serratula cernua

Serratula cernua, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive nodding habit.

Serratula cernua is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
