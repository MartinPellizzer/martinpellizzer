Silene comollina

Silene comollina, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Silene comollina is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological zones.
