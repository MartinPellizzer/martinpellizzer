Sasa phyllophorrhachis

Sasa phyllophorrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can be useful for erosion control.

Sasa phyllophorrhachis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
