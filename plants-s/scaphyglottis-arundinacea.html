Scaphyglottis arundinacea

Scaphyglottis arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or marshy areas. Its leaves are long and narrow, often forming a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are usually pale green to yellowish in color and attract various pollinators.
