Solanum chacoense f. glabrescens

Solanum chacoense f. glabrescens, commonly known as the Chacoan nightshade, is a variant of the Solanum chacoense species native to South America.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) foliage and stems, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, rounded berries that are edible but often bitter.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in regions with low rainfall.
