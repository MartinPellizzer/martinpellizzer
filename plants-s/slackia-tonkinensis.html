Slackia tonkinensis

Slackia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms with pale blue or violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and open fields.

Slackia tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
