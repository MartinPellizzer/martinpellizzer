Salsola diffusa

Salsola diffusa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, often growing in arid and semi-arid environments due to its drought-resistant properties. Its leaves are typically succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

Salsola diffusa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
