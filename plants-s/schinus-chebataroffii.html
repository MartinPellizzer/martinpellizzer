Schinus chebataroffii

Schinus chebataroffii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red berries. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and fruit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
