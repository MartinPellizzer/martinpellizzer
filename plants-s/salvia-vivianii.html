Salvia vivianii

Salvia vivianii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
