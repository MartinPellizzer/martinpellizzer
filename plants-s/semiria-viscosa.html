Semiria viscosa

Semiria viscosa, commonly known as the sticky semiria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapts well to moist, tropical environments.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in local herbal remedies.
