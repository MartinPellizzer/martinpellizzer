Strophopappus bicolor

Strophopappus bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its propagation and ecological role.
