Stadtmannia glauca

Stadtmannia glauca, commonly known as the blue star, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
