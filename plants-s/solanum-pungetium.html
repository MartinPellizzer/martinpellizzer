Solanum pungetium

Solanum pungetium, commonly known as the Pungetium nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often found in shaded, moist environments and is not commonly cultivated.
