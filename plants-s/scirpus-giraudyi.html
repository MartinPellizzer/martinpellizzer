Scirpus giraudyi

Scirpus giraudyi, commonly known as the Giraud's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly rough texture, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus giraudyi is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
