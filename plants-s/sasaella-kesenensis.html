Sasaella kesenensis

Sasaella kesenensis, commonly known as the Kesen mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often at high altitudes. Its culms are slender and erect, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Sasaella kesenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping in its native habitat.
