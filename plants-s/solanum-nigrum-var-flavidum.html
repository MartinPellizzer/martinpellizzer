Solanum nigrum var. flavidum

Solanum nigrum var. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, wastelands, and along roadsides.
