Scirpus brevis

Scirpus brevis, commonly known as the short bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland environments in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus brevis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
