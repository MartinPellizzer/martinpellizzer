Syagrus lilliputiana

Syagrus lilliputiana, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, ornamental palm native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact size, typically reaching only 2 to 3 meters in height, with a dense crown of feather-like leaves. The plant has a short, stout trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Syagrus lilliputiana is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and is valued for its unique appearance and resilience.
