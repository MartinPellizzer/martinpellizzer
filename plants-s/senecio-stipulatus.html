Senecio stipulatus

Senecio stipulatus, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio stipulatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
