Scutellaria dentata

Scutellaria dentata, commonly known as the toothed skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scutellaria dentata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
