Specklinia brighamii

Specklinia brighamii, commonly known as the Brigham's cryptostele, is a rare and unique orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
