Saxifraga magellanica f. subsessiliflora

Saxifraga magellanica f. subsessiliflora, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The subsessiliflora form is distinguished by its short or absent pedicels, giving the flowers a more compact appearance. It thrives in rocky or coastal environments, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
