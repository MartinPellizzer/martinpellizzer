Sulcorebutia christiei

Sulcorebutia christiei, commonly known as the Christi's crested cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny, and often branched stems that form a dense, compact shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Sulcorebutia christiei is a popular choice for cactus collectors due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
