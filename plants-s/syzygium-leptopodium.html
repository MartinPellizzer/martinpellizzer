Syzygium leptopodium

Syzygium leptopodium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit is a drupe that turns from green to yellowish when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
