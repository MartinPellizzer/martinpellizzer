Stelis onychosepala

Stelis onychosepala, commonly known as the hook-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings. The flowers are typically small, fragrant, and arranged in a spike, featuring a unique combination of colors and shapes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
