Solanum worsleyi

Solanum worsleyi, commonly known as the Worsley's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
