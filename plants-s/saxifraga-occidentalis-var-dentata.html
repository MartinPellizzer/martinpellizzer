Saxifraga occidentalis var. dentata

Saxifraga occidentalis var. dentata, commonly known as the western saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
