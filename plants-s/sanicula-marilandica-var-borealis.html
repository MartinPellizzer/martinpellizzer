Sanicula marilandica var. borealis

Sanicula marilandica var. borealis, commonly known as northern sanicle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
