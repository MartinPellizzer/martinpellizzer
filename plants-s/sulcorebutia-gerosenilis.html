Sulcorebutia gerosenilis

Sulcorebutia gerosenilis, commonly known as the Gerosa's sun rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular ornamental cactus among collectors.
