Silene jeniseensis f. dasyphylla

Silene jeniseensis f. dasyphylla, commonly known as the thick-fruit lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer, providing a tactile and visual contrast to the vibrant flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
