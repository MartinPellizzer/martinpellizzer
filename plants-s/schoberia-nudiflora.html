Schoberia nudiflora

Schoberia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Schoberia nudiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
