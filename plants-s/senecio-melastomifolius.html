Senecio melastomifolius

Senecio melastomifolius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It has a compact growth habit, making it popular as a houseplant, and prefers bright, indirect light.

Senecio melastomifolius is also noted for its relatively low maintenance and ability to produce offsets, which can be used for propagation.
