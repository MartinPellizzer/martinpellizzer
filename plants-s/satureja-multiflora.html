Satureja multiflora

Satureja multiflora, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic value.

Satureja multiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
