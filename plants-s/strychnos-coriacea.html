Strychnos coriacea

Strychnos coriacea, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, coral-like clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with ovate leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Strychnos coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
