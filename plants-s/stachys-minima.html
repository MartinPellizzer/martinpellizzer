Stachys minima

Stachys minima, commonly known as woolly betony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys minima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
