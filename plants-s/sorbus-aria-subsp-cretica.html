Sorbus aria subsp. cretica

Sorbus aria subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
