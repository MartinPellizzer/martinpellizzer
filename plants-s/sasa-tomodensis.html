Sasa tomodensis

Sasa tomodensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Sasa tomodensis is well adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded forest understories.
