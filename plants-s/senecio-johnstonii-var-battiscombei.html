Senecio johnstonii var. battiscombei

Senecio johnstonii var. battiscombei, commonly known as the Johnston's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
