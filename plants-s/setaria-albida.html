Setaria albida

Setaria albida, commonly known as silver panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale-green spikelets that are typically arranged in loose clusters along the stems. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.

Setaria albida is also used in some traditional medicinal practices due to its potential health benefits.
