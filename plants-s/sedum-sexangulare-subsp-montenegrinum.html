Sedum sexangulare subsp. montenegrinum

Sedum sexangulare subsp.

montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the Balkan region. It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and thrive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
