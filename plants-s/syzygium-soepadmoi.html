Syzygium soepadmoi

Syzygium soepadmoi, commonly known as the Java apple or *Sawo* in Indonesia, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional dishes. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium soepadmoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
