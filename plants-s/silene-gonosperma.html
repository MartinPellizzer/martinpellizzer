Silene gonosperma

Silene gonosperma, commonly known as the red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene gonosperma is an important species for pollinators and is also used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
