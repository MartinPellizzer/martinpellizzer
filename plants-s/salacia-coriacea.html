Salacia coriacea

Salacia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Salacia coriacea is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
