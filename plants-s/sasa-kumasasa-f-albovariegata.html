Sasa kumasasa f. albovariegata

Sasa kumasasa f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa kumasasa plant native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that belongs to the grass family (Poaceae). The plant is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display white or yellowish streaks against a green background. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
