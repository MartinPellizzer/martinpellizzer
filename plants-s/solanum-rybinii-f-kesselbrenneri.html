Solanum rybinii f. kesselbrenneri

Solanum rybinii f. kesselbrenneri, commonly known as the Kesselbrenner's nightshade, is a rare and unique botanical variant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
