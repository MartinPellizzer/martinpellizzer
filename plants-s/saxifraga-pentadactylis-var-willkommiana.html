Saxifraga pentadactylis var. willkommiana

Saxifraga pentadactylis var. willkommiana, commonly known as the Willkomm saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, rosette-like foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on short, upright stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments with cool, moist conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure compared to other members of the species.
