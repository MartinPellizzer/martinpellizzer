Solanum plousianthemum

Solanum plousianthemum, commonly known as the purple potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant purple foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berry-like fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its tubers, though not typically eaten, are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Solanum plousianthemum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
