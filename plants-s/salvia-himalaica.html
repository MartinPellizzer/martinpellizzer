Salvia himalaica

Salvia himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems, lance-shaped leaves, and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia himalaica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine environments.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
