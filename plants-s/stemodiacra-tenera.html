Stemodiacra tenera

Stemodiacra tenera, commonly known as the slender stemodia, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, branching structure.

It possesses a stem that is typically thin and wiry, with a smooth, slightly hairy surface. The leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged alternately along the stem. This plant is native to specific regions in Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and receives ample sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
