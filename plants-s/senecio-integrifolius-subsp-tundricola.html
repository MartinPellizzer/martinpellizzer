Senecio integrifolius subsp. tundricola

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

tundricola, commonly known as the tundra groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America. It has erect, sparsely branched stems that are usually less than 30 cm tall.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the tip of the stem. Its leaves are typically basal, with a waxy surface and entire margins.

It is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
