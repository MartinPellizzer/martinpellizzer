Sida planicaulis

Sida planicaulis, commonly known as the slender sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Sida planicaulis is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
