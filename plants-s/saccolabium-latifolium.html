Saccolabium latifolium

Saccolabium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
