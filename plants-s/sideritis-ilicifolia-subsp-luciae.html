Sideritis ilicifolia subsp. luciae

Sideritis ilicifolia subsp.

luciae, commonly known as the silky rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.

The subspecies luciae is distinguished by its more delicate foliage and slightly different flower morphology compared to the main species.
