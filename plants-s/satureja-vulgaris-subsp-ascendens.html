Satureja vulgaris subsp. ascendens

Satureja vulgaris subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
