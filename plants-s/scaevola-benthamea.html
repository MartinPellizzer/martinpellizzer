Scaevola benthamea

Scaevola benthamea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces showy, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola benthamea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
