Saxifraga montana f. rubra

Saxifraga montana f. rubra, commonly known as the red mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
