Satureja forbesii var. altitudinum

Satureja forbesii var. altitudinum, commonly known as highland savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, slightly minty flavor.

The small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
