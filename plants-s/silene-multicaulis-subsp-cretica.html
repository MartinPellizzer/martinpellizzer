Silene multicaulis subsp. cretica

Silene multicaulis subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the island of Crete. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
