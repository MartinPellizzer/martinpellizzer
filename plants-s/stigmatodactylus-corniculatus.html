Stigmatodactylus corniculatus

Stigmatodactylus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble those of the common dandelion. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single, erect flowering stem. Its scientific name reflects its unique floral structure, with "corniculatus" referring to the horn-like appearance of its flower.

Stigmatodactylus corniculatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
