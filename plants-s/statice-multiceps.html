Statice multiceps

Statice multiceps, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of tiny, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice multiceps is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate maritime conditions.
