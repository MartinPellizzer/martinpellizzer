Solanum circaeifolium f. lobatum

Solanum circaeifolium f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobate nightshade, is a variant of the annual shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves and small, typically purple or yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
