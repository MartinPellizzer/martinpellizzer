Senna charlesiana

Senna charlesiana, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna charlesiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
