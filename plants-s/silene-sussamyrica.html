Silene sussamyrica

Silene sussamyrica, commonly known as the Sussamyric catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold, dry environments.

Silene sussamyrica is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
