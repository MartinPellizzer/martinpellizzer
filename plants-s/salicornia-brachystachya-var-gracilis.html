Salicornia brachystachya var. gracilis

Salicornia brachystachya var. gracilis, commonly known as sea beans or glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or pink, and its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.

Salicornia brachystachya var. gracilis is also used in culinary applications for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
