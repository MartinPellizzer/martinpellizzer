Streblus asper var. monoicus

Streblus asper var. monoicus, commonly known as the rough ash or white ash, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are yellow when mature.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
