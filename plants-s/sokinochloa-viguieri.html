Sokinochloa viguieri

Sokinochloa viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in swampy areas of tropical regions.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
