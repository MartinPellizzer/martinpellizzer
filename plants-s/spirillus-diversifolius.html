Spirillus diversifolius

Spirillus diversifolius, commonly known as the spiral-leaf spirillus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Spirillaceae family and is native to tropical regions where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are broad and exhibit a variety of colors, ranging from deep green to vibrant red, depending on the season. Spirillus diversifolius is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers, though small, are typically clustered and can produce a subtle fragrance during the evening hours.
