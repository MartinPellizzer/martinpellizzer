Spiraea denudata

Spiraea denudata, commonly known as the naked spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color during the growing season. Its flowers are produced in terminal panicles, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

Spiraea denudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
