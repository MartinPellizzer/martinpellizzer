Silene lomalasinensis

Silene lomalasinensis, commonly known as the Loma Linda catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
