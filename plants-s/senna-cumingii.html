Senna cumingii

Senna cumingii, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery segments.

Senna cumingii is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
