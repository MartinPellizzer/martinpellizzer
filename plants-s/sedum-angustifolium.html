Sedum angustifolium

Sedum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sedum angustifolium is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.
