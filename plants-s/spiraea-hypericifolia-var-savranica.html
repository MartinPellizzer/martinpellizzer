Spiraea hypericifolia var. savranica

Spiraea hypericifolia var. savranica, commonly known as the Savran spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
