Sabina squamata var. wilsonii

Sabina squamata var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's Chinese juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
