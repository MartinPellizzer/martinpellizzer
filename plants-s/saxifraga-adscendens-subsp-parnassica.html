Saxifraga adscendens subsp. parnassica

Saxifraga adscendens subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the high mountain regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The plant features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

This species is adapted to cold, harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
