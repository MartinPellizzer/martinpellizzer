Spergularia rubra subsp. marginata

Spergularia rubra subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas or dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and is known for its resilience in arid conditions.
