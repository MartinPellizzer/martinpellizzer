Saussurea chetchozensis var. glabrescens

Saussurea chetchozensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Tibetan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
