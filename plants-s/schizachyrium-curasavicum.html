Schizachyrium curasavicum

Schizachyrium curasavicum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in dry to moist prairie environments.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery-red in the fall, making it a visually striking plant in native landscapes. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads in the autumn, which contribute to its ornamental value and aid in seed dispersal. Little bluestem is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
