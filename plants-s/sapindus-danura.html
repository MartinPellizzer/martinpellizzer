Sapindus danura

Sapindus danura, commonly known as the Indian soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The fruit is a spherical, spiny capsule that contains seeds surrounded by a white, soapy pulp. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various cultures.
