Syringa emodi var. aurea

Syringa emodi var. aurea, commonly known as the golden mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
