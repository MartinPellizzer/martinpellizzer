Syzygium perpuncticulatum var. brachythyrsum

Syzygium perpuncticulatum var. brachythyrsum, commonly known as the spotted gum tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often displays a distinctive red or pinkish hue. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green upper surface. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
