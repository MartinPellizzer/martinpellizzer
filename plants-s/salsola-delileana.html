Salsola delileana

Salsola delileana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, and it has a robust root system adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Salsola delileana is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal water availability.
