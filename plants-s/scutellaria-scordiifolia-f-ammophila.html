Scutellaria scordiifolia f. ammophila

Scutellaria scordiifolia f. ammophila, commonly known as the sand grass sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is adapted to sandy or coastal environments, exhibiting a low-growing habit to withstand wind and salt spray. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its specialized habitat and potential medicinal properties.
