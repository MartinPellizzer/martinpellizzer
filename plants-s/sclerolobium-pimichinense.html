Sclerolobium pimichinense

Sclerolobium pimichinense, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Sclerolobium pimichinense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
