Swartzia radiale

Swartzia radiale, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. Swartzia radiale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warmer climates.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a versatile and popular choice for landscaping.
