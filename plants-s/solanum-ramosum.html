Solanum ramosum

Solanum ramosum, commonly known as the wild tomato or golden tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

Solanum ramosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
