Senecio cinereus

Senecio cinereus, commonly known as grayweed or dusty grayweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio cinereus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
