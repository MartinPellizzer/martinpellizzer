Satureja spicata

Satureja spicata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces aromatic, slightly minty leaves.

Satureja spicata is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a seasoning in cooking.
