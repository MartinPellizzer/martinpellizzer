Saussurea triptera var. mikurasimensis

Saussurea triptera var. mikurasimensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme altitudes and cold climates. Its fibrous roots and hardy nature make it well-suited for harsh mountainous conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact floral structure compared to the typical form of Saussurea triptera.
