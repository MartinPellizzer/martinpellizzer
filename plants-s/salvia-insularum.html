Salvia insularum

Salvia insularum, commonly known as the island sage, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia insularum is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
