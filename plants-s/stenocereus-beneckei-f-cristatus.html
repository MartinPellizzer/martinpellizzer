Stenocereus beneckei f. cristatus

Stenocereus beneckei f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate organ pipe cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, ribbed stems that form a tree-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its crinkled, ridged appearance is due to a natural mutation called "cristate," which gives it a unique, ornamental look.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
