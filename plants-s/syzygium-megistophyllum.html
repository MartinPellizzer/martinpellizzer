Syzygium megistophyllum

Syzygium megistophyllum, commonly known as the large-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by large, woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. Its fruit is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
