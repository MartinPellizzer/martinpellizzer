Sisymbrium crassipes

Sisymbrium crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in racemes during the spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Sisymbrium crassipes is also noted for its role in ecological succession and as a food source for various insects.
