Stellaria glochidisperma

Stellaria glochidisperma, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender stems and small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in harsh, cold environments.

Stellaria glochidisperma is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
