Sagittaria palifolia var. heterophylla

Sagittaria palifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a wetland plant characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, with a pointed tip, and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

This variant is known for its adaptability to different aquatic habitats and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
