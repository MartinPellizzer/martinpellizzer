Salix × sacharowii

Salix × sacharowii, commonly known as the Russian willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found near rivers, streams, and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces catkins as its primary floral structure. Salix × sacharowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its genetic diversity and resilience in different climates.
