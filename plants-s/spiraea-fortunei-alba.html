Spiraea fortunei alba

Spiraea fortunei alba, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has arching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and showy floral display.
