Scirpus × duvalii

Scirpus × duvalii, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant that thrives in marshy and aquatic environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Scirpus americanus and Scirpus olneyi, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant forms dense stands with tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are typically pink or purplish in color.

Scirpus × duvalii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
