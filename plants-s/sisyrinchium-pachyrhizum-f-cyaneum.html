Sisyrinchium pachyrhizum f. cyaneum

Sisyrinchium pachyrhizum f. cyaneum, commonly known as the blue-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to regions of the southwestern United States. It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, rhizomatous root system.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
