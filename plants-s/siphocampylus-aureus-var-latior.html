Siphocampylus aureus var. latior

Siphocampylus aureus var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are broad and slightly hairy, with a yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal spikes. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
