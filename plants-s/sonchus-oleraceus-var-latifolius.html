Sonchus oleraceus var. latifolius

Sonchus oleraceus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive white vein pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
