Sarcoyucca macrocarpa

Sarcoyucca macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and lined with sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Sarcoyucca macrocarpa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
