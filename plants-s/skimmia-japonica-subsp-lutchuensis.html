Skimmia japonica subsp. lutchuensis

Skimmia japonica subsp.

lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu skimmia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
