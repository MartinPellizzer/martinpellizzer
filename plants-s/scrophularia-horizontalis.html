Scrophularia horizontalis

Scrophularia horizontalis, commonly known as the creeping scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, covered with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
