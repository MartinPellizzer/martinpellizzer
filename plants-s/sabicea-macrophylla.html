Sabicea macrophylla

Sabicea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sabicea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
