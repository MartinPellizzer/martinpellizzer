Silene douglasii

Silene douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It has basal leaves that are often lobed and may have a bluish-gray coating.

Silene douglasii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
