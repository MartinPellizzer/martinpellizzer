Salix × pseudoglauca

Salix × pseudoglauca, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a graceful and elegant look. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-white underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
