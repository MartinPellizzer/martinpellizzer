Sarcochilus tenuifolius

Sarcochilus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed saxifrage orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, stocky rhizome and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sarcochilus tenuifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
