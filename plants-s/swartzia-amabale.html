Swartzia amabale

Swartzia amabale, commonly known as African ebony, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flattened, elliptical seeds.

Swartzia amabale plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is also used in traditional medicine.
