Sorbus × beckii

Sorbus × beckii, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its ornamental value and ecological significance.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sorbus × beckii is native to Europe and is often found in woodland habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
