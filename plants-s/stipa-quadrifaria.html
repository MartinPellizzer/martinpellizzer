Stipa quadrifaria

Stipa quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristles, which are often used for seed dispersal. Stipa quadrifaria thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
