Streptocaulon tomentosum var. latifolium

Streptocaulon tomentosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved streptocaulon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
