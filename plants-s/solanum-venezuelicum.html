Solanum venezuelicum

Solanum venezuelicum, commonly known as the Venezuelan nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its toxic properties.

Due to its potential hazards, it is important to handle the plant with care.
