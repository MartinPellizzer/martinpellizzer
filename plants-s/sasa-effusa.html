Sasa effusa

Sasa effusa, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sasa effusa is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a common understory plant in forested regions.
