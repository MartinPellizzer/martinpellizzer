Sclerolaena muricata var. birchii

Sclerolaena muricata var. birchii, commonly known as the serrated sour fig, is a variant of the sour fig plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but have a tart, sour flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its potential use in traditional medicine.
