Salvia axilliflora

Salvia axilliflora, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Salvia axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
