Solanum giganteum

Solanum giganteum, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
