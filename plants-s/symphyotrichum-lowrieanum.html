Symphyotrichum lowrieanum

Symphyotrichum lowrieanum, commonly known as Lowrie's New England aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
