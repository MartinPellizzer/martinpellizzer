Sedum paradoxum

Sedum paradoxum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its leaves, making it highly drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Sedum paradoxum is popular in rock gardens and as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
