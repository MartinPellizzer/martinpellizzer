Sagus taedigera

Sagus taedigera, commonly known as the red-flowered sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued as an ornamental species in gardens and landscapes due to its unique foliage.
