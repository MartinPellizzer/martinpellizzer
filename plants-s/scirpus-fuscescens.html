Scirpus fuscescens

Scirpus fuscescens, commonly known as blackstem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, slender, cylindrical stems that are usually dark green to reddish-brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Scirpus fuscescens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
