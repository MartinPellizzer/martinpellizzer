Sukaminea

Sukaminea, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Sukaminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
