Stereochilus dalatensis

Stereochilus dalatensis, commonly known as the Dalat stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it forms dense, low-lying mats. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
