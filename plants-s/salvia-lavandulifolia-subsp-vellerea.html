Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. vellerea

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

vellerea, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its use in herbal teas and as a source of essential oils.
