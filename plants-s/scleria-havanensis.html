Scleria havanensis

Scleria havanensis, commonly known as the Havana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its grass-like foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.
