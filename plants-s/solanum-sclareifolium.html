Solanum sclareifolium

Solanum sclareifolium, commonly known as the white star apple, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe, often containing several seeds.

Solanum sclareifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
