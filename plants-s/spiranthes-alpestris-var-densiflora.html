Spiranthes alpestris var. densiflora

Spiranthes alpestris var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to alpine and subalpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraled arrangement of pale green to white flowers along a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
