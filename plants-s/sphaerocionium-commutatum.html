Sphaerocionium commutatum

Sphaerocionium commutatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, silver-green leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
