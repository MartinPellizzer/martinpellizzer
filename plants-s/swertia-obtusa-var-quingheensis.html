Swertia obtusa var. quingheensis

Swertia obtusa var. quingheensis, commonly known as the Yunnan swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
