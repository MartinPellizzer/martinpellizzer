Salvia collinsii

Salvia collinsii, commonly known as Collins' sage, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant landscapes.
