Silene boryi var. thalackeri

Silene boryi var. thalackeri, commonly known as the Thalacker's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to cold and harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
