Spiraeanthemum bougainvillense

Spiraeanthemum bougainvillense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by these colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.
