Syzygium bernieri

Syzygium bernieri, commonly known as the Bornean apple or white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and is characterized by its smooth, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are typically pale yellow to red in color. Syzygium bernieri is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit.
