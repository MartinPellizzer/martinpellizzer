Stachytarpheta × debilis

Stachytarpheta × debilis, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic and low-maintenance qualities.
