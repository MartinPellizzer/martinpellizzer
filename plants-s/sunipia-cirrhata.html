Sunipia cirrhata

Sunipia cirrhata, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils.

Sunipia cirrhata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
