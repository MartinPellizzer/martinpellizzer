Solanum xanti var. montanum

Solanum xanti var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to red or black as they mature.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
