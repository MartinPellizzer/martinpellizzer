Sutera fragilis

Sutera fragilis, commonly known as the brittle leaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Sutera fragilis is known for its delicate, easily broken stems, which contribute to its common name.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
