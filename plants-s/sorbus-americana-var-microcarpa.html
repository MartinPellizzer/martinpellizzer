Sorbus americana var. microcarpa

Sorbus americana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
