Solanum albotomentosum

Solanum albotomentosum, commonly known as the white-hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Solanum albotomentosum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
