Scilla spetana

Scilla spetana, commonly known as the Spanish squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers on tall stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Scilla spetana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
