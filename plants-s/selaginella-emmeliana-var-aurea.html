Selaginella emmeliana var. aurea

Selaginella emmeliana var. aurea, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a striking golden hue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Selaginella emmeliana var. aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
