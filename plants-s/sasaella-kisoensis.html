Sasaella kisoensis

Sasaella kisoensis, commonly known as the Kiso sasa, is a grass-like plant native to Japan, specifically found in the mountainous regions of central Honshu.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
