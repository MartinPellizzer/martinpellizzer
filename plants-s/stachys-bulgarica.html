Stachys bulgarica

Stachys bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian hedge parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Stachys bulgarica thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
