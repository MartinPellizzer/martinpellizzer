Salvia palmeri

Salvia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and lance-shaped, with a fuzzy texture.

Salvia palmeri is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
