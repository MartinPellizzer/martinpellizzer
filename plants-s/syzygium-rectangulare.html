Syzygium rectangulare

Syzygium rectangulare, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely on the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow up to 15 meters in height.

Syzygium rectangulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
