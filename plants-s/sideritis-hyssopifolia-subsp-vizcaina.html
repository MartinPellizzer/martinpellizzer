Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. vizcaina

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

vizcaina, commonly known as the Canary Islands sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
