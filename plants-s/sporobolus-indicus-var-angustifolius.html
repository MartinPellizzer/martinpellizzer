Sporobolus indicus var. angustifolius

Sporobolus indicus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf spora, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
