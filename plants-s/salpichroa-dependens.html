Salpichroa dependens

Salpichroa dependens, commonly known as the hanging grass or the pendulous grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that hang downward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it is often found in moist, open habitats.

Salpichroa dependens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique, drooping flower clusters.
