Solanum ureyi

Solanum ureyi, commonly known as the Yucatán nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

Solanum ureyi is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.
