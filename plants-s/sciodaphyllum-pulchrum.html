Sciodaphyllum pulchrum

Sciodaphyllum pulchrum, commonly known as the radiant shadow pine, is a rare evergreen conifer native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and dark green, needle-like leaves that give it a unique, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded, forested environments. Its cones are small and inconspicuous, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss, Sciodaphyllum pulchrum is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
