Schedonorus × loliaceus

Schedonorus × loliaceus, commonly known as the fine fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing turf. The plant is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance lawns and erosion control. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in early summer, and it reproduces primarily through seed.

Schedonorus × loliaceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
