Sesleria coerulans f. solacolui

Sesleria coerulans f. solacolui, commonly known as the blue-flowered sesleria, is a rare and ornamental grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive blue hue in the flower spikes. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
