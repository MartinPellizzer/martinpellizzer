Sedum sexangulare f. elatum

Sedum sexangulare f. elatum, commonly known as the Shrub Stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant's leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly waxy texture, and they are arranged in a compact, mound-like form. It is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its low maintenance requirements.

The flowering period usually occurs in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
