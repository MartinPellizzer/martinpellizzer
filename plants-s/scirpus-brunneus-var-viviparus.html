Scirpus brunneus var. viviparus

Scirpus brunneus var. viviparus, commonly known as the brown reed or viviparous reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense stands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. One notable feature is its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which can root and grow into new plants while still attached to the parent.

This adaptation aids in its propagation in moist, often disturbed habitats.
