Senecio aquifoliaceus

Senecio aquifoliaceus, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Senecio aquifoliaceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
