Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp. pectinata

Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the woolly santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is densely covered with fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on short, branched stems. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
