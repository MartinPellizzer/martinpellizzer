Sorbus vasconica

Sorbus vasconica, commonly known as the Basque rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Sorbus vasconica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
