Sideritis kebdanensis

Sideritis kebdanensis, commonly known as the Kebdan mountain sida, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Sideritis kebdanensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies.
