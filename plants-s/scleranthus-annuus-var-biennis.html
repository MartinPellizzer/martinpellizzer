Scleranthus annuus var. biennis

Scleranthus annuus var. biennis, commonly known as the two-yearly sandspurry, is an annual to biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
