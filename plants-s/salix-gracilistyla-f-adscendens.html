Salix gracilistyla f. adscendens

Salix gracilistyla f. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending willow, is a unique variant of the slender willow species.

It is characterized by its ascending growth habit, which distinguishes it from the more typical shrub-like form of its parent species. The plant features slender, often drooping branches covered in small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wet meadows, where it can access the water it requires for survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
