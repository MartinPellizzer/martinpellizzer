Sporobolus schlechteri

Sporobolus schlechteri, commonly known as Schlechter's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Sporobolus schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
