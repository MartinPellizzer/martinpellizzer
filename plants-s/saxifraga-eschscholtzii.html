Saxifraga eschscholtzii

Saxifraga eschscholtzii, commonly known as the California saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in coastal areas or mountainous regions. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or white, clustered in a terminal inflorescence, and bloom in early spring.

Saxifraga eschscholtzii is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
