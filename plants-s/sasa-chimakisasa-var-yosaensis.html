Sasa chimakisasa var. yosaensis

Sasa chimakisasa var. yosaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or yosa bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense thickets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
