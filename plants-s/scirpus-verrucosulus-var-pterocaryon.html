Scirpus verrucosulus var. pterocaryon

Scirpus verrucosulus var. pterocaryon, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine, hair-like structures.

This variant is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to thrive in saturated soils.
