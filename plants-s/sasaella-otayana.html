Sasaella otayana

Sasaella otayana, commonly known as the Otay sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
