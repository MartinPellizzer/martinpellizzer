Salix × hugueninii

Salix × hugueninii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
