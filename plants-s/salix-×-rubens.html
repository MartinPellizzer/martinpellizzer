Salix × rubens

Salix × rubens, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that drape like curtains, creating a distinctive, graceful appearance. The plant has slender, elongated leaves that are typically bright green on top and pale white or silvery underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, streams, and wetlands.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
