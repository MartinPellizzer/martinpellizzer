Silene pardoi

Silene pardoi, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Silene pardoi plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for various insects.
