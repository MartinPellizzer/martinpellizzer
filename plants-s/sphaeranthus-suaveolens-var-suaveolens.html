Sphaeranthus suaveolens var. suaveolens

Sphaeranthus suaveolens var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces small, round, hairy fruits.

It is often found in disturbed areas and has a low-growing, mat-like structure.
