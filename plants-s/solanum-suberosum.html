Solanum suberosum

Solanum suberosum, commonly known as the rough bush tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into small, round berries containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and agricultural studies.
