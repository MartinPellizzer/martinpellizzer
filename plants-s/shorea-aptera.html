Shorea aptera

Shorea aptera, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, pale wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds.

Shorea aptera is typically found in lowland rainforests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
