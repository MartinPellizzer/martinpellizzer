Sida coccinea

Sida coccinea, commonly known as scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, velvety bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or disturbed soils. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Sida coccinea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and adaptability.
