Sempervivella sedoides

Sempervivella sedoides, commonly known as the stonecrop sedoides, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered rare and requires careful cultivation to ensure its survival.
