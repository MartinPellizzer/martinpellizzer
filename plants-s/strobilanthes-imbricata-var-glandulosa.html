Strobilanthes imbricata var. glandulosa

Strobilanthes imbricata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular strobilanthes, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and tubular, often arranged in dense spikes.

This variant is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
