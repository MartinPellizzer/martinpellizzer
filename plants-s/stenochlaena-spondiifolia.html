Stenochlaena spondiifolia

Stenochlaena spondiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.
