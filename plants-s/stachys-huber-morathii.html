Stachys huber-morathii

Stachys huber-morathii, commonly known as the Huber-Morath's betony, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stachys huber-morathii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
