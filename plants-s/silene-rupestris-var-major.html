Silene rupestris var. major

Silene rupestris var. major, commonly known as the Great Rock-Cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Silene rupestris var. major is a hardy perennial that is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
