Silene gallica var. rosella

Silene gallica var. rosella, commonly known as the Rose Campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color.

Silene gallica var. rosella is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
