Saccolabium setulense

Saccolabium setulense, commonly known as the hairy saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy structures. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Saccolabium setulense is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
