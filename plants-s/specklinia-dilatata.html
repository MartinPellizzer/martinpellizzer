Specklinia dilatata

Specklinia dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed polka dot plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with distinctive white or cream-colored spots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Specklinia dilatata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
