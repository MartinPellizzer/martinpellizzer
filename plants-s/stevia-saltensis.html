Stevia saltensis

Stevia saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush or saltbush stevia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food products.

Stevia saltensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in challenging conditions.
