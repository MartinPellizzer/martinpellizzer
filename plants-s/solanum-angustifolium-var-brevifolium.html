Solanum angustifolium var. brevifolium

Solanum angustifolium var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it its varietal name "brevifolium.". The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
