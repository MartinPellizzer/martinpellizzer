Silene minae

Silene minae, commonly known as the Jordanian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Silene minae has small, ovate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
