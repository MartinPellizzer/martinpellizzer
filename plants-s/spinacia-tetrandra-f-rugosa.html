Spinacia tetrandra f. rugosa

Spinacia tetrandra f. rugosa, commonly known as the curly leaf spinach, is a cultivated form of the common spinach plant.

It is characterized by its crinkled, textured leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is a cool-season crop, thriving in temperate climates and requiring well-drained soil. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including iron and vitamins A and C, making it a valuable addition to the diet.

This variety is often preferred for its mild flavor and tender texture compared to other spinach types.
