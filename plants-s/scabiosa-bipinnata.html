Scabiosa bipinnata

Scabiosa bipinnata, commonly known as the bi-pinnate scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Scabiosa bipinnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
