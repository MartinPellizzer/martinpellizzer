Stachys rupestris

Stachys rupestris, commonly known as rock mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in sunny habitats.

Stachys rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
