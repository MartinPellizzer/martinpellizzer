Saurauia leucocarpa var. angustifolia

Saurauia leucocarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed white-fruited saurauia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, white, fleshy berries in the summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
