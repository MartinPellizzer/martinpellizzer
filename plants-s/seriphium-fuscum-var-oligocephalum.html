Seriphium fuscum var. oligocephalum

Seriphium fuscum var. oligocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed hawksbeard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
