Sideritis kurdica

Sideritis kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish mountain tea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis kurdica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
