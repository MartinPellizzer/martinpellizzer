Sporobolus nudiramus

Sporobolus nudiramus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of lightweight seeds that are easily carried by the wind, enhancing its ability to spread across wetland habitats.

The botanical features of Sporobolus nudiramus make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
