Salvia pratensis subsp. pozegensis

Salvia pratensis subsp.

pozegensis, commonly known as the Pozega bluebottle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its square stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
