Sclerocactus terrae-canyonae

Sclerocactus terrae-canyonae, commonly known as the canyon rock cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky crevices and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
