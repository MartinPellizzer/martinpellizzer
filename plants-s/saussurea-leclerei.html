Saussurea leclerei

Saussurea leclerei, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
