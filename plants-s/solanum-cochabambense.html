Solanum cochabambense

Solanum cochabambense, commonly known as the Cochabamba nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.
