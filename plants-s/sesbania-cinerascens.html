Sesbania cinerascens

Sesbania cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in some regions.
