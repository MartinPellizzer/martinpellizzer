Syzygium munroi

Syzygium munroi, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Syzygium munroi is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
