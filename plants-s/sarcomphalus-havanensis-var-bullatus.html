Sarcomphalus havanensis var. bullatus

Sarcomphalus havanensis var. bullatus, commonly known as the Cuban starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and succulent, allowing it to retain moisture in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
