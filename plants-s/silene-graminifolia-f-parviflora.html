Silene graminifolia f. parviflora

Silene graminifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
