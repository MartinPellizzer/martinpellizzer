Sophora exigua var. exigua

Sophora exigua var. exigua, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height under favorable conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
