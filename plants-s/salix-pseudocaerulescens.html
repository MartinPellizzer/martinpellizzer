Salix pseudocaerulescens

Salix pseudocaerulescens, commonly known as the blue poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and distinctive blue-green foliage that becomes more pronounced in the summer. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its unique appearance.

Salix pseudocaerulescens is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
