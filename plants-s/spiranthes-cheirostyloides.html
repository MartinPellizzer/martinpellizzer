Spiranthes cheirostyloides

Spiranthes cheirostyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence composed of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, slender stem.

This orchid is often found in association with other orchid species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
