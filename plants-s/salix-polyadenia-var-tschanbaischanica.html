Salix polyadenia var. tschanbaischanica

Salix polyadenia var. tschanbaischanica, commonly known as the Tschanbaischanica willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically pollinated by wind, a common trait among willow species. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often found in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations.
