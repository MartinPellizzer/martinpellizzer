Scabiosa pallida

Scabiosa pallida, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Scabiosa pallida is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
