Sanguisorba annua

Sanguisorba annua, commonly known as the annual sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in panicle clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Sanguisorba annua is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
