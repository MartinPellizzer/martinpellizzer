Salix annulifera

Salix annulifera, commonly known as the ringed willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various Arctic and subarctic wildlife.
