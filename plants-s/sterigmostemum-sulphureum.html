Sterigmostemum sulphureum

Sterigmostemum sulphureum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal remedies.
