Spermacoce commutata

Spermacoce commutata, commonly known as the small false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
