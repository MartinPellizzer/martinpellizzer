Sinningia pusilla

Sinningia pusilla, commonly known as the miniature gloxinia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features tubular, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, which bloom in the spring. It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sinningia pusilla thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
