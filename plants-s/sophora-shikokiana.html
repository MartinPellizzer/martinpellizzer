Sophora shikokiana

Sophora shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The seeds of Sophora shikokiana are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by wind or animals.
