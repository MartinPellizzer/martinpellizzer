Sisymbrium millefolium

Sisymbrium millefolium, commonly known as sweet rocket, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Sisymbrium millefolium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
