Sedum malacophyllum var. japonicum

Sedum malacophyllum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for a variety of climates.
