Senecio multicaulis var. dissectus

Senecio multicaulis var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, blue-gray leaves with a distinctive dissected appearance, giving it a feathery look. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
