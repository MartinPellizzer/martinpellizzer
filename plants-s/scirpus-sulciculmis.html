Scirpus sulciculmis

Scirpus sulciculmis, commonly known as the ridged flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbed leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. Scirpus sulciculmis is adapted to moist or water-saturated soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
