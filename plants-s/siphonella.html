Siphonella

Siphonella, commonly known as a type of marine algae, is a genus of filamentous, colonial cyanobacteria.

These organisms are often found in coastal waters and are capable of photosynthesis, contributing to primary production in marine ecosystems. Siphonella species are characterized by their long, branching filaments and the presence of heterocysts, which are specialized cells for nitrogen fixation. They play a significant role in nutrient cycling and are important indicators of water quality in aquatic environments.

Due to their ability to form blooms, they can also impact marine biodiversity and water clarity.
