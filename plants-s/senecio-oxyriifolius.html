Senecio oxyriifolius

Senecio oxyriifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish or grayish hue, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
