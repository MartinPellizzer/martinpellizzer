Senecio usambarensis

Senecio usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the East African mountains, particularly Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within montane forests.
