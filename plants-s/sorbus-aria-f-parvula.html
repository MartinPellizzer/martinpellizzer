Sorbus aria f. parvula

Sorbus aria f. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a compact form of the common whitebeam, characterized by its smaller size and dense foliage. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
