Sisymbrium doumetianum

Sisymbrium doumetianum, commonly known as the Doumetian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its seeds are small and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
