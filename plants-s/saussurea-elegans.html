Saussurea elegans

Saussurea elegans, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow or the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea elegans is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
