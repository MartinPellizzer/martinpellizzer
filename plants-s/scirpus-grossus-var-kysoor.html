Scirpus grossus var. kysoor

Scirpus grossus var. kysoor, commonly known as the Kysoor bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist environments such as marshes and pond margins.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
