Stevia collina var. hyptifolia

Stevia collina var. hyptifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweet herb, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with tiny, sweet-tasting glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Stevia collina var. hyptifolia is valued for its natural sweetness and is often used as a sugar substitute in food and beverages.
