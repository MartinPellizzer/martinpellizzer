Saussurea gyacaensis

Saussurea gyacaensis, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
