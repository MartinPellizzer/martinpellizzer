Sagina micropetala f. intricata

Sagina micropetala f. intricata, commonly known as the intricate sandwort, is a low-growing succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
