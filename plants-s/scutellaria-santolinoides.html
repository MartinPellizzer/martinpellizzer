Scutellaria santolinoides

Scutellaria santolinoides, commonly known as the Santolin-like skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Scutellaria santolinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
