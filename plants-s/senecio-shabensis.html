Senecio shabensis

Senecio shabensis, commonly known as Shabean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

Senecio shabensis is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
