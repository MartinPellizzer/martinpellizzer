Seringia velutina

Seringia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf seringia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

Seringia velutina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
