Sasa stenophylla

Sasa stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Sasa stenophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.
