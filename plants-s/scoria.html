Scoria

Scoria, commonly known as volcanic rock, is not a plant but a type of igneous rock formed from volcanic activity.

It is characterized by its dark color, porous texture, and lightweight nature. Scoria is often used in construction and as a lightweight aggregate in concrete. It can also be found in soil as a result of volcanic eruptions.

While it is not a botanical subject, it plays a role in soil composition and can support plant growth in certain conditions.
