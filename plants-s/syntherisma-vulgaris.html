Syntherisma vulgaris

Syntherisma vulgaris, commonly known as the common sunpetal, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit with oval, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Syntherisma vulgaris is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
