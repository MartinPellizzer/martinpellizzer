Securidaca atroviolacea

Securidaca atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violaceum or black velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
