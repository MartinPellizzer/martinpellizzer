Senecio gelidus f. latifolius

Senecio gelidus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf Senecio, is a variant of the Senecio gelidus species native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
