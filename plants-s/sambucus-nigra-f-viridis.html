Sambucus nigra f. viridis

Sambucus nigra f. viridis, commonly known as the green elderberry, is a variant of the common elderberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers, which differ from the typical red or white blooms of the species. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible when fully ripe. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display.
