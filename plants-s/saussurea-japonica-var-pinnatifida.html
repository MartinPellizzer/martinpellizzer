Saussurea japonica var. pinnatifida

Saussurea japonica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as Japanese yarrow or Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
