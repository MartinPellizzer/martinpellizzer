Sophora tetraptera

Sophora tetraptera, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of fragrant, purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it the species name "tetraptera.". Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
