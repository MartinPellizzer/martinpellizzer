Satyrium dahlianum

Satyrium dahlianum, commonly known as the Dahlian's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Due to habitat destruction, Satyrium dahlianum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to protect its dwindling populations.
