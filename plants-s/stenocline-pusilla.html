Stenocline pusilla

Stenocline pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
