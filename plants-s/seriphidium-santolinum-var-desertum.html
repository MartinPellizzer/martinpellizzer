Seriphidium santolinum var. desertum

Seriphidium santolinum var. desertum, commonly known as the desert anthemwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often bluish-gray, with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the spring.

Seriphidium santolinum var. desertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
