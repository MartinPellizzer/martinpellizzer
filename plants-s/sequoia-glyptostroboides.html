Sequoia glyptostroboides

Sequoia glyptostroboides, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a towering coniferous tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its massive size, with some individuals reaching heights of over 300 feet and diameters exceeding 20 feet. The tree has a distinctive reddish-brown bark that is thick and furrowed, providing protection against forest fires. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
