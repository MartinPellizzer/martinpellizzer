Sinningia

Sinningia, commonly known as the gloxinia, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Sinningia species are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their ornamental value. They have tuberous roots that allow them to survive in cooler temperatures, making them popular among indoor gardeners.

The leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.
