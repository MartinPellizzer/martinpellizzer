Scutellaria chorassanica

Scutellaria chorassanica, commonly known as the Afghan skullcap, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Scutellaria chorassanica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
