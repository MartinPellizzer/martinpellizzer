Serruria villosa

Serruria villosa, commonly known as the velvet bushveld daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun. Its scientific name reflects its serrated leaf margins and hairy texture.

Serruria villosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
