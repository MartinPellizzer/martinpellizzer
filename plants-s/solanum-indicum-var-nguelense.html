Solanum indicum var. nguelense

Solanum indicum var. nguelense, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds inside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
