Siphocampylus onagrius var. undulatus

Siphocampylus onagrius var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulated willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
