Seseli libanotis var. bipinnata

Seseli libanotis var. bipinnata, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot and upright stems that are often covered in dense, white to grayish hairs. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a bi-pinnate structure, giving the plant its specific epithet. Its showy, yellow flowers appear in clusters at the top of the stems during the summer months.

This species is adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
