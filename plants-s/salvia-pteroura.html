Salvia pteroura

Salvia pteroura, commonly known as the golden salvia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
