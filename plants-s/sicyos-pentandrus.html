Sicyos pentandrus

Sicyos pentandrus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, which are divided into numerous small, finger-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing water surfaces and providing habitat for aquatic life. Its reproductive structures include a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family, and it produces both male and female flowers.

Sicyos pentandrus is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
