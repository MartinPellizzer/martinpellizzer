Silene inflata var. patagonica

Silene inflata var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like capsules that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces a distinctive, showy bloom.

Silene inflata var. patagonica is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in open landscapes.
