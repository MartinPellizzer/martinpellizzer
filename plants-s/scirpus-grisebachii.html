Scirpus grisebachii

Scirpus grisebachii, commonly known as the gray bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a grayish bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base.

Scirpus grisebachii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
