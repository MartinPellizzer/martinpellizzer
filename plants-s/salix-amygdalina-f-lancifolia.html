Salix amygdalina f. lancifolia

Salix amygdalina f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf white willow, is a botanical variant of the white willow species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and small, inconspicuous flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found near water bodies.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
