Salsola tuberculatiformis

Salsola tuberculatiformis, commonly known as the crested sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, saline soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Salsola tuberculatiformis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
