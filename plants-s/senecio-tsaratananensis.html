Senecio tsaratananensis

Senecio tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana groundsel, is an endemic plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the humid, mountainous climate of its native habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
