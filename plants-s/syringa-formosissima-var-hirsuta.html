Syringa formosissima var. hirsuta

Syringa formosissima var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its varietal name. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
