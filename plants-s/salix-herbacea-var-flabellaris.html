Salix herbacea var. flabellaris

Salix herbacea var. flabellaris, commonly known as the flabellate willow, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it is often found in moist or wetland habitats. It is a member of the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is known for its adaptability to wet environments.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral structure, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
