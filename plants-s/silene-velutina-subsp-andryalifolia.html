Silene velutina subsp. andryalifolia

Silene velutina subsp.

andryalifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a velvety appearance.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
