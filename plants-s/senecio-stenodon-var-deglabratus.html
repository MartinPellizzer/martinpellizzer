Senecio stenodon var. deglabratus

Senecio stenodon var. deglabratus, commonly known as the naked-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically linear and may have a slight pubescence, though they are generally less hairy than those of the typical variety. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
