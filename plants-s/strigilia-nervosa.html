Strigilia nervosa

Strigilia nervosa, commonly known as the veined grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Strigilia nervosa is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in grasslands and disturbed habitats.
