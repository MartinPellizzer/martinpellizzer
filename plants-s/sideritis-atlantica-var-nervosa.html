Sideritis atlantica var. nervosa

Sideritis atlantica var. nervosa, commonly known as the Atlantic sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
