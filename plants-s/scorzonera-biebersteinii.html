Scorzonera biebersteinii

Scorzonera biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive bluish-gray coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera biebersteinii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
