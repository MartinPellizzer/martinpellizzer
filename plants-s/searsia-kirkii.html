Searsia kirkii

Searsia kirkii, commonly known as the yellow bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible for some animals but not for humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Searsia kirkii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
