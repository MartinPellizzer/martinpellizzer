Salix × capnoides

Salix × capnoides, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
