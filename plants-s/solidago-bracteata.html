Solidago bracteata

Solidago bracteata, commonly known as bracted goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems covered in yellow flower clusters. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Bracted goldenrod is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
