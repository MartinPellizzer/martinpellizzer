Simenia

Simenia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and compact, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often with a rosette of leaves at the base. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Simenia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
