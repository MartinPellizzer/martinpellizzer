Selenicereus grandiflorus var. irradians

Selenicereus grandiflorus var. irradians, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is a member of the Cactaceae family. The plant is notable for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
