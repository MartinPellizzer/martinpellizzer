Selenicereus brevispinus

Selenicereus brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its short, spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its succulent stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant's unique botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical research.
