Salix × dichroa var. oblongata

Salix × dichroa var. oblongata, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The leaves are elongated and narrow, with a bluish-green upper surface and a silvery underside, contributing to its striking visual appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
