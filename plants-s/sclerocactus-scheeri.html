Sclerocactus scheeri

Sclerocactus scheeri, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, covered with a thick layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Sclerocactus scheeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
