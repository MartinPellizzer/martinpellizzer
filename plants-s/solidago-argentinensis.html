Solidago argentinensis

Solidago argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago argentinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
