Sideritis scordioides var. chamaedryfolia

Sideritis scordioides var. chamaedryfolia, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

This variant is distinguished by its foliage resembling that of the genus Chamaedrya, giving it its specific epithet.
