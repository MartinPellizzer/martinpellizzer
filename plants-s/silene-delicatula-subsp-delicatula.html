Silene delicatula subsp. delicatula

Silene delicatula subsp.

delicatula, commonly known as the slender campion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
