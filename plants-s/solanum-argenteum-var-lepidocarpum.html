Solanum argenteum var. lepidocarpum

Solanum argenteum var. lepidocarpum, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
