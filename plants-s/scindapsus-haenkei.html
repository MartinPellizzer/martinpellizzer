Scindapsus haenkei

Scindapsus haenkei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-green leaves that often feature a variegated pattern. The plant is a climbing or trailing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on supports. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Scindapsus haenkei is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
