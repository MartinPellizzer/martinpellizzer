Sterculia ivira

Sterculia ivira, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds, which are used to make shea butter. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic contributions, particularly in local communities.
