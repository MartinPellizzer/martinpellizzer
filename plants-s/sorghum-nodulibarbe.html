Sorghum nodulibarbe

Sorghum nodulibarbe, commonly known as the nodular beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted inflorescence and hairy nodes along the stem. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Sorghum nodulibarbe is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
