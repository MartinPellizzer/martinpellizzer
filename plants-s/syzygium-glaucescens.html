Syzygium glaucescens

Syzygium glaucescens, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Syzygium glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
