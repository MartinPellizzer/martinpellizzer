Santiria laevigata f. glabrifolia

Santiria laevigata f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved santiria, is a variant of the Santiria laevigata species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in terminal inflorescences, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of this plant make it of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
