Syngonanthus crispus

Syngonanthus crispus, commonly known as the hairy false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Syngonanthus crispus is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
