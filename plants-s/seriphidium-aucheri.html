Seriphidium aucheri

Seriphidium aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and has a compact, bushy growth habit.
