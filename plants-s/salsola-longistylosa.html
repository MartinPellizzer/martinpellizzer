Salsola longistylosa

Salsola longistylosa, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is a shrub or perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in desert environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or barren areas effectively.

Salsola longistylosa is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water and high temperatures.
