Solanum gnaphalocarpum

Solanum gnaphalocarpum, commonly known as the sandhill nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish berries. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and often have a slight pubescence.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
