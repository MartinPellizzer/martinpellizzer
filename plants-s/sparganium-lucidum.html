Sparganium lucidum

Sparganium lucidum, commonly known as the golden club, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble small umbels. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in shallow, freshwater environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.

Sparganium lucidum plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing habitat and food for various insects and aquatic organisms.
