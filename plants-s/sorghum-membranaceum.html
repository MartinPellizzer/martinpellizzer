Sorghum membranaceum

Sorghum membranaceum, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Sorghum membranaceum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in areas with high temperatures and low rainfall.
