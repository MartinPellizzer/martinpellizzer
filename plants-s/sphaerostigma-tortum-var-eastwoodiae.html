Sphaerostigma tortum var. eastwoodiae

Sphaerostigma tortum var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's starburst cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its spherical stem with a star-like pattern of raised tubercles, which are covered in short, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is particularly prized by cactus enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
