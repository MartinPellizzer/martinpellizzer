Sorghum virgatum

Sorghum virgatum, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its tall, slender stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Sorghum virgatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also known for its drought tolerance.
