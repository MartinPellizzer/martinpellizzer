Silene cucubalus var. brachiata

Silene cucubalus var. brachiata, commonly known as the long-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
