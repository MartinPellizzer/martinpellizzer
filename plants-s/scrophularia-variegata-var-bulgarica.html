Scrophularia variegata var. bulgarica

Scrophularia variegata var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers from June to August. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the Scrophulariaceae family.
