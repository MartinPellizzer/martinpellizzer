Solanum erianthum var. adulterinum

Solanum erianthum var. adulterinum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), sharing botanical traits with other members of the genus Solanum.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
