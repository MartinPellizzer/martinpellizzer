Saxifraga wolleana

Saxifraga wolleana, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring or early summer, typically arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Saxifraga wolleana is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
