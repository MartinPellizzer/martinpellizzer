Serjania simulata

Serjania simulata, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it an attractive species for ornamental purposes.
