Selaginella nayarii

Selaginella nayarii, commonly known as the Mexican spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic feature of its ancient lineage.

Selaginella nayarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
