Syzygium bonii

Syzygium bonii, commonly known as the Bornean apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Syzygium bonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native region.
