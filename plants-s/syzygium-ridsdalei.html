Syzygium ridsdalei

Syzygium ridsdalei, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Syzygium ridsdalei thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
