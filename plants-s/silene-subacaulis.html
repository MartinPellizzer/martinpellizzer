Silene subacaulis

Silene subacaulis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Silene subacaulis thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra or mountainous habitats.
