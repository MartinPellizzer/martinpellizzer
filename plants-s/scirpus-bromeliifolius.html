Scirpus bromeliifolius

Scirpus bromeliifolius, commonly known as the bromeliad sedge, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus bromeliifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
