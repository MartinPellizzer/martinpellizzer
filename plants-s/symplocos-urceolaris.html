Symplocos urceolaris

Symplocos urceolaris, commonly known as the urn-shaped evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos urceolaris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
