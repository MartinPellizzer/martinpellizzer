Strychnodaphne salicifolia

Strychnodaphne salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved strychnodaphne, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries such as Thailand and Malaysia. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, some of which are toxic.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different soil types.
