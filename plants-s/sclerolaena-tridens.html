Sclerolaena tridens

Sclerolaena tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with three distinct teeth along the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Sclerolaena tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
