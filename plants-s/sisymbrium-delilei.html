Sisymbrium delilei

Sisymbrium delilei, commonly known as the Delile's mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants known for their characteristic four-petaled flowers and seed pods.
