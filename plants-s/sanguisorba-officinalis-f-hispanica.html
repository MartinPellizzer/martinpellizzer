Sanguisorba officinalis f. hispanica

Sanguisorba officinalis f. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish sorrel, is a variant of the sorrel plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
