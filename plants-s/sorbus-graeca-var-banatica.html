Sorbus graeca var. banatica

Sorbus graeca var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat cherry or Banat rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
