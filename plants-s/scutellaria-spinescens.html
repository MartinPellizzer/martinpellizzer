Scutellaria spinescens

Scutellaria spinescens, commonly known as the spiny skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with fine, spiny hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive hooded upper lip.

Scutellaria spinescens is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems.
