Scutellaria pilosa var. ovalifolia

Scutellaria pilosa var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
