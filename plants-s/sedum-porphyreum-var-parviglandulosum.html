Sedum porphyreum var. parviglandulosum

Sedum porphyreum var. parviglandulosum, commonly known as the compact sedum, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and oval, arranged in a dense rosette pattern.

It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
