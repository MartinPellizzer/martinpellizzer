Syzygium pulchellum

Syzygium pulchellum, commonly known as the golden apple gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in rainforests and moist sclerophyll forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that matures in late summer.

Syzygium pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
