Sorbus pseudobakonyensis

Sorbus pseudobakonyensis, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a species of rowan tree native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
