Sophora pentaphylla

Sophora pentaphylla, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
