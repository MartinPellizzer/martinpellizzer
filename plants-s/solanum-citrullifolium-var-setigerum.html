Solanum citrullifolium var. setigerum

Solanum citrullifolium var. setigerum, commonly known as the hairy citrullifolium tomato, is a variant of the citrullifolium tomato species.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and fruit that resemble small, yellow-orange tomatoes. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to South America. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The hairy texture of its leaves is a distinguishing feature, setting it apart from other tomato varieties.
