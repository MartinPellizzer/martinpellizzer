Salvia speciosa

Salvia speciosa, commonly known as the showy sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia speciosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
