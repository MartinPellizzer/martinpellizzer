Scorpiurus laevigatus var. glaberrimus

Scorpiurus laevigatus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smoothleaf scorpiurus, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Scorpiurus laevigatus var. glaberrimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
