Salvia arnassensis

Salvia arnassensis, commonly known as the Arnold's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia arnassensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
