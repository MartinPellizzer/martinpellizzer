Sarcocaulon flavescens

Sarcocaulon flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flax lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive yellowish-green thallus with a velvety texture and is often found on decaying wood or bark. The lichen's structure consists of a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in forest ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
