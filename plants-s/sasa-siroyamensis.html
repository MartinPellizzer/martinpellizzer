Sasa siroyamensis

Sasa siroyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa siroyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
