Solanum tuberosum var. chubutense

Solanum tuberosum var. chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut potato, is a botanical variety native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common potatoes. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and features simple, alternate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local culinary traditions.
