Silene macrostemma

Silene macrostemma, commonly known as the showy silene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Silene macrostemma plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
