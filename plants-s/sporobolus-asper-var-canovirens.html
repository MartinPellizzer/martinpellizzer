Sporobolus asper var. canovirens

Sporobolus asper var. canovirens, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a characteristic feature of many grasses in its habitat.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping.
