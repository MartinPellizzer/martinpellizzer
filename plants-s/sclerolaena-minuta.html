Sclerolaena minuta

Sclerolaena minuta, commonly known as the dwarf chaffflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Sclerolaena minuta is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species well adapted to its native environment.
