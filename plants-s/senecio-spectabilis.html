Senecio spectabilis

Senecio spectabilis, commonly known as the showy senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio spectabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
