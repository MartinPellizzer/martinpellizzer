Saccharum narenga var. aristata

Saccharum narenga var. aristata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a variant of the genus Saccharum, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silvery sheen on the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
