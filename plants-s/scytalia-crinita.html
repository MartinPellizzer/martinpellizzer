Scytalia crinita

Scytalia crinita, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herb native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scytalia crinita thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
