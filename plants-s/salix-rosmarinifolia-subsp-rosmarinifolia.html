Salix rosmarinifolia subsp. rosmarinifolia

Salix rosmarinifolia subsp.

rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary willow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
