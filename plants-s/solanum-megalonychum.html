Solanum megalonychum

Solanum megalonychum, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, which is toxic if ingested, is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
