Senecio karataviensis

Senecio karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavinskaya groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
