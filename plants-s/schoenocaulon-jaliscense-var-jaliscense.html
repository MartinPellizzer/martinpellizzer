Schoenocaulon jaliscense var. jaliscense

Schoenocaulon jaliscense var. jaliscense, commonly known as the Jalisco rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
