Solanum phyllanthus var. minus

Solanum phyllanthus var. minus, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that mature to a deep red or purple color.

It is often found in coastal and disturbed habitats, and while it has some ornamental value, it can be invasive in certain regions.
