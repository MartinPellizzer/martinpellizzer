Salacia blainii

Salacia blainii, commonly known as the Blain's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Salacia blainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
