Saxifraga hederacea var. scotophila

Saxifraga hederacea var. scotophila, commonly known as the mossy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in small umbels, and bloom in early spring.

The species is adapted to cool, humid conditions and is often found in areas with rich, acidic soil.
