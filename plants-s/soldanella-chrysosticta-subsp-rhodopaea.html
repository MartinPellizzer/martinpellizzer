Soldanella chrysosticta subsp. rhodopaea

Soldanella chrysosticta subsp.

rhodopaea, commonly known as the red-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly hair. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
