Senecio lugens var. blaisdellii

Senecio lugens var. blaisdellii, commonly known as Blaisdell's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are generally blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Blaisdell's blueweed is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
