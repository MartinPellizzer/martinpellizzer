Stachys circinata subsp. zaiana

Stachys circinata subsp.

zaiana, commonly known as the Zaian hound's-tongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, spiny bracts that surround its small, purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
