Senecio anomalus

Senecio anomalus, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with gray-green, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio anomalus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
