Senecio subarachnoideus

Senecio subarachnoideus, commonly known as the silky senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
