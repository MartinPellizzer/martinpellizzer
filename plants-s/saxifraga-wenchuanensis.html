Saxifraga wenchuanensis

Saxifraga wenchuanensis, commonly known as the Wenchuan saxifrage, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments, often found in crevices or on slopes with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
