Sorghum decolorans

Sorghum decolorans, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sorghum decolorans is also valued as a forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional content.
