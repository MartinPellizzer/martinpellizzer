Solanum croatii

Solanum croatii, commonly known as the Croatian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Solanum croatii is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
