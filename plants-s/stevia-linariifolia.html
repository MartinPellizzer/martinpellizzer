Stevia linariifolia

Stevia linariifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. Stevia linariifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Its leaves are typically harvested for their sweet compounds, which have minimal caloric content.
