Satureja ericifolia

Satureja ericifolia, commonly known as bearberry sage, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Satureja ericifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
