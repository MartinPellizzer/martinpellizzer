Spermacoce suffruticosa

Spermacoce suffruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf sida, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Spermacoce suffruticosa is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
