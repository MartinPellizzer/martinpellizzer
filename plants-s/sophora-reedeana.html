Sophora reedeana

Sophora reedeana, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sophora reedeana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
