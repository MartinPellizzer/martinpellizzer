Stellaria nitida

Stellaria nitida, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has delicate, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Stellaria nitida is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
