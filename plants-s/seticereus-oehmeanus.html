Seticereus oehmeanus

Seticereus oehmeanus, commonly known as the Oehm's candelabra cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and southern Florida.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its branched, tree-like structure with multiple stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
