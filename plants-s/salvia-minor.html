Salvia minor

Salvia minor, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Salvia minor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
