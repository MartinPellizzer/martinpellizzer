Scaevola auriculata

Scaevola auriculata, commonly known as the "ear-shaped sandmat," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
