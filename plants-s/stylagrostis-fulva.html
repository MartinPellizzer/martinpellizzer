Stylagrostis fulva

Stylagrostis fulva, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Stylagrostis fulva is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
