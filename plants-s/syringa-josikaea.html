Syringa josikaea

Syringa josikaea, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Syringa josikaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
