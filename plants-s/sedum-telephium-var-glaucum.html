Sedum telephium var. glaucum

Sedum telephium var. glaucum, commonly known as the Blue Sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for low-maintenance landscaping.
