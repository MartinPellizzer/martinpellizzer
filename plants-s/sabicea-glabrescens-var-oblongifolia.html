Sabicea glabrescens var. oblongifolia

Sabicea glabrescens var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually oblong and have a smooth, glabrous surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often yellow or orange in color and attract various pollinators.
