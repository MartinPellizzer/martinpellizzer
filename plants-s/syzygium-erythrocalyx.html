Syzygium erythrocalyx

Syzygium erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red calyx syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its striking red calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are often consumed by birds. Syzygium erythrocalyx typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
