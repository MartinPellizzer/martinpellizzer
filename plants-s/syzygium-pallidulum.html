Syzygium pallidulum

Syzygium pallidulum, commonly known as the pale apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Syzygium pallidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
