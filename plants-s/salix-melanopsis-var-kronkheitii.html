Salix melanopsis var. kronkheitii

Salix melanopsis var. kronkheitii, commonly known as the black willow variant, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, often fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along streams.

This variant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
