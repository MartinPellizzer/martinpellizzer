Sanguisorba officinalis var. auriculata

Sanguisorba officinalis var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, though it is more commonly appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
