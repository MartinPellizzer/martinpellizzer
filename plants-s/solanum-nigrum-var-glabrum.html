Solanum nigrum var. glabrum

Solanum nigrum var. glabrum, commonly known as black nightshade, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The flowers are typically yellow with a purple calyx, and the plant is known for its toxic properties when consumed in large quantities.
