Sycomorus riparia

Sycomorus riparia, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in riverine habitats and is often found along watercourses.

Sycomorus riparia is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
