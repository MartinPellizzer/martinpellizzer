Scutellaria woronowii

Scutellaria woronowii, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria woronowii is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
