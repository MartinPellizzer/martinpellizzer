Stelechocarpus expansus

Stelechocarpus expansus, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamanu, is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. Its botanical significance lies in the unique chemical composition of its seeds, which contain valuable oils used in traditional medicine and cosmetics.

The species is also noted for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
