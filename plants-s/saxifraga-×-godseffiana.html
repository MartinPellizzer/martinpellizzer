Saxifraga × godseffiana

Saxifraga × godseffiana, commonly known as the Godseff's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
