Samolus ebracteatus subsp. alyssoides

Samolus ebracteatus subsp.

alyssoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved samolus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
