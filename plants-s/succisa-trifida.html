Succisa trifida

Succisa trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Succisa trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
