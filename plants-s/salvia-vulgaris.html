Salvia vulgaris

Salvia vulgaris, commonly known as common sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in whorls along the stem. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.

Salvia vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and traditional herbal remedies.
