Silene mollissima

Silene mollissima, commonly known as the silky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene mollissima is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms and specialized adaptations to arid environments.
