Salvia × degenii

Salvia × degenii, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southwestern regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and violet, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a grayish or silver underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia × degenii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
