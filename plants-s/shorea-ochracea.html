Shorea ochracea

Shorea ochracea, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy wood with a yellowish-brown color. The plant typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is valued for its timber and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
