Salsola autranii

Salsola autranii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the world.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Salsola autranii is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in conditions with limited rainfall.
