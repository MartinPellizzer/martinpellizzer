Staphidiastrum polystachyum

Staphidiastrum polystachyum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
