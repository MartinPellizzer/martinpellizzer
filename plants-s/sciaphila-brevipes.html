Sciaphila brevipes

Sciaphila brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed shadow plant, is a rare and delicate tropical epiphyte native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are pale greenish-white and are borne in clusters, adding a subtle elegance to its appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Sciaphila brevipes is considered endangered and is difficult to cultivate outside its natural range.
