Senecio smallii

Senecio smallii, commonly known as the small's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio smallii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
