Spergula arvensis f. rubra

Spergula arvensis f. rubra, commonly known as red false oat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various insects.
