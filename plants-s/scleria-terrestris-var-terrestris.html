Scleria terrestris var. terrestris

Scleria terrestris var. terrestris, commonly known as the common speargrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense tufts, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
