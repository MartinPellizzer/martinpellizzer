Silphion

Silphion, commonly known as silphium, is an extinct plant that was highly valued in ancient times for its medicinal and culinary properties.

It was native to the region of Cyrenaica in North Africa and was characterized by its tall, erect growth and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant's roots contained a resinous substance that was used as an aphrodisiac, contraceptive, and treatment for various ailments. Due to overharvesting and environmental factors, silphion became extinct by the 5th century CE.

Its legacy remains as a symbol of ancient botanical knowledge and medicinal use.
