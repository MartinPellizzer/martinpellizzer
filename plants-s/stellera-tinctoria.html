Stellera tinctoria

Stellera tinctoria, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Stellera tinctoria is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
