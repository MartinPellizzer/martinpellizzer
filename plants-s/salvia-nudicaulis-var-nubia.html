Salvia nudicaulis var. nubia

Salvia nudicaulis var. nubia, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and striking floral display make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
