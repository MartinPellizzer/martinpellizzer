Silene commutata var. longifolia

Silene commutata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longstem evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and hairy, while its stems are erect and can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
