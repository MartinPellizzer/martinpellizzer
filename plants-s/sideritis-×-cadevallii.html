Sideritis × cadevallii

Sideritis × cadevallii, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scordioides and Sideritis aquatica, characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Sideritis × cadevallii is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
