Saussurea glomerata f. alata

Saussurea glomerata f. alata, commonly known as the winged musk gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant is distinguished by its unique, wing-like structures on the bracts that surround the flower cluster. It thrives in alpine environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
