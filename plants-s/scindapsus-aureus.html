Scindapsus aureus

Scindapsus aureus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant with waxy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Scindapsus aureus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
