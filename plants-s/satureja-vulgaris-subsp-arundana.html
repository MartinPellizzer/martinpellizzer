Satureja vulgaris subsp. arundana

Satureja vulgaris subsp.

arundana, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It contains essential oils rich in thymol and other volatile compounds, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
