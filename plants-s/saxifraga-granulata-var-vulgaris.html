Saxifraga granulata var. vulgaris

Saxifraga granulata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the granulate saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga granulata var. vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
