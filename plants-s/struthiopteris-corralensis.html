Struthiopteris corralensis

Struthiopteris corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis shield fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in forest ecosystems.
