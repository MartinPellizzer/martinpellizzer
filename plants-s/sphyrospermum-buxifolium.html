Sphyrospermum buxifolium

Sphyrospermum buxifolium, commonly known as the boxleaf sphyrospermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its compact growth habit and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative shrub in gardens.
