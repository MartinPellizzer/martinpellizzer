Silene rosella

Silene rosella, commonly known as the rose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in a variety of habitats including meadows and rocky soils. Silene rosella is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
