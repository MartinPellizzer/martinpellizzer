Stachys officinalis subsp. skipetarum

Stachys officinalis subsp.

skipetarum, commonly known as the Balkan hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
