Solanum tuberosum f. pulo

Solanum tuberosum f. pulo, commonly known as the Pulo potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes a distinctive skin color and texture. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and shares the same basic growth habits as other potato species. Its tubers are typically smaller than standard potatoes and have a more elongated shape.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary potential.
