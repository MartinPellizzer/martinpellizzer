Stachys tysonii

Stachys tysonii, commonly known as the Tysons' hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its small, pinkish-white flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, dry fruits called nutlets.

Stachys tysonii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
