Sanguisorba officinalis f. cordifolia

Sanguisorba officinalis f. cordifolia, commonly known as heart-leaved sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces clusters of pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
