Salicornia peruviana

Salicornia peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. This species thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other high-salinity habitats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by wind or insects.

Salicornia peruviana is an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
