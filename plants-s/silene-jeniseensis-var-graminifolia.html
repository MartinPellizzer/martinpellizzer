Silene jeniseensis var. graminifolia

Silene jeniseensis var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene jeniseensis var. graminifolia is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
