Sanguisorba stipulata var. bracteosa

Sanguisorba stipulata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate dock, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by bracts. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.
