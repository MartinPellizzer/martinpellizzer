Scirpus virens

Scirpus virens, commonly known as green bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in marshy or waterlogged soils.

Scirpus virens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
