Senecio vittarifolius

Senecio vittarifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
