Salix fulvopubescens var. eriostroma

Salix fulvopubescens var. eriostroma, commonly known as the western willow or the yellow-wool willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and have a yellowish pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
