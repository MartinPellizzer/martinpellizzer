Selenipedium × baconis

Selenipedium × baconis, commonly known as the bacon's spider-orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms once a year, producing clusters of dark purple or reddish-brown flowers that are highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
