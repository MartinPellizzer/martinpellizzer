Stevia pilosa

Stevia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stevia pilosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its leaves contain compounds that may have mild sweetening properties.
