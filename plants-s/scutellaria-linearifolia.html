Scutellaria linearifolia

Scutellaria linearifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

Scutellaria linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
