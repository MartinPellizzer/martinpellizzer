Symphyotrichum glabrifolium

Symphyotrichum glabrifolium, commonly known as smoothleaf heath aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
