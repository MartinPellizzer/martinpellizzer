Sasaella phalerata

Sasaella phalerata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Phalerata bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Sasaella phalerata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
