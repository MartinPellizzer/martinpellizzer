Salix recurvigemmata f. variegata

Salix recurvigemmata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping willow, is a unique cultivar of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and yellow tones. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender, drooping branches create a graceful, cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its role in erosion control along waterways.
