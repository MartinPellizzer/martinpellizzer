Scirpus leptolepis

Scirpus leptolepis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which develop into spikelets that disperse seeds by wind.

Scirpus leptolepis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
