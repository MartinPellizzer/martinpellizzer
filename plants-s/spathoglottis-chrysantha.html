Spathoglottis chrysantha

Spathoglottis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden spathoglottis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, with fleshy roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, resupinate flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
