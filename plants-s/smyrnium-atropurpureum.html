Smyrnium atropurpureum

Smyrnium atropurpureum, commonly known as purple smyrnium, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, where it typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the underside, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed schizocarps that release tiny seeds when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
