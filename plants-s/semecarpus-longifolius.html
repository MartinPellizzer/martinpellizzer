Semecarpus longifolius

Semecarpus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a sticky resin.
