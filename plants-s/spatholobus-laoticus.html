Spatholobus laoticus

Spatholobus laoticus, commonly known as the Laotian wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
