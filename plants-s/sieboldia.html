Sieboldia

Sieboldia, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Magnoliaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Sieboldia heptaphylla, which is native to Japan and Korea. The plants are characterized by their showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. They are deciduous trees or shrubs, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Sieboldia species are valued for their ornamental qualities and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their aesthetic appeal.
