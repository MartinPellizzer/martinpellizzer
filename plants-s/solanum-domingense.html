Solanum domingense

Solanum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
