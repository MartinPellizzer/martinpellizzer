Scirpus × triquetriformis

Scirpus × triquetriformis, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Scirpus maritimus and Scirpus tabernaemontani, characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in brackish or freshwater marshes, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life. Its rhizomes help in nutrient cycling and can tolerate varying salinity levels, making it adaptable to different wetland environments.

Scirpus × triquetriformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
