Syngonanthus humboldtii var. macrocephalus

Syngonanthus humboldtii var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the Humboldt's mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
