Sphaeranthus gallensis

Sphaeranthus gallensis, commonly known as the gallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and yellow to orange flowers.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
