Symplocos robinsonii var. robinsonii

Symplocos robinsonii var. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are small, black, and somewhat fleshy, providing food for various wildlife species.

The plant is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
