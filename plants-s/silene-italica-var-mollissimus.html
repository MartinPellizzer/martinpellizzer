Silene italica var. mollissimus

Silene italica var. mollissimus, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
