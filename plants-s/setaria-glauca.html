Setaria glauca

Setaria glauca, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria glauca is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil quality.
