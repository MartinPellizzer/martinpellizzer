Sarcochilus hirtulus

Sarcochilus hirtulus, commonly known as the hairy leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a distinctive, leek-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces a single, upright flower spike bearing several pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rainforest or heathland habitats.

Sarcochilus hirtulus is valued for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
