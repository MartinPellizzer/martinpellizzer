Salix pyrenaica var. nitidula

Salix pyrenaica var. nitidula, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on both sides. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
