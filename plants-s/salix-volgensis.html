Salix volgensis

Salix volgensis, commonly known as the flowing willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
