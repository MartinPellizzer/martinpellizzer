Sonneratia × gulngai

Sonneratia × gulngai, commonly known as the mangrove hybrid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sonneratia alba and Sonneratia caseolaris.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree that thrives in brackish water environments, such as mangrove forests. The plant has aromatic leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.

This species plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
