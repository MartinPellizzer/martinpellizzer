Solanum bombense

Solanum bombense, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white. Solanum bombense produces berries that are toxic when unripe but can be edible when fully mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
