Spirillus amplifolius

Spirillus amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf spirilla, is a unique plant characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves.

Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that spiral around the stem in a distinctive helical pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or swampy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the leaves.

Spirillus amplifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual leaf structure.
