Stranvaesia digyna

Stranvaesia digyna, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stranvaesia digyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
