Sagittaria pseudohermaphroditica

Sagittaria pseudohermaphroditica, commonly known as the swamp arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and small, white, clustered flowers that bloom above water. The plant is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual, which facilitates self-pollination. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in shallow water.

The species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
