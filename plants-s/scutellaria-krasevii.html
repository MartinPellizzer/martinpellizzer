Scutellaria krasevii

Scutellaria krasevii, commonly known as Krasevii blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Scutellaria krasevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
