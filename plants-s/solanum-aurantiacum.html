Solanum aurantiacum

Solanum aurantiacum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
