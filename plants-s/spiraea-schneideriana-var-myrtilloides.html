Spiraea schneideriana var. myrtilloides

Spiraea schneideriana var. myrtilloides, commonly known as the purpleleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy clusters of white to pale pink flowers that appear in early summer. The foliage is a striking feature, transitioning from reddish-bronze in spring to deep purple in summer and then to red in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
