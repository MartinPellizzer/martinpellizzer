Syzygium codyense

Syzygium codyense, commonly known as the Cody's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are yellow when ripe and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Syzygium codyense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental landscaping and agroforestry systems.
