Scrophularia nodosa subsp. foliosa

Scrophularia nodosa subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
