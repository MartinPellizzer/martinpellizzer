Syringa vulgaris var. coerulea

Syringa vulgaris var. coerulea, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color beneath. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Syringa vulgaris var. coerulea is also known for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
