Synaedrys densiflora

Synaedrys densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered synaedrys, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its dense foliage appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
