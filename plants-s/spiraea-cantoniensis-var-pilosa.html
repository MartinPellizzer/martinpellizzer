Spiraea cantoniensis var. pilosa

Spiraea cantoniensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "pilosa" designation. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
