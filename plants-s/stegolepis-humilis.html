Stegolepis humilis

Stegolepis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant typically has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny white or yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
