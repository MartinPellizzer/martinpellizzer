Salix taraikensis

Salix taraikensis, commonly known as the Tariqan willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically male and female, and it is often found in moist, temperate habitats.

Its wood is lightweight and has been traditionally used for construction and crafts in its native regions.
