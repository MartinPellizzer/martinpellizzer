Spiraea besseriana

Spiraea besseriana, commonly known as the Siberian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems and an upright, rounded form. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea besseriana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
