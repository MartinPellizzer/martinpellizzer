Syzygium fischeri

Syzygium fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
