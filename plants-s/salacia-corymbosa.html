Salacia corymbosa

Salacia corymbosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the family Simaroubaceae and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
