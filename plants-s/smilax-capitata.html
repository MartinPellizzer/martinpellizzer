Smilax capitata

Smilax capitata, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead or yellow smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp, curved spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and typically have a rough texture.

Smilax capitata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
