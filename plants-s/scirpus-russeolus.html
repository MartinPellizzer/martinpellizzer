Scirpus russeolus

Scirpus russeolus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a tall, emergent aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and tapering, typically growing in dense clusters at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into brown, cylindrical seeds.

Scirpus russeolus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
