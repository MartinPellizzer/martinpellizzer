Salsola daghestanica

Salsola daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and saline areas. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Salsola daghestanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
