Spiraea pumila var. bumalda

Spiraea pumila var. bumalda, commonly known as the dwarf spiraea, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a lush appearance throughout the growing season. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
