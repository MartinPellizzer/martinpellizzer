Sideritis hyssopifolia proles peyrei

Sideritis hyssopifolia proles peyrei, commonly known as the Peyre's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
