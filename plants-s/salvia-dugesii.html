Salvia dugesii

Salvia dugesii, commonly known as desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purplish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Salvia dugesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
