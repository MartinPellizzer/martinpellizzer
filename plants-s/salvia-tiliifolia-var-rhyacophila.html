Salvia tiliifolia var. rhyacophila

Salvia tiliifolia var. rhyacophila, commonly known as the water sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, two-lipped blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
