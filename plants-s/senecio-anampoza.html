Senecio anampoza

Senecio anampoza, commonly known as the mountain senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Senecio anampoza produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
