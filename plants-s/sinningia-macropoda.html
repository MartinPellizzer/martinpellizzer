Sinningia macropoda

Sinningia macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaved gloxinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in bright, indirect light. It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sinningia macropoda prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity for optimal growth.
