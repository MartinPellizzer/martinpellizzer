Saussurea japonica var. japonica

Saussurea japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, branched stems covered in grayish-green, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued species in traditional herbal medicine and horticulture.
