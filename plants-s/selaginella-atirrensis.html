Selaginella atirrensis

Selaginella atirrensis, commonly known as the Atlantic Selaginella, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the genus Selaginella.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. This plant is characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths. Selaginella atirrensis is native to the coastal regions of Brazil, where it thrives in sandy soils near the Atlantic Ocean.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to tolerate humid, coastal conditions.
