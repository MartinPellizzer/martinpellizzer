Solanum rhamphidacanthum

Solanum rhamphidacanthum, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Solanum rhamphidacanthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
