Senecio fraternus

Senecio fraternus, commonly known as the blueweed or fraternus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio fraternus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
