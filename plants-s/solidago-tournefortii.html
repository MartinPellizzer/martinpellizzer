Solidago tournefortii

Solidago tournefortii, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of darker yellow florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
