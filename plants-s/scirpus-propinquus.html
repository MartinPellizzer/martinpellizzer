Scirpus propinquus

Scirpus propinquus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters near water bodies. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing up to 1 meter in height.

Scirpus propinquus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
