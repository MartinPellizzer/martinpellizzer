Saxifraga subverticillata

Saxifraga subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga subverticillata is often found in mountainous areas and is a hardy species that can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
