Sindora

Sindora, commonly known as the Indian bell pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

The botanical features of Sindora make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
