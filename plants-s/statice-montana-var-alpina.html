Statice montana var. alpina

Statice montana var. alpina, commonly known as alpine statice, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its drought tolerance and ability to withstand cold temperatures make it a hardy choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
