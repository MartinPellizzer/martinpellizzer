Stissera curcuma

Stissera curcuma, commonly known as turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant features a rhizome that is the primary part used for its medicinal and culinary properties. The rhizome is characterized by its yellow-orange color, which comes from the compound curcumin.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic benefits.
