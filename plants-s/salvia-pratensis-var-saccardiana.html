Salvia pratensis var. saccardiana

Salvia pratensis var. saccardiana, commonly known as the Saccardian blue hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
