Smilax austrozhejiangensis

Smilax austrozhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang smilax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Smilacaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Zhejiang province, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in spines, and its compound leaves with a distinctive three-lobed shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and its role in the local ecosystem.
