Salsola hottentottica

Salsola hottentottica, commonly known as the hot tent caterpillar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water storage mechanisms. It often grows in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Salsola hottentottica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
