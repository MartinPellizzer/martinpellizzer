Saxifraga wardii var. wardii

Saxifraga wardii var. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often on limestone substrates, and is adapted to cool, moist climates. The plant features basal leaves that are often rosette-shaped, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, usually pink or white, and are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, known for its diverse and often hardy species.
