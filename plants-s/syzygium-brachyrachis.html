Syzygium brachyrachis

Syzygium brachyrachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The tree produces edible, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Syzygium brachyrachis thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
