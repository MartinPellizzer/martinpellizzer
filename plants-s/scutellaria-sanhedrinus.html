Scutellaria sanhedrinus

Scutellaria sanhedrinus, commonly known as the Sanhedrin skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Scutellaria sanhedrinus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
