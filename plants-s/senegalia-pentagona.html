Senegalia pentagona

Senegalia pentagona, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in drought conditions, making it well-adapted to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Senegalia pentagona is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
