Spiraea filipendulina

Spiraea filipendulina, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The plant has finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Spiraea filipendulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
