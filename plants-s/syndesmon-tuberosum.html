Syndesmon tuberosum

Syndesmon tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous syndesmon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, underground stems called tubers, which store nutrients and water. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Syndesmon tuberosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
