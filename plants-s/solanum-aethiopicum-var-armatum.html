Solanum aethiopicum var. armatum

Solanum aethiopicum var. armatum, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, spiny stems. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, egg-shaped fruits. The fruit is edible, though it has a slightly bitter taste, and is often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
