Scirpus cespitosus var. austriacus

Scirpus cespitosus var. austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
