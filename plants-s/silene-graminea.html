Silene graminea

Silene graminea, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name.

Silene graminea is often found in prairie and coastal environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
