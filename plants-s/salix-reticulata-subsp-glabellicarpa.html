Salix reticulata subsp. glabellicarpa

Salix reticulata subsp.

glabellicarpa, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on the upper surface and pale white underneath.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary floral structures, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist soils and is commonly found near water bodies.

This subspecies is closely related to other willow species and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
