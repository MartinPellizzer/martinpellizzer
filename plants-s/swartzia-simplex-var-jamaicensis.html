Swartzia simplex var. jamaicensis

Swartzia simplex var. jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich reddish-brown color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
