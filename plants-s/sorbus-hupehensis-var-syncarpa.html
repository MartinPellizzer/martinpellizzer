Sorbus hupehensis var. syncarpa

Sorbus hupehensis var. syncarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
