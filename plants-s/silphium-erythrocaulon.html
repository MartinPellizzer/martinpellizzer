Silphium erythrocaulon

Silphium erythrocaulon, commonly known as red-stemmed silphium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, reddish-brown stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its historical use by indigenous peoples and its ecological importance, it is considered a valuable native plant.
