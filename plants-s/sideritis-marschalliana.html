Sideritis marschalliana

Sideritis marschalliana, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Sideritis marschalliana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
