Silene melandrioides var. acutifolia

Silene melandrioides var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically linear and sharply pointed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and wetlands.

The plant produces pink or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
