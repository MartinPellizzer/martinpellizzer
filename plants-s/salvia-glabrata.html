Salvia glabrata

Salvia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are tubular and typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stem.

Salvia glabrata is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
