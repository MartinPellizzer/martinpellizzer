Sideritis × embergeri

Sideritis × embergeri, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis × embergeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various garden environments.
