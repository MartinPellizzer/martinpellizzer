Solidago ouachitensis

Solidago ouachitensis, commonly known as Ouachita goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland and swampy areas. The plant features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ouachita goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
