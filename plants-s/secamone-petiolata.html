Secamone petiolata

Secamone petiolata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Secamone petiolata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
