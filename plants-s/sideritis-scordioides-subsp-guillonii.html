Sideritis scordioides subsp. guillonii

Sideritis scordioides subsp.

guillonii, commonly known as the Guillon's sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
