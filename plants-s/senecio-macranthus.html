Senecio macranthus

Senecio macranthus, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its toxic properties.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised because of its potential harm to livestock.
