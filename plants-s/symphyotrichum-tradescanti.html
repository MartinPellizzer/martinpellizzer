Symphyotrichum tradescanti

Symphyotrichum tradescanti, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces showy purple or blue flowers in the fall. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Symphyotrichum tradescanti is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
