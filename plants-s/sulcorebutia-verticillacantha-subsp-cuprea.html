Sulcorebutia verticillacantha subsp. cuprea

Sulcorebutia verticillacantha subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as the copper-tipped sun rose, is a succulent native to South America. It features compact, fleshy stems that often branch out into a rosette shape.

The plant produces vibrant orange to reddish flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically green with a bluish tinge and may have a slight woolly texture.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
