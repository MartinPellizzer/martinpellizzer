Stramonium fastuosum

Stramonium fastuosum, commonly known as the showy thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant to handle.
