Sibbaldia tridentata

Sibbaldia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high elevations where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions. Its root system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in poor soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.

Sibbaldia tridentata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
