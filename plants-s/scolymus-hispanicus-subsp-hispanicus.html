Scolymus hispanicus subsp. hispanicus

Scolymus hispanicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant features a robust root system and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and branching.

It is valued for its edible roots and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
