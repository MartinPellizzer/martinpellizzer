Sesbania hirtistyla var. hirtistyla

Sesbania hirtistyla var. hirtistyla, commonly known as the hairy sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Sesbania hirtistyla is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration contexts.
