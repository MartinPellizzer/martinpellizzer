Stipa pulcherrima subsp. grafiana

Stipa pulcherrima subsp.

grafiana, commonly known as the Grafiana feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
