Solidago pendula

Solidago pendula, commonly known as the weeping goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
