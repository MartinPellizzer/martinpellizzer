Solanum tuberosum var. marmoratum

Solanum tuberosum var. marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique mottled or marbled skin that displays a mix of colors, typically including purple, red, and white. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to the Andes region of South America. Its tubers are generally smaller than standard potatoes and have a slightly different texture and flavor profile.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary uses.
