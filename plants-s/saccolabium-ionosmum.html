Saccolabium ionosmum

Saccolabium ionosmum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its stem is upright and branched.

Saccolabium ionosmum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
