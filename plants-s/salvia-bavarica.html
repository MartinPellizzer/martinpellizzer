Salvia bavarica

Salvia bavarica, commonly known as the German sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
