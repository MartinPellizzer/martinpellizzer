Sabina foetidissima var. bonatiana

Sabina foetidissima var. bonatiana, commonly known as the Italian juniper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but have a strong, pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
