Selinum chabraei var. autumnale

Selinum chabraei var. autumnale, commonly known as autumn parsley, is a decorative plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of parsley, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Selinum chabraei var. autumnale is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
