Saccharum holcoides var. warmingianum

Saccharum holcoides var. warmingianum, commonly known as the Warming's bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential bioenergy applications.
