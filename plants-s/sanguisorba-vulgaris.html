Sanguisorba vulgaris

Sanguisorba vulgaris, commonly known as common sheep's sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Sanguisorba vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
