Sporobolus filiculmis

Sporobolus filiculmis, commonly known as the fringed spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along creek banks. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Sporobolus filiculmis plays an important ecological role in riparian and wetland ecosystems.
