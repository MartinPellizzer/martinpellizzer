Saxifraga iochanensis

Saxifraga iochanensis, commonly known as the Kashmir saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates with high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
