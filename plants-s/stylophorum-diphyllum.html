Stylophorum diphyllum

Stylophorum diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved false spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are typically divided into three to five lobes. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in early spring, arranged in a single terminal umbel. Its stems are hairy and often reddish in color, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Stylophorum diphyllum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
