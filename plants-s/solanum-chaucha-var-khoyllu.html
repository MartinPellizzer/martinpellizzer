Solanum chaucha var. khoyllu

Solanum chaucha var. khoyllu, commonly known as the Khoyllu nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features woody stems and produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its fruits are berries that change from green to red as they mature, often containing several seeds.

This species is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
