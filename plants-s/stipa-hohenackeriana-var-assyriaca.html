Stipa hohenackeriana var. assyriaca

Stipa hohenackeriana var. assyriaca, commonly known as the Assyrian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine and rocky garden environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture, and the inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet cluster.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
