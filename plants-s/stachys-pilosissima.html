Stachys pilosissima

Stachys pilosissima, commonly known as fuzzy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
