Serratula transcaucasica

Serratula transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are often hairy and slightly lobed.

Serratula transcaucasica is an annual herb that plays a role in local ecosystems, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
