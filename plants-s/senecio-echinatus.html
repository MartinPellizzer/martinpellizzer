Senecio echinatus

Senecio echinatus, commonly known as the spiny senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, spiny stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio echinatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique look and drought tolerance.
