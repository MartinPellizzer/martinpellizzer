Sauromatum brevipes

Sauromatum brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or green in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.
