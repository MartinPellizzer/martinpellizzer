Shorea chrysophylla

Shorea chrysophylla, commonly known as golden leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, durable wood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
