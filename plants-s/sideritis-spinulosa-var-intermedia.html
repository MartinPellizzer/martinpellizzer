Sideritis spinulosa var. intermedia

Sideritis spinulosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spikeweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features wiry stems and small, hairy leaves, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in spike-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variant is distinguished by its intermediate characteristics between the typical forms of Sideritis spinulosa.
