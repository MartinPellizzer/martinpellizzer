Silene antarctica

Silene antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in the harsh conditions of the continent's cold, dry climate. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the brief Antarctic summer. Silene antarctica is adapted to survive with minimal water and nutrients, showcasing remarkable resilience.

Its presence provides important ecological value in one of the most isolated regions on Earth.
