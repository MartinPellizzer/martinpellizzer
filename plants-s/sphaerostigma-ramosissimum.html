Sphaerostigma ramosissimum

Sphaerostigma ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy sphaerostigma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white blooms that are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Sphaerostigma ramosissimum is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions.
