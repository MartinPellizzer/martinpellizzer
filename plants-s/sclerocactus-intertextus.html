Sclerocactus intertextus

Sclerocactus intertextus, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape, often resembling a hedgehog. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Sclerocactus intertextus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
