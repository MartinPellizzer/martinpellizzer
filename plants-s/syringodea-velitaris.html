Syringodea velitaris

Syringodea velitaris, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. Syringodea velitaris thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
