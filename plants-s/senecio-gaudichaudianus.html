Senecio gaudichaudianus

Senecio gaudichaudianus, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
