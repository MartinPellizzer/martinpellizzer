Salvia tsaiana

Salvia tsaiana, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, minty scent. Its flowers are typically blue to violet, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late summer.

Salvia tsaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
