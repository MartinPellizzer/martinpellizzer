Symplocos hylandii

Symplocos hylandii, commonly known as Hyland's waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
