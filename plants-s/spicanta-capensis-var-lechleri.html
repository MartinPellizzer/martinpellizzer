Spicanta capensis var. lechleri

Spicanta capensis var. lechleri, commonly known as the African bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested or shaded areas.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional African medicine.
