Sibbaldianthe adpressa

Sibbaldianthe adpressa, commonly known as the dwarf rock melon, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
