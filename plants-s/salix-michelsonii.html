Salix michelsonii

Salix michelsonii, commonly known as the Michelson's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and small, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of willow species, and it produces small, round fruits.

Salix michelsonii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local wildlife.
