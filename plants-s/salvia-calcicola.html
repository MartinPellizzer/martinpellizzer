Salvia calcicola

Salvia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in limestone-rich habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
