Symplocos henschelii var. henschelii

Symplocos henschelii var. henschelii, commonly known as Henschel's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
