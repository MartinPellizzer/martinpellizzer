Styrax platanifolius var. mollis

Styrax platanifolius var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark red berries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
