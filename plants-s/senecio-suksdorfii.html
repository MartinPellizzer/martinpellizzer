Senecio suksdorfii

Senecio suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually yellow, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
