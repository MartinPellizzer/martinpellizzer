Spiraea salicifolia var. linearis

Spiraea salicifolia var. linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, arching stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and moderate growth habit.
