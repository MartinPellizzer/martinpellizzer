Scirpus perrieri

Scirpus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline ecosystems. Its cylindrical, grass-like stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Scirpus perrieri plays a vital role in wetland habitats by providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
