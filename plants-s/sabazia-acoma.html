Sabazia acoma

Sabazia acoma, commonly known as the Acoma sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert environments.

Sabazia acoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Native American medicine.
