Solanum oligolobum

Solanum oligolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a narrow lobed margin, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow stamen.

Solanum oligolobum produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans but may be consumed by certain wildlife species.
