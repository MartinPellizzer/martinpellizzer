Stachys paraplesia

Stachys paraplesia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Stachys paraplesia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
