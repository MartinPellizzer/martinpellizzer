Salix chlorophylla var. monica

Salix chlorophylla var. monica, commonly known as the Monica willow, is a variant of the willow tree characterized by its unique leaf morphology and growth habits.

It possesses narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a dense network of veins that enhance its photosynthetic efficiency. The plant exhibits a spreading growth habit, often forming a rounded or weeping shape, which makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands.

The botanical features of Salix chlorophylla var. monica make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
