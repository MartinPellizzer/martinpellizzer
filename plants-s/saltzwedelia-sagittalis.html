Saltzwedelia sagittalis

Saltzwedelia sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowleaf saltzwedel, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant's foliage is typically gray-green, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are small and often pale blue or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Saltzwedelia sagittalis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
