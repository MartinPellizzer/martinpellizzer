Stipa zalesskyi subsp. canescens

Stipa zalesskyi subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the gray feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, often silver-gray or bluish-gray blades, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant, grass-like form.
