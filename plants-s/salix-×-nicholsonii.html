Salix × nicholsonii

Salix × nicholsonii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix cinerea and Salix viminalis, characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as streams and ditches. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and turn a striking yellow in the autumn.

Salix × nicholsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
