Salix alatavica

Salix alatavica, commonly known as the Central Asian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically yellow-green in color, and its leaves are lanceolate with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

The bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
