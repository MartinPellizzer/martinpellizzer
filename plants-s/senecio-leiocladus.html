Senecio leiocladus

Senecio leiocladus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
