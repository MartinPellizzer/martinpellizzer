Stevia jaliscensis

Stevia jaliscensis, commonly known as Mexican stevia, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
