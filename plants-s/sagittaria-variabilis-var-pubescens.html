Sagittaria variabilis var. pubescens

Sagittaria variabilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike, emerging above the water surface.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
