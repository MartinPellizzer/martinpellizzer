Stachys argolica

Stachys argolica, commonly known as the Argolic mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in areas with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance, while its flowers are small and tubular, usually in shades of purple or blue.

Stachys argolica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
