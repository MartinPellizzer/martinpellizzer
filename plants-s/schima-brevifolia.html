Schima brevifolia

Schima brevifolia, commonly known as the Chinese teak or Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Schima brevifolia is adapted to acidic soils and is often found in subtropical regions with mild climates.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
