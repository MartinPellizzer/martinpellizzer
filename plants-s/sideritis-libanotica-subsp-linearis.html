Sideritis libanotica subsp. linearis

Sideritis libanotica subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the linear lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, linear leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. Sideritis libanotica subsp.

linearis is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
