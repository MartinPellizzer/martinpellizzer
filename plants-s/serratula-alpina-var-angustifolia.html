Serratula alpina var. angustifolia

Serratula alpina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf alpine sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
