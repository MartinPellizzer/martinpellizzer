Siphonia cahuchu

Siphonia cahuchu, commonly known as the red sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

Siphonia cahuchu is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
