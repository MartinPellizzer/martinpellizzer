Symplocos pulchra subsp. villosa

Symplocos pulchra subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet-purple berries that appear in the fall.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
