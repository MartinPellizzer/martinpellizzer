Serjania foveata

Serjania foveata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is also noted for its toxicity, as some parts of the plant contain harmful compounds.
