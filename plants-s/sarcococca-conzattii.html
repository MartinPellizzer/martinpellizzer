Sarcococca conzattii

Sarcococca conzattii, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by red berries that attract birds. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a ground cover. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

Sarcococca conzattii is also noted for its slow growth and long-lasting foliage.
