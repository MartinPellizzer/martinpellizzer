Silene sibirica var. holopetala

Silene sibirica var. holopetala, commonly known as the Siberian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Silene sibirica var. holopetala is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
