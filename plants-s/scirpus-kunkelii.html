Scirpus kunkelii

Scirpus kunkelii, commonly known as the Kunkel's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spadix-like flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus kunkelii is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
