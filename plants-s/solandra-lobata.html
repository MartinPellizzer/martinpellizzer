Solandra lobata

Solandra lobata, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Solandra lobata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
