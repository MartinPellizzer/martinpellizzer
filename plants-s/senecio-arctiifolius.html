Senecio arctiifolius

Senecio arctiifolius, commonly known as the Arcticleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-gray leaves that are adapted to cold and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
