Strophanthus senegambiae

Strophanthus senegambiae, commonly known as the "Cape leadplant," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Strophanthus senegambiae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
