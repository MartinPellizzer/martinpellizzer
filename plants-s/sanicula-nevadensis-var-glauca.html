Sanicula nevadensis var. glauca

Sanicula nevadensis var. glauca, commonly known as the blue sanicle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray hue. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in mountainous regions of Nevada and California.
