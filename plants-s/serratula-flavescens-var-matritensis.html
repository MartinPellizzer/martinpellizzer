Serratula flavescens var. matritensis

Serratula flavescens var. matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid yellow cudweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its yellowish stems and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
