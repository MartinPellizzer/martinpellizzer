Sorbus whiteana

Sorbus whiteana, commonly known as the white mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
