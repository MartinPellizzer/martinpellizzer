Stellaria discolor var. ramipilosa

Stellaria discolor var. ramipilosa, commonly known as white stitchwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
