Scrophularia georgica

Scrophularia georgica, commonly known as Georgian figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Scrophularia georgica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
