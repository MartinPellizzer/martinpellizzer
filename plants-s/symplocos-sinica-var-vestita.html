Symplocos sinica var. vestita

Symplocos sinica var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandoric.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
