Setaria decipiens f. latifolia

Setaria decipiens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf false panic grass, is a variant of the species Setaria decipiens, characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in ecological studies and landscaping due to its hardy nature.
