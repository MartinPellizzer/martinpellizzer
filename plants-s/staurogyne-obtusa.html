Staurogyne obtusa

Staurogyne obtusa, commonly known as broadleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or just at the water's surface.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
