Sisymbrium grayanum

Sisymbrium grayanum, commonly known as the gray anthriscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its foliage has a mild, aromatic scent, making it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
