Sedum barrancae

Sedum barrancae, commonly known as the Barranca sedum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, and bloom during the warmer months.

Sedum barrancae is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
