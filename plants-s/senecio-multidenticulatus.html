Senecio multidenticulatus

Senecio multidenticulatus, commonly known as the many-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with numerous small teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
