Sonchus regis-jubae

Sonchus regis-jubae, commonly known as the royal mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow and ray-floretted, resembling a dandelion, and bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
