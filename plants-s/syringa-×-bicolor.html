Syringa × bicolor

Syringa × bicolor, commonly known as weeping cherry or Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the cultivar. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

Syringa × bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its showy spring blossoms and attractive foliage.
