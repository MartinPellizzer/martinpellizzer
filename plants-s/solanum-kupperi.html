Solanum kupperi

Solanum kupperi, commonly known as the Kupper's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Solanum kupperi is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
