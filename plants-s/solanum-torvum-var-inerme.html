Solanum torvum var. inerme

Solanum torvum var. inerme, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into yellow or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids such as solasodine and scopolamine.
