Scabiosa leucophylla subsp. dalmatica

Scabiosa leucophylla subsp.

dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian scabious, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, fluffy center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
