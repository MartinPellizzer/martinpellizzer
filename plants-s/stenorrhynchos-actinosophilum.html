Stenorrhynchos actinosophilum

Stenorrhynchos actinosophilum, commonly known as the star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its intricate, star-shaped flowers that exhibit a range of vibrant colors, including yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
