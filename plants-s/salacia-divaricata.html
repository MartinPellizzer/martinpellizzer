Salacia divaricata

Salacia divaricata, commonly known as the creeping salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Salacia divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
