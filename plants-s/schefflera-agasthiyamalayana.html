Schefflera agasthiyamalayana

Schefflera agasthiyamalayana, commonly known as the Agasthiyamalai schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of palmately compound leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
