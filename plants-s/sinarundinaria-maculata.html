Sinarundinaria maculata

Sinarundinaria maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purplish or reddish spots on its leaves. The plant is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, fertile soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands.

Sinarundinaria maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
