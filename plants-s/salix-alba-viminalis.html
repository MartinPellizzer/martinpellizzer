Salix alba-viminalis

Salix alba-viminalis, commonly known as white willow or weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant is known for its fibrous bark, which has been historically used for making paper and baskets. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins during the spring.
