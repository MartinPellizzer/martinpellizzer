Serruria saxicola

Serruria saxicola, commonly known as the rock serruria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

Serruria saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
