Sedum rubroglaucum

Sedum rubroglaucum, commonly known as the bluish sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
