Stachys iva var. ochroleuca

Stachys iva var. ochroleuca, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
