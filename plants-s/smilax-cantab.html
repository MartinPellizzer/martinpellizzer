Smilax cantab

Smilax cantab, commonly known as the Spanish smilax, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Smilax cantab is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and persistent fruits.
