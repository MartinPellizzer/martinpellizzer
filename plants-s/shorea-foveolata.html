Shorea foveolata

Shorea foveolata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Shorea foveolata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
