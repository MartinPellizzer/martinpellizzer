Silene matutina

Silene matutina, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. Silene matutina is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and vibrant purple or pink blossoms.

It is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
