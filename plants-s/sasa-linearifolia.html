Sasa linearifolia

Sasa linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a perennial evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide. The plant forms dense clumps through its rhizomatous growth system, creating thickets in its natural habitat. Its culms, or stems, are hollow and segmented, typical of bamboo species.

Sasa linearifolia is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
