Sasaella kiboensis

Sasaella kiboensis, commonly known as the Kibo bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mount Kilimanjaro in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Sasaella kiboensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
