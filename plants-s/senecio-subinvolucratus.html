Senecio subinvolucratus

Senecio subinvolucratus, commonly known as the African iron cross, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Senecio subinvolucratus is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
