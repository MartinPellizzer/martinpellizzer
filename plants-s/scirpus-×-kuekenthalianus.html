Scirpus × kuekenthalianus

Scirpus × kuekenthalianus, commonly known as the marsh reed or purple reed, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it helps stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
