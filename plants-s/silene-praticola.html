Silene praticola

Silene praticola, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a compact growth habit.

Silene praticola is often found in sunny, open habitats and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
