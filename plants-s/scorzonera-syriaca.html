Scorzonera syriaca

Scorzonera syriaca, commonly known as Syrian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible tubers, which have a nutty, earthy flavor. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and can grow up to 20 cm in length. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are borne on tall, slender stems.

Scorzonera syriaca is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued both for its culinary use and ornamental appeal.
