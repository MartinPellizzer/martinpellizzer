Solanum chiliadenium

Solanum chiliadenium, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solanum chiliadenium is also noted for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a versatile addition to gardens and greenhouses.
