Silene graminifolia lusus vulgaris

Silene graminifolia lusus vulgaris, commonly known as the meadow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene graminifolia is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
