Sigesbeckia portoriccensis

Sigesbeckia portoriccensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces numerous seeds enclosed in a distinctive, spiny pome-like structure. It thrives in moist, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

Sigesbeckia portoriccensis is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
