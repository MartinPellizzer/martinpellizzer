Styrax americanus f. genuinus

Styrax americanus f. genuinus, commonly known as the American styrax, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
