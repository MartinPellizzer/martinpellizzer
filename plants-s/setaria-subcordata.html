Setaria subcordata

Setaria subcordata, commonly known as slender bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are followed by hard, cylindrical seed heads.

Setaria subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
