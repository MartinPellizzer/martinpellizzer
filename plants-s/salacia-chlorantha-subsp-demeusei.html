Salacia chlorantha subsp. demeusei

Salacia chlorantha subsp.

demeusei, commonly known as the white-flowered salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring humid and cool climates. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in lowland and montane regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more delicate floral structure compared to the main species.
