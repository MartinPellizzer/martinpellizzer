Selaginella raynaliana

Selaginella raynaliana, commonly known as the Raynalian spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are borne on the upper parts of the plant.

Selaginella raynaliana is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
