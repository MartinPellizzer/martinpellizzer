Swertia candelabrum

Swertia candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with slender, branching stems that resemble a candlestick. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Swertia candelabrum thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
