Syzygium thomsenii

Syzygium thomsenii, commonly known as the Thomsen's clove, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties. Syzygium thomsenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
