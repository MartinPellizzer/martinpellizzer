Scirpus grossus

Scirpus grossus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stem, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus grossus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
