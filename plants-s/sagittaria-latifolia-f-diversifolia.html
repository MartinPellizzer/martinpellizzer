Sagittaria latifolia f. diversifolia

Sagittaria latifolia f. diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

This aquatic plant typically grows in shallow water, such as marshes and ponds, and thrives in wetland environments. Its leaves are often broad and arrowhead-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
