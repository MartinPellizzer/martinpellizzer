Senecio densiflorus var. parishii

Senecio densiflorus var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's denseflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are typically arranged in heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
