Salix qamdoensis

Salix qamdoensis, commonly known as the Qamdo willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting cold tolerance and drought resistance. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a silvery underside.

Salix qamdoensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
