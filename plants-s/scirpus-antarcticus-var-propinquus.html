Scirpus antarcticus var. propinquus

Scirpus antarcticus var. propinquus, commonly known as the Antarctic marsh reed, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This variety thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and shores, where it forms dense vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing up to several meters in height.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
