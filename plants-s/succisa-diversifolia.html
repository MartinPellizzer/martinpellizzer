Succisa diversifolia

Succisa diversifolia, commonly known as the variable bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant is often found in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are typically reduced to scale-like structures, and it reproduces primarily through vegetative propagation.

The species is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic life and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
