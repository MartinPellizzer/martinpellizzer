Syzygium purpureum

Syzygium purpureum, commonly known as the purple-leafed syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn a striking purple hue under certain environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium purpureum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape gardening and horticultural research.
