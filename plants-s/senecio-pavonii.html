Senecio pavonii

Senecio pavonii, commonly known as the rainbow chalice, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, colorful bracts that resemble a chalice, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio pavonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
