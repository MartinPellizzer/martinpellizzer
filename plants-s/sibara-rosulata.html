Sibara rosulata

Sibara rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Sibara, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, Sibara rosulata is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
