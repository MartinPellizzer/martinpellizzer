Sanguisorba vulgaris var. glaucescens

Sanguisorba vulgaris var. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, bluish-white waxy coating, which gives the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
