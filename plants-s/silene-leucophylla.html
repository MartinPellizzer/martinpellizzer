Silene leucophylla

Silene leucophylla, commonly known as the whiteleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene leucophylla produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
