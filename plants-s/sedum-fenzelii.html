Sedum fenzelii

Sedum fenzelii, commonly known as the Fenzel's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, Sedum fenzelii is a popular plant among gardeners in arid and semi-arid climates.
