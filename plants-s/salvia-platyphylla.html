Salvia platyphylla

Salvia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of purple to violet flowers. Salvia platyphylla is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
