Salix babylonica var. lavallei

Salix babylonica var. lavallei, commonly known as the Weeping Willow Lavallei, is a cultivar of the weeping willow characterized by its cascading branches and delicate, narrow leaves.

It is native to China and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along water features. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.

Its foliage turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
