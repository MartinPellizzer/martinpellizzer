Solenophora calycosa subsp. australis

Solenophora calycosa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian calycous solenophora, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native region.
