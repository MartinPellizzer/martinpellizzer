Solanum intonsum

Solanum intonsum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling those of a passionflower. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Solanum intonsum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
