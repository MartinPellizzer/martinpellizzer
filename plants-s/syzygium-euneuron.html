Syzygium euneuron

Syzygium euneuron, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Syzygium euneuron is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
