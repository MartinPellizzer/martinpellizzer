Saxegothaea gracilis

Saxegothaea gracilis, commonly known as the slender Alaska cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Alaska.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other coniferous species in the temperate rainforest ecosystem.

Saxegothaea gracilis plays an important role in providing habitat and stabilizing the coastal environment.
