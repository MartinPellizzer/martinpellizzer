Salvia lavandulifolia var. blancoana

Salvia lavandulifolia var. blancoana, commonly known as the white sage or Mexican lavender, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a decorative plant.
