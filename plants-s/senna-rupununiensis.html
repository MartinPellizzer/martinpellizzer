Senna rupununiensis

Senna rupununiensis, commonly known as the Amazonian senna, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Senna rupununiensis is valued for its ecological role and has potential medicinal uses.
