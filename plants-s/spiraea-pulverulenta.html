Spiraea pulverulenta

Spiraea pulverulenta, commonly known as the dustflower spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Spiraea pulverulenta is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
