Solidago nana

Solidago nana, commonly known as dwarf goldenrod, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Solidago nana is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
