Stylosanthes viscosa var. acutifolia

Stylosanthes viscosa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the hairy stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage species.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability enhances soil fertility, supporting its use in pasture and land rehabilitation.
