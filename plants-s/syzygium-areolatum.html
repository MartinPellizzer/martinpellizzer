Syzygium areolatum

Syzygium areolatum, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark that features a honeycomb-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spherical fruits resembling small cherries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
