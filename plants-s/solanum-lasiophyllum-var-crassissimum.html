Solanum lasiophyllum var. crassissimum

Solanum lasiophyllum var. crassissimum, commonly known as the thick-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or purple. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The species belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
