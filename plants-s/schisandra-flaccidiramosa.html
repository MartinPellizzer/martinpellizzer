Schisandra flaccidiramosa

Schisandra flaccidiramosa, commonly known as the soft-armed schisandra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Schisandraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall and are often used in traditional medicine.

Schisandra flaccidiramosa is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruit.
