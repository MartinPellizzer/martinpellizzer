Saraca celebica

Saraca celebica, commonly known as the Celebes flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Saraca celebica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
