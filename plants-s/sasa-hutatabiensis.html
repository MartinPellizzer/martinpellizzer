Sasa hutatabiensis

Sasa hutatabiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in traditional landscaping and as a source of material for crafts. Sasa hutatabiensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its rhizomatous structure allows it to spread and form thick mats, making it an effective ground cover.
