Silene douglasii var. macrocalyx

Silene douglasii var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the Douglas's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

This variety is distinguished by its larger floral bracts compared to other Silene species.
