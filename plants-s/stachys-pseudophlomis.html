Stachys pseudophlomis

Stachys pseudophlomis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of purple or violet flowers in late summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and historical use in herbal medicine.
