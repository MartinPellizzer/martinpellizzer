Sparganium minimum subvar. septentrionale

Sparganium minimum subvar. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern lesser bur-reed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, spiky, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for aquatic and avian wildlife.
