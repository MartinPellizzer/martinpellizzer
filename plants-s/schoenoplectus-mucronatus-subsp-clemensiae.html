Schoenoplectus mucronatus subsp. clemensiae

Schoenoplectus mucronatus subsp.

clemensiae, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes and pond edges. Its flowering spikes are usually pink to reddish in color, adding visual interest to its habitat.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
