Salvia incurvata

Salvia incurvata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Salvia incurvata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
