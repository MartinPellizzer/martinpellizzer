Saussurea vaniotii

Saussurea vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Saussurea vaniotii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
