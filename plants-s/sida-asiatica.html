Sida asiatica

Sida asiatica, commonly known as the Asian mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its mucilaginous seeds that can be used in traditional medicine. Sida asiatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
