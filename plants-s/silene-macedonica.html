Silene macedonica

Silene macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Silene macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
