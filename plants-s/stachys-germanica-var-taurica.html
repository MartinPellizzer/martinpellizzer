Stachys germanica var. taurica

Stachys germanica var. taurica, commonly known as the Taurian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered pink or purple flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
