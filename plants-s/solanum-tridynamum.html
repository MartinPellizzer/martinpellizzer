Solanum tridynamum

Solanum tridynamum, commonly known as the three-chambered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum tridynamum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
