Sporobolus confusus

Sporobolus confusus, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It has narrow, blue-green leaves with a rough texture and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Sporobolus confusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
