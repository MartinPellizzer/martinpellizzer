Sida filiformis

Sida filiformis, commonly known as slender sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Sida filiformis is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in ecological succession.
