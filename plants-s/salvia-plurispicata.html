Salvia plurispicata

Salvia plurispicata, commonly known as the purple flowering sage, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
