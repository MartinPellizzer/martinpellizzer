Sagittaria longirostra

Sagittaria longirostra, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and marshes, and has long, narrow leaves that are arrowhead-shaped. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Sagittaria longirostra is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.
