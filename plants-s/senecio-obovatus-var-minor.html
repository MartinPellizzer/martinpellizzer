Senecio obovatus var. minor

Senecio obovatus var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and obovate, with a bluish-gray underside. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
