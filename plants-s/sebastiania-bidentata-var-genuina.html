Sebastiania bidentata var. genuina

Sebastiania bidentata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright purple berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are not particularly showy.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
