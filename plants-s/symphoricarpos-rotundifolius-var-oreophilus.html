Symphoricarpos rotundifolius var. oreophilus

Symphoricarpos rotundifolius var. oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage is typically dense and has rounded, broad leaves that provide habitat for insects and birds.
