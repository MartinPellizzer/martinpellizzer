Stipa petriei

Stipa petriei, commonly known as the Petrie's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Stipa petriei is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
