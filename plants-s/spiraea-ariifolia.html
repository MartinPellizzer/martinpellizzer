Spiraea ariifolia

Spiraea ariifolia, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Spiraea ariifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
