Spiranthes bonariensis

Spiranthes bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, tuberous roots.

Spiranthes bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
