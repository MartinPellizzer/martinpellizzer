Scrophularia lucida subsp. boissieri

Scrophularia lucida subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
