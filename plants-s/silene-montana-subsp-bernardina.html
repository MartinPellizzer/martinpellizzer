Silene montana subsp. bernardina

Silene montana subsp.

bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
