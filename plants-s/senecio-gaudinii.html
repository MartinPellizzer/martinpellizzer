Senecio gaudinii

Senecio gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
