Scirpus palustris subsp. uniglumis

Scirpus palustris subsp.

uniglumis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers.

The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
