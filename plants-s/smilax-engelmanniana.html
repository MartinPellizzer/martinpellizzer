Smilax engelmanniana

Smilax engelmanniana, commonly known as Engelmann's smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
