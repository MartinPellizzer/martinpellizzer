Silene caroliniana subsp. pensylvanica

Silene caroliniana subsp.

pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
