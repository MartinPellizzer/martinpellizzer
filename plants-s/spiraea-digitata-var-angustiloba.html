Spiraea digitata var. angustiloba

Spiraea digitata var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
