Saxifraga tricuspidata f. lutescens

Saxifraga tricuspidata f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, basal leaves that are often reddish or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is distinguished by its striking yellowish foliage, which gives it its common name.
