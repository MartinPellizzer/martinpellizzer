Sinningia tuberosa

Sinningia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots that store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions. The plant produces rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with showy, spurred petals that bloom in clusters.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
