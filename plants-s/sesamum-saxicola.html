Sesamum saxicola

Sesamum saxicola, commonly known as the rock sesame, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Sesamum saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
