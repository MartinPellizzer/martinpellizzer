Spiraea × longifolia

Spiraea × longifolia, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its arching, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea × longifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
