Scirpus pretorianus

Scirpus pretorianus, commonly known as the Pretoria sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Scirpus pretorianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
