Silene antirrhina var. albiflora

Silene antirrhina var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.
