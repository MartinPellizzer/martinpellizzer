Salix petiolaris var. grisea

Salix petiolaris var. grisea, commonly known as the gray willow, is a variant of the willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often grayish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant thrives in moist or wet environments, such as riverbanks and marshy areas, and is well adapted to waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.

The gray willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
