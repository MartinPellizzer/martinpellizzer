Stanhopea eburnea var. punctata

Stanhopea eburnea var. punctata, commonly known as the Spotted Orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow, with distinctive spotted patterns on the petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
