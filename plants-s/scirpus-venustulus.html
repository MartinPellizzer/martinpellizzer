Scirpus venustulus

Scirpus venustulus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Scirpus venustulus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
