Stellaria washingtoniana

Stellaria washingtoniana, commonly known as the Washington star, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Stellaria washingtoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
