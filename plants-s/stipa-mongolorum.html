Stipa mongolorum

Stipa mongolorum, commonly known as Mongolian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and steppe environments.

Stipa mongolorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
