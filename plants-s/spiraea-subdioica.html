Spiraea subdioica

Spiraea subdioica, commonly known as the yellow meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks. The plant features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Spiraea subdioica is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
