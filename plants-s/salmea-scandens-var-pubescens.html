Salmea scandens var. pubescens

Salmea scandens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy climbing salmea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along fences.

Its hairy texture provides a unique adaptation for moisture retention and protection against herbivores.
