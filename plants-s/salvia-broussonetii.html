Salvia broussonetii

Salvia broussonetii, commonly known as the desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia broussonetii is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
