Syzygium flavidum

Syzygium flavidum, commonly known as the yellow syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe, containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Syzygium flavidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
