Sasa hirta

Sasa hirta, commonly known as green needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Sasa hirta plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
