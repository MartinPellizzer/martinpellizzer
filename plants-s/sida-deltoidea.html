Sida deltoidea

Sida deltoidea, commonly known as deltoid sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant has alternate, ovate to deltate leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the summer months.

Sida deltoidea is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
