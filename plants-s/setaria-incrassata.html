Setaria incrassata

Setaria incrassata, commonly known as rough millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy agricultural crop.

Setaria incrassata is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
