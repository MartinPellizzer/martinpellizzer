Solanum sumacaspi

Solanum sumacaspi, commonly known as the Sumac spurge.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Solanum sumacaspi is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
