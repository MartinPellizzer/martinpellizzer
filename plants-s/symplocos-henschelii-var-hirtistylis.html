Symplocos henschelii var. hirtistylis

Symplocos henschelii var. hirtistylis, commonly known as the hairy-styled symplocos, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
