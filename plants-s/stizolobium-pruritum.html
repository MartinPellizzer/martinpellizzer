Stizolobium pruritum

Stizolobium pruritum, commonly known as the prickly acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has small, spiny pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for wildlife.
