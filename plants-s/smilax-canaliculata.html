Smilax canaliculata

Smilax canaliculata, commonly known as the channel-veined smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a woody stem with prominent, grooved ridges and produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a distinctive, waxy texture.

Smilax canaliculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
