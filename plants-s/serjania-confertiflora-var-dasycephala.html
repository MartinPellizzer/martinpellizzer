Serjania confertiflora var. dasycephala

Serjania confertiflora var. dasycephala, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
