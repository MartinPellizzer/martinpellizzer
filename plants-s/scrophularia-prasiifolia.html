Scrophularia prasiifolia

Scrophularia prasiifolia, commonly known as the spring scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
