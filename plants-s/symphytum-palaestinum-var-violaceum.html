Symphytum palaestinum var. violaceum

Symphytum palaestinum var. violaceum, commonly known as the Palestine comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for healing wounds and inflammation.
