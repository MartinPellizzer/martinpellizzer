Sebastiania desertorum

Sebastiania desertorum, commonly known as the desert sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

Sebastiania desertorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
