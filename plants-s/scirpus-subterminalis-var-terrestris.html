Scirpus subterminalis var. terrestris

Scirpus subterminalis var. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its stems are often spongy and hollow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spike-like inflorescences, which are usually found at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and can reach several feet in length.

This variety is valued for its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
