Siphocampylus campanulatus

Siphocampylus campanulatus, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
