Syngonanthus squarrosus var. elatior

Syngonanthus squarrosus var. elatior, commonly known as the greater wooly tamarack, is a variant of the woolly tamarack, native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually grayish-white to silver in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
