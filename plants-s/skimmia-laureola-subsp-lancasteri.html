Skimmia laureola subsp. lancasteri

Skimmia laureola subsp.

lancasteri, commonly known as the Lancashire skimmia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring.

The plant is characterized by its slow growth and dense, compact form, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and is often used as a decorative hedging plant.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Skimmia laureola.
