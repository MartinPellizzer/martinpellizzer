Silphium collinum

Silphium collinum, commonly known as the hill silphium, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silphium collinum is adapted to prairie and dry woodland habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
