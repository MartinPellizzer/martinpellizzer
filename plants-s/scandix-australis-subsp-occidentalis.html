Scandix australis subsp. occidentalis

Scandix australis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Australian sandblow, is a perennial herb native to the southwest region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, hairy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and inland plains. Scandix australis subsp.

occidentalis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
