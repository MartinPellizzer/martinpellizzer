Senecio doria var. biebersteinii

Senecio doria var. biebersteinii, commonly known as the Doria's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its foliage is usually silver-gray, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.
