Scutellaria elliptica var. elliptica

Scutellaria elliptica var. elliptica, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
