Saxifraga tridactylites f. americana

Saxifraga tridactylites f. americana, commonly known as the American three-fingered saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves with three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or arctic regions. It produces white or pink flowers in early summer, and its compact growth form makes it well adapted to harsh environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
