Solanum gula

Solanum gula, commonly known as the "sweet potato vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to red tuberous roots, which are edible. Solanum gula is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible roots.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
