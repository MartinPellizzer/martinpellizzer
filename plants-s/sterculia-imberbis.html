Sterculia imberbis

Sterculia imberbis, commonly known as the naked-stalk sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairless bark and elongated, slender fruits that hang in clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
