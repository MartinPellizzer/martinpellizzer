Stanhopea graveolens var. alba

Stanhopea graveolens var. alba, commonly known as the white stanhopea, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a subtle iridescence. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically in the early morning. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
