Schoenus xipholepis

Schoenus xipholepis, commonly known as the spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color.

Schoenus xipholepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
