Sinapidendron frutescens

Sinapidendron frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered kudzu, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sinapidendron frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
