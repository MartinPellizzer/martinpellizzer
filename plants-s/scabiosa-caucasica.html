Scabiosa caucasica

Scabiosa caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa caucasica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
