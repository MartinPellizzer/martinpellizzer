Sphaeralcea grossulariifolia var. fumariensis

Sphaeralcea grossulariifolia var. fumariensis, commonly known as the fumariensis globe-mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Sphaeralcea genus, which are known for their ecological importance to pollinators.
