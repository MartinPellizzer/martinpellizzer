Spiranthes gracilis var. floridana

Spiranthes gracilis var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender flowering stem.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
