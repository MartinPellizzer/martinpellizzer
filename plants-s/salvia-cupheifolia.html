Salvia cupheifolia

Salvia cupheifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
