Swartzia platygyne

Swartzia platygyne, commonly known as the flat-veined swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is recognized for its dense, dark wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

The tree typically grows in humid, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
