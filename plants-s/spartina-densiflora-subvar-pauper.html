Spartina densiflora subvar. pauper

Spartina densiflora subvar. pauper, commonly known as coastal cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland species.

Its adaptability to saltwater makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
