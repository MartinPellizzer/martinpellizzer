Sempervivum disciforme

Sempervivum disciforme, commonly known as the rosette stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, fleshy leaves arranged in a compact rosette, often with a bluish or silver-green hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking resilient, ornamental plants.
