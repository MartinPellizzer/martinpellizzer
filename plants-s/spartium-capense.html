Spartium capense

Spartium capense, commonly known as the Cape spartium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. Spartium capense is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of tannins and in herbal medicine.
