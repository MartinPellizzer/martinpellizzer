Solanum jacquemontii

Solanum jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its berries are usually yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

Solanum jacquemontii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique flower structure.
