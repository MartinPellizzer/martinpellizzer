Syzygium heloanthum

Syzygium heloanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant white flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Syzygium heloanthum is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
