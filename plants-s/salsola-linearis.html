Salsola linearis

Salsola linearis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, linear leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

Salsola linearis is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
