Serratula pinnatifida var. glabrata

Serratula pinnatifida var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, hairless leaves and stems that are typically unbranched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry, open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
