Symphyotrichum laeve var. concinnum

Symphyotrichum laeve var. concinnum, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white to pale pink petals and a yellow-orange disk in the center. Its botanical characteristics include hairy calyces and a generally compact growth habit.

It is often found in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils.
