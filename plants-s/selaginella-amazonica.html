Selaginella amazonica

Selaginella amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian spikemoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive morphology with closely packed, needle-like leaves and a short, upright stem. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic feature of its evolutionary lineage.

Selaginella amazonica is also known for its ability to survive extreme desiccation, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
