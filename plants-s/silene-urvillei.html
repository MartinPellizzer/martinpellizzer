Silene urvillei

Silene urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene urvillei is a perennial species with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its botanical features make it an attractive subject for botanists and horticulturists interested in alpine flora.
