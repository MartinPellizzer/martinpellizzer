Salvia purepecha

Salvia purepecha, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia purepecha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Mexican medicine.
