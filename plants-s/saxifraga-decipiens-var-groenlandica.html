Saxifraga decipiens var. groenlandica

Saxifraga decipiens var. groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically succulent, helping to retain moisture in nutrient-poor soils.

Saxifraga decipiens var. groenlandica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small arctic organisms.
