Swertia aucheri

Swertia aucheri, commonly known as Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Swertia aucheri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
