Sorbus cretica var. banatica

Sorbus cretica var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat narrowleaf whitebeam, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
