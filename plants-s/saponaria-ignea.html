Saponaria ignea

Saponaria ignea, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that can be soapy when mixed with water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Saponaria ignea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
