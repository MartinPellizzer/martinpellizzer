Salvia aegyptiaca f. albiflora

Salvia aegyptiaca f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Egyptian sage, is a cultivated form of the native Egyptian sage.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. The plant is a perennial herb with square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
