Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp. grandiflora

Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the greater sweet william, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, almost cometary appearance.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
