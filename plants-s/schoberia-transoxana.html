Schoberia transoxana

Schoberia transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Schoberia transoxana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
