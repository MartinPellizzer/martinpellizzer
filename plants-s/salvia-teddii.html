Salvia teddii

Salvia teddii, commonly known as the teddy bear sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its unique appearance and compact growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Salvia teddii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
