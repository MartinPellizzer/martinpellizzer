Seriphium niveum

Seriphium niveum, commonly known as the snowdrop thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Seriphium niveum is a hardy perennial that is often found in meadows and open slopes at higher elevations.
