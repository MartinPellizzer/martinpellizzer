Seriphidium sawanense

Seriphidium sawanense, commonly known as the Sawan wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.

Due to its resilience and ecological significance, it is often found in semi-desert and steppe ecosystems.
