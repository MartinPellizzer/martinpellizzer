Soldanella alpina var. rufescens

Soldanella alpina var. rufescens, commonly known as the Alpine soldanella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it forms dense mats with hairy, glandular leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in association with other alpine flora.

This variety is distinguished by its reddish tinge in the flower petals, which gives it its specific epithet "rufescens.".
