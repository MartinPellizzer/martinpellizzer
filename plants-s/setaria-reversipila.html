Setaria reversipila

Setaria reversipila, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Setaria reversipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
