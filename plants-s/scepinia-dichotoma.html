Scepinia dichotoma

Scepinia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched sea holly, is a coastal plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to sandy, saline environments. It is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.

Scepinia dichotoma is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in full sun.
