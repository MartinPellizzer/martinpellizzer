Sabina virginiana var. crebra

Sabina virginiana var. crebra, commonly known as creeping juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features dense, compact foliage composed of scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover in landscaping.
