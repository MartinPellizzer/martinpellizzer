Salpichlaena volubilis

Salpichlaena volubilis, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread along the ground or ascend tree trunks. The fronds are usually pinnately compound, with a dark green, leathery texture and a serrated margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
