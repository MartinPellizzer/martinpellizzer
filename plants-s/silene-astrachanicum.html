Silene astrachanicum

Silene astrachanicum, commonly known as the Astrachan poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink petals and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Silene astrachanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
