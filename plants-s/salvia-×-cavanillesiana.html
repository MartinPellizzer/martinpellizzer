Salvia × cavanillesiana

Salvia × cavanillesiana, commonly known as the pineapple sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pineapple-scented leaves and showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
