Symplocos melanochroa

Symplocos melanochroa, commonly known as black-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Symplocos melanochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
