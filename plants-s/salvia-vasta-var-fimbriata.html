Salvia vasta var. fimbriata

Salvia vasta var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
