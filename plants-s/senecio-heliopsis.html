Senecio heliopsis

Senecio heliopsis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features serrated leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
