Saussurea nakaiana

Saussurea nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea nakaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
