Sabadilla drummondii

Sabadilla drummondii, commonly known as the Mexican lily or drummond's lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, round berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
