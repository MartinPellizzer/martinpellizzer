Syzygium forrestii

Syzygium forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in incense and furniture.

Syzygium forrestii is also an important species in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
