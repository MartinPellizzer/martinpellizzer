Schinus saheria

Schinus saheria, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Schinus saheria is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
