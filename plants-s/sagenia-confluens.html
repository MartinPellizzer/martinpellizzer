Sagenia confluens

Sagenia confluens, commonly known as the clustered sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Sagenia confluens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
