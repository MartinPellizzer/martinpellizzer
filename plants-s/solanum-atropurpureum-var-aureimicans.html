Solanum atropurpureum var. aureimicans

Solanum atropurpureum var. aureimicans, commonly known as the golden bell pepper plant, is a variant of the common nightshade species.

It is characterized by its vibrant, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small bells, typically harvested when fully mature. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It has hairy, glandular leaves and produces flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which have a sweet and slightly tangy flavor.
