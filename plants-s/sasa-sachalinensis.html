Sasa sachalinensis

Sasa sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its rhizomes form a network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Sasa sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
