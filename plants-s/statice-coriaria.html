Statice coriaria

Statice coriaria, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like flower heads composed of numerous small, blue to purple florets. The plant has silver-gray, linear leaves that are adapted to coastal environments, providing it with drought tolerance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal cliffs.

Statice coriaria is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
