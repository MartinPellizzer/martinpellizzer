Stipa depauperata var. atroviolacea

Stipa depauperata var. atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet false wheat, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically dark violet in color, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
