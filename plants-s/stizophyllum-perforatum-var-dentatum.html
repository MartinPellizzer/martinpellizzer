Stizophyllum perforatum var. dentatum

Stizophyllum perforatum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed perforate stizophyllum, is a unique species of liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It exhibits a thalloid structure with a distinct, leaf-like arrangement that is perforated, giving it its scientific name. The plant's leaves are characterized by their serrated or toothed margins, which distinguish it from other varieties. It thrives in humid, forested areas where it can absorb moisture from the air and soil.

This species plays a role in nutrient cycling and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
