Salix siuzevii

Salix siuzevii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and riparian zones. It has a shallow root system, making it susceptible to erosion but also allowing it to colonize disturbed areas quickly.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
