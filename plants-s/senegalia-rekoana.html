Senegalia rekoana

Senegalia rekoana, commonly known as the Kudu tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Additionally, its wood is used locally for fuel and construction.
