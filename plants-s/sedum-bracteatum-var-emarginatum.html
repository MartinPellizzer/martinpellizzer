Sedum bracteatum var. emarginatum

Sedum bracteatum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the emarginate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in drought conditions.
