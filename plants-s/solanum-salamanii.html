Solanum salamanii

Solanum salamanii, commonly known as the Salaman's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and chemical composition.
