Symphytum foliosum

Symphytum foliosum, commonly known as the broadleaf comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant has a robust root system that contributes to its ability to thrive in moist, fertile soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its mucilaginous properties in its roots.
