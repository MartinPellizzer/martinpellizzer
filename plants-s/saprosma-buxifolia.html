Saprosma buxifolia

Saprosma buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf saprosma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, with leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shaded environments.
