Sclerolaena obliquicuspis

Sclerolaena obliquicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Sclerolaena obliquicuspis is adapted to arid environments and is resilient to drought conditions.
