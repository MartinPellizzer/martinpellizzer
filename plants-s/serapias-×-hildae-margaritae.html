Serapias × hildae-margaritae

Serapias × hildae-margaritae, commonly known as the hybrid orchidgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in horticulture for its unique floral display.
