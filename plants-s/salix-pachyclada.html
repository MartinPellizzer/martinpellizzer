Salix pachyclada

Salix pachyclada, commonly known as the broadleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers, typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The bark is thick and flaky, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
