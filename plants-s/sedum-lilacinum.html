Sedum lilacinum

Sedum lilacinum, commonly known as the blue sedum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Sedum lilacinum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
