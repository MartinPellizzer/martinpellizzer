Stipa ramosissima

Stipa ramosissima, commonly known as the tufted feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa ramosissima is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
