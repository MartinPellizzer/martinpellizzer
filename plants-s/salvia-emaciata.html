Salvia emaciata

Salvia emaciata, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salvia emaciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
