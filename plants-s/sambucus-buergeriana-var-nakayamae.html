Sambucus buergeriana var. nakayamae

Sambucus buergeriana var. nakayamae, commonly known as the Nakayama elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
