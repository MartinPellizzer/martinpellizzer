Solanum sorianum

Solanum sorianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical regions.

Solanum sorianum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
