Sebastiania ampla

Sebastiania ampla, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Sebastiania ampla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
