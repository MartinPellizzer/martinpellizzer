Sempervivum chrysanthum

Sempervivum chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves with a distinctive golden hue along the edges. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking visual element. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
