Salix × fragilis f. basfordiana

Salix × fragilis f. basfordiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive weeping habit.

It features long, cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a striking visual effect. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks through its extensive root system.
