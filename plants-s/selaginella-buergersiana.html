Selaginella buergersiana

Selaginella buergersiana, commonly known as the Burghers' spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, needle-like leaves that are often spirally arranged. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella buergersiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
