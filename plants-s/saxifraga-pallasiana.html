Saxifraga pallasiana

Saxifraga pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Saxifraga pallasiana is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
