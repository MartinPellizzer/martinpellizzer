Sida unidentata

Sida unidentata, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are enclosed in small, three-lobed capsules, and the plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Sida unidentata is adapted to arid environments and is considered a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
