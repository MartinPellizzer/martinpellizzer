Sesbania campylocarpa

Sesbania campylocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, which contribute to its common name.
