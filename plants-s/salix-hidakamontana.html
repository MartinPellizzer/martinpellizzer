Salix hidakamontana

Salix hidakamontana, commonly known as the Japanese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
