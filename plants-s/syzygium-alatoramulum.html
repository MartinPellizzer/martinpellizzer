Syzygium alatoramulum

Syzygium alatoramulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium alatoramulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It also has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
