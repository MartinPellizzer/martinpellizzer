Selaginella crassipes

Selaginella crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
