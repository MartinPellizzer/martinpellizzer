Sida pimpinellifolia

Sida pimpinellifolia, commonly known as common sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from summer to autumn. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sida pimpinellifolia is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
