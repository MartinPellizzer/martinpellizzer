Selaginella novae-hollandiae

Selaginella novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Selaginella novae-hollandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to survive in various environmental conditions.
