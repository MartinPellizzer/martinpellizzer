Sporobolus indicus var. africanus

Sporobolus indicus var. africanus, commonly known as African speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often loosely arranged.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.
