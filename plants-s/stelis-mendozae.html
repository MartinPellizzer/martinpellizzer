Stelis mendozae

Stelis mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. Stelis mendozae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
