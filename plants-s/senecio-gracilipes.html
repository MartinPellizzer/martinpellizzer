Senecio gracilipes

Senecio gracilipes, commonly known as the slender rayless groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It has thin, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Senecio gracilipes is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
