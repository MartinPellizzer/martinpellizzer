Salacia sessiliflora

Salacia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its woody vine structure with opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Salacia sessiliflora is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland forests.
