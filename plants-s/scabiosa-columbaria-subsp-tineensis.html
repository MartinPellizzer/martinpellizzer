Scabiosa columbaria subsp. tineensis

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

tineensis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in dry habitats, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
