Salvia pseudoprivoides

Salvia pseudoprivoides, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia pseudoprivoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
