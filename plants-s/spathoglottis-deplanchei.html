Spathoglottis deplanchei

Spathoglottis deplanchei, commonly known as the flat-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
