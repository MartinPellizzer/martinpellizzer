Satureja acinos var. canescens

Satureja acinos var. canescens, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.
