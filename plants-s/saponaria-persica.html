Saponaria persica

Saponaria persica, commonly known as Persian soapwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is saponin-rich, giving it its common name and making it useful in natural cleaning products. Its foliage is typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Saponaria persica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
