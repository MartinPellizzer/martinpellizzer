Silene menziesii

Silene menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene menziesii is often found in disturbed or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
