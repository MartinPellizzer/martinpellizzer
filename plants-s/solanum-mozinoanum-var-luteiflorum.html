Solanum mozinoanum var. luteiflorum

Solanum mozinoanum var. luteiflorum, commonly known as the Mozambique yellowflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
