Sedum dasyphyllum var. elisae

Sedum dasyphyllum var. elisae, commonly known as the "Elisae stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are thick, waxy, and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a striking addition to succulent collections.
