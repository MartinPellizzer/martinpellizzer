Senecio erlangeri

Senecio erlangeri, commonly known as the Erlanger's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
