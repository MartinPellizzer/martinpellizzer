Solanum sipolisii

Solanum sipolisii, commonly known as the Sipaliwna nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the island of Sipaliwna in the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

Its berries, which are initially green and turn red when mature, are highly poisonous and contain toxic compounds such as solasodine and solasodine-related alkaloids.
