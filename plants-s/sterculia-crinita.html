Sterculia crinita

Sterculia crinita, commonly known as the hairy sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and often covered with a dense layer of hairs, giving it its common name.

Sterculia crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for various purposes.
