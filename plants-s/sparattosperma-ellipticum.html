Sparattosperma ellipticum

Sparattosperma ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic sparrowtree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
