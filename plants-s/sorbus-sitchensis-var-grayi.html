Sorbus sitchensis var. grayi

Sorbus sitchensis var. grayi, commonly known as the Sitka mountain-ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
