Sesamoides purpurascens subsp. prostrata

Sesamoides purpurascens subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its prostrate growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
