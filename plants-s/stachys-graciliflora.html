Stachys graciliflora

Stachys graciliflora, commonly known as slenderflower betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Stachys graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
