Scleranthus banaticus

Scleranthus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Scleranthus banaticus is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in dry, open habitats.
