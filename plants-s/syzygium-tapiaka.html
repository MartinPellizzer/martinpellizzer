Syzygium tapiaka

Syzygium tapiaka, commonly known as the Tapiak tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Syzygium tapiaka thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
