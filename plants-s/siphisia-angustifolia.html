Siphisia angustifolia

Siphisia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf siphisia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Siphisia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
