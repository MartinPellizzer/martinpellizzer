Saussurea parviflora

Saussurea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
