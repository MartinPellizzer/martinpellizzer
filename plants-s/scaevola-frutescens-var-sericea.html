Scaevola frutescens var. sericea

Scaevola frutescens var. sericea, commonly known as silver scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, rounded form and profuse display of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine, silky hairs that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
