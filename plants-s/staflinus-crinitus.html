Staflinus crinitus

Staflinus crinitus, commonly known as the curly hair lichen, is a member of the Lecanoromycetes class.

It is a foliose lichen that forms a dense, crust-like growth on rocks and tree bark. The thallus is typically yellowish-green to brownish in color, with a distinctive, branched structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for forest health.
