Saxifraga cespitosa subsp. leucantha

Saxifraga cespitosa subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the white saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, glaucous leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Saxifraga cespitosa subsp.

leucantha is often found in mountainous and tundra environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
