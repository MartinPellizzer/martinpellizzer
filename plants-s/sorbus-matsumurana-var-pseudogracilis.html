Sorbus matsumurana var. pseudogracilis

Sorbus matsumurana var. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions. The plant features small, oval leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a bright red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
