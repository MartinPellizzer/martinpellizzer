Syzygium nitens

Syzygium nitens, commonly known as the white tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing a single seed surrounded by a sticky, acidic pulp. Syzygium nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its wood is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
