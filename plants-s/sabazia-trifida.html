Sabazia trifida

Sabazia trifida, commonly known as the three-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Sabazia trifida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
