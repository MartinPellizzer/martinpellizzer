Salvia leucophora

Salvia leucophora, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of white to pale blue flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Salvia leucophora is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a fragrant ornamental plant.
