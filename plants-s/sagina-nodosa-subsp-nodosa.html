Sagina nodosa subsp. nodosa

Sagina nodosa subsp.

nodosa, commonly known as moss campion, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments. Sagina nodosa subsp.

nodosa is a hardy species that is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in harsh conditions.
