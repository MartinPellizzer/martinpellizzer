Salvia monclovensis

Salvia monclovensis, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia monclovensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
