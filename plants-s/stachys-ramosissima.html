Stachys ramosissima

Stachys ramosissima, commonly known as redstem deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Stachys ramosissima is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
