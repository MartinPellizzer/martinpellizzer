Statice pectinata var. divaricata

Statice pectinata var. divaricata, commonly known as the spreading sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, branched growth habit and small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its foliage is linear and gray-green, with a slightly succulent texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
