Senegalia saltilloensis

Senegalia saltilloensis, commonly known as the Saltillo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically reduced to spines or modified into small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and high temperatures.

Senegalia saltilloensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
