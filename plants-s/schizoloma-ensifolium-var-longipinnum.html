Schizoloma ensifolium var. longipinnum

Schizoloma ensifolium var. longipinnum, commonly known as the long-pinioned schizoloma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
