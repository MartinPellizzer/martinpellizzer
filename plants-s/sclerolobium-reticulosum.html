Sclerolobium reticulosum

Sclerolobium reticulosum, commonly known as the honeycomb acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
