Sasa australis

Sasa australis, commonly known as the southern bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional basket-making in some regions.
