Saxifraga sedoides var. colorata

Saxifraga sedoides var. colorata, commonly known as the colored saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with bright green leaves that often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or red during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh alpine environments.
