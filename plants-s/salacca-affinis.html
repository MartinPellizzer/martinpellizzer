Salacca affinis

Salacca affinis, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits. The fruits are typically consumed fresh or processed into beverages and traditional foods.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
