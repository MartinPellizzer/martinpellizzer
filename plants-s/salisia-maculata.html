Salisia maculata

Salisia maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Salisia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
