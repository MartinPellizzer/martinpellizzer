Senecillis wilsoniana

Senecillis wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
