Smilax nervomarginata var. liukiuensis

Smilax nervomarginata var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu Smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features woody stems with thorns and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
