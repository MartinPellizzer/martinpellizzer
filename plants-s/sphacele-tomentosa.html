Sphacele tomentosa

Sphacele tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sphacele, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a dense covering of fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Sphacele tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
