Symplocos domingensis

Symplocos domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
