Stobaea membranifolia

Stobaea membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous leaf stobaea, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thin, and membranous leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or white, and it produces berries that are edible for certain wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
