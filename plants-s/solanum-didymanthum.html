Solanum didymanthum

Solanum didymanthum, commonly known as the didymanthum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles.
