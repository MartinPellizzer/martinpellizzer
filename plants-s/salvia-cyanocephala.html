Salvia cyanocephala

Salvia cyanocephala, commonly known as bluehead sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Salvia cyanocephala is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
