Syringa rotundifolia

Syringa rotundifolia, commonly known as mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and is well-suited for garden landscapes due to its ornamental value. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Syringa rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
