Sanango

Sanango, commonly known as the "cinnamon tree," is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
