Silene melanophylla

Silene melanophylla, commonly known as the black-leafed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its life cycle is closely tied to seasonal rainfall patterns, and it often blooms in late spring or early summer.

Silene melanophylla is also noted for its unique seed dispersal mechanism, where the seeds are released in response to touch.
