Selaginella thysanophylla

Selaginella thysanophylla, commonly known as the feather moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures called strobila.

Selaginella thysanophylla plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
