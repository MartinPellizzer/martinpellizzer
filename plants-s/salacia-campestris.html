Salacia campestris

Salacia campestris, commonly known as the field salacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often hairy. Salacia campestris is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
