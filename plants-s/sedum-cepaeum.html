Sedum cepaeum

Sedum cepaeum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum cepaeum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
