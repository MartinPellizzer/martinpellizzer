Setaria macrocarpa

Setaria macrocarpa, commonly known as giant dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flowers that mature into bristly, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Setaria macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
