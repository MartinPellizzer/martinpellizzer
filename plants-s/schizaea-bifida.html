Schizaea bifida

Schizaea bifida, commonly known as the forked moss, is a member of the family Schizaeaceae.

It is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive branching structure, with its stems splitting into two at the base, giving it its common name. Its leaves are small, linear, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Schizaea bifida plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
