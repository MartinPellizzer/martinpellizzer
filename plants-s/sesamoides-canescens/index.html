Sesamoides Canescens

Desert cottonwood, scientifically known as Sesamoides canescens, is a hardy tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in harsh desert environments where water is scarce and temperatures can be extreme. Despite its delicate appearance, this plant possesses deep root systems that allow it to survive prolonged droughts. Its fluffy, cotton-like seeds are dispersed by the wind, enabling it to colonize new areas. The desert cottonwood plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
