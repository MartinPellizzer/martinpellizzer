Saxifraga bronchialis f. burseriana

Saxifraga bronchialis f. burseriana, commonly known as the Burserian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
