Strombocactus disciformis

Strombocactus disciformis, commonly known as the disc-shaped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Strombocactus disciformis is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
