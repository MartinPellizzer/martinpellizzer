Satureja origanoides

Satureja origanoides, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, ovoid fruits.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for their aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
