Seriola pubescens

Seriola pubescens, commonly known as the silver pomfret, is a marine fish species belonging to the family Serranidae.

It is characterized by its silver-scaled body and a distinctive pattern of dark spots along its sides. The plant-like appearance of its scales and the way it interacts with its aquatic environment have led to some botanical comparisons in scientific literature. While not a true plant, its ecological role in coastal ecosystems is significant.

Researchers often study its habitat and growth patterns to understand marine biodiversity better.
