Selaginella cochleata

Selaginella cochleata, commonly known as the spiral liverwort, is a type of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky areas. It has a simple structure with no true roots, stems, or leaves, and relies on spores for reproduction.

Selaginella cochleata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in specific microhabitats.
