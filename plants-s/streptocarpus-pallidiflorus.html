Streptocarpus pallidiflorus

Streptocarpus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Streptocarpus pallidiflorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
