Spergularia colorata var. longipes

Spergularia colorata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are linear and hairy, while the stems are elongated and often branched.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
