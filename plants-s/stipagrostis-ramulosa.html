Stipagrostis ramulosa

Stipagrostis ramulosa, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Stipagrostis ramulosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals in its native range.
