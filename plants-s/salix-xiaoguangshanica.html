Salix xiaoguangshanica

Salix xiaoguangshanica, commonly known as the Xiaoguangshan willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions. It has a relatively short lifespan and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
