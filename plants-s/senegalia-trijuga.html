Senegalia trijuga

Senegalia trijuga, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Senegalia trijuga is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion and providing forage for livestock.

Its adaptability to drought makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
