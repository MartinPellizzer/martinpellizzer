Scillopsis isopetala

Scillopsis isopetala, commonly known as the narrowleaf squill, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Scillopsis isopetala thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
