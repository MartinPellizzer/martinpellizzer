Salix boseensis

Salix boseensis, commonly known as Bose's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a silvery underside, while its catkins are distinctive and play a key role in its reproductive cycle.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
