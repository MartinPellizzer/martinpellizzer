Syringa dilatata var. alba

Syringa dilatata var. alba, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and hairy underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
