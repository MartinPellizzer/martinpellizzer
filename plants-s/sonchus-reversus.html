Sonchus reversus

Sonchus reversus, commonly known as the reverse sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine white or gray pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiky, bristly seed heads. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Sonchus reversus is an annual herb that is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
