Streptopus ajanensis f. atrocarpus

Streptopus ajanensis f. atrocarpus, commonly known as the black-stemmed streptopus, is a rare and unique variant of the streptopus genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that contrast with its green foliage. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately balanced species in its ecosystem.
