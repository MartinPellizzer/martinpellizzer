Stachys integrifolia

Stachys integrifolia, commonly known as broadleaf betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
