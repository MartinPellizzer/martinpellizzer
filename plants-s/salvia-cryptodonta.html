Salvia cryptodonta

Salvia cryptodonta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
