Syzygium mulgraveanum

Syzygium mulgraveanum, commonly known as the Mulgrave apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia, typically growing in rainforests and coastal areas. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

Syzygium mulgraveanum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
