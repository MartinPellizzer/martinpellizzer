Sedum balfourii

Sedum balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Sedum balfourii is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
