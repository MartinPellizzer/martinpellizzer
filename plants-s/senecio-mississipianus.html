Senecio mississipianus

Senecio mississipianus, commonly known as the Mississippian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by small, rounded seeds.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture and can have a slightly hairy surface.
