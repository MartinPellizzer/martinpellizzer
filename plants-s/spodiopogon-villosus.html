Spodiopogon villosus

Spodiopogon villosus, commonly known as the hairy spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Spodiopogon villosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
