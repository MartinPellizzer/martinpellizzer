Senecio vernalis var. caucasicus

Senecio vernalis var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian spring everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

This variant is closely related to other Senecio species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
