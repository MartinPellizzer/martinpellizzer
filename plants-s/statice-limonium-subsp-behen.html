Statice limonium subsp. behen

Statice limonium subsp.

behen, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean. It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments and coastal grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly succulent, adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Statice limonium subsp.

behen is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
