Stachys palaestina var. hypoleuca

Stachys palaestina var. hypoleuca, commonly known as pale-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves that are typically pale green to silver in color. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
