Solanum cochoae

Solanum cochoae, commonly known as the Cochoa nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with smooth, often pubescent stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible but contain toxic compounds.
