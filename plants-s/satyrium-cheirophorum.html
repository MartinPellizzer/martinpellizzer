Satyrium cheirophorum

Satyrium cheirophorum, commonly known as the yellow moon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
