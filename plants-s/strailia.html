Strailia

Strailia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Strailia is a shrub that can grow up to two meters tall, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
