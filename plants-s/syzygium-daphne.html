Syzygium daphne

Syzygium daphne, commonly known as the Daphne syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

Syzygium daphne is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
