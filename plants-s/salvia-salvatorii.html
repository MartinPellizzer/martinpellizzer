Salvia salvatorii

Salvia salvatorii, commonly known as the sacred sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and healing properties.

Salvia salvatorii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in various indigenous practices.
