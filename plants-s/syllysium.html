Syllysium

Syllysium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense, humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer with a faint bioluminescent glow at night, a trait that aids in its pollination by nocturnal insects. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers that emit a subtle fragrance, attracting specific species of moths. Its root system is highly fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Syllysium is considered a critically endangered species.
