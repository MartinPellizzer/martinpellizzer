Syzygium rambutyense

Syzygium rambutyense, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy, spiny fruit exterior. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, hairy fruits. Syzygium rambutyense thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.
