Sideritis hispida

Sideritis hispida, commonly known as rough sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Sideritis hispida typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
