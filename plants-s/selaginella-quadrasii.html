Selaginella quadrasii

Selaginella quadrasii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a compact, low-growing structure with whorled leaves that are often dark green and leathery in texture. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella quadrasii is also notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
