Scirpus californicus

Scirpus californicus, commonly known as California bulrush, is a tall, reed-like plant native to the western United States.

It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fluffy flowers that are often white or pale pink. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus californicus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
