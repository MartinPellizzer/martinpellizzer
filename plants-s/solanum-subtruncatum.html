Solanum subtruncatum

Solanum subtruncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, green berries which turn red when mature. Its leaves are narrow and often have a truncate or notched base.

Solanum subtruncatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
