Scolymus pectinatus

Scolymus pectinatus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitats.
