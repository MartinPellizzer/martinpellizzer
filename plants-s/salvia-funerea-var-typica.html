Salvia funerea var. typica

Salvia funerea var. typica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia funerea var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
