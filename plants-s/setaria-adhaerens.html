Setaria adhaerens

Setaria adhaerens, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that is typically yellow or golden in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Setaria adhaerens is a common weed that can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
