Spilanthes arnicoides var. setosa

Spilanthes arnicoides var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy pellitory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The species is notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
