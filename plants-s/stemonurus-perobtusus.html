Stemonurus perobtusus

Stemonurus perobtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved stemonurus, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are characterized by their broad, wavy margins and a slightly hairy surface.

Stemonurus perobtusus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
