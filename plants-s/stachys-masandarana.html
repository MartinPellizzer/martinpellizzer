Stachys masandarana

Stachys masandarana, commonly known as the Malabar hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Stachys masandarana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
