Solanum pinnatum

Solanum pinnatum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum pinnatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
