Schoenus squamellatus

Schoenus squamellatus, commonly known as the flat-tongued sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and stabilize soil in its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
