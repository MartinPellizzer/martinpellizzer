Sphaerocionium axillare

Sphaerocionium axillare, commonly known as the axillary sphaerocion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release minute seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species in botanical studies.
