Schlumbergera microsphaerica f. parvula

Schlumbergera microsphaerica f. parvula, commonly known as the miniature holiday cactus, is a hybrid species within the Schlumbergera genus, native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. The plant features thick, segmented stems that are often used for propagation through grafting or cuttings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is popular for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
