Sinapis nuttalliana

Sinapis nuttalliana, commonly known as Nuttall's mustard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing several seeds.

Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and have a slightly bitter taste.
