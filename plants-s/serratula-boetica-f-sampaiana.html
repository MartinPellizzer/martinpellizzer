Serratula boetica f. sampaiana

Serratula boetica f. sampaiana, commonly known as the Sampaian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is often found in grasslands and wastelands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
