Solanum oldfieldii

Solanum oldfieldii, commonly known as the Oldfield's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
