Silphium scaberrimum

Silphium scaberrimum, commonly known as rough silphium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.

Due to its historical significance and ecological value, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
