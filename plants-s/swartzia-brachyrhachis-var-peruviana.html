Swartzia brachyrhachis var. peruviana

Swartzia brachyrhachis var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark wood and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
