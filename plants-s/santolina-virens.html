Santolina virens

Santolina virens, commonly known as silver thread, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the spring, which are followed by spherical, blue-gray seed heads. Santolina virens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
