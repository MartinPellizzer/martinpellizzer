Swartzia emarginata

Swartzia emarginata, commonly known as the African ebony, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Swartzia emarginata is valued for its hard, heavy timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
