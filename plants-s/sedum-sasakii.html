Sedum sasakii

Sedum sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in summer. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in well-drained soil.

Sedum sasakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in gardens.
