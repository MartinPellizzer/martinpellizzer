Stobaea atractyloides

Stobaea atractyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Stobaea atractyloides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
