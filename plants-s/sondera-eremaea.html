Sondera eremaea

Sondera eremaea, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Sondera eremaea is well adapted to harsh, low-nutrient soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
