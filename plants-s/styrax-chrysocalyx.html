Styrax chrysocalyx

Styrax chrysocalyx, commonly known as golden cup styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, smooth, and glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with a subtle aromatic scent.

Styrax chrysocalyx is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
