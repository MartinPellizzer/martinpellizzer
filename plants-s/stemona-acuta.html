Stemona acuta

Stemona acuta, commonly known as acute stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem that is often branched and covered with small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
