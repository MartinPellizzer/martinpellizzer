Solanum tuberosum f. wila-k'oyu

Solanum tuberosum f. wila-k'oyu, commonly known as the Wila-k'oyu potato, is a unique botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its distinct tuber morphology, often featuring a high starch content and a rich, nutty flavor. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with dark green, broad leaves and erect stems. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, typical of the Solanaceae family.

This variety is valued for its culinary qualities and is often cultivated for both local consumption and traditional use.
