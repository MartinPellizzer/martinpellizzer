Salix cinerea var. crispa

Salix cinerea var. crispa, commonly known as the silver willow with crisped leaves, is a variant of the silver willow native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and finely serrated, crisped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows along stream banks and in moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and graceful growth habit.
