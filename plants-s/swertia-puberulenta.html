Swertia puberulenta

Swertia puberulenta, commonly known as the woolly swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the summer months and attract various pollinators.
