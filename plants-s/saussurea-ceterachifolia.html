Saussurea ceterachifolia

Saussurea ceterachifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flower heads arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
