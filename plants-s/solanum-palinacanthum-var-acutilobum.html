Solanum palinacanthum var. acutilobum

Solanum palinacanthum var. acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed nightshade, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with sharply pointed lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and usually white or pale green, appearing in clusters.

The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasodine.
