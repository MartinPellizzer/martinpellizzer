Silene italica var. strandjensis

Silene italica var. strandjensis, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
