Santolina annua

Santolina annua, commonly known as annual santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces compact, upright stems that are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value. Santolina annua thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
