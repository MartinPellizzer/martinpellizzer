Salvia nemorosa var. fugax

Salvia nemorosa var. fugax, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a grayish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its attractive foliage and extended blooming period, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
