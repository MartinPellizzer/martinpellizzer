Sorbus unguiculata

Sorbus unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and have a distinctively toothed margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
