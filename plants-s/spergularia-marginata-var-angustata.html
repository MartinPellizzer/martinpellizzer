Spergularia marginata var. angustata

Spergularia marginata var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

It is often found in sandy soils and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
