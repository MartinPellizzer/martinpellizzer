Salsola armata

Salsola armata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Salsola armata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
