Stenorrhynchos speciosum var. nutans

Stenorrhynchos speciosum var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging stenorrhynchos, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often hanging from the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers are usually a mix of white and pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.
