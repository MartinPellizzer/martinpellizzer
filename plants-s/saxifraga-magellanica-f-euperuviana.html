Saxifraga magellanica f. euperuviana

Saxifraga magellanica f. euperuviana, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and rosette-forming leaves. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
