Solidago virgaurea subsp. lapponica

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in tundra and boreal forest environments.

The plant features bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small ray flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in supporting local pollinators in its native habitats.
