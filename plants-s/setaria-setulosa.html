Setaria setulosa

Setaria setulosa, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Setaria setulosa is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
