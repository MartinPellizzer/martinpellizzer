Scirpus mexicanus

Scirpus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican bulrush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
