Saccoloma hookerianum

Saccoloma hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's saccoloma, is a member of the Lepidomycetes order, characterized by its unique fungal-like structure.

It is a saprophytic plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a filamentous growth pattern, with a central axis covered in small, scale-like structures. Its reproductive structures are specialized, producing spores through a complex developmental process.

Saccoloma hookerianum plays a role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem by decomposing organic matter.
