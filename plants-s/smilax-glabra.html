Smilax glabra

Smilax glabra, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and its ability to climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Smilax glabra is also known for its edible berries, which are typically yellow or red when ripe.
