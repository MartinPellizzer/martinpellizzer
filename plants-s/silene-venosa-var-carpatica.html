Silene venosa var. carpatica

Silene venosa var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene venosa var. carpatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in mountainous regions.
