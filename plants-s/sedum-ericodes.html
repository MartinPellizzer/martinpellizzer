Sedum ericodes

Sedum ericodes, commonly known as the red sedum, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Sedum ericodes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
