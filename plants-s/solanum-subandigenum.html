Solanum subandigenum

Solanum subandigenum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

Solanum subandigenum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
