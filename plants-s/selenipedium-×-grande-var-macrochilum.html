Selenipedium × grande var. macrochilum

Selenipedium × grande var. macrochilum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. Native to the cloud forests of Central America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has a unique growth habit, often forming a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
