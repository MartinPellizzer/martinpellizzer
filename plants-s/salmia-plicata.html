Salmia plicata

Salmia plicata, commonly known as the pleated salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Salmia plicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
