Selaginella plumea

Selaginella plumea, commonly known as the plumegrass, is a species of spiky, low-growing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Selaginella plumea is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique ability to survive in arid conditions through its specialized structures.

Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are essential for its propagation and ecological role in its native habitat.
