Senecio subverticillatus

Senecio subverticillatus, commonly known as the clustered groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
