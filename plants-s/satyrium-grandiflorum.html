Satyrium grandiflorum

Satyrium grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered satyrium, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and the flowers can vary in color from white to pink or purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
