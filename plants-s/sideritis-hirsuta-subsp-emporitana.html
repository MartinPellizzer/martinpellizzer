Sideritis hirsuta subsp. emporitana

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

emporitana, commonly known as the Emoritana ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological preferences, making it a notable species in its native range.
