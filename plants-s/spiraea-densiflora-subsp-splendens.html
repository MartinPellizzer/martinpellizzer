Spiraea densiflora subsp. splendens

Spiraea densiflora subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the showy spiraea, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
