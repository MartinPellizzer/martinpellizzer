Solanum vespertilio subsp. vespertilio

Solanum vespertilio subsp.

vespertilio, commonly known as the evening bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a deep blue or black, containing several seeds.
