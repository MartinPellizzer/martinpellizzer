Schizostachyum sanguineum

Schizostachyum sanguineum, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Schizostachyum sanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
