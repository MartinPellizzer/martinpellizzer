Swartzia madagascariensis

Swartzia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests, where it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.
