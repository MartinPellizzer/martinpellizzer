Senecio aristianus

Senecio aristianus, commonly known as the arrowleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and narrow, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
