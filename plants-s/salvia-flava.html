Salvia flava

Salvia flava, commonly known as yellow sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other well-known herbs like mint and lavender. Salvia flava typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, preferring full sun.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
