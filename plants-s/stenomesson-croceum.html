Stenomesson croceum

Stenomesson croceum, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of acacia trees in arid regions of Africa.

It has small, green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant relies on its host for nutrients and water, and its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Stenomesson croceum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, though its parasitic nature can sometimes impact the health of its host trees.
