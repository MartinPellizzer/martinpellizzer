Sponia pumila

Sponia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf spongeplant, is a small, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Sponia pumila thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
