Senecio sudeticus

Senecio sudeticus, commonly known as the Sudetic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio sudeticus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of the Carpathian region.
