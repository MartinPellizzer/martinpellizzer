Senecio hemmendorffii

Senecio hemmendorffii, commonly known as the Hemmendorff's groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
