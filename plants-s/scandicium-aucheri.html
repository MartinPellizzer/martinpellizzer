Scandicium aucheri

Scandicium aucheri, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Scandicium aucheri thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
