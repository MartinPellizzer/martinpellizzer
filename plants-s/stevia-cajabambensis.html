Stevia cajabambensis

Stevia cajabambensis, commonly known as the Andean sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its intense sweet taste, which comes from steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique properties, it is increasingly being studied for potential use in the food and pharmaceutical industries.
