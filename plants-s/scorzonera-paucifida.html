Scorzonera paucifida

Scorzonera paucifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust, fleshy root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Scorzonera paucifida is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
