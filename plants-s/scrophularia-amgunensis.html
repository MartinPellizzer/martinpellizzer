Scrophularia amgunensis

Scrophularia amgunensis, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy, often bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Scrophularia amgunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
