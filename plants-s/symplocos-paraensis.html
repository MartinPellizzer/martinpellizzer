Symplocos paraensis

Symplocos paraensis, commonly known as the Para raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, red, raspberry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has dark green, elliptical leaves. It produces fragrant white flowers during the wet season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
