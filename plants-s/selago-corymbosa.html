Selago corymbosa

Selago corymbosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring a dense, branching structure. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, creating a striking visual display. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

Selago corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
