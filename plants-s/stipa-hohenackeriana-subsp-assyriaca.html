Stipa hohenackeriana subsp. assyriaca

Stipa hohenackeriana subsp.

assyriaca, commonly known as the Assyrian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
