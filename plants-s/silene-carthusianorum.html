Silene carthusianorum

Silene carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene carthusianorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
