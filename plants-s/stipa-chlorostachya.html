Stipa chlorostachya

Stipa chlorostachya, commonly known as blue grass or blue needle grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive blue-green coloration, which is due to the presence of a waxy coating on the foliage. The plant produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with blue or purple hues. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Stipa chlorostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
