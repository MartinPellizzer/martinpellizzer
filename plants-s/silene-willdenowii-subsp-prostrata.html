Silene willdenowii subsp. prostrata

Silene willdenowii subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate willdenow's campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a dense mat.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
