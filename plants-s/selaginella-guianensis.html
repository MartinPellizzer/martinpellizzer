Selaginella guianensis

Selaginella guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. Selaginella guianensis is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stabilization and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
