Senacia octogona

Senacia octogona, commonly known as the eight-angled senacia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, eight-sided stem that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
