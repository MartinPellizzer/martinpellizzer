Scaphyglottis baudoana

Scaphyglottis baudoana, commonly known as the Baudoan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a distinctive lip or labellum. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
