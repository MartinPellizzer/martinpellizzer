Sinapis alba var. trichocarpa

Sinapis alba var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a variant of the white mustard plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are typically used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The hairy texture of its foliage is a distinguishing feature that helps in its identification.
