Solanum oxycarpum

Solanum oxycarpum, commonly known as the sharp-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries with a distinctive sharp, pointed calyx. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a pubescent surface.

Solanum oxycarpum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can be toxic if ingested.
