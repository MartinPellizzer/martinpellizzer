Schoenoplectus × triquetriformis

Schoenoplectus × triquetriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along lake shores. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered brown flowers.

Schoenoplectus × triquetriformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
