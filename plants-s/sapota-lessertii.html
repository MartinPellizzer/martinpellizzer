Sapota lessertii

Sapota lessertii, commonly known as the lesser sapota, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sapotaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a smooth, leathery skin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
