Sesbania mossambicensis

Sesbania mossambicensis, commonly known as the African sesbania, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sesbania mossambicensis is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
