Syzygium pseudojambolana

Syzygium pseudojambolana, commonly known as the Java apple or surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible, red or yellow fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. Its ornamental value is also notable, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Syzygium pseudojambolana thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, tropical environment for optimal growth.
