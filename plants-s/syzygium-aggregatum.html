Syzygium aggregatum

Syzygium aggregatum, commonly known as the rose apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits with a pinkish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, often with a reddish hue when young.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.
