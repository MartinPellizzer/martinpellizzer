Sideritis libanotica var. linearis

Sideritis libanotica var. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaved rock sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Sideritis libanotica var. linearis is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs such as mint and sage.
