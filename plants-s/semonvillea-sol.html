Semonvillea sol

Semonvillea sol, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in response to sunlight. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow or orange petals with a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Semonvillea sol is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to survive in drought conditions.
