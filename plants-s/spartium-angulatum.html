Spartium angulatum

Spartium angulatum, commonly known as angular spartium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Spartium angulatum is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
