Solanum pachycalyx

Solanum pachycalyx, commonly known as the thick-calyx nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy calyx that persists on the fruit, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Solanum pachycalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
