Scrophularia thesioides

Scrophularia thesioides, commonly known as the Greek figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia thesioides is often found in open habitats, including grasslands and disturbed areas.
