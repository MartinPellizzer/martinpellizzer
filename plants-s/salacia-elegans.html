Salacia elegans

Salacia elegans, commonly known as the elegant salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salacia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
