Stenocarpus umbellatus var. billardierei

Stenocarpus umbellatus var. billardierei, commonly known as the coastal holly or broad-leafed holly, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native wildlife. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.

The variety billardierei is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Stenocarpus umbellatus.
