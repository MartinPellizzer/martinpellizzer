Swartzia parvipetala

Swartzia parvipetala, commonly known as the small-flowered swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, lowland forest habitats.
