Sida hederacea var. sulphurea

Sida hederacea var. sulphurea, commonly known as golden sida, is a variant of the common sida species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and often bear glandular dots. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, producing a striking golden appearance.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.
