Sasa geibiensis

Sasa geibiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Sasa geibiensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
