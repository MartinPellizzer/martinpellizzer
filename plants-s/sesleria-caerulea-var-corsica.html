Sesleria caerulea var. corsica

Sesleria caerulea var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a slightly bluish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes in early summer, which are often tinged with purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
