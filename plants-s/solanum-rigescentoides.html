Solanum rigescentoides

Solanum rigescentoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy, reddish stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds, and it is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.
