Stachys inflata subsp. albopannosa

Stachys inflata subsp.

albopannosa, commonly known as the white-pelted hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or pale underside.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
