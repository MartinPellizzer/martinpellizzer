Silene hayekiana

Silene hayekiana, commonly known as the Hayek's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Silene hayekiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
