Stellaria longipes subsp. monantha

Stellaria longipes subsp.

monantha, commonly known as the slender starwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems that often lie prostrate on the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a degree of drought tolerance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface.
