Senecio sericeus var. pinnatifidus

Senecio sericeus var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
