Staurogyne aristata

Staurogyne aristata, commonly known as the slender waterwheel plant, is a semi-aquatic herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with the petioles extending downward into the water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its roots are adapted for aquatic environments, allowing it to anchor itself in muddy substrates.

Staurogyne aristata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
