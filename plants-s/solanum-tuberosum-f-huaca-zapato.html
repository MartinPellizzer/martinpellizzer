Solanum tuberosum f. huaca-zapato

Solanum tuberosum f. huaca-zapato, commonly known as the Huaca-Zapato potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato with distinctive morphological traits.

This variety is characterized by its unique tuber shape, often featuring a flattened or irregular form, and a skin that may vary in color from brown to purple. It is native to the Andean region, where it has been cultivated for generations by indigenous communities. The plant typically grows in cooler climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural research and botanical classification.
