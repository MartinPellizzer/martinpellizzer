Sida pentacarpos

Sida pentacarpos, commonly known as the five-seeded mallow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features a branched stem covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are arranged alternately along the stem. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release five small, smooth seeds.

Sida pentacarpos is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
