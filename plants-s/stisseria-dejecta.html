Stisseria dejecta

Stisseria dejecta, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its roots are deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from underground sources.

Stisseria dejecta plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
