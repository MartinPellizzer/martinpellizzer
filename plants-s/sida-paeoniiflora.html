Sida paeoniiflora

Sida paeoniiflora, commonly known as the peonyleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the peony, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sida paeoniiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
