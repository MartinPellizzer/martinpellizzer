Syringa oblata var. alba

Syringa oblata var. alba, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elongated leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
