Salix cariotii

Salix cariotii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
