Stylocoryna roxburghii

Stylocoryna roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan evergreen.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Stylocoryna roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
