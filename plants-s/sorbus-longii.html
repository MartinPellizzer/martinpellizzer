Sorbus longii

Sorbus longii, commonly known as the longii whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, and its distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble small apples. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

It is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
