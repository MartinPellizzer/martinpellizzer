Salix × zhataica

Salix × zhataica, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist environments and is often planted near water bodies for its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is used in both landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
