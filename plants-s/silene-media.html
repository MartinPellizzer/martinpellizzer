Silene media

Silene media, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals. Silene media is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
