Scutellaria alta

Scutellaria alta, commonly known as tall skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria alta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
