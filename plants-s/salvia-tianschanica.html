Salvia tianschanica

Salvia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Salvia tianschanica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.
