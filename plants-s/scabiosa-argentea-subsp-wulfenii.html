Scabiosa argentea subsp. wulfenii

Scabiosa argentea subsp.

wulfenii, commonly known as silver scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are silver-white with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are upright with a rough, hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
