Selaginella hintonii

Selaginella hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, which are often covered in a thin layer of moss. It is characterized by its ability to reproduce via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella hintonii is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
