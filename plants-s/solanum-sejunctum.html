Solanum sejunctum

Solanum sejunctum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Solanum sejunctum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
