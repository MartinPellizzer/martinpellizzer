Saubinetia

Saubinetia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often vibrant red or purple flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where they thrive in humid, shaded environments. Saubinetia species usually have slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern.
