Salvia mellifera var. revoluta

Salvia mellifera var. revoluta, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
