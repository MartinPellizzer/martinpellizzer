Succisa dentata

Succisa dentata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by green, fleshy capsules containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Succisa dentata is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its latex-containing sap.
