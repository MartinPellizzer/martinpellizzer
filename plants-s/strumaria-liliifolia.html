Strumaria liliifolia

Strumaria liliifolia, commonly known as the lily-leaved strumaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Strumaria liliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms.
