Selenicereus undatus

Selenicereus undatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant bloom that lasts only a few hours, typically during the evening. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal animals such as bats and moths.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
