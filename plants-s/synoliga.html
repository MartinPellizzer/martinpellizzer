Synoliga

Synoliga, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Synoliga is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
