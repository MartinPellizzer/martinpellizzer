Stachys subargentea

Stachys subargentea, commonly known as silver hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable species within the Lamiaceae family.
