Salvia japonica var. integrifolia

Salvia japonica var. integrifolia, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

This species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
