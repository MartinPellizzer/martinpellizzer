Senecio polifolius

Senecio polifolius, commonly known as the many-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles.

Senecio polifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
