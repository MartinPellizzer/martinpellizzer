Sedum villosum subsp. aristatum

Sedum villosum subsp.

aristatum, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America. It features dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
