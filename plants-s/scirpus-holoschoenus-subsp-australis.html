Scirpus holoschoenus subsp. australis

Scirpus holoschoenus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize wetland soils.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in freshwater or brackish wetlands, often in muddy or shallow waters.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in water filtration and providing habitat for aquatic life.
