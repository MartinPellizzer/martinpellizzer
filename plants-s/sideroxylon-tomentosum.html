Sideroxylon tomentosum

Sideroxylon tomentosum, commonly known as the silky ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds.

This species is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
