Senecio acanthifolius subsp. virens

Senecio acanthifolius subsp.

virens, commonly known as the greenleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
