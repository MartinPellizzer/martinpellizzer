Stachys argillicola

Stachys argillicola, commonly known as the clay plant or claywort, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This species is typically found in moist, clay-rich soils, often in woodland or meadow environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, usually with purple or pink hues, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Stachys argillicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
