Sedum laxum subsp. perplexum

Sedum laxum subsp.

perplexum, commonly known as the variable sedum, is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial succulent that typically forms dense mats of fleshy, upright stems and leaves.

The plant is characterized by its variable leaf shape and color, which can range from blue-green to silver-gray depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal water.
