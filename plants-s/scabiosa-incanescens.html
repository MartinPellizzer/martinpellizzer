Scabiosa incanescens

Scabiosa incanescens, commonly known as the silver scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa incanescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
