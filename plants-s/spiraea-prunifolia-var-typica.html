Spiraea prunifolia var. typica

Spiraea prunifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
