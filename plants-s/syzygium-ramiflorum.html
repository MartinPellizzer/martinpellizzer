Syzygium ramiflorum

Syzygium ramiflorum, commonly known as the ramiflorum apple or slender apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, smooth, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, small, yellow or orange fruits that are similar in appearance to apples.

Syzygium ramiflorum is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
