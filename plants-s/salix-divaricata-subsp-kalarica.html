Salix divaricata subsp. kalarica

Salix divaricata subsp.

kalarica, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
