Scoparia montevidensis var. grisebachii

Scoparia montevidensis var. grisebachii, commonly known as the Grey-haired Scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, greyish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
