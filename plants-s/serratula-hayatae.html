Serratula hayatae

Serratula hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese serrated wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
