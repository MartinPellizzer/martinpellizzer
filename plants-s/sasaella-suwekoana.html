Sasaella suwekoana

Sasaella suwekoana, commonly known as the Suwekoan bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of up to 3 meters and has narrow, elongated leaves with a grass-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasaella suwekoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited uses in traditional medicine and basket weaving.
