Scilla cydonia

Scilla cydonia, commonly known as the sea squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in coastal areas and rocky soils. Scilla cydonia is adapted to saline environments and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its bulbs are also historically used in traditional medicine.
