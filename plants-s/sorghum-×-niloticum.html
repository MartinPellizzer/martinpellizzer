Sorghum × niloticum

Sorghum × niloticum, commonly known as kafir corn, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from sorghum and millet.

It is a tall, annual grass with narrow, blade-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its hard, inedible seeds that are often used for animal feed or traditional crafts. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture in challenging environments.
