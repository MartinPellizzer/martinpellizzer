Stachys micheliana

Stachys micheliana, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers with purple upper lips and white lower lips, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
