Solidago vulgaris var. coriacea

Solidago vulgaris var. coriacea, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a whitish or grayish bloom. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
