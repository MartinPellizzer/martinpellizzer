Solanum sisymbriifolium f. lilacinum

Solanum sisymbriifolium f. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
