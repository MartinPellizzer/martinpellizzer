Silene setaesperma

Silene setaesperma, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. Silene setaesperma is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
