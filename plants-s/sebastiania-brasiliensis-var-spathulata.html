Sebastiania brasiliensis var. spathulata

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. spathulata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that provide a striking visual appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or spatulate in shape, with a glossy surface that reflects light. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
