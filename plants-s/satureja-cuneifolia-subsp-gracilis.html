Satureja cuneifolia subsp. gracilis

Satureja cuneifolia subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed.
