Scandix grandiflora var. lasiactina

Scandix grandiflora var. lasiactina, commonly known as the velvetleaf false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an invasive species in many regions.
