Syringa tetanoloba

Syringa tetanoloba, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-shaped clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

Syringa tetanoloba is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
