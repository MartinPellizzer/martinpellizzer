Scutellaria pseudotenax

Scutellaria pseudotenax, commonly known as blue beakviolet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria pseudotenax typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
