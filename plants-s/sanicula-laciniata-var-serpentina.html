Sanicula laciniata var. serpentina

Sanicula laciniata var. serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose, branched cluster, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas.
