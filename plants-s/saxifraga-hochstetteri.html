Saxifraga hochstetteri

Saxifraga hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of New Zealand.

It features rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga hochstetteri is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
