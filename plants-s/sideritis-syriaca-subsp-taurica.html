Sideritis syriaca subsp. taurica

Sideritis syriaca subsp.

taurica, commonly known as Syrian rock sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers are characteristic features of the species.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
