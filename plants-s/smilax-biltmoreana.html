Smilax biltmoreana

Smilax biltmoreana, commonly known as the Biltmore smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax biltmoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
