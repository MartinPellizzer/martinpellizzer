Stipa × majalis subsp. majalis

Stipa × majalis subsp.

majalis, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and its flowering period occurs in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
