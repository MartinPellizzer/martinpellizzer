Salix × pommaretii

Salix × pommaretii, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main trunk, creating a striking visual effect. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features or in areas with high groundwater.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
