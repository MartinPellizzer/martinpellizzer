Silene cucubalus var. tenella

Silene cucubalus var. tenella, commonly known as the small-flowered catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins. It thrives in moist, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Silene cucubalus var. tenella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
