Scirpus compressus var. gracilis

Scirpus compressus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly compressed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
