Senna angustisiliqua var. inaguensis

Senna angustisiliqua var. inaguensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are narrow and often trifoliate. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.

The seeds of this plant are also used as a source of dietary fiber and in the production of natural dyes.
