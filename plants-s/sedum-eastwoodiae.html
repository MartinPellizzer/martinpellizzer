Sedum eastwoodiae

Sedum eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
