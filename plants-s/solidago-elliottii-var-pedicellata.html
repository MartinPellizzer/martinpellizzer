Solidago elliottii var. pedicellata

Solidago elliottii var. pedicellata, commonly known as Elliott's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often hairy beneath.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated flower clusters and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
