Salvia rigida var. cryptocaulos

Salvia rigida var. cryptocaulos, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

The species is also valued for its aromatic leaves and ability to attract pollinators.
