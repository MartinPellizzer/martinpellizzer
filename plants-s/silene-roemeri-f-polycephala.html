Silene roemeri f. polycephala

Silene roemeri f. polycephala, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological role in its native environment.
