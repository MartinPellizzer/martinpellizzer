Solanum soukupii var. espinarense

Solanum soukupii var. espinarense, commonly known as the Espinarensis nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the berries are typically red when ripe.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
