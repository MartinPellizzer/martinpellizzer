Sorocea pubivena subsp. oligotricha

Sorocea pubivena subsp.

oligotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorocea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive pubescent margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
