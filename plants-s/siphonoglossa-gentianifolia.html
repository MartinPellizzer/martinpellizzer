Siphonoglossa gentianifolia

Siphonoglossa gentianifolia, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
