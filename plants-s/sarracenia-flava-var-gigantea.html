Sarracenia flava var. gigantea

Sarracenia flava var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant yellow pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant yellow pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires a humid, sunny environment to flourish. Its pitchers can grow up to 12 inches in length, making it one of the largest species within the Sarracenia genus.

This variety is prized by plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological role in trapping insects.
