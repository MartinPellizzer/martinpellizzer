Sida leiophylla

Sida leiophylla, commonly known as silky sida, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

Sida leiophylla is adapted to wetland and marsh environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
