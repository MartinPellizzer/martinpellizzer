Stelis ingramii

Stelis ingramii, commonly known as the Ingram's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Stelis ingramii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
