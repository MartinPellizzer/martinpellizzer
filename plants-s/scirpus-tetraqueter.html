Scirpus tetraqueter

Scirpus tetraqueter, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland habitats.

Scirpus tetraqueter plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
