Stanhopea oculata var. lindleyi

Stanhopea oculata var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature bright yellow petals and a contrasting purple or reddish lip. The plant produces its flowers on tall, branching inflorescences, often reaching several feet in height. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native, often arid environments.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
