Symphyotrichum patens var. gracile

Symphyotrichum patens var. gracile, commonly known as slender New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
