Senecio adrianicus

Senecio adrianicus, commonly known as the Adrian's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
