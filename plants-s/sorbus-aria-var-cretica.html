Sorbus aria var. cretica

Sorbus aria var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are elliptical with finely toothed margins and a dark green upper surface.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
