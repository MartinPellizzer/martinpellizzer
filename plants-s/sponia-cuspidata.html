Sponia cuspidata

Sponia cuspidata, commonly known as the horned sponia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Sponia cuspidata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
