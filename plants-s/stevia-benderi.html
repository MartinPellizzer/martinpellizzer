Stevia benderi

Stevia benderi, commonly known as the dwarf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained attention as a natural sweetener alternative.
