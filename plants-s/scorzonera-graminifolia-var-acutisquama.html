Scorzonera graminifolia var. acutisquama

Scorzonera graminifolia var. acutisquama, commonly known as the narrowleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera graminifolia var. acutisquama thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
