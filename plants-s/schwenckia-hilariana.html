Schwenckia hilariana

Schwenckia hilariana, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small cups, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Schwenckia hilariana is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
