Stellaria holostea f. apetala

Stellaria holostea f. apetala, commonly known as the apetalous starwort, is a variant of the starwort plant characterized by its reduced or absent flowers.

This botanical form exhibits a unique floral morphology, with the typical five-petaled structure modified or entirely lacking. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation, offering insights into floral evolution and genetic diversity within the species.
