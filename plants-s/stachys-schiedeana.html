Stachys schiedeana

Stachys schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish to violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Stachys schiedeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
