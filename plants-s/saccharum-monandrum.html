Saccharum monandrum

Saccharum monandrum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its jointed stalks and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces a high amount of sucrose in its stems, making it a key crop for sugar production. It thrives in warm climates with abundant rainfall and requires well-drained soil.

Saccharum monandrum is also used in the production of biofuels and has various industrial applications.
