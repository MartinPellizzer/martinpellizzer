Steris sartorii

Steris sartorii, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Steris sartorii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
