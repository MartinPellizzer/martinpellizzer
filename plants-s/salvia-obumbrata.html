Salvia obumbrata

Salvia obumbrata, commonly known as the shadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Salvia obumbrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
