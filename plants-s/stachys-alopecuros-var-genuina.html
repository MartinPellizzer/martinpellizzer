Stachys alopecuros var. genuina

Stachys alopecuros var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
