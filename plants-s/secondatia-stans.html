Secondatia stans

Secondatia stans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. Its most distinctive botanical feature is its clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
