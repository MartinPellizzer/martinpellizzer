Silene oropediorum

Silene oropediorum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

Silene oropediorum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open slopes and meadows at high elevations.
