Saurauia leucocarpa var. willdenowii

Saurauia leucocarpa var. willdenowii, commonly known as the white-fruited saurauia, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable component of its native ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
