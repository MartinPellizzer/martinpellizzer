Senecio atropurpureus

Senecio atropurpureus, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-pink flower heads and gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its use in both garden and ecological landscapes.
