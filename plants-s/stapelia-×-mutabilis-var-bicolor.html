Stapelia × mutabilis var. bicolor

Stapelia × mutabilis var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored African milk bush, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically small and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
