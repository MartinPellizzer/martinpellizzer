Sorbus alandica

Sorbus alandica, commonly known as the Baltic rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along riverbanks.

Sorbus alandica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
