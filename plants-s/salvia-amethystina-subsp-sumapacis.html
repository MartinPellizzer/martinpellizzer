Salvia amethystina subsp. sumapacis

Salvia amethystina subsp.

sumapacis, commonly known as the Sumapacis sage, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique color and fragrance.
