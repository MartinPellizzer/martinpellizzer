Silene lanceolata var. forbesii

Silene lanceolata var. forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' narrowleaf catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
