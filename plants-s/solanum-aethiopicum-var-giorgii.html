Solanum aethiopicum var. giorgii

Solanum aethiopicum var. giorgii, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
