Salix gracilistyla f. variegata

Salix gracilistyla f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping willow, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its slender, drooping branches and distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often displaying a striking variegation of white, yellow, and green patterns. This cultivar thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to China and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ability to provide shade.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
