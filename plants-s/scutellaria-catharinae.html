Scutellaria catharinae

Scutellaria catharinae, commonly known as Catherine's skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scutellaria catharinae is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
