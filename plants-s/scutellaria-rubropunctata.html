Scutellaria rubropunctata

Scutellaria rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive red or purple spot on each leaf. The plant produces blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Scutellaria rubropunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
