Sideritis incana var. arairae

Sideritis incana var. arairae, commonly known as the Armenian sidereal, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
