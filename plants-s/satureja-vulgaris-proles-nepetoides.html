Satureja vulgaris proles nepetoides

Satureja vulgaris proles nepetoides, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces tiny, pale purple or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics include a square stem and a distinctive, pungent fragrance reminiscent of thyme and oregano.
