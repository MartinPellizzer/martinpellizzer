Shibataea chinensis f. aureostriata

Shibataea chinensis f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-stripped evergreen holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive golden-yellow veins, which give it its unique appearance. The plant is evergreen, meaning it retains its foliage throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by red berries that provide food for birds and wildlife.
