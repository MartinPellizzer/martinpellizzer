Solidago glaberrima var. moritura

Solidago glaberrima var. moritura, commonly known as the Florida goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and produces abundant yellow flowers in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
