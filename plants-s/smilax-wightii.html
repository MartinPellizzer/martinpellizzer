Smilax wightii

Smilax wightii, commonly known as the Wight's catbrier, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct leaflet arrangement.

Smilax wightii is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as swamps and forest edges.
