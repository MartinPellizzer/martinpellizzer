Schouwia arabica var. schimperi

Schouwia arabica var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a variant of the coffee species native to Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its unique flavor profile and is often cultivated for its aromatic and robust coffee beans.
