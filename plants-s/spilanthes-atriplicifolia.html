Spilanthes atriplicifolia

Spilanthes atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Indian mustard or black mustard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and can be found in open woods, along stream banks, and in prairies. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The plant is also known for its pungent odor and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
