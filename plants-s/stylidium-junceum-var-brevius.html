Stylidium junceum var. brevius

Stylidium junceum var. brevius, commonly known as the slender triggerplant, is a small, flowering herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism, which aid in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
