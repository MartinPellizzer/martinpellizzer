Simocheilus pubescens

Simocheilus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
