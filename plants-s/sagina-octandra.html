Sagina octandra

Sagina octandra, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has dark green, fleshy leaves that are opposite or whorled in arrangement. It produces white or pinkish flowers with eight stamens, which is reflected in its scientific name "octandra.".

Sagina octandra thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or disturbed areas.
