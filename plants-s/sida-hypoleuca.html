Sida hypoleuca

Sida hypoleuca, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and coastal dunes.

Sida hypoleuca is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
