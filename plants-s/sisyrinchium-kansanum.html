Sisyrinchium kansanum

Sisyrinchium kansanum, commonly known as the Kansas blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy inflorescences.

Sisyrinchium kansanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
