Solanum andigenum f. lapazense

Solanum andigenum f. lapazense, commonly known as the Lapaz potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are typically harvested when mature. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are typically blue or violet, adding to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and its role in traditional Andean agriculture.
