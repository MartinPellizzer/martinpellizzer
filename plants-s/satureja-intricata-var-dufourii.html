Satureja intricata var. dufourii

Satureja intricata var. dufourii, commonly known as the Dufour's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
