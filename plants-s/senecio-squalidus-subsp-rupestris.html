Senecio squalidus subsp. rupestris

Senecio squalidus subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.
