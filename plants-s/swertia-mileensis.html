Swertia mileensis

Swertia mileensis, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Swertia mileensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
