Saxifraga groenlandica f. flavescens

Saxifraga groenlandica f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking splash of color to rocky habitats. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in cold, dry environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

This variety is notable for its vibrant yellow floral display, which distinguishes it from the more commonly white-flowered parent species.
