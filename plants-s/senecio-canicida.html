Senecio canicida

Senecio canicida, commonly known as the dog's tooth senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, conical clusters, which are typical of the daisy family. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
