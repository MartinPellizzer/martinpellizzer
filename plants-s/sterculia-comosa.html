Sterculia comosa

Sterculia comosa, commonly known as the woolly sterculia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, leaves and flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, tropical forests and is adapted to thrive in humid environments.
