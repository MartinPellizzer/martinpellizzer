Saxifraga × izarii

Saxifraga × izarii, commonly known as the Izarii saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga cespitosa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers in late summer. The plant is native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Saxifraga × izarii is often found in tundra and mountain meadow environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
