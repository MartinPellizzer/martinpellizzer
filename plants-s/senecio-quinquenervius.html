Senecio quinquenervius

Senecio quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branched stems and hairy leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are clustered in heads and bloom in the summer months.

Senecio quinquenervius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
