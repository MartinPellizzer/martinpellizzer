Siebera cherlerioides

Siebera cherlerioides, commonly known as the Sierra Madre maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle clusters, followed by orange-red, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
