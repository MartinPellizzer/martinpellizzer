Statice scorpius

Statice scorpius, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.

Statice scorpius is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
