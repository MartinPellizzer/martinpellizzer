Stachys karstiana

Stachys karstiana, commonly known as Karst's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone-rich soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and have a distinctive purple hue with pale yellow stamens.

Stachys karstiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
