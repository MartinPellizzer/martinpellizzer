Syzygium glaucissimum

Syzygium glaucissimum, commonly known as the white-leafed tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically a pale green to white in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, followed by small, edible fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
