Saponaria prostrata subsp. calvertii

Saponaria prostrata subsp.

calvertii, commonly known as creeping soapwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
