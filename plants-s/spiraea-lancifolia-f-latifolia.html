Spiraea lancifolia f. latifolia

Spiraea lancifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiraea, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
