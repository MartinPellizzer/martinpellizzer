Streptolirion volubile var. angustifolia

Streptolirion volubile var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ivy-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that can climb or trail along surfaces, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are funnel-shaped, typically displaying shades of blue, purple, or white. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
