Siraitia siamensis var. silomaradjae

Siraitia siamensis var. silomaradjae, commonly known as the Silomaradja giant watermelon, is a variant of the giant watermelon species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its exceptionally large, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The plant is a climbing vine with thick, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit has a thick rind and a sweet, juicy flesh, making it a unique and sought-after crop in its native region.
