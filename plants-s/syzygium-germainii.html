Syzygium germainii

Syzygium germainii, commonly known as the Germain's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium germainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
