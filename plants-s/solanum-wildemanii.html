Solanum wildemanii

Solanum wildemanii, commonly known as the wildemanii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple color. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and often have a mild toxic alkaloid content.

Solanum wildemanii is typically found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
