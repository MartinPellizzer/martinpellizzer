Salvia amethystina subsp. amethystina

Salvia amethystina subsp.

amethystina, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
