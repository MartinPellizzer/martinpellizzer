Scabiosa columbaria subsp. uniseta

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

uniseta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
