Strigilia longiflora

Strigilia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered strigil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Strigilia longiflora is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
