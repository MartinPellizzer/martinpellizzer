Saccolabium niveum

Saccolabium niveum, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Saccolabium niveum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
