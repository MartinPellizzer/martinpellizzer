Saxifraga umbrosa var. spathularis

Saxifraga umbrosa var. spathularis, commonly known as the shaded saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga umbrosa var. spathularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
