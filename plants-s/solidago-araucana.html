Solidago araucana

Solidago araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Solidago araucana plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
