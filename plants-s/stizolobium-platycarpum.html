Stizolobium platycarpum

Stizolobium platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters.

Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
