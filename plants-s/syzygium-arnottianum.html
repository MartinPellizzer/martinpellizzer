Syzygium arnottianum

Syzygium arnottianum, commonly known as the satinwood or Australian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are highly valued for their flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium arnottianum thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
