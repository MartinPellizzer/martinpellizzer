Syringa × correlata

Syringa × correlata, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Syringa × correlata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
