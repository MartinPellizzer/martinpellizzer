Samyda niviana

Samyda niviana, commonly known as the snowbush, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Samyda niviana is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
