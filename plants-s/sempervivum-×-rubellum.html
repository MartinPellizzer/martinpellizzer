Sempervivum × rubellum

Sempervivum × rubellum, commonly known as the red-silver houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax, giving them a silvery appearance. The leaves are often tinged with shades of red, pink, or purple, especially under bright light conditions. This plant is well adapted to rocky or arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
