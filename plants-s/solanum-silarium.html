Solanum silarium

Solanum silarium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white pubescent leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that changes color from green to yellow as it matures.

Solanum silarium is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
