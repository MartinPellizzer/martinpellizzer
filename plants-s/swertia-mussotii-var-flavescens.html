Swertia mussotii var. flavescens

Swertia mussotii var. flavescens, commonly known as the Yellow Swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in racemes. Its medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of iridoid glycosides and other bioactive compounds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective effects.
