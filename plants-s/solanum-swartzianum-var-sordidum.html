Solanum swartzianum var. sordidum

Solanum swartzianum var. sordidum, commonly known as the sordid nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, though they are toxic and not edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
