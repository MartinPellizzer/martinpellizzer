Scutellaria rubicunda subsp. linnaeana

Scutellaria rubicunda subsp.

linnaeana, commonly known as the Linnaean scullion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The species produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded or partially sunny areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
