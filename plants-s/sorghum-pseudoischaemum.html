Sorghum pseudoischaemum

Sorghum pseudoischaemum, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter and features narrow, blade-like leaves. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use in marginal lands.
