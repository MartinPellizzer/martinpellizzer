Solanum racemigerum

Solanum racemigerum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and are edible, though they contain toxic compounds in their raw form.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
