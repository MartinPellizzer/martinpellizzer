Sedum rhodiola var. latifolium

Sedum rhodiola var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is drought-tolerant once established.
