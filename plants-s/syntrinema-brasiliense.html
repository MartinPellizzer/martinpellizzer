Syntrinema brasiliense

Syntrinema brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of polypodiaceae native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that grows on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. Its rhizome is short and produces numerous roots that help it anchor to its host.

The botanical features of Syntrinema brasiliense make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
