Symplocos cochinchinensis var. thwaitesii

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
