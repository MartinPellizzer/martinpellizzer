Syreniopsis

Syreniopsis, commonly known as the glassy-winged sharpshooter, is a type of planthopper native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its transparent wings and elongated body, which help it blend into its environment. The plant plays a role in the ecosystem as both a herbivore and a vector for plant pathogens. Syreniopsis feeds on the sap of various plants, including woody shrubs and trees.

Its presence can have significant impacts on agricultural crops due to its ability to transmit diseases.
