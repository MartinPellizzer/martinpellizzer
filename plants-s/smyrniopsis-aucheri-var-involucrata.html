Smyrniopsis aucheri var. involucrata

Smyrniopsis aucheri var. involucrata, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
