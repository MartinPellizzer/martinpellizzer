Saccharum aegyptiacum var. sinense

Saccharum aegyptiacum var. sinense, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or Chinese reed, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
