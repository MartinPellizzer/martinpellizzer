Schoenus lucidus

Schoenus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy sedgeland grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Schoenus lucidus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
