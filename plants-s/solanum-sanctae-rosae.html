Solanum sanctae-rosae

Solanum sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Rosary Apple, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, orange or yellow fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Solanum sanctae-rosae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
