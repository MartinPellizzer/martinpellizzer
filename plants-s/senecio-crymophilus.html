Senecio crymophilus

Senecio crymophilus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help it withstand cold temperatures and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
