Senecio cristimontanus

Senecio cristimontanus, commonly known as the mountain crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
