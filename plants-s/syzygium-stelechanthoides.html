Syzygium stelechanthoides

Syzygium stelechanthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stem and clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

Syzygium stelechanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
