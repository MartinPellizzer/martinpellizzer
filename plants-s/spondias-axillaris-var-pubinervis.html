Spondias axillaris var. pubinervis

Spondias axillaris var. pubinervis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a slender, often multi-stemmed trunk with smooth, grey bark that peels in thin layers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a pubescent underside, giving it the varietal name "pubinervis.". It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
