Stipa boczantzevii

Stipa boczantzevii, commonly known as the Boczaczewski needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awned lemmas. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Stipa boczantzevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
