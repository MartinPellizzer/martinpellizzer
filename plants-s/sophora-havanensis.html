Sophora havanensis

Sophora havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban sophora, is a small, deciduous tree native to Cuba.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

The tree has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
