Stipa hohenackeriana subsp. grossheimeri

Stipa hohenackeriana subsp.

grossheimeri, commonly known as the Grossheimer's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that resemble delicate plumes.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
