Salvia striata

Salvia striata, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.

Salvia striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
