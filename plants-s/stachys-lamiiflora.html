Stachys lamiiflora

Stachys lamiiflora, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of purple or violet flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys lamiiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
