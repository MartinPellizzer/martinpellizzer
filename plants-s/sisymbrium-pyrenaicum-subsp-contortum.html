Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp. contortum

Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp.

contortum, commonly known as twisted shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the stems are often twisted, giving the species its common name.
