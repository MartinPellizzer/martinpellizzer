Senecio nemophilus

Senecio nemophilus, commonly known as the woodland groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Senecio nemophilus is often found in areas with high humidity and is considered a delicate, herbaceous perennial.
