Senecio lanceolatus

Senecio lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
