Selaginella parkeri

Selaginella parkeri, commonly known as the tropical spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This species is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. Selaginella parkeri thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures, including microspores and megaspores, are produced on specialized leaves, contributing to its unique life cycle.
