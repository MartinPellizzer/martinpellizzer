Salvia × bernardina

Salvia × bernardina, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Salvia gregaria and Salvia leucantha, resulting in a plant with striking ornamental features. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This plant is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
