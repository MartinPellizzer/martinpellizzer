Sorbus vajdae

Sorbus vajdae, commonly known as the Kumaon whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, persistent fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Sorbus vajdae is typically found in montane forests and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
