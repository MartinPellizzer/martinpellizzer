Sclerolobium setiferum

Sclerolobium setiferum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Sclerolobium setiferum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
