Sarcanthus multiflorus

Sarcanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom sarcanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sarcanthus multiflorus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
