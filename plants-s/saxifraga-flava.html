Saxifraga flava

Saxifraga flava, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer, and its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas where the climate is cool and moist. Saxifraga flava is a perennial species that is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Saxifragaceae family.
