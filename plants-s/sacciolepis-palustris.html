Sacciolepis palustris

Sacciolepis palustris, commonly known as the marsh sacciolepis, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Sacciolepis palustris thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.
