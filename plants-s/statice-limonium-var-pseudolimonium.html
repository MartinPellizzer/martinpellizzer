Statice limonium var. pseudolimonium

Statice limonium var. pseudolimonium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salty air and coastal conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
