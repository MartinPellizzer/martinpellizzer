Sempervivum ambiguum

Sempervivum ambiguum, commonly known as the "Hedgehog Plant," is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions and its attractive appearance contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
