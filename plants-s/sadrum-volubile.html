Sadrum volubile

Sadrum volubile, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its twining stems that climb along supports, often forming dense foliage. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, releasing a subtle fragrance when crushed.

Sadrum volubile is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
