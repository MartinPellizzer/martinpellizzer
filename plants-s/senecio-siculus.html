Senecio siculus

Senecio siculus, commonly known as Sicilian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio siculus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
