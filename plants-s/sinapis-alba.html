Sinapis alba

Sinapis alba, commonly known as white mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces round, smooth pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in temperate climates.

Its rapid growth cycle makes it a common weed in agricultural fields.
