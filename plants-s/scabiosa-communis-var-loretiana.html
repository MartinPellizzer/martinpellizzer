Scabiosa communis var. loretiana

Scabiosa communis var. loretiana, commonly known as the Loretana lovegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive central disc. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
