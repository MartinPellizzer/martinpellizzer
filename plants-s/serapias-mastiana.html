Serapias mastiana

Serapias mastiana, commonly known as the Alpine marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall flowering stems with clustered, yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Serapias mastiana is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
