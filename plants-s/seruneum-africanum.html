Seruneum africanum

Seruneum africanum, commonly known as the African seru, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Seruneum africanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
