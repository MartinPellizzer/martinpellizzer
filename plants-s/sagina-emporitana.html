Sagina emporitana

Sagina emporitana, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny white or pink flowers in early spring. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and showy blooms.

Sagina emporitana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
