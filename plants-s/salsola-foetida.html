Salsola foetida

Salsola foetida, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Salsola foetida is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it a common species in desert ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for certain wildlife.
