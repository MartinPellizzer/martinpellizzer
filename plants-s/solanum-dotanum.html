Solanum dotanum

Solanum dotanum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often as a shrub or perennial herb. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces fruit that can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.

Solanum dotanum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers.
