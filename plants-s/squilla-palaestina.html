Squilla palaestina

Squilla palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine squill, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a robust bulb that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

Squilla palaestina is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
