Scirpus paschalis

Scirpus paschalis, commonly known as the Paschal sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, pale green to brown flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in swamps. Scirpus paschalis is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
