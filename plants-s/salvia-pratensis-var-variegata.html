Salvia pratensis var. variegata

Salvia pratensis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
