Silene viscariopsis

Silene viscariopsis, commonly known as the sticky bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its unique feature is the presence of sticky, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Silene viscariopsis is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
