Salix mutsuensis

Salix mutsuensis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by elongated, narrow fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along water bodies.
