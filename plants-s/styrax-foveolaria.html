Styrax foveolaria

Styrax foveolaria, commonly known as the Florida ash or sweet ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark, warty fruits. Its wood is aromatic and has been historically used in the production of perfumes and medicinal products.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
