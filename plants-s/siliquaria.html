Siliquaria

Siliquaria, commonly known as the "siliquaria plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive siliqua fruits, which are dry, dehiscent pods that split open to release seeds. The plant typically features alternate, simple leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Siliquaria species are often found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
