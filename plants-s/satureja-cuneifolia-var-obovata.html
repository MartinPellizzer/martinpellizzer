Satureja cuneifolia var. obovata

Satureja cuneifolia var. obovata, commonly known as narrow-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.
