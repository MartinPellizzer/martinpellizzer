Styrax albus

Styrax albus, commonly known as the white styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, dark blue berries in the fall.

Styrax albus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal.
