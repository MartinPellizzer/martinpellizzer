Solidago lithospermifolia

Solidago lithospermifolia, commonly known as the rock sandwort goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Solidago lithospermifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
