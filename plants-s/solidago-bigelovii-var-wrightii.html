Solidago bigelovii var. wrightii

Solidago bigelovii var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, cone-shaped inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
