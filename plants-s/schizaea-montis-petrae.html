Schizaea montis-petrae

Schizaea montis-petrae, commonly known as the mountain rock fern, is a rare and specialized member of the Schizaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome and a dense cluster of small, oval-shaped fronds. The plant thrives in rocky, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures are distinctive, with sporangia clustered in specialized indusia on the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Schizaea montis-petrae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
