Sinningia allagophylla

Sinningia allagophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf gesneriad, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish-white coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers are usually small, tubular, and come in shades of pink, purple, or white.

Sinningia allagophylla is prized in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
