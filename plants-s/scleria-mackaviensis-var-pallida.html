Scleria mackaviensis var. pallida

Scleria mackaviensis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
