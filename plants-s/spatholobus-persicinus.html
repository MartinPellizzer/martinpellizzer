Spatholobus persicinus

Spatholobus persicinus, commonly known as the pink wisteria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically borne in pendant racemes, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Spatholobus persicinus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
