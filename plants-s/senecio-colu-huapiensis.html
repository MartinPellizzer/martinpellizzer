Senecio colu-huapiensis

Senecio colu-huapiensis, commonly known as the Huapiensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
