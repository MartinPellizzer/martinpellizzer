Symplocos ophirensis subsp. cumingiana

Symplocos ophirensis subsp.

cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's waxleaf. It is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers.

The leaves are thick and waxy, often with a glossy appearance, and the plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
