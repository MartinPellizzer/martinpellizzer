Silene cretica var. annulata

Silene cretica var. annulata, commonly known as the ringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish, and clustered in dense spikes at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
