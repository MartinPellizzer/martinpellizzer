Satureja ovata var. cinereotomentosa

Satureja ovata var. cinereotomentosa, commonly known as silver-hairy savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their pungent, peppery flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the sage family, sharing similar structural and chemical properties.
