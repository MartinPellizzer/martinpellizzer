Sarothra gentianoides

Sarothra gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically clustered in inflorescences at the top of its slender stems. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its roots are often harvested for their chemical compounds.

Sarothra gentianoides thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.
