Syzygium fruticosum var. glaucissimum

Syzygium fruticosum var. glaucissimum, commonly known as the glaucous syzygium, is a flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that often have a bluish or whitish waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, making it visually striking and attractive to birds and other wildlife.
