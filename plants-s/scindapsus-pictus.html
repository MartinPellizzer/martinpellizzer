Scindapsus pictus

Scindapsus pictus, commonly known as the satin plant or marble queen, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with intricate patterns, giving it a decorative appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus pictus is a popular indoor plant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
