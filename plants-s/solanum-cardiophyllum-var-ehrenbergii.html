Solanum cardiophyllum var. ehrenbergii

Solanum cardiophyllum var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry," is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are encased in a spiky, lantern-shaped calyx. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed raw.
