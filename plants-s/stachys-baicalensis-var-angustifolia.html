Stachys baicalensis var. angustifolia

Stachys baicalensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Chinese skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential health benefits, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
