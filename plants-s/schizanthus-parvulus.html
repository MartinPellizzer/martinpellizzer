Schizanthus parvulus

Schizanthus parvulus, commonly known as the small-flowered love-lies-bleeding, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental for its vibrant blooms. Schizanthus parvulus thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include small, ovate leaves and a unique floral structure that resembles a lantern.
