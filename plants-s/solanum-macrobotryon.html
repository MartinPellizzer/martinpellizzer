Solanum macrobotryon

Solanum macrobotryon, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum macrobotryon is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
