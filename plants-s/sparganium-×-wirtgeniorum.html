Sparganium × wirtgeniorum

Sparganium × wirtgeniorum, commonly known as the hybrid bur rush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Sparganium emersum and Sparganium strictum. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its most distinctive feature is the cylindrical, spiky inflorescence that develops in late summer.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and filtering water in wetland ecosystems.
