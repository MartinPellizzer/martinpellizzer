Sundamomum luteum

Sundamomum luteum, commonly known as the yellow sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow ray florets that surround a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
