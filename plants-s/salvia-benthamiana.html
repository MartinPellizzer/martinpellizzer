Salvia benthamiana

Salvia benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's sage, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy violet or purple flowers. It is widely used in scientific research, particularly in plant biology and genetics.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a valuable model organism in botanical studies.
