Sinobambusa parvifolia

Sinobambusa parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and smooth. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which appear at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sinobambusa parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its elegant appearance.
