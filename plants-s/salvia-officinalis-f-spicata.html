Salvia officinalis f. spicata

Salvia officinalis f. spicata, commonly known as spearmint, is a cultivar of the common sage plant.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Spearmint is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic essential oils.
