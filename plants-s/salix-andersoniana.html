Salix andersoniana

Salix andersoniana, commonly known as Anderson's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
