Senecio tagetes

Senecio tagetes, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio tagetes is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
