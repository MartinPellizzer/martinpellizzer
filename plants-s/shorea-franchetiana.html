Shorea franchetiana

Shorea franchetiana, commonly known as the Vietnamese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
