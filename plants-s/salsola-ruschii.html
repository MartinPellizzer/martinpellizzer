Salsola ruschii

Salsola ruschii, commonly known as Russian saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert and steppe ecosystems. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Salsola ruschii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
