Solanum hebephorum

Solanum hebephorum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color as they ripen. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum hebephorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
