Stylophyllum insulare

Stylophyllum insulare, commonly known as the island stonewort, is a species of freshwater algae belonging to the order Coleochaetales.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure composed of segmented, unbranched filaments. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its morphology includes a distinctive arrangement of cells that form a dense, green mat on the surface of the water.

Stylophyllum insulare plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to primary production and serving as a food source for various aquatic organisms.
