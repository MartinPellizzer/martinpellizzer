Scleria glabra var. pallidior

Scleria glabra var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale sand sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or near freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
