Sebastiania phyllanthiformis

Sebastiania phyllanthiformis, commonly known as the golden-seed tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, yellow aril, which gives the plant its common name.

Sebastiania phyllanthiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
