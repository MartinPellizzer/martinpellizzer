Sasa matsudae var. nikkoensis

Sasa matsudae var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa matsudae species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical culms with a smooth, light green to yellowish-brown surface. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly waxy texture and a pale underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
