Saguaster perbrevis

Saguaster perbrevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf saguaro, is a unique cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often branch into multiple arms, resembling the more famous saguaro cactus. The plant has spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that reduce water loss and aid in photosynthesis. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and attracting pollinators like bees and bats.

Saguaster perbrevis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
