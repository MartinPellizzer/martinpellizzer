Salaxis hexandra

Salaxis hexandra, commonly known as the red salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salaxis hexandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
