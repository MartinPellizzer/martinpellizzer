Salsola mutica

Salsola mutica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly and become invasive in non-native regions.

Salsola mutica has a deep taproot system that enables it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.
