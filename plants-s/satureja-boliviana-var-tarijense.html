Satureja boliviana var. tarijense

Satureja boliviana var. tarijense, commonly known as Tarijense thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.

The small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
