Shorea koordersii

Shorea koordersii, commonly known as the Korth's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
