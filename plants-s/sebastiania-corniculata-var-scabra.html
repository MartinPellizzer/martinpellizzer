Sebastiania corniculata var. scabra

Sebastiania corniculata var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-seeded sebastiania, is a shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
