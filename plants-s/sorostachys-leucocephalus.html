Sorostachys leucocephalus

Sorostachys leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
