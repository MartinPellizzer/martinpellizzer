Syagrus chloroleuca

Syagrus chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are typically a striking white or pale green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Syagrus chloroleuca is adapted to grow in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the Atlantic Ocean.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in Brazilian coastal ecosystems.
