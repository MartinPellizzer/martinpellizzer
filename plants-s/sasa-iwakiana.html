Sasa iwakiana

Sasa iwakiana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue. Sasa iwakiana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to create a dense, upright growth habit.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
