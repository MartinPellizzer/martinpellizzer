Setaria viridis var. brevifolia

Setaria viridis var. brevifolia, commonly known as slender green millet, is a grass species native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical spikelets. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers and produces seeds that are often used as a food source.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
