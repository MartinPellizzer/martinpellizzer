Sacciolepis polystachys

Sacciolepis polystachys, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Sacciolepis polystachys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
