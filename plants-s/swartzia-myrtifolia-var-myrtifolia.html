Swartzia myrtifolia var. myrtifolia

Swartzia myrtifolia var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the pinkwood tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pinkish-brown bark and lanceolate, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
