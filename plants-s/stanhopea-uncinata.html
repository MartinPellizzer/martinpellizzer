Stanhopea uncinata

Stanhopea uncinata, commonly known as the hooked Stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in a striking yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting its unique growth habit.

Stanhopea uncinata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
