Selaginella tereticaulos

Selaginella tereticaulos, commonly known as the slender spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinct appearance, with slender, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which release spores for reproduction.

Selaginella tereticaulos is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
