Sonneratia acida var. mossambicensis

Sonneratia acida var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the African mangrove, is a variant of the Sonneratia acida species native to coastal regions of Africa.

This plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in saline or brackish water environments. It has smooth, dark green leaves that are alternate and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in spikes, while the fruits are three-lobed berries that contain a single seed.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
