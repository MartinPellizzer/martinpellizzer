Salix × gracilenta

Salix × gracilenta, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green color during the growing season. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by narrow, woody fruits.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.
