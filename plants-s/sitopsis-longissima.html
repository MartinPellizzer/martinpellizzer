Sitopsis longissima

Sitopsis longissima, commonly known as the longleaf sitopsis, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spring, and it produces capsule fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
