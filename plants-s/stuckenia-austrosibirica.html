Stuckenia austrosibirica

Stuckenia austrosibirica, commonly known as the Siberian water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water bodies such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands, where it forms dense stands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating at the surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pale green to white in color.

Stuckenia austrosibirica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
