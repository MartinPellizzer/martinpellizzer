Symplocos lundii var. glabra

Symplocos lundii var. glabra, commonly known as smooth symplocos, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
