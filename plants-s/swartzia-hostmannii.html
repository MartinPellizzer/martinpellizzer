Swartzia hostmannii

Swartzia hostmannii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Swartzia hostmannii is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
