Stachys germanica var. lusitanica

Stachys germanica var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antiseptic properties.
