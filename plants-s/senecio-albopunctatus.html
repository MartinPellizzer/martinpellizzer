Senecio albopunctatus

Senecio albopunctatus, commonly known as the White-spotted Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with white spots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio albopunctatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
