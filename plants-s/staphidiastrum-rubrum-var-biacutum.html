Staphidiastrum rubrum var. biacutum

Staphidiastrum rubrum var. biacutum, commonly known as the red-stemmed goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are typically reddish in color, which distinguish it from other goldenrod species. The plant produces small, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
