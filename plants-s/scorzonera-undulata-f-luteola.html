Scorzonera undulata f. luteola

Scorzonera undulata f. luteola, commonly known as the yellow scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
