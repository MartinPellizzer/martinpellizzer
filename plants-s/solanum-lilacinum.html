Solanum lilacinum

Solanum lilacinum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum lilacinum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
