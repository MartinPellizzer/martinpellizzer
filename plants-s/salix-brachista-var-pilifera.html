Salix brachista var. pilifera

Salix brachista var. pilifera, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow with hairy leaves, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
