Salvia phlomoides var. elata

Salvia phlomoides var. elata, commonly known as tall blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a drought-tolerant choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
