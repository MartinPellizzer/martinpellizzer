Spathiphyllum amazonicum

Spathiphyllum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon peace lily, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spathe and spadix inflorescence structure. The plant features broad, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often variegated. It produces small, white flowers enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe.

Spathiphyllum amazonicum is prized for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying qualities.
