Satureja intricata var. innota

Satureja intricata var. innota, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
