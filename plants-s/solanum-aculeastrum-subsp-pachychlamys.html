Solanum aculeastrum subsp. pachychlamys

Solanum aculeastrum subsp.

pachychlamys, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, globose berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
