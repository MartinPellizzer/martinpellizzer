Spiraea occidentalis

Spiraea occidentalis, commonly known as western spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features multiple stems that branch out from the base, forming a rounded or upright shape. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Spiraea occidentalis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
