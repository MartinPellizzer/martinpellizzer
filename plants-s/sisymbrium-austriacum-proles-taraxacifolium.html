Sisymbrium austriacum proles taraxacifolium

Sisymbrium austriacum proles taraxacifolium, commonly known as the Austrian cuckoo's wing, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that resemble those of dandelions, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity conservation.
