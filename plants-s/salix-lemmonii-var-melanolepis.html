Salix lemmonii var. melanolepis

Salix lemmonii var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
