Serophyton pilosissimum

Serophyton pilosissimum, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its ability to store water, it is well adapted to drought conditions.
