Senecio petasioides

Senecio petasioides, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
