Silene pakistanica

Silene pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene pakistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
