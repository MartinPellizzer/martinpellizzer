Silene ciliata

Silene ciliata, commonly known as the fringed pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine, hair-like fringes along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene ciliata is often found in habitats with partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
