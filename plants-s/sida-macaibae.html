Sida macaibae

Sida macaibae, commonly known as the coastal sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a slight pubescence.

Sida macaibae is adapted to tolerate saline conditions and is often found in brackish wetlands.
