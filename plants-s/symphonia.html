Symphonia

Symphonia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and includes several species, such as Symphonia globulifera. The plants are characterized by their sturdy, often gnarled trunks and compound leaves. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers and are known for their hard, dense wood.

Symphonia species are often found in moist, lowland forests and are valued for their ecological and potential medicinal properties.
