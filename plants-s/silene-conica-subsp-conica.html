Silene conica subsp. conica

Silene conica subsp.

conica, commonly known as the nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its upright stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that nod downward.

The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a capsule, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
