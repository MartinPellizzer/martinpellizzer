Sida celtidifolia

Sida celtidifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Velvetleaf is an annual herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of study in plant ecology.
