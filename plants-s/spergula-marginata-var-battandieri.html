Spergula marginata var. battandieri

Spergula marginata var. battandieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It produces white to pale green florets that are clustered in dense spikes.

This variety is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
