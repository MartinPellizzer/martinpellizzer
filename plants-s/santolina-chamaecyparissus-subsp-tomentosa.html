Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp. tomentosa

Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as woolly santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
