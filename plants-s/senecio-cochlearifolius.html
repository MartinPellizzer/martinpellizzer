Senecio cochlearifolius

Senecio cochlearifolius, commonly known as the helmet flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio cochlearifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
