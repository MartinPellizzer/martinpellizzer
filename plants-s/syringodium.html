Syringodium

Syringodium, commonly known as shoal grass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and is typically found in shallow coastal waters and estuaries. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Syringodium plays an important role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

It is adapted to brackish and saltwater environments, making it a key component of seagrass ecosystems.
