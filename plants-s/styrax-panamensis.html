Styrax panamensis

Styrax panamensis, commonly known as Panama ash or white ash, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
