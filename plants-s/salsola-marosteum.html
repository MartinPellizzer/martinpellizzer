Salsola marosteum

Salsola marosteum, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure that helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Salsola marosteum is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
