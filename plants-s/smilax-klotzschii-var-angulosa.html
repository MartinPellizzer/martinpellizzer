Smilax klotzschii var. angulosa

Smilax klotzschii var. angulosa, commonly known as the angular smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
