Stachys recta subsp. serpentini

Stachys recta subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as serpent's tail, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
