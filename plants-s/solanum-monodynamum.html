Solanum monodynamum

Solanum monodynamum, commonly known as the single-seed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains a single seed, hence its common name.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
