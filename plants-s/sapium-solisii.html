Sapium solisii

Sapium solisii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It has alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually 5–10 cm long and 2–5 cm wide. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native plant species.
