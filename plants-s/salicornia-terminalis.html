Salicornia terminalis

Salicornia terminalis, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, and it is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Salicornia terminalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
