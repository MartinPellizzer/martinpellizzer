Syzygium korthalsianum

Syzygium korthalsianum, commonly known as the Java plum or Indian blackberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Syzygium korthalsianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
