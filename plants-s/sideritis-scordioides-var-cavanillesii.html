Sideritis scordioides var. cavanillesii

Sideritis scordioides var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Balkan sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
