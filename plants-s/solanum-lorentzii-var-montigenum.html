Solanum lorentzii var. montigenum

Solanum lorentzii var. montigenum, commonly known as the montigenum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
