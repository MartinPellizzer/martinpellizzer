Saussurea ajanensis

Saussurea ajanensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
