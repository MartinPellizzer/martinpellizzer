Scirpus dialgamensis

Scirpus dialgamensis, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Scirpus dialgamensis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
