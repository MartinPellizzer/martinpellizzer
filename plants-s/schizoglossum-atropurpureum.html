Schizoglossum atropurpureum

Schizoglossum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple fringed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-purple flowers with distinctive fringed petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soils it inhabits.

Schizoglossum atropurpureum is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
