Soranthe imberbis

Soranthe imberbis, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a succulent, branching stem covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the same waxy coating.

Soranthe imberbis is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
