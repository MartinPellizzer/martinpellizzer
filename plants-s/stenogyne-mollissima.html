Stenogyne mollissima

Stenogyne mollissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and robust, woody stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Stenogyne mollissima plays an important role in the ecological systems of Hawaii, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
