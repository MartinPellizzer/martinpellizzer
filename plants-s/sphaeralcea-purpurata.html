Sphaeralcea purpurata

Sphaeralcea purpurata, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and produces small, round seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
