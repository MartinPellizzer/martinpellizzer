Senecio bigelovii var. bigelovii

Senecio bigelovii var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow ray flowers and is often found in open grasslands and desert scrub.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
