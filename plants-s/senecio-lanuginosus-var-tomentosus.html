Senecio lanuginosus var. tomentosus

Senecio lanuginosus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
