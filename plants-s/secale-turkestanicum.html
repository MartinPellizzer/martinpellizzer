Secale turkestanicum

Secale turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan rye, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally used in ecological restoration projects.
