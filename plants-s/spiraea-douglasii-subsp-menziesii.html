Spiraea douglasii subsp. menziesii

Spiraea douglasii subsp.

menziesii, commonly known as Menzies' spiraea, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along streambanks.

The plant features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators and wildlife.
