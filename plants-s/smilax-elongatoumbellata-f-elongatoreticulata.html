Smilax elongatoumbellata f. elongatoreticulata

Smilax elongatoumbellata f. elongatoreticulata, commonly known as the reticulated smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features compound leaves with a reticulated pattern, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are thorny, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
