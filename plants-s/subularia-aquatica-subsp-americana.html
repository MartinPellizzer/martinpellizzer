Subularia aquatica subsp. americana

Subularia aquatica subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American water scurf, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments across North America. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its minute, leaf-like structures that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant is usually submerged or floating, with short, branched stems that help it anchor in the substrate. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying mainly on vegetative propagation for spread.

It plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems but is an indicator of water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
