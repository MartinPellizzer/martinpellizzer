Schismus fasciculatus

Schismus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs. Schismus fasciculatus is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it thrives in full sun.

It is an important plant for local wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small insects.
