Salicornia quinqueflora subsp. tasmanica

Salicornia quinqueflora subsp.

tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is native to coastal regions of Tasmania and grows in intertidal zones and salt marshes. Its small, green flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is used in some culinary applications due to its salty, crunchy texture.
