Syagrus botryophora

Syagrus botryophora, commonly known as the "bottle tree" or "bottle palm," is a species of palm native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk that resembles a bottle, hence its common name. The plant has large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, providing a striking visual appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits.

Syagrus botryophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
