Stenocoelium trichocarpum

Stenocoelium trichocarpum, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
