Salvia ruthenica

Salvia ruthenica, commonly known as the Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is narrow and gray-green, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Salvia ruthenica is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
