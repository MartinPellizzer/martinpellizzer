Stipulicida lacerata

Stipulicida lacerata, commonly known as the jagged leaf fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, lacerated leaf blades.

The leaves are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae, which includes various epiphytic and terrestrial ferns.
