Senecio lautus var. discoideus

Senecio lautus var. discoideus, commonly known as the "blueweed" or "pale senecio," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The varietal name *discoideus* refers to its disc-like flower head structure.
