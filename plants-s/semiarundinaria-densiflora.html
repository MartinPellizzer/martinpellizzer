Semiarundinaria densiflora

Semiarundinaria densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, dense flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Semiarundinaria densiflora is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
