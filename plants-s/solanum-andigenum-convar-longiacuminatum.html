Solanum andigenum convar. longiacuminatum

Solanum andigenum convar. longiacuminatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are typically toxic but have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
