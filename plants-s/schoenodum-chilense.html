Schoenodum chilense

Schoenodum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
