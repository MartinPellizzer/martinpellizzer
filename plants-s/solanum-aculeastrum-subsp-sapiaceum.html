Solanum aculeastrum subsp. sapiaceum

Solanum aculeastrum subsp.

sapiaceum, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces berries that are typically white or yellow when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
