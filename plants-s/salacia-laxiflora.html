Salacia laxiflora

Salacia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
