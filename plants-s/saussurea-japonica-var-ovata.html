Saussurea japonica var. ovata

Saussurea japonica var. ovata, commonly known as Japanese yarrow or Japanese mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
