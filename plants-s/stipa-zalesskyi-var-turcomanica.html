Stipa zalesskyi var. turcomanica

Stipa zalesskyi var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
