Scirpus paraguayensis

Scirpus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds and rivers.

Scirpus paraguayensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
