Saussurea radiata

Saussurea radiata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower or the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine. Its roots and flowers are used to treat various ailments, including inflammation and digestive issues.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
