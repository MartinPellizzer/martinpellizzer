Stachys teucriformis

Stachys teucriformis, commonly known as the creeping betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys teucriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
