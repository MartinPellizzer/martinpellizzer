Solanum poinsettiifolium

Solanum poinsettiifolium, commonly known as the flameleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble leaves, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and peppers. Its foliage is typically green with a glossy appearance, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
