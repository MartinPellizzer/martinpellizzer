Scilla autumnalis var. corsica

Scilla autumnalis var. corsica, commonly known as the Corsican squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in the autumn. The plant is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it tolerates mild winters and hot summers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
