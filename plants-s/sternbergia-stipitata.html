Sternbergia stipitata

Sternbergia stipitata, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet or purple flowers in the autumn. The plant's flowers have three petals and three sepals, with a distinctive spadix in the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The autumn crocus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
