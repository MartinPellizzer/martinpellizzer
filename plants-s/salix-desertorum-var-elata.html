Salix desertorum var. elata

Salix desertorum var. elata, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkin-like clusters. The leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish underside, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is adapted to low water availability and can thrive in well-drained soils.

This plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
