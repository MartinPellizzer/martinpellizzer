Senecio ponticus

Senecio ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Senecio ponticus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
