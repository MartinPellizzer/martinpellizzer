Silene margaritae

Silene margaritae, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its pink or white flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the shoreline. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene margaritae is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in coastal ecosystems.
