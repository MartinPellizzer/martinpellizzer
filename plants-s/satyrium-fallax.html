Satyrium fallax

Satyrium fallax, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns.

Satyrium fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
