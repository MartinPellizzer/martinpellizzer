Senecio rabani

Senecio rabani, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Senecio rabani is also known for its toxic properties, making it a concern in livestock areas.
