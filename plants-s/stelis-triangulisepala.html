Stelis triangulisepala

Stelis triangulisepala, commonly known as the triangular-lipped stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, typically found growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, with three sepals and three petals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
