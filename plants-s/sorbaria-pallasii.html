Sorbaria pallasii

Sorbaria pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches with opposite, ovate leaves. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Sorbaria pallasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
