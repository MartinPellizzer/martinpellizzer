Styphnolobium japonicum var. pubescens

Styphnolobium japonicum var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

The variety pubescens is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Styphnolobium.
