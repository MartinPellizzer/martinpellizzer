Scirpus striatus

Scirpus striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed or striated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of its stems, typically in late summer. Scirpus striatus thrives in shallow water and is often found in freshwater habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
