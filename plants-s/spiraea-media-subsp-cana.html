Spiraea media subsp. cana

Spiraea media subsp.

cana, commonly known as the silver-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that cascade with clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early summer.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
