Salvia bachtiarica

Salvia bachtiarica, commonly known as Persian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

Salvia bachtiarica is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in herbal medicine.
