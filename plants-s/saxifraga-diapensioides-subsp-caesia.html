Saxifraga diapensioides subsp. caesia

Saxifraga diapensioides subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the blue saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value in shaded gardens.
