Scleropyrum aurantiacum

Scleropyrum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange sycamore, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India. The plant is characterized by its thick, bark-like exodermis that gives it a unique, rugged appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or orange in color, contributing to its striking visual appeal.
