Silene farsistanica

Silene farsistanica, commonly known as the Persian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and small, clustered pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Silene farsistanica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountain slopes.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
