Struthiola floribunda

Struthiola floribunda, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Struthiola floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
