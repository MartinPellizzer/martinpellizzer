Silene nuratavica

Silene nuratavica, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene nuratavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
