Sida javensis subsp. expilosa

Sida javensis subsp.

expilosa, commonly known as the slender sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, woolly leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is noted for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
