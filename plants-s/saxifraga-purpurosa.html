Saxifraga purpurosa

Saxifraga purpurosa, commonly known as purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions, often found in rocky or barren landscapes. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Saxifraga purpurosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native habitats.
