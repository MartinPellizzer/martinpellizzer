Steiropteris setulosa

Steiropteris setulosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Steiropteris setulosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
