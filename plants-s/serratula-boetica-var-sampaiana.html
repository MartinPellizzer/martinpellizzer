Serratula boetica var. sampaiana

Serratula boetica var. sampaiana, commonly known as the Sampaian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
