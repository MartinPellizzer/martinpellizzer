Sida spinosa var. sennaarensis

Sida spinosa var. sennaarensis, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often lobed or trifoliate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture, and it is often used for forage by livestock.

The plant has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
