Senecio × viscidulus

Senecio × viscidulus, commonly known as the sticky groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family.

Senecio × viscidulus is often found in temperate regions of southern Africa.
