Sapium intercedens

Sapium intercedens, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in certain ecosystems.
