Seymeria gypsophila

Seymeria gypsophila, commonly known as the gypsum lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or gypsum-rich environments. Seymeria gypsophila is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
