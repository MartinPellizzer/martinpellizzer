Socratea affinis

Socratea affinis, commonly known as the "socratea" or "bottle tree," is a unique rainforest plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive swollen trunk, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant has a crown of broad, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in humid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in green or red hues, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various animals.

Socratea affinis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
