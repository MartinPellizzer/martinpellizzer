Struthanthus uruguayensis

Struthanthus uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its delicate floral display.

Struthanthus uruguayensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
