Scabiosa orophila

Scabiosa orophila, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Scabiosa orophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
