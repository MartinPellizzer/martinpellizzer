Sasa okuyezoensis

Sasa okuyezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Sasa okuyezoensis is valued for its ability to form dense thickets, making it useful in erosion control and landscaping.
