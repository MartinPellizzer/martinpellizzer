Sporobolus regis

Sporobolus regis, commonly known as the king's spikerush, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture.

Sporobolus regis is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ecological value.
