Solaria curacavina

Solaria curacavina, commonly known as the Curacava or Curacava sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solaria curacavina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
