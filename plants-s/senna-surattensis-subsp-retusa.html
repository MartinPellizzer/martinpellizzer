Senna surattensis subsp. retusa

Senna surattensis subsp.

retusa, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
