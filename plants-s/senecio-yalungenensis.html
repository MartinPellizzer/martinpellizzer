Senecio yalungenensis

Senecio yalungenensis, commonly known as the Yalungenensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
