Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp. nigridens

Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp.

nigridens, commonly known as the black-flowered streptocarpus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking dark blue to almost black flowers, which contrast sharply with its green foliage.

The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
