Saussurea nikoensis var. involucrata

Saussurea nikoensis var. involucrata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are generally basal, forming a rosette, and the inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a head.

This variety is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional Tibetan and Chinese medicine.
