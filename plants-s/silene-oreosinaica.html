Silene oreosinaica

Silene oreosinaica, commonly known as the Oreosinaica campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the short growing season in its harsh environment. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in the cold, arid conditions it inhabits. Silene oreosinaica is typically found in rocky soils and is often associated with alpine ecosystems.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in extreme altitudes and low temperatures.
