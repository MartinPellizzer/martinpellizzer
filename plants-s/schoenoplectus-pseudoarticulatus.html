Schoenoplectus pseudoarticulatus

Schoenoplectus pseudoarticulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
