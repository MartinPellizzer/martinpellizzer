Spiraea × sanssouciana

Spiraea × sanssouciana, commonly known as the hybrid weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage turns vibrant yellow in the autumn, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Spiraea × sanssouciana is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
