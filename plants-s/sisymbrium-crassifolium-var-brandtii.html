Sisymbrium crassifolium var. brandtii

Sisymbrium crassifolium var. brandtii, commonly known as Brandt's broad-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.
