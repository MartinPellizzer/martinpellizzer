Spigelia hassleriana

Spigelia hassleriana, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking clusters of red, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
