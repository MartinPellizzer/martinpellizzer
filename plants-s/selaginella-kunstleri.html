Selaginella kunstleri

Selaginella kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It produces tiny, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Selaginella kunstleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
