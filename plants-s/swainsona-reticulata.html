Swainsona reticulata

Swainsona reticulata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded canopy. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and have a distinctive reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Swainsona reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
