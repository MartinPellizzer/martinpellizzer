Schoenus bonannii

Schoenus bonannii, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Schoenus bonannii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
