Salvia guacana

Salvia guacana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia guacana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
