Selinocarpus lanceolatus var. megaphyllus

Selinocarpus lanceolatus var. megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are often divided into numerous segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal and ecological value.
