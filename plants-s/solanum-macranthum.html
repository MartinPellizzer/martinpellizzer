Solanum macranthum

Solanum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are large, often exceeding 5 centimeters in diameter, and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.
