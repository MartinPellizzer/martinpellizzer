Sanguisorba azovtsevii

Sanguisorba azovtsevii, commonly known as the Azov sanguisorbe, is a perennial herb native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces bracteate inflorescences that are often mistaken for flowers, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
