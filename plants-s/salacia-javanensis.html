Salacia javanensis

Salacia javanensis, commonly known as the Javan salacia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Salacia javanensis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
