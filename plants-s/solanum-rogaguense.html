Solanum rogaguense

Solanum rogaguense, commonly known as the Rogaguense nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds, and like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds such as solanine.
