Scirpus fuscorubens

Scirpus fuscorubens, commonly known as the red-stem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are reddish-brown in color, which give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Scirpus fuscorubens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
