Symplocos vernicosa

Symplocos vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy teak or Indian teak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos vernicosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
