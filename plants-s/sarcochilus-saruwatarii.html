Sarcochilus saruwatarii

Sarcochilus saruwatarii, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It features a distinctive, narrow, and often hairy stem with a single, showy, pale green to white flower. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing close to the ground.

It is noted for its unique morphology and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
