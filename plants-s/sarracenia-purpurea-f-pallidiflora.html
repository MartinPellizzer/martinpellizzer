Sarracenia purpurea f. pallidiflora

Sarracenia purpurea f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered purple pitcher plant, is a variant of the purple pitcher plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped structures used to trap insects. The flowers are typically pale pink or white, distinguishing it from the more vibrant red or purple flowers of the typical S. purpurea. This form thrives in acidic, boggy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous nature make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and horticultural interest.
