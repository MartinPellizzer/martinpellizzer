Saxifraga stracheyi

Saxifraga stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of northern Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and tundra environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga stracheyi is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
