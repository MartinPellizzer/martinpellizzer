Syzygium lineare

Syzygium lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits. Syzygium lineare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
