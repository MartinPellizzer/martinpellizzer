Saxifraga diversifolia var. haematophylla

Saxifraga diversifolia var. haematophylla, commonly known as the bloodleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
