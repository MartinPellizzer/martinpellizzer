Sedum caeruleum

Sedum caeruleum, commonly known as blue sedum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
