Sobralia violacea

Sobralia violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and can also be found in rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
