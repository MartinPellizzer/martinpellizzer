Salvia setosa

Salvia setosa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Salvia setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also beneficial to pollinators like bees and butterflies.
