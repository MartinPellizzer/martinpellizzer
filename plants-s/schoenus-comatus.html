Schoenus comatus

Schoenus comatus, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Schoenus comatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
