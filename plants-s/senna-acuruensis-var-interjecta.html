Senna acuruensis var. interjecta

Senna acuruensis var. interjecta, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
