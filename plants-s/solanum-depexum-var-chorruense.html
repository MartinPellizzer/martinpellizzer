Solanum depexum var. chorruense

Solanum depexum var. chorruense, commonly known as the Chorruense nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.
