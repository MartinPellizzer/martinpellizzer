Stewartia corchoroides

Stewartia corchoroides, commonly known as the Chinese stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its attractive, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by elongated, red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with distinctive horizontal lenticels.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
