Sambucus hookeri

Sambucus hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
