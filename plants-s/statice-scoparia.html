Statice scoparia

Statice scoparia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal and maritime environments. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Statice scoparia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
