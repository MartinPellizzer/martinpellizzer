Silphium gummiferum

Silphium gummiferum, commonly known as the gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. Silphium gummiferum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to flourish.
