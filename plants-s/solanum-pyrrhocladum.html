Solanum pyrrhocladum

Solanum pyrrhocladum, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
