Selaginella riddellii

Selaginella riddellii, commonly known as the Riddell's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in the southeastern United States.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit. The plant has small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces spores in specialized structures called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest floors.

Selaginella riddellii is an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
