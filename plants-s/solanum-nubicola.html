Solanum nubicola

Solanum nubicola, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, and it produces berries that are often toxic.

Solanum nubicola is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
