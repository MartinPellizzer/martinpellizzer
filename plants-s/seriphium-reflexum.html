Seriphium reflexum

Seriphium reflexum, commonly known as the bent-thorned hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and resemble a dandelion.

Seriphium reflexum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
