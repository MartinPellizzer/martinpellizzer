Senecio caucasigenus

Senecio caucasigenus, commonly known as the Caucasian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
