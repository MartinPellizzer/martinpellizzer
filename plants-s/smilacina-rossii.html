Smilacina rossii

Smilacina rossii, commonly known as rose twining vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, often climbing trees or shrubs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Smilacina rossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
