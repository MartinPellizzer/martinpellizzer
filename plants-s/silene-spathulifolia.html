Silene spathulifolia

Silene spathulifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
