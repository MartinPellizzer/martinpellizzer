Sorbus alnifolia var. submollis

Sorbus alnifolia var. submollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
