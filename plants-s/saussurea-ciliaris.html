Saussurea ciliaris

Saussurea ciliaris, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are yellowish-green and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
