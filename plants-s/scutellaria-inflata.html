Scutellaria inflata

Scutellaria inflata, commonly known as the inflated skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features distinctive inflated calyces that resemble small skulls, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with two-lipped petals and a prominent tube.

Scutellaria inflata is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
