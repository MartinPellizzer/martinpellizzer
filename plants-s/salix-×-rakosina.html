Salix × rakosina

Salix × rakosina, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that are elongated and pointed. It thrives in moist environments, often found near rivers, ponds, and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
