Symplocos caerulescens

Symplocos caerulescens, commonly known as bluebead lily, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, bead-like blue berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Symplocos caerulescens thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
