Saussurea monocephala

Saussurea monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flower heads, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its habitat includes rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cold and arid conditions.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal value, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
