Schedonorus transparens

Schedonorus transparens, commonly known as the transparent grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spikelets. Schedonorus transparens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
