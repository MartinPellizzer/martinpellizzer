Solanum schumannianum var. austerum

Solanum schumannianum var. austerum, commonly known as the Schumann's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural contexts.
