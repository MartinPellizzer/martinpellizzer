Schinus pubescens

Schinus pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and have a pubescent surface. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
