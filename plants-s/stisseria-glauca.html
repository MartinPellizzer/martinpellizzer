Stisseria glauca

Stisseria glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Stisseria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Stisseria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
