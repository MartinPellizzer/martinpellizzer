Silene burchellii var. meruensis

Silene burchellii var. meruensis, commonly known as the Meru catchfly, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
