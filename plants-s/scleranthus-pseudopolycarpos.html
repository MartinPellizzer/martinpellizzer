Scleranthus pseudopolycarpos

Scleranthus pseudopolycarpos, commonly known as the false polycarp, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often with a rough texture.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
