Solanum senecioides

Solanum senecioides, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, white or purple flowers and produces berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
