Scrophularia multicaulis

Scrophularia multicaulis, commonly known as the tall dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Scrophularia multicaulis is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
