Stachys romana

Stachys romana, commonly known as Roman hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or along rocky slopes.

Stachys romana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
