Senecio albicans

Senecio albicans, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-white foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap. It thrives in arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some regions and can be harmful to livestock.
