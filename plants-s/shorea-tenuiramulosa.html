Shorea tenuiramulosa

Shorea tenuiramulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive narrow shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
