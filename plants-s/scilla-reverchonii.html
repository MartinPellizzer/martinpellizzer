Scilla reverchonii

Scilla reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Scilla reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
