Suaeda tenuifolia

Suaeda tenuifolia, commonly known as sea spurge, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salinity and arid conditions. Its succulent stems store water, aiding survival in harsh climates.

Suaeda tenuifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
