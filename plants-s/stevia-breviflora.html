Stevia breviflora

Stevia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Stevia breviflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
