Selaginella plumieri

Selaginella plumieri, commonly known as the spike moss, is a member of the spore-bearing plant group called lycophytes.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Selaginella plumieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in soil stabilization.
