Succisa coriacea

Succisa coriacea, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are covered in a layer of fine, gritty hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to harsh conditions, with a compact growth form and thickened leaves that store water.
