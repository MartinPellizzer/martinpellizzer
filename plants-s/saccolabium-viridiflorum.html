Saccolabium viridiflorum

Saccolabium viridiflorum, commonly known as the greenflower saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and compound leaves. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.

Saccolabium viridiflorum is adapted to low-water conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
