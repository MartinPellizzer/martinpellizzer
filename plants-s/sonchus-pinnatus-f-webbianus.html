Sonchus pinnatus f. webbianus

Sonchus pinnatus f. webbianus, commonly known as the webb's sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lobed leaves that are typically alternate and pinnately divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are followed by small, spiky achenes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.
