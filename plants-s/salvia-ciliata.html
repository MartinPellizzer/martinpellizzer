Salvia ciliata

Salvia ciliata, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia ciliata is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
