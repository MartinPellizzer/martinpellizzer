Symplocos ophirensis var. densireticulata

Symplocos ophirensis var. densireticulata, commonly known as the dense-netted symplocos, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a dense network of veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers clustered in small inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is typically red when mature.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
