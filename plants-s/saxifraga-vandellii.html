Saxifraga vandellii

Saxifraga vandellii, commonly known as the Vandell's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga vandellii is often found in crevices and on slopes, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
