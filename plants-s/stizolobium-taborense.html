Stizolobium taborense

Stizolobium taborense, commonly known as the Tabore acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is well adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

The plant is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
