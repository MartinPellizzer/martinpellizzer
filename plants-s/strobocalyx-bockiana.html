Strobocalyx bockiana

Strobocalyx bockiana, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
