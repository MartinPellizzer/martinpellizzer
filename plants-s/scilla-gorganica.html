Scilla gorganica

Scilla gorganica, commonly known as the sea squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in coastal areas and sandy soils. Scilla gorganica is adapted to saline environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and maritime landscapes.
