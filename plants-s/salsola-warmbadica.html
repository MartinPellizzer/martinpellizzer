Salsola warmbadica

Salsola warmbadica, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous and short-lived. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought and salt.

Salsola warmbadica plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
