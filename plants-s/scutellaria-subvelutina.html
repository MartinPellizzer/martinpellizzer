Scutellaria subvelutina

Scutellaria subvelutina, commonly known as velvet scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated by horticulturists.
