Sorghum melanocarpum

Sorghum melanocarpum, commonly known as black sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which contribute to its striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
