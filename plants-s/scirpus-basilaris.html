Scirpus basilaris

Scirpus basilaris, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense clusters of small, cylindrical flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus basilaris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
