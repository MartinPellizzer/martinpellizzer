Senecio acerifolius

Senecio acerifolius, commonly known as the mapleleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features palmate, maple-like leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio acerifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
