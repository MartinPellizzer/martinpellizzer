Saxofridericia brasiliensis

Saxofridericia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal regions and is known for its resilience to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
