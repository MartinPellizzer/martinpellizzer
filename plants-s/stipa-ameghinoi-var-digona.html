Stipa ameghinoi var. digona

Stipa ameghinoi var. digona, commonly known as the Argentine needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
