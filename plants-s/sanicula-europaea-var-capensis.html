Sanicula europaea var. capensis

Sanicula europaea var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
