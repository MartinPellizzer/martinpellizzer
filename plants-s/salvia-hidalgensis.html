Salvia hidalgensis

Salvia hidalgensis, commonly known as Hidalgo sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, minty scent.

Salvia hidalgensis is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
