Stanhopea pozoi

Stanhopea pozoi, commonly known as the Pozo's orchid, is a species native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its blooms feature vibrant colors, often with a mix of red, orange, and yellow, making it visually distinctive.

Due to habitat loss, Stanhopea pozoi is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
