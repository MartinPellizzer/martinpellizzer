Salix retusa subf. integrifolia

Salix retusa subf.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-margined.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
