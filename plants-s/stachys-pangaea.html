Stachys pangaea

Stachys pangaea, commonly known as the broadleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys pangaea is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
