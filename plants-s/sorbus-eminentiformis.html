Sorbus eminentiformis

Sorbus eminentiformis, commonly known as the mountain whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a red or orange color in the fall.

Sorbus eminentiformis plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing food for various wildlife species.
