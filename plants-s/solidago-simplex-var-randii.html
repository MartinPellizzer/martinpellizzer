Solidago simplex var. randii

Solidago simplex var. randii, commonly known as the plains goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
