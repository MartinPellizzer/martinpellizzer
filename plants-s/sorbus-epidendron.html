Sorbus epidendron

Sorbus epidendron, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus epidendron is typically found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
