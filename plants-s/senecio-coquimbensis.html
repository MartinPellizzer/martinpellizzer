Senecio coquimbensis

Senecio coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo senecio, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
