Syzygium jienfunicum

Syzygium jienfunicum, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing edible seeds surrounded by a sour pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
