Symplocos pycnantha

Symplocos pycnantha, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Indian holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
