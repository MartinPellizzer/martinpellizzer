Senecio jacobaea proles erraticus

Senecio jacobaea proles erraticus, commonly known as the ragged robin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces a milky sap that can be toxic to livestock.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
