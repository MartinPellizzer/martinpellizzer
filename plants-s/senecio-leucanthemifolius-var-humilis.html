Senecio leucanthemifolius var. humilis

Senecio leucanthemifolius var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
