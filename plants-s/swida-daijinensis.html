Swida daijinensis

Swida daijinensis, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.

Swida daijinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
