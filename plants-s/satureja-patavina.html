Satureja patavina

Satureja patavina, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Satureja patavina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.
