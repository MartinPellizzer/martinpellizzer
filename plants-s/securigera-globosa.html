Securigera globosa

Securigera globosa, commonly known as globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiky, silver-gray leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, achene-like seeds. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Securigera globosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking, architectural flowers and drought tolerance.
