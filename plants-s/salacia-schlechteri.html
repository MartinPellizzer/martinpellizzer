Salacia schlechteri

Salacia schlechteri, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
