Sida corymbosa

Sida corymbosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Sida corymbosa is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
