Sarothra saginoides

Sarothra saginoides, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Sarothra saginoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
