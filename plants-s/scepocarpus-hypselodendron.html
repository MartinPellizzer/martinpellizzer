Scepocarpus hypselodendron

Scepocarpus hypselodendron, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic life. The plant produces showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it to the muddy substrate, while its stems float on the water's surface.

The plant plays an important role in the ecosystem by contributing to water oxygenation and supporting biodiversity.
