Sida parens

Sida parens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Sida parens is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is also noted for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
