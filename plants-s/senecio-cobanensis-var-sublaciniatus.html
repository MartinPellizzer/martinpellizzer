Senecio cobanensis var. sublaciniatus

Senecio cobanensis var. sublaciniatus, commonly known as the jagged leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features elongated, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
