Solanum muricatum var. parvifolium

Solanum muricatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf gooseberry, is a variant of the Andean gooseberry.

It is a shrub native to the Andes mountains, characterized by its small, lobed leaves and clusters of yellow-orange berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically tart and acidic, often used in jams and beverages. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is valued for both its culinary and ornamental uses.
