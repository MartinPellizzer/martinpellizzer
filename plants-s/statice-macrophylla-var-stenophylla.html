Statice macrophylla var. stenophylla

Statice macrophylla var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy purple or pink flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments and coastal marshes. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
