Symphyotrichum hendersonii

Symphyotrichum hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's New England aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in open areas with partial to full sunlight.
