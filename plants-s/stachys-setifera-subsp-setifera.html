Stachys setifera subsp. setifera

Stachys setifera subsp.

setifera, commonly known as the slender betony, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
