Sambucus racemosa var. xanthocarpa

Sambucus racemosa var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
