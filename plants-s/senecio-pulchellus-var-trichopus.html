Senecio pulchellus var. trichopus

Senecio pulchellus var. trichopus, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
