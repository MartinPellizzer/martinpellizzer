Sisymbrium incisum var. californicum

Sisymbrium incisum var. californicum, commonly known as the California hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a peppery scent when crushed.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in California's coastal and inland regions.
