Soehrensia formosa var. maxima

Soehrensia formosa var. maxima, commonly known as the giant whiteflowered plectranthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
