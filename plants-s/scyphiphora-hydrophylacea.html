Scyphiphora hydrophylacea

Scyphiphora hydrophylacea, commonly known as water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually floating or partially submerged. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

Scyphiphora hydrophylacea is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions and is sometimes used in water gardens.
