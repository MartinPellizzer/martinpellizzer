Senecio scopolii subsp. scopolii

Senecio scopolii subsp.

scopolii, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a radiate head, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system.
