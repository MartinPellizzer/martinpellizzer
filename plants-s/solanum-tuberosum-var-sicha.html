Solanum tuberosum var. sicha

Solanum tuberosum var. sicha, commonly known as the Sichuan potato, is a variant of the common potato native to China.

It is characterized by its starchy tuberous roots, which are typically smaller and have a slightly bitter taste compared to standard potatoes. The plant produces violet flowers and has hairy stems, typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a cool growing season.

This variety is often cultivated for local consumption and has cultural significance in certain regions of China.
