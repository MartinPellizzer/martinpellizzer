Scirpus boraeanus

Scirpus boraeanus, commonly known as the northern bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Scirpus boraeanus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
