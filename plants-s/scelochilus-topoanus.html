Scelochilus topoanus

Scelochilus topoanus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
