Sideritis mohamedii

Sideritis mohamedii, commonly known as the Mohamed's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
