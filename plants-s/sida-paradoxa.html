Sida paradoxa

Sida paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxal sida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with a rough texture. Sida paradoxa has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
