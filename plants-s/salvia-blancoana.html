Salvia blancoana

Salvia blancoana, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia blancoana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
