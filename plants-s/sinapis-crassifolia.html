Sinapis crassifolia

Sinapis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in dry conditions.

Sinapis crassifolia is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
