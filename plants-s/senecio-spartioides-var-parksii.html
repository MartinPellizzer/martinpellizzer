Senecio spartioides var. parksii

Senecio spartioides var. parksii, commonly known as the Parks' spineless dandelion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in its native ecosystem.
