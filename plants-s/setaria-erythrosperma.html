Setaria erythrosperma

Setaria erythrosperma, commonly known as red-seeded foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, red-brown seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Setaria erythrosperma is a competitive weed that can affect crop yields if not managed effectively.
