Senecio argutus

Senecio argutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio argutus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
