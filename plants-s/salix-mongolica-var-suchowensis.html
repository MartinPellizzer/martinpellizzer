Salix mongolica var. suchowensis

Salix mongolica var. suchowensis, commonly known as the Suchow willow, is a shrub native to northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
