Scorzonera koelpinioides

Scorzonera koelpinioides, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped tubers that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Scorzonera koelpinioides is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
