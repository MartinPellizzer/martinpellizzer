Spiraea beauverdiana

Spiraea beauverdiana, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has slender, arching branches covered with fine, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy floral display and compact growth habit.
