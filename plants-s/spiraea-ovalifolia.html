Spiraea ovalifolia

Spiraea ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
