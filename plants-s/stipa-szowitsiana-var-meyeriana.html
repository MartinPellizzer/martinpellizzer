Stipa szowitsiana var. meyeriana

Stipa szowitsiana var. meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, needle-like leaves that form dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces feathery, branched inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine, grass-like foliage.
