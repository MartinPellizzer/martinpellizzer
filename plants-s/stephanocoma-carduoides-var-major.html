Stephanocoma carduoides var. major

Stephanocoma carduoides var. major, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its growth habit includes a compact, bushy form with multiple stems, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
