Selaginella harveyi

Selaginella harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with closely packed, needle-like leaves that form dense, moss-like mats. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophylls.

Selaginella harveyi is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
