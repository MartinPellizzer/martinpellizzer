Sedum purpureoviride

Sedum purpureoviride, commonly known as the purple-green stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a purplish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
