Solanum forsteri

Solanum forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is noted for its toxic properties due to the presence of solanine and other alkaloids.
