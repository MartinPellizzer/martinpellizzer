Silene paranadena

Silene paranadena, commonly known as the Parana rosebay, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in parts of Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it typically grows in moist, shady habitats.

Silene paranadena is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
