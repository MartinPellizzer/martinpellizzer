Smilax herbacea var. lasioneuron

Smilax herbacea var. lasioneuron, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and often bear small, sharp spines.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
