Sorbus sibirica

Sorbus sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Sorbus sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
