Scutellaria ovata subsp. versicolor

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable skullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the Scutellariaceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable flower coloration, which can range from white to deep violet.
