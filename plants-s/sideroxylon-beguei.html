Sideroxylon beguei

Sideroxylon beguei, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
