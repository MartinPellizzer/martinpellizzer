Serratula ambigua

Serratula ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Serratula ambigua is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
