Syringa × metensis

Syringa × metensis, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 4 to 10 feet and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Syringa × metensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
