Sedum melanoleucum

Sedum melanoleucum, commonly known as the silver stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
