Syzygium elopurae

Syzygium elopurae, commonly known as the purple-leaf syzygium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn a striking purple hue under certain conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Syzygium elopurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
