Saxifraga globulifera var. riphaea

Saxifraga globulifera var. riphaea, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh alpine environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky soils and high altitudes, often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cold climates and poor soil conditions.
