Suttonia apodocarpa

Suttonia apodocarpa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Suttonia apodocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
