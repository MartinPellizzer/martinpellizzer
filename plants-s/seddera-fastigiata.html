Seddera fastigiata

Seddera fastigiata, commonly known as the upright seddera, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in horticultural settings.
