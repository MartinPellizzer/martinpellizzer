Sulcorebutia pampagrandensis

Sulcorebutia pampagrandensis, commonly known as the Pampagrande crested cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and distinctive, ribbed structure. The plant features short, spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the growing season. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a shallow root system and thickened stems for water storage.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
