Struthiopteris amabilis

Struthiopteris amabilis, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of polypody fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow fronds that emerge in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces clustered, pinnate leaves.

Struthiopteris amabilis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
