Saxifraga excavata

Saxifraga excavata, commonly known as the "cleft-leaf saxifrage," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal habitats. The plant features small, succulent leaves that are deeply notched or cleft, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.

Saxifraga excavata is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
