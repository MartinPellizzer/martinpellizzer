Saussurea alpina

Saussurea alpina, commonly known as alpine helve, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette.

Saussurea alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
