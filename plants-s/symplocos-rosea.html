Symplocos rosea

Symplocos rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Symplocos rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
