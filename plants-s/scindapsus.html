Scindapsus

Scindapsus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that often features silver or white markings. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, making the plant a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Scindapsus species are known for their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and require minimal maintenance.

They are also appreciated for their air-purifying qualities and aesthetic appeal.
