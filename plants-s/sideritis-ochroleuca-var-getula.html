Sideritis ochroleuca var. getula

Sideritis ochroleuca var. getula, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
