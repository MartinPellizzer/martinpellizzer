Spiraea ulmaria var. florepleno

Spiraea ulmaria var. florepleno, commonly known as the double meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, double-flowered inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn.

Spiraea ulmaria var. florepleno prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream sides.
