Salacca rupicola

Salacca rupicola, commonly known as the rock date palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its clustering growth habit and long, slender leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to dates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
