Senecio addoensis

Senecio addoensis, commonly known as Addo's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in the Western Cape region. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio addoensis is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
