Senecio picardae

Senecio picardae, commonly known as the Picard's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Senecio picardae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its compact size and attractive blooms.
