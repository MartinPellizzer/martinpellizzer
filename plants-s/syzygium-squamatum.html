Syzygium squamatum

Syzygium squamatum, commonly known as the silver apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Syzygium squamatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
