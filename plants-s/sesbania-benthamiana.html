Sesbania benthamiana

Sesbania benthamiana, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are edible and have been utilized in traditional cuisines.
