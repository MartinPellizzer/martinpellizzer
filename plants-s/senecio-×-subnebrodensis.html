Senecio × subnebrodensis

Senecio × subnebrodensis, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
