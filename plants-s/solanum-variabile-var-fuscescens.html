Solanum variabile var. fuscescens

Solanum variabile var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy, often reddish stems and small, egg-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential medicinal properties.
