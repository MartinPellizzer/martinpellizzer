Sinopodophyllum hexandrum var. emodi

Sinopodophyllum hexandrum var. emodi, commonly known as Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped cluster of leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the summer. Its roots contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.
