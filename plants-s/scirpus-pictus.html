Scirpus pictus

Scirpus pictus, commonly known as the painted bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Scirpus pictus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
