Simaba insignis

Simaba insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe containing a single, hard seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
