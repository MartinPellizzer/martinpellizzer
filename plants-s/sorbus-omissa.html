Sorbus omissa

Sorbus omissa, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten by birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Sorbus omissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
