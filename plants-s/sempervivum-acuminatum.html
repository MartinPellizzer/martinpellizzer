Sempervivum acuminatum

Sempervivum acuminatum, commonly known as the pointed houseleek, is a hardy succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues under stress. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Sempervivum acuminatum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
