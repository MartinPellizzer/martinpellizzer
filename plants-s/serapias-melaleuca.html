Serapias melaleuca

Serapias melaleuca, commonly known as the honeycomb orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure resembling a honeycomb. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive pattern of longitudinal ridges.

Serapias melaleuca is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
