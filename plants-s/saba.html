Saba

Saba, commonly known as the "sabaya" or "saba banana," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family, which also includes bananas and plantains. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces a cluster of edible, starchy tubers underground. These tubers are often used in various culinary dishes across the region.

Saba is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in traditional agriculture and local economies.
