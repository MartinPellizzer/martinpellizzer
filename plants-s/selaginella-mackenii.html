Selaginella mackenii

Selaginella mackenii, commonly known as the Mackenii spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a dense, mat-forming structure with upright, needle-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color. Selaginella mackenii is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, which are located on specialized leaves called microphylls.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of humid conditions.
