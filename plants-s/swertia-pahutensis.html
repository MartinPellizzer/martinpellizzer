Swertia pahutensis

Swertia pahutensis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia pahutensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
