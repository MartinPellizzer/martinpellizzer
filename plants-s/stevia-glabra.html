Stevia glabra

Stevia glabra, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Stevia glabra is renowned for its leaves containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural, zero-calorie sweeteners.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
