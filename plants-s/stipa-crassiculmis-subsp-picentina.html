Stipa crassiculmis subsp. picentina

Stipa crassiculmis subsp.

picentina, commonly known as the Picentine needlegrass, is a grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has a low-growing habit with rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
