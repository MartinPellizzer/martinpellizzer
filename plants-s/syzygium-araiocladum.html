Syzygium araiocladum

Syzygium araiocladum, commonly known as the golden apple or red apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Syzygium araiocladum is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
