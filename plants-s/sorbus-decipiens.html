Sorbus decipiens

Sorbus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature of the plant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus decipiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
