Silene inflata var. viridiflorus

Silene inflata var. viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces inflated, bladder-like seed pods that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

Silene inflata var. viridiflorus is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies.
