Solanum pteleifolium

Solanum pteleifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxicity, it is typically avoided in horticultural settings.
