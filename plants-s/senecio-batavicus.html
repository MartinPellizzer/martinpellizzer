Senecio batavicus

Senecio batavicus, commonly known as the Batavian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio batavicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
