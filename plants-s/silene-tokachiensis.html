Silene tokachiensis

Silene tokachiensis, commonly known as the Tokachi catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often arranged in a cluster at the top of the plant.

Silene tokachiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
