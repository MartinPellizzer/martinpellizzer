Scirpus proliferoramosus

Scirpus proliferoramosus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus proliferoramosus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
