Salvia protracta

Salvia protracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia protracta is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, such as slopes and canyons.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
