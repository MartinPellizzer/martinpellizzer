Sasa aureostriata

Sasa aureostriata, commonly known as golden stripe bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered with a distinctive golden stripe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth, slightly waxy texture.

Sasa aureostriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
