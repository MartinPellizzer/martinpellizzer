Sonninia

Sonninia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, particularly in the savannas and woodlands. The plants are characterized by their woody stems and compound leaves, often with a distinctive reddish hue. They produce small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Sonninia species are valued for their hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and craftsmanship.
