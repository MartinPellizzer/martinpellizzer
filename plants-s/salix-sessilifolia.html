Salix sessilifolia

Salix sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its distinctive leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, typical of willow species. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Salix sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
