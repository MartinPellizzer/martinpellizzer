Salix longistamina var. glabra

Salix longistamina var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth catkin willow, is a variant of the long-staminal willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and catkin-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

This species is often found in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
