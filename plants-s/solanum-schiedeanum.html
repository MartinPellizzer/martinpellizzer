Solanum schiedeanum

Solanum schiedeanum, commonly known as the Schiedea nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow or orange berries.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and coastal areas.
