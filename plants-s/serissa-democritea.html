Serissa democritea

Serissa democritea, commonly known as the silver serissa, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
