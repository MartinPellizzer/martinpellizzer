Salix × taoensis var. pedicellata

Salix × taoensis var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate willow, is a hybrid willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, catkin-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil along water bodies.
