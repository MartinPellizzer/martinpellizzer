Syngonium laterinervium

Syngonium laterinervium, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name "laterinervium," meaning "with lateral veins.". The plant is a climbing or trailing vine that thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and cream.

Syngonium laterinervium is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
