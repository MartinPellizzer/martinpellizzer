Salvia ourophylla

Salvia ourophylla, commonly known as the redstem sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia ourophylla is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
