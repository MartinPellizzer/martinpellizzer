Swartzia tomentosa var. sagotii

Swartzia tomentosa var. sagotii, commonly known as the Sagot's satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and smooth, often whitish or grayish bark. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate, with a velvety texture. The flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in panicle inflorescences.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
