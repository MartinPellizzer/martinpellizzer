Sida sordida

Sida sordida, commonly known as black sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or subshrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish and are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Sida sordida is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
