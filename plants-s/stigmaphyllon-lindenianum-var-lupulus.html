Stigmaphyllon lindenianum var. lupulus

Stigmaphyllon lindenianum var. lupulus, commonly known as the yellow stigmaphyllon, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
