Sorbus aria f. macrocarpa

Sorbus aria f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, yellowish-red berries that remain on the tree throughout winter. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn a striking orange-red in the fall. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
