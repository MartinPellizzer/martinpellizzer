Salvia lyrata

Salvia lyrata, commonly known as meadow sage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia lyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
