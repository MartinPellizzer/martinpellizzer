Spiranthes ulaei

Spiranthes ulaei, commonly known as the Florida ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraled inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Spiranthes ulaei is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
