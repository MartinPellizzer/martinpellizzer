Stelis capillaris

Stelis capillaris, commonly known as the hairleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name.

Stelis capillaris prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
