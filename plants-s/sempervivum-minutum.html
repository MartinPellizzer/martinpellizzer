Sempervivum minutum

Sempervivum minutum, commonly known as the common houseleek, is a small, hardy succulent native to Europe.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils and can tolerate drought conditions. It produces small, white or pink flowers on tall stems during the summer months.

Sempervivum minutum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
