Senecio paludicola

Senecio paludicola, commonly known as the marsh groundsel, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on moist soil. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to retain moisture.

Senecio paludicola plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
