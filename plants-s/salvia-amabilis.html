Salvia amabilis

Salvia amabilis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking purple to pink tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia amabilis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
