Santolina chamaecyparissus var. homophylla

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. homophylla, commonly known as silver spruce santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens, borders, and as a ground cover.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil for optimal growth.
