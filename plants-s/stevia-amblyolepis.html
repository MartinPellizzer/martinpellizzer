Stevia amblyolepis

Stevia amblyolepis, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, serrated leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it adaptable to different environments. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia amblyolepis is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweeteners.
