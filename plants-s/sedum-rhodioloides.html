Sedum rhodioloides

Sedum rhodioloides, commonly known as the red stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
