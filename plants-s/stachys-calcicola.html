Stachys calcicola

Stachys calcicola, commonly known as the limestone betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky, limestone-rich soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale purple to blue, with a distinctive tubular shape.

Stachys calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
