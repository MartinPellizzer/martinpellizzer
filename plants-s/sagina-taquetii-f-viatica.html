Sagina taquetii f. viatica

Sagina taquetii f. viatica, commonly known as the Viatic Sagina, is a rare and distinctive succulent plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.
