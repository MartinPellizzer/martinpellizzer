Sorghum procerum

Sorghum procerum, commonly known as the tall sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically pink to purplish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Sorghum procerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
