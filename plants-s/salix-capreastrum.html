Salix capreastrum

Salix capreastrum, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a small shrub belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streams and lakeshores.

Salix capreastrum is notable for its early spring flowering, producing catkins that are an important food source for pollinators.
