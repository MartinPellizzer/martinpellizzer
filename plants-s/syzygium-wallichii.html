Syzygium wallichii

Syzygium wallichii, commonly known as the Indian star fruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow or green when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate in shape. Syzygium wallichii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is valued for its tangy flavor and nutritional content.
