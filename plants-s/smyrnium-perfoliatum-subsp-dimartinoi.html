Smyrnium perfoliatum subsp. dimartinoi

Smyrnium perfoliatum subsp.

dimartinoi, commonly known as the Perfoliate Burnet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of finely toothed, aromatic leaves that often have a reddish or purplish hue.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
