Senecio paranensis

Senecio paranensis, commonly known as the Parana Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves to conserve water. Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
