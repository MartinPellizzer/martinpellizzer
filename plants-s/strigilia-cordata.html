Strigilia cordata

Strigilia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf strigil, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Strigilia cordata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
