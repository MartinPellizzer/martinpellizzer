Scirpus pterigospermus

Scirpus pterigospermus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Scirpus pterigospermus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
