Scorzonera variifolia

Scorzonera variifolia, commonly known as the variable scorzonera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, and its roots are often tuberous. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Scorzonera variifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
