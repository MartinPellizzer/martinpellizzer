Saxifraga rufidula

Saxifraga rufidula, commonly known as the redstem saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals that are pink or red in color.

Saxifraga rufidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
