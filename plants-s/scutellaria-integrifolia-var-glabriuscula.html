Scutellaria integrifolia var. glabriuscula

Scutellaria integrifolia var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
