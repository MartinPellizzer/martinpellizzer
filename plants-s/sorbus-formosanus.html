Sorbus formosanus

Sorbus formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
