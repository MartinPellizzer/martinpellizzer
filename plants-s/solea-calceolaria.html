Solea calceolaria

Solea calceolaria, commonly known as the shoe flower, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble small shoes, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically ovate with a waxy texture and a dark green color.

Solea calceolaria thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
