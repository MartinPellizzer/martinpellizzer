Shibataea lancifolia

Shibataea lancifolia, commonly known as the Japanese fern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and a distinctive, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Shibataea lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
