Solanum cestrifolium

Solanum cestrifolium, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries are typically red or yellow when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It often grows in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
