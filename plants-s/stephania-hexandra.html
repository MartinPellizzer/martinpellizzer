Stephania hexandra

Stephania hexandra, commonly known as the giant betel vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, palmate fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its use in traditional medicine and its role in local ecosystems.
