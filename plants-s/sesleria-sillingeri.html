Sesleria sillingeri

Sesleria sillingeri, commonly known as the Sillinger's tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown, adding subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as part of a native plant collection.

Sesleria sillingeri is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
