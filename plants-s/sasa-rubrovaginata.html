Sasa rubrovaginata

Sasa rubrovaginata, commonly known as the red-barked sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green, with a distinctive red or reddish-brown sheath around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa rubrovaginata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional landscaping and basket-making in some regions.
