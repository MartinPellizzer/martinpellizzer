Stachys baicalensis var. japonica

Stachys baicalensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale purple to pinkish flowers in whorled clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
