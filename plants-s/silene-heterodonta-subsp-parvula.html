Silene heterodonta subsp. parvula

Silene heterodonta subsp.

parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often noted for its short stature and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
