Senna tocotana

Senna tocotana, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna tocotana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
