Solanum leucopogon

Solanum leucopogon, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its fruits are small berries that change color as they mature, often starting green and turning red or yellow.

Solanum leucopogon is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
