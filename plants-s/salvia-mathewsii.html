Salvia mathewsii

Salvia mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its violet to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia mathewsii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
