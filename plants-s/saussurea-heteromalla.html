Saussurea heteromalla

Saussurea heteromalla, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Tibet, Nepal, and northern India.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Saussurea heteromalla is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
