Solanum aquartia var. fuertesii

Solanum aquartia var. fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertesii nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, green, egg-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
