Solanum looseri

Solanum looseri, commonly known as the Looser's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum looseri is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid environments.
