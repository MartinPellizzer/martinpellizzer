Sykesia arenaria

Sykesia arenaria, commonly known as the sand dune sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in sandy and saline environments, often found in dune ecosystems where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing sand dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Sykesia arenaria is an important component of the flora in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
