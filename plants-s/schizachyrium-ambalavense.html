Schizachyrium ambalavense

Schizachyrium ambalavense, commonly known as the Ambala panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia, particularly India.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as riverbanks and marshy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, paniculate inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and culms that are usually erect and somewhat branched.

Schizachyrium ambalavense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
