Styrax japonicus f. jippei-kawakamii

Styrax japonicus f. jippei-kawakamii, commonly known as the Japanese fringed styrax, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a variant of the species Styrax japonicus, characterized by its distinctive fringed petals on the flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is valued for its ornamental qualities. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually white or pale pink and emit a subtle fragrance.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
