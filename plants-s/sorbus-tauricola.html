Sorbus tauricola

Sorbus tauricola, commonly known as the Taurian rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically parts of southern Russia and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with serrated edges.

Sorbus tauricola is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
