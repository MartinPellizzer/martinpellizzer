Sagraea setulosa

Sagraea setulosa, commonly known as the hairy sagraea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Sagraea setulosa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
