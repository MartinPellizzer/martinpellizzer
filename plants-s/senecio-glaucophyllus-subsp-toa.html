Senecio glaucophyllus subsp. toa

Senecio glaucophyllus subsp.

toa, commonly known as the Toa Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features distinctive blue-green foliage with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
