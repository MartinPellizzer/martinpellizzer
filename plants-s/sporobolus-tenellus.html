Sporobolus tenellus

Sporobolus tenellus, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sporobolus tenellus is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in poor soil environments.
