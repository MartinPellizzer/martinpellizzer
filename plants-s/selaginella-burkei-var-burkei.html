Selaginella burkei var. burkei

Selaginella burkei var. burkei, commonly known as the Burkill's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation, due to its specialized spore-producing structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage, with spores being the primary means of reproduction.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitats.
