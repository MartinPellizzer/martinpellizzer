Serapias ovalis

Serapias ovalis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and a compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually yellow or orange with a deep, hooded lip.

Serapias ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
