Schwenckia americana var. macedo

Schwenckia americana var. macedo, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, lavender or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
