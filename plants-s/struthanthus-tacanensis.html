Struthanthus tacanensis

Struthanthus tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, hairy layer, which helps reduce water loss.

Struthanthus tacanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
