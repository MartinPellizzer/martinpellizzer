Sedum telephium subsp. alboroseum

Sedum telephium subsp.

alboroseum, commonly known as the White Rose Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
