Sorbus latifolia var. obtusata

Sorbus latifolia var. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dull green on the upper surface and hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
