Silene ramburiana

Silene ramburiana, commonly known as the Rambur's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are erect and branching.

Silene ramburiana is relatively rare and is considered a species of conservation concern in its native habitat.
