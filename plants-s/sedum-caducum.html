Sedum caducum

Sedum caducum, commonly known as the winter sedum, is a perennial succulent native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a hardy addition to various landscapes.
