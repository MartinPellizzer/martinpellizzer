Syagrus dyerana

Syagrus dyerana, commonly known as the Dyer's palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-trunked structure and feather-like, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange fruits. Syagrus dyerana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
