Silene macropoda var. media

Silene macropoda var. media, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
