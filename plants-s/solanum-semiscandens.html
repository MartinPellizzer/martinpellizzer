Solanum semiscandens

Solanum semiscandens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum semiscandens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
