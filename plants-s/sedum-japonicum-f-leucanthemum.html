Sedum japonicum f. leucanthemum

Sedum japonicum f. leucanthemum, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a variant of the Japanese stonecrop.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clustered, star-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, giving it a delicate appearance. Native to Japan, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This ornamental plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
