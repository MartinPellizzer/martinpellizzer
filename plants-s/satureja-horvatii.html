Satureja horvatii

Satureja horvatii, commonly known as Horvat's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

Satureja horvatii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to temperate climates.
