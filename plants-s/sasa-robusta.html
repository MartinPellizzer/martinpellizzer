Sasa robusta

Sasa robusta, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of hair. Sasa robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
