Saussurea brassicifolia

Saussurea brassicifolia, commonly known as the Brassica-leaved Saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Saussurea brassicifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
