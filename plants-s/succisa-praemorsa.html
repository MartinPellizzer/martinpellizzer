Succisa praemorsa

Succisa praemorsa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Succisa praemorsa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its visual appeal.
