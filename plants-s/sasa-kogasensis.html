Sasa kogasensis

Sasa kogasensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa kogasensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
