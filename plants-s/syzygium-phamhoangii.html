Syzygium phamhoangii

Syzygium phamhoangii, commonly known as the Pham Hoang apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a crisp, sweet flesh. Syzygium phamhoangii thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
