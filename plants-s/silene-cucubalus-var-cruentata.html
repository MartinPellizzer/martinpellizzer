Silene cucubalus var. cruentata

Silene cucubalus var. cruentata, commonly known as the red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
