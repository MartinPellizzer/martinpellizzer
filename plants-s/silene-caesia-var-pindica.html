Silene caesia var. pindica

Silene caesia var. pindica, commonly known as the Pindus campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
