Stipa stuposa

Stipa stuposa, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and often bend toward the ground, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stipa stuposa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
