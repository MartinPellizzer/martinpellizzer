Sypharissa

Sypharissa, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, poisonous berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Sypharissa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
