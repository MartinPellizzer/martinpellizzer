Setaria mombassana

Setaria mombassana, commonly known as the Mombassa grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant.
