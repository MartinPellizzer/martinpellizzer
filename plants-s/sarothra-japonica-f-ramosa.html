Sarothra japonica f. ramosa

Sarothra japonica f. ramosa, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a variant of the Japanese yew species.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features dark green, glossy, needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters. It produces small, reddish-purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
