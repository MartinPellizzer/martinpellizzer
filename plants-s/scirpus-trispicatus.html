Scirpus trispicatus

Scirpus trispicatus, commonly known as the three-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist to wet soils.

Scirpus trispicatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
