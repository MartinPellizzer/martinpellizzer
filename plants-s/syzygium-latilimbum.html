Syzygium latilimbum

Syzygium latilimbum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, acidic fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Syzygium latilimbum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
