Setaria aurea var. pallida

Setaria aurea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a pale green or bluish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
