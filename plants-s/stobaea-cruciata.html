Stobaea cruciata

Stobaea cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-like blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

Stobaea cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
