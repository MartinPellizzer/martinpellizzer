Saurauia ruiziana var. peduncularis

Saurauia ruiziana var. peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered saurauia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
