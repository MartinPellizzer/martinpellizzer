Solanum tuberosum f. lelekkoya

Solanum tuberosum f. lelekkoya, commonly known as the Lelekkoya potato, is a botanical variety characterized by its unique tuber morphology and genetic traits.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to high-altitude regions of the Andes. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Its tubers are typically elongated and have a smooth skin, with a flavor profile that sets it apart from common potato varieties.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability to specific environmental conditions and its potential use in breeding programs for improved agricultural traits.
