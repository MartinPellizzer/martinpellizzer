Sidalcea vitifolia

Sidalcea vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf checkerbloom, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves that are typically divided into lobed or rounded segments, often with a silvery or fuzzy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal prairies and dunes.

Sidalcea vitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
