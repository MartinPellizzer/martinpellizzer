Scrophularia canina var. humifusa

Scrophularia canina var. humifusa, commonly known as the creeping dog's mouth, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It is characterized by its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

The variety humifusa differs from the typical form by its more prostrate growth habit and smaller flowers.
