Symphoricarpos rotundifolius var. vaccinioides

Symphoricarpos rotundifolius var. vaccinioides, commonly known as the red sandcherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features include woody stems, showy inflorescences, and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
