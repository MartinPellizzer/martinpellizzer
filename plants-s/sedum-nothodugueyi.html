Sedum nothodugueyi

Sedum nothodugueyi, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
