Sebaea crassulifolia var. lanceolata

Sebaea crassulifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate sebaea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically lance-shaped, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
