Stachys oblongifolia var. leptopoda

Stachys oblongifolia var. leptopoda, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
