Sonchus kabarensis

Sonchus kabarensis, commonly known as the Kabare sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
