Syringa × josiflexa

Syringa × josiflexa, commonly known as the weeping bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, arching branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Syringa × josiflexa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
