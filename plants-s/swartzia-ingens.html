Swartzia ingens

Swartzia ingens, commonly known as the giant swartzia, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters.

The fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release its seeds.
