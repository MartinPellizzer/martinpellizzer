Solanum phyllanthum

Solanum phyllanthum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
