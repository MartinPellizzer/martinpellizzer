Selaginella vestiens

Selaginella vestiens, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves that give it a moss-like appearance. Selaginella vestiens is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in various microclimates.
