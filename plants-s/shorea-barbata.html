Shorea barbata

Shorea barbata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and pale yellow, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round fruits.

Shorea barbata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
