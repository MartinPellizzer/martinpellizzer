Sphaeranthus cotuloides

Sphaeranthus cotuloides, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as a low-growing annual herb with hairy, narrow leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
