Sisyrinchium hastile

Sisyrinchium hastile, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Sisyrinchium hastile is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
