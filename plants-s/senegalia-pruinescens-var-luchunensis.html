Senegalia pruinescens var. luchunensis

Senegalia pruinescens var. luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by long, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and forage.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
