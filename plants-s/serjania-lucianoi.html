Serjania lucianoi

Serjania lucianoi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian redwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Serjania.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Serjania lucianoi is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
