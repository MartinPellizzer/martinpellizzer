Sparganium englerianum

Sparganium englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's bur-reed, is a species of aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spiky, cone-like structures that contain the seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The plant plays an important role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
