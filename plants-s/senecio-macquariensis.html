Senecio macquariensis

Senecio macquariensis, commonly known as the Macquarie Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Senecio macquariensis is well adapted to drought conditions and is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
