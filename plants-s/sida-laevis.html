Sida laevis

Sida laevis, commonly known as silky sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Sida laevis is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
