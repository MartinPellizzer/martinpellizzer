Stapelia ciliata var. cristata

Stapelia ciliata var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that form a star-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its distinctive appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
