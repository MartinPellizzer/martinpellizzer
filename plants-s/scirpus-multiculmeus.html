Scirpus multiculmeus

Scirpus multiculmeus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes contribute to its ability to spread and stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus multiculmeus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
