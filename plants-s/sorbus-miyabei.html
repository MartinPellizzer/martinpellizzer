Sorbus miyabei

Sorbus miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by clusters of red, berry-like fruits in the autumn. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and colorful fruit display.

Sorbus miyabei is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
