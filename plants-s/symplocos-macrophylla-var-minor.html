Symplocos macrophylla var. minor

Symplocos macrophylla var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
