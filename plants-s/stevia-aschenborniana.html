Stevia aschenborniana

Stevia aschenborniana, commonly known as the Aschenborn's stevia, is a species native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners that are much sweeter than table sugar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary potential, it has gained interest in herbal and organic gardening circles.
