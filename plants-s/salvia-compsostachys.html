Salvia compsostachys

Salvia compsostachys, commonly known as the golden sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
