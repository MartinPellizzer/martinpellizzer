Stachys closii

Stachys closii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Stachys closii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
