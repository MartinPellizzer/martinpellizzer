Sorbus aria grandifolia

Sorbus aria grandifolia, commonly known as the whitebeam or largeleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with finely serrated edges, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.
