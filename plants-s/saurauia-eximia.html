Saurauia eximia

Saurauia eximia, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Saurauia eximia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
