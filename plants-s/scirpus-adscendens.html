Scirpus adscendens

Scirpus adscendens, commonly known as the ascending bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and form dense mats that help stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Scirpus adscendens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
