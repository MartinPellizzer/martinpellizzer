Scilla secunda

Scilla secunda, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hardy, drought-resistant nature. Scilla secunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental foliage.
