Solanum solisii

Solanum solisii, commonly known as the Solisii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum solisii is often found in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
