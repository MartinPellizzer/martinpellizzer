Salvia lozanoi

Salvia lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Salvia lozanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
