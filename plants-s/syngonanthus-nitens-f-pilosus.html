Syngonanthus nitens f. pilosus

Syngonanthus nitens f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy mountain mallow, is a variant of the mountain mallow species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This horticultural form is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
