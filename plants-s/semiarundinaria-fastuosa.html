Semiarundinaria fastuosa

Semiarundinaria fastuosa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, with a distinctive golden hue to its leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
