Solanum amplexicaule var. pubescens

Solanum amplexicaule var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
