Satureja hochreutineri var. citriodora

Satureja hochreutineri var. citriodora, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in culinary and aromatic applications.

The variety is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and fragrance profiles.
