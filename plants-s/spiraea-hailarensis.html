Spiraea hailarensis

Spiraea hailarensis, commonly known as the Hailar spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to northeastern China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Spiraea hailarensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
