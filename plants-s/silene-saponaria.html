Silene saponaria

Silene saponaria, commonly known as the soapwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its roots contain saponins, which give it its common name and have been used historically in soap-making.

Silene saponaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
