Senecio transvaalensis

Senecio transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

Senecio transvaalensis is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
