Sorbus caucasigena f. subintegerrima

Sorbus caucasigena f. subintegerrima, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin, and the plant exhibits a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in mountainous environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
