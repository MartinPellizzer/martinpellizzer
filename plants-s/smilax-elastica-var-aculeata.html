Smilax elastica var. aculeata

Smilax elastica var. aculeata, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a vigorous, woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, slender stems with stout, sharp spines along its stems and leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a rough, textured surface, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it a plant to handle with care.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
