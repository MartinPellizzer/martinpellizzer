Sarothamnus affinis

Sarothamnus affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthrift, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has small, narrow, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sarothamnus affinis is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
