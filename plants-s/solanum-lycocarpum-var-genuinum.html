Solanum lycocarpum var. genuinum

Solanum lycocarpum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its oval, red or orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
