Senecio altissimus

Senecio altissimus, commonly known as the Blueweed or Great Blueweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head, making it easily identifiable.
