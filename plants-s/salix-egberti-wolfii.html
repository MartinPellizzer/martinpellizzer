Salix egberti-wolfii

Salix egberti-wolfii, commonly known as the Egbert's willow, is a species of willow native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, often riparian habitats. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

Salix egberti-wolfii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing streambanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
