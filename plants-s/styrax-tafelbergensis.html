Styrax tafelbergensis

Styrax tafelbergensis, commonly known as the Table Mountain sweetgum, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its unique, star-shaped fruit that splits open to reveal seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky, mountainous regions.

Styrax tafelbergensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
