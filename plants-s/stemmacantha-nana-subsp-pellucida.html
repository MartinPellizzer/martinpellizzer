Stemmacantha nana subsp. pellucida

Stemmacantha nana subsp.

pellucida, commonly known as the translucent dwarf stemmacantha, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems that are often covered in a waxy, translucent coating.

The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a soft, velvety texture and a pale green coloration. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits.

This species is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments and is often found in humid forest understories.
