Swertia rex

Swertia rex, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Swertia rex is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
