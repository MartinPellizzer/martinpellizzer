Sebastiania corniculata var. occidentalis

Sebastiania corniculata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western flame tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
