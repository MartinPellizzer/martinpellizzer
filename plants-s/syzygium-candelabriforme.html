Syzygium candelabriforme

Syzygium candelabriforme, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, umbrella-like canopy of broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spherical berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
