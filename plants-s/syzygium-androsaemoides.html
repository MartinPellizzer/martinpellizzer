Syzygium androsaemoides

Syzygium androsaemoides, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is often found growing in humid, shaded environments, typically in lowland rainforests.
