Schnella longiseta

Schnella longiseta, commonly known as the long-stalked snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with prominent veins.

Schnella longiseta is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots.
