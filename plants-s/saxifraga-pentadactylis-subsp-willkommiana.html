Saxifraga pentadactylis subsp. willkommiana

Saxifraga pentadactylis subsp.

willkommiana, commonly known as the Willkomm's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are typically covered with a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
