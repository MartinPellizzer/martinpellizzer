Senna polyphylla

Senna polyphylla, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Senna polyphylla is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
