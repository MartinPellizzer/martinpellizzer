Salvia proxima

Salvia proxima, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia proxima typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal applications.
