Scutellaria galericulata f. angustifolia

Scutellaria galericulata f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or as a border plant.
