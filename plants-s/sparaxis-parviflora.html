Sparaxis parviflora

Sparaxis parviflora, commonly known as the lesser spearthorn, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Sparaxis parviflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
