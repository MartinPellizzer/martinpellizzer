Saxifraga hieraciifolia var. typica

Saxifraga hieraciifolia var. typica, commonly known as the mossy saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in dense, moss-like foliage. Its small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
