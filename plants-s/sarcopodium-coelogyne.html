Sarcopodium coelogyne

Sarcopodium coelogyne, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Sarcopodium coelogyne is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
