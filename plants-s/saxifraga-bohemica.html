Saxifraga bohemica

Saxifraga bohemica, commonly known as Bohemian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga bohemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
