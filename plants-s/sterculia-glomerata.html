Sterculia glomerata

Sterculia glomerata, commonly known as the African mahogany or kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces a hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Its fruit is a capsule containing several large, starchy seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
