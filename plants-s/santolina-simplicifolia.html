Santolina simplicifolia

Santolina simplicifolia, commonly known as Mediterranean santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with fine, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on slender stalks. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina simplicifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
