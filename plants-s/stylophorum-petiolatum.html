Stylophorum petiolatum

Stylophorum petiolatum, commonly known as the common white violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by showy flowers and often toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a short, thick rhizome and fibrous roots that help it anchor in rich, organic soil.
