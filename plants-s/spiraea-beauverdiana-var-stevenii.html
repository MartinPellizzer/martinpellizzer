Spiraea beauverdiana var. stevenii

Spiraea beauverdiana var. stevenii, commonly known as Steven's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, turning a striking yellow in the fall. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal color and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
