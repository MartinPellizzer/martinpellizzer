Saccolabium camptocentrum

Saccolabium camptocentrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
