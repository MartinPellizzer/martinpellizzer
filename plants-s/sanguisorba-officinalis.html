Sanguisorba officinalis

Sanguisorba officinalis, commonly known as the bloodwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Sanguisorba officinalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.
