Sesbania benthamiana var. campylocarpa

Sesbania benthamiana var. campylocarpa, commonly known as the campylocarp sesbania, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that are typically curved or twisted. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields. It has a short life cycle, often completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
