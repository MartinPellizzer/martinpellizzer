Stachys alpina var. turkestanica

Stachys alpina var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
