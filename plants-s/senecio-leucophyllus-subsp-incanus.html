Senecio leucophyllus subsp. incanus

Senecio leucophyllus subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
