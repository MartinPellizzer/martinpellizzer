Selaginella kochii

Selaginella kochii, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a rosette of spirally arranged leaves and a short, upright stem that bears spore-bearing structures called strobila. Selaginella kochii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.
