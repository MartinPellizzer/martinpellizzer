Sulettaria linearicrista

Sulettaria linearicrista, commonly known as the linear-ribbed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower spike that bears small, pale green to white orchid-like blooms. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Sulettaria linearicrista is considered endangered and is often found in specialized botanical gardens.
