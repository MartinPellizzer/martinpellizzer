Syzygium bharathii

Syzygium bharathii, commonly known as the Nilgiri plum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, particularly found in the Nilgiri and Anamalai hills. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the winter season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in evergreen forests.

Syzygium bharathii is valued for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
