Scirpus corymbosus var. junciformis

Scirpus corymbosus var. junciformis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the top of its stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

This plant thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
