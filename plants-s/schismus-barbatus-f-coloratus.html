Schismus barbatus f. coloratus

Schismus barbatus f. coloratus, commonly known as the colored spiny grass, is a variant of the spiny grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows in low, compact clusters, with slender, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This ornamental grass is often cultivated for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
