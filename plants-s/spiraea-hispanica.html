Spiraea hispanica

Spiraea hispanica, commonly known as Spanish spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dense, rounded clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a serrated margin, and are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
