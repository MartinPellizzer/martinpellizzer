Senecio spathulifolius var. ligulatus

Senecio spathulifolius var. ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, spoon-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, disc-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
