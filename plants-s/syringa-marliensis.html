Syringa marliensis

Syringa marliensis, commonly known as the Marliens lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Syringa marliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral displays.
