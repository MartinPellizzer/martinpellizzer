Struthanthus tetraqueter

Struthanthus tetraqueter, commonly known as the four-lobed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, four-petaled flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to arid environments and has a short growing season, blooming from spring to early summer.
