Scirpus appendiculatus

Scirpus appendiculatus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Scirpus appendiculatus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
