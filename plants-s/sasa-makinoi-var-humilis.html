Sasa makinoi var. humilis

Sasa makinoi var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a low-growing, clump-forming plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that rarely exceed 30 cm in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
