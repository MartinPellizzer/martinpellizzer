Salsola yazdiana

Salsola yazdiana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Salsola yazdiana is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to survive in extreme drought conditions.
