Solanum chomatophilum

Solanum chomatophilum, commonly known as the "chocolate berry" or "chocolate plant," is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small, dark purple berry, is edible and has a sweet, chocolate-like flavor.

Solanum chomatophilum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and unique fruit.
