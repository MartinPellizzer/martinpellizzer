Sempervivum calyciforme var. spurium

Sempervivum calyciforme var. spurium, commonly known as the "Himalayan houseleek," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
