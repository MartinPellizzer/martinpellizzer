Stipa speciosa var. manqueclensis

Stipa speciosa var. manqueclensis, commonly known as the Manqueclens golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of golden to bronze-colored florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-gray sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
