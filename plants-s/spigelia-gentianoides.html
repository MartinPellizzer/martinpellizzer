Spigelia gentianoides

Spigelia gentianoides, commonly known as the redroot or scarletroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Spigelia gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
