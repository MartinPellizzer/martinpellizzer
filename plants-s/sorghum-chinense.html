Sorghum chinense

Sorghum chinense, commonly known as Chinese sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it monoecious. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and ecological significance in its native habitat.
