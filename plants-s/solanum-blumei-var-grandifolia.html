Solanum blumei var. grandifolia

Solanum blumei var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming in the evening. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that mature in the fall, containing the seeds.

This plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important crops and ornamental species.
