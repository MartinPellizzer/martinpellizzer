Saussurea congesta

Saussurea congesta, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Saussurea congesta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
