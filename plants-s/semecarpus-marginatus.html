Semecarpus marginatus

Semecarpus marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental features and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
