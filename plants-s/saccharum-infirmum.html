Saccharum infirmum

Saccharum infirmum, commonly known as the weak cane or dwarf sugarcane, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is often found in wetlands or areas with moist soil. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, white or pale green flowers.

Saccharum infirmum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
