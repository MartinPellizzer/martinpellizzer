Scabiosa succisa

Scabiosa succisa, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink, purple, or white flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Scabiosa succisa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
