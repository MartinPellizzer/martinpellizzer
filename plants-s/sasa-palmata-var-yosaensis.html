Sasa palmata var. yosaensis

Sasa palmata var. yosaensis, commonly known as the Yosa bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa palmata species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and stout, clustered stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
