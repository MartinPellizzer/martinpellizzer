Stevia puberula

Stevia puberula, commonly known as the woolly stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides. Stevia puberula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and potential as a natural sweetener have made it increasingly popular in horticulture and herbal medicine.
