Syzygium synaptoneurum

Syzygium synaptoneurum, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruits that contain a sweet, white, edible pulp. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is valued not only for its unique fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
