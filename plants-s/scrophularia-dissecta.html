Scrophularia dissecta

Scrophularia dissecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. Its flowers are typically tubular, with two lips, and are usually pale purple or white in color.

Scrophularia dissecta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
