Salacca zalacca

Salacca zalacca, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers at the base of its leaves, which eventually develop into edible fruits. The fruits are typically consumed fresh or processed into various products.

Botanically, Salacca zalacca is valued for its economic and ecological contributions in its native habitats.
