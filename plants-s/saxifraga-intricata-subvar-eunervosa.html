Saxifraga intricata subvar. eunervosa

Saxifraga intricata subvar. eunervosa, commonly known as the intricate saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
