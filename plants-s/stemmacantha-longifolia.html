Stemmacantha longifolia

Stemmacantha longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Stemmacantha longifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
