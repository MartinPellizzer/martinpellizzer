Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. tenuipes

Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and moist meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive whitish or grayish underside.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Symphyotrichum lateriflorum.
