Senecio akrabatensis

Senecio akrabatensis, commonly known as the Akrabat's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
