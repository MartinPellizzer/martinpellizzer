Saussurea tridactyla subsp. maiduoganla

Saussurea tridactyla subsp.

maiduoganla, commonly known as the Maiduoganla safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Tibetan and Chinese medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and ecological niche within alpine ecosystems.
