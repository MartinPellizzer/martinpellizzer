Stisseria conspurcata

Stisseria conspurcata, commonly known as the spotted stisseria, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with distinct purplish spots and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in dense forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Stisseria conspurcata is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
