Solanum bitterianum

Solanum bitterianum, commonly known as the bitter apple or bitter tomato, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a shrub-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically bitter and not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Solanum bitterianum is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which contribute to its distinctive botanical appearance.
