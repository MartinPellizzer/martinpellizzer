Sinapis arvensis var. dasycarpa

Sinapis arvensis var. dasycarpa, commonly known as hairy field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods that are densely hairy, giving the variety its name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in various habitats.
