Staphylea bumalda var. microphylla

Staphylea bumalda var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf sweet flag, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
