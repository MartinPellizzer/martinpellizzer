Salix × hungarica

Salix × hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are essential for its pollination process.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.
