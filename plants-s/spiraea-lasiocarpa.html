Spiraea lasiocarpa

Spiraea lasiocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough, hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
