Silene pseudovelutina

Silene pseudovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Silene pseudovelutina is typically found in regions with low to moderate rainfall and well-drained soils.
