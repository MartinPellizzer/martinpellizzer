Syringa vulgaris plena

Syringa vulgaris plena, commonly known as the double shrub lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and is well-suited for garden borders or as a specimen plant.

Syringa vulgaris plena is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
