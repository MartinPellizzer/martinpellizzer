Scutellaria rupestris subsp. cephalonica

Scutellaria rupestris subsp.

cephalonica, commonly known as the Greek skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming low mats. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
