Saussurea chenopodiifolia

Saussurea chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the desert gentian, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, branched stems and narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and dry gardens.
