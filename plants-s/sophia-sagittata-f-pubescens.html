Sophia sagittata f. pubescens

Sophia sagittata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its hairy foliage.

This fern-like plant features delicate, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. The plant's botanical structure includes rhizomatous growth and compound leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to humid conditions.
