Sorbus tianschanica

Sorbus tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
