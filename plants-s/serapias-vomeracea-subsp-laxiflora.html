Serapias vomeracea subsp. laxiflora

Serapias vomeracea subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its relatively lax, spreading inflorescence compared to the more upright flowers of the typical form. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This plant thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.
