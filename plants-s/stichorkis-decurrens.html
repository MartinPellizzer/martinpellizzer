Stichorkis decurrens

Stichorkis decurrens, commonly known as the creeping thornapple, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a trailing or ascending growth habit with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Stichorkis decurrens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent stems and leaves.
