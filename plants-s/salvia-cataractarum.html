Salvia cataractarum

Salvia cataractarum, commonly known as the weeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of violet-blue flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia cataractarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
