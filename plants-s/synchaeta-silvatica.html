Synchaeta silvatica

Synchaeta silvatica, commonly known as the forest thread moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It has delicate, elongated leaves that are often curved and covered in a fine layer of tiny, translucent cells. The plant produces small, cylindrical spore capsules that are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in forest ecosystems.

Synchaeta silvatica is often found in temperate and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with dense tree cover.
