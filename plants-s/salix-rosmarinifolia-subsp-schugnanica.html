Salix rosmarinifolia subsp. schugnanica

Salix rosmarinifolia subsp.

schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnanica willow, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath, with a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
