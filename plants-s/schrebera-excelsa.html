Schrebera excelsa

Schrebera excelsa, commonly known as the giant schrebera, is a striking tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Schrebera excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
