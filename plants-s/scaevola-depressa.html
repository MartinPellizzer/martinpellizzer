Scaevola depressa

Scaevola depressa, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Scaevola depressa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
