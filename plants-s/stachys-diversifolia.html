Stachys diversifolia

Stachys diversifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys diversifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
