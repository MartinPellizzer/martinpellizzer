Sinapis pubescens var. cyrenaica

Sinapis pubescens var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional herbal medicine.
