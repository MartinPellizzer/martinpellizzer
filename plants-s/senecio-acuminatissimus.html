Senecio acuminatissimus

Senecio acuminatissimus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its foliage is often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
