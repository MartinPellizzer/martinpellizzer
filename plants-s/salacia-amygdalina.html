Salacia amygdalina

Salacia amygdalina, commonly known as the African almond tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its ornamental leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and thrives in well-drained soils.
