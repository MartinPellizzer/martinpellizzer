Smilacina uniflora

Smilacina uniflora, commonly known as greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Smilacina uniflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
