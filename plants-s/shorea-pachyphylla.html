Shorea pachyphylla

Shorea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates. It plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also threatened by deforestation and habitat loss.
