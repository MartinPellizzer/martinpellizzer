Sticherus monticola

Sticherus monticola, commonly known as the mountain sticherus, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant produces spore capsules that are typically reddish-brown in color and are borne on short, lateral branches. It is commonly found in rocky soils and on tree trunks in temperate regions.

Sticherus monticola plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
