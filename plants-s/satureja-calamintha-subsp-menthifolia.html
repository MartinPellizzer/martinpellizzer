Satureja calamintha subsp. menthifolia

Satureja calamintha subsp.

menthifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
