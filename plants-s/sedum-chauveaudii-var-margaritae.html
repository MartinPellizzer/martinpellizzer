Sedum chauveaudii var. margaritae

Sedum chauveaudii var. margaritae, commonly known as the White-Flowered Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
