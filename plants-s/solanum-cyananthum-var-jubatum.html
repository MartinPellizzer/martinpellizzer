Solanum cyananthum var. jubatum

Solanum cyananthum var. jubatum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves with prominent veins, and its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
