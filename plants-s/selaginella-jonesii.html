Selaginella jonesii

Selaginella jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle, including a distinct spore-producing phase known as the sporophyte. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a short, upright stem with whorled leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped.

Selaginella jonesii is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in ecological succession in woodland ecosystems.
