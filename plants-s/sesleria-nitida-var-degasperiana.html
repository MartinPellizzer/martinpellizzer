Sesleria nitida var. degasperiana

Sesleria nitida var. degasperiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like blades that are typically green, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats in shaded environments.
