Satureja glabella var. angustifolia

Satureja glabella var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional dishes.
