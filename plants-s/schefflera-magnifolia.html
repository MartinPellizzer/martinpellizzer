Schefflera magnifolia

Schefflera magnifolia, commonly known as the giant umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit with a single trunk and multiple branches. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries.

Schefflera magnifolia is often cultivated as an indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various lighting conditions.
