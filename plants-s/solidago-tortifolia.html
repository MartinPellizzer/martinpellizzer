Solidago tortifolia

Solidago tortifolia, commonly known as rough-leaved goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are rough and serrated, giving it its common name, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Solidago tortifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
