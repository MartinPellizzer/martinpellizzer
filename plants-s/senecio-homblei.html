Senecio homblei

Senecio homblei, commonly known as the Hombli's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features upright, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Senecio homblei is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
