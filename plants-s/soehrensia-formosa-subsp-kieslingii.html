Soehrensia formosa subsp. kieslingii

Soehrensia formosa subsp.

kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
