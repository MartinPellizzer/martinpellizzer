Soldanella rugosa

Soldanella rugosa, commonly known as the rough soldanella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
