Simplicia laxa

Simplicia laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale green. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its mild antiseptic properties.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.
