Smilax ripogonum

Smilax ripogonum, commonly known as the climbing smilax, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which can climb or trail along the ground, and its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax ripogonum is known for its aggressive growth habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
