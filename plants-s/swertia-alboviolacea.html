Swertia alboviolacea

Swertia alboviolacea, commonly known as the white violet gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

Swertia alboviolacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
