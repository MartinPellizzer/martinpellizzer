Salsola exasperata

Salsola exasperata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and drought-resistant properties. It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed habitats.

Salsola exasperata reproduces through seeds that are easily dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
