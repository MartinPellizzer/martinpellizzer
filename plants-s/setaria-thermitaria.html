Setaria thermitaria

Setaria thermitaria, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Setaria thermitaria is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its rapid growth and nutritional value.
