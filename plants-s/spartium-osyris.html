Spartium osyris

Spartium osyris, commonly known as redhead grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in small, leathery pods, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Spartium osyris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
