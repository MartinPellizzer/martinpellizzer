Satureja myriantha var. wellmanii

Satureja myriantha var. wellmanii, commonly known as wellman's savory, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. The plant features small, fragrant leaves with a mint-like aroma, and it produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of the genus.
