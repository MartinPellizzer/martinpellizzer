Syzygium armstrongii

Syzygium armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, elongated pod that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Syzygium armstrongii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
