Sasaella hisauchii

Sasaella hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It forms thick mats of foliage and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Sasaella hisauchii is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its resilience to humid conditions.
