Suaeda maritima var. bacciformis

Suaeda maritima var. bacciformis, commonly known as sea spurge or saltwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding survival in arid coastal regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind or water.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
