Stylisma tenella

Stylisma tenella, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Stylisma tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
