Sporobolus filipes

Sporobolus filipes, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical seed heads that resemble bottle brushes, which are typically pink to red in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and can grow in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
