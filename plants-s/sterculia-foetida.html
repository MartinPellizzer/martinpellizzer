Sterculia foetida

Sterculia foetida, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in various indigenous communities.
