Salvia aridicola

Salvia aridicola, commonly known as the desert sage, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and thick, waxy foliage. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia aridicola is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
