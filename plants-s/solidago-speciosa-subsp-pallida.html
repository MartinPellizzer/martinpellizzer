Solidago speciosa subsp. pallida

Solidago speciosa subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces a distinctive, sparsely hairy flower head.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
