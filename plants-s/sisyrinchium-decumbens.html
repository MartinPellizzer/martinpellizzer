Sisyrinchium decumbens

Sisyrinchium decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, spreading stems that lie close to the ground, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Sisyrinchium decumbens is a member of the Iridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like foliage and delicate floral structure.
