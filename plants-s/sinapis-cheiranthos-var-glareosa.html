Sinapis cheiranthos var. glareosa

Sinapis cheiranthos var. glareosa, commonly known as the glabrous mustard, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are siliques, which contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
