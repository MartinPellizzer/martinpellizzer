Solanum meizonanthum

Solanum meizonanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed.

Solanum meizonanthum is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
