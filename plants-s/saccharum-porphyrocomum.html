Saccharum porphyrocomum

Saccharum porphyrocomum, commonly known as the crimson cane grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking purple or red inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other sugarcane varieties. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a fibrous structure, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and requires a warm climate with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for both ornamental and agricultural study.
