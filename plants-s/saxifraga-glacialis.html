Saxifraga glacialis

Saxifraga glacialis, commonly known as the glacier saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky and gravelly habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the short growing season. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Saxifraga glacialis is a hardy species that thrives in cold climates with limited soil.
