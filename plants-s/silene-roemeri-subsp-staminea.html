Silene roemeri subsp. staminea

Silene roemeri subsp.

staminea, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced stamen number and unique floral structure compared to other Silene species.
