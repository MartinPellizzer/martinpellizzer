Sedum hillebrandii

Sedum hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Sedum hillebrandii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
