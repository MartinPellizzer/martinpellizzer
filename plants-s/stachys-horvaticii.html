Stachys horvaticii

Stachys horvaticii, commonly known as the Horvath's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Stachys horvaticii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
