Senecio icoglossus

Senecio icoglossus, commonly known as the Bluebell Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio icoglossus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
