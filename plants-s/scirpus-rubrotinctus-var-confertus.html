Scirpus rubrotinctus var. confertus

Scirpus rubrotinctus var. confertus, commonly known as the red-stained bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that often display a reddish tinge, especially during the flowering season. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and avian life.
