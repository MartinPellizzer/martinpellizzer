Salvia mayorii

Salvia mayorii, commonly known as the Chinese sage or mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia mayorii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in herbal practices.
