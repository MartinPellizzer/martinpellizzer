Swertia tashiroi f. plena

Swertia tashiroi f. plena, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
