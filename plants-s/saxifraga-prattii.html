Saxifraga prattii

Saxifraga prattii, commonly known as the Pratt's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, often succulent leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga prattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
