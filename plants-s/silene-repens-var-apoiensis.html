Silene repens var. apoiensis

Silene repens var. apoiensis, commonly known as the Apoiensis dogbane, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, sprawling stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene repens var. apoiensis is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various invertebrates.
