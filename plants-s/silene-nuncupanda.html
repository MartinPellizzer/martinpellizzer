Silene nuncupanda

Silene nuncupanda, commonly known as the "crying plant," is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a velvety texture.

Silene nuncupanda is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
