Sideritis endressii subsp. littoralis

Sideritis endressii subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal sideritis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
