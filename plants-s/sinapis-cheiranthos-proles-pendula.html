Sinapis cheiranthos proles pendula

Sinapis cheiranthos proles pendula, commonly known as the hanging wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous racemes of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a cut flower.
