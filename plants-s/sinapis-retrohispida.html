Sinapis retrohispida

Sinapis retrohispida, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known members like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Sinapis retrohispida is an annual herb that produces small, round pods containing several seeds.
