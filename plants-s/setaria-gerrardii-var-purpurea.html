Setaria gerrardii var. purpurea

Setaria gerrardii var. purpurea, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
