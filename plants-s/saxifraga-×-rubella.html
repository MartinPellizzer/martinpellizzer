Saxifraga × rubella

Saxifraga × rubella, commonly known as the red saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga cespitosa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or Arctic environments. It is well adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing structure that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden settings.
