Syngonanthus capillaceus

Syngonanthus capillaceus, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is adapted to survive in sandy soils with low moisture availability.

Syngonanthus capillaceus is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.
