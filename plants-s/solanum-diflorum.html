Solanum diflorum

Solanum diflorum, commonly known as the two-flower nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
