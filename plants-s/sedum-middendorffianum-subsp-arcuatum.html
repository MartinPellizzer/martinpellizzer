Sedum middendorffianum subsp. arcuatum

Sedum middendorffianum subsp.

arcuatum, commonly known as the arcuate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments in Central Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a low-growing, mat-like structure.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
