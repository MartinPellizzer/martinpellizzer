Scrophularia borealikoreana

Scrophularia borealikoreana, commonly known as the northern scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Scrophularia borealikoreana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
