Solanum tribulosum

Solanum tribulosum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum tribulosum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
