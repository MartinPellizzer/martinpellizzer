Silene williamsii

Silene williamsii, commonly known as Williams' catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.
