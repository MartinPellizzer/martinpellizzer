Solanum dulcamara var. albiflorum

Solanum dulcamara var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower bindweed, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and showy, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, green stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its fruits are bright red berries that mature in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
