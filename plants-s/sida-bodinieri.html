Sida bodinieri

Sida bodinieri, commonly known as the Madagascar mallow, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Sida bodinieri is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
