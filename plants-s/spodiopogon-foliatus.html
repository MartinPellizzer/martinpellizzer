Spodiopogon foliatus

Spodiopogon foliatus, commonly known as the leafy spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in eastern North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the top of the stems, which are typically greenish or brownish in hue. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Spodiopogon foliatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
