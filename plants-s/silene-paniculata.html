Silene paniculata

Silene paniculata, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene paniculata thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
