Sedum amplexicaule var. uncatum

Sedum amplexicaule var. uncatum, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems that bear opposite, spoon-shaped leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and drought conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
