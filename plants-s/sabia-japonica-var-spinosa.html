Sabia japonica var. spinosa

Sabia japonica var. spinosa, commonly known as Japanese spiky sabia, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features upright stems with spiny projections, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
