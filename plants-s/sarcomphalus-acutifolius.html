Sarcomphalus acutifolius

Sarcomphalus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sarcomphalus acutifolius is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to flourish in challenging conditions.
