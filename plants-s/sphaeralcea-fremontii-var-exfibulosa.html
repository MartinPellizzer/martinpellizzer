Sphaeralcea fremontii var. exfibulosa

Sphaeralcea fremontii var. exfibulosa, commonly known as the Fremont's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, forming dense mats or small shrubs.
