Sorbus aria subsp. cyclophylla

Sorbus aria subsp.

cyclophylla, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries in the autumn.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. This subspecies is known for its ornamental value, especially in gardens, due to its attractive foliage and fruiting display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
