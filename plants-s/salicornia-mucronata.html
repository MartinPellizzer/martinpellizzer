Salicornia mucronata

Salicornia mucronata, commonly known as sea beans or glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are reduced to scales, and it thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries.

Salicornia mucronata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
