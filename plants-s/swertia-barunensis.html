Swertia barunensis

Swertia barunensis, commonly known as the Barun gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia barunensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
