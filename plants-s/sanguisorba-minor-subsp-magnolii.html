Sanguisorba minor subsp. magnolii

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

magnolii, commonly known as the dwarf sheep's sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a reddish tinge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
