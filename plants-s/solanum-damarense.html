Solanum damarense

Solanum damarense, commonly known as the Damara tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that resemble tomatoes. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with purple markings, and it produces berries that are edible when ripe.

Solanum damarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
