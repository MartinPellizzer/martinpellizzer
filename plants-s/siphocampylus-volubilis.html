Siphocampylus volubilis

Siphocampylus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, blooming in summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Siphocampylus volubilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
