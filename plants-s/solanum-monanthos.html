Solanum monanthos

Solanum monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces flowers that are predominantly white or pale green. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of solanine.

Solanum monanthos is often found in arid regions and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
