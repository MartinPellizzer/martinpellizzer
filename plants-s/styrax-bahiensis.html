Styrax bahiensis

Styrax bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and perfume-making. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Styrax bahiensis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
