Sedum telephium var. erubescens

Sedum telephium var. erubescens, commonly known as Crimson Stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
