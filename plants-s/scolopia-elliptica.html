Scolopia elliptica

Scolopia elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in the spring. Scolopia elliptica is often found in moist, shady woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
