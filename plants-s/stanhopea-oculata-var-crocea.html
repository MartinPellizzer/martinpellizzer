Stanhopea oculata var. crocea

Stanhopea oculata var. crocea, commonly known as the crocea stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
