Smilax tamnoides

Smilax tamnoides, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny projections and is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax tamnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
