Saussurea hypsipeta

Saussurea hypsipeta, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower or Himalayan safflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid, rocky environments and is adapted to cold and high-altitude conditions. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Tibetan medicine, where it is used for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in alpine ecosystems.
