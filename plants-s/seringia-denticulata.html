Seringia denticulata

Seringia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed seringia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins, giving it its common name. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color.

Seringia denticulata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
