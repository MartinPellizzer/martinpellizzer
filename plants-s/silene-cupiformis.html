Silene cupiformis

Silene cupiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small pink or white flowers arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Silene cupiformis is an annual or biennial species, flowering from late spring to early autumn.
