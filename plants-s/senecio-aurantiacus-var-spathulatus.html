Senecio aurantiacus var. spathulatus

Senecio aurantiacus var. spathulatus, commonly known as the orange coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a conical arrangement of bracts that surround the flower head. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
