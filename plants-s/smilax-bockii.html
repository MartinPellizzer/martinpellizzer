Smilax bockii

Smilax bockii, commonly known as the Bock's greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with spiny margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Smilax bockii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
