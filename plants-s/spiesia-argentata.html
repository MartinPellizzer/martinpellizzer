Spiesia argentata

Spiesia argentata, commonly known as silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, waxy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
