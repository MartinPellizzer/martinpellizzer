Siparuna cujabana var. plebeia

Siparuna cujabana var. plebeia, commonly known as the red-flowered siparuna, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its striking red bracts that surround its small white flowers. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a dense canopy in its natural habitat.

Siparuna cujabana var. plebeia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
