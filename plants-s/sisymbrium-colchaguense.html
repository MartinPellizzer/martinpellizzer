Sisymbrium colchaguense

Sisymbrium colchaguense, commonly known as the Chilean shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
