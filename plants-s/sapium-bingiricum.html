Sapium bingiricum

Sapium bingiricum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red berries containing three seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The tree has a shallow root system and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.
