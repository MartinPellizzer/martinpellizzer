Solidago chrysantha

Solidago chrysantha, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering heads are arranged in clusters.

Solidago chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
