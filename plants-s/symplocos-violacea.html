Symplocos violacea

Symplocos violacea, commonly known as the purpleleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple leaves, which are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins.

Symplocos violacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
