Septas capensis var. promontorii

Septas capensis var. promontorii, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the winter months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in local biodiversity.
