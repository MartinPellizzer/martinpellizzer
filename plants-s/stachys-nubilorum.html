Stachys nubilorum

Stachys nubilorum, commonly known as cloud violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Stachys nubilorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
