Smilax pottingeri

Smilax pottingeri, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Smilax pottingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
