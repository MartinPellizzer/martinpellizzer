Stachys malacophylla

Stachys malacophylla, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Stachys malacophylla is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
