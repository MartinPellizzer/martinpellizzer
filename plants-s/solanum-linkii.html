Solanum linkii

Solanum linkii, commonly known as the Link's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Solanum linkii is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
