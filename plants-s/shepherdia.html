Shepherdia

Shepherdia, commonly known as autumn sage, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

It includes several species, such as Shepherdia argentea and Shepherdia humifusa, which are native to North America. These plants typically have woody stems and are characterized by their yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the fall. The leaves are usually alternate and may be elliptic or ovate in shape.

Shepherdia species are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
