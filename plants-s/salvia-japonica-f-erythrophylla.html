Salvia japonica f. erythrophylla

Salvia japonica f. erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered Japanese sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
