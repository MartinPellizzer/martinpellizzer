Samanea dinklagei

Samanea dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's legume, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Samanea dinklagei is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a potential source of forage for livestock.
