Syringa vulgaris var. emodi

Syringa vulgaris var. emodi, commonly known as the Tibetan lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and a preference for well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
