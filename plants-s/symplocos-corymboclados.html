Symplocos corymboclados

Symplocos corymboclados, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by clusters of vibrant purple berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, reddish-bark texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.
