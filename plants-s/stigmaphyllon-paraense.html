Stigmaphyllon paraense

Stigmaphyllon paraense, commonly known as the Brazilian starleaf, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a strong, unpleasant odor. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous small seeds.

Stigmaphyllon paraense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
