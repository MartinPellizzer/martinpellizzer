Scleria bulbifera var. pallidiflora

Scleria bulbifera var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bulb-plant, is a species of sedge native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of pale, slender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bulb-like structures at the base of its stems, which can give rise to new plants, a feature that aids in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
