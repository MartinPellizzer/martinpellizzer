Syzygium leptoneurum

Syzygium leptoneurum, commonly known as the slender-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour fruits. Its fruit pulp is used in traditional cuisines and has medicinal properties.

Syzygium leptoneurum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in tropical forests.
