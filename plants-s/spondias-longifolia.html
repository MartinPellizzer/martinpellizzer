Spondias longifolia

Spondias longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf spondias, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and turn red when ripe, often containing a single seed.
