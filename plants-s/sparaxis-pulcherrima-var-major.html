Sparaxis pulcherrima var. major

Sparaxis pulcherrima var. major, commonly known as the "Rainbow Sparaxis," is a striking South African bulbous plant characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa. The plant produces showy, brightly colored blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that die back after flowering, giving way to new growth in the following season.
