Senecio pygmaeus

Senecio pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf groundsels, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
