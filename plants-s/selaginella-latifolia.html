Selaginella latifolia

Selaginella latifolia, commonly known as the spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and clusters of small, needle-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are often found at the tips of its branches.

Selaginella latifolia is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
