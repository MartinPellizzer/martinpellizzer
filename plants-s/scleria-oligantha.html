Scleria oligantha

Scleria oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are usually white or pale blue. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scleria oligantha is also an important plant for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
