Solanum diplocincinnum

Solanum diplocincinnum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in the leaf axils.

The fruit matures to a deep purple color, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
