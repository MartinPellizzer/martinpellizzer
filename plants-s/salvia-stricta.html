Salvia stricta

Salvia stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, slightly hairy texture.

Salvia stricta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
