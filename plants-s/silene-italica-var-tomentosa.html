Silene italica var. tomentosa

Silene italica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Native to southern Europe, this species thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
