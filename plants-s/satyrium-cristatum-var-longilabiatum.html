Satyrium cristatum var. longilabiatum

Satyrium cristatum var. longilabiatum, commonly known as the long-labied marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and is often found in association with other wetland flora. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
