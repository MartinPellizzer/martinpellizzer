Spiraea lasiocarpa var. villosa

Spiraea lasiocarpa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
