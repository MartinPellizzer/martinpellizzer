Sisymbrium hartwegianum

Sisymbrium hartwegianum, commonly known as Hartwick's tansy, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pods that split open to release its seeds. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
