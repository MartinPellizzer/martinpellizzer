Scrophularia ebulifolia subsp. schmitzii

Scrophularia ebulifolia subsp.

schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is generally low-growing, with a compact habit that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
