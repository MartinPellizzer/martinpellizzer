Spigelia laurina

Spigelia laurina, commonly known as the redroot sandroot, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes plants known for their milky sap and often ornamental characteristics. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Spigelia laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
