Solidago nemoralis subsp. haleana

Solidago nemoralis subsp.

haleana, commonly known as the Hale's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are bright yellow, clustered in terminal panicles, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
