Saussurea nupuripoensis

Saussurea nupuripoensis, commonly known as the Nupuripo snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, yellowish-white flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cold and arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
