Senecio helenitis subsp. candidus

Senecio helenitis subsp.

candidus, commonly known as the white-flowered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine white coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
