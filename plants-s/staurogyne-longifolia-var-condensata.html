Staurogyne longifolia var. condensata

Staurogyne longifolia var. condensata, commonly known as the dense-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branched stems that often form dense mats in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The leaves are opposite, linear, and often crowded, contributing to its dense growth habit. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface.

This plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
