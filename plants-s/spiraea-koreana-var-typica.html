Spiraea koreana var. typica

Spiraea koreana var. typica, commonly known as Korean weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its graceful appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
