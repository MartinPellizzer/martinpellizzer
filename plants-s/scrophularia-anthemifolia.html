Scrophularia anthemifolia

Scrophularia anthemifolia, commonly known as the meadow scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are usually arranged in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Scrophularia anthemifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value in traditional herbal practices.
