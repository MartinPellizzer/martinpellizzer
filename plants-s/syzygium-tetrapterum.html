Syzygium tetrapterum

Syzygium tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, four-winged fruits that resemble small, elongated pods. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its unique fruiting bodies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
