Spondias purpurea var. venulosa

Spondias purpurea var. venulosa, commonly known as the red mombin or purple mombin, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features include a dioecious habit, where separate male and female plants are required for fruiting, and it thrives in well-drained soils in warm climates.
