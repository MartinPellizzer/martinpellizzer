Salacia tuberculata

Salacia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves with a rough texture, and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
