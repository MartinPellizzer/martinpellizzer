Scirpus campestris var. paludosus

Scirpus campestris var. paludosus, commonly known as the swamp bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a fibrous, spongy texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in muddy or saturated soils. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize wetland areas effectively.

Scirpus campestris var. paludosus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and wetland organisms.
