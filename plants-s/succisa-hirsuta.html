Succisa hirsuta

Succisa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cushion plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid climates.
