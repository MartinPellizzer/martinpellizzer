Sasa moderabilis

Sasa moderabilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, erect culms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy texture.

Sasa moderabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
