Stephanotis longiflora

Stephanotis longiflora, commonly known as the sweet olive, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling jasmine. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
