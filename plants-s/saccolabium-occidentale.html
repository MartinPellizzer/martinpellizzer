Saccolabium occidentale

Saccolabium occidentale, commonly known as the western saccolabium, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod, which aids in dispersal by wind or animals.

Saccolabium occidentale plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
