Salvia pentstemonoides

Salvia pentstemonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
