Silene saxifraga subsp. balcanica

Silene saxifraga subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
