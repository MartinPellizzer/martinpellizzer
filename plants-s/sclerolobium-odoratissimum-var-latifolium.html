Sclerolobium odoratissimum var. latifolium

Sclerolobium odoratissimum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf silky oak, is a species native to northern Australia.

It is a large, deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
