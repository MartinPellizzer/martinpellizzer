Sindora javanica

Sindora javanica, commonly known as the Java sindora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
