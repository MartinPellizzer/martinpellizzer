Scilla hemisphaerica var. glabra

Scilla hemisphaerica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Scilla. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize over time.
