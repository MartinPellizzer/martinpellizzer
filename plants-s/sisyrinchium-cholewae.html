Sisyrinchium cholewae

Sisyrinchium cholewae, commonly known as the Cholewae blue-eyed grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
