Senecio spathulatus var. latifructus

Senecio spathulatus var. latifructus, commonly known as the broad-fruited groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually broad and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
