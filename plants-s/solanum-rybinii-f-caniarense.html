Solanum rybinii f. caniarense

Solanum rybinii f. caniarense, commonly known as the Canary Islands nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

This species is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of the Canary Islands.
