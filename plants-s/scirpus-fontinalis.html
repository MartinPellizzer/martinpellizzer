Scirpus fontinalis

Scirpus fontinalis, commonly known as the spring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic environments.

Scirpus fontinalis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
