Scopolia kwangdokensis

Scopolia kwangdokensis, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scopolia kwangdokensis is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
