Saussurea pubifolia var. lhasaensis

Saussurea pubifolia var. lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, arid conditions. Its leaves are typically covered with fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
