Scutellaria oldhamii

Scutellaria oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Scutellaria oldhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
