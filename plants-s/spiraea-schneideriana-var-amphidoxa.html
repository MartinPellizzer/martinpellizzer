Spiraea schneideriana var. amphidoxa

Spiraea schneideriana var. amphidoxa, commonly known as the dwarf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, compact growth with small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its tidy form and seasonal color changes, from vibrant green in spring to fiery red in autumn.
