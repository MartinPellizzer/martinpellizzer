Salix incana

Salix incana, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, greenish female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or wetlands.

Salix incana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
