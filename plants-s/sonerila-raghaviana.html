Sonerila raghaviana

Sonerila raghaviana, commonly known as the Malabar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a distinct fragrance.

Sonerila raghaviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local biodiversity.
