Sophora robusta

Sophora robusta, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of blue or violet flowers, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds.

Sophora robusta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
