Scutellaria agrestis

Scutellaria agrestis, commonly known as field scullion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Scutellaria agrestis thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
