Sambucus cerulea var. neomexicana

Sambucus cerulea var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico elderberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
