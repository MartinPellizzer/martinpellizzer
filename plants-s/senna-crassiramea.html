Senna crassiramea

Senna crassiramea, commonly known as the broad-leaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats. Senna crassiramea is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in arid regions.
