Senecio buschianus

Senecio buschianus, commonly known as the Buschian's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the spring.

Senecio buschianus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
