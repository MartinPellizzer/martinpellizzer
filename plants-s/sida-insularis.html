Sida insularis

Sida insularis, commonly known as the Christmas morning flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture and a slight pubescence.

Sida insularis is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it a common species in beach environments.
