Senecio canus

Senecio canus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio canus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
