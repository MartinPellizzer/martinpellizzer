Systellantha serratifolia var. dolichophylla

Systellantha serratifolia var. dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf sys, is a variant of the serrated sys plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
