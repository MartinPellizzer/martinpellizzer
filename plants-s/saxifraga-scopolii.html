Saxifraga scopolii

Saxifraga scopolii, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are rounded or heart-shaped, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Saxifraga scopolii is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species in its native habitat.
