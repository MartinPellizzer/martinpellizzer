Sasaella siroyamensis

Sasaella siroyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features slender, arching stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green color and a slightly hairy surface. Sasaella siroyamensis is often found in shaded, moist forest environments, where it forms thick mats on the forest floor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to create a dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
