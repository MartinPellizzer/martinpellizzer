Sedum danjoense

Sedum danjoense, commonly known as the Danjo sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Sedum danjoense is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
