Sideritis × yvesiana

Sideritis × yvesiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
