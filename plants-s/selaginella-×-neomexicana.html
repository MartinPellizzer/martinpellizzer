Selaginella × neomexicana

Selaginella × neomexicana, commonly known as the Arizona clubmoss, is a hybrid species of the genus Selaginella.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves. Native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its succulent stems.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
