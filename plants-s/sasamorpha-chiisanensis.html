Sasamorpha chiisanensis

Sasamorpha chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.
