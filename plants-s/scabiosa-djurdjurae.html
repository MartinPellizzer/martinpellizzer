Scabiosa djurdjurae

Scabiosa djurdjurae, commonly known as the Afghan scabious, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, which are composed of many tiny, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Scabiosa djurdjurae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
