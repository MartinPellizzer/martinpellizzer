Samolus cuneatus

Samolus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf samolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds and streams. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Samolus cuneatus has a fibrous root system and is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich soils.
