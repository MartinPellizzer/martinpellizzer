Sapindus baccatus

Sapindus baccatus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The fruit's outer layer contains a soap-like substance, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
