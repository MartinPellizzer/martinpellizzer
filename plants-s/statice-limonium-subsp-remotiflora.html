Statice limonium subsp. remotiflora

Statice limonium subsp.

remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in clusters. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal cliffs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
