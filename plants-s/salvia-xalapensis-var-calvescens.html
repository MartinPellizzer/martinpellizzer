Salvia xalapensis var. calvescens

Salvia xalapensis var. calvescens, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
