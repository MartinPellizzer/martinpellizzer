Solanum paraguariense

Solanum paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are toxic and should not be consumed.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
