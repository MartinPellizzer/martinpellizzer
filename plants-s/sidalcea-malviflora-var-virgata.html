Sidalcea malviflora var. virgata

Sidalcea malviflora var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower or Western Meadow Beauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces fluffy, cotton-like seed pods.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in open habitats with well-drained soil.
