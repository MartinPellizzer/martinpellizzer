Scirpus setaceus subsp. gracillimus

Scirpus setaceus subsp.

gracillimus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are often taller than the leaves, and the flowers are small and clustered.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
