Sorbus hybrida f. fastigiata

Sorbus hybrida f. fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar rowan or spindle-shaped whitebeam, is a unique cultivar of the rowan tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit, making it ideal for small spaces or as a hedge. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, typical of the Sorbus genus.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
