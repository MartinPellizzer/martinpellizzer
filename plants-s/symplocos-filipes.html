Symplocos filipes

Symplocos filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Symplocos filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
