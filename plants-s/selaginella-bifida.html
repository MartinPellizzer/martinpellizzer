Selaginella bifida

Selaginella bifida, commonly known as the forked spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern ally that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and clustered, scale-like leaves. Its sporophylls are arranged in whorls and produce spores in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella bifida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
