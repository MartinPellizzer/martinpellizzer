Struthiola ericina

Struthiola ericina, commonly known as the common struthiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Struthiola ericina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
