Salvia szovitsiana

Salvia szovitsiana, commonly known as the Szovits sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Salvia szovitsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
