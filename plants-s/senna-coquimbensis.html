Senna coquimbensis

Senna coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo senna, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and coastal climates. It is often found in dunes and along the shores of Chile and Peru.

Senna coquimbensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
