Secale cereale subsp. tsitsinii

Secale cereale subsp.

tsitsinii, commonly known as Tsitsinii rye, is a subspecies of rye that is native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers. This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts due to its resilience and genetic diversity.
