Serapias mutata

Serapias mutata, commonly known as the variable orchid or the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and somewhat succulent, adapted to conserve water.

Serapias mutata is known for its variability in flower color and form, which contributes to its common name.
