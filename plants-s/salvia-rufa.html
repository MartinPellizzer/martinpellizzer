Salvia rufa

Salvia rufa, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular red or purplish-pink flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in appearance. Salvia rufa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
