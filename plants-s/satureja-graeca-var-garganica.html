Satureja graeca var. garganica

Satureja graeca var. garganica, commonly known as Greek thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their strong, pungent aroma.
