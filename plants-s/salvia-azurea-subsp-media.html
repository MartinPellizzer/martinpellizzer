Salvia azurea subsp. media

Salvia azurea subsp.

media, commonly known as the blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its lance-shaped leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
