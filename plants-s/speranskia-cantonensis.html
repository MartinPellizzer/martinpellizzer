Speranskia cantonensis

Speranskia cantonensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are opposite and alternate in arrangement. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
