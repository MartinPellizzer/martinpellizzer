Stevia lucida var. oaxacana

Stevia lucida var. oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.

Due to its sweet compounds, it has been used traditionally for its sweetening properties and medicinal uses.
