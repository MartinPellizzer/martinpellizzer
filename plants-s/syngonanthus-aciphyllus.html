Syngonanthus aciphyllus

Syngonanthus aciphyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves that are often obscured by its dense, fleshy berries. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in forest ecosystems.
