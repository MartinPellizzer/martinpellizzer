Senna viminea

Senna viminea, commonly known as the silver senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, bean-like pods containing seeds. Senna viminea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
