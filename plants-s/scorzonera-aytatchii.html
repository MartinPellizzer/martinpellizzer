Scorzonera aytatchii

Scorzonera aytatchii, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold climate to complete its life cycle.

Scorzonera aytatchii is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
