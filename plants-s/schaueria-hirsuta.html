Schaueria hirsuta

Schaueria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy schaueria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Schaueria hirsuta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to xeriscapes.
