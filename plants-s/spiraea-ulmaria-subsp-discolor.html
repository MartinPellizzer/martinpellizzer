Spiraea ulmaria subsp. discolor

Spiraea ulmaria subsp.

discolor, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
