Sambucus melanocarpa f. furstenbergii

Sambucus melanocarpa f. furstenbergii, commonly known as the black elderberry, is a subspecies of the elderberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are clustered in loose panicles. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–6 meters and features compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are used in culinary and medicinal applications, though the plant contains toxic compounds in its raw form.
