Salsola baccata

Salsola baccata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its prickly, succulent stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to Eurasia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in various regions.

Salsola baccata is also known for its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline conditions, enhancing its ecological resilience.
