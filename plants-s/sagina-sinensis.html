Sagina sinensis

Sagina sinensis, commonly known as lesser starwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically opposite and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Sagina sinensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ground cover in horticulture.
