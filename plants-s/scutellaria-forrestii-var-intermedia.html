Scutellaria forrestii var. intermedia

Scutellaria forrestii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
