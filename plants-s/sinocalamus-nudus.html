Sinocalamus nudus

Sinocalamus nudus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features hollow, jointed stems that are typically smooth and covered in a thin layer of white, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly waxy texture. Sinocalamus nudus is valued for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
