Symplocos reticulata

Symplocos reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, leathery berries in the fall. Symplocos reticulata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in horticulture and wildlife habitats.
