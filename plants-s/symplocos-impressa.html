Symplocos impressa

Symplocos impressa, commonly known as the roughleaf wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, raised dots. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Symplocos impressa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
