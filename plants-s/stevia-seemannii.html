Stevia seemannii

Stevia seemannii, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are highly aromatic and intensely sweet. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Stevia seemannii is renowned for its natural sweeteners, stevioside and rebaudioside A, which are used as sugar substitutes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
