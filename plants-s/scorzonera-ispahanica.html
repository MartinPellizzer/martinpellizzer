Scorzonera ispahanica

Scorzonera ispahanica, commonly known as Persian parsley or Persian cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It features a taproot that is often harvested for its edible, peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scorzonera ispahanica thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
