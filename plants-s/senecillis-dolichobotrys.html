Senecillis dolichobotrys

Senecillis dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stalked senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellowish to orange flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
