Senecio pseudalmeidae

Senecio pseudalmeidae, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
