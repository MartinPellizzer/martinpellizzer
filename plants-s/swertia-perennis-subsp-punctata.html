Swertia perennis subsp. punctata

Swertia perennis subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in spikes.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
