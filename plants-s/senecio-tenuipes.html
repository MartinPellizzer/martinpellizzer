Senecio tenuipes

Senecio tenuipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners interested in succulent plants.
