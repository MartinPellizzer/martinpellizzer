Solanum betaceum

Solanum betaceum, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, fleshy, yellow or red berries. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a waxy coating.

The fruit is edible, though the seeds are usually removed before consumption.
