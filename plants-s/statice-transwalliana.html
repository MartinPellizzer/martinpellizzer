Statice transwalliana

Statice transwalliana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and silver-gray, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Statice transwalliana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
