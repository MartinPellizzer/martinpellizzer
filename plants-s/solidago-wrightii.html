Solidago wrightii

Solidago wrightii, commonly known as Wright's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow blooms. This perennial herb typically grows in moist to dry soils in prairies, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its showy flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
