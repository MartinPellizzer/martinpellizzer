Salvia verbenaca subsp. claudi

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

claudi, commonly known as Claudia sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant choice for gardeners in warm climates.
