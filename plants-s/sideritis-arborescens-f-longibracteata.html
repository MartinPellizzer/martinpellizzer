Sideritis arborescens f. longibracteata

Sideritis arborescens f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracteed mountain sedge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
