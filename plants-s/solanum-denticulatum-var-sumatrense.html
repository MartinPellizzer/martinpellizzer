Solanum denticulatum var. sumatrense

Solanum denticulatum var. sumatrense, commonly known as the Sumatran nightshade, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and ovate, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is related to other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
