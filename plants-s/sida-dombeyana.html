Sida dombeyana

Sida dombeyana, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sida dombeyana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
