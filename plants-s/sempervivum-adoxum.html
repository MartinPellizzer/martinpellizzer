Sempervivum adoxum

Sempervivum adoxum, commonly known as the hen and chicks plant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh conditions, including frost and drought.
