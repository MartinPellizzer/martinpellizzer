Salvia acuminata

Salvia acuminata, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Salvia acuminata is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
