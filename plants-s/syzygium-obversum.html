Syzygium obversum

Syzygium obversum, commonly known as the white apple or white lilly pill, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, white or yellow fruits that are edible but have a mild, sweet flavor. Syzygium obversum is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils.
