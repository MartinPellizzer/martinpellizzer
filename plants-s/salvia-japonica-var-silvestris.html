Salvia japonica var. silvestris

Salvia japonica var. silvestris, commonly known as Japanese woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
