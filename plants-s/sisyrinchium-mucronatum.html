Sisyrinchium mucronatum

Sisyrinchium mucronatum, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow corolla, and its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Sisyrinchium mucronatum is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
