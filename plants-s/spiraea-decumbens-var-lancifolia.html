Spiraea decumbens var. lancifolia

Spiraea decumbens var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features arching stems that produce clusters of white to pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and turn red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
