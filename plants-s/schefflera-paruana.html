Schefflera paruana

Schefflera paruana, commonly known as the Brazilian schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical forests.

Schefflera paruana is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
