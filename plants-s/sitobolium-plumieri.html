Sitobolium plumieri

Sitobolium plumieri, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. Sitobolium plumieri is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
