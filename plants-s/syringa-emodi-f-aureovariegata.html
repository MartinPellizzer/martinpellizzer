Syringa emodi f. aureovariegata

Syringa emodi f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden-variegated lilac, is a cultivar of the Syringa emodi species, which is native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of yellow, white, and green colors, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in landscaping and botanical gardens.
