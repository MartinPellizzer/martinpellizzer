Salvia matsudae

Salvia matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional Japanese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia matsudae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in herbal practices.
