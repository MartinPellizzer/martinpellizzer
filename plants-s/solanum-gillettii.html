Solanum gillettii

Solanum gillettii, commonly known as the Gillett's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals, and it produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided for cultivation and human consumption.
