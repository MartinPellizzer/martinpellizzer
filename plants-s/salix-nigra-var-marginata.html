Salix nigra var. marginata

Salix nigra var. marginata, commonly known as the black willow with white margins, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive white or silvery margin. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is often found along stream banks and moist woodland areas. Its foliage turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
