Senecio leucophorbius

Senecio leucophorbius, commonly known as the white-veined groundsel, is a South African endemic plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with prominent white centers, which bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
