Senecio toluccanus var. microdontus

Senecio toluccanus var. microdontus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are generally narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

The yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
