Shorea pauciflora

Shorea pauciflora, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and typically grows in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
