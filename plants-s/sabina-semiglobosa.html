Sabina semiglobosa

Sabina semiglobosa, commonly known as the globe juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces blue or black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
