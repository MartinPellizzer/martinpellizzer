Salvia sinica f. purpurea

Salvia sinica f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple Chinese sage, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It is typically evergreen, though it may shed some leaves in colder climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
