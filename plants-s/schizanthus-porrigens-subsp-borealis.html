Schizanthus porrigens subsp. borealis

Schizanthus porrigens subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
