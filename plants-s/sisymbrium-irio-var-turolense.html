Sisymbrium irio var. turolense

Sisymbrium irio var. turolense, commonly known as the Turolense mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, peppery aroma.
