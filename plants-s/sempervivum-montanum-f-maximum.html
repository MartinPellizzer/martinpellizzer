Sempervivum montanum f. maximum

Sempervivum montanum f. maximum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It produces small, greenish flowers in the summer, adding a subtle ornamental touch to its appearance.
