Strychnos oblongifolia

Strychnos oblongifolia, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fibrous coating.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
