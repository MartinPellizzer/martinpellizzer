Specklinia jesupiorum

Specklinia jesupiorum, commonly known as the Jesup's speckled orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, strap-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its unique speckled patterns on the leaves make it a visually striking species among orchids.

Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from habitat destruction, it is considered endangered in the wild.
