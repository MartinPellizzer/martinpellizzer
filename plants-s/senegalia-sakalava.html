Senegalia sakalava

Senegalia sakalava, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
