Stipa clandestina

Stipa clandestina, commonly known as the hidden grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and tough, with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss.

Stipa clandestina plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to arid environments.
