Sophora mollis subsp. sylvestris

Sophora mollis subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters.

The plant features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name, and produces yellow flowers in racemes during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
