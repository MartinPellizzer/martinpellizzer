Streptopus lanuginosus

Streptopus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly star, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a distinctive, woolly appearance with soft, grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a leathery texture, contributing to its unique botanical profile.
