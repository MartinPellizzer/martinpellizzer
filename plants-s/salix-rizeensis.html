Salix rizeensis

Salix rizeensis, commonly known as Rize willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to Turkey and is typically found in moist, temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
