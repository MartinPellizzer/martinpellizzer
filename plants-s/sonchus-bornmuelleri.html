Sonchus bornmuelleri

Sonchus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Sierra madre dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
