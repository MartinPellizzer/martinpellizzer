Silene laxiflora var. micropetala

Silene laxiflora var. micropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is a subspecies of Silene laxiflora, distinguished by its reduced petal size compared to the typical form.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
