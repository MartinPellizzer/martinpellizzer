Scrophularia canina subsp. frutescens

Scrophularia canina subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as dog's mouth or scarlet pimpernel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. Scrophularia canina subsp.

frutescens is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
