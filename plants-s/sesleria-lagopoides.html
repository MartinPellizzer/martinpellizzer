Sesleria lagopoides

Sesleria lagopoides, commonly known as the hare's foot grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy, grass-like leaves that resemble the fur of a hare. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sesleria lagopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
