Solanum manucaling

Solanum manucaling, commonly known as the Manucaling nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, purple berries that are toxic to humans but are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its flowers are typically white or pale blue.

Solanum manucaling thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
