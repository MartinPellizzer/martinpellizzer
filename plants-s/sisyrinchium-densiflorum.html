Sisyrinchium densiflorum

Sisyrinchium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and blooms from late spring to early summer.

Sisyrinchium densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
