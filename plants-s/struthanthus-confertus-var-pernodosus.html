Struthanthus confertus var. pernodosus

Struthanthus confertus var. pernodosus, commonly known as the desert starflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically borne on hairy stems, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in desert ecosystems.
