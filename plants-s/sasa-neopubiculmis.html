Sasa neopubiculmis

Sasa neopubiculmis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a bluish-green color that helps it blend into forested environments.

Sasa neopubiculmis is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist conditions, often found in mountainous regions.
