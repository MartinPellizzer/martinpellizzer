Solanum subtruncatum var. remotidens

Solanum subtruncatum var. remotidens, commonly known as the remote-stemmed nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
