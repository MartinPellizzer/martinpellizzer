Stellaria glabra

Stellaria glabra, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom from spring to summer, and its leaves are typically rounded and hairy. The plant grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along pathways. It has a shallow root system and is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit.

Stellaria glabra is also known for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
