Setaria lenis

Setaria lenis, commonly known as slender foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its seeds are small and numerous, contributing to its ability to spread easily.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many important agricultural and ecological grasses.
