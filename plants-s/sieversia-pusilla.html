Sieversia pusilla

Sieversia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sievegrass, is a small perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils. Sieversia pusilla is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
