Sideritis arenaria var. villosissima

Sideritis arenaria var. villosissima, commonly known as the hairy rock sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, silky pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
