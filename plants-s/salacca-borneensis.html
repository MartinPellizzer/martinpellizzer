Salacca borneensis

Salacca borneensis, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling pineapples. Its ornamental value makes it popular in landscaping, while its fruit is used in traditional cuisine.

Salacca borneensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
