Solanum subsylvestre

Solanum subsylvestre, commonly known as the wild tomato or forest tomato, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Solanum subsylvestre is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
