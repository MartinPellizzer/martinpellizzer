Styrax prancei

Styrax prancei, commonly known as the Hawaiian styrax, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a member of the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, resinous fruits that are used in traditional Hawaiian medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
