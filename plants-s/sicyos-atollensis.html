Sicyos atollensis

Sicyos atollensis, commonly known as the atoll bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, inflated fruit that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, often used for storage or as a container. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and typically unisexual.

Sicyos atollensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing on coastal atolls and sandy soils.
