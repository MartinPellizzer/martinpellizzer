Salvia killipiana

Salvia killipiana, commonly known as the Killip's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
