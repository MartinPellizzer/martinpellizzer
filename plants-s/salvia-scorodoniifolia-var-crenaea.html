Salvia scorodoniifolia var. crenaea

Salvia scorodoniifolia var. crenaea, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly wavy leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet "crenaea.".
