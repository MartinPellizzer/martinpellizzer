Sasaella okadana

Sasaella okadana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Okadana bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasaella okadana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
