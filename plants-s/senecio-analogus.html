Senecio analogus

Senecio analogus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Senecio analogus is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
