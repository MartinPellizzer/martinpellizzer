Senecio grisebachii var. grisebachii

Senecio grisebachii var. grisebachii, commonly known as the Grisebach's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
