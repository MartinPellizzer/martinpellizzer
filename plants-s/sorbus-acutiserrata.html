Sorbus acutiserrata

Sorbus acutiserrata, commonly known as the serrated whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that mature to a red or orange color.

This species is often found in woodlands and rocky habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
