Scleranthus imbricatus

Scleranthus imbricatus, commonly known as the common sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
