Saccharum officinarum var. otaheitense

Saccharum officinarum var. otaheitense, commonly known as the Otaheite grass, is a variant of sugarcane native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and coarse, fibrous leaves. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a high sugar content in its stems.

It has been historically significant for both its agricultural and horticultural uses.
