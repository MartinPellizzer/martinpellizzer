Sisymbrium pinnatifidum subsp. lapeyrousianum

Sisymbrium pinnatifidum subsp.

lapeyrousianum, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
