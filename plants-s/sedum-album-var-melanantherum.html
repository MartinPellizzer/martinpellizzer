Sedum album var. melanantherum

Sedum album var. melanantherum, commonly known as the black-flowered stonecrop, is a variant of the common sedum species.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its fleshy, blue-green leaves. This succulent plant is native to regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant's unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
