Saxifraga orfilae

Saxifraga orfilae, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Saxifraga orfilae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it a notable species in mountainous ecosystems.
