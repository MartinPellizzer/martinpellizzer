Smilax siderophylla

Smilax siderophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with spiny edges. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall and are edible for some wildlife.

Smilax siderophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
