Sonchus cornutus

Sonchus cornutus, commonly known as the hairy prickly lettuce, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features distinctive, spiny stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

Sonchus cornutus is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
