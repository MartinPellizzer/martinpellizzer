Spathiostemon moniliformis

Spathiostemon moniliformis, commonly known as the bead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bead-like inflorescences that are composed of small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape.

The bead vine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
