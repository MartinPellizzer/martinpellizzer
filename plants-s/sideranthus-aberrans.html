Sideranthus aberrans

Sideranthus aberrans, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and short life cycle make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
