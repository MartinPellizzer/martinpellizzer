Sorbaria gilgitensis

Sorbaria gilgitensis, commonly known as the Gilgit sandthorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture.

Sorbaria gilgitensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal values.
