Senecio acuminatus

Senecio acuminatus, commonly known as the sharpleaf groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the common name "sharpleaf," while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Senecio acuminatus is often used in rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
