Symplocos frondosa

Symplocos frondosa, commonly known as the broadleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, violet-purple berries that ripen in the fall, attracting various bird species. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Symplocos frondosa thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
