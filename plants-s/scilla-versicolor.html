Scilla versicolor

Scilla versicolor, commonly known as the variable scilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Scilla versicolor is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
