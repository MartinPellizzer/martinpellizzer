Silene legionensis

Silene legionensis, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Silene legionensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
