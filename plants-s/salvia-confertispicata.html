Salvia confertispicata

Salvia confertispicata, commonly known as the dense-bracted sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and aromatic foliage.
