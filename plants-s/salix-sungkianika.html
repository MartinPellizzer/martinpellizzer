Salix sungkianika

Salix sungkianika, commonly known as the Sungkianika willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, specifically found in parts of Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth texture, often turning yellow in the fall.

Salix sungkianika is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories.
