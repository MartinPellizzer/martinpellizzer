Solanum astrochlaenoides

Solanum astrochlaenoides, commonly known as the starleaf nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive star-like appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
