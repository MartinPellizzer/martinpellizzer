Solidago arguta var. harrisii

Solidago arguta var. harrisii, commonly known as the Harris' blue eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flower heads that resemble small eyes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
