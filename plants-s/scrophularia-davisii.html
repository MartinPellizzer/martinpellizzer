Scrophularia davisii

Scrophularia davisii, commonly known as Davis' figwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and are arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

Davis' figwort is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for insects.
