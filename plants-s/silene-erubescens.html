Silene erubescens

Silene erubescens, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its erect stems and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene erubescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
