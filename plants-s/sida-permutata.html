Sida permutata

Sida permutata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Sida permutata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
