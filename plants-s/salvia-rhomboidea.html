Salvia rhomboidea

Salvia rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and long blooming period.
