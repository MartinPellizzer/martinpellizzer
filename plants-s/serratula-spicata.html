Serratula spicata

Serratula spicata, commonly known as spiky sow-thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a spiky cluster, which gives it its common name. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.

Serratula spicata is an annual or biennial herb that can grow up to 60 cm tall.
