Sparganium septentrionale

Sparganium septentrionale, commonly known as northern bur-reed, is a perennial wetland plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that grow in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense stands that help stabilize aquatic environments.

Sparganium septentrionale plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
