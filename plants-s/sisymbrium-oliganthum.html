Sisymbrium oliganthum

Sisymbrium oliganthum, commonly known as the lesser jointed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
