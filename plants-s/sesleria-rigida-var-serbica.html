Sesleria rigida var. serbica

Sesleria rigida var. serbica, commonly known as the Serbica false oat, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
