Senecio haloragis var. glandulosus

Senecio haloragis var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
