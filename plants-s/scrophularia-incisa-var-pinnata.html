Scrophularia incisa var. pinnata

Scrophularia incisa var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and often have a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pale purple to pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
