Smeathmannia laevigata var. nigerica

Smeathmannia laevigata var. nigerica, commonly known as the black-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on eucalyptus trees in southeastern Australia.

It has dark, glossy leaves that are usually leathery in texture and are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds. It is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its unique ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

The variety is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to other varieties of Smeathmannia laevigata.
