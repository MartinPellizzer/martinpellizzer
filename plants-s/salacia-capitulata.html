Salacia capitulata

Salacia capitulata, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

Salacia capitulata is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
