Solanum tuberosum var. agrostans

Solanum tuberosum var. agrostans, commonly known as the creeping potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that forms a dense mat of stolons and tubers near the ground. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish-white. This variety is known for its ability to produce tubers along its stolons, making it suitable for certain agricultural conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and genetics.
