Syzygium cylindricum

Syzygium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a crisp, juicy texture. Syzygium cylindricum thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Myrtaceae family.
