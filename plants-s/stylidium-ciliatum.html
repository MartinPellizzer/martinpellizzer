Stylidium ciliatum

Stylidium ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a unique structure with a spurred labellum and a short, erect stem. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
