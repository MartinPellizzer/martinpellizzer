Stachytarpheta fiebrigii

Stachytarpheta fiebrigii, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces both blue and purple flower colors, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
