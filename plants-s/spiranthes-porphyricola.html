Spiranthes porphyricola

Spiranthes porphyricola, commonly known as the purple fringed checkerspot, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with fringed petals that give it its common name. The plant grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish colonies in suitable habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bees, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.
