Syzygium decoriflorum

Syzygium decoriflorum, commonly known as the golden apple or *Pomelo* in some regions, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces large, round fruits with thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Syzygium decoriflorum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
