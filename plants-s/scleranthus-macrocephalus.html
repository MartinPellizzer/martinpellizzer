Scleranthus macrocephalus

Scleranthus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed sandweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Scleranthus macrocephalus is often found in disturbed areas and along coastal regions.
