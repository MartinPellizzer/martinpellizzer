Salvia hermesiana

Salvia hermesiana, commonly known as Hermes' sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia hermesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
