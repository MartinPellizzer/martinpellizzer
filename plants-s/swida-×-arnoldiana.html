Swida × arnoldiana

Swida × arnoldiana, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree resulting from the cross between Swida humilis and Swida alata.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features thorny branches with opposite, ovate leaves that have a serrated margin. The plant produces white or pink flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Swida × arnoldiana is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its adaptability to various climates.
