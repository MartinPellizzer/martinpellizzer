Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. flagellare

Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. flagellare, commonly known as the slenderleaf heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structure and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
