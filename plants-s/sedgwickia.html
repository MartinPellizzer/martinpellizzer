Sedgwickia

Sedgwickia, commonly known as the sedgwickia grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plants typically thrive in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Sedgwickia species are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
