Sclarea spinosa

Sclarea spinosa, commonly known as the spiny angelica, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

Sclarea spinosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its medicinal and historical uses.
