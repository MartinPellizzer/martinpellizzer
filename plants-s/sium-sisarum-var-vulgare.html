Sium sisarum var. vulgare

Sium sisarum var. vulgare, commonly known as water parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.
