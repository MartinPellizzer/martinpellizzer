Stipagrostis papposa

Stipagrostis papposa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces small, fluffy seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
