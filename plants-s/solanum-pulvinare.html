Solanum pulvinare

Solanum pulvinare, commonly known as the pulvinate nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black hue. Solanum pulvinare is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
