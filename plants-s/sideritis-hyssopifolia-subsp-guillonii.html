Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. guillonii

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

guillonii, commonly known as the Greek heath or mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
