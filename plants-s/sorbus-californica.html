Sorbus californica

Sorbus californica, commonly known as the California white-leaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries in the summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The fruit is a source of food for various birds and small mammals.
