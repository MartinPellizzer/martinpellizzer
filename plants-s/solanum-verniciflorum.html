Solanum verniciflorum

Solanum verniciflorum, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption or cultivation.
