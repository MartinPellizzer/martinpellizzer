Seneciodes cinereum

Seneciodes cinereum, commonly known as the gray coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
