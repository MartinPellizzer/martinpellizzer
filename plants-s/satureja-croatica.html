Satureja croatica

Satureja croatica, commonly known as Croatian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.
