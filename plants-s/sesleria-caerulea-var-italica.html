Sesleria caerulea var. italica

Sesleria caerulea var. italica, commonly known as the Italian sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and subtle floral display.
