Scleranthus verticillatus subsp. delortii

Scleranthus verticillatus subsp.

delortii, commonly known as the Delort's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and alternate, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
