Solidago polycephala

Solidago polycephala, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of many small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago polycephala plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
