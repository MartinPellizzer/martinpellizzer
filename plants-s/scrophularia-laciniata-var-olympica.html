Scrophularia laciniata var. olympica

Scrophularia laciniata var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic cowslip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
