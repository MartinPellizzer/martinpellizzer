Scilla rigidifolia var. acerosa

Scilla rigidifolia var. acerosa, commonly known as the rigid-leaved scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, stiff leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.

The variety acerosa is distinguished by its particularly rigid and sword-like foliage.
