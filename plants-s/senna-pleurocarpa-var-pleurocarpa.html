Senna pleurocarpa var. pleurocarpa

Senna pleurocarpa var. pleurocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
