Syringa patula var. venosa

Syringa patula var. venosa, commonly known as the fragrant white bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Syringa patula var. venosa is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
