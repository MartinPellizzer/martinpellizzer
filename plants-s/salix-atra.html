Salix atra

Salix atra, commonly known as black willow, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and slender, drooping branches. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a pointed tip with a serrated margin. It produces catkins as its reproductive structures, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This species is often found near water sources, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
