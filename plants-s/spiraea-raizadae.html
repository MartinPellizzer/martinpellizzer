Spiraea raizadae

Spiraea raizadae, commonly known as the mountain spiraea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its profuse display of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Spiraea raizadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
