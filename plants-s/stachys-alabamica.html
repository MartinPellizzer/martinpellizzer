Stachys alabamica

Stachys alabamica, commonly known as Alabama betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

Stachys alabamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
