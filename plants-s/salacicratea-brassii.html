Salacicratea brassii

Salacicratea brassii, commonly known as the golden thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland and coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
