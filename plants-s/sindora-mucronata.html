Sindora mucronata

Sindora mucronata, commonly known as the Malabar spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
