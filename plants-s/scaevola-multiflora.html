Scaevola multiflora

Scaevola multiflora, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola multiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
