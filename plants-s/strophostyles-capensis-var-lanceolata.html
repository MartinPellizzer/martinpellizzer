Strophostyles capensis var. lanceolata

Strophostyles capensis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Cape spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
