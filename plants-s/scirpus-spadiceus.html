Scirpus spadiceus

Scirpus spadiceus, commonly known as purple bulrush, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in marshes and pond edges.

Scirpus spadiceus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
