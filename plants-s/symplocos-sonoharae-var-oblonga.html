Symplocos sonoharae var. oblonga

Symplocos sonoharae var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblongleaf stonecrop, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually densely packed on the stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
