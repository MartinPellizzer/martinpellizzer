Silene sinensis

Silene sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Silene sinensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
