Swartzia anomala

Swartzia anomala, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. Swartzia anomala is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

However, due to overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species in many regions.
