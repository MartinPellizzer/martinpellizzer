Stipa bromoides

Stipa bromoides, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a reddish or purple hue. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Stipa bromoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
