Sisymbrium erysimoides var. xerophilum

Sisymbrium erysimoides var. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
