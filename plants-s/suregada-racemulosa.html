Suregada racemulosa

Suregada racemulosa, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance.
