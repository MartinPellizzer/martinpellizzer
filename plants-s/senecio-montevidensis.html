Senecio montevidensis

Senecio montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Senecio montevidensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
