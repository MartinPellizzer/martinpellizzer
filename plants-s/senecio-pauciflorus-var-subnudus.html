Senecio pauciflorus var. subnudus

Senecio pauciflorus var. subnudus, commonly known as the naked-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and may be covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is often found in open vegetation types such as fynbos and renosterveld.
