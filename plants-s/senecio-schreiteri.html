Senecio schreiteri

Senecio schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio schreiteri is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at elevations above 3,000 meters.
