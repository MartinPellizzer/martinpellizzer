Serapias × oulmesiaca

Serapias × oulmesiaca, commonly known as the hybrid orchid orchis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias orchidioides.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive hooded lip, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Serapias × oulmesiaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
