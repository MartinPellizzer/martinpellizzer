Sabal parviflora

Sabal parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf cabbage palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 3 to 6 feet. The plant has a short, stout trunk and is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves that are light green and have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sabal parviflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
