Silene sojakii

Silene sojakii, commonly known as the Sojak's campion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Silene sojakii is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
