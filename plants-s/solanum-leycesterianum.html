Solanum leycesterianum

Solanum leycesterianum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum leycesterianum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in tropical gardens for its visual appeal.
