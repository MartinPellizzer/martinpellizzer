Scleria fasciculata

Scleria fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spikeplant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Poaceae family.

Scleria fasciculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, clustered appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
