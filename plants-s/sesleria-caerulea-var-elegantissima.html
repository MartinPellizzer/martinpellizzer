Sesleria caerulea var. elegantissima

Sesleria caerulea var. elegantissima, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like foliage that is often a deep green with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
