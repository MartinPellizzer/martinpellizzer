Spondias pubescens

Spondias pubescens, commonly known as the rough spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruits are often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
