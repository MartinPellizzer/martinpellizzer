Sambucus racemosa f. macrophylla

Sambucus racemosa f. macrophylla, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible when ripe but toxic when unripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
