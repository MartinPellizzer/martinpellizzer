Sporobolus creber

Sporobolus creber, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Sporobolus creber is drought-tolerant and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
