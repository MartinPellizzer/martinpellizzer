Silene gallica subvar. agrestina

Silene gallica subvar. agrestina, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically five-petaled. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Silene gallica subvar. agrestina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also found in wildflower displays.
