Stellaria pseudoglandulifera

Stellaria pseudoglandulifera, commonly known as the woolly starwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Stellaria pseudoglandulifera is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
