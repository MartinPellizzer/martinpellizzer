Saxifraga etrusca

Saxifraga etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in late spring. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga etrusca is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
