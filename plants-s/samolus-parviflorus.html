Samolus parviflorus

Samolus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered samolus, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Samolus parviflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
