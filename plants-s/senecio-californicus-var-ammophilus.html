Senecio californicus var. ammophilus

Senecio californicus var. ammophilus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is drought-tolerant.

This species is often found in coastal sage scrub and grassland habitats.
