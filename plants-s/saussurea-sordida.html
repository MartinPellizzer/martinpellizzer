Saussurea sordida

Saussurea sordida, commonly known as the black saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool, dry climates. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Saussurea sordida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
