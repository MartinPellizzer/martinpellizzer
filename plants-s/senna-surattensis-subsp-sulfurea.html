Senna surattensis subsp. sulfurea

Senna surattensis subsp.

sulfurea, commonly known as golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
