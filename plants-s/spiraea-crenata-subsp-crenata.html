Spiraea crenata subsp. crenata

Spiraea crenata subsp.

crenata, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
