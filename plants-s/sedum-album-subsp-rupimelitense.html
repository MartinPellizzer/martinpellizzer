Sedum album subsp. rupimelitense

Sedum album subsp.

rupimelitense, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
