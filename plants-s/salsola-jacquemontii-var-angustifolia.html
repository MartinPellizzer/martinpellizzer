Salsola jacquemontii var. angustifolia

Salsola jacquemontii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Russian thistle, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, hard, dry fruits. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
