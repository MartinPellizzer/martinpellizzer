Solanum approximatum

Solanum approximatum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellow or orange color, often containing toxic compounds.

Solanum approximatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
