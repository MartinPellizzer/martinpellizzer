Serapias × ambigua var. timbalii

Serapias × ambigua var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalii orchid, is a hybrid species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with several flower buds. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
