Stemodia stricta

Stemodia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf stemodia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
