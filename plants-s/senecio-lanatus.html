Senecio lanatus

Senecio lanatus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, white to gray-green foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio lanatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
