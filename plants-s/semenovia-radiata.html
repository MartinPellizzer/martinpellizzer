Semenovia radiata

Semenovia radiata, commonly known as the radiate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its hairy stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
