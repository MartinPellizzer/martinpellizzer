Sisymbrium sinapifolium

Sisymbrium sinapifolium, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lobed or pinnately divided, with the upper leaves being more linear. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Sisymbrium sinapifolium is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
