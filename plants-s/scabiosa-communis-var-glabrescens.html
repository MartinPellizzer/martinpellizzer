Scabiosa communis var. glabrescens

Scabiosa communis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white or pink flower heads that resemble a ball of wool, giving it its common name. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
