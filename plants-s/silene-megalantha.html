Silene megalantha

Silene megalantha, commonly known as the giant catchfly, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it one of the tallest species in the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene megalantha is often found in rocky or open areas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
