Strychnos lanata

Strychnos lanata, commonly known as the hairy strangler fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant bears yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Strychnos lanata is known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
