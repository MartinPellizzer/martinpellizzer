Scleria venezuelensis

Scleria venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and fibrous root system.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Scleria venezuelensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
