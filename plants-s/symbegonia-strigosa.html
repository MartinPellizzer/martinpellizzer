Symbegonia strigosa

Symbegonia strigosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Symbegonia strigosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to water gardens and bog landscapes.
