Strobocalyx elaeagnifolia

Strobocalyx elaeagnifolia, commonly known as silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green berries. The plant belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.
