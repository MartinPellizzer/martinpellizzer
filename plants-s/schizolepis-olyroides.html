Schizolepis olyroides

Schizolepis olyroides, commonly known as the silky olyroid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture.

Schizolepis olyroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
