Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. melanolepis

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved xeranthemum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a distinctive blackish-purple underside, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
