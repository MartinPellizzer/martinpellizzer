Saxifraga pasumensis

Saxifraga pasumensis, commonly known as the Pasumenskaya saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
