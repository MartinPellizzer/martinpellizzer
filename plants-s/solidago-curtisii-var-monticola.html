Solidago curtisii var. monticola

Solidago curtisii var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
