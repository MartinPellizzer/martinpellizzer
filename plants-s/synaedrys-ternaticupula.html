Synaedrys ternaticupula

Synaedrys ternaticupula, commonly known as the cupula orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring a distinctive cup-like hood that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
