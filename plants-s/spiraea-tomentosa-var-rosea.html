Spiraea tomentosa var. rosea

Spiraea tomentosa var. rosea, commonly known as the rosepink weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of pinkish-white to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, rounded form with grayish-white, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy blooms and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
