Scirpus mauritanicus

Scirpus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in saline and freshwater wetlands, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the plant's ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Scirpus mauritanicus plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
