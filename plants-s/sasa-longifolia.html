Sasa longifolia

Sasa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf sasa, is a perennial bamboo-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Sasa longifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
