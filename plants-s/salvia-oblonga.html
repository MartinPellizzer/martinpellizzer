Salvia oblonga

Salvia oblonga, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or xeriscapes. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
