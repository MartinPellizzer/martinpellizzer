Sinocalamus oldhamii f. revolutus

Sinocalamus oldhamii f. revolutus, commonly known as the revolute bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

This plant is native to eastern China and thrives in humid, subtropical climates. Its leaves are distinctly revolute, meaning they curl under at the edges, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is closely related to other bamboo species.
