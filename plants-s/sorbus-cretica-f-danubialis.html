Sorbus cretica f. danubialis

Sorbus cretica f. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in riparian habitats.
