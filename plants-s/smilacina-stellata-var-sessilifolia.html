Smilacina stellata var. sessilifolia

Smilacina stellata var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf greenbriar, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface and often have a reddish tinge when mature.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
