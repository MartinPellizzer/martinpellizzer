Seris integrifolia

Seris integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf seris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Seris integrifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
