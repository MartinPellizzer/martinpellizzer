Salvia speirematoides

Salvia speirematoides, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces spikes of showy violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its medicinal properties have been utilized by indigenous peoples for various purposes.

Salvia speirematoides is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
