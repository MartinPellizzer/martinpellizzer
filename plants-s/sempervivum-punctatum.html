Sempervivum punctatum

Sempervivum punctatum, commonly known as the Stonecrop or Hens and Chicks, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The leaves are typically green with red or purple markings, and the plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is a perennial plant that is easy to propagate through offsets, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it well-suited for growing in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.
