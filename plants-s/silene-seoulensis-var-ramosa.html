Silene seoulensis var. ramosa

Silene seoulensis var. ramosa, commonly known as the Seoul aster, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
