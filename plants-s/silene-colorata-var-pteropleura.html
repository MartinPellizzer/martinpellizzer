Silene colorata var. pteropleura

Silene colorata var. pteropleura, commonly known as the purple-stemmed catchfly, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are typically tinged with purple or red, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene colorata var. pteropleura is valued in horticulture for its visual appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
