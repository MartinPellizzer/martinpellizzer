Senecio summapacis

Senecio summapacis, commonly known as the Maputia Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
