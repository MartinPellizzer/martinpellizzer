Solanum dumicola

Solanum dumicola, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Solanum dumicola is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important plants.
