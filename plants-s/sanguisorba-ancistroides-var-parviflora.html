Sanguisorba ancistroides var. parviflora

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sweetflag, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of tiny, reddish-brown flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
