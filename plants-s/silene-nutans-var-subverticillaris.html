Silene nutans var. subverticillaris

Silene nutans var. subverticillaris, commonly known as the nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variant is notable for its subtle floral structure and ecological adaptability.
