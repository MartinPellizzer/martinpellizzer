Stachys tamaulipana

Stachys tamaulipana, commonly known as Tamaulipan betony, is a flowering plant native to the Tamaulipan moist forests of northeastern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stachys tamaulipana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
