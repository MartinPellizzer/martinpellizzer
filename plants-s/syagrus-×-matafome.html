Syagrus × matafome

Syagrus × matafome, commonly known as the matafome palm, is a hybrid species within the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange fruits.

Syagrus × matafome is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
