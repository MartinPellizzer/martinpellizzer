Serjania atrolineata

Serjania atrolineata, commonly known as the blackline serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes several species of shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Serjania atrolineata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
