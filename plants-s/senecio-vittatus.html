Senecio vittatus

Senecio vittatus, commonly known as the variegated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white and green. The plant produces small, yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
