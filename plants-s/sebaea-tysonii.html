Sebaea tysonii

Sebaea tysonii, commonly known as the Townsend's sebaea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Sebaea tysonii is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
