Setcreasea brevifolia var. pulchella

Setcreasea brevifolia var. pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its distinctive foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
