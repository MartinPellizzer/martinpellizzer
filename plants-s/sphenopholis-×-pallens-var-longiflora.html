Sphenopholis × pallens var. longiflora

Sphenopholis × pallens var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered sphenopholis, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flower size.
