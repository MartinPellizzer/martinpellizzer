Saurauia peduncularis var. veraniana

Saurauia peduncularis var. veraniana, commonly known as the Veraniana snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous white flowers that bloom in the spring, giving it a bell-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
