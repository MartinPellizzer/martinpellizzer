Sarracenia rubra var. acuminata

Sarracenia rubra var. acuminata, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped traps. The plant's vibrant red coloration, especially on the tips of its leaves, is a key feature that aids in attracting prey. Its pitchers are filled with a digestive fluid that breaks down insects, providing essential nutrients in its nutrient-poor habitat.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique trapping mechanism.
