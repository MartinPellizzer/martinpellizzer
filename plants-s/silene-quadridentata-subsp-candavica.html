Silene quadridentata subsp. candavica

Silene quadridentata subsp.

candavica, commonly known as the Whiteflower Catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, showy flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
