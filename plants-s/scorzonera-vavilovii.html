Scorzonera vavilovii

Scorzonera vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera vavilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
