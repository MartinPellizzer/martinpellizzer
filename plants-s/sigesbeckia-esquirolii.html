Sigesbeckia esquirolii

Sigesbeckia esquirolii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Sigesbeckia esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
