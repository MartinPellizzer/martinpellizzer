Schismus arabicus var. violaceus

Schismus arabicus var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments.

Schismus arabicus var. violaceus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
