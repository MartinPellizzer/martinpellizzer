Syringa wilsonii

Syringa wilsonii, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale purple in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa wilsonii is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
