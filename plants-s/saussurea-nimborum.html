Saussurea nimborum

Saussurea nimborum, commonly known as the Tibetan cloud safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
