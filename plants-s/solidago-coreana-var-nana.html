Solidago coreana var. nana

Solidago coreana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Korean goldenrod, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Korea.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in forested areas or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
