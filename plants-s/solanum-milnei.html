Solanum milnei

Solanum milnei, commonly known as the Milne's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. Its berries are typically red when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum milnei is often found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests.
