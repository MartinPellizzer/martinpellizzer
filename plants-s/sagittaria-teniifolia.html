Sagittaria teniifolia

Sagittaria teniifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive narrow leaf blade. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sagittaria teniifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
