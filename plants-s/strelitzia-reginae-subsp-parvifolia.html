Strelitzia reginae subsp. parvifolia

Strelitzia reginae subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the Parvifolia Bird of Paradise, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that form a rosette and its unique, bird-like inflorescences with vibrant blue and orange petals.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
