Saxifraga brachypodoidea

Saxifraga brachypodoidea, commonly known as the short-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous environments where the climate is cold and the growing season is short. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
