Sorghum nitidum var. fulvum

Sorghum nitidum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden finger grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking golden hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
