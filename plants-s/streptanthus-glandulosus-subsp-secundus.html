Streptanthus glandulosus subsp. secundus

Streptanthus glandulosus subsp.

secundus, commonly known as the sticky stalked rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its glandular, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the western United States. Its leaves are alternate and often have a pungent odor, typical of the mustard family.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
