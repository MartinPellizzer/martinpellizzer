Schoenoplectiella × naritaensis var. bizenensis

Schoenoplectiella × naritaensis var. bizenensis, commonly known as the Bizen reed, is a hybrid grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
