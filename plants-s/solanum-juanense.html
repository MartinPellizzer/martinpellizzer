Solanum juanense

Solanum juanense, commonly known as Juan's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with woody stems and alternate leaves.

Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans but may be used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
