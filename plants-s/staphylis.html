Staphylis

Staphylis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually silvery or grayish, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Staphylis species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and hardy nature.
