Senna nana

Senna nana, commonly known as dwarf senna, is a small, herbaceous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are rich in nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain compounds used in traditional medicine.

Senna nana thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
