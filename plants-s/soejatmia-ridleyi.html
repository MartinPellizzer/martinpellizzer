Soejatmia ridleyi

Soejatmia ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Soejatmia ridleyi is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
