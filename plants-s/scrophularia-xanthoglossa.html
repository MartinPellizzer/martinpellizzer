Scrophularia xanthoglossa

Scrophularia xanthoglossa, commonly known as yellow-lipped scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with yellow petals that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
