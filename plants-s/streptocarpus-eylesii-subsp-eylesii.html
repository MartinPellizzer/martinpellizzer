Streptocarpus eylesii subsp. eylesii

Streptocarpus eylesii subsp.

eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
