Scutellaria nervosa var. ambigua

Scutellaria nervosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
