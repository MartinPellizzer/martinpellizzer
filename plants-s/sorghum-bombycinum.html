Sorghum bombycinum

Sorghum bombycinum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other sorghum species. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib.

Sorghum bombycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
