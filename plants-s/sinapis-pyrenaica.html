Sinapis pyrenaica

Sinapis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and has small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Sinapis pyrenaica is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.
