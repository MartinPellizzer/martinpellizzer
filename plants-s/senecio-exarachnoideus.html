Senecio exarachnoideus

Senecio exarachnoideus, commonly known as the silky senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper underground.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a common sight in shrublands and grasslands.
