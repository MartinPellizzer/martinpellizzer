Sioja sanguinaria

Sioja sanguinaria, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a single, palmately lobed leaf. Its showy white flowers appear in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Sioja sanguinaria is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
