Seseli rigidum subsp. rigidum

Seseli rigidum subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as rigid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has compound leaves with slender, linear leaflets, and it produces small, dry fruits called legumes. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Seseli rigidum subsp.

rigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
