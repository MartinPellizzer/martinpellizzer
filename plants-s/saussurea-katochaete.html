Saussurea katochaete

Saussurea katochaete, commonly known as the Katochaete arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble arrowheads, giving it its common name.

The flower heads are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
