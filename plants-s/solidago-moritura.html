Solidago moritura

Solidago moritura, commonly known as the death's-head twayblade, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
