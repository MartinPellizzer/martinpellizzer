Salvia bertolonii

Salvia bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Italy.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia bertolonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
