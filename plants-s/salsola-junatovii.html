Salsola junatovii

Salsola junatovii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. Salsola junatovii is adapted to extreme temperatures and can survive with minimal water, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
