Solanum erythrocarpon

Solanum erythrocarpon, commonly known as the red-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.
