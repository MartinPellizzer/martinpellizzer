Scorzonera hotanica

Scorzonera hotanica, commonly known as the Hotan scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Scorzonera hotanica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
