Syngonium atrovirens

Syngonium atrovirens, commonly known as the Black Velvet Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a velvety texture, often displaying a striking contrast of deep green and purplish veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Syngonium atrovirens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
