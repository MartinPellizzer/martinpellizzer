Semecarpus grandifolius

Semecarpus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its bark and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
