Senecio angulatus

Senecio angulatus, commonly known as the angular senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its flowers are usually yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

Senecio angulatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
