Selaginella hewittii

Selaginella hewittii, commonly known as the Hewitt's spikemoss, is a low-growing, evergreen fern ally native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Selaginella hewittii is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience through its ability to survive desiccation.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in xerophytic plant communities.
