Sorindeia puberula

Sorindeia puberula, commonly known as the silky sorindeia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its foliage is often described as velvety due to the dense pubescence on the leaf surfaces.

Sorindeia puberula is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
