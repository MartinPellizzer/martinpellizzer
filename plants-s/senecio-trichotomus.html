Senecio trichotomus

Senecio trichotomus, commonly known as the three-branched groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
