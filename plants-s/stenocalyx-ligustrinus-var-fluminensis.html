Stenocalyx ligustrinus var. fluminensis

Stenocalyx ligustrinus var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian privet, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by black, spherical berries. It is often used in landscaping due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
