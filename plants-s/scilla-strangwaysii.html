Scilla strangwaysii

Scilla strangwaysii, commonly known as the Sierra scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scilla strangwaysii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
