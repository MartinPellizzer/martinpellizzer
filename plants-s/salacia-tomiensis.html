Salacia tomiensis

Salacia tomiensis, commonly known as the Tomi's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, although it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
