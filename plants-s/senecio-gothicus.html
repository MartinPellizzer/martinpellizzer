Senecio gothicus

Senecio gothicus, commonly known as the ghostweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

Senecio gothicus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
