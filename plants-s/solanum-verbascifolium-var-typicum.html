Solanum verbascifolium var. typicum

Solanum verbascifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are an important food source for certain bird species.
