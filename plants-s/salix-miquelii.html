Salix miquelii

Salix miquelii, commonly known as Miquel's willow, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks or wetlands. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the plant is known for its adaptability to wet environments.
