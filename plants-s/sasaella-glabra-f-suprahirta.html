Sasaella glabra f. suprahirta

Sasaella glabra f. suprahirta, commonly known as the smooth sasa, is a variant of the sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of this plant make it well adapted to cool, humid climates and partially shaded conditions.
