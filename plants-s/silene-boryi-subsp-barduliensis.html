Silene boryi subsp. barduliensis

Silene boryi subsp.

barduliensis, commonly known as the Bardulian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its alpine environment.
