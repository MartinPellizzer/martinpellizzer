Symphyotrichum chapmanii

Symphyotrichum chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Chapman's heath aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
