Salix glaucops var. villosa

Salix glaucops var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a variant of the glaucops willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and hairy stems, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in mountainous regions.

The flowers are catkins, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
