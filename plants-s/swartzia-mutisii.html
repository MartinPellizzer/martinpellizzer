Swartzia mutisii

Swartzia mutisii, commonly known as the Colombian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Swartzia mutisii is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
