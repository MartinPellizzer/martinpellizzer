Saribus rotundifolius

Saribus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leafed screw pine, is a unique monocot native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and the presence of a distinctive, woody stem that is often covered in a fibrous layer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used for making ropes and other textiles.

Saribus rotundifolius is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments.
