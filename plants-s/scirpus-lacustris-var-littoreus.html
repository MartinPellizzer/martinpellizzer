Scirpus lacustris var. littoreus

Scirpus lacustris var. littoreus, commonly known as the marsh clubrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus lacustris var. littoreus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
