Sedum pumilum var. congdonii

Sedum pumilum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's dwarf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of North America.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
