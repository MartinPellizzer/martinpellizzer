Senecio windhoekensis

Senecio windhoekensis, commonly known as the Namibian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Namibia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
