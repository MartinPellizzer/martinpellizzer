Scleranthus annuus subsp. collinus

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is an annual herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes, where it thrives in saline and well-drained environments.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and have five petals that are usually pink or purple in color.

It is known for its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
