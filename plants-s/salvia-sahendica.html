Salvia sahendica

Salvia sahendica, commonly known as the Sahenda sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Salvia sahendica is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
