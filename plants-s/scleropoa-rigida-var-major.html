Scleropoa rigida var. major

Scleropoa rigida var. major, commonly known as the rigid feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its stiff, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Scleropoa rigida var. major is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid landscapes.
