Sporobolus pyramidatus

Sporobolus pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramid grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Sporobolus pyramidatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
