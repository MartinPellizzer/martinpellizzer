Sphaerochloa

Sphaerochloa, commonly known as bluebunch wheatgrass, is a genus of grasses native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, spherical seed heads. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are typically narrow and bluish-green, and it is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.

Sphaerochloa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife in arid and semi-arid regions.
