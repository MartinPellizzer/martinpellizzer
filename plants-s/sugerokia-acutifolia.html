Sugerokia acutifolia

Sugerokia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sugarcane, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and robust growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often cultivated for its high sugar content in its stalks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
