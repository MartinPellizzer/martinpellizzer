Seriphidium nitrosum var. kasakorum

Seriphidium nitrosum var. kasakorum, commonly known as the Kashgar wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
