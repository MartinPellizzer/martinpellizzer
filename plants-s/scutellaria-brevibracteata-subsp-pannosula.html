Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp. pannosula

Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp.

pannosula, commonly known as the pannose scullion, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
