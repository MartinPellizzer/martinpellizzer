Stylogyne ambigua

Stylogyne ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous stylogynum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stylogyne ambigua is known for its unique morphology and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
