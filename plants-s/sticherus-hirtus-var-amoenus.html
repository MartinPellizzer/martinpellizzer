Sticherus hirtus var. amoenus

Sticherus hirtus var. amoenus, commonly known as the woolly sticherus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
