Saxifraga magellanica var. ovatiloba

Saxifraga magellanica var. ovatiloba, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to harsh, maritime climates. The plant features succulent, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against strong winds and salt spray. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
