Scleria congolensis

Scleria congolensis, commonly known as the Congo sedge, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Scleria congolensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
