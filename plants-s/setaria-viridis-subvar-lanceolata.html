Setaria viridis subvar. lanceolata

Setaria viridis subvar. lanceolata, commonly known as slender green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, arid environments and is often used as a forage crop.

Its rapid growth and high biomass make it valuable for ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
