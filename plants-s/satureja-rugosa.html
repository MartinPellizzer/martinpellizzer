Satureja rugosa

Satureja rugosa, commonly known as rough savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are used in cooking for their pungent, thyme-like flavor, and it also has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Satureja rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
