Sidalcea multifida

Sidalcea multifida, commonly known as the fringed checkerspot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Sidalcea multifida is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
