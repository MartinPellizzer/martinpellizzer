Solanum andigenum var. colombianum

Solanum andigenum var. colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
