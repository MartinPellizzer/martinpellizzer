Syzygium sargentianum

Syzygium sargentianum, commonly known as the Sargent's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds but have a distinct flavor. Syzygium sargentianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
