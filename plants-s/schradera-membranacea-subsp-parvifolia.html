Schradera membranacea subsp. parvifolia

Schradera membranacea subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved schradera, is a member of the Malvaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
