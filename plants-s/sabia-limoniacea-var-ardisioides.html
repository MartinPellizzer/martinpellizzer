Sabia limoniacea var. ardisioides

Sabia limoniacea var. ardisioides, commonly known as the lemon-scented sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct lemony fragrance, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a desirable plant in horticulture.
