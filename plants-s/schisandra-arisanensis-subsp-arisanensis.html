Schisandra arisanensis subsp. arisanensis

Schisandra arisanensis subsp.

arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, berry-like fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant is valued for its aromatic, resinous fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
