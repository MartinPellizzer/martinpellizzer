Silene cucubalus f. marginata

Silene cucubalus f. marginata, commonly known as the margined bladder campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers and hairy stems.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive marginal coloration, which gives it its specific epithet.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
