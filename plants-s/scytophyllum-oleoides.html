Scytophyllum oleoides

Scytophyllum oleoides, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has feathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Scytophyllum oleoides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that is often branched and covered in glandular dots.
