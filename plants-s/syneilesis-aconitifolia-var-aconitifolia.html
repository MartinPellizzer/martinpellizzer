Syneilesis aconitifolia var. aconitifolia

Syneilesis aconitifolia var. aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
