Salvia bullata

Salvia bullata, commonly known as the "bull's ear sage," is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a bull's ear, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia bullata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
