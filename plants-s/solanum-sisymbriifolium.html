Solanum sisymbriifolium

Solanum sisymbriifolium, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that are enclosed in a pear-shaped calyx, resembling a tomatillo. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and high temperatures.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
