Solidago decumbens var. chlorolepis

Solidago decumbens var. chlorolepis, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
