Stachys alpina subsp. alpina

Stachys alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats. Stachys alpina subsp.

alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
