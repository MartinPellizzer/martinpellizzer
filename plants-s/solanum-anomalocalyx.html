Solanum anomalocalyx

Solanum anomalocalyx, commonly known as the irregular calyx nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its distinctive calyx that remains attached to the fruit even after ripening. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
