Scilla livida

Scilla livida, commonly known as the blue scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early spring blooming habit.

Scilla livida is also known for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies over time.
