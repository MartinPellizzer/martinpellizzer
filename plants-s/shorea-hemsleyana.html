Shorea hemsleyana

Shorea hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is valued for its dense, aromatic wood used in furniture and musical instruments. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

Shorea hemsleyana thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and plays a significant role in local ecosystems.
