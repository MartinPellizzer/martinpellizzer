Stanhopea tigrina var. nigropurpurea

Stanhopea tigrina var. nigropurpurea, commonly known as the Black-purple Tigrina orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple and black flowers with intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, fragrant blooms that appear during the dry season. It typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

The variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
