Schismatoglottis lansbergiana

Schismatoglottis lansbergiana, commonly known as the dwarf velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schismatoglottis lansbergiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
