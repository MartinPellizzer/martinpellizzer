Saxifraga jarmilae

Saxifraga jarmilae, commonly known as the Jarmilae saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

Saxifraga jarmilae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
