Solanum chancayense

Solanum chancayense, commonly known as the Chancay nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
