Scopella marginata

Scopella marginata, commonly known as the marginated sea hare, is a species of sea slug found in marine environments.

It belongs to the family Aplysiidae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated body covered in a layer of mucus. The animal has a soft, gelatinous body with a distinctive marginal ridge along its dorsal surface. Scopella marginata is primarily herbivorous, feeding on algae and other marine plants.
