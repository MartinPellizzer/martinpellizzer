Salix cordata var. crux-aurae

Salix cordata var. crux-aurae, commonly known as the golden willow, is a variety of the weeping willow that exhibits striking golden-yellow foliage.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features long, cascading branches that create a soft, flowing appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, turning a vibrant yellow in the fall.

This variety is often planted near water features or in areas with high humidity due to its preference for wet conditions.
