Scirpus viridis

Scirpus viridis, commonly known as green bulrush, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important species for wetland stabilization.

Scirpus viridis is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
