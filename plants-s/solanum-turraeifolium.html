Solanum turraeifolium

Solanum turraeifolium, commonly known as the Turraeifolium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
