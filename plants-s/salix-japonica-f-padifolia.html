Salix japonica f. padifolia

Salix japonica f. padifolia, commonly known as Japanese weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green during the growing season and turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its graceful appearance and is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils.
