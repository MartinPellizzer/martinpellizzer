Sedum vaginatum

Sedum vaginatum, commonly known as the silver rose or desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
