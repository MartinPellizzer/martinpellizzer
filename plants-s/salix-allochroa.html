Salix allochroa

Salix allochroa, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
