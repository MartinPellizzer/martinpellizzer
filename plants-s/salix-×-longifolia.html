Salix × longifolia

Salix × longifolia, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

The bark is smooth and gray, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
