Spiraea pulchra

Spiraea pulchra, commonly known as the showy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is dark green, turning reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea pulchra is often used in landscaping for its attractive blooms and moderate maintenance requirements.
