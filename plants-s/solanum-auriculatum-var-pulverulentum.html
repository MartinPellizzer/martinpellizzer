Solanum auriculatum var. pulverulentum

Solanum auriculatum var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are berries that start green and ripen to a deep red, containing toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.
