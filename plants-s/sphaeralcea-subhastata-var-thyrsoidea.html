Sphaeralcea subhastata var. thyrsoidea

Sphaeralcea subhastata var. thyrsoidea, commonly known as the thyrsoidean globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, erect stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
