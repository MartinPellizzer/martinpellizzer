Salix × vitellina

Salix × vitellina, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive drooping appearance. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
