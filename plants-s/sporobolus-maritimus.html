Sporobolus maritimus

Sporobolus maritimus, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas quickly.

Sporobolus maritimus plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
