Senecio pinnatus var. sulfureus

Senecio pinnatus var. sulfureus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
