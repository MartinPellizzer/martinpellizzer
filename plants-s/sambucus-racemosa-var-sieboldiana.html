Sambucus racemosa var. sieboldiana

Sambucus racemosa var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets that are elliptical and serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
