Stipa variegata

Stipa variegata, commonly known as the variegated feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silver-white or grayish-green leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors. The plant produces feathery plumes of light yellow or brown flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stipa variegata is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative foliage and drought tolerance.
