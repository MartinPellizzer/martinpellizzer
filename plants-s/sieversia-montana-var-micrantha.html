Sieversia montana var. micrantha

Sieversia montana var. micrantha, commonly known as the Sierra mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. It has dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
