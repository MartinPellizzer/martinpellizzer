Silene pogonocalyx

Silene pogonocalyx, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Silene pogonocalyx is an important species for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
