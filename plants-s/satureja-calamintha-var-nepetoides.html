Satureja calamintha var. nepetoides

Satureja calamintha var. nepetoides, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive mint-like scent, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its foliage is often used to flavor dishes or brewed into teas.
