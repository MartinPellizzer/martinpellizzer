Sphaeralcea floribunda var. pauciflora

Sphaeralcea floribunda var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.
