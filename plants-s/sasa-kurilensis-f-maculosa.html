Sasa kurilensis f. maculosa

Sasa kurilensis f. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked with contrasting colors, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The ornamental value of Sasa kurilensis f. maculosa makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy ornamental plant.
