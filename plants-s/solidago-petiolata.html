Solidago petiolata

Solidago petiolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinct central vein.

Solidago petiolata plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
