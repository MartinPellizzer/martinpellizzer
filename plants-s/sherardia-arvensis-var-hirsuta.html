Sherardia arvensis var. hirsuta

Sherardia arvensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy field gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in wet to moderately wet soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent yellow throat, blooming from July to September.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
