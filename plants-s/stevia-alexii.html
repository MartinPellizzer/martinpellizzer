Stevia alexii

Stevia alexii, commonly known as the Mexican stevia, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food products. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia alexii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
