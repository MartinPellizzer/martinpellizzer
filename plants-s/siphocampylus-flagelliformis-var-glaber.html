Siphocampylus flagelliformis var. glaber

Siphocampylus flagelliformis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in opposite pairs along the stem.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
