Solanum subumbellatum

Solanum subumbellatum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
