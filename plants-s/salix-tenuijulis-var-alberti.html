Salix tenuijulis var. alberti

Salix tenuijulis var. alberti, commonly known as the Albert's willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
