Senecio croceus

Senecio croceus, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disc of smaller, yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Senecio croceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
