Statice anfracta

Statice anfracta, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Statice anfracta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
