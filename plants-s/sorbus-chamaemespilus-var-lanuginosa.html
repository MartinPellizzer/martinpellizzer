Sorbus chamaemespilus var. lanuginosa

Sorbus chamaemespilus var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly-gray pubescence covering the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a pale underside. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
