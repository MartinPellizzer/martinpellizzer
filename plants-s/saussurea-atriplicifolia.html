Saussurea atriplicifolia

Saussurea atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Indian ginseng or the Himalayan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
