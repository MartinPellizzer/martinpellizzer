Salvia paohsingensis

Salvia paohsingensis, commonly known as the Hainan sage, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within subtropical forests. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with two-lipped petals typical of the genus Salvia.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
