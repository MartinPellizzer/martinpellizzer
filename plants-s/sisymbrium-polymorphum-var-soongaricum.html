Sisymbrium polymorphum var. soongaricum

Sisymbrium polymorphum var. soongaricum, commonly known as the soongaric wild radish, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.
