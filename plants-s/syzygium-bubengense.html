Syzygium bubengense

Syzygium bubengense, commonly known as the Bubeng Mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when mature, often used in local traditional medicine.

Syzygium bubengense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
