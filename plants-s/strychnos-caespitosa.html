Strychnos caespitosa

Strychnos caespitosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or African sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and possesses small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Strychnos caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
