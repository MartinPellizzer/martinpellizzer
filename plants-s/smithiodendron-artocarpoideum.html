Smithiodendron artocarpoideum

Smithiodendron artocarpoideum, commonly known as the golden fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its edible fruit, which resembles a small fig and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Smithiodendron artocarpoideum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit yield.
