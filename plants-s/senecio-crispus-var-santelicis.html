Senecio crispus var. santelicis

Senecio crispus var. santelicis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
