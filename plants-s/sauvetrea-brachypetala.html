Sauvetrea brachypetala

Sauvetrea brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
