Solanum inaequilaterale

Solanum inaequilaterale, commonly known as the unequal-armed nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three, followed by berries that range in color from green to red.

It is often found in humid, tropical environments and is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
