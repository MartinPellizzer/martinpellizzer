Solanum demissum f. tlaxpehualcoense

Solanum demissum f. tlaxpehualcoense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces violet-colored berries that mature to a dark purple, which are typically consumed by birds. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and emit a mild fragrance when crushed.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological significance.
