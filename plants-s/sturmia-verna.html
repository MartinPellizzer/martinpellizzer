Sturmia verna

Sturmia verna, commonly known as spring sturmia, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Sturmia verna prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
