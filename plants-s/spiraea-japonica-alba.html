Spiraea japonica alba

Spiraea japonica alba, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of small, white flowers during the spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea japonica alba is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
