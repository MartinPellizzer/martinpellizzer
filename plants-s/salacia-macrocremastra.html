Salacia macrocremastra

Salacia macrocremastra, commonly known as the large-leaf salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
