Sparganium simplex proles longissimum

Sparganium simplex proles longissimum, commonly known as the slender bur reed, is a aquatic plant native to wetlands and shallow waters.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, followed by spiky, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating on the water surface.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
