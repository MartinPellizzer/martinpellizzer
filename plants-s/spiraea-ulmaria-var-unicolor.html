Spiraea ulmaria var. unicolor

Spiraea ulmaria var. unicolor, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
