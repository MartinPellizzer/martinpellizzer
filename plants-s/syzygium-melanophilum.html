Syzygium melanophilum

Syzygium melanophilum, commonly known as the black-leaf clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Syzygium melanophilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
