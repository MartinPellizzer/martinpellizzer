Salacia columna

Salacia columna, commonly known as the cliff or rock salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
