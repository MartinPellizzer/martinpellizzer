Scorzonera hispanica var. longifolia

Scorzonera hispanica var. longifolia, commonly known as Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible properties. The plant produces basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent. Its yellow flowers are borne in clusters and have five petals, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Scorzonera hispanica var. longifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
