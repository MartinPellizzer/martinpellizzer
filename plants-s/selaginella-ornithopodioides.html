Selaginella ornithopodioides

Selaginella ornithopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of its branches.

Selaginella ornithopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
