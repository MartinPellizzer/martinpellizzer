Saraca harmandiana

Saraca harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it is important to note that it may have invasive tendencies in certain regions.
