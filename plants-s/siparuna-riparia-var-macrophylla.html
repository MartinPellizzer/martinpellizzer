Siparuna riparia var. macrophylla

Siparuna riparia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed siparuna, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
