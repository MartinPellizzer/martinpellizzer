Stachys longifolia

Stachys longifolia, commonly known as slender wood betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Stachys longifolia is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
