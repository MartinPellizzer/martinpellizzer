Syzygium vanderwateri

Syzygium vanderwateri, commonly known as the Vanderwater's syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
