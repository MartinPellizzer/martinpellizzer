Sesbania marchionica

Sesbania marchionica, commonly known as the yellow burr gorse, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Sesbania marchionica is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
