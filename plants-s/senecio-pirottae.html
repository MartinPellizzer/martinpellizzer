Senecio pirottae

Senecio pirottae, commonly known as the Pirotta's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a single head, surrounded by bracts.

Senecio pirottae is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
