Solenopsis laurentia subsp. parvula

Solenopsis laurentia subsp.

parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered laurentia, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
