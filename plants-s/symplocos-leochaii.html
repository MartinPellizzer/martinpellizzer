Symplocos leochaii

Symplocos leochaii, commonly known as the purple-leafed evergreen, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn reddish or purplish in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and conservation purposes.
