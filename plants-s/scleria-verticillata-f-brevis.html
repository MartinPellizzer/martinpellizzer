Scleria verticillata f. brevis

Scleria verticillata f. brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulrush, is a variant of the broad-leaf bulrush, characterized by its shorter, more compact leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist or aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small white or pale blue flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
