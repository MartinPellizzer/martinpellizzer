Selaginella mucronata

Selaginella mucronata, commonly known as the spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a dense, cushion-like growth form and small, scale-like leaves. It possesses a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the spore-producing organs.

Selaginella mucronata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
