Salix × tsugaluensis var. latifolia

Salix × tsugaluensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a silvery-green underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
