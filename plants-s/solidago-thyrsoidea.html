Solidago thyrsoidea

Solidago thyrsoidea, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Solidago thyrsoidea plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
