Stuckenia macrocarpa

Stuckenia macrocarpa, commonly known as greater pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that often grow in shallow waters. The plant produces small, green, oval leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense spikes, and typically bloom in late summer.

Stuckenia macrocarpa plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
