Scutellaria pekinensis var. ussuriensis

Scutellaria pekinensis var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri scullion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive calyx and bracts.
