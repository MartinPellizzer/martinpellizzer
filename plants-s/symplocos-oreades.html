Symplocos oreades

Symplocos oreades, commonly known as mountain beauty, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn bright red in the fall.

Symplocos oreades is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
