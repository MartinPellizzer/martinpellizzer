Sternbergia vernalis

Sternbergia vernalis, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow or golden-orange flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its bulbs are relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed.

Sternbergia vernalis is valued for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal in gardens.
