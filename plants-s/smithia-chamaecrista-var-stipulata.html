Smithia chamaecrista var. stipulata

Smithia chamaecrista var. stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of climbing plants and their adaptations.
