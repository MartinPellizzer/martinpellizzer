Sauromatum guttatum var. punctatum

Sauromatum guttatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces striking purple or red flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a showy spathe. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses for its dramatic foliage and floral display.
