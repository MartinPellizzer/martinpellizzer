Scabiosa maritima var. atropurpurea

Scabiosa maritima var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The leaves are typically gray-green, hairy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
