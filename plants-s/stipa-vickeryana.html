Stipa vickeryana

Stipa vickeryana, commonly known as the Vickery's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are often a striking silver or white, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Stipa vickeryana is valued for its low maintenance and ability to provide habitat for small wildlife.
