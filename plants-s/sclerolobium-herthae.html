Sclerolobium herthae

Sclerolobium herthae, commonly known as the Hertha's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Sclerolobium herthae is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
