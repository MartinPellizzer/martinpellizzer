Salsola ferganica

Salsola ferganica, commonly known as Fergana saltwort, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline or alkaline environments, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water, and it produces small, greenish flowers. It is native to Central Asia and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.

Salsola ferganica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
