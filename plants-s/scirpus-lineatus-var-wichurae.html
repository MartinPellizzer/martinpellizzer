Scirpus lineatus var. wichurae

Scirpus lineatus var. wichurae, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life makes it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
