Senecio decurrens

Senecio decurrens, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio decurrens is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a decorative plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
