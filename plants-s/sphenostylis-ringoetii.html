Sphenostylis ringoetii

Sphenostylis ringoetii, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

Sphenostylis ringoetii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
