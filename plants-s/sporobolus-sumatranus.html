Sporobolus sumatranus

Sporobolus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in dry, sandy habitats. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Sporobolus sumatranus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy soils.
