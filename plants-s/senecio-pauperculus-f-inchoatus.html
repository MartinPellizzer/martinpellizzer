Senecio pauperculus f. inchoatus

Senecio pauperculus f. inchoatus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features small, pale green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
