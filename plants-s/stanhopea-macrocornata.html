Stanhopea macrocornata

Stanhopea macrocornata, commonly known as the large-fruited stanhopea, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with red and orange markings. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stanhopea macrocornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
