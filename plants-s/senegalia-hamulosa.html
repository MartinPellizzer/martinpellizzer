Senegalia hamulosa

Senegalia hamulosa, commonly known as the burro bush, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds with hooked tendrils, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
