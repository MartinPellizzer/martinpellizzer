Serrafalcus macrostachys var. secundus

Serrafalcus macrostachys var. secundus, commonly known as the secondary serrated fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, serrated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with a unique, recurved shape and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

This variant is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
