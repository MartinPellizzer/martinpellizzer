Senecio johnstonii subsp. battiscombei

Senecio johnstonii subsp.

battiscombei, commonly known as the Johnston's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky, arid environments.

The plant features a short, thick stem with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

It is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
