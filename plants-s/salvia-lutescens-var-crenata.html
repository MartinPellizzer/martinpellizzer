Salvia lutescens var. crenata

Salvia lutescens var. crenata, commonly known as the curled-leaved sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive curled margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
