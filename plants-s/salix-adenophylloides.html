Salix adenophylloides

Salix adenophylloides, commonly known as the alpine willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Salix adenophylloides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
