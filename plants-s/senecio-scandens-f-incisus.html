Senecio scandens f. incisus

Senecio scandens f. incisus, commonly known as the serrated ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
