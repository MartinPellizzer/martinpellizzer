Scabiosa gramuntia subsp. tomentosa

Scabiosa gramuntia subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
