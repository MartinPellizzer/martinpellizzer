Stachys venezuelana

Stachys venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan betony, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in summer.

Stachys venezuelana is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
