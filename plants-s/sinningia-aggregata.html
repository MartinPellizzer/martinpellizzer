Sinningia aggregata

Sinningia aggregata, commonly known as the violet, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant features tubular flowers that are often visited by hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Sinningia aggregata is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
