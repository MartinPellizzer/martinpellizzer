Solanum peranomalum

Solanum peranomalum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces white or pale yellow flowers.

Solanum peranomalum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
