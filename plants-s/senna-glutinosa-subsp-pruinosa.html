Senna glutinosa subsp. pruinosa

Senna glutinosa subsp.

pruinosa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, typically during the rainy season. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
