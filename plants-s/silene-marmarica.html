Silene marmarica

Silene marmarica, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene marmarica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
