Saccolabium perpusillum

Saccolabium perpusillum, commonly known as the small-flowered saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces pods that contain seeds.

It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland habitats.
