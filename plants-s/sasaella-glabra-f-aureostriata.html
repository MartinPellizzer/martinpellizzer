Sasaella glabra f. aureostriata

Sasaella glabra f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-striped sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub-like perennial with slender, bamboo-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and have a pale yellow stripe along the midrib, giving it its distinctive appearance. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage.
