Salix reinii subf. cyclophylloides

Salix reinii subf.

cyclophylloides, commonly known as the cyclophylloides willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, typical of the Salicaceae family, which contain both male and female flowers. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing slopes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
