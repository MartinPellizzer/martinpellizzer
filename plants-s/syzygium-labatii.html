Syzygium labatii

Syzygium labatii, commonly known as the Labat's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to New Guinea and the Moluccas, belonging to the Myrtaceae family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible, apple-like fruits that are sweet and aromatic.

Syzygium labatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
