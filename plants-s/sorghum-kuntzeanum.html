Sorghum kuntzeanum

Sorghum kuntzeanum, commonly known as Kuntze's sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sorghum kuntzeanum is sometimes used as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
