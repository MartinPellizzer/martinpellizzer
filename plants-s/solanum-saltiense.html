Solanum saltiense

Solanum saltiense, commonly known as the Salties nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming at night. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Solanum saltiense is often found in rocky outcrops and along coastal areas.
