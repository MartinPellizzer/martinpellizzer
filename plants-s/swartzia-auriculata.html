Swartzia auriculata

Swartzia auriculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Swartzia auriculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
