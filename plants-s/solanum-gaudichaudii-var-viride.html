Solanum gaudichaudii var. viride

Solanum gaudichaudii var. viride, commonly known as green nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its greenish-yellow flowers and smooth, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its berries, which are small and green when mature, contain toxic alkaloids and are not suitable for human consumption.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
