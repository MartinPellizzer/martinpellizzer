Silene scouleri var. concolor

Silene scouleri var. concolor, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
