Sterculia elata

Sterculia elata, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
