Statice minuta

Statice minuta, commonly known as dwarf statice, is a small flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the flowers appearing in shades of pink, red, and white.

Statice minuta is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in coastal and sandy environments.
