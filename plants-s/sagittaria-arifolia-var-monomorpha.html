Sagittaria arifolia var. monomorpha

Sagittaria arifolia var. monomorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late summer.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
