Syzygium sublaetum

Syzygium sublaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are narrow and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, subacid berries. Syzygium sublaetum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
