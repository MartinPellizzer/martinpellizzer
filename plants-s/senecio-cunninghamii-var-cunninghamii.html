Senecio cunninghamii var. cunninghamii

Senecio cunninghamii var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and woolly, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It is an erect, herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with small, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
