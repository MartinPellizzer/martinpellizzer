Syzygium longistylum

Syzygium longistylum, commonly known as the longistylum apple or long-stemmed apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, round fruits with a sweet and tart flavor, similar to lychee. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Syzygium longistylum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
