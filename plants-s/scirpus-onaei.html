Scirpus onaei

Scirpus onaei, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually green to reddish in color and appear at the top of the stems.

Scirpus onaei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
