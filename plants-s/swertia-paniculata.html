Swertia paniculata

Swertia paniculata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Swertia paniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
