Salacia simtata

Salacia simtata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves seasonally, and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, while its leaves are alternate and ovate in shape.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
