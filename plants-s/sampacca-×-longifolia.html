Sampacca × longifolia

Sampacca × longifolia, commonly known as the hybrid sampacca, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom in spring. Sampacca × longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.
