Salvia teresae

Salvia teresae, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Salvia teresae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
