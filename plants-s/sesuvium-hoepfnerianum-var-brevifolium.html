Sesuvium hoepfnerianum var. brevifolium

Sesuvium hoepfnerianum var. brevifolium, commonly known as coastal sandplum, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand salty air. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular ornamental plant in arid and coastal landscapes.
