Stachys rosmarinifolia

Stachys rosmarinifolia, commonly known as lemon balm mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a subtle lemony scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Stachys rosmarinifolia is often used in herbal teas and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential calming effects.
