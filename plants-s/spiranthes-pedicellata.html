Spiranthes pedicellata

Spiranthes pedicellata, commonly known as the pink spikerush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are small and white or pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
