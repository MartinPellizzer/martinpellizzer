Saxofridericia petiolata

Saxofridericia petiolata, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, waxy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that provide year-round interest.

The waxflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
