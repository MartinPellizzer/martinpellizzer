Spiesia dubia

Spiesia dubia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a compact, low-growing structure with fleshy leaves adapted to store water. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a ghostly appearance.

Spiesia dubia is a rare and specialized species, thriving in extreme environmental conditions.
