Satureja oligantha

Satureja oligantha, commonly known as mountain savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces tiny, pink or purple flowers in the summer. Satureja oligantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and potential therapeutic uses.
