Sphaeralcea axillaris var. violacea

Sphaeralcea axillaris var. violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
