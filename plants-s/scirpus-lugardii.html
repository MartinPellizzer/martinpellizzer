Scirpus lugardii

Scirpus lugardii, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale green flowers at the top of its stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies in wetland environments.

Scirpus lugardii is well adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
