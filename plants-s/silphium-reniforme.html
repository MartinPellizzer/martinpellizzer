Silphium reniforme

Silphium reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf compass plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the central and eastern United States, where it thrives in prairies and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Silphium reniforme is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
