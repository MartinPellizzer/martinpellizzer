Statice cantabrica subsp. pubinervis

Statice cantabrica subsp.

pubinervis, commonly known as the Cantabrian statice, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with similar structural and ecological traits.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
