Scleranthus polycarpos

Scleranthus polycarpos, commonly known as the many-seeded thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs, and its small, pale yellow flowers appear in clusters. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Scleranthus polycarpos is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
