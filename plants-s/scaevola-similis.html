Scaevola similis

Scaevola similis, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical flowers, which are typically blue or violet in color. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Scaevola similis is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
