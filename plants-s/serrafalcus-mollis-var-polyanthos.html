Serrafalcus mollis var. polyanthos

Serrafalcus mollis var. polyanthos, commonly known as the Polyanthus Serrafalcus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in multiple colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are often arranged in inflorescences.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental gardening.
