Sorbus chamaemespilus var. tomentosa

Sorbus chamaemespilus var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by white to reddish berries.

This plant is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
