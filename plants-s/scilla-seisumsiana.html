Scilla seisumsiana

Scilla seisumsiana, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla seisumsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
