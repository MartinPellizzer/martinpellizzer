Sterculia javanica

Sterculia javanica, commonly known as the Java sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds surrounded by a fibrous aril. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
