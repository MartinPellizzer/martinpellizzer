Sanicula officinarum

Sanicula officinarum, commonly known as common sanicle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a compound umbel, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sanicula officinarum prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
