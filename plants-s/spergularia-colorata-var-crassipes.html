Spergularia colorata var. crassipes

Spergularia colorata var. crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
