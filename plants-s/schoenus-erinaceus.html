Schoenus erinaceus

Schoenus erinaceus, commonly known as the bristly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-ranked spikelets that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soil conditions.

Schoenus erinaceus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
