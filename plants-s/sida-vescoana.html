Sida vescoana

Sida vescoana, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sida vescoana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
