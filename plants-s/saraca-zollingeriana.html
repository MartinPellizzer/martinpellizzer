Saraca zollingeriana

Saraca zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has pinnate leaves with an odd number of leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Saraca zollingeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
