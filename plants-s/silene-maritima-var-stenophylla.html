Silene maritima var. stenophylla

Silene maritima var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in coastal habitats.

Silene maritima var. stenophylla plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
