Sempervivum × alidae

Sempervivum × alidae, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant that belongs to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Sempervivum tectorum and Sempervivum montanum, resulting in a plant with unique foliage characteristics. The leaves are typically rosette-shaped, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its ability to thrive in low light and well-draining soil makes it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
