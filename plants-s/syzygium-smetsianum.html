Syzygium smetsianum

Syzygium smetsianum, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated edge.

Syzygium smetsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
