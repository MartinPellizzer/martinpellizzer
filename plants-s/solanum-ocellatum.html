Solanum ocellatum

Solanum ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and ovate, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum ocellatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
