Solanum americanum var. odishense

Solanum americanum var. odishense, commonly known as the Odisha nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, a small berry, turns red when mature and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and is found in tropical and subtropical regions.
