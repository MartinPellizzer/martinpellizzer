Silene pseudotenuis

Silene pseudotenuis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Silene pseudotenuis is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
