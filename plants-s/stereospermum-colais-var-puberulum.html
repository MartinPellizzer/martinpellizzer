Stereospermum colais var. puberulum

Stereospermum colais var. puberulum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
