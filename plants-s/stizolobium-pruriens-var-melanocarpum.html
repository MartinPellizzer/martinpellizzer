Stizolobium pruriens var. melanocarpum

Stizolobium pruriens var. melanocarpum, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces a resin that can cause skin irritation, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
