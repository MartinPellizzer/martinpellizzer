Sophora saxicola

Sophora saxicola, commonly known as the rock sophora, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and China.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated seed pods. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sophora saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
