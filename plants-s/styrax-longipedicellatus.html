Styrax longipedicellatus

Styrax longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed fringed woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Styrax longipedicellatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
