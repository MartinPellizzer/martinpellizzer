Spartina Pectinata Var. Suttiei

Salt marsh cordgrass, scientifically known as Spartina pectinata var. suttiei, is a hardy grass species that thrives in the brackish waters of coastal salt marshes. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and filtering pollutants from tidal waters. Its dense growth helps prevent erosion and provides habitat for a variety of marine and terrestrial wildlife. Adapted to withstand regular flooding and high salinity, it is a keystone species in estuarine ecosystems. Despite its ecological importance, it faces threats from habitat loss and invasive species competition.
