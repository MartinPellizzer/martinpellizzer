Salvia parciflora

Salvia parciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features gray-green, narrow leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage.

Salvia parciflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
