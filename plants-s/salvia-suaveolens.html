Salvia suaveolens

Salvia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet sage or scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Salvia suaveolens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
