Sedum jahandiezii

Sedum jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically green, sometimes with a bluish tint, and are arranged in a dense, compact form.

Sedum jahandiezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
