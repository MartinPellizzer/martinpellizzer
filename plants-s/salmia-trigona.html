Salmia trigona

Salmia trigona, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. Salmia trigona typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features make it a striking and sought-after specimen in horticulture and botanical gardens.
