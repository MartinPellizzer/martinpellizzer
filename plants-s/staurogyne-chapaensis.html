Staurogyne chapaensis

Staurogyne chapaensis, commonly known as the Chapa's waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Neotropical region.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. The plant has submerged leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

Staurogyne chapaensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
