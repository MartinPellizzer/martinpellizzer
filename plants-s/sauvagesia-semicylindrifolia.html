Sauvagesia semicylindrifolia

Sauvagesia semicylindrifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed sauvagesia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, slightly ribbed stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
