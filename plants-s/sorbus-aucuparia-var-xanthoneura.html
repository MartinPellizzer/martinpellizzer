Sorbus aucuparia var. xanthoneura

Sorbus aucuparia var. xanthoneura, commonly known as the yellow-leafed rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree characterized by its striking yellow foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seasonal color changes.
