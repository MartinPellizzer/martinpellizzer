Solenopsis corsica

Solenopsis corsica, commonly known as the Corsican ant.

It is a species of ant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. The plant is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Solenopsis corsica plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil aeration and serving as a food source for various insects and small animals.
