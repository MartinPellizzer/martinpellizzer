Salsola suaeda

Salsola suaeda, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and can tolerate high salinity levels in the soil.

Salsola suaeda is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value and hardiness.
