Solidago armena

Solidago armena, commonly known as Armenian goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago armena is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
