Solanum pterocaulon var. deppei

Solanum pterocaulon var. deppei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are typically edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
