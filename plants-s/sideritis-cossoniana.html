Sideritis cossoniana

Sideritis cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct minty aroma, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Sideritis cossoniana is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
