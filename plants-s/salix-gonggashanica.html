Salix gonggashanica

Salix gonggashanica, commonly known as Gonggashan willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, forested areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkins, which are typical of willow species. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
