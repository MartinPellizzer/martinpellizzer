Solanum distichum

Solanum distichum, commonly known as the two-row nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Solanum distichum is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants.
