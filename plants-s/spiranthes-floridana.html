Spiranthes floridana

Spiranthes floridana, commonly known as Florida ladies' tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is listed as threatened due to habitat loss and is often found in association with specific soil types and microclimates.
