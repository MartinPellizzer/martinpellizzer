Spartium monospermum

Spartium monospermum, commonly known as white needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are encased in a tough, pod-like structure, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Spartium monospermum is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sandy soils.
