Stevia totalcoana

Stevia totalcoana, commonly known as the Brazilian sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds.

Stevia totalcoana is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and economic value.
