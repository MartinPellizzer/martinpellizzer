Sida urens var. aurea

Sida urens var. aurea, commonly known as golden knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and serrated edges. This species is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, open environments.

It plays a role in ecological succession and serves as a food source for various insects and birds.
