Salvia chunganensis

Salvia chunganensis, commonly known as the Chungan sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Salvia chunganensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
