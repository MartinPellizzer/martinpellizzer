Serjania rubicaulis

Serjania rubicaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
