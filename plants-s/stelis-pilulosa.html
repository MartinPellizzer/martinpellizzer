Stelis pilulosa

Stelis pilulosa, commonly known as the pill-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a single, fleshy, pill-like stem. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Stelis pilulosa is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
