Searsia nitida

Searsia nitida, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, finely hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Searsia nitida is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and adaptability to different climates.
