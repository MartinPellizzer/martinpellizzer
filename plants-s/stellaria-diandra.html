Stellaria diandra

Stellaria diandra, commonly known as the starwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other plants like carnations and sweet William. The plant is characterized by its small, star-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Stellaria diandra typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil.
