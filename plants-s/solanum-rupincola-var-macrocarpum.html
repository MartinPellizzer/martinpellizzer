Solanum rupincola var. macrocarpum

Solanum rupincola var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
