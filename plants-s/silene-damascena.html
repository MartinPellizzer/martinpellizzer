Silene damascena

Silene damascena, commonly known as the Damascus poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are five-petaled and feature a prominent central stigma, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Silene damascena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
