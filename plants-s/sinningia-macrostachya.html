Sinningia macrostachya

Sinningia macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed gloxinia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Sinningia macrostachya thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
