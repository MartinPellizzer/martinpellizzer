Solanum heterantherum

Solanum heterantherum, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, with heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, featuring five petals and a distinctive spur at the base of the pistil.

Solanum heterantherum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
