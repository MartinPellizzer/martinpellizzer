Solanum spiraeoides

Solanum spiraeoides, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

Solanum spiraeoides is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
