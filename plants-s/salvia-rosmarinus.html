Salvia rosmarinus

Salvia rosmarinus, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves that are needle-like and gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple, depending on the variety. Rosemary thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy, cooking, and traditional medicine.
