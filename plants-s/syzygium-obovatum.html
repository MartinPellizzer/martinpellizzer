Syzygium obovatum

Syzygium obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
