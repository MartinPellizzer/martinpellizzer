Sporobolus cryptandrus var. giganteus

Sporobolus cryptandrus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant sand grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical seed heads and fibrous root system, which help stabilize sandy soils. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and its resilience to drought conditions.
