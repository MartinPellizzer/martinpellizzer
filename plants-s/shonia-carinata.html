Shonia carinata

Shonia carinata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Shonia carinata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
