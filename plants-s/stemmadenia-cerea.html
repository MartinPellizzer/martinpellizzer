Stemmadenia cerea

Stemmadenia cerea, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It has a slender, smooth stem with a reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.
