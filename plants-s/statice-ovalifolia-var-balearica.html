Statice ovalifolia var. balearica

Statice ovalifolia var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
