Salix rorida f. roridiformis

Salix rorida f. roridiformis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, often found near water sources such as rivers and streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins during the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
