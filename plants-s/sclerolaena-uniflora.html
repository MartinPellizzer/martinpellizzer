Sclerolaena uniflora

Sclerolaena uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sclerolaena uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
