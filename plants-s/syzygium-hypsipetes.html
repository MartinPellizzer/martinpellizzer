Syzygium hypsipetes

Syzygium hypsipetes, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the summer months. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, leathery skin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
