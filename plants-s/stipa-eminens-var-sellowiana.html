Stipa eminens var. sellowiana

Stipa eminens var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and compact growth habit make it an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
