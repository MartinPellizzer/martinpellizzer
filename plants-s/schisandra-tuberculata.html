Schisandra tuberculata

Schisandra tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-fruited fivefinger, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, berry-like fruits. Schisandra tuberculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its berries contain compounds that may have antioxidant properties.
