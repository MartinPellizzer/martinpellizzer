Salix iranica

Salix iranica, commonly known as the Iranian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are catkins, which are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Salix iranica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for ecological and botanical studies.
