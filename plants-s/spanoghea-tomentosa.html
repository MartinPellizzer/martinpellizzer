Spanoghea tomentosa

Spanoghea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly spanoghea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give it a woolly appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
