Salacia whytei var. whytei

Salacia whytei var. whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
