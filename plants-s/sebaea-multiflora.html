Sebaea multiflora

Sebaea multiflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has hairy leaves and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Sebaea multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
