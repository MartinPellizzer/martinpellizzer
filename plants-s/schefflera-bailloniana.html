Schefflera bailloniana

Schefflera bailloniana, commonly known as the African schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaflets, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of opposite leaflets, typically ranging from 10 to 20 pairs per leaf. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

Schefflera bailloniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
