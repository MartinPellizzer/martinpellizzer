Sphaeritis setigera

Sphaeritis setigera, commonly known as the clustered sphaeritid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sphaeritidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and distinctive, fleshy, orange to red berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Sphaeritis setigera is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
