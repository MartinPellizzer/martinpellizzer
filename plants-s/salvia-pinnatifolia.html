Salvia pinnatifolia

Salvia pinnatifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to gardens and landscapes.
