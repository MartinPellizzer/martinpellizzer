Sanguisorba bracteosa

Sanguisorba bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Sanguisorba bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
