Senecio × boiteaui

Senecio × boiteaui, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
