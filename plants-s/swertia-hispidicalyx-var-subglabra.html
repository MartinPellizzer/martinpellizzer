Swertia hispidicalyx var. subglabra

Swertia hispidicalyx var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a robust rhizome and lanceolate leaves that are generally smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from its varietal counterpart. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties, with traditional use in treating ailments such as fever and inflammation.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and pharmacological potential.
