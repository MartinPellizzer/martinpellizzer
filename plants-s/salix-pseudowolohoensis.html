Salix pseudowolohoensis

Salix pseudowolohoensis, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix pseudowolohoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
