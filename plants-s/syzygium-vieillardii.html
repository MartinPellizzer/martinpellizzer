Syzygium vieillardii

Syzygium vieillardii, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a strong aromatic resin.

Syzygium vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
