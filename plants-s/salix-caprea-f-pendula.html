Salix caprea f. pendula

Salix caprea f. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its cascading branches that dangle like curtains.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a naturally occurring form of the common willow, Salix caprea. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
