Styrax martii

Styrax martii, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

Styrax martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
