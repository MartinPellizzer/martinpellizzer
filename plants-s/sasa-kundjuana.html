Sasa kundjuana

Sasa kundjuana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact shrub-like structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.

Sasa kundjuana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for various garden settings.
