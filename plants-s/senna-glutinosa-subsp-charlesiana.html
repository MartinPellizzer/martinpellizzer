Senna glutinosa subsp. charlesiana

Senna glutinosa subsp.

charlesiana, commonly known as the Charles's senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
