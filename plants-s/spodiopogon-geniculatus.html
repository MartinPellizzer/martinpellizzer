Spodiopogon geniculatus

Spodiopogon geniculatus, commonly known as the bamboo grass or false bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
