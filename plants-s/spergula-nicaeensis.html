Spergula nicaeensis

Spergula nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its slender, triangular stems are covered in fine, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Spergula nicaeensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
