Stylagrostis phalaroides

Stylagrostis phalaroides, commonly known as the slenderleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
