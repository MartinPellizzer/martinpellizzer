Sasa paniculata var. oseana

Sasa paniculata var. oseana, commonly known as the Osean bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many bamboo species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
