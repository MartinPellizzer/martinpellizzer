Stachys triloba

Stachys triloba, commonly known as caraway weed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Stachys triloba is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
