Solanum galpinii

Solanum galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Solanum galpinii is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to arid environments.
