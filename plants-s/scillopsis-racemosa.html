Scillopsis racemosa

Scillopsis racemosa, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
