Salvia chalarothyrsa

Salvia chalarothyrsa, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems and produces spikes of purple to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
