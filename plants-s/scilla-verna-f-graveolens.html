Scilla verna f. graveolens

Scilla verna f. graveolens, commonly known as the sweet scilla, is a hybrid form of the spring snowflake.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe, characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

The fragrance of its flowers is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
