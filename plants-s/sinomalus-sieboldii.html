Sinomalus sieboldii

Sinomalus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese white wood, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
