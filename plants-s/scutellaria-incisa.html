Scutellaria incisa

Scutellaria incisa, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Scutellaria incisa belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic foliage and four-lobed flowers.
