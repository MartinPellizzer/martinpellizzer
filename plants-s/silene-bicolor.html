Silene bicolor

Silene bicolor, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to lavender flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in dry, well-drained soils. Silene bicolor is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
