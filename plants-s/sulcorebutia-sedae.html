Sulcorebutia sedae

Sulcorebutia sedae, commonly known as the Sede's sun rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Sulcorebutia sedae is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
