Selaginella xiphophylla

Selaginella xiphophylla, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, evergreen, needle-like leaves that form a dense, fern-like appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, typical of bryophytes and primitive vascular plants.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
