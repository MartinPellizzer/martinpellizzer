Silene pseudoindica

Silene pseudoindica, commonly known as the Indian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Silene pseudoindica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
