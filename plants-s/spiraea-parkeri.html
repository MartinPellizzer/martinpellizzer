Spiraea parkeri

Spiraea parkeri, commonly known as the rose willow or white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early to mid-spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea parkeri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
