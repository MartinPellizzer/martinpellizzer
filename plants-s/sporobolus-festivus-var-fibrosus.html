Sporobolus festivus var. fibrosus

Sporobolus festivus var. fibrosus, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fibrous, cylindrical seed heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
