Scorzonera virgata

Scorzonera virgata, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera virgata is also valued as a food plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
