Senecio haughtii

Senecio haughtii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Senecio haughtii is well adapted to drought conditions and is considered a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
