Scorzonera austriaca var. subacaulis

Scorzonera austriaca var. subacaulis, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are typically basal and compact. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is notable for being thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments.
