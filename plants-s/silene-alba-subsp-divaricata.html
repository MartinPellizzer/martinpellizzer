Silene alba subsp. divaricata

Silene alba subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Silene alba subsp.

divaricata is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
