Salvia paryskii

Salvia paryskii, commonly known as the Paryskii sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia paryskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
