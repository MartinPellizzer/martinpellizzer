Scutellaria discolor var. cyrtopoda

Scutellaria discolor var. cyrtopoda, commonly known as the purple skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, which attract various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
