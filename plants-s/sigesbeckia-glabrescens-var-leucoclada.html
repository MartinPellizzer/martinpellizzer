Sigesbeckia glabrescens var. leucoclada

Sigesbeckia glabrescens var. leucoclada, commonly known as the white-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
