Sulcorebutia crispata subsp. aureicapillata

Sulcorebutia crispata subsp.

aureicapillata, commonly known as the golden-haired sun cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to central Mexico. It features a dense rosette of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or yellowish in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
