Sesleria tenerrima

Sesleria tenerrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sesleria tenerrima is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes due to its hardy nature.
