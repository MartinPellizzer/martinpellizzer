Stachys ciliaris

Stachys ciliaris, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys ciliaris is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.
