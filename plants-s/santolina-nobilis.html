Santolina nobilis

Santolina nobilis, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that features silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, mint-like scent when crushed. Santolina nobilis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal remedies.
