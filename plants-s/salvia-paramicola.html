Salvia paramicola

Salvia paramicola, commonly known as the Ecuadorian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia paramicola thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
