Solanum pertenue

Solanum pertenue, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, egg-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum pertenue is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
