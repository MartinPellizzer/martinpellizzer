Solanum convolvuliforme

Solanum convolvuliforme, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Solanum convolvuliforme belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
