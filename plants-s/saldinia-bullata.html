Saldinia bullata

Saldinia bullata, commonly known as the bull's horn plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, yellow-orange seed heads that resemble horns, giving it its common name. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical shapes.

Saldinia bullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
