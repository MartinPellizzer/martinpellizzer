Saponaria hirsuta

Saponaria hirsuta, commonly known as hairy soapwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured look.

Saponaria hirsuta is known for its mild soap-like scent and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
