Solanum endopogon subsp. endopogon

Solanum endopogon subsp.

endopogon, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant produces small, rounded berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
