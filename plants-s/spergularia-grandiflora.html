Spergularia grandiflora

Spergularia grandiflora, commonly known as the sea sandspurre, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in sandy and saline environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh maritime conditions. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing dunes. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, reducing water loss in arid conditions.

Spergularia grandiflora is also notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
