Stipa caudata

Stipa caudata, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
