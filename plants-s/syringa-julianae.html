Syringa julianae

Syringa julianae, commonly known as the Julian syringa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Syringa julianae prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
