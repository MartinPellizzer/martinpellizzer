Sasa laevissima

Sasa laevissima, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its ability to form dense colonies.

Sasa laevissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
