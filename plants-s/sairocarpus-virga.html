Sairocarpus virga

Sairocarpus virga, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a unique plant with striking botanical features.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture. Its most notable feature is its large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
