Satyrium flavum

Satyrium flavum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
