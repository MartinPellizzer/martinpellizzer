Sapium caribaeum

Sapium caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous communities.
