Selago cucullata

Selago cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal or sandy environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Selago cucullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
