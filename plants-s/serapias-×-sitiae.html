Serapias × sitiae

Serapias × sitiae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in regions with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Serapias × sitiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
