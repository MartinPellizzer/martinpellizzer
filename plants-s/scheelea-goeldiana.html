Scheelea goeldiana

Scheelea goeldiana, commonly known as the Goeld's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Scheelea goeldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
