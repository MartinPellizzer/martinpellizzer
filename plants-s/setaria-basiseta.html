Setaria basiseta

Setaria basiseta, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with short, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Setaria basiseta is often used in native plant landscapes due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
