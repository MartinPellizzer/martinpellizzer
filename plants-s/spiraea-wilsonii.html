Spiraea wilsonii

Spiraea wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 feet and features aromatic, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The botanical features of Spiraea wilsonii make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
