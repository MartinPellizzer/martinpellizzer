Sedum hispanicum var. minus

Sedum hispanicum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser Spanish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
