Salix rehderiana

Salix rehderiana, commonly known as Rehder's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often found in moist, temperate environments.

Salix rehderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
