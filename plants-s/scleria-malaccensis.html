Scleria malaccensis

Scleria malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca sedge, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Scleria malaccensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
