Silene longiflora

Silene longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red, tubular flowers. Silene longiflora is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
