Silene debilis

Silene debilis, commonly known as the weak bladderwort, is a small flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, and it is often found in shallow water or damp environments. Silene debilis is a herbaceous annual that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its structure and habitat make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
