Senna hamersleyensis

Senna hamersleyensis, commonly known as the Hamersley senna, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Senna hamersleyensis is often found in sandy soils and is a component of the local flora in the Pilbara region.
