Schefflera pagiophylla

Schefflera pagiophylla, commonly known as the coastal schefflera, is a shrub native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dunes.

Schefflera pagiophylla is well adapted to salt spray and can withstand moderate drought conditions.
