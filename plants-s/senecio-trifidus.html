Senecio trifidus

Senecio trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Senecio trifidus is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
