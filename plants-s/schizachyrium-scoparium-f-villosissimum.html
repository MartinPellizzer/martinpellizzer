Schizachyrium scoparium f. villosissimum

Schizachyrium scoparium f. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a variant of the eastern grassland plant Schizachyrium scoparium.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, spikelike inflorescences that range in color from pale blue to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This ornamental grass is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
