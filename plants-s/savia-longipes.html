Savia longipes

Savia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed spurge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the spurge genus. It is native to parts of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
