Spiraea × ruberrima

Spiraea × ruberrima, commonly known as the rosehip spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically pink to red, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

Spiraea × ruberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
