Swertia corymbosa var. lawii

Swertia corymbosa var. lawii, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are clustered in cymes.

Swertia corymbosa var. lawii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
