Syzygium ciliatosetosum

Syzygium ciliatosetosum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed syzygium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
