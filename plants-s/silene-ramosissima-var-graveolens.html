Silene ramosissima var. graveolens

Silene ramosissima var. graveolens, commonly known as the fragrant willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

Silene ramosissima var. graveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
