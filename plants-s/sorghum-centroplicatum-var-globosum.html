Sorghum centroplicatum var. globosum

Sorghum centroplicatum var. globosum, commonly known as the globosum sorghum, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a tall, grass-like plant with long, narrow leaves and produces large, clustered inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in hot, dry climates. It is often cultivated for its edible grains and has some ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
