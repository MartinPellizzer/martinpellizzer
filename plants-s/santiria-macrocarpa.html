Santiria macrocarpa

Santiria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is known for its large, compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a long, woody pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Santiria macrocarpa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
