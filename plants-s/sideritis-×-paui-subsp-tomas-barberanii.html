Sideritis × paui subsp. tomas-barberanii

Sideritis × paui subsp.

tomas-barberanii, commonly known as the Corsican ironweed, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia, where it thrives in rocky and sunny habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
