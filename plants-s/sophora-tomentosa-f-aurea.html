Sophora tomentosa f. aurea

Sophora tomentosa f. aurea, commonly known as the golden sophora, is a variant of the silky sophora, native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
