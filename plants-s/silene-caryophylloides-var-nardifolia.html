Silene caryophylloides var. nardifolia

Silene caryophylloides var. nardifolia, commonly known as the sweet willow or nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems that bear small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage is aromatic and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
