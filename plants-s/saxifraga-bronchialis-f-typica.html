Saxifraga bronchialis f. typica

Saxifraga bronchialis f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the bronchial saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found growing at high altitudes, where it faces harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
