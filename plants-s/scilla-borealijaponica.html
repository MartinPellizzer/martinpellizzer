Scilla borealijaponica

Scilla borealijaponica, commonly known as the Japanese squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla borealijaponica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
