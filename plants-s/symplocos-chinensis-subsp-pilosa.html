Symplocos chinensis subsp. pilosa

Symplocos chinensis subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "pilosa.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are a food source for various birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
