Scirpus trachycaulos

Scirpus trachycaulos, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dense, tussock-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are stiff and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scirpus trachycaulos plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
