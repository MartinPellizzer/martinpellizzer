Salsola tamamschjanae

Salsola tamamschjanae, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils and low moisture conditions.

Salsola tamamschjanae plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
