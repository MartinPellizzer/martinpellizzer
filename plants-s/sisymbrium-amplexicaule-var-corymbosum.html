Sisymbrium amplexicaule var. corymbosum

Sisymbrium amplexicaule var. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive triangular seed pods, which are often used as a culinary herb in traditional cuisines. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
