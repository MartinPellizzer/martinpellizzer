Saccharum spontaneum var. roxburghii

Saccharum spontaneum var. roxburghii, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers at the top of its stems. It is a hardy species that thrives in wetland and marshy environments.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for sugarcane breeding programs.
