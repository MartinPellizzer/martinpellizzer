Sutera gracilis

Sutera gracilis, commonly known as the slender sutera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sutera gracilis typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a delicate appearance that makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
