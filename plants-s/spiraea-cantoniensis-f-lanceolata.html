Spiraea cantoniensis f. lanceolata

Spiraea cantoniensis f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast to its clusters of white, flat-topped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and delicate floral displays.
