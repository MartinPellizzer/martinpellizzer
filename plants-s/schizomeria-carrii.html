Schizomeria carrii

Schizomeria carrii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, particularly in California. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Schizomeria carrii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
