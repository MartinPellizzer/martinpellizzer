Sanguisorba myriophylla

Sanguisorba myriophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Sanguisorba myriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
