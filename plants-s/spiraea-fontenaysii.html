Spiraea fontenaysii

Spiraea fontenaysii, commonly known as Fontenay's weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture.
