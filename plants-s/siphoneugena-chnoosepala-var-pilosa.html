Siphoneugena chnoosepala var. pilosa

Siphoneugena chnoosepala var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy siphonous fern, is a unique variant of the siphonous fern family.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pilose, texture on its stems and leaves, which distinguishes it from other varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually elongated and may have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Botanically, it exhibits a complex structure with a well-developed rhizome and specialized reproductive structures.
