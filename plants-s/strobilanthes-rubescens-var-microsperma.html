Strobilanthes rubescens var. microsperma

Strobilanthes rubescens var. microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded flameflower, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
