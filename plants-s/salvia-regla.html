Salvia regla

Salvia regla, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia regla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
