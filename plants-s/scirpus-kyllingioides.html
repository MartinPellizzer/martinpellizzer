Scirpus kyllingioides

Scirpus kyllingioides, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Scirpus kyllingioides has tall, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to wetland stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
