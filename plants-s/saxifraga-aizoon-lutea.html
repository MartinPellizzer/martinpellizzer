Saxifraga aizoon lutea

Saxifraga aizoon lutea, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in harsh, arid environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky crevices and on mountain slopes, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Saxifraga aizoon lutea is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and resilience.
