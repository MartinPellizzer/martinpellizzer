Solanum undatifolium

Solanum undatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum undatifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
