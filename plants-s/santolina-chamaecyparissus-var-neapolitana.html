Santolina chamaecyparissus var. neapolitana

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its dense growth habit helps suppress weeds and provides habitat for beneficial insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in Mediterranean climates.
