Saxifraga aizoon var. brachyphylla

Saxifraga aizoon var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra foothill saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine, or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are often succulent, with short, thick stems that support small, clustered flowers. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Saxifragaceae family.

The species is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
