Solidago fasciculata

Solidago fasciculata, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces numerous small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Solidago fasciculata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
