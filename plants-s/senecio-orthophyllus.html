Senecio orthophyllus

Senecio orthophyllus, commonly known as the upright groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio orthophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
