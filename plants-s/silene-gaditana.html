Silene gaditana

Silene gaditana, commonly known as the Canary Islands catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Silene gaditana is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its native habitat.
