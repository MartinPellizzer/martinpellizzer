Senecio eurycephalus var. major

Senecio eurycephalus var. major, commonly known as the giant yellow gentian, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
