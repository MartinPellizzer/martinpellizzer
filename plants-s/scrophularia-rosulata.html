Scrophularia rosulata

Scrophularia rosulata, commonly known as the clustered figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

Scrophularia rosulata is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
