Stipa semenowii

Stipa semenowii, commonly known as the Semenow's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Stipa semenowii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
