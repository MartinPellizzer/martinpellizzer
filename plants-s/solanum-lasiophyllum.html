Solanum lasiophyllum

Solanum lasiophyllum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Solanum lasiophyllum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic compounds in its berries and leaves.
