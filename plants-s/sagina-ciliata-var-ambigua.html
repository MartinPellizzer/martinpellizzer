Sagina ciliata var. ambigua

Sagina ciliata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous snowflake plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with tiny, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
