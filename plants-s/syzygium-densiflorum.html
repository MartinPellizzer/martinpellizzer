Syzygium densiflorum

Syzygium densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance.

Syzygium densiflorum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
