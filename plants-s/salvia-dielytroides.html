Salvia dielytroides

Salvia dielytroides, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Salvia dielytroides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
