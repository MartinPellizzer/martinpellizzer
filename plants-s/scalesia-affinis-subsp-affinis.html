Scalesia affinis subsp. affinis

Scalesia affinis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the Galápagos tree, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Galápagos Islands. It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

affinis is also notable for its role in seed dispersal by birds and its adaptation to the unique environmental conditions of the Galápagos.
