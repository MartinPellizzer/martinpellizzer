Sisyrinchium sertiflorum

Sisyrinchium sertiflorum, commonly known as the desert bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sisyrinchium sertiflorum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
