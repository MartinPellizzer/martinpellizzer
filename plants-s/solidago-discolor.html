Solidago discolor

Solidago discolor, commonly known as the pale goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purple-tinged.

Solidago discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
