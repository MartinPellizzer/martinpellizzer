Sasa omokoensis var. pilosa

Sasa omokoensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant features slender, green shoots that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
