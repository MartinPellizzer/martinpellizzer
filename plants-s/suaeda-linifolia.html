Suaeda linifolia

Suaeda linifolia, commonly known as sea spurry, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other arid regions with high salinity. Its roots are adapted to absorb and tolerate high levels of salt, which is crucial for its survival.

Suaeda linifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
