Scrophularia eriocalyx

Scrophularia eriocalyx, commonly known as the woolly scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features distinctive hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular, pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Scrophularia eriocalyx is often used in phytotherapy for its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting effects.
