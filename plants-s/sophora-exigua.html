Sophora exigua

Sophora exigua, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Sophora exigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
