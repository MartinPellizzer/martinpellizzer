Senecio corsicus

Senecio corsicus, commonly known as Corsican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Senecio corsicus has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
