Setaria roemeri

Setaria roemeri, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water.

Setaria roemeri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
