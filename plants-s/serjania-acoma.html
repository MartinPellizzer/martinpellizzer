Serjania acoma

Serjania acoma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the pink serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Serjania acoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
