Soulangia cordata

Soulangia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomatous root system is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other familiar plants like taro and calla lilies.
