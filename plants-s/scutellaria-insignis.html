Scutellaria insignis

Scutellaria insignis, commonly known as the blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Scutellaria insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
