Scirpus atacamensis

Scirpus atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to two meters in height, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, adapted to the extreme conditions of its environment. Scirpus atacamensis has a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor, saline soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the desert ecosystem, providing habitat and stabilizing the soil.
