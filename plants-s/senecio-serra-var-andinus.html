Senecio serra var. andinus

Senecio serra var. andinus, commonly known as the Andean ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
