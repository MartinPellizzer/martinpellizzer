Scrophularia sambucifolia var. mellifera

Scrophularia sambucifolia var. mellifera, commonly known as the honey-leaved scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
