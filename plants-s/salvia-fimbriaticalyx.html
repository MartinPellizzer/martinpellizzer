Salvia fimbriaticalyx

Salvia fimbriaticalyx, commonly known as the fringed-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically violet to blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Salvia fimbriaticalyx is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
