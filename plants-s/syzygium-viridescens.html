Syzygium viridescens

Syzygium viridescens, commonly known as the green-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that range from dark green to a lighter green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, woody pods containing flat, seed-containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.

The tree thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
