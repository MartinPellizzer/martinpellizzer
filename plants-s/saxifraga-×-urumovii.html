Saxifraga × urumovii

Saxifraga × urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces showy, pink or white flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Saxifraga × urumovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
