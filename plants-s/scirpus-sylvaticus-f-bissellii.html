Scirpus sylvaticus f. bissellii

Scirpus sylvaticus f. bissellii, commonly known as the bissellii sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
