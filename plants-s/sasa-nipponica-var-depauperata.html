Sasa nipponica var. depauperata

Sasa nipponica var. depauperata, commonly known as the Japanese fern bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale green inflorescences that are not showy but are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
