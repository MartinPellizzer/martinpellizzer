Symplocos guillauminii

Symplocos guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos guillauminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
