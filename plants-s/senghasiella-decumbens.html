Senghasiella decumbens

Senghasiella decumbens, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in a prostrate or decumbent habit, spreading across the ground with hairy stems. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Senghasiella decumbens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
