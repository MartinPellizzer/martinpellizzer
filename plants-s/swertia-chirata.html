Swertia chirata

Swertia chirata, commonly known as Indian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Swertia chirata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
