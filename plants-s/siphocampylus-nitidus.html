Siphocampylus nitidus

Siphocampylus nitidus, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a loose spike, characteristic of the Orchidaceae family.
