Salvia tigrina

Salvia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger salvia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia tigrina is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
