Sicyos caumii

Sicyos caumii, commonly known as the Mexican bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its thick, ridged stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
