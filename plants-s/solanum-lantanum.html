Solanum lantanum

Solanum lantanum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in conical clusters, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain the seeds.

Solanum lantanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
