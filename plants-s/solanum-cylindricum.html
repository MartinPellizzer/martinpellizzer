Solanum cylindricum

Solanum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylinder nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its unique floral structure and chemical composition.
