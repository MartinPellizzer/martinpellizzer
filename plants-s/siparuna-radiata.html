Siparuna radiata

Siparuna radiata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Siparuna radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
