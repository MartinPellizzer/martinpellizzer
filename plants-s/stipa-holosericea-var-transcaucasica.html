Stipa holosericea var. transcaucasica

Stipa holosericea var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
