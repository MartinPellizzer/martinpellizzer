Silene venosa var. pubigera

Silene venosa var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
