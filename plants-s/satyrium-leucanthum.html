Satyrium leucanthum

Satyrium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
