Symphonia pauciflora

Symphonia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered symphonia, is a unique tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
