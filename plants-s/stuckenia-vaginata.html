Stuckenia vaginata

Stuckenia vaginata, commonly known as the broad-leaved waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Stuckenia vaginata is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
