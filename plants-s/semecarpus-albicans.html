Semecarpus albicans

Semecarpus albicans, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces distinctive, woody capsules that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its showy inflorescences and seasonal foliage changes.
