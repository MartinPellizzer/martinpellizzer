Solanum villosum var. alatum

Solanum villosum var. alatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic compounds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
