Spergula sativa

Spergula sativa, commonly known as common spurge, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Spergula sativa is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
