Sinapidendron angustifolium

Sinapidendron angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sinapidendron, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fibrous roots and woody stem make it a hardy species adapted to moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
