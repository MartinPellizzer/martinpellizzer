Semenovia vachanica

Semenovia vachanica, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by insects such as bees and wasps.

Semenovia vachanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
