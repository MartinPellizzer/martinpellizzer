Scoparia australis

Scoparia australis, commonly known as the Australian sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Scoparia australis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in dunes or open grasslands.
