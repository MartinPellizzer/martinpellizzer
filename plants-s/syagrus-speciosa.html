Syagrus speciosa

Syagrus speciosa, commonly known as the royal palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching fronds and a tall, unbranched trunk that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces large, creamy-white inflorescences that emerge from the crown, and its fruit is a bright orange, fleshy drupe containing a single seed. Syagrus speciosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to provide shade in landscaped areas.
