Silene longicaulis var. brachypoda

Silene longicaulis var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
