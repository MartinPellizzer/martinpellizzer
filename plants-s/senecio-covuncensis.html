Senecio covuncensis

Senecio covuncensis, commonly known as the Covuncensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in the Western Cape region. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
