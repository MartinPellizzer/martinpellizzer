Salvia aurasiaca

Salvia aurasiaca, commonly known as the Ethiopian sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Salvia aurasiaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
