Sesamum zanguebarium

Sesamum zanguebarium, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
