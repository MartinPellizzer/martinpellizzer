Senecio anethifolius subsp. anethifolius

Senecio anethifolius subsp.

anethifolius, commonly known as the celery-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of aromatic, finely divided leaves that resemble those of celery.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
