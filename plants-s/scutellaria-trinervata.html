Scutellaria trinervata

Scutellaria trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Scutellaria trinervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
