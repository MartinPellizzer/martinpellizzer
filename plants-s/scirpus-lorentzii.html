Scirpus lorentzii

Scirpus lorentzii, commonly known as Lorentz's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often reaching heights of up to two meters.

Scirpus lorentzii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
