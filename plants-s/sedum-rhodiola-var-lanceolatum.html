Sedum rhodiola var. lanceolatum

Sedum rhodiola var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh conditions, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
