Sedum aleurodes

Sedum aleurodes, commonly known as the silver sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
