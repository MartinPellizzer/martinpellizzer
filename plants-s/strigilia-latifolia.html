Strigilia latifolia

Strigilia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed strigil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically broad at the base and taper toward the tips. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. Its root system is fibrous and capable of storing nutrients, aiding in its survival in varied soil conditions.

Strigilia latifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
