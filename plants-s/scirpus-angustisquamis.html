Scirpus angustisquamis

Scirpus angustisquamis, commonly known as narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus angustisquamis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
