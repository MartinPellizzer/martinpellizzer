Stephanomeria exigua subsp. exigua

Stephanomeria exigua subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, glandular leaves and yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of ray-like petals and are pollinated primarily by insects.
