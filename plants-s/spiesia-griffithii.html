Spiesia griffithii

Spiesia griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir apple or Himalayan apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Spiesia griffithii is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity in mountainous regions.
