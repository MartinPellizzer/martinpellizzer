Scorzonera phaeopappa

Scorzonera phaeopappa, commonly known as the purple scurvygrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the sunflower family. Scorzonera phaeopappa is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
