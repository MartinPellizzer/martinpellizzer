Salix helvetica var. velutina

Salix helvetica var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture on the upper surface. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
