Sageraea listeri

Sageraea listeri, commonly known as the Florida flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Sageraea listeri is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
