Senecio rhizomatus

Senecio rhizomatus, commonly known as the African violet or African violet-like plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Senecio rhizomatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
