Symplocos ferruginea var. delectans

Symplocos ferruginea var. delectans, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and attractive fruiting display.
