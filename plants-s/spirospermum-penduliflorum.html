Spirospermum penduliflorum

Spirospermum penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging flower or pendulous bellflower, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
