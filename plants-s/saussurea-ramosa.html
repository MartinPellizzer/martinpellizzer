Saussurea ramosa

Saussurea ramosa, commonly known as the Himalayan spirea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in cold, arid conditions and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Saussurea ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
