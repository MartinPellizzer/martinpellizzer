Schedonorus lividus

Schedonorus lividus, commonly known as the silver bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in its alpine habitat. The plant typically forms dense mats or tufts, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and open slopes.

Schedonorus lividus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
