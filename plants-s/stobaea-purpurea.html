Stobaea purpurea

Stobaea purpurea, commonly known as the purple stobaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Stobaea purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
