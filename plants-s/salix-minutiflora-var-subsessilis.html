Salix minutiflora var. subsessilis

Salix minutiflora var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-flowered willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to stabilize stream banks.
