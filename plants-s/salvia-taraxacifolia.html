Salvia taraxacifolia

Salvia taraxacifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
