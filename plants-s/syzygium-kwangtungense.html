Syzygium kwangtungense

Syzygium kwangtungense, commonly known as the Guangdong tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, conical fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
