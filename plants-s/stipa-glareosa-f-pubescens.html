Stipa glareosa f. pubescens

Stipa glareosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces slender, branching spikelets that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
