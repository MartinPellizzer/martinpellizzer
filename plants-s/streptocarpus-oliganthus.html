Streptocarpus oliganthus

Streptocarpus oliganthus, commonly known as the rock streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers bright, indirect light. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

Streptocarpus oliganthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
