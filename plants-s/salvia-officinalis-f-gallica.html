Salvia officinalis f. gallica

Salvia officinalis f. gallica, commonly known as garden sage, is a cultivar of the common sage plant.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and lavender-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are gray-green, aromatic, and often used in cooking and herbal remedies. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
