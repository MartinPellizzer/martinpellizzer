Savastana odorata

Savastana odorata, commonly known as the fragrant wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible leaves that have a tangy, lemony flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Savastana odorata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
