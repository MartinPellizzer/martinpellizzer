Sida fastuosa

Sida fastuosa, commonly known as the showy sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Sida fastuosa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
