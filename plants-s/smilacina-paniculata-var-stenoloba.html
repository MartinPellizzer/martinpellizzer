Smilacina paniculata var. stenoloba

Smilacina paniculata var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found along streambanks and in thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
