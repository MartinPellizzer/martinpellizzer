Sasaella yamadoriana

Sasaella yamadoriana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Yamadori bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, green shoots that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.

Sasaella yamadoriana prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
