Sideritis armeniaca

Sideritis armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Sideritis armeniaca thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
