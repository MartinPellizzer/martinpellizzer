Solanum melongena var. viride

Solanum melongena var. viride, commonly known as the green eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its dark green, glossy skin and slightly smaller, more oval-shaped fruits compared to the typical purple eggplant.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly India and China. The plant is an annual herb with upright, branching stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and dark green.

Its flowers are white or pale purple, and it produces edible, firm fruits that are often used in various culinary preparations.
