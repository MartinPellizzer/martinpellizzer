Sedum paui

Sedum paui, commonly known as the pale stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in tight inflorescences. Sedum paui thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
