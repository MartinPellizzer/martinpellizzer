Saxifraga thysanodes

Saxifraga thysanodes, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive, thread-like appendage at the tip.

Saxifraga thysanodes plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is often found in alpine and tundra habitats.
