Solanum demerarense

Solanum demerarense, commonly known as the Demerara nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is adapted to tropical climates.
