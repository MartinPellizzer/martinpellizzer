Stelis decipula

Stelis decipula, commonly known as the deceptive stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has aerial roots that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and resemble those of other orchids, which may aid in pollination.

Stelis decipula is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments with high humidity.
