Shorea nervosa

Shorea nervosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its heavy, pale-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecosystem of its native habitat.
