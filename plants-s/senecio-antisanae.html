Senecio antisanae

Senecio antisanae, commonly known as the Antisanae groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
