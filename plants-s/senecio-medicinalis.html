Senecio medicinalis

Senecio medicinalis, commonly known as the medicinal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
