Senecio lachnorhizus

Senecio lachnorhizus, commonly known as the blueweed or narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to dry conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio lachnorhizus is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained habitats.
