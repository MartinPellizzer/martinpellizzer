Synsepalum cinereum

Synsepalum cinereum, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Synsepalum cinereum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
