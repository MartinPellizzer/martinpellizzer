Senecio chaenocephalus

Senecio chaenocephalus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that resemble a poker, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Senecio chaenocephalus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
