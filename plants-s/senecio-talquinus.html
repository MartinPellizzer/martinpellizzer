Senecio talquinus

Senecio talquinus, commonly known as the Talquin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio talquinus is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
