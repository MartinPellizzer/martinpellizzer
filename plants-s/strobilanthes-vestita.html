Strobilanthes vestita

Strobilanthes vestita, commonly known as the silver hair veldt, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Strobilanthes vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
