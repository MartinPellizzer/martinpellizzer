Scutellaria discolor var. hirta

Scutellaria discolor var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and leaves, is a distinguishing feature of this variety.

Scutellaria discolor var. hirta is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
