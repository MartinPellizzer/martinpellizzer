Syzygium ovalifolium

Syzygium ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, tart seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Syzygium ovalifolium thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
