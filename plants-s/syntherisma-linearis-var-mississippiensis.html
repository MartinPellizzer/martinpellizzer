Syntherisma linearis var. mississippiensis

Syntherisma linearis var. mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi marsh sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, grass-like cluster. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
