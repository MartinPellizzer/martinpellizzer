Solanum pseudoauriculatum

Solanum pseudoauriculatum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Solanum pseudoauriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
