Silene oligantha subsp. parnesia

Silene oligantha subsp.

parnesia, commonly known as the parnesian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky or coastal habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
