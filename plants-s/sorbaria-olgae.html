Sorbaria olgae

Sorbaria olgae, commonly known as the Mongolian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sorbaria olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
