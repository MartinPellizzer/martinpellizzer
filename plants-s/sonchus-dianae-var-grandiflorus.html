Sonchus dianae var. grandiflorus

Sonchus dianae var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern China.

It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive basal arrangement. The plant produces large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its edible leaves and medicinal properties.
