Senecio lycopifolius

Senecio lycopifolius, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Senecio lycopifolius is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
