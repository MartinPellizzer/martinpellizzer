Spiraea × syringiflora

Spiraea × syringiflora, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its graceful, arching branches.

It features small, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea × syringiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
