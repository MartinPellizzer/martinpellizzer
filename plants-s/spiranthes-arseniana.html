Spiranthes arseniana

Spiranthes arseniana, commonly known as the yellow spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and pine flatwoods. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of yellowish-green to golden flowers.

It is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
