Scrophularia burdurensis

Scrophularia burdurensis, commonly known as the Burdurensis figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Scrophularia burdurensis is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
