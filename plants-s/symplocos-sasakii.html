Symplocos sasakii

Symplocos sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Symplocos sasakii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
