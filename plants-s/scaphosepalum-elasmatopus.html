Scaphosepalum elasmatopus

Scaphosepalum elasmatopus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex structure with specialized petals and sepals that aid in pollination.
