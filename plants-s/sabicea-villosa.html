Sabicea villosa

Sabicea villosa, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Sabicea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
