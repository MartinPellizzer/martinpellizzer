Saussurea lanceifolia

Saussurea lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots and robust stems allow it to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Saussurea lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
