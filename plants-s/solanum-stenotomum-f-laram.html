Solanum stenotomum f. laram

Solanum stenotomum f. laram, commonly known as the Laramie nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals, and the plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
