Sperihedium triste

Sperihedium triste, commonly known as the melancholy spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its upright, spire-like clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
