Solidago narbonensis

Solidago narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago narbonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
