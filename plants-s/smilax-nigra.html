Smilax nigra

Smilax nigra, commonly known as blackberry vine, is a perennial climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Smilax nigra is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
