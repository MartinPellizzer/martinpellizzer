Sesamum papillosum

Sesamum papillosum, commonly known as the hairy sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oil-rich seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
