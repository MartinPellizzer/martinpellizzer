Sasamorpha gracilis

Sasamorpha gracilis, commonly known as the slender sasamorpha, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Sasamorpha gracilis is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
