Selaginella commutata

Selaginella commutata, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. Native to various regions of the United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species.

Its reproductive structures are typically found at the tips of its stems, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
