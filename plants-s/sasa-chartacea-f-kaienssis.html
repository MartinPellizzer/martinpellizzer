Sasa chartacea f. kaienssis

Sasa chartacea f. kaienssis, commonly known as the Kaienssis form of the broadleaf sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a type of bamboo-like grass that grows in dense clumps, forming thick mats on forest floors. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, temperate forests.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
