Senecio aphanactis

Senecio aphanactis, commonly known as the ghostweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are often overlooked, but they play a role in attracting pollinators.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
