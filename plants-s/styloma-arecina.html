Styloma arecina

Styloma arecina, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a thick, woody trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its reproductive structures include large, cone-like structures that produce both male and female spores.

Styloma arecina is native to Madagascar and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
