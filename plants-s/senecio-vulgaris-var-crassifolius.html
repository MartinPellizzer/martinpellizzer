Senecio vulgaris var. crassifolius

Senecio vulgaris var. crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems with opposite, rough, and broad ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in terminal clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
