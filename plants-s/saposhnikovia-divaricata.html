Saposhnikovia divaricata

Saposhnikovia divaricata, commonly known as Chinese angelica or huang qi, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive, branched root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months.

The botanical characteristics of S. divaricata make it an important species in traditional Chinese medicine and herbal remedies.
