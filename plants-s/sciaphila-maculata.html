Sciaphila maculata

Sciaphila maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with dark markings. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually oval and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Sciaphila maculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
