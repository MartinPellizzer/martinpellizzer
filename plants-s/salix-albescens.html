Salix albescens

Salix albescens, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a silvery underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
