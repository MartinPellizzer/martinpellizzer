Synaedrys pseudomolucca

Synaedrys pseudomolucca, commonly known as the Moluccan spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance with elongated, slender petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small but highly specialized, designed to attract specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and wasps.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and environmental changes.
