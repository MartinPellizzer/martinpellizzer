Senecio exul

Senecio exul, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late summer. Senecio exul is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
