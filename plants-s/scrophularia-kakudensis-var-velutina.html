Scrophularia kakudensis var. velutina

Scrophularia kakudensis var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
