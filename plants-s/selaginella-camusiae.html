Selaginella camusiae

Selaginella camusiae, commonly known as the Camusia spikemoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Selaginella camusiae is a spore-bearing plant, reproducing through the release of spores rather than seeds.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
