Schizaea spicata

Schizaea spicata, commonly known as the spicate whisk fern, is a member of the family Psilotaceae and is characterized by its unique, primitive structure.

It is a vascular plant that lacks true leaves and roots, instead having root-like structures called rhizomes and stem-like shoots. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in spicules along the stems. Schizaea spicata thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its simple morphology makes it an important subject in the study of early plant evolution.
