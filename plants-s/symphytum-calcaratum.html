Symphytum calcaratum

Symphytum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, pale purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose, upright inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
