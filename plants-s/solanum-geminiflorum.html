Solanum geminiflorum

Solanum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe.

Solanum geminiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
