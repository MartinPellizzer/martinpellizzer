Spicanta elongata

Spicanta elongata, commonly known as the elongated spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in a milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family.
