Serratula neglecta

Serratula neglecta, commonly known as common serratula, is an annual or biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is considered a weed in some regions.

Serratula neglecta is also known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic in high doses.
