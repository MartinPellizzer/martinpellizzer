Sopubia decumbens

Sopubia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping sopubia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.

Sopubia decumbens is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
