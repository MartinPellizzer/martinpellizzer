Syzygium chanlos

Syzygium chanlos, commonly known as the white clydesdale or white apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, white or pale yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. Syzygium chanlos thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a pleasant scent when crushed.
