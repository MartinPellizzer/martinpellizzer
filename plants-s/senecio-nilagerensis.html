Senecio nilagerensis

Senecio nilagerensis, commonly known as the Nilager Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
