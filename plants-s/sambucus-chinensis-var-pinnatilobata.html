Sambucus chinensis var. pinnatilobata

Sambucus chinensis var. pinnatilobata, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental features and has been used in traditional medicine.
