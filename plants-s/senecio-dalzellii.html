Senecio dalzellii

Senecio dalzellii, commonly known as Dalzell's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
