Solanum schreberianum

Solanum schreberianum, commonly known as the Schreber's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellow or orange hue, containing toxic alkaloids.

Solanum schreberianum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
