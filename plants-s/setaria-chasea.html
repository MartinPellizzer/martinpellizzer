Setaria chasea

Setaria chasea, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its seed heads are golden in color.

Setaria chasea is a hardy plant that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
