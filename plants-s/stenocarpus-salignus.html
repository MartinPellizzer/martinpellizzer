Stenocarpus salignus

Stenocarpus salignus, commonly known as the silky kowhai, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
