Syzygium laxeracemosum

Syzygium laxeracemosum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
