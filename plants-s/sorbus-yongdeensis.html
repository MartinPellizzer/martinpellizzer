Sorbus yongdeensis

Sorbus yongdeensis, commonly known as the Yongde rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province, and is known for its distinctive ornamental features. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its visual appeal.

Sorbus yongdeensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
