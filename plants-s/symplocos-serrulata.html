Symplocos serrulata

Symplocos serrulata, commonly known as the serrated wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Symplocos serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
