Swainsona pedunculata

Swainsona pedunculata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets.
