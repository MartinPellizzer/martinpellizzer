Spergula fimbriata f. tenuis

Spergula fimbriata f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender fimbriate grass, is a variant of the fimbriate grass species.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, delicate leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are adorned with feathery bristles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
