Salvia bangii

Salvia bangii, commonly known as the violet hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia bangii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
