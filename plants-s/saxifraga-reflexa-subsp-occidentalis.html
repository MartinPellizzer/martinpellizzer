Saxifraga reflexa subsp. occidentalis

Saxifraga reflexa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western reflexed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and mountain slopes.

The plant features dark green, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and is often found in areas with cool, temperate climates.
