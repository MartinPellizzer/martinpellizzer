Saxifraga tellimoides

Saxifraga tellimoides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Saxifraga tellimoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
