Synaedrys amygdalifolia f. castanopsifolia

Synaedrys amygdalifolia f. castanopsifolia, commonly known as the variegated almondleaf, is a horticultural form of the species Synaedrys amygdalifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and white or cream-colored markings. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage in their landscapes.
