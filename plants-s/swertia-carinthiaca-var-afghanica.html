Swertia carinthiaca var. afghanica

Swertia carinthiaca var. afghanica, commonly known as Afghan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
