Spiraea flabellata var. crenata

Spiraea flabellata var. crenata, commonly known as the Crenate Meadow Spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive wavy or crenate margin, giving it its botanical name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
