Setaria aurea var. longiseta

Setaria aurea var. longiseta, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
