Sida coronata

Sida coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive blue-gray coloration. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and are often surrounded by white or pale blue bracts.

Sida coronata is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
