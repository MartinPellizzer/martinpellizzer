Simaba praecox

Simaba praecox, commonly known as the early simaba, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
