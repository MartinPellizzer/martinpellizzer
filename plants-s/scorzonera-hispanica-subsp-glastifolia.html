Scorzonera hispanica subsp. glastifolia

Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

glastifolia, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features a robust, fleshy root system that can reach several centimeters in length, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

glastifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
