Sideritis hirsuta subsp. gypsicola

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum sideritis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found on gypsum soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
