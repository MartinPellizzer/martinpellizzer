Sarcopharyngia ventricosa

Sarcopharyngia ventricosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, enabling it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Sarcopharyngia ventricosa is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
