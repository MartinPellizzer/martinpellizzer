Scirpus siculus

Scirpus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale brown flowers at the top of its stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense stands along wetlands and marshy areas.

Scirpus siculus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
