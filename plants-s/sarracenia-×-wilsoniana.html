Sarracenia × wilsoniana

Sarracenia × wilsoniana, commonly known as the Wilson's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant's pitcher structures are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive hood that helps funnel prey into the trap. Sarracenia × wilsoniana thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and predatory behavior make it a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
