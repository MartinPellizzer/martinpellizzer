Stanleya pinnata var. pinnata

Stanleya pinnata var. pinnata, commonly known as the white gaura, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
