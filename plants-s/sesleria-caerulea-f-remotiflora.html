Sesleria caerulea f. remotiflora

Sesleria caerulea f. remotiflora, commonly known as the remote-flowered bluegrass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
