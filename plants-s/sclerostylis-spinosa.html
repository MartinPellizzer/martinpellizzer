Sclerostylis spinosa

Sclerostylis spinosa, commonly known as the spiny lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Sclerostylis spinosa is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
