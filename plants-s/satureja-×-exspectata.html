Satureja × exspectata

Satureja × exspectata, commonly known as hybrid savory, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Satureja hortensis and Satureja montana, resulting in a plant with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.

Satureja × exspectata prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
