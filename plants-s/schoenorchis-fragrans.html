Schoenorchis fragrans

Schoenorchis fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white with a subtle, sweet scent, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
