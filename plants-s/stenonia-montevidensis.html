Stenonia montevidensis

Stenonia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo stenonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
