Senecio adamantinus var. adamantinus

Senecio adamantinus var. adamantinus, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense, blue-gray foliage that resembles ice crystals. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle floral display. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in gardens.
