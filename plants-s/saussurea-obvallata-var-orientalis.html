Saussurea obvallata var. orientalis

Saussurea obvallata var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow ray florets surrounded by pale yellow disk florets. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, particularly in alpine ecosystems.
