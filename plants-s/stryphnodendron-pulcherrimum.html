Stryphnodendron pulcherrimum

Stryphnodendron pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Pernambuco, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy green leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, deep red color and durability, making it popular in the production of musical instruments.

The plant also has medicinal uses, with some parts being used in traditional remedies.
