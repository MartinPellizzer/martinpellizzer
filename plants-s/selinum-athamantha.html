Selinum athamantha

Selinum athamantha, commonly known as greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and erect, branched stems that are often covered in a yellowish or reddish resin. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are alternate, broad, and have a distinctive wavy or notched margin.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it notable in traditional medicine and botanical research.
