Spiesia ambigua

Spiesia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features alternate, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Spiesia ambigua is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
