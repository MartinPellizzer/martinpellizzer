Solanum serratifolium

Solanum serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its lobed, serrated leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. Its fruits are berries that change from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
