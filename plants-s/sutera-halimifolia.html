Sutera halimifolia

Sutera halimifolia, commonly known as sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Sutera halimifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
