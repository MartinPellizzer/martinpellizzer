Senecio galpinii

Senecio galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio galpinii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
