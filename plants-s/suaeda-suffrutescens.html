Suaeda suffrutescens

Suaeda suffrutescens, commonly known as sea spurge, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. Its small yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer.

Suaeda suffrutescens plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
