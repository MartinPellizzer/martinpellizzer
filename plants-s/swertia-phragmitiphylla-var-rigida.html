Swertia phragmitiphylla var. rigida

Swertia phragmitiphylla var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid swamp gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern China.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
