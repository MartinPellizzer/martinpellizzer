Salvia laciniata

Salvia laciniata, commonly known as the fringed sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia laciniata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
