Salvia serranoae

Salvia serranoae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in dense whorls along the stems.

Salvia serranoae is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
