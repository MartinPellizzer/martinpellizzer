Statice purpurea

Statice purpurea, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of violet to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are gray-green in color.

Statice purpurea is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant for its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
