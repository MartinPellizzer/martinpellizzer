Silene intonsa

Silene intonsa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds. Silene intonsa thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
