Stipa pennata subsp. pulcherrima

Stipa pennata subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of silky, purple-tinged inflorescences that give it a striking appearance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fine, feathery texture that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture for its distinctive color and form.
