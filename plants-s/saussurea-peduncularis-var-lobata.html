Saussurea peduncularis var. lobata

Saussurea peduncularis var. lobata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine. Its roots and flowers are used to treat various ailments, including inflammation and digestive issues.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
