Senecio umbellatus

Senecio umbellatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that resembles a dandelion, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.
