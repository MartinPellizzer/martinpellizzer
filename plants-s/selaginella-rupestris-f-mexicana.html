Selaginella rupestris f. mexicana

Selaginella rupestris f. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a low-growing, evergreen fern ally native to arid regions of Mexico.

It features delicate, linear leaves arranged in whorls around its branching stems, giving it a compact, shrub-like appearance. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained, dry environments. Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
