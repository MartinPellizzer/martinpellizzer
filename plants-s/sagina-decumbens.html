Sagina decumbens

Sagina decumbens, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Sagina decumbens is known for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
