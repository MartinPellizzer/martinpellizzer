Serapias apulica

Serapias apulica, commonly known as the Apulian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive hooded lip that attracts pollinators.

Serapias apulica is known for its resilience and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
