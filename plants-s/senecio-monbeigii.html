Senecio monbeigii

Senecio monbeigii, commonly known as the Canary Islands groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Senecio monbeigii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's Mediterranean climate.
