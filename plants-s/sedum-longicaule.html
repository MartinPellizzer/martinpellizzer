Sedum longicaule

Sedum longicaule, commonly known as the tall stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum longicaule is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
