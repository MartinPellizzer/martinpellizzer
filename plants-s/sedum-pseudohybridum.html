Sedum pseudohybridum

Sedum pseudohybridum, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to red, usually in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum pseudohybridum is known for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats over time.
