Selaginella durvillaei var. aspericaulis

Selaginella durvillaei var. aspericaulis, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called strobila.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant with a rosette-like growth habit, featuring scale-like leaves that are often rough to the touch, as indicated by its specific epithet "aspericaulis.". The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of southern Africa.

Its reproductive structures are prominently displayed, making it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its habitat.
