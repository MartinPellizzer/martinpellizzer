Statice littoralis subsp. filicaulis

Statice littoralis subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the sea lavender or coastal statice, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Statice littoralis subsp.

filicaulis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
