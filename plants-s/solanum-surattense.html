Solanum surattense

Solanum surattense, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
