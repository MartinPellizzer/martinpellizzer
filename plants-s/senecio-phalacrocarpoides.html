Senecio phalacrocarpoides

Senecio phalacrocarpoides, commonly known as the coastal senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods.

The species is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
