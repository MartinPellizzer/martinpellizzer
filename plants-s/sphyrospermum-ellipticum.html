Sphyrospermum ellipticum

Sphyrospermum ellipticum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with smooth, dark green foliage.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
