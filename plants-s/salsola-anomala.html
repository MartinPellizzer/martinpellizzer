Salsola anomala

Salsola anomala, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Salsola anomala is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.
