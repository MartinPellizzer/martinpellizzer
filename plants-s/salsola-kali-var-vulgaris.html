Salsola kali var. vulgaris

Salsola kali var. vulgaris, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Eurasia. The plant has spiny, lobed leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize degraded lands, making it both a weed and a pioneer species.

Its ecological impact is significant due to its resilience and reproductive strategies.
