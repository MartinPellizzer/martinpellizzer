Sieruela kermesina var. plebeia

Sieruela kermesina var. plebeia, commonly known as the common sieruela, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and shrublands. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
