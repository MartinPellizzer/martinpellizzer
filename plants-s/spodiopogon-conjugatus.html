Spodiopogon conjugatus

Spodiopogon conjugatus, commonly known as the paired spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, paired spikelets that give it its scientific name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Spodiopogon conjugatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
