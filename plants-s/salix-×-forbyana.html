Salix × forbyana

Salix × forbyana, commonly known as the Forby's willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. It is native to eastern North America and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
