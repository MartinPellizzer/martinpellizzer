Sterculia diguensis

Sterculia diguensis, commonly known as the African mallow, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, hairy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
