Smilax lata

Smilax lata, commonly known as the trailing smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces sharp, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Smilax lata is often found in woodland areas and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread aggressively.
