Senna santanderensis

Senna santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander senna, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senna santanderensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
