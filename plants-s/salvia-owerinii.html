Salvia owerinii

Salvia owerinii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
