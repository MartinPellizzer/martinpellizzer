Stipagrostis hochstetteriana var. hochstetteriana

Stipagrostis hochstetteriana var. hochstetteriana, commonly known as the Hochstetter's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological importance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
