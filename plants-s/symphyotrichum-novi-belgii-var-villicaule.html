Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. villicaule

Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. villicaule, commonly known as the New York ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety villicaule is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Symphyotrichum novi-belgii.
