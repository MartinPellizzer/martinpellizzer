Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. diversifolia

Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. diversifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with striking variegation of white and green, making it visually appealing in gardens. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and xeriscaping.
