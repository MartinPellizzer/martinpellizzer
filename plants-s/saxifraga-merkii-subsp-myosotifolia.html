Saxifraga merkii subsp. myosotifolia

Saxifraga merkii subsp.

myosotifolia, commonly known as the blue saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically blue or violet in color, giving it a delicate and attractive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
