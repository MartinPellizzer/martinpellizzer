Saussurea sericea

Saussurea sericea, commonly known as the silver safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Saussurea sericea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
