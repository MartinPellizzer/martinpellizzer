Saxifraga tricuspidata f. woodruffii

Saxifraga tricuspidata f. woodruffii, commonly known as the Woodruff's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
