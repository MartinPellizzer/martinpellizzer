Sciodaphyllum tamanum

Sciodaphyllum tamanum, commonly known as the Tamanu tree, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
