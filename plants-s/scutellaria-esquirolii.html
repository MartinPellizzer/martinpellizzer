Scutellaria esquirolii

Scutellaria esquirolii, commonly known as the Sierra madre skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria esquirolii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
