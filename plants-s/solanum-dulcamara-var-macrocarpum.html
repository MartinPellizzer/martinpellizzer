Solanum dulcamara var. macrocarpum

Solanum dulcamara var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant bindweed, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its flowers are typically yellow with a white or pink center, blooming in clusters.

The fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
