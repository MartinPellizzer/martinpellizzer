Sisyrolepis muricata

Sisyrolepis muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Sisyrolepis muricata is often used in native plant gardens due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
