Stevia elliptica

Stevia elliptica, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are harvested and used in various food products as a calorie-free sweetener.

Stevia elliptica is also valued for its medicinal properties and is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
