Sedum bhattacharyyae

Sedum bhattacharyyae, commonly known as the Bhattacharya's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
