Sideritis hyssopifolia

Sideritis hyssopifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sideritis hyssopifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
