Sedum ebracteatum var. rubricaule

Sedum ebracteatum var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are typically reddish in color, which helps it store water and reflect sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. Its leaves are usually blue-green and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water once established.
