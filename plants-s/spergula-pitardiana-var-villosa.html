Spergula pitardiana var. villosa

Spergula pitardiana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky areas.
