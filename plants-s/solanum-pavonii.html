Solanum pavonii

Solanum pavonii, commonly known as the purple potato or pavonia potato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are used as a food source. The plant produces violet or purple flowers, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Solanum pavonii is adapted to grow in high-altitude regions and is cultivated for both its edible tubers and ornamental value.
