Saussurea gossipiphora

Saussurea gossipiphora, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are often mistaken for grains of saffron, though it is not related to the true saffron crocus. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Saussurea gossipiphora thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
