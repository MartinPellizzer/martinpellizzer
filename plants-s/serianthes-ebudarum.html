Serianthes ebudarum

Serianthes ebudarum, commonly known as the ebony tree, is a tropical tree species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Serianthes ebudarum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
