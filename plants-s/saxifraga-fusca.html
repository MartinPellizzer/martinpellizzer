Saxifraga fusca

Saxifraga fusca, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Saxifraga fusca plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
