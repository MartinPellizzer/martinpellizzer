Swietenia febrifuga

Swietenia febrifuga, commonly known as the febrifuge mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, durable wood that has been historically valued for furniture and construction. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by winged fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating fever and other ailments.
