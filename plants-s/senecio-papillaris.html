Senecio papillaris

Senecio papillaris, commonly known as the papillate senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, and hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
