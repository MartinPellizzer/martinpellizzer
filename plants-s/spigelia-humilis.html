Spigelia humilis

Spigelia humilis, commonly known as the low spigelia, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other toxic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds due to their shape and color.

Spigelia humilis is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
