Selaginella labordei

Selaginella labordei, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, compact rosette. Selaginella labordei is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Mexico and Central America.
