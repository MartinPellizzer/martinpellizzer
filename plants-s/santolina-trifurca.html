Santolina trifurca

Santolina trifurca, commonly known as silver thyme or golden santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers. The leaves are silvery-gray, covered in fine hairs, and emit a subtle, herbaceous scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Santolina trifurca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
