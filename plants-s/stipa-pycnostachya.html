Stipa pycnostachya

Stipa pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stemmed grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Stipa pycnostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
