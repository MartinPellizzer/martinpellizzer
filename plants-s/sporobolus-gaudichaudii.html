Sporobolus gaudichaudii

Sporobolus gaudichaudii, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Silver bluestem is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
