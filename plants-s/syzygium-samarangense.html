Syzygium samarangense

Syzygium samarangense, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit exterior, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns bright red, indicating maturity.
