Sweetia fruticosa

Sweetia fruticosa, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Sweetia fruticosa plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
