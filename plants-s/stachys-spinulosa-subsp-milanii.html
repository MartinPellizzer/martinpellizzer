Stachys spinulosa subsp. milanii

Stachys spinulosa subsp.

milanii, commonly known as the Milan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a low-growing perennial with erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
