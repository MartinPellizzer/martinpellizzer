Silene olgiana

Silene olgiana, commonly known as the Olgiana catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Silene olgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
