Saxifraga rubriflora

Saxifraga rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are striking, with bright red petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
