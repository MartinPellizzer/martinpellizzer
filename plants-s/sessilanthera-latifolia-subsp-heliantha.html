Sessilanthera latifolia subsp. heliantha

Sessilanthera latifolia subsp.

heliantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved sun-plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
