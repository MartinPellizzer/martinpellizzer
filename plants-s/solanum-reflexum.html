Solanum reflexum

Solanum reflexum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are often used in traditional medicine.

Solanum reflexum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
