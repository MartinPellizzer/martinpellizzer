Saxifraga corsica subsp. cossoniana

Saxifraga corsica subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Corsican saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
