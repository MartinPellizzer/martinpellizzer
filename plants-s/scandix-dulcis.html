Scandix dulcis

Scandix dulcis, commonly known as sweet angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate flowers.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, temperate environments. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to their sweet, spicy aroma.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
