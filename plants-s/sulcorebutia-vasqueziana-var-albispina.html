Sulcorebutia vasqueziana var. albispina

Sulcorebutia vasqueziana var. albispina, commonly known as the white-spined sun rose, is a striking succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in a white or pale waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically green with a slight bluish tint and can be adorned with white spines, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and plenty of sunlight to thrive.
