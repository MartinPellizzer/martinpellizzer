Smilax panduriformis

Smilax panduriformis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with spiny nodes and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax panduriformis is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes.
