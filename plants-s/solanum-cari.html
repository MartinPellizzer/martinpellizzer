Solanum cari

Solanum cari, commonly known as the purple tomato, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored fruits that are typically harvested when they reach a mature purple hue. Solanum cari belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is related to other economically important crops like tomatoes and peppers.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
