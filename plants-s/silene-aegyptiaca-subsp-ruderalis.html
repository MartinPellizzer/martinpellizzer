Silene aegyptiaca subsp. ruderalis

Silene aegyptiaca subsp.

ruderalis, commonly known as the Egyptian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in ecological succession.
