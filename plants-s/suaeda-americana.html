Suaeda americana

Suaeda americana, commonly known as American saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, demonstrating remarkable tolerance to high salinity and drought conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor in unstable, dry environments.

Suaeda americana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
