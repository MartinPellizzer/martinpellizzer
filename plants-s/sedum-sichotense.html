Sedum sichotense

Sedum sichotense, commonly known as the Sichuan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, usually in shades of yellow or white.

Sedum sichotense is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
