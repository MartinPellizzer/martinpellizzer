Sphenopholis intermedia var. pilosa

Sphenopholis intermedia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sphenopholis, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in terminal spikes, which are typical of the Sphenophyllum genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
