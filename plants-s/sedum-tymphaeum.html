Sedum tymphaeum

Sedum tymphaeum, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and can tolerate full sun. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and serve as water storage organs.

Sedum tymphaeum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
