Solanum tequilense

Solanum tequilense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. Solanum tequilense is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
