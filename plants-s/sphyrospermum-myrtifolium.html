Sphyrospermum myrtifolium

Sphyrospermum myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved sphaerocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Sphaerocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy, red fruits that resemble tiny berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or woodland habitats. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct myrtle-like scent, contributing to its common name.

Sphyrospermum myrtifolium is adapted to arid conditions and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
