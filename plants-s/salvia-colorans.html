Salvia colorans

Salvia colorans, commonly known as the variable sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Salvia colorans is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
