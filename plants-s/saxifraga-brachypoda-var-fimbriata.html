Saxifraga brachypoda var. fimbriata

Saxifraga brachypoda var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves with a distinctive fringed or toothed edge. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
