Selwynia

Selwynia, commonly known as the mountain gum or selwynia gum, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the myrtle family, Myrtaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, often showy flowers. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Selwynia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique foliage.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
