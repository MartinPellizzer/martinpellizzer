Statice nicotrae

Statice nicotrae, commonly known as Nicotra's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

Statice nicotrae is often used in ornamental horticulture for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
