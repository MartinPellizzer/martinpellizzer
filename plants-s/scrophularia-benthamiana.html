Scrophularia benthamiana

Scrophularia benthamiana, commonly known as Bentham's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips that resemble those of a foxglove.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
