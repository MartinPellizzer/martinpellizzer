Stenorrhynchos pteryganthum

Stenorrhynchos pteryganthum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
