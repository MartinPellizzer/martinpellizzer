Syringa komarowii

Syringa komarowii, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Syringa komarowii is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also noted for its adaptability to different climates and its resistance to some common pests and diseases.
