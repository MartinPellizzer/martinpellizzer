Stylidium bellidifolium

Stylidium bellidifolium, commonly known as the golden sun-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Australia.

This herbaceous plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling sun rays. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Stylidium bellidifolium is known for its short blooming period, usually during the warmer months, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
