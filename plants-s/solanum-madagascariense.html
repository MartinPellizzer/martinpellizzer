Solanum madagascariense

Solanum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar nightshade, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant features small, white or purple flowers and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is adapted to the tropical climate of Madagascar and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and humans.
