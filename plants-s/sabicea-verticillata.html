Sabicea verticillata

Sabicea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
