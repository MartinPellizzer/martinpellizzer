Sageretia hamosa var. hamosa

Sageretia hamosa var. hamosa, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by small, fleshy berries that are red when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to different climates.
