Salix maboulasensis

Salix maboulasensis, commonly known as the Maboulas willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, primarily in Nepal and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in catkin-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
