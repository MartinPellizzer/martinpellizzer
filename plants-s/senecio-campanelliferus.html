Senecio campanelliferus

Senecio campanelliferus, commonly known as the bluebell of the mountains, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, but it can be invasive in certain environments.
