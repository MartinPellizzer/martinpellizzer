Sorghum basiplicatum var. rubellum

Sorghum basiplicatum var. rubellum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and vibrant red or purple pigmentation in its leaves and stems. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typical of many sorghum species.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a forage crop.
