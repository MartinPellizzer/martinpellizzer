Scopolia tenuifolia

Scopolia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved scopolia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces distinctive, waxy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Scopolia tenuifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in shaded garden environments.
