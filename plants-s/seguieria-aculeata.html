Seguieria aculeata

Seguieria aculeata, commonly known as the spiny spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and disturbed areas.

Seguieria aculeata has a low-growing habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
