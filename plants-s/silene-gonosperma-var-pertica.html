Silene gonosperma var. pertica

Silene gonosperma var. pertica, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments.

Silene gonosperma var. pertica is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in alpine meadows.
