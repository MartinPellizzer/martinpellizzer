Scrophularia peregrina f. macrophylla

Scrophularia peregrina f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved foxglove, is a horticultural form of the species Scrophularia peregrina.

It is characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form, which are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
