Suaeda kossinskyi

Suaeda kossinskyi, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found along coastal dunes and inland salt flats. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Suaeda kossinskyi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
