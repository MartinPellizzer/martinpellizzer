Souliea vaginata

Souliea vaginata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody stem, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
