Saussurea daurica

Saussurea daurica, commonly known as Daurian gromwell, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces tall, branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional herbal medicine.
