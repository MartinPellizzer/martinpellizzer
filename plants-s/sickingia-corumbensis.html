Sickingia corumbensis

Sickingia corumbensis, commonly known as the Corumbensis ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the winter months.

Sickingia corumbensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
