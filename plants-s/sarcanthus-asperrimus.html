Sarcanthus asperrimus

Sarcanthus asperrimus, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in coarse, bristly hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its robust structure and adaptive features, it is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
