Sempervivum globiferum var. hillebrandtii

Sempervivum globiferum var. hillebrandtii, commonly known as the Hillebrandt's Globeflower, is a compact, evergreen succulent with rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves.

It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
