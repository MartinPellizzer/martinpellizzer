Serratula nudicaulis var. transcaucasica

Serratula nudicaulis var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian slender sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous areas. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This variant is distinguished by its more slender stems and reduced leaf size compared to other varieties of Serratula nudicaulis.
