Salsola albisepala

Salsola albisepala, commonly known as white-sepal saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves and white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Salsola albisepala plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
