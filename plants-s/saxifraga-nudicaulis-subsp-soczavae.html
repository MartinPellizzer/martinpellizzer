Saxifraga nudicaulis subsp. soczavae

Saxifraga nudicaulis subsp.

soczavae, commonly known as the Soczava's leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its short, creeping stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience.
