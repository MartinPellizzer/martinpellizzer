Scorzonera luristanica var. lanata

Scorzonera luristanica var. lanata, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is tuberous, which is a notable feature for its horticultural and potential medicinal uses.

Scorzonera luristanica var. lanata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
