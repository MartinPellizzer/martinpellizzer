Sesleria calcarea var. podperae

Sesleria calcarea var. podperae, commonly known as the limestone bent grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
