Strophanthus preussii f. crebrinervis

Strophanthus preussii f. crebrinervis, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with prominent veins, giving it the specific epithet 'crebrinervis,' meaning 'with dense veins.'. It produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in African herbal practices.
