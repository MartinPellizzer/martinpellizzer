Sameraria armena subsp. flaccida

Sameraria armena subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the Armenian flaccid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations.
