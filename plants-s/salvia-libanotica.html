Salvia libanotica

Salvia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in both gardens and horticultural collections.
