Seymeria pectinata subsp. pectinata

Seymeria pectinata subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features distinctive, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Seymeria pectinata subsp.

pectinata is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
