Santolina cupressiformis

Santolina cupressiformis, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, aromatic foliage composed of small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
