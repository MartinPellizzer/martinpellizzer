Silene oregana

Silene oregana, commonly known as mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Silene oregana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
