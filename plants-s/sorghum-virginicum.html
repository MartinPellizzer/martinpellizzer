Sorghum virginicum

Sorghum virginicum, commonly known as Eastern gamagrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to heights of 2 to 6 feet and has long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often purple or green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Sorghum virginicum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
