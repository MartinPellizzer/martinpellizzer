Senecio integrifolius

Senecio integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped, smooth-edged leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio integrifolius is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
