Syngonanthus costatus

Syngonanthus costatus, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in the spring.

Syngonanthus costatus is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
