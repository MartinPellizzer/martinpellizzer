Salsola dinteri

Salsola dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Salsola dinteri plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
