Solanum luteiflorum var. subintegrifolium

Solanum luteiflorum var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the orange tree tomato, is a variant of the tree tomato species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces yellow to orange fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is edible, often used in culinary applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.
