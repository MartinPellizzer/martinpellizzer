Salix × schinziana

Salix × schinziana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green in the summer and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soil and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × schinziana is valued for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
