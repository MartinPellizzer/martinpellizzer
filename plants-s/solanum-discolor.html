Solanum discolor

Solanum discolor, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers and smooth, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Solanum discolor belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and open fields.
