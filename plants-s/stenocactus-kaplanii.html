Stenocactus kaplanii

Stenocactus kaplanii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. This plant is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late summer.

Stenocactus kaplanii is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and unique botanical specimen.
