Sphaerine secundifolia

Sphaerine secundifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf sphaerine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving the plant its distinctive silver appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This plant is often grown as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
