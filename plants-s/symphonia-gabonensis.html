Symphonia gabonensis

Symphonia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense canopy. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a wide, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
