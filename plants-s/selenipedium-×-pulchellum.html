Selenipedium × pulchellum

Selenipedium × pulchellum, commonly known as the hybrid giant spurge, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a hybrid between Selenipedium albertii and Selenipedium pulchellum, characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its botanical features include elongated, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
