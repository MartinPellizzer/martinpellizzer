Steviopsis thyrsiflora var. solidaginifolia

Steviopsis thyrsiflora var. solidaginifolia, commonly known as the goldenflower stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble those of the goldenrod genus. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage.
