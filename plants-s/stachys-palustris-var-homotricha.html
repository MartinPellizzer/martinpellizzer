Stachys palustris var. homotricha

Stachys palustris var. homotricha, commonly known as swamp betony, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Stachys palustris var. homotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
