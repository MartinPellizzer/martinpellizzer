Senecio aureus var. croceus

Senecio aureus var. croceus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
