Salix brachycarpa var. glabellicarpa

Salix brachycarpa var. glabellicarpa, commonly known as the smooth-barked willow, is a variant of the brachycarpa willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.
