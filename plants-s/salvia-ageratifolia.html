Salvia ageratifolia

Salvia ageratifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms.
