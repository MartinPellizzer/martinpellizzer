Salsola minkvitziae

Salsola minkvitziae, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water in dry soils. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline conditions, making it a common species in desert ecosystems.

Salsola minkvitziae plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
