Solidago vulgaris var. angustifolia

Solidago vulgaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
