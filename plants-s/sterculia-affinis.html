Sterculia affinis

Sterculia affinis, commonly known as the African tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some African communities.
