Senecio fasciculatus

Senecio fasciculatus, commonly known as the bluegum tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Senecio fasciculatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
