Stewartia serrata f. sericea

Stewartia serrata f. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by ornamental, cone-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
