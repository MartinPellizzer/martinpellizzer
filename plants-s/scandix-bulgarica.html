Scandix bulgarica

Scandix bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian scurvygrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Scandix bulgarica is known for its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
