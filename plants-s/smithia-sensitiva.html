Smithia sensitiva

Smithia sensitiva, commonly known as the sensitive rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, four-petaled white flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for ecological health.
