Saxifraga × potternensis

Saxifraga × potternensis, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features dark green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
