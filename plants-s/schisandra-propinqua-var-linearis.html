Schisandra propinqua var. linearis

Schisandra propinqua var. linearis, commonly known as the linear schisandra, is a rare variant of the Schisandra genus native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that mature in late summer. The plant is adapted to cool, humid climates and thrives in shaded forest environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
