Scheelea macrolepis

Scheelea macrolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved spurge, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family. Scheelea macrolepis is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
