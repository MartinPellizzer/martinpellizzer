Siphanthera arenaria

Siphanthera arenaria, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained, saline environments and is often found along dunes and coastal vegetation.

Siphanthera arenaria plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small coastal fauna.
