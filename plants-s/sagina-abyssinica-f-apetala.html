Sagina abyssinica f. apetala

Sagina abyssinica f. apetala, commonly known as the apetalous stonecress, is a variant of the species Sagina abyssinica, characterized by its reduced or absent flowers.

This succulent plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help conserve water, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and morphological variation.
