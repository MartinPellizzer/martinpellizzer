Solanum nigrum f. longepetiolatum

Solanum nigrum f. longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade species.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
