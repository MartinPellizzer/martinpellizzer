Sanvitalia

Sanvitalia, commonly known as the "Firecracker" plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, resembling tiny firecrackers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Sanvitalia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
