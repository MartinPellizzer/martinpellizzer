Syagrus × andrequiceana

Syagrus × andrequiceana, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a natural hybrid between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus acuminata.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Brazil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical landscapes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a combination of traits from both parent species, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
