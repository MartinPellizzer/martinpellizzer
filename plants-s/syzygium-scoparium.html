Syzygium scoparium

Syzygium scoparium, commonly known as the red-fruited satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Syzygium scoparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its wood is also used in traditional crafts and furniture making due to its fine texture and durability.
