Senecio vulgaris subvar. crassifolius

Senecio vulgaris subvar. crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. The plant has alternate, broad, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is an annual or biennial plant that can spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
