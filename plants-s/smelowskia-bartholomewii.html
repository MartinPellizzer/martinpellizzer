Smelowskia bartholomewii

Smelowskia bartholomewii, commonly known as the Bartholomew's smelowskia, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-arranged leaves that form a dense rosette structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the early morning. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
