Spiraea tomentosa var. tomentosa

Spiraea tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as silver-leaf weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
