Salix × schneideri

Salix × schneideri, commonly known as the Schneiders willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its fast growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is native to Central Asia and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental and ecological purposes. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands.

Due to its hardiness and rapid growth, it is sometimes used in reforestation and erosion control projects.
