Salvia maxima

Salvia maxima, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

Salvia maxima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various growing conditions.
