Stanhopea hoppii

Stanhopea hoppii, commonly known as the Orange-bellied Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-colored flowers with a distinctive, bell-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stanhopea hoppii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
