Scirpus ardea

Scirpus ardea, commonly known as the great tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense, cylindrical clusters of dark brown or blackish spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense vegetation that stabilizes soil and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms. Its leaves are narrow and fibrous, contributing to its structural strength.

Scirpus ardea plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
