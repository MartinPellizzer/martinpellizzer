Saxifraga paniculata f. leptophylla

Saxifraga paniculata f. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
