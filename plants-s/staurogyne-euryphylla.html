Staurogyne euryphylla

Staurogyne euryphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating leaves, which are typically broad and oval-shaped, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of rivers. Staurogyne euryphylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
