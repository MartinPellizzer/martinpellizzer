Spiraea northcroftii

Spiraea northcroftii, commonly known as the Northcroft spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn a striking red or bronze color in the fall.

Spiraea northcroftii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.
