Stenorrhynchos cinnabarinum

Stenorrhynchos cinnabarinum, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, deep red petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex arrangement of sepals and petals that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
