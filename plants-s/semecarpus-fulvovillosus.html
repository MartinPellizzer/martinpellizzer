Semecarpus fulvovillosus

Semecarpus fulvovillosus, commonly known as the yellow hairy sycamore, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Sapindaceae.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with elongated, yellowish-green leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
