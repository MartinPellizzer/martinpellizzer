Senecio occidentalis var. rotundatus

Senecio occidentalis var. rotundatus, commonly known as the rounded spearthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
