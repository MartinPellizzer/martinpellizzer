Sedum middendorffianum

Sedum middendorffianum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum middendorffianum is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
