Solanum xanthocarpum var. jacquinii

Solanum xanthocarpum var. jacquinii, commonly known as the yellow-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and ovate, pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by yellow or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is generally avoided for cultivation or consumption.
