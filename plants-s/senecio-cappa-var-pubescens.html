Senecio cappa var. pubescens

Senecio cappa var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly cappa groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
