Senecio diversipinnus var. discoideus

Senecio diversipinnus var. discoideus, commonly known as the Desert Honeysuckle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, silvery-blue leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in desert botany.
