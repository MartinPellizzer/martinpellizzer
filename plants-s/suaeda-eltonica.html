Suaeda eltonica

Suaeda eltonica, commonly known as the Elton's saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is native to regions such as the Arabian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

Suaeda eltonica plays a role in stabilizing coastal and desert soils due to its extensive root system.
