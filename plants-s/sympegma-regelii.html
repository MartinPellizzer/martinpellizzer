Sympegma regelii

Sympegma regelii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and rosette-forming leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with silky hairs for wind dispersal.
