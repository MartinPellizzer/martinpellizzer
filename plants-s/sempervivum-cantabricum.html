Sempervivum cantabricum

Sempervivum cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, greenish flowers on tall stems during the summer months.

Sempervivum cantabricum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
