Sidalcea malviflora subsp. californica

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

californica, commonly known as California checker-mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and cotton.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
