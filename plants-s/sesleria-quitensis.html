Sesleria quitensis

Sesleria quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, feathery appearance, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Sesleria quitensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
