Salacia buddinghii

Salacia buddinghii, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Salicorniaceae and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
