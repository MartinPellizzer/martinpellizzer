Solanum aethiopoides

Solanum aethiopoides, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum aethiopoides belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
