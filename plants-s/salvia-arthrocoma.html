Salvia arthrocoma

Salvia arthrocoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia arthrocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
