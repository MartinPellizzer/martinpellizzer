Selago prunastri

Selago prunastri, commonly known as the common milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Selago prunastri is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
