Senecio mustersii var. dentatus

Senecio mustersii var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive serrated or toothed edges, which give it its scientific name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
