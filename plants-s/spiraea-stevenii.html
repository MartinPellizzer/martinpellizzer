Spiraea stevenii

Spiraea stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright growth with finely cut leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Spiraea stevenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
