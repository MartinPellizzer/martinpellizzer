Solanum geminifolium

Solanum geminifolium, commonly known as the twinleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often appear in pairs, giving it its common name. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces berries that range in color from yellow to red.

Solanum geminifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
