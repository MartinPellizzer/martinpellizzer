Serapias longipetala var. parviflora

Serapias longipetala var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, and it produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant prefers rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
