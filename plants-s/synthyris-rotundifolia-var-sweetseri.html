Synthyris rotundifolia var. sweetseri

Synthyris rotundifolia var. sweetseri, commonly known as the Sweetser's roundleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fleshy, round leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
