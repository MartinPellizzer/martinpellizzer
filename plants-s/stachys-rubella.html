Stachys rubella

Stachys rubella, commonly known as redstem betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish tinge along the veins. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Stachys rubella is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
