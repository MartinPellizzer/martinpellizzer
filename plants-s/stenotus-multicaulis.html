Stenotus multicaulis

Stenotus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered stenotus, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with multiple stems arising from the base. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Stenotus multicaulis thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
