Stereospermum senegalense

Stereospermum senegalense, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody capsules containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
