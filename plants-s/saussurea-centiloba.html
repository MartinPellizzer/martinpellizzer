Saussurea centiloba

Saussurea centiloba, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flower heads that are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
