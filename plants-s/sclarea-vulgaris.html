Sclarea vulgaris

Sclarea vulgaris, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Sclarea vulgaris is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
