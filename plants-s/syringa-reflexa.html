Syringa reflexa

Syringa reflexa, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. Syringa reflexa typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a spreading, weeping habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
