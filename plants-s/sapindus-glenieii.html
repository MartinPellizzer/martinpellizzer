Sapindus glenieii

Sapindus glenieii, commonly known as the Mexican soapberry, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. The fruits contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red aril, which is edible but has a soapy taste.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
