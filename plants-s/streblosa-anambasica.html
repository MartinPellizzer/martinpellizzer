Streblosa anambasica

Streblosa anambasica, commonly known as the Anambasica balsam plant, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical features include opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.
