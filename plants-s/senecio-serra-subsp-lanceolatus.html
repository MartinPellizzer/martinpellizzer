Senecio serra subsp. lanceolatus

Senecio serra subsp.

lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
