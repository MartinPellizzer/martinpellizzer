Siebera effusa

Siebera effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf siebera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen to deciduous foliage depending on the climate. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for certain wildlife.

Siebera effusa is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.
