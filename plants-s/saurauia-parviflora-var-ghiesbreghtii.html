Saurauia parviflora var. ghiesbreghtii

Saurauia parviflora var. ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's small-flowered saurauia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are borne on short pedicels.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
