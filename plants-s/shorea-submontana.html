Shorea submontana

Shorea submontana, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree typically grows in lowland to hill dipterocarp forests, often reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and prominent veins.

The plant plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
