Stenochilus pubiflorus

Stenochilus pubiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-flowered stenochilus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small but striking, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
