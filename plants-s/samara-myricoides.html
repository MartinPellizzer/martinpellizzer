Samara myricoides

Samara myricoides, commonly known as the smoothleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, winged fruits called samaras that are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability to various soil types.

The plant's morphology and reproductive strategies contribute to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
