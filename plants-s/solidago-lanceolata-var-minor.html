Solidago lanceolata var. minor

Solidago lanceolata var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early fall. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Solidago lanceolata.
