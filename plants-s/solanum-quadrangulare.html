Solanum quadrangulare

Solanum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Solanum quadrangulare is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
