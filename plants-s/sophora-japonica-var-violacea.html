Sophora japonica var. violacea

Sophora japonica var. violacea, commonly known as the purple pagoda tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring, forming dense clusters at the ends of its branches. The tree has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and urban landscapes.
