Selenipedium × grande

Selenipedium × grande, commonly known as the giant spider-orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, thin petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include a pseudobulbous stem and long, strap-like leaves.

Selenipedium × grande is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
