Silene marschallii subsp. propinqua

Silene marschallii subsp.

propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and prominent stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
