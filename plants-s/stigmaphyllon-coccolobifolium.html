Stigmaphyllon coccolobifolium

Stigmaphyllon coccolobifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, edible berries that are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
