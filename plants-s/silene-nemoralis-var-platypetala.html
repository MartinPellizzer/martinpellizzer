Silene nemoralis var. platypetala

Silene nemoralis var. platypetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene nemoralis var. platypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
