Sedum erici-magnusii subsp. chilianense

Sedum erici-magnusii subsp.

chilianense, commonly known as the Chilian sedum, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.
