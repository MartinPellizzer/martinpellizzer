Samama citrifolia

Samama citrifolia, commonly known as the custard apple tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a rough, green exterior and a creamy, aromatic pulp that is often used in desserts and beverages. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a fruit that is botanically classified as a berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit, which contains high levels of nutrients.
