Salvia zacualpanensis

Salvia zacualpanensis, commonly known as the Zacualpan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
