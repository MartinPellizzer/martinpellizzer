Silene colorata var. angustifolia

Silene colorata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueta, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has linear, grass-like leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
