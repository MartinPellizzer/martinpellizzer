Stellera circinata

Stellera circinata, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its stems are slender and can grow up to 30 inches tall, often branching near the top.

Stellera circinata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
