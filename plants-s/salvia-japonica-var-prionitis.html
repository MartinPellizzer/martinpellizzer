Salvia japonica var. prionitis

Salvia japonica var. prionitis, commonly known as Japanese sage or narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
