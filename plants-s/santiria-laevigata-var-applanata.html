Santiria laevigata var. applanata

Santiria laevigata var. applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf santiria, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a smooth surface, and it produces small yellow flowers.
