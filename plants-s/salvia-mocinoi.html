Salvia mocinoi

Salvia mocinoi, commonly known as Mocino's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Salvia mocinoi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
