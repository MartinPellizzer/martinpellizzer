Schefflera crassifolia

Schefflera crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor environments.
