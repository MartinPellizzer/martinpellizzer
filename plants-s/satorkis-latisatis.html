Satorkis latisatis

Satorkis latisatis, commonly known as the broadleaf satorkis, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its sturdy, upright stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and extensively branched, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Satorkis latisatis is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local pollinator populations.
