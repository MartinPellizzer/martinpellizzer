Sinocalamus sang

Sinocalamus sang, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Sinocalamus sang is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
