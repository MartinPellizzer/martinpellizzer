Sorbus alnifolia var. lobulata

Sorbus alnifolia var. lobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that have a distinctive lobed shape, giving it its specific epithet "lobulata.". The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
