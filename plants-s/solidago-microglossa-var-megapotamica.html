Solidago microglossa var. megapotamica

Solidago microglossa var. megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller leaves and slightly different floral structure compared to other Solidago species.
