Salix × ambigens

Salix × ambigens, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a species resulting from the cross between Salix alba and Salix triandra.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant features slender, zigzagging branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Salix × ambigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
