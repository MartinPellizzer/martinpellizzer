Stizolobium niveum

Stizolobium niveum, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its striking white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Stizolobium niveum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
