Saxifraga tricrenata var. minutifolia

Saxifraga tricrenata var. minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
