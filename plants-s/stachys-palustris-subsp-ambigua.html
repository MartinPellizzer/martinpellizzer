Stachys palustris subsp. ambigua

Stachys palustris subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other waterlogged areas.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed wetlands and can serve as an indicator of soil moisture conditions.
