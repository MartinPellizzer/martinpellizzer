Succisa gigantea

Succisa gigantea, commonly known as the giant succis, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that store water, a trait typical of succulent plants. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its arid environment. Its growth is slow and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Succisa gigantea is considered a species of conservation concern.
