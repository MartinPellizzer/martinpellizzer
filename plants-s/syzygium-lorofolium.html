Syzygium lorofolium

Syzygium lorofolium, commonly known as the Loro Folium apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines, characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium lorofolium thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
