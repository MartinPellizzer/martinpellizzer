Salsola postii

Salsola postii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Salsola postii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in disturbed soils and often found in deserts and along roadsides.

It is an invasive species in many regions, spreading rapidly and forming dense stands that can outcompete native vegetation.
