Sempervivum montanum f. minimum

Sempervivum montanum f. minimum, commonly known as the miniature mountain houseleek, is a compact, low-growing succulent with rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves.

It is native to the alpine regions of Central Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique size and hardy nature make it a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
