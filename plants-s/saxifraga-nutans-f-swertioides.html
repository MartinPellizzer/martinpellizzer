Saxifraga nutans f. swertioides

Saxifraga nutans f. swertioides, commonly known as the hanging saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
