Sorbus borosiana

Sorbus borosiana, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
