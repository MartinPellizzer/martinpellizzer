Saxifraga sikkimensis

Saxifraga sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Saxifraga sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
