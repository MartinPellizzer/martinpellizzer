Siphocampylus yumuriensis

Siphocampylus yumuriensis, commonly known as the Yumuriensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing vibrant yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
