Scirpus wichurae f. borealis

Scirpus wichurae f. borealis, commonly known as the northern bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence.

Scirpus wichurae f. borealis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic wildlife species.
