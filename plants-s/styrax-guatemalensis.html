Styrax guatemalensis

Styrax guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by hard, woody capsules containing glossy black seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological applications.
