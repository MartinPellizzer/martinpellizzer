Sericocoma capitata

Sericocoma capitata, commonly known as the clustered sericocoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Sericocoma capitata is often found in rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Africa.
