Spiraea dumosa

Spiraea dumosa, commonly known as evergreen spiraea, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Its foliage remains green throughout the year, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens.

Spiraea dumosa is also known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
