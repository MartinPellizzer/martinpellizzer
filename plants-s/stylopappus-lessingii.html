Stylopappus lessingii

Stylopappus lessingii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant produces a distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind. Stylopappus lessingii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions.
