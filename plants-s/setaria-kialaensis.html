Setaria kialaensis

Setaria kialaensis, commonly known as the Kiala grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Setaria kialaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
