Silene cucubalus var. petraea

Silene cucubalus var. petraea, commonly known as the rock catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can often be found in mountainous or coastal regions. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Silene cucubalus var. petraea is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping.
