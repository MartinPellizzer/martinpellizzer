Smilax chingii var. papillosifolia

Smilax chingii var. papillosifolia, commonly known as the hairy-leaf smilax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Smilacaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
