Smilax glaucophylla var. randaiensis

Smilax glaucophylla var. randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, waxy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible roots.
