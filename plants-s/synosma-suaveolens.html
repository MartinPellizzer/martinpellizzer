Synosma suaveolens

Synosma suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented synosma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential aromatic properties.
