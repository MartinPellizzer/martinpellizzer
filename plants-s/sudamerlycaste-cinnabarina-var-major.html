Sudamerlycaste cinnabarina var. major

Sudamerlycaste cinnabarina var. major, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow floral markings, which are particularly prominent on its showy, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of dense rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is noted for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
