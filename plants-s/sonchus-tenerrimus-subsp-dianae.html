Sonchus tenerrimus subsp. dianae

Sonchus tenerrimus subsp.

dianae, commonly known as the Mexican sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into spiny, oval-shaped fruits.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
