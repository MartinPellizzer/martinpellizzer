Silene parvifolia

Silene parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

Silene parvifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
