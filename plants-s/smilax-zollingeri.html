Smilax zollingeri

Smilax zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red or orange berries. Smilax zollingeri thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its hardy nature and ability to climb or trail make it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
