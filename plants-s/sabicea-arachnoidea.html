Sabicea arachnoidea

Sabicea arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
