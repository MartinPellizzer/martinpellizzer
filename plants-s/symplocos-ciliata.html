Symplocos ciliata

Symplocos ciliata, commonly known as the hairy waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. Symplocos ciliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
