Setaria splendida

Setaria splendida, commonly known as the golden millet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, often with a golden hue. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Setaria splendida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
