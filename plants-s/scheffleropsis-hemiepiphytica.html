Scheffleropsis hemiepiphytica

Scheffleropsis hemiepiphytica, commonly known as the climbing schefflera, is a unique plant belonging to the Araliaceae family.

It exhibits hemiepiphytic growth, meaning it begins its life on another plant, typically a tree, and later develops roots that anchor it to the substrate. The plant has a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it ascend towards light. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with multiple leaflets, often arranged in a whorled pattern.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
