Scorzonera mollis subsp. idaea

Scorzonera mollis subsp.

idaea, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, elongated stems and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, adapted to store nutrients in harsh alpine environments. Scorzonera mollis subsp.

idaea is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
