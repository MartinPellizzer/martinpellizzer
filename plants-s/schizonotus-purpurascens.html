Schizonotus purpurascens

Schizonotus purpurascens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid environment. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in crevices, while its flowers are highly specialized for pollination by specific insect species.

Schizonotus purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
