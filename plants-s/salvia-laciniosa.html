Salvia laciniosa

Salvia laciniosa, commonly known as the Mexican mint or woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
