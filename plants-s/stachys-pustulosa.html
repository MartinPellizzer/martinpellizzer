Stachys pustulosa

Stachys pustulosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Stachys pustulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
