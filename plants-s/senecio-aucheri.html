Senecio aucheri

Senecio aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping stem and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Senecio aucheri is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
