Sophora chrysophylla subsp. glabrata

Sophora chrysophylla subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the golden-leaf sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its bright yellow leaves, which are densely packed and give the plant a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant foliage.
