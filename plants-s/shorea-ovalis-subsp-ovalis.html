Shorea ovalis subsp. ovalis

Shorea ovalis subsp.

ovalis, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface.

The tree produces heavy, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in mixed dipterocarp forests.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
