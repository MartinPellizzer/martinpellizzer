Sclerolaena muricata var. villosa

Sclerolaena muricata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bristly hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden use.
