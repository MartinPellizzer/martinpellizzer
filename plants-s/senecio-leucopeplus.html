Senecio leucopeplus

Senecio leucopeplus, commonly known as the whiteleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pale green to brown seed heads.

Senecio leucopeplus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
