Sida hirta

Sida hirta, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in warm, sunny environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its ability to establish quickly in various soil types.

Sida hirta has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
