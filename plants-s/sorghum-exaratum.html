Sorghum exaratum

Sorghum exaratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in challenging climates.

Sorghum exaratum is often used for forage and has potential agricultural and ecological applications.
