Stylochaeton natalense subsp. maximum

Stylochaeton natalense subsp.

maximum, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems.

The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
