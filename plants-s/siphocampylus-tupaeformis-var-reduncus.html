Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. reduncus

Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. reduncus, commonly known as the reduncus variant of the mountain penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the arid, mountainous environment. This variety is known for its striking appearance and is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

It plays a role in pollination by attracting various insects, including bees and butterflies.
