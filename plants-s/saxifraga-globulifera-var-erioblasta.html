Saxifraga globulifera var. erioblasta

Saxifraga globulifera var. erioblasta, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevations. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine botany and a subject of ecological study.
