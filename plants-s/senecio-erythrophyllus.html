Senecio erythrophyllus

Senecio erythrophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant is notable for its striking red or orange foliage, which contrasts vividly with its yellow central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
