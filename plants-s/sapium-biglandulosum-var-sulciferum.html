Sapium biglandulosum var. sulciferum

Sapium biglandulosum var. sulciferum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese lantern, is a variant of the Chinese lantern plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to China, often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bright orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.
