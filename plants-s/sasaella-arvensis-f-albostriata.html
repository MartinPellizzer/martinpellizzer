Sasaella arvensis f. albostriata

Sasaella arvensis f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped field sasa, is a variant of the field sasa, a grass-like plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are usually inconspicuous.

This form is notable for its distinctive white streaking on the leaf sheaths, which sets it apart from other variants of the species.
