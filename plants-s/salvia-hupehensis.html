Salvia hupehensis

Salvia hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia hupehensis is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
