Sonchus canariae f. angustissimus

Sonchus canariae f. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf common sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Sonchus canariae.
