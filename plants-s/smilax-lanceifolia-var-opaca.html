Smilax lanceifolia var. opaca

Smilax lanceifolia var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
