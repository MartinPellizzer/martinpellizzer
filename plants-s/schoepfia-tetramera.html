Schoepfia tetramera

Schoepfia tetramera, commonly known as the four-leaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Schoepfia tetramera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various healing purposes.
