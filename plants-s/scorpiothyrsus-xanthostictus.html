Scorpiothyrsus xanthostictus

Scorpiothyrsus xanthostictus, commonly known as the yellow-streaked devil's claw, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
