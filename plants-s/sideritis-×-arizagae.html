Sideritis × arizagae

Sideritis × arizagae, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scardica and Sideritis arizae, both native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, pink or purple flowers are clustered in whorls, and the leaves are often hairy and aromatic.

Sideritis × arizagae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
