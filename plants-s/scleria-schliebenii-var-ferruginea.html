Scleria schliebenii var. ferruginea

Scleria schliebenii var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct reddish-brown sheath, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in swampy areas.
