Saccolabium obtusifolium

Saccolabium obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Saccolabium obtusifolium is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
