Simira hirsuta

Simira hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy simira, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
