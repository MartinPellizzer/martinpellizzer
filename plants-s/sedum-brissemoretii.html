Sedum brissemoretii

Sedum brissemoretii, commonly known as the Bristlemere stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in June and July. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
