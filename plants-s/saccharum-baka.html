Saccharum baka

Saccharum baka, commonly known as the bamboo cane grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized for their fibrous material, which can be processed into various products.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, Saccharum baka plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
