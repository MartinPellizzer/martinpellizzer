Senecio cancellatus

Senecio cancellatus, commonly known as the laceleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio cancellatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
