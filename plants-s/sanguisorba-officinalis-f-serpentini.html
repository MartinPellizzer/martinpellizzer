Sanguisorba officinalis f. serpentini

Sanguisorba officinalis f. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a variant of the common sorrel.

It is a perennial herb with striking, zigzagging stems and bright red to orange flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Its botanical name reflects its medicinal uses and distinctive appearance.
