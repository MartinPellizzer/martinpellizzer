Saxifraga lingulata var. lantoscana

Saxifraga lingulata var. lantoscana, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It typically grows in crevices and on slopes, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. It is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
