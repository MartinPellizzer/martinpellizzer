Scoria tomentosa

Scoria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly scoria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Scoria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
