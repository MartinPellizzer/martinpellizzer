Setiscapella

Setiscapella, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular choice for evening gardens.
