Sarcogonum volcanicum

Sarcogonum volcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic rock fern, is a unique plant species adapted to extreme environments.

It thrives in volcanic soils, where it forms dense mats that stabilize the soil and prevent erosion. The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor volcanic substrates. Its leaves are small and leathery, reducing water loss in arid conditions.

Sarcogonum volcanicum plays a crucial role in the ecological succession of volcanic landscapes.
