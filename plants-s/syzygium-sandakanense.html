Syzygium sandakanense

Syzygium sandakanense, commonly known as the Bornean satinash, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium sandakanense plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
