Solidago laevicaulis

Solidago laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its stiff, smooth stems and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Solidago laevicaulis plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
