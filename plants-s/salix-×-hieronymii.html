Salix × hieronymii

Salix × hieronymii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or reddish in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
