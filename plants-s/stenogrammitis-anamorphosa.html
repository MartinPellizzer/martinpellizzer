Stenogrammitis anamorphosa

Stenogrammitis anamorphosa, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The plant's structure and reproductive features are typical of the Polypodiaceae family, with a focus on spore dispersal.
