Setaria reverchonii

Setaria reverchonii, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Setaria reverchonii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
