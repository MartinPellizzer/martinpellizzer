Scutellaria footeana

Scutellaria footeana, commonly known as Foote's blue skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Scutellaria footeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
