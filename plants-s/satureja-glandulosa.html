Satureja glandulosa

Satureja glandulosa, commonly known as glandular savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their peppery, minty flavor.

Satureja glandulosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
