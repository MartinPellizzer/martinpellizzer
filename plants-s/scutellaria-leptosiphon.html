Scutellaria leptosiphon

Scutellaria leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Scutellaria leptosiphon is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
