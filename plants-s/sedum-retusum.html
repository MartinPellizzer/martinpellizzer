Sedum retusum

Sedum retusum, commonly known as the bluntleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Sedum retusum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
