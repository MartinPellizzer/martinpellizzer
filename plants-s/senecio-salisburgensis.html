Senecio salisburgensis

Senecio salisburgensis, commonly known as the Salzburg groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio salisburgensis is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
