Stevia collodes

Stevia collodes, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Stevia collodes is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides.

It is widely used as a natural sweetener in various food and beverage products.
