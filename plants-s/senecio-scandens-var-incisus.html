Senecio scandens var. incisus

Senecio scandens var. incisus, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that forms dense mats of foliage. The leaves are typically lobed or toothed, with a grayish-green color and a fuzzy texture. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
