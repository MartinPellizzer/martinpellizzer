Scindapsus siamensis

Scindapsus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam spider plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil. Its most distinctive feature is its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

It is often used as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
