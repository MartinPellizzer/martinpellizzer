Salvia evansiana

Salvia evansiana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Salvia evansiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
