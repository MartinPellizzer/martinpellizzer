Scutellaria scarlatina

Scutellaria scarlatina, commonly known as scarlet skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to scarlet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shady areas.

Scutellaria scarlatina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
