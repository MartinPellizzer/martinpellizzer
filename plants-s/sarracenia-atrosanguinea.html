Sarracenia atrosanguinea

Sarracenia atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black-sanguine sarracenia, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark red to black pitchers that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. The plant's leaves form tubular structures that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires a humid environment with ample sunlight.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
