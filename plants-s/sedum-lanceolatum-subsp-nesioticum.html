Sedum lanceolatum subsp. nesioticum

Sedum lanceolatum subsp.

nesioticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, such as gravelly slopes and dry meadows.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
