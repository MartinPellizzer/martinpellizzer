Soldanella montana subsp. villosa

Soldanella montana subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly soldanella, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, or villosa, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine and subalpine flora.
