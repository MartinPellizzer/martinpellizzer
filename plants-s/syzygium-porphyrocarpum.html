Syzygium porphyrocarpum

Syzygium porphyrocarpum, commonly known as the purple-fruit sandpaper apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically dark purple when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
