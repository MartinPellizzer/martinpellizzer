Schismatoclada villiflora

Schismatoclada villiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved schismatoclada, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
