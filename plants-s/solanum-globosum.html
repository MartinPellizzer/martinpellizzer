Solanum globosum

Solanum globosum, commonly known as the globular nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Solanum globosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
