Strychnos rupicola

Strychnos rupicola, commonly known as the rock apple or mountain apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
