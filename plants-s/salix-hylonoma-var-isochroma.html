Salix hylonoma var. isochroma

Salix hylonoma var. isochroma, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
