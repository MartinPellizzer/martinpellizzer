Sorghum argenteum

Sorghum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, which are typically green or purplish in color. Silverleaf sorghum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also valued for its potential use in biofuel production and as a forage crop.
