Silene peduncularis var. macrocalyx

Silene peduncularis var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is noted for its showy, short-lived flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
