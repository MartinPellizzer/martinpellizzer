Sideritis × angustifolia subsp. mediovalentina

Sideritis × angustifolia subsp.

mediovalentina, commonly known as the Balkan ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
