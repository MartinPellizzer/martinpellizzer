Salix amygdalina var. glaucophylla

Salix amygdalina var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the white-barked weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white bark and long, cascading branches that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
