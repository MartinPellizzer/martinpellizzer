Sideritis × valentina subsp. laderoi

Sideritis × valentina subsp.

laderoi, commonly known as the Greek violet hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
