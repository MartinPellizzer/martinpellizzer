Stanhopea aurea major

Stanhopea aurea major, commonly known as the golden stanhopea, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are long-lasting and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
