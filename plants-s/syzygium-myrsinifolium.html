Syzygium myrsinifolium

Syzygium myrsinifolium, commonly known as the red lily or red hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its showy, red bracts that resemble petals. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive red calyx that persists after the petals fall.

Syzygium myrsinifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types in its native habitat.
