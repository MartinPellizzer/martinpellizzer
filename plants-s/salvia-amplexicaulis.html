Salvia amplexicaulis

Salvia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, fuzzy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia amplexicaulis is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
