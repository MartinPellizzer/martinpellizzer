Smilax campestris var. marginulata

Smilax campestris var. marginulata, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with yellow margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
