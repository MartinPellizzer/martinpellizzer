Sasa senanensis

Sasa senanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Sasa senanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
