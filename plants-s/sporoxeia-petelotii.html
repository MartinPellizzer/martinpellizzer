Sporoxeia petelotii

Sporoxeia petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's spore plant, is a unique member of the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cone-like formations called strobili. These sporangia produce and release spores, which are essential for the plant's reproduction. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of ancient plant evolution and spore dispersal mechanisms.
