Salsola sedifolia

Salsola sedifolia, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are typically succulent, helping it conserve water in harsh conditions.

Salsola sedifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
