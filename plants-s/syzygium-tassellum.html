Syzygium tassellum

Syzygium tassellum, commonly known as the Tassell's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Syzygium tassellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native region.
