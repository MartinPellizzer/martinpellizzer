Senecio otuscensis

Senecio otuscensis, commonly known as the Otuscensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
