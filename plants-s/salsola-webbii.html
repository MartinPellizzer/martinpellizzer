Salsola webbii

Salsola webbii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or sandy soils effectively.

Salsola webbii plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
