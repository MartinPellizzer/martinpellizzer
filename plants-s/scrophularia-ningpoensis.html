Scrophularia ningpoensis

Scrophularia ningpoensis, commonly known as Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia ningpoensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
