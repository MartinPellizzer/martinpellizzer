Satureja discolor var. manaurensis

Satureja discolor var. manaurensis, commonly known as the Manaure mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of high-altitude flora.
