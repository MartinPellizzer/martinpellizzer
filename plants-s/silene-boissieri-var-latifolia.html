Silene boissieri var. latifolia

Silene boissieri var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Silene boissieri var. latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
