Stanhopea intermedia

Stanhopea intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in a spiral arrangement along the stem. The plant produces pendulous, showy blooms with a complex structure, featuring a lip that is often brightly colored. Stanhopea intermedia prefers warm, humid environments and is often grown as a terrestrial orchid in cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the orchid family's evolutionary diversity.
