Swertia shastaensis

Swertia shastaensis, commonly known as Shasta gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Swertia shastaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
