Schisandra repanda

Schisandra repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf fiveleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-leafed structure on its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Schisandra repanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species within its native habitat.
