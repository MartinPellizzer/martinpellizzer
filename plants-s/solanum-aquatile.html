Solanum aquatile

Solanum aquatile, commonly known as water nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and often clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Solanum aquatile belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
