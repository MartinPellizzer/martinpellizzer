Solanum andigenum convar. acutifolium

Solanum andigenum convar. acutifolium, commonly known as the Andean potato plant, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with acute, lanceolate tips. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by berries that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on local conditions.

Botanically, it is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
