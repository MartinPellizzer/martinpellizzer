Strophanthus arboreus

Strophanthus arboreus, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its cardiac glycosides.
