Satureja insularis

Satureja insularis, commonly known as island savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
