Syzygium occlusum

Syzygium occlusum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that give way to round, yellow or orange fruits resembling apples. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
