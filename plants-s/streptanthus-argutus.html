Streptanthus argutus

Streptanthus argutus, commonly known as the roughleaf stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is known for its strong, pungent odor, which is released when the plant is disturbed.
