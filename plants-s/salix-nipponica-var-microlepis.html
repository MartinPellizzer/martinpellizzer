Salix nipponica var. microlepis

Salix nipponica var. microlepis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often turning yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.
