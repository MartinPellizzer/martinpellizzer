Sorghum schlumbergeri

Sorghum schlumbergeri, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or red flowers, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to store water in its stems, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
