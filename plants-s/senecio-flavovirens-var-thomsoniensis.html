Senecio flavovirens var. thomsoniensis

Senecio flavovirens var. thomsoniensis, commonly known as Thomson's yellow-green groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
