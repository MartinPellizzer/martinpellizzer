Sasa paniculata f. uyetsuensis

Sasa paniculata f. uyetsuensis, commonly known as the Uyetsu bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
