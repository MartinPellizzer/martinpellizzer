Senecio delphinifolius

Senecio delphinifolius, commonly known as the blue-eared senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
