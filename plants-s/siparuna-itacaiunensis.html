Siparuna itacaiunensis

Siparuna itacaiunensis, commonly known as the pink sylph, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Siparuna itacaiunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
