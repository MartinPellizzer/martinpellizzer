Scilla verna f. versicolor

Scilla verna f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable squill, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to woodland gardens.
