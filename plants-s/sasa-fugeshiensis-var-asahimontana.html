Sasa fugeshiensis var. asahimontana

Sasa fugeshiensis var. asahimontana, commonly known as the Asahimontan bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are lance-shaped and provide a dense canopy, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
