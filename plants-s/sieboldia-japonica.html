Sieboldia japonica

Sieboldia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-yellow seed cones that mature over several years. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

Sieboldia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
