Salmea nitida

Salmea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "silverleaf" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
