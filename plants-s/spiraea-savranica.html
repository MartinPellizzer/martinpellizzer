Spiraea savranica

Spiraea savranica, commonly known as the Savran thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Spiraea savranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
