Solanum tuberosum f. camota

Solanum tuberosum f. camota, commonly known as the Camote or sweet potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its swollen, tuberous roots that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Americas. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both its edible tubers and ornamental value.
