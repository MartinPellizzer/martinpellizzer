Saccus bracteatus

Saccus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
