Senecio retortus

Senecio retortus, commonly known as the twisted everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, papery bracts that resemble petals, giving it a unique appearance.

Senecio retortus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking form and drought tolerance.
