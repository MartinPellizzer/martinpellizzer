Sericocarpus conyzoides var. plantaginifolius

Sericocarpus conyzoides var. plantaginifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bracts that persist into winter.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
