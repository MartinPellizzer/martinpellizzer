Scoparia neglecta var. intermedia

Scoparia neglecta var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate scurvygrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in disturbed soils and coastal areas, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with prostrate stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, sharing characteristics with other mustard family plants.
