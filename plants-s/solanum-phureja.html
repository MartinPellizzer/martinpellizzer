Solanum phureja

Solanum phureja, commonly known as the purple potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive violet-colored tubers. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and has compound leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally cultivated for food.

Solanum phureja is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in gardens for its unique appearance.
