Salvia tiliifolia var. albiflora

Salvia tiliifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive, mist-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and covered in a soft, velvety texture.

It is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
