Sideranthus cotula

Sideranthus cotula, commonly known as little false flax, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
