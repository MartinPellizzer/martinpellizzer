Sophia pinnata var. purpureola

Sophia pinnata var. purpureola, commonly known as the purpleleaf sophia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely pinnate leaves that turn a striking purplish-red color in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
