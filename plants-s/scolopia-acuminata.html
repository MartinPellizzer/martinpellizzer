Scolopia acuminata

Scolopia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed scolopia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Scolopia acuminata is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
