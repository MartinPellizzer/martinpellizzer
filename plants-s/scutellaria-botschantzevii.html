Scutellaria botschantzevii

Scutellaria botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchantzev's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
