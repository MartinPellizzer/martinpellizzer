Stenocarpus deltoidifolius

Stenocarpus deltoidifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of lanceolate to deltoid leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clustered, spore-bearing structures called sori. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive, leathery leaves and adaptability to various soil types.
