Salsola huabica

Salsola huabica, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Salsola huabica is adapted to survive in saline and nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
