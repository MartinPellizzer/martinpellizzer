Syzygium tramnion

Syzygium tramnion, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium tramnion is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
