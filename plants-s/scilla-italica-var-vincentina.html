Scilla italica var. vincentina

Scilla italica var. vincentina, commonly known as the Vincent's squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in woodland areas and along rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
