Sempervivum × rhaeticum

Sempervivum × rhaeticum, commonly known as the "Himalayan houseleek," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under bright light. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, though it prefers cooler temperatures.
