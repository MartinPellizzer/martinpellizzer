Stipa laxiflora

Stipa laxiflora, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in early summer.

Stipa laxiflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
