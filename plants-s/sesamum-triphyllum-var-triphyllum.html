Sesamum triphyllum var. triphyllum

Sesamum triphyllum var. triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique triphyllous leaves, which consist of three distinct leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been the subject of various botanical and pharmacological studies.
