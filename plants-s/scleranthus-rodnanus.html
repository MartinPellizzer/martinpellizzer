Scleranthus rodnanus

Scleranthus rodnanus, commonly known as the rodnanus thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Scleranthus rodnanus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
