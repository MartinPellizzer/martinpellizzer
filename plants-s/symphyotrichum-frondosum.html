Symphyotrichum frondosum

Symphyotrichum frondosum, commonly known as frondosa New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy purple to pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
