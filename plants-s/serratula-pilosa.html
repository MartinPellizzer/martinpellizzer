Serratula pilosa

Serratula pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Serratula pilosa is an annual herb that can be invasive in certain regions.
