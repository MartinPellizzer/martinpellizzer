Scutellaria lushuiensis

Scutellaria lushuiensis, commonly known as the Lushui skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
