Solidago sempervirens subsp. azorica

Solidago sempervirens subsp.

azorica, commonly known as the Azores goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Azores Islands. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
