Sesbania wildemannii

Sesbania wildemannii, commonly known as the wildemann's sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Sesbania wildemannii is often used for forage and soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
