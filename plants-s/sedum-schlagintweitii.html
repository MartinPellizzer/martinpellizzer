Sedum schlagintweitii

Sedum schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Schlagintweit stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid climates.
