Scutellaria aurea var. conzattii

Scutellaria aurea var. conzattii, commonly known as the golden skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
