Sedum surculosum

Sedum surculosum, commonly known as the cushion sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
