Setaria pyramidata

Setaria pyramidata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Setaria pyramidata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
