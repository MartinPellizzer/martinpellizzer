Statice conspicua

Statice conspicua, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the western coast of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes.

Statice conspicua is a hardy perennial that blooms in summer, adding vibrant color to coastal landscapes.
