Spiraea media var. oblongifolia

Spiraea media var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
