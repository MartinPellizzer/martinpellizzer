Salvia carduacea

Salvia carduacea, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-green, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.

Salvia carduacea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
