Scleranthus aequidens

Scleranthus aequidens, commonly known as the common sicklethorn, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the fruits are small, dry capsules that disperse seeds by wind.

Scleranthus aequidens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
