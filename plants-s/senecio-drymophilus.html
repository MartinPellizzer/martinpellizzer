Senecio drymophilus

Senecio drymophilus, commonly known as the dry-mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio drymophilus is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
