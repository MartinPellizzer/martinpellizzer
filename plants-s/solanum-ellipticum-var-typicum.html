Solanum ellipticum var. typicum

Solanum ellipticum var. typicum, commonly known as the elliptic nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
