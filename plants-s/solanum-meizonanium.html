Solanum meizonanium

Solanum meizonanium, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are often purple or violet in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Solanum meizonanium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
