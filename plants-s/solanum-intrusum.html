Solanum intrusum

Solanum intrusum, commonly known as the invasive nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is considered a significant weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
