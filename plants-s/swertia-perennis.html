Swertia perennis

Swertia perennis, commonly known as the perennial gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Swertia perennis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
