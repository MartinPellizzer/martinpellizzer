Senecio racemosus subsp. kirghisicus

Senecio racemosus subsp.

kirghisicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz Senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including drought and extreme temperatures.
