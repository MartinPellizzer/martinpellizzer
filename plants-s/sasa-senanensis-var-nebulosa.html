Sasa senanensis var. nebulosa

Sasa senanensis var. nebulosa, commonly known as the clouded bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa senanensis species native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming evergreen shrub that belongs to the grass family Poaceae, though it is often classified within the bamboo subfamily. The plant is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that give it a mat-like appearance, and its stems are typically short and stout. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of China and Japan.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
