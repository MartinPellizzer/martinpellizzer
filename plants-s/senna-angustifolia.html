Senna angustifolia

Senna angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Senna angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
