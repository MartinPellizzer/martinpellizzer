Sasaella viridistriata

Sasaella viridistriata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, characteristic of the grass family. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a striking green stripe running along the midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
