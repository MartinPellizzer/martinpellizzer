Spergula rubra subsp. radicans

Spergula rubra subsp.

radicans, commonly known as red sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces flowering stems that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
