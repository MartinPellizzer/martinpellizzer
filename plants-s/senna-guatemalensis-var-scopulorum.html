Senna guatemalensis var. scopulorum

Senna guatemalensis var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in rocky soils and can withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it useful in ecological restoration projects.
