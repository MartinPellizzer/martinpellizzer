Sarcoglottis metallica

Sarcoglottis metallica, commonly known as the silver-stemmed orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, silver-hued stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Sarcoglottis metallica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
