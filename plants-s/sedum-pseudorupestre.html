Sedum pseudorupestre

Sedum pseudorupestre, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a succulent plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum pseudorupestre is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
