Sisymbrella pseudoboissieri

Sisymbrella pseudoboissieri, commonly known as the Armenian rock cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are characterized by four petals.

Sisymbrella pseudoboissieri is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in high-altitude areas with limited soil.
