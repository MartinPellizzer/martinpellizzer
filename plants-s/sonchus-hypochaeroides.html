Sonchus hypochaeroides

Sonchus hypochaeroides, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by small, spiky fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
