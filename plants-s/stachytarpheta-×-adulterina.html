Stachytarpheta × adulterina

Stachytarpheta × adulterina, commonly known as the hybrid purpletop, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Stachytarpheta × adulterina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
