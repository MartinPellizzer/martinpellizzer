Schizostachyum hantu

Schizostachyum hantu, commonly known as the Hantu bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is often found in wetland and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Schizostachyum hantu is valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
