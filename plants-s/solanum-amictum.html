Solanum amictum

Solanum amictum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its fruits are edible when ripe and have a tangy, acidic flavor.

Solanum amictum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid climates.
