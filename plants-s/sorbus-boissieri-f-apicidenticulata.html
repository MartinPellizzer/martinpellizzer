Sorbus boissieri f. apicidenticulata

Sorbus boissieri f. apicidenticulata, commonly known as the apicidenticulate whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam genus Sorbus.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a serrated apex with small, sharp teeth. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that mature to a reddish color, providing food for various wildlife.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological significance.
