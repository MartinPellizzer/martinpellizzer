Spiraea ferganensis

Spiraea ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana spirea, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Fergana Valley.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea ferganensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
