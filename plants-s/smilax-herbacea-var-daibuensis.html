Smilax herbacea var. daibuensis

Smilax herbacea var. daibuensis, commonly known as the Japanese catbriar, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features woody stems with thorny projections and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the genus Smilax, with unique adaptations for survival in its native habitat.
