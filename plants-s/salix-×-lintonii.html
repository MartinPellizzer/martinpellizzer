Salix × lintonii

Salix × lintonii, commonly known as the Linton's willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine down. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
