Senecio pilquensis

Senecio pilquensis, commonly known as the Bolivian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are generally hairy and serrated, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio pilquensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
