Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. hirtula

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as rough-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
