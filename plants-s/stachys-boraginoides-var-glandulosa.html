Stachys boraginoides var. glandulosa

Stachys boraginoides var. glandulosa, commonly known as sticky betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and sticky to the touch. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its sticky leaves and ornamental flowers make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
