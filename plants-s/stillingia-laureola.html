Stillingia laureola

Stillingia laureola, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange roots, which have been historically used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Stillingia laureola thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
