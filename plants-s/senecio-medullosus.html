Senecio medullosus

Senecio medullosus, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescence that resembles a snowflake, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Senecio medullosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
