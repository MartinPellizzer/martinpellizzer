Scutellaria meehanioides var. meehanioides

Scutellaria meehanioides var. meehanioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet, tubular, and arranged in whorls along the stems.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and aromatic foliage.
