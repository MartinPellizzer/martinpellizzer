Salix nigricans var. firma

Salix nigricans var. firma, commonly known as black willow firma, is a variant of the black willow species native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows along stream banks and moist soils. The plant features slender, gray-barked branches and narrow, elongated leaves with a serrated margin. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to wetland environments.
