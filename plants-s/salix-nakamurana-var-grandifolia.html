Salix nakamurana var. grandifolia

Salix nakamurana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered willow, is a variant of the Nakamura willow native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
