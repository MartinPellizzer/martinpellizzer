Syngrammatopsis terrestris

Syngrammatopsis terrestris, commonly known as the groundsel or common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
