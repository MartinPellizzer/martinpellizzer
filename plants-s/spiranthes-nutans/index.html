Spiranthes Nutans

Twisting spike-rush, scientifically known as Spiranthes nutans, is a striking member of the orchid family that captivates with its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence. Native to North America, this plant thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. Its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, adding a touch of elegance to the natural landscape. Despite its beauty, the Twisting spike-rush remains a relatively understudied plant, with much still to be learned about its biology and conservation status. As an indicator species, it serves as a barometer for the health of wetland ecosystems across the continent.
