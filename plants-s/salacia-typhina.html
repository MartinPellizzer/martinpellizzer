Salacia typhina

Salacia typhina, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Its fruits, which are small berries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Salacia typhina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit production.
