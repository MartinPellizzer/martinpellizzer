Schizostachyum iraten var. longispiculatum

Schizostachyum iraten var. longispiculatum, commonly known as the long-spined bamboo, is a variant of the Schizostachyum iraten species, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted foliage, often growing in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth texture, while the inflorescence consists of clustered, spike-like structures.

This variant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in diverse environmental conditions.
