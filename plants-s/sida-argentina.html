Sida argentina

Sida argentina, commonly known as the Argentine mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used traditionally for their textile and medicinal properties.

Sida argentina is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
