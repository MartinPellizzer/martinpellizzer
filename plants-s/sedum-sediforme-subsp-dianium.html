Sedum sediforme subsp. dianium

Sedum sediforme subsp.

dianium, commonly known as the silver rose sedum, is a succulent plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that form a rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
