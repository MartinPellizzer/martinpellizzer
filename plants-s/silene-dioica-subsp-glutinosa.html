Silene dioica subsp. glutinosa

Silene dioica subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, sticky texture due to glandular hairs.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and woodland edges.
