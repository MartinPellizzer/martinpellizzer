Salvia nubia

Salvia nubia, commonly known as the cloud sage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Ethiopian Highlands.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and silver-gray foliage, which reflect its adaptation to arid and cool environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowering stems often rise above the foliage. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia nubia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
