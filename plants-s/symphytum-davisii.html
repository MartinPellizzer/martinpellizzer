Symphytum davisii

Symphytum davisii, commonly known as Davis's comfrey, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for healing purposes.
