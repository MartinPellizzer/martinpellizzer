Solanum deflexum

Solanum deflexum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and are followed by small, toxic berries.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
