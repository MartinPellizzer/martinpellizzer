Senecio canescens var. ecuadoriensis

Senecio canescens var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian white hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves with a distinctive white to grey pubescence that gives it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the daisy family.

This variant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, temperate climates.
