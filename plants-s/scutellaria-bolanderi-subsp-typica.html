Scutellaria bolanderi subsp. typica

Scutellaria bolanderi subsp.

typica, commonly known as Bolander's bluebug, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in dry environments. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.
