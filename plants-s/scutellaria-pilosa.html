Scutellaria pilosa

Scutellaria pilosa, commonly known as hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria pilosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
