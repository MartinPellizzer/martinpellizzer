Santiria lamprocarpa

Santiria lamprocarpa, commonly known as the golden sandburs, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish fruiting bodies called burrs, which are covered in hooked bracts for seed dispersal. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Santiria lamprocarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
