Symplocos indochinensis

Symplocos indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
