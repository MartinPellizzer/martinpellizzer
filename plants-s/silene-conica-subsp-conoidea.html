Silene conica subsp. conoidea

Silene conica subsp.

conoidea, commonly known as the conical campion, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive conical shape, with flowers that transition from white to pink as they age.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually glabrous. Silene conica subsp.

conoidea is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in temperate climates.
