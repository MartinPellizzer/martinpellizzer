Senecio multilobatus var. standleyi

Senecio multilobatus var. standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky slopes or in sandy soils. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a silvery or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.
