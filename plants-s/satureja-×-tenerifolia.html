Satureja × tenerifolia

Satureja × tenerifolia, commonly known as Canary Island thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces compact clusters of pink to purple flowers, which appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both garden use and traditional herbal purposes.
