Spinulum canadense

Spinulum canadense, commonly known as the Canadian spinulum, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
