Solanum maglia var. chubutense

Solanum maglia var. chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
