Solanum reineckii

Solanum reineckii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum reineckii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
