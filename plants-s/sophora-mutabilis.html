Sophora mutabilis

Sophora mutabilis, commonly known as the variable sophora, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
