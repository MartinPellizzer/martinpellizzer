Selaginella cladorrhizans

Selaginella cladorrhizans, commonly known as the club-moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spore-bearing strobila at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a scale-like structure.

Selaginella cladorrhizans is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
