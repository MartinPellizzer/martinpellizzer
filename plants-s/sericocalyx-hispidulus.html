Sericocalyx hispidulus

Sericocalyx hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy calyx thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, hairy calyx that surrounds its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Sericocalyx hispidulus is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a valuable component of native vegetation in its ecosystem.
