Syzygium leytense

Syzygium leytense, commonly known as the leytense apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor.

Syzygium leytense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
