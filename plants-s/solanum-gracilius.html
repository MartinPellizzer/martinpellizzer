Solanum gracilius

Solanum gracilius, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle it with care.
