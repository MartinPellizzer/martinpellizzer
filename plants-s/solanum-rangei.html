Solanum rangei

Solanum rangei, commonly known as the Range's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and are edible, though they contain toxic compounds.

Solanum rangei is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
