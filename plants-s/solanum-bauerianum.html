Solanum bauerianum

Solanum bauerianum, commonly known as the Bauer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. These berries are toxic to humans and animals due to the presence of alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
