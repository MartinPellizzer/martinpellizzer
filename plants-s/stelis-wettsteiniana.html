Stelis wettsteiniana

Stelis wettsteiniana, commonly known as the Wettstein's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Stelis wettsteiniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
