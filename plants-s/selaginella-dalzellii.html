Selaginella dalzellii

Selaginella dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often green or reddish in color. Selaginella dalzellii reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its natural habitat.
