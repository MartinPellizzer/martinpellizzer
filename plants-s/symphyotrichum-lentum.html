Symphyotrichum lentum

Symphyotrichum lentum, commonly known as the slender New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, violet-blue to purple petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Symphyotrichum lentum plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the fall season.
