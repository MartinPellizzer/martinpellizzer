Senecio cicatricosus

Senecio cicatricosus, commonly known as the scarred senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
