Senecio affinis

Senecio affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
