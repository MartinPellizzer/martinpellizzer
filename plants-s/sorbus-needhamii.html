Sorbus needhamii

Sorbus needhamii, commonly known as the Needham's whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
