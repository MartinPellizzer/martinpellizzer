Streptosema verrucosa

Streptosema verrucosa, commonly known as the warty streptosema, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

Streptosema verrucosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
