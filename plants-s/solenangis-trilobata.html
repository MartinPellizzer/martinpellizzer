Solenangis trilobata

Solenangis trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with three distinct lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Solenangis trilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
