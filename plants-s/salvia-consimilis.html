Salvia consimilis

Salvia consimilis, commonly known as the similar sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often emitting a mild minty scent.

Salvia consimilis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
