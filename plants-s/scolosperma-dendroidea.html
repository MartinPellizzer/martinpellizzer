Scolosperma dendroidea

Scolosperma dendroidea, commonly known as the African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scolosperma dendroidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
