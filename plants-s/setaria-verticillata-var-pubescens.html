Setaria verticillata var. pubescens

Setaria verticillata var. pubescens, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
