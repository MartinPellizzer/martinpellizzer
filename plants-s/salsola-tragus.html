Salsola tragus

Salsola tragus, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are greenish-white and appear in clusters, often in late summer.

Salsola tragus is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
