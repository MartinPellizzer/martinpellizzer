Senegalia polyacantha subsp. campylacantha

Senegalia polyacantha subsp.

campylacantha, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by dense, spreading branches.

The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are often shed during dry periods, and it produces yellow flowers followed by long, bean-like pods. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity for drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
