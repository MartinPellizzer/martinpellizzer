Schinus benghalensis

Schinus benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian poisonwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree's ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
