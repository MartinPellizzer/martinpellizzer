Scabiosa micrantha f. unicapitulata

Scabiosa micrantha f. unicapitulata, commonly known as the single-headed scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is native to southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable choice for gardeners interested in cultivating native or wildflower species.
