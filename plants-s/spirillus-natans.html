Spirillus natans

Spirillus natans, commonly known as the spiral water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped fronds.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant's fronds are composed of overlapping, ribbon-like leaves that spiral around a central axis, creating a visually striking appearance. Spirillus natans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its growth is typically influenced by water quality and light availability.
