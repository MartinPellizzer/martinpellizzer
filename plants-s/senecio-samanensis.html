Senecio samanensis

Senecio samanensis, commonly known as the San Benito dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitats.
