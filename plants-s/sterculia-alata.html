Sterculia alata

Sterculia alata, commonly known as the fan-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, fan-like appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
