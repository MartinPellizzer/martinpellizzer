Senecio microglossus

Senecio microglossus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio microglossus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
