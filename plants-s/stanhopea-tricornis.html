Stanhopea tricornis

Stanhopea tricornis, commonly known as the three-horned orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, producing blooms that range in color from white to yellow and orange. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil, adapting to its often humid and shaded habitat.

Stanhopea tricornis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
