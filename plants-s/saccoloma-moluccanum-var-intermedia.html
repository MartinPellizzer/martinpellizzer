Saccoloma moluccanum var. intermedia

Saccoloma moluccanum var. intermedia, commonly known as the Moluccan saccoloma, is a variant of the saccoloma genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fern-like plant characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and a distinctive rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its fronds are often leathery and have a bluish-green hue, with sporangia borne on the undersides of the leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
