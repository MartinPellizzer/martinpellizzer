Silene armeniaca

Silene armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Silene armeniaca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
