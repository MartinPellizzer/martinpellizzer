Saxifraga giziana

Saxifraga giziana, commonly known as the Giza saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white woolly hair.

Saxifraga giziana is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
