Saccolabium congestum

Saccolabium congestum, commonly known as the clustered saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Saccolabium congestum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
