Seruneum filipes

Seruneum filipes, commonly known as the slender featherplant, is a unique species characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are long and narrow, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in length, and are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Seruneum filipes is known for its ability to purify the air and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
