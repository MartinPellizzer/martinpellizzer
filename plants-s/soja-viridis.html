Soja viridis

Soja viridis, commonly known as green soybean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, green, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Soja viridis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
