Sedum donatae

Sedum donatae, commonly known as the Donata's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum donatae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
