Serratula lancifolia

Serratula lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Serratula lancifolia is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat, though it is not widely cultivated.
