Silene sicula

Silene sicula, commonly known as Sicilian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene sicula is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
