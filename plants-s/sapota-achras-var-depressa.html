Sapota achras var. depressa

Sapota achras var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf sapodilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet, starchy fruits with a distinctive brown, bumpy skin. Its fruit, known as the "chikoo" in some regions, is highly valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
