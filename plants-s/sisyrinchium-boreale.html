Sisyrinchium boreale

Sisyrinchium boreale, commonly known as the alpine blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Sisyrinchium boreale is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
