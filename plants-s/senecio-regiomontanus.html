Senecio regiomontanus

Senecio regiomontanus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio regiomontanus is often used in rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
