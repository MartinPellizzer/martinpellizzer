Syngonanthus eriophyllus var. calvescens

Syngonanthus eriophyllus var. calvescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has a low-growing habit with branching stems and is well adapted to arid conditions.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
