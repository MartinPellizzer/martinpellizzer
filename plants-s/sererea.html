Sererea

Sererea, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Sererea is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year under proper conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
