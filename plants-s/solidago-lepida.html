Solidago lepida

Solidago lepida, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago lepida is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
