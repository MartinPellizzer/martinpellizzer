Sesamoides suffruticosa

Sesamoides suffruticosa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
