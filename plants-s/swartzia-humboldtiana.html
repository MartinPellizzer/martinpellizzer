Swartzia humboldtiana

Swartzia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a significant component of the rainforest canopy.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.
