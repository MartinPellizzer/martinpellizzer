Sarothamnus cantabricus var. dieckii

Sarothamnus cantabricus var. dieckii, commonly known as the Cantabrian thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pinkish-white flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in Mediterranean ecosystems.
