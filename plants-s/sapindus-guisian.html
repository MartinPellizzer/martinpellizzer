Sapindus guisian

Sapindus guisian, commonly known as the Chinese soapnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, soap-like substance. This substance is used traditionally for cleaning and has mild detergent properties.

The tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
