Salvia pineticola

Salvia pineticola, commonly known as the redstem sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
