Sarcocapnos pulcherrima

Sarcocapnos pulcherrima, commonly known as the red sanders, is a rare and valuable tree native to the dry regions of southern India.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark that is highly sought after in the traditional medicine and woodworking industries. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

Due to overexploitation, it is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
