Smilax ramiflora

Smilax ramiflora, commonly known as the trailing smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with thorned stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax ramiflora has a long growing season and is adapted to humid, temperate climates.
