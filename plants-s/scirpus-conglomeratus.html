Scirpus conglomeratus

Scirpus conglomeratus, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical spikes of small, usually green or brown flowers. Native to wetlands and marshy areas, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant forms tussocks, which help prevent erosion and provide habitat for various organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish dense colonies in suitable environments.
