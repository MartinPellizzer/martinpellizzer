Solanum phlomidifolium var. gardneri

Solanum phlomidifolium var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.
