Stephanomeria exigua subsp. macrocarpa

Stephanomeria exigua subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the bigfruit globemallow, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued species in native plant gardens.
