Sorindeia aurantiodora

Sorindeia aurantiodora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in landscaped areas.
