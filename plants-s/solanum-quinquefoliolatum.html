Solanum quinquefoliolatum

Solanum quinquefoliolatum, commonly known as the five-leaf nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often deeply divided into five or more leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.

Solanum quinquefoliolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
