Saussurea gnaphalodes var. glabrata

Saussurea gnaphalodes var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth saussurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flower heads. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
