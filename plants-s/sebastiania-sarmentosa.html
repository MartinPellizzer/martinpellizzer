Sebastiania sarmentosa

Sebastiania sarmentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their characteristic appearance.

Sebastiania sarmentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
