Scrophularia henryi var. glabrescens

Scrophularia henryi var. glabrescens, commonly known as Henry's scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and range in color from pale pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in woodland margins and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
