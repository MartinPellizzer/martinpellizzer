Silene otites var. pseudotites

Silene otites var. pseudotites, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.
