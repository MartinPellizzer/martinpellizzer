Saussurea yamatonus

Saussurea yamatonus, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stem and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
