Senna ovalifolia

Senna ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a glossy green appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Senna ovalifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
