Sterculia rubiginosa var. setistipula

Sterculia rubiginosa var. setistipula, commonly known as the red-stemmed sterculia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
