Swertia nepalensis

Swertia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Swertia nepalensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic remedies.
