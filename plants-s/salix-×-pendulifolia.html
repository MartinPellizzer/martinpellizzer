Salix × pendulifolia

Salix × pendulifolia, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main stem, creating a unique, flowing appearance. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

Salix × pendulifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
