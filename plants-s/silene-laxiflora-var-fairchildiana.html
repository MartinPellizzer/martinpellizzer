Silene laxiflora var. fairchildiana

Silene laxiflora var. fairchildiana, commonly known as the Fairchild's looseflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
