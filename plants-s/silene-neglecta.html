Silene neglecta

Silene neglecta, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear mullein, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces tall, slender stems with pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Silene neglecta is often used in traditional medicine and is also a popular ornamental plant.
