Sideritis phlomifolia

Sideritis phlomifolia, commonly known as woolly ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis phlomifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
