Sesleria coerulans subsp. bielzii

Sesleria coerulans subsp.

bielzii, commonly known as the blue-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers clustered in dense spikes.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and localized species.
