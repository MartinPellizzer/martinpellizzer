Salix hutchinsii

Salix hutchinsii, commonly known as the Hutchins willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. This shrub or small tree typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry conditions. It has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins that are characteristic of willow species.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
