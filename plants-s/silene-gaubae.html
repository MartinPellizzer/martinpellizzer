Silene gaubae

Silene gaubae, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Silene gaubae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions.
