Salvia booleana

Salvia booleana, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
