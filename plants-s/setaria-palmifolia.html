Setaria palmifolia

Setaria palmifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that resemble the shape of a palm, hence its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Setaria palmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
