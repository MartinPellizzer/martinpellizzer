Saccharum balansae

Saccharum balansae, commonly known as Balansae grass, is a species of tall grass native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant is often found in wetland habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

However, it is not as widely cultivated or economically significant as other sugarcane species.
