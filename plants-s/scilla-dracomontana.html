Scilla dracomontana

Scilla dracomontana, commonly known as dragon's blood scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is closely related to the genus Scilla, which includes several species of flowering plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its name is derived from the reddish-brown sap it exudes, which resembles the color of dragon's blood.
