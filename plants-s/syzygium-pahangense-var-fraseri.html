Syzygium pahangense var. fraseri

Syzygium pahangense var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's tamarind, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
