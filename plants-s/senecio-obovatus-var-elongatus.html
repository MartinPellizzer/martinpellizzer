Senecio obovatus var. elongatus

Senecio obovatus var. elongatus, commonly known as the longleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, obovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
