Salacia doeringii

Salacia doeringii, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host tree, typically members of the Dipterocarpaceae family. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Salacia doeringii plays a minor ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
