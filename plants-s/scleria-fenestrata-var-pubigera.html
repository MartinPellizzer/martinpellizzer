Scleria fenestrata var. pubigera

Scleria fenestrata var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy-scented sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The variety 'pubigera' is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
