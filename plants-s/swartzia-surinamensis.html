Swartzia surinamensis

Swartzia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Swartzia surinamensis thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
