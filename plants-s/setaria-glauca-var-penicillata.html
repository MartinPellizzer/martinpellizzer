Setaria glauca var. penicillata

Setaria glauca var. penicillata, commonly known as needlegrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a blue or purple hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
