Statice capensis

Statice capensis, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments and coastal areas. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Statice capensis is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
