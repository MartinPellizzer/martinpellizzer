Santolina villosissima

Santolina villosissima, commonly known as woolly santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden design and herbal use.
