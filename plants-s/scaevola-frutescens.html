Scaevola frutescens

Scaevola frutescens, commonly known as the coastal loveplant, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs.

Scaevola frutescens is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate drought conditions.
