Salvia tsusimensis

Salvia tsusimensis, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia tsusimensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
