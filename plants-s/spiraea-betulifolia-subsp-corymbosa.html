Spiraea betulifolia subsp. corymbosa

Spiraea betulifolia subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the redleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscape design.
