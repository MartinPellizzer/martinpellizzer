Syngramma quinata

Syngramma quinata, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Syngramma quinata is native to Australia and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Its parasitic nature and unique appearance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
