Senecio antunesii

Senecio antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
