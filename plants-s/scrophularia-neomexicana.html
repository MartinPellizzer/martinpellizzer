Scrophularia neomexicana

Scrophularia neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico figwort, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and often purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
