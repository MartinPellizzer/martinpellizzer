Seruneum canescens

Seruneum canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf seruneum, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Seruneum canescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
