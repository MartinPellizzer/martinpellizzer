Silene saxifraga subsp. hayekiana

Silene saxifraga subsp.

hayekiana, commonly known as the Hayek's campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open, sunny habitats.

The plant produces striking pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its arid environment. Silene saxifraga subsp.

hayekiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
