Salvia anomala

Salvia anomala, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Salvia anomala is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
