Sorbus aria subsp. luristanica

Sorbus aria subsp.

luristanica, commonly known as the Luristan whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to parts of the Caucasus and Turkey. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that mature in late summer.

The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
