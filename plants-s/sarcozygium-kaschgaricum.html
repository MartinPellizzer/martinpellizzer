Sarcozygium kaschgaricum

Sarcozygium kaschgaricum, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
