Silphium tetragonum

Silphium tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angle silphium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. Silphium tetragonum is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along streambanks.

It is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible roots.
