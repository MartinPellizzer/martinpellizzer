Sesleria sphaerocephala var. leucocephala

Sesleria sphaerocephala var. leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rosette-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spherical flower heads that are white or pale pink, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
