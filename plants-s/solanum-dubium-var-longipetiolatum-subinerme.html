Solanum dubium var. longipetiolatum-subinerme

Solanum dubium var. longipetiolatum-subinerme, commonly known as the long-petioled nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It typically features elongated petioles that support broad, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The flowers are usually small, clustered in umbels, and exhibit a range of colors from white to pale yellow. This plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
