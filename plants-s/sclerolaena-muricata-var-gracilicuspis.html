Sclerolaena muricata var. gracilicuspis

Sclerolaena muricata var. gracilicuspis, commonly known as the slender-cusped rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
