Sedum linearifolium var. sacrum

Sedum linearifolium var. sacrum, commonly known as the sacred stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a dense rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in dry, rocky environments with minimal water.
