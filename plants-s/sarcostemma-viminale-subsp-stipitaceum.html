Sarcostemma viminale subsp. stipitaceum

Sarcostemma viminale subsp.

stipitaceum, commonly known as the stipitate milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often have a milky sap, which is a common feature among the Asclepiadaceae family.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
