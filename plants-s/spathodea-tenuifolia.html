Spathodea tenuifolia

Spathodea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Spathodea tenuifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
