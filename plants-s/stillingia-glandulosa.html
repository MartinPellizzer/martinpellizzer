Stillingia glandulosa

Stillingia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky mallow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often have a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Stillingia glandulosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
