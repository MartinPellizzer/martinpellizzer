Stenandrium macraganum

Stenandrium macraganum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged in an alternate pattern. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often exuding a sticky resin.

Stenandrium macraganum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
