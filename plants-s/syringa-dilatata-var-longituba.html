Syringa dilatata var. longituba

Syringa dilatata var. longituba, commonly known as the long-tubed lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features elongated, tubular flower buds that open into purple or pink flowers, making it distinctive from other lilac varieties. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
