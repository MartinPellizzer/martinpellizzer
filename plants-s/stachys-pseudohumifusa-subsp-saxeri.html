Stachys pseudohumifusa subsp. saxeri

Stachys pseudohumifusa subsp.

saxeri, commonly known as the saxerian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in sunny, well-drained areas.

The plant features hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and produce pale purple to pinkish blooms.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
