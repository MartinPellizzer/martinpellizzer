Sabatia kennedyana

Sabatia kennedyana, commonly known as Kennedy's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Sabatia kennedyana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
