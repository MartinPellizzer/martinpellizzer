Solanum schaeferi

Solanum schaeferi, commonly known as the Schaefer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and yellow stamens, and it produces small, round berries that contain toxic alkaloids.
