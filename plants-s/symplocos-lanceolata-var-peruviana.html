Symplocos lanceolata var. peruviana

Symplocos lanceolata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
