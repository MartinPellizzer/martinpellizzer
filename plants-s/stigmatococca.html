Stigmatococca

Stigmatococca, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care. Stigmatococca species are typically herbaceous and thrive in well-draining soil with moderate light.

They are often grown as houseplants due to their adaptability to indoor conditions.
