Sedum × hommelsii

Sedum × hommelsii, commonly known as the autumn sedum, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces vibrant clusters of yellow flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fleshy, blue-green leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum × hommelsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
