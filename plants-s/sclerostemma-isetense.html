Sclerostemma isetense

Sclerostemma isetense, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique flowering behavior.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
