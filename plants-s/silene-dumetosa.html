Silene dumetosa

Silene dumetosa, commonly known as the common sandcatcher, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Silene dumetosa is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
