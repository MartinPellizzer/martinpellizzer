Stoechas rotundifolia

Stoechas rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved eryngium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Stoechas rotundifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
