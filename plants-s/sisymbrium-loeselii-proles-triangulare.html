Sisymbrium loeselii proles triangulare

Sisymbrium loeselii proles triangulare, commonly known as the triangular false flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and has hairy, linear leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in grasslands and along roadsides.
