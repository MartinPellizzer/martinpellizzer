Seriphidium vallesianum

Seriphidium vallesianum, commonly known as the Andean dwarf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Seriphidium vallesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
