Saxifraga × lainzii

Saxifraga × lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that originates from the European Alps.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
