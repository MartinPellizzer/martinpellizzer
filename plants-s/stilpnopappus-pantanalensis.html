Stilpnopappus pantanalensis

Stilpnopappus pantanalensis, commonly known as the Pantanal goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Pantanal wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stilpnopappus pantanalensis is adapted to the seasonal flooding of its native environment, showcasing resilience to wet and dry conditions.
