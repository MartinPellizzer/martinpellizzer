Scutellaria megalodonta

Scutellaria megalodonta, commonly known as the large-toothed blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent teeth, giving it its common name.

Scutellaria megalodonta is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
