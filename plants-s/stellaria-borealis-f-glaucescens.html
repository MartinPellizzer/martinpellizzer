Stellaria borealis f. glaucescens

Stellaria borealis f. glaucescens, commonly known as the northern starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a bluish-gray hue. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in polar and high-altitude ecosystems.
