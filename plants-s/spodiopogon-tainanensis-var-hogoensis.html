Spodiopogon tainanensis var. hogoensis

Spodiopogon tainanensis var. hogoensis, commonly known as the Tainan sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
