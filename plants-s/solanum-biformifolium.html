Solanum biformifolium

Solanum biformifolium, commonly known as the two-lobed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature, often from green to red.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
