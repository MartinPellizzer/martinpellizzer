Senecio megalophron

Senecio megalophron, commonly known as the giant-eared senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, ear-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
