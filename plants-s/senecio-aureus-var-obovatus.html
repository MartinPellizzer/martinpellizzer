Senecio aureus var. obovatus

Senecio aureus var. obovatus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a stout stem and a compact growth habit, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
