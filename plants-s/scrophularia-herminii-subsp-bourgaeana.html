Scrophularia herminii subsp. bourgaeana

Scrophularia herminii subsp.

bourgaeana, commonly known as the Bourganeau scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
