Solanum williamsii

Solanum williamsii, commonly known as Williams' nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum williamsii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
