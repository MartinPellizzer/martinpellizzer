Selaginella undata

Selaginella undata, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain the spores.

Selaginella undata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to survive in arid conditions due to its ability to enter a state of dormancy.
