Senecio galicianus

Senecio galicianus, commonly known as Galician groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early spring.

Senecio galicianus is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
