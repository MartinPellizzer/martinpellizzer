Spiraea × pachystachys

Spiraea × pachystachys, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from Spiraea and a willow species.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, spreading form. Its foliage is usually dark green, turning reddish in the fall, and it produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer.

Spiraea × pachystachys thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
