Stevia subpubescens var. intermedia

Stevia subpubescens var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is noted for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have contributed to its popularity among horticulturists and herbalists.
