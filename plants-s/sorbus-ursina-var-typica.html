Sorbus ursina var. typica

Sorbus ursina var. typica, commonly known as the European rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin layers as the tree matures.
