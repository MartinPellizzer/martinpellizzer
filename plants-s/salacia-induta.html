Salacia induta

Salacia induta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a glossy surface that reflects light, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Salacia induta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
