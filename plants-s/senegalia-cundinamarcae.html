Senegalia cundinamarcae

Senegalia cundinamarcae, commonly known as the yellowbrush acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
