Sophronitis wittigiana var. brevipedunculata

Sophronitis wittigiana var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, clustered flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its beauty and fragrance.
