Senecio ulopterus

Senecio ulopterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually yellow and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
