Senecio mikaniifolius

Senecio mikaniifolius, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, mat-forming growth habit, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
