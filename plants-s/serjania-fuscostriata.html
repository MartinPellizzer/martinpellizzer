Serjania fuscostriata

Serjania fuscostriata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and long, slender leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a striking ornamental plant, often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
