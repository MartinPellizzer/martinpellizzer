Scirpus lineatus var. lushanensis

Scirpus lineatus var. lushanensis, commonly known as the Lushan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
