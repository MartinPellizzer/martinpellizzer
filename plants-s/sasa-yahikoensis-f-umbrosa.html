Sasa yahikoensis f. umbrosa

Sasa yahikoensis f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa yahikoensis species, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a soft, velvety texture. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in low-light conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to the understory vegetation in its native habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
