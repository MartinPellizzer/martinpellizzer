Sida glaucophylla

Sida glaucophylla, commonly known as coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.

Sida glaucophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
