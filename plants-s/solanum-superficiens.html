Solanum superficiens

Solanum superficiens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows along the ground, with trailing stems that can reach lengths of up to several meters. Its small, white or purple flowers appear in clusters and are followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
