Solanum betroka

Solanum betroka, commonly known as the "Betroka nightshade," is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its berries, which are toxic, contain alkaloids and are not suitable for human consumption.

The botanical characteristics of Solanum betroka make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
