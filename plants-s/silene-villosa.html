Silene villosa

Silene villosa, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene villosa is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar botanical features.
