Saxifraga engleri

Saxifraga engleri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh environments.

Saxifraga engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
