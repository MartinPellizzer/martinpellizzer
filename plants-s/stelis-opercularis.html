Stelis opercularis

Stelis opercularis, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Stelis opercularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
