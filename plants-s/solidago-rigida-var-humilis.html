Solidago rigida var. humilis

Solidago rigida var. humilis, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of many small, rayless flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
