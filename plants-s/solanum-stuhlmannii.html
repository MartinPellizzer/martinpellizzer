Solanum stuhlmannii

Solanum stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, often containing toxic alkaloids.

Solanum stuhlmannii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
