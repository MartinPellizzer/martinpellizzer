Scirpus flaccidus

Scirpus flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus flaccidus is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the environment.
