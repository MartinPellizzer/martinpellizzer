Salacia lanceolata

Salacia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
