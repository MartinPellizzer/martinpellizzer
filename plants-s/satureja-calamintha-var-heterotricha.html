Satureja calamintha var. heterotricha

Satureja calamintha var. heterotricha, commonly known as narrow-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong, pungent aroma.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and medicinal properties.
