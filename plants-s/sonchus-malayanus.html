Sonchus malayanus

Sonchus malayanus, commonly known as Malayan sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in moist environments such as grasslands and agricultural fields. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive bitter taste.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
