Sparrmannia discolor var. discolor

Sparrmannia discolor var. discolor, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and along the edges of bogs. It is well adapted to low-nutrient environments and can thrive in partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
