Saguerus gamuto

Saguerus gamuto, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and vibrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant for its visual impact.
