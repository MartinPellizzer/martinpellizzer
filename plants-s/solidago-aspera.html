Solidago aspera

Solidago aspera, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered with fine, stiff hairs.
