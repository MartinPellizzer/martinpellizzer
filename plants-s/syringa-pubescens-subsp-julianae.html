Syringa pubescens subsp. julianae

Syringa pubescens subsp.

julianae, commonly known as the Julian syringa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and adaptability to various climates.
