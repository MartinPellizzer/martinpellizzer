Silene chihuahuensis

Silene chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene chihuahuensis is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to extreme environmental conditions.
