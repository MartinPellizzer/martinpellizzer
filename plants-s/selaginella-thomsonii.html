Selaginella thomsonii

Selaginella thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella thomsonii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
