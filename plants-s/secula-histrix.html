Secula histrix

Secula histrix, commonly known as the spiked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
