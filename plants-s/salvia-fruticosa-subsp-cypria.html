Salvia fruticosa subsp. cypria

Salvia fruticosa subsp.

cypria, commonly known as Cyprus sage, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their medicinal properties.
