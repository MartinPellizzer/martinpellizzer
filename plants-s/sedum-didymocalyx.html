Sedum didymocalyx

Sedum didymocalyx, commonly known as the snow sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
