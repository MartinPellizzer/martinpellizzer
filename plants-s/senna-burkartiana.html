Senna burkartiana

Senna burkartiana, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds to the ground.
