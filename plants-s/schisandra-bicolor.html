Schisandra bicolor

Schisandra bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored five-finger, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow berries that ripen in the fall. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is known for its aromatic, woody stems and palmate leaves. Schisandra bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

Its berries contain compounds that have been studied for potential health benefits.
