Senecio arboreus

Senecio arboreus, commonly known as the tree heath, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features woody stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Senecio arboreus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
