Salvia latibracteata

Salvia latibracteata, commonly known as broad-bract sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia latibracteata is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
