Sasa spiculosa var. subpubescens

Sasa spiculosa var. subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa spiculosa species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, bamboo-like culms. The leaves are narrow and grassy, with a slightly hairy texture on the underside, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
