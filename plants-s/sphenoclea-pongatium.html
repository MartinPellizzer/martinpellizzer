Sphenoclea pongatium

Sphenoclea pongatium, commonly known as the African enigma plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Sphenocleaceae and is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
