Sarcochilus subulatus

Sarcochilus subulatus, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, leek-like foliage.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
