Spiraea sinensis

Spiraea sinensis, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Spiraea sinensis thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its graceful appearance.
