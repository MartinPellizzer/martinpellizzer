Sorbus toringo

Sorbus toringo, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Sorbus toringo is often cultivated for its attractive form and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
