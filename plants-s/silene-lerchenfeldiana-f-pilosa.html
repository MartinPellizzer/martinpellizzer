Silene lerchenfeldiana f. pilosa

Silene lerchenfeldiana f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant is native to mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its hairy foliage, which gives it its name, helps to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
