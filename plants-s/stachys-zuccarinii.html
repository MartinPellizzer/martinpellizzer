Stachys zuccarinii

Stachys zuccarinii, commonly known as the yellow betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stachys zuccarinii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
