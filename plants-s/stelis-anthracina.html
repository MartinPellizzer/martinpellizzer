Stelis anthracina

Stelis anthracina, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Stelis anthracina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
