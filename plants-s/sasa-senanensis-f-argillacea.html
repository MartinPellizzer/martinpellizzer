Sasa senanensis f. argillacea

Sasa senanensis f. argillacea, commonly known as the clay sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial bamboo-like grass that grows in dense clumps, characterized by its short, stiff culms and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a subtle fragrance when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique texture and form.
