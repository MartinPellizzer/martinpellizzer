Spiraea × schinabeckii

Spiraea × schinabeckii, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a spreading, upright habit. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Spiraea × schinabeckii is well-suited for gardens and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
