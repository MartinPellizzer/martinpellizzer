Salvia carnosa var. gilmanii

Salvia carnosa var. gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
