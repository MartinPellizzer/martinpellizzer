Saccharum fulvum

Saccharum fulvum, commonly known as the wild sugarcane or red sugarcane, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and distinctive reddish-pink coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant produces a sweet, syrupy juice from its stems, which can be processed into various forms of sugar. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

Saccharum fulvum is often cultivated for both its economic value and ornamental appeal.
