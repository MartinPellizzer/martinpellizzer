Sterculia scaphigera

Sterculia scaphigera, commonly known as the African sycamore or the red sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and for timber.
