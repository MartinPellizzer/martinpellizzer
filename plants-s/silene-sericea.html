Silene sericea

Silene sericea, commonly known as silky pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene sericea is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
