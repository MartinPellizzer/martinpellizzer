Streblosa urticina var. stapfii

Streblosa urticina var. stapfii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs on its leaves and stems, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.
