Salvia uliginosa var. rufescens

Salvia uliginosa var. rufescens, commonly known as purple swamp milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to pink flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, swampy habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important food source for monarch butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
