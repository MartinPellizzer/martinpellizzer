Senecio raphanifolius

Senecio raphanifolius, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that resemble ginger and are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a useful plant in gardens and agriculture.
