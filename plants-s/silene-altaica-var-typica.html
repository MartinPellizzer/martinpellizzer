Silene altaica var. typica

Silene altaica var. typica, commonly known as the Altai campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals that are usually slightly notched.

Silene altaica var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
