Symplocos lancifolia var. leptostachys

Symplocos lancifolia var. leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stalked wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
