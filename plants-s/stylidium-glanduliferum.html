Stylidium glanduliferum

Stylidium glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular spurred orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a prominent spur, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Stylidium glanduliferum is often found in coastal regions and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
