Solanum ipecacuanha var. obovata

Solanum ipecacuanha var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or obovate nightshade, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped, obovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
