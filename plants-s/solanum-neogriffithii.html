Solanum neogriffithii

Solanum neogriffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Myanmar, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, ovate leaves, and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum neogriffithii is often found in open woodlands and along forest margins, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
