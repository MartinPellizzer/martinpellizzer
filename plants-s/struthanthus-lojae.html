Struthanthus lojae

Struthanthus lojae, commonly known as the Loja sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, sun-like blooms.

Struthanthus lojae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
