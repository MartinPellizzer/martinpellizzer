Strychnos rondeletioides

Strychnos rondeletioides, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a member of the Loganiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, woody capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a strong odor.

The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which are toxic, making it a subject of both botanical and pharmacological interest.
