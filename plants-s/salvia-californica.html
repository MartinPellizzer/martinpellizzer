Salvia californica

Salvia californica, commonly known as the California sagebrush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features erect, hairy stems and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, blue, or red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
