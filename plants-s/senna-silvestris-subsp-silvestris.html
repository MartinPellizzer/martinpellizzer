Senna silvestris subsp. silvestris

Senna silvestris subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as the wild senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
