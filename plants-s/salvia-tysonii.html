Salvia tysonii

Salvia tysonii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
