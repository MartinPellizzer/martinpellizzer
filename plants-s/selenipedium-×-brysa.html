Selenipedium × brysa

Selenipedium × brysa, commonly known as the hybrid giant spider-orchid, is a rare and unique hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Selenipedium albidum and Selenipedium bryantii.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale green flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Selenipedium × brysa is known for its slow growth and specific cultivation requirements, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Selenipedium genus.
