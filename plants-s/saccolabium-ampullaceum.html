Saccolabium ampullaceum

Saccolabium ampullaceum, commonly known as the bottlebrush acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped seed pods, which give the plant its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
