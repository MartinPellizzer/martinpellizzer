Sedum telephium var. eupatorioides

Sedum telephium var. eupatorioides, commonly known as red stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, which help it store water and withstand harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a versatile addition to both garden and landscape designs.
