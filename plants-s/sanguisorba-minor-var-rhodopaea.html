Sanguisorba minor var. rhodopaea

Sanguisorba minor var. rhodopaea, commonly known as the red stemmed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that often turn reddish in color, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late summer, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly succulent texture, with a mild, tangy flavor.

Sanguisorba minor var. rhodopaea is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
