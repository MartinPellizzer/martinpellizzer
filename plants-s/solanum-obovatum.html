Solanum obovatum

Solanum obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, round berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Solanum obovatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
