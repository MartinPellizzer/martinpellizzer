Silphium tomentosum

Silphium tomentosum, commonly known as wooly silphium, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Silphium tomentosum is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas.
