Stachys alba

Stachys alba, commonly known as white betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys alba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
