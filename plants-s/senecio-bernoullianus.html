Senecio bernoullianus

Senecio bernoullianus, commonly known as the Bernoulli's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and green, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It produces small, dandelion-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
