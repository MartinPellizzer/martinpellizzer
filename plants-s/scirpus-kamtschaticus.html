Scirpus kamtschaticus

Scirpus kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Siberian bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, lakes, and along riverbanks. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form dense stands, and its flowering spikes produce small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Scirpus kamtschaticus plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some cultures.
