Solanum schumannianum var. schumannianum

Solanum schumannianum var. schumannianum, commonly known as Schumann's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
