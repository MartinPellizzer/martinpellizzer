Sarcopygme pacifica

Sarcopygme pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific sarcopygme, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, ridged texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
