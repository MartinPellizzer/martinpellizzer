Sisymbrium macrorrhizum

Sisymbrium macrorrhizum, commonly known as the large-rooted mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year typically spent as a rosette of basal leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. Its root system is notably thick and fleshy, contributing to its common name.

Sisymbrium macrorrhizum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
