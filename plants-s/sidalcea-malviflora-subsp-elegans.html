Sidalcea malviflora subsp. elegans

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
