Spiraea mollifolia var. denudata

Spiraea mollifolia var. denudata, commonly known as the smooth spiraea or denuded spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture and a bluish-green hue. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
