Selinum berula

Selinum berula, commonly known as scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and aromatic, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Selinum berula is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
