Senecio pseudaureus f. ecoronatus

Senecio pseudaureus f. ecoronatus, commonly known as the Crowned Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive white or pale yellow crown at the center. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
