Setaria biflora

Setaria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two distinct flowering branches, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall.

Setaria biflora is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
