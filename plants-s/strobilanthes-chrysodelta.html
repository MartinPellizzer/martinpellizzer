Strobilanthes chrysodelta

Strobilanthes chrysodelta, commonly known as the golden delta or delta flame, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant yellow and orange flowers arranged in a whorled pattern, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
