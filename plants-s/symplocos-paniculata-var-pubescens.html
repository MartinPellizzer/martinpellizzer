Symplocos paniculata var. pubescens

Symplocos paniculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
