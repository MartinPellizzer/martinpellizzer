Scopolia carnosula

Scopolia carnosula, commonly known as the sweet coltsfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, emerging in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scopolia carnosula is also known for its mild, fragrant scent and has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
