Sauvagesia insignis

Sauvagesia insignis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a smooth, greyish bark and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
