Saussurea platyphyllaria

Saussurea platyphyllaria, commonly known as the flat-leafed saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Saussurea platyphyllaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
