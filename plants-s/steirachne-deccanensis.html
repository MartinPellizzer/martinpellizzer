Steirachne deccanensis

Steirachne deccanensis, commonly known as the Deccan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Steirachne deccanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
