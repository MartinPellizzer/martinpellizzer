Saxifraga tricuspidata var. typica

Saxifraga tricuspidata var. typica, commonly known as the three-lobed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga tricuspidata var. typica is often found in mountainous areas and is a popular subject in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
