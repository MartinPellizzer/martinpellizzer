Solanum phureja var. flavum

Solanum phureja var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped fruits that mature to a deep orange-red color. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit development.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, slightly tangy flavor and is widely used in culinary applications.
