Senna lechriosperma

Senna lechriosperma, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy, spiny pod and are adapted for dispersal by animals.
