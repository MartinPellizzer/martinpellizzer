Silene italica var. rosea

Silene italica var. rosea, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its stems are usually hairy, and the leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape.

It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
