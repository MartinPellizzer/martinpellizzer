Serratula polycephala var. ortholepis

Serratula polycephala var. ortholepis, commonly known as the mountain sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
