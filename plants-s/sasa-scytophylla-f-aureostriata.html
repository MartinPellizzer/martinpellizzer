Sasa scytophylla f. aureostriata

Sasa scytophylla f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-stripped bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often adorned with distinctive golden stripes along the nodes. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
