Sedum × engadinense

Sedum × engadinense, commonly known as the Engadine stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh, arid environments. The plant typically produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
