Sedum pedicellatum var. lusitanicum

Sedum pedicellatum var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as cliffs and gravelly soils.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
