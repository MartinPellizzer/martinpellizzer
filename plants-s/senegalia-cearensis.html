Senegalia cearensis

Senegalia cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Prosopis, is a shrub or small tree native to northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil and providing forage for livestock.
