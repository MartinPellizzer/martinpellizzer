Sickingia fragrans

Sickingia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Sickingia fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and visual beauty.
