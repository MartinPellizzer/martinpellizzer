Silphium mohrii

Silphium mohrii, commonly known as the Mohr's silphium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Silphium mohrii is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
