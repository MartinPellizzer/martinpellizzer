Sabulina rimarum

Sabulina rimarum, commonly known as the sand lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has a shallow root system adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sabulina rimarum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
