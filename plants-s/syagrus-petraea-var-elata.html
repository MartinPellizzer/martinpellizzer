Syagrus petraea var. elata

Syagrus petraea var. elata, commonly known as the slender palm or the rock palm, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
