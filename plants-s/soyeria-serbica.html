Soyeria serbica

Soyeria serbica, commonly known as the Serbian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Soyeria serbica is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local biodiversity.
