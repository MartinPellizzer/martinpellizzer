Sesleria coerulans var. tenerrima

Sesleria coerulans var. tenerrima, commonly known as the blue mountain sesleria, is a low-growing, evergreen grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
