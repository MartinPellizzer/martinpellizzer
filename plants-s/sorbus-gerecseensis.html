Sorbus gerecseensis

Sorbus gerecseensis, commonly known as the Gerecse rowan, is a rare and endemic species native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
