Salvia cardinalis

Salvia cardinalis, commonly known as cardinal flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cardinalis is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
