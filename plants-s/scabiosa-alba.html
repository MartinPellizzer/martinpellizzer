Scabiosa alba

Scabiosa alba, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a grayish-green color.

Scabiosa alba is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
