Spiraea salicifolia var. pensylvanica

Spiraea salicifolia var. pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of white, fluffy flowers in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in garden settings.
