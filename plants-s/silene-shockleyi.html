Silene shockleyi

Silene shockleyi, commonly known as Shockley's campion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It has small, ovate leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Silene shockleyi is often found in desert scrub habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
