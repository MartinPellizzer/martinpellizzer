Sasaella auriculata

Sasaella auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate sasa, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base on the lower leaves, which is a key identifying feature. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasaella auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
