Spergularia lignosa

Spergularia lignosa, commonly known as the red sandspur, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces pink or red flowers in clusters, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Spergularia lignosa is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and ability to survive in harsh environments.
