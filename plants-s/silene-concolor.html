Silene concolor

Silene concolor, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.
