Senecio mogrere

Senecio mogrere, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its frost-tender nature requires protection in colder climates, but it is drought-resistant and easy to care for once established.
