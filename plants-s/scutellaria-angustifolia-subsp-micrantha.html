Scutellaria angustifolia subsp. micrantha

Scutellaria angustifolia subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands.

The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. The species is characterized by its distinct subsp.

micrantha traits, including shorter flowers and smaller leaves compared to the typical form.
