Sonchus lactucoides

Sonchus lactucoides, commonly known as the milk thistle or Italian thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and distinctive purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap that oozes from broken stems. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
