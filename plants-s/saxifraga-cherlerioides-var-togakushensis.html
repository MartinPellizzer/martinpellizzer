Saxifraga cherlerioides var. togakushensis

Saxifraga cherlerioides var. togakushensis, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
