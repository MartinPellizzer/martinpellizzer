Sinocrassula indica var. viridiflava

Sinocrassula indica var. viridiflava, commonly known as the Yellow-flowered Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically fleshy and green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
