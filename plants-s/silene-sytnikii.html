Silene sytnikii

Silene sytnikii, commonly known as the Sytnikii campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene sytnikii is also noted for its small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
