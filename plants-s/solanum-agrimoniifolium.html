Solanum agrimoniifolium

Solanum agrimoniifolium, commonly known as the agrimonyleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
