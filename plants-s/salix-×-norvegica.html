Salix × norvegica

Salix × norvegica, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and has heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth along the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

Salix × norvegica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
