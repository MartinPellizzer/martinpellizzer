Salvia confertiflora var. brachyantha

Salvia confertiflora var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
