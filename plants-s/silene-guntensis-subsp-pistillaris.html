Silene guntensis subsp. pistillaris

Silene guntensis subsp.

pistillaris, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
