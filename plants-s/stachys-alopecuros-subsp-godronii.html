Stachys alopecuros subsp. godronii

Stachys alopecuros subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its subspecific name, *godronii*, honors the French botanist Jean-Baptiste Godron.
