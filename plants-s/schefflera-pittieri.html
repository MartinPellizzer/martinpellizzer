Schefflera pittieri

Schefflera pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican umbrella tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in diameter. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of 10 to 20 feet. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a prominent central stem from which the leaflets radiate.

Schefflera pittieri is known for its adaptability to various light conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
