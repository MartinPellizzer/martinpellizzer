Senegalia ortegae

Senegalia ortegae, commonly known as the Ocotillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems that are covered in spines and bear clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are typically shed during dry periods, conserving water.

Senegalia ortegae plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
