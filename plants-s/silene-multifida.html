Silene multifida

Silene multifida, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Silene multifida is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
