Specklinia mirifica

Specklinia mirifica, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble speckles, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and dark green, providing a contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Specklinia mirifica is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
