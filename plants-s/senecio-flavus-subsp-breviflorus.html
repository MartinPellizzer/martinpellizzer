Senecio flavus subsp. breviflorus

Senecio flavus subsp.

breviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flower yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
