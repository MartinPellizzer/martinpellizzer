Syzygium iliasii

Syzygium iliasii, commonly known as the Java apple or cempaka, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sour-sweet flavor.

Syzygium iliasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
