Stephanopodium sessiliflorum

Stephanopodium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stephanopodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, temperate climates. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters and are typically white or pale green in color.

Stephanopodium sessiliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine displays.
