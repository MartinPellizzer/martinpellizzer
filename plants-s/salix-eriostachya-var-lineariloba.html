Salix eriostachya var. lineariloba

Salix eriostachya var. lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
