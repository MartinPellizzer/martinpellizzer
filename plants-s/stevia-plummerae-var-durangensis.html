Stevia plummerae var. durangensis

Stevia plummerae var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango stevia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal and commercial potential, it has attracted interest for use in natural sweetener production.
