Sorbus dacica

Sorbus dacica, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Sorbus dacica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
