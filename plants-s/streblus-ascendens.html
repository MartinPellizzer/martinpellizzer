Streblus ascendens

Streblus ascendens, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces edible fig-like fruits.

Streblus ascendens is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
