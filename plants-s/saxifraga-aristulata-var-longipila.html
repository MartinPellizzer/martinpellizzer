Saxifraga aristulata var. longipila

Saxifraga aristulata var. longipila, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height, adorned with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pinkish, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
