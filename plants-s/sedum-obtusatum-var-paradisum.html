Sedum obtusatum var. paradisum

Sedum obtusatum var. paradisum, commonly known as the "Paradise Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
