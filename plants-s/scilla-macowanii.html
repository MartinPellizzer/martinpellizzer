Scilla macowanii

Scilla macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive harsh winters.

Scilla macowanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
