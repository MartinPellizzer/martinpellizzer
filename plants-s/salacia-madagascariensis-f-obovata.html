Salacia madagascariensis f. obovata

Salacia madagascariensis f. obovata, commonly known as the Madagascar yellow wood, is a unique plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and rough, woody bark. The leaves are typically obovate, which means they are broad at the tip and narrow towards the base. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential medicinal research.
