Senecio rubiginosus

Senecio rubiginosus, commonly known as the rusty groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a grayish or rusty appearance, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable plant in its native habitat.
