Sanguinaria canadensis f. multiplex

Sanguinaria canadensis f. multiplex, commonly known as the Canadian bloodroot variegated form, is a botanical variant of the bloodroot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant features a single, waxy, white flower that blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that emerge after the flowers.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance in gardens.
