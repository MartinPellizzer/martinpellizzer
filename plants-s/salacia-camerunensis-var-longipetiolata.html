Salacia camerunensis var. longipetiolata

Salacia camerunensis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled salacia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves with long petioles, which help it grasp and climb through the dense canopy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
