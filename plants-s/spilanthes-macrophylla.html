Spilanthes macrophylla

Spilanthes macrophylla, commonly known as the giant devil's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust, branched stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Spilanthes macrophylla is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
