Scutellaria affinis

Scutellaria affinis, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its genus name, Scutellaria, refers to the cap-like structure of its seed pods.

Scutellaria affinis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
