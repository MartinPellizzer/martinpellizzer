Sachsia coronopifolia

Sachsia coronopifolia, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with gray-green, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Sachsia coronopifolia is well adapted to drought and full sun, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
