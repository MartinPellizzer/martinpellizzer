Sabicea fulva var. clipeolaria

Sabicea fulva var. clipeolaria, commonly known as the golden flame acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in dry forests and savannas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
