Sorbus aucuparia f. canadensis

Sorbus aucuparia f. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, often turning bright red in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
