Selaginella microdendron

Selaginella microdendron, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered around the stems. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Selaginella microdendron is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique ability to reproduce through spores.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying plant adaptation and evolution in temperate climates.
