Scirpus colchicus

Scirpus colchicus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus colchicus plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
