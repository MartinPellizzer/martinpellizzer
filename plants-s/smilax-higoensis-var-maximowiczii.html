Smilax higoensis var. maximowiczii

Smilax higoensis var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features sturdy, thorned stems that allow it to climb and entangle other plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries are bright red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

The species is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
