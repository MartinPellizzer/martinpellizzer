Sparganium erectum var. macrocarpum

Sparganium erectum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited bur reed, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in water or very wet soil. The plant produces large, spiky, brownish-red cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
