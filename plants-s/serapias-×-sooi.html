Serapias × sooi

Serapias × sooi, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Serapias and Sooi.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, often adorned with intricate patterns. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
