Stipa hyalina

Stipa hyalina, commonly known as the silvergrass or white feather grass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. Stipa hyalina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
