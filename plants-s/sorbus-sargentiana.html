Sorbus sargentiana

Sorbus sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
