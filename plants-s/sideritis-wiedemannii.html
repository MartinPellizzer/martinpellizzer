Sideritis wiedemannii

Sideritis wiedemannii, commonly known as the Greek heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Sideritis wiedemannii is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
