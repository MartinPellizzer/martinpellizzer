Selenipedium gracile

Selenipedium gracile, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its botanical features include long, ribbon-like leaves and a unique reproductive system involving mycorrhizal associations.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Selenipedium gracile is considered a conservation concern.
