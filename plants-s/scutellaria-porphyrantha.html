Scutellaria porphyrantha

Scutellaria porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually hairy, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Scutellaria porphyrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
