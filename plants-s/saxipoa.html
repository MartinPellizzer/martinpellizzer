Saxipoa

Saxipoa, commonly known as the "saxifrage," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Saxifragaceae.

These plants are typically found in alpine and rocky environments, where they thrive in well-drained soils. Saxipoa species are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and showy, often pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. They are adapted to harsh climates and can tolerate cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
