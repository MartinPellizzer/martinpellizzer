Setaria viridis var. secunda

Setaria viridis var. secunda, commonly known as green millet or variegated millet, is a variant of the common green millet species.

It is a grassy plant native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green coloration, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, pale grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a forage crop or for its ornamental value.

This plant is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
