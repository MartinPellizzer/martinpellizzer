Saussurea parviflora subsp. parviflora

Saussurea parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments.

The plant features a sturdy, branched stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish to brownish, and are arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
