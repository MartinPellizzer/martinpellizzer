Sagittaria leucopetala

Sagittaria leucopetala, commonly known as the white arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a distinctive white underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Sagittaria leucopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
