Scypharia senticosa

Scypharia senticosa, commonly known as the sweet-scented scypharia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a strong, pleasant scent.

Scypharia senticosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
