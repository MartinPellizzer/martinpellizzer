Sinapis cheiranthos var. arenosa

Sinapis cheiranthos var. arenosa, commonly known as sand mustard, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Sinapis cheiranthos var. arenosa is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
