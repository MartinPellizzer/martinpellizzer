Scrophularia syriaca

Scrophularia syriaca, commonly known as Syrian figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
