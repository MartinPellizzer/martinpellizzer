Salix × heteromera

Salix × heteromera, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix schwerinii (a species from Central Asia). The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of wind pollination.

Salix × heteromera is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
