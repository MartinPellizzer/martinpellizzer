Salicornia europaea subsp. disarticulata

Salicornia europaea subsp.

disarticulata, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It is a succulent annual or biennial species that thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils.

The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often red or pink in color, and it produces small, green flowers. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to accumulate salt in its tissues.

This subspecies is an important component of coastal ecosystems and is used in some regions for culinary and medicinal purposes.
