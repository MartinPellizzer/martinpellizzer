Sophora godleyi

Sophora godleyi, commonly known as the Hawaiian silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also considered an endangered species in its native range.
