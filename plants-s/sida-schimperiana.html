Sida schimperiana

Sida schimperiana, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in racemes. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
