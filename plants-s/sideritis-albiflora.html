Sideritis albiflora

Sideritis albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs.

Sideritis albiflora is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
