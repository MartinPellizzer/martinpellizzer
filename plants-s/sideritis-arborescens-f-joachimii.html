Sideritis arborescens f. joachimii

Sideritis arborescens f. joachimii, commonly known as the Joachim's rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often growing in open, sunny habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
