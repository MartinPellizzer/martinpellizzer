Solidago serratifolia

Solidago serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its serrated leaves and robust root system make it a hardy species often found in wetland ecosystems.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late season.
