Saussurea manshurica var. pinnatifida

Saussurea manshurica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea or Siberian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flower heads that are typically arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine and subarctic environments.
