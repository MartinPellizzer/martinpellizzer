Spiraea integrifolia

Spiraea integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth margin, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
