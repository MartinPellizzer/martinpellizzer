Sedum iwarenge

Sedum iwarenge, commonly known as the Namibian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch of color to its rocky habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is a popular plant among gardeners in semi-arid climates.
