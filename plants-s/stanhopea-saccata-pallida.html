Stanhopea saccata pallida

Stanhopea saccata pallida, commonly known as the pale sac sapphire orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elongated, often hairy pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive sapphire-like hue. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
