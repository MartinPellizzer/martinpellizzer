Stachys minor

Stachys minor, commonly known as caraway weed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Stachys minor is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
