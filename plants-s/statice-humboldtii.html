Statice humboldtii

Statice humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and tidal zones. The plant produces showy, spike-like flower clusters in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Statice humboldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
