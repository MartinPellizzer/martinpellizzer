Silene graminifolia lusus odorata

Silene graminifolia lusus odorata, commonly known as the fragrant grass pink, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

The species is noted for its subtle, sweet fragrance, which becomes more pronounced as the flowers mature.
