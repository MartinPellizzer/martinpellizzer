Scopolia caucasica

Scopolia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive, tuberous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
