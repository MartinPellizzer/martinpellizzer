Satureja calamintha var. calaminthoides

Satureja calamintha var. calaminthoides, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves with a pungent, peppery scent. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
