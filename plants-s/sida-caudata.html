Sida caudata

Sida caudata, commonly known as the woolly sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Sida caudata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
