Staehelinia

Staehelinia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Staehelinia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
