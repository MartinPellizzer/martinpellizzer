Shiia longicaudata

Shiia longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed shiia, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, trailing stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture, making it resilient in wet conditions.

Shiia longicaudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
