Solanum dryadeum

Solanum dryadeum, commonly known as the dryad's solanum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that ripens in the summer, containing several seeds.

Solanum dryadeum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
