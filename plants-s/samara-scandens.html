Samara scandens

Samara scandens, commonly known as the climbing samara or climbing maple, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant colors in autumn. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by winged fruits called samaras, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
