Selaginella flaccida

Selaginella flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are usually green to reddish in color, depending on environmental conditions. Selaginella flaccida reproduces via spores, which are produced in cone-like structures called strobila.

This plant is valued for its resilience and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
