Statice cyrenaica

Statice cyrenaica, commonly known as the Canary Island statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Statice cyrenaica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
