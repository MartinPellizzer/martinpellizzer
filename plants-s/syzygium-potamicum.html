Syzygium potamicum

Syzygium potamicum, commonly known as the potamicum apple or swamp apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color. Syzygium potamicum is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, tropical environments.

Its fruits are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
