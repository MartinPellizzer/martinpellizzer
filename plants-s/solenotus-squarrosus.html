Solenotus squarrosus

Solenotus squarrosus, commonly known as the hairy solenotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive appearance, featuring hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Solenotus squarrosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
