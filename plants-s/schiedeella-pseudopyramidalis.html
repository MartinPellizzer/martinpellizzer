Schiedeella pseudopyramidalis

Schiedeella pseudopyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces white, fleshy flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It relies on specific bird species for seed dispersal, as the berries are not palatable to most animals.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered vulnerable and is protected in some regions.
