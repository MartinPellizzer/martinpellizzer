Serissa glomerata

Serissa glomerata, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate ornamental touch. Serissa glomerata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in containers.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for bonsai and decorative gardening.
