Smithia speciosa

Smithia speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Smithia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
