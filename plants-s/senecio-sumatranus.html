Senecio sumatranus

Senecio sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio sumatranus is often found in humid, tropical regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
