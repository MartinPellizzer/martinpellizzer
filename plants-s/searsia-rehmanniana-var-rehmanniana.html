Searsia rehmanniana var. rehmanniana

Searsia rehmanniana var. rehmanniana, commonly known as the silver cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that turn a striking silver-white color in autumn, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its seasonal color changes.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various climate conditions.
