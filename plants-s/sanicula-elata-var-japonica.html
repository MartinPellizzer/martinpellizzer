Sanicula elata var. japonica

Sanicula elata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant has large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels, typical of the carrot family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
