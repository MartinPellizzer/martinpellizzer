Stemona stenophylla

Stemona stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stemona, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem that is often branched and covered with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue or black berries. Its leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it a subject of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
