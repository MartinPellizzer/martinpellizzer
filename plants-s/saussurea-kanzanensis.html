Saussurea kanzanensis

Saussurea kanzanensis, commonly known as the Kanzanensis Globe Thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical cluster of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and hairy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
