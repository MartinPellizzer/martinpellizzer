Scirpus collinus var. boeckelerianus

Scirpus collinus var. boeckelerianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This variety thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
