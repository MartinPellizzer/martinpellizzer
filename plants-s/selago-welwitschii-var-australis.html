Selago welwitschii var. australis

Selago welwitschii var. australis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyces that enclose the fruit, which resembles a small, lantern-like structure. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
