Solanecio tuberosus

Solanecio tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous solanecio, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots are often cultivated for their ornamental value and sometimes for their edible qualities.

Solanecio tuberosus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
