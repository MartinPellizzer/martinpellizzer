Solanum philippinense

Solanum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, star-shaped white or pale purple flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or vine and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

The fruit is a small, green berry that turns red when mature, and it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
