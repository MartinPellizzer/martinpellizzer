Salix jessoensis subsp. serissifolia

Salix jessoensis subsp.

serissifolia, commonly known as the Korean weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its long, drooping catkins and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, while the twigs are slender and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian zones.
