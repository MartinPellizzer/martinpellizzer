Soja

Soja, commonly known as soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and oil. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Soja is cultivated for its seeds, which are used to make a variety of products, including tofu, soy milk, and oil.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
