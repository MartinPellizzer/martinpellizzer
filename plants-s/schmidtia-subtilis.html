Schmidtia subtilis

Schmidtia subtilis, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Schmidtia subtilis is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
