Solidago lepida var. molina

Solidago lepida var. molina, commonly known as Molina's golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
