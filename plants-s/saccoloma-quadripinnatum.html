Saccoloma quadripinnatum

Saccoloma quadripinnatum, commonly known as the four-pinnate fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are divided into multiple pinnae, giving it a finely branched appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
