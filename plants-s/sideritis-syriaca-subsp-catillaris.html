Sideritis syriaca subsp. catillaris

Sideritis syriaca subsp.

catillaris, commonly known as Syrian catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, minty aroma when crushed.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
