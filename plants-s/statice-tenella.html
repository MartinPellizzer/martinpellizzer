Statice tenella

Statice tenella, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and spikes of small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, are often used in dried floral arrangements.

Statice tenella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize dune soils.
