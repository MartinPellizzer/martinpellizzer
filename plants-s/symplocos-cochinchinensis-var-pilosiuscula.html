Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pilosiuscula

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy-leaved sandoric, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with short, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
