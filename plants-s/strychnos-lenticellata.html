Strychnos lenticellata

Strychnos lenticellata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark that exudes a distinctive, unpleasant odor when cut. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Strychnos lenticellata is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
