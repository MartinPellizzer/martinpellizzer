Syzygium formosanum

Syzygium formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy coconut. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by the distinctive, bumpy, red or yellow fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, with a slightly aromatic scent.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible, sweet fruit.
