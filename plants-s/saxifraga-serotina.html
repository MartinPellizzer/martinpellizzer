Saxifraga serotina

Saxifraga serotina, commonly known as late saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer to early autumn, typically from August to September. Its leaves are typically rounded or ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Saxifraga serotina thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky habitats.
