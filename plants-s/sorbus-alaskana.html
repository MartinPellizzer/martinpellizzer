Sorbus alaskana

Sorbus alaskana, commonly known as the Alaska whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus alaskana is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
