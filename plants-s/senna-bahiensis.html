Senna bahiensis

Senna bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it has pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
