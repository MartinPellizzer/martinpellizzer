Sapota zapotilla

Sapota zapotilla, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, creamy fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is highly valued for its flavor. The fruit's thick, inedible skin encases a soft, white flesh containing numerous black seeds.

Sapota zapotilla thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
