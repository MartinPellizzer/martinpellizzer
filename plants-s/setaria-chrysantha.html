Setaria chrysantha

Setaria chrysantha, commonly known as golden millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, golden-bracted inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking yellow coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Setaria chrysantha is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some areas.
