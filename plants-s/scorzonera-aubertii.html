Scorzonera aubertii

Scorzonera aubertii, commonly known as the Aubert's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Scorzonera aubertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
