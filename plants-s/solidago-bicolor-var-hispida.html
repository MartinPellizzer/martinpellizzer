Solidago bicolor var. hispida

Solidago bicolor var. hispida, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow and orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-green hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in meadows and open woodlands.

This variant is distinguished by its more robust growth and dense foliage compared to other goldenrod species.
