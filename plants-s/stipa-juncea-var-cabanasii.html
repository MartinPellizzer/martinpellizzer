Stipa juncea var. cabanasii

Stipa juncea var. cabanasii, commonly known as Cabanas' needlegrass, is a grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
