Scholtzia longipedata subsp. procera

Scholtzia longipedata subsp.

procera, commonly known as the desert larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It is part of the tribe Lophanthae within the genus Scholtzia, which is closely related to the larkspur family.
