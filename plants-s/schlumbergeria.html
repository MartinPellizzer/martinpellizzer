Schlumbergeria

Schlumbergeria, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a genus of epiphytic cacti native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often brightly colored and used for photosynthesis. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Schlumbergeria species are popular as houseplants due to their ease of care and long blooming period.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
