Sorghum saccharatum var. atrum

Sorghum saccharatum var. atrum, commonly known as black sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and dark purple to black bracts that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its high biomass production and adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often cultivated for forage, erosion control, and in some cases, as a source of biofuel.
