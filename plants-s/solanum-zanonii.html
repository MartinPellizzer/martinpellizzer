Solanum zanonii

Solanum zanonii, commonly known as the Zanonii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive violet or purple calyx.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.
