Scordium inerme

Scordium inerme, commonly known as the common scordium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It is not typically cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
