Silene uralensis subsp. montana

Silene uralensis subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to cold and harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Silene uralensis subsp.

montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
