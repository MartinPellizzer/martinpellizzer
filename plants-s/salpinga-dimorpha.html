Salpinga dimorpha

Salpinga dimorpha, commonly known as the two-formed trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, depending on the support it receives.
