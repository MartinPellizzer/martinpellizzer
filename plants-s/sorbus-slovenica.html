Sorbus slovenica

Sorbus slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its bark is often cracked and flaky, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Sorbus slovenica is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in alpine environments.
