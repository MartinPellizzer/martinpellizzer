Sedum variegatum

Sedum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Eurasia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, triangular leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for xeriscaping.
