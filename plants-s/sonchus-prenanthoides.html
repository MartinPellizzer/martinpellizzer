Sonchus prenanthoides

Sonchus prenanthoides, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

It is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
