Sambucus pubens var. pubescens

Sambucus pubens var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
