Symplocos laurina var. bodinieri

Symplocos laurina var. bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic foliage and adaptability to various soil types make it a notable species in its native habitat.
