Sporobolus factorovskyi

Sporobolus factorovskyi, commonly known as Factorovsky's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
