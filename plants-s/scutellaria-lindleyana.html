Scutellaria lindleyana

Scutellaria lindleyana, commonly known as Lindley's skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Scutellaria lindleyana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
