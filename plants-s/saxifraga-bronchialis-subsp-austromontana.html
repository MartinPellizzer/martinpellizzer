Saxifraga bronchialis subsp. austromontana

Saxifraga bronchialis subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as the Austromontana saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southern South America. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in mountainous environments.

The plant features compact rosettes of dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with harsh environmental conditions.
