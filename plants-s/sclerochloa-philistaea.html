Sclerochloa philistaea

Sclerochloa philistaea, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are adapted to the harsh, dry environments it inhabits. Sclerochloa philistaea is well-suited for drought tolerance and has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
