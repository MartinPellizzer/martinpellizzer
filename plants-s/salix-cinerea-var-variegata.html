Salix cinerea var. variegata

Salix cinerea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and striking variegated leaves that display white and yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers appear in early spring, producing catkins that release lightweight seeds.

This ornamental species is valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to various garden environments.
