Saxifraga moschata subsp. allionii

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
