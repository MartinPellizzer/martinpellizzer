Sison majus

Sison majus, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and latex, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
