Scandicium stellatum var. hirsutum

Scandicium stellatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its star-shaped flowers and hairy foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually white or pale yellow and arranged in clusters.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy texture, enhancing its distinctive appearance.
