Saxifraga × geum var. cuatrecasasii

Saxifraga × geum var. cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and have a distinctive, often hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
