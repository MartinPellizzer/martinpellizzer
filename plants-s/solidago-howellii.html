Solidago howellii

Solidago howellii, commonly known as Howell's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators.

Howell's goldenrod is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
