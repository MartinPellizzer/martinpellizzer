Sabina blancoi

Sabina blancoi, commonly known as the White Sage, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional ceremonies. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
