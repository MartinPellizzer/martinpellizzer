Selaginella armata var. eatonii

Selaginella armata var. eatonii, commonly known as the Eaton's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, linear leaves that form dense, mat-like clusters. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are typically found at the tips of the stems and contain spores.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
