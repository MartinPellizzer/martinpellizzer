Sabal maritima

Sabal maritima, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a hardy perennial plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, forming dense stands in shallow water. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in brackish and freshwater environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional basket-making and have cultural significance among indigenous communities.

Sabal maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
