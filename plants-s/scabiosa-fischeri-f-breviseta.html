Scabiosa fischeri f. breviseta

Scabiosa fischeri f. breviseta, commonly known as the dwarf scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
