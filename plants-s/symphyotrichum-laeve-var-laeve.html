Symphyotrichum laeve var. laeve

Symphyotrichum laeve var. laeve, commonly known as smooth coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along streambanks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species for ecological diversity.
