Sophora chrysophylla f. makuaensis

Sophora chrysophylla f. makuaensis, commonly known as the Makua acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
