Sedum sediforme var. dianium

Sedum sediforme var. dianium, commonly known as the silver rose sedum, is a succulent plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to store water in its fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance contribute to its adaptability in various arid environments.
