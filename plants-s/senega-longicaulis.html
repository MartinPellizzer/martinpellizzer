Senega longicaulis

Senega longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed senega, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Senega longicaulis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
