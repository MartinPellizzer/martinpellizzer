Stipa chrysophylla f. minuta

Stipa chrysophylla f. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf golden feather grass, is a compact, low-growing grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, bright golden-yellow leaves that are densely packed, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with delicate, awned florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative element in dry landscapes.
