Saxifraga × wildiana

Saxifraga × wildiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Saxifraga × wildiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
