Solanum pentapetalum

Solanum pentapetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color, containing several seeds.
