Scleria flagellum

Scleria flagellum, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Scleria flagellum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
