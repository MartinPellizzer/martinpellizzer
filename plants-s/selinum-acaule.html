Selinum acaule

Selinum acaule, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Selinum acaule is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
