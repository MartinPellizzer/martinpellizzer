Spiraea expansa

Spiraea expansa, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a spreading, rounded form. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

Spiraea expansa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
