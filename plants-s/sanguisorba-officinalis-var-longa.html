Sanguisorba officinalis var. longa

Sanguisorba officinalis var. longa, commonly known as long-stemmed sweet grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in floral arrangements due to their durability and aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
