Salix × ludibunda

Salix × ludibunda, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that is widely appreciated for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bright green color during the growing season.

Salix × ludibunda thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features or in areas with high groundwater.
