Satureja acinos subsp. meridionalis

Satureja acinos subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as southern mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The essential oils derived from its leaves are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.
