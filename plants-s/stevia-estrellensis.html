Stevia estrellensis

Stevia estrellensis, commonly known as the Estrella stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it is also cultivated for use in herbal remedies and as a natural sweetener alternative.
