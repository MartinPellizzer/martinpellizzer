Shorea blumutensis

Shorea blumutensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a thick, flaky bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
