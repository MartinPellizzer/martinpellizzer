Sporobolus multiramosus

Sporobolus multiramosus, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

The species is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.
