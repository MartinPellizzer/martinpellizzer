Sida philippica

Sida philippica, commonly known as Philippine sida, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Sida philippica is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it adaptable to different environments.

It is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
