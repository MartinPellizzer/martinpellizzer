Schnella flexuosa

Schnella flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible snakeweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent stems. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits. Schnella flexuosa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
