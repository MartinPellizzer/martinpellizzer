Sebaea repens

Sebaea repens, commonly known as the creeping sebaea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
