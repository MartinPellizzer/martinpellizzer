Staphylea colchica var. coulombieri

Staphylea colchica var. coulombieri, commonly known as the Caucasian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by edible, hazelnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
