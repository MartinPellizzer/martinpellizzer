Salix spodiophylla f. liocarpa

Salix spodiophylla f. liocarpa, commonly known as the red-leaf willow, is a rare and unique form of willow native to the high-altitude regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown leaves, which contrast sharply with its slender, weeping branches. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, woody fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
