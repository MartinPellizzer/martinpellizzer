Salvia alborosea

Salvia alborosea, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of pale blue to white flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia alborosea is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in various cultural practices.
