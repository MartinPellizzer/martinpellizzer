Sasa veitchii var. veitchii

Sasa veitchii var. veitchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Veitch's bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa veitchii var. veitchii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
