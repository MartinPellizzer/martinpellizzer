Sideritis glacialis f. virens

Sideritis glacialis f. virens, commonly known as the alpine sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold, rocky environments.

Sideritis glacialis f. virens is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
