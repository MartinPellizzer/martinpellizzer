Scirpus caldwellii

Scirpus caldwellii, commonly known as Caldwell's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
