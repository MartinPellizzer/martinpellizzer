Setaria argentina

Setaria argentina, commonly known as Argentine foxtail, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and disturbed soils, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria argentina is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and ability to thrive in marginal habitats.
