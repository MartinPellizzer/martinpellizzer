Smilax havanensis

Smilax havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban smilax, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and parts of the Bahamas.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. The plant has a distinctive, spiny stem and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, waxy texture.

Smilax havanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
