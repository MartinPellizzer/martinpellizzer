Silene distans

Silene distans, commonly known as the distant cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals that often have distinctive markings.

Silene distans is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
