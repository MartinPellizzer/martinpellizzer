Senecio hallii var. discoidea

Senecio hallii var. discoidea, commonly known as the "Beaded Groundsel," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and grayish-green foliage covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a disc-like cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
