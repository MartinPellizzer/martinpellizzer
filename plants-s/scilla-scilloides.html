Scilla scilloides

Scilla scilloides, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental plants.

Scilla scilloides is valued in gardens for its early blooming and delicate appearance.
