Salix tenuijulis var. subintegrifolia

Salix tenuijulis var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter beneath. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary floral structures, and it is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks or in wetlands. It has moderate drought tolerance once established and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This variety is noted for its fine texture and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
