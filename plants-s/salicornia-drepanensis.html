Salicornia drepanensis

Salicornia drepanensis, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, where it tolerates high salt concentrations. The plant has jointed, succulent stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, scale-like leaves. It produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Salicornia drepanensis is an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
