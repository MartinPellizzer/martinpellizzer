Solanum zoeae

Solanum zoeae, commonly known as the Zoea nightshade, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum zoeae is often found in dry, open habitats and is characterized by its unique fruit structure.
