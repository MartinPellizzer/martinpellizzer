Sideranthus pinnatifidus

Sideranthus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
