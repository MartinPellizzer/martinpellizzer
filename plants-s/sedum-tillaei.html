Sedum tillaei

Sedum tillaei, commonly known as the blue stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum tillaei is also noted for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh, rocky conditions.
