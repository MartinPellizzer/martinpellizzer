Sedum radiatum subsp. ciliosum

Sedum radiatum subsp.

ciliosum, commonly known as the silverleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes.
