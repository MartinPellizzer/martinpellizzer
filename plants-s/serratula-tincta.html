Serratula tincta

Serratula tincta, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Serratula tincta is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
