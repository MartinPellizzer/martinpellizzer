Sclerocactus brevihamatus subsp. tobuschii

Sclerocactus brevihamatus subsp.

tobuschii, commonly known as the Tobusch hedgehog cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
