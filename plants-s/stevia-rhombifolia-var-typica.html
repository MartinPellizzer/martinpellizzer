Stevia rhombifolia var. typica

Stevia rhombifolia var. typica, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Its use in natural sweeteners and traditional medicine highlights its botanical and practical significance.
