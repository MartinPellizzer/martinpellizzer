Schnella vulpina

Schnella vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the genus Schnella in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.
