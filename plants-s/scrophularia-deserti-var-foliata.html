Scrophularia deserti var. foliata

Scrophularia deserti var. foliata, commonly known as the desert scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its foliage and floral structure are well-suited for pollination by bees and other insects.
