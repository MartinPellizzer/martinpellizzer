Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. alpina

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
