Saussurea neopulchella

Saussurea neopulchella, commonly known as the Himalayan snow lotus, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellowish-white flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Due to its habitat in remote and fragile ecosystems, it is considered a vulnerable species.
