Sphacopsis

Sphacopsis, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are typically thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water flow and light absorption.

Sphacopsis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
