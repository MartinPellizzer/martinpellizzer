Salix × fragilis var. vitellina

Salix × fragilis var. vitellina, commonly known as the golden willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows along stream banks and moist soils. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollinators.

This plant is valued for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize riverbanks while providing habitat for wildlife.
