Scilla beirana

Scilla beirana, commonly known as the Iberian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a bulbous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Scilla beirana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
