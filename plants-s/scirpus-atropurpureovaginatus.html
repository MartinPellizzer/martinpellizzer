Scirpus atropurpureovaginatus

Scirpus atropurpureovaginatus, commonly known as purple stem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that often display a striking purple hue.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, with some varieties showing a reddish tinge. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus atropurpureovaginatus is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
