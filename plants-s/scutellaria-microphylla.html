Scutellaria microphylla

Scutellaria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Scutellaria microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
