Senecio buglossus

Senecio buglossus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Senecio buglossus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
