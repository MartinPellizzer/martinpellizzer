Sagittaria variabilis var. sagittifolia

Sagittaria variabilis var. sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowhead plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with submerged leaves that are narrower and more elongated. The plant produces small, white flowers that rise above the water, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Sagittaria variabilis var. sagittifolia is often used in aquatic landscaping and serves as a food source for various aquatic animals.
