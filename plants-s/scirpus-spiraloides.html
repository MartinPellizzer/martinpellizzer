Scirpus spiraloides

Scirpus spiraloides, commonly known as the spiral bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often spiral around one another, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are clustered at the top of the plant.

Scirpus spiraloides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
