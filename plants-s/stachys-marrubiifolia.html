Stachys marrubiifolia

Stachys marrubiifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
