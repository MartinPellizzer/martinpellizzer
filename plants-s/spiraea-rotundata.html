Spiraea rotundata

Spiraea rotundata, commonly known as roundleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Spiraea rotundata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
