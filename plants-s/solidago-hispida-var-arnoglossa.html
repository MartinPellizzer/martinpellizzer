Solidago hispida var. arnoglossa

Solidago hispida var. arnoglossa, commonly known as the arnoglossa goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions and support pollinators.
