Sterculia firmiana

Sterculia firmiana, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used for medicinal and construction purposes due to their durable and aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization in its native habitat.
