Sunaptea pachyphylla

Sunaptea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved sunaptea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature typical of plants adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to green in color. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
