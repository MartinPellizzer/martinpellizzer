Scutellaria mussooriensis

Scutellaria mussooriensis, commonly known as the Himalayan skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
