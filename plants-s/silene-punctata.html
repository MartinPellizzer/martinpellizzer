Silene punctata

Silene punctata, commonly known as the dotted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Silene punctata thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a short-lived perennial species.
