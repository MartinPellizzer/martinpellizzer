Sida depressa

Sida depressa, commonly known as the prostrate sida, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Americas.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically along the stems. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Sida depressa is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
