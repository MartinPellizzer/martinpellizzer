Satureja horvatii subsp. macrophylla

Satureja horvatii subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and emit a strong, pungent fragrance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
