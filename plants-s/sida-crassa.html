Sida crassa

Sida crassa, commonly known as rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in capsules that split open to release the seeds.

Sida crassa is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
