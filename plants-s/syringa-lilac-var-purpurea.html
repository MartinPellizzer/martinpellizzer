Syringa lilac var. purpurea

Syringa lilac var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clusters of pale purple to deep violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, compact form.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
