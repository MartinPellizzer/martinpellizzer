Scutellaria heterotricha

Scutellaria heterotricha, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria heterotricha is often found in moist, open woodlands and is known for its hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
