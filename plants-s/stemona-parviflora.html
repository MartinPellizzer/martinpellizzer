Stemona parviflora

Stemona parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thin, smooth bark. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters along the stems, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
