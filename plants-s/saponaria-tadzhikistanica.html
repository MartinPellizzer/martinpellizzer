Saponaria tadzhikistanica

Saponaria tadzhikistanica, commonly known as the Tajikistan soapwort, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains saponins, which are natural detergents. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Saponaria tadzhikistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
