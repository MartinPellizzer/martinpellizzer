Scilla alvesiana

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, white berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
