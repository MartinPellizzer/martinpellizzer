Schinus meyeri

Schinus meyeri, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, and its small, bright red berries that contain toxic seeds. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Schinus meyeri is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
