Sporobolus brasiliensis

Sporobolus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus brasiliensis is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil degradation or ecological change.
