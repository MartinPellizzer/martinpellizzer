Smilax mauritanica

Smilax mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania arrowhead, is a climbing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features woody stems with spiny projections and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Smilax mauritanica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
