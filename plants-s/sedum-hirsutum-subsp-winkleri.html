Sedum hirsutum subsp. winkleri

Sedum hirsutum subsp.

winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange, during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
