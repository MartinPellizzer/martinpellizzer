Spartina foliosa

Spartina foliosa, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines and provide habitat for various wildlife. Spartina foliosa plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by filtering water and reducing erosion.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas quickly.
