Sisymbrium sinapis

Sisymbrium sinapis, commonly known as broccolini or Chinese broccoli, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender, branching stems and pinnately divided leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisine. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Sisymbrium sinapis is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
