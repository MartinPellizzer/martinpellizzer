Scutellaria edelbergii subsp. squarrosissima

Scutellaria edelbergii subsp.

squarrosissima, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
