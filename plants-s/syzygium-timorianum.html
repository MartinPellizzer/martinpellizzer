Syzygium timorianum

Syzygium timorianum, commonly known as the Java apple or timore apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
