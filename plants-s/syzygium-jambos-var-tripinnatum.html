Syzygium jambos var. tripinnatum

Syzygium jambos var. tripinnatum, commonly known as the wild mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive three-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to the characteristic purple, segmented fruits. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, tropical climates with high rainfall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
