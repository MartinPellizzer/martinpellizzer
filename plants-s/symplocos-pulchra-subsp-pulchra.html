Symplocos pulchra subsp. pulchra

Symplocos pulchra subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red berries that appear in late summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.
