Stachys petrogenes

Stachys petrogenes, commonly known as the purple mint or purple betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Stachys petrogenes is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
