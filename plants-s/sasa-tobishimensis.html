Sasa tobishimensis

Sasa tobishimensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Sasa tobishimensis forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.
