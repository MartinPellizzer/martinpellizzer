Smilax pulverulenta

Smilax pulverulenta, commonly known as the dust-vein smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Smilax pulverulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
