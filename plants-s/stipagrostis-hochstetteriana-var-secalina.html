Stipagrostis hochstetteriana var. secalina

Stipagrostis hochstetteriana var. secalina, commonly known as the dwarf wheatgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
