Stomatanthes pinnatipartitus

Stomatanthes pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique blooming habit.
