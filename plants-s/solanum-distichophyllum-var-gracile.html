Solanum distichophyllum var. gracile

Solanum distichophyllum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender two-rowed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid conditions with its compact growth form. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive two-row arrangement of veins, giving it its specific epithet.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.
