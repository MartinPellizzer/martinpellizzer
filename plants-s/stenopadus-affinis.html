Stenopadus affinis

Stenopadus affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments. The plant is known for its ability to grow in partial shade and requires well-drained, loamy soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
