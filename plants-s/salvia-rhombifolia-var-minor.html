Salvia rhombifolia var. minor

Salvia rhombifolia var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
