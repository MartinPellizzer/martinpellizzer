Senecio hieraciifolius

Senecio hieraciifolius, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio hieraciifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
