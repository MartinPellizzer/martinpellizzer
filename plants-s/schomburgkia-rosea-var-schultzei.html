Schomburgkia rosea var. schultzei

Schomburgkia rosea var. schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's Ghost Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white, fragrant flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This orchid is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its rarity and unique floral beauty.
