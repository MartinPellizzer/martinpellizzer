Symphoricarpos rivularis

Symphoricarpos rivularis, commonly known as the river currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for wildlife and some humans. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland areas.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
