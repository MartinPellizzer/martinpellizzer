Silene bourgaei

Silene bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene bourgaei is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal scrub and limestone soils.
