Silene nicaeensis var. latifolia

Silene nicaeensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its vibrant flowers and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
