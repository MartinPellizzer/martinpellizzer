Sphaeralcea densiflora var. viscida

Sphaeralcea densiflora var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that gives it a sticky texture, and its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has small, oblong leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
