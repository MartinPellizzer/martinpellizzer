Silene muradica

Silene muradica, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene muradica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
