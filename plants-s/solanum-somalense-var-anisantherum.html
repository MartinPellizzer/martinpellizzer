Solanum somalense var. anisantherum

Solanum somalense var. anisantherum, commonly known as the Somali nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting adaptations to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its flowers are usually yellow or white, attracting pollinators.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that may contain toxic compounds, typical of many species in the Solanum genus.
