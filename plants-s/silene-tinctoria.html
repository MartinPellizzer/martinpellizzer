Silene tinctoria

Silene tinctoria, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a unique underground tuber, which is used in traditional medicine and dye-making. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Silene tinctoria thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
