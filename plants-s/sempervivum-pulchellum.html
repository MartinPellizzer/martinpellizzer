Sempervivum pulchellum

Sempervivum pulchellum, commonly known as the "Cape Hottentot" or "Alpine Houseplant," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple markings, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Sempervivum pulchellum is also known for its ability to survive harsh winter conditions, showcasing its hardy nature.
