Schizanthus carlomunozii var. carlomunozii

Schizanthus carlomunozii var. carlomunozii, commonly known as the Carlomunoz's fairyflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its delicate, trailing growth habit and profuse flowering. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals arranged in a funnel shape, and they bloom throughout the growing season. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easy-to-grow ornamental plant for gardeners.
