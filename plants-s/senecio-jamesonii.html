Senecio jamesonii

Senecio jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are generally gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

Senecio jamesonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
