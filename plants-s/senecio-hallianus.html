Senecio hallianus

Senecio hallianus, commonly known as the Hallian's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio hallianus is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
