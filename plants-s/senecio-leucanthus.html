Senecio leucanthus

Senecio leucanthus, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble a coneflower. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
