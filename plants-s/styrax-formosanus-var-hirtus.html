Styrax formosanus var. hirtus

Styrax formosanus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it has a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
