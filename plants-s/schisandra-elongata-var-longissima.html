Schisandra elongata var. longissima

Schisandra elongata var. longissima, commonly known as the long-fruit schisandra, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous climbing shrub characterized by its elongated, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of this variety make it distinct within the Schisandra genus.
