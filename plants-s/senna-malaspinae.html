Senna malaspinae

Senna malaspinae, commonly known as the Malaspina senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Senna malaspinae is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
