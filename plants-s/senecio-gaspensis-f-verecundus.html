Senecio gaspensis f. verecundus

Senecio gaspensis f. verecundus, commonly known as the Gaspersberg senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. It produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
