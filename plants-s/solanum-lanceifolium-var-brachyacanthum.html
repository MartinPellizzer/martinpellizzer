Solanum lanceifolium var. brachyacanthum

Solanum lanceifolium var. brachyacanthum, commonly known as the short-spined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in dry, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
