Shorea macroptera

Shorea macroptera, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases winged seeds for wind dispersal.

Shorea macroptera is valued for its hard, heavy timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
