Sauvagesia laciniata

Sauvagesia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery foliage.
