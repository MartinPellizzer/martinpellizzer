Salvia erythrostoma subsp. isabelina

Salvia erythrostoma subsp.

isabelina, commonly known as Isabelina sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and low maintenance requirements.
