Sorghum basiplicatum var. paniculatellum

Sorghum basiplicatum var. paniculatellum, commonly known as the narrow panicled sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, drooping cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop in arid environments.
