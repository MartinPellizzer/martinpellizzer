Senecio schulzeanus

Senecio schulzeanus, commonly known as the Schulze's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
