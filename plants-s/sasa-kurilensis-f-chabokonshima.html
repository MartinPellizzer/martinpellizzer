Sasa kurilensis f. chabokonshima

Sasa kurilensis f. chabokonshima, commonly known as the Chabokonshima sasa, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to the islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in local cultures.
