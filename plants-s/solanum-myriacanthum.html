Solanum myriacanthum

Solanum myriacanthum, commonly known as the many-thorned nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided by livestock and humans.
