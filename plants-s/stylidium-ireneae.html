Stylidium ireneae

Stylidium ireneae, commonly known as the Irene's sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and a compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur that houses the nectar.

Stylidium ireneae is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and interesting species for botanical study.
