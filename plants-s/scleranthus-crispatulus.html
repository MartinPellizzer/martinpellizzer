Scleranthus crispatulus

Scleranthus crispatulus, commonly known as the hairy thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scleranthus crispatulus is typically found in disturbed soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
