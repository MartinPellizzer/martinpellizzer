Stevia alpina var. alpina

Stevia alpina var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Stevia alpina var. alpina is valued for its intense sweet taste, making it a popular natural sweetener.
