Smilax glabra var. maculata

Smilax glabra var. maculata, commonly known as the scarlet smilax, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a pale green underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its vigorous growth and ornamental features make it a popular choice for garden use.
