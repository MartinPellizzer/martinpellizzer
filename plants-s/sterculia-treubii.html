Sterculia treubii

Sterculia treubii, commonly known as the African breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that resemble small breadfruit, which are consumed by both humans and animals. Its bark and leaves have traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
