Spatholobus sinensis

Spatholobus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria or Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Spatholobus sinensis thrives in well-drained soils and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
