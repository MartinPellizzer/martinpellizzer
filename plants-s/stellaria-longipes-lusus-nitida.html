Stellaria longipes lusus nitida

Stellaria longipes lusus nitida, commonly known as the shining starweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a soft texture. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
