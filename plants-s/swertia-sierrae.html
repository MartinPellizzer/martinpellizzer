Swertia sierrae

Swertia sierrae, commonly known as Sierra gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia sierrae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
