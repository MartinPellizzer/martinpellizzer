Salix chenostachya

Salix chenostachya, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval in shape, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkins as its reproductive structures, typical of willow species.

This plant thrives in moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
