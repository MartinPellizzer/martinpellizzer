Syzygium bicolor

Syzygium bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its strikingly contrasting white and pink flowers, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

Syzygium bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
