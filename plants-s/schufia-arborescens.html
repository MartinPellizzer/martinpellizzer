Schufia arborescens

Schufia arborescens, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Schufia arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
