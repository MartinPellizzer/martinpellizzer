Salsola laricifolia

Salsola laricifolia, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its succulent stems and leaves allow it to store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Salsola laricifolia often thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in deserts, grasslands, and along roadsides.
