Stevia oligocephala

Stevia oligocephala, commonly known as the "sweetleaf" or "stevia plant," is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are intensely sweet due to the presence of steviol glycosides. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia oligocephala is widely used as a natural sweetener and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits.
