Saxifraga aizoon elongata

Saxifraga aizoon elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
