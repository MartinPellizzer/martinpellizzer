Salix callicarpaea f. ameralikensis

Salix callicarpaea f. ameralikensis, commonly known as the Aeralikensis willow, is a rare and specialized variant of the callicarpaea willow species.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive floral structures. This subspecies is adapted to specific ecological niches, often found in alpine or high-altitude environments. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a relict species, offering insights into evolutionary adaptations to extreme climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for ecological and conservation research.
