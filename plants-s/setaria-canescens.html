Setaria canescens

Setaria canescens, commonly known as sanddrop grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically brown or beige in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Setaria canescens is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for livestock forage.
