Scilla autumnalis

Scilla autumnalis, commonly known as autumn squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla autumnalis is often used in rock gardens and as a naturalized plant in woodland settings.
