Senna perrieri

Senna perrieri, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. Its seeds are encased in a pod and contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically important in certain regions.
