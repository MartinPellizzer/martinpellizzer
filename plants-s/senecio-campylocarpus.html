Senecio campylocarpus

Senecio campylocarpus, commonly known as the wooly sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, gray-green foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, spherical seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
