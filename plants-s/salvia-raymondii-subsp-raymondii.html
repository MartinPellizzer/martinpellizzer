Salvia raymondii subsp. raymondii

Salvia raymondii subsp.

raymondii, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through early summer.
