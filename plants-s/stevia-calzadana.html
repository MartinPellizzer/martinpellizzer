Stevia calzadana

Stevia calzadana, commonly known as Mexican stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Stevia calzadana is often used as a natural sweetener and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
