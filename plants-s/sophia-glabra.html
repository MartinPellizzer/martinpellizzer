Sophia glabra

Sophia glabra, commonly known as the smooth sophia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its smooth, hairless stems and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Sophia glabra make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
