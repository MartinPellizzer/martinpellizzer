Sollya fusiformis

Sollya fusiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Sollya fusiformis is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought conditions.
