Sarga leioclada

Sarga leioclada, commonly known as the silky desert rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments. Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
