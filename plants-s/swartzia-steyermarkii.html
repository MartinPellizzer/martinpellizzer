Swartzia steyermarkii

Swartzia steyermarkii, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist forested areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
