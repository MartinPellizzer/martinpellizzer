Salvia galloana

Salvia galloana, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Salvia galloana is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks, making it a valuable plant for pollinators.
