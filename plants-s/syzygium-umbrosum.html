Syzygium umbrosum

Syzygium umbrosum, commonly known as the dark-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish when ripe, containing edible seeds. Syzygium umbrosum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique and important species in its native ecosystems.
