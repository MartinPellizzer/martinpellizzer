Solanum ficifolium

Solanum ficifolium, commonly known as the figleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a fig leaf. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal properties, though it is not widely used in traditional medicine.
