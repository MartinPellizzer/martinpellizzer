Senecio micranthus

Senecio micranthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It prefers bright, indirect light and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Senecio micranthus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
