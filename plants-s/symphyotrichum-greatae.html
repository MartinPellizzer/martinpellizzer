Symphyotrichum greatae

Symphyotrichum greatae, commonly known as the Great Basin blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum greatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid ecosystems.
