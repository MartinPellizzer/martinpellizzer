Stranvaesia undulata

Stranvaesia undulata, commonly known as the undulating lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
