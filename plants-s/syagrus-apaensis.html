Syagrus apaensis

Syagrus apaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the golden palm, is a species native to the northeastern region of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a distinctive crown of long, narrow leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. Syagrus apaensis thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to grow optimally.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
