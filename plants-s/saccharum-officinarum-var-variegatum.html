Saccharum officinarum var. variegatum

Saccharum officinarum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated sugarcane, is a variant of sugarcane characterized by its distinct variegated foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves exhibit a striking pattern of green and white or yellow markings, making it visually appealing. The variegation is due to genetic mutations affecting chlorophyll distribution.

While primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it can also be used for sugar production in certain conditions.
