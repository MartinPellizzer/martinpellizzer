Stapelia semota

Stapelia semota, commonly known as the "Carrion Flower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and unique, meaty-looking flowers that resemble decaying flesh. The plant produces large, yellowish-green blooms with a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators like flies. Stapelia semota is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stems.

It thrives in arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.
