Salvia wendelboi

Salvia wendelboi, commonly known as Wendelboi sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia wendelboi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
