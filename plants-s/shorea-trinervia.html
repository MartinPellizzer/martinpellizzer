Shorea trinervia

Shorea trinervia, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have three to five nerves, giving it its scientific name.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
