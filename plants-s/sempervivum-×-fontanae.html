Sempervivum × fontanae

Sempervivum × fontanae, commonly known as the variegated houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are often variegated with white, pink, or yellow markings. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens, containers, or as a houseplant.

Sempervivum × fontanae is also known for its ability to propagate easily through offsets, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
