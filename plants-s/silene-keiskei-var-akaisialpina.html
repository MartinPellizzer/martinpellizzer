Silene keiskei var. akaisialpina

Silene keiskei var. akaisialpina, commonly known as the alpine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest in botanical studies.
