Saxifraga arundana subsp. kunzeana

Saxifraga arundana subsp.

kunzeana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
