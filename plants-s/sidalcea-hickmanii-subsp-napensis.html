Sidalcea hickmanii subsp. napensis

Sidalcea hickmanii subsp.

napensis, commonly known as the Napens Mountaincheck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers and basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and cliffs, where it thrives in full sun. Its small, lance-shaped leaves and showy blooms make it a distinctive member of the legume family.
