Senecio longiflorus subsp. madagascariensis

Senecio longiflorus subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
