Senecio homogynefolius

Senecio homogynefolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
