Selaginella caudispica

Selaginella caudispica, commonly known as the forked clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Selaginella caudispica reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in humid environments.
