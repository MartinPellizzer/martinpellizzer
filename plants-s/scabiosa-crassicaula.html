Scabiosa crassicaula

Scabiosa crassicaula, commonly known as the thick-leaved scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
