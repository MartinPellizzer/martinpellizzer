Sideranthus laevis

Sideranthus laevis, commonly known as the silky sida, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Sideranthus laevis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
