Senecio huachucanus

Senecio huachucanus, commonly known as the Huachuca blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Senecio huachucanus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
