Syringa × chinensis var. sanguinea

Syringa × chinensis var. sanguinea, commonly known as the weeping cherry or Japanese weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance and seasonal beauty.
