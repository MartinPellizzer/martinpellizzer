Senecio julianus

Senecio julianus, commonly known as the Julian's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio julianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
