Senecio linariifolius var. aclonetus

Senecio linariifolius var. aclonetus, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
