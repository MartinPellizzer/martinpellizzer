Sida myriantha

Sida myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in riparian zones.

Sida myriantha has been studied for its potential ecological roles and medicinal properties.
