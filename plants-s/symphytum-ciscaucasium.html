Symphytum ciscaucasium

Symphytum ciscaucasium, commonly known as the Caucasian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
