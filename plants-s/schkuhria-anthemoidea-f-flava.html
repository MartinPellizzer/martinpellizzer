Schkuhria anthemoidea f. flava

Schkuhria anthemoidea f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered form of the common meadow bell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces slender, branching stems. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in early summer.
