Sophora calyptrata var. glabra

Sophora calyptrata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth false indigo, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
