Sium latifolium var. grandifolium

Sium latifolium var. grandifolium, commonly known as broadleaf waterparsley, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often floating or submerged, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to aquatic environments, and its stems are typically hollow. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
