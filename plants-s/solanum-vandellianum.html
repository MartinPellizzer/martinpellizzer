Solanum vandellianum

Solanum vandellianum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
