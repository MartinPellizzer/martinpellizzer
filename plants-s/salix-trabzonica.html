Salix trabzonica

Salix trabzonica, commonly known as Trabzon willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Black Sea region of Turkey.

It typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, temperate climates, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Salix trabzonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
