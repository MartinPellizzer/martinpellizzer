Scleranthus echinophorus

Scleranthus echinophorus, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, and the stems are covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

Scleranthus echinophorus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or wasteland areas.
