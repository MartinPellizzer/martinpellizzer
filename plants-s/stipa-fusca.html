Stipa fusca

Stipa fusca, commonly known as purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend at the base. The leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive purplish tinge on the underside. Its flowering spikes are compact and produce small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish.

This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.
