Sanctambrosia manicata

Sanctambrosia manicata, commonly known as the sacred touch plant, is a rare flowering species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and branched. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
