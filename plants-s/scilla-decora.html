Scilla decora

Scilla decora, commonly known as the Persian glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Scilla decora is often used in rock gardens and as a spring-flowering ornamental plant.
