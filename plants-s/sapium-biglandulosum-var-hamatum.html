Sapium biglandulosum var. hamatum

Sapium biglandulosum var. hamatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-red, inflated capsules that resemble small lanterns. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with prominent veins and a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate various soil types and is relatively drought-resistant.
