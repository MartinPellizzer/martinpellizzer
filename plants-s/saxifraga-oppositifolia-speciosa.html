Saxifraga oppositifolia speciosa

Saxifraga oppositifolia speciosa, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga oppositifolia speciosa is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
