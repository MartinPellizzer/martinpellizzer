Syzygium kuranda

Syzygium kuranda, commonly known as the Kuranda apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed. Syzygium kuranda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
