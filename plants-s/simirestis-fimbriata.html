Simirestis fimbriata

Simirestis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are often adorned with delicate fringes along the edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which help to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
