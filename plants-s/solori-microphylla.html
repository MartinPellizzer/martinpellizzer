Solori microphylla

Solori microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved solori, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in native or rare plants.
