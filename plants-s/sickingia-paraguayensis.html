Sickingia paraguayensis

Sickingia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay ice plant, is a succulent species native to South America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Sickingia paraguayensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
