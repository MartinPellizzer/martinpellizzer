Solanum ochraceo-ferrugineum

Solanum ochraceo-ferrugineum, commonly known as the yellow and rust-colored nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and often have a reddish or rust-colored underside.

The fruit is a small, yellow berry that matures to a reddish hue, containing several seeds.
