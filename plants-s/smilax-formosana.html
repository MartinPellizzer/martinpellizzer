Smilax formosana

Smilax formosana, commonly known as the Formosan arrowroot or Japanese arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

Smilax formosana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
