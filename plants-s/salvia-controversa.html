Salvia controversa

Salvia controversa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract beneficial insects, enhancing ecological balance in the landscape.
