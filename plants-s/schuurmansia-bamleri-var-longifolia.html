Schuurmansia bamleri var. longifolia

Schuurmansia bamleri var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Schuurmansia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
