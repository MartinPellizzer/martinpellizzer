Sedum lugdunense

Sedum lugdunense, commonly known as the Alpine stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum lugdunense is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
