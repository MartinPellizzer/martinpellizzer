Sorbus henryi

Sorbus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Sorbus henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
