Sempervivum triste

Sempervivum triste, commonly known as the weeping houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, blue-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Sempervivum triste is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, demonstrating its resilience and adaptability.
