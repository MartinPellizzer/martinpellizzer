Salix muliensis

Salix muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix muliensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and mountainous regions.
