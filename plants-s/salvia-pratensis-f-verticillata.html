Salvia pratensis f. verticillata

Salvia pratensis f. verticillata, commonly known as the whorled blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
