Stipa borysthenica var. marchica

Stipa borysthenica var. marchica, commonly known as the Marchian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
