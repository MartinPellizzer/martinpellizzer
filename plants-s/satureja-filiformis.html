Satureja filiformis

Satureja filiformis, commonly known as slender savory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often used in cooking for their mild, peppery flavor.

Satureja filiformis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
