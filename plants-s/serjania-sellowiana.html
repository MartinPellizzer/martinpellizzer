Serjania sellowiana

Serjania sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
