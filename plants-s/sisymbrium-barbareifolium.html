Sisymbrium barbareifolium

Sisymbrium barbareifolium, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sisymbrium barbareifolium is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
