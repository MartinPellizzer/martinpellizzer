Silene squamigera subsp. vesiculifera

Silene squamigera subsp.

vesiculifera, commonly known as the bladderpod catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Caryophyllaceae family.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
