Saccolabium tridentatum

Saccolabium tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Saccolabium tridentatum is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
