Spiraea × microthyrsa

Spiraea × microthyrsa, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring arching stems that are often covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in corymbs. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

Spiraea × microthyrsa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
