Scilla pubens

Scilla pubens, commonly known as the woolly scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla pubens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
