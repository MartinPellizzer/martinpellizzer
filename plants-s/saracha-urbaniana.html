Saracha urbaniana

Saracha urbaniana, commonly known as the urban tree, is a species native to urban environments in South America.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and its ability to thrive in polluted air, making it a hardy plant for city settings. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that provides structural support. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Saracha urbaniana plays a role in urban greening efforts due to its resilience and aesthetic value.
