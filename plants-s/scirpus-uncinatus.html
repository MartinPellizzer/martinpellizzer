Scirpus uncinatus

Scirpus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked flatsedge, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Scirpus uncinatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
