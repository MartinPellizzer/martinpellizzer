Saccolabium flexum

Saccolabium flexum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Saccolabium flexum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
