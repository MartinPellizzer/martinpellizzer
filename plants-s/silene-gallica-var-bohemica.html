Silene gallica var. bohemica

Silene gallica var. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
