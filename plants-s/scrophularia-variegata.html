Scrophularia variegata

Scrophularia variegata, commonly known as the variegated figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
