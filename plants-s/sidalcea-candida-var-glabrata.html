Sidalcea candida var. glabrata

Sidalcea candida var. glabrata, commonly known as the white silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered white flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to dry soils. It thrives in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, often found in the Great Basin and adjacent regions.
