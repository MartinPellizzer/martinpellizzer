Silene boryi var. penyalarensis

Silene boryi var. penyalarensis, commonly known as the Penyalarensis campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
