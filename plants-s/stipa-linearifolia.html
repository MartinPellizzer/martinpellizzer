Stipa linearifolia

Stipa linearifolia, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a species of grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Stipa linearifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
