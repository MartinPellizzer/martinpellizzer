Scleranthus biennis

Scleranthus biennis, commonly known as the annual bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow from 20 to 60 centimeters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scleranthus biennis is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
