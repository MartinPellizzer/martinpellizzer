Stachys corsica var. micrantha

Stachys corsica var. micrantha, commonly known as Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic leaves and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens.
