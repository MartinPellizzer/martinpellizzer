Silene platyphylla

Silene platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, which helps conserve water.

Silene platyphylla produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation in suitable conditions.
