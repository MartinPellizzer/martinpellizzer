Saccharum filiforme

Saccharum filiforme, commonly known as the slender cane grass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas near water sources.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
