Sasa myojinensis

Sasa myojinensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa myojinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
