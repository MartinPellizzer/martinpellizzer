Simaba cuneata

Simaba cuneata, commonly known as the Caribbean wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. Its small, white flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Simaba cuneata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
