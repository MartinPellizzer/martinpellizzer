Satyrium macrophyllum

Satyrium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms make it an important subject in botanical studies.
