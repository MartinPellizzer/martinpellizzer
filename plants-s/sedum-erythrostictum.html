Sedum erythrostictum

Sedum erythrostictum, commonly known as the red-streaked stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Sedum erythrostictum is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular among gardeners in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
