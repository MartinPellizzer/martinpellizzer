Sideritis pusilla subsp. osteoxylla

Sideritis pusilla subsp.

osteoxylla, commonly known as the small rock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is a small herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
