Salsola spissa

Salsola spissa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and dry grasslands. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain water in harsh conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are usually clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Salsola spissa is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed areas.
