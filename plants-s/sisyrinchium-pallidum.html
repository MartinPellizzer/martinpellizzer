Sisyrinchium pallidum

Sisyrinchium pallidum, commonly known as the pale blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to white flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to dry, well-drained conditions.

Sisyrinchium pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
