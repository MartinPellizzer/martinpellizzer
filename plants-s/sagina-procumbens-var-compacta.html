Sagina procumbens var. compacta

Sagina procumbens var. compacta, commonly known as dwarf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes or disturbed areas. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers are tiny, white or pink, and appear in clusters near the base of the stems.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
