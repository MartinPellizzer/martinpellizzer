Spartium pungens

Spartium pungens, commonly known as spiny sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its prickly, bristly texture.

Spartium pungens is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
