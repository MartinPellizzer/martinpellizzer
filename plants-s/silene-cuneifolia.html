Silene cuneifolia

Silene cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow leaves. Its flowers are surrounded by bracts and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Silene cuneifolia is often found in open meadows and along forest edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
