Smilax moranensis var. schaffneriana

Smilax moranensis var. schaffneriana, commonly known as the Schaffner's smilax, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spines, which are typical features of the Smilax genus. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure and growth habits make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
