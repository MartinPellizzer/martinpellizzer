Staurogyne neesii

Staurogyne neesii, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Staurogyne neesii thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
