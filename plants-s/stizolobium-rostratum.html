Stizolobium rostratum

Stizolobium rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive swollen base, and its pods are typically flattened and ribbed.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
