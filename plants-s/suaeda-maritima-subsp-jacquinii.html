Suaeda maritima subsp. jacquinii

Suaeda maritima subsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as saltbush or sea blite, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments. It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high soil salinity.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss and aid in salt excretion. Its flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, and it produces small, round seeds. Suaeda maritima subsp.

jacquinii plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local wildlife.
