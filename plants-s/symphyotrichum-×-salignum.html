Symphyotrichum × salignum

Symphyotrichum × salignum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in composite heads, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum × salignum is often found in moist to wet habitats, including meadows, marshes, and woodland edges.
