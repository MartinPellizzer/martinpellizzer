Smilax populnea

Smilax populnea, commonly known as the cane bluestem, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. Smilax populnea thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
