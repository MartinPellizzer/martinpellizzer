Saccharum officinarum var. luridum

Saccharum officinarum var. luridum, commonly known as the lurid sugarcane.

This variant is characterized by its striking yellowish to reddish hues in its foliage and stalks, which distinguish it from standard sugarcane varieties. It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant exhibits tall, robust stalks that can reach several meters in height, making it suitable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for botanical study and cultivation.
