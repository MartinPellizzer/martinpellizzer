Selaginella mearnsii

Selaginella mearnsii, commonly known as the common spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping rhizome with dense clusters of spirally arranged, needle-like leaves. Selaginella mearnsii is characterized by its ability to reproduce through spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
