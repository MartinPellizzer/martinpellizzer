Solanum boerhaviifolium var. calvum

Solanum boerhaviifolium var. calvum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its toxicity and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
