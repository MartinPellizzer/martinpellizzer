Salvia eizi-matudae

Salvia eizi-matudae, commonly known as the Matudae sage, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
