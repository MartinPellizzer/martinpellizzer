Syringa vulgaris var. virginalis

Syringa vulgaris var. virginalis, commonly known as the White Hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its ability to produce large, showy clusters of white flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green and provide a striking contrast to the pale blooms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.

It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
