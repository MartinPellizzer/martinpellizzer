Shorea parvistipulata subsp. parvistipulata

Shorea parvistipulata subsp.

parvistipulata, commonly known as the small-leafed shorea, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Dipterocarpaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
