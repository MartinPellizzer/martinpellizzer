Schizoloma davallioides

Schizoloma davallioides, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green to white in color.

Schizoloma davallioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
