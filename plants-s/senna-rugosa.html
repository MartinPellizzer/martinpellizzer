Senna rugosa

Senna rugosa, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Senna rugosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
