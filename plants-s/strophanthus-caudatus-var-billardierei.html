Strophanthus caudatus var. billardierei

Strophanthus caudatus var. billardierei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though its use requires caution due to the presence of toxic alkaloids.
