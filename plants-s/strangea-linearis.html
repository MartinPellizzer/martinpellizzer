Strangea linearis

Strangea linearis, commonly known as the slender strangler fig, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its long, thin stems that wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy texture.

Strangea linearis is often found in forest understories, where it utilizes its climbing habit to reach sunlight.
