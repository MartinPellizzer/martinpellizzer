Salvia yukoyukparum

Salvia yukoyukparum, commonly known as the Yukon sage, is a perennial herb native to the Yukon region of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salvia yukoyukparum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
