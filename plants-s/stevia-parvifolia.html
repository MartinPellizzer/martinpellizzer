Stevia parvifolia

Stevia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing steviol glycosides, natural compounds used as a sweetener. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained attention as a sustainable alternative to sugar.
