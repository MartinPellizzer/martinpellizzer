Spiraea aruncus macrocarpa

Spiraea aruncus macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-flowered weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that persist into winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Spiraea aruncus macrocarpa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
