Sebastiania hispida var. intercedens

Sebastiania hispida var. intercedens, commonly known as the velvetleaf sebastiania, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
