Syzygium pseudocrenulatum

Syzygium pseudocrenulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing sweet, sticky pulp.

Syzygium pseudocrenulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
