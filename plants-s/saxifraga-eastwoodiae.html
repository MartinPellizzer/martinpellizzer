Saxifraga eastwoodiae

Saxifraga eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually succulent, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Saxifraga eastwoodiae is often found in rocky crevices and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
