Sedum douglasii

Sedum douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments.

Sedum douglasii is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
