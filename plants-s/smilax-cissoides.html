Smilax cissoides

Smilax cissoides, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a robust, woody stem with spiny nodes and heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
