Syringa lilac var. violacea

Syringa lilac var. violacea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flower color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often used in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
