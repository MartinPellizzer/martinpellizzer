Silene nocturna subsp. capraria

Silene nocturna subsp.

capraria, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its unique blooming habit and ecological role in supporting night-active pollinators.
