Solanum ovalifolium var. campylacanthum

Solanum ovalifolium var. campylacanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, typically white or pale purple flowers. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Argentina and Chile.
