Spathoglottis patentibractea

Spathoglottis patentibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract spathoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, while its leaves are typically broad and strap-shaped. Spathoglottis patentibractea thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
