Sycomorus schimperiana

Sycomorus schimperiana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, lobed leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, inedible fig-like fruits that are important for wildlife.

The plant is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
