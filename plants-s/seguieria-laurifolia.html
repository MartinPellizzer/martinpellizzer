Seguieria laurifolia

Seguieria laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Seguieria laurifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
