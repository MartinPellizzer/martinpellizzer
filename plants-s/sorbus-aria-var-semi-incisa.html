Sorbus aria var. semi-incisa

Sorbus aria var. semi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
