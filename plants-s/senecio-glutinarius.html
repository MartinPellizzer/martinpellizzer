Senecio glutinarius

Senecio glutinarius, commonly known as sticky senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The bright yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.
