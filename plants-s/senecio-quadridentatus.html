Senecio quadridentatus

Senecio quadridentatus, commonly known as the four-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
