Senecio scopolii subsp. floccosus

Senecio scopolii subsp.

floccosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
