Sanguisorba ancistroides subsp. parviflora

Sanguisorba ancistroides subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and streambanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers in late summer. Its flowers are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
