Salix alba var. opaca

Salix alba var. opaca, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is known for its rapid growth rate. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The bark of this species is smooth and pale, with a distinctive white or ashen appearance.
