Senecio macedonicus subsp. barckhausiifolius

Senecio macedonicus subsp.

barckhausiifolius, commonly known as the Macedonian Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
