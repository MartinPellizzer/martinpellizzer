Sedum dendroideum

Sedum dendroideum, commonly known as the tree sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its upright, branching growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum dendroideum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
