Salvia stibalii

Salvia stibalii, commonly known as the Stibal's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Salvia stibalii is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
