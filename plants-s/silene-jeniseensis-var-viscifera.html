Silene jeniseensis var. viscifera

Silene jeniseensis var. viscifera, commonly known as the sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that produce a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purplish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
