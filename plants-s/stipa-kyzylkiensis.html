Stipa kyzylkiensis

Stipa kyzylkiensis, commonly known as the Kyzylkent needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes, often in regions with low precipitation.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems.
