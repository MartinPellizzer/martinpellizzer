Sericocomopsis welwitschii

Sericocomopsis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia plant, is a unique succulent native to Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk that can reach heights of over 5 meters and is covered in a thick, protective layer of bark. Sericocomopsis welwitschii produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season.

It is adapted to arid environments and has a long lifespan, sometimes living over 1,000 years.
