Solanum tarmense

Solanum tarmense, commonly known as Tarmian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshades. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, rounded berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
