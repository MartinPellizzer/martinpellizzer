Salix dolia var. rehderiana

Salix dolia var. rehderiana, commonly known as Rehder's willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue on top and a silvery-white underside. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
