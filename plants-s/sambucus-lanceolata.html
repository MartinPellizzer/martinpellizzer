Sambucus lanceolata

Sambucus lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when fully ripe, though they are often used in jams and wines. It typically grows in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Sambucus lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
