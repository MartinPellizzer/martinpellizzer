Salix × tambaensis

Salix × tambaensis, commonly known as the Tamba willow, is a hybrid species native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

Salix × tambaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
