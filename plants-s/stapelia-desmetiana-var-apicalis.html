Stapelia desmetiana var. apicalis

Stapelia desmetiana var. apicalis, commonly known as the Apical Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive, waxy flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
