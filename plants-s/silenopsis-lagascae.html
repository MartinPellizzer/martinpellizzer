Silenopsis lagascae

Silenopsis lagascae, commonly known as the yellow silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Silenopsis lagascae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
