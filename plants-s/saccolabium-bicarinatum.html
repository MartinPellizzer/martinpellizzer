Saccolabium bicarinatum

Saccolabium bicarinatum, commonly known as the two-ribbed saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and for forage in some regions.
