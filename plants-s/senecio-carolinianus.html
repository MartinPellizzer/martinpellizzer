Senecio carolinianus

Senecio carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has hairy, alternate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio carolinianus is often found in the southeastern states and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
