Sideritis hirsuta subsp. aculeata

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
