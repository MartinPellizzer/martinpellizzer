Scirpus subterminalis

Scirpus subterminalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus subterminalis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
