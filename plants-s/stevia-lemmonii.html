Stevia lemmonii

Stevia lemmonii, commonly known as Lemon stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Stevia lemmonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
