Scutellaria lateriflora var. axillaris

Scutellaria lateriflora var. axillaris, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Botanically, it is noted for its distinctive calyx and corolla structure, which are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
