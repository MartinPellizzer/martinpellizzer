Sanguisorba muricata

Sanguisorba muricata, commonly known as the red sanders or rosebay willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish, covered with stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sanguisorba muricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
