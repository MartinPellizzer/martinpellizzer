Spatholobus littoralis

Spatholobus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal wisteria, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its long, flexible stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near the coast and is often found in shrublands and coastal forests.

Spatholobus littoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
