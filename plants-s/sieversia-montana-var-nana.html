Sieversia montana var. nana

Sieversia montana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf mountain sieversia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
