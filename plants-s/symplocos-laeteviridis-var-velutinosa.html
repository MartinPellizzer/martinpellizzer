Symplocos laeteviridis var. velutinosa

Symplocos laeteviridis var. velutinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
