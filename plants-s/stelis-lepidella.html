Stelis lepidella

Stelis lepidella, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Stelis lepidella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
