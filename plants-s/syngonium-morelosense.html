Syngonium morelosense

Syngonium morelosense, commonly known as the Mexican arrowhead plant, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically has heart-shaped to arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white, cream, or yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Syngonium morelosense is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
