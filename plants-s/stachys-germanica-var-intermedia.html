Stachys germanica var. intermedia

Stachys germanica var. intermedia, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its lance-shaped leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
