Spartium junceum f. plenum

Spartium junceum f. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is notable for its high floral density, which makes it an attractive plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
