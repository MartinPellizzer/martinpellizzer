Saracha procumbens var. repandodentata

Saracha procumbens var. repandodentata, commonly known as the creeping saracha, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes to form dense mats. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, blooming in late summer.

This plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.
