Sinapis schlosseri

Sinapis schlosseri, commonly known as Schlosser's mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean regions.

Sinapis schlosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
