Saussurea grandifolia

Saussurea grandifolia, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow or broadleafed saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential pharmacological properties.
