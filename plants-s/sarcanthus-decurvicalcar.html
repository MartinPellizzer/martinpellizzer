Sarcanthus decurvicalcar

Sarcanthus decurvicalcar, commonly known as the curly-spined sarcanthus, is a unique member of the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted spines that adorn its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Sarcanthus decurvicalcar is native to certain regions of South America and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
