Solanum ueleense

Solanum ueleense, commonly known as the Uele nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue or black color. Solanum ueleense is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
