Stenorrhynchos acianthiformis

Stenorrhynchos acianthiformis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
