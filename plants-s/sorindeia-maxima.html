Sorindeia maxima

Sorindeia maxima, commonly known as the giant sorindeia, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stem is covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its tropical habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sorindeia maxima is considered a conservation concern.
