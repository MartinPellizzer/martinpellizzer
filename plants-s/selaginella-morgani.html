Selaginella morgani

Selaginella morgani, commonly known as the Morgani's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. The plant has a distinctive appearance with short, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces sporophytes that bear spore capsules, which are clustered in small, cone-like structures.

Selaginella morgani is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
