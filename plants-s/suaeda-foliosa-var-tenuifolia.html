Suaeda foliosa var. tenuifolia

Suaeda foliosa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, linear leaves that reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Its succulent stems and leaves help it store water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.
