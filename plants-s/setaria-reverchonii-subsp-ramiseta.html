Setaria reverchonii subsp. ramiseta

Setaria reverchonii subsp.

ramiseta, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in arid landscapes.
