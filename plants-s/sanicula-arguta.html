Sanicula arguta

Sanicula arguta, commonly known as the slender sanicle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.
