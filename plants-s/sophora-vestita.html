Sophora vestita

Sophora vestita, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Sophora vestita is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
