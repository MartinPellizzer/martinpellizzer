Salvia jailicola

Salvia jailicola, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
