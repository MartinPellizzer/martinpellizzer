Senecio cremeiflorus

Senecio cremeiflorus, commonly known as the Creamflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white or grayish coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio cremeiflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
