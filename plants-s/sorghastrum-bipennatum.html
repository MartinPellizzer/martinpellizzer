Sorghastrum bipennatum

Sorghastrum bipennatum, commonly known as black grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or loamy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its botanical features make it a key component of native grassland ecosystems.
