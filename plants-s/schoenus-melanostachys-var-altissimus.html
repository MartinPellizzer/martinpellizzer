Schoenus melanostachys var. altissimus

Schoenus melanostachys var. altissimus, commonly known as the tall blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, dark-colored flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
