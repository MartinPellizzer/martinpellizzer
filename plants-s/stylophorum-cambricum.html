Stylophorum cambricum

Stylophorum cambricum, commonly known as the Welsh buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grassy areas. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are followed by bright orange, hairy seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Stylophorum cambricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
