Stilbanthus scandens

Stilbanthus scandens, commonly known as the climbing legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow or white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found growing along fences or in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.

Its ecological role includes providing food for insects and contributing to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
