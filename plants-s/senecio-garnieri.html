Senecio garnieri

Senecio garnieri, commonly known as the Blueweed or Bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio garnieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
