Senecio domingensis

Senecio domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its natural setting.

Senecio domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
