Salix × sakaii

Salix × sakaii, commonly known as the Sakai willow, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salix viminalis and Salix integra.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on top and silvery-white underneath, giving it a striking appearance. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

Salix × sakaii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
