Scrophularia praeterita

Scrophularia praeterita, commonly known as the lesser dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia praeterita is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
