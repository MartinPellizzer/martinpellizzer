Scirpus tasmanicus

Scirpus tasmanicus, commonly known as the Tasmanian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Tasmania.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus tasmanicus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
