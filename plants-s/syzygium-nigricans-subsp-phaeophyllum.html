Syzygium nigricans subsp. phaeophyllum

Syzygium nigricans subsp.

phaeophyllum, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that contains a white, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and has glossy, elliptical leaves with a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are prized for their taste and medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker fruit color and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Syzygium species.
