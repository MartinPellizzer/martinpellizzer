Sideritis tragoriganum

Sideritis tragoriganum, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Sideritis tragoriganum thrives in rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
