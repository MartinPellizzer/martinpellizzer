Serratula salicina

Serratula salicina, commonly known as the desert tansy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Serratula salicina is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
