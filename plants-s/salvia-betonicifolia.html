Salvia betonicifolia

Salvia betonicifolia, commonly known as the purple stem sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, opposite leaves, and bracteate inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
