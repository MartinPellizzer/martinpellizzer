Stylophorum simplicifolium

Stylophorum simplicifolium, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

The plant is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
