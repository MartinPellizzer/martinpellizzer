Saxifraga × luizetii

Saxifraga × luizetii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga tridactylites. This plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, and it has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
