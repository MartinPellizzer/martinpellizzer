Santolina scariosa

Santolina scariosa, commonly known as silver santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina scariosa is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects.
