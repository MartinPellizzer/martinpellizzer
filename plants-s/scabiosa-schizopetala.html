Scabiosa schizopetala

Scabiosa schizopetala, commonly known as the divided scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that are clustered in umbels, surrounded by finely divided, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa schizopetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
