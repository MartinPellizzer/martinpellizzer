Stachys shirini

Stachys shirini, commonly known as Persian hedge parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Stachys shirini thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.
