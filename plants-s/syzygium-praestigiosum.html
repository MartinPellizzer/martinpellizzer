Syzygium praestigiosum

Syzygium praestigiosum, commonly known as the purple-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium praestigiosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
