Solidago bicolor var. lanata

Solidago bicolor var. lanata, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
