Senegalia alemquerensis

Senegalia alemquerensis, commonly known as the Mexican burrobrush, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in rocky areas and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
