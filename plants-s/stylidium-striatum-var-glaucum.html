Stylidium striatum var. glaucum

Stylidium striatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue-stemmed jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, with a distinctive waxy coating. This variant is distinguished by its bluish-green stem coloration, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
