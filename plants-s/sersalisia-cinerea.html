Sersalisia cinerea

Sersalisia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf sersalisia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sersalisia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
