Senna artemisioides subsp. helmsii

Senna artemisioides subsp.

helmsii, commonly known as Helms' senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
