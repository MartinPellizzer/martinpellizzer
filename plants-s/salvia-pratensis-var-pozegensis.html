Salvia pratensis var. pozegensis

Salvia pratensis var. pozegensis, commonly known as the Pozega Meadow Sage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
