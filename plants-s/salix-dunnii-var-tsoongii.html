Salix dunnii var. tsoongii

Salix dunnii var. tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese willow or Tsoong's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically reddish-brown when young and turn dark green as they mature. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
