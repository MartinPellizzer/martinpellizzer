Scabiosa graminifolia subvar. argyrea

Scabiosa graminifolia subvar. argyrea, commonly known as silver scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
