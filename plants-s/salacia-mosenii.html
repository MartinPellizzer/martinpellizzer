Salacia mosenii

Salacia mosenii, commonly known as the Mosen's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color, which are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Salacia mosenii is often found growing in shaded, humid environments, typically along stream banks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and less-studied species in botanical research.
