Salacia juruana

Salacia juruana, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host trees, while also performing photosynthesis. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in its habitat.
