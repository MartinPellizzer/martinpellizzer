Syagrus emasensis

Syagrus emasensis, commonly known as the Emas palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into small, round fruits containing edible seeds. Syagrus emasensis is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
