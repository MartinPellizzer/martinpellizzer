Sideritis briquetiana

Sideritis briquetiana, commonly known as the Briquet's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sideritis briquetiana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
