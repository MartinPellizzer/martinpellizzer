Silene italica var. pogonocalyx

Silene italica var. pogonocalyx, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
