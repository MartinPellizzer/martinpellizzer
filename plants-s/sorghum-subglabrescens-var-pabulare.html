Sorghum subglabrescens var. pabulare

Sorghum subglabrescens var. pabulare, commonly known as the coastal sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
