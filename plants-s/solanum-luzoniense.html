Solanum luzoniense

Solanum luzoniense, commonly known as the Luzon nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
