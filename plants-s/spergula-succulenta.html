Spergula succulenta

Spergula succulenta, commonly known as the succulent sedge, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, erect leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils, such as those in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in environments with fluctuating moisture levels.

Spergula succulenta plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
