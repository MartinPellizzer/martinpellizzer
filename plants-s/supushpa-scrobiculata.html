Supushpa scrobiculata

Supushpa scrobiculata, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Supushpa scrobiculata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
