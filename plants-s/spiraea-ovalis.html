Spiraea ovalis

Spiraea ovalis, commonly known as the oval spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea ovalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
