Scirpus fernaldii

Scirpus fernaldii, commonly known as Fernald's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that rise above narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Scirpus fernaldii plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
