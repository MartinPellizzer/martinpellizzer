Senecio occidentalis

Senecio occidentalis, commonly known as the western ragwort, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Senecio occidentalis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
