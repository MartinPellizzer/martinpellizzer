Syzygium nervosum var. paniala

Syzygium nervosum var. paniala, commonly known as the Paniala apple or mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy bark and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, fragrant flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
