Sebaea crassulifolia var. breviflora

Sebaea crassulifolia var. breviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sebaea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens or containers.
