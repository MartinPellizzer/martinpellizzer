Stachys germanica var. alpina

Stachys germanica var. alpina, commonly known as alpine betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
