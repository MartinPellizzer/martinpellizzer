Sasa nishigoensis f. albostriata

Sasa nishigoensis f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a variant of the Japanese bamboo species characterized by its distinctive white or pale green stripes on the culms.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories in Japan. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color, contrasting with the lighter colored stems. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing visual interest in both winter and summer.
