Schenckia blumenaviensis

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with fine, silvery hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial shade. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Schenckia blumenaviensis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
