Solanum piligerum

Solanum piligerum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, green berries. These berries turn red when mature and are edible, though they contain toxic compounds in high quantities.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
