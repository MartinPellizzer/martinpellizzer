Sorbus graeca var. taurica

Sorbus graeca var. taurica, commonly known as the Crimean rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
