Setaria gracilis f. megalantha

Setaria gracilis f. megalantha, commonly known as the giant slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable ornamental in landscaping.
