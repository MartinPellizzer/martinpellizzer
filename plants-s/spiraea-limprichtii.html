Spiraea limprichtii

Spiraea limprichtii, commonly known as the dwarf spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, creating a delicate, cascading effect. The plant has slender, arching branches and elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Spiraea limprichtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance.
