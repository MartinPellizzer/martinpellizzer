Senecio subulatus var. macranthus

Senecio subulatus var. macranthus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
