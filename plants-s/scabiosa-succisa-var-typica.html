Scabiosa succisa var. typica

Scabiosa succisa var. typica, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and form dense, spherical clusters known as cymes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower meadows.
