Serapias × dickmannii

Serapias × dickmannii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a cross between Serapias nemorosa and Serapias microchila.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its robust, hairy stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme and have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Serapias × dickmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
