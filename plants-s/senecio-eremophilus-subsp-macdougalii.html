Senecio eremophilus subsp. macdougalii

Senecio eremophilus subsp.

macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
