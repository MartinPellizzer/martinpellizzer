Solanum monteiroi

Solanum monteiroi, commonly known as Monteiro's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
