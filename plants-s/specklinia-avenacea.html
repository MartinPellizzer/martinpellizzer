Specklinia avenacea

Specklinia avenacea, commonly known as the slender pigmy date palm, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and conservatories.
