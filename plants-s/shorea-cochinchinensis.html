Shorea cochinchinensis

Shorea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
