Salvia halaensis

Salvia halaensis, commonly known as Halaean sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fragrant scent.

Salvia halaensis is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is considered a rare and endemic species.
