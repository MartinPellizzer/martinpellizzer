Sesamum indicum var. angustifolium

Sesamum indicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf sesame, is a variety of the sesame plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, capsule-like fruits that contain oily seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight. Its seeds are valued for their high oil content and are used in both culinary and industrial applications.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for cultivation in warm climates.
