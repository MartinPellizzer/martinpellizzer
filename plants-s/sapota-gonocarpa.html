Sapota gonocarpa

Sapota gonocarpa, commonly known as the chikoo or chiku, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, inedible outer layer and a soft, creamy pulp containing large, starchy seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its unique fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
