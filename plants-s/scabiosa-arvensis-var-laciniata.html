Scabiosa arvensis var. laciniata

Scabiosa arvensis var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are finely divided and lobed, giving the plant a lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed ground.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
