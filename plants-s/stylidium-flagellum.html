Stylidium flagellum

Stylidium flagellum, commonly known as the whip-stalked sun-orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

This plant is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower spike with a single, showy bloom. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a rosette pattern.

The plant's specialized structure and habitat preferences make it a rare and fascinating subject in botanical study.
