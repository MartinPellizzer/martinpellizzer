Saxifraga baimashanensis

Saxifraga baimashanensis, commonly known as the Baimashan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and stipitate leaves, which help in reducing water loss.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
