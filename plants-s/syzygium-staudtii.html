Syzygium staudtii

Syzygium staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, woody pod that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
