Scrophularia crassicaulis

Scrophularia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, producing showy, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia crassicaulis is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
