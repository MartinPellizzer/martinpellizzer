Syzygium oligomyrum

Syzygium oligomyrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits. Its botanical features include a rough, bark-like exocarp and a fibrous mesocarp.

Syzygium oligomyrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in tropical gardens.
