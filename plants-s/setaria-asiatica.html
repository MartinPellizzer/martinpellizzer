Setaria asiatica

Setaria asiatica, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a flowering plant native to Asia and belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a warm-season annual grass that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a panicle. Its seeds are enclosed in spikelets, which are the primary reproductive structures of the plant.

Setaria asiatica is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
