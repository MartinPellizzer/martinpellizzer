Satyrium mechowianum

Satyrium mechowianum, commonly known as the Mechowian orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically feature a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes. Its blooming period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is known for its pale greenish-yellow petals and intricate floral patterns.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
