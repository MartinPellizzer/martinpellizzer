Saxifraga cortusifolia

Saxifraga cortusifolia, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette-like arrangement and a slightly hairy texture.

Saxifraga cortusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environments.
