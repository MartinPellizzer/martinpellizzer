Senecio antennaria var. caulescens

Senecio antennaria var. caulescens, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of hairy, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
