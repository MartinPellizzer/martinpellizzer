Sasa niitakayamensis var. microcarpa

Sasa niitakayamensis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa niitakayamensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant features slender, hollow stems with a distinctive silver-gray sheath, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. Its small, pale green to white flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and growth habit.
