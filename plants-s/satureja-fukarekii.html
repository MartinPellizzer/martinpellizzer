Satureja fukarekii

Satureja fukarekii, commonly known as Fukarek's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Satureja fukarekii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
