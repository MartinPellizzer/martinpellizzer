Stevia jujuyensis

Stevia jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in sustainable agriculture and herbal medicine.
