Sarcopera sessiliflora

Sarcopera sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower sarcopera, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sarcopera sessiliflora is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
