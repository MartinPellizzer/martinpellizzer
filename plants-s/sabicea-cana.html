Sabicea cana

Sabicea cana, commonly known as the white sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Sabicea cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
