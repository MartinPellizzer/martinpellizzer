Stenandrium eustachyum

Stenandrium eustachyum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow floral bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are short-lived but highly conspicuous, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Stenandrium eustachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
