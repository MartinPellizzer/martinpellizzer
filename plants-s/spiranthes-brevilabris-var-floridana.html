Spiranthes brevilabris var. floridana

Spiranthes brevilabris var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiraling arrangement of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, elongated flower spike.

This orchid is known for its delicate beauty and is often found in association with other native flora in its natural environment.
