Solidago faucibus

Solidago faucibus, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Solidago faucibus plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
