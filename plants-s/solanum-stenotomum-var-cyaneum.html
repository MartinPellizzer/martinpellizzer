Solanum stenotomum var. cyaneum

Solanum stenotomum var. cyaneum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in panicles. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth habit and climbing nature. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the fruits are small, fleshy berries that mature to a red or orange color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
