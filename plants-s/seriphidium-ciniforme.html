Seriphidium ciniforme

Seriphidium ciniforme, commonly known as the ciniform hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Seriphidium ciniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
