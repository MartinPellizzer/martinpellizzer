Scopolia tangutica

Scopolia tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Scopolia tangutica is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
