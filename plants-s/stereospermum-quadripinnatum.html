Stereospermum quadripinnatum

Stereospermum quadripinnatum, commonly known as the four-pinnate starfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Its botanical features include a distinctive branching pattern and a preference for warm, humid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable environments.
