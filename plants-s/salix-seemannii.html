Salix seemannii

Salix seemannii, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

Salix seemannii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
