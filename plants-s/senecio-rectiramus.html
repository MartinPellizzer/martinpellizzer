Senecio rectiramus

Senecio rectiramus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio rectiramus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
