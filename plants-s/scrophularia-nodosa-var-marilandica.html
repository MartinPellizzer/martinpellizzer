Scrophularia nodosa var. marilandica

Scrophularia nodosa var. marilandica, commonly known as Maryland dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
