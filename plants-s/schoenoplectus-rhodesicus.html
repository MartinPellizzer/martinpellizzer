Schoenoplectus rhodesicus

Schoenoplectus rhodesicus, commonly known as Rhodes' bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems that grow from rhizomes and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which are borne on spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, lake margins, and riverbanks.

Schoenoplectus rhodesicus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
