Sasa veitchii f. persimilis

Sasa veitchii f. persimilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its foliage is often described as soft and velvety, giving it its common name.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
