Streptanthus coulteri var. lemmonii

Streptanthus coulteri var. lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
