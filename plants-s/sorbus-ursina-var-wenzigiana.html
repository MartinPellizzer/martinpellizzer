Sorbus ursina var. wenzigiana

Sorbus ursina var. wenzigiana, commonly known as the Wenzig's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The fruit is a persistent, aggregate berry that turns red in autumn, providing food for wildlife. This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
