Setaria verticillata var. congesta

Setaria verticillata var. congesta, commonly known as curled foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence that forms a cylindrical or conical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and the stems are erect and fibrous.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
