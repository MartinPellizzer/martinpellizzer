Spergularia echinosperma

Spergularia echinosperma, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically thrives in sandy or calcareous soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, and its fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
