Stanhopea novogaliciana

Stanhopea novogaliciana, commonly known as the Galiano orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with broad, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its native habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Its unique floral morphology and limited distribution contribute to its status as a botanical curiosity.
