Senecio agapatensis

Senecio agapatensis, commonly known as the Agape's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio agapatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
