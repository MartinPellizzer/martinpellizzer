Saussurea lappa

Saussurea lappa, commonly known as Chinese angelica or costus root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiky, woody fruits. Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea lappa is valued for its aromatic compounds and has been used in herbal remedies for various health purposes.
