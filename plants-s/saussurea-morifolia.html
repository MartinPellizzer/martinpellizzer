Saussurea morifolia

Saussurea morifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant is known for its tall, sparsely branched stems and dark blue or purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Saussurea morifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
