Smilacina oleracea f. acuminata

Smilacina oleracea f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed false Solomon's seal, is a botanical variant of the false Solomon's seal species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red at the base.

The fruit is a bright red, berry-like structure that matures in the fall and is an important food source for wildlife.
