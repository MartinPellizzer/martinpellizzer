Sarcochilus divitiflorus

Sarcochilus divitiflorus, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a distinctive, fleshy, tuberous root system adapted to its cool, moist habitat. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a unique, torch-like shape that attracts pollinators. It typically grows in forested areas and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.

Botanically, it is characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves.
