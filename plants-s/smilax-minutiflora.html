Smilax minutiflora

Smilax minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
