Silene vulgaris f. macrophylla

Silene vulgaris f. macrophylla, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
