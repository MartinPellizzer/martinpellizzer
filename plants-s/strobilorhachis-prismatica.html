Strobilorhachis prismatica

Strobilorhachis prismatica, commonly known as the prismatic strobilar, is a rare and unique member of the strobilar family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, prism-like structure in its reproductive organs, which set it apart from other plants in the group. The plant exhibits a complex arrangement of sporangia and antheridia, typical of strobilar plants. It is primarily found in specific, moist forest environments, often in association with other specialized flora.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of great interest to botanists studying plant evolution and reproductive strategies.
