Syzygium doctersii

Syzygium doctersii, commonly known as the Kauri apple, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea and northern Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and cloves, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing a single seed.

Syzygium doctersii is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruiting habit.
