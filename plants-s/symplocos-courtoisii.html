Symplocos courtoisii

Symplocos courtoisii, commonly known as the Courtois's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos courtoisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
