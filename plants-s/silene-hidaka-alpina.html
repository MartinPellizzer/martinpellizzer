Silene hidaka-alpina

Silene hidaka-alpina, commonly known as the Japanese fireweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene hidaka-alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
