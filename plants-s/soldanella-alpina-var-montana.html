Soldanella alpina var. montana

Soldanella alpina var. montana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.
