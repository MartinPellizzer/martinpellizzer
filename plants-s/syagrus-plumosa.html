Syagrus plumosa

Syagrus plumosa, commonly known as the palm tree, is a species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Syagrus plumosa is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
