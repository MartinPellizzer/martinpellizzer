Shorea stenoptera var. stenoptera

Shorea stenoptera var. stenoptera, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content. The plant typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and is valued for its durable, dark-colored timber. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
