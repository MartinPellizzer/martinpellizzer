Syzygium subrufum var. smalianum

Syzygium subrufum var. smalianum, commonly known as the red-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
