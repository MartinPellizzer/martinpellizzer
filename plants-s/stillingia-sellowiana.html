Stillingia sellowiana

Stillingia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian bleeding heart, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red heart-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Stillingia sellowiana is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
