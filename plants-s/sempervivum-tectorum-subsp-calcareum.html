Sempervivum tectorum subsp. calcareum

Sempervivum tectorum subsp.

calcareum, commonly known as the limestone houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found growing on walls or in crevices.

The plant has rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a powdery waxy coating. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
