Salvia lusitanica

Salvia lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
