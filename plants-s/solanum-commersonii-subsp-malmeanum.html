Solanum commersonii subsp. malmeanum

Solanum commersonii subsp.

malmeanum, commonly known as the Malmean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
