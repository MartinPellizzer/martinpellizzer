Silene ehrenbergiana

Silene ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Silene ehrenbergiana plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
