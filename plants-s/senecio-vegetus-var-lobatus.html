Senecio vegetus var. lobatus

Senecio vegetus var. lobatus, commonly known as the lobate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to thrive.
