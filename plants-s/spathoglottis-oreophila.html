Spathoglottis oreophila

Spathoglottis oreophila, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments at high elevations, often found in montane forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Spathoglottis oreophila is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
