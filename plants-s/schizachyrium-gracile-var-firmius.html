Schizachyrium gracile var. firmius

Schizachyrium gracile var. firmius, commonly known as stiff panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest in gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
