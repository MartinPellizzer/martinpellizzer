Sempervivum tectorum var. constrictum

Sempervivum tectorum var. constrictum, commonly known as the stonecrop or houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The leaves are often marked with a narrow, dark green stripe running along the midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This hardy plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
