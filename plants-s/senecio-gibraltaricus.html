Senecio gibraltaricus

Senecio gibraltaricus, commonly known as the Gibraltar groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of Gibraltar and southern Spain.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
