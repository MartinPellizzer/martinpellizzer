Symphyobasis macroplectra

Symphyobasis macroplectra, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant features large, broad leaves that are often a vibrant green, with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowering is rare, producing clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that attract various pollinators.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
