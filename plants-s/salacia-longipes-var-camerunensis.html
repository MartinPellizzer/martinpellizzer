Salacia longipes var. camerunensis

Salacia longipes var. camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon salacia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaves, and a distinct, aromatic scent.
