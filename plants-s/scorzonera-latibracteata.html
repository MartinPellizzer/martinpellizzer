Scorzonera latibracteata

Scorzonera latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots and distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Scorzonera latibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
