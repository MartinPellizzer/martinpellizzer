Senecio nemorensis var. apuanus

Senecio nemorensis var. apuanus, commonly known as the Apuan Alps groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Apuan Alps in Tuscany, Italy.

It is a low-growing perennial with silver-gray foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native region.
