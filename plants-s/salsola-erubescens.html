Salsola erubescens

Salsola erubescens, commonly known as blushing saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Salsola erubescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
