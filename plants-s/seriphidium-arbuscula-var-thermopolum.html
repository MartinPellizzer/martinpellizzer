Seriphidium arbuscula var. thermopolum

Seriphidium arbuscula var. thermopolum, commonly known as the Thermopolum dwarf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
