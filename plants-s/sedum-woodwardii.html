Sedum woodwardii

Sedum woodwardii, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Sedum woodwardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
