Syzygium torricellianum

Syzygium torricellianum, commonly known as the Torricellian apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, which is typically small and edible, has a mild, sweet flavor.

Syzygium torricellianum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
