Spergularia modesta var. salinaria

Spergularia modesta var. salinaria, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and western Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain water in arid conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is considered an indicator plant of coastal habitats.
