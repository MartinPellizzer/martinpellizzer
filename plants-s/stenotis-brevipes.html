Stenotis brevipes

Stenotis brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Stenotis brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
