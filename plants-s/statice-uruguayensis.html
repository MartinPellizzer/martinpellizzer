Statice uruguayensis

Statice uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, allowing it to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant features compact, bushy growth with numerous small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Statice uruguayensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
