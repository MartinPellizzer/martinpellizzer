Syngonanthus caulescens var. caulescens

Syngonanthus caulescens var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
