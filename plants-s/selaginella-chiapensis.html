Selaginella chiapensis

Selaginella chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan mossy clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the state of Chiapas, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with a creeping stem covered in small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are produced on specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella chiapensis is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and adaptation to tropical ecosystems.
