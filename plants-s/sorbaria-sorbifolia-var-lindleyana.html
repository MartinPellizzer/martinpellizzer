Sorbaria sorbifolia var. lindleyana

Sorbaria sorbifolia var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are small, red drupes that attract birds and are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
