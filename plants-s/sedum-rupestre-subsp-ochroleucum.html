Sedum rupestre subsp. ochroleucum

Sedum rupestre subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to Europe. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense rosettes.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
