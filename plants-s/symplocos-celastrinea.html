Symplocos celastrinea

Symplocos celastrinea, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Symplocos celastrinea is valued for its ornamental berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
