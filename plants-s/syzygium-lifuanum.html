Syzygium lifuanum

Syzygium lifuanum, commonly known as the Lifuan roseapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and produces a small, edible fruit that is similar in appearance to a lychee. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and reddish in color.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
