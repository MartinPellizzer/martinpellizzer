Stipa landbeckii

Stipa landbeckii, commonly known as the Landbeck needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, bract-like structures.

Stipa landbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
