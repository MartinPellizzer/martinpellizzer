Solanum bonariense f. intermedium

Solanum bonariense f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
