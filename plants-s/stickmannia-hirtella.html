Stickmannia hirtella

Stickmannia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy stickman plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by fine, thread-like hairs. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture in its dry habitat.

Stickmannia hirtella is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of its ecosystem.
