Sparganium simplex var. fluctuans

Sparganium simplex var. fluctuans, commonly known as the floating burr rush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating, spiky fruiting structures that resemble small, spiny balls, which help in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often submerged or floating at the water's surface.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
