Sonchus ledermannii

Sonchus ledermannii, commonly known as Ledermann's sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It features lobed, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
