Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. angustifolia

Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical features include a branching stem and small, rounded fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
