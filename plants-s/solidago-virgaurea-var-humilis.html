Solidago virgaurea var. humilis

Solidago virgaurea var. humilis, commonly known as the lesser goldenrod, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
