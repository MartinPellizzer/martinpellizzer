Stanhopea stenochila

Stanhopea stenochila, commonly known as the narrow-lipped stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of yellow, green, and red hues. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with fleshy leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually borne on erect inflorescences and bloom during the dry season.

Stanhopea stenochila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
