Sedum maximum var. glaucum

Sedum maximum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
