Stipa brachyglumis

Stipa brachyglumis, commonly known as the short-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soils.

Stipa brachyglumis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
