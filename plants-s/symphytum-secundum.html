Symphytum secundum

Symphytum secundum, commonly known as the lesser comfrey, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant contains allantoin, a compound known for its soothing properties, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Symphytum secundum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.
