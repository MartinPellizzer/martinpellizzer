Sphaerostephanos doodioides

Sphaerostephanos doodioides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves arranged in a compact rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
