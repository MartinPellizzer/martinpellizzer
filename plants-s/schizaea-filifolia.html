Schizaea filifolia

Schizaea filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaf spikerush, is a member of the family Schizaeaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a fern-like plant that lacks true leaves, instead having specialized structures called stolons and rhizomes for vegetative propagation. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spike-like formations. Schizaea filifolia typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an important indicator species for certain wetland habitats.
