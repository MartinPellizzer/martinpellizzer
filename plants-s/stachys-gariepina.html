Stachys gariepina

Stachys gariepina, commonly known as the Gariep knotted grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Stachys gariepina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
