Samara paniculata

Samara paniculata, commonly known as the silky willow or silky tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, silky hairs that cover its leaves and fruits, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, winged fruits called samaras, which aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture.

Samara paniculata is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions, often near water bodies.
