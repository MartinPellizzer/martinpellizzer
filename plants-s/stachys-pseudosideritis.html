Stachys pseudosideritis

Stachys pseudosideritis, commonly known as false ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Stachys pseudosideritis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
