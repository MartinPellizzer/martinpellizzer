Salsola heliaramiae

Salsola heliaramiae, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability. The plant features fleshy, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Salsola heliaramiae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is adapted to high levels of sunlight and temperature fluctuations.
