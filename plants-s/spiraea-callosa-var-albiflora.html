Spiraea callosa var. albiflora

Spiraea callosa var. albiflora, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, with a fine, hairy texture on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
