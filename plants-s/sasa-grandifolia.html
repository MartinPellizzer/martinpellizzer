Sasa grandifolia

Sasa grandifolia, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the nodes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Japanese culture.
