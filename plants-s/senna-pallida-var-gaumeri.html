Senna pallida var. gaumeri

Senna pallida var. gaumeri, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
