Stevia reinana

Stevia reinana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring compounds. Stevia reinana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.

Its leaves are often used as a natural sweetener and in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
