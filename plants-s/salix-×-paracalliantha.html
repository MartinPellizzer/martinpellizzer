Salix × paracalliantha

Salix × paracalliantha, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its flexible, slender branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. Salix × paracalliantha is often found in riparian zones, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species for erosion control and habitat restoration.
