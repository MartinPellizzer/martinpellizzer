Swartzia picta

Swartzia picta, commonly known as the pictured swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants. The tree is characterized by its striking, ornate flowers that range from white to pink and red, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain its seeds.

Swartzia picta is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
