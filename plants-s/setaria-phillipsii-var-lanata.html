Setaria phillipsii var. lanata

Setaria phillipsii var. lanata, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and spikelet clusters that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of bract-like structures that enclose the flowering parts, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
