Serratula nudicaulis subsp. demissa

Serratula nudicaulis subsp.

demissa, commonly known as the slender serrated wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
