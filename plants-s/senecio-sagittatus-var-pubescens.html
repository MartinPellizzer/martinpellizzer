Senecio sagittatus var. pubescens

Senecio sagittatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
