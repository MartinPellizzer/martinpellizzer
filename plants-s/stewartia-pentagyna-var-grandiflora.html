Stewartia pentagyna var. grandiflora

Stewartia pentagyna var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese Stewartia, is a deciduous flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, elongated leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, elliptical fruits.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
