Serratula heleniifolia

Serratula heleniifolia, commonly known as the Helen's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
