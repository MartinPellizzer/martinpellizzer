Stipa megapotamia

Stipa megapotamia, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and mountain slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa megapotamia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
