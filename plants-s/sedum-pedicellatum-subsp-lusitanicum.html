Sedum pedicellatum subsp. lusitanicum

Sedum pedicellatum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange.

The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to thrive in dry conditions.
