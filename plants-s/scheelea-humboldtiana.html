Scheelea humboldtiana

Scheelea humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
