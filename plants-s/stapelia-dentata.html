Stapelia dentata

Stapelia dentata, commonly known as the "Bishop's Cap" or "Coral Stapelia," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often brightly colored flowers that resemble coral or other marine organisms. The plant produces large, fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves that are covered in small, spine-like structures. Stapelia dentata is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its unique floral morphology and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
