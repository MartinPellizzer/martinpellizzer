Solanum sideroxyloides var. ocellatum

Solanum sideroxyloides var. ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, green berries with a reddish tint when mature.

Its berries contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and livestock.
