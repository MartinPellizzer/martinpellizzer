Schisandra marmorata

Schisandra marmorata, commonly known as the marble five-finger, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive marbled pattern on its leaves and fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are highly prized for their medicinal properties. Schisandra marmorata thrives in cool, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
