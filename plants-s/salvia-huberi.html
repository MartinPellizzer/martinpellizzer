Salvia huberi

Salvia huberi, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, minty aroma. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia huberi is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
