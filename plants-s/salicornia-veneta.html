Salicornia veneta

Salicornia veneta, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, and it relies on wind pollination.

Salicornia veneta is commonly found in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
