Socratea elegans

Socratea elegans, commonly known as the "socratea" or "giant cycad," is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the cycad family and is characterized by its stout, tree-like trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a thick, fibrous trunk that can grow up to several meters in height. It produces cones rather than flowers, and its reproductive structures are separate, with male and female cones on different plants.

Socratea elegans is also notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it an important component of the understory in tropical rainforests.
