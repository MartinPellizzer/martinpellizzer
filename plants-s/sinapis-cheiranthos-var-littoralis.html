Sinapis cheiranthos var. littoralis

Sinapis cheiranthos var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal mustard, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lobed and often have a distinctive mustard scent when crushed.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides nectar for pollinators.
