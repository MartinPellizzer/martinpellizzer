Senecio pentadactylus

Senecio pentadactylus, commonly known as the five-fingered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
