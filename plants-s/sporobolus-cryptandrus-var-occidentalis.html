Sporobolus cryptandrus var. occidentalis

Sporobolus cryptandrus var. occidentalis, commonly known as western sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
