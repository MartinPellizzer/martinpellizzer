Sorbus amurensis var. latifoliolata

Sorbus amurensis var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
