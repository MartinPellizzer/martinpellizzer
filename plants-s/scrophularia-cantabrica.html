Scrophularia cantabrica

Scrophularia cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence.

Scrophularia cantabrica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some species.
