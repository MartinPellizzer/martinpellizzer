Sesleria angustifolia

Sesleria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Sesleria angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
