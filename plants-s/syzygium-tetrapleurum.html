Syzygium tetrapleurum

Syzygium tetrapleurum, commonly known as the four-pleurum or the Australian satinash, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in rainforests and moist subtropical regions. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or purple as it matures.

Syzygium tetrapleurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
