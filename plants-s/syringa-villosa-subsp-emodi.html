Syringa villosa subsp. emodi

Syringa villosa subsp.

emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan sweetwood, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
