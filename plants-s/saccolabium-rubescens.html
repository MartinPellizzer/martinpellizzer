Saccolabium rubescens

Saccolabium rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Saccolabium rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
