Salvia fallax

Salvia fallax, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
