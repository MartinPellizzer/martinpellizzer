Serratula nishimurae

Serratula nishimurae, commonly known as the Nishimura's thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually serrated, giving it the genus name "Serratula.".

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
