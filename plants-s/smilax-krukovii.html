Smilax krukovii

Smilax krukovii, commonly known as the Krukov's smilax, is a species of climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth, which help it climb and protect itself from herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal umbels. Its fruits are bright red berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Smilax krukovii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to different soil conditions.
