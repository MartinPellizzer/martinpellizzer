Serjania hebecarpa f. platycephala

Serjania hebecarpa f. platycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed serjania, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Serjaniaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, tropical environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
