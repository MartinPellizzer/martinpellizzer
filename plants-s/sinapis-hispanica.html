Sinapis hispanica

Sinapis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Sinapis hispanica is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates.
