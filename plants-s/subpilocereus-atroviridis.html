Subpilocereus atroviridis

Subpilocereus atroviridis, commonly known as the black-tipped night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
