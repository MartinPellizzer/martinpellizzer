Statice patagonica

Statice patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian glassweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Statice patagonica is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, crisp petals.
