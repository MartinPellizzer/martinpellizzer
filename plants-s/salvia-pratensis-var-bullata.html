Salvia pratensis var. bullata

Salvia pratensis var. bullata, commonly known as the Bull's-eye sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that form a distinctive bull’s-eye pattern, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
