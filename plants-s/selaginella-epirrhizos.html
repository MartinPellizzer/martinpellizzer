Selaginella epirrhizos

Selaginella epirrhizos, commonly known as the clubmoss or spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are borne on the upper parts of the plant.

Selaginella epirrhizos is notable for its ability to survive in a variety of soil conditions and its historical significance in the study of plant evolution.
