Saponaria smithii

Saponaria smithii, commonly known as Smith's soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, woolly seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a somewhat succulent texture.

Saponaria smithii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
