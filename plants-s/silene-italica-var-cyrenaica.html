Silene italica var. cyrenaica

Silene italica var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaic campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This variety is often found in coastal and inland areas where it thrives in full sun.
