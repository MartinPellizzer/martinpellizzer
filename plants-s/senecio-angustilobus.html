Senecio angustilobus

Senecio angustilobus, commonly known as narrow-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
