Sapindus saponaria f. microcarpus

Sapindus saponaria f. microcarpus, commonly known as the soapberry tree or Chinese soapberry, is a variant of the soapberry tree native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a starchy, edible seed. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
