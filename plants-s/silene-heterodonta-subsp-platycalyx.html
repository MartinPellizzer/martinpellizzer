Silene heterodonta subsp. platycalyx

Silene heterodonta subsp.

platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its subspecies name, *platycalyx*, refers to its broad, flat calyx that encloses the flower buds.

It is a perennial herb with a short lifespan, blooming in late spring to early summer.
