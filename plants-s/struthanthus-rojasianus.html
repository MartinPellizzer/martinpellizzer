Struthanthus rojasianus

Struthanthus rojasianus, commonly known as the desert evening glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its unique blooming pattern and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
