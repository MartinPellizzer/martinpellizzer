Symphoricarpos albus var. albus

Symphoricarpos albus var. albus, commonly known as snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, spherical berries that are visually striking and often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
