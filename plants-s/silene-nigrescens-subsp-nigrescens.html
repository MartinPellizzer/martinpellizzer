Silene nigrescens subsp. nigrescens

Silene nigrescens subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is a herbaceous perennial with a low-growing habit, featuring dense clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Silene nigrescens subsp.

nigrescens is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity in high-altitude habitats.
