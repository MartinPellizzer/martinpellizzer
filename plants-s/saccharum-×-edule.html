Saccharum × edule

Saccharum × edule, commonly known as hybrid sugarcane, is a cross between Saccharum spontaneum and Saccharum officinarum.

It is a tall, perennial grass with long, fibrous stalks that contain high concentrations of sucrose. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample rainfall. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its stem is the primary source of sugar production.

Saccharum × edule is widely cultivated for its economic importance in the sugar and biofuel industries.
