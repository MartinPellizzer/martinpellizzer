Sedum muyaicum

Sedum muyaicum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum muyaicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to propagate through both seeds and stem cuttings.
