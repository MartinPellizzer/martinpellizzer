Salix zayunica

Salix zayunica, commonly known as the Yungui willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, winged seeds.

Salix zayunica is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in wetlands.
