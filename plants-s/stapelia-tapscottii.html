Stapelia tapscottii

Stapelia tapscottii, commonly known as the yellow star cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a distinctive yellow, star-shaped flower that blooms in late summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Stapelia tapscottii is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
