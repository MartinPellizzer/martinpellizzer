Salvia beckeri

Salvia beckeri, commonly known as Becker's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its blooming period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Salvia beckeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
