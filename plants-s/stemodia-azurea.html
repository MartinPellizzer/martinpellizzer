Stemodia azurea

Stemodia azurea, commonly known as bluestem or bluestem grass, is a perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often blue-green stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.
