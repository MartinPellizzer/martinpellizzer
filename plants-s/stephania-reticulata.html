Stephania reticulata

Stephania reticulata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
