Salacca glabrescens

Salacca glabrescens, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional thatching and basket-making.

Salacca glabrescens thrives in humid, warm climates and is well adapted to coastal environments.
