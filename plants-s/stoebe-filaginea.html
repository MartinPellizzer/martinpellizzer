Stoebe filaginea

Stoebe filaginea, commonly known as narrow-leaved devil's-bit scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.
