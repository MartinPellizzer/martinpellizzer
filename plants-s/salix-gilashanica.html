Salix gilashanica

Salix gilashanica, commonly known as the Gilashan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges or along stream banks.

The bark of Salix gilashanica is smooth and light gray, and it may have some medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
