Senecio tephrosioides

Senecio tephrosioides, commonly known as the Cape mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing capsules that aid in wind dispersal.

Senecio tephrosioides is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
