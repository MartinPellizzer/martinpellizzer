Smilax guianensis

Smilax guianensis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or Guianan smilacina, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing several seeds.

Smilax guianensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
