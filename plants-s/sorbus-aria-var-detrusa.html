Sorbus aria var. detrusa

Sorbus aria var. detrusa, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
