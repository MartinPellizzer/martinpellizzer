Seriphidium tenuisectum

Seriphidium tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has slender, branching stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
