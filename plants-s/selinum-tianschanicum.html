Selinum tianschanicum

Selinum tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tian Shan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its chemical composition includes various volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
