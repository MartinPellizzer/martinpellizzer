Syringa reticulata var. mandshurica

Syringa reticulata var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
