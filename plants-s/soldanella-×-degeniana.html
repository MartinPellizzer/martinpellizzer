Soldanella × degeniana

Soldanella × degeniana, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of 10–20 cm and features hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly observed in the wild.
