Solanum mexicanum var. albiflorum

Solanum mexicanum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
