Solenostemon chevalieri

Solenostemon chevalieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
