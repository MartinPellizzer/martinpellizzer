Syzygium contractum

Syzygium contractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a sweet, acidic pulp. Syzygium contractum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
