Solanum turneroides

Solanum turneroides, commonly known as Turner's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption or cultivation.
