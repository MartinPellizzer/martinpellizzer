Salix glauca var. pseudolapponum

Salix glauca var. pseudolapponum, commonly known as the white willow or grey willow, is a shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, grayish branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
