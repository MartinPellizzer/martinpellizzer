Sida caffra

Sida caffra, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sida caffra is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
