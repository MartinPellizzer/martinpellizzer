Syzygium subracemosum

Syzygium subracemosum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, spherical fruits that are yellow when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
