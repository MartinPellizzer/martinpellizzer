Saxifraga cinerascens

Saxifraga cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, gritty soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes.

Saxifraga cinerascens is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
