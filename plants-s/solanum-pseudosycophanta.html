Solanum pseudosycophanta

Solanum pseudosycophanta, commonly known as the red devil or red bush tomato, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, red, berry-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, oval-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
