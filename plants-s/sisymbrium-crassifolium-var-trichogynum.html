Sisymbrium crassifolium var. trichogynum

Sisymbrium crassifolium var. trichogynum, commonly known as the hairy female bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves, which are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in its native habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and it is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It is a short-lived perennial that thrives in temperate regions, particularly in Europe and parts of Asia.
