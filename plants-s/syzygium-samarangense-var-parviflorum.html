Syzygium samarangense var. parviflorum

Syzygium samarangense var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia, characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers and small, fragrant buds. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is closely related to the common clove tree, Syzygium aromaticum. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
