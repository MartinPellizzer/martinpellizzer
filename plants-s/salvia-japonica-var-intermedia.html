Salvia japonica var. intermedia

Salvia japonica var. intermedia, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
