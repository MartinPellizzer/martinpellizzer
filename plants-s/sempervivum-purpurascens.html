Sempervivum purpurascens

Sempervivum purpurascens, commonly known as the red-flowered houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountains of Europe.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish hue, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Sempervivum purpurascens is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, which allows it to spread easily in suitable environments.
