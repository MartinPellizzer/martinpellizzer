Salvia verbenaca var. oblongata

Salvia verbenaca var. oblongata, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
