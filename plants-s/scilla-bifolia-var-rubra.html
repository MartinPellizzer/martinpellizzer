Scilla bifolia var. rubra

Scilla bifolia var. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the base. Its bulbs are fleshy and store nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
