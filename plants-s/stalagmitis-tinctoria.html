Stalagmitis tinctoria

Stalagmitis tinctoria, commonly known as the dye-stalk plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescences that resemble miniature stalagmites, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces vividly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Stalagmitis tinctoria is also valued for its historical use in natural dye extraction, contributing to its cultural significance in traditional practices.
