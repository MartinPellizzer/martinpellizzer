Senecio indecorus f. burkei

Senecio indecorus f. burkei, commonly known as the Burke's ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a dark center. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
