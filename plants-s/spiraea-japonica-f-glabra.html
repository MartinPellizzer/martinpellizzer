Spiraea japonica f. glabra

Spiraea japonica f. glabra, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often green, with some cultivars displaying variegated leaves.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
