Salvia obtusa

Salvia obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Salvia obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
