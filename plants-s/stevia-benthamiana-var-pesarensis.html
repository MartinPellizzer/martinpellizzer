Stevia benthamiana var. pesarensis

Stevia benthamiana var. pesarensis, commonly known as the Pesaran stevia, is a rare variant of the stevia plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses due to its intense sweetness.
