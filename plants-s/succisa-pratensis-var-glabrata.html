Succisa pratensis var. glabrata

Succisa pratensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth meadow cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are pink or purple, arranged in terminal clusters, and bloom from June to August.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also a food source for various pollinators.
