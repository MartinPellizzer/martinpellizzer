Salsola zeyheri

Salsola zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's saltbush, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small animals.
