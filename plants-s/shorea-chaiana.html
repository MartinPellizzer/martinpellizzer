Shorea chaiana

Shorea chaiana, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically yellowish-green, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
