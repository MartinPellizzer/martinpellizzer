Silene vulgaris var. alpina

Silene vulgaris var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces pink or white flowers with five petals, arranged in a raceme, and has a distinctive capsule-like fruit that disperses its seeds. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate low light conditions.

Silene vulgaris var. alpina is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
