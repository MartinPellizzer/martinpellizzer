Senecio jacobaea var. pratensis

Senecio jacobaea var. pratensis, commonly known as the lesser ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are rough and lobed, with a distinctive blue-green color, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is considered invasive in some regions and can have toxic effects on livestock.
