Salix yadongensis

Salix yadongensis, commonly known as the Yadong poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, typical of willow species, and is adapted to temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
