Scepinia

Scepinia, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It has a distinctive appearance with its fleshy, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that can curl up when dry. The plant is known for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a dormant state. When watered, it can rapidly revive and bloom with vibrant pink or red flowers.

Its resilience and striking transformation make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and symbolic use in various cultures.
