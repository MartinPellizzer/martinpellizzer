Salsola turcica

Salsola turcica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. Native to Central Asia, it has been introduced to many regions around the world, often becoming an invasive species. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
