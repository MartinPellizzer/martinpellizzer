Schisandra nigra var. hypoglauca

Schisandra nigra var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale bluish schisandra, is a rare variant of the five-flavor schisandra.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its small, dark berries that contain a complex mixture of bioactive compounds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green surface.

This variant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and unique flavor profile.
