Stachys cretica subsp. smyrnaea

Stachys cretica subsp.

smyrnaea, commonly known as Smyrna betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
