Sedum magellense subsp. olympicum

Sedum magellense subsp.

olympicum, commonly known as Olympic stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid or rocky environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a hardy and resilient species.
