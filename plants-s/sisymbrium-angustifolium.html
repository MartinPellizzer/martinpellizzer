Sisymbrium angustifolium

Sisymbrium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its mustard oils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments.
