Selaginella valida

Selaginella valida, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its whorled arrangement of linear leaves and a short, thick stem. Selaginella valida reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions.

Its vascular system is well-developed, allowing it to efficiently transport water and nutrients.
