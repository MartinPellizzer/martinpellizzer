Senecio glossophyllus

Senecio glossophyllus, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio glossophyllus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
