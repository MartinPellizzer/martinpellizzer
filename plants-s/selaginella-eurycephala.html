Selaginella eurycephala

Selaginella eurycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid regions of North America.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually dark green and have a leathery texture. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with specialized structures that help retain moisture.

Selaginella eurycephala is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
