Salvia subspathulata

Salvia subspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Salvia subspathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
