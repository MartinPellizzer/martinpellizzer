Strobilanthes glomerata var. speciosa

Strobilanthes glomerata var. speciosa, commonly known as the "Clustered flameflower," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a slightly waxy appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
