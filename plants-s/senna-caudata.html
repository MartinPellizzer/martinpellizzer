Senna caudata

Senna caudata, commonly known as the golden senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds. Senna caudata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
