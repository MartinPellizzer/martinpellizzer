Syngonanthus umbellatus

Syngonanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Syngonanthus umbellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
