Salvia cordobensis

Salvia cordobensis, commonly known as Cordoba sage, is a perennial herb native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cordobensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
