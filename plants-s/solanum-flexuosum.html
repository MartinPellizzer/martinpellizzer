Solanum flexuosum

Solanum flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. Solanum flexuosum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family, known for its ecological and chemical significance.
