Spilotantha tonsijmii

Spilotantha tonsijmii, commonly known as the Tonsil Mountains daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
