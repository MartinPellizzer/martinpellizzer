Saussurea brachycephala

Saussurea brachycephala, commonly known as the short-headed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dark blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and densely packed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
