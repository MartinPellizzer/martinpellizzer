Solanum coibae

Solanum coibae, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate, hairy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Solanum coibae belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
