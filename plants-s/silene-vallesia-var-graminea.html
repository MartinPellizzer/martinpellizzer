Silene vallesia var. graminea

Silene vallesia var. graminea, commonly known as the grassy campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
