Smilax gracilior

Smilax gracilior, commonly known as slender greenbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features climbing stems with sharp spines and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax gracilior is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its thorny structure and persistent foliage.
