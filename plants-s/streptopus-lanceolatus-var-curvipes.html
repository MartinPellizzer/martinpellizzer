Streptopus lanceolatus var. curvipes

Streptopus lanceolatus var. curvipes, commonly known as the curly-stemmed dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features slender, often curling stems that are covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and it produces distinctive, elongated berries that ripen to a deep red.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
