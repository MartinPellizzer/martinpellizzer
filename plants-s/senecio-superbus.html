Senecio superbus

Senecio superbus, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Senecio superbus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
