Scutellaria zaprjagaevii

Scutellaria zaprjagaevii, commonly known as the Zaprjagaev's skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

Scutellaria zaprjagaevii is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
