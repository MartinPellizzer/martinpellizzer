Stachys caffra

Stachys caffra, commonly known as the African mint or redstem hedge.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Stachys caffra thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
