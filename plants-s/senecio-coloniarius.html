Senecio coloniarius

Senecio coloniarius, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio coloniarius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
