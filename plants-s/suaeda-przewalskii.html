Suaeda przewalskii

Suaeda przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and desert regions where it thrives in high-salinity soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Suaeda przewalskii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
