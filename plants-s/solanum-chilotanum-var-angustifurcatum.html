Solanum chilotanum var. angustifurcatum

Solanum chilotanum var. angustifurcatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with narrow, elongated lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical berries, which are typically yellow when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in temperate regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
