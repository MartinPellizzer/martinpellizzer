Succisa ambrosioides

Succisa ambrosioides, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often strongly scented leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils, particularly thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent highlights its botanical significance.
