Sesbania longifolia

Sesbania longifolia, commonly known as the longpod sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and elongated, slender pods that contain small, rounded seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Sesbania longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
