Sterculia xolocotzii

Sterculia xolocotzii, commonly known as the Mexican tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous ellipsoid seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
