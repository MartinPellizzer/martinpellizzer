Sisymbrium incisum var. sonnei

Sisymbrium incisum var. sonnei, commonly known as the Sonne's toothed mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive toothed margin.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
