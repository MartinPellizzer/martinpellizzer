Sideroxylon bancanum

Sideroxylon bancanum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, often grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, typically with a waxy surface.

Sideroxylon bancanum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in forest ecosystems.
