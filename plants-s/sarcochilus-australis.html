Sarcochilus australis

Sarcochilus australis, commonly known as the Australian tree fern, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like appearance with a central stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in rainforest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
