Solanum phyllanthum var. aberrans

Solanum phyllanthum var. aberrans, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is noted for its variability in morphology, which contributes to its common name.
