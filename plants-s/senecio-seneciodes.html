Senecio seneciodes

Senecio seneciodes, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
