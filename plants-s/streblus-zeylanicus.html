Streblus zeylanicus

Streblus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and musical instruments.

Additionally, it plays an important role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
