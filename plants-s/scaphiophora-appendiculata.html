Scaphiophora appendiculata

Scaphiophora appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or lavender in color, with a distinctive appendage at the base of the corolla. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Scaphiophora appendiculata is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display and ecological benefits.
