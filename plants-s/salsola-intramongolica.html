Salsola intramongolica

Salsola intramongolica, commonly known as Mongolian saltwort, is a halophytic plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert steppes and coastal areas. The plant has succulent leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its ability to tolerate extreme drought and high salinity makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Salsola intramongolica plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
