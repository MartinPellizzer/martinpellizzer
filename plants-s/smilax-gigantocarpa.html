Smilax gigantocarpa

Smilax gigantocarpa, commonly known as the giant arrowhead vine, is a striking member of the Smilacaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This climbing plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often with a waxy texture and a distinctive purple-tinged underside. Its stems are armed with sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in climbing and deterring herbivores. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax gigantocarpa is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
