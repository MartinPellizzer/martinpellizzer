Senecio bicolor var. plattii

Senecio bicolor var. plattii, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
