Senecio aschersonianus

Senecio aschersonianus, commonly known as the ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Senecio aschersonianus is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
