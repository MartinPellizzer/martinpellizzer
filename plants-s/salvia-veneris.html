Salvia veneris

Salvia veneris, commonly known as the sacred sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia veneris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in herbal gardens.
