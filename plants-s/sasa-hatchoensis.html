Sasa hatchoensis

Sasa hatchoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hachioji bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant forms stout, cylindrical culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Sasa hatchoensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall aesthetic and functional versatility.
