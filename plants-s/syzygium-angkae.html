Syzygium angkae

Syzygium angkae, commonly known as the Kampot red ginger, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the coastal regions of southern Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright red, showy flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy green surface.

Syzygium angkae is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
