Saccharum koenigii

Saccharum koenigii, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in various cultural practices.

Saccharum koenigii is also used in some culinary applications, though it is not commonly consumed as food.
