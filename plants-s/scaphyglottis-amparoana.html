Scaphyglottis amparoana

Scaphyglottis amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and clustered, showy flowers. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive hooded shape that gives the genus its name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
