Symplocos flavifolia

Symplocos flavifolia, commonly known as the yellow-flowered woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple branches and a dense, compact form.

Symplocos flavifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist, shady areas.
