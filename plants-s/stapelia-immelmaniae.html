Stapelia immelmaniae

Stapelia immelmaniae, commonly known as the Immelmania stapelioid, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers emit a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators like flies.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
