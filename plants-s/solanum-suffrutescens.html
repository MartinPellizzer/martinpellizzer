Solanum suffrutescens

Solanum suffrutescens, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum suffrutescens is known for its bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
