Spilanthes nervosa

Spilanthes nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed devil's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Spilanthes nervosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
