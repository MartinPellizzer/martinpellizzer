Sericographis aurantiaca

Sericographis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

Sericographis aurantiaca is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
