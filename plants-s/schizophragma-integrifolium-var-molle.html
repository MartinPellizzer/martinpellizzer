Schizophragma integrifolium var. molle

Schizophragma integrifolium var. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the summer months. Its berries are small, dark blue to black, and serve as a food source for various birds and wildlife.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
