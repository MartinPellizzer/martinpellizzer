Stachys germanica subsp. salviifolia

Stachys germanica subsp.

salviifolia, commonly known as purple betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
