Salix stylaris var. undulata

Salix stylaris var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
