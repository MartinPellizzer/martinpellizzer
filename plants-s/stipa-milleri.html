Stipa milleri

Stipa milleri, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, silver-blue foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Stipa milleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
