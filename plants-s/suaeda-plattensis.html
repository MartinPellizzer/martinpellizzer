Suaeda plattensis

Suaeda plattensis, commonly known as the saltbush or desert shrub, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in dry soils.

Suaeda plattensis is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and desert margins, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.
