Setaria italica var. macrostachya

Setaria italica var. macrostachya, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail. The plant produces small, pale green to golden seeds that are rich in carbohydrates and protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy crop for arid climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
