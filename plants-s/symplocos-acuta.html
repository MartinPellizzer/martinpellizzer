Symplocos acuta

Symplocos acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf sandpaper bush, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Symplocos acuta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions.
