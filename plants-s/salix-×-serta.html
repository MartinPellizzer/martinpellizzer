Salix × serta

Salix × serta, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and may exhibit a reddish or grayish hue, depending on the variety.

Salix × serta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
