Siphocampylus sanguineus

Siphocampylus sanguineus, commonly known as the redstem gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems that are typically red-tinged, especially when mature, and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Siphocampylus sanguineus is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
