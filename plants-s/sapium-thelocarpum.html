Sapium thelocarpum

Sapium thelocarpum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by yellow-orange fruits that split open to release fluffy seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
