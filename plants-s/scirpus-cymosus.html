Scirpus cymosus

Scirpus cymosus, commonly known as the tufted bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. Scirpus cymosus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
