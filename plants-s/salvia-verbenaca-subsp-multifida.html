Salvia verbenaca subsp. multifida

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

multifida, commonly known as the Narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
