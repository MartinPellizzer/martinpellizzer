Sedum pinetorum

Sedum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum pinetorum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
