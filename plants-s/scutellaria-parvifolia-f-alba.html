Scutellaria parvifolia f. alba

Scutellaria parvifolia f. alba, commonly known as white small-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its small, white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to parts of eastern North America, it typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces its flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
