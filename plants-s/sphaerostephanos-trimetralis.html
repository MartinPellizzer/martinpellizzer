Sphaerostephanos trimetralis

Sphaerostephanos trimetralis, commonly known as the golden globe bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
