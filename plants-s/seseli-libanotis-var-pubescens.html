Seseli libanotis var. pubescens

Seseli libanotis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Lebanon sesame, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a pubescent texture, contributing to its overall hairy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, dainty look.
