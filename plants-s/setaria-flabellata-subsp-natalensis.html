Setaria flabellata subsp. natalensis

Setaria flabellata subsp.

natalensis, commonly known as Natal fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense tufts or clumps.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the genus Setaria. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its glabrous leaves and the presence of bracts that are often shorter than the spikelets.
