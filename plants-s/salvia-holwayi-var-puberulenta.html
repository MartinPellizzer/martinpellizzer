Salvia holwayi var. puberulenta

Salvia holwayi var. puberulenta, commonly known as the pubescent sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
