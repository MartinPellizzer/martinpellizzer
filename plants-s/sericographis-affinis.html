Sericographis affinis

Sericographis affinis, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silky white coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for native birds. Sericographis affinis is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in warm, dry climates.
