Scaphochlamys gracilipes

Scaphochlamys gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-winged orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its unique morphology includes a distinctive scaphochlamys, a specialized structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
