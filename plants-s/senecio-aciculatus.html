Senecio aciculatus

Senecio aciculatus, commonly known as the needleleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Senecio aciculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
