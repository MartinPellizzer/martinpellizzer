Silene cerastoides var. tridentata

Silene cerastoides var. tridentata, commonly known as the three-lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lobed, giving it the common name, and it has a distinctive seed capsule that splits open when mature.

The species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
