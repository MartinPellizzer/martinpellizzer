Seridia sonchifolia

Seridia sonchifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed at the base. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and it produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Seridia sonchifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
