Stachys grahamii

Stachys grahamii, commonly known as Graham's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
