Senecio streptanthifolius var. laetiflorus

Senecio streptanthifolius var. laetiflorus, commonly known as the showy senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
