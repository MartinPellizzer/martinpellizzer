Sitopsis bicornis

Sitopsis bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-horned calyx that resembles the shape of a small bell. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals that are slightly reflexed at the edges.

Sitopsis bicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
