Setaria polygonata

Setaria polygonata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-hued leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
