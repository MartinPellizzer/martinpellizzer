Sesbania sesban

Sesbania sesban, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Sesbania sesban is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it suitable for use in reforestation and soil improvement projects.
