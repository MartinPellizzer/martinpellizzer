Schoenomorphus capitatus

Schoenomorphus capitatus, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive inflorescence.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often glaucous in appearance, and its flowering heads are compact, with yellowish to golden spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is found in wetlands and marshy areas. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
