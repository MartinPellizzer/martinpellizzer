Sorbus lushanensis

Sorbus lushanensis, commonly known as the Lushan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
