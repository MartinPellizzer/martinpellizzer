Setaria erythraeae

Setaria erythraeae, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish tinge at the base.

Setaria erythraeae is often found in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
