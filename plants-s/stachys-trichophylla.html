Stachys trichophylla

Stachys trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys trichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
