Spiraea crenifolia var. parvifolia

Spiraea crenifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is prized for its delicate foliage and graceful, cascading form.
