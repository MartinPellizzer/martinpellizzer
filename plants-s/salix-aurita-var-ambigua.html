Salix aurita var. ambigua

Salix aurita var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a bluish-green hue on the upper surface. This plant thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it can establish dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant, often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
