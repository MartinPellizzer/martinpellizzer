Streptocarpus × cantabrigiensis

Streptocarpus × cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge streptocarpus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Streptocarpus clevelandii and Streptocarpus sect.

Streptocarpus. It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features upright, branching stems and ovate, waxy leaves that are often hairy.

Native to South Africa, it is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with moderate light.
