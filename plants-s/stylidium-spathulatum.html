Stylidium spathulatum

Stylidium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, sun-like appearance. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces striking, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Stylidium spathulatum is an ephemeral plant, flowering after rainfall and often dying back during dry periods.
