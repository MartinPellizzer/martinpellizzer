Sorghum polyptychon

Sorghum polyptychon, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking red or purple coloration in its leaves and bracts. The plant is often cultivated for its edible grains, which are used in various food products. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions with limited agricultural resources.
