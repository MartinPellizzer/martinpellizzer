Syzygium calyptrocalyx

Syzygium calyptrocalyx, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
