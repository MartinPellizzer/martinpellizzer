Sasa ramosissima

Sasa ramosissima, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height, with a dark green to almost black coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense thickets.
