Smilax discolor

Smilax discolor, commonly known as the red-veined smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with red veins that contrast with the green foliage. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Smilax discolor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common sight in the underbrush of its native habitat.
