Stipa hohenackeriana subsp. ordubadica

Stipa hohenackeriana subsp.

ordubadica, commonly known as the Ordubad needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awned spikelets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
