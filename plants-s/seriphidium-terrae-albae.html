Seriphidium terrae-albae

Seriphidium terrae-albae, commonly known as the white soil wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Seriphidium terrae-albae is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
