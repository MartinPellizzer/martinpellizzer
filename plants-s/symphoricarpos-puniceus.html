Symphoricarpos puniceus

Symphoricarpos puniceus, commonly known as the fireberry or redcurrant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that appear in late summer. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Symphoricarpos puniceus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.
