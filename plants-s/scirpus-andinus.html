Scirpus andinus

Scirpus andinus, commonly known as the Andean bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies.

Scirpus andinus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
