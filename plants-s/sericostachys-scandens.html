Sericostachys scandens

Sericostachys scandens, commonly known as the climbing grass or silver grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often twine around other vegetation. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sericostachys scandens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
