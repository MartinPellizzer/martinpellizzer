Syzygium havilandii

Syzygium havilandii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters tall.

Syzygium havilandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic beauty.
