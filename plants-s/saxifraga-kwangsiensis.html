Saxifraga kwangsiensis

Saxifraga kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Guangxi region.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
