Symplocos barbacenae

Symplocos barbacenae, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos barbacenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
