Senecio viejensis

Senecio viejensis, commonly known as the Viejones groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Senecio viejensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
