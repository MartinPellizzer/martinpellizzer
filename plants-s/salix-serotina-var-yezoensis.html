Salix serotina var. yezoensis

Salix serotina var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo willow, is a shrub native to Japan and parts of eastern Russia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like inflorescences in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a silvery underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
