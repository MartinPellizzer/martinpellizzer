Sunipia scariosa

Sunipia scariosa, commonly known as the scaly sunipia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a distinctive waxy, scaly coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
