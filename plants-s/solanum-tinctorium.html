Solanum tinctorium

Solanum tinctorium, commonly known as the groundcherry or yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and dyeing practices. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Solanum tinctorium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique fruiting habit.
