Seriola taraxacifolia

Seriola taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion-like seriola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, spoon-shaped leaves that resemble those of the dandelion. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the genus. It is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
