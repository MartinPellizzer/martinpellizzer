Solidago correllii

Solidago correllii, commonly known as the Correll's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of golden flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago correllii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
