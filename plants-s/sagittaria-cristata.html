Sagittaria cristata

Sagittaria cristata, commonly known as the crowned arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dark berries that are dispersed by water.

Sagittaria cristata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
