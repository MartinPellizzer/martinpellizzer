Sisymbrium bourgaeanum

Sisymbrium bourgaeanum, commonly known as the Bourgaon mustard, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The seeds are small, oblong, and yellowish, typically maturing in late summer.
