Senecio thomsianus

Senecio thomsianus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which attract various pollinators.

Senecio thomsianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
