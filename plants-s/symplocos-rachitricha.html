Symplocos rachitricha

Symplocos rachitricha, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Symplocos rachitricha thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
