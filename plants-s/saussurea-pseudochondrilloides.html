Saussurea pseudochondrilloides

Saussurea pseudochondrilloides, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow, hairy, and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Saussurea pseudochondrilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
