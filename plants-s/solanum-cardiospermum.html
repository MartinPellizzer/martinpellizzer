Solanum cardiospermum

Solanum cardiospermum, commonly known as the "love apple" or "heartseed," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped seeds. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans but are used in traditional medicine in some regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy flowers that attract pollinators.
