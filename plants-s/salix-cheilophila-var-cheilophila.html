Salix cheilophila var. cheilophila

Salix cheilophila var. cheilophila, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and along streams. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine down. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
