Scirpus pedicellatus f. viviparus

Scirpus pedicellatus f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush native to wetland environments in North America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. One of its most distinctive features is the presence of viviparous shoots, which are small, leaf-like structures that develop at the base of the plant and can root and grow into new plants. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
