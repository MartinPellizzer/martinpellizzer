Strumaria truncata

Strumaria truncata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Strumaria truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
