Scirpus benghalensis

Scirpus benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian bulrush, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant thrives in marshy environments and is often found in shallow water bodies such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form extensive underwater networks, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus benghalensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
