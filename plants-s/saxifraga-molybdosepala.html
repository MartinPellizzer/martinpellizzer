Saxifraga molybdosepala

Saxifraga molybdosepala, commonly known as the lead-sepaled saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its scientific name reflects the presence of lead-like structures in its sepals, a unique morphological feature.

Saxifraga molybdosepala is often found in high-altitude meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations.
