Sida crassifolia

Sida crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Sida crassifolia is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its adaptability to different environments.
