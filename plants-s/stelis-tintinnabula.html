Stelis tintinnabula

Stelis tintinnabula, commonly known as the bell-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, bell-like flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers bloom in clusters.

Stelis tintinnabula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
