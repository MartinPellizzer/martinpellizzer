Sasa borealis subsp. viridescens

Sasa borealis subsp.

viridescens, commonly known as the northern bamboo or greenish sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant has slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silky hair. It reproduces through rhizomes, spreading vegetatively to form extensive colonies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
