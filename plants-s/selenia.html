Selenia

Selenia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. Selenia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular choice for moonlit gardens.
