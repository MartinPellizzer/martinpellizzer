Syzygium leonhardii

Syzygium leonhardii, commonly known as the Lombok apple or Lombok cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian island of Lombok.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red-orange fruits resembling small apples.

Syzygium leonhardii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.
