Senecio iloensis

Senecio iloensis, commonly known as the Iloensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and somewhat hairy.

Senecio iloensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
