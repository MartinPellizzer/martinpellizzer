Sorbus hungarica

Sorbus hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in woodland edges, rocky soils, and mountainous regions.

Sorbus hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
