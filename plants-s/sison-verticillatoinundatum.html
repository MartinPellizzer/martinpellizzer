Sison verticillatoinundatum

Sison verticillatoinundatum, commonly known as the spiral-leaved water plant, is a aquatic species characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender stems that often root at the base, while its leaves are narrow and arranged in a whorled pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
