Scirpus filiformis

Scirpus filiformis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetlands.

Scirpus filiformis is an important component of marsh ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
