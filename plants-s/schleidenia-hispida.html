Schleidenia hispida

Schleidenia hispida, commonly known as the rough Schleidenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences and are usually greenish-white in color.

Schleidenia hispida is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
