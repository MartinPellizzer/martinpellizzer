Sasa kuntaensis

Sasa kuntaensis, commonly known as the Kunta grass, is a perennial bamboo-like plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Sasa kuntaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
