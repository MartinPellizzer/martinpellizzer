Selaginella apus

Selaginella apus, commonly known as the common spike moss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and upright, spore-bearing cones. Its leaves are usually scale-like and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Selaginella apus is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
