Symplocos platyphylla var. subserrata

Symplocos platyphylla var. subserrata, commonly known as the serrated waxleaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically smooth but have subtle serrations along the edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
