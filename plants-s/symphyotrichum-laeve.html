Symphyotrichum laeve

Symphyotrichum laeve, commonly known as smooth coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant features smooth, lance-shaped leaves and a conical, pinkish-purple flower head. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Smooth coneflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
