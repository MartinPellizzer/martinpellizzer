Salvia shaofengkouensis

Salvia shaofengkouensis, commonly known as the Shaofengkou sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and it blooms during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
