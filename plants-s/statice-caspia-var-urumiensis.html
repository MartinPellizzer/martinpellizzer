Statice caspia var. urumiensis

Statice caspia var. urumiensis, commonly known as the Urumieh statice, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.
