Silene densiflora

Silene densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and sagebrush steppe. Silene densiflora produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its native environment.
