Saxicolella ijim

Saxicolella ijim, commonly known as the rock-dwelling moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in rocky and shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and dark green to reddish-brown foliage. The plant lacks true leaves but has leaf-like structures called phyllidia that are arranged in two rows. It reproduces via spores, which are released from capsules that develop on the stems.

Saxicolella ijim is often found in high-altitude regions and is adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
