Senecio ambiguus subsp. taygeteus

Senecio ambiguus subsp.

taygeteus, commonly known as the Colorado blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk florets, with linear, gray-green leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed soils.
