Solaria atropurpurea

Solaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 2 meters tall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Solaria atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
