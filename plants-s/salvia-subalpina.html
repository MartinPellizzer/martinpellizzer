Salvia subalpina

Salvia subalpina, commonly known as alpine sage, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
