Sempervivum micranthes

Sempervivum micranthes, commonly known as the common houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Sempervivum micranthes is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.
