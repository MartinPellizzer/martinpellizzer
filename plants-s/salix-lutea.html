Salix lutea

Salix lutea, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
