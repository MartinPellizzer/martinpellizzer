Strobilanthes congesta

Strobilanthes congesta, commonly known as the "Kerala flame flower," is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. These flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.

Strobilanthes congesta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
