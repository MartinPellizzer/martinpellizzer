Scirpus drummondii

Scirpus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has tall, cylindrical stems with narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Scirpus drummondii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
