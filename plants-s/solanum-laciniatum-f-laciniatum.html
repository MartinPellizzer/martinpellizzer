Solanum laciniatum f. laciniatum

Solanum laciniatum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are typically small, yellow berries that mature in the fall.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
