Sorghastrum viride

Sorghastrum viride, commonly known as green lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas, contributing to ecological diversity.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in landscaping and restoration projects.
