Sigesbeckia gracilis

Sigesbeckia gracilis, commonly known as slender sigesbeckia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides or in wetlands.

Sigesbeckia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
