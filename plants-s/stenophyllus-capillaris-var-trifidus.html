Stenophyllus capillaris var. trifidus

Stenophyllus capillaris var. trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed moss, is a rare and specialized variety of moss found in specific microhabitats.

It exhibits a unique morphology characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaf structure, which distinguishes it from other members of the Stenophyllus genus. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often on decaying wood or rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many mosses, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
