Sterculia indica

Sterculia indica, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or wild chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces edible, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in moist, tropical or subtropical regions.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
