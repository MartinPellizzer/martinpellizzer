Scrophularia macrobotrys

Scrophularia macrobotrys, commonly known as the Chinese toadflax or the broad-leaved scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scrophularia macrobotrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
