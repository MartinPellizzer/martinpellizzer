Schoenus punctatus

Schoenus punctatus, commonly known as the punctate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in small, compact groups.

Schoenus punctatus typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
