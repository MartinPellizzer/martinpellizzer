Schima multibracteata

Schima multibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese teak or red-flowered schima, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, showy red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Schima multibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
