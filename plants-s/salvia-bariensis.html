Salvia bariensis

Salvia bariensis, commonly known as the sacred basil or holy basil, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces vibrant violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Salvia bariensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
