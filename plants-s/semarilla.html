Semarilla

Semarilla, commonly known as the "red bean tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a leguminous species that produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisine and have nutritional value.
