Syzygium urceolatum subsp. palembanicum

Syzygium urceolatum subsp.

palembanicum, commonly known as the Palembang tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
