Silene expansa

Silene expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene expansa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for early successional habitats.
