Scleria baldwinii var. costata

Scleria baldwinii var. costata, commonly known as the ribbed flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive ribbed texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Scleria baldwinii var. costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
