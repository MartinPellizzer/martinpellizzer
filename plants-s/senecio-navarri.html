Senecio navarri

Senecio navarri, commonly known as the Navarre groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Senecio navarri is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
