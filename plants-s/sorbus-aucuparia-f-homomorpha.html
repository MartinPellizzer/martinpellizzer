Sorbus aucuparia f. homomorpha

Sorbus aucuparia f. homomorpha, commonly known as the white-berried rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in mountainous regions. It produces small, serrated leaves and is typically found in acidic soils.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive fruit color.
