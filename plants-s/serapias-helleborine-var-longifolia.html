Serapias helleborine var. longifolia

Serapias helleborine var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces erect, branched stems that bear the showy inflorescences. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
