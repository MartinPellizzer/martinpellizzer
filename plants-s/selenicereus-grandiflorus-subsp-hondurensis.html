Selenicereus grandiflorus subsp. hondurensis

Selenicereus grandiflorus subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves.

The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking blooms and unique flowering habit, it is a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
