Scabiosa tatarica

Scabiosa tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian clover or mountain scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Scabiosa tatarica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
