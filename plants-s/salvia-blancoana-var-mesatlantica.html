Salvia blancoana var. mesatlantica

Salvia blancoana var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
