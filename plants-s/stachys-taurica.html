Stachys taurica

Stachys taurica, commonly known as Taurian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Stachys taurica is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
