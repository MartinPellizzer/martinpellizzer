Senecio spatulifolius

Senecio spatulifolius, commonly known as the spoon-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
