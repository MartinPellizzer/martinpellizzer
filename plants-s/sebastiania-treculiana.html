Sebastiania treculiana

Sebastiania treculiana, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Sebastiania treculiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
