Sutera brunnea var. macrophylla

Sutera brunnea var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed sutera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and conservation efforts.
