Styloglossum clavatum

Styloglossum clavatum, commonly known as the club-stalked styloglossum, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, club-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
