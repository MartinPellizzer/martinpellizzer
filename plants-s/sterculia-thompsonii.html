Sterculia thompsonii

Sterculia thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitats.
