Saussurea angustifolia var. yukonensis

Saussurea angustifolia var. yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon saffron, is a perennial herb native to the Yukon and Alaska.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers. The plant produces fibrous roots and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in alpine and subarctic environments, growing in well-drained soils at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
