Sasaella tiutaroana

Sasaella tiutaroana, commonly known as the Tiutaroan bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tutiara in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form thickets in its natural habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
