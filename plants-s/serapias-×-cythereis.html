Serapias × cythereis

Serapias × cythereis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias acaulis and Serapias orchidioides.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded labellum, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils.

Serapias × cythereis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
