Swartzia subspicata

Swartzia subspicata, commonly known as the redwood tree or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Swartzia subspicata is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
