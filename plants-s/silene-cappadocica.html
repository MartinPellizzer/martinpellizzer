Silene cappadocica

Silene cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene cappadocica is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
