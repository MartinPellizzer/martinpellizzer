Sageraea grandiflora

Sageraea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sagera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Sageraea grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
