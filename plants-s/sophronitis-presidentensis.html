Sophronitis presidentensis

Sophronitis presidentensis, commonly known as the President's Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sophronitis presidentensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
