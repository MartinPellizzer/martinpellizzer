Syzygium brassii

Syzygium brassii, commonly known as the mountain satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid forests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium brassii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
