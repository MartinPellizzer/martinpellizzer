Sebaea micrantha var. intermedia

Sebaea micrantha var. intermedia, commonly known as the common sebaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to dry climates. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscapes.
