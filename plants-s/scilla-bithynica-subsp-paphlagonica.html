Scilla bithynica subsp. paphlagonica

Scilla bithynica subsp.

paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to alpine gardens and rockeries.
