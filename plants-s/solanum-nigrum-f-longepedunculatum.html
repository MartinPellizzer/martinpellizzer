Solanum nigrum f. longepedunculatum

Solanum nigrum f. longepedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate black nightshade, is a botanical variant of the common black nightshade.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the inflorescence. The plant typically features small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
