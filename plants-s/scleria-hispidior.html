Scleria hispidior

Scleria hispidior, commonly known as the hairy Scleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Scleria hispidior produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering during the summer months.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along riverbanks.
