Salix dissa f. angustifolia

Salix dissa f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of pollen dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
