Salvia carnosa var. pilosa

Salvia carnosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
