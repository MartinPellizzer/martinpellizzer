Solanum megalochiton

Solanum megalochiton, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum megalochiton produces berries that are toxic to humans but are used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
