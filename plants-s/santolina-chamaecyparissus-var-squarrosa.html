Santolina chamaecyparissus var. squarrosa

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with a fluffy texture, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are typically borne in clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves and compact growth make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
