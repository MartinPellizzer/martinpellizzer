Sorghum halepense subvar. aristatum

Sorghum halepense subvar. aristatum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both dry and semi-arid conditions.
