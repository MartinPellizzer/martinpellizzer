Sagraea ossaeiformis

Sagraea ossaeiformis, commonly known as the bone-shaped sagraea, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble bones, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white and green.

Due to its rare and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
