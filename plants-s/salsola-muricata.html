Salsola muricata

Salsola muricata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is a perennial shrub that is native to Eurasia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a robust root system and produces small, green to reddish leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Salsola muricata is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
