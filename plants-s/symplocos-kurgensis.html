Symplocos kurgensis

Symplocos kurgensis, commonly known as the Kurgan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Symplocos kurgensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
