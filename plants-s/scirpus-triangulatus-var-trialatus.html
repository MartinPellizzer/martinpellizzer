Scirpus triangulatus var. trialatus

Scirpus triangulatus var. trialatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It features slender, triangular stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense stands along the edges of ponds and marshes.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
