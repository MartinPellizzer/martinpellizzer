Senecio adenophylloides

Senecio adenophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
