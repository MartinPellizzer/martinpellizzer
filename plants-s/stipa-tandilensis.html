Stipa tandilensis

Stipa tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil needlegrass, is a species of grass native to Argentina.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa tandilensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native environment.
