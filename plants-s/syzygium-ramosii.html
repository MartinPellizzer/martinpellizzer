Syzygium ramosii

Syzygium ramosii, commonly known as the purple-leafed tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark purple leaves that contrast with its fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Syzygium ramosii thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
