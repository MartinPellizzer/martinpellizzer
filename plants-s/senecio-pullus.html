Senecio pullus

Senecio pullus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple or pink flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a conical shape.

Senecio pullus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
