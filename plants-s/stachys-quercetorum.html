Stachys quercetorum

Stachys quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, often resembling the shape of oak leaves, hence its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Stachys quercetorum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
