Schultesia aptera var. aptera

Schultesia aptera var. aptera, commonly known as the smooth schultesia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairless. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
