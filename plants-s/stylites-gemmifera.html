Stylites gemmifera

Stylites gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmed stalk plant, is a unique species characterized by its elongated, unbranched stem that bears clusters of small, gemmae-like structures at the apex.

The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water availability. Its growth form allows it to conserve moisture and reduce exposure to harsh environmental conditions. The gemmae produced by Stylites gemmifera are capable of vegetative reproduction, enabling the plant to propagate efficiently in its challenging habitat.

This adaptation makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
