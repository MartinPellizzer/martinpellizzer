Syzygium brevicymum

Syzygium brevicymum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental purposes and local culinary use.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
