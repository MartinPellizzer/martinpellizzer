Senecio stapeliiformis

Senecio stapeliiformis, commonly known as the elephant's foot or stapeliopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble an elephant's foot, which store water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
