Salacia pengheensis

Salacia pengheensis, commonly known as the Pengheng salacia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
