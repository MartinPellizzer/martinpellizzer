Selaginella imbricans

Selaginella imbricans, commonly known as the stair-step moss or zigzag spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper part of the plant and contain spores.

Selaginella imbricans is notable for its ability to survive in a variety of soil types and its use in horticulture as an ornamental plant.
