Somera italica

Somera italica, commonly known as the Italian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

The botanical name reflects its historical association with the Italian region.
