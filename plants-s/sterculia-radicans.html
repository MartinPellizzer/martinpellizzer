Sterculia radicans

Sterculia radicans, commonly known as the climbing sterculia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 20 meters in length, featuring compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
