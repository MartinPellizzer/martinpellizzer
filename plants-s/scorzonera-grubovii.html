Scorzonera grubovii

Scorzonera grubovii, commonly known as the Caucasian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals. Scorzonera grubovii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible roots.
