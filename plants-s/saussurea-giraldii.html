Saussurea giraldii

Saussurea giraldii, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional Tibetan medicine for various ailments.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
