Sisymbrium magellanicum f. hispidum

Sisymbrium magellanicum f. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its "hispid" designation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It is native to South America and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
