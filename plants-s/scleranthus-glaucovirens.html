Scleranthus glaucovirens

Scleranthus glaucovirens, commonly known as the glaucous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish or grayish hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus glaucovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
