Senecio muehlenbergii

Senecio muehlenbergii, commonly known as the blueweed or blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Senecio muehlenbergii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
