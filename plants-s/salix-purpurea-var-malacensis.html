Salix purpurea var. malacensis

Salix purpurea var. malacensis, commonly known as the purple willow variant Malacensis, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its main floral structures, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. It is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
