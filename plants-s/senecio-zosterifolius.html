Senecio zosterifolius

Senecio zosterifolius, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
