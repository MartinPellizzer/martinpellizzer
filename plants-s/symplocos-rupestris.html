Symplocos rupestris

Symplocos rupestris, commonly known as the rock symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red drupes that are edible.

Symplocos rupestris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
