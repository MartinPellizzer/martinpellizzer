Silene diffusa

Silene diffusa, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Silene diffusa is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
