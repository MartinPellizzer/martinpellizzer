Stemmatella urticifolia var. eglandulosa

Stemmatella urticifolia var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular stemmatella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features a slender, erect stem that is often covered with small, glandular hairs. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
