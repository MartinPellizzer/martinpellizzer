Scleranthus syvaschicus

Scleranthus syvaschicus, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered with stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Scleranthus syvaschicus is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in dry, rocky soils and cold climates.
