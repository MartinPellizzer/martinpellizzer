Swertia perennis subsp. cuspidata

Swertia perennis subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
