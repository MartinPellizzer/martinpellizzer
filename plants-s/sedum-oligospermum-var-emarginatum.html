Sedum oligospermum var. emarginatum

Sedum oligospermum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the Emarginate Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
