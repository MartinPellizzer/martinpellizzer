Stipa eriopus

Stipa eriopus, commonly known as the threadgrass or slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Stipa eriopus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for landscaping and restoration projects.
