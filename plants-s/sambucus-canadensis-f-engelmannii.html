Sambucus canadensis f. engelmannii

Sambucus canadensis f. engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann elderberry, is a subspecies of the American elderberry.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, which are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. It is native to the central and northern United States and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and berries.
