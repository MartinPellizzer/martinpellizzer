Solidago tarda

Solidago tarda, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, cylindrical clusters at the top of the plant.
