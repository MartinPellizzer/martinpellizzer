Synsepalum dulcificum

Synsepalum dulcificum, commonly known as the kola nut tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain the aromatic kola nuts, which are rich in caffeine and other alkaloids. These nuts have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its economic and cultural significance.
