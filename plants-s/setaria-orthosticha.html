Setaria orthosticha

Setaria orthosticha, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
