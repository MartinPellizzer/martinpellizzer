Sinapis dichotoma

Sinapis dichotoma, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring a stout, branching stem. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Sinapis dichotoma is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
