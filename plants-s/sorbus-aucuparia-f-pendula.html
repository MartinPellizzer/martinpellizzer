Sorbus aucuparia f. pendula

Sorbus aucuparia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping whitebeam, is a cultivar of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that hang gracefully, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its foliage is typically dark green and retains its color throughout the growing season.

This ornamental tree is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
