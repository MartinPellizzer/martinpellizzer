Scilla inflata

Scilla inflata, commonly known as the inflated scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. Scilla inflata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
