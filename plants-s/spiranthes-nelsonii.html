Spiranthes nelsonii

Spiranthes nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's spirea, is a species of orchid native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods.

Spiranthes nelsonii is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological diversity.
