Solanum jubeba

Solanum jubeba, commonly known as the yellow bell apple, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, yellow, bell-shaped fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.
