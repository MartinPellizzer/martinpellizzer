Solanum zuccagnianum var. allogonum

Solanum zuccagnianum var. allogonum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption or cultivation in gardens.
