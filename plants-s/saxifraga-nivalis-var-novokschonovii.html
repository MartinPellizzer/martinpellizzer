Saxifraga nivalis var. novokschonovii

Saxifraga nivalis var. novokschonovii, commonly known as the Novokschonovii rockfoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, typically between June and August. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, helping it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
