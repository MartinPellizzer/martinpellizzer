Saccidium pilosum

Saccidium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy saccidium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saccidium pilosum is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
