Spergula arvensis subsp. maxima

Spergula arvensis subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the greater fieldweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
