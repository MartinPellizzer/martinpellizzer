Sarcopyramis napalensis var. bodinieri

Sarcopyramis napalensis var. bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's sarcopyramis, is a rare and unique plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, pyramid-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vividly colored blooms, typically in shades of red and orange, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the high-altitude environment where it thrives.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
