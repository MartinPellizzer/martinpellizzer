Sedum filiforme

Sedum filiforme, commonly known as the threadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky regions of North America.

It features small, thread-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum filiforme is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
