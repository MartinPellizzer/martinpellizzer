Spiraea triloba

Spiraea triloba, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Spiraea triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
