Sonchus ustulatus var. latifolius

Sonchus ustulatus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to Europe and Asia. It has a hairy stem and produces small, achene fruits.

This species is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
