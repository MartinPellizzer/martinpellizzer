Senecio ampullaceus var. glaberrimus

Senecio ampullaceus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are typically covered in a smooth, waxy coating. It produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are attractive to pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
