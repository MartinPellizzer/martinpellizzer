Scirpus cornatus

Scirpus cornatus, commonly known as the common tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, ribbed stems and dense, upright clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and wet meadows.

Scirpus cornatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
