Saribus woodfordii

Saribus woodfordii, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tall, slender plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top of its trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its stems are segmented and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Saribus woodfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
