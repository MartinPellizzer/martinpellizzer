Stellera rosea

Stellera rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf starwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Stellera rosea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Capparaceae family.
