Sacciolepis squamigera

Sacciolepis squamigera, commonly known as the scalycush plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
