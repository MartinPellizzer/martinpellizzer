Senna glutinosa subsp. chatelainiana

Senna glutinosa subsp.

chatelainiana, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, angular pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
