Setaria ventenatii

Setaria ventenatii, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, parts of Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind.

Setaria ventenatii is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
