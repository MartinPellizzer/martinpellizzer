Smilax papyracea

Smilax papyracea, commonly known as the paperplant or wild sarsaparilla, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Smilax papyracea thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Its fibrous roots and durable stems make it a resilient plant with potential applications in traditional medicine and horticulture.
