Syndesmanthus erinus

Syndesmanthus erinus, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and pleasant scent make it a popular choice among gardeners.
