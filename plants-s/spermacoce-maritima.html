Spermacoce maritima

Spermacoce maritima, commonly known as sea spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Spermacoce maritima is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
