Solanum caeruleum

Solanum caeruleum, commonly known as the blueberry nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, blue or purple berries that are edible but contain toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy surface, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Despite its ornamental value, it is often considered invasive in certain regions.
