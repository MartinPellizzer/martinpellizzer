Sedum trifidum

Sedum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sedum trifidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
