Scirpus trachyspermus

Scirpus trachyspermus, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Scirpus trachyspermus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
