Scleranthus verticillatus

Scleranthus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features prostrate or ascending stems with small, clustered leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, followed by the production of dry, capsule-like fruits.

Scleranthus verticillatus thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
