Strophopappus pohlii

Strophopappus pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, contributing to its drought tolerance.
