Sedum batallae

Sedum batallae, commonly known as the Batalla's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the growing season. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
