Silene parnassica

Silene parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene parnassica is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
