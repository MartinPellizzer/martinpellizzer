Saxifraga × trifida

Saxifraga × trifida, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often rosette-shaped, and its clusters of small, pink or white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle scent when touched.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
