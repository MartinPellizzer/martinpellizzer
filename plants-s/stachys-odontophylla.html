Stachys odontophylla

Stachys odontophylla, commonly known as the toothed leaf hedge parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys odontophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
