Symphyotrichum drummondii

Symphyotrichum drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's New England aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has upright, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in composite heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drummond's New England aster is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
