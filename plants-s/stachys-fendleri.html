Stachys fendleri

Stachys fendleri, commonly known as desert mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of purple or blue flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and antiseptic properties.

Stachys fendleri is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
