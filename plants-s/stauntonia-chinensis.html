Stauntonia chinensis

Stauntonia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, orange-red flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 10 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
