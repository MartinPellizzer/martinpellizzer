Satureja × caroli-paui

Satureja × caroli-paui, commonly known as the hybrid savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, slightly minty flavor.

Satureja × caroli-paui thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.
