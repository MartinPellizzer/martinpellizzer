Silene microphylla

Silene microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene microphylla is also noted for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
