Stipagrostis zeyheri subsp. macropus

Stipagrostis zeyheri subsp.

macropus, commonly known as the tall speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in semi-arid regions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
