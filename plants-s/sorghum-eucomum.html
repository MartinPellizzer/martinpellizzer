Sorghum eucomum

Sorghum eucomum, commonly known as white sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa.

It produces large, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. The plant is characterized by its coarse, silver-green leaves and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes.

Sorghum eucomum is also valued for its drought resistance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
