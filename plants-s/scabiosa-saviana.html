Scabiosa saviana

Scabiosa saviana, commonly known as the "Cupid's bow" or "blueweed," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pink to violet flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often branched. Scabiosa saviana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
