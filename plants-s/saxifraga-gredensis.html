Saxifraga gredensis

Saxifraga gredensis, commonly known as the Gredensk saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant features small, often clustered leaves that are typically succulent and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga gredensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
