Salvia congesta

Salvia congesta, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional medicine.

Salvia congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
