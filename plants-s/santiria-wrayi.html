Santiria wrayi

Santiria wrayi, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Santiria wrayi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is adaptable to various soil types.
