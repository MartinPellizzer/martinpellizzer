Silene calycina

Silene calycina, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Silene calycina is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
