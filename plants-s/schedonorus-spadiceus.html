Schedonorus spadiceus

Schedonorus spadiceus, commonly known as the fine fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted flower heads, which are typically pale green to brown in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It has a clumping growth habit and is drought-tolerant once established.

Schedonorus spadiceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
