Silene multicaulis subsp. stenocalycina

Silene multicaulis subsp.

stenocalycina, commonly known as the narrowcalyx campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower calyces compared to the typical form of Silene multicaulis. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
