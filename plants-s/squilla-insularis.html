Squilla insularis

Squilla insularis, commonly known as the island squilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Squilla insularis is often found growing in rocky soils and coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
