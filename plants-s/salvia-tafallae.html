Salvia tafallae

Salvia tafallae, commonly known as the Tafallae sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and gray-green, woolly foliage. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Salvia tafallae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
