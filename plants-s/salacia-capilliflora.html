Salacia capilliflora

Salacia capilliflora, commonly known as the silky salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
