Salvia pratensis var. albiflora

Salvia pratensis var. albiflora, commonly known as white meadow sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, pale to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and naturalized areas.
