Seymeria pectinata

Seymeria pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Seymeria pectinata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
