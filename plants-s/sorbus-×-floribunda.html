Sorbus × floribunda

Sorbus × floribunda, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid between Sorbus aria and Sorbus torminalis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is notable for its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Sorbus × floribunda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
