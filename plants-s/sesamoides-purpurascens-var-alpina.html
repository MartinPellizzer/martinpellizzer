Sesamoides purpurascens var. alpina

Sesamoides purpurascens var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine purple sesamoid, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, enhancing its ornamental value.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
