Symphyotrichum hallii

Symphyotrichum hallii, commonly known as Hall's Newcomb's aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
