Selaginella riograndensis

Selaginella riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with whorled leaves that form dense, compact clusters. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are borne on specialized branches called sporophylls.

Selaginella riograndensis is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique adaptations to conserve moisture.
