Spiraea bracteata

Spiraea bracteata, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant is characterized by its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Spiraea bracteata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy floral display.
