Samanea adiantifolia

Samanea adiantifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its shade-providing capabilities.
