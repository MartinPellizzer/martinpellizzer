Symphytum × discolor

Symphytum × discolor, commonly known as the spotted lungwort, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often spotted with purple or red markings. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, lilac, or white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
