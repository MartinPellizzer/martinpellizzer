Sideritis × angustifolia var. lusitanica

Sideritis × angustifolia var. lusitanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.
