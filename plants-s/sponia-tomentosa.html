Sponia tomentosa

Sponia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sponia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
