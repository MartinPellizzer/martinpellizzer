Sedum trullipetalum var. gagei

Sedum trullipetalum var. gagei, commonly known as the Gage's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It typically blooms in late summer, adding a delicate floral display to rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
