Schefflera patula

Schefflera patula, commonly known as the Hawaiian schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a distinctive, umbrella-like pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Schefflera patula is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
