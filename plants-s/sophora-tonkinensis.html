Sophora tonkinensis

Sophora tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that mature in the fall.

Sophora tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
