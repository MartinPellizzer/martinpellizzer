Sasa ohwii

Sasa ohwii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Sasa ohwii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain slopes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
