Saxifraga × wettsteinii

Saxifraga × wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga × wettsteinii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
