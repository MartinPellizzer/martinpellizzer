Sisyranthus rhodesicus

Sisyranthus rhodesicus, commonly known as the Rhodesian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring linear, grass-like leaves and a single, erect stem. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color, aiding in pollination by insects.

Sisyranthus rhodesicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
