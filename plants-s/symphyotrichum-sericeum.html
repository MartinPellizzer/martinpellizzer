Symphyotrichum sericeum

Symphyotrichum sericeum, commonly known as silky aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum sericeum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
