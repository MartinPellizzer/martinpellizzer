Silamnus procumbens

Silamnus procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate silamnus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous species characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silamnus procumbens is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
