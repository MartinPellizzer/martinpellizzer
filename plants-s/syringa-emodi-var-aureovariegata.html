Syringa emodi var. aureovariegata

Syringa emodi var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and yellow tones. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender during late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its adaptability to temperate climates.
