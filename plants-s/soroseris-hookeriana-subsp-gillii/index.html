Soroseris Hookeriana Subsp. Gillii

Hooker's soroseris, scientifically known as Soroseris hookeriana subsp. gillii, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the high-altitude regions of the Andes. This flowering plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from threats such as climate change and habitat loss.
