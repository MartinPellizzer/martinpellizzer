Setaria sphacelata var. sericea

Setaria sphacelata var. sericea, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristly, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
