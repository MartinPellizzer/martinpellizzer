Sempervivum armenum var. insigne

Sempervivum armenum var. insigne, commonly known as the Armenian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
