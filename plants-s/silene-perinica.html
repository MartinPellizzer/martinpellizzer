Silene perinica

Silene perinica, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.
