Scutellaria parvifolia var. vulgaris

Scutellaria parvifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the small-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist soil conditions.

The species is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
