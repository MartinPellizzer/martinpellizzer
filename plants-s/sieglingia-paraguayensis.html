Sieglingia paraguayensis

Sieglingia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features long, slender stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Sieglingia paraguayensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
