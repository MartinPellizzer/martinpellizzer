Spiraea incarnata

Spiraea incarnata, commonly known as the rose-flowered weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features arching stems that bear clusters of pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil.

Spiraea incarnata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
