Sphaerospora flexuosa

Sphaerospora flexuosa, commonly known as the curled sponge spore, is a small, aquatic plant belonging to the family Sphaerosporeaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, gelatinous, spherical spores that are often found in freshwater environments. The plant is typically found in slow-moving or still waters, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its morphology is simple, with no true roots, stems, or leaves, and it reproduces primarily through the release of these spores.

Due to its small size and specialized habitat, it plays a minor but ecologically significant role in aquatic ecosystems.
