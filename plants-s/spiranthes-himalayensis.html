Spiranthes himalayensis

Spiranthes himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The orchid has a rhizomatous root system and is known for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
