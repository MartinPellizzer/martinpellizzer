Scilla siberica f. robusta

Scilla siberica f. robusta, commonly known as the Siberian squill, is a botanical variant of the scilla genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
