Scandia rosifolia

Scandia rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf scandia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses, apples, and cherries, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy leaves, and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun.

Scandia rosifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
