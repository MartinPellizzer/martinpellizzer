Sadiria subsessilifolia

Sadiria subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Sadiria subsessilifolia is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
