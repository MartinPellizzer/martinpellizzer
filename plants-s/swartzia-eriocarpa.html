Swartzia eriocarpa

Swartzia eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvetwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. Swartzia eriocarpa typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
