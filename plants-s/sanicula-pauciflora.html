Sanicula pauciflora

Sanicula pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered sanicle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, clustered umbels of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

Sanicula pauciflora is often found in the eastern United States and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
