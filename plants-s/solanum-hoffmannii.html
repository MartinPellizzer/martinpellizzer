Solanum hoffmannii

Solanum hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries are typically red or purple when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
