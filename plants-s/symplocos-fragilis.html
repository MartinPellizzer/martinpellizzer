Symplocos fragilis

Symplocos fragilis, commonly known as the brittle alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos fragilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
