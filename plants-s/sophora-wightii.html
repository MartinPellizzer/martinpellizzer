Sophora wightii

Sophora wightii, commonly known as the Indian locust tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. It produces elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
