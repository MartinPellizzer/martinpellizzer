Sempervivum globiferum

Sempervivum globiferum, commonly known as the Globeflower Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a thin layer of white wax. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Sempervivum globiferum is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
