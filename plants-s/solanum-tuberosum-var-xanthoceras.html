Solanum tuberosum var. xanthoceras

Solanum tuberosum var. xanthoceras, commonly known as the yellow potato, is a variant of the potato plant characterized by its yellow or orange-colored tubers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces starchy, edible tubers that are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. Its flowers are typically white or purple, and the leaves are broad and green.

This variety is valued for its unique color and flavor, making it a popular choice in certain culinary traditions.
