Salvia propinqua

Salvia propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Salvia propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
