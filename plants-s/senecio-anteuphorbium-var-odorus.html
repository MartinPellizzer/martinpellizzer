Senecio anteuphorbium var. odorus

Senecio anteuphorbium var. odorus, commonly known as the fragrant senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The variety 'odorus' is distinguished by its stronger fragrance compared to other varieties of Senecio anteuphorbium.
