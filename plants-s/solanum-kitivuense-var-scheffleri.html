Solanum kitivuense var. scheffleri

Solanum kitivuense var. scheffleri, commonly known as the Kitivu nightshade, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature, often from green to red or yellow.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
