Senecio disciformis

Senecio disciformis, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open grasslands. The plant features a rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
