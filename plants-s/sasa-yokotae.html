Sasa yokotae

Sasa yokotae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Sasa yokotae forms dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal medicine.
