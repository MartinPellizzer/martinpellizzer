Securidaca savannarum

Securidaca savannarum, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive appearance.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
