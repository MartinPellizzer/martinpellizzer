Syzygium valdevenosum

Syzygium valdevenosum, commonly known as the white lily of the valley, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium valdevenosum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
