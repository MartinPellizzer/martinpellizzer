Syringa lilac

Syringa lilac, commonly known as common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple in the spring. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated edge.

Syringa lilac is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
