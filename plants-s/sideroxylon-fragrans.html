Sideroxylon fragrans

Sideroxylon fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in certain ecosystems.
