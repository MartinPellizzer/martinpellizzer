Spiesia heratensis

Spiesia heratensis, commonly known as the Herat spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
