Symplocos ovalis

Symplocos ovalis, commonly known as the oval wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Symplocos ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
