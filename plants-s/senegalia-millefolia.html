Senegalia millefolia

Senegalia millefolia, commonly known as bluebrush acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil and high temperatures.

Senegalia millefolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in its native ecosystems.
