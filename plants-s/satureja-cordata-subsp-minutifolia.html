Satureja cordata subsp. minutifolia

Satureja cordata subsp.

minutifolia, commonly known as dwarf savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves with a pungent, slightly minty scent, and produces tiny, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and flowers compared to the typical form of Satureja cordata.
