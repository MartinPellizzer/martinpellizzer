Saxifraga dielsiana

Saxifraga dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early summer.

Saxifraga dielsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
