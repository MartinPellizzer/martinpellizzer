Selago swaziensis

Selago swaziensis, commonly known as the Swazi sandbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Selago swaziensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
