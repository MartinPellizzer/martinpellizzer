Scirpus triqueter subsp. littoralis

Scirpus triqueter subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and coastal environments. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight triangular cross-section.

This subspecies is adapted to brackish or saline conditions, making it a common component of tidal marshes and estuarine habitats.
