Saxifraga stella-aurea var. polyadena

Saxifraga stella-aurea var. polyadena, commonly known as the golden star saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves with a distinctive yellowish-white bloom that resembles a star. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white to pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in alpine gardens.
