Setaria yucatana

Setaria yucatana, commonly known as Yucatan millet, is a grass species native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and potential use in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
