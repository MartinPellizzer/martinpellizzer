Sideritis imbrex

Sideritis imbrex, commonly known as the wrinkled sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Sideritis imbrex is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
