Spiraea ulmaria var. mixta

Spiraea ulmaria var. mixta, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a hybrid variety of the common spiraea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and naturalized areas.
