Syngonanthus curralensis

Syngonanthus curralensis, commonly known as the Curralense sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Syngonanthus curralensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
