Saussurea amurensis

Saussurea amurensis, commonly known as the Amur Saussurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces large, showy composite flower heads, typically with yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with dense white or grayish hairs.

Saussurea amurensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.
