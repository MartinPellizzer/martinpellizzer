Syzygium vaupelii

Syzygium vaupelii, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a flowering plant species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and timber.

Syzygium vaupelii is also important in traditional Aboriginal use and ecological systems.
