Solidago celtidifolia

Solidago celtidifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago celtidifolia is an important nectar source for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
