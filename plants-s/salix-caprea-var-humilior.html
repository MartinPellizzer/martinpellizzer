Salix caprea var. humilior

Salix caprea var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf goat willow, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a smaller variant of the common goat willow, typically growing to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features slender, often drooping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in woodlands.
