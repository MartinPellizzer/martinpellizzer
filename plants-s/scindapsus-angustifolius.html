Scindapsus angustifolius

Scindapsus angustifolius, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often exhibit a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Scindapsus angustifolius is also noted for its ability to purify indoor air, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
