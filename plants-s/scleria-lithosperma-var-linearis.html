Scleria lithosperma var. linearis

Scleria lithosperma var. linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockburr, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
