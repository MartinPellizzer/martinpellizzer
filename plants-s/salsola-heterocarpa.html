Salsola heterocarpa

Salsola heterocarpa, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across harsh environments.

Salsola heterocarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
