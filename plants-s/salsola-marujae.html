Salsola marujae

Salsola marujae, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Salsola marujae is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
