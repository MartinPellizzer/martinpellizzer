Syzygium giorgii

Syzygium giorgii, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a brown, leathery exterior and a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a glossy surface.

Syzygium giorgii is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
