Scutellaria scordiifolia f. pubescens

Scutellaria scordiifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy scutellaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine pubescence, which gives it its specific epithet "pubescens.".

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
