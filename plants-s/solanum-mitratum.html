Solanum mitratum

Solanum mitratum, commonly known as the Mitra's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or purple with five petals.

Solanum mitratum produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may serve as a food source for certain wildlife.
