Selera

Selera, commonly known as the "blueberry bush," is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has alternate, simple, and usually oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soil.

Selera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
