Siphocampylus persicifolius

Siphocampylus persicifolius, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
