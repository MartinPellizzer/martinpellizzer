Sagina micrantha

Sagina micrantha, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it a hardy species.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses in traditional herbal practices.
