Syreniopsis cuspidata

Syreniopsis cuspidata, commonly known as the spikeless water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, allowing it to spread rapidly in still waters.

Syreniopsis cuspidata plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms and can contribute to water quality by absorbing nutrients.
