Sophoclesia subscandens

Sophoclesia subscandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that allow it to ascend trees or other vegetation. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

The botanical significance of Sophoclesia subscandens lies in its role within tropical ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
