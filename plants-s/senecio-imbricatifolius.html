Senecio imbricatifolius

Senecio imbricatifolius, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are fleshy, blue-green in color, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
