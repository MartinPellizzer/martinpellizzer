Senecio uintahensis

Senecio uintahensis, commonly known as Uintah groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species, often found in specific habitats across Utah and Colorado.
