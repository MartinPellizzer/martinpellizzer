Stomatanthes reticulatus

Stomatanthes reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, reticulated venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Botanically, it is noted for its specialized structures that facilitate pollination by specific insect species.
