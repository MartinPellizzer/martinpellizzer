Saldinia subacuminata

Saldinia subacuminata, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or gravelly soils.

Saldinia subacuminata is often found in rocky outcrops and along coastal dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
