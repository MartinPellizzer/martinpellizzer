Staurochilus corazoniae

Staurochilus corazoniae, commonly known as the Red-veined Ctenanthe, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves with striking red veins that run along the midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This ornamental plant is appreciated for its unique foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
