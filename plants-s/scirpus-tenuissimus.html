Scirpus tenuissimus

Scirpus tenuissimus, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus tenuissimus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to water filtration and biodiversity.
