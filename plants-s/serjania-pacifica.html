Serjania pacifica

Serjania pacifica, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Serjania pacifica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
