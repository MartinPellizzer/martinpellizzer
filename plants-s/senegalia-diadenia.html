Senegalia diadenia

Senegalia diadenia, commonly known as the stinkbush or desert honeycomb acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. Its bark and leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is known for its strong, unpleasant odor.

Senegalia diadenia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in its native ecosystems.
