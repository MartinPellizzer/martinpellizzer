Sedum telephium var. pachyphyllum

Sedum telephium var. pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its adaptability and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
