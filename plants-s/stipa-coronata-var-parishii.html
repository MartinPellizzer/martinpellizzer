Stipa coronata var. parishii

Stipa coronata var. parishii, commonly known as the Parson's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
