Sunhangia elegans var. handelii

Sunhangia elegans var. handelii, commonly known as the Handel's elegant sunhangia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a subtle blue or purple hue. The plant produces delicate, pendulous inflorescences with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in shady, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plant species.
