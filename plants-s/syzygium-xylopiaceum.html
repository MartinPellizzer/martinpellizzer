Syzygium xylopiaceum

Syzygium xylopiaceum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red to black berries that contain the seeds used as a spice. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, pungent scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economically valuable fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
