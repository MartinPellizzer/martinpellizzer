Schinopsis peruviana

Schinopsis peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian copal or copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru, Bolivia, and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its bark contains a resinous substance, which has been traditionally used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a long lifespan, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
