Salix babylonica var. pekinensis

Salix babylonica var. pekinensis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
