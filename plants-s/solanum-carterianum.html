Solanum carterianum

Solanum carterianum, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that turn orange when mature, often referred to as "cape strawberries.". These fruits are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Solanum carterianum thrives in warm climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
