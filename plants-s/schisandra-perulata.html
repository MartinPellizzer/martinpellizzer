Schisandra perulata

Schisandra perulata, commonly known as the Peruvian Schisandra, is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Andes Mountains. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Schisandra perulata is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its botanical and pharmacological significance.
