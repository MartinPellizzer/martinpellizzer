Spartium sessilifolium

Spartium sessilifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved spartia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Spartium sessilifolium is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
