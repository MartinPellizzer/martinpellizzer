Spirella tylophoroides var. angustifolia

Spirella tylophoroides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spirea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
