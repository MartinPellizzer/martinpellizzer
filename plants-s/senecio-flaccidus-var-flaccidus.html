Senecio flaccidus var. flaccidus

Senecio flaccidus var. flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
