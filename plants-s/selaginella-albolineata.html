Selaginella albolineata

Selaginella albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique combination of moss-like and fern-like characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in spirals, with a distinctive white or pale green stripe along the midrib.

Selaginella albolineata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its resilience to periods of drought.
