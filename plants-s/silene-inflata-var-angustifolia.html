Silene inflata var. angustifolia

Silene inflata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, bladder-like stems that are often pale pink or bluish in color. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant in gardens.
