Scutellaria muriculata

Scutellaria muriculata, commonly known as the rough skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, clustered in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria muriculata is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
