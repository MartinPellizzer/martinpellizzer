Styloncerus brachypappus

Styloncerus brachypappus, commonly known as the short-pappus styloncerus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically features slender, branching stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall appearance.

Styloncerus brachypappus is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often seen along roadsides and in dry, sandy environments.
