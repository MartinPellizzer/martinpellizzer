Scolopia riparia

Scolopia riparia, commonly known as the river hawthorn, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pink.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds and small mammals.
