Strychnos vestita

Strychnos vestita, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, velvety coating. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Strychnos vestita is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
