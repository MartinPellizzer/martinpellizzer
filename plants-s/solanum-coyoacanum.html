Solanum coyoacanum

Solanum coyoacanum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and eggplants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

The fruit is often used in traditional Mexican cuisine, though the plant contains toxic compounds in its leaves and stems.
