Secale cereale var. hepaticum

Secale cereale var. hepaticum, commonly known as liver rye or hepatic rye, is a variant of rye that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, typically found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. Its growth habit is often low and spreading, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This variety is notable for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage crop.
