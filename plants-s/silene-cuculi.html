Silene cuculi

Silene cuculi, commonly known as the nightshade of the woods, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene cuculi is known for its nocturnal pollination, attracting moths and other night-active insects.
