Solenidium peruvianum

Solenidium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian solenidium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with a compact form that allows it to thrive in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
