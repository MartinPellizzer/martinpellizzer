Spiraea × notha

Spiraea × notha, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea × notha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
