Salvia cyanantha

Salvia cyanantha, commonly known as blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and aromatic foliage, which emit a strong, minty scent. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia cyanantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
