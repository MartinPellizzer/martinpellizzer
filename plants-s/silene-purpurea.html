Silene purpurea

Silene purpurea, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals and prominent stamens, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene purpurea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its ecological importance to pollinators.
