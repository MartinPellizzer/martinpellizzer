Salacia elliptica var. apiculata

Salacia elliptica var. apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a pointed apex, giving it its specific epithet "apiculata.". It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in catkins.

This species is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
