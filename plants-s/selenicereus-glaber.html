Selenicereus glaber

Selenicereus glaber, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is known for its large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Selenicereus glaber is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique blooming habit.
