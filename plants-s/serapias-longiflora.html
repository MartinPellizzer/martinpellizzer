Serapias longiflora

Serapias longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Serapias longiflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
