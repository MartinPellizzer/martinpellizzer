Scilla amethystina

Scilla amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst windflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Scilla amethystina is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
