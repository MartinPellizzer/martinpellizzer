Salvia pseudocomosa

Salvia pseudocomosa, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators.

Salvia pseudocomosa is known for its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
