Saxifraga insularis

Saxifraga insularis, commonly known as the island saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky outcrops. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain water. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

Saxifraga insularis is known for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed or nutrient-poor soils.
