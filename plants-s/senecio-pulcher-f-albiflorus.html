Senecio pulcher f. albiflorus

Senecio pulcher f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinguishing feature is the white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
