Swertia punctata

Swertia punctata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia punctata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
