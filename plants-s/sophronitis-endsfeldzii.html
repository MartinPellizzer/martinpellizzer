Sophronitis endsfeldzii

Sophronitis endsfeldzii, commonly known as the End's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air, a feature common in epiphytic orchids.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
