Senna cumingii var. eremobia

Senna cumingii var. eremobia, commonly known as the coastal senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and salty soils, making it resilient in coastal environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
