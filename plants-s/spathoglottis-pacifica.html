Spathoglottis pacifica

Spathoglottis pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers.

Spathoglottis pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens worldwide.
