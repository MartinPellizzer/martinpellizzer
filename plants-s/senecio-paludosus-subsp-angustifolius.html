Senecio paludosus subsp. angustifolius

Senecio paludosus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
