Salvia castanea f. glabrescens

Salvia castanea f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are smooth and hairless, distinguishing it from other salvia species. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple to blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
