Sesleria sphaerocephala subf. reducta

Sesleria sphaerocephala subf.

reducta, commonly known as the compact bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue to white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is typically used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
