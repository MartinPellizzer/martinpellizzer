Senecio kingianus

Senecio kingianus, commonly known as the King's Crown, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear bright yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
