Sedopsis saxifragoides

Sedopsis saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
