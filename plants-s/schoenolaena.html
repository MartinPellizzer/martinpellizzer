Schoenolaena

Schoenolaena, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in sandy or coastal soils. Schoenolaena species are perennial and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their unique appearance.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a striking visual effect when in bloom.
