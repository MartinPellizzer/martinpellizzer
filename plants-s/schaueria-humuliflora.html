Schaueria humuliflora

Schaueria humuliflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Schaueria humuliflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
