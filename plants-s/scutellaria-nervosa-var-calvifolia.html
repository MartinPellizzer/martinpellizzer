Scutellaria nervosa var. calvifolia

Scutellaria nervosa var. calvifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
