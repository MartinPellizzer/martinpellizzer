Serratula liatroides

Serratula liatroides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Serratula liatroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
