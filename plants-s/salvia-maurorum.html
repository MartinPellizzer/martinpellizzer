Salvia maurorum

Salvia maurorum, commonly known as the Portuguese mint, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a minty aroma. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia maurorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
