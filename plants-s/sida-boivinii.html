Sida boivinii

Sida boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Sida boivinii is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
