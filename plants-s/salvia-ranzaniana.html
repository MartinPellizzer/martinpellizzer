Salvia ranzaniana

Salvia ranzaniana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces striking violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in both gardens and herbal practices.
