Syncodon glomeratum

Syncodon glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Syncodon glomeratum is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
