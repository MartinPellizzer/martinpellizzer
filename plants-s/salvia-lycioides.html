Salvia lycioides

Salvia lycioides, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and produces violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant.
