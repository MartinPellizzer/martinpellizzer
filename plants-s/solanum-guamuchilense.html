Solanum guamuchilense

Solanum guamuchilense, commonly known as the Guamuchil or wild tobacco plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum guamuchilense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
