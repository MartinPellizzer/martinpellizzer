Stipa inconspicua

Stipa inconspicua, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its foliage is usually bluish-green, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Stipa inconspicua plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native range.
