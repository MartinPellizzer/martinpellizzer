Salix neolasiogyne

Salix neolasiogyne, commonly known as the New Guinea willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
