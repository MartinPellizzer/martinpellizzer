Spiraea hartwegiana

Spiraea hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Spiraea hartwegiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
