Scilla dubia

Scilla dubia, commonly known as the Sicilian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which is often used in traditional medicine. Scilla dubia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a relatively low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and woodland settings.
