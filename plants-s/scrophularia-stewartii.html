Scrophularia stewartii

Scrophularia stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, known for its diverse range of species with similar floral structures.

Scrophularia stewartii is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
