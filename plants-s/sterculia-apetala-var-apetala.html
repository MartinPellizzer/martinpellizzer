Sterculia apetala var. apetala

Sterculia apetala var. apetala, commonly known as the naked flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, cream-colored flowers that lack petals, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
