Sannantha collina

Sannantha collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sannantha, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, narrow leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive spurred shape, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Sannantha collina is often found in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
