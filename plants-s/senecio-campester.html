Senecio campester

Senecio campester, commonly known as the Campster groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio campester is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
