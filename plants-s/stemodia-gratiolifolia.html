Stemodia gratiolifolia

Stemodia gratiolifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a delicate white to pale yellow color that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
