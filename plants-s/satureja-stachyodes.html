Satureja stachyodes

Satureja stachyodes, commonly known as Mexican thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple and are arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Satureja stachyodes is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
