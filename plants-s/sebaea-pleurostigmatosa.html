Sebaea pleurostigmatosa

Sebaea pleurostigmatosa, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance.

Sebaea pleurostigmatosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
