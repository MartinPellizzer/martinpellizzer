Schnabelia oligophylla var. oligophylla

Schnabelia oligophylla var. oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf snub-nose bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
