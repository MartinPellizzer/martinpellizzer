Salvia oxyphora

Salvia oxyphora, commonly known as the Cape sage, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom from autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia oxyphora is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
