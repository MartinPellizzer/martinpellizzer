Salvia africana-lutea

Salvia africana-lutea, commonly known as the golden sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.
