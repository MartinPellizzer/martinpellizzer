Senecio eriospermus

Senecio eriospermus, commonly known as the woolly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Senecio eriospermus is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
