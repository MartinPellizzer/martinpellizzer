Schismatoglottis pudenda

Schismatoglottis pudenda, commonly known as the naked lady orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a lady's dress, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy surface, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat.

Schismatoglottis pudenda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a potted plant.
