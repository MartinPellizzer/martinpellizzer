Silene muscipula

Silene muscipula, commonly known as the purple globeflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Silene muscipula is a perennial species that plays an important ecological role in its native environments.
