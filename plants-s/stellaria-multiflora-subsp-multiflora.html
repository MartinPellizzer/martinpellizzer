Stellaria multiflora subsp. multiflora

Stellaria multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers with five petals arranged in a star shape, giving it its common name.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
