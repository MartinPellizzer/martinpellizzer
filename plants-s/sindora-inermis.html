Sindora inermis

Sindora inermis, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or wild starfruit, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
