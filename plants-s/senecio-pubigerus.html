Senecio pubigerus

Senecio pubigerus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
