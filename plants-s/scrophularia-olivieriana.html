Scrophularia olivieriana

Scrophularia olivieriana, commonly known as the Olivier's figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
