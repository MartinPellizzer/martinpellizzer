Styrax fortunei

Styrax fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the production of sticky, resinous sap. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive aromatic scent when cut.

Styrax fortunei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
