Saccharum glaza

Saccharum glaza, commonly known as the glaze sugarcane, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fibrous leaves. The plant produces a high concentration of sucrose in its stem, making it valuable for sugar production. Its growth is typically favored in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its adaptability, Saccharum glaza is often cultivated in regions with a long growing season.
