Syzygium koordersianum

Syzygium koordersianum, commonly known as the koord's apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
