Scutellaria integrifolia f. rhodantha

Scutellaria integrifolia f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The most distinctive feature is its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting red or purple throat. This variety is a horticultural form of Scutellaria integrifolia, noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
