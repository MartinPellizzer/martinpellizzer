Satureja kunthii var. parvifolia

Satureja kunthii var. parvifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
