Spiraea pseudoprunifolia var. typica

Spiraea pseudoprunifolia var. typica, commonly known as the roseleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a glossy green surface, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders and landscape beds.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
