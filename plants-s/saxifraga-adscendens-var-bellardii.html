Saxifraga adscendens var. bellardii

Saxifraga adscendens var. bellardii, commonly known as the cliff saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh, arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
