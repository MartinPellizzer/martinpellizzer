Spilanthes javanica

Spilanthes javanica, commonly known as the "stinkweed" or "devil's tongue," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its pungent odor, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
