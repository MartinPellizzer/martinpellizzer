Serjania confertiflora f. confertiflora

Serjania confertiflora f. confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Its wood is highly valued for its density and rich color, making it a sought-after material in the furniture and musical instrument industries.
