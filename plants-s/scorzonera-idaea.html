Scorzonera idaea

Scorzonera idaea, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Scorzonera idaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
