Saxifraga oppositifolia subsp. speciosa

Saxifraga oppositifolia subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where temperatures are low and sunlight is abundant.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and visually striking species in its native habitat.
