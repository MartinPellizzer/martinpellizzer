Senecio avasimontanus

Senecio avasimontanus, commonly known as the Avasimontan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
