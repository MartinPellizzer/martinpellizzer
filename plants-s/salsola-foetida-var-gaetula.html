Salsola foetida var. gaetula

Salsola foetida var. gaetula, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.

Its robust root system allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed or degraded landscapes.
