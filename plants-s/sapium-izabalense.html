Sapium izabalense

Sapium izabalense, commonly known as the Izabal sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
