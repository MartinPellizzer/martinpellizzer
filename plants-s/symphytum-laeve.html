Symphytum laeve

Symphytum laeve, commonly known as the smooth comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients and water.

Symphytum laeve is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for healing wounds and inflammation.
