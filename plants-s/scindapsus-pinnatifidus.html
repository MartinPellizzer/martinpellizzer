Scindapsus pinnatifidus

Scindapsus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the Shingleplant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often feature a combination of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant has long, slender stems that can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on trellises. Its foliage is glossy and somewhat heart-shaped, with a unique, almost mosaic-like appearance.

Scindapsus pinnatifidus prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in humid environments with well-draining soil.
