Selinum rochelii

Selinum rochelii, commonly known as the Rochel's garlic, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features slender, hollow stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pungent scent. Its small white flowers are arranged in compound umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Selinum rochelii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
