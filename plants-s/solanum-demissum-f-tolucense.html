Solanum demissum f. tolucense

Solanum demissum f. tolucense, commonly known as the Tolucan nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
