Sclerolobium paniculatum var. peruvianum

Sclerolobium paniculatum var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that appear in panicles. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
