Solanum laurifolium

Solanum laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel leaf nightshade, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum laurifolium is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
