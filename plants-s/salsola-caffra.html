Salsola caffra

Salsola caffra, commonly known as welwitschias, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Salsola caffra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
