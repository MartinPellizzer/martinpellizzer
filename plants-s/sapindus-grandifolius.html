Sapindus grandifolius

Sapindus grandifolius, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, soap-like pulp. Its fruit is used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a natural soap substitute.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
