Solidago juncea

Solidago juncea, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
