Spiraea douglasii var. menziesii

Spiraea douglasii var. menziesii, commonly known as Menzies' weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
