Salix × pithoensis

Salix × pithoensis, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Salix viminalis and Salix schwerinii.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are catkin-like, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Salix × pithoensis is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
