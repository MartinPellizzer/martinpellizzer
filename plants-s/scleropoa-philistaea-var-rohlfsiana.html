Scleropoa philistaea var. rohlfsiana

Scleropoa philistaea var. rohlfsiana, commonly known as the Rohlfs' sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
