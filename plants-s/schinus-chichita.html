Schinus chichita

Schinus chichita, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and have a fragrant, resinous scent. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Schinus chichita is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
