Scirpus sylvaticus f. angustifolius

Scirpus sylvaticus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its narrow leaves are usually less than 1 cm wide, distinguishing it from other bulrush species.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
