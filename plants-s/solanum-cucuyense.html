Solanum cucuyense

Solanum cucuyense, commonly known as the Cucuy mountain nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

Solanum cucuyense is often found in moist, shaded habitats at higher elevations, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects and birds.
