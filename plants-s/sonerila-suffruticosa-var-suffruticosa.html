Sonerila suffruticosa var. suffruticosa

Sonerila suffruticosa var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the slender sonerila, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
