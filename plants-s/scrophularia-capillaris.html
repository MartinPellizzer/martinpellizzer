Scrophularia capillaris

Scrophularia capillaris, commonly known as common figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
