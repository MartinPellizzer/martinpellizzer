Sterculia populifolia

Sterculia populifolia, commonly known as the silky fig or white silky tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a large, fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
