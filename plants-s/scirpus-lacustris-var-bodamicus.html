Scirpus lacustris var. bodamicus

Scirpus lacustris var. bodamicus, commonly known as the Bodamin reed, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow lakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spadices, which are cylindrical flower clusters. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable indicator species for water quality and wetland health.
