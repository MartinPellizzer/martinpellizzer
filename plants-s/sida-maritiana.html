Sida maritiana

Sida maritiana, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its hairy leaves and robust stems help it withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Sida maritiana is often found in dunes and salt marshes, playing a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
