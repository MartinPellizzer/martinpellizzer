Scirpus nitens

Scirpus nitens, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white to pale green flowers, which appear in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in marshy or aquatic environments.

Scirpus nitens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
