Saxifraga × chlorantha

Saxifraga × chlorantha, commonly known as the green saxifrage, is a hybrid plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically green, sometimes with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a single umbel at the center of the rosette. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga × chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens.
