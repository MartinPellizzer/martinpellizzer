Solenanthus stamineus var. cuneatifolius

Solenanthus stamineus var. cuneatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
