Steironema ciliatum var. occidentale

Steironema ciliatum var. occidentale, commonly known as the Western hairroot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like roots and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
