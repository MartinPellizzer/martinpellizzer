Salvia michoacana

Salvia michoacana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Salvia michoacana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Mexican medicine.
