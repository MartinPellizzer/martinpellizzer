Symplocos cochinchinensis var. floresana

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. floresana, commonly known as the Floresan tamarind.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic leaves, and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
