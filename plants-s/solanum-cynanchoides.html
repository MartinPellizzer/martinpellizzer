Solanum cynanchoides

Solanum cynanchoides, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum cynanchoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
