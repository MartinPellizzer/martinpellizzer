Syzygium latifolium

Syzygium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits. Syzygium latifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family.
