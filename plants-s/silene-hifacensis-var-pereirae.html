Silene hifacensis var. pereirae

Silene hifacensis var. pereirae, commonly known as the Hifacense catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
