Stevia zacatecana

Stevia zacatecana, commonly known as Mexican sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Stevia zacatecana is also used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
