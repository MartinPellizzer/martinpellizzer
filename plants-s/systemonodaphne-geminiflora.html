Systemonodaphne geminiflora

Systemonodaphne geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered sandalwood, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in pairs, giving it its specific epithet "geminiflora.". Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in humid forests.
