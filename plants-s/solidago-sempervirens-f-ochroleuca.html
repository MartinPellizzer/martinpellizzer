Solidago sempervirens f. ochroleuca

Solidago sempervirens f. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-stemmed goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flower clusters, which contrast with its light green to white stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
