Swertia crassiuscula var. leucantha

Swertia crassiuscula var. leucantha, commonly known as white swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
