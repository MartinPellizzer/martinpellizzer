Salvia × palmeri

Salvia × palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
