Solanum biflorum var. glabrum

Solanum biflorum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which open in the evening. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep blue or black color, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
