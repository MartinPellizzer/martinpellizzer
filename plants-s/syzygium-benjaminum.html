Syzygium benjaminum

Syzygium benjaminum, commonly known as the Benjamin's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae, native to northern Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a unique, slightly tart flavor.

Syzygium benjaminum is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
