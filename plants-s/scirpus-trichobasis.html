Scirpus trichobasis

Scirpus trichobasis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Scirpus trichobasis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and wetland organisms.
