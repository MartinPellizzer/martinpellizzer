Salix glauca var. latifolia

Salix glauca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and lance-shaped leaves that are broad and have a silvery underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
