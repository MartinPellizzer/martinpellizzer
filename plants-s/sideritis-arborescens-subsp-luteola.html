Sideritis arborescens subsp. luteola

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

luteola, commonly known as yellow beardroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
