Senecio refractisquamatus

Senecio refractisquamatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer.

Senecio refractisquamatus typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
