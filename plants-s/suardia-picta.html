Suardia picta

Suardia picta, commonly known as the painted suardia, is a striking member of the Rutaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, ornamental foliage featuring intricate patterns of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. Its leaves are evergreen and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Suardia picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
