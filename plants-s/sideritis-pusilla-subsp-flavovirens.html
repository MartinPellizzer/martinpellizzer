Sideritis pusilla subsp. flavovirens

Sideritis pusilla subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green sainfoin, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its delicate, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, which helps it blend into its surroundings. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
