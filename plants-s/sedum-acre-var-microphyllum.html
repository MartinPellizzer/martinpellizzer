Sedum acre var. microphyllum

Sedum acre var. microphyllum, commonly known as the common stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically thick and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
