Salix × richteri

Salix × richteri, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree characterized by its weeping habit and long, cascading branches.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix babylonica (BABYLON willow). The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, though it prefers moist conditions.
