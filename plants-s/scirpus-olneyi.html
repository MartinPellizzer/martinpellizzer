Scirpus olneyi

Scirpus olneyi, commonly known as Olney's bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in dense, fibrous leaves. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, clustered brown flowers.

Scirpus olneyi plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
