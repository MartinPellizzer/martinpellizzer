Stephanotis vinciflora

Stephanotis vinciflora, commonly known as the sweetheart vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Stephanotis vinciflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its pleasant scent and elegant appearance.
