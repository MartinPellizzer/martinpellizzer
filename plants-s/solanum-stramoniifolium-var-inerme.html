Solanum stramoniifolium var. inerme

Solanum stramoniifolium var. inerme, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into rounded, fleshy berries. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solanin.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.
