Sporadanthus ferrugineus

Sporadanthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange or red tubular flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Sporadanthus ferrugineus is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
