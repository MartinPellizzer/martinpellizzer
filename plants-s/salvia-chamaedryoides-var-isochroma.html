Salvia chamaedryoides var. isochroma

Salvia chamaedryoides var. isochroma, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-blue hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
