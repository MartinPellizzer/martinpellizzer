Senecio nigrapicus

Senecio nigrapicus, commonly known as the black-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio nigrapicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
