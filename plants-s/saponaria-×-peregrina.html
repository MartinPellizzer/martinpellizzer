Saponaria × peregrina

Saponaria × peregrina, commonly known as the candytuft, is a hybrid plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that resemble candy drops, hence its common name. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and borders due to its ornamental value.

Saponaria × peregrina is also known for its mild soap-like scent, which gives it its genus name "saponaria.".
