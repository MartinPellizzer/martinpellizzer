Sequoia sempervirens pendula

Sequoia sempervirens pendula, commonly known as the weeping giant redwood, is a unique cultivar of the coast redwood.

It features long, cascading branches that create a distinctive, drooping appearance. This plant is native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, needle-like leaves that provide dense coverage.

The weeping giant redwood is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to adapt to various soil types.
