Silene seoulensis

Silene seoulensis, commonly known as the Seoul Catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
