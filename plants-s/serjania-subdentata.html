Serjania subdentata

Serjania subdentata, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes several species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
