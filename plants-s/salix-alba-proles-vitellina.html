Salix alba proles vitellina

Salix alba proles vitellina, commonly known as the white willow with yellow catkins, is a variety of the white willow tree.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish-barked branches and long, pendulous catkins that produce yellowish pollen. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found near water bodies.

It is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
