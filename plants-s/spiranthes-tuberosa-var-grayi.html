Spiranthes tuberosa var. grayi

Spiranthes tuberosa var. grayi, commonly known as Gray's pink spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking pink to reddish floral spikes. The flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern, a distinctive feature of the genus Spiranthes, and are borne on tall, erect stems. This variety is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native environment.
