Siemensia pendula

Siemensia pendula, commonly known as the hanging senna, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Siemensia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
