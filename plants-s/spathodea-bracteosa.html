Spathodea bracteosa

Spathodea bracteosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

Spathodea bracteosa is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
