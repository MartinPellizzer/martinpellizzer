Spilanthes filicaulis

Spilanthes filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed devil's lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the genus Spilanthes, which is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is also noted for its peppery scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
