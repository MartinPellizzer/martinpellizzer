Scirpus cubensis var. minor

Scirpus cubensis var. minor, commonly known as the Cuban bulrush, is a marshland plant native to the wetlands of Cuba and the Florida Keys.

It is a perennial herb with tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to two meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Scirpus cubensis var. minor plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
