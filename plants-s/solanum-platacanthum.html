Solanum platacanthum

Solanum platacanthum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits, which are typically orange or red in color. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its ornamental value.

However, like many plants in the Solanaceae family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
