Sasa makinoi

Sasa makinoi, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Makino's bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome propagation, creating thickets in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its culms are hollow and jointed.

Sasa makinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
