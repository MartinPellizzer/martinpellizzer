Sinarundinaria violascens

Sinarundinaria violascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking purple to pink flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Sinarundinaria violascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
