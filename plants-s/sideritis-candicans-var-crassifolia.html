Sideritis candicans var. crassifolia

Sideritis candicans var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive rough texture.

It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
