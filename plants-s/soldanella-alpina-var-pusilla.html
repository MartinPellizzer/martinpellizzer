Soldanella alpina var. pusilla

Soldanella alpina var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf alpine bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a low-growing rosette near the ground.

This variety is well adapted to harsh mountain environments, showcasing resilience to cold and strong winds.
