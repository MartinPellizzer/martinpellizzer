Sedastrum pachucense

Sedastrum pachucense, commonly known as the Mexican sedum, is a succulent plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
