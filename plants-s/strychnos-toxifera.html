Strychnos toxifera

Strychnos toxifera, commonly known as the poison apple or yellow strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds. The plant belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is known for its medicinal and toxic properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains potent alkaloids that can be harmful.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
