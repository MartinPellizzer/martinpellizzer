Salix cordata subsp. rigida

Salix cordata subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the rigid willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
