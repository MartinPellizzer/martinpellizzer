Sieglingia seslerioides var. intermedia

Sieglingia seslerioides var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Sieglingia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside due to a fine layer of trichomes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
