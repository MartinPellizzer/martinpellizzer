Scorzonera luntaiensis

Scorzonera luntaiensis, commonly known as the Luntai scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and hairy, lobed leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
