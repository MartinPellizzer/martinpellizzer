Sorbus kamunensis

Sorbus kamunensis, commonly known as the Kamun mountain whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
