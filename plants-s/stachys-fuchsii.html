Stachys fuchsii

Stachys fuchsii, commonly known as betony or wild betony, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Stachys fuchsii is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
