Solanum nemophilum

Solanum nemophilum, commonly known as the forest nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solanines. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its foliage is usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
