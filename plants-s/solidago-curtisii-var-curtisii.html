Solidago curtisii var. curtisii

Solidago curtisii var. curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Curtis's goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
