Sertula infesta

Sertula infesta, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking appearance during the holiday season. Sertula infesta typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for floral arrangements and as a potted plant.
