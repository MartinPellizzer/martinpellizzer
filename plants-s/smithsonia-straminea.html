Smithsonia straminea

Smithsonia straminea, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Smithsonia straminea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
