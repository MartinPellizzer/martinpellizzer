Salix tagawana

Salix tagawana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by narrow, woody fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
