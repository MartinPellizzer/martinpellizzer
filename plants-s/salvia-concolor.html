Salvia concolor

Salvia concolor, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia concolor is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
