Salvia odontochlamys

Salvia odontochlamys, commonly known as the toothed chlamys sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin, and its spikes of violet to purple flowers bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
