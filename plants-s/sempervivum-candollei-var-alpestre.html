Sempervivum candollei var. alpestre

Sempervivum candollei var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, upright leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in both garden and horticultural settings.
