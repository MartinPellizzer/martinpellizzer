Senecio conzattii

Senecio conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. This species typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in desert regions.
