Smithia pumila

Smithia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high elevations of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
