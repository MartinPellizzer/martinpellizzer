Salicornia arabica

Salicornia arabica, commonly known as sea beans or glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of salt crystals. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, showcasing its remarkable ability to tolerate high salinity.

Salicornia arabica is also valued for its nutritional content and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
