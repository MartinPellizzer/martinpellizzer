Senecio neodendroides

Senecio neodendroides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers.

Senecio neodendroides is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
