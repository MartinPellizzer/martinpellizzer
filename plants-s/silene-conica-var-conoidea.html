Silene conica var. conoidea

Silene conica var. conoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Silene conica var. conoidea is a herbaceous perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
