Salsola potaninii

Salsola potaninii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has spiny leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments due to its deep root system and ability to store water.

Salsola potaninii is also known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
