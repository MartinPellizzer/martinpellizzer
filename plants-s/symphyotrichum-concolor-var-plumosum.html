Symphyotrichum concolor var. plumosum

Symphyotrichum concolor var. plumosum, commonly known as the grayleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with purple centers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and is often found in open woodlands or wet meadows.

Its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
