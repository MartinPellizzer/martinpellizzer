Sesleria varia f. mutica

Sesleria varia f. mutica, commonly known as the wrinkled wood sedge, is a compact, low-growing perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive wavy or wrinkled texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.
