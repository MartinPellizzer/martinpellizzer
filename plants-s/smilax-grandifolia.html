Smilax grandifolia

Smilax grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf greenbrier, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax grandifolia is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is known for its vigorous growth and ornamental value.
