Senecio linifolius

Senecio linifolius, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio linifolius is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
