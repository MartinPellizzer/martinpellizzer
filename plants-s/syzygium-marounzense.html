Syzygium marounzense

Syzygium marounzense, commonly known as the Marounzense tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that turns red when ripe.

Syzygium marounzense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
