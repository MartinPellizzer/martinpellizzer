Sicyos lasiocephalus

Sicyos lasiocephalus, commonly known as the hairy-headed gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that mature to a pale green or yellowish color. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
