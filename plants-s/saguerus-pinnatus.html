Saguerus pinnatus

Saguerus pinnatus, commonly known as the featherleaf saguerus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Saguerus pinnatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
