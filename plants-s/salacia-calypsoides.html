Salacia calypsoides

Salacia calypsoides, commonly known as the mountain salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough texture.

Salacia calypsoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
