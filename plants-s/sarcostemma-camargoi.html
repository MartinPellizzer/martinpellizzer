Sarcostemma camargoi

Sarcostemma camargoi, commonly known as the Camargue milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, fluffy seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Sarcostemma camargoi is also noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock and humans.
