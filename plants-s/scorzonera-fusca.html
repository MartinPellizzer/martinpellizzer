Scorzonera fusca

Scorzonera fusca, commonly known as the purple scurvygrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale purple to violet, with five petals and yellow stamens.

Scorzonera fusca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
