Sipanea galioides

Sipanea galioides, commonly known as the small white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in regions such as South Africa and Namibia. The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact, low-growing habit.

Sipanea galioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
