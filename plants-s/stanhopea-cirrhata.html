Stanhopea cirrhata

Stanhopea cirrhata, commonly known as the orchid with the curled lip, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower with a distinctive, curled lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Stanhopea cirrhata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
