Scutellaria edelbergii subsp. chitralensis

Scutellaria edelbergii subsp.

chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine habitats, and features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
