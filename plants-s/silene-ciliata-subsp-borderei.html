Silene ciliata subsp. borderei

Silene ciliata subsp.

borderei, commonly known as the border's campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and ciliate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
