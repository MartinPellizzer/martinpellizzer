Scirpus paradoxus

Scirpus paradoxus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, ribbed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often exhibit a zigzag pattern. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpus paradoxus thrives in saturated soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
