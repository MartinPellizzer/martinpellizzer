Symplocos neillii

Symplocos neillii, commonly known as the Neill's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos neillii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
