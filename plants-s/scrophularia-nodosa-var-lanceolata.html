Scrophularia nodosa var. lanceolata

Scrophularia nodosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive basal rosette structure. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
