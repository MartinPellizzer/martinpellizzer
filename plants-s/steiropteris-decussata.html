Steiropteris decussata

Steiropteris decussata, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are arranged in a double-row pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick, fibrous layer of old leaf bases. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Steiropteris decussata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
