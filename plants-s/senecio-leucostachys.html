Senecio leucostachys

Senecio leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
