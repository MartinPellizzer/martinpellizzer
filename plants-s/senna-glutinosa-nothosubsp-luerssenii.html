Senna glutinosa nothosubsp. luerssenii

Senna glutinosa nothosubsp.

luerssenii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
