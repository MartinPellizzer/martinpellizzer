Sphalmanthus calycinus

Sphalmanthus calycinus, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem, which allows it to climb and spread along supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Sphalmanthus calycinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping or as a decorative element in gardens.
