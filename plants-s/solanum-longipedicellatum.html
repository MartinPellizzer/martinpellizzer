Solanum longipedicellatum

Solanum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, contain toxic compounds and are not edible for humans.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it often grows in dry, open habitats.
