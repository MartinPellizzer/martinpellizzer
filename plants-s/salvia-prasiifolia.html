Salvia prasiifolia

Salvia prasiifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of tiny, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
