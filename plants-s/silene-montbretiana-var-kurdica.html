Silene montbretiana var. kurdica

Silene montbretiana var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean and parts of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
