Schoenoplectus × intermedius

Schoenoplectus × intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate bulrush, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Schoenoplectus californicus and Schoenoplectus acutifolius.

It is a tall, perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and the edges of lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces spadix-like inflorescences that are often covered in small, clustered flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in regions with fluctuating water levels.
