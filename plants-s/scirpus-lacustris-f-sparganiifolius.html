Scirpus lacustris f. sparganiifolius

Scirpus lacustris f. sparganiifolius, commonly known as the spiky bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush, characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence.

It is a wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or saturated soils, often found in marshes and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable indicator of wetland health and water quality.
