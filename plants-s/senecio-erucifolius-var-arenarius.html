Senecio erucifolius var. arenarius

Senecio erucifolius var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments, with a deep root system to access moisture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
