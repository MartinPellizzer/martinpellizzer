Sabicea solitaria

Sabicea solitaria, commonly known as the solitary sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, also known as the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.
