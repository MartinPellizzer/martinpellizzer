Solandra maxima

Solandra maxima, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the early morning. Solandra maxima is a vigorous climber with thick, waxy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
