Syngonanthus gracilis var. setaceus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. setaceus, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and bear small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense colonies in shallow water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse aquatic life.
