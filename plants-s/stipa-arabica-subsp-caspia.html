Stipa arabica subsp. caspia

Stipa arabica subsp.

caspia, commonly known as the Caspian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
