Swertia occidentalis

Swertia occidentalis, commonly known as Western gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Swertia occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
