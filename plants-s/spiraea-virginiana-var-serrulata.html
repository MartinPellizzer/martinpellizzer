Spiraea virginiana var. serrulata

Spiraea virginiana var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features dense, upright clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
