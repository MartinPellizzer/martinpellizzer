Streptanthus carinatus

Streptanthus carinatus, commonly known as the ridgepod or canyon streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions.
