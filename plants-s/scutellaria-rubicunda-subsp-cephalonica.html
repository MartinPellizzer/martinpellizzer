Scutellaria rubicunda subsp. cephalonica

Scutellaria rubicunda subsp.

cephalonica, commonly known as the Cephalonic skullcup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
