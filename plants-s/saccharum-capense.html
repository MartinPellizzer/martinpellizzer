Saccharum capense

Saccharum capense, commonly known as the Cape sugar cane, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Saccharum capense is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
