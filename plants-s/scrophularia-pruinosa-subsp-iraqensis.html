Scrophularia pruinosa subsp. iraqensis

Scrophularia pruinosa subsp.

iraqensis, commonly known as the Iraqi horseweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in a distinctive powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
