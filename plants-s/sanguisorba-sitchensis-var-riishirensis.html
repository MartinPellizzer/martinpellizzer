Sanguisorba sitchensis var. riishirensis

Sanguisorba sitchensis var. riishirensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
