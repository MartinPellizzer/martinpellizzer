Solidago virgaurea subsp. fallit-tirones

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

fallit-tirones, commonly known as the Florida goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, yellow-orange inflorescences.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually alternate and covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
