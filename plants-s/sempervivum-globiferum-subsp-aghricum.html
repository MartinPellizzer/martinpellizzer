Sempervivum globiferum subsp. aghricum

Sempervivum globiferum subsp.

aghricum, commonly known as the Alpini's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of the Carpathian Mountains. It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
