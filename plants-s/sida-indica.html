Sida indica

Sida indica, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida indica is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.

It has a fibrous root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
