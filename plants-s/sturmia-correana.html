Sturmia correana

Sturmia correana, commonly known as the Korean mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and unique fruit structure.
