Sedum anthoxanthum

Sedum anthoxanthum, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating, helping it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum anthoxanthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
