Scabiosa dallaportae

Scabiosa dallaportae, commonly known as the Italian honeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that resemble tiny honeycombs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa dallaportae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
