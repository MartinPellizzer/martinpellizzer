Sapium cuneatum

Sapium cuneatum, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruits are three-winged samaras that disperse easily by wind.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value but is considered invasive in some regions.
