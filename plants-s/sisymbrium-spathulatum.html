Sisymbrium spathulatum

Sisymbrium spathulatum, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are short, pod-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

Sisymbrium spathulatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and fields.
