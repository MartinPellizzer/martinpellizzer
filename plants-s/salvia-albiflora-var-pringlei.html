Salvia albiflora var. pringlei

Salvia albiflora var. pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy white to pale lavender flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

Its flowering stems are typically erect and branched, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
