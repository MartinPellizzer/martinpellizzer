Solidago nitidula

Solidago nitidula, commonly known as the glossy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance.

Solidago nitidula produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
