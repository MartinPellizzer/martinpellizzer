Syzygium fraternum

Syzygium fraternum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a striking red-purple drupe, often covered in a thin, leathery outer layer. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests near coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in certain regions.
