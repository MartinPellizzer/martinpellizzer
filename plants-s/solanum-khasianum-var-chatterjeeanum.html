Solanum khasianum var. chatterjeeanum

Solanum khasianum var. chatterjeeanum, commonly known as Khasi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. Its berries, which are toxic, are often found in clusters and change color from green to red as they mature.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
