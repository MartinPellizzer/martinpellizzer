Salvia runcinata

Salvia runcinata, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
