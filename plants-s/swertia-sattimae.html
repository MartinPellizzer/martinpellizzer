Swertia sattimae

Swertia sattimae, commonly known as the Sattimae gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Swertia sattimae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
