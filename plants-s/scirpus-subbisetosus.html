Scirpus subbisetosus

Scirpus subbisetosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct white or silver-colored midrib, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scirpus subbisetosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shoreline soils and providing habitat for aquatic and wetland organisms.
