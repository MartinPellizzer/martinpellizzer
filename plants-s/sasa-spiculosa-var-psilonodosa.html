Sasa spiculosa var. psilonodosa

Sasa spiculosa var. psilonodosa, commonly known as the narrow-node bamboo, is a variant of the sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes form dense clumps, contributing to its ability to spread vegetatively.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
