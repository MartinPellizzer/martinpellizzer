Scabiosa corniculata

Scabiosa corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Scabiosa corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its visual interest.
