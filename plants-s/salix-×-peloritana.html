Salix × peloritana

Salix × peloritana, commonly known as the Sicilian willow, is a hybrid species native to the island of Sicily.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments. Its flowers are catkins, which are inconspicuous but essential for wind pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
