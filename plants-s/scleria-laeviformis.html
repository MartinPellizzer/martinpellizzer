Scleria laeviformis

Scleria laeviformis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically smooth and narrow. Scleria laeviformis produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
