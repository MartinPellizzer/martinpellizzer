Salvia hierosolymitana

Salvia hierosolymitana, commonly known as Jerusalem sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in both ornamental and medicinal gardening.
