Solidago chilensis

Solidago chilensis, commonly known as the Chile goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
