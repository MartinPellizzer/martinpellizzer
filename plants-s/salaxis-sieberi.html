Salaxis sieberi

Salaxis sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's salaxis, is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers.

It is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five lobes, and are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.
