Scrophularia canina subsp. pinnatifida

Scrophularia canina subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
