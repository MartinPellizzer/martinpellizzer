Seruneum spilanthoides

Seruneum spilanthoides, commonly known as the spilanthoid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads.

Seruneum spilanthoides is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
