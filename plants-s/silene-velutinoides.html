Silene velutinoides

Silene velutinoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Silene velutinoides is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
