Scutellaria orientalis var. glandulosissima

Scutellaria orientalis var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular skullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
