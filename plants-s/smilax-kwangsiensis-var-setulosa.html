Smilax kwangsiensis var. setulosa

Smilax kwangsiensis var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy smilax, is a climbing shrub native to southern China.

It features woody stems with thorns and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spicate inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow when mature and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax kwangsiensis var. setulosa is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
