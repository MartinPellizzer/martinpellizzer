Senecio jacobaea var. gotlandicus

Senecio jacobaea var. gotlandicus, commonly known as Gotland Hawkweed, is a variant of the common hawkweed, characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe, particularly found in the Baltic region. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
