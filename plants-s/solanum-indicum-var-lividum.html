Solanum indicum var. lividum

Solanum indicum var. lividum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
