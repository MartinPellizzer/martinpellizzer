Stevia selloi var. ypacarayensis

Stevia selloi var. ypacarayensis, commonly known as Ypacaray stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its medicinal and commercial value.
