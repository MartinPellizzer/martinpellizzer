Saxifraga occidentalis var. latipetiolata

Saxifraga occidentalis var. latipetiolata, commonly known as the western stonecrop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
