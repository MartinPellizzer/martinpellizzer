Sorghum pallidum

Sorghum pallidum, commonly known as the white sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, arid climates.

Sorghum pallidum is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed.
