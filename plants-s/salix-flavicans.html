Salix flavicans

Salix flavicans, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to water-rich soils. The plant features slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow on the underside. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Salix flavicans plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
