Scleroptychis cretica

Scleroptychis cretica, commonly known as the crested spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Scleroptychis cretica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
