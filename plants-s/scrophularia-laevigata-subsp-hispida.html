Scrophularia laevigata subsp. hispida

Scrophularia laevigata subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the rough scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
