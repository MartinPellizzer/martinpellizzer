Syzygium paradoxum

Syzygium paradoxum, commonly known as the golden apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium paradoxum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
