Syzygium trivene

Syzygium trivene, commonly known as the three-veined sand apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have three distinct veins, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.
