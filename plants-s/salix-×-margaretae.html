Salix × margaretae

Salix × margaretae, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix babylonica (babylon willow). The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
