Sphaerosciadium denaense

Sphaerosciadium denaense, commonly known as the Denaese globe cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common among cacti, which helps reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Sphaerosciadium denaense is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
