Sanguisorba vulgaris subvar. magnolii

Sanguisorba vulgaris subvar. magnolii, commonly known as the white sorrel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
