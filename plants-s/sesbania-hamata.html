Sesbania hamata

Sesbania hamata, commonly known as the bristly sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Sesbania hamata is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
