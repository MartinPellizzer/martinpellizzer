Sisymbrium canescens var. minus

Sisymbrium canescens var. minus, commonly known as the lesser hoary brookweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has hairy, grayish foliage that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
