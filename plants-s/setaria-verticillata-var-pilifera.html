Setaria verticillata var. pilifera

Setaria verticillata var. pilifera, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and floodplains. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by water, contributing to its ability to colonize wetland areas.

The species is known for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
