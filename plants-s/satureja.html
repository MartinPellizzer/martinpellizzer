Satureja

Satureja, commonly known as summer savory, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plants typically have square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Satureja species are often aromatic, with a pungent or spicy scent due to the presence of essential oils.

They are commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications for their distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
