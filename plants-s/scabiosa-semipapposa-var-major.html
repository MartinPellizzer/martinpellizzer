Scabiosa semipapposa var. major

Scabiosa semipapposa var. major, commonly known as the greater scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, grayish down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
