Senecio hiberniflorus

Senecio hiberniflorus, commonly known as the winter-flowering senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom during the winter months, making it unique in its growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
