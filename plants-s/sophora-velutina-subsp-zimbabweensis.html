Sophora velutina subsp. zimbabweensis

Sophora velutina subsp.

zimbabweensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwe velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in racemose inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
