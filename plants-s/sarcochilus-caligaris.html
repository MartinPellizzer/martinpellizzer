Sarcochilus caligaris

Sarcochilus caligaris, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a deep purple lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sarcochilus caligaris is noted for its unique appearance and is a species of interest in botanical conservation.
