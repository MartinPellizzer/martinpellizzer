Salvia atrocaulis

Salvia atrocaulis, commonly known as blackstem sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple stems and violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to arid gardens.
