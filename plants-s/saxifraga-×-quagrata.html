Saxifraga × quagrata

Saxifraga × quagrata, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant in the family Saxifragaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga myrsinites. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in cold, rocky environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white, and its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
