Swertia obtusa

Swertia obtusa, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Swertia obtusa is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Tibetan medicine for their purported health benefits.
