Sauropus forcipatus

Sauropus forcipatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf soursop, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits with a rough, spiny exterior.
