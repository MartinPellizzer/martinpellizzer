Scolymocephalus speciosus

Scolymocephalus speciosus, commonly known as the showy scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Scolymocephalus speciosus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
