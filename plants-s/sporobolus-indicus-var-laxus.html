Sporobolus indicus var. laxus

Sporobolus indicus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. It has fibrous roots and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate both heat and drought conditions.
