Sobralia splendida

Sobralia splendida, commonly known as the showy sobralia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sobralia splendida is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is well-suited for greenhouse or indoor growing conditions.
