Sinapis chinensis

Sinapis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, pungent odor. Its leaves are edible and are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The seeds of Sinapis chinensis are also used in cooking and have been historically valued for their medicinal properties.
