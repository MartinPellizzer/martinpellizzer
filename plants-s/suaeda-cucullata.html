Suaeda cucullata

Suaeda cucullata, commonly known as the saltbush or desert sandmat, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Suaeda cucullata is native to regions such as the Mediterranean, North Africa, and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
