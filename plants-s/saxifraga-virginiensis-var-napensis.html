Saxifraga virginiensis var. napensis

Saxifraga virginiensis var. napensis, commonly known as the Napensis saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It prefers partial to full shade and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
