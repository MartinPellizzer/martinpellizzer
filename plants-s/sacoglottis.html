Sacoglottis

Sacoglottis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their tuberous roots and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. Sacoglottis species are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
