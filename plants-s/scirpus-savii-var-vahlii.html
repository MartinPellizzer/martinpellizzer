Scirpus savii var. vahlii

Scirpus savii var. vahlii, commonly known as the Vahl's bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetland environments in southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in suitable moist habitats.

Scirpus savii var. vahlii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
