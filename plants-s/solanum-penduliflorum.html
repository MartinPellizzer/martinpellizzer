Solanum penduliflorum

Solanum penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging flower or pendulous nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds, making them potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, cascading appearance.

Solanum penduliflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
