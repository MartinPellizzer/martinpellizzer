Silene remotiflora

Silene remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Silene remotiflora plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
