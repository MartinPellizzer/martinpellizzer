Solori philippinensis

Solori philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine soler's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps, where it thrives in high humidity. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Solori philippinensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
