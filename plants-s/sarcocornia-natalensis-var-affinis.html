Sarcocornia natalensis var. affinis

Sarcocornia natalensis var. affinis, commonly known as the Natal glassworts, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline environments such as mudflats and salt marshes, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. The plant exhibits a succulent, herbaceous growth form with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water and salt. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The species is well adapted to high salinity and fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
