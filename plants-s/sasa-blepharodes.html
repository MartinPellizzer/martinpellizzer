Sasa blepharodes

Sasa blepharodes, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered with a fine, silky hair.

Sasa blepharodes is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
