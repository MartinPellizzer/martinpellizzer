Silene uralensis var. mollis

Silene uralensis var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its unique morphology includes specialized structures that aid in water retention, typical of many succulent species.

Silene uralensis var. mollis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
