Sida saltii

Sida saltii, commonly known as the saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Sida saltii plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
