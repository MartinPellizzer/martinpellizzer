Sarcochlamys pulcherrima

Sarcochlamys pulcherrima, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and somewhat woody.

Sarcochlamys pulcherrima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
