Salvia catarioides

Salvia catarioides, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Salvia catarioides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
