Solidago purshii

Solidago purshii, commonly known as Pursh's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago purshii is an important species for pollinators and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
