Salvia polyclonos

Salvia polyclonos, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
