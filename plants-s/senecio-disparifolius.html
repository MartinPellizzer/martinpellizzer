Senecio disparifolius

Senecio disparifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often asymmetrical leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and often branched.

Senecio disparifolius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
