Salix divaricata var. metaformosa

Salix divaricata var. metaformosa, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds. Its catkins are a key feature, with male and female flowers appearing on separate branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
