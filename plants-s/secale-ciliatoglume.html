Secale ciliatoglume

Secale ciliatoglume, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
