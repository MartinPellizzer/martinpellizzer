Scutellaria leptostegia

Scutellaria leptostegia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from blue to purple.

Scutellaria leptostegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
