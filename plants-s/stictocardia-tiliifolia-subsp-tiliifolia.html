Stictocardia tiliifolia subsp. tiliifolia

Stictocardia tiliifolia subsp.

tiliifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
