Senecio calvus

Senecio calvus, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Senecio calvus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
