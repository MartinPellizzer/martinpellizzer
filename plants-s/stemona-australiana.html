Stemona australiana

Stemona australiana, commonly known as the Australian stemona, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, upright stem that is often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
