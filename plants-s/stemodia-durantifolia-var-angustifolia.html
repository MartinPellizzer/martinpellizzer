Stemodia durantifolia var. angustifolia

Stemodia durantifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved stemodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with a height ranging from 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged alternately along the stem, and are covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
