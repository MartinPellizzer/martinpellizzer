Solenophora erubescens

Solenophora erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed marsh herb, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often floating, aiding in photosynthesis in waterlogged habitats.

Solenophora erubescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing soil erosion.
