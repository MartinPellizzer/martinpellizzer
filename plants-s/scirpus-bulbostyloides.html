Scirpus bulbostyloides

Scirpus bulbostyloides, commonly known as the bulbous stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features tall, triangular stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on long, slender stalks, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Scirpus bulbostyloides has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
