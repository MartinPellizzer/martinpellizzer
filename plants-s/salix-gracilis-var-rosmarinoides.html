Salix gracilis var. rosmarinoides

Salix gracilis var. rosmarinoides, commonly known as the rosemary willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically blooms in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage, which resembles that of rosemary, hence its common name.
