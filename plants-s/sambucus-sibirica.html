Sambucus sibirica

Sambucus sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers in early summer, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are edible when fully ripe but contain toxic seeds.

Sambucus sibirica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
