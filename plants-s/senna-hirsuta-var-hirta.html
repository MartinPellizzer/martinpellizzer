Senna hirsuta var. hirta

Senna hirsuta var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in racemes. It produces pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive foliage and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
