Serapias × aquatinae

Serapias × aquatinae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a cross between Serapias nemorosa and Serapias aphylla.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
