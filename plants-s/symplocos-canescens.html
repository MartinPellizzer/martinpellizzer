Symplocos canescens

Symplocos canescens, commonly known as the gray symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Symplocos canescens is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
