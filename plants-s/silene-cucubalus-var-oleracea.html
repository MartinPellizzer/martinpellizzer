Silene cucubalus var. oleracea

Silene cucubalus var. oleracea, commonly known as the common bladder campion, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, bladder-like seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Silene cucubalus var. oleracea is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine.
