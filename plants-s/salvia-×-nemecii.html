Salvia × nemecii

Salvia × nemecii, commonly known as the Mexican bush sage, is a hybrid perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia × nemecii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
