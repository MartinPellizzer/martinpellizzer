Sonchus splendens

Sonchus splendens, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
