Salvia crucis

Salvia crucis, commonly known as the cross-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia crucis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
