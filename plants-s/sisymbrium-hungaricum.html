Sisymbrium hungaricum

Sisymbrium hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian broomrape, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and branched, leafless stems that often grow in clusters. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
