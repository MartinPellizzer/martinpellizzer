Silene vulgaris f. fallacina

Silene vulgaris f. fallacina, commonly known as the fallacious catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are hairy and opposite, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

This variant is noted for its variability in flower color and morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
