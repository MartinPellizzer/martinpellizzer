Soyeria sibirica

Soyeria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Soyeria sibirica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
