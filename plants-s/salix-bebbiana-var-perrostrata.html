Salix bebbiana var. perrostrata

Salix bebbiana var. perrostrata, commonly known as the Bebb willow.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as riverbanks, wet meadows, and marshes. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.
