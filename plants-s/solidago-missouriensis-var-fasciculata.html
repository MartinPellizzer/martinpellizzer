Solidago missouriensis var. fasciculata

Solidago missouriensis var. fasciculata, commonly known as the Missouri goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, disk-like flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
