Scirpus umbellaris

Scirpus umbellaris, commonly known as umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense, umbrella-like clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tussocks.

Scirpus umbellaris plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
