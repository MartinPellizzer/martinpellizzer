Scirpus brachyceras

Scirpus brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that develop into dark brown, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges.

Scirpus brachyceras plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
