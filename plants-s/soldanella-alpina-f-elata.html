Soldanella alpina f. elata

Soldanella alpina f. elata, commonly known as the Alpine soldanella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and parts of Asia.
