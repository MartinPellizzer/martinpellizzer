Senecio vernicosus var. microphyllus

Senecio vernicosus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
