Solanum monocarpum

Solanum monocarpum, commonly known as the single-seeded nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a one-seeded capsule that matures into a dry, brittle structure.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique reproductive strategy and its role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
