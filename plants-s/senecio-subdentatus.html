Senecio subdentatus

Senecio subdentatus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with finely serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Senecio subdentatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
