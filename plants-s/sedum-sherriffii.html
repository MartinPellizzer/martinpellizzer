Sedum sherriffii

Sedum sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
