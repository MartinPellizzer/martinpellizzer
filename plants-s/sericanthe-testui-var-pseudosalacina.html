Sericanthe testui var. pseudosalacina

Sericanthe testui var. pseudosalacina, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-centered flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
