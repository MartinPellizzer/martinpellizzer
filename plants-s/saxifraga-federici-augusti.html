Saxifraga federici-augusti

Saxifraga federici-augusti, commonly known as the Emperor's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga federici-augusti is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.
