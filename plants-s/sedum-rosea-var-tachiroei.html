Sedum rosea var. tachiroei

Sedum rosea var. tachiroei, commonly known as the Tachiroi rose sedum, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often branch out, covered in dense clusters of small, rose-like flowers. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
