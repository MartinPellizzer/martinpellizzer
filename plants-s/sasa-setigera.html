Sasa setigera

Sasa setigera, commonly known as the bamboo fern or Japanese bamboo, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base of the plant. Sasa setigera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming thickets that can dominate certain habitats.
