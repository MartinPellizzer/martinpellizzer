Selaginella tenera var. macraei

Selaginella tenera var. macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's spikemoss, is a low-growing, evergreen plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of its branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
