Scolymanthus cnicoides

Scolymanthus cnicoides, commonly known as the Indian or Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
