Spiraea media

Spiraea media, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features arching stems that produce clusters of delicate, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a light green color.

Spiraea media is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
