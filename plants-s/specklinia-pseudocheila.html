Specklinia pseudocheila

Specklinia pseudocheila, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant green leaves with a distinctive golden hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
