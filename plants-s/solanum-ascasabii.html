Solanum ascasabii

Solanum ascasabii, commonly known as the Ascasabii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are yellow when ripe.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution.
