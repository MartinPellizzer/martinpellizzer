Syzygium curtisii var. holttumii

Syzygium curtisii var. holttumii, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
