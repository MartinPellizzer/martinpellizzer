Selaginella tanyclada

Selaginella tanyclada, commonly known as the basket fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a dense, clustered growth habit and long, strap-like leaves that form a rosette. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain the spores.

Selaginella tanyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
