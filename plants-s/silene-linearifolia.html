Silene linearifolia

Silene linearifolia, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thin, linear leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Silene linearifolia is an annual or biennial species, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
