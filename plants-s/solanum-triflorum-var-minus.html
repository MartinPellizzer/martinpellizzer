Solanum triflorum var. minus

Solanum triflorum var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
