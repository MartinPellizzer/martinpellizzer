Sesamum triphyllum var. grandiflorum

Sesamum triphyllum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant swamp sesame, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 1 meter in height. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
