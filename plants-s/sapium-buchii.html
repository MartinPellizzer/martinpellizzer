Sapium buchii

Sapium buchii, commonly known as the Chinese sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sapium buchii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
