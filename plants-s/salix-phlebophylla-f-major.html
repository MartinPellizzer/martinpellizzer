Salix phlebophylla f. major

Salix phlebophylla f. major, commonly known as the large-leaf willow, is a variant of the willow species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams and riverbanks. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
