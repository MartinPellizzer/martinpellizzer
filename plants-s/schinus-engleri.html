Schinus engleri

Schinus engleri, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants such as poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
