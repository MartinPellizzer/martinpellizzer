Saxifraga × tyrolensis

Saxifraga × tyrolensis, commonly known as the Tyrol saxifrage, is a hybrid plant species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, often hairy, leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga × tyrolensis is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the alpine flora diversity.
