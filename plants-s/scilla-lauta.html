Scilla lauta

Scilla lauta, commonly known as the tall squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or seed.

Scilla lauta is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize over time.
