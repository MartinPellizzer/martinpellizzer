Salvia aratocensis subsp. suratensis

Salvia aratocensis subsp.

suratensis, commonly known as the Surat sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western India. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
