Sphondylium decipiens

Sphondylium decipiens, commonly known as the false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
