Scirpus cespitosus var. germanicus

Scirpus cespitosus var. germanicus, commonly known as the German bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

The plant is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
