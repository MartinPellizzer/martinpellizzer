Stipa japonica

Stipa japonica, commonly known as Japanese feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-green plumes that resemble feathers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Stipa japonica is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
