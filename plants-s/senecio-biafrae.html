Senecio biafrae

Senecio biafrae, commonly known as the Biafra Senecio, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Nigeria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Senecio biafrae is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
