Symphyotrichum ericoides var. ericoides

Symphyotrichum ericoides var. ericoides, commonly known as heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils in open woodlands and heathlands. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
