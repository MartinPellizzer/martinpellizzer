Symplocos pustulosa

Symplocos pustulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by dark blue or black berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Symplocos pustulosa is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
