Scirpus medianus

Scirpus medianus, commonly known as the intermediate bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Scirpus medianus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
