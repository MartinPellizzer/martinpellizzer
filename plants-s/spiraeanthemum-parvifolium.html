Spiraeanthemum parvifolium

Spiraeanthemum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved spirea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
