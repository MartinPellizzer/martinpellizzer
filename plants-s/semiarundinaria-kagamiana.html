Semiarundinaria kagamiana

Semiarundinaria kagamiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed seeds.

Semiarundinaria kagamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
