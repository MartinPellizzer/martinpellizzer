Spathiphyllum longirostre

Spathiphyllum longirostre, commonly known as the long-beaked peace lily, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and a striking, white spatulate flower that resembles a peace sign. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties and low maintenance requirements.

The spathe and spadix are not only ornamental but also play a role in the plant's reproductive process.
