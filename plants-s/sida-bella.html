Sida bella

Sida bella, commonly known as the pretty sida or smallleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Sida bella is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to dry conditions.

It has a fibrous root system and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
