Symplocos latifolia

Symplocos latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf evergreen or broadleaf symplocos, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Symplocos latifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
