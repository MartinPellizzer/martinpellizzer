Solanum fugax var. albidiochraceum

Solanum fugax var. albidiochraceum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, and it produces small, toxic berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.
