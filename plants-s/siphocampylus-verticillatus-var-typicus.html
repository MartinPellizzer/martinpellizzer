Siphocampylus verticillatus var. typicus

Siphocampylus verticillatus var. typicus, commonly known as the mountain beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in open meadows or along streambanks. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
