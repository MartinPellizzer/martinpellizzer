Saguaster normanbyi

Saguaster normanbyi, commonly known as the Normanby's saguaster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. Its botanical characteristics are adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments, with features such as reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems.

Saguaster normanbyi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
