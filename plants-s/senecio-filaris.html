Senecio filaris

Senecio filaris, commonly known as the slender groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio filaris is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
