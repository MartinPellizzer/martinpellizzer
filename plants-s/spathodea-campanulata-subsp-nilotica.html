Spathodea campanulata subsp. nilotica

Spathodea campanulata subsp.

nilotica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
