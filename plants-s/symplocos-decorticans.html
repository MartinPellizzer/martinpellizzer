Symplocos decorticans

Symplocos decorticans, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and therapeutic uses.
