Sorbus aria var. ovoidea

Sorbus aria var. ovoidea, commonly known as the egg-shaped whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elliptical, egg-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or on rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
