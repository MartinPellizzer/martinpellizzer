Saussurea lehmannii

Saussurea lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
