Salvia pomifera subsp. pomifera

Salvia pomifera subsp.

pomifera, commonly known as the Chinese sage or red sage, is a perennial shrub native to China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
