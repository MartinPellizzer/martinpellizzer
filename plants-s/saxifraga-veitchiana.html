Saxifraga veitchiana

Saxifraga veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, toothed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga veitchiana is relatively low-maintenance and prefers full sun to partial shade.
