Scirpus cernuus

Scirpus cernuus, commonly known as the drooping bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus cernuus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
