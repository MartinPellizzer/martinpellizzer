Smilax sebeana

Smilax sebeana, commonly known as the canebrake or swamp smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by red berries. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in wet, swampy environments. Its stems are covered in sharp, curved spines, making it a challenging plant to handle.

Smilax sebeana is often found in dense thickets and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
