Scaphosepalum macrodactylum

Scaphosepalum macrodactylum, commonly known as the large-fingered sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, yellowish-green rhizomes. The plant produces tall, slender stems with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in a loose spike, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

This species is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grassland habitats.
