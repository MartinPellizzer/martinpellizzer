Stapelia gettliffei

Stapelia gettliffei, commonly known as the carrion flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, meaty, and often foul-smelling flowers that mimic decaying flesh to attract pollinators. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers typically open at night, enhancing its resemblance to rotting meat.

Stapelia gettliffei is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
