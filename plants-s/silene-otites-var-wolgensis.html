Silene otites var. wolgensis

Silene otites var. wolgensis, commonly known as the Siberian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and parts of northern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
