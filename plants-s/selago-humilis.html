Selago humilis

Selago humilis, commonly known as the dwarf selago, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Aizoaceae, also known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Selago humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.
