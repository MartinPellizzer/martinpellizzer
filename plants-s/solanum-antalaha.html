Solanum antalaha

Solanum antalaha, commonly known as Antalaha nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are ovate and pubescent, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
