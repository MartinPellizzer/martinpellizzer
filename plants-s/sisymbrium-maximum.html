Sisymbrium maximum

Sisymbrium maximum, commonly known as the hoary vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that dehisce to release the seeds upon maturity.
