Stachys × dordracensis

Stachys × dordracensis, commonly known as the hybrid mint or velvetleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, nutlet-like seeds.

Stachys × dordracensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
