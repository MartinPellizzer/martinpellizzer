Silene undulata subsp. undulata

Silene undulata subsp.

undulata, commonly known as the undulating campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces pink to red flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are undulating in shape, giving it its common name.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty air and drought conditions.
