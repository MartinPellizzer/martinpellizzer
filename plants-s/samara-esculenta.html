Samara esculenta

Samara esculenta, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces distinctive spiny fruits called samaras, which contain one or two seeds. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
