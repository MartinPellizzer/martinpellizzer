Solanum warszewiczii

Solanum warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may be consumed by certain animals.

Solanum warszewiczii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
