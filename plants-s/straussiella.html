Straussiella

Straussiella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers that bloom on a single stem, often without leaves. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, making it highly dependent on its environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Straussiella is considered endangered and is found primarily in the wetlands of South America.
