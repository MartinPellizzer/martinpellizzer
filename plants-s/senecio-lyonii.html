Senecio lyonii

Senecio lyonii, commonly known as Lyon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio lyonii is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
