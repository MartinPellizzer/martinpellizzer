Solanum grandiflorum var. macrocarpum

Solanum grandiflorum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
