Senna pendula var. indistincta

Senna pendula var. indistincta, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
