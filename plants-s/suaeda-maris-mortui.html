Suaeda maris-mortui

Suaeda maris-mortui, commonly known as the salt marsh arrowgrass, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
