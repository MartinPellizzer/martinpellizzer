Silene karakotchanensis

Silene karakotchanensis, commonly known as the Karakotchan's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene karakotchanensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
