Spergula arvensis subsp. linicola

Spergula arvensis subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the slender field sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in moist to wet conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used as a forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
