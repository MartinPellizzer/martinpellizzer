Sphinga prehensilis

Sphinga prehensilis, commonly known as the clinging sphinx, is a unique species of plant characterized by its specialized adaptations for survival in arid environments.

It possesses a thick, waxy cuticle on its leaves to minimize water loss, a crucial feature in its native habitat. The plant's leaves are often modified into structures that aid in water collection and retention. Its flowering pattern is typically nocturnal, which helps reduce daytime transpiration.

Sphinga prehensilis also exhibits a form of vegetative propagation through its rhizomes, allowing it to spread and colonize new areas efficiently.
