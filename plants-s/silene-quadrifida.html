Silene quadrifida

Silene quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant features delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Silene quadrifida is characterized by its four-parted petals, which give it its common name.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.
