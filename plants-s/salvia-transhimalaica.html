Salvia transhimalaica

Salvia transhimalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Salvia transhimalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
