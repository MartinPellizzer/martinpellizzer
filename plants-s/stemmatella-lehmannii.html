Stemmatella lehmannii

Stemmatella lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's stemmatella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its stem is woody and often branched, with a smooth, greyish bark.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
