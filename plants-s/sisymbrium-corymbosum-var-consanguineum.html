Sisymbrium corymbosum var. consanguineum

Sisymbrium corymbosum var. consanguineum, commonly known as sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Sweet clover is also known for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
