Schefflera hellwigiana

Schefflera hellwigiana, commonly known as the Hellwig's umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Schefflera hellwigiana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
