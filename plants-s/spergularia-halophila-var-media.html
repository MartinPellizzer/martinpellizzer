Spergularia halophila var. media

Spergularia halophila var. media, commonly known as salt marsh redshank, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in saline and coastal environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
