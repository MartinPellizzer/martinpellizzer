Solanum gourlayi subsp. saltense

Solanum gourlayi subsp.

saltense, commonly known as the saltbush nightshade, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, globose berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

This subspecies is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
