Sabulina fontinalis

Sabulina fontinalis, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Sabulina fontinalis is known for its early spring blooming habit, making it an indicator of forest health.
