Salix shangchengensis

Salix shangchengensis, commonly known as the Shangcheng willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a shade tree in urban environments.
