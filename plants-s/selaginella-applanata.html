Selaginella applanata

Selaginella applanata, commonly known as the flat mossy spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its flattened, leaf-like structures arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate cones, which is a characteristic feature of its reproductive biology.

Selaginella applanata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
