Satyria triloba

Satyria triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Satyria triloba is adapted to cold climates and has a widespread distribution across the northern hemisphere.
