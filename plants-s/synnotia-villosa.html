Synnotia villosa

Synnotia villosa, commonly known as the silky sandbur, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, spiky seed pods that are adapted for wind dispersal, a common strategy among arid-region flora. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Synnotia villosa is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
