Sesbania occidentalis var. pubescens

Sesbania occidentalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Western sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It typically grows in open areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves, as indicated by the variety name *pubescens*, is a distinctive botanical feature.
