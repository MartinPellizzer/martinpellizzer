Solanum chrysophyllum

Solanum chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Solanum chrysophyllum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
