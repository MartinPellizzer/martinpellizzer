Sida supina

Sida supina, commonly known as prostrate sida, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sida supina is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
