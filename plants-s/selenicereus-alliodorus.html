Selenicereus alliodorus

Selenicereus alliodorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem with spines and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking nighttime blooms.
