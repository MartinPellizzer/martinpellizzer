Salvia martensii

Salvia martensii, commonly known as Martens' sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its violet to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia martensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
