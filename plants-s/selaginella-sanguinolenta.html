Selaginella sanguinolenta

Selaginella sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its bright red or orange spore-bearing structures, which give it its common name. Its leaves are usually spirally arranged and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions.

Selaginella sanguinolenta is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.
