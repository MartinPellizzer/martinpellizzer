Scopolia parviflora f. grandiflora

Scopolia parviflora f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora form of the lesser scopolia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
