Securinega durissima

Securinega durissima, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are known for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
