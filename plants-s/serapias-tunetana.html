Serapias tunetana

Serapias tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Tunisia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange flowers with a striking, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Serapias tunetana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
