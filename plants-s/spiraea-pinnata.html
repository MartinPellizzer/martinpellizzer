Spiraea pinnata

Spiraea pinnata, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of white or pink in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often reddish-brown and covered in fine hairs. Spiraea pinnata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
