Spodiopogon kawakamii var. sativus

Spodiopogon kawakamii var. sativus, commonly known as Japanese millet, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its seeds are used as a food source, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Spodiopogon kawakamii var. sativus is also cultivated for erosion control due to its dense root system.
