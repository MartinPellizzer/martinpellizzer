Seriphidium borotalense

Seriphidium borotalense, commonly known as the Borotala wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Seriphidium borotalense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
