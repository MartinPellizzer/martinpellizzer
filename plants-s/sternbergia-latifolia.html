Sternbergia latifolia

Sternbergia latifolia, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical name honors the Swedish botanist and physician Carl Sternberg.
