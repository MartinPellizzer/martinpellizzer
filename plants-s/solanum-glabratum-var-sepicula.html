Solanum glabratum var. sepicula

Solanum glabratum var. sepicula, commonly known as the small-flowered nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, often white or pale purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, and its fruits are small berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
