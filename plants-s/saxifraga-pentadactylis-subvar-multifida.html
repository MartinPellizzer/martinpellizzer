Saxifraga pentadactylis subvar. multifida

Saxifraga pentadactylis subvar. multifida, commonly known as the forked saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often divided into multiple segments, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its small, white or pink flowers are arranged in a compact cluster and bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
