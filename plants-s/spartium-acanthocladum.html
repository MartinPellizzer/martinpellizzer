Spartium acanthocladum

Spartium acanthocladum, commonly known as the thorned spartium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Spartium acanthocladum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
