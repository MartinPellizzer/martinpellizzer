Spiranthes parviflora

Spiranthes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flower ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
