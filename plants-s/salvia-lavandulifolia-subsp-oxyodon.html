Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. oxyodon

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

oxyodon, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
