Sedum lineare var. minus

Sedum lineare var. minus, commonly known as narrowleaf stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial succulent native to arid and rocky environments.

It features linear, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ability to store water in its leaves make it an adaptable and hardy plant for various climates.
