Solidago linifolia

Solidago linifolia, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely textured, linear leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago linifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
