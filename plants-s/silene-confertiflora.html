Silene confertiflora

Silene confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite and linear, often covered in fine pubescence.

Silene confertiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
