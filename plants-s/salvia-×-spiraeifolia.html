Salvia × spiraeifolia

Salvia × spiraeifolia, commonly known as the meadow sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Salvia × spiraeifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
