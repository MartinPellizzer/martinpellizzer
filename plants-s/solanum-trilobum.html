Solanum trilobum

Solanum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Solanum trilobum contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
