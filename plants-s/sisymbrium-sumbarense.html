Sisymbrium sumbarense

Sisymbrium sumbarense, commonly known as the Sumbarian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and derives nutrients from its host.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is found in regions of Central Asia. It has a short lifespan and reproduces through seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have negative impacts on its host plants, particularly in agricultural settings.
