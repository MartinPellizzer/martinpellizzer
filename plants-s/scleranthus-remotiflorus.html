Scleranthus remotiflorus

Scleranthus remotiflorus, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish.

Scleranthus remotiflorus is an annual herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
