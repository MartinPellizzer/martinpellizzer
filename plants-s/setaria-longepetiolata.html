Setaria longepetiolata

Setaria longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-leaf millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are small and oval, often used as a food source in some traditional cuisines.

Setaria longepetiolata is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in agricultural settings.
