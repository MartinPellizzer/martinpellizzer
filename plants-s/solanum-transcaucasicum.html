Solanum transcaucasicum

Solanum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate, with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
