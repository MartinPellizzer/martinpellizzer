Smilax sanguinea

Smilax sanguinea, commonly known as red smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Smilax sanguinea is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground, making it a common sight in naturalistic landscapes.
