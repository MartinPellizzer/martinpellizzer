Sorghum malacostachyum

Sorghum malacostachyum, commonly known as the purpletop sorghum, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking purple-tinted leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. It is native to Africa and thrives in warm, arid climates.

Sorghum malacostachyum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
