Sasa megalophylla var. ohdana

Sasa megalophylla var. ohdana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Ohdana bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa megalophylla species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo that exhibits narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by small, round berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
