Setaria aurea f. minor

Setaria aurea f. minor, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant characterized by its fine, upright leaves and dense clusters of small, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and compact growth habit.

It is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
