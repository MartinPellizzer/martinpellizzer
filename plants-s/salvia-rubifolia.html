Salvia rubifolia

Salvia rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purplish foliage and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

Salvia rubifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
