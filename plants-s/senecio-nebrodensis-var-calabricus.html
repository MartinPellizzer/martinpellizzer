Senecio nebrodensis var. calabricus

Senecio nebrodensis var. calabricus, commonly known as the Calabrian Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in scrubland or woodland habitats. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
