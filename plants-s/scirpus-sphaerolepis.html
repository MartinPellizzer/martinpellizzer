Scirpus sphaerolepis

Scirpus sphaerolepis, commonly known as the globeflower bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scirpus sphaerolepis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
