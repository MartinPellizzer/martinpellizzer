Saglorithys othonis

Saglorithys othonis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Saglorithys othonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
