Sapindus oblongifolius

Sapindus oblongifolius, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oblong, serrated leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The tree produces smooth, spherical fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single hard seed. The bark is smooth and grayish, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
