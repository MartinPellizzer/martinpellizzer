Syzygium barotsense

Syzygium barotsense, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a star, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, often grown in home gardens and small-scale agriculture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
