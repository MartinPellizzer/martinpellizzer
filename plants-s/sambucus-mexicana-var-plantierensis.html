Sambucus mexicana var. plantierensis

Sambucus mexicana var. plantierensis, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
