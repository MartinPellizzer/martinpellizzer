Sasaella nikkomontana

Sasaella nikkomontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella nikkomontana is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
