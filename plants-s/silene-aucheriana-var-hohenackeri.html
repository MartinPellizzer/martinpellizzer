Silene aucheriana var. hohenackeri

Silene aucheriana var. hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
