Salomonia canarana

Salomonia canarana, commonly known as the Canary Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

Salomonia canarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
