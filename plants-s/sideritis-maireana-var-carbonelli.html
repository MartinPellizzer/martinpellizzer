Sideritis maireana var. carbonelli

Sideritis maireana var. carbonelli, commonly known as Maire's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally valued for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
