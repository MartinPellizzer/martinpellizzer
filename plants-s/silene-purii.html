Silene purii

Silene purii, commonly known as the Purii catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Silene purii thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
