Setaria aurea var. breviseta

Setaria aurea var. breviseta, commonly known as golden millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, golden-yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional landscaping.
