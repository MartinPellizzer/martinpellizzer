Scolopendrium officinale var. crispum

Scolopendrium officinale var. crispum, commonly known as the crested clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often crinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cones along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a valuable component of certain ecosystems.
