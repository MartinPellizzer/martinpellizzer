Stemodia harleyi

Stemodia harleyi, commonly known as the coastal stemodia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features woody stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale blue or violet in color.

It is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
