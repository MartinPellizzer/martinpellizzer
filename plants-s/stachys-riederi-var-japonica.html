Stachys riederi var. japonica

Stachys riederi var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hedge nectar, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to pink flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
