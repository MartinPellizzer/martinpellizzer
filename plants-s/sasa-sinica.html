Sasa sinica

Sasa sinica, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Sasa sinica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It also plays a role in traditional medicine and is sometimes utilized as a food source in certain regions.
