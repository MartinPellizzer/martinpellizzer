Sonerila parameswaranii

Sonerila parameswaranii, commonly known as the Parameswaranii Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with the colorful bracts serving as the primary ornamental feature.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
