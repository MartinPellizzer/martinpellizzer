Sideritis luteola

Sideritis luteola, commonly known as yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Sideritis luteola is characterized by its slender stems and hairy foliage.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
