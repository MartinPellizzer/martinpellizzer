Senecio denisii

Senecio denisii, commonly known as the Denison's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio denisii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
