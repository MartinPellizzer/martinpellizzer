Sphenopus

Sphenopus, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in whorls, and are usually a striking blue or purple color.

Sphenopus is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unusual appearance.
