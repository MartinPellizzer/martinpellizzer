Syzygiopsis oppositifolia

Syzygiopsis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved syzygiopsis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of small, elliptical leaves along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
