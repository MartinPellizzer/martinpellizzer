Scutellaria floribunda

Scutellaria floribunda, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
