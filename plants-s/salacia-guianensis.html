Salacia guianensis

Salacia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese salacia, is a woody vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to the genus Salix. The plant is characterized by its elongated, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
