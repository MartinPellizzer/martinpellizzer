Shawia nummulariifolia

Shawia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silver coin plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, round, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Shawia nummulariifolia is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
