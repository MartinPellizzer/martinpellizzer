Senecio subrosulatus

Senecio subrosulatus, commonly known as the Rosy Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer to autumn.

Senecio subrosulatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
