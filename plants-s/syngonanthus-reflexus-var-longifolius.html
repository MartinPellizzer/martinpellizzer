Syngonanthus reflexus var. longifolius

Syngonanthus reflexus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, tropical forests.
