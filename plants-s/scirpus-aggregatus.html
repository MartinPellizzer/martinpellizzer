Scirpus aggregatus

Scirpus aggregatus, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of stems that form tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Scirpus aggregatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
