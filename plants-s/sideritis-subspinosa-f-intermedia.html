Sideritis subspinosa f. intermedia

Sideritis subspinosa f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sideburn grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
