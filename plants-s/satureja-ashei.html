Satureja ashei

Satureja ashei, commonly known as Ashe's savory, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used traditionally for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Satureja ashei is valued for its distinctive flavor and its role in local ecosystems.
