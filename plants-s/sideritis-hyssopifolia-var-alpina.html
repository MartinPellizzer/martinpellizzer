Sideritis hyssopifolia var. alpina

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. alpina, commonly known as alpine blue mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild antiseptic and calming properties.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically significant plant.
