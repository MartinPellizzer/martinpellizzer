Saussurea lanuginosa

Saussurea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its harsh, high-altitude environment. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
