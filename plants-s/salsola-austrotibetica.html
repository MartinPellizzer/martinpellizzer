Salsola austrotibetica

Salsola austrotibetica, commonly known as Tibetan saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in regions with high salinity and low precipitation, such as deserts and coastal areas.

Salsola austrotibetica plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
