Salix simulans

Salix simulans, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands.

Salix simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
