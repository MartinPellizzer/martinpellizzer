Selaginella cristalensis

Selaginella cristalensis, commonly known as the cristalensis spikeweed, is a species of spikeweed native to South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a creeping stem with upright, spiky leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on the undersides of the leaves.

Selaginella cristalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
