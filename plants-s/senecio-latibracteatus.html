Senecio latibracteatus

Senecio latibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring silvery-gray foliage with broad, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
