Styrax platanifolius var. stellatus

Styrax platanifolius var. stellatus, commonly known as the star-studded sweetgum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
