Sedum oishii

Sedum oishii, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. It is known for its ability to store water in its thick leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

Sedum oishii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
