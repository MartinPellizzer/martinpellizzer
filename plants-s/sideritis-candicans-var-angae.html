Sideritis candicans var. angae

Sideritis candicans var. angae, commonly known as the Greek ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Greece and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in dense, truss-like clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and somewhat woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
