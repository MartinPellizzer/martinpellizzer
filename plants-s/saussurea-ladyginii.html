Saussurea ladyginii

Saussurea ladyginii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Saussurea ladyginii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
