Satureja revoluta

Satureja revoluta, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are used in cooking for their fragrant, thyme-like aroma.

Satureja revoluta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
