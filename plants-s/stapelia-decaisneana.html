Stapelia decaisneana

Stapelia decaisneana, commonly known as the Ghost Flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and its unique, waxy flowers that resemble decaying flesh, a trait known as carrion mimicry. The plant produces large, star-shaped blooms in shades of yellow to orange, which emit a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators like flies. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Stapelia decaisneana is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unusual floral display.
