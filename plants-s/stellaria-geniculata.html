Stellaria geniculata

Stellaria geniculata, commonly known as the bentweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often curved stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are typically hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Stellaria geniculata thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.
