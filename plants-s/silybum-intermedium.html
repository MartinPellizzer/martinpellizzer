Silybum intermedium

Silybum intermedium, commonly known as the lesser milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant contains compounds such as silymarin, which is known for its potential hepatoprotective properties. Silybum intermedium typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and of interest in herbal medicine.
