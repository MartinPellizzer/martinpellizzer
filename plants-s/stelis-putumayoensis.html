Stelis putumayoensis

Stelis putumayoensis, commonly known as the Putumayo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the dry season. Stelis putumayoensis thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
