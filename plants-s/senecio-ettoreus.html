Senecio ettoreus

Senecio ettoreus, commonly known as the Ettore's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a unique appearance.

Senecio ettoreus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
