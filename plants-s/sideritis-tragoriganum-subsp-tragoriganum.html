Sideritis tragoriganum subsp. tragoriganum

Sideritis tragoriganum subsp.

tragoriganum, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.
