Solanum calvefactum

Solanum calvefactum, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, bell-shaped fruits that turn from green to bright red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is notable for its extremely spicy fruits, which contain high levels of capsaicin.
