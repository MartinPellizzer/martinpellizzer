Scirpus × osoreyamensis

Scirpus × osoreyamensis, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Scirpus californicus and Scirpus acutus, characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus × osoreyamensis is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
