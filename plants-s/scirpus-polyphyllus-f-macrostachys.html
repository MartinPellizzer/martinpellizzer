Scirpus polyphyllus f. macrostachys

Scirpus polyphyllus f. macrostachys, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a prominent feature in marshes and shallow water habitats. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of dense clusters of pale to brownish flowers, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Additionally, it serves as a habitat and food source for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife species.
