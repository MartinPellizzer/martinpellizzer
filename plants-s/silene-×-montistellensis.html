Silene × montistellensis

Silene × montistellensis, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Silene dioica and Silene latifolia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its pink or purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a short life cycle and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in studies of plant hybridization and ecological adaptation.
