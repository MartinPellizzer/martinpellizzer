Syringodea filifolia

Syringodea filifolia, commonly known as the silky fern, is a delicate, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a soft, silken appearance. Syringodea filifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
