Scabiosa cinerea

Scabiosa cinerea, commonly known as the gray scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to violet flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are composed of many small, petal-like bracts that form a flat-topped cluster.

Scabiosa cinerea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
