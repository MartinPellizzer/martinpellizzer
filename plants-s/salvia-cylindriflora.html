Salvia cylindriflora

Salvia cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylinderflower sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage and produces striking, tubular-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Salvia cylindriflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
