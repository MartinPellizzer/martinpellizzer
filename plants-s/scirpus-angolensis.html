Scirpus angolensis

Scirpus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its flowering spikes are cylindrical and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Scirpus angolensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
