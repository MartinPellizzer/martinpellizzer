Setaria tenuispica

Setaria tenuispica, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are small and produce a light, powdery coating, which aids in wind dispersal.

Setaria tenuispica is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
