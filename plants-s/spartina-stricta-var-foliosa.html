Spartina stricta var. foliosa

Spartina stricta var. foliosa, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Spartina stricta var. foliosa is an important component of salt marsh ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to nutrient cycling.
