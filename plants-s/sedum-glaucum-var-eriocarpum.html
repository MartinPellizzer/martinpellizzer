Sedum glaucum var. eriocarpum

Sedum glaucum var. eriocarpum, commonly known as the silver rose sedum, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
