Smelowskia calycina var. americana

Smelowskia calycina var. americana, commonly known as the American smelowskia, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.
