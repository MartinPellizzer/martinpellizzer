Sorbus × floribunda var. glabrescens

Sorbus × floribunda var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of white flowers in spring, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. The plant is native to the British Isles and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

The species is also noted for its resilience to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
