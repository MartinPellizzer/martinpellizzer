Scrophularia ebulifolia var. schmitzii

Scrophularia ebulifolia var. schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
