Scutellaria poliochlora

Scutellaria poliochlora, commonly known as the blue scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Scutellaria poliochlora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
