Silene amoena

Silene amoena, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Silene amoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
