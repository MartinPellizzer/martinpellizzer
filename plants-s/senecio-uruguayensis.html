Senecio uruguayensis

Senecio uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Senecio uruguayensis is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
