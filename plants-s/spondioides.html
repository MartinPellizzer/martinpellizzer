Spondioides

Spondioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Spondioides species are typically perennial, with some varieties thriving in both indoor and outdoor environments. They prefer well-draining soil and moderate light, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes several species, such as Spondioides corymbosus and Spondioides hirtella, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
