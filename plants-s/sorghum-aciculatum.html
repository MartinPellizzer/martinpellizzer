Sorghum aciculatum

Sorghum aciculatum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and panicles that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is typically drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its roots are fibrous and can help prevent soil erosion.

Sorghum aciculatum is often used in traditional landscaping and as a forage crop for livestock.
