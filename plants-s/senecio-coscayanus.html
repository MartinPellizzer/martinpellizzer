Senecio coscayanus

Senecio coscayanus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio coscayanus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
