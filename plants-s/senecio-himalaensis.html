Senecio himalaensis

Senecio himalaensis, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio himalaensis is valued for its potential ethnobotanical uses, including traditional medicinal applications in local communities.
