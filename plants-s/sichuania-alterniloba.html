Sichuania alterniloba

Sichuania alterniloba, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it its common name.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are composed of alternating, curved segments that create a zigzag pattern, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing structures, typical of ferns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
