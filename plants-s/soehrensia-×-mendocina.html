Soehrensia × mendocina

Soehrensia × mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino huckleberry, is a hybrid plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats. The plant produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in horticultural and conservation efforts.
