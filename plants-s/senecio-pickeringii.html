Senecio pickeringii

Senecio pickeringii, commonly known as Pickering's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats such as cliffs and slopes. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
