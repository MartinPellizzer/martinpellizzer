Scutellaria indica f. lilacina

Scutellaria indica f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lilac or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
