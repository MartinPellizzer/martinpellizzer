Scirpus lacustris subvar. fluitans

Scirpus lacustris subvar. fluitans, commonly known as the floating bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in water or very moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.
