Solanum coarense

Solanum coarense, commonly known as the Coari cherry tomato, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces small, cherry-sized fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Solanum coarense is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
