Sasa kutcharoensis

Sasa kutcharoensis, commonly known as the Korean bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Sasa kutcharoensis is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
