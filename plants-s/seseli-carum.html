Seseli carum

Seseli carum, commonly known as caraway, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, hollow stems and feather-like leaves. Its small, white or greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

The seeds, often called caraway seeds, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
