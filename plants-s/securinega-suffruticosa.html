Securinega suffruticosa

Securinega suffruticosa, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its toxicity requires caution in use.
