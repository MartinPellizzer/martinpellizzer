Spartina arundinacea

Spartina arundinacea, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. This plant is highly adapted to saline and brackish environments, making it a common species in estuaries and tidal marshes. Spartina arundinacea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, which can influence local ecosystems.

Its rhizomes contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
