Solanum pastillum

Solanum pastillum, commonly known as the pastilla tomato, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and the fruits mature in clusters.

Solanum pastillum is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.
