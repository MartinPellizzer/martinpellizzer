Serapias strictiflora var. elsae

Serapias strictiflora var. elsae, commonly known as the Elsae's sandwort, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal. The plant features delicate, spurred orchid flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or brown lip. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

This variety is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements.
