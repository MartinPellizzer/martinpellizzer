Senecio stygius

Senecio stygius, commonly known as the Stygian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a somewhat hairy texture.

Senecio stygius is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, exhibiting drought tolerance and slow growth.
