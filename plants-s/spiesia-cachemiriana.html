Spiesia cachemiriana

Spiesia cachemiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Spiesia cachemiriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
