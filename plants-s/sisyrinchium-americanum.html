Sisyrinchium americanum

Sisyrinchium americanum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Sisyrinchium americanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
