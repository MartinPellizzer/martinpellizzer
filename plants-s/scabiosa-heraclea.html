Scabiosa heraclea

Scabiosa heraclea, commonly known as the honeyflower or rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are hairy and opposite, with a rough, rocky habitat preference. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Scabiosa heraclea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
