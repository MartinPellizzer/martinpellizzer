Swertia

Swertia, commonly known as the gentian family member, is a genus of flowering plants native to Asia, Africa, and Europe.

These plants typically have showy blue or yellow flowers and are often found in mountainous regions. Swertia species are characterized by their opposite, simple leaves and usually erect or spreading growth habits. Many species are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The genus includes over 100 species, some of which are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
