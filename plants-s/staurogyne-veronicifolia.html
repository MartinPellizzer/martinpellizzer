Staurogyne veronicifolia

Staurogyne veronicifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf water plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a semi-aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that resemble feathers, which help it float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that are clustered in whorls, adding ornamental value to aquatic environments. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater and is often used in pond and aquarium settings.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easy-to-care-for aquatic plant.
