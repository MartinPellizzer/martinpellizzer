Solanum bonariense

Solanum bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and ripen to a bright red, containing the toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
