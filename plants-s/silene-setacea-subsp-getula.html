Silene setacea subsp. getula

Silene setacea subsp.

getula, commonly known as the Getulian flaxflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny habitats.

The plant features slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Silene setacea subsp.

getula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.
