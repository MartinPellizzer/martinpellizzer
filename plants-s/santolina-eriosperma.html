Santolina eriosperma

Santolina eriosperma, commonly known as silverleaf santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and herbal use.
