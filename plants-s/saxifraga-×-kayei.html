Saxifraga × kayei

Saxifraga × kayei, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, usually clustered in umbels at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga × kayei is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
