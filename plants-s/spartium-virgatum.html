Spartium virgatum

Spartium virgatum, commonly known as slender spartium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Spartium virgatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
