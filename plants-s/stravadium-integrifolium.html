Stravadium integrifolium

Stravadium integrifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-veined leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice among gardeners and conservationists alike.
