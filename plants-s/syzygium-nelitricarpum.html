Syzygium nelitricarpum

Syzygium nelitricarpum, commonly known as the narrow-berried tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its tart flavor.
