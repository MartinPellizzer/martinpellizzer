Senecio mlilwanensis

Senecio mlilwanensis, commonly known as the Eastern Cape groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators.

Senecio mlilwanensis is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and shrublands.
