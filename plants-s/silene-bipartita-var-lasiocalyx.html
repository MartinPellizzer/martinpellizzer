Silene bipartita var. lasiocalyx

Silene bipartita var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Silene bipartita var. lasiocalyx is an herbaceous perennial with a compact growth habit.
