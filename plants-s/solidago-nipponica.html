Solidago nipponica

Solidago nipponica, commonly known as Japanese goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Solidago nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
