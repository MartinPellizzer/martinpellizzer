Silene coccinea

Silene coccinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a rosette of basal leaves. Its scientific name refers to the red color of its flowers, which resemble blood.

Silene coccinea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
