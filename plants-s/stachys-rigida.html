Stachys rigida

Stachys rigida, commonly known as rigid betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Stachys rigida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
