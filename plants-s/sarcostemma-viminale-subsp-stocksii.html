Sarcostemma viminale subsp. stocksii

Sarcostemma viminale subsp.

stocksii, commonly known as the slender milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to parts of southern Africa. It features thin, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, often climbing over other vegetation.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles that contain the characteristic milky sap. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique growth habit.
