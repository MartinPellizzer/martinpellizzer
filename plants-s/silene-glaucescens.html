Silene glaucescens

Silene glaucescens, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Silene glaucescens is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
