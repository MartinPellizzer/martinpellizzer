Silene multiflora var. multiflora

Silene multiflora var. multiflora, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
