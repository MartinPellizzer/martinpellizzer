Soliva pygmaea

Soliva pygmaea, commonly known as dwarf soliva, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
