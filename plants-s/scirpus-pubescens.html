Scirpus pubescens

Scirpus pubescens, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water or moist soils.

Scirpus pubescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
