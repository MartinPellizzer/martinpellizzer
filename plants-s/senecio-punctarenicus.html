Senecio punctarenicus

Senecio punctarenicus, commonly known as the spotted groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinct appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
