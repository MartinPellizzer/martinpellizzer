Sarcochilus viridiflorus

Sarcochilus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered sarcocarp, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a distinctive, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
