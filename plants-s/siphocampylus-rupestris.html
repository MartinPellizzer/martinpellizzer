Siphocampylus rupestris

Siphocampylus rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a yellow throat. It is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
