Schinus lentiscifolia var. pilosa

Schinus lentiscifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy torchwood tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive hairy texture on the leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

The hairy pilosity of its leaves is a key identifying feature, contributing to its unique appearance and ecological role.
