Solanum ochrophyllum var. schmidtii

Solanum ochrophyllum var. schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its foliage consists of simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
