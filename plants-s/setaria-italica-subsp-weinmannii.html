Setaria italica subsp. weinmannii

Setaria italica subsp.

weinmannii, commonly known as Italian foxtail, is a grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail, typically ranging from 10 to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
