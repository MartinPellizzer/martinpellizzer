Spiraea × constantiae

Spiraea × constantiae, commonly known as the hybrid weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its graceful, cascading branches.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for borders and foundation plantings.
