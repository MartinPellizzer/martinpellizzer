Silene burchellii var. pilosellifolia

Silene burchellii var. pilosellifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
