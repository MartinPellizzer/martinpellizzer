Shorea sumatrana

Shorea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Indonesian islands of Sumatra and Borneo.

It is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored timber, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Shorea sumatrana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
