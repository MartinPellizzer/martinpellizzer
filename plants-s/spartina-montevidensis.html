Spartina montevidensis

Spartina montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and tidal conditions. This plant thrives in brackish and saltwater marshes, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.

Spartina montevidensis is also an important food source for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
