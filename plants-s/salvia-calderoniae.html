Salvia calderoniae

Salvia calderoniae, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant blue to violet flowers in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia calderoniae is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
