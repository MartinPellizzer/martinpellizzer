Senna armata

Senna armata, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of India.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senna armata is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.
