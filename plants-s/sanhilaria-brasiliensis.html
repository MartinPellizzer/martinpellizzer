Sanhilaria brasiliensis

Sanhilaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid undergrowth.

Sanhilaria brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
