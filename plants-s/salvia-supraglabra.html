Salvia supraglabra

Salvia supraglabra, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia supraglabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
