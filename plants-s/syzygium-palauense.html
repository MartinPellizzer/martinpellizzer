Syzygium palauense

Syzygium palauense, commonly known as the Pohnpei apple or white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caroline Islands and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits with a sweet and tart flavor.

Syzygium palauense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional food in its native region.
