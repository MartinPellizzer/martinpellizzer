Sorbus aucuparia f. xanthocarpa

Sorbus aucuparia f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited rowan, is a cultivar of the rowan tree characterized by its striking yellow berries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is typically a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is a small, orange-yellow pome that ripens in late summer to early autumn.
