Syzygium cordatum var. gracile

Syzygium cordatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The wood of this species is also prized for its fine grain and durability in craftsmanship.
