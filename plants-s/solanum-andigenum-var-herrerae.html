Solanum andigenum var. herrerae

Solanum andigenum var. herrerae, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to grow in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
