Sarga stipoidea

Sarga stipoidea, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in a fine, hairy layer.

Sarga stipoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
