Sida helleri

Sida helleri, commonly known as Heller's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Sida helleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
