Solanum nodiflorum var. petiolastrum

Solanum nodiflorum var. petiolastrum, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique flowering pattern and adaptability to various growing conditions.
