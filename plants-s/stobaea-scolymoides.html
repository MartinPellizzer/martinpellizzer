Stobaea scolymoides

Stobaea scolymoides, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Stobaea scolymoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
