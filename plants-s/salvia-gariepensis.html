Salvia gariepensis

Salvia gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Salvia gariepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
