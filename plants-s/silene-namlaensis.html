Silene namlaensis

Silene namlaensis, commonly known as the Namlaensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Silene namlaensis is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates with limited soil nutrients.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
