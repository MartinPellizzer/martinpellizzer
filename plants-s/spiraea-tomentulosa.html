Spiraea tomentulosa

Spiraea tomentulosa, commonly known as snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has grayish-white, fuzzy foliage that becomes more pronounced as the seasons progress. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers partial to full sun.

Spiraea tomentulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
