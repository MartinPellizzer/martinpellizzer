Senecio fulvus

Senecio fulvus, commonly known as the Orange Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio fulvus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
