Sherardia arvensis var. neglecta

Sherardia arvensis var. neglecta, commonly known as the common field gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged opposite along the stem, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often considered a weed but has ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers.
