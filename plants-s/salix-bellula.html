Salix bellula

Salix bellula, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a small shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and tiny, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers early in the spring. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and coastal environments.

Salix bellula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various arctic wildlife species.
