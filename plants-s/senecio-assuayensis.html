Senecio assuayensis

Senecio assuayensis, commonly known as the Assuay's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
