Sasa veitchii var. myojinensis

Sasa veitchii var. myojinensis, commonly known as the Myojin bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plant species.
