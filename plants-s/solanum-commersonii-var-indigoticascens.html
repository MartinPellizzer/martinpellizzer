Solanum commersonii var. indigoticascens

Solanum commersonii var. indigoticascens, commonly known as the Chilean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
