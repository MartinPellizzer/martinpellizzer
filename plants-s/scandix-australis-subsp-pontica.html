Scandix australis subsp. pontica

Scandix australis subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic water fern, is a aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, spoon-shaped segments.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate to high light exposure. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds. Scandix australis subsp.

pontica plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality maintenance.
