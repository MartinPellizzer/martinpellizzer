Senegalia incerta

Senegalia incerta, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and pairs of small, bean-like pods. The plant produces yellow flowers and is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its leaves are typically small and feathery, and it is often used for forage by livestock.

Senegalia incerta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
