Scrophularia geminiflora

Scrophularia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a hairy, square stem that grows up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Scrophularia geminiflora is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
