Stevia miahuatlana

Stevia miahuatlana, commonly known as the Mexican stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Stevia miahuatlana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local medicine.
