Scirpus pseudoschoenus

Scirpus pseudoschoenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense, cylindrical clusters. Scirpus pseudoschoenus thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic habitats.
