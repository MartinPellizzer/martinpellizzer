Staurogyne elegans

Staurogyne elegans, commonly known as the slender waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Staurogyne elegans plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
