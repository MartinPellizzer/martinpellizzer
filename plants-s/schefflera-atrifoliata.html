Schefflera atrifoliata

Schefflera atrifoliata, commonly known as the black-leaf schefflera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, umbrella-like shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
