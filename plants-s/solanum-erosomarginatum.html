Solanum erosomarginatum

Solanum erosomarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-margin nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with narrow margins and clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum erosomarginatum is typically found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
