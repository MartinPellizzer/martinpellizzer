Swida instolonea

Swida instolonea, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows on rocky or coastal soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, while its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

Swida instolonea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
