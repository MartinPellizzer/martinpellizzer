Sideritis × valentina var. fontqueriana

Sideritis × valentina var. fontqueriana, commonly known as the Valentina fontqueriana sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
