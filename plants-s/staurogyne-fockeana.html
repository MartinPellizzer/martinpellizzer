Staurogyne fockeana

Staurogyne fockeana, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

Staurogyne fockeana plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
