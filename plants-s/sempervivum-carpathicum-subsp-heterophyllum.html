Sempervivum carpathicum subsp. heterophyllum

Sempervivum carpathicum subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as the Carpathian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with reddish or purplish hues.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
