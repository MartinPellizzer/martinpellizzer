Salvia schenckii

Salvia schenckii, commonly known as Schenck's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often with a minty scent.

Salvia schenckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
