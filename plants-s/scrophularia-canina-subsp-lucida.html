Scrophularia canina subsp. lucida

Scrophularia canina subsp.

lucida, commonly known as the shining dog's mouth, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers.

This subspecies is distinguished by its brighter, more reflective leaves compared to the typical variety.
