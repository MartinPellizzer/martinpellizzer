Serratula decurrens

Serratula decurrens, commonly known as creeping tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that give it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Serratula decurrens is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
