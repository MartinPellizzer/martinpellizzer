Syringa fauriei

Syringa fauriei, commonly known as Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Syringa fauriei is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its resistance to many common pests and diseases.
