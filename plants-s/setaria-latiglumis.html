Setaria latiglumis

Setaria latiglumis, commonly known as rough-fingered millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny grains, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.
