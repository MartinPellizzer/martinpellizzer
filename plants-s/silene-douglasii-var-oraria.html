Silene douglasii var. oraria

Silene douglasii var. oraria, commonly known as the Douglas's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its stems are often branched and hairy. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Silene douglasii var. oraria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
