Senegalia globosa

Senegalia globosa, commonly known as the globe mescal bean, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
