Solanum phureja var. pujeri

Solanum phureja var. pujeri, commonly known as the purple passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive purple to orange-colored fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

The fruit is known for its aromatic, sweet, and tangy flavor, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant.
