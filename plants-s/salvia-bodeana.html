Salvia bodeana

Salvia bodeana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and attractant for pollinators.
