Sideritis hyssopifolia var. peyrei

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. peyrei, commonly known as Peyre's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics compared to other Sideritis species.
