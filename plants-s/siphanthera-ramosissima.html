Siphanthera ramosissima

Siphanthera ramosissima, commonly known as the silky tree or silky ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, rounded crown, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by winged fruits, aiding in seed dispersal by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery or fuzzy underside, contributing to its common name.

Siphanthera ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
