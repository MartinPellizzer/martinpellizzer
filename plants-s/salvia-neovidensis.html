Salvia neovidensis

Salvia neovidensis, commonly known as the Neovic sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia neovidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
