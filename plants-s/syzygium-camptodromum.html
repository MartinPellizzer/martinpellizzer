Syzygium camptodromum

Syzygium camptodromum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium camptodromum is also valued for its timber, which is used in local construction and furniture-making.
