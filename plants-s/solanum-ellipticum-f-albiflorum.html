Solanum ellipticum f. albiflorum

Solanum ellipticum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflowered eggplant, is a horticultural form of the species Solanum ellipticum.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or violet blooms of the species. The plant is a herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are usually green when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage is typically broad and elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.
