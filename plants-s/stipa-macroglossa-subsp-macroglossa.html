Stipa macroglossa subsp. macroglossa

Stipa macroglossa subsp.

macroglossa, commonly known as the large-lip grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
