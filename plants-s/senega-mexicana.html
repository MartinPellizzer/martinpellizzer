Senega mexicana

Senega mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
