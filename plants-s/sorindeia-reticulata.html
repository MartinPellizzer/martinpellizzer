Sorindeia reticulata

Sorindeia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate sorindeia, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
