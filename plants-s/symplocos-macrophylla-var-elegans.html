Symplocos macrophylla var. elegans

Symplocos macrophylla var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant waxleaf sycamore, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

This variant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
