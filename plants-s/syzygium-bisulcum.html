Syzygium bisulcum

Syzygium bisulcum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, ridged bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid climates.
