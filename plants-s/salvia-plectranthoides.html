Salvia plectranthoides

Salvia plectranthoides, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia plectranthoides is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
