Salvia harleyana

Salvia harleyana, commonly known as Harley's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia harleyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
