Salicornia cuscoensis

Salicornia cuscoensis, commonly known as the Andean glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal and desert areas. The plant has succulent stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions. It produces small, green or red flowers that bloom in clusters.

Salicornia cuscoensis is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional Andean cuisine.
