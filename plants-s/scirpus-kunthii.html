Scirpus kunthii

Scirpus kunthii, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown flowers in spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems. Scirpus kunthii is also valued for its ability to filter water and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to its ecological significance.
