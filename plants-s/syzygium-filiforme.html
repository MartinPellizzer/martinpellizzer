Syzygium filiforme

Syzygium filiforme, commonly known as the slender satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium filiforme is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
