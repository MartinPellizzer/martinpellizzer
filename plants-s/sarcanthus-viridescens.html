Sarcanthus viridescens

Sarcanthus viridescens, commonly known as the green tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Sarcanthus viridescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
