Sterculia decandra

Sterculia decandra, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. The tree's fruits are woody capsules that split open to release smooth, glossy seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, making it a significant resource in traditional industries.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional healing practices.
