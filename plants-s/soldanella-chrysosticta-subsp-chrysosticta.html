Soldanella chrysosticta subsp. chrysosticta

Soldanella chrysosticta subsp.

chrysosticta, commonly known as the golden-streaked soldanella, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
