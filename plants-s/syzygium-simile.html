Syzygium simile

Syzygium simile, commonly known as the white apple or white carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a distinctive elongated shape and a crisp, juicy texture with a sweet and tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
