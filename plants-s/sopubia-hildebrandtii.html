Sopubia hildebrandtii

Sopubia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures.

Sopubia hildebrandtii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
