Selaginella blepharostachya

Selaginella blepharostachya, commonly known as the "spleenwort spikemoss," is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinct appearance with its creeping stems and clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Selaginella blepharostachya is characterized by its spore-bearing structures, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
