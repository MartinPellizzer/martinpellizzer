Sanguisorba officinalis var. altissima

Sanguisorba officinalis var. altissima, commonly known as tall sweetgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological landscapes.
