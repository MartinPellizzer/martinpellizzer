Salsola diplantha

Salsola diplantha, commonly known as Russian thornstep, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh, desert-like environments. It has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Salsola diplantha is also known for its ability to survive in extreme temperatures and low water conditions.
