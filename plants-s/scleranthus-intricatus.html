Scleranthus intricatus

Scleranthus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate sandspur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called achenes, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Scleranthus intricatus thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of plant adaptation and distribution.
