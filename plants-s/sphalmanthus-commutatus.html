Sphalmanthus commutatus

Sphalmanthus commutatus, commonly known as the yellow poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and alternate leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
