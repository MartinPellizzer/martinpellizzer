Sclerochloa festuciformis

Sclerochloa festuciformis, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Sclerochloa festuciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
