Solena bowieana

Solena bowieana, commonly known as the Bowie's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Solena bowieana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
