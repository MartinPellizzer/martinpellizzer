Sporoxeia rosea

Sporoxeia rosea, commonly known as the rose-like spore plant, is a unique member of the Sporoxeaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, rose-like floral structures that resemble miniature roses, though it is not a true flowering plant. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, which are released from specialized sporangia located on its stems. Its growth is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or damp rock crevices.

Sporoxeia rosea is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and its role in certain ecological niches.
