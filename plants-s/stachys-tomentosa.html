Stachys tomentosa

Stachys tomentosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and spikes of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Stachys tomentosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
