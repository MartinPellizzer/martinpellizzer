Stipa wakoolica

Stipa wakoolica, commonly known as the Wakool needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Stipa wakoolica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
