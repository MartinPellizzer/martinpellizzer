Senecio longeligulatus

Senecio longeligulatus, commonly known as the long-leaved senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in arid regions.
