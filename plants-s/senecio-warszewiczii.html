Senecio warszewiczii

Senecio warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warshawicz's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio warszewiczii is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
