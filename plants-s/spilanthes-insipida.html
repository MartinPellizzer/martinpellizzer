Spilanthes insipida

Spilanthes insipida, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, sometimes glandular leaves. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Spilanthes insipida is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more noticeable when the plant is crushed.
