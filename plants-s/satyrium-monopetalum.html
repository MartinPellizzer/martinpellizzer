Satyrium monopetalum

Satyrium monopetalum, commonly known as the single-petaled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, low-growing habit and produces small, delicate flowers. The plant has a single, fleshy leaf and a short, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
