Senecio ionophyllus var. intrepidus

Senecio ionophyllus var. intrepidus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
