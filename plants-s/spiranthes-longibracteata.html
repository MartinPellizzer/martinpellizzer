Spiranthes longibracteata

Spiranthes longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that spiral around the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
