Salsola strobilifera

Salsola strobilifera, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Salsola strobilifera is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
