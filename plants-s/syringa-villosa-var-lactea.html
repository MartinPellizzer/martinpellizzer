Syringa villosa var. lactea

Syringa villosa var. lactea, commonly known as the white shrub honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-white, hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
