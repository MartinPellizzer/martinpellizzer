Solanum hamatile

Solanum hamatile, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Solanum hamatile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
