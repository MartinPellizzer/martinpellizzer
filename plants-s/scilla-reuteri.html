Scilla reuteri

Scilla reuteri, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value in gardens. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Scilla reuteri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
