Scrophularia longifolia var. insolita

Scrophularia longifolia var. insolita, commonly known as the long-flowered scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
