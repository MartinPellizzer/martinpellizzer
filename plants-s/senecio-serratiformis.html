Senecio serratiformis

Senecio serratiformis, commonly known as the serratedleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
