Sida montana subsp. camporum

Sida montana subsp.

camporum, commonly known as prairie sida, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and the stems are often branched.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and other open areas.
