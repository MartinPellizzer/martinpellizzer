Spiraea ovalis var. ovalis

Spiraea ovalis var. ovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed along the branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Spiraea ovalis var. ovalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide early season color.
