Swertia petiolata

Swertia petiolata, commonly known as the leafy swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Swertia petiolata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Its leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
