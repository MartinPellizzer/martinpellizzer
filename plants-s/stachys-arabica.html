Stachys arabica

Stachys arabica, commonly known as the Arabian hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Stachys arabica is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
