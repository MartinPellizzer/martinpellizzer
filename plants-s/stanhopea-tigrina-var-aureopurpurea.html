Stanhopea tigrina var. aureopurpurea

Stanhopea tigrina var. aureopurpurea, commonly known as the golden-purple tigrina orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a unique combination of golden and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it thrive in shaded, high-altitude conditions.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and botanical uniqueness.
