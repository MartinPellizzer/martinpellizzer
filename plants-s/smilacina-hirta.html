Smilacina hirta

Smilacina hirta, commonly known as hairy greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Smilacina hirta thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
