Sedum pagetodes

Sedum pagetodes, commonly known as the Paget's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a reddish tinge, and are arranged in a compact rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
