Santalodes ligulatum

Santalodes ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandalwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic shrub that grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, white or yellowish flowers. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The plant is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
