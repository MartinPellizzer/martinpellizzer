Sinocalamus suberosus

Sinocalamus suberosus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, Vietnam, and Myanmar. This plant is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thick, fibrous layer. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Sinocalamus suberosus is valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in traditional crafts and construction.
