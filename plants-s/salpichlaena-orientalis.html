Salpichlaena orientalis

Salpichlaena orientalis, commonly known as the oriental spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

The species is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
