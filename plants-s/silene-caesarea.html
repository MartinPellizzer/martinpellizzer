Silene caesarea

Silene caesarea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, pointed leaves and produces a single, erect flowering stem. Silene caesarea is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
