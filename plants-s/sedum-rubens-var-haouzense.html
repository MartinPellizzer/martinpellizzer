Sedum rubens var. haouzense

Sedum rubens var. haouzense, commonly known as the Redstone sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially under stress. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
