Saprosma pubescens

Saprosma pubescens, commonly known as the hairy saprosma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Saprosma pubescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
