Setaria leiocarpa

Setaria leiocarpa, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Setaria leiocarpa is a hardy perennial that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for wildlife.
