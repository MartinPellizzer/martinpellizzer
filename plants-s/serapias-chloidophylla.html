Serapias chloidophylla

Serapias chloidophylla, commonly known as the green-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinct hooded lip. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green, though they may exhibit a slight bluish tinge. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Serapias chloidophylla is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
