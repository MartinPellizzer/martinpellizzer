Sisyrinchium halophilum

Sisyrinchium halophilum, commonly known as saltmeadow blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline environments, such as salt marshes and dunes, and is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects.
