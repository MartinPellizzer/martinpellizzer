Sedum stenopetalum subsp. typicum

Sedum stenopetalum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically blue-green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
