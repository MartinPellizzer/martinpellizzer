Succisella inflexa f. beckii

Succisella inflexa f. beckii, commonly known as the Beck's bentgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it is well adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for erosion control and as a ground cover.

The variety 'beckii' is distinguished by its slightly more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Succisella inflexa.
