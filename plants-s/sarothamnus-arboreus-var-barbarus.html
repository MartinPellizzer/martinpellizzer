Sarothamnus arboreus var. barbarus

Sarothamnus arboreus var. barbarus, commonly known as the tree saxifrage, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact, bushy form with woody stems that can reach heights of up to two meters.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
