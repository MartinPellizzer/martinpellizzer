Sedum millspaughii

Sedum millspaughii, commonly known as the Millspaugh's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular plant among gardeners in arid climates.
