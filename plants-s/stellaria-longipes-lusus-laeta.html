Stellaria longipes lusus laeta

Stellaria longipes lusus laeta, commonly known as the happy starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and cheerful blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
