Sideritis villosa

Sideritis villosa, commonly known as the hairy ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender.

Sideritis villosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
