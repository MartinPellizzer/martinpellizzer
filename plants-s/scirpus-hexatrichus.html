Scirpus hexatrichus

Scirpus hexatrichus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spike-like flowers.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in aquatic environments.

Scirpus hexatrichus plays a vital role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
