Salix pedicellaris

Salix pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, often wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its flexible branches and rapid growth make it adaptable to a variety of wetland environments.

Salix pedicellaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
