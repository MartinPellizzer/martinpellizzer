Sasa asoensis

Sasa asoensis, commonly known as the Aso bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grassy aroma.

Sasa asoensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
