Sideritis guyoniana

Sideritis guyoniana, commonly known as the Greek ladies' mantle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly serrated edge.

Sideritis guyoniana is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, minty fragrance.
