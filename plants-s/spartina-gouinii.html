Spartina gouinii

Spartina gouinii, commonly known as Gouin's cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Spartina gouinii plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
