Stereoxylon corymbosum

Stereoxylon corymbosum, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
