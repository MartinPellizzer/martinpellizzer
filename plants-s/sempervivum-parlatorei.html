Sempervivum parlatorei

Sempervivum parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatorei houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum parlatorei is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
