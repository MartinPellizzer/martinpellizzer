Solanum trutel

Solanum trutel, commonly known as the Trutel nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum trutel belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
