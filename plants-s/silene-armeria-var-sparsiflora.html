Silene armeria var. sparsiflora

Silene armeria var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to rocky and coastal areas of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
