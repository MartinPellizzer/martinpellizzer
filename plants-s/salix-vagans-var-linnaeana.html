Salix vagans var. linnaeana

Salix vagans var. linnaeana, commonly known as the Linnaean willow, is a variant of the weeping willow native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its long, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
