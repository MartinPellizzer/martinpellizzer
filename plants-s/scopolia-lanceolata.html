Scopolia lanceolata

Scopolia lanceolata, commonly known as the lancolate monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, hood-shaped flowers. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal. Scopolia lanceolata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is noted for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
