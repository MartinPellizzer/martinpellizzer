Solanum pygmaeum var. latifolium

Solanum pygmaeum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, usually purple or yellow flowers and broad, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
