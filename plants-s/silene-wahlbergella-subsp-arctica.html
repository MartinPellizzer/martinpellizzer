Silene wahlbergella subsp. arctica

Silene wahlbergella subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic campion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments, often found on tundra and rocky soils.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
