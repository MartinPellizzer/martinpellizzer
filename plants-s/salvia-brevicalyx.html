Salvia brevicalyx

Salvia brevicalyx, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Salvia brevicalyx is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
