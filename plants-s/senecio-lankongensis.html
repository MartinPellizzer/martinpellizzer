Senecio lankongensis

Senecio lankongensis, commonly known as the Lankong's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
