Scrophularia tagetifolia subsp. diversifolia

Scrophularia tagetifolia subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the diverseleaf dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and vary in shape, contributing to its subspecies name. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with tubular corollas that are typically pink or purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
