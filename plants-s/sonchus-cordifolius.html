Sonchus cordifolius

Sonchus cordifolius, commonly known as heartleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Sonchus cordifolius is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
