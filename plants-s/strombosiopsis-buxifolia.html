Strombosiopsis buxifolia

Strombosiopsis buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf strombosiopsis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
