Setaria italica var. purpurea

Setaria italica var. purpurea, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of millet characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish bracts and inflorescence.

It is an annual grass that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of seeds that are encased in narrow, pointed leaves. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it suitable for use in gardens and as a cover crop.

The vibrant coloration of its foliage and seed heads adds visual interest to landscapes.
