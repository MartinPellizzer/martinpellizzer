Spennera rubricaulis

Spennera rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed spennera, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. Spennera rubricaulis thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
