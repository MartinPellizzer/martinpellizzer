Semecarpus zeylanicus

Semecarpus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds.
