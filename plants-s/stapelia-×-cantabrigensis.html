Stapelia × cantabrigensis

Stapelia × cantabrigensis, commonly known as the hybrid African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stem-like leaves and distinctive, odoriferous flowers. The plant produces large, waxy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with prominent purple markings, resembling the appearance of a corpse. It is a hybrid between Stapelia hahndorfi and Stapelia kirkii, and it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
