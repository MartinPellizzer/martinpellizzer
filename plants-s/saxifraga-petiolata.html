Saxifraga petiolata

Saxifraga petiolata, commonly known as the trailing saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga petiolata is often used in rock gardens and alpine arrangements due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
