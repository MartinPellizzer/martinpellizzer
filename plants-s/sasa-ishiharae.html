Sasa ishiharae

Sasa ishiharae, commonly known as Ishihara's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa ishiharae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
