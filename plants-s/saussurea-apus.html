Saussurea apus

Saussurea apus, commonly known as the Himalayan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small yellow to orange flowers.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
