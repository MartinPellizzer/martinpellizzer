Syngonanthus anomalus var. stolanaceus

Syngonanthus anomalus var. stolanaceus, commonly known as the stolonic form of the anomalous bamboo, is a unique variant characterized by its stolons, which are horizontal stems that root at the nodes.

This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include slender, erect culms with narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant exhibits a clump-forming growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It is notable for its adaptability and ability to spread vegetatively through its stolons.
