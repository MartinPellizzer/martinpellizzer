Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. cubense

Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. cubense, commonly known as eastern grassland panicum, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle of spikelets that bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
