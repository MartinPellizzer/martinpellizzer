Sindroa longisquama

Sindroa longisquama, commonly known as the long-scale yew, is a rare and unique coniferous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring elongated, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, a characteristic typical of many yew species. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Sindroa longisquama is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
