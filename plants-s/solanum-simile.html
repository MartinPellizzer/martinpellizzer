Solanum simile

Solanum simile, commonly known as the similar nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five lobes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
