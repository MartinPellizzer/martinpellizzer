Salomonia stricta

Salomonia stricta, commonly known as the upright salomonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salomonia stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
