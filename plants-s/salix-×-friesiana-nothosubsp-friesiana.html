Salix × friesiana nothosubsp.

friesiana, commonly known as the European weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus. It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Its flowers are catkins, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.
