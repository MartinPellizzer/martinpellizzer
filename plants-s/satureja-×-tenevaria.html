Satureja × tenevaria

Satureja × tenevaria, commonly known as the hybrid savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, peppery flavor.

Satureja × tenevaria thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
