Selaginella stolonifera var. poeppigiana

Selaginella stolonifera var. poeppigiana, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its creeping, stoloniferous growth habit.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its use in horticultural settings for ornamental purposes.
