Syzygium roxburghianum

Syzygium roxburghianum, commonly known as the Indian laurel or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, while its flowers are small and white or pale pink.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its edible, sweet, and aromatic pulp.
