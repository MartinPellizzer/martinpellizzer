Silene muslimii

Silene muslimii, commonly known as the Turkish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Silene muslimii is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
