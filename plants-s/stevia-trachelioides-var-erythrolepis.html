Stevia trachelioides var. erythrolepis

Stevia trachelioides var. erythrolepis, commonly known as red-leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that range from green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal and culinary applications.
