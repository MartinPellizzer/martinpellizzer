Sterculia schefflerifolia

Sterculia schefflerifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral displays.
