Scelochilus mirthae

Scelochilus mirthae, commonly known as the Mirtha's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Scelochilus mirthae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
