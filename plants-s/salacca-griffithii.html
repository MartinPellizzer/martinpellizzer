Salacca griffithii

Salacca griffithii, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, while the sap can be tapped for a sweet, syrup-like substance.

Salacca griffithii thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
