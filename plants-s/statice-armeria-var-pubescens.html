Statice armeria var. pubescens

Statice armeria var. pubescens, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or salty soils. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
