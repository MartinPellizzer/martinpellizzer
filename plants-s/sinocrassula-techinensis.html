Sinocrassula techinensis

Sinocrassula techinensis, commonly known as the Tibetan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sinocrassula techinensis is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found growing on cliffs or in alpine meadows.
