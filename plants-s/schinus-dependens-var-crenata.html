Schinus dependens var. crenata

Schinus dependens var. crenata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or crenate Brazilian pepper, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

This species is native to South America and is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
