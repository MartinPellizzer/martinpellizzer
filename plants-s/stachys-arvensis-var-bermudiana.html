Stachys arvensis var. bermudiana

Stachys arvensis var. bermudiana, commonly known as Bermuda betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of the southeastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
