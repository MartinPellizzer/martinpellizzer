Silene jeniseensis f. parviflora

Silene jeniseensis f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered form of the Jenise River catchfly, is a botanical variant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its diminutive, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to its natural habitat.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
