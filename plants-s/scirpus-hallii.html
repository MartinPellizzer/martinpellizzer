Scirpus hallii

Scirpus hallii, commonly known as Hall's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus hallii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
