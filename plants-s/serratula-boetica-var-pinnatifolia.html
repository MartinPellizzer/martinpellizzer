Serratula boetica var. pinnatifolia

Serratula boetica var. pinnatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
