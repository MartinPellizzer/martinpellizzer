Scirpus nervosus var. siculus

Scirpus nervosus var. siculus, commonly known as Sicilian bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical clusters of brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight zigzag pattern along the midrib.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
