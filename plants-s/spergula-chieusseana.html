Spergula chieusseana

Spergula chieusseana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Spergula chieusseana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
