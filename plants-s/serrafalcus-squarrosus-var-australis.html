Serrafalcus squarrosus var. australis

Serrafalcus squarrosus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian serrafalcus, is a distinctive plant characterized by its serrated, falcate leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence.

Native to the temperate regions of southern Australia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which appear in clusters during the spring. Its unique morphology and adaptability to arid conditions make it a notable species within the Serrafalcus genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological resilience in native gardens.
