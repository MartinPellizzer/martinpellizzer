Salicornia dolichostachya subsp. pojarkovae

Salicornia dolichostachya subsp.

pojarkovae, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed glasswort, is a halophyte species native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert environments and along coastal areas.

The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces dense clusters of tiny, pinkish flowers during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
