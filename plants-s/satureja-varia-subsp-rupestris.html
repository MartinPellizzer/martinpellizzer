Satureja varia subsp. rupestris

Satureja varia subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as rock savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though its fragrance is more subtle compared to other members of the genus. Satureja varia subsp.

rupestris is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
