Saxifraga redofskyi

Saxifraga redofskyi, commonly known as the Redofsky's saxifrage, is a rare alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it survive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which reduces water loss in arid conditions.

Saxifraga redofskyi is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes where it can anchor itself securely.
