Struthanthus buxifolius

Struthanthus buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood plants. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a short, branched stem.

Struthanthus buxifolius is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
