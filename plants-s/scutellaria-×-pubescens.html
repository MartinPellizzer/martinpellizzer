Scutellaria × pubescens

Scutellaria × pubescens, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Scutellaria × pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
