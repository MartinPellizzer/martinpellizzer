Stellaria × ambigua

Stellaria × ambigua, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, white or pink flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a hairy texture, and the plant often forms dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in gardens, lawns, and along pathways.

Stellaria × ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
