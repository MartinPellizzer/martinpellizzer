Scilla revoluta

Scilla revoluta, commonly known as the purple scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to their attractive blooms.

Scilla revoluta is relatively easy to cultivate and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
