Salix kawajanagii

Salix kawajanagii, commonly known as Kawaja willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, slightly glossy surface.

Salix kawajanagii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
