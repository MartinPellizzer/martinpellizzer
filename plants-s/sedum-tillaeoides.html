Sedum tillaeoides

Sedum tillaeoides, commonly known as the blue sedum, is a succulent plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum tillaeoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
