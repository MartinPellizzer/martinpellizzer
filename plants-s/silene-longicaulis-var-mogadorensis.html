Silene longicaulis var. mogadorensis

Silene longicaulis var. mogadorensis, commonly known as the Mogador's slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution.
