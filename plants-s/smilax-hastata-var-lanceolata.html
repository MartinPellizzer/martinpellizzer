Smilax hastata var. lanceolata

Smilax hastata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its thorny stems and adaptability make it a resilient plant in various ecological settings.
