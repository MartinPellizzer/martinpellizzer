Solanum apiculatibaccatum

Solanum apiculatibaccatum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
