Saxifraga hoffmeisteri

Saxifraga hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pink or white petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
