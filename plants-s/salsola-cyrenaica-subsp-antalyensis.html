Salsola cyrenaica subsp. antalyensis

Salsola cyrenaica subsp.

antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. Its structure includes a low-growing habit with branching stems and clustered inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
