Setaria caudata var. pauciflora

Setaria caudata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and slender, drooping inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
