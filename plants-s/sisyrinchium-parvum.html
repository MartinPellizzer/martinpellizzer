Sisyrinchium parvum

Sisyrinchium parvum, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers are composed of three petals and three sepals, typical of the Iridaceae family.

Sisyrinchium parvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
