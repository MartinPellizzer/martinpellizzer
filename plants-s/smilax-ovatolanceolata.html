Smilax ovatolanceolata

Smilax ovatolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catbrier, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, spiny berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax ovatolanceolata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
