Siebera compressa

Siebera compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf siebera, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Siebera compressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
