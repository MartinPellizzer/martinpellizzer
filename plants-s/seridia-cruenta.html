Seridia cruenta

Seridia cruenta, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Seridia cruenta is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
