Saussurea subsinuata

Saussurea subsinuata, commonly known as the Himalayan swamp saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to reddish floral heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, mountainous environments.

Due to its unique ecological niche and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
