Salvia cerinopruinosa

Salvia cerinopruinosa, commonly known as the silky purple sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Salvia cerinopruinosa is also a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and shrubs.
