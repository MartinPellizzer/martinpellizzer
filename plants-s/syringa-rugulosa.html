Syringa rugulosa

Syringa rugulosa, commonly known as meadow hyacinth, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in late spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This plant is often used in naturalized landscapes for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
