Sporobolus congoensis

Sporobolus congoensis, commonly known as Congo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Sporobolus congoensis is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
