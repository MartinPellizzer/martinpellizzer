Salix reinii f. aomoriensis

Salix reinii f. aomoriensis, commonly known as the Aomori willow, is a rare shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
