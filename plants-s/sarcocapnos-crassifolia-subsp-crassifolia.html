Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp. crassifolia

Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sarcocapnos, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.
