Sorghum nutans var. stipoides

Sorghum nutans var. stipoides, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and as a forage crop.
