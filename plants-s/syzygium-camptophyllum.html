Syzygium camptophyllum

Syzygium camptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
