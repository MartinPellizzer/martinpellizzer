Stachys recta var. chrysophaea

Stachys recta var. chrysophaea, commonly known as golden hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
