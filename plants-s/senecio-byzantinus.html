Senecio byzantinus

Senecio byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in shades of yellow and white.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
