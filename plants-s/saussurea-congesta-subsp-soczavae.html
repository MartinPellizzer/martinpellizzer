Saussurea congesta subsp. soczavae

Saussurea congesta subsp.

soczavae, commonly known as the Soczavae Globe Thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spherical, spiky inflorescence and dark blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
