Shorea laxa

Shorea laxa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and is known for its tall, straight trunk. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.
