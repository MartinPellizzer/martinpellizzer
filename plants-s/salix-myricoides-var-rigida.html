Salix myricoides var. rigida

Salix myricoides var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, stiff leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in riparian zones. Its botanical structure includes a flexible stem with a greyish-bark, and it is adapted to withstand periodic flooding.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for wildlife.
