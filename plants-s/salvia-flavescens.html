Salvia flavescens

Salvia flavescens, commonly known as yellow sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
