Senecio otophorus var. panduratus

Senecio otophorus var. panduratus, commonly known as the Blueweed or Bluecup Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
