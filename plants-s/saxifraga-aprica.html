Saxifraga aprica

Saxifraga aprica, commonly known as the spring saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in crevices or on rocky slopes. It is characterized by its ability to withstand cold temperatures and is often found in mountainous regions.

Saxifraga aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
