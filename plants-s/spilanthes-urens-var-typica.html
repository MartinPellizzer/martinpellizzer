Spilanthes urens var. typica

Spilanthes urens var. typica, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its strong, pungent odor and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant contains compounds that can cause a tingling or numbing sensation when touched or ingested.
