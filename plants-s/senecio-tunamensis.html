Senecio tunamensis

Senecio tunamensis, commonly known as the Tunamensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with hairy, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, dry fruits called cypselae.

Senecio tunamensis is well-suited to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.
