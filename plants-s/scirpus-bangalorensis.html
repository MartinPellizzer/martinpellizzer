Scirpus bangalorensis

Scirpus bangalorensis, commonly known as the Bangalore bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern India.

It typically grows in shallow waters or moist soils, thriving in environments with high humidity and moderate temperatures. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes produce small, clustered brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus bangalorensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
