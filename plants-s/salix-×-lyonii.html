Salix × lyonii

Salix × lyonii, commonly known as the Lyon's willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often silvery-white underneath. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to the western United States and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

Salix × lyonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
