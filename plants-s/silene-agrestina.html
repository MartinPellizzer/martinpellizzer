Silene agrestina

Silene agrestina, commonly known as the field catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene agrestina is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
