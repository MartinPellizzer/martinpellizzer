Swertia hookeri

Swertia hookeri, commonly known as Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Swertia hookeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
