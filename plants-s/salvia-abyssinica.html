Salvia abyssinica

Salvia abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while the stems are woody and branching.

Salvia abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
