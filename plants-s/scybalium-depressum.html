Scybalium depressum

Scybalium depressum, commonly known as the dwarf scybalium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in clusters.

Scybalium depressum is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its ornamental potential in shaded garden settings.
