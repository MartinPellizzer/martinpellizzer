Spiraea × concinna

Spiraea × concinna, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to gardens.

Spiraea × concinna is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
