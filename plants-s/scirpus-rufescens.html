Scirpus rufescens

Scirpus rufescens, commonly known as redstem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that range in color from green to reddish-brown, especially when mature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, typically in late summer. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, making it a common species in marshes, ponds, and other wetland habitats.

Scirpus rufescens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
