Sedum gypsicola subsp. truwcriae

Sedum gypsicola subsp.

truwcriae, commonly known as the gypsum sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils, particularly in environments with high levels of gypsum.

This subspecies is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in dry, sunny habitats.
