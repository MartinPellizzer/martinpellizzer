Salomonia seguinii

Salomonia seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's salvia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
