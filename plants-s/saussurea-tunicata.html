Saussurea tunicata

Saussurea tunicata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower or tibetan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a single, terminal capitulum. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

Saussurea tunicata is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
