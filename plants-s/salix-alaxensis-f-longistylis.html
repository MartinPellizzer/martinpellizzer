Salix alaxensis f. longistylis

Salix alaxensis f. longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled willow, is a rare and unique variant of the Alaska willow.

It is characterized by its elongated stamens, which distinguish it from other forms of Salix alaxensis. This plant typically grows in alpine or subarctic regions, where it thrives in cold, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of silvery hairs.

The long-styled willow plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
