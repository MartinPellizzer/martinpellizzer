Selaginella polycephala

Selaginella polycephala, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the spore-producing organs. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte phase, typical of bryophyte-like reproduction in vascular plants.

Selaginella polycephala plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
