Sauropus ceratogynum

Sauropus ceratogynum, commonly known as the horned pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
