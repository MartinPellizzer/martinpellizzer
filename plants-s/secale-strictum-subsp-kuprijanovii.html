Secale strictum subsp. kuprijanovii

Secale strictum subsp.

kuprijanovii, commonly known as the Kuprijanovii ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish spikelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
