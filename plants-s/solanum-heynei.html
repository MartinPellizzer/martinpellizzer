Solanum heynei

Solanum heynei, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are yellow or orange berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum heynei is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
