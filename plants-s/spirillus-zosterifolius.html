Spirillus zosterifolius

Spirillus zosterifolius, commonly known as the ribbon leaf spirillus, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant's leaves are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, providing a striking visual effect in water gardens. Spirillus zosterifolius is known for its ability to absorb excess nutrients, making it beneficial for water quality management.

However, it can become invasive if not controlled, as it spreads rapidly through vegetative propagation.
