Scilla scilloides f. albiflora

Scilla scilloides f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered scilla, is a botanical variant of the scilla genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year in suitable conditions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to naturalize in mild climates.
