Solanum vaccinioides var. muhlenbeckiifolium

Solanum vaccinioides var. muhlenbeckiifolium, commonly known as the muhlenbeckiifolium variant of the waxleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by yellow berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in subtropical climates.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
