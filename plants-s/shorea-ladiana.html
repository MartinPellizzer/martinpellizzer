Shorea ladiana

Shorea ladiana, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and typically grows in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed.
