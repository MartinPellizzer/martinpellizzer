Saldinia axillaris

Saldinia axillaris, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Saldinia axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
