Sida cydoniifolia

Sida cydoniifolia, commonly known as the appleleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Sida cydoniifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
