Serapias lancifera

Serapias lancifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Serapias lancifera is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.
