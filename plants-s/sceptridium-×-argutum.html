Sceptridium × argutum

Sceptridium × argutum, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit with fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used in horticulture.
