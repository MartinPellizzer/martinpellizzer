Siparuna episciifolia

Siparuna episciifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or white-flowered siparuna, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are often accompanied by a strong, sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Siparuna episciifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
