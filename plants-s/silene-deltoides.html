Silene deltoides

Silene deltoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Silene deltoides is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
