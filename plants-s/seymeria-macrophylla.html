Seymeria macrophylla

Seymeria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, typically blooming at night. Seymeria macrophylla thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize the sediment and improve water quality in its ecosystem.
