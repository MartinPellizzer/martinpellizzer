Stylocoryna refracta

Stylocoryna refracta, commonly known as the glassy tree, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that resemble glass, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Stylocoryna refracta is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
