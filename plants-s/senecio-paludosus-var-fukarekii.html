Senecio paludosus var. fukarekii

Senecio paludosus var. fukarekii, commonly known as the Fukarek's swamp senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from a rhizomatous base, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in elongated clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to its wetland habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
