Stereospermum ghorta

Stereospermum ghorta, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
