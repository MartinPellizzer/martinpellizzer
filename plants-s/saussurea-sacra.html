Saussurea sacra

Saussurea sacra, commonly known as the sacred saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive yellow-orange resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and religious practices. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a complex inflorescence structure.

Saussurea sacra is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in various regions.
