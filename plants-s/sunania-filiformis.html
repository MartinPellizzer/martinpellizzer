Sunania filiformis

Sunania filiformis, commonly known as the slender sunania, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
