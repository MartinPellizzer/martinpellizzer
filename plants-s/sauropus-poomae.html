Sauropus poomae

Sauropus poomae, commonly known as the Poomae sarsaparilla, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by three-lobed capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
