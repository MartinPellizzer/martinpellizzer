Sorbus commixta var. typica

Sorbus commixta var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a finely toothed margin. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest and hardiness.
