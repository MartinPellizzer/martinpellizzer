Suaeda physophora var. papillosa

Suaeda physophora var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. Its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is commonly found in coastal and inland salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
