Solanum subscandens

Solanum subscandens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It features woody stems that can grow along the ground or climb other plants, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids.

Solanum subscandens is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.
