Secale strictum subsp. balcanum

Secale strictum subsp.

balcanum, commonly known as the Balkan ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
