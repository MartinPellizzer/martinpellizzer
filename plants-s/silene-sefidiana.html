Silene sefidiana

Silene sefidiana, commonly known as the Persian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Silene sefidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
