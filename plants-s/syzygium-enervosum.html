Syzygium enervosum

Syzygium enervosum, commonly known as the pink-veined tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and elliptical leaves with prominent pink veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium enervosum is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
