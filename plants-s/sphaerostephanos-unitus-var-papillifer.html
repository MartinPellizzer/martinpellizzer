Sphaerostephanos unitus var. papillifer

Sphaerostephanos unitus var. papillifer, commonly known as the Papillifer Caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, ball-shaped fruit that resembles a caltrop, with four hooked bracts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
