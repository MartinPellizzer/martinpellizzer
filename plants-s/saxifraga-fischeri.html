Saxifraga fischeri

Saxifraga fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Saxifraga fischeri is often found in mountainous areas and is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.
