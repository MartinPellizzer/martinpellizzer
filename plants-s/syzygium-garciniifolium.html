Syzygium garciniifolium

Syzygium garciniifolium, commonly known as the purple-leafed tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
