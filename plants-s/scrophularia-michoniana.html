Scrophularia michoniana

Scrophularia michoniana, commonly known as the Michon's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
