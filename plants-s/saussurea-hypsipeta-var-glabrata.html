Saussurea hypsipeta var. glabrata

Saussurea hypsipeta var. glabrata, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense inflorescences of yellow to orange-colored florets. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are rich in saffron-like compounds, making it valuable for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.
