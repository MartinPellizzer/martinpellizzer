Selliguea membranacea var. fluminalis

Selliguea membranacea var. fluminalis, commonly known as the river moss, is a variant of the common moss Selliguea membranacea.

It is a small, acrocarpous moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in wetlands. The plant has a delicate, filamentous appearance with a dense, cushion-like growth form. Its leaves are narrow and often recurved, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This variant is well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
