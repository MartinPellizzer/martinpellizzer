Stipa kuhitangi

Stipa kuhitangi, commonly known as the Kuhitangi needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and forest margins. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
