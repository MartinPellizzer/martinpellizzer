Scorzonera cana

Scorzonera cana, commonly known as the white scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features a taproot that is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a white center.

Scorzonera cana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
