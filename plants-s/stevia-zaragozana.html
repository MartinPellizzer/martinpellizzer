Stevia zaragozana

Stevia zaragozana, commonly known as Zaragoza stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners that are much sweeter than table sugar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Due to its medicinal and commercial potential, it has gained interest in herbal and natural product industries.
