Sorbus torminalis f. pisifera

Sorbus torminalis f. pisifera, commonly known as the whitebeams or white-leafed whitebeam, is a horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green leaves that contrast with its small, yellowish fruits. This plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The foliage and fruit make it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
