Spergularia nicaeensis

Spergularia nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Spergularia nicaeensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.
