Sanguisorba hybrida

Sanguisorba hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Sanguisorba hybrida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
