Scirpus angulatus

Scirpus angulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of green to brown. Scirpus angulatus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
