Streptocarpus semijunctus

Streptocarpus semijunctus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and tubular, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or purple. The plant features ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Streptocarpus semijunctus is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
