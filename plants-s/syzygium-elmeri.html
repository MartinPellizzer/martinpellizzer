Syzygium elmeri

Syzygium elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour-sweet berries.

Syzygium elmeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
