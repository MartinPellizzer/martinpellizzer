Solanum pinetorum

Solanum pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple solanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Solanum pinetorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
