Sphaeropteris fusca

Sphaeropteris fusca, commonly known as the broad-leafed cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length, providing a striking visual presence. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
