Sasa neoyutakana

Sasa neoyutakana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish-green hue.

Sasa neoyutakana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and traditional landscaping.
