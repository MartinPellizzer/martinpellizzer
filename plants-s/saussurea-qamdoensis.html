Saussurea qamdoensis

Saussurea qamdoensis, commonly known as the Qamdo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
