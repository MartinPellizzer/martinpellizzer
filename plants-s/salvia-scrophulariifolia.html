Salvia scrophulariifolia

Salvia scrophulariifolia, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is valued in both horticulture and native plant landscaping.
