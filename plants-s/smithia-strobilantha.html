Smithia strobilantha

Smithia strobilantha, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive cone-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters.

Smithia strobilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
