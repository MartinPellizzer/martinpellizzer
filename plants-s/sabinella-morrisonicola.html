Sabinella morrisonicola

Sabinella morrisonicola, commonly known as the Morrison's sabinella, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a unique reproductive system involving wind pollination.
