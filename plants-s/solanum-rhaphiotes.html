Solanum rhaphiotes

Solanum rhaphiotes, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink and white. The plant produces egg-shaped, waxy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

Solanum rhaphiotes belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
