Schinus weinmanniifolia f. glabrescens

Schinus weinmanniifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Brazilian pepper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants known for their toxic compounds and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
