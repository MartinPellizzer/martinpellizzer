Solanum bojeri var. houyanum

Solanum bojeri var. houyanum, commonly known as the Houyan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
