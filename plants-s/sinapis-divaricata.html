Sinapis divaricata

Sinapis divaricata, commonly known as the wild mustard or field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. Sinapis divaricata is native to Europe and western Asia but has been naturalized in various parts of the world.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
