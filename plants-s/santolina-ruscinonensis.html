Santolina ruscinonensis

Santolina ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Sicilian santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are gray-green and emit a strong, pleasant scent when crushed.

Santolina ruscinonensis is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant in Mediterranean climates.
