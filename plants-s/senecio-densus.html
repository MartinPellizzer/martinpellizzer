Senecio densus

Senecio densus, commonly known as the dense senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio densus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
