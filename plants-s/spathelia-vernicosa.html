Spathelia vernicosa

Spathelia vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy spathelia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence that consists of a single, elongated spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Spathelia vernicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
