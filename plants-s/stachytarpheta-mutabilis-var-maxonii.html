Stachytarpheta mutabilis var. maxonii

Stachytarpheta mutabilis var. maxonii, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bracts that surround the small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
