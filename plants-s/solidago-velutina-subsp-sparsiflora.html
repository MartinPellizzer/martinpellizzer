Solidago velutina subsp. sparsiflora

Solidago velutina subsp.

sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparseflower goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
