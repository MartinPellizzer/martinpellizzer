Setaria glauca var. pallide-fusca

Setaria glauca var. pallide-fusca, commonly known as silvergrass or pale-fusca grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the upper surface, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
