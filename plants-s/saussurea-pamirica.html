Saussurea pamirica

Saussurea pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are often harvested for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Saussurea pamirica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local herbal medicine.
