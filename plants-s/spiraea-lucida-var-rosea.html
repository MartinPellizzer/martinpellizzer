Spiraea lucida var. rosea

Spiraea lucida var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with oval to elliptical leaves that are typically a glossy green, turning red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers in early summer, which are borne on the previous year's wood. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular ornamental shrub in landscaping.
