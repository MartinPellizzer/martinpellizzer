Solanum hasslerianum

Solanum hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
