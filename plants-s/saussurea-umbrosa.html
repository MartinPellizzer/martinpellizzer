Saussurea umbrosa

Saussurea umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a composite head, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
