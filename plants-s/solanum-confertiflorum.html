Solanum confertiflorum

Solanum confertiflorum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
