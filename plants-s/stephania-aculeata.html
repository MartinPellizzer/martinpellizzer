Stephania aculeata

Stephania aculeata, commonly known as the prickly or hairy stephania, is a climbing plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stephania aculeata is native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.
