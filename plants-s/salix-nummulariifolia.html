Salix nummulariifolia

Salix nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
