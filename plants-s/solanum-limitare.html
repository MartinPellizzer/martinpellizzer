Solanum limitare

Solanum limitare, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
