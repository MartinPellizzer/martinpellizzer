Saxifraga canaliculata f. vulgaris

Saxifraga canaliculata f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink or red.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early spring blooms.
