Silene acaulis var. bryoides

Silene acaulis var. bryoides, commonly known as the cushion pink, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant is adapted to rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and closely arranged, contributing to its cushion-like appearance.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
