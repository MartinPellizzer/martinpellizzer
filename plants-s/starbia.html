Starbia

Starbia, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its star-shaped, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun or partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers range in color from white to deep purple.

Starbia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
