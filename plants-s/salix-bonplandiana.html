Salix bonplandiana

Salix bonplandiana, commonly known as the Bonpland willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, often swampy habitats and is adapted to wetland environments.

Salix bonplandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
