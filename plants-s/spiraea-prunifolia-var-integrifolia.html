Spiraea prunifolia var. integrifolia

Spiraea prunifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with smooth margins and a glossy green color. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal interest.
