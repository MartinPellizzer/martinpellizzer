Spermacoce suaveolens

Spermacoce suaveolens, commonly known as sweet false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, smooth-seeded fruits that are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Spermacoce suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
