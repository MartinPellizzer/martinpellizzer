Senecio hintonii

Senecio hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
