Solanum maglia var. guaytecarum

Solanum maglia var. guaytecarum, commonly known as the Guaytecarum nightshade, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and oval, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
