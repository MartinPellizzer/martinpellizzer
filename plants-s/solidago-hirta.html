Solidago hirta

Solidago hirta, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its hairy leaves and sturdy growth make it easily identifiable.

Solidago hirta plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
