Sporobolus glaucus

Sporobolus glaucus, commonly known as bluegrass dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Sporobolus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
