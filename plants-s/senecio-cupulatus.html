Senecio cupulatus

Senecio cupulatus, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its scientific name reflects its historical association with the Crown of Thorns used in Christian tradition.

Senecio cupulatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
