Salix pedicellaris var. hypoglauca

Salix pedicellaris var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, narrow leaves that are often a pale green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude ecosystems.
