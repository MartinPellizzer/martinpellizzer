Sempervivum arvernense var. glabrescens

Sempervivum arvernense var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Alpilles houseleek, is a low-growing succulent plant native to southern France.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
