Salacia volubilis

Salacia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing salacia, is a vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth habit and long, flexible stems that allow it to climb and entangle other plants. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by bright orange berries that are edible but have a slightly bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salacia volubilis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
