Sium graveolens

Sium graveolens, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source by some indigenous communities.

Sium graveolens has a distinctive musty odor, which is more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.
