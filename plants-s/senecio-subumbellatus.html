Senecio subumbellatus

Senecio subumbellatus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticultural enthusiasts.
