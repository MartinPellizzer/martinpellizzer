Senecio mezquitalanus

Senecio mezquitalanus, commonly known as the Mexican ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio mezquitalanus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments with minimal water.
