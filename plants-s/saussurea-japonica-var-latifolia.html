Saussurea japonica var. latifolia

Saussurea japonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
