Salvia patens

Salvia patens, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a minty aroma. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia patens is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
