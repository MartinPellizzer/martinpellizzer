Sasaella masamuneana

Sasaella masamuneana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Masamune bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasaella masamuneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
