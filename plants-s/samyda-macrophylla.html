Samyda macrophylla

Samyda macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating canopy on the water's surface. The plant's roots anchor in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, while its stems support the massive leaves above. Samyda macrophylla produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and shade for various aquatic life.
