Senna cuthbertsonii

Senna cuthbertsonii, commonly known as the Cuthbertson's senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Senna cuthbertsonii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
