Sasaella agrestis

Sasaella agrestis, commonly known as the rough sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow, rough in texture, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella agrestis is valued in traditional medicine and is also used as a decorative plant in horticulture.
