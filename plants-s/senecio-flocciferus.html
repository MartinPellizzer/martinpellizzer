Senecio flocciferus

Senecio flocciferus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
