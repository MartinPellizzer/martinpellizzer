Spiraea argunensis

Spiraea argunensis, commonly known as the Argun spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, providing a textured appearance.

Spiraea argunensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
