Scrophularia aquatica subsp. jallui

Scrophularia aquatica subsp.

jallui, commonly known as the water figwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
