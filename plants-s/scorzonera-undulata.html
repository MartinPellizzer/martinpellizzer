Scorzonera undulata

Scorzonera undulata, commonly known as the wavy scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of elongated, wavy-edged leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera undulata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
