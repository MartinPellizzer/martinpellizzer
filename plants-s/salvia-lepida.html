Salvia lepida

Salvia lepida, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, gray-green leaves and produces striking violet to blue-purple flower clusters. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
