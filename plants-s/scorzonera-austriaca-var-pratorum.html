Scorzonera austriaca var. pratorum

Scorzonera austriaca var. pratorum, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. The plant produces a thick, edible root that is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera austriaca var. pratorum is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
