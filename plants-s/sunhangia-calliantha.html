Sunhangia calliantha

Sunhangia calliantha, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the vine's stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Sunhangia calliantha is also valued for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
