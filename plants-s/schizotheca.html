Schizotheca

Schizotheca, commonly known as the "pennyroyal" or "false pennyroyal," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Schizotheca species are native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

They are often found in dry, rocky habitats and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
