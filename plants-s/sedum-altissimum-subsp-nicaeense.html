Sedum altissimum subsp. nicaeense

Sedum altissimum subsp.

nicaeense, commonly known as the Nicaean sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically blue-gray, adapted to store water and withstand arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
