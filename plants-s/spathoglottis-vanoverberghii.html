Spathoglottis vanoverberghii

Spathoglottis vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's spathoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its tropical environment. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive lip, and it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

This orchid is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
