Silene yarmalii var. yarmalii

Silene yarmalii var. yarmalii, commonly known as Yarmal's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

Silene yarmalii var. yarmalii is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
