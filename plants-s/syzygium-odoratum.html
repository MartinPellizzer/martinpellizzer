Syzygium odoratum

Syzygium odoratum, commonly known as the white cempaka or frangipani, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a smooth texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Syzygium odoratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
