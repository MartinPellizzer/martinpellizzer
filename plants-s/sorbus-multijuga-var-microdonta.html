Sorbus multijuga var. microdonta

Sorbus multijuga var. microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
