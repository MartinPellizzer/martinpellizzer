Symphyotrichum tenuifolium var. aphyllum

Symphyotrichum tenuifolium var. aphyllum, commonly known as the slenderleaf heath aster, is a variant of the heath aster species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in open, dry habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. It has a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, and its flowers are usually pale to dark purple in color.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
