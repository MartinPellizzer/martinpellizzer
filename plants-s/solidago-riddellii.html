Solidago riddellii

Solidago riddellii, commonly known as Riddell's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Riddell's goldenrod produces showy yellow inflorescences in late summer to early autumn, contributing to its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
