Stichoneuron bognerianum

Stichoneuron bognerianum, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and whorled leaves, which are remnants of its evolutionary adaptations. This plant played a significant role in the formation of coal deposits due to its abundance and rapid growth. Its structure suggests it was a vascular plant with a complex system for water and nutrient transport.

Fossil records of Stichoneuron bognerianum provide valuable insights into ancient ecosystems and early plant evolution.
