Silene saxifraga var. triflora

Silene saxifraga var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
