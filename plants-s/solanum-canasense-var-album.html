Solanum canasense var. album

Solanum canasense var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and the fruits are small, green berries that turn red when mature.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
