Solanum cavanillesii

Solanum cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing toxic seeds.

Solanum cavanillesii is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
