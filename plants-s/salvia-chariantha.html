Salvia chariantha

Salvia chariantha, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia chariantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
