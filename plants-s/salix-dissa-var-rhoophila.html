Salix dissa var. rhoophila

Salix dissa var. rhoophila, commonly known as the river willow, is a variant of the willow genus native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow catkins. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found along riverbanks and stream edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dull green upper surface.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
