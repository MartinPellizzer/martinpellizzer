Stryphnodendron orinocense

Stryphnodendron orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy, making it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
