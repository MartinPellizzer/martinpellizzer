Scutellaria immaculata

Scutellaria immaculata, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. Scutellaria immaculata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
