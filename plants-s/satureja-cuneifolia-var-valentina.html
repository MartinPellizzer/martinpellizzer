Satureja cuneifolia var. valentina

Satureja cuneifolia var. valentina, commonly known as Valentina savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its sharp, peppery aroma.

Valentina savory is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
