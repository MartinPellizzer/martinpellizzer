Salix × mairei

Salix × mairei, commonly known as the Maire willow, is a hybrid species belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Salix × mairei is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
