Schizachyrium crinizonatum

Schizachyrium crinizonatum, commonly known as silky grass blazingstar, is a tall, upright perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-blue foliage that turns reddish-brown in the fall, and its fluffy, pinkish-white seed heads are prominent in late summer and early fall. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects. It is drought-tolerant once established and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
