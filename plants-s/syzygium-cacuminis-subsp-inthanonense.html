Syzygium cacuminis subsp. inthanonense

Syzygium cacuminis subsp.

inthanonense, commonly known as the Inthanon rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its unique scent and durability.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine and woodworking.
