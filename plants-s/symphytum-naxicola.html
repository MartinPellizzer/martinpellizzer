Symphytum naxicola

Symphytum naxicola, commonly known as the Sicilian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems topped with clusters of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
