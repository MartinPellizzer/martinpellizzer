Silene axillaris

Silene axillaris, commonly known as the rockpink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Silene axillaris is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or arid habitats.
