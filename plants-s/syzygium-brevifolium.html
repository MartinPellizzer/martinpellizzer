Syzygium brevifolium

Syzygium brevifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional cuisine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
