Sporobolus australasicus

Sporobolus australasicus, commonly known as the Australian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its seeds are dispersed by wind due to the presence of specialized structures called awns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Sporobolus australasicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
