Stenandrium pedunculatum

Stenandrium pedunculatum, commonly known as the red-veined stenandrium, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is mainly due to its unique foliage, making it a sought-after plant in tropical gardens.
