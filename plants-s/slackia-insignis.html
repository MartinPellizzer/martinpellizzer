Slackia insignis

Slackia insignis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing herb with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Slackia insignis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
