Senecio papyraceus

Senecio papyraceus, commonly known as the paperleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often brightly colored leaves that can range from green to yellow or red. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
