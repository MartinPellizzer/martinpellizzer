Scrophularia luridiflora

Scrophularia luridiflora, commonly known as the yellow scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia luridiflora is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
