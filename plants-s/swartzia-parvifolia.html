Swartzia parvifolia

Swartzia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered swartzia, is a tropical tree native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Swartzia parvifolia is typically found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
