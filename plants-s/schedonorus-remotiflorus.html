Schedonorus remotiflorus

Schedonorus remotiflorus, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are usually less than 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated.

Schedonorus remotiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
