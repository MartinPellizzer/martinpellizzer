Solanum pseudocapsicum

Solanum pseudocapsicum, commonly known as the nightshade or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
