Sonchus tenerrimus var. amicus

Sonchus tenerrimus var. amicus, commonly known as the "Friendly Beggar's Tick," is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has some ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
