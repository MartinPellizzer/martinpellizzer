Streptanthus glandulosus

Streptanthus glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Streptanthus glandulosus thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
