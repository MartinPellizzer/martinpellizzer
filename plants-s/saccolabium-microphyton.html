Saccolabium microphyton

Saccolabium microphyton, commonly known as the small-leaved saccolabium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its small size and adaptive features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
