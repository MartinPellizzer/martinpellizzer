Scirpus arvensis

Scirpus arvensis, commonly known as swamp couch grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and ditches. Its rhizomes are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Scirpus arvensis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
