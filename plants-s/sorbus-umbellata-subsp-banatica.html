Sorbus umbellata subsp. banatica

Sorbus umbellata subsp.

banatica, commonly known as the Bornean whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged, and its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring.

The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a red or orange color in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
