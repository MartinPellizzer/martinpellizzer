Sclerochloa multiculmis

Sclerochloa multiculmis, commonly known as the desert grass or sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Sclerochloa multiculmis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for livestock.
