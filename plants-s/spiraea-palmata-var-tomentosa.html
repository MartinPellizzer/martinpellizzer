Spiraea palmata var. tomentosa

Spiraea palmata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and turn a striking yellow in the fall.

Spiraea palmata var. tomentosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
