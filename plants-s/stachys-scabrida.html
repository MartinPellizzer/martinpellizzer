Stachys scabrida

Stachys scabrida, commonly known as rough hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Stachys scabrida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
