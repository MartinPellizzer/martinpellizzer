Salvia subpalmatinervis

Salvia subpalmatinervis, commonly known as the palmate-veined sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are typically violet to purple, clustered in whorls along the stem, and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia subpalmatinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
