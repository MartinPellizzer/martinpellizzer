Solidago arguta

Solidago arguta, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Solidago arguta is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various wetland environments.
