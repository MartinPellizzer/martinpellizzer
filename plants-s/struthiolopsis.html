Struthiolopsis

Struthiolopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with a single leaf and a flower that resembles a ghostly figure. Native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, it thrives in wet, swampy habitats. The plant is notable for its lack of a visible stem and its ability to grow epiphytically on trees.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Struthiolopsis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
