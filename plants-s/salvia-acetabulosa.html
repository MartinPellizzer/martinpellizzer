Salvia acetabulosa

Salvia acetabulosa, commonly known as the Mexican bush sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, minty fragrance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
