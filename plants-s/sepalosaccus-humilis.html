Sepalosaccus humilis

Sepalosaccus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf sepalosaccus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under dense forest canopies. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
