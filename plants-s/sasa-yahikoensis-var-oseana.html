Sasa yahikoensis var. oseana

Sasa yahikoensis var. oseana, commonly known as the Osean bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa yahikoensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cylindrical spikelets.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
