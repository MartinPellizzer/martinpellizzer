Sempervivum luxurians

Sempervivum luxurians, commonly known as the chicken's foot or the everlasting plant, is a succulent species native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, flowering stalks. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum luxurians is also known for its ability to form offsets, allowing it to spread and colonize new areas over time.
