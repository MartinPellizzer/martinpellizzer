Syzygium micropetalum

Syzygium micropetalum, commonly known as the small-petaled clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It produces small, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Syzygium micropetalum is typically found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
