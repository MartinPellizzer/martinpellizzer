Sycopsis griffithiana

Sycopsis griffithiana, commonly known as the Kashmir apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are prized for their sweet, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
