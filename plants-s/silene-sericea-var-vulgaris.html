Silene sericea var. vulgaris

Silene sericea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
