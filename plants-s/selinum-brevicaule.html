Selinum brevicaule

Selinum brevicaule, commonly known as narrow-leaved wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves with a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Selinum brevicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
