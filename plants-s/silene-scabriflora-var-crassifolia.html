Silene scabriflora var. crassifolia

Silene scabriflora var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
