Stevia entreriensis f. typica

Stevia entreriensis f. typica, commonly known as the typical stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Its use in herbal medicine and as a natural sweetener has gained significant popularity worldwide.
