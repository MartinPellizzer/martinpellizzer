Solidago insularis

Solidago insularis, commonly known as the island goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting well to saline environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Solidago insularis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
