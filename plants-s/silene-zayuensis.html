Silene zayuensis

Silene zayuensis, commonly known as the Yunnan campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene zayuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
