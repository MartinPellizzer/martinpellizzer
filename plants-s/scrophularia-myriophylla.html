Scrophularia myriophylla

Scrophularia myriophylla, commonly known as the mountain scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in fine hairs.

Scrophularia myriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
