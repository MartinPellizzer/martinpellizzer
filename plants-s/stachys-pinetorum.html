Stachys pinetorum

Stachys pinetorum, commonly known as pine woods betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering spikes produce showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stachys pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
