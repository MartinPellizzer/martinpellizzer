Sykesia macrobotrys

Sykesia macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent lip and spurred petals. Sykesia macrobotrys thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a flagship species for the biodiversity of Madagascar's unique ecosystems.
