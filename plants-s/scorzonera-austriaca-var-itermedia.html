Scorzonera austriaca var. itermedia

Scorzonera austriaca var. itermedia, commonly known as the Austrian scurvygrass, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a single head, and its roots are tuberous, which can be edible. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a food source.
