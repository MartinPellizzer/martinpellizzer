Stipa ruiz-lealii

Stipa ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Ruiz-Leal's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as Andean slopes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like structures that bear small, two-ranked flowers.

Stipa ruiz-lealii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
