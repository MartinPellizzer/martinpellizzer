Sebaea ramosissima

Sebaea ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf sebaea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Sebaea ramosissima is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
