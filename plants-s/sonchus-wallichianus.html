Sonchus wallichianus

Sonchus wallichianus, commonly known as the Indian oxeye, is an annual flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are broadly heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed but has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
