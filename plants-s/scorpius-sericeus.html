Scorpius sericeus

Scorpius sericeus, commonly known as the silky scorpion plant, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer, adding minimal visual impact but significant ecological value. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species from further decline.
