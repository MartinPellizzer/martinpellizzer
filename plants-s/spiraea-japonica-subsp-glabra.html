Spiraea japonica subsp. glabra

Spiraea japonica subsp.

glabra, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in early spring, usually in shades of white or pink. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
