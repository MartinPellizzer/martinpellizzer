Stipa yatesensis

Stipa yatesensis, commonly known as Yates' needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.

Stipa yatesensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
