Senna procumbens

Senna procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate senna, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Senna procumbens is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves and flowers used in traditional remedies.

It also serves as a valuable source of nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
