Saxifraga × angelisii

Saxifraga × angelisii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Saxifraga × angelisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
