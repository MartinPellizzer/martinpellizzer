Stenophyllus capillaris var. ciliatus

Stenophyllus capillaris var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy leaf stenophyllus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage with finely hairy margins, which give it its varietal name "ciliatus.". The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
