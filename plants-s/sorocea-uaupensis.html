Sorocea uaupensis

Sorocea uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside, which is a characteristic of the genus Sorocea. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Sorocea uaupensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or clayey soils.
