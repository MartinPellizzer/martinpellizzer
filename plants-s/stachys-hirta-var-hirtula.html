Stachys hirta var. hirtula

Stachys hirta var. hirtula, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
