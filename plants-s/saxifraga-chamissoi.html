Saxifraga chamissoi

Saxifraga chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and fragile species.
