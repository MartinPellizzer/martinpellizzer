Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. lanceolatum

Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink composite flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
