Solanum trinominum

Solanum trinominum, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often opening in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, yellowish seeds.

Solanum trinominum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
