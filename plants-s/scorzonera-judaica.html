Scorzonera judaica

Scorzonera judaica, commonly known as the Jewish scurvygrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive tubular shape, which bloom in early spring. Its roots are fleshy and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera judaica thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
