Saussurea gubanovii

Saussurea gubanovii, commonly known as the Siberian gromwell, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive bluish-green color with hairy surfaces.

Saussurea gubanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
