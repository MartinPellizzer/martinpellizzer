Scleria doradoensis

Scleria doradoensis, commonly known as the Golden Scleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically white or pale yellow, giving it its common name. Scleria doradoensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
