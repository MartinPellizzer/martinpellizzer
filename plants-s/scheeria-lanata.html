Scheeria lanata

Scheeria lanata, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scheeria lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
