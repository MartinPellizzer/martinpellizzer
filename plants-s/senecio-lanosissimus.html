Senecio lanosissimus

Senecio lanosissimus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio lanosissimus is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
