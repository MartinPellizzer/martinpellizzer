Sabulina gracilis

Sabulina gracilis, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Sabulina gracilis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
