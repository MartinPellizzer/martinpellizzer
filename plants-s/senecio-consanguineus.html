Senecio consanguineus

Senecio consanguineus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, spiky flower heads that resemble torches. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Senecio consanguineus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
