Sinocalamus rectocuneatus

Sinocalamus rectocuneatus, commonly known as the straight-edged bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive straight edge, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its structural strength and aesthetic appeal, it is valued both for practical applications and ornamental purposes.
