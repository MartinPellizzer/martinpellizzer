Sporobolus mobigiensis

Sporobolus mobigiensis, commonly known as the African speargrass, is a perennial grass native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions, making it adaptable to both arid and semi-arid environments. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in restoration projects.

Sporobolus mobigiensis is also valued for its forage quality and potential in sustainable agriculture.
