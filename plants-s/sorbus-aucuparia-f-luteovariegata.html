Sorbus aucuparia f. luteovariegata

Sorbus aucuparia f. luteovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated rowan, is a cultivar of the rowan tree characterized by its striking foliage.

The leaves exhibit a unique variegation pattern with yellow and green tones, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. It retains the typical small, red berries of the species, which are edible but often used for wildlife food. This variety thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small gardens and container cultivation.
