Spiranthes autumnalis f. bracteata

Spiranthes autumnalis f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its spiral arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spiral cluster of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. This terrestrial orchid typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as bogs and wet meadows.

It is a perennial plant that forms rhizomes and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
