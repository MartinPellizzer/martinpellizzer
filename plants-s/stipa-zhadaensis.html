Stipa zhadaensis

Stipa zhadaensis, commonly known as the Zhada grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet, China, where it thrives in cold and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Stipa zhadaensis plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
