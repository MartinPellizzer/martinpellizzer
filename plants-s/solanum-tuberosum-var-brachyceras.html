Solanum tuberosum var. brachyceras

Solanum tuberosum var. brachyceras, commonly known as the short-stemmed potato, is a variety of the potato plant characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to the standard potato.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces tubers that are generally smaller in size but can have a higher starch content. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and flowers that are typically white or violet in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique morphology and potential agricultural adaptability.
