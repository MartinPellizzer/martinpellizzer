Serjania psilophylla

Serjania psilophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Serjaniae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Serjania psilophylla is often found in lowland forests and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
