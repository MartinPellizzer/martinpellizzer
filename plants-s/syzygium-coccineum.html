Syzygium coccineum

Syzygium coccineum, commonly known as the red apple or red syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces edible, red, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to apples, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium coccineum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
