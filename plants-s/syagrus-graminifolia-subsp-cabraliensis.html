Syagrus graminifolia subsp. cabraliensis

Syagrus graminifolia subsp.

cabraliensis, commonly known as the Cabraliense palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves with a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its restricted habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
