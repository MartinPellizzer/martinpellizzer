Scorzonera franchetii

Scorzonera franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Scorzonera franchetii thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and medicinal studies.
