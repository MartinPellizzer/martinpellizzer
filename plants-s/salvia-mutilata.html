Salvia mutilata

Salvia mutilata, commonly known as the crimson sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
