Serjania cardiospermoides var. cardiospermoides

Serjania cardiospermoides var. cardiospermoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hillside areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
