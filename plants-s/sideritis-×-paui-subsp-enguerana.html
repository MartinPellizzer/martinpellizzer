Sideritis × paui subsp. enguerana

Sideritis × paui subsp.

enguerana, commonly known as the Greek mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems, hairy leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Sideritis species are valued for their medicinal properties, with this subspecies being used traditionally for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
