Scirpus acuminatus

Scirpus acuminatus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus acuminatus is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some cultures.
