Schefflera leptophylla

Schefflera leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
