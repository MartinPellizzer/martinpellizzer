Sedum × battandieri

Sedum × battandieri, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Sedum × battandieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
