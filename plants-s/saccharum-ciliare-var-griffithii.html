Saccharum ciliare var. griffithii

Saccharum ciliare var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's cane, is a variant of the sugarcane species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and broad, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in wetland areas. Its growth habit and morphology are similar to other sugarcane varieties, though it exhibits distinct genetic traits.

It is primarily valued for its potential in sugar production and as a forage crop in certain agricultural systems.
