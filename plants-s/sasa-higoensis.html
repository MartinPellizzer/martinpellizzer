Sasa higoensis

Sasa higoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Higo bamboo, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa higoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
