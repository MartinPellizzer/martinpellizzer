Sphaerostema propinquum

Sphaerostema propinquum, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers, which come in shades of purple, pink, and white, bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sphaerostema propinquum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
