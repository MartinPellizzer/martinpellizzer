Sinapis alba var. foliosa

Sinapis alba var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy white mustard, is a variant of the common mustard plant.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a short growing season and is an annual herb.

The leaves and seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
