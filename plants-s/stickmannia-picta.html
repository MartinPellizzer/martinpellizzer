Stickmannia picta

Stickmannia picta, commonly known as the painted stickman, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and segmented leaves that resemble the limbs of a human figure. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Stickmannia picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
