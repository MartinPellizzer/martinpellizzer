Sonchus adenophora

Sonchus adenophora, commonly known as the woolly beggarweed, is an annual herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its invasive potential in various ecosystems.

Botanically, it is recognized for its rapid growth and adaptability to a wide range of soil conditions.
