Sasa kurilensis f. konshima

Sasa kurilensis f. konshima, commonly known as the Konshima sasa, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical culms that are typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
