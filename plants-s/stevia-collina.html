Stevia collina

Stevia collina, commonly known as mountain stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and high-altitude environments. Its leaves are used to produce stevia sweeteners, which are naturally low in calories.

Stevia collina is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
