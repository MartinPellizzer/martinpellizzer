Salix hoppeana

Salix hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. Salix hoppeana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing stream banks.
