Senecio manitobensis

Senecio manitobensis, commonly known as Manitoba groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Canada, particularly Manitoba.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. It has alternate, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Senecio manitobensis is often found in prairie ecosystems and is adapted to the region's climate and soil conditions.
