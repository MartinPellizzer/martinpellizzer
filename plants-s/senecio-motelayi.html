Senecio motelayi

Senecio motelayi, commonly known as the Motelayi groundsel, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
