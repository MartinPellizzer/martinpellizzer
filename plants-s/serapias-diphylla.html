Serapias diphylla

Serapias diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Serapias diphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
