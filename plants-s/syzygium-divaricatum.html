Syzygium divaricatum

Syzygium divaricatum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

Syzygium divaricatum is often found in rainforest and coastal scrub habitats.
