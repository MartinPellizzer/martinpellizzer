Schizoglossum parcum

Schizoglossum parcum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Schizoglossum parcum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
