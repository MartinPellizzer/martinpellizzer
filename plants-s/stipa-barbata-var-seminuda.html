Stipa barbata var. seminuda

Stipa barbata var. seminuda, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, thread-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
