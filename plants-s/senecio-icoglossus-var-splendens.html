Senecio icoglossus var. splendens

Senecio icoglossus var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It is a succulent perennial, often growing in a compact, bushy form.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
