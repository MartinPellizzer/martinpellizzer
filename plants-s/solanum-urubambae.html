Solanum urubambae

Solanum urubambae, commonly known as the Urubamba nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often purple or white in color.

Solanum urubambae is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
