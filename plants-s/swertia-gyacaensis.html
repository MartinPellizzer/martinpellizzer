Swertia gyacaensis

Swertia gyacaensis, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Swertia gyacaensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in Tibetan medicine.
