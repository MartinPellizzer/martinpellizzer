Stellarioides gambosana

Stellarioides gambosana, commonly known as the Cape sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Stellarioides gambosana is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
