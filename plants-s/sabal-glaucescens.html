Sabal glaucescens

Sabal glaucescens, commonly known as the Florida state tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a slender trunk with smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.

Sabal glaucescens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
