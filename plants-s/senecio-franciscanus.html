Senecio franciscanus

Senecio franciscanus, commonly known as the Franciscan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to California, specifically found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Senecio franciscanus is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in well-drained soils.

It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
