Scirpus savii var. gracillimus

Scirpus savii var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus savii var. gracillimus is often found in marshes, pond margins, and along the edges of lakes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
