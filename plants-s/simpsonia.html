Simpsonia

Simpsonia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals and a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soil. Simpsonia produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
