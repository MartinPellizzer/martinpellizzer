Stelis salpingantha

Stelis salpingantha, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, hood-like flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a prominent lip that enhances its visual appeal.

Stelis salpingantha has lanceolate leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
