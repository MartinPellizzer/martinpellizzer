Solanum dunalianum var. puberius

Solanum dunalianum var. puberius, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features ovate to elliptical leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The species belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
