Senecio canescens var. macrocephalus

Senecio canescens var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grey-green, woolly leaves and produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
