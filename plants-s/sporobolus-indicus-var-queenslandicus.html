Sporobolus indicus var. queenslandicus

Sporobolus indicus var. queenslandicus, commonly known as Queensland false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sandy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its native range.
