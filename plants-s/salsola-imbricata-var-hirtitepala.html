Salsola imbricata var. hirtitepala

Salsola imbricata var. hirtitepala, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and saline environments.

It features a low-growing, mat-like growth form with densely packed, fleshy, spiny stems and leaves. The leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its hirtitepala variety name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This plant is often found in desert regions and is well-suited for stabilizing sandy soils.
