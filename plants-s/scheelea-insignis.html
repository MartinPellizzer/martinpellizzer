Scheelea insignis

Scheelea insignis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Scheelea insignis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
