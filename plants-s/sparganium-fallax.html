Sparganium fallax

Sparganium fallax, commonly known as the fragrant bur reed, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spiky, brownish flowers that mature into woody, cone-like structures. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Sparganium fallax is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
