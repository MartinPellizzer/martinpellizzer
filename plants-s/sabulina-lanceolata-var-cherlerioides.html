Sabulina lanceolata var. cherlerioides

Sabulina lanceolata var. cherlerioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ability to tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
