Schomburgkia galeottiana var. kimballiana

Schomburgkia galeottiana var. kimballiana, commonly known as Kimball's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, slender, spider-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered endangered in its natural range.
