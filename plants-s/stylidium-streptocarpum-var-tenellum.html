Stylidium streptocarpum var. tenellum

Stylidium streptocarpum var. tenellum, commonly known as the small-stemmed triggerplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a member of the family Haemodoraceae and is known for its unique trigger mechanism that facilitates pollination.
