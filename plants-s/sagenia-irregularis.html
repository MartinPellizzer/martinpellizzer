Sagenia irregularis

Sagenia irregularis, commonly known as the irregular sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sagenia irregularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
