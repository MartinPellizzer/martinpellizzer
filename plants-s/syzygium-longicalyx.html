Syzygium longicalyx

Syzygium longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaf clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, spherical berries.

Syzygium longicalyx thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
