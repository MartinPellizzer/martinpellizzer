Satureja grandiflora subsp. baborensis

Satureja grandiflora subsp.

baborensis, commonly known as the Babar Valley savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
