Silene graminifolia var. lychnidea

Silene graminifolia var. lychnidea, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with thin, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, and it is pollinated primarily by insects.

Silene graminifolia var. lychnidea is known for its hardy nature and is often found in tundra and alpine meadows.
