Salvia sphacelioides subsp. trianae

Salvia sphacelioides subsp.

trianae, commonly known as Trian's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native plant gardens.
