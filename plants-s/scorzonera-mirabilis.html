Scorzonera mirabilis

Scorzonera mirabilis, commonly known as the miracle root or alpine salsify, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Scorzonera mirabilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
