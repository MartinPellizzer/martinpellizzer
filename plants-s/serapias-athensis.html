Serapias athensis

Serapias athensis, commonly known as the Aethiopian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Serapias, which is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant features showy, butterfly-shaped flowers with a distinctive yellow and orange coloration. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

Serapias athensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
