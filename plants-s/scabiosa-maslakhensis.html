Scabiosa maslakhensis

Scabiosa maslakhensis, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa maslakhensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
