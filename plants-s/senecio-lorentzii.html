Senecio lorentzii

Senecio lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, blue-green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio lorentzii is often found in grasslands and shrublands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
