Sorbus aucuparia f. subintegra

Sorbus aucuparia f. subintegra, commonly known as the white-fruited rowan, is a horticultural form of the common rowan.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow berries, which contrast with the red berries of the typical species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 2 to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and unique fruiting characteristics.
