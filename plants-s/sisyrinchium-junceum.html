Sisyrinchium junceum

Sisyrinchium junceum, commonly known as slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sisyrinchium junceum is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
