Saccharum versicolor

Saccharum versicolor, commonly known as rainbow cane, is a striking member of the grass family, Poaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that display a range of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and purple. The plant produces long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Saccharum versicolor is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential in sustainable landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
