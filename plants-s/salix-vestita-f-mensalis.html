Salix vestita f. mensalis

Salix vestita f. mensalis, commonly known as the silver willow or white willow, is a botanical variant of the willow tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The plant typically grows along waterways and in moist soils, showcasing its preference for wetland habitats. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
