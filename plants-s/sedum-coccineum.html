Sedum coccineum

Sedum coccineum, commonly known as red stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant red flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
