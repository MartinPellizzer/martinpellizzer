Senegalia hoehnei

Senegalia hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the legume family. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Senegalia hoehnei is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
