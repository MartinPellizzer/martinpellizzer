Sapium biglandulosum var. serratum

Sapium biglandulosum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it the species name "biglandulosum.".

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
