Scirpus rhodesicus

Scirpus rhodesicus, commonly known as Rhodes bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stems, typically blooming in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats, which help stabilize wetland soils.

Scirpus rhodesicus is adapted to wet or waterlogged environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
