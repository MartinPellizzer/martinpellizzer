Stachys cooperi

Stachys cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale purple to pinkish flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Stachys cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
