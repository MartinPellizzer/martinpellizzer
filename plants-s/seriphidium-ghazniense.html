Seriphidium ghazniense

Seriphidium ghazniense, commonly known as the Ghazni thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid climates. It features a tall, spiny stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, making it a distinctive and resilient plant in its native environment.
