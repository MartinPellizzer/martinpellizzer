Spergularia media subsp. sauvagei

Spergularia media subsp.

sauvagei, commonly known as the coastal sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand salty air and strong coastal winds.
