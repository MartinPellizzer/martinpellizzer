Stipa melanosperma var. erythrina

Stipa melanosperma var. erythrina, commonly known as red-stemmed needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and its distinctive red-brown stems that become more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
