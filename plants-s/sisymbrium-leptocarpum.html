Sisymbrium leptocarpum

Sisymbrium leptocarpum, commonly known as slenderpod wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that dehisces to release the seeds.

The leaves are usually alternate and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
