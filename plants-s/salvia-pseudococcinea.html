Salvia pseudococcinea

Salvia pseudococcinea, commonly known as desert sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and spikes of showy violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and heat resistance. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
