Sabatia pumila

Sabatia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sabatia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
