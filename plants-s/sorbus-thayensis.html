Sorbus thayensis

Sorbus thayensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
