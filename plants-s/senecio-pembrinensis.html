Senecio pembrinensis

Senecio pembrinensis, commonly known as the Pembrin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features small, daisy-like flowers that are usually yellow or white in color.

Senecio pembrinensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
