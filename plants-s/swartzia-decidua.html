Swartzia decidua

Swartzia decidua, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It is often found in dry savannas and is valued for its durable timber and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
