Sobralia triandra

Sobralia triandra, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has long, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Sobralia triandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
