Solanum brenesii

Solanum brenesii, commonly known as the Brenes's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
