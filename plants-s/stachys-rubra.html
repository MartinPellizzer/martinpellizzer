Stachys rubra

Stachys rubra, commonly known as redstem betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Stachys rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
