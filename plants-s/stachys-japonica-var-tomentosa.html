Stachys japonica var. tomentosa

Stachys japonica var. tomentosa, commonly known as Japanese hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
