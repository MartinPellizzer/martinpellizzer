Swartzia leptopetala

Swartzia leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-petaled swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Swartzia leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
