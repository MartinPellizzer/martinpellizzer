Syzygium cochinchinense

Syzygium cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese rose or white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and leathery elliptical leaves. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
