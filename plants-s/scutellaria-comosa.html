Scutellaria comosa

Scutellaria comosa, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Scutellaria comosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
