Salix × pocutica

Salix × pocutica, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. Salix × pocutica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet or moist environments make it a common choice for riparian zones and erosion control.
