Senecio conchilobus

Senecio conchilobus, commonly known as the spiral senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
