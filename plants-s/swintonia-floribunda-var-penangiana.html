Swintonia floribunda var. penangiana

Swintonia floribunda var. penangiana, commonly known as the Penang hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring thick, dark green leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers with a red throat. The plant thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
