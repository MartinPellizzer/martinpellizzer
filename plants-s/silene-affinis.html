Silene affinis

Silene affinis, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from June to August.

Silene affinis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
