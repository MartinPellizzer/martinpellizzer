Stemonoporus gracilis

Stemonoporus gracilis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
