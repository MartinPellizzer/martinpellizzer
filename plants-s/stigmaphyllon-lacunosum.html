Stigmaphyllon lacunosum

Stigmaphyllon lacunosum, commonly known as the laceleaf stigmaphyllon, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves with a distinctive lacey appearance due to its finely divided lobes. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling a cluster of tiny bells. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Stigmaphyllon lacunosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
