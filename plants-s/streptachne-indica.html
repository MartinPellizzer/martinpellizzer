Streptachne indica

Streptachne indica, commonly known as Indian panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
