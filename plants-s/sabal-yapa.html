Sabal yapa

Sabal yapa, commonly known as the Florida palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a straight trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to coastal and wetland environments. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous, spongy material.

Sabal yapa plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
