Scirpus baldwinianus

Scirpus baldwinianus, commonly known as Baldwin's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with dense, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Scirpus baldwinianus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
