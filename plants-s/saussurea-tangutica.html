Saussurea tangutica

Saussurea tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, woolly leaves that are adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

Saussurea tangutica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
