Scirpus valdivianus

Scirpus valdivianus, commonly known as the Valdivian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
