Solanum loxense

Solanum loxense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Solanum loxense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
