Serjania meridionalis

Serjania meridionalis, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Serjania meridionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
