Schizachyrium filiforme

Schizachyrium filiforme, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass that thrives in prairie and woodland environments.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive, slender, hair-like leaves. This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or loamy soils. During the fall, it turns a striking copper or bronze color, adding seasonal visual interest.

Schizachyrium filiforme plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
