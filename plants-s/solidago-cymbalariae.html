Solidago cymbalariae

Solidago cymbalariae, commonly known as the lesser goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can reach up to 10 centimeters in length.

Solidago cymbalariae is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting pollinators during the fall season.
