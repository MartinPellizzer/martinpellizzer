Styrax mathewsii

Styrax mathewsii, commonly known as the Chinese styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Styrax mathewsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
