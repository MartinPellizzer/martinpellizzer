Sanicula canadensis var. typica

Sanicula canadensis var. typica, commonly known as Canadian sanguisorb.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
