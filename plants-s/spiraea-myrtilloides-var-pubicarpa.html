Spiraea myrtilloides var. pubicarpa

Spiraea myrtilloides var. pubicarpa, commonly known as the pubescent spiraea, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
