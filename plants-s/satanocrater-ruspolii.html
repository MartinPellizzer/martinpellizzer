Satanocrater ruspolii

Satanocrater ruspolii, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that resemble a claw, which help in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The genus name "Satanocrater" refers to its devilish appearance, while "ruspolii" honors the Italian botanist who first described it.
