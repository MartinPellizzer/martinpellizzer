Salix hupehensis

Salix hupehensis, commonly known as the Weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, winged seeds.

Salix hupehensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and ability to thrive in moist environments.
