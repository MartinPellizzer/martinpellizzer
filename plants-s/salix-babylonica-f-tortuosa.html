Salix babylonica f. tortuosa

Salix babylonica f. tortuosa, commonly known as the weeping willow with twisted branches, is a decorative cultivar of the willow tree.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that droop in a weeping manner, creating a striking visual effect. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a light green color that turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
