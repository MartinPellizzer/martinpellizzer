Sobralia aerata

Sobralia aerata, commonly known as the skyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features elongated, pendulous flower spikes that can reach up to several feet in length. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a unique structure that resembles a cluster of small, delicate blooms.

Sobralia aerata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
