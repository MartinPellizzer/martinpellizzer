Sedum sexfidum

Sedum sexfidum, commonly known as the six-leaf stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a succulent species, characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing yellowish-green flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually arranged in whorls of six, giving it its common name.

Sedum sexfidum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
