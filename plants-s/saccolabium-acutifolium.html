Saccolabium acutifolium

Saccolabium acutifolium, commonly known as the sharpleaf saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Saccolabium acutifolium is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
