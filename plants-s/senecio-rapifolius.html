Senecio rapifolius

Senecio rapifolius, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Senecio rapifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
