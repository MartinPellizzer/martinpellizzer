Shorea maxwelliana

Shorea maxwelliana, commonly known as Maxwell's rosewood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
