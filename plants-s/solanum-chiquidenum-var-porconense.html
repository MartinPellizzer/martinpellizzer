Solanum chiquidenum var. porconense

Solanum chiquidenum var. porconense, commonly known as the Porconense nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a red or yellow color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
