Saussurea semilyrata

Saussurea semilyrata, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea semilyrata is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
