Strelitzia reginae subsp. mzimvubuensis

Strelitzia reginae subsp.

mzimvubuensis, commonly known as the Mzimvubu strelitzia, is a unique variant of the bird-of-paradise plant native to South Africa. It is distinguished by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical reginae subspecies.

The plant produces striking, orange-and-blue inflorescences that resemble a bird in flight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
