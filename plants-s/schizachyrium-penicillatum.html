Schizachyrium penicillatum

Schizachyrium penicillatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in prairie and open woodland habitats.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that turn reddish-brown in the fall. This plant is drought-tolerant and plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a valuable component of native plant communities.

Schizachyrium penicillatum is also used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
