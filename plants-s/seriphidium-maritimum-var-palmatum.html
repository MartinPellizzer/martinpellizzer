Seriphidium maritimum var. palmatum

Seriphidium maritimum var. palmatum, commonly known as the Mediterranean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with dense, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family, which are followed by small, woody, spiny fruits. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This plant is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, showing resistance to drought and salt spray.
