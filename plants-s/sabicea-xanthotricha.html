Sabicea xanthotricha

Sabicea xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired sabicea, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in lowland forests and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
