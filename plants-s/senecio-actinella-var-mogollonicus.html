Senecio actinella var. mogollonicus

Senecio actinella var. mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. This perennial plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
