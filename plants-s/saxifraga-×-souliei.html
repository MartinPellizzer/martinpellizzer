Saxifraga × souliei

Saxifraga × souliei, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a hybrid evergreen perennial shrub native to China.

It features dense, compact growth with dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
