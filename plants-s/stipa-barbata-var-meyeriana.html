Stipa barbata var. meyeriana

Stipa barbata var. meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's feather grass, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
