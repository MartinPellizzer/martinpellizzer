Soldanella pusilla var. carestiae

Soldanella pusilla var. carestiae, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
