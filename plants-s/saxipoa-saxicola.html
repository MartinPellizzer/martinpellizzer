Saxipoa saxicola

Saxipoa saxicola, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a hardy plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Saxipoa saxicola is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and characteristic species of its ecosystem.
