Silene sibirica

Silene sibirica, commonly known as Siberian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and meadows. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Silene sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
