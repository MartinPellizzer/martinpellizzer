Sasa permadescens

Sasa permadescens, commonly known as the everlasting bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly rough texture.

Sasa permadescens is valued for its durability and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
