Securidaca elliptica

Securidaca elliptica, commonly known as the African arrowroot or wild arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. The roots are harvested and processed for use in herbal remedies and as a food source in some regions.
