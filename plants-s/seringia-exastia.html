Seringia exastia

Seringia exastia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf seringia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Seringia exastia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
