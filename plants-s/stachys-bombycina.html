Stachys bombycina

Stachys bombycina, commonly known as woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine, woolly hairs.

Stachys bombycina is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
