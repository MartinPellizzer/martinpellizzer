Saccus chaplasha

Saccus chaplasha, commonly known as the Indian rose or chaplasha, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
