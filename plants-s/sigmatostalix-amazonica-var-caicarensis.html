Sigmatostalix amazonica var. caicarensis

Sigmatostalix amazonica var. caicarensis, commonly known as the Amazonian star-leaf plant, is a tropical species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used by indigenous communities for their anti-inflammatory and healing benefits.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Sigmatostalix.
