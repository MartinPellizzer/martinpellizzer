Satureja pamphylica

Satureja pamphylica, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications and have medicinal properties due to their high concentration of essential oils.
