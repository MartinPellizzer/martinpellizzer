Serratula melanocheila

Serratula melanocheila, commonly known as the black-veined sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a bluish or purplish tint, while the flowers are yellow with dark veins.

Serratula melanocheila is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
