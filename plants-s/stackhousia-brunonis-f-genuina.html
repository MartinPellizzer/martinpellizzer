Stackhousia brunonis f. genuina

Stackhousia brunonis f. genuina, commonly known as the Western Australian tea tree.

It is a shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
