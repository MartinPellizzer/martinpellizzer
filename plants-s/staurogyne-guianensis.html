Staurogyne guianensis

Staurogyne guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan starwort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the freshwater regions of South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes and ponds, where it forms dense underwater mats. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often floating or submerged, aiding in water oxygenation. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne on short stalks and are pollinated by insects.

Staurogyne guianensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
