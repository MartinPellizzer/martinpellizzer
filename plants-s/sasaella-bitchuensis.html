Sasaella bitchuensis

Sasaella bitchuensis, commonly known as the Bitchu bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and smooth. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
