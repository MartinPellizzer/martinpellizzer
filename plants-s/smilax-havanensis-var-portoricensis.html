Smilax havanensis var. portoricensis

Smilax havanensis var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican arrowleaf.

It is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean, characterized by its waxy, arrow-shaped leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Caribbean flora.
