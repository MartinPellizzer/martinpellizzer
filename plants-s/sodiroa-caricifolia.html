Sodiroa caricifolia

Sodiroa caricifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and is typically found in brackish water environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
