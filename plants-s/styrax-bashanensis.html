Styrax bashanensis

Styrax bashanensis, commonly known as the Bashan styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Styrax bashanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
