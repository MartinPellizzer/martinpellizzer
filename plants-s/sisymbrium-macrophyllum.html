Sisymbrium macrophyllum

Sisymbrium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and lobed, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as fields and roadsides. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
