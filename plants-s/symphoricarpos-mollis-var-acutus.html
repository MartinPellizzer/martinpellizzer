Symphoricarpos mollis var. acutus

Symphoricarpos mollis var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-fruited snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The leaves are ovate, with serrated edges, and the stems are typically covered in fine, white hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
