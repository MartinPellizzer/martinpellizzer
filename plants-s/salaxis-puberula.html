Salaxis puberula

Salaxis puberula, commonly known as the hairy salaxis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
