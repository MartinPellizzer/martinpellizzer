Smilax ecirrhata var. hugeri

Smilax ecirrhata var. hugeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, thorny stems that climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
