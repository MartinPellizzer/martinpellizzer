Senecio bodinieri var. elatissimus

Senecio bodinieri var. elatissimus, commonly known as the Giant Senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, woolly, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flower heads in late summer, which are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It is a succulent that stores water in its fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique form and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a specimen plant.
