Stachys pseudohumifusa

Stachys pseudohumifusa, commonly known as the purple carpet bugweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Stachys pseudohumifusa thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
