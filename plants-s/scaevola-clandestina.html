Scaevola clandestina

Scaevola clandestina, commonly known as the hidden fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small fans. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Scaevola clandestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
