Silene menziesii var. typica

Silene menziesii var. typica, commonly known as the Menzies' campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene menziesii var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
