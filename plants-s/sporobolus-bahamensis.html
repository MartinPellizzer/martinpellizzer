Sporobolus bahamensis

Sporobolus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian spikerush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in tropical wetland ecosystems.
