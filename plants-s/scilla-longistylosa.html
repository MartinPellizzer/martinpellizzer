Scilla longistylosa

Scilla longistylosa, commonly known as the long-styled scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Scilla longistylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
