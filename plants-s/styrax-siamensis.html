Styrax siamensis

Styrax siamensis, commonly known as the Siam styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fragrant resins.

Styrax siamensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
