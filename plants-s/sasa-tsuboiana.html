Sasa tsuboiana

Sasa tsuboiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or narrow-leaved sasa, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, arching culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa tsuboiana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, compact clumps.
