Stipa iberica f. pseudodasyphylla

Stipa iberica f. pseudodasyphylla, commonly known as the Iberian needlegrass, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of compact, cylindrical spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
