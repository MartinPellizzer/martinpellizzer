Saxifraga mucronulatoides

Saxifraga mucronulatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are generally narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Saxifraga mucronulatoides is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
