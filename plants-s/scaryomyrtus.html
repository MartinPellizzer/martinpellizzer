Scaryomyrtus

Scaryomyrtus, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a unique genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Scaryomyrtus species are typically shrubs or small trees, often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
