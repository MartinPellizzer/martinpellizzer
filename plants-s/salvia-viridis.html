Salvia viridis

Salvia viridis, commonly known as green sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a valued plant in traditional herbal practices.
