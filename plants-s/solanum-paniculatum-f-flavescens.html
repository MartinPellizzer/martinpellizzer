Solanum paniculatum f. flavescens

Solanum paniculatum f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a variant of the passionflower genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature prominent stamens and a central reproductive structure. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
