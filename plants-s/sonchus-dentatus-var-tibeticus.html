Sonchus dentatus var. tibeticus

Sonchus dentatus var. tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates. Its leaves are broadly lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a daisy-like pattern.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
