Scirpus asper

Scirpus asper, commonly known as rough arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. Scirpus asper thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds and lakes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
