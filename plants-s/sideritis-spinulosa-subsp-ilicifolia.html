Sideritis spinulosa subsp. ilicifolia

Sideritis spinulosa subsp.

ilicifolia, commonly known as the spiny sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced spines and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
