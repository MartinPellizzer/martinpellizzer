Stephanomeria pauciflora var. parishii

Stephanomeria pauciflora var. parishii, commonly known as Parish's narrowleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ecological significance in arid environments.
