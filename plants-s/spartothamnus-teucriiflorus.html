Spartothamnus teucriiflorus

Spartothamnus teucriiflorus, commonly known as desert gorse, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Spartothamnus teucriiflorus is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has deep root systems to access groundwater.
