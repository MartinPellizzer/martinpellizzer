Scabiosa tomentosa

Scabiosa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pink flower heads that resemble small clouds, surrounded by soft, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, well-drained environments.

Scabiosa tomentosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
