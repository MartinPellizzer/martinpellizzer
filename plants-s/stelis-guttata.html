Stelis guttata

Stelis guttata, commonly known as the spotted stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, fleshy stem with clustered, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, with prominent purple or brown markings.

Stelis guttata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
