Syringa vulgaris f. plena

Syringa vulgaris f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac that is prized for its dense, fragrant blossoms.

This variety features fully double flowers with numerous petals, creating a lush, showy display in late spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, with dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Syringa vulgaris f. plena is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
