Spiropetalum solanderi

Spiropetalum solanderi, commonly known as the Solander's spiral orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of petals, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
