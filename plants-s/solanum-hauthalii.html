Solanum hauthalii

Solanum hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum hauthalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
