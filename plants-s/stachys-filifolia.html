Stachys filifolia

Stachys filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairless stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow, thread-like, and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with blue or purple petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Stachys filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
