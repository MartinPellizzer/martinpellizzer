Saracha pallascana

Saracha pallascana, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Saracha pallascana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
