Stoebe vermiculata

Stoebe vermiculata, commonly known as slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Stoebe vermiculata is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
