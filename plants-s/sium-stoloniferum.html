Sium stoloniferum

Sium stoloniferum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, carrot-like roots and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels.

Sium stoloniferum is often found in temperate regions of North America and Asia.
