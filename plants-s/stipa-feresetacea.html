Stipa feresetacea

Stipa feresetacea, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. This plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing in open slopes and meadows at high elevations. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Stipa feresetacea plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
