Senecio farfarus

Senecio farfarus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio farfarus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
