Saponaria andicola

Saponaria andicola, commonly known as the Andean soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the genus Saponaria, which is part of the family Caryophyllaceae, known for its showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant features erect, branching stems and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saponaria andicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its saponin content.
