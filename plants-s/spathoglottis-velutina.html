Spathoglottis velutina

Spathoglottis velutina, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Spathoglottis velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
