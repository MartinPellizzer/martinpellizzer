Senna hirsuta var. leptocarpa

Senna hirsuta var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, ribbed pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Senna hirsuta var. leptocarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
