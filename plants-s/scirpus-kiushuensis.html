Scirpus kiushuensis

Scirpus kiushuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fibrous layer, and its leaves are long and narrow. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months.

Scirpus kiushuensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
