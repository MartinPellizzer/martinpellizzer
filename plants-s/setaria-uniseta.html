Setaria uniseta

Setaria uniseta, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

Its foliage is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
