Styrax glaber

Styrax glaber, commonly known as the smooth styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

Styrax glaber is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
