Sapium contortum

Sapium contortum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It has smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing sticky, oily seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
