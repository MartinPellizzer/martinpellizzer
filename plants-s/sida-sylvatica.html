Sida sylvatica

Sida sylvatica, commonly known as woodland sida, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, hairy texture.

Sida sylvatica plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often found in early successional habitats.
