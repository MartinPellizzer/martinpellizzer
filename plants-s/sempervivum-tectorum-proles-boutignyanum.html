Sempervivum tectorum proles boutignyanum

Sempervivum tectorum proles boutignyanum, commonly known as the Bouquet Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for alpine and xeriscaping gardens.
