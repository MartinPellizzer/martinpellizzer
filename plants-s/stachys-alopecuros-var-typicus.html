Stachys alopecuros var. typicus

Stachys alopecuros var. typicus, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common subject in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
