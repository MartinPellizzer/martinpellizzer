Stachys graeca

Stachys graeca, commonly known as Greek betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in soft, grayish hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Stachys graeca is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
