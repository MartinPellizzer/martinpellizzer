Sorbus aria subsp. latifolia

Sorbus aria subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
