Sanguisorba ancistroides var. aracneosa

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. aracneosa, commonly known as the redroot sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a fibrous, reddish root system.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
