Smilacina hondoensis

Smilacina hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo's greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Honduras.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the early spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Smilacina hondoensis is often found in humid, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
