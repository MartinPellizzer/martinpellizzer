Scorzonera inula

Scorzonera inula, commonly known as the lesser scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its root system is fibrous and somewhat tuberous.

Scorzonera inula is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
