Solanum tuberosum var. villaroella

Solanum tuberosum var. villaroella, commonly known as the Andean potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its tubers, which are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard variety. This plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers are usually blue or violet in color.

The tubers of this variety are valued for their unique flavor and are often used in traditional Andean cuisine.
