Sutera pauciflora

Sutera pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sutera, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale blue. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Sutera pauciflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
