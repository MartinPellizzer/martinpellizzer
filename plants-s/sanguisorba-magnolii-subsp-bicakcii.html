Sanguisorba magnolii subsp. bicakcii

Sanguisorba magnolii subsp.

bicakcii, commonly known as the Turkish sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
