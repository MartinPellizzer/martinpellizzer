Salsola hirsuta

Salsola hirsuta, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, hirsute hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Salsola hirsuta is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
