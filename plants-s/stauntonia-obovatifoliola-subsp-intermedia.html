Stauntonia obovatifoliola subsp. intermedia

Stauntonia obovatifoliola subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the broad-leafed stauntonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
