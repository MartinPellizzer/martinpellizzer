Silene inflata var. spathulata

Silene inflata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and branching.

Silene inflata var. spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
