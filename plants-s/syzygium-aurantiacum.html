Syzygium aurantiacum

Syzygium aurantiacum, commonly known as the red guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium aurantiacum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
