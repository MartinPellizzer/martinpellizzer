Serratula tinctoria var. vulpii

Serratula tinctoria var. vulpii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in dye-making and traditional medicine.
