Sansevieria ascendens

Sansevieria ascendens, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with yellow or white margins. Its air-purifying qualities have also made it a favored plant for improving indoor air quality.

Sansevieria ascendens produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the plant, often in late spring or early summer.
