Spiranthes pulchra

Spiranthes pulchra, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The showy lady's tresses is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
