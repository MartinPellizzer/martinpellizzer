Solanum gibbsiae

Solanum gibbsiae, commonly known as Gibbs' nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or violet. The plant produces small, round berries that are yellow when ripe, containing toxic seeds.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique floral structure.
