Stevia vacana

Stevia vacana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are intensely sweet due to the presence of steviol glycosides. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are harvested and used as a natural sweetener, offering a zero-calorie alternative to sugar.

Stevia vacana thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
