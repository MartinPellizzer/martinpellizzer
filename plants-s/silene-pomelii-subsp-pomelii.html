Silene pomelii subsp. pomelii

Silene pomelii subsp.

pomelii, commonly known as the Pomelii catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Silene pomelii subsp.

pomelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
