Sericographis cyrtantheriformis

Sericographis cyrtantheriformis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.
