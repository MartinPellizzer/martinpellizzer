Secale

Secale, commonly known as ryegrass, is a genus of grasses that includes several species widely used in agriculture.

These plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and dense, sometimes hairy foliage. They are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types. Ryegrass species are important for forage, erosion control, and as cover crops.

Their ability to thrive in both cool and warm climates makes them versatile in different agricultural systems.
