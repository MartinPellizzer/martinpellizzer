Senecio ligularioides

Senecio ligularioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower structure.

Senecio ligularioides is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
