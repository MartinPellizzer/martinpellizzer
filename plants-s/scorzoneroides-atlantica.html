Scorzoneroides atlantica

Scorzoneroides atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to saline and sandy soils, thriving in coastal dunes and rocky shores. It is a perennial species with a short growing season, typically flowering in late spring to early summer.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
