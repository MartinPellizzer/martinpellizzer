Sarcorhachis convallariodorum

Sarcorhachis convallariodorum, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Sarcorhachis convallariodorum is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
