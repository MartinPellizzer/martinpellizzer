Serratula tinctoria proles macrocephala

Serratula tinctoria proles macrocephala, commonly known as the giant head ragwort, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces a milky latex when damaged. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed and can be harmful to livestock.
