Smilacina trifolia

Smilacina trifolia, commonly known as the eastern false Solomon's seal, is a deciduous herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in forest understories.

Smilacina trifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
