Soleniscia pulchella

Soleniscia pulchella, commonly known as the delicate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Soleniscia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
