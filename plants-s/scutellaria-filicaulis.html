Scutellaria filicaulis

Scutellaria filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Scutellaria filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
