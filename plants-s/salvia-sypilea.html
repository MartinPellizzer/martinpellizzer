Salvia sypilea

Salvia sypilea, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
