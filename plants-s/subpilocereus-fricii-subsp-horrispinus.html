Subpilocereus fricii subsp. horrispinus

Subpilocereus fricii subsp.

horrispinus, commonly known as the Horr's spine cactus, is a small, columnar cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is typically found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant produces white, night-blooming flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
