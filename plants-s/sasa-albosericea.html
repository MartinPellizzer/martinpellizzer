Sasa albosericea

Sasa albosericea, commonly known as the silver-haired bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered with a distinctive silvery-white sheath. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, silky texture.

Sasa albosericea is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
