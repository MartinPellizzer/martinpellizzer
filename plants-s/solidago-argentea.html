Solidago argentea

Solidago argentea, commonly known as silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

Silverleaf goldenrod is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
