Salix pulchra var. looffiae

Salix pulchra var. looffiae, commonly known as the Looff's weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-green underside that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic beauty and is commonly used in landscaping and garden settings.
