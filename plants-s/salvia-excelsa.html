Salvia excelsa

Salvia excelsa, commonly known as the tall salvia or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Salvia excelsa thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
