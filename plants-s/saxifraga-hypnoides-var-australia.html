Saxifraga hypnoides var. australia

Saxifraga hypnoides var. australia, commonly known as the Australian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
