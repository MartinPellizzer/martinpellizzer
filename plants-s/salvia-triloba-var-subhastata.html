Salvia triloba var. subhastata

Salvia triloba var. subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
