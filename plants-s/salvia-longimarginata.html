Salvia longimarginata

Salvia longimarginata, commonly known as the long-margin sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
