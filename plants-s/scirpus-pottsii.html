Scirpus pottsii

Scirpus pottsii, commonly known as Potts' bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its tall, cylindrical flower spikes are usually greenish or reddish in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus pottsii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
