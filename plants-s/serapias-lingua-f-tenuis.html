Serapias lingua f. tenuis

Serapias lingua f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender-tongue orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structures that resemble a tongue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a contrasting lip, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This rare and ornamental species is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
