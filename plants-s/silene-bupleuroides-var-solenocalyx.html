Silene bupleuroides var. solenocalyx

Silene bupleuroides var. solenocalyx, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight bluish or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.
