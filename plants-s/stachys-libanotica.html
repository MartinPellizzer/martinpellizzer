Stachys libanotica

Stachys libanotica, commonly known as Lebanon betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are characterized by their blue to violet coloration.

Stachys libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
