Senecio tomentosus f. alabamensis

Senecio tomentosus f. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama woolyweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, white to grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a woolly appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
