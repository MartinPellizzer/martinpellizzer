Satureja alpina var. nebrodensis

Satureja alpina var. nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden savory, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, peppery aroma when crushed.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
