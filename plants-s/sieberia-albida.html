Sieberia albida

Sieberia albida, commonly known as the white sieberia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Sieberia albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
