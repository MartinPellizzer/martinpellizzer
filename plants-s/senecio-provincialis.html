Senecio provincialis

Senecio provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and serrated.

Senecio provincialis is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to arid conditions.
