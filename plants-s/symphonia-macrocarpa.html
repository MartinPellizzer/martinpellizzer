Symphonia macrocarpa

Symphonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in various tropical environments.
