Sinapis hispida

Sinapis hispida, commonly known as the hispid mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
