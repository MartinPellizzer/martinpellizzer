Silene melanopotamica

Silene melanopotamica, commonly known as the Black Potamica catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene melanopotamica is a perennial species that plays a role in its local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
