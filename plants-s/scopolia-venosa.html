Scopolia venosa

Scopolia venosa, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant has a tuberous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

Scopolia venosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
