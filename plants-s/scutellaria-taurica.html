Scutellaria taurica

Scutellaria taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features erect, branched stems covered in short, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Scutellaria taurica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
