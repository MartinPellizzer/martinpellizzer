Silene retroflexa

Silene retroflexa, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.
