Salacia venosa

Salacia venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble tamarinds, hence its common name. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
