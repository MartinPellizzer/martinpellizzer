Syzygium llanosii

Syzygium llanosii, commonly known as the Llanos apple or llanosii apple, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Llanos region of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Syzygium llanosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
