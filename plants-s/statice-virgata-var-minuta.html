Statice virgata var. minuta

Statice virgata var. minuta, commonly known as dwarf sea lavender, is a compact, flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiky blue or violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant species.

Its low-growing habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
