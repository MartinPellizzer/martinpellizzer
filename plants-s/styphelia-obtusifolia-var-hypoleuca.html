Styphelia obtusifolia var. hypoleuca

Styphelia obtusifolia var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-stemmed snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically a dull green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
