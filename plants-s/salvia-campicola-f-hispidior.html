Salvia campicola f. hispidior

Salvia campicola f. hispidior, commonly known as the hairy hill sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
