Stachys serbica

Stachys serbica, commonly known as Serbian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Stachys serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
