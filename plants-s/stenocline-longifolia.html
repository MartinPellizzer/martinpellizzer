Stenocline longifolia

Stenocline longifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf stenocline, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Stenocline longifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
