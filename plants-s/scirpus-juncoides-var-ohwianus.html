Scirpus juncoides var. ohwianus

Scirpus juncoides var. ohwianus, commonly known as the Ohwian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for waterfowl.
