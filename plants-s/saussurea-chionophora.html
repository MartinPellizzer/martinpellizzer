Saussurea chionophora

Saussurea chionophora, commonly known as the white snow saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense, fluffy white or pale yellow flower heads. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.

Saussurea chionophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
