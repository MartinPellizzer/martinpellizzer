Sedum nussbaumerianum

Sedum nussbaumerianum, commonly known as the Nussbaumer's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it store water efficiently.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to rocky gardens.
