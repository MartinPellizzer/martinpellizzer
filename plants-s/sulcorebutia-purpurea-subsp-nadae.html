Sulcorebutia purpurea subsp. nadae

Sulcorebutia purpurea subsp.

nadae, commonly known as the Nadae's sun rose, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often adorned with prominent, woolly spines.

The plant produces vibrant clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
