Sinapis nigra var. villosa

Sinapis nigra var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a type of dry, dehiscent fruit typical of the mustard family.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
