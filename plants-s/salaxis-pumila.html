Salaxis pumila

Salaxis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf salaxis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Salaxis pumila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
