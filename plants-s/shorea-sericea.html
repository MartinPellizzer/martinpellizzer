Shorea sericea

Shorea sericea, commonly known as the silky lauan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces heavy, fragrant fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local timber industries.

Shorea sericea plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
