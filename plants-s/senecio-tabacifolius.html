Senecio tabacifolius

Senecio tabacifolius, commonly known as the tobacco leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that resemble tobacco leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often growing in full sun.

Senecio tabacifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
