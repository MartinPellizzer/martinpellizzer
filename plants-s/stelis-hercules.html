Stelis hercules

Stelis hercules, commonly known as the Hercules stelis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in seasonal climates.

Its unique floral morphology and growth habits make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
