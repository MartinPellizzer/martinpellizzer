Saxifraga × iserana

Saxifraga × iserana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Saxifraga × iserana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
