Salvia candelabrum subsp. blancoana

Salvia candelabrum subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as the Blanco's candlestick sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that consist of multiple whorls of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
