Silene variegata var. samothracica

Silene variegata var. samothracica, commonly known as the Samothrace Silene, is a flowering plant native to the Greek island of Samothrace.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or pale green markings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
