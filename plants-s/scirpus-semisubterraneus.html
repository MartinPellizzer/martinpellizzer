Scirpus semisubterraneus

Scirpus semisubterraneus, commonly known as the partial subterranean bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters in marshy or aquatic environments. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots form extensive rhizomes, allowing it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Scirpus semisubterraneus plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
