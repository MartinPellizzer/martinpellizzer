Sipanea pubinoda

Sipanea pubinoda, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, pubescent leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its stems are flexible and often twine around supporting structures.

Sipanea pubinoda is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
