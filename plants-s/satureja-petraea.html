Satureja petraea

Satureja petraea, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Satureja petraea is valued for its fragrant foliage, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
