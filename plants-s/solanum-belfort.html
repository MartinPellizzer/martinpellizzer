Solanum belfort

Solanum belfort, commonly known as Belfort nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, green berries. These berries are toxic when unripe but become edible when fully mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
