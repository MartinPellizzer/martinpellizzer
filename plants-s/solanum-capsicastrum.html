Solanum capsicastrum

Solanum capsicastrum, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

The fruit is a small, yellowish, bell-shaped berry that ripens to orange or red, containing several seeds.
