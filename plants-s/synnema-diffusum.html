Synnema diffusum

Synnema diffusum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high air humidity to flourish. Its rhizomes are often covered in a fine, silky coating, which helps in water retention.

Synnema diffusum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in hanging baskets or as a terrarium plant.
