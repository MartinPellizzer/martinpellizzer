Salix purpurea var. rossmaessleri

Salix purpurea var. rossmaessleri, commonly known as the Rossmaessler willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and striking purple-tinged leaves that turn coppery in autumn. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
