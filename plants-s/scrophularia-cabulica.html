Scrophularia cabulica

Scrophularia cabulica, commonly known as the Afghan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and come in shades of pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia cabulica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
