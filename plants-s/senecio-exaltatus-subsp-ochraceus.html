Senecio exaltatus subsp. ochraceus

Senecio exaltatus subsp.

ochraceus, commonly known as the Orange-stemmed Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are typically covered in a yellowish or orange hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
