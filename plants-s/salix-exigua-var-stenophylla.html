Salix exigua var. stenophylla

Salix exigua var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf willow, is a variant of the common willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in width. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its botanical features include catkins and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring.
