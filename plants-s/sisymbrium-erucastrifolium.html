Sisymbrium erucastrifolium

Sisymbrium erucastrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves and tall, branching stems that bear small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and potential to become invasive, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
