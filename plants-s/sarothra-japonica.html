Sarothra japonica

Sarothra japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sarothra japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
