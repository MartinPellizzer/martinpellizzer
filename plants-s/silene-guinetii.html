Silene guinetii

Silene guinetii, commonly known as the Guinet's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Silene guinetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
