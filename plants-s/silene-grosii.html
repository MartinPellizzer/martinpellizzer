Silene grosii

Silene grosii, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Silene grosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
