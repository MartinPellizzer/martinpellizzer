Senecio kassnerianus

Senecio kassnerianus, commonly known as the Kassner's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, linear leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Senecio kassnerianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
