Scaevola parvifolia subsp. acuminata

Scaevola parvifolia subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a fan shape.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments. Scaevola parvifolia subsp.

acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
