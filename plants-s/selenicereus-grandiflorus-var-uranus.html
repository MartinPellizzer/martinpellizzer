Selenicereus grandiflorus var. uranus

Selenicereus grandiflorus var. uranus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has a unique structure, with a brightly colored upper portion known as the "pseudostem" and a green, photosynthetic base called the "gastropod.". It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
