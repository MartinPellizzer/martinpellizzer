Schisandra longipes

Schisandra longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled five-flavor vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are highly aromatic. Schisandra longipes is valued for its medicinal properties, with the berries containing compounds like schisandrin and other lignans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.
