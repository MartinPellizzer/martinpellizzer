Scirpus holoschoenus f. monosphaericus

Scirpus holoschoenus f. monosphaericus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This subspecies is typically found in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland biodiversity.
