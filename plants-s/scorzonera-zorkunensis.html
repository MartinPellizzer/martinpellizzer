Scorzonera zorkunensis

Scorzonera zorkunensis, commonly known as the Central Asian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow ray florets. The plant produces a stout, tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera zorkunensis thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine environments.
