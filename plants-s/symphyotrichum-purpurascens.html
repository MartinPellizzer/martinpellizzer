Symphyotrichum purpurascens

Symphyotrichum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its cone-shaped flower heads are surrounded by pale green bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
