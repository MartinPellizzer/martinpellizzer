Senecio infirmus

Senecio infirmus, commonly known as the brittlestem gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or orange centers, blooming in summer.

Senecio infirmus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
