Sedum jerzedowskii

Sedum jerzedowskii, commonly known as the Jerzedowski's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
