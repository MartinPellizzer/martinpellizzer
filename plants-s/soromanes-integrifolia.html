Soromanes integrifolia

Soromanes integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf soromanes, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
