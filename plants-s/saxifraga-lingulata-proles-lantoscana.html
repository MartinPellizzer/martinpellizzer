Saxifraga lingulata proles lantoscana

Saxifraga lingulata proles lantoscana, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
