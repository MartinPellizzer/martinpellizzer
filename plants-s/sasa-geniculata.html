Sasa geniculata

Sasa geniculata, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or sasa, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps of stout, somewhat triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles, followed by triangular, edible young shoots known as "bamboo shoots.". Sasa geniculata is often used in traditional Asian cuisine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions make it a versatile plant in various ecological settings.
