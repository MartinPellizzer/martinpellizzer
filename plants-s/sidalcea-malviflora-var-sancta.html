Sidalcea malviflora var. sancta

Sidalcea malviflora var. sancta, commonly known as the Santa Rosa soapweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
