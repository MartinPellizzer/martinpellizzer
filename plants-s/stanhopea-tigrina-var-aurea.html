Stanhopea tigrina var. aurea

Stanhopea tigrina var. aurea, commonly known as the golden tigrina orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variety is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals, which contrast beautifully with its deep green sepals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
