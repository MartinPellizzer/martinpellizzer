Syzygium robinsonii

Syzygium robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, red, apple-like berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Syzygium robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
