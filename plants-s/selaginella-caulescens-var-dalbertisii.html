Selaginella caulescens var. dalbertisii

Selaginella caulescens var. dalbertisii, commonly known as the Dalbert's spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, which are often covered in a thin layer of moss-like structures. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

This variety is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas.
