Solidago nepalensis

Solidago nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its bright yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago nepalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
