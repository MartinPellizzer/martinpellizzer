Sterculia pachyclados

Sterculia pachyclados, commonly known as the broad-leaved sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, leathery seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
