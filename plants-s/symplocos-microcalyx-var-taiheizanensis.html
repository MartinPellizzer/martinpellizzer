Symplocos microcalyx var. taiheizanensis

Symplocos microcalyx var. taiheizanensis, commonly known as the Taiheizan evergreen holly, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly found in China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a pale underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
