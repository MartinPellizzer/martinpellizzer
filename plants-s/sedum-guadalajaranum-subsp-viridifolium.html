Sedum guadalajaranum subsp. viridifolium

Sedum guadalajaranum subsp.

viridifolium, commonly known as the Greenleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green, sometimes with a bluish tint, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
