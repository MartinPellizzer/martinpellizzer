Syzygium cacuminis

Syzygium cacuminis, commonly known as the yellow rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish-purple fruit that turns yellow when mature. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and the edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor.
