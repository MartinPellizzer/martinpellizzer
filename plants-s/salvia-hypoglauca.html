Salvia hypoglauca

Salvia hypoglauca, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Salvia hypoglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
