Strophanthus pierrei

Strophanthus pierrei, commonly known as the African blood lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has a fibrous root system and can grow up to 3 meters tall, with palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators like birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
