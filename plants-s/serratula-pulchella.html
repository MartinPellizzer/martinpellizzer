Serratula pulchella

Serratula pulchella, commonly known as the showy serratula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head with both ray and disk florets.

Serratula pulchella is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
