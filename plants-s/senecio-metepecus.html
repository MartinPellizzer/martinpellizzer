Senecio metepecus

Senecio metepecus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.
