Stachys asperata

Stachys asperata, commonly known as rough hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Stachys asperata is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
