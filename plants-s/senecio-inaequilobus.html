Senecio inaequilobus

Senecio inaequilobus, commonly known as the unequal lobed groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaf lobes. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a clustered inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
