Serapias hirsuta var. reichenbachiana

Serapias hirsuta var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the hairy serapias, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Serapias, which is part of the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family. This variant is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in rocky and sunny habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

The plant is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
