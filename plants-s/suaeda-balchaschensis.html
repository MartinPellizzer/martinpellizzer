Suaeda balchaschensis

Suaeda balchaschensis, commonly known as the Central Asian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It thrives in coastal and inland salt marshes, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Suaeda balchaschensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
