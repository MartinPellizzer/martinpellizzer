Salvinia verticillata

Salvinia verticillata, commonly known as water fern, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that allow it to float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are typically found in clusters. It is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on water surfaces.

Salvinia verticillata is often used in aquariums and as a water garden plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
