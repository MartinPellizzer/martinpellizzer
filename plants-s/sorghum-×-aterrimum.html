Sorghum × aterrimum

Sorghum × aterrimum, commonly known as sudangrass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and large, serrated leaves. The plant is primarily cultivated for its forage value, providing nutritious feed for livestock. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy crop in various agricultural settings.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, sorghum and millet, which contribute to its adaptability and utility.
