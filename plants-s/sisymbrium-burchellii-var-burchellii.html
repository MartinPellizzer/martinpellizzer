Sisymbrium burchellii var. burchellii

Sisymbrium burchellii var. burchellii, commonly known as the African mustards, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender stems with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
