Saussurea tanakae var. intermedia

Saussurea tanakae var. intermedia, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a robust root system and produces dense, branched stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
