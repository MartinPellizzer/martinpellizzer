Solanum ahlbergii

Solanum ahlbergii, commonly known as the Ahlberg's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Solanum ahlbergii produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds, and it is often found in humid, tropical environments.
