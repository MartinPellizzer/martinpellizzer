Schinus terebinthifolia var. ternifolia

Schinus terebinthifolia var. ternifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or copaiba, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or trifoliolate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is often found in coastal and dry forest regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in traditional medicine.
