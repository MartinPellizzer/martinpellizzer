Salvia angustifolia var. glabra

Salvia angustifolia var. glabra, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
