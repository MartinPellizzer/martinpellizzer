Swartzia apetala var. glabra

Swartzia apetala var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth swartzia, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
