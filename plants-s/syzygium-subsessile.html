Syzygium subsessile

Syzygium subsessile, commonly known as the slender apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

Syzygium subsessile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
