Scandix australis var. ambiguum

Scandix australis var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Australian scurvy grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically thrives in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often covered in fine hairs.

Scandix australis var. ambiguum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
