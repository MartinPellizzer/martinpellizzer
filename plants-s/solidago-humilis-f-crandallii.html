Solidago humilis f. crandallii

Solidago humilis f. crandallii, commonly known as the Crandall's goldenrod, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
