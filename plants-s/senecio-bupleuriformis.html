Senecio bupleuriformis

Senecio bupleuriformis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant has silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
