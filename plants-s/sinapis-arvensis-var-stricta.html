Sinapis arvensis var. stricta

Sinapis arvensis var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops, it is considered a weed in many regions.
