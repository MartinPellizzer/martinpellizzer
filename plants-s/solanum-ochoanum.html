Solanum ochoanum

Solanum ochoanum, commonly known as the Ochoan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum ochoanum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
