Scilla jacquinii

Scilla jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Scilla jacquinii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early bloom.
