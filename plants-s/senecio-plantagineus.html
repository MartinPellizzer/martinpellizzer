Senecio plantagineus

Senecio plantagineus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio plantagineus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
