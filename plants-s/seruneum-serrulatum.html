Seruneum serrulatum

Seruneum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated seruneum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
