Senecio divisorius

Senecio divisorius, commonly known as the blue-green chandelier plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and delicate yellow flowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Senecio divisorius prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
