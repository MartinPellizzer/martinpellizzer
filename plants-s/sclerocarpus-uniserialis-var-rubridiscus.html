Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. rubridiscus

Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. rubridiscus, commonly known as the red disk lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, disc-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a bulbous underground stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clumps.

The botanical features of this species make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to arid landscapes.
