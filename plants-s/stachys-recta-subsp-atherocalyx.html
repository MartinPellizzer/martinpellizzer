Stachys recta subsp. atherocalyx

Stachys recta subsp.

atherocalyx, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
