Silene maritima subsp. islandica

Silene maritima subsp.

islandica, commonly known as the coastal campion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the British Isles and parts of northern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along sea cliffs and dunes.

The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray. Silene maritima subsp.

islandica is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, maritime environments.
