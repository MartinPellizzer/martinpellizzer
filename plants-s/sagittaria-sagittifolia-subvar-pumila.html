Sagittaria sagittifolia subvar. pumila

Sagittaria sagittifolia subvar. pumila, commonly known as the small arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in a spike, blooming in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
