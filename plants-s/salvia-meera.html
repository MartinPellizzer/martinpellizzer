Salvia meera

Salvia meera, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

Salvia meera thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
