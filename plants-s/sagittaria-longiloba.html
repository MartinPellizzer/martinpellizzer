Sagittaria longiloba

Sagittaria longiloba, commonly known as the longleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Sagittaria longiloba is also an important food source for various aquatic animals and birds.
