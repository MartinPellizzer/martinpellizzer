Sedum album var. erythranthum

Sedum album var. erythranthum, commonly known as the red-flowered stonecrop, is a variant of the common stonecrop.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that features fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy, red-pink flowers. Native to parts of Europe, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and full sun. The plant is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
