Syzygium baladense

Syzygium baladense, commonly known as the Baladense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its botanical features include a smooth, grey-brown bark and a dense, spreading canopy.

Syzygium baladense is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
