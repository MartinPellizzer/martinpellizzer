Spiraea lancifolia f. intermedia

Spiraea lancifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early to mid-spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
