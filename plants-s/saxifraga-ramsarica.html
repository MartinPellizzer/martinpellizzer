Saxifraga ramsarica

Saxifraga ramsarica, commonly known as the Ramsar saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
