Suaeda kareliniana

Suaeda kareliniana, commonly known as the Karelin's saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Central Asia, particularly regions with arid and semi-arid climates. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Suaeda kareliniana plays a role in stabilizing soil in saline habitats and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
