Stapelia macowanii

Stapelia macowanii, commonly known as the macowan's stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive, hooded flowers. The plant produces large, waxy flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color, with a strong, unpleasant odor resembling decaying flesh. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Stapelia macowanii is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to dry climates.
