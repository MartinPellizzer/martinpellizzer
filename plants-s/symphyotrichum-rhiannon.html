Symphyotrichum rhiannon

Symphyotrichum rhiannon, commonly known as Rhiannon's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Symphyotrichum rhiannon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
