Strychnos grandis

Strychnos grandis, commonly known as the African star apple or *Euclea delgadii*, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
