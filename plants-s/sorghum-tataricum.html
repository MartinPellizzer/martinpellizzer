Sorghum tataricum

Sorghum tataricum, commonly known as prairie grass or Tatarian sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers that are often pink to red in hue, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in various climates.

Sorghum tataricum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
