Saxifraga calopetala

Saxifraga calopetala, commonly known as the showy saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are succulent and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Saxifraga calopetala is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
