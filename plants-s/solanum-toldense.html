Solanum toldense

Solanum toldense, commonly known as the Toldense nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant contains toxic compounds typical of the Solanaceae family.

It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, such as parts of Chile and Argentina.
