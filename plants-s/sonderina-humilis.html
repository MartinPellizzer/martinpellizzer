Sonderina humilis

Sonderina humilis, commonly known as the dwarf sonderia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the warmer months. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with similar growth habits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
