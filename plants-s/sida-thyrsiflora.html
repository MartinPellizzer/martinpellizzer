Sida thyrsiflora

Sida thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thornless cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and coastal areas.

Sida thyrsiflora is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
