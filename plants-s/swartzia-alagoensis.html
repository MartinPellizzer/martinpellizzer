Swartzia alagoensis

Swartzia alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
