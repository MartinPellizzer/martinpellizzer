Seseli coloratum var. asperulum

Seseli coloratum var. asperulum, commonly known as the rough blue bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-shaped blooms.
