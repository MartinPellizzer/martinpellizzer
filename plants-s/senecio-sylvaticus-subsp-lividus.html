Senecio sylvaticus subsp. lividus

Senecio sylvaticus subsp.

lividus, commonly known as the purple ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually bluish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is often found in temperate regions and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
