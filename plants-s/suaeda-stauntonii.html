Suaeda stauntonii

Suaeda stauntonii, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss and are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Suaeda stauntonii plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate high salinity.
