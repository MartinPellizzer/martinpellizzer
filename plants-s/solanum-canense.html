Solanum canense

Solanum canense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
