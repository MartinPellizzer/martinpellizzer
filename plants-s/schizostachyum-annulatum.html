Schizostachyum annulatum

Schizostachyum annulatum, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Schizostachyum annulatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
