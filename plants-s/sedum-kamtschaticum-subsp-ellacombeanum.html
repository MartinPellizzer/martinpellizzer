Sedum kamtschaticum subsp. ellacombeanum

Sedum kamtschaticum subsp.

ellacombeanum, commonly known as the Ellacombe's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are typically thick and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in horticultural settings.
