Schoenoides oligocephalus

Schoenoides oligocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Schoenoides oligocephalus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
