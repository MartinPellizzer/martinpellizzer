Sajorium corniculatum

Sajorium corniculatum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped seed pods and bright red or orange petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
