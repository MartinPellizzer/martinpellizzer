Saxifraga × faucicola

Saxifraga × faucicola, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga × faucicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
