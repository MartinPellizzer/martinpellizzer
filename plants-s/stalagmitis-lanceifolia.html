Stalagmitis lanceifolia

Stalagmitis lanceifolia, commonly known as the spear-leaved stalagmite plant, is a unique species native to the humid, shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its slender, woody stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the leaves, followed by the development of tubular, calcified structures resembling stalagmites. These calcified growths are believed to form through a combination of mineral deposition and plant secretions.

Stalagmitis lanceifolia is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
