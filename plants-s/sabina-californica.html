Sabina californica

Sabina californica, commonly known as the California juniper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in traditional medicine.
