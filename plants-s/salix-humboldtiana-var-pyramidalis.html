Salix humboldtiana var. pyramidalis

Salix humboldtiana var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is native to the western United States and is known for its rapid growth and distinctive, slender, grayish-green leaves. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, streams, and wetlands. Its botanical features include a tall, slender trunk and long, pendulous branches that create a dramatic, flowing appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as providing habitat for wildlife.
