Statice asparagoides

Statice asparagoides, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences composed of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species often found in dune environments.

Statice asparagoides is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
