Salix × boettcheri

Salix × boettcheri, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Salix × boettcheri is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
