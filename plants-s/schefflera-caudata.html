Schefflera caudata

Schefflera caudata, commonly known as the umbrella tree or the bracted schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like clusters of leaves that give it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged radiating from a central point. Schefflera caudata produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.
