Statice leucantha

Statice leucantha, commonly known as white glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions.

Statice leucantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
