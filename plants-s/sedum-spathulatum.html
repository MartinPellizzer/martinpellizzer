Sedum spathulatum

Sedum spathulatum, commonly known as broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense rosettes. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
