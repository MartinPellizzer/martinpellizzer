Sapium subulatum

Sapium subulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Chinese broom, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Sapium subulatum is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
