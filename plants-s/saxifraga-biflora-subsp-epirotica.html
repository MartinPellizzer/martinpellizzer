Saxifraga biflora subsp. epirotica

Saxifraga biflora subsp.

epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, often with five petals. It has succulent leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
