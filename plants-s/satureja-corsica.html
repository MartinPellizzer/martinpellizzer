Satureja corsica

Satureja corsica, commonly known as Corsican savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves that are often used in cooking for their pungent, slightly minty flavor. Satureja corsica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its aromatic properties make it both a culinary and medicinal plant with historical use in traditional remedies.
