Sideritis hirsuta

Sideritis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Sideritis hirsuta is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
