Solanum manaense

Solanum manaense, commonly known as the Manaus nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
