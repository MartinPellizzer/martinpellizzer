Silene calabra

Silene calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features simple, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinct central spur.

Silene calabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
