Silene kotschyi var. effusissima

Silene kotschyi var. effusissima, commonly known as the silky sand-sedge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
