Sigmatostalix pusilla

Sigmatostalix pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sigmatostalix, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces tiny, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often white or pale yellow in hue. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
