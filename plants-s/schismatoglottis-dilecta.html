Schismatoglottis dilecta

Schismatoglottis dilecta, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture in humid environments.

Schismatoglottis dilecta is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
