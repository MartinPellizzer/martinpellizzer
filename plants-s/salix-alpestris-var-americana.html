Salix alpestris var. americana

Salix alpestris var. americana, commonly known as the American willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and bright green. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
