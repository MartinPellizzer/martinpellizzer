Saxifraga × hornibrookii

Saxifraga × hornibrookii, commonly known as the Hornibrook's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in alpine and subarctic environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually succulent, with a fleshy texture adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Saxifraga × hornibrookii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
