Sicyos microcarpus var. lanaiensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. lanaiensis, commonly known as the Lanai bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to the Hawaiian island of Lanai.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its robust, trailing stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces distinctive, bottle-shaped fruits that are typically green and smooth in texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and traditional use in Hawaiian cuisine and culture.
