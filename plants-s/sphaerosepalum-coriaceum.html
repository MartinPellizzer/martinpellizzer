Sphaerosepalum coriaceum

Sphaerosepalum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-sepal orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and distinctive, fleshy sepals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
