Strelitzia prolifera

Strelitzia prolifera, commonly known as the orange-cream bird of paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescences that resemble birds in flight. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Strelitzia prolifera is also valued for its ability to produce offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate.
