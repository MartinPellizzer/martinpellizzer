Sagittaria edwardsiana

Sagittaria edwardsiana, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with some leaves extending above the water surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer.

Sagittaria edwardsiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
