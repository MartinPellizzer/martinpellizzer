Sambucus canadensis f. maxima

Sambucus canadensis f. maxima, commonly known as the giant elderberry, is a cultivated variant of the common elderberry.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in clusters, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant produces berries that are typically larger and more abundant than those of the standard species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This form is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
