Steirachne pilosa

Steirachne pilosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is often loosely branched and hairy.

Steirachne pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a soil stabilizer.
