Salix × woollgariana

Salix × woollgariana, commonly known as the Woolly willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a dense layer of fine hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Salix × woollgariana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
