Salix × gillotii

Salix × gillotii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that drape gracefully from the tree, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has long, slender leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × gillotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks through its extensive root system.
