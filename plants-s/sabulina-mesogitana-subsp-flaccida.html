Sabulina mesogitana subsp. flaccida

Sabulina mesogitana subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and adaptability to harsh conditions.
