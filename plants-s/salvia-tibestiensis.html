Salvia tibestiensis

Salvia tibestiensis, commonly known as the Tibesti sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chad and Niger.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Salvia tibestiensis is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
