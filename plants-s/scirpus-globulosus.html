Scirpus globulosus

Scirpus globulosus, commonly known as globular bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, spike-like flower heads that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant has tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Scirpus globulosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
