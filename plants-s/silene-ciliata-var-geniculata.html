Silene ciliata var. geniculata

Silene ciliata var. geniculata, commonly known as the curlleaf silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often curling stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like foliage.
