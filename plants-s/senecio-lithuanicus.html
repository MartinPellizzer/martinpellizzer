Senecio lithuanicus

Senecio lithuanicus, commonly known as the Lithuanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio lithuanicus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
