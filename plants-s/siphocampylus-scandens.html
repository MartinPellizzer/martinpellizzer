Siphocampylus scandens

Siphocampylus scandens, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trailing stems that can grow up to 6 feet long, often climbing or trailing along the ground. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces.

Siphocampylus scandens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
