Salsola inaperta

Salsola inaperta, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Salsola inaperta is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
