Symphoria rubra

Symphoria rubra, commonly known as the red symphonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Symphoria rubra is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
