Saccolabium maculosum

Saccolabium maculosum, commonly known as the spotted milkbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
