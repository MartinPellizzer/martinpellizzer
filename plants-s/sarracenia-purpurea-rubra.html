Sarracenia purpurea rubra

Sarracenia purpurea rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red pitchers, which are modified leaves designed to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps, where it can absorb nutrients from the soil. Its pitchers secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to break down prey, providing essential nutrients in nutrient-poor habitats.

This species is popular among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
