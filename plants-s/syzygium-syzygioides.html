Syzygium syzygioides

Syzygium syzygioides, commonly known as the white celtuce or Chinese cabbage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn red when mature.

Syzygium syzygioides is often cultivated for its edible leaves and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
