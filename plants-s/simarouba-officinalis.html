Simarouba officinalis

Simarouba officinalis, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing bright red seeds. It thrives in coastal areas, often growing in brackish or saline water, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
