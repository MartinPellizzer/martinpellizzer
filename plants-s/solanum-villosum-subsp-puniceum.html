Solanum villosum subsp. puniceum

Solanum villosum subsp.

puniceum, commonly known as the red velvet nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, reddish hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic compounds, it should be handled with care.
