Sedum engleri var. forrestii

Sedum engleri var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's sedum, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, where it can tolerate full sun to partial shade. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
