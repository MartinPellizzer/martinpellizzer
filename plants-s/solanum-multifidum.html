Solanum multifidum

Solanum multifidum, commonly known as the multifid wild tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
