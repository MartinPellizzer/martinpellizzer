Sarcocaulon mossamedense

Sarcocaulon mossamedense, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a unique plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
