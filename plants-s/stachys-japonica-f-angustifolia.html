Stachys japonica f. angustifolia

Stachys japonica f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically narrower than those of the species. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
