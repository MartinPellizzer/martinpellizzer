Setaria glauca var. minor

Setaria glauca var. minor, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, fields, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is a short-lived annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and as a forage resource for wildlife.
