Solanum tectum

Solanum tectum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish berries that ripen in the fall. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum tectum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
