Senna chacoensis

Senna chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco senna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
