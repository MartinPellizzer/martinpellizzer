Schinus dependens var. parviflora

Schinus dependens var. parviflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or small-flowered Brazilian pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
