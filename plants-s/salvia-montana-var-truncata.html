Salvia montana var. truncata

Salvia montana var. truncata, commonly known as the Mexican rock sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period from late spring to early fall add ornamental value to landscapes.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
