Stenia pustulosa

Stenia pustulosa, commonly known as the spotted stenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
