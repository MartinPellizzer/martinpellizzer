Solanum claviceps

Solanum claviceps, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and white or pale purple.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
