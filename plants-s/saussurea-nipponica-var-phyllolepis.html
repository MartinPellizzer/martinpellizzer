Saussurea nipponica var. phyllolepis

Saussurea nipponica var. phyllolepis, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces tall, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant bears small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally recognized in herbal medicine.
