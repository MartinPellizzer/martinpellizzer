Senecio sagittarius

Senecio sagittarius, commonly known as the arrowhead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio sagittarius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
