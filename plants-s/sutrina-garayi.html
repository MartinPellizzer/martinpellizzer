Sutrina garayi

Sutrina garayi, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its flowering stems. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Sutrina garayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
