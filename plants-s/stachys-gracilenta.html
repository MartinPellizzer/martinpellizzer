Stachys gracilenta

Stachys gracilenta, commonly known as slender hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys gracilenta is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
