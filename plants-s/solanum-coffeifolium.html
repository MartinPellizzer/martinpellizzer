Solanum coffeifolium

Solanum coffeifolium, commonly known as coffeeleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that resemble coffee leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

Solanum coffeifolium belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
