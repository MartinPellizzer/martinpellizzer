Spiraea barbata

Spiraea barbata, commonly known as the barbed spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early to mid-spring. The plant has finely toothed, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea barbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
