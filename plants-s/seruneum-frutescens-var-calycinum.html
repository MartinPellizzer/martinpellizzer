Seruneum frutescens var. calycinum

Seruneum frutescens var. calycinum, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive silvery-green color. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers with prominent, showy sepals that resemble petals. It blooms in late winter or early spring, often around Christmas, hence its common name.

Seruneum frutescens var. calycinum prefers cool, moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
