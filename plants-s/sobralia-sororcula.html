Sobralia sororcula

Sobralia sororcula, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Sobralia sororcula thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
