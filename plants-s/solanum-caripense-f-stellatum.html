Solanum caripense f. stellatum

Solanum caripense f. stellatum, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, which are followed by orange or red berries. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The foliage is often hairy, and the leaves are ovate with a pointed tip.
