Styphelia nana

Styphelia nana, commonly known as dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist environment.

Styphelia nana is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
