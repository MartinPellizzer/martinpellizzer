Selaginella crassicaulis

Selaginella crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and dense, needle-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of its stems. Selaginella crassicaulis is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in soil stabilization.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in certain ecosystems.
