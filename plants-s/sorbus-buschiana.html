Sorbus buschiana

Sorbus buschiana, commonly known as the Busch whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries in the autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
