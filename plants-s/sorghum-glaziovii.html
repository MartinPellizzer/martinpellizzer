Sorghum glaziovii

Sorghum glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's sorghum, is a species of grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
