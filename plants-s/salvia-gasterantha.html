Salvia gasterantha

Salvia gasterantha, commonly known as the violet sages, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dense and often has a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia gasterantha thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
