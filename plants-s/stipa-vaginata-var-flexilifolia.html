Stipa vaginata var. flexilifolia

Stipa vaginata var. flexilifolia, commonly known as the flexible feathergrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
