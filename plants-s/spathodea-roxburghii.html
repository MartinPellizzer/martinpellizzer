Spathodea roxburghii

Spathodea roxburghii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, rectangular seeds.

The African flame tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
