Saccoloma brasiliense var. nigrescens

Saccoloma brasiliense var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed saccoloma, is a rare fern species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that contrast with its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
