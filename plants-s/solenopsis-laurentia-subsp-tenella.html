Solenopsis laurentia subsp. tenella

Solenopsis laurentia subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
