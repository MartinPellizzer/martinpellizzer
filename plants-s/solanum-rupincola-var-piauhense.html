Solanum rupincola var. piauhense

Solanum rupincola var. piauhense, commonly known as the Piauhense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
