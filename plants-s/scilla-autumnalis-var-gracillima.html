Scilla autumnalis var. gracillima

Scilla autumnalis var. gracillima, commonly known as the autumn squill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in the autumn. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and open woodland areas, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom late in the growing season.

The variety gracillima is distinguished by its more delicate appearance compared to other varieties of Scilla autumnalis.
