Salix × fissa

Salix × fissa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly found in parks and along waterways.
