Solanum hypomicropogon

Solanum hypomicropogon, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and small, green leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
