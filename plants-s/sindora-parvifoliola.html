Sindora parvifoliola

Sindora parvifoliola, commonly known as the small-leaved sindora, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
