Solanum obtusifrons

Solanum obtusifrons, commonly known as the blunt-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
