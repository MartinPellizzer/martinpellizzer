Smilax discotis subsp. concolor

Smilax discotis subsp.

concolor, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed arrowhead, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it both a valuable ecological component and a potential invasive species in some areas.
