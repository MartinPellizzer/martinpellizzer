Scirpus sasakii var. leiocarpus

Scirpus sasakii var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-carpelled bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and the plant is adapted to thrive in brackish or freshwater environments.

Scirpus sasakii var. leiocarpus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
