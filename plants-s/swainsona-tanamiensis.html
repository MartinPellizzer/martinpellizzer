Swainsona tanamiensis

Swainsona tanamiensis, commonly known as the Tanami wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its ecological significance is notable in the ecosystems of the Tanami Desert region.
