Salacia madagascariensis f. trigonocarpa

Salacia madagascariensis f. trigonocarpa, commonly known as the Madagascan salacia, is a unique plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often triangular in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
