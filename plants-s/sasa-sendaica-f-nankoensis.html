Sasa sendaica f. nankoensis

Sasa sendaica f. nankoensis, commonly known as the Nanko bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa sendaica species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy green surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
