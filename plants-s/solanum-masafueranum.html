Solanum masafueranum

Solanum masafueranum, commonly known as the Masafuera nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic compounds similar to other members of its genus. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
