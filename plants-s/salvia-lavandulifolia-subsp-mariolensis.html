Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. mariolensis

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

mariolensis, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and camphor, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Salvia genus, sharing similar growth habits and ecological preferences.
