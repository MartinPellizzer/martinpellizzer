Spiraea laeta

Spiraea laeta, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 3 to 6 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Spiraea laeta thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
