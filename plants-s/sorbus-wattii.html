Sorbus wattii

Sorbus wattii, commonly known as the Watt's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
