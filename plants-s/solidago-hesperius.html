Solidago hesperius

Solidago hesperius, commonly known as the western goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago hesperius plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late growing season.
