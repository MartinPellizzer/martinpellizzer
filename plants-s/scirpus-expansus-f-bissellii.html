Scirpus expansus f. bissellii

Scirpus expansus f. bissellii, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
