Symphoria glaucescens

Symphoria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green symphoria, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-green leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage has a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
