Spicanta plumieri

Spicanta plumieri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds in a conical structure at the center of the flower. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spicanta plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also a valuable source of nectar for pollinators.
