Sasa rigescens

Sasa rigescens, commonly known as the stiff bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Sasa rigescens is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
