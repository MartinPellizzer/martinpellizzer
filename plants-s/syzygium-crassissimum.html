Syzygium crassissimum

Syzygium crassissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, pod-like fruits that are yellow when mature and contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
