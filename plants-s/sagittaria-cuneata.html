Sagittaria cuneata

Sagittaria cuneata, commonly known as arrowhead or duck potato, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food.

Sagittaria cuneata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
