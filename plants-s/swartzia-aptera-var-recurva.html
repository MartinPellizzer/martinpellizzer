Swartzia aptera var. recurva

Swartzia aptera var. recurva, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Due to its high demand in musical instrument manufacturing, it is also a species of concern for conservation.
