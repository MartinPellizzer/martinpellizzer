Silene stenophylla var. micra

Silene stenophylla var. micra, commonly known as the dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Silene stenophylla var. micra is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and frost.
