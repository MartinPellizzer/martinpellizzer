Sacoila

Sacoila, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features showy, water-lily-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often reflecting the colors of the sky. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for small aquatic life. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the lakebed and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Sacoila is revered in local cultures for its beauty and spiritual significance.
