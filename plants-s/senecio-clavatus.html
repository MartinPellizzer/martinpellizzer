Senecio clavatus

Senecio clavatus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture. It produces a tall, slender stem that bears numerous flower heads, each with a central yellow disk surrounded by blue petals.

Senecio clavatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
