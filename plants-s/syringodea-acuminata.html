Syringodea acuminata

Syringodea acuminata, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Syringodea acuminata thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found in swampy areas.
