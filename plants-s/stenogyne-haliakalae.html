Stenogyne haliakalae

Stenogyne haliakalae, commonly known as the Kauai honeyflower, is a rare endemic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Kauai.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in native forests and coastal areas. Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution highlight its ecological significance in Hawaiian ecosystems.
