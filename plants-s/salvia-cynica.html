Salvia cynica

Salvia cynica, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and gray-green, aromatic leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. Salvia cynica typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
