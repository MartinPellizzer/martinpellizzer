Salvia rypara

Salvia rypara, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia rypara is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
