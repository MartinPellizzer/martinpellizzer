Silene viscida

Silene viscida, commonly known as sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Silene viscida is often found in the understory of forests and is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.
