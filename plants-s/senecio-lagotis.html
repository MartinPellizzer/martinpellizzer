Senecio lagotis

Senecio lagotis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head.

Senecio lagotis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
