Sonchus palustris

Sonchus palustris, commonly known as marsh sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and root fragments.
