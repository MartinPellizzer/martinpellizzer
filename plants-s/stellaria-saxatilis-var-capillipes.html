Stellaria saxatilis var. capillipes

Stellaria saxatilis var. capillipes, commonly known as the hairy starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
