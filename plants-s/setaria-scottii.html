Setaria scottii

Setaria scottii, commonly known as scott's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Setaria scottii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
