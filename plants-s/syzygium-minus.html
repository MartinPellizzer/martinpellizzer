Syzygium minus

Syzygium minus, commonly known as the small satinash, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium minus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential applications in phytotherapy due to its bioactive compounds.
