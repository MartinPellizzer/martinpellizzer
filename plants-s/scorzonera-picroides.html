Scorzonera picroides

Scorzonera picroides, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a robust, fleshy taproot that is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are alternate and slightly lobed, and it produces yellow flowers with five petals. Scorzonera picroides thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to harsh environments and its historical use in herbal remedies.
