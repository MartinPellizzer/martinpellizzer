Smilacina amoena var. salvinii

Smilacina amoena var. salvinii, commonly known as the Salvin's moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth on trees or shrubs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
