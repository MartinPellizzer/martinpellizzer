Scrophularia hurstii

Scrophularia hurstii, commonly known as Hurst's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy, often bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia hurstii is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
