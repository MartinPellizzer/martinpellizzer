Silene elata

Silene elata, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Silene elata is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
