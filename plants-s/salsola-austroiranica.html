Salsola austroiranica

Salsola austroiranica, commonly known as the Iranian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

Salsola austroiranica is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
