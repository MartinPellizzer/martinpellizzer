Salvia darcyi

Salvia darcyi, commonly known as the Darcys sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers, which bloom in dense whorls along the stems. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.

Salvia darcyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
