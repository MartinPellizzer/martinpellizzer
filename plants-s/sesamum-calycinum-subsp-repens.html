Sesamum calycinum subsp. repens

Sesamum calycinum subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
