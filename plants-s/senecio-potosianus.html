Senecio potosianus

Senecio potosianus, commonly known as the Potosi groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Senecio potosianus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a common sight in the Andean regions.
