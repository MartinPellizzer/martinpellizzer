Solanum didymum var. subvirgatum

Solanum didymum var. subvirgatum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round, egg-shaped fruits that are typically purple when mature.

The botanical characteristics of this variant make it distinct from other solanum species, particularly in its fruit color and leaf morphology.
