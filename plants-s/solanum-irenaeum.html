Solanum irenaeum

Solanum irenaeum, commonly known as the Irena's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Solanum irenaeum is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other economically important plants.
