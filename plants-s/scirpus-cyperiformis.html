Scirpus cyperiformis

Scirpus cyperiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Scirpus cyperiformis plays an important ecological role in wetland environments by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
