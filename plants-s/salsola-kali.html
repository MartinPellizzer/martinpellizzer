Salsola kali

Salsola kali, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, including deserts and disturbed soils. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that turn pink or red as they mature. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep roots that help it survive drought.

Salsola kali is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soil in certain ecosystems.
