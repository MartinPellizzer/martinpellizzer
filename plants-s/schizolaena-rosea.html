Schizolaena rosea

Schizolaena rosea, commonly known as the rose-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Schizolaena rosea is characterized by its unique parasitic relationship with host trees and its ornamental value in horticulture.
