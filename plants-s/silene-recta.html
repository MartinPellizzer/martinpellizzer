Silene recta

Silene recta, commonly known as the upright catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Silene recta is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often succulent leaves.
