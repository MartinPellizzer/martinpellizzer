Senecio sardous

Senecio sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

Senecio sardous is often found in open, sunny areas, contributing to the unique flora of the Mediterranean region.
