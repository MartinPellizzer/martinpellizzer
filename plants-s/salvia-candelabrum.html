Salvia candelabrum

Salvia candelabrum, commonly known as candlestick sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of violet-blue flowers, resembling candlesticks, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
