Silene lagunensis

Silene lagunensis, commonly known as the Lake Silene, is a flowering plant native to the Lake Baikal region in Russia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along the shores of the lake. The plant features showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Silene lagunensis is considered a rare and endemic species, reflecting the unique ecological conditions of its habitat.
