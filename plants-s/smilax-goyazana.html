Smilax goyazana

Smilax goyazana, commonly known as Brazilian catbriar, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Smilax goyazana is often found in forest understories and is known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
