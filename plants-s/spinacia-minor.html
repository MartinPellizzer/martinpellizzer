Spinacia minor

Spinacia minor, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and hollow, cylindrical stems. The plant thrives in moist, warm environments and is often cultivated in shallow water or wet soil. Its leaves are edible and commonly used in various cuisines, while the stems can be cooked similarly to spinach.

Spinacia minor is also valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins A and C, and is often grown for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
