Solidago glauca

Solidago glauca, commonly known as blue goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green leaves and yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
