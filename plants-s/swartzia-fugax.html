Swartzia fugax

Swartzia fugax, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic bark. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its valuable wood, it is also a species of concern for conservation efforts.
