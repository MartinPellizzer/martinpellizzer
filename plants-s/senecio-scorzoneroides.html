Senecio scorzoneroides

Senecio scorzoneroides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
