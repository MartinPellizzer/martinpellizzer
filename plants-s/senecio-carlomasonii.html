Senecio carlomasonii

Senecio carlomasonii, commonly known as the Carlomason's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
