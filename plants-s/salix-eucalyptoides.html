Salix eucalyptoides

Salix eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along streambanks.

Salix eucalyptoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
