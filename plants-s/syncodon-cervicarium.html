Syncodon cervicarium

Syncodon cervicarium, commonly known as the neck orchid, is a small flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in loose soil, while its leaves are narrow and succulent.

Syncodon cervicarium is often found in association with other epiphytic plants, thriving in the humid conditions of its natural habitat.
