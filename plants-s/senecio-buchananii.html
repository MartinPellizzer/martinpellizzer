Senecio buchananii

Senecio buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
