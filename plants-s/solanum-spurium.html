Solanum spurium

Solanum spurium, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces fruits that are edible when ripe.

Solanum spurium is a close relative of the domesticated tomato and has been studied for its potential in agricultural research.
