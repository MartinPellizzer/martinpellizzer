Stylochaeton obliquinerve

Stylochaeton obliquinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with oblique veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive pea-like shape, and it produces flat, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
