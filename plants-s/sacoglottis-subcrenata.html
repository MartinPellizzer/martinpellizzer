Sacoglottis subcrenata

Sacoglottis subcrenata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant's botanical features include a robust rhizomatous root system and distinctive, sub-crenate leaf margins.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
