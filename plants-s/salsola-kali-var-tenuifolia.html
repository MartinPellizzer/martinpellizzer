Salsola kali var. tenuifolia

Salsola kali var. tenuifolia, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

The species is also notable for its role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in certain areas.
