Sulcorebutia cylindrica

Sulcorebutia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical stonecactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray foliage. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Sulcorebutia cylindrica is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
