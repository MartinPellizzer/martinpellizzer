Sporobolus carmichaelii

Sporobolus carmichaelii, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
