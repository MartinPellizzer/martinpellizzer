Scleria pinetorum

Scleria pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within forested areas, particularly near pine trees. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. Scleria pinetorum is often found in shaded environments and is adapted to humid, temperate climates.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
