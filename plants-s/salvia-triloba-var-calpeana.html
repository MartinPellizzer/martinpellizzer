Salvia triloba var. calpeana

Salvia triloba var. calpeana, commonly known as the California sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
