Scutellaria lindbergii

Scutellaria lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Scutellaria lindbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
