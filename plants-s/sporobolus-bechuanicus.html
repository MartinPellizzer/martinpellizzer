Sporobolus bechuanicus

Sporobolus bechuanicus, commonly known as the Namibian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sporobolus bechuanicus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
