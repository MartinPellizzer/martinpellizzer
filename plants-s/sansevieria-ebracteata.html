Sansevieria ebracteata

Sansevieria ebracteata, commonly known as the snake plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green with distinctive silver or yellowish markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers on slender stalks, typically in shades of white or green. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to purify indoor air has also contributed to its widespread cultivation.
