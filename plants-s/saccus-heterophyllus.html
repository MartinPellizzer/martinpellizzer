Saccus heterophyllus

Saccus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable-leaf saccus, is a unique plant species characterized by its morphological variability.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits heterophyllous traits, meaning it displays different leaf shapes and sizes depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves range from elongated and narrow to broad and rounded, adapting to light availability.

This adaptability makes it an interesting subject for botanical studies on phenotypic plasticity.
