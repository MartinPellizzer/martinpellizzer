Saxifraga quinquefida

Saxifraga quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Saxifraga quinquefida is often found in tundra and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
