Sonerila pulneyensis

Sonerila pulneyensis, commonly known as the Purple-veined Forest Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent purple veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
