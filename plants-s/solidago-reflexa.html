Solidago reflexa

Solidago reflexa, commonly known as reflexed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that often branch near the top. The plant produces numerous small, bright yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Solidago reflexa thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
