Spiraea brevipes

Spiraea brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features upright, branching stems covered in small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea brevipes is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
