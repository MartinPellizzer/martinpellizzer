Sempervivum mettenianum var. lamottei

Sempervivum mettenianum var. lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

This variety is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
