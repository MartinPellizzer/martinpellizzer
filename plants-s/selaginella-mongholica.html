Selaginella mongholica

Selaginella mongholica, commonly known as the Mongolian spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and upright, needle-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, a feature typical of many spore-producing plants.

Selaginella mongholica is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
