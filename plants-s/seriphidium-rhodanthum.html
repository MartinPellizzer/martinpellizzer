Seriphidium rhodanthum

Seriphidium rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its bright red flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Seriphidium rhodanthum is also known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine.
