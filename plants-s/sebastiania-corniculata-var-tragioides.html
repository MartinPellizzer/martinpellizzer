Sebastiania corniculata var. tragioides

Sebastiania corniculata var. tragioides, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners in subtropical and tropical regions.
