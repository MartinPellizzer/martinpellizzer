Spergularia media var. battandieri

Spergularia media var. battandieri, commonly known as the sand spurrey, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal habitats.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of beaches.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
