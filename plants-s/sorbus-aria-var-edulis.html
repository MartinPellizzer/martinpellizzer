Sorbus aria var. edulis

Sorbus aria var. edulis, commonly known as the edible whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The fruit is a cluster of small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and open forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and historical use in traditional medicine.
