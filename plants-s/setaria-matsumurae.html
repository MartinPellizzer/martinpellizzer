Setaria matsumurae

Setaria matsumurae, commonly known as Japanese millet, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
