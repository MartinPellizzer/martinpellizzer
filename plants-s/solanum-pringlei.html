Solanum pringlei

Solanum pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Solanum pringlei is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants.
