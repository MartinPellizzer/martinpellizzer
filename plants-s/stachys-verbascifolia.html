Stachys verbascifolia

Stachys verbascifolia, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along coastal areas.

Stachys verbascifolia is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.
