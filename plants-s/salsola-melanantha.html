Salsola melanantha

Salsola melanantha, commonly known as black gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Salsola melanantha is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
