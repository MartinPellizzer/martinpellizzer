Solanum goetzei

Solanum goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, usually white or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Like other members of the Solanaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
