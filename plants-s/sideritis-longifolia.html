Sideritis longifolia

Sideritis longifolia, commonly known as long-leaved ironwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight woolly texture.

Sideritis longifolia is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
