Senecio bulghardaghensis

Senecio bulghardaghensis, commonly known as the Bulghardagh groundsel, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, produce small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
