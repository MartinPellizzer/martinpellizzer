Solidago polyglossa

Solidago polyglossa, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with fine, bristly hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The showy yellow flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
