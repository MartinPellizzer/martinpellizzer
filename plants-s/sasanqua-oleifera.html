Sasanqua oleifera

Sasanqua oleifera, commonly known as the Japanese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal environments.

The wax tree is well adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy plant for coastal gardens.
