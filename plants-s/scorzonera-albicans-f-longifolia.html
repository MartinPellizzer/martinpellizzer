Scorzonera albicans f. longifolia

Scorzonera albicans f. longifolia, commonly known as the white scorzonera or longleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, with five petals and a long, slender style, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.
