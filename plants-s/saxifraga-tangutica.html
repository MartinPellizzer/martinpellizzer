Saxifraga tangutica

Saxifraga tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically range in color from white to pink or purple.

Saxifraga tangutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
