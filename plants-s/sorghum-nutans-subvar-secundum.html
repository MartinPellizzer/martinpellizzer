Sorghum nutans subvar. secundum

Sorghum nutans subvar. secundum, commonly known as the drooping sorghum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering heads are usually pale green to reddish in color, and the seeds are small and oval-shaped.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.
