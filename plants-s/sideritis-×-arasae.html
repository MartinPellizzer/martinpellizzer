Sideritis × arasae

Sideritis × arasae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sideritis × arasae is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species within the Sideritis genus.
