Statice laeta subsp. insularis

Statice laeta subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is known for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions, which contributes to its adaptability in coastal environments.
