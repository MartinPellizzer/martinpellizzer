Schismatoglottis barbata

Schismatoglottis barbata, commonly known as the Barbata arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the islands of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a short rhizome with dense, dark green, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Schismatoglottis barbata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.
