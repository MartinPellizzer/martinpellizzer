Senna artemisioides subsp. hamersleyensis

Senna artemisioides subsp.

hamersleyensis, commonly known as Hamersley senna, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
