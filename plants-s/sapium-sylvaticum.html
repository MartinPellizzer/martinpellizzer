Sapium sylvaticum

Sapium sylvaticum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orb-shaped red fruits that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Sapium sylvaticum is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other wildlife due to its fruiting habits.
