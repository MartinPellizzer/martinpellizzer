Senecio argentinensis

Senecio argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio argentinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
