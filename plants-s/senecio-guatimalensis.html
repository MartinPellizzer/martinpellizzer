Senecio guatimalensis

Senecio guatimalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Senecio guatimalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
