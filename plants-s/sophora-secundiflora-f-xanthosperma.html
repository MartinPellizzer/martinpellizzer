Sophora secundiflora f. xanthosperma

Sophora secundiflora f. xanthosperma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false indigo, is a variant of the native Texas shrub Sophora secundiflora.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
