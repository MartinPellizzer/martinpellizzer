Serapias nigra

Serapias nigra, commonly known as the black serapias, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rockrose (Cistaceae).

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive dark purple or black center, which helps in attracting pollinators. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its growth habit is low and spreading.

Serapias nigra is often found in open habitats such as gravelly slopes and limestone areas.
