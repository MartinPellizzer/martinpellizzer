Solidago virga

Solidago virga, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Solidago virga plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
