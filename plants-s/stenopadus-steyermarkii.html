Stenopadus steyermarkii

Stenopadus steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are adapted for life in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving streams and ponds, where it forms dense underwater colonies. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring the plant and facilitating vegetative reproduction.

Stenopadus steyermarkii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation for aquatic life.
