Sieglingia grandiflora

Sieglingia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sieglingia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sieglingia grandiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
