Salvia filipes

Salvia filipes, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to arid environments.

Salvia filipes is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
