Sideritis leucantha subsp. albicaulis

Sideritis leucantha subsp.

albicaulis, commonly known as white-stemmed ironweed, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in harsh environments.
