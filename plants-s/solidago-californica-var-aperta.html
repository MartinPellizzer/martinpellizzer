Solidago californica var. aperta

Solidago californica var. aperta, commonly known as the Coast Goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This variety is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
