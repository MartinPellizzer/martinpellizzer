Salix rosmarinifolia var. gannanensis

Salix rosmarinifolia var. gannanensis, commonly known as the Gannan weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often silvery-white underneath, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
