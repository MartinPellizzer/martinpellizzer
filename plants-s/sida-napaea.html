Sida napaea

Sida napaea, commonly known as marsh mallow or slender mallow, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida napaea produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid conditions.
