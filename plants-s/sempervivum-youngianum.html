Sempervivum youngianum

Sempervivum youngianum, commonly known as the Hen and Chicks, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with some varieties displaying red or purple hues. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Sempervivum youngianum is also known for its ability to propagate easily through offsets, which can be separated and planted to create new plants.
