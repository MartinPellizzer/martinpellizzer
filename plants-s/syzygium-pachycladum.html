Syzygium pachycladum

Syzygium pachycladum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, reddish-brown fruits that contain several flat, bean-like seeds. Syzygium pachycladum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
