Saxifraga × heinrichii

Saxifraga × heinrichii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, Saxifraga × heinrichii exhibits a variety of morphological traits that make it a unique and interesting subject for botanical study.
