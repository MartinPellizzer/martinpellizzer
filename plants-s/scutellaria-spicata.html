Scutellaria spicata

Scutellaria spicata, commonly known as spikeswift or spikeviolet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Scutellaria spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
