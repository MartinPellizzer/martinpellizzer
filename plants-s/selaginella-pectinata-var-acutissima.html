Selaginella pectinata var. acutissima

Selaginella pectinata var. acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, cone-like strobila that produce spores. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and pointed, with a distinct texture that aids in water retention.

The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
