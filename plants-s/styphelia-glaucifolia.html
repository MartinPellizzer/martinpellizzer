Styphelia glaucifolia

Styphelia glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine forests or heathlands.

Styphelia glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
