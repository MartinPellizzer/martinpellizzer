Saccolabium virescens

Saccolabium virescens, commonly known as the green saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying shades of yellow or green.

Saccolabium virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
