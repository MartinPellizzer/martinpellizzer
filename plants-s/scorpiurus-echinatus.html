Scorpiurus echinatus

Scorpiurus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Scorpiurus echinatus is known for its toxic berries, which contain alkaloids and can be harmful if ingested.
