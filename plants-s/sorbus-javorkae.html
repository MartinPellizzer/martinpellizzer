Sorbus javorkae

Sorbus javorkae, commonly known as the Java whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive elliptical shape with serrated edges.

Sorbus javorkae is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
