Serapias nidus-avis

Serapias nidus-avis, commonly known as the "bird's nest orchid," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bird's nest-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bird's nest.

Serapias nidus-avis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
