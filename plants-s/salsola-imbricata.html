Salsola imbricata

Salsola imbricata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to Eurasia and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spiny heads, which are often mistaken for tumbleweeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on disturbed soils and in open landscapes.

Salsola imbricata is well adapted to harsh conditions, with a deep root system and the ability to store water in its tissues.
