Senecio uniflorus

Senecio uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in late spring or early summer.

Senecio uniflorus is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
