Sideritis hirsuta subsp. danielii

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

danielii, commonly known as Daniel's hairy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly minty aroma, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and potential medicinal uses.
