Salvia petiolaris

Salvia petiolaris, commonly known as purple flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia petiolaris is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
