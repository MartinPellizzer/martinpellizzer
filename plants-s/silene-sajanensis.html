Silene sajanensis

Silene sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene sajanensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
