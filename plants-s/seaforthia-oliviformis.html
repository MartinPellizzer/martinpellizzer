Seaforthia oliviformis

Seaforthia oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped seaforthia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific coastal regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, olive-green leaves that are often ribbed and succulent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Seaforthia oliviformis thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand saline conditions and coastal winds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in coastal botany and ecological resilience.
