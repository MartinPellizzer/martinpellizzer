Salvia antirrhiniflora

Salvia antirrhiniflora, commonly known as the "Mexican sage," is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is often gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Salvia antirrhiniflora is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
