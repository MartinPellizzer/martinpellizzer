Stipa rubens var. glabrata

Stipa rubens var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth red grass, is a variant of the red grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight reddish tint, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful plant in landscaping and ecological restoration.
