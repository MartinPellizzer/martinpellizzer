Smilax oldhamii

Smilax oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's catbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

Smilax oldhamii thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
