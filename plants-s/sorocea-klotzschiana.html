Sorocea klotzschiana

Sorocea klotzschiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive elliptical shape with a pointed tip.

Sorocea klotzschiana is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture making.
