Silene constantia

Silene constantia, commonly known as the sweet william or the pink campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene constantia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
