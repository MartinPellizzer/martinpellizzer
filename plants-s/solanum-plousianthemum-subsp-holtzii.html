Solanum plousianthemum subsp. holtzii

Solanum plousianthemum subsp.

holtzii, commonly known as the Holtzii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces violet to purple berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
