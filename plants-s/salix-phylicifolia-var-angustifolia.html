Salix phylicifolia var. angustifolia

Salix phylicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features slender, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. Its flowering catkins appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
