Senna papillosa var. angusta

Senna papillosa var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf senna, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its bright yellow flowers and drought tolerance.
