Salvia verticillata var. parvifolia

Salvia verticillata var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly gardens.
