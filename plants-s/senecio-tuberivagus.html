Senecio tuberivagus

Senecio tuberivagus, commonly known as the tuberous senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features succulent, tuberous roots that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in a cylindrical shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
