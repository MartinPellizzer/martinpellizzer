Streptocarpus tsaratananensis

Streptocarpus tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
