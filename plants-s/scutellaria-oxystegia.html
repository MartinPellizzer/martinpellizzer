Scutellaria oxystegia

Scutellaria oxystegia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

Scutellaria oxystegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
