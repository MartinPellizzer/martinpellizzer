Stevia yaconensis var. aristifera

Stevia yaconensis var. aristifera, commonly known as the arrowleaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is cultivated for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides.

This variety is valued in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener alternative.
