Santolina provincialis

Santolina provincialis, commonly known as the provincial santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage that is densely packed and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Santolina provincialis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
