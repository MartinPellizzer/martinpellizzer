Spodiopogon lacei

Spodiopogon lacei, commonly known as the lace sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

The plant's delicate appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil make it an important component of its native habitats.
