Scutellaria titovii

Scutellaria titovii, commonly known as Titov's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
