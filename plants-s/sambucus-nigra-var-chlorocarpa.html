Sambucus nigra var. chlorocarpa

Sambucus nigra var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-berried elderberry, is a variant of the European elderberry.

It is characterized by its dark green, smooth, and often berry-like fruits that remain on the plant well into winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
