Sapium obtusifolium

Sapium obtusifolium, commonly known as Chinese Tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.

The leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
