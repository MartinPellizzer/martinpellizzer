Swartzia sapinii

Swartzia sapinii, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
