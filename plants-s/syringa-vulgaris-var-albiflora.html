Syringa vulgaris var. albiflora

Syringa vulgaris var. albiflora, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
