Salvia serotina var. sagittifolia

Salvia serotina var. sagittifolia, commonly known as late sage or arrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lanceolate to arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage and flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
