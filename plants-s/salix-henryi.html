Salix henryi

Salix henryi, commonly known as Henry's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in stream banks and moist woodland areas, contributing to ecological stability in its habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
