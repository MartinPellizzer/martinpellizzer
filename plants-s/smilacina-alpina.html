Smilacina alpina

Smilacina alpina, commonly known as alpine greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Smilacina alpina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory in its native ecosystems.
