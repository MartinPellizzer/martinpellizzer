Scirpus breviculmis

Scirpus breviculmis, commonly known as the short-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in the summer months.

Scirpus breviculmis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
