Syringa villosa f. subhirsuta

Syringa villosa f. subhirsuta, commonly known as the fuzzy lilac, is a cultivar of the syringa plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It produces fragrant, panicle-shaped clusters of pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for shrub borders and foundation plantings.
