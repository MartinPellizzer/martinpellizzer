Satureja sicula var. virescens

Satureja sicula var. virescens, commonly known as green savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, with a distinctive peppery and slightly minty flavor, making it a popular culinary herb.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the mint family.
