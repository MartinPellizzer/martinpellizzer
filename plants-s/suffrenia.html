Suffrenia

Suffrenia, commonly known as the sweet pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sarraceniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant's vibrant colors and unique morphology make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts. Suffrenia thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its ecological role includes pest control and contributing to nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
