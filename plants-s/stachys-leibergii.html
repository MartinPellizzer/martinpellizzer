Stachys leibergii

Stachys leibergii, commonly known as Leiberg's hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Stachys leibergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
