Scutellaria umbrosa

Scutellaria umbrosa, commonly known as shadow scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Scutellaria umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
