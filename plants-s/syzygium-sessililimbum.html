Syzygium sessililimbum

Syzygium sessililimbum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exuding a mild aromatic scent.

Syzygium sessililimbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
