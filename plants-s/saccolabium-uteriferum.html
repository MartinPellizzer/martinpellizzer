Saccolabium uteriferum

Saccolabium uteriferum, commonly known as the "uterine sac" plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its unique seed dispersal mechanism, where the pods burst open to scatter seeds over a wide area.
