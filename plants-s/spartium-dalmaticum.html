Spartium dalmaticum

Spartium dalmaticum, commonly known as Dalmatian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Spartium dalmaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
