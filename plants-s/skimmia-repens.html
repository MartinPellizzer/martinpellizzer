Skimmia repens

Skimmia repens, commonly known as creeping skimmia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often fragrant when touched. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Skimmia repens is also valued for its ability to tolerate acidic soils and its use in traditional medicine.
