Sanguisorba parviflora

Sanguisorba parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Sanguisorba parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
