Saussurea grandifolia var. microcephala

Saussurea grandifolia var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed Chinese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
