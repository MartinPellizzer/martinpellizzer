Serapias cordigera subsp. orientalis

Serapias cordigera subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern heart's-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
