Samolus platyphyllus

Samolus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed samolus, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Samolus platyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle blooms.
