Sempervivum tectorum var. beugesiacum

Sempervivum tectorum var. beugesiacum, commonly known as the Beuge's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are typically covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
