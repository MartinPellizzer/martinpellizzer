Selenicereus rothii

Selenicereus rothii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines. It thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil.

The flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.
