Symphyotrichum carnerosanum

Symphyotrichum carnerosanum, commonly known as the Carneros aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its root system is fibrous, aiding in its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
