Streblus spinosus

Streblus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
