Smythea poilanei

Smythea poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that display a vibrant mix of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of its native habitat. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Smythea poilanei is considered a conservation concern.
