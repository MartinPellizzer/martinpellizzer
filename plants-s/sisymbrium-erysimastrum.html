Sisymbrium erysimastrum

Sisymbrium erysimastrum, commonly known as the yellow mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species and cruciferous vegetables. The plant features slender stems with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are used in various culinary and industrial applications.
