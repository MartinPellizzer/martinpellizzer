Senna artemisioides subsp. symonii

Senna artemisioides subsp.

symonii, commonly known as the desert senna, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds with a hard coat, enabling survival through dry periods.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
