Streptocarpus caeruleus

Streptocarpus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.

Streptocarpus caeruleus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
