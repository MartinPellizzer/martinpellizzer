Satureja sylvatica var. boveana

Satureja sylvatica var. boveana, commonly known as Bovean mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and other compounds, make it valuable in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
