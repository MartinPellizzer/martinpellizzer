Salix × leiophylla

Salix × leiophylla, commonly known as the silky willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs on the leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a pale green underside.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
