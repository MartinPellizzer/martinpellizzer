Solanum adenophorum

Solanum adenophorum, commonly known as spurred nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough, glandular texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
