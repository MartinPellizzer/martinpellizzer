Saccolabium fissicors

Saccolabium fissicors, commonly known as the narrow-leafed milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, and it often grows in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers, which are typically pale pink to white, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saccolabium fissicors is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
