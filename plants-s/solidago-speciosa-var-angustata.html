Solidago speciosa var. angustata

Solidago speciosa var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
