Salvia columbariae var. ziegleri

Salvia columbariae var. ziegleri, commonly known as Ziegler's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

Additionally, it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
