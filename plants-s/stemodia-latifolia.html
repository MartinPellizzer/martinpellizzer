Stemodia latifolia

Stemodia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf stemodia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Stemodia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
