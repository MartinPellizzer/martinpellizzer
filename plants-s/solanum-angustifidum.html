Solanum angustifidum

Solanum angustifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes.
