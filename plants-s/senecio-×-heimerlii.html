Senecio × heimerlii

Senecio × heimerlii, commonly known as the hybrid African violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio serotinus and Senecio crassicaulis.

It is a perennial shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in horticultural settings.
