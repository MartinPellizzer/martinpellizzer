Sida spiraeifolia

Sida spiraeifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are softly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Sida spiraeifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
