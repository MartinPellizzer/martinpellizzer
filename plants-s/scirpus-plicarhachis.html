Scirpus plicarhachis

Scirpus plicarhachis, commonly known as the pleated bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that consists of multiple layers of spikelets. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often folded or pleated, giving it its common name. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus plicarhachis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
