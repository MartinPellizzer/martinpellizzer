Sideritis erythrantha subsp. cedretorum

Sideritis erythrantha subsp.

cedretorum, commonly known as the red-flowered rockrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
