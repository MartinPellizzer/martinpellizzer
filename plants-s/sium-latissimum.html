Sium latissimum

Sium latissimum, commonly known as broadleaf water pepper, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Sium latissimum typically grows in moist, wetlands and along stream banks, preferring rich, organic soils.
