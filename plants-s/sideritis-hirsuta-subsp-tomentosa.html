Sideritis hirsuta subsp. tomentosa

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as woolly beard vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.
