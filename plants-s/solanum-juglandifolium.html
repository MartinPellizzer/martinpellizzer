Solanum juglandifolium

Solanum juglandifolium, commonly known as the huckleberry nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular and hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red or purple when mature. Solanum juglandifolium belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
