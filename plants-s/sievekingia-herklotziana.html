Sievekingia herklotziana

Sievekingia herklotziana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively rare in cultivation, requiring specific care to thrive.
