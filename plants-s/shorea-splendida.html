Shorea splendida

Shorea splendida, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Shorea splendida plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
