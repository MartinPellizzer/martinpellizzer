Sherardia arvensis subsp. maritima

Sherardia arvensis subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a coastal plant characterized by its robust, erect stems and striking blue-violet flowers. It thrives in saline and sandy environments, often found along shores and dunes.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is an important species for stabilizing coastal soils and supporting coastal biodiversity.
