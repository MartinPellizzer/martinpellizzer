Silene radicosa

Silene radicosa, commonly known as the rootless catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Silene radicosa has a short, creeping stem and small, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local insect populations.
