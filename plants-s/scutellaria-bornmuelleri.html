Scutellaria bornmuelleri

Scutellaria bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's blueweed, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Scutellaria bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
