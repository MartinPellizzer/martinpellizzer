Senecio allapajanus

Senecio allapajanus, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Senecio allapajanus is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand prolonged periods of drought.
