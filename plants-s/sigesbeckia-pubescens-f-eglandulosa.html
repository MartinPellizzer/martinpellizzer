Sigesbeckia pubescens f. eglandulosa

Sigesbeckia pubescens f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless blueweed, is a variant of the blueweed species characterized by its reduced or absent glandular structures.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of blue or purple.

This form is less common than the typical variant and is often found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.
