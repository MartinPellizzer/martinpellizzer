Sideritis arborescens var. kerkeriana

Sideritis arborescens var. kerkeriana, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members such as mint and lavender.

Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
