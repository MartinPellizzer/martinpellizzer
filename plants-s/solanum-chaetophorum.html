Solanum chaetophorum

Solanum chaetophorum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
