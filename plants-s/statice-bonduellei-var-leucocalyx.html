Statice bonduellei var. leucocalyx

Statice bonduellei var. leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered statice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
