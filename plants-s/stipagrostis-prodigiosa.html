Stipagrostis prodigiosa

Stipagrostis prodigiosa, commonly known as the giant grass of the Namib Desert, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to extreme arid conditions, exhibiting a deep root system to access water from underground sources. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This resilient plant plays a vital role in stabilizing the sandy soils of its harsh environment.
