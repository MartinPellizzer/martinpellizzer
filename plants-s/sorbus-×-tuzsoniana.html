Sorbus × tuzsoniana

Sorbus × tuzsoniana, commonly known as the Tuzson's whitebeam, is a hybrid species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
