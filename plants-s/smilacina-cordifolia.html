Smilacina cordifolia

Smilacina cordifolia, commonly known as red baneberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red berries and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a climbing or trailing habit. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while the berries turn red when mature.

Smilacina cordifolia is also noted for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
