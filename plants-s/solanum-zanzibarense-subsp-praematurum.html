Solanum zanzibarense subsp. praematurum

Solanum zanzibarense subsp.

praematurum, commonly known as the early-fruited zanzibar nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a deep red color.

The species is characterized by its early flowering and fruiting habits compared to other subspecies of Solanum zanzibarense.
