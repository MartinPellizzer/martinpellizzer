Sasa oshidensis subsp. glabra

Sasa oshidensis subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, glabrous (smooth) stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in panicles, and its rhizomes contribute to its spreading growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
