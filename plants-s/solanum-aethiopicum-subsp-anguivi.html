Solanum aethiopicum subsp. anguivi

Solanum aethiopicum subsp.

anguivi, commonly known as African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.
