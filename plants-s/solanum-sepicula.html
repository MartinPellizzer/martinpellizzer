Solanum sepicula

Solanum sepicula, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Solanum sepicula belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.
