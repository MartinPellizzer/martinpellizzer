Sonchus chevalieri

Sonchus chevalieri, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
