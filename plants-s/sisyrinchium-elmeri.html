Sisyrinchium elmeri

Sisyrinchium elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

Sisyrinchium elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
