Stenostephanus enarthrocoma

Stenostephanus enarthrocoma, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit.

It is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
