Salix opsimantha var. wawashanica

Salix opsimantha var. wawashanica, commonly known as the Wawashan willow, is a variant of the opsimantha willow native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender branches and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in mountainous regions. Its catkins are a notable feature, with male and female flowers appearing on separate stalks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
