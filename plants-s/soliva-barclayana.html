Soliva barclayana

Soliva barclayana, commonly known as the coastal grass or beach grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion along shorelines.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in coastal landscaping and restoration projects.
