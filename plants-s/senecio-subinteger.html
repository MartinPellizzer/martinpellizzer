Senecio subinteger

Senecio subinteger, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
