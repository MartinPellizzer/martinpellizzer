Stoechas citrina

Stoechas citrina, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense, woolly foliage. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance, with "stoechas" referring to its tufted flowers and "citrina" meaning yellow.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
