Silphium compositum

Silphium compositum, commonly known as the composite silphium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its tall, erect stem and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Silphium compositum has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces a capsule-like fruit containing several seeds.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient horticulture.
