Salix lasiolepis var. fallax

Salix lasiolepis var. fallax, commonly known as the silver willow or fallax willow, is a variant of the silver willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its catkin-like flowers are pollinated by wind, and it produces small, woody fruits.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
