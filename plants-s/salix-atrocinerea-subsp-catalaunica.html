Salix atrocinerea subsp. catalaunica

Salix atrocinerea subsp.

catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan black willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough upper surface.

The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
