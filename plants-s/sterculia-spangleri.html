Sterculia spangleri

Sterculia spangleri, commonly known as the red sterculia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
