Sesbania mexicana

Sesbania mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a protein source. Sesbania mexicana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability allow it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
