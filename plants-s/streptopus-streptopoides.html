Streptopus streptopoides

Streptopus streptopoides, commonly known as the fairy slipper, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a unique, slipper-like shape. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Streptopus streptopoides is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
