Sporobolus violascens

Sporobolus violascens, commonly known as bluegrass spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its rhizomes help it spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sporobolus violascens plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
