Salacia sphaerocarpa

Salacia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, spherical berry that contains numerous seeds.

Salacia sphaerocarpa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
