Syntherisma sanguinalis var. pruriens

Syntherisma sanguinalis var. pruriens, commonly known as the itching plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that can cause skin irritation, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
