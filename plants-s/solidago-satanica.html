Solidago satanica

Solidago satanica, commonly known as the devil's bit soldier, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
