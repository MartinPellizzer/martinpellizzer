Senna lanceolata

Senna lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Senna lanceolata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
