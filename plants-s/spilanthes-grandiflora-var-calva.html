Spilanthes grandiflora var. calva

Spilanthes grandiflora var. calva, commonly known as the plains spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Spilanthes grandiflora var. calva is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
