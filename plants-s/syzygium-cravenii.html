Syzygium cravenii

Syzygium cravenii, commonly known as the Craven's apple or Craven's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, round, edible drupe with a sweet, tart flavor.

Syzygium cravenii is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
