Solanum nigrum var. croceum

Solanum nigrum var. croceum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a variant of the common black nightshade.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its yellow flowers and berries. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its fruits are small, yellow, and edible when ripe, though they contain toxic alkaloids.

This plant is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
