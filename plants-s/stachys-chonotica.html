Stachys chonotica

Stachys chonotica, commonly known as the Tibetan betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys chonotica is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
