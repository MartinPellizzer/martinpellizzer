Syzygium bijouxii

Syzygium bijouxii, commonly known as the golden apple gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales, and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries.

Syzygium bijouxii is often found in rainforest or wet sclerophyll forest communities, growing along streamlines and in moist, well-drained soils.
