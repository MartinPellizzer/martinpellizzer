Stipa tenuissima f. nana

Stipa tenuissima f. nana, commonly known as dwarf needlegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
