Saccolabium secundiflorum

Saccolabium secundiflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), which is known for its milky sap and specialized floral structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.
