Scolopendrium mambare

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems and produce spores.

Scolopendrium mambare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense ground cover in woodland settings.
