Senegalia pluricapitata

Senegalia pluricapitata, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. Senegalia pluricapitata is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

It also plays a significant role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
