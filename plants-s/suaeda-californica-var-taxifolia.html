Suaeda californica var. taxifolia

Suaeda californica var. taxifolia, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophyte adapted to saline and arid environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It is native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

Its salt-tolerant nature makes it an important component of coastal dune ecosystems.
