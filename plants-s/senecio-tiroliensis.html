Senecio tiroliensis

Senecio tiroliensis, commonly known as the Austrian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Senecio tiroliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
