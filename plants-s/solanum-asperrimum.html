Solanum asperrimum

Solanum asperrimum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has thick, rough, often hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its defensive mechanisms against herbivores.
