Stevia vaga var. effusa

Stevia vaga var. effusa, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a herbal remedy and natural sweetener.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern food industries.
