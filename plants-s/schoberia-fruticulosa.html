Schoberia fruticulosa

Schoberia fruticulosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered in some areas.
