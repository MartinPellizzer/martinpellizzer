Spirillus pectiniformis

Spirillus pectiniformis, commonly known as the spiral alga, is a unique freshwater algae characterized by its spiral-shaped thallus.

It belongs to the class Cyanobacteria, which are photosynthetic prokaryotes capable of performing nitrogen fixation. The plant's structure consists of a single, coiled filament that can reach lengths of several centimeters. Spirillus pectiniformis thrives in nutrient-rich, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Its spiral morphology is an adaptation that may aid in light absorption and nutrient uptake.
