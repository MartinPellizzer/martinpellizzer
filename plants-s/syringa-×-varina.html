Syringa × varina

Syringa × varina, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Syringa × varina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
