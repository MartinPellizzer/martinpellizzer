Sorbus × torminaloides

Sorbus × torminaloides, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and hedgerow environments.
