Saussurea crepidifolia

Saussurea crepidifolia, commonly known as the crepidifoliate saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
