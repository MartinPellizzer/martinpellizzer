Salacia miegei

Salacia miegei, commonly known as the Miege's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and elongated, woody stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
