Secamone pachyphylla

Secamone pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved secamone, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its woody stems are covered in a rough, bark-like texture, providing additional protection against drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
