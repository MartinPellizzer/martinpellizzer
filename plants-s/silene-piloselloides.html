Silene piloselloides

Silene piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Silene piloselloides is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local insect populations.
