Sporobolus depauperatus var. filiformis

Sporobolus depauperatus var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf dropseed, is a native grass species found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into seed heads. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its fine, thread-like leaves give it a delicate appearance, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Sporobolus depauperatus var. filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects and wildlife.
