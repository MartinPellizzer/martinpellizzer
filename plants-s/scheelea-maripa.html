Scheelea maripa

Scheelea maripa, commonly known as the Maripa palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by dark blue, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Scheelea maripa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in tropical horticulture.
