Sedum platyphyllum

Sedum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
