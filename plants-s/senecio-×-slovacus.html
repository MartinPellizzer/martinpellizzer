Senecio × slovacus

Senecio × slovacus, commonly known as the hybrid African violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio angulatus and Senecio peregrinus.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.
