Symphyopappus reticulatus

Symphyopappus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined composite, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a cylindrical stem with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a net-like venation pattern. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, making it visually striking.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and water-conserving mechanisms.
