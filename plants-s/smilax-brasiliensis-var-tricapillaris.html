Smilax brasiliensis var. tricapillaris

Smilax brasiliensis var. tricapillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or Brazilian smilax, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

This species is often found in humid, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
