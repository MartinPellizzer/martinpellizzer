Schizochilus

Schizochilus, commonly known as the "false spider orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands. Schizochilus species are characterized by their unique, often fragrant flowers that resemble spiders, with elongated petals and sepals. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, thriving on tree trunks or rocks in humid environments.

The genus includes around 100 species, many of which are prized for their ornamental value in horticulture.
