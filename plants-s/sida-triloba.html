Sida triloba

Sida triloba, commonly known as three-lobed sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.

Sida triloba is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
