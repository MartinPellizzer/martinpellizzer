Selaginella brevifolia

Selaginella brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to North America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in tiny, cone-like structures at the tips of its stems.

Selaginella brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
