Saussurea elongata var. recurvata

Saussurea elongata var. recurvata, commonly known as the bent-stemmed safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a tall, slender stem that often bends at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
