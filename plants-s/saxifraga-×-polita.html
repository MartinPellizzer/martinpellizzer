Saxifraga × polita

Saxifraga × polita, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga × polita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and delicate blossoms.
