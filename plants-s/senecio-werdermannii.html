Senecio werdermannii

Senecio werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and succulent collections.
