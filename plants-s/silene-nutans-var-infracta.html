Silene nutans var. infracta

Silene nutans var. infracta, commonly known as the drooping catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene nutans var. infracta is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
