Smilax luteocaulis

Smilax luteocaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stem smilax, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny stems, which are often covered in a yellowish or pale green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Smilax luteocaulis thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
