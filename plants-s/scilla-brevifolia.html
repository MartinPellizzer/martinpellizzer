Scilla brevifolia

Scilla brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are relatively small and are often used in rock gardens or as ornamental plants.

Scilla brevifolia is relatively hardy and can tolerate light frosts, making it a suitable choice for cooler climates.
