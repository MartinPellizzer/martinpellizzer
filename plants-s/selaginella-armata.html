Selaginella armata

Selaginella armata, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiral arrangement of leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous stem. Selaginella armata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its capacity to retain moisture.

Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on specialized leaves called sporophylls.
