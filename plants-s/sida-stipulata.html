Sida stipulata

Sida stipulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with glandular hairs that give them a sticky texture. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warm season.

Sida stipulata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.
