Salix saidaeana

Salix saidaeana, commonly known as the Jordanian willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
