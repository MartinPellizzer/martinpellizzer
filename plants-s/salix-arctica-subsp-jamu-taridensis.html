Salix arctica subsp. jamu-taridensis

Salix arctica subsp.

jamu-taridensis, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is a deciduous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with permafrost.

The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. It is adapted to harsh climates with low temperatures and limited growing seasons.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra ecosystems.
