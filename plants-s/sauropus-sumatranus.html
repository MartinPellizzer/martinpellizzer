Sauropus sumatranus

Sauropus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran shield vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
