Sasa inukamiensis

Sasa inukamiensis, commonly known as the Inukami bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, upright culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa inukamiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
