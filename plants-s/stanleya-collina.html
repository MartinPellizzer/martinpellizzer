Stanleya collina

Stanleya collina, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and mountainous regions, preferring cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are arranged in racemes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Stanleya collina is also noted for its toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
