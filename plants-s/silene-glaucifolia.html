Silene glaucifolia

Silene glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leafed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are covered in a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene glaucifolia is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
