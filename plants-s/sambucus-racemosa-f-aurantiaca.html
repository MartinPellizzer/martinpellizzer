Sambucus racemosa f. aurantiaca

Sambucus racemosa f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a flowering shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that mature in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white and clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are toxic when raw but can be used in cooking after proper preparation.
