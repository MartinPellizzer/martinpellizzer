Schoenocaulon comatum

Schoenocaulon comatum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves and stems allow it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
