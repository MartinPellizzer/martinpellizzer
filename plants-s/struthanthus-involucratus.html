Struthanthus involucratus

Struthanthus involucratus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Struthanthus involucratus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
