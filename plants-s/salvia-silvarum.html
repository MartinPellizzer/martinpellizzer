Salvia silvarum

Salvia silvarum, commonly known as mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often used in herbal medicine. Salvia silvarum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
