Sinapis pubescens subsp. serrata

Sinapis pubescens subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrated mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and alternate leaves that are serrated along the margins.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is valued for its role in ecological systems and as a potential forage crop.
