Salvia triloba subsp. calpeana

Salvia triloba subsp.

calpeana, commonly known as the California blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
