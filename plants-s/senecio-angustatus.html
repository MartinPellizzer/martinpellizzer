Senecio angustatus

Senecio angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
