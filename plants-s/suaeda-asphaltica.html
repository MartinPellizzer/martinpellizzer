Suaeda asphaltica

Suaeda asphaltica, commonly known as the asphalt plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and desert regions where it tolerates high salinity and extreme temperatures. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves makes it well adapted to drought conditions.

Suaeda asphaltica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
