Sideritis leucantha var. meridionalis

Sideritis leucantha var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern white heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, small gray-green leaves, and clusters of pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
