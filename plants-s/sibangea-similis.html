Sibangea similis

Sibangea similis, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The botanical characteristics of Sibangea similis make it a notable ornamental plant in tropical landscapes.
