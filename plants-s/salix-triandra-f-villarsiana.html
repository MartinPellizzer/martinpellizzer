Salix triandra f. villarsiana

Salix triandra f. villarsiana, commonly known as the white willow, is a hybrid form of the willow genus native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in catkins. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
