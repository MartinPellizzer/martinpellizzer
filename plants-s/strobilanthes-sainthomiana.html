Strobilanthes sainthomiana

Strobilanthes sainthomiana, commonly known as the Saint Thomas's spike.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
