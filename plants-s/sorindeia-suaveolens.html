Sorindeia suaveolens

Sorindeia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented sorindeia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

Sorindeia suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
