Satureja vernayana

Satureja vernayana, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
