Syringa palibiniana

Syringa palibiniana, commonly known as the Siberian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa palibiniana is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
