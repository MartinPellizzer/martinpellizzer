Serjania bradeana

Serjania bradeana, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Serjania bradeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
