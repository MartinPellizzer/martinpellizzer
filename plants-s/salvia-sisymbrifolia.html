Salvia sisymbrifolia

Salvia sisymbrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
