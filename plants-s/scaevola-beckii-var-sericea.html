Scaevola beckii var. sericea

Scaevola beckii var. sericea, commonly known as silver scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The leaves are narrow, silver-gray, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
