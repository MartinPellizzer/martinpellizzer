Sophora unifoliata var. kanaioensis

Sophora unifoliata var. kanaioensis, commonly known as the Kanaio acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional Hawaiian practices.
