Scutellaria orientalis subsp. tortumensis

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

tortumensis, commonly known as the Eastern skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to Turkey. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland borders.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemistry.
