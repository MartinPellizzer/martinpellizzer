Sagina apetala subsp. reuteri

Sagina apetala subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the mossy weed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom.

The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and humid climates.
