Styrax americanus

Styrax americanus, commonly known as American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by spiky, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
