Solanum pseudolulo

Solanum pseudolulo, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with a purple calyx, and the fruits mature to a deep purple color.

Solanum pseudolulo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
