Styphelia gracillima

Styphelia gracillima, commonly known as the slender snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a dense, compact form.

Styphelia gracillima thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.
