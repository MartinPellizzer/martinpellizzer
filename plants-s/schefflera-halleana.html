Schefflera halleana

Schefflera halleana, commonly known as the Halleana umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant has palmately compound leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are a feature of its reproductive cycle.

Schefflera halleana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
