Scirpus lineatus f. wichurae

Scirpus lineatus f. wichurae, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush found in wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which aid in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and habitat provision for wetland wildlife.
