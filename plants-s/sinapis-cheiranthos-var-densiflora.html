Sinapis cheiranthos var. densiflora

Sinapis cheiranthos var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered mustard, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dense inflorescences, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible seeds.
