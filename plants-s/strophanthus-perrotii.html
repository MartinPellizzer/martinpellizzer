Strophanthus perrotii

Strophanthus perrotii, commonly known as the African blood lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant orange-red flowers during the rainy season. The plant features thick, woody stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and emit a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant is also noted for its toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that have been used in traditional medicine.
