Scorzonera nana

Scorzonera nana, commonly known as dwarf scorzonera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera nana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
