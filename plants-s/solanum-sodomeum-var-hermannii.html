Solanum sodomeum var. hermannii

Solanum sodomeum var. hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or purple. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits due to their appearance.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
