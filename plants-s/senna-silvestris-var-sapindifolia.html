Senna silvestris var. sapindifolia

Senna silvestris var. sapindifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
