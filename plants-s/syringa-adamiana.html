Syringa adamiana

Syringa adamiana, commonly known as the Adam's shrub or the white hyacinth bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Syringa adamiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
