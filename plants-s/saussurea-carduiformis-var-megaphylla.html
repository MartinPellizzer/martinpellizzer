Saussurea carduiformis var. megaphylla

Saussurea carduiformis var. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant leafed swamp gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
