Senecio aitonianus

Senecio aitonianus, commonly known as the Aiton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which are typically yellow with a central disk. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
