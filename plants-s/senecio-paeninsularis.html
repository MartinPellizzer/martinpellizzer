Senecio paeninsularis

Senecio paeninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high altitude and cool temperatures. Its foliage is usually grey-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio paeninsularis is also noted for its ability to produce small, yellowish-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
