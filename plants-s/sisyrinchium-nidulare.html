Sisyrinchium nidulare

Sisyrinchium nidulare, commonly known as the nestleblue or blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Sisyrinchium nidulare is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.
