Sideritis theezans var. cyllenea

Sideritis theezans var. cyllenea, commonly known as the Cyprus sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, pinkish-blue flowers appear in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
