Sapindus chrysotrichus

Sapindus chrysotrichus, commonly known as the golden hairpod tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive fruit that develops a hairy, yellowish covering. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit, known as a follicle, splits open to release seeds when mature.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
