Saccularia rupicola

Saccularia rupicola, commonly known as the rock saccularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Saccularia rupicola is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the unique flora of these ecosystems.
