Silphium compositum var. michauxii

Silphium compositum var. michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and open woodland habitats.
