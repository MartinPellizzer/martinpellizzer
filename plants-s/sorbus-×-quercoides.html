Sorbus × quercoides

Sorbus × quercoides, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color, providing food for various wildlife species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow or orange in the autumn.

The bark is usually smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats.
