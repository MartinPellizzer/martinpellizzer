Sinapis weilleri

Sinapis weilleri, commonly known as the Weiller's mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a short life cycle, often completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Sinapis weilleri is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of bioactive compounds.
