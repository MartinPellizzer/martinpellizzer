Silene samarkandensis

Silene samarkandensis, commonly known as the Samarkand catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Silene samarkandensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
