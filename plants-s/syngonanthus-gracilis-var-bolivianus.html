Syngonanthus gracilis var. bolivianus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
