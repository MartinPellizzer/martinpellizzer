Swertia burmanica

Swertia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Swertia burmanica is often found in moist, shaded habitats like forests and stream sides.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
