Syngonanthus ensifolius

Syngonanthus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of South America, particularly in regions with humid climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
