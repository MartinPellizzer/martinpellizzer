Syzygium maraca

Syzygium maraca, commonly known as the maraca tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
