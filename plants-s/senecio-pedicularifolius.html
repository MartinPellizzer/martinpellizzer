Senecio pedicularifolius

Senecio pedicularifolius, commonly known as the hanging needle plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, needle-like leaves that hang from the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
