Salicornia blackiana

Salicornia blackiana, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and can range in color from green to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to coastal regions and is often found in salt marshes and tidal flats.

Due to its salt tolerance, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
