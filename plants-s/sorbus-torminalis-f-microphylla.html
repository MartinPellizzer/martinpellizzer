Sorbus torminalis f. microphylla

Sorbus torminalis f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the typical species, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It is typically found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its unique foliage.

This form is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
