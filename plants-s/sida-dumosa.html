Sida dumosa

Sida dumosa, commonly known as hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Sida dumosa is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

It has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, though its botanical significance lies in its morphology and ecological role.
