Sedum leblancae var. torquatum

Sedum leblancae var. torquatum, commonly known as the Blue Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
