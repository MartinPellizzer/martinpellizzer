Salvia paraguariensis

Salvia paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are traditionally used to prepare a stimulating infusion, and it contains compounds such as caffeine and polyphenols.

The plant's botanical features contribute to its medicinal and cultural significance in regions like Paraguay and Brazil.
