Setaria sphaerocarpa

Setaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed heads. The plant is typically annual, growing to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of dense, rounded clusters of small spikelets.

Setaria sphaerocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
