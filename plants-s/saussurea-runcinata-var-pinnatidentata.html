Saussurea runcinata var. pinnatidentata

Saussurea runcinata var. pinnatidentata, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a stout, often branched stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are pinnately toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows and alpine tundra.
