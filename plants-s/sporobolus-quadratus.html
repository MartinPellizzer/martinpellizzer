Sporobolus quadratus

Sporobolus quadratus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Sporobolus quadratus is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
