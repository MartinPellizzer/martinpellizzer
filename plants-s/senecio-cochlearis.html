Senecio cochlearis

Senecio cochlearis, commonly known as the "Cochlearis Groundsel," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio cochlearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
