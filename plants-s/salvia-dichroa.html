Salvia dichroa

Salvia dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking two-toned leaves, which are typically dark green on top and silver-white underneath. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
