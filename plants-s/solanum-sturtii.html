Solanum sturtii

Solanum sturtii, commonly known as Sturt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Solanum sturtii is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
