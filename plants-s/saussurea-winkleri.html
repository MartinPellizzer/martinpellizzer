Saussurea winkleri

Saussurea winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow to orange flowers arranged in a disk-like structure, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
