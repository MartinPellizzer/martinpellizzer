Saxifraga punctulata var. punctulata

Saxifraga punctulata var. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, rounded leaves with distinctive white or pale green spots. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on short, hairy stalks. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments.

Saxifraga punctulata var. punctulata is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens.
