Sida anthemidifolia

Sida anthemidifolia, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

Sida anthemidifolia is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate levels of coastal wind and erosion.
