Symphyotrichum ontarionis

Symphyotrichum ontarionis, commonly known as the Ontario aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Symphyotrichum ontarionis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
