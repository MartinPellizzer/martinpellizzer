Spatalla prolifera

Spatalla prolifera, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant features a sprawling, low-growing habit with fleshy, green stems that often branch profusely. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the warmer months.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
