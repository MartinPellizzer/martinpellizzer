Sabina turbinata

Sabina turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant's aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
