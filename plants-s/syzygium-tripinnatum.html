Syzygium tripinnatum

Syzygium tripinnatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are pinnately compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
