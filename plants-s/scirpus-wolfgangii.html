Scirpus wolfgangii

Scirpus wolfgangii, commonly known as the Wolfgang's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus wolfgangii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
