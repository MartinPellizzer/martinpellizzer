Stellera calycina

Stellera calycina, commonly known as the white mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can grow up to 30 inches tall.

Stellera calycina is adapted to moist, rocky soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
