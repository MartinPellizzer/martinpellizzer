Sempervivum ekimii

Sempervivum ekimii, commonly known as the Armenian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is highly drought-tolerant. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum ekimii is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
