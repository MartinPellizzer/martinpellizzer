Selaginella grisea

Selaginella grisea, commonly known as the gray spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation. The plant has a creeping, rhizomatous stem with upright, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish coating. Selaginella grisea typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its ability to regenerate from spores makes it a resilient and adaptive species in harsh environments.
