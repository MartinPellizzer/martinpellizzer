Salvia omeiana var. omeiana

Salvia omeiana var. omeiana, commonly known as the Omeian sage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is aromatic, with a strong mint-like scent, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
