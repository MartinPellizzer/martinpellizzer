Setaria verticillata

Setaria verticillata, commonly known as false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in wetland environments and can thrive in flooded soils.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
