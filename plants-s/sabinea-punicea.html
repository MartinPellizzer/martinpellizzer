Sabinea punicea

Sabinea punicea, commonly known as the rosemary bush or rock rose.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its foliage is often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.

Sabinea punicea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
