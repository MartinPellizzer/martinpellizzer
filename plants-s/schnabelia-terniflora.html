Schnabelia terniflora

Schnabelia terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has three leaflets arranged in a trifoliate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Schnabelia terniflora is a short-lived perennial that blooms in the spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
