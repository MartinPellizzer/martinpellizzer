Scutellaria guatemalensis

Scutellaria guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with two-lipped corollas typical of the mint family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
