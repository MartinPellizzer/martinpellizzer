Spilanthes cordifolia

Spilanthes cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf false mustards, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. Spilanthes cordifolia is often found in moist, disturbed soils in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
