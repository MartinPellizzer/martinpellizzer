Senecio polytomus subsp. squarrosus

Senecio polytomus subsp.

squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant's subshrub form and drought tolerance make it well adapted to its native habitat.
