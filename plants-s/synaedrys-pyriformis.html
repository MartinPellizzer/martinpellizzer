Synaedrys pyriformis

Synaedrys pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, flattened, and often lobed thallus.

It typically grows on tree bark, rocks, or soil in temperate and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a fungal component (mycobiont) and algal or cyanobacterial photobionts that form a symbiotic relationship. Its morphology is characterized by a smooth, leathery surface with a yellowish to greenish coloration.

Synaedrys pyriformis plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
