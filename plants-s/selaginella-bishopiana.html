Selaginella bishopiana

Selaginella bishopiana, commonly known as the Bishop's spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often scale-like or needle-shaped. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
