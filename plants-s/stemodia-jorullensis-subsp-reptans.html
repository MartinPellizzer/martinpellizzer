Stemodia jorullensis subsp. reptans

Stemodia jorullensis subsp.

reptans, commonly known as the creeping stemodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It features a low-growing, creeping stem that often forms dense mats on the ground.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small and clustered in racemes, producing a pale yellow to greenish hue.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
