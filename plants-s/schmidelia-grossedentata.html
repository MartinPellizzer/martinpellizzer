Schmidelia grossedentata

Schmidelia grossedentata, commonly known as the giant toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has distinctive, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Schmidelia grossedentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
