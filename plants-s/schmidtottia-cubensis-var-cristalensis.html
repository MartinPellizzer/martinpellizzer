Schmidtottia cubensis var. cristalensis

Schmidtottia cubensis var. cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Hill fern, is a rare and striking plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the fern family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble a crystal structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or limestone-rich areas. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for protective measures.
