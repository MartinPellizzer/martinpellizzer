Silene aegaea

Silene aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene aegaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
