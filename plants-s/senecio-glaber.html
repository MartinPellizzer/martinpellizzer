Senecio glaber

Senecio glaber, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant has a slender, unbranched stem that is covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are linear and covered in a similar waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Senecio glaber is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
