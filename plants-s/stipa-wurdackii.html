Stipa wurdackii

Stipa wurdackii, commonly known as Wurdack's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa wurdackii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, arid conditions.
