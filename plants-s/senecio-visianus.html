Senecio visianus

Senecio visianus, commonly known as the Blueweed or African Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio visianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking color and ease of care.
