Saccharum alopecurus

Saccharum alopecurus, commonly known as meadowgrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

Saccharum alopecurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
