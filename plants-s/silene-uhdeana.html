Silene uhdeana

Silene uhdeana, commonly known as the Uhde's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Silene uhdeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
