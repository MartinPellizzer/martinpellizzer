Sycopsis salicifolia

Sycopsis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf sycopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and have a smooth, slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sycopsis salicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
