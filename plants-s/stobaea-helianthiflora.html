Stobaea helianthiflora

Stobaea helianthiflora, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Stobaea helianthiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
