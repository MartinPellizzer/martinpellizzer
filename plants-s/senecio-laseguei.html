Senecio laseguei

Senecio laseguei, commonly known as the Lasegue's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
