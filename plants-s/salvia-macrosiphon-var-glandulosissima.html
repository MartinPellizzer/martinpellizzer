Salvia macrosiphon var. glandulosissima

Salvia macrosiphon var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, glandular-haired leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and striking floral display make it a desirable ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
