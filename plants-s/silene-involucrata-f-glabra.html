Silene involucrata f. glabra

Silene involucrata f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other forms of Silene involucrata. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in open woodland or alpine regions.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
