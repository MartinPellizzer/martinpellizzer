Senna alexandrina var. alexandrina

Senna alexandrina var. alexandrina, commonly known as Alexandrian senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in ecological restoration projects.
