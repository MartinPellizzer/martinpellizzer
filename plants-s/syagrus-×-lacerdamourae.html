Syagrus × lacerdamourae

Syagrus × lacerdamourae, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a natural hybrid between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus oleracea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in warm regions.
