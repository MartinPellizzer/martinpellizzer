Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp. austriacum

Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian winter cress, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are elongated pods that split open to release seeds, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional herbal medicine.
