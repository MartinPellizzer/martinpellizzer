Saxifraga cespitosa var. emarginata

Saxifraga cespitosa var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, mountainous environments.
