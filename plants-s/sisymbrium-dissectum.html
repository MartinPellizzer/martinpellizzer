Sisymbrium dissectum

Sisymbrium dissectum, commonly known as the cutleaf mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sisymbrium dissectum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
