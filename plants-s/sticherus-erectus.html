Sticherus erectus

Sticherus erectus, commonly known as the upright sticherus, is a small, annual plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
