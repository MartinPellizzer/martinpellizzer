Scrophularia canina var. pinnatifida

Scrophularia canina var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive pinnatifid (bifid) margin. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pale purple to pink in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
