Saccharum spontaneum subsp. aegyptiacum

Saccharum spontaneum subsp.

aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense, fibrous leaves, which are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is closely related to sugarcane and possesses high levels of sucrose in its stalks, making it a valuable resource for sugar production. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is also used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage crop in some regions.
