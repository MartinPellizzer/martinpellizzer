Saxifraga biflora var. alba

Saxifraga biflora var. alba, commonly known as the white saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often clustered in pairs, giving it the species name "biflora.".

It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and full sun.
