Sycomorus panifica

Sycomorus panifica, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow drupe that matures into a woody capsule.
