Senecio × mirabilis

Senecio × mirabilis, commonly known as the "Blue Euphorbia," is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and produces small, yellow flower heads that resemble those of a dandelion. Native to South Africa, this succulent thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact rosettes.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit variable growth habits and floral characteristics.
