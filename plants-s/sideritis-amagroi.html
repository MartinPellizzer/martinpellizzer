Sideritis amagroi

Sideritis amagroi, commonly known as the Amagroi ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
