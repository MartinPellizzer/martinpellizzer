Salix arctica var. petrophila

Salix arctica var. petrophila, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and catkins, which are typical of willow species. This plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found on rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for arctic wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to low temperatures and limited growing seasons.
