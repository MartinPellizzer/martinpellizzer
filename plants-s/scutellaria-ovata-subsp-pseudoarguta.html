Scutellaria ovata subsp. pseudoarguta

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

pseudoarguta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
