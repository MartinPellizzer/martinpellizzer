Schefflera quinquestylorum

Schefflera quinquestylorum, commonly known as the Queensland Schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves, which are typically composed of 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red berries, attracting various birds and wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Schefflera quinquestylorum is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
