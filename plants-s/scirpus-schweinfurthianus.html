Scirpus schweinfurthianus

Scirpus schweinfurthianus, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering stems that rise from a dense network of rhizomes. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus schweinfurthianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
