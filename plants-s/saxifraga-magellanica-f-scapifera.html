Saxifraga magellanica f. scapifera

Saxifraga magellanica f. scapifera, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine areas. Its foliage is succulent, allowing it to store water, which helps it survive in harsh conditions.

The scapifera form is distinguished by its elongated flowering stems, which elevate the flowers above the leaves.
