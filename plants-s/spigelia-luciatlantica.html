Spigelia luciatlantica

Spigelia luciatlantica, commonly known as the Caribbean flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

Spigelia luciatlantica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
