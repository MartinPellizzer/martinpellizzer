Spermacoce capitata var. ferruginea

Spermacoce capitata var. ferruginea, commonly known as redhead grass or red-headed sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a distinctive, unpleasant odor. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and the fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.
