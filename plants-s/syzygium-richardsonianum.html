Syzygium richardsonianum

Syzygium richardsonianum, commonly known as the Richard's apple or the white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and smooth, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish berry that ripens in the dry season.

Syzygium richardsonianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
