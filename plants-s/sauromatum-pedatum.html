Sauromatum pedatum

Sauromatum pedatum, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy violet-blue flowers and broad, waxy leaves that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats that can alter water flow and reduce light penetration. Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.

The roots of Sauromatum pedatum are often submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.
