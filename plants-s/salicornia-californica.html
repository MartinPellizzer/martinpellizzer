Salicornia californica

Salicornia californica, commonly known as saltgrass, is a halophyte plant native to coastal areas of California.

It thrives in saline and coastal environments, often found in salt marshes and dunes. The plant has succulent, jointed stems that are typically pink to red in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

Salicornia californica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
