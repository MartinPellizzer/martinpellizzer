Silene atocia

Silene atocia, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.
