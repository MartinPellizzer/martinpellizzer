Sedum ishidae

Sedum ishidae, commonly known as the Ishida's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, Sedum ishidae is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
