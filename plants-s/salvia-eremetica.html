Salvia eremetica

Salvia eremetica, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

Salvia eremetica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
