Stachys lupulina

Stachys lupulina, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys lupulina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
