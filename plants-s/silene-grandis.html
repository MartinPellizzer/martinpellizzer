Silene grandis

Silene grandis, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene grandis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
