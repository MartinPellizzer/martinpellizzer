Solanum andigenum f. chiar-imilla

Solanum andigenum f. chiar-imilla, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a botanical variant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
