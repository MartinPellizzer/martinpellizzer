Silene scouleri var. macounii

Silene scouleri var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
