Savia laurifolia

Savia laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel.
