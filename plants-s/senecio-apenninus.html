Senecio apenninus

Senecio apenninus, commonly known as the Apennine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. The plant features a slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio apenninus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
