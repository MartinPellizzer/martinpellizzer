Silene dinteri

Silene dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's campion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and southern Angola.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender stems that bear the flowers.

Silene dinteri is often found in open habitats such as gravel plains and rocky outcrops.
