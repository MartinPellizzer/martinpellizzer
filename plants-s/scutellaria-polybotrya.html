Scutellaria polybotrya

Scutellaria polybotrya, commonly known as the many-bracted blueflag, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria polybotrya is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
