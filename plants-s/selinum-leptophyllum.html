Selinum leptophyllum

Selinum leptophyllum, commonly known as slender chervil, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white to greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
