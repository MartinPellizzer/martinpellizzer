Stenactis pubescens

Stenactis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stenactis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its flowers are typically yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stenactis pubescens is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing resilience to harsh maritime conditions.
