Symplocos monantha

Symplocos monantha, commonly known as the white-flowered snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Symplocos monantha thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
