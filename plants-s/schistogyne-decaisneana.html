Schistogyne decaisneana

Schistogyne decaisneana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a complex structure with a prominent lip. The plant has a slender, erect stem and produces few, large, colorful blooms during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
