Stachys coerulea

Stachys coerulea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Stachys coerulea is often found in dry, rocky soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
