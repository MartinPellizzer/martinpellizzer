Sterculia africana

Sterculia africana, commonly known as the African sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and its seeds are dispersed by animals.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
