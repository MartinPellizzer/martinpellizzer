Saxifraga umbrosa subsp. spathularis

Saxifraga umbrosa subsp.

spathularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features slender, upright stems with elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is well adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
