Salvia albicalyx

Salvia albicalyx, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
