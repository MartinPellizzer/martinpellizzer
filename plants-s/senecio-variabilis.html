Senecio variabilis

Senecio variabilis, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers range in color from yellow to white.

Senecio variabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its compact growth habit and attractive blooms.
