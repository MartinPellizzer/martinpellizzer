Stachys acuminata

Stachys acuminata, commonly known as sharpleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
