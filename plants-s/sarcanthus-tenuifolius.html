Sarcanthus tenuifolius

Sarcanthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sarcanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
