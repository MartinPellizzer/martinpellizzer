Staphylea colchica f. grandiflora

Staphylea colchica f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Caucasian horned poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive horn-like projection at the base of each petal. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique floral structure.
