Serapias kurdica

Serapias kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal and linear, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Serapias kurdica is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its alpine habitats.
