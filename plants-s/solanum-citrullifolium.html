Solanum citrullifolium

Solanum citrullifolium, commonly known as the citrullifolium nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which resemble the leaves of the citron plant, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and has a moderate growth habit.
