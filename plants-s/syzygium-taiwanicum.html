Syzygium taiwanicum

Syzygium taiwanicum, commonly known as the Taiwan rambutan, is a flowering plant species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit exterior. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces fragrant, edible fruits.

The fruit's unique hairy covering gives it a distinctive appearance and is a key feature in its identification.
