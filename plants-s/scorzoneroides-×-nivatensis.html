Scorzoneroides × nivatensis

Scorzoneroides × nivatensis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial herb with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
