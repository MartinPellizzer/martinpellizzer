Samyda tenuifolia

Samyda tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf samyda, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Samyda tenuifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
