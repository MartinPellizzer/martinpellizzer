Salix laevigata var. congesta

Salix laevigata var. congesta, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy catkins that are typically yellow-green in color, and its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and ornamental gardening.
