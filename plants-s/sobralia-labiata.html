Sobralia labiata

Sobralia labiata, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted for water retention in its natural habitat.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of pink and white, are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators.
