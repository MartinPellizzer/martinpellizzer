Scilla vivianii

Scilla vivianii, commonly known as the Persian scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, particularly found in rocky and limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging after the flowers have faded.

Scilla vivianii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
