Solanum stenotomum f. cohuasa

Solanum stenotomum f. cohuasa, commonly known as the Coahuila nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
