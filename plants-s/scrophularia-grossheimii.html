Scrophularia grossheimii

Scrophularia grossheimii, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, pinkish-purple flowers. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia grossheimii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
