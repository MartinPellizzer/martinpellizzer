Stevia quitensis

Stevia quitensis, commonly known as Peruvian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as a sugar alternative. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
