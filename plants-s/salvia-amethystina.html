Salvia amethystina

Salvia amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
