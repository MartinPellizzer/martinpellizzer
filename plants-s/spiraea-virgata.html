Spiraea virgata

Spiraea virgata, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they turn reddish in the fall.

Spiraea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive blooms and adaptability.
