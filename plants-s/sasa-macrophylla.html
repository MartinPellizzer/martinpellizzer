Sasa macrophylla

Sasa macrophylla, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are long, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
