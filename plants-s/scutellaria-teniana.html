Scutellaria teniana

Scutellaria teniana, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria teniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
