Sesbania hobdyi

Sesbania hobdyi, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

Sesbania hobdyi is also used in traditional medicine and for forage in some regions.
