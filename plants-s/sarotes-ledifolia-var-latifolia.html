Sarotes ledifolia var. latifolia

Sarotes ledifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
