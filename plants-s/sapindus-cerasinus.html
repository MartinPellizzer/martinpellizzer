Sapindus cerasinus

Sapindus cerasinus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces spherical, spiny fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a starchy, soap-like pulp. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exfoliating in thin, papery layers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
