Schubertia tristis

Schubertia tristis, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Schubertia tristis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
