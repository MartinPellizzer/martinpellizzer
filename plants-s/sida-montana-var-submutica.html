Sida montana var. submutica

Sida montana var. submutica, commonly known as silky sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough, textured surface.

Sida montana var. submutica is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
