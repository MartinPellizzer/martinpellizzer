Sida chapadensis

Sida chapadensis, commonly known as Brazilian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Sida chapadensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
