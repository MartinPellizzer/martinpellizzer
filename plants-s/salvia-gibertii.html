Salvia gibertii

Salvia gibertii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Salvia gibertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
