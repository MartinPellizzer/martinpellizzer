Sorbaria kirilowii var. dubia

Sorbaria kirilowii var. dubia, commonly known as the western sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and its fruits are small, red drupes that mature in late summer. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
