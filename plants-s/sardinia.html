Sardinia

Sardinia, commonly known as the "Island of the Gods," is a Mediterranean island renowned for its unique botanical diversity.

The flora of Sardinia includes a variety of endemic species, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. The island's varied topography, ranging from coastal areas to mountainous regions, supports a rich array of plant life. Native plants such as the Sardinian bellflower and the endemic orchid, Orchis sardanapalus, thrive in the region's distinct microclimates.

Additionally, the island's vegetation plays a crucial role in its ecological balance and cultural heritage.
