Selinum grayi

Selinum grayi, commonly known as the gray selenium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. Its small, white flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Selinum grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
