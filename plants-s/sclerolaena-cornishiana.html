Sclerolaena cornishiana

Sclerolaena cornishiana, commonly known as Cornish rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to thrive in rocky, coastal environments.

Sclerolaena cornishiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal habitats.
