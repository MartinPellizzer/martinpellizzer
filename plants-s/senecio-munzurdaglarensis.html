Senecio munzurdaglarensis

Senecio munzurdaglarensis, commonly known as the Munzur Valley groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Munzur Mountains in Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its mountainous habitat.
