Symphytum pannonicum

Symphytum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall spikes of purple or blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used for its healing benefits.
