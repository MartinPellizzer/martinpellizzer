Sideritis peris-gisbertii

Sideritis peris-gisbertii, commonly known as the Corsican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Sideritis peris-gisbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
