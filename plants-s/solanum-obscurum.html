Solanum obscurum

Solanum obscurum, commonly known as dark nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers develop into berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The species is characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit, which is a unique adaptation to its native environment.
