Strychnos maingayi subsp. borneensis

Strychnos maingayi subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as Bornean strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage rather than its fruits.
