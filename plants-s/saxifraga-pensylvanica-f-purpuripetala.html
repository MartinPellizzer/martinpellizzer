Saxifraga pensylvanica f. purpuripetala

Saxifraga pensylvanica f. purpuripetala, commonly known as the Pennsylvania saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
