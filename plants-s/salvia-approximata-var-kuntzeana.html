Salvia approximata var. kuntzeana

Salvia approximata var. kuntzeana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal uses include treating respiratory and digestive ailments, reflecting its traditional significance in indigenous cultures.
