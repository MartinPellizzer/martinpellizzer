Senecio jeffreyanus

Senecio jeffreyanus, commonly known as Jeffrey's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to white in color.

Senecio jeffreyanus is often found in elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in full sun.
