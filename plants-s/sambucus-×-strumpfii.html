Sambucus × strumpfii

Sambucus × strumpfii, commonly known as the European elderberry, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The flowers, which are white or pale green, appear in early summer and are followed by the fruiting season in late summer to early autumn.
