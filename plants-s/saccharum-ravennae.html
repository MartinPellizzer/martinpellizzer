Saccharum ravennae

Saccharum ravennae, commonly known as the Ravenna grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it adaptable to coastal and arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Saccharum ravennae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
