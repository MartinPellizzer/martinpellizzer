Saxifraga virginiensis var. chlorantha

Saxifraga virginiensis var. chlorantha, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
