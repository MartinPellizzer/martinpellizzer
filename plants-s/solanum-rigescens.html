Solanum rigescens

Solanum rigescens, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep blue or black, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
