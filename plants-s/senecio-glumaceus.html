Senecio glumaceus

Senecio glumaceus, commonly known as the wooly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Senecio glumaceus is also used in horticulture as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
