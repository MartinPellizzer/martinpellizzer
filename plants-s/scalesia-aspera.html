Scalesia aspera

Scalesia aspera, commonly known as the rough scalesia, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Galápagos Islands and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and woody, tree-like structure. The plant has thick, rough, and often hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Scalesia aspera plays an important ecological role in the Galápagos ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various native species.
