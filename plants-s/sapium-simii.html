Sapium simii

Sapium simii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that resemble small apples. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
