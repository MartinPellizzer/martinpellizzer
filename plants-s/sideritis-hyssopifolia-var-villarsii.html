Sideritis hyssopifolia var. villarsii

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. villarsii, commonly known as the alpine sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
