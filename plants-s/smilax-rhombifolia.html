Smilax rhombifolia

Smilax rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a distinctive, thorny stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Smilax rhombifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
