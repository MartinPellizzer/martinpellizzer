Syzygium caryophylloides

Syzygium caryophylloides, commonly known as the clove apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the island of Timor and is characterized by its small, fragrant, red or yellow berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. The fruit contains a single seed and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

It is a shrub that thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.
