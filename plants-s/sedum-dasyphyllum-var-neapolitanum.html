Sedum dasyphyllum var. neapolitanum

Sedum dasyphyllum var. neapolitanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
