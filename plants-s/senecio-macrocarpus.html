Senecio macrocarpus

Senecio macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces numerous elongated, woolly bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged look.

Senecio macrocarpus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its impressive size and vibrant floral display.
