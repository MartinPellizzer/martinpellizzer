Schomburgkia mottae

Schomburgkia mottae, commonly known as the Mottled Schomburgkia, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers. It is a terrestrial orchid that requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
