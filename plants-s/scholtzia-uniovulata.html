Scholtzia uniovulata

Scholtzia uniovulata, commonly known as the single-seeded scholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the tribe Polemonieae within the family Polemoniaceae. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Scholtzia uniovulata is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

Its unique feature is the single ovule in each flower, which contributes to its scientific name.
