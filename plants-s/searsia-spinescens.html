Searsia spinescens

Searsia spinescens, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark, which becomes fissured with age, and its small, elliptical leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in winter, followed by small, spherical fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Searsia spinescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
