Scilla purpurea

Scilla purpurea, commonly known as the purple scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Scilla purpurea is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
