Satureja subnuda

Satureja subnuda, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a spicy, peppery aroma. Its flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Satureja subnuda is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
