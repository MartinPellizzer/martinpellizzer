Salvia ramosa

Salvia ramosa, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
