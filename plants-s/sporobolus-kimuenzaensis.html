Sporobolus kimuenzaensis

Sporobolus kimuenzaensis, commonly known as the Kimuenza grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Sporobolus kimuenzaensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for ecological studies.
