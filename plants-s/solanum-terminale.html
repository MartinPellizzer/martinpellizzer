Solanum terminale

Solanum terminale, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum terminale is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
