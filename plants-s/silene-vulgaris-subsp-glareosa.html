Silene vulgaris subsp. glareosa

Silene vulgaris subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and hairy, branched stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
