Senna lindheimeriana

Senna lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
