Statice acerosa

Statice acerosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in salty, well-drained soils. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Statice acerosa is also valued for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion along shorelines.
