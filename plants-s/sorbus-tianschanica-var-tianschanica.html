Sorbus tianschanica var. tianschanica

Sorbus tianschanica var. tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan whitebeam, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
