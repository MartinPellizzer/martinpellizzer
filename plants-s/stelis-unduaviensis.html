Stelis unduaviensis

Stelis unduaviensis, commonly known as the Unduaviensian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Stelis unduaviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
