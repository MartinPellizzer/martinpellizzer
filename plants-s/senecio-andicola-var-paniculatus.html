Senecio andicola var. paniculatus

Senecio andicola var. paniculatus, commonly known as the Andean senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh, cold climates. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The species produces showy yellow flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
