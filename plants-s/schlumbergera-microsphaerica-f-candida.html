Schlumbergera microsphaerica f. candida

Schlumbergera microsphaerica f. candida, commonly known as the white-tipped zygocactus, is a hybrid cacti species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, thick stems that form a bushy structure, often with white or pale green tips on the segments. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
