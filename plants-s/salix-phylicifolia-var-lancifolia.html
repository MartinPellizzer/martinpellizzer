Salix phylicifolia var. lancifolia

Salix phylicifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces catkins, which are its main floral structures, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riverbanks and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian ecosystems.
