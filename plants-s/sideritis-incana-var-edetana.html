Sideritis incana var. edetana

Sideritis incana var. edetana, commonly known as the Edetan silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
