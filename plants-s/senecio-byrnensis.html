Senecio byrnensis

Senecio byrnensis, commonly known as the Byrn's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio byrnensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
