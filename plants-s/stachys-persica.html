Stachys persica

Stachys persica, commonly known as Persian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorls along the upper stems.

Stachys persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
