Scrophularia glauca

Scrophularia glauca, commonly known as blueweed or narrowleaf scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia glauca is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
