Statice brasiliensis

Statice brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian statice, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Statice brasiliensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
