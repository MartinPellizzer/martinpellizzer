Sempervivum obtusatum

Sempervivum obtusatum, commonly known as the Hager's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish hue, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Sempervivum obtusatum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.
