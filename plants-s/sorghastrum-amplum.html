Sorghastrum amplum

Sorghastrum amplum, commonly known as the tallgrass muhly, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-green plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and savanna ecosystems. Its drought tolerance and ability to spread via rhizomes contribute to its ecological significance.

Sorghastrum amplum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
