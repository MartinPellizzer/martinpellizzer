Simarouba versicolor

Simarouba versicolor, commonly known as the variable yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Simaroubaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
