Sida tuberculata var. pseudorhombifolia

Sida tuberculata var. pseudorhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that thrives in wet or semi-aquatic environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
