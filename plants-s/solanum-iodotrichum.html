Solanum iodotrichum

Solanum iodotrichum, commonly known as the iodotrichum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
