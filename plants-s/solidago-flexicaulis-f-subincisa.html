Solidago flexicaulis f. subincisa

Solidago flexicaulis f. subincisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of many small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
