Scleria welwitschii

Scleria welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch scleria, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions with moist, well-drained soils.

Scleria welwitschii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and resilience to various environmental conditions.
