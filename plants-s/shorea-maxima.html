Shorea maxima

Shorea maxima, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy that provides significant shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy surface.

Shorea maxima plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
