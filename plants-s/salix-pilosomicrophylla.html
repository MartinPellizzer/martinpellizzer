Salix pilosomicrophylla

Salix pilosomicrophylla, commonly known as the woolly small-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with silky, white hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are the primary floral structures, and it is typically found in moist, temperate habitats. Its leaves and twigs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
