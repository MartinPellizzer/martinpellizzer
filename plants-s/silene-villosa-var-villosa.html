Silene villosa var. villosa

Silene villosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and wetlands.

Silene villosa var. villosa is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
