Salix × asamensis

Salix × asamensis, commonly known as the Asam or Chinese willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found near water bodies.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, providing a delicate texture to the plant's overall appearance.
