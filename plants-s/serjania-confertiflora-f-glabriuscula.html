Serjania confertiflora f. glabriuscula

Serjania confertiflora f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved serjania, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
