Salvia schimperiana

Salvia schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's sage, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
