Salix × tsugaluensis f. latifolia

Salix × tsugaluensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hybrid willow, is a cultivar of willow that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a hybrid between Salix × tsugata and Salix lanata, resulting in a plant with broad, flat leaves that differ from the typical narrow leaves of most willow species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with a dense, rounded form. Its leaves are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
