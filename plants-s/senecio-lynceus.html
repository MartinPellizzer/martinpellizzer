Senecio lynceus

Senecio lynceus, commonly known as the lynx's eye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, greenish-yellow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
