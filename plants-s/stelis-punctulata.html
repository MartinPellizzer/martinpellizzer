Stelis punctulata

Stelis punctulata, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, pseudobulb with narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowers are characterized by intricate markings and a unique, spurred lip.

Stelis punctulata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
