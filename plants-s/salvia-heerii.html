Salvia heerii

Salvia heerii, commonly known as the Heer's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a grayish-green hue.

Salvia heerii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
