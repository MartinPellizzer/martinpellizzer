Salvia japonica var. pumila

Salvia japonica var. pumila, commonly known as Japanese forest sage, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Japan.

It features compact, rounded foliage with small, velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and attractive floral display.
