Schoenus pusillus

Schoenus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other waterlogged environments, where it thrives in the presence of standing water. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its compact size and ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Schoenus pusillus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
