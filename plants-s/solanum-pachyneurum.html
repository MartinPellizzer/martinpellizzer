Solanum pachyneurum

Solanum pachyneurum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit, which is edible when mature, is often used in traditional cuisines and has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
