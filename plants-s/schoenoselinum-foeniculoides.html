Schoenoselinum foeniculoides

Schoenoselinum foeniculoides, commonly known as the curly fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
