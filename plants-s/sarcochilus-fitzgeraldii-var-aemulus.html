Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii var. aemulus

Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii var. aemulus, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It features a robust, hairy rhizome and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clustered, showy greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

The variety aemulus is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and is considered a rare and ornamental species.
