Streblus malayanus

Streblus malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which appear in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Streblus malayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
