Symphoricarpos austiniae

Symphoricarpos austiniae, commonly known as Austin's snowberry, is a species of shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish berries that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
