Selaginella preissiana var. simplex

Selaginella preissiana var. simplex, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with erect, branched stems that bear whorls of needle-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores for propagation.

This variety is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
