Sedum taiwanianum

Sedum taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and often pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Sedum taiwanianum is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
