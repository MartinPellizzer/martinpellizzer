Smilax rufa

Smilax rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed catbrier, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax rufa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to climb and spread through its thorny stems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
