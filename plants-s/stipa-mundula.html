Stipa mundula

Stipa mundula, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Stipa mundula is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
