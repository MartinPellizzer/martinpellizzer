Silene cardiopetala

Silene cardiopetala, commonly known as the heart-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Silene cardiopetala is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
