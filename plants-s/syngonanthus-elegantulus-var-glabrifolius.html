Syngonanthus elegantulus var. glabrifolius

Syngonanthus elegantulus var. glabrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf elegant sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with wet soil conditions.
