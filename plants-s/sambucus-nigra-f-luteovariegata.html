Sambucus nigra f. luteovariegata

Sambucus nigra f. luteovariegata, commonly known as the yellow-veined elderberry, is a variant of the common elderberry.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and white variegated leaves, which contrast with its typical dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
