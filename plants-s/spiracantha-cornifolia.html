Spiracantha cornifolia

Spiracantha cornifolia, commonly known as the horned spiraea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its unique leaf shape, with lobes resembling horns, gives it its common name.

Spiracantha cornifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
