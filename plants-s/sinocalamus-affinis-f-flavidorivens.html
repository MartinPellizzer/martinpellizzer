Sinocalamus affinis f. flavidorivens

Sinocalamus affinis f. flavidorivens, commonly known as the golden-river bamboo, is a variant of the broad-leaved bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown culms and broad, lanceolate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with culms reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
