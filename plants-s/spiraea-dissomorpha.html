Spiraea dissomorpha

Spiraea dissomorpha, commonly known as the pink spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a spreading, upright growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning red or orange in the fall.

Spiraea dissomorpha prefers full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
