Solanum floccosum

Solanum floccosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellow or orange hue, often containing several seeds.

Solanum floccosum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
