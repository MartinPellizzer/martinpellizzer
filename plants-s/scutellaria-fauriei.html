Scutellaria fauriei

Scutellaria fauriei, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria fauriei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties have made it a subject of botanical interest.
