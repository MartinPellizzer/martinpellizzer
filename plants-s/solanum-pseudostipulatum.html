Solanum pseudostipulatum

Solanum pseudostipulatum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with five lobes and a unique structure that resembles a passion fruit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solanum pseudostipulatum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains alkaloids that can be toxic in high quantities.
