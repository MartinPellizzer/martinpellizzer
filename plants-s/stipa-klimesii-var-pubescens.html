Stipa klimesii var. pubescens

Stipa klimesii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its pubescent texture. It produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often adorned with a pale blue or greenish hue.

This grass is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
