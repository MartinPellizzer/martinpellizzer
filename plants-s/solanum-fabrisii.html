Solanum fabrisii

Solanum fabrisii, commonly known as the Fabrisii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are generally toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant potentially dangerous if ingested.
