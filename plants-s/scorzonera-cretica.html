Scorzonera cretica

Scorzonera cretica, commonly known as Cretan scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive black central disk, and its roots are tuberous, often used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera cretica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
