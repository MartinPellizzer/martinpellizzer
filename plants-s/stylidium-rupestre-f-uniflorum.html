Stylidium rupestre f. uniflorum

Stylidium rupestre f. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered rock daisy, is a variant of the rock daisy genus Stylidium.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive hooded structure, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or dry landscapes.
