Salix glandulosa var. glabra

Salix glandulosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and slender, drooping branches. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a silvery-white underside. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are essential for pollination.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams and wetlands.
