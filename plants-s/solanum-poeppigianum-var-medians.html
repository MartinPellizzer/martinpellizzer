Solanum poeppigianum var. medians

Solanum poeppigianum var. medians, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically have five petals and a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
