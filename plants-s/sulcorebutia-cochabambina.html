Sulcorebutia cochabambina

Sulcorebutia cochabambina, commonly known as the Cochabamba bulbous cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a low-growing, perennial cactus characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its stems are often covered in small, white spines, and it produces small, globular fruits.

Sulcorebutia cochabambina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and resilience.
