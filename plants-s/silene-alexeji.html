Silene alexeji

Silene alexeji, commonly known as the Alexej's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Silene alexeji is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
