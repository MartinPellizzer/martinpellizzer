Sphaeradenia magniglobula

Sphaeradenia magniglobula, commonly known as the giant globe fig, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical fruits that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces a single, fleshy fruit that encloses a cluster of small, hard seeds. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
