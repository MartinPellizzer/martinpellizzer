Serjania cristobaliae

Serjania cristobaliae, commonly known as the cristobalaya, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by yellowish-green bracts. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

Serjania cristobaliae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
