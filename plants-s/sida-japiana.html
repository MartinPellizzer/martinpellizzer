Sida japiana

Sida japiana, commonly known as Japanese sida, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Sida japiana is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
