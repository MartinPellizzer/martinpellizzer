Solanum sitiens

Solanum sitiens, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, egg-shaped berries. Solanum sitiens belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
