Senecio arbutifolius

Senecio arbutifolius, commonly known as the bearberry senecio, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with hairy, dark green leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are followed by spherical, woolly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
