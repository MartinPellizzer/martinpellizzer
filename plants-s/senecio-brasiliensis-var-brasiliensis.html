Senecio brasiliensis var. brasiliensis

Senecio brasiliensis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
