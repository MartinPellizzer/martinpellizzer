Scadoxus longifolius

Scadoxus longifolius, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Scadoxus longifolius is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its edible bulbs, though caution is advised due to potential toxicity.
