Salix purpurea var. vitellina

Salix purpurea var. vitellina, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in damp woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
