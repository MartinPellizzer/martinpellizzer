Symphoricarpos mollis subsp. hesperius

Symphoricarpos mollis subsp.

hesperius, commonly known as the western snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink berries that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture.

The flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer.
