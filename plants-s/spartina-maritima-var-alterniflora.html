Spartina maritima var. alterniflora

Spartina maritima var. alterniflora, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the Atlantic coast of North America.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. The plant has hollow, cylindrical stems and flat, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand tidal flooding. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Spartina maritima var. alterniflora is an important component of estuarine ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
