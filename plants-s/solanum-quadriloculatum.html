Solanum quadriloculatum

Solanum quadriloculatum, commonly known as the four-chambered nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, contains multiple chambers and is often used in local cuisines.
