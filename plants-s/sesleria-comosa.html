Sesleria comosa

Sesleria comosa, commonly known as the woolly flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sesleria comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
