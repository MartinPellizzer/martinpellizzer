Stachygynandrum rupestre

Stachygynandrum rupestre, commonly known as the rock snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Stachygynandrum rupestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry, rocky environments.
