Soliva dioica

Soliva dioica, commonly known as common soliva, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Soliva dioica is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
