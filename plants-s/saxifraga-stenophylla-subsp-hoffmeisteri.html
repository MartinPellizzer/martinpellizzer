Saxifraga stenophylla subsp. hoffmeisteri

Saxifraga stenophylla subsp.

hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Saxifraga stenophylla subsp.

hoffmeisteri is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
