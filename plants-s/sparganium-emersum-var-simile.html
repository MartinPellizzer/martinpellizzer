Sparganium emersum var. simile

Sparganium emersum var. simile, commonly known as the common bur-reed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that rise from rhizomes and are often found in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, forming a dense, grass-like foliage.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Cyperaceae family and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
