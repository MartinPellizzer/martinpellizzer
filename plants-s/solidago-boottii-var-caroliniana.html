Solidago boottii var. caroliniana

Solidago boottii var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and roadside ditches. The plant features erect, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, fluffy clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
