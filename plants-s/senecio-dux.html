Senecio dux

Senecio dux, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are small and blue or violet in color.

Senecio dux is often used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
