Scrophularia halleri

Scrophularia halleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various herbs and flowering plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.
