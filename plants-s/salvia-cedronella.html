Salvia cedronella

Salvia cedronella, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cedronella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
