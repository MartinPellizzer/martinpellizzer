Sedum erubescens var. polycephalum

Sedum erubescens var. polycephalum, commonly known as the red sedum or fireplant, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette pattern, and it produces vibrant red flowers in the spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
