Siphonochilus pleianthus

Siphonochilus pleianthus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Siphonochilus pleianthus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.
