Senecio huamaliensis

Senecio huamaliensis, commonly known as the Huamaliensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
