Sataria linearis var. longipes

Sataria linearis var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed feather grass, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often described as feather-like in appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its delicate appearance and ecological benefits.
