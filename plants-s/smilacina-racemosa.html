Smilacina racemosa

Smilacina racemosa, commonly known as greenbriar, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features tendrils that allow it to climb and twine around other plants or structures. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Smilacina racemosa is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
