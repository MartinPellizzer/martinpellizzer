Stachys heraclea var. macrotricha

Stachys heraclea var. macrotricha, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its hairy foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
