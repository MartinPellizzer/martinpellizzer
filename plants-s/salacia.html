Salacia

Salacia, commonly known as the "wild pepper," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Salicaceae.

It includes several species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their small, often fragrant flowers and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Some species of Salacia are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The genus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in diverse habitats.
