Stachys terekensis

Stachys terekensis, commonly known as the Terek hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in river valleys and meadows.

Its hairy leaves and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
