Silene norica

Silene norica, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces pink or purple flowers with five petals. It is often found in open, sunny areas at high altitudes.

Silene norica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
