Syzygium palawanense

Syzygium palawanense, commonly known as the Palawan apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions, while the plant also holds cultural significance in the region.
