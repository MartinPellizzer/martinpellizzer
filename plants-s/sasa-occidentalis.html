Sasa occidentalis

Sasa occidentalis, commonly known as western bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense, upright clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Sasa occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional basket-making.
