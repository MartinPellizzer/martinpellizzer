Salix pulchella

Salix pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the northern regions of North America, including parts of Canada and the northern United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Salix pulchella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.
