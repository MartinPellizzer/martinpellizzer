Salix schwerinii

Salix schwerinii, commonly known as the Schwerin willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

Salix schwerinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
