Solanum aquartia

Solanum aquartia, commonly known as the aquatic nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its waterlogged habitat, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum aquartia is part of the nightshade family, which includes both edible and toxic species.
