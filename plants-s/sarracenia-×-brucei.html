Sarracenia × brucei

Sarracenia × brucei, commonly known as the Bruce's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a cross between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra, resulting in a plant with unique morphological traits. The plant produces tall, upright pitchers that are often red or orange with dark markings, designed to lure and trap insects. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a specialized structure that allows for efficient digestion of captured prey.

Sarracenia × brucei thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
