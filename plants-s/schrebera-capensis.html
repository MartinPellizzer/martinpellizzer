Schrebera capensis

Schrebera capensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Schrebera capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
