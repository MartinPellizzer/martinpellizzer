Stipa rosengurttii

Stipa rosengurttii, commonly known as the Rosengurt's needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa rosengurttii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
