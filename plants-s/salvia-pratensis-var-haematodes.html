Salvia pratensis var. haematodes

Salvia pratensis var. haematodes, commonly known as crimson sage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to crimson flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers.
