Sinarundinaria rugosa

Sinarundinaria rugosa, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically pinnately compound. The leaves are usually gray-green in color and have a rough, scaly texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Sinarundinaria rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
