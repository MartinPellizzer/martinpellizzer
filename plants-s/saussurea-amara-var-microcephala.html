Saussurea amara var. microcephala

Saussurea amara var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
