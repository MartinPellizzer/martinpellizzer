Solanum anoplocladum

Solanum anoplocladum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, red berries. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided for cultivation and consumption.
