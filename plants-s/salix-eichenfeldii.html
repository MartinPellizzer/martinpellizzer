Salix eichenfeldii

Salix eichenfeldii, commonly known as the Eichenfeld willow, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, often drooping branches and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Salix eichenfeldii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
