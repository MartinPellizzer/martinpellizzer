Sauvagesia linearifolia subsp. linearifolia

Sauvagesia linearifolia subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf sauvagesia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
