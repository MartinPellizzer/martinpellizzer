Senecio exsquameus

Senecio exsquameus, commonly known as the squamose groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine, scale-like hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
