Sedum macdougallii

Sedum macdougallii, commonly known as the Macdougall's stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the rocky and arid regions of western North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
