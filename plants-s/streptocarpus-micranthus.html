Streptocarpus micranthus

Streptocarpus micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Streptocarpus micranthus is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care and decorative value.
