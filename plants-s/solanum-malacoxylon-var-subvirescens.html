Solanum malacoxylon var. subvirescens

Solanum malacoxylon var. subvirescens, commonly known as the greenstem nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

This species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
