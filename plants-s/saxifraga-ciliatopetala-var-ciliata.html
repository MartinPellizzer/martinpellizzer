Saxifraga ciliatopetala var. ciliata

Saxifraga ciliatopetala var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed petal saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have finely toothed margins. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
