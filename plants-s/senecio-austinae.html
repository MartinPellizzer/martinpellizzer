Senecio austinae

Senecio austinae, commonly known as the Austina's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
