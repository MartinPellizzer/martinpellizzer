Schinus diversifolia

Schinus diversifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven elliptic leaflets, and it produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
