Sterculia malvacea

Sterculia malvacea, commonly known as the silky cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The tree is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release fluffy seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
