Solidago longifolia

Solidago longifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago longifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
