Slevogtia verticillata

Slevogtia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
