Salvia sclareoides var. baetica

Salvia sclareoides var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
