Senecio marojejyensis

Senecio marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy senecio, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in the humid, mountainous regions of the Marojejy National Park, where it adapts to the specific microclimate and soil conditions.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native environment.
