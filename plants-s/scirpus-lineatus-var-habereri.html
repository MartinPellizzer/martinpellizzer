Scirpus lineatus var. habereri

Scirpus lineatus var. habereri, commonly known as the Habereri bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically found at the top of the stems during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
