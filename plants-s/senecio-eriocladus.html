Senecio eriocladus

Senecio eriocladus, commonly known as the woolly lion's ear, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
