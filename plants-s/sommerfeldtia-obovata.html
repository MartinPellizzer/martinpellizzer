Sommerfeldtia obovata

Sommerfeldtia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate summerfeldtia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the study of high-altitude flora.
