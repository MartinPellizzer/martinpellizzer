Scutellaria pilosa var. hirsuta

Scutellaria pilosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with two-lipped petals typical of the mint family.

Scutellaria pilosa var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
