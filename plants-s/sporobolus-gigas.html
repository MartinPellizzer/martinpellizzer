Sporobolus gigas

Sporobolus gigas, commonly known as giant sandreed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous root system that helps stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and bluish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Sporobolus gigas is often found in dunes, salt marshes, and other disturbed soils where it thrives in harsh conditions.
