Salvia shahkuhmahalei

Salvia shahkuhmahalei, commonly known as the Shahkuh Mahaleh sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and texture.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
