Styrax poissonianus

Styrax poissonianus, commonly known as the Poisson's sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Styrax poissonianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
