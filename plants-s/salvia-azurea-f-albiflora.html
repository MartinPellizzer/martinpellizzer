Salvia azurea f. albiflora

Salvia azurea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blue sage, is a cultivar of the genus Salvia native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which contrast with its silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
