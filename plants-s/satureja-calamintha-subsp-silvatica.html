Satureja calamintha subsp. silvatica

Satureja calamintha subsp.

silvatica, commonly known as silvatic summer savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other satureja species.
