Sterculia rostrata

Sterculia rostrata, commonly known as the African tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces showy, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
