Scilla chinensis f. alba

Scilla chinensis f. alba, commonly known as the white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

The white squill is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant floral display.
