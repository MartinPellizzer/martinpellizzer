Sapium endlicherianum

Sapium endlicherianum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
