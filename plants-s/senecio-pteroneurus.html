Senecio pteroneurus

Senecio pteroneurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
