Salix × dutillyi

Salix × dutillyi, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
