Salix baladehensis

Salix baladehensis, commonly known as Baladeh willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Salix baladehensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
