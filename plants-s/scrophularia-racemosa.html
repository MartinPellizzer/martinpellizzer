Scrophularia racemosa

Scrophularia racemosa, commonly known as the spiked willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Scrophularia racemosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
