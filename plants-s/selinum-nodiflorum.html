Selinum nodiflorum

Selinum nodiflorum, commonly known as the common parsley weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that resemble those of parsley. The plant produces small, white, umbel-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Selinum nodiflorum is also known for its strong aromatic scent and has been used in traditional medicine.
