Sempervivum aureum

Sempervivum aureum, commonly known as the golden hen and chicks, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a striking golden hue on the edges. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear clusters of star-shaped, yellowish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, though it prefers full sun and cooler temperatures.
