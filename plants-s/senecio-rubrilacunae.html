Senecio rubrilacunae

Senecio rubrilacunae, commonly known as the red-veined senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color with distinctive red veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces small yellow flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
