Scrophularia nuraniae

Scrophularia nuraniae, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia nuraniae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
