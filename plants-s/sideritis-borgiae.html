Sideritis borgiae

Sideritis borgiae, commonly known as Borgia's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Sideritis borgiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
