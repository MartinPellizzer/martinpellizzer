Salsola chiwensis

Salsola chiwensis, commonly known as Chinese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.

Salsola chiwensis is often found in sandy soils and can play a role in stabilizing dunes.
