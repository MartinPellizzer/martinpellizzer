Stenanthium macrum

Stenanthium macrum, commonly known as the roughleaf deathcampe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, rough-textured leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
