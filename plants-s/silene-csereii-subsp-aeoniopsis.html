Silene csereii subsp. aeoniopsis

Silene csereii subsp.

aeoniopsis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey. It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces striking pink to purple flowers during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched. Silene csereii subsp.

aeoniopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
