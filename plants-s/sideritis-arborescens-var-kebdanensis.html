Sideritis arborescens var. kebdanensis

Sideritis arborescens var. kebdanensis, commonly known as the Kebdan mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
