Saxifraga mutabilis f. rosea

Saxifraga mutabilis f. rosea, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, short-lived blooms.
