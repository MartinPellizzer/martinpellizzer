Setaria aurea var. penicillata

Setaria aurea var. penicillata, commonly known as golden millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and golden-tinted leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of birdseed.
