Salix × pendulina

Salix × pendulina, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has slender, elongated leaves that are typically light green in color and have a pointed tip. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

This species is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
