Senecio hallii

Senecio hallii, commonly known as the Hall's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

Senecio hallii is often found in rocky soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
