Syzygium ugoense

Syzygium ugoense, commonly known as the Ugoense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
