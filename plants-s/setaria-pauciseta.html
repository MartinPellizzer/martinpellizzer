Setaria pauciseta

Setaria pauciseta, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, tan-colored seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Setaria pauciseta is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
