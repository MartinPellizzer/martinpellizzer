Solanum delagoense var. brachyastrotrichum

Solanum delagoense var. brachyastrotrichum, commonly known as the small-stalked nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, berry-like, and typically turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
