Stipa × fallax

Stipa × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid bluegrass, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Stipa spica and Stipa viridula.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted grass-like appearance with narrow, blue-green blades that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in open, sunny environments such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Although it does not produce viable seeds, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in the study of grassland ecology and horticulture.
