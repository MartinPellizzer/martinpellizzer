Salix wangii

Salix wangii, commonly known as the Wang's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Salix wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
