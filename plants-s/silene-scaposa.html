Silene scaposa

Silene scaposa, commonly known as the scapose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Silene scaposa has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
