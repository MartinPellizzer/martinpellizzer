Sasa pseudocernua var. psilonodosa

Sasa pseudocernua var. psilonodosa, commonly known as the narrow-noded bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms with a smooth, often glaucous surface. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib and a leathery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural landscapes.
