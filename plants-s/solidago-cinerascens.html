Solidago cinerascens

Solidago cinerascens, commonly known as the gray goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to grayish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Solidago cinerascens plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
