Sarcochilus englerianus

Sarcochilus englerianus, commonly known as the Engler's sarcochilus, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow or green throat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
