Silene uralensis subsp. attenuata

Silene uralensis subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
