Seriphidium ghoratense

Seriphidium ghoratense, commonly known as the Afghan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and attract various pollinators.

Seriphidium ghoratense is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
