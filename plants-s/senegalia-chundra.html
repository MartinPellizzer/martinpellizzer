Senegalia chundra

Senegalia chundra, commonly known as the Indian broom.

It is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of India and Pakistan. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
