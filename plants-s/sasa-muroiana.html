Sasa muroiana

Sasa muroiana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight waxy coating.

Sasa muroiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Japanese garden design.
