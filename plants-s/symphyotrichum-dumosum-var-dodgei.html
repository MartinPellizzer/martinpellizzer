Symphyotrichum dumosum var. dodgei

Symphyotrichum dumosum var. dodgei, commonly known as the dwarf New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy, purple to pinkish-purple composite flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
