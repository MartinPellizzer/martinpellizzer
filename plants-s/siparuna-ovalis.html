Siparuna ovalis

Siparuna ovalis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Siparuna ovalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
