Salvia malacitana

Salvia malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice among gardeners.

Salvia malacitana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
