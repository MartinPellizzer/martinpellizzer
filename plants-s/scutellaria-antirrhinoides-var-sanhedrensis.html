Scutellaria antirrhinoides var. sanhedrensis

Scutellaria antirrhinoides var. sanhedrensis, commonly known as the Jerusalem sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the mint family.
