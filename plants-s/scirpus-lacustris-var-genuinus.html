Scirpus lacustris var. genuinus

Scirpus lacustris var. genuinus, commonly known as the northern bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along lake shores.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
