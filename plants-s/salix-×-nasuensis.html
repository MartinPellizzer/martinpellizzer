Salix × nasuensis

Salix × nasuensis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and turn yellow in the fall.

Salix × nasuensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along water features due to its aesthetic appeal.
