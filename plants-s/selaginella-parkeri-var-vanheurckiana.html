Selaginella parkeri var. vanheurckiana

Selaginella parkeri var. vanheurckiana, commonly known as the Vanheurck's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage. This variety is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its structure includes a short, upright stem with clustered leaves and sporangia that produce spores.

The plant is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
