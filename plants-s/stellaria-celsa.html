Stellaria celsa

Stellaria celsa, commonly known as the starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as carnations and sweet William. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often arranged in star-like patterns, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Stellaria celsa is often found in moist, grassy areas and is known for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
