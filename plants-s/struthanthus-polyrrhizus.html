Struthanthus polyrrhizus

Struthanthus polyrrhizus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, hairy leaves and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Struthanthus polyrrhizus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
