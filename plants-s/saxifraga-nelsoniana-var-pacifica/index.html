Saxifraga Nelsoniana Var. Pacifica

Saxifraga nelsoniana var. pacifica is a rare and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Oregon and northern California. It thrives in rocky, wind-swept habitats where few other species can survive. This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its scientific name honors botanist Robert Nelson, while the varietal name reflects its geographic distribution along the Pacific coast. Due to its limited range and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
