Selaginella homomorpha

Selaginella homomorpha, commonly known as the "spike moss," is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores, which are essential for its reproduction.

Selaginella homomorpha is also notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience.
