Senecio pseudotamoides

Senecio pseudotamoides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
