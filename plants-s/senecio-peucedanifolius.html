Senecio peucedanifolius

Senecio peucedanifolius, commonly known as the blueweed or the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
