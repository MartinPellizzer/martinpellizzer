Sisymbrium sophia f. minus

Sisymbrium sophia f. minus, commonly known as the lesser sweetcaraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
