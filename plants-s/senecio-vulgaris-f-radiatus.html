Senecio vulgaris f. radiatus

Senecio vulgaris f. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that are arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
