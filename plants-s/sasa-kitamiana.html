Sasa kitamiana

Sasa kitamiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grassy texture.

Sasa kitamiana thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.
