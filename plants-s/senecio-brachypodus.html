Senecio brachypodus

Senecio brachypodus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
