Saussurea odorata

Saussurea odorata, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the greater corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been traditionally used in various medicinal applications. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a complex system of rhizomes.

Saussurea odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
