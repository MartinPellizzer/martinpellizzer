Solanum nigrum var. grossedentatum

Solanum nigrum var. grossedentatum, commonly known as the giant black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces dark purple to black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and stems that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
