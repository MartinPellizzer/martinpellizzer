Solanum validinervium

Solanum validinervium, commonly known as the red bignay, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, red berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies in its native habitat.
