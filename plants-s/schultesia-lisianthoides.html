Schultesia lisianthoides

Schultesia lisianthoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal or disturbed areas.

Schultesia lisianthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
