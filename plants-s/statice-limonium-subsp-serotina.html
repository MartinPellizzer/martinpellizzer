Statice limonium subsp. serotina

Statice limonium subsp.

serotina, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is known for its resilience to harsh coastal conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
