Scilla hispidula

Scilla hispidula, commonly known as the hairy scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Scilla hispidula is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
