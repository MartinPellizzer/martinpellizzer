Solanum indicum var. pseudogeminifolium

Solanum indicum var. pseudogeminifolium, commonly known as the Indian nightshade variant, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
