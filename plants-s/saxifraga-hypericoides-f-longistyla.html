Saxifraga hypericoides f. longistyla

Saxifraga hypericoides f. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, often with a slightly serrated margin, and are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated stamens and pistil, which contribute to its unique floral morphology.
