Solanum humboldtii var. diacanthum

Solanum humboldtii var. diacanthum, commonly known as the Humboldt's nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Peru and Ecuador.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and oval, green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
