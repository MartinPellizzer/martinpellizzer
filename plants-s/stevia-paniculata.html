Stevia paniculata

Stevia paniculata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are highly aromatic and intensely sweet. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Stevia paniculata is widely cultivated for its natural sweetening properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
