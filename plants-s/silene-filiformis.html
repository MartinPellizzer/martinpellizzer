Silene filiformis

Silene filiformis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the stems are often hairless and slightly hairy.

Silene filiformis is often found in open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
