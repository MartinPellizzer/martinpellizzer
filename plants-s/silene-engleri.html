Silene engleri

Silene engleri, commonly known as the Engler's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
