Senecio juniperinus var. epitrachys

Senecio juniperinus var. epitrachys, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, shrub-like growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to withstand coastal conditions make it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
