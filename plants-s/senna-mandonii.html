Senna mandonii

Senna mandonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and bipinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senna mandonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
