Senegalia paraensis

Senegalia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom during the dry season, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is highly adapted to drought conditions, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Senegalia paraensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in traditional medicine.
