Salmonopuntia salmiana f. rosea

Salmonopuntia salmiana f. rosea, commonly known as the Pink Ghost Cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored stems, which are often adorned with a waxy, powdery coating. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Its stems are segmented and typically grow in a sprawling or upright manner, depending on environmental conditions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
