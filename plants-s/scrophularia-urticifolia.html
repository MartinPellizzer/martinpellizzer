Scrophularia urticifolia

Scrophularia urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettleleaf scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and can cause skin irritation, similar to stinging nettle.

It produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.
