Sesleria variabilis

Sesleria variabilis, commonly known as the variable sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sesleria variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
