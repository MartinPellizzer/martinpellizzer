Scabiosa incana

Scabiosa incana, commonly known as the hoary scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa incana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
