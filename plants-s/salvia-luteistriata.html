Salvia luteistriata

Salvia luteistriata, commonly known as the golden stripe sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features vibrant yellow and green striped leaves, and its flowers are typically lavender to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia luteistriata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
