Sucrea

Sucrea, commonly known as the sugar plant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Saccharum and is closely related to sugarcane. The plant produces long, grass-like leaves and has a robust root system that stores a high concentration of sucrose. Its stalks are harvested for their sugar content, which is extracted through various processing methods.

Sucrea plays a significant role in global agriculture and the production of refined sugar.
