Saxifraga granulata var. hochstetteri

Saxifraga granulata var. hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, granular texture, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
