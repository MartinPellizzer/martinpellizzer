Senecio spartioides var. granularis

Senecio spartioides var. granularis, commonly known as the granular senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
