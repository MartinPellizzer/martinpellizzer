Sedum rhodocarpum subsp. edwardsii

Sedum rhodocarpum subsp.

edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
