Sarcolobus globosus subsp. globosus

Sarcolobus globosus subsp.

globosus, commonly known as the globular sarcolobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence that consists of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
