Schoenus subbarbatus

Schoenus subbarbatus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lakeshores, and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
