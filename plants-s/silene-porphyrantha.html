Silene porphyrantha

Silene porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Silene porphyrantha is often found in areas with well-drained soil and moderate sunlight.
