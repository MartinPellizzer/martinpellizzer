Scorzonera mollis var. mucida

Scorzonera mollis var. mucida, commonly known as the sticky scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive sticky texture. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. Its root system is thick and fleshy, which contributes to its classification as a member of the sunflower family.

This species is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
