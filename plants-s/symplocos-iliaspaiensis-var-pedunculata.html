Symplocos iliaspaiensis var. pedunculata

Symplocos iliaspaiensis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate symplocos, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
