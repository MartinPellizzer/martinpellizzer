Solidago cuprea

Solidago cuprea, commonly known as copperleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Copperleaf goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
