Silene antirrhina var. confinis

Silene antirrhina var. confinis, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in high-elevation environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is noted for its ecological importance in alpine and subalpine habitats.
