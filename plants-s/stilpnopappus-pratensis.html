Stilpnopappus pratensis

Stilpnopappus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, producing seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
