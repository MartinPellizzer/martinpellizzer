Stanhopea tigrina var. grandiflora

Stanhopea tigrina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex floral structure with multiple whorls of petals and sepals.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
