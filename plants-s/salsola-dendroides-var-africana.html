Salsola dendroides var. africana

Salsola dendroides var. africana, commonly known as African saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its fleshy fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by animals and the wind.

This species is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
