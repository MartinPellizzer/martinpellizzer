Sarcochilus virescens

Sarcochilus virescens, commonly known as the emerald orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, dark green appearance. The plant produces striking greenish-yellow flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Sarcochilus virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
