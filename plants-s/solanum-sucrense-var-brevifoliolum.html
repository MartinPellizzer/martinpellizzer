Solanum sucrense var. brevifoliolum

Solanum sucrense var. brevifoliolum, commonly known as the short-leaved nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
