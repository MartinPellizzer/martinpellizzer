Sideritis libanotica subsp. libanotica

Sideritis libanotica subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as Lebanon germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple.

The plant has aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Sideritis libanotica subsp.

libanotica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
