Senecio dryadeus

Senecio dryadeus, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in forested areas, often along streams or in moist soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a single head. Senecio dryadeus is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
