Spiesia trichocalycina

Spiesia trichocalycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
