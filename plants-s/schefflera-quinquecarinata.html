Schefflera quinquecarinata

Schefflera quinquecarinata, commonly known as the five-ribbed umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are divided into five lobes, each with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
