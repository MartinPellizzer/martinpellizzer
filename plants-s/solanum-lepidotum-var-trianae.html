Solanum lepidotum var. trianae

Solanum lepidotum var. trianae, commonly known as the Trianas potato, is a tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped tubers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bell-shaped with yellow stamens.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
