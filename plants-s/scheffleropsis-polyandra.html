Scheffleropsis polyandra

Scheffleropsis polyandra, commonly known as the laceleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves that are finely divided and resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Scheffleropsis polyandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
