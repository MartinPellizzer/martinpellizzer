Solanum fendleri var. arizonicum

Solanum fendleri var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
