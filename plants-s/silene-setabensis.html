Silene setabensis

Silene setabensis, commonly known as the Setabens lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Silene setabensis is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
