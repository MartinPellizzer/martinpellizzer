Senefeldera multiflora var. acutifolia

Senefeldera multiflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senefeldera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its varietal name. Its small white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
