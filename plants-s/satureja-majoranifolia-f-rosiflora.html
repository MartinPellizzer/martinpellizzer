Satureja majoranifolia f. rosiflora

Satureja majoranifolia f. rosiflora, commonly known as sweet savory or rosemary savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, pungent fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a culinary herb.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional cooking and herbal medicine.
