Salsola lana

Salsola lana, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is an annual or biennial plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in late summer.

Salsola lana is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
