Sigesbeckia nudicaulis

Sigesbeckia nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender sigesbeckia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
