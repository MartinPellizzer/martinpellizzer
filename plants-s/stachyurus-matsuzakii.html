Stachyurus matsuzakii

Stachyurus matsuzakii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping elder, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, maple-like leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Stachyurus matsuzakii is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant, weeping form and seasonal interest.
