Solanum guaraniticum var. angustisectum

Solanum guaraniticum var. angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. It produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
