Stevia subpubescens

Stevia subpubescens, commonly known as the lesser stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stevia subpubescens is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides.

It is often used as a natural sweetener and has potential medicinal properties.
