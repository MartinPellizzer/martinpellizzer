Salix hylonoma f. liocarpa

Salix hylonoma f. liocarpa, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a variant of the willow genus native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage and slender, weeping branches. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its catkins are a notable feature in its reproductive cycle.

This form is often studied for its ecological role and potential horticultural applications.
