Syringa pubescens subsp. patula

Syringa pubescens subsp.

patula, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental in gardens.

Its unique, cascading form adds visual interest and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
