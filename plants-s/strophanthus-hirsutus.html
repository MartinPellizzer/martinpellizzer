Strophanthus hirsutus

Strophanthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy strophanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

The hairy strophanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
