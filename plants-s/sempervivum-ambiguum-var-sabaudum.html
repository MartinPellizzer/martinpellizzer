Sempervivum ambiguum var. sabaudum

Sempervivum ambiguum var. sabaudum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, pink or white flowers in the summer months.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its hardiness and decorative appearance.
