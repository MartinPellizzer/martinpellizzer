Saussurea cordifolia

Saussurea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
