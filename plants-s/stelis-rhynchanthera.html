Stelis rhynchanthera

Stelis rhynchanthera, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Stelis rhynchanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
