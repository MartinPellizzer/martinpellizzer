Salvia oblongifolia

Salvia oblongifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly gardens.
