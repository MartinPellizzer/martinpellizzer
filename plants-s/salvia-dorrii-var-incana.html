Salvia dorrii var. incana

Salvia dorrii var. incana, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of white or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant due to its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
