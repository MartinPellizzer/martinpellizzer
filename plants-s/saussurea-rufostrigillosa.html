Saussurea rufostrigillosa

Saussurea rufostrigillosa, commonly known as the red-haired saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces a fibrous root system and has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered with a fine, reddish pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
