Serapias uxentina

Serapias uxentina, commonly known as the Mexican sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Serapias uxentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
