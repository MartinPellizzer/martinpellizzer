Schefflera combouiensis

Schefflera combouiensis, commonly known as the Combouien Schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Schefflera combouiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in indoor environments.
