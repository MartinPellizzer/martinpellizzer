Sporobolus collettii

Sporobolus collettii, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Sporobolus collettii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
