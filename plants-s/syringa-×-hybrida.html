Syringa × hybrida

Syringa × hybrida, commonly known as the weeping cherry or Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. Syringa × hybrida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy spring blooms and attractive foliage.
