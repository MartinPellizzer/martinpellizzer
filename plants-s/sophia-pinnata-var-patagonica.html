Sophia pinnata var. patagonica

Sophia pinnata var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian thornbush, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, elongated fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
