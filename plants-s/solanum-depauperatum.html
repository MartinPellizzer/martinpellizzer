Solanum depauperatum

Solanum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub-like species with small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum depauperatum is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
