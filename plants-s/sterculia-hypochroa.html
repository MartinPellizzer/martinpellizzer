Sterculia hypochroa

Sterculia hypochroa, commonly known as the yellow sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

The plant has traditional uses in indigenous cultures for its timber and medicinal properties.
