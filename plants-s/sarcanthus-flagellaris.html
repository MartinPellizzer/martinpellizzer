Sarcanthus flagellaris

Sarcanthus flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its name refers to the flagellum-like structures on its pollen grains, which are unique to the genus.

Sarcanthus flagellaris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
