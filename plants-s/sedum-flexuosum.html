Sedum flexuosum

Sedum flexuosum, commonly known as the weeping sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has fleshy, upright leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it thrives in well-draining soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.

Sedum flexuosum is drought-tolerant and can survive in a variety of environmental conditions.
