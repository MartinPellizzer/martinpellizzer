Syzygium multipuncticulatum

Syzygium multipuncticulatum, commonly known as the spotted apple or wild apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, round, and often spotted fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are white or pink, clustered in small groups, and it produces edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
