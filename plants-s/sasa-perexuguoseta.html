Sasa perexuguoseta

Sasa perexuguoseta, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa perexuguoseta is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
