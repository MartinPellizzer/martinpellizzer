Salvia velutina

Salvia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Its compact growth habit makes it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
