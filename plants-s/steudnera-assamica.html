Steudnera assamica

Steudnera assamica, commonly known as the Assam arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its rhizomes are harvested for culinary purposes, particularly in the preparation of arrowroot flour.

Steudnera assamica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.
