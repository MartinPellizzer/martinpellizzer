Stereosandra javanica var. papuana

Stereosandra javanica var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to parts of Indonesia and Papua, where it typically grows in lowland to montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, heartwood that is highly valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a timber. It has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, reddish pulp.
