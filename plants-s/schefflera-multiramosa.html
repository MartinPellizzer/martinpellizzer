Schefflera multiramosa

Schefflera multiramosa, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, multi-branched structure and large, leathery, dark green leaves that are glossy on both sides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Schefflera multiramosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
