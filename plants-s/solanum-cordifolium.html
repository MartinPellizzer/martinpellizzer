Solanum cordifolium

Solanum cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep blue or black color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
