Silene argentinensis

Silene argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine campion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, erect stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate and linear, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Silene argentinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
