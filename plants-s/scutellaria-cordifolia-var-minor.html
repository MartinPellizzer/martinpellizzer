Scutellaria cordifolia var. minor

Scutellaria cordifolia var. minor, commonly known as the heart-shaped skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped, heart- or kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
