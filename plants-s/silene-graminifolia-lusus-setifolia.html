Silene graminifolia lusus setifolia

Silene graminifolia lusus setifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene graminifolia lusus setifolia is a perennial herb that blooms in early summer.
