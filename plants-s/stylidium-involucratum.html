Stylidium involucratum

Stylidium involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are borne on short pedicels.

Stylidium involucratum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
