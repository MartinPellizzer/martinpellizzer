Salicornia lutescens

Salicornia lutescens, commonly known as yellow glassworts, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal marshes and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branching inflorescences. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is often found in association with other salt-tolerant species.

Salicornia lutescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
