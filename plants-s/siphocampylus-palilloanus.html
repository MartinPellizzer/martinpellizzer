Siphocampylus palilloanus

Siphocampylus palilloanus, commonly known as the Palilloan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disk flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
