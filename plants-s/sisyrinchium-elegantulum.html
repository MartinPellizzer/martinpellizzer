Sisyrinchium elegantulum

Sisyrinchium elegantulum, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Sisyrinchium elegantulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
