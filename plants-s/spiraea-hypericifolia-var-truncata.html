Spiraea hypericifolia var. truncata

Spiraea hypericifolia var. truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are ovate with a distinctive truncate (cut-off) base, giving the plant its specific epithet. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.
