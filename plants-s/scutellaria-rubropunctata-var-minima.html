Scutellaria rubropunctata var. minima

Scutellaria rubropunctata var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf scarlet pimpernel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
