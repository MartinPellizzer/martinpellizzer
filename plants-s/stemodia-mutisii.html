Stemodia mutisii

Stemodia mutisii, commonly known as the white-flowered stemodia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the dry season. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
