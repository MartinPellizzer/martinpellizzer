Sutera intertexta

Sutera intertexta, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is a perennial shrub with hairy stems and ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Sutera intertexta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
