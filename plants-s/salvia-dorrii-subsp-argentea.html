Salvia dorrii subsp. argentea

Salvia dorrii subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
