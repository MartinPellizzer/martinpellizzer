Shorea affinis

Shorea affinis, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and develops hard, heavy seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in construction and furniture.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
