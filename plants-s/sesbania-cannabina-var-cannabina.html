Sesbania cannabina var. cannabina

Sesbania cannabina var. cannabina, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth, it is both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed.
