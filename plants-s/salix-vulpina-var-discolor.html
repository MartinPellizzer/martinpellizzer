Salix vulpina var. discolor

Salix vulpina var. discolor, commonly known as the white willow or silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found near streams, wetlands, and other water sources.

The bark of this species is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.
