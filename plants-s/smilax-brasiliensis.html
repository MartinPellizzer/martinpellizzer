Smilax brasiliensis

Smilax brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian arrowroot or Brazilian smilax, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Smilax brasiliensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
