Solidago odora var. glabra

Solidago odora var. glabra, commonly known as sweet goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
