Saussurea sovietica

Saussurea sovietica, commonly known as the Soviet saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the composite flower structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
