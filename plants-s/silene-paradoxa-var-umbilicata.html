Silene paradoxa var. umbilicata

Silene paradoxa var. umbilicata, commonly known as the umbilicated catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branched.
