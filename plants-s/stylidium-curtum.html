Stylidium curtum

Stylidium curtum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sun-orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Stylidium curtum is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
