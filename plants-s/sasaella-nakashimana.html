Sasaella nakashimana

Sasaella nakashimana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its flowering inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets, which are often inconspicuous.

Sasaella nakashimana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
