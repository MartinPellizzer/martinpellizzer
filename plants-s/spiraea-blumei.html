Spiraea blumei

Spiraea blumei, commonly known as the Blume's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its arching, slender stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Spiraea blumei is valued for its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
