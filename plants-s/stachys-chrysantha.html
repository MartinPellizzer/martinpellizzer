Stachys chrysantha

Stachys chrysantha, commonly known as yellow hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, hairy calyx surrounding each flower. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
