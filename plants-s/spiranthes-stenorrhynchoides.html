Spiranthes stenorrhynchoides

Spiranthes stenorrhynchoides, commonly known as the narrow-lobed ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling arrangement of small, white to pale pink flowers along a single, slender stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
