Stenocoelium depressum

Stenocoelium depressum, commonly known as the narrow-celled bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water and has submerged leaves with specialized glands. Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and tiny, scale-like leaves.

It is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family, which includes other carnivorous plants.
