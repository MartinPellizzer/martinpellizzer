Sasa tsukubensis var. ashikagaensis

Sasa tsukubensis var. ashikagaensis, commonly known as the Ashikaga bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a subtle bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecological systems.
