Solanum mocinianum

Solanum mocinianum, commonly known as the Mocinian nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the plant has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Solanum mocinianum is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks.
