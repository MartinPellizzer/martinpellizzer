Scleria ovinux

Scleria ovinux, commonly known as the woolly bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, bulbous underground stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, three-angled seeds.

Scleria ovinux is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
