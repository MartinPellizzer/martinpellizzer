Stobaea erysithalis

Stobaea erysithalis, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Stobaea erysithalis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
