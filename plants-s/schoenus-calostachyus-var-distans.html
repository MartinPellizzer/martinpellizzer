Schoenus calostachyus var. distans

Schoenus calostachyus var. distans, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical culms that often form dense tussocks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
