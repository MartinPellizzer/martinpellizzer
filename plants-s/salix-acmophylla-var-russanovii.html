Salix acmophylla var. russanovii

Salix acmophylla var. russanovii, commonly known as the Russian willow, is a variant of the acmophylla willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
