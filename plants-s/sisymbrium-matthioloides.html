Sisymbrium matthioloides

Sisymbrium matthioloides, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis. The plant typically has slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often reduced in size due to its parasitic nature.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.
