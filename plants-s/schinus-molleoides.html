Schinus molleoides

Schinus molleoides, commonly known as the Chilean persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they can be astringent when unripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
