Styrax acuminatus

Styrax acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during early spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Styrax acuminatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
