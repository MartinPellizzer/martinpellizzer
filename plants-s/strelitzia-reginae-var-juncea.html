Strelitzia reginae var. juncea

Strelitzia reginae var. juncea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bird of paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves and its distinctive, brightly colored flower spikes that resemble a bird in flight. The flowers, which are typically orange with a blue-purple hood, are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
