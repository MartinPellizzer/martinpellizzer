Sesleria sphaerocephala var. coerulescens

Sesleria sphaerocephala var. coerulescens, commonly known as the Blue-eyed Sesleria, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue to white flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
