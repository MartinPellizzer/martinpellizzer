Sidalcea oregana subsp. valida

Sidalcea oregana subsp.

valida, commonly known as the Showy Silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and slender stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
