Sasa fortunei

Sasa fortunei, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and sturdy, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its upright, elegant appearance.

Sasa fortunei is also known for its ability to form thickets, which can provide habitat for various wildlife species.
