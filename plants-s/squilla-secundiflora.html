Squilla secundiflora

Squilla secundiflora, commonly known as the desert squill, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant with a rosette of narrow, blue-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Squilla secundiflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
