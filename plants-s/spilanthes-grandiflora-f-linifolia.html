Spilanthes grandiflora f. linifolia

Spilanthes grandiflora f. linifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian mustard, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is known for its pungent aroma and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
