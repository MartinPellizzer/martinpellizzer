Spicanta volubilis

Spicanta volubilis, commonly known as the creeping thornvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often trailing along the ground or up supports. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by berry-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Spicanta volubilis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain ecosystems.
