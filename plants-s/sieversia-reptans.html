Sieversia reptans

Sieversia reptans, commonly known as the creeping sieversia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Sieversia reptans is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
