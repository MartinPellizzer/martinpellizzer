Scutellaria scordiifolia var. hirta

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the tips of the stems.

The genus Scutellaria is known for its distinctive spurred petals and is often used in traditional medicine.
