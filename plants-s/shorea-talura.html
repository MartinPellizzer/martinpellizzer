Shorea talura

Shorea talura, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Shorea talura plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
