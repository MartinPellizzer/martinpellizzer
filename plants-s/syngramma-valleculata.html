Syngramma valleculata

Syngramma valleculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is commonly found in disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Syngramma valleculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
