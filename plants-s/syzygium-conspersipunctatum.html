Syzygium conspersipunctatum

Syzygium conspersipunctatum, commonly known as the speckled tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. The fruit's pulp is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.
