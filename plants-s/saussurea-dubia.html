Saussurea dubia

Saussurea dubia, commonly known as the Himalayan swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, alpine environments and is often found in wet meadows and mountainous tundra. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

Saussurea dubia plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
