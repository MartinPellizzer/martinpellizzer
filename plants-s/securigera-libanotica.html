Securigera libanotica

Securigera libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese senna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and often appear in clusters, while the leaves are bipinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
