Salvia grandiflora subsp. rotundifolia

Salvia grandiflora subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are round or ovate in shape.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
