Sabal jamaicensis

Sabal jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican saw palmetto, is a palm-like plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of the Greater Antilles.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its clustering growth habit and fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its fruit, which is a bright red drupe, contains the seeds and is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Sabal jamaicensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
