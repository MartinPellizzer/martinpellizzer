Syringa × saugeana

Syringa × saugeana, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping viburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance, often with pink or white flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Syringa × saugeana is popular in gardens for its beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
