Syzygium saxatile

Syzygium saxatile, commonly known as the rock apple or mountain apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium saxatile thrives in rocky or mountainous habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for the nutritional value of its fruit.
