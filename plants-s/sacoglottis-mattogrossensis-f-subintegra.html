Sacoglottis mattogrossensis f. subintegra

Sacoglottis mattogrossensis f. subintegra, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon region of Brazil.

It features a rhizomatous stem system that allows for vegetative propagation and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential as a sustainable food source and its role in traditional medicine.
