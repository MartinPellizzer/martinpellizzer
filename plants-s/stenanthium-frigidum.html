Stenanthium frigidum

Stenanthium frigidum, commonly known as the cold mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves.

Stenanthium frigidum is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh alpine conditions.
