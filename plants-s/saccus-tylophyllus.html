Saccus tylophyllus

Saccus tylophyllus, commonly known as the woolly leaf bladder, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structures.

These specialized organs, known as pseudobulbs, serve as water storage and photosynthetic organs, adapting the plant to arid environments. The leaves are thick and fleshy, with a woolly texture that aids in reducing water loss. It is native to certain regions of South America, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
