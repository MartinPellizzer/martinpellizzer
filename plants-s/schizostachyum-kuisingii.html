Schizostachyum kuisingii

Schizostachyum kuisingii, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. Schizostachyum kuisingii is highly valued for its use in construction, handicrafts, and as a source of sustainable bamboo products.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
