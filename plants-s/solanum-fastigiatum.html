Solanum fastigiatum

Solanum fastigiatum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale purple.

The fruit is edible when ripe and is often used in local cuisines for its unique flavor and texture.
