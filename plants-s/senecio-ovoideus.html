Senecio ovoideus

Senecio ovoideus, commonly known as the oval-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features succulent, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

Senecio ovoideus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
