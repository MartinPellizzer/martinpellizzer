Stachys serrata

Stachys serrata, commonly known as the serrated hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Stachys serrata is known for its hairy stems and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
