Solanum heliotropiifolium

Solanum heliotropiifolium, commonly known as the sunflower leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are small, berry-like capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
