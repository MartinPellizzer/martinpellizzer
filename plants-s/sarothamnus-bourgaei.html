Sarothamnus bourgaei

Sarothamnus bourgaei, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant foliage.
