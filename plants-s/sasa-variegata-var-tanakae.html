Sasa variegata var. tanakae

Sasa variegata var. tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or variegated sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative element in gardens.
