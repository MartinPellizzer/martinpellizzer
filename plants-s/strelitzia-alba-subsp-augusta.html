Strelitzia alba subsp. augusta

Strelitzia alba subsp.

augusta, commonly known as the white bird of paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, white, banana-like leaves and its showy, orange-and-white inflorescence that resembles a bird in flight.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering during the growing season. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds. Strelitzia alba subsp.

augusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
