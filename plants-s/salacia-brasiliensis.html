Salacia brasiliensis

Salacia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian salacia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains tiny seeds, and it is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Salacia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
