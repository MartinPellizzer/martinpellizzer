Salvia blancoana var. mariolensis

Salvia blancoana var. mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolense sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
