Syzygium brachiatum

Syzygium brachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
