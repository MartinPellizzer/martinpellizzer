Spiranthes cranichoides

Spiranthes cranichoides, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its habitat.
