Salvia dorrii subsp. dorrii

Salvia dorrii subsp.

dorrii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill sage, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
