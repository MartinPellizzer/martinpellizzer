Solidago spathulata var. racemosa

Solidago spathulata var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the inflorescence consists of numerous small, radiant yellow blooms. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
