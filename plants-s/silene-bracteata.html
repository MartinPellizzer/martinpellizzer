Silene bracteata

Silene bracteata, commonly known as the bracted campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Silene bracteata produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
