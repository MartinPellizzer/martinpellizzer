Scirpus thouarsianus var. bicolor

Scirpus thouarsianus var. bicolor, commonly known as the rainbow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescence that displays two different flower colors, typically white and pink, on the same spike. The plant grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a reddish or bronze tint, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
