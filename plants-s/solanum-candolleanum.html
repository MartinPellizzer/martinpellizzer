Solanum candolleanum

Solanum candolleanum, commonly known as the Himalayan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful if ingested.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
