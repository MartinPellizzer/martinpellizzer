Satureja obovata var. gracilis

Satureja obovata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are lanceolate to obovate in shape, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage make it suitable for rock gardens and alpine plantings.

It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
