Sechium

Sechium, commonly known as the chayote, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It produces a fruit that is typically pear-shaped with a white, edible flesh and a tough, spiny rind. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sechium is valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins C and B-complex, as well as its versatility in culinary uses.
