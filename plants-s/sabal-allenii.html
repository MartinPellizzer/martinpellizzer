Sabal allenii

Sabal allenii, commonly known as the Allen's sable, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves with a waxy, blue-green appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits.

Sabal allenii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
