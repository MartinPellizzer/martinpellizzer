Sedum sartorianum subsp. ponticum

Sedum sartorianum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid environments.
