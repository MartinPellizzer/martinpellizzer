Senecillis tangutica

Senecillis tangutica, commonly known as the Tangutica Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
