Salvia argentea var. patula

Salvia argentea var. patula, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
