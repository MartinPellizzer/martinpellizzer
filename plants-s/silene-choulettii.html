Silene choulettii

Silene choulettii, commonly known as the Choulet's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Silene choulettii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
