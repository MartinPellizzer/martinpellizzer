Stipa dasyantha

Stipa dasyantha, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of fine, silver-haired inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stipa dasyantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
