Solanum quitoense var. septentrionale

Solanum quitoense var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern Ecuadorian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when mature.

The plant is characterized by its adaptability to high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, shaded forest areas.
