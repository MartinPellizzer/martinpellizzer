Statice aegyptiaca

Statice aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early autumn, producing pink, red, or purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Statice aegyptiaca is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
