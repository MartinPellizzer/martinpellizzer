Staurogyne filisepala

Staurogyne filisepala, commonly known as the thread-leaved waterplantain, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, often emerging from the water surface. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the substrate in wetlands and marshes.

Staurogyne filisepala is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, contributing to aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
