Syagrus arenicola

Syagrus arenicola, commonly known as the arenicola palm, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a small, slow-growing palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various bird species.

Syagrus arenicola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
