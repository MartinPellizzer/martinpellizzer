Senecio dolichocephalus

Senecio dolichocephalus, commonly known as the long-headed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio dolichocephalus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
