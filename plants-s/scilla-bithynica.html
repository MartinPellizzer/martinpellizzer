Scilla bithynica

Scilla bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically between March and May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla bithynica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
