Saccolabium cerinum

Saccolabium cerinum, commonly known as the yellow burr bush, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Saccolabium cerinum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of local ecosystems.
