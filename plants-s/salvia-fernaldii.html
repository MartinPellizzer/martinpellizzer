Salvia fernaldii

Salvia fernaldii, commonly known as Fernald's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
