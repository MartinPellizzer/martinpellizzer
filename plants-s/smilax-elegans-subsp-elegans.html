Smilax elegans subsp. elegans

Smilax elegans subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, arrow-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries are dark red to black and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is known for its vigorous growth and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
