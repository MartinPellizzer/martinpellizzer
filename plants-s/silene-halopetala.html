Silene halopetala

Silene halopetala, commonly known as the Tibetan sand saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Silene halopetala plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
