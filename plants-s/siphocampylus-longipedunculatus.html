Siphocampylus longipedunculatus

Siphocampylus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long peduncle bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
