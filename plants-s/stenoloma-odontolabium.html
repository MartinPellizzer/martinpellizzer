Stenoloma odontolabium

Stenoloma odontolabium, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a distinctive lip with a tooth-like projection, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are short and often bear multiple flower spikes.

Stenoloma odontolabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
