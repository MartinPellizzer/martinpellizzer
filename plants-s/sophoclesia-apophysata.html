Sophoclesia apophysata

Sophoclesia apophysata, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid red petals and a distinctive, elongated spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Sophoclesia apophysata make it a visually appealing and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
