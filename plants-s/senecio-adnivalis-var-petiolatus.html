Senecio adnivalis var. petiolatus

Senecio adnivalis var. petiolatus, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in mountainous areas of Argentina and Chile.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in extreme environments.
