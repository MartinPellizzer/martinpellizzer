Stipa joannis f. krausiana

Stipa joannis f. krausiana, commonly known as the silvergrass or silverstem grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce light green to golden seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
