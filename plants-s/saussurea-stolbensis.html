Saussurea stolbensis

Saussurea stolbensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, yellowish inflorescences that resemble saffron threads, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in arid, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
