Stelis flacca

Stelis flacca, commonly known as the slender stelis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, forming a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it prefers shaded, humid environments.

Stelis flacca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
