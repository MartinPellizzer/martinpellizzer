Sorbus pubens

Sorbus pubens, commonly known as the Downy Whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions of western North America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red or black color, providing food for various wildlife species.
