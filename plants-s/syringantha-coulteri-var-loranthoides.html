Syringantha coulteri var. loranthoides

Syringantha coulteri var. loranthoides, commonly known as the Coulter's syringa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
