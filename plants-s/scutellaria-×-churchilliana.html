Scutellaria × churchilliana

Scutellaria × churchilliana, commonly known as Churchill's blueweed, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
