Senecio tundricola

Senecio tundricola, commonly known as the Arctic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine meadows.

Senecio tundricola plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is an indicator species of high-altitude ecosystems.
