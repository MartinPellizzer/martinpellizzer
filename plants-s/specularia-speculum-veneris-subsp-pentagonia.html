Specularia speculum-veneris subsp. pentagonia

Specularia speculum-veneris subsp.

pentagonia, commonly known as the Venus' looking-glass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its striking, pentagonal-shaped leaves that resemble mirrors, hence its common name.

The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
