Scutellaria ovata subsp. ovata

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the ovate skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces violet to blue flowers in whorls along the upper stem, which bloom from June to September. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
