Sarcanthus roseus

Sarcanthus roseus, commonly known as the roseum sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Sarcanthus roseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
