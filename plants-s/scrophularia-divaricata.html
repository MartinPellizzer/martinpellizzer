Scrophularia divaricata

Scrophularia divaricata, commonly known as the spreading scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five lobes and a distinctive spur. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scrophularia divaricata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
