Spartium fragrans

Spartium fragrans, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, fluffy flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Spartium fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
