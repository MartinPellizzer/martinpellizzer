Sophronitis × fidelensis

Sophronitis × fidelensis, commonly known as the Fidelensis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Sophronitis censu and Sophronitis phalaenopsis.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, pendulous flowers in shades of white and pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
