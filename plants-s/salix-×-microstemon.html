Salix × microstemon

Salix × microstemon, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
