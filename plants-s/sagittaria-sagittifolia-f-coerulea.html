Sagittaria sagittifolia f. coerulea

Sagittaria sagittifolia f. coerulea, commonly known as blue arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant blue-green color.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and marshes, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to wetland environments. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This cultivar is prized in water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
