Scrophularia subcrispa

Scrophularia subcrispa, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Scrophularia subcrispa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
