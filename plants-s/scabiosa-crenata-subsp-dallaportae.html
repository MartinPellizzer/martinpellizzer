Scabiosa crenata subsp. dallaportae

Scabiosa crenata subsp.

dallaportae, commonly known as the Dallaporta scabiosa, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, with a distinctive crenate (notched) margin. This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
