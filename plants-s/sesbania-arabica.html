Sesbania arabica

Sesbania arabica, commonly known as the sicklepod or Egyptian sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Sesbania arabica has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.
