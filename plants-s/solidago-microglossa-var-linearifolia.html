Solidago microglossa var. linearifolia

Solidago microglossa var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late summer to early fall. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its slender foliage and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
