Solidago arguta var. boottii

Solidago arguta var. boottii, commonly known as the Boott's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, downy hairs.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
