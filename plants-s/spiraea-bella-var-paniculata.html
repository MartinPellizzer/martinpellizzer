Spiraea bella var. paniculata

Spiraea bella var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and showy floral display.
