Saxifraga zhidoensis

Saxifraga zhidoensis, commonly known as the Zhidong saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure, and are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Saxifraga zhidoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
