Syzygium caudatilimbum

Syzygium caudatilimbum, commonly known as the long-tailed apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. Syzygium caudatilimbum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a valuable species in both horticulture and local cuisine.
