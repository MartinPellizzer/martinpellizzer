Salvia chamaedryoides

Salvia chamaedryoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
