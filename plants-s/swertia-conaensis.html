Swertia conaensis

Swertia conaensis, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Swertia conaensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
