Senecio × helwingii

Senecio × helwingii, commonly known as the hybrid Senecio, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio sanguineus and Senecio hirtellus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa, characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Senecio × helwingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for under suitable growing conditions.
