Silene tatarinowii var. albiflora

Silene tatarinowii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
