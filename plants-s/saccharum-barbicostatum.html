Saccharum barbicostatum

Saccharum barbicostatum, commonly known as the barbed costate cane.

It is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stalks and serrated leaf margins. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a distinctive barbed texture along the edges, which gives it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its potential use in biofuel production and as a source of natural fibers.
