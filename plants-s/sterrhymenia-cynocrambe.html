Sterrhymenia cynocrambe

Sterrhymenia cynocrambe, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
