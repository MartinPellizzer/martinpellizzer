Solidago laxiflora

Solidago laxiflora, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Solidago laxiflora plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
