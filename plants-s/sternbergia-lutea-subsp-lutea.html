Sternbergia lutea subsp. lutea

Sternbergia lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as irises and gladiolus.

The subspecies lutea is distinguished by its vivid yellow blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
