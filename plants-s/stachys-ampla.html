Stachys ampla

Stachys ampla, commonly known as broadleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported calming and digestive properties.
