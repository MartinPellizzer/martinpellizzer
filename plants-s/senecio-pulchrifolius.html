Senecio pulchrifolius

Senecio pulchrifolius, commonly known as the beautiful-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
