Stanleya albescens

Stanleya albescens, commonly known as the white helenium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale yellow.

Stanleya albescens is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
