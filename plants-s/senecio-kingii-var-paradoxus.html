Senecio kingii var. paradoxus

Senecio kingii var. paradoxus, commonly known as the "Paradox Senecio," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers in summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
