Sieversia campanulata

Sieversia campanulata, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
