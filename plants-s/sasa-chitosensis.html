Sasa chitosensis

Sasa chitosensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa chitosensis is known for its ability to form dense clumps and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in various soil types.
