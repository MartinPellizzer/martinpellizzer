Salix acutifolia f. pendula

Salix acutifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a striking ornamental shrub or small tree characterized by its cascading branches that hang like a curtain.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a fresh green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
