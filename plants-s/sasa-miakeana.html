Sasa miakeana

Sasa miakeana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Sasa miakeana is also valued for its role in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
