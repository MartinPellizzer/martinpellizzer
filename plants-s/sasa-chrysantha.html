Sasa chrysantha

Sasa chrysantha, commonly known as golden sasa, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming plant with slender, erect culms that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bright green color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
