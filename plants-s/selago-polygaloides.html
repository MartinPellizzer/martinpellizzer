Selago polygaloides

Selago polygaloides, commonly known as the coastal greenbrier, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Selago polygaloides plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various small insects and birds.
