Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp. rosmarinifolia

Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp.

rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silver spruce or Mediterranean santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features compact, aromatic foliage that is typically gray-green in color, with silvery white undersides.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its drought tolerance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style landscaping.
