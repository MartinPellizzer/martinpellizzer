Sciodaphyllum brownei

Sciodaphyllum brownei, commonly known as the Brown's shade tree, is a unique evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and robust, woody structure, which allows it to thrive in low-light conditions under the forest canopy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Sciodaphyllum brownei is often found in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.

Its slow growth and ornamental appearance make it a sought-after plant for tropical gardens and conservation efforts.
