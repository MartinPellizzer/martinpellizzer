Solanum merkeri var. tobleri

Solanum merkeri var. tobleri, commonly known as the Tobler's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes, and is characterized by its poisonous alkaloids. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

Its berries, which are usually yellow or orange when mature, are toxic and should not be consumed.
