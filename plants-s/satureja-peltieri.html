Satureja peltieri

Satureja peltieri, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and dry conditions. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
