Strobilanthes denticulata

Strobilanthes denticulata, commonly known as the "Kangkong flower" or "Ceylon flame," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing as a fiery display during the dry season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in open areas with well-drained soil.

Strobilanthes denticulata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in certain regions.
