Silphium asteriscus var. latifolium

Silphium asteriscus var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered an important species for ecological restoration projects.
