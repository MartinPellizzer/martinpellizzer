Solanum lycopersicum var. ribisiodes

Solanum lycopersicum var. ribisiodes, commonly known as the ribisiodes tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a lobed or serrated edge that distinguishes it from standard tomato varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, producing clusters of small, often yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its distinct genetic traits and potential for hybrid breeding.
