Spiranthes nitida

Spiranthes nitida, commonly known as the slender ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
