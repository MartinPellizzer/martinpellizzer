Stachys euphratica

Stachys euphratica, commonly known as the Euphrates hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys euphratica is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native habitat.
