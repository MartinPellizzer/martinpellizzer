Sideritis rosea

Sideritis rosea, commonly known as pink sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

Sideritis rosea is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
