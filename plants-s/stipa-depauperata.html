Stipa depauperata

Stipa depauperata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color.

Stipa depauperata is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.
