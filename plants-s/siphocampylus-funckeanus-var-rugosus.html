Siphocampylus funckeanus var. rugosus

Siphocampylus funckeanus var. rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf siphocampylus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
