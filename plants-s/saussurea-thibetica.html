Saussurea thibetica

Saussurea thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that is highly valued in traditional medicine. Its habitat is typically in alpine meadows and rocky areas at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal uses, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
