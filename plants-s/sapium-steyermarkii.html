Sapium steyermarkii

Sapium steyermarkii, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain three winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
