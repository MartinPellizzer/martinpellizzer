Scirpus sundanus

Scirpus sundanus, commonly known as the Sundanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies. It has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs.

Its flowering spikes are usually brownish or reddish, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
