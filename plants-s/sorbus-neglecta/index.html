Sorbus Neglecta

Sorbus neglecta is a small, deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern Asia. It is known for its clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are often overlooked but are an important food source for birds. Its compact size and ornamental features make it a popular choice for gardeners. Despite its beauty, Sorbus neglecta remains relatively unknown to the general public.
