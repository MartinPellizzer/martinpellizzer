Sideritis cretica subsp. spicata

Sideritis cretica subsp.

spicata, commonly known as Cretan ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Sideritis cretica subsp.

spicata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
