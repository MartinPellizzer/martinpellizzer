Syzygium triphyllum

Syzygium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved satinwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in wet, acidic soils along stream banks and in swamps. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often three-lobed, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
