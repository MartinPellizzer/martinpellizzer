Spinacia oleracea var. heinemannii

Spinacia oleracea var. heinemannii, commonly known as the Hungarian spinach, is a cultivated variety of spinach characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, tender leaves.

It is native to Central Europe and is prized for its mild flavor and high nutritional value. The plant thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as an annual, reaching maturity within about 40 to 50 days from sowing. Its leaves are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet.

The variety is also notable for its resistance to certain pests and diseases, which makes it a popular choice among organic gardeners.
