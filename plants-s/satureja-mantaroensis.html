Satureja mantaroensis

Satureja mantaroensis, commonly known as the Mantaro mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, aromatic leaves used in traditional Andean medicine.

Satureja mantaroensis is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal properties.
