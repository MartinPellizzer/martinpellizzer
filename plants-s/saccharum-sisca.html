Saccharum sisca

Saccharum sisca, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a tropical grass native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and aromatic leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. The plant is often used in traditional ceremonies and crafts due to its pleasant fragrance and pliable texture. Its growth habit includes tall, slender stalks that can reach up to two meters in height.

Saccharum sisca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
