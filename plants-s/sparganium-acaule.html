Sparganium acaule

Sparganium acaule, commonly known as the dwarf bur reed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often near the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features a short, compact stem with whorled leaves that are narrow and grass-like. Its most distinctive feature is the spiny, cone-shaped inflorescence that appears in late summer.

Sparganium acaule plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
