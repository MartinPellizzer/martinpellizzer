Shorea confusa

Shorea confusa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood. The plant has large, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its fruits are woody capsules containing winged seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
