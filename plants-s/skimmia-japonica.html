Skimmia japonica

Skimmia japonica, commonly known as Japanese skimmia, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features evergreen foliage with dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Skimmia japonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
