Saussurea limprichtii

Saussurea limprichtii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saussurea limprichtii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
