Sipanea ovalifolia

Sipanea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf sipanea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sipanea ovalifolia typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
