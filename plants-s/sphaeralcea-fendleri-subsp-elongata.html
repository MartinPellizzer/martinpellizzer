Sphaeralcea fendleri subsp. elongata

Sphaeralcea fendleri subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
