Scutellaria galericulata

Scutellaria galericulata, commonly known as blue beakviolet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, elongated lower lip that resembles a beak. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Scutellaria galericulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a pollinator plant.
