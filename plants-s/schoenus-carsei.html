Schoenus carsei

Schoenus carsei, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

Schoenus carsei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
