Spondias mangifera

Spondias mangifera, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and large, oval-shaped leaves. The fruit's pericarp is segmented and contains a single seed, making it distinct from other members of the genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
