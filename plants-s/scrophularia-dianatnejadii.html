Scrophularia dianatnejadii

Scrophularia dianatnejadii, commonly known as the Persian figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Scrophularia dianatnejadii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in high-altitude habitats.
