Spiraea schlothauerae

Spiraea schlothauerae, commonly known as the Schlothauer spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white to pink inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its foliage is dark green and becomes reddish in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Spiraea schlothauerae is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
