Strumaria pygmaea

Strumaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a slight purple tint. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

Strumaria pygmaea is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance and ease of care.
