Silene vespertina proles disticha

Silene vespertina proles disticha, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is noted for its short stature and is often found in small colonies, making it a subtle yet distinctive member of the flora.
