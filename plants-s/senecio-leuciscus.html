Senecio leuciscus

Senecio leuciscus, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are often mistaken for single blooms. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open grasslands.

Senecio leuciscus is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
