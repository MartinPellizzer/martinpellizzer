Silene sciotica

Silene sciotica, commonly known as the shadowplant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Silene sciotica is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a unique addition to woodland gardens.
