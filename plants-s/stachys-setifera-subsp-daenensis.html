Stachys setifera subsp. daenensis

Stachys setifera subsp.

daenensis, commonly known as the Afghan betony, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two-lipped structures typical of the mint family.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
