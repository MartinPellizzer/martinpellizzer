Sida bihamata

Sida bihamata, commonly known as the two-horned sida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Sida bihamata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.
