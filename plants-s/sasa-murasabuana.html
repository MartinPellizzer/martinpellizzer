Sasa murasabuana

Sasa murasabuana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Sasa murasabuana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
