Strophanthus kombe

Strophanthus kombe, commonly known as the "Cape leadplant," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Strophanthus kombe is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
