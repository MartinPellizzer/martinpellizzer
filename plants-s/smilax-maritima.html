Smilax maritima

Smilax maritima, commonly known as sea grape or beach spinach, is a perennial vine native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and along the coast.

Smilax maritima is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal plant communities.
