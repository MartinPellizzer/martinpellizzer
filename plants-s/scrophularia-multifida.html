Scrophularia multifida

Scrophularia multifida, commonly known as the forked figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scrophularia multifida is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
