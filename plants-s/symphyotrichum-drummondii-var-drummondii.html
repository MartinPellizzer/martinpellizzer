Symphyotrichum drummondii var. drummondii

Symphyotrichum drummondii var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Drummond's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
