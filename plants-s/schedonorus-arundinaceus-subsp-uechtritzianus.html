Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp. uechtritzianus

Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp.

uechtritzianus, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
