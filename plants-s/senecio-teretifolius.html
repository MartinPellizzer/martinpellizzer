Senecio teretifolius

Senecio teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is generally narrow and linear, with a grayish-green hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
