Swertia engleri

Swertia engleri, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are slender and branching.

Swertia engleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
