Sium luteum

Sium luteum, commonly known as yellow water-pit, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.

Sium luteum is typically found in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important part of wetland ecosystems.
