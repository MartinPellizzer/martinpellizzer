Stipa saposhnikowii

Stipa saposhnikowii, commonly known as the Chinese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Stipa saposhnikowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
