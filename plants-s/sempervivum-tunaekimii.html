Sempervivum tunaekimii

Sempervivum tunaekimii, commonly known as the Canary Islands stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its ability to store water in its thick leaves. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum tunaekimii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
