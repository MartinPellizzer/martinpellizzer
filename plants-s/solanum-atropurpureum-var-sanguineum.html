Solanum atropurpureum var. sanguineum

Solanum atropurpureum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the purple eggplant or blood eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its deep purple to almost black foliage and elongated, egg-shaped fruits that range in color from purple to reddish-brown. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces berries that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
