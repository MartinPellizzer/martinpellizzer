Salix × taoensis

Salix × taoensis, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous branches that create a drooping, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
