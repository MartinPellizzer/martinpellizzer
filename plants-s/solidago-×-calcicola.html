Solidago × calcicola

Solidago × calcicola, commonly known as the limestone goldenrod, is a hybrid plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
