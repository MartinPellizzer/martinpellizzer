Sasaella chikatsuafumiana

Sasaella chikatsuafumiana, commonly known as the Chikatsuafumian sasa, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its culms are hollow and jointed, typical of the grass family, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
