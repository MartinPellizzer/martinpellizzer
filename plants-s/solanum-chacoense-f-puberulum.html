Solanum chacoense f. puberulum

Solanum chacoense f. puberulum, commonly known as the Chaco nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
