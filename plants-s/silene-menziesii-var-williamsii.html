Silene menziesii var. williamsii

Silene menziesii var. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native environment.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to local pollinators.
