Struthanthus haenkei

Struthanthus haenkei, commonly known as the desert star or yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Struthanthus haenkei is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
