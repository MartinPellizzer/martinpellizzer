Spathodea serrulata

Spathodea serrulata, commonly known as the serrated flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
