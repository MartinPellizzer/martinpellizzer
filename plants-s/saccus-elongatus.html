Saccus elongatus

Saccus elongatus, commonly known as the elongated sac plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive sac-like structures.

These sacs, which are specialized leaf modifications, serve as water storage organs, allowing the plant to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the sacs are elongated and fleshy.

Saccus elongatus is primarily found in Mediterranean climates, where it adapts to seasonal droughts through its efficient water storage mechanism.
