Scandix wilhelmsii

Scandix wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize the substrate and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.

Scandix wilhelmsii is valued for its ecological role in water ecosystems and its aesthetic appeal in aquatic gardens.
