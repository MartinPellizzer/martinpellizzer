Sorghum saccharoides

Sorghum saccharoides, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous stalks and large, blade-like leaves. The plant is particularly notable for its high sugar content in its juice, which makes it valuable for biofuel and syrup production. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Sorghum saccharoides is also drought-resistant, making it a hardy crop for arid regions.
