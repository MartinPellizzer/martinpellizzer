Scabiosa officinarum-arvensis

Scabiosa officinarum-arvensis, commonly known as the common scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with a fine downy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
