Stizolobium bulbosum

Stizolobium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bulbous base and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. Stizolobium bulbosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
