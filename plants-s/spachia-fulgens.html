Spachia fulgens

Spachia fulgens, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant color and unique appearance make it a standout in any landscape.
