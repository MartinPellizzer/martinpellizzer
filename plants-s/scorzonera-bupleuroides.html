Scorzonera bupleuroides

Scorzonera bupleuroides, commonly known as the blue scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with basal leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Scorzonera bupleuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
