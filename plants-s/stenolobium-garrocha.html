Stenolobium garrocha

Stenolobium garrocha, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, with five lobed petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.
