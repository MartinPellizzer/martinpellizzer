Salix coccinea

Salix coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright red catkins and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams and wetlands. Its flowers appear in early spring, producing fluffy, yellowish-green catkins that release pollen.

Salix coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
