Solanum divaricatum

Solanum divaricatum, commonly known as the wild tomato or creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum divaricatum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
