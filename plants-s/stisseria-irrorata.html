Stisseria irrorata

Stisseria irrorata, commonly known as the silverleaf stisseria, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Stisseria irrorata is valued for its unique appearance and is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.
