Spathoglottis tomentosa

Spathoglottis tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescence. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
