Sclerolobium dwyeri

Sclerolobium dwyeri, commonly known as the dwarf silky oak, is a small, evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a compact growth habit with a rounded crown and produces woody pods containing small, round seeds. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
