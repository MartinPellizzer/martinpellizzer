Senecio campestris proles aurantiacus

Senecio campestris proles aurantiacus, commonly known as the Orange-stemmed Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
