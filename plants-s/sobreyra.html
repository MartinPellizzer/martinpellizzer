Sobreyra

Sobreyra, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
