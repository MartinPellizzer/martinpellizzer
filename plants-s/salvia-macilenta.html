Salvia macilenta

Salvia macilenta, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia macilenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
