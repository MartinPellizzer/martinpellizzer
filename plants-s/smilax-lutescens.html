Smilax lutescens

Smilax lutescens, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with thorned stems that help it ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a waxy texture and a pointed tip.

Smilax lutescens is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
