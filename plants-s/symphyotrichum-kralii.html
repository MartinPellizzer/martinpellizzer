Symphyotrichum kralii

Symphyotrichum kralii, commonly known as Kral's aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, usually blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Symphyotrichum kralii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
