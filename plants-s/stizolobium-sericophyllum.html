Stizolobium sericophyllum

Stizolobium sericophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
