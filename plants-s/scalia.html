Scalia

Scalia, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. The plants typically have square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Scalia species are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
