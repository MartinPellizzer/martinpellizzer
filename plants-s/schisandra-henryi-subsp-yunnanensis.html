Schisandra henryi subsp. yunnanensis

Schisandra henryi subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan schisandra, is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is native to China. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, red berries that turn dark purple when mature.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions. Its berries contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits compared to other Schisandra species.
