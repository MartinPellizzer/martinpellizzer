Salvia africana-caerulea

Salvia africana-caerulea, commonly known as the African skyblue sage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.
