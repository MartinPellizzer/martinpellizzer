Salacia oblonga

Salacia oblonga, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.

Salacia oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
