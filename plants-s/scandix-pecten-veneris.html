Scandix pecten-veneris

Scandix pecten-veneris, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which resemble the shell of the Venus' flower basket. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky bracts that surround the flowers. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats.

Scandix pecten-veneris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
