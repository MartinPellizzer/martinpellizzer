Solanum diaboli

Solanum diaboli, commonly known as the devil's apple, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive, pungent odor.

Solanum diaboli is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
