Spathelia coccinea

Spathelia coccinea, commonly known as the red orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble a spathe, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the warm season, attracting various pollinators.

Spathelia coccinea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
