Senecio emmae

Senecio emmae, commonly known as Emma's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Senecio emmae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
