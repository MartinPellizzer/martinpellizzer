Salacia hippocrateoides

Salacia hippocrateoides, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
