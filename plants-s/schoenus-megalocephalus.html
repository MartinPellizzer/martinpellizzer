Schoenus megalocephalus

Schoenus megalocephalus, commonly known as the giant-headed sedge, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, rounded inflorescences that give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Schoenus megalocephalus is typically found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
