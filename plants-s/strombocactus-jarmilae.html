Strombocactus jarmilae

Strombocactus jarmilae, commonly known as the Jarmilae barrel cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that tapers towards the top, forming a distinctive shape reminiscent of a drum or a bottle. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its spines are short and widely spaced, providing minimal protection but allowing for good air circulation.

Strombocactus jarmilae is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive prolonged droughts.
