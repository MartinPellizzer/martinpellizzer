Sorbus badensis

Sorbus badensis, commonly known as the Baden mountain rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, often reddish or orange-colored berries. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
