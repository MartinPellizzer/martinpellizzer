Sedum schoenlandii

Sedum schoenlandii, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.

Sedum schoenlandii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky landscapes.
