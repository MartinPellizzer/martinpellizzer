Satureja montana subsp. pisidia

Satureja montana subsp.

pisidia, commonly known as Pisidian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their pungent, slightly minty flavor.
