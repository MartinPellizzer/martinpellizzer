Salvia aurita var. aurita

Salvia aurita var. aurita, commonly known as the aurita sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
