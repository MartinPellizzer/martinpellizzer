Scutellaria glabrata

Scutellaria glabrata, commonly known as the smooth skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria glabrata is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its genus name refers to the shape of the seed structure, known as a scutellum.
