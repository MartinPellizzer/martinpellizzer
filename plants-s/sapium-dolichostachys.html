Sapium dolichostachys

Sapium dolichostachys, commonly known as the long-stalked Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The plant's latex is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
