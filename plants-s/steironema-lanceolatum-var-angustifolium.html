Steironema lanceolatum var. angustifolium

Steironema lanceolatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved steironema, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often arranged alternately along the stem, and the flowers are borne on elongated pedicels.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
