Streptocarpus denticulatus

Streptocarpus denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, spurred flowers. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, giving it its common name. The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive lower lip that extends beyond the upper lip.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
