Scabiosa columbaria subsp. spreta

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

spreta, commonly known as the Sweet William. It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, showy blooms throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
