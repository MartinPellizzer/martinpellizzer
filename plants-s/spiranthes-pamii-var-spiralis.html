Spiranthes pamii var. spiralis

Spiranthes pamii var. spiralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces a single, erect flowering stem that bears numerous small, fragrant blooms.

This orchid is pollinated primarily by bees and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
