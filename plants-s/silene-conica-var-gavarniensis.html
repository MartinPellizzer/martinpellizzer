Silene conica var. gavarniensis

Silene conica var. gavarniensis, commonly known as the Gavarnie bellflower, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains in France.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
