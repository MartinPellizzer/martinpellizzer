Stelis sanctae-rosae

Stelis sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Rosy Saint's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging habitat.

Stelis sanctae-rosae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
