Simsia auriculata

Simsia auriculata, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small cups, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Simsia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
