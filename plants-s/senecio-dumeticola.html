Senecio dumeticola

Senecio dumeticola, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio dumeticola is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal dunes.
