Selera gossypioides

Selera gossypioides, commonly known as cottonleaf or cottonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive cotton-like texture when young. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing fluffy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Selera gossypioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
