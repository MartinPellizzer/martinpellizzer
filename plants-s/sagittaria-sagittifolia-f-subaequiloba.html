Sagittaria sagittifolia f. subaequiloba

Sagittaria sagittifolia f. subaequiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and shallow water environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and adaptability to aquatic conditions.
