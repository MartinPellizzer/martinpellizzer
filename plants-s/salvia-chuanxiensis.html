Salvia chuanxiensis

Salvia chuanxiensis, commonly known as the Chuanxi sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
