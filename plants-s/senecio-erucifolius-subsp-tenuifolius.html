Senecio erucifolius subsp. tenuifolius

Senecio erucifolius subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
