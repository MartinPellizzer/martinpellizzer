Sorbus aucuparia f. minutidens

Sorbus aucuparia f. minutidens, commonly known as the dwarf white thorn, is a compact variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its small, thorn-bearing branches and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that turn red in autumn. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for small spaces.
