Salvia haenkei

Salvia haenkei, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it popular in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
