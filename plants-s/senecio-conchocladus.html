Senecio conchocladus

Senecio conchocladus, commonly known as the elephant's foot or African snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, swollen, tuberous base that resembles an elephant's foot, and its clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to adapt to dry environments.

Senecio conchocladus prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
