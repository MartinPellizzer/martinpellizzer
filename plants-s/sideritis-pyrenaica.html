Sideritis pyrenaica

Sideritis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 600 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

Sideritis pyrenaica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
