Silene helmandica

Silene helmandica, commonly known as the Afghan campion, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan and parts of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Silene helmandica has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
