Stenocereus griseus

Stenocereus griseus, commonly known as the gray hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
