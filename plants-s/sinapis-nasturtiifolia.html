Sinapis nasturtiifolia

Sinapis nasturtiifolia, commonly known as the garden mustard or narrow-leaved mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is known for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications or as a green manure crop.
