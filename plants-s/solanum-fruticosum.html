Solanum fruticosum

Solanum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
