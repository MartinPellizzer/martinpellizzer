Scabiosa rotata

Scabiosa rotata, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and meadows.

Scabiosa rotata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
