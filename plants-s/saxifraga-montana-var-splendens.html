Saxifraga montana var. splendens

Saxifraga montana var. splendens, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold, alpine climates. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
