Stipagrostis fallax

Stipagrostis fallax, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Stipagrostis fallax plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
