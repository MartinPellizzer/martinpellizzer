Sida hermanniifolia

Sida hermanniifolia, commonly known as Hermann's sida, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Sida hermanniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
