Scilla bifolia subsp. buekkensis

Scilla bifolia subsp.

buekkensis, commonly known as the Hungarian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Carpathian Basin. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It is also valued for its medicinal properties, though its use is limited due to its toxicity.
