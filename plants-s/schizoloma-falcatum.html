Schizoloma falcatum

Schizoloma falcatum, commonly known as the flatleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive, flat, leaf-like structure with a leathery texture and a pale green to yellowish color. The thallus is composed of a fungal matrix that hosts various algae, which contribute to its nutritional needs. It is often found on decaying wood or soil in temperate regions.

This lichen plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of environmental conditions.
