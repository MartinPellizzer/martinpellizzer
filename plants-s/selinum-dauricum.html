Selinum dauricum

Selinum dauricum, commonly known as Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Selinum dauricum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
