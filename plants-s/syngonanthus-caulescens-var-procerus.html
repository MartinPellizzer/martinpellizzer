Syngonanthus caulescens var. procerus

Syngonanthus caulescens var. procerus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is native to parts of South America and is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
