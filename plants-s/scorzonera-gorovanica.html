Scorzonera gorovanica

Scorzonera gorovanica, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera gorovanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive flower structure.
