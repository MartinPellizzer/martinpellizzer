Senecio laetiflorus

Senecio laetiflorus, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
