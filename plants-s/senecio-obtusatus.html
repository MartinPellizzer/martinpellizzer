Senecio obtusatus

Senecio obtusatus, commonly known as the bluntleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally broad and rounded, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio obtusatus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
