Sphaerocephalus szowitsii

Sphaerocephalus szowitsii, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
