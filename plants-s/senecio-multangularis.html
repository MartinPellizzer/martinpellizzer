Senecio multangularis

Senecio multangularis, commonly known as the angular senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features angular, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio multangularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
