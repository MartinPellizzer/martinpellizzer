Stapelia beukmanii

Stapelia beukmanii, commonly known as the Beukman's meatplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick stems that store water. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. These flowers are designed to attract pollinators such as flies and beetles, mimicking decaying flesh.

Stapelia beukmanii is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.
