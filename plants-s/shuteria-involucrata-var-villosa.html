Shuteria involucrata var. villosa

Shuteria involucrata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy shuteria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
