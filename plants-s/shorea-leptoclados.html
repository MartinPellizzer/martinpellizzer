Shorea leptoclados

Shorea leptoclados, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
