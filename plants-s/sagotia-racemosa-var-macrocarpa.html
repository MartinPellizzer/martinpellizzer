Sagotia racemosa var. macrocarpa

Sagotia racemosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sagotia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, racemose inflorescence that bears small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
