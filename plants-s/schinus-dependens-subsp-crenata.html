Schinus dependens subsp. crenata

Schinus dependens subsp.

crenata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or crenate Brazilian pepper, is a shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
