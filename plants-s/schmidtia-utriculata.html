Schmidtia utriculata

Schmidtia utriculata, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. The plant is characterized by its unique seed pods, which are inflated and often referred to as "bladders," giving it its common name. Schmidtia utriculata thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
