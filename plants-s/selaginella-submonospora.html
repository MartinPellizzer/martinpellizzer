Selaginella submonospora

Selaginella submonospora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, typical of its evolutionary lineage.

Selaginella submonospora is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
