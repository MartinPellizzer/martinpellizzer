Solidago linearifolia

Solidago linearifolia, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago linearifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
