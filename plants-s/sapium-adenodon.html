Sapium adenodon

Sapium adenodon, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
