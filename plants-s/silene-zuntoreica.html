Silene zuntoreica

Silene zuntoreica, commonly known as the Zunțărea iceplant, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Carpathian Mountains in Romania.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Silene zuntoreica is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and rare species within its native habitat.
