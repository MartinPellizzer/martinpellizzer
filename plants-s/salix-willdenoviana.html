Salix willdenoviana

Salix willdenoviana, commonly known as the Chinese willow or the Weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant has slender, grayish-brown branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm in length. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
