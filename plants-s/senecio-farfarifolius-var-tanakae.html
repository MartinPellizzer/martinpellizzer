Senecio farfarifolius var. tanakae

Senecio farfarifolius var. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions where it can outcompete native vegetation.
