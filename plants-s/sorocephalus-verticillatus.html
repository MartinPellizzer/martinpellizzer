Sorocephalus verticillatus

Sorocephalus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its flowers are usually yellow or brown, and they bloom in late winter to early spring.

Sorocephalus verticillatus is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.
