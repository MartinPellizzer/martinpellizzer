Senecio megacephalus

Senecio megacephalus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant features a woody stem with thick, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it requires careful management in cultivation.
