Spergularia uliginosa

Spergularia uliginosa, commonly known as bog stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the surface of peat bogs and other moist soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color, and it is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed pods that resemble a stork's bill. Spergularia uliginosa is adapted to nutrient-poor, waterlogged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing peatland ecosystems.

It is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance.
