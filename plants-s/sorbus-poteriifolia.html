Sorbus poteriifolia

Sorbus poteriifolia, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible berries that turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
