Solanum davisense

Solanum davisense, commonly known as Davis's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum davisense is often found in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in cooler, high-altitude climates.
