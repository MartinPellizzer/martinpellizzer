Solidago virgaurea var. reticulata

Solidago virgaurea var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
