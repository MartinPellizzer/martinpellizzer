Symphytonema crassifolium

Symphytonema crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf symphyton, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
