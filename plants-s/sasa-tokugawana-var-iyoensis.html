Sasa tokugawana var. iyoensis

Sasa tokugawana var. iyoensis, commonly known as the Iyoensis bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in the production of bamboo products.
