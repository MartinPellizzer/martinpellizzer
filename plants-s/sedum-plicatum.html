Sedum plicatum

Sedum plicatum, commonly known as the zigzag sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a zigzag pattern along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Sedum plicatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
