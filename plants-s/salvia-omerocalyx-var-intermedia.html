Salvia omerocalyx var. intermedia

Salvia omerocalyx var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
