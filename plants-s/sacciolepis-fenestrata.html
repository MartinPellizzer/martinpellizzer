Sacciolepis fenestrata

Sacciolepis fenestrata, commonly known as the windowleaf poinsettia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant's foliage is often marked with distinctive, window-like patterns, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sacciolepis fenestrata is a popular ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season due to its association with festive decorations.
