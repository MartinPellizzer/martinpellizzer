Sisymbrium hastatum

Sisymbrium hastatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sisymbrium hastatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
