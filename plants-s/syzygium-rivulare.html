Syzygium rivulare

Syzygium rivulare, commonly known as the river syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium rivulare thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
