Senegalia pennata subsp. kerrii

Senegalia pennata subsp.

kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are usually straight or slightly curved.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by flat, linear pods containing seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
