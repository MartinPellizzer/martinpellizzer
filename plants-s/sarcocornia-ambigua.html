Sarcocornia ambigua

Sarcocornia ambigua, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access nutrients and water from deep within the soil.

Sarcocornia ambigua is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other arid or saline habitats.
