Syzygium vanuatuense

Syzygium vanuatuense, commonly known as the Vanuatu apple or Kava apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the islands of Vanuatu and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet, fragrant aroma. Syzygium vanuatuense thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its fruits are not only consumed locally but also hold cultural significance in traditional practices.
