Solanum yamobambense

Solanum yamobambense, commonly known as the Bambana tomato, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with a pointed shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The botanical characteristics of Solanum yamobambense make it a unique and valuable species within the Solanaceae family.
