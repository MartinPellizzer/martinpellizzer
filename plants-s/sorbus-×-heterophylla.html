Sorbus × heterophylla

Sorbus × heterophylla, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its diverse leaf morphology.

It typically exhibits a range of leaf shapes, from broadly ovate to narrow and lanceolate, which contributes to its variability. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are usually white or yellow in color. It is often found in temperate regions and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
