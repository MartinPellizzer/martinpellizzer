Strychnos smilacina

Strychnos smilacina, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains harmful alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
