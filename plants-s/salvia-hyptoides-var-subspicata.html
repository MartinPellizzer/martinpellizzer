Salvia hyptoides var. subspicata

Salvia hyptoides var. subspicata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. The plant produces spikes of vibrant blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
