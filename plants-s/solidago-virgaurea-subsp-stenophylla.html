Solidago virgaurea subsp. stenophylla

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Asteraceae family.
