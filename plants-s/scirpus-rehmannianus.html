Scirpus rehmannianus

Scirpus rehmannianus, commonly known as the rehmannian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Scirpus rehmannianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
