Stellaria neglecta subsp. cupaniana

Stellaria neglecta subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the common starwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features small, star-shaped white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its characteristic flower structures and often hairy foliage.
