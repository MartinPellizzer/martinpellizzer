Scolopia stolzii var. riparia

Scolopia stolzii var. riparia, commonly known as the river scolopia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
