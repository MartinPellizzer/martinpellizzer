Statice sartorii

Statice sartorii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Statice sartorii is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower.
