Sanchezia scandens

Sanchezia scandens, commonly known as the arrowroot vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant with heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its roots are rich in starch and have been historically used as a food source.

Sanchezia scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
