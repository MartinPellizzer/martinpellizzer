Staurogyne glutinosa

Staurogyne glutinosa, commonly known as sticky waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often form dense underwater mats. The leaves are narrow and slightly curved, with a mucilaginous texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in shallow waters.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
