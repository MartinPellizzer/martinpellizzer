Spiraea papillosa var. papillosa

Spiraea papillosa var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
