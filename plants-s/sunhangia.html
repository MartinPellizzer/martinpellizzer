Sunhangia

Sunhangia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Sunhangia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
