Solanum trachycyphum

Solanum trachycyphum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
