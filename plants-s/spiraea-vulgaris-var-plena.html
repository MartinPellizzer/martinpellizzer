Spiraea vulgaris var. plena

Spiraea vulgaris var. plena, commonly known as the double meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has arching stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Spiraea vulgaris var. plena is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
