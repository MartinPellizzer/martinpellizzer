Saxifraga cherlerioides f. laxa

Saxifraga cherlerioides f. laxa, commonly known as the loose saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and cool climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in alpine gardens.
