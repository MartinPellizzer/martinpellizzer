Syzygium dyerianum

Syzygium dyerianum, commonly known as the Dyer's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible drupes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Syzygium dyerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
