Sideritis incana var. nervosa

Sideritis incana var. nervosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features grayish-white, hairy foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception, containing compounds with potential health benefits.
