Sasa propinqua

Sasa propinqua, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa propinqua is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, low-growing mats.
