Silene occidentalis subsp. typica

Silene occidentalis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the western evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
