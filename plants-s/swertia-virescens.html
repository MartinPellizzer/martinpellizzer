Swertia virescens

Swertia virescens, commonly known as the green swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Swertia virescens is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain meadows.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
