Seriphidium ramosum

Seriphidium ramosum, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep taproots and a low profile to reduce water loss. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Seriphidium ramosum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
