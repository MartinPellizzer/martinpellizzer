Sarothra laxa f. simplex

Sarothra laxa f. simplex, commonly known as the simple form of the slender sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its simple inflorescence and compact growth habit make it a distinctive member of the Verbenaceae family.
