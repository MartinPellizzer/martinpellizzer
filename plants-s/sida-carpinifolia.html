Sida carpinifolia

Sida carpinifolia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. Sida carpinifolia is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a food source for certain insects.
