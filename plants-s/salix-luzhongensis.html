Salix luzhongensis

Salix luzhongensis, commonly known as the Luzhong willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
