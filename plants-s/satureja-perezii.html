Satureja perezii

Satureja perezii, commonly known as Peruvian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, and its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and cooking. Satureja perezii thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
