Satureja graeca subsp. congesta

Satureja graeca subsp.

congesta, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, ovate leaves and pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its strong, pungent aroma.

Its botanical features include compact growth habit and glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.
