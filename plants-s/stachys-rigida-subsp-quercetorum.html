Stachys rigida subsp. quercetorum

Stachys rigida subsp.

quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaves that are opposite and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in association with other woodland flora.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
