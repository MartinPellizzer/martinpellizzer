Silene indica var. cashmeriana

Silene indica var. cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir pink, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces striking pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment.

Silene indica var. cashmeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
