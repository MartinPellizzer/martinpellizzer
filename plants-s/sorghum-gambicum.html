Sorghum gambicum

Sorghum gambicum, commonly known as giant broomcorn millet, is a tall, annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, feathery inflorescence and can grow up to 4 meters in height. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with low rainfall. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the stems are sturdy and fibrous.

It is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food, animal feed, and traditional beverages.
