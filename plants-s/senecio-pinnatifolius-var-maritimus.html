Senecio pinnatifolius var. maritimus

Senecio pinnatifolius var. maritimus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is well adapted to saline and windy conditions, making it a resilient plant in coastal ecosystems.
