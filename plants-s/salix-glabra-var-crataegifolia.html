Salix glabra var. crataegifolia

Salix glabra var. crataegifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elongated, often with a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
