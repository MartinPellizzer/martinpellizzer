Stanhopea moliana

Stanhopea moliana, commonly known as the Molian's stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, with intricate patterns resembling a spider's web. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found in the canopies of humid rainforests. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Stanhopea moliana is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
