Shorea assamica

Shorea assamica, commonly known as white teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, light-colored wood. The tree typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin.

Shorea assamica is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a significant species in both ecological and economic contexts.
