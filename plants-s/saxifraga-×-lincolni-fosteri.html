Saxifraga × lincolni-fosteri

Saxifraga × lincolni-fosteri, commonly known as the Lincoln-Foster's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are generally succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
