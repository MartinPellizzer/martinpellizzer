Styrax iopilinus

Styrax iopilinus, commonly known as the Mexican styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Styrax iopilinus thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to subtropical climates.
