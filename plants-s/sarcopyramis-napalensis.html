Sarcopyramis napalensis

Sarcopyramis napalensis, commonly known as the Napali pyramidal cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the high Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems that form a pyramidal shape, which provides structural support in its harsh, high-altitude environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss in the arid conditions it inhabits.

Sarcopyramis napalensis plays a role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert-dwelling animals.
