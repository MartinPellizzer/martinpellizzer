Sonchus simplicissimus

Sonchus simplicissimus, commonly known as lesser sonchus, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. The plant features alternate, rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated edge. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
