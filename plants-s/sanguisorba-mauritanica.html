Sanguisorba mauritanica

Sanguisorba mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian sorrel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Sanguisorba mauritanica is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other sorrel species known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
