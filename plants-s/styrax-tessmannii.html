Styrax tessmannii

Styrax tessmannii, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a sticky, aromatic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Styrax tessmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators and wildlife.
