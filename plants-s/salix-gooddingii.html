Salix gooddingii

Salix gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Salix gooddingii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing streambanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
