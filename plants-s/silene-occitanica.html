Silene occitanica

Silene occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often erect and branched.

Silene occitanica is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
