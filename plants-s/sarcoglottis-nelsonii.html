Sarcoglottis nelsonii

Sarcoglottis nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's sarcoglottis, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with a cluster of leaves emerging from its base. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Sarcoglottis nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
