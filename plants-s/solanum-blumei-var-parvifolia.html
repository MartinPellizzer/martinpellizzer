Solanum blumei var. parvifolia

Solanum blumei var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
