Solanum tuberosum var. merceri

Solanum tuberosum var. merceri, commonly known as the Mercier potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are typically larger and more elongated compared to standard potato varieties. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many Solanum species.

This variety is valued for its culinary properties and has been cultivated for its high yield and adaptability to various soil conditions.
