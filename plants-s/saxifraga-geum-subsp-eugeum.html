Saxifraga geum subsp. eugeum

Saxifraga geum subsp.

eugeum, commonly known as the red-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, adorned with clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a rough, hairy texture. Saxifraga geum subsp.

eugeum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
