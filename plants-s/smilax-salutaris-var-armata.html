Smilax salutaris var. armata

Smilax salutaris var. armata, commonly known as the prickly pear or armed smilax, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, defensive spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
