Sorghum pauciflorum

Sorghum pauciflorum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Sorghum pauciflorum is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
