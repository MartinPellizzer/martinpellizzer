Spathulopetalum

Spathulopetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, and often colorful petals that are typically arranged in a showy inflorescence. The plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Spathulopetalum species usually thrive in humid environments and require well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Their unique floral structure makes them popular among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
