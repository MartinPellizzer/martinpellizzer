Schismatopera martiana

Schismatopera martiana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex inflorescence with numerous small, showy blooms.

Schismatopera martiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
