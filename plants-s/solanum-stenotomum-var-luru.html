Solanum stenotomum var. luru

Solanum stenotomum var. luru, commonly known as the Luru nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
