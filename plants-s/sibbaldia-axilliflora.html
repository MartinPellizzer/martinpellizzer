Sibbaldia axilliflora

Sibbaldia axilliflora, commonly known as the Kashmir snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often at high altitudes. The plant features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Sibbaldia axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
