Spiraea wilsonii var. stenophylla

Spiraea wilsonii var. stenophylla, commonly known as Wilson's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its compact form and seasonal interest.
