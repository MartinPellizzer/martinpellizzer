Sporobolus albicans

Sporobolus albicans, commonly known as silverleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spiky brush, with small white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Sporobolus albicans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
