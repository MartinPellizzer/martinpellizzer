Sidalcea malviflora subsp. patula

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada checker-mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
