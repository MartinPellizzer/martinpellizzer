Salix myrsinites var. grandifolia

Salix myrsinites var. grandifolia, commonly known as the largeleaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
