Sisyrinchium angustifolium var. mucronatum

Sisyrinchium angustifolium var. mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The leaves are narrow and linear, often slightly hairy, and the plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in the Pacific Northwest.

The plant is pollinated primarily by bees and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
