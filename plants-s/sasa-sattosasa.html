Sasa sattosasa

Sasa sattosasa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa sattosasa forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating thick vegetation cover.

It is often used in landscaping and has cultural significance in traditional Japanese practices.
