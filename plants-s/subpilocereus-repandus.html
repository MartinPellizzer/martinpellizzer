Subpilocereus repandus

Subpilocereus repandus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically modified into spineless, paddle-shaped structures.

Subpilocereus repandus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
