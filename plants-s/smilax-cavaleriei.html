Smilax cavaleriei

Smilax cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or sweet sarsapilla, is a perennial vine native to eastern Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem with thorny tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Smilax cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
