Spathandra roborea

Spathandra roborea, commonly known as the Roborean spathanthus, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a spatula, hence its genus name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring rich, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and green, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Spathandra roborea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
