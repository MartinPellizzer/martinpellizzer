Suaeda anonyma

Suaeda anonyma, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor in loose, sandy soils.

Suaeda anonyma plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
