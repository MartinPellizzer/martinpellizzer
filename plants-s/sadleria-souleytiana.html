Sadleria souleytiana

Sadleria souleytiana, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink flowers, which resemble small slippers, and its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.

Sadleria souleytiana is also valued for its unique pollination mechanism, where its flowers attract specific insects for reproduction.
