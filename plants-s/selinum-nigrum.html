Selinum nigrum

Selinum nigrum, commonly known as hemlock, is a toxic herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant contains potent toxins, including cicutoxin and other alkaloids, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is considered a dangerous plant and should never be ingested or handled without proper expertise.
