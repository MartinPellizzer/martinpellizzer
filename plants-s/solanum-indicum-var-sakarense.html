Solanum indicum var. sakarense

Solanum indicum var. sakarense, commonly known as the Sakaransh or Sakaransh plant, is a herbaceous species native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
