Sauromatum listeri

Sauromatum listeri, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, showy spathe that resembles a ghostly face, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by the modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a unique ability to attract specific pollinators.
