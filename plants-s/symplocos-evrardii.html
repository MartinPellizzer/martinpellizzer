Symplocos evrardii

Symplocos evrardii, commonly known as the red-fruited inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that persist into the winter. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Symplocos evrardii is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
