Sorghum nardus

Sorghum nardus, commonly known as narrowleaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Sorghum nardus is often used for forage and has some traditional medicinal uses in certain regions.
