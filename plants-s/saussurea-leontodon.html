Saussurea leontodon

Saussurea leontodon, commonly known as the leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange disk flowers. The plant produces a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea leontodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
