Serratula coronata var. alpina

Serratula coronata var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sow-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula coronata var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
