Sedum farinosum

Sedum farinosum, commonly known as the floury stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, blue-green leaves covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine regions.

Sedum farinosum is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
