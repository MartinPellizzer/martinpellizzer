Senecio mairetianus

Senecio mairetianus, commonly known as the Mairetianus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a distinctive fuzzy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
