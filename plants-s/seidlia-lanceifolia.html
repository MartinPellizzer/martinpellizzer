Seidlia lanceifolia

Seidlia lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf seidlia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

Seidlia lanceifolia is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
