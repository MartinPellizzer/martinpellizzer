Solanum tuberosum var. vuchefeldicum

Solanum tuberosum var. vuchefeldicum, commonly known as the Vuchefeldicum potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which often includes a distinctive coloration and texture compared to standard potato varieties. This variety is native to the Andean region of South America, where it has been cultivated for generations. Its genetic makeup contributes to its resistance to certain diseases, making it a valuable resource for agricultural research.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and plant genetics.
