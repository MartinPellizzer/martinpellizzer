Scabiosa cretica subsp. carpatha

Scabiosa cretica subsp.

carpatha, commonly known as Carpathian scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, five-petaled blue or purple flowers.

The leaves are typically gray-green, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
