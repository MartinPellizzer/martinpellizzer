Solanum uncinatum

Solanum uncinatum, commonly known as the curly top nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces oval, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum uncinatum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
