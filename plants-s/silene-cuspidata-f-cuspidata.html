Silene cuspidata f. cuspidata

Silene cuspidata f. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is known for its resilience in arid environments and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
