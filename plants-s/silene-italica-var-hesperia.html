Silene italica var. hesperia

Silene italica var. hesperia, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Silene italica var. hesperia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
