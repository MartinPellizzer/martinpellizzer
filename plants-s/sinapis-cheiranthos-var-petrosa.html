Sinapis cheiranthos var. petrosa

Sinapis cheiranthos var. petrosa, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in small, glandular hairs. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.
