Solanum alatibaccatum

Solanum alatibaccatum, commonly known as the winged baccatum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in dry, open habitats and can thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
