Stoebe cinerea

Stoebe cinerea, commonly known as hoary bellflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Stoebe cinerea is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
