Solidago texensis

Solidago texensis, commonly known as Texas goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
