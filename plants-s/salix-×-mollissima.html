Salix × mollissima

Salix × mollissima, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that hang like a curtain, giving it a graceful appearance. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-green underside. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environments.
