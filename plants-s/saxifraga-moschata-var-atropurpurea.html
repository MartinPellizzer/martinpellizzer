Saxifraga moschata var. atropurpurea

Saxifraga moschata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features dark purple to reddish foliage and small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh climates.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand extreme environmental conditions.
