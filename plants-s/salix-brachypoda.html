Salix brachypoda

Salix brachypoda, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated and contribute to its role in early succession ecosystems. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, making it an important species for stabilizing riparian areas.

Salix brachypoda is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
