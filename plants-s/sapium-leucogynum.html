Sapium leucogynum

Sapium leucogynum, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
