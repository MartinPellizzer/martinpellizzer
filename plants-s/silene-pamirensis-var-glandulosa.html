Silene pamirensis var. glandulosa

Silene pamirensis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Pamir sandstem, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene pamirensis var. glandulosa is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
