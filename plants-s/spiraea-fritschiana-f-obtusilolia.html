Spiraea fritschiana f. obtusilolia

Spiraea fritschiana f. obtusilolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf spiraea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The leaves are elliptical and have a rounded tip, distinguishing it from other spiraea species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
