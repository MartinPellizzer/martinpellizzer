Salvia scapiformis var. arisanensis

Salvia scapiformis var. arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
