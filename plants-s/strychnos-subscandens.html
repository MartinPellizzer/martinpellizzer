Strychnos subscandens

Strychnos subscandens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
