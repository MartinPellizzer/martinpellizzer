Stereoxylon crenulatum

Stereoxylon crenulatum, commonly known as the wavy-ribbed wood plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-ribbed bark that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique aromatic scent that attracts pollinators.
