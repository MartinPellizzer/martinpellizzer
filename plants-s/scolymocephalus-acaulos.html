Scolymocephalus acaulos

Scolymocephalus acaulos, commonly known as the naked stem scolymus, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stem and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often studied for its ecological adaptations in arid environments.
