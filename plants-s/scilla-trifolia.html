Scilla trifolia

Scilla trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are strap-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in the spring. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Scilla trifolia is adapted to well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
