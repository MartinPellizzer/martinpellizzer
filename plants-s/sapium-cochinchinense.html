Sapium cochinchinense

Sapium cochinchinense, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
