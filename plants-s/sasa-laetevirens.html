Sasa laetevirens

Sasa laetevirens, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa laetevirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
