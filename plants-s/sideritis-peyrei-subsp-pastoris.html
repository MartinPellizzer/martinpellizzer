Sideritis peyrei subsp. pastoris

Sideritis peyrei subsp.

pastoris, commonly known as the Balkan ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes.

Sideritis species are often valued for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception.
