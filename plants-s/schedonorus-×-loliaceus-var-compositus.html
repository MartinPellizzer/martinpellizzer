Schedonorus × loliaceus var. compositus

Schedonorus × loliaceus var. compositus, commonly known as meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically 5–15 cm long. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is used in forage and turfgrass applications.
