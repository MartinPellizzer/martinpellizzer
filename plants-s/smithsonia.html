Smithsonia

Smithsonia, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in acidic soils. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Smithsonia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
