Syzygium scalarinerve

Syzygium scalarinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Syzygium scalarinerve is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in tropical horticulture.
