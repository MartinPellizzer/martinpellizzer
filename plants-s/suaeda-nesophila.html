Suaeda nesophila

Suaeda nesophila, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, often found in coastal and inland salt marshes. The plant has succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its ability to tolerate extreme drought and high salinity makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
