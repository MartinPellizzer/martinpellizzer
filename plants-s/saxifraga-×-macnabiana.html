Saxifraga × macnabiana

Saxifraga × macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats on limestone or gravelly soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × macnabiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
