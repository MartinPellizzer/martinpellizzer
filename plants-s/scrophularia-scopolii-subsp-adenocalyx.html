Scrophularia scopolii subsp. adenocalyx

Scrophularia scopolii subsp.

adenocalyx, commonly known as the pale scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as limestone soils and open woodlands.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
