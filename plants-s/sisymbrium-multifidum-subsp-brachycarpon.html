Sisymbrium multifidum subsp. brachycarpon

Sisymbrium multifidum subsp.

brachycarpon, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and western Asia.

This species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
