Sapium naiguatense

Sapium naiguatense, commonly known as the Guianese sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
