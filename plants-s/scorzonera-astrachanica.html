Scorzonera astrachanica

Scorzonera astrachanica, commonly known as the Astrachan scarlet globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Scorzonera astrachanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
