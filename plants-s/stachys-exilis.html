Stachys exilis

Stachys exilis, commonly known as slender betony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Stachys exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
