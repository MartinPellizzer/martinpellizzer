Solanum tunariense

Solanum tunariense, commonly known as the Tunari nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Solanum tunariense is typically found in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
