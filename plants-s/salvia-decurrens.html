Salvia decurrens

Salvia decurrens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant produces tubular flowers that are attractive to pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it thrives in partial to full sun.

Salvia decurrens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
