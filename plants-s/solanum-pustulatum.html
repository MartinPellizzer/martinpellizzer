Solanum pustulatum

Solanum pustulatum, commonly known as the pustulate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.
