Senecillis dentata

Senecillis dentata, commonly known as the toothed senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its serrated, tooth-like leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
