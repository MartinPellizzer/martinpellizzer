Scutellaria hyssopifolia

Scutellaria hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Scutellaria hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
