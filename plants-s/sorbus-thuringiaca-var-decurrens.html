Sorbus thuringiaca var. decurrens

Sorbus thuringiaca var. decurrens, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
