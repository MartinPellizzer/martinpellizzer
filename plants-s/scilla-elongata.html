Scilla elongata

Scilla elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, upright stems. Scilla elongata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
