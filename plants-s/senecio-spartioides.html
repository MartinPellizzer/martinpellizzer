Senecio spartioides

Senecio spartioides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical structure supports its survival in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
