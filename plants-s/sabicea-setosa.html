Sabicea setosa

Sabicea setosa, commonly known as the woolly sycamore or silky sycamore, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Sabicea setosa make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitats.
