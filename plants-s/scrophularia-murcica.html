Scrophularia murcica

Scrophularia murcica, commonly known as the Murcia scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals arranged in a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Scrophularia murcica is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
