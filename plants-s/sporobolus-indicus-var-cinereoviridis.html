Sporobolus indicus var. cinereoviridis

Sporobolus indicus var. cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-green needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and degraded grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
