Stanhopeastrum cirrhatum

Stanhopeastrum cirrhatum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are arranged in an alternate pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Stanhopeastrum cirrhatum is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in landscaped areas.
