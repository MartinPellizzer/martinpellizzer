Scleroglossum crassifolium

Scleroglossum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock plant, is a succulent species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers during the warmer months.

Scleroglossum crassifolium is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and minimal rainfall.
