Salvia pratensis var. lusitanica

Salvia pratensis var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian blue violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear whorled clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing biodiversity in natural habitats.
