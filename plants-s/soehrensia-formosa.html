Soehrensia formosa

Soehrensia formosa, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a prominent central stamen that resembles a star. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Soehrensia formosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
