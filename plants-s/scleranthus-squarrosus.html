Scleranthus squarrosus

Scleranthus squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is known for its hairy, woolly appearance, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
