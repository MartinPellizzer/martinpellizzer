Solanum chavinense

Solanum chavinense, commonly known as the Chavin eggplant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Solanum chavinense is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
