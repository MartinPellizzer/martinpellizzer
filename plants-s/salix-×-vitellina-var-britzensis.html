Salix × vitellina var. britzensis

Salix × vitellina var. britzensis, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its striking, cascading foliage.

It is native to the wetlands and riparian areas of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features long, narrow leaves that hang in drooping clusters, giving it a graceful, ornamental appearance. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along water bodies.
