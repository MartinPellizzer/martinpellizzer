Saccharum fallax var. aristatum

Saccharum fallax var. aristatum, commonly known as the aristate sugarcane.

This variant is a less common subspecies of sugarcane characterized by its unique morphological features. It typically exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to standard sugarcane varieties. The plant is notable for its elongated, bristle-like structures along the leaf sheaths, which give it its common name.

It is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its distinct genetic makeup and potential for hybridization with other sugarcane species.
