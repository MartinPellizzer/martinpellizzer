Satureja nubigena

Satureja nubigena, commonly known as the cloud sage, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Satureja nubigena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage.
