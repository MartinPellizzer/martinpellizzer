Silene saxifraga var. lanceolata

Silene saxifraga var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, often succulent leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
