Syzygium cinereum

Syzygium cinereum, commonly known as the silver apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, smooth, white or yellowish drupe that turns red when ripe. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
