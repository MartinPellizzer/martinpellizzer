Saxofridericia duidae subsp. marahuacensis

Saxofridericia duidae subsp.

marahuacensis, commonly known as the Marahuacan waxflower, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments. Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth habit compared to other members of the genus.
