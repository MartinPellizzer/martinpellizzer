Senna coimbrae

Senna coimbrae, commonly known as the Coimbra senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in elongated, ribbed pods, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Senna coimbrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
