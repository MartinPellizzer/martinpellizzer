Sisymbrium elegans

Sisymbrium elegans, commonly known as slender broomrape, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and its reliance on parasitic relationships with host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecological systems by influencing the growth and distribution of its host plants.
