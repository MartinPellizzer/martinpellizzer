Stevia catharinensis

Stevia catharinensis, commonly known as Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and commercial uses.

Its leaves are typically harvested and used in teas, herbal remedies, and as a natural sweetener alternative.
