Sagina papuana

Sagina papuana, commonly known as the Papuan carpetweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
