Symphipappus dichotomus

Symphipappus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its unique branching pattern and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
