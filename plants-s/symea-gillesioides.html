Symea gillesioides

Symea gillesioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with high humidity and partial sunlight.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing erosion.
