Saldinia subacuminata var. strigosa

Saldinia subacuminata var. strigosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and color.
