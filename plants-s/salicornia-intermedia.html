Salicornia intermedia

Salicornia intermedia, commonly known as glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and coastal areas.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green or red flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically reduced to scale-like structures, which help minimize water evaporation.

Salicornia intermedia plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
