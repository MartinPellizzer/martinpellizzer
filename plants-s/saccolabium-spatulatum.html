Saccolabium spatulatum

Saccolabium spatulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, spatulate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Saccolabium spatulatum is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
