Simaba baileyana

Simaba baileyana, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

The botanical features of Simaba baileyana make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
