Salix medwedewii

Salix medwedewii, commonly known as the Medwedewii willow, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers early in the growing season. It thrives in moist, often waterlogged soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Salix medwedewii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
