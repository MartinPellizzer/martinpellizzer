Stelis itatiayae

Stelis itatiayae, commonly known as the Itatiaya orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stelis itatiayae is often found in shaded, humid environments within the undergrowth of forests.
