Sida prolifica

Sida prolifica, commonly known as the prolific sida, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sida prolifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
