Silene latifolia subsp. persica

Silene latifolia subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian campion, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes.

The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
