Salvia fragarioides

Salvia fragarioides, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking red to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Salvia fragarioides thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
