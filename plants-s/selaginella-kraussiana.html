Selaginella kraussiana

Selaginella kraussiana, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its narrow, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella kraussiana is also noted for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
