Senecio angulosus

Senecio angulosus, commonly known as the angular senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are angular and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio angulosus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
