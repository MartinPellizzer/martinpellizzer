Salvia issanchou

Salvia issanchou, commonly known as the pink flowering sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties. Salvia issanchou is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
