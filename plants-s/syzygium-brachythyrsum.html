Syzygium brachythyrsum

Syzygium brachythyrsum, commonly known as the small-leaved tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
