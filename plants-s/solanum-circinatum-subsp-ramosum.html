Solanum circinatum subsp. ramosum

Solanum circinatum subsp.

ramosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its branched stems, which give it the subspecific name "ramosum," and its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a bright yellow or orange, often containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family, known for its ecological and medicinal significance.
