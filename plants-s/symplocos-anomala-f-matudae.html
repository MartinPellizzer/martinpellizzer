Symplocos anomala f. matudae

Symplocos anomala f. matudae, commonly known as the Matudae's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, hard berries. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
