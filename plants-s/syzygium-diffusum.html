Syzygium diffusum

Syzygium diffusum, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Syzygium diffusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
