Salix arctica var. minor

Salix arctica var. minor, commonly known as Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Salicaceae family and is adapted to harsh, cold environments. The plant typically has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces catkins during the spring. It is often found in tundra and rocky habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
