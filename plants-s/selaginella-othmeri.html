Selaginella othmeri

Selaginella othmeri, commonly known as the Othmer's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid regions of North America.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features whorled leaves that are spirally arranged around its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Selaginella othmeri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
