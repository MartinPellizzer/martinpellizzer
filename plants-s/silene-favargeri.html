Silene favargeri

Silene favargeri, commonly known as the favargeri campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are opposite and linear, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Silene favargeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
