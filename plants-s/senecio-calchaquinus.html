Senecio calchaquinus

Senecio calchaquinus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, giving it its common name. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its striking appearance in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
