Saccharum palisotii

Saccharum palisotii, commonly known as the bamboo cane grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands. Its rhizomes are capable of rapid growth, allowing it to spread extensively in suitable habitats.

Saccharum palisotii is valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.
