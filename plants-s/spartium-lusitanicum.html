Spartium lusitanicum

Spartium lusitanicum, commonly known as Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Spartium lusitanicum is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
