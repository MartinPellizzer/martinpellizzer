Sequoia sempervirens var. adpressa

Sequoia sempervirens var. adpressa, commonly known as the coastal redwood, is a subspecies of the redwood tree native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dark reddish-brown bark. This variety thrives in moist, cool climates and is adapted to frequent fog and rainfall. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and tightly packed, providing a dense canopy.

The tree can live for thousands of years, making it one of the longest-living organisms on Earth.
