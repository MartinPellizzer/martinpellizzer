Schefflera petiolosa

Schefflera petiolosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered leaves that form a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Schefflera petiolosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
