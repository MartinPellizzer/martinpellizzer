Semecarpus obtusifolius

Semecarpus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves with a blunt tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its durable wood and medicinal properties.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a distinctive buttressed trunk.
