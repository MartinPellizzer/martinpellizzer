Setaria hassleri

Setaria hassleri, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, helping it conserve moisture in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dryland areas.
