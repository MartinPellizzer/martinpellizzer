Steiremis ficoidea

Steiremis ficoidea, commonly known as the fig-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the common fig. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit, which is edible, resembles a small fig and has a sweet, pulpy interior.

Steiremis ficoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
