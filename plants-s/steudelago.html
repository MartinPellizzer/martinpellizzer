Steudelago

Steudelago, commonly known as the "false kudzu," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Steudelago species are often characterized by their climbing habit and ability to form dense thickets.

Botanically, they are valued for their potential medicinal properties and ecological roles in soil stabilization.
