Sedum chrysicaulum

Sedum chrysicaulum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a subtle yellowish tint. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.
