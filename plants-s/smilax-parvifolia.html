Smilax parvifolia

Smilax parvifolia, commonly known as smallleaf greenbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax parvifolia is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes.
