Stenocactus tetraxiphus

Stenocactus tetraxiphus, commonly known as the four-armed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that typically branches into four arms, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, spherical fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
