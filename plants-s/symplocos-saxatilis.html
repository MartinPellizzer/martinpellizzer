Symplocos saxatilis

Symplocos saxatilis, commonly known as the rock symplocos, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in forested or scrubland habitats. The plant features dense, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, round drupe.

Symplocos saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
