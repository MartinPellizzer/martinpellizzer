Silphium laciniatum var. robinsonii

Silphium laciniatum var. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's cutleaf silphium, is a rare and ancient plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for its healing benefits. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and is protected in several states.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in ecological systems and its historical importance in herbal medicine.
