Spiranthes lucayana

Spiranthes lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan Ladies' Tresses, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral arrangement of flowers, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, elliptical leaves.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
