Scorpiurus oliveri

Scorpiurus oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's scorpionvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Scorpiurus oliveri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
