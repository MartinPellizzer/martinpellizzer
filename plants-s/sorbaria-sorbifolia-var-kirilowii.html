Sorbaria sorbifolia var. kirilowii

Sorbaria sorbifolia var. kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's snowbell, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
