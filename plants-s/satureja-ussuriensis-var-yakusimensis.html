Satureja ussuriensis var. yakusimensis

Satureja ussuriensis var. yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakutian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of Siberia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in alpine or subarctic regions. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong, spicy fragrance.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.
