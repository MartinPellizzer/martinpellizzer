Scorzonera boetica subsp. ebusitana

Scorzonera boetica subsp.

ebusitana, commonly known as the Ebusitana scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Scorzonera boetica subsp.

ebusitana is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
