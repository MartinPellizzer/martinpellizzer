Syzygium nivae

Syzygium nivae, commonly known as the snow apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, whitish fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium nivae thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its fruits are valued for their sweet, aromatic pulp and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
