Schinus uruguayensis

Schinus uruguayensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, and its small, clustered yellow flowers. Schinus uruguayensis produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
