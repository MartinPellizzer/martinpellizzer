Satureja ovalifolia

Satureja ovalifolia, commonly known as oval-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in cooking for their pungent, slightly minty flavor, and the plant is also valued in traditional medicine.

Satureja ovalifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
