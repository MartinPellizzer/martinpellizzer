Scorzonera glastifolia var. asphodeloides

Scorzonera glastifolia var. asphodeloides, commonly known as the asphodel-like scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are sometimes harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
