Scirpidium aciculare

Scirpidium aciculare, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping rhizome with whorled, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage.

Scirpidium aciculare is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
