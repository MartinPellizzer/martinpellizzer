Senecio caulanthifolius

Senecio caulanthifolius, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
