Salvadora surinamensis

Salvadora surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam toothbrush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and papery, ovate leaves. The plant is notable for its unique structure, with its leaves having a toothed margin that resembles a toothbrush, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Salvadora surinamensis is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
