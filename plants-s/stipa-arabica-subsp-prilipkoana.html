Stipa arabica subsp. prilipkoana

Stipa arabica subsp.

prilipkoana, commonly known as the Arabian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low nutrient soils.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and its potential for use in restoration projects.
