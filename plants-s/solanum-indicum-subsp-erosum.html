Solanum indicum subsp. erosum

Solanum indicum subsp.

erosum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic compounds such as solanine.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity necessitates caution in use.
