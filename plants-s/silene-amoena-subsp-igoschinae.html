Silene amoena subsp. igoschinae

Silene amoena subsp.

igoschinae, commonly known as the Igoschina sweetwilliam, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
