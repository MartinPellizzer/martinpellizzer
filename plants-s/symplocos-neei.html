Symplocos neei

Symplocos neei, commonly known as the Himalayan wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. Symplocos neei thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
