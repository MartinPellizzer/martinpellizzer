Sebastiania serrulata var. genuina

Sebastiania serrulata var. genuina, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
