Soldanella × richteri

Soldanella × richteri, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Soldanella alpina and Soldanella lanata, characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, rounded leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in its native habitat in Europe.
