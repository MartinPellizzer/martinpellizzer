Solanum bullatorugosum

Solanum bullatorugosum, commonly known as the rough-hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, and it contains toxic alkaloids similar to those found in other nightshades.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
