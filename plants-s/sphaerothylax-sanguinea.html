Sphaerothylax sanguinea

Sphaerothylax sanguinea, commonly known as the blood-red cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically a deep red or pinkish hue, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant's distinctive appearance and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
