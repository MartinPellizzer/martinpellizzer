Symphyotrichum pilosum var. pringlei

Symphyotrichum pilosum var. pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
