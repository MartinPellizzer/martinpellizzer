Stevia hirsuta var. chortiana

Stevia hirsuta var. chortiana, commonly known as the Chortian stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemistry.
