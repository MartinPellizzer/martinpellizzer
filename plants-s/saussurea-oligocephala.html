Saussurea oligocephala

Saussurea oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally recognized in various folk remedies.

The plant's morphology includes a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
