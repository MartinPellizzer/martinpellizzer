Sarcostemma lindenianum

Sarcostemma lindenianum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five lobes, followed by distinctive capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.

The latex it secretes is toxic and has been used in various herbal remedies.
