Senecio incanus var. italicus

Senecio incanus var. italicus, commonly known as the Italian hairpin or Italian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

This plant is generally hardy and can be cultivated in gardens with full sun exposure.
