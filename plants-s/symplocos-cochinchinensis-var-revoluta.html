Symplocos cochinchinensis var. revoluta

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. revoluta, commonly known as the glossy teak or red sanders, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
