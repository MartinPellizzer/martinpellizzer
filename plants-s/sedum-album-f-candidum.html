Sedum album f. candidum

Sedum album f. candidum, commonly known as the white stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of white flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a desirable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.

The white flowers bloom in late summer, adding a subtle elegance to the garden.
