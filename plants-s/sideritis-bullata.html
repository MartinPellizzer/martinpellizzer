Sideritis bullata

Sideritis bullata, commonly known as the bullock's heart, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis bullata is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
