Seriola apargioides

Seriola apargioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf seriola, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Seriola apargioides is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
