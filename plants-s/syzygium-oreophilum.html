Syzygium oreophilum

Syzygium oreophilum, commonly known as the lilac apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The fruit has a sweet and sour flavor, making it a popular ingredient in traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
