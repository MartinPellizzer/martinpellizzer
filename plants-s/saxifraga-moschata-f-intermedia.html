Saxifraga moschata f. intermedia

Saxifraga moschata f. intermedia, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which range from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The fragrance of the flowers is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
