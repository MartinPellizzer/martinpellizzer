Solanum meridionale

Solanum meridionale, commonly known as the southern nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits due to their appearance.

Solanum meridionale is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, and it thrives in warm, arid environments.
