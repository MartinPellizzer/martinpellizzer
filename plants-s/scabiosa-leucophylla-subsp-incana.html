Scabiosa leucophylla subsp. incana

Scabiosa leucophylla subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies incana is distinguished by its more densely hairy leaves compared to other varieties of Scabiosa leucophylla.
