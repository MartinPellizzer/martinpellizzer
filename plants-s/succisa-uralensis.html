Succisa uralensis

Succisa uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh, arid environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer to reduce water loss.

Succisa uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
