Specklinia emarginata

Specklinia emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its roots while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with a unique shape that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Specklinia emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
