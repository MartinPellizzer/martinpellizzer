Suriana

Suriana, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a fine, white coating that helps reduce water loss.

Suriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife in desert ecosystems.
