Syngonanthus niveus var. strigosus

Syngonanthus niveus var. strigosus, commonly known as the silverleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fibrous texture, and its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
