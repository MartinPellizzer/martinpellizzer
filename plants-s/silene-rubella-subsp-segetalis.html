Silene rubella subsp. segetalis

Silene rubella subsp.

segetalis, commonly known as the field campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
