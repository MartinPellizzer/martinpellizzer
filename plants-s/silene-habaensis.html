Silene habaensis

Silene habaensis, commonly known as the Haba Mountains catchfly, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Silene habaensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
