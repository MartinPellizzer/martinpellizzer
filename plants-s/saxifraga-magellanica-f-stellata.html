Saxifraga magellanica f. stellata

Saxifraga magellanica f. stellata, commonly known as the star saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
