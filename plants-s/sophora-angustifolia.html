Sophora angustifolia

Sophora angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
