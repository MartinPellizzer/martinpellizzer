Sclerolobium aureum var. polyphyllum

Sclerolobium aureum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the golden wattle variant, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
