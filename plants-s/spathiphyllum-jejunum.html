Spathiphyllum jejunum

Spathiphyllum jejunum, commonly known as the slender peace lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, spatulate flowers that are enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, fertile soil.

Spathiphyllum jejunum is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
