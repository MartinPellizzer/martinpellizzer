Scutellaria persica

Scutellaria persica, commonly known as Persian skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. Scutellaria persica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within its native habitat.
