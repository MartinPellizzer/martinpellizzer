Sasa borealis

Sasa borealis, commonly known as the northern bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It forms dense clumps with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts by indigenous communities. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments such as mountainous regions and boreal forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Sasa borealis is valued for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it an important component of ecological succession in cold climates.
