Spiraea hypericifolia subsp. obovata

Spiraea hypericifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are elliptical, with a pointed tip and a smooth, glossy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and showy floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
