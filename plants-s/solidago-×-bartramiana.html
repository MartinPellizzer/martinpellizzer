Solidago × bartramiana

Solidago × bartramiana, commonly known as the Bartram's goldenrod, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Solidago × bartramiana plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the fall season.
