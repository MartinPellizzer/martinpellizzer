Stipa pennata subsp. slovaca

Stipa pennata subsp.

slovaca, commonly known as the Slovak feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often adorned with silky hairs.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
