Solanum gonyrhachis

Solanum gonyrhachis, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
