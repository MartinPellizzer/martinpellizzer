Schisandra hypoglauca

Schisandra hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Schisandra hypoglauca is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than its more famous relative, Schisandra chinensis.
