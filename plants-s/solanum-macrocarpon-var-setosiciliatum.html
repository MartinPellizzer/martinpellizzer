Solanum macrocarpon var. setosiciliatum

Solanum macrocarpon var. setosiciliatum, commonly known as the Sicilian wild tomato, is a variant of the wild tomato species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "setosiciliatum.". The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are typically less fleshy compared to cultivated tomatoes. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic similarities with other nightshade plants.
