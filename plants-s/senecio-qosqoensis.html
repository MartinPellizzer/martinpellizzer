Senecio qosqoensis

Senecio qosqoensis, commonly known as the Qosqo groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and diverse ecological adaptations. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid climates.

Senecio qosqoensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
