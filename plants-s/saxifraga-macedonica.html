Saxifraga macedonica

Saxifraga macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga macedonica is often found in mountainous regions of Greece, Albania, and North Macedonia.
