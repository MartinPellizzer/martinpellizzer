Symphyllophyton gradatum

Symphyllophyton gradatum, commonly known as the graduated symphyllophyte, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate and layered botanical structure.

It features a basal stem that branches into multiple symphyllophytic lobes, which are specialized for photosynthesis and nutrient absorption. The leaves are modified into scale-like structures that provide protection while allowing for efficient gas exchange. Its roots are highly developed and form a dense network that enhances water and mineral uptake.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in temperate forest understories.
