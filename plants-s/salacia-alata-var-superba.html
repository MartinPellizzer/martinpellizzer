Salacia alata var. superba

Salacia alata var. superba, commonly known as the giant devil's backbone, is a striking climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, serpentine stems and dark, glossy leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

However, due to its vigorous growth, it requires careful management to prevent it from overtaking its surroundings.
