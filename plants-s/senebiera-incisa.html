Senebiera incisa

Senebiera incisa, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forested areas.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for ornamental purposes due to their distinctive shape.
