Stipagrostis pennata var. scabriglumis

Stipagrostis pennata var. scabriglumis, commonly known as the scabrous glume grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, glabrous or scabrous leaf blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden bracts. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it useful for erosion control and as a forage plant in certain regions.
