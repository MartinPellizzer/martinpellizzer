Salix × wyomingensis

Salix × wyomingensis, commonly known as the Wyoming willow, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are elongated and have a rough texture, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Salix × wyomingensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
