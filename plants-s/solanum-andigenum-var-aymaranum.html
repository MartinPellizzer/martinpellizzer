Solanum andigenum var. aymaranum

Solanum andigenum var. aymaranum, commonly known as the Aymara pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into egg-shaped, red or orange berries when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional use in local cuisines.
