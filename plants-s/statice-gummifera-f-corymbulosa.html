Statice gummifera f. corymbulosa

Statice gummifera f. corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered gumweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often pink or red, bract-like flowers that form dense clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is succulent, with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture.

This ornamental plant is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
