Senecio saluenensis

Senecio saluenensis, commonly known as the Salween Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio saluenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
