Spathoglottis aurea

Spathoglottis aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has erect, hairy stems and produces lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.

Spathoglottis aurea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
