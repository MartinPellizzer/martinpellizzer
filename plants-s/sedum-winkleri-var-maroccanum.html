Sedum winkleri var. maroccanum

Sedum winkleri var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and withstand drought. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
