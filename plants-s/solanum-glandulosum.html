Solanum glandulosum

Solanum glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum glandulosum is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
