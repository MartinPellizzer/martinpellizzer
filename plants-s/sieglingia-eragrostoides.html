Sieglingia eragrostoides

Sieglingia eragrostoides, commonly known as the African grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Sieglingia eragrostoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
