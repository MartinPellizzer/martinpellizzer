Solanum incanum var. kavirondoense

Solanum incanum var. kavirondoense, commonly known as the Kavirondo gooseberry, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in traditional medicine.
