Saussurea caeruleoviolacea

Saussurea caeruleoviolacea, commonly known as the blue-violet saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flower heads, which are often surrounded by pale blue bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
