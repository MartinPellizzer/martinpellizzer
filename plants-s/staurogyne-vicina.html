Staurogyne vicina

Staurogyne vicina, commonly known as the small water clover, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that often form dense mats on the bottom of ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and have a slightly waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.
