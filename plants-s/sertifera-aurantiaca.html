Sertifera aurantiaca

Sertifera aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden shield plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. Sertifera aurantiaca thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
