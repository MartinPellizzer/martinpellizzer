Senecio balsamitae var. thomsoniensis

Senecio balsamitae var. thomsoniensis, commonly known as Thomson's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
