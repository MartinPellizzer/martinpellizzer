Senecio pectioides

Senecio pectioides, commonly known as the Desert Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray foliage that helps it conserve water in arid environments. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Senecio pectioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
