Salvia × rociana nothosubsp. paisarum

Salvia × rociana nothosubsp.

paisarum, commonly known as the paisa sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it is notable for its hybrid origin and unique floral morphology that distinguishes it from its parent species.
