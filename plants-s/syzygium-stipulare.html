Syzygium stipulare

Syzygium stipulare, commonly known as the stipulate syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
