Sambucus racemosa subsp. miquelii

Sambucus racemosa subsp.

miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated margins, while its stems are reddish-brown and often hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and woodlands.
