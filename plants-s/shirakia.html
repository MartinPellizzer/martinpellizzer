Shirakia

Shirakia, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It features woody stems and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Shirakia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
