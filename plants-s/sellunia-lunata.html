Sellunia lunata

Sellunia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
