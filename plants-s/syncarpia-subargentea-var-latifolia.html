Syncarpia subargentea var. latifolia

Syncarpia subargentea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved silky ash, is a species native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, woody fruits. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
