Syzygium campylocarpum

Syzygium campylocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, elongated fruits that are typically reddish when mature. Its fruit is edible, with a sour flavor, and is often used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

Syzygium campylocarpum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
