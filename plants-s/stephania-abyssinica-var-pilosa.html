Stephania abyssinica var. pilosa

Stephania abyssinica var. pilosa, commonly known as the Abyssinian stephania, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The varietal form 'pilosa' distinguishes it by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves.
