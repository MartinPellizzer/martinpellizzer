Stenocactus acroacanthus

Stenocactus acroacanthus, commonly known as the hooked stenocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and spiny, outward-curving bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
