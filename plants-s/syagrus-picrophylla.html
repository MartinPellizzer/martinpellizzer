Syagrus picrophylla

Syagrus picrophylla, commonly known as the picrophylla palm, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, edible fruits. Syagrus picrophylla thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to sandy soils near the coast.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
