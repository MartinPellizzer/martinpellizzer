Satureja balcanica

Satureja balcanica, commonly known as Balkan savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.
