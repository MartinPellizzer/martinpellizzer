Schefflera lucumoides

Schefflera lucumoides, commonly known as the giant umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant features a robust, woody stem with a dense canopy of leaflets arranged in a radial pattern. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, followed by orange-red berries.

Schefflera lucumoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
