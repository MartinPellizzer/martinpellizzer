Senecio himalayensis

Senecio himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are usually narrow and sometimes hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio himalayensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
