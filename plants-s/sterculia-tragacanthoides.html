Sterculia tragacanthoides

Sterculia tragacanthoides, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The tree is also valued for its gum, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
