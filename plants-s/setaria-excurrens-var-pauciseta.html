Setaria excurrens var. pauciseta

Setaria excurrens var. pauciseta, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and slender, erect flowering stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
