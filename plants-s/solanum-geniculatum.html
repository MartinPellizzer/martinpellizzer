Solanum geniculatum

Solanum geniculatum, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often using tendrils for support. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and typically white or pale purple, blooming in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.
