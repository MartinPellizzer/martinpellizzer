Spiraea callosa var. typica

Spiraea callosa var. typica, commonly known as the rough spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval with a rough, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
