Sanguisorba lasiocarpa

Sanguisorba lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Sanguisorba lasiocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
