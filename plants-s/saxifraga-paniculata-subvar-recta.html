Saxifraga paniculata subvar. recta

Saxifraga paniculata subvar. recta, commonly known as the upright saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Saxifraga paniculata subvar. recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
