Stachytarpheta laxiflora

Stachytarpheta laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower verbena, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Stachytarpheta laxiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
