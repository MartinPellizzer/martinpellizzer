Sterculia cuneifolia

Sterculia cuneifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
