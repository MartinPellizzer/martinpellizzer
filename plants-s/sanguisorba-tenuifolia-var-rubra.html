Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. rubra

Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. rubra, commonly known as red stemmed filaree, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that turn a striking red or purple color in the fall. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
