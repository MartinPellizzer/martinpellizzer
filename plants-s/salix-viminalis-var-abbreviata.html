Salix viminalis var. abbreviata

Salix viminalis var. abbreviata, commonly known as dwarf willow, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as streambanks and wetlands. Its twigs are flexible and often used in basket weaving.

The flowers are catkin-like, appearing in early spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to wet conditions.
