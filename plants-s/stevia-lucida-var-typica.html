Stevia lucida var. typica

Stevia lucida var. typica, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia lucida var. typica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
