Shorea ovalis

Shorea ovalis, commonly known as the yellow lauan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The plant typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers and is valued for its timber in construction and furniture-making.
