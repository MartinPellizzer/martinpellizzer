Senna artemisioides nothosubsp. petiolaris

Senna artemisioides nothosubsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems and waxy leaf surfaces.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and use in ecological restoration projects.
