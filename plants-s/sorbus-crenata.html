Sorbus crenata

Sorbus crenata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with rounded tips and a finely toothed margin.

Sorbus crenata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
