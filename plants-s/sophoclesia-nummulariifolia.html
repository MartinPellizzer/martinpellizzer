Sophoclesia nummulariifolia

Sophoclesia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
