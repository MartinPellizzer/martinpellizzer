Satureja macrantha

Satureja macrantha, commonly known as mountain savory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and thyme. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, and its aromatic, slightly minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Satureja macrantha is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
