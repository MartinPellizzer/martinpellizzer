Sapindus pinnatus

Sapindus pinnatus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
