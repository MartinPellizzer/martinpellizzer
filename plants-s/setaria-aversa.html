Setaria aversa

Setaria aversa, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Setaria aversa is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
