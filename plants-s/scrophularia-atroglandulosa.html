Scrophularia atroglandulosa

Scrophularia atroglandulosa, commonly known as black glandular scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have glandular dots. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
