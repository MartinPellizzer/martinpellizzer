Swertia congesta

Swertia congesta, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia congesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
