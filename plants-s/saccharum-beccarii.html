Saccharum beccarii

Saccharum beccarii, commonly known as the Borneo grass or beccari grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ecological importance and potential for use in bioenergy, it has attracted interest in both conservation and sustainable agriculture.
