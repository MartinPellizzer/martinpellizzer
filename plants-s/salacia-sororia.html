Salacia sororia

Salacia sororia, commonly known as the golden balm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its attractive, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Salacia sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
