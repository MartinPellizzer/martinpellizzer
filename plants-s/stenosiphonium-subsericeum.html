Stenosiphonium subsericeum

Stenosiphonium subsericeum, commonly known as the narrow-siphon grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Stenosiphonium subsericeum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
