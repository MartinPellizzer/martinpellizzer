Syzygium evenulosum

Syzygium evenulosum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet-tart fruits that are similar in appearance to small apples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Syzygium evenulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
