Senecio luembensis

Senecio luembensis, commonly known as the Lualaba groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
