Sideritis scordioides var. peyrei

Sideritis scordioides var. peyrei, commonly known as the Peyre's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent odor, similar to garlic.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
