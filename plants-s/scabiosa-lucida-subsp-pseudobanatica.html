Scabiosa lucida subsp. pseudobanatica

Scabiosa lucida subsp.

pseudobanatica, commonly known as the white scabious, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a distinctive, spherical shape.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
