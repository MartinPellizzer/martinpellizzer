Sinningia canescens

Sinningia canescens, commonly known as the silver leaf gloxinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
