Symplocos nitens var. bahiensis

Symplocos nitens var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahian sweet pepper or Brazilian sweet pepper, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.
