Sargentodoxa

Sargentodoxa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a faint, sweet fragrance. Sargentodoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and as a decorative element in shaded areas.
