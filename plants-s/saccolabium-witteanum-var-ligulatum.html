Saccolabium witteanum var. ligulatum

Saccolabium witteanum var. ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattled legume, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the branches. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers, which are usually pale yellow or cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
