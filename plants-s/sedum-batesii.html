Sedum batesii

Sedum batesii, commonly known as the Bates' stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum batesii is also valued for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
