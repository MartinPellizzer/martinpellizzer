Solanum chaucha

Solanum chaucha, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum chaucha is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments.
