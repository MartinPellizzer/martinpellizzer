Solanum indecorum

Solanum indecorum, commonly known as the bitter nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its purple or violet flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
