Senecio filipes

Senecio filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
