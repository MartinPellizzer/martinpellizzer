Salvia × adulterina

Salvia × adulterina, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a cross between Salvia splendens and Salvia leucantha.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia × adulterina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
