Samolus valerandi var. africanus

Samolus valerandi var. africanus, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
