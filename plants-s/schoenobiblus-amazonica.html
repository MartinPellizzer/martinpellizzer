Schoenobiblus amazonica

Schoenobiblus amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian grass or the Amazonian sedge, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize wetland environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of riparian and swamp ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural resilience.

Schoenobiblus amazonica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
