Sinapis cheiranthos subsp. montana

Sinapis cheiranthos subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are usually lanceolate or oblanceolate in shape. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological importance in high-elevation ecosystems.
