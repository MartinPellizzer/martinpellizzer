Syngonanthus eriopus var. appressa

Syngonanthus eriopus var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The variety appressa is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Syngonanthus eriopus.
