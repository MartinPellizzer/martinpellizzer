Saprosma dichotoma

Saprosma dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched saprosma, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a woody stem that often branches at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ornamental purposes and ecological restoration projects.
