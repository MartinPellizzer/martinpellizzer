Salix rigida var. watsonii

Salix rigida var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's willow, is a variant of the common willow found in specific regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
