Syzygium pyrrophloeum

Syzygium pyrrophloeum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium pyrrophloeum is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
