Salix rashuwensis

Salix rashuwensis, commonly known as the Rashuwan willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
