Symphyotrichum bracteolatum

Symphyotrichum bracteolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum bracteolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
