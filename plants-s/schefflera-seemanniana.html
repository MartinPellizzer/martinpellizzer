Schefflera seemanniana

Schefflera seemanniana, commonly known as the Solomon Islands umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various light conditions.
