Smythea oblongifolia

Smythea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf smythea, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Smythea oblongifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for shaded garden settings.
