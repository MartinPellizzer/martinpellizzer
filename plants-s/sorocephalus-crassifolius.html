Sorocephalus crassifolius

Sorocephalus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sorocephalus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
