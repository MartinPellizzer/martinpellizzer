Senecio eriobasis

Senecio eriobasis, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Senecio eriobasis is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
