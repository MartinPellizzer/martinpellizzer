Schizonotus argenteus var. dumosus

Schizonotus argenteus var. dumosus, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

Schizonotus argenteus var. dumosus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various conditions.
