Silene involucrata subsp. tenella

Silene involucrata subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and reduced floral structures compared to other Silene species. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
