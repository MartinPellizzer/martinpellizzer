Sarothra japonica f. vulgaris

Sarothra japonica f. vulgaris, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
