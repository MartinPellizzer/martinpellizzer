Solanum dasyphyllum var. inerme

Solanum dasyphyllum var. inerme, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
