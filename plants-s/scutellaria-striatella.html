Scutellaria striatella

Scutellaria striatella, commonly known as the stripeflower, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and narrow, striated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Scutellaria striatella is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
