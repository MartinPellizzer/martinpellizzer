Salsola hissopifolia

Salsola hissopifolia, commonly known as Russian thorn or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Salsola hissopifolia is well adapted to harsh environments, with extensive root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in sandy soils and is considered a pioneer species in desert ecosystems.
