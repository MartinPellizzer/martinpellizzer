Silene burchellii var. syngei

Silene burchellii var. syngei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that transition into bright orange, inflated calyces resembling small lanterns. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
