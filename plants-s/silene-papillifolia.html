Silene papillifolia

Silene papillifolia, commonly known as the papillate catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.
