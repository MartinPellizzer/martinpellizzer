Symplocos glandulifera

Symplocos glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular evergreen or Indian hawthorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a mild, acidic taste. Symplocos glandulifera thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
