Saussurea eriolepis

Saussurea eriolepis, commonly known as the Himalayan corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Saussurea eriolepis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
