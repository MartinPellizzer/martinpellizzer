Saxifraga jamalensis

Saxifraga jamalensis, commonly known as Jamal's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga jamalensis is often found in high-altitude meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
