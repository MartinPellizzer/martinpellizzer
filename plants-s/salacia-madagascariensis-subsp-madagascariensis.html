Salacia madagascariensis subsp. madagascariensis

Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yellow wood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and yellowish bark.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
