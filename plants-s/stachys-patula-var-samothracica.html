Stachys patula var. samothracica

Stachys patula var. samothracica, commonly known as the Samothrace mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and possesses opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and compact growth habit.
