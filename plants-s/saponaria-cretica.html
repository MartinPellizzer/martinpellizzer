Saponaria cretica

Saponaria cretica, commonly known as the Cretan soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble miniature clouds, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often hairy as well.

Saponaria cretica is often grown in gardens for its attractive flowers and its historical use in soap-making due to the saponin content in its roots.
