Silphium laeve

Silphium laeve, commonly known as the smooth silphium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Silphium laeve has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its medicinal properties and was historically valued in ancient Greece and Rome.
