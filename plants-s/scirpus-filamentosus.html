Scirpus filamentosus

Scirpus filamentosus, commonly known as the filamentous bulrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Scirpus filamentosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
