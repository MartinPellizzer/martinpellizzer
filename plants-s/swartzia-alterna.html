Swartzia alterna

Swartzia alterna, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and fragrant flowers. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds.

Swartzia alterna is valued for its timber but is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
