Salpichroa origanifolia

Salpichroa origanifolia, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. Its botanical features include a square stem, hairy foliage, and showy white to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Salpichroa origanifolia is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
