Salix bebbiana var. luxurians

Salix bebbiana var. luxurians, commonly known as the Bebb willow or luxuriant willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, narrow leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian areas.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
