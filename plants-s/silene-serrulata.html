Silene serrulata

Silene serrulata, commonly known as the serrated catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Silene serrulata is an annual or biennial plant that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
