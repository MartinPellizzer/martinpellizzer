Scilla pseudoperuviana

Scilla pseudoperuviana, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are toxic and contain cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful if ingested.

Scilla pseudoperuviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
