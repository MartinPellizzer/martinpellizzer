Sisarum sisaroideum

Sisarum sisaroideum, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its indeterminate growth habit and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Its seeds are edible and can be used as a protein-rich food source.
