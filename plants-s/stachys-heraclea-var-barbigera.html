Stachys heraclea var. barbigera

Stachys heraclea var. barbigera, commonly known as hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine make it a notable plant in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
