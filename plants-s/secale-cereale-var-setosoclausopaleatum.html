Secale cereale var. setosoclausopaleatum

Secale cereale var. setosoclausopaleatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rye.

This variant is a less common cultivar of rye, characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and unique floral structures. It exhibits variations in leaf morphology compared to standard rye, with a more pronounced pubescence on both the leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and genetic research.
