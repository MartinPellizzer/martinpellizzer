Solanum torvoideum

Solanum torvoideum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple in color. Solanum torvoideum produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and can be toxic if ingested, making it a plant of caution.
