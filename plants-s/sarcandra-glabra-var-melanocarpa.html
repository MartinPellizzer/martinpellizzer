Sarcandra glabra var. melanocarpa

Sarcandra glabra var. melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem sarcandra, is a variant of the sarcandra plant native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark, almost black stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
