Serjania cardiospermoides f. leptothyrsa

Serjania cardiospermoides f. leptothyrsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and distinctive yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are adapted for insect pollination, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
