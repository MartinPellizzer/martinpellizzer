Senecio crassiflorus var. tricuspis

Senecio crassiflorus var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, oblanceolate leaves with a distinctive three-lobed margin, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This perennial species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
