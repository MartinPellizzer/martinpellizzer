Symphyotrichum falcatum

Symphyotrichum falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive whitish or silver-gray underside.

Symphyotrichum falcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
