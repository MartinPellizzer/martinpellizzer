Senecio campylodes

Senecio campylodes, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
