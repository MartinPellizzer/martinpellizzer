Scorzonera humilis subsp. parviflora

Scorzonera humilis subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Scorzonera humilis subsp.

parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
