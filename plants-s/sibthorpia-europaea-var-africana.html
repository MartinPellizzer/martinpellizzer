Sibthorpia europaea var. africana

Sibthorpia europaea var. africana, commonly known as the African rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, and the flowers bloom in spring or early summer.

This species is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
