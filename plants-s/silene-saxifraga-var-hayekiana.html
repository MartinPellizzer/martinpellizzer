Silene saxifraga var. hayekiana

Silene saxifraga var. hayekiana, commonly known as the Hayek's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
