Sarcomphalus microdictyus

Sarcomphalus microdictyus, commonly known as the small-leaved sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in three rows, a typical feature of sedges. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-angled berries as its fruit.

Sarcomphalus microdictyus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
