Scabiosa macrochaete

Scabiosa macrochaete, commonly known as the tall scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower meadows.

Scabiosa macrochaete is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
