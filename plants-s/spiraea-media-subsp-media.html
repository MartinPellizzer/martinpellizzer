Spiraea media subsp. media

Spiraea media subsp.

media, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its arching, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a dense, rounded form. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The species is well-suited for use in gardens and landscapes due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
