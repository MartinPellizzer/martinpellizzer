Spiradiclis rubescens

Spiradiclis rubescens, commonly known as the red spiradiclis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Spiradiclis rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
