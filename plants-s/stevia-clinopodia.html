Stevia clinopodia

Stevia clinopodia, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is well-known for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Stevia clinopodia is widely used as a natural sweetener and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.
