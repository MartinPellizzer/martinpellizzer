Senecio alleizettei subsp. pleianthus

Senecio alleizettei subsp.

pleianthus, commonly known as the Namib Desert groundsel, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Namibia. It features dense, cushion-like growth with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in small heads. It is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This subspecies is a rare and endemic species, highlighting the unique botanical diversity of the Namib Desert ecosystem.
