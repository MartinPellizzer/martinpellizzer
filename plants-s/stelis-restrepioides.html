Stelis restrepioides

Stelis restrepioides, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Stelis restrepioides thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to bloom successfully.
