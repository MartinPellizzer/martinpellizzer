Succisa pinnatifida

Succisa pinnatifida, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Succisa pinnatifida is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
