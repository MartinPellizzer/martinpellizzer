Stevia anisostemma var. typica

Stevia anisostemma var. typica, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

This variety is often cultivated for its sweet taste and use in herbal teas and natural sweetening applications.
