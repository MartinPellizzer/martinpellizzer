Scorzonera kurdica

Scorzonera kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is tuberous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera kurdica thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
