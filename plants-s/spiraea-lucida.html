Spiraea lucida

Spiraea lucida, commonly known as shining spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy, waxy appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
