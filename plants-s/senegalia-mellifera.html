Senegalia mellifera

Senegalia mellifera, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and turn a striking red or orange color during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Senegalia mellifera also plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
