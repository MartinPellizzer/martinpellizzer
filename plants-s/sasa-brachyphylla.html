Sasa brachyphylla

Sasa brachyphylla, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, upright culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dense, evergreen foliage. Sasa brachyphylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ability to form dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded gardens and as a ground cover.
