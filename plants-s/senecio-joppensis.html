Senecio joppensis

Senecio joppensis, commonly known as the Joppa groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio joppensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
