Sterculia fanaiho

Sterculia fanaiho, commonly known as the Maluku nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense crown, making it a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soils.
