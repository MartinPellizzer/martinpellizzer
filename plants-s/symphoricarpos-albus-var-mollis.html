Symphoricarpos albus var. mollis

Symphoricarpos albus var. mollis, commonly known as snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, spherical berries that persist through the winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
