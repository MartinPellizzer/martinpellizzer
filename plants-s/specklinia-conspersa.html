Specklinia conspersa

Specklinia conspersa, commonly known as the speckled fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are covered in tiny, scattered spores. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical forests. Its leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture, and they possess a distinctive speckled appearance due to the arrangement of its sori.

Specklinia conspersa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to indoor cultivation.
