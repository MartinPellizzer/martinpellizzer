Salacia melitocarpa

Salacia melitocarpa, commonly known as the Maltese date tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, date-like fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible produce.
