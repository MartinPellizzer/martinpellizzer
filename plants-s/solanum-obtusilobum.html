Solanum obtusilobum

Solanum obtusilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
