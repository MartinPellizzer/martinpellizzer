Sida picta

Sida picta, commonly known as the painted sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, and its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs. Sida picta is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its unique appearance and resilience.
