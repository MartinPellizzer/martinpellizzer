Sedum telephium f. verticillatum

Sedum telephium f. verticillatum, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent with fleshy, basal leaves arranged in a whorled pattern.

It features bright green foliage with striking white or yellow margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
