Salix miyabeana f. gilgiana

Salix miyabeana f. gilgiana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are elongated with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in landscaping.
