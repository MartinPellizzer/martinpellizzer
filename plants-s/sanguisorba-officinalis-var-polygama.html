Sanguisorba officinalis var. polygama

Sanguisorba officinalis var. polygama, commonly known as red sanders, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
