Selaginella pseudostauntoniana

Selaginella pseudostauntoniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, and it produces sporangia in specialized structures called strobila. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
