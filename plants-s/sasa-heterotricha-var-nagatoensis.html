Sasa heterotricha var. nagatoensis

Sasa heterotricha var. nagatoensis, commonly known as the Nagato bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense tufts. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a smooth texture with a bluish-green hue. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
