Statice graeca var. hyssopifolia

Statice graeca var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as sea lavender or Greek statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and dry meadows. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
