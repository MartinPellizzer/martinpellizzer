Senecio kirilowii

Senecio kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio kirilowii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
