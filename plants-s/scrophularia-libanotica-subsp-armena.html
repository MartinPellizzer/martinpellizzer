Scrophularia libanotica subsp. armena

Scrophularia libanotica subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of the Middle East. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls, with tubular corollas that are pink to purple in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
