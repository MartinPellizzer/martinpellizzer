Selaginella erythrospora

Selaginella erythrospora, commonly known as the red-spored clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include sporophylls that bear red spores, giving it its common name.

Selaginella erythrospora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
