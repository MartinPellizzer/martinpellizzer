Saussurea nomurae

Saussurea nomurae, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea nomurae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
