Syngonanthus schlechteri subsp. appendiculatus

Syngonanthus schlechteri subsp.

appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy with a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
