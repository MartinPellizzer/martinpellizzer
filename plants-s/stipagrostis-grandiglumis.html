Stipagrostis grandiglumis

Stipagrostis grandiglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh environments.

Stipagrostis grandiglumis is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
