Sorbus latifolia f. acutiloba

Sorbus latifolia f. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
