Serratula media

Serratula media, commonly known as the common serrated centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Serratula media is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
