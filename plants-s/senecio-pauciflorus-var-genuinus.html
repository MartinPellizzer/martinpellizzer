Senecio pauciflorus var. genuinus

Senecio pauciflorus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for studying xerophytic adaptations in the Compositae family.
