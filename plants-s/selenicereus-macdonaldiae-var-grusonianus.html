Selenicereus macdonaldiae var. grusonianus

Selenicereus macdonaldiae var. grusonianus, commonly known as the Night Blooming Cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a climbing cactus, often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking nighttime blooms.
