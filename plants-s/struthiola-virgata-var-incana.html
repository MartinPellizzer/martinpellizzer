Struthiola virgata var. incana

Struthiola virgata var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf struthiola, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and Mediterranean-style gardens.
