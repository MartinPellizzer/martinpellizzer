Straussia hillebrandii

Straussia hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, and it is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to habitat loss, Straussia hillebrandii is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
