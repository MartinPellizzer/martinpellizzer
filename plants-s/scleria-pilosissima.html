Scleria pilosissima

Scleria pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy leaves that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scleria pilosissima typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
