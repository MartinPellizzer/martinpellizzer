Styrax grandifolius var. grandidentatus

Styrax grandifolius var. grandidentatus, commonly known as the broad-toothed frutex styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with prominent, broad teeth along the margins. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple, woody berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in woodland gardens.
