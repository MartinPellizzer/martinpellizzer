Sorbus praetorminalis

Sorbus praetorminalis, commonly known as the alpine whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Sorbus praetorminalis is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
