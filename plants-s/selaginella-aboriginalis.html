Selaginella aboriginalis

Selaginella aboriginalis, commonly known as the Aboriginal clubmoss, is a low-growing fern-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves that form a dense mat. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or under dense vegetation. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for small organisms.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is also valued in horticulture and traditional practices.
