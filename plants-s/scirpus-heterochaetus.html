Scirpus heterochaetus

Scirpus heterochaetus, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has triangular stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus heterochaetus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
