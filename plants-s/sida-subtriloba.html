Sida subtriloba

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its structure includes a slender stem with opposite branching and small, hairy leaves.
