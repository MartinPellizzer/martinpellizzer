Silene myongcheonensis

Silene myongcheonensis, commonly known as the Myongcheong catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene myongcheonensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
