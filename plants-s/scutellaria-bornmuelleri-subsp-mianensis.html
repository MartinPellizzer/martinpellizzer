Scutellaria bornmuelleri subsp. mianensis

Scutellaria bornmuelleri subsp.

mianensis, commonly known as the Mian Mountains skullcap, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
