Saxifraga chinensis

Saxifraga chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
