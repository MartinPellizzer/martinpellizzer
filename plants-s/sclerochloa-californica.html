Sclerochloa californica

Sclerochloa californica, commonly known as California needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its wiry, grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sclerochloa californica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
