Spiraea hypericifolia var. acutifolia

Spiraea hypericifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and have a pointed tip, giving it its "acutifolia" designation. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable plant for garden borders or woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
