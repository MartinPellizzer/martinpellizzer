Sedum subtile subsp. chinense

Sedum subtile subsp.

chinense, commonly known as the Chinese stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to China. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water, allowing the plant to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
