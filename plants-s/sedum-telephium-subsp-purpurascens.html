Sedum telephium subsp. purpurascens

Sedum telephium subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the Blushing Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to blue-gray in color.

The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden and landscape designs.
