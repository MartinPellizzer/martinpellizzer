Sophora tomentosa var. angustifoliolata

Sophora tomentosa var. angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white pubescent stems and compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for pollinators.
