Strychnos pansa

Strychnos pansa, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are characteristic of the genus Strychnos. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are small and often covered in a sticky substance.
