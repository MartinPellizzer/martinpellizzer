Schoenus filum

Schoenus filum, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense stands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in marshy areas.

Schoenus filum is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
