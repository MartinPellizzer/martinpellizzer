Stachys sieboldii var. glabrescens

Stachys sieboldii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed betony, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
