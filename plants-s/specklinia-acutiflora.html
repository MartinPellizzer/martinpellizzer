Specklinia acutiflora

Specklinia acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flowered pigmy orchid, is a compact, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically clustered closely together, and it produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care.

Specklinia acutiflora is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the year under optimal conditions.
