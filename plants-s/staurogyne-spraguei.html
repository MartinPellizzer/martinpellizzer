Staurogyne spraguei

Staurogyne spraguei, commonly known as the Florida waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Staurogyne spraguei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories.
