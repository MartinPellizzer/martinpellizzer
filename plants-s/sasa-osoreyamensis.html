Sasa osoreyamensis

Sasa osoreyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Sasa osoreyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
