Stachys odoratissima

Stachys odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet cicely or sweet cicely herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a sweet, anise-like scent when crushed.

Stachys odoratissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
