Saraca crassifolia

Saraca crassifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca crassifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
