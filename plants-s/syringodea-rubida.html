Syringodea rubida

Syringodea rubida, commonly known as the red-flowered syringodea, is a striking member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the bright floral display.

Syringodea rubida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.
