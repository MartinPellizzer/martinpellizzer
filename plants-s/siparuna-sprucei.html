Siparuna sprucei

Siparuna sprucei, commonly known as the purple-leafed siparuna, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves that turn a vibrant purple during the flowering season. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Siparuna sprucei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
