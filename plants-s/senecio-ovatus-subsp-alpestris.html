Senecio ovatus subsp. alpestris

Senecio ovatus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
