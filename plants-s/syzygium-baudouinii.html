Syzygium baudouinii

Syzygium baudouinii, commonly known as the Baudouin's apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Syzygium baudouinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
