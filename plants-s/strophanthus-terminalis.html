Strophanthus terminalis

Strophanthus terminalis, commonly known as the African Crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin.

Strophanthus terminalis is also notable for its medicinal properties, as it contains cardiac glycosides used in traditional medicine.
