Salix paraplesia f. lanceolata

Salix paraplesia f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, with separate male and female flowers. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or grayish hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
