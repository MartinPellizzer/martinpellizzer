Senecio pterocarpus

Senecio pterocarpus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for tool handles and other small crafts.
