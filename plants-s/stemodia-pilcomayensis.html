Stemodia pilcomayensis

Stemodia pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo stemodia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. The plant has woody stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of yellow or green, and bloom during the warmer months.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
