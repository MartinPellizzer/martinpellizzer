Spergularia macrotheca var. scariosa

Spergularia macrotheca var. scariosa, commonly known as the scaly sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in a waxy, scaly coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The varietal name "scariosa" refers to the distinctive scaly appearance of its leaves.
