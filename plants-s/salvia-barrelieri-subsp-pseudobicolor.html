Salvia barrelieri subsp. pseudobicolor

Salvia barrelieri subsp.

pseudobicolor, commonly known as the pink sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to pink floral bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
