Swertia anomala

Swertia anomala, commonly known as Indian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Swertia anomala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
