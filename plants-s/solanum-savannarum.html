Solanum savannarum

Solanum savannarum, commonly known as the savanna nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum savannarum is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants.
