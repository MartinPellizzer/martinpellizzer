Solidago lindheimeriana

Solidago lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
