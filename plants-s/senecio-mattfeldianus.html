Senecio mattfeldianus

Senecio mattfeldianus, commonly known as the Mattfeld's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
