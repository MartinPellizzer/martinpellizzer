Sheffieldia incana

Sheffieldia incana, commonly known as the stiff hair grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features stiff, erect stems covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually grayish-green in color.

Sheffieldia incana produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by cylindrical seed heads that are dispersed by wind.
