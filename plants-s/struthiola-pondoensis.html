Struthiola pondoensis

Struthiola pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo sandthorn, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Struthiola pondoensis plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
