Selago hyssopifolia subsp. hyssopifolia

Selago hyssopifolia subsp.

hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf selago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, tubular flowers.

The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
