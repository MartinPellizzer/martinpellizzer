Sabicea capitellata

Sabicea capitellata, commonly known as the clustered sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, flat pods as fruit.

Sabicea capitellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
