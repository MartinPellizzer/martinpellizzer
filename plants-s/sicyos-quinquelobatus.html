Sicyos quinquelobatus

Sicyos quinquelobatus, commonly known as the five-lobed gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vigorous, trailing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces large, lobed fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Sicyos quinquelobatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
