Syzygium kurzii

Syzygium kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's apple or Kurz's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe with a sweet and sour flavor, often used in local cuisines. Syzygium kurzii thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native regions.
