Sisymbrium tanacetifolium var. suffruticosum

Sisymbrium tanacetifolium var. suffruticosum, commonly known as sweet rocket or winter cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with a low, mat-like structure, often forming dense colonies. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, with a peppery aroma when crushed. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
