Solidago mollis var. angustata

Solidago mollis var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its narrow, linear leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
