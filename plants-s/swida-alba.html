Swida alba

Swida alba, commonly known as white swida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Swida alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
