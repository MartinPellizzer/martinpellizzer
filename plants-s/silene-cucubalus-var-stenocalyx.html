Silene cucubalus var. stenocalyx

Silene cucubalus var. stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow calyx evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a robust root system and fibrous roots, which help it survive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
