Syzygium warburgii

Syzygium warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's apple or the Philippine apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium warburgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
