Sinapis napus

Sinapis napus, commonly known as oilseed rape, is an annual flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and large, triangular leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds used for oil extraction. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its economic value.

Sinapis napus is also known for its role in agricultural crop rotation and as a source of biofuel.
