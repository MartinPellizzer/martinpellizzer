Solidago microcephala

Solidago microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, rounded flower heads are usually yellow, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Solidago microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
