Sageretia thea var. tomentosa

Sageretia thea var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly sageretia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, textured surface.

The wooly sageretia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
