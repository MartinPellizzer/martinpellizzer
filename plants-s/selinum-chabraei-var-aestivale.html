Selinum chabraei var. aestivale

Selinum chabraei var. aestivale, commonly known as summer parsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild, peppery flavor, which adds a unique touch to dishes.
