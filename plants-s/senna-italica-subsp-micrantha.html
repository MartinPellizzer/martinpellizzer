Senna italica subsp. micrantha

Senna italica subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered senna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flower buds and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are dispersed by wind or animals.
