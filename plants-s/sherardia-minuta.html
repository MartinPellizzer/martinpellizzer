Sherardia minuta

Sherardia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf Indian rose, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and features delicate, fragrant flowers that are pink to white in color. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Sherardia minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
