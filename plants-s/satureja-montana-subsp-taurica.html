Satureja montana subsp. taurica

Satureja montana subsp.

taurica, commonly known as Crimean savory, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
