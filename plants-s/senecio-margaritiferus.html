Senecio margaritiferus

Senecio margaritiferus, commonly known as the blue marguerite or blue-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy composite flowers. Senecio margaritiferus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
