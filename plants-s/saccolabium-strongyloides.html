Saccolabium strongyloides

Saccolabium strongyloides, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it often grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its adaptability to poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
