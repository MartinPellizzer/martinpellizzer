Selinum carvifolia subsp. broteroi

Selinum carvifolia subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It features aromatic, slender leaves with a distinctive serrated edge and a strong, slightly bitter scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to its parent species.
