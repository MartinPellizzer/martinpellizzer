Spiranthes faucisanguinea

Spiranthes faucisanguinea, commonly known as the redroot spirea, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils along the edges of ponds and bogs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist environments and its unique floral structure.
