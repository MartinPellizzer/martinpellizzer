Salicornia virginica

Salicornia virginica, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is a succulent shrub that grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other brackish habitats. The plant's stems are fleshy and cylindrical, often tinged with red or pink, and they store water to aid in survival. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Salicornia virginica plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
