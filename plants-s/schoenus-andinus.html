Schoenus andinus

Schoenus andinus, commonly known as the Andean bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has tall, slender stems that often form dense stands, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Schoenus andinus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
