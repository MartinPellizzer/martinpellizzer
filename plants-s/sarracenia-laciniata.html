Sarracenia laciniata

Sarracenia laciniata, commonly known as the slitted pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, tubular pitchers that are often red or orange with a distinctive white or yellow rim. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized traps that secrete digestive enzymes to break down insect prey. Its habitat typically includes wet, acidic bogs and marshes where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor conditions.

Sarracenia laciniata is valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in controlling insect populations.
