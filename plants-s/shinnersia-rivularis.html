Shinnersia rivularis

Shinnersia rivularis, commonly known as the river saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland margins. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Shinnersia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
