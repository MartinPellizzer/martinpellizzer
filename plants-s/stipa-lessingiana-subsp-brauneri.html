Stipa lessingiana subsp. brauneri

Stipa lessingiana subsp.

brauneri, commonly known as the Braun's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, often calcareous habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually erect and compact.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in marginal habitats.
