Sonchus gomeraensis

Sonchus gomeraensis, commonly known as the Gomera sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It features a basal rosette of leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
