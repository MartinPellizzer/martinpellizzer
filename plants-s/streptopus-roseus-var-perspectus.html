Streptopus roseus var. perspectus

Streptopus roseus var. perspectus, commonly known as the glassy starwort, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble glass.

Native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its unique leaf structure allows it to retain moisture efficiently, adapting to its high-altitude habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or botanical collections.
