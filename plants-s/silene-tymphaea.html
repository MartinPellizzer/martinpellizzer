Silene tymphaea

Silene tymphaea, commonly known as the Lake Silene, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near lakes and ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene tymphaea plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
