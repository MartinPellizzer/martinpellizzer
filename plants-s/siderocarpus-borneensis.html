Siderocarpus borneensis

Siderocarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and typically white or greenish.

The fruit is a hard, woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
