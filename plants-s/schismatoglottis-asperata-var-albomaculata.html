Schismatoglottis asperata var. albomaculata

Schismatoglottis asperata var. albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted schismatoglottis, is a rare and intriguing member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features broad, leathery leaves with a distinctive white or pale green mottling pattern on the upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and by experienced growers.
