Setaria caudata f. ramosa

Setaria caudata f. ramosa, commonly known as hairy bristlegrass, is a variant of the grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of hairy structures along its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of bristly, spikelet-bearing branches.

The botanical features of this grass make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
