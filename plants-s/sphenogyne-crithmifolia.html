Sphenogyne crithmifolia

Sphenogyne crithmifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, showing a preference for full sun.

Sphenogyne crithmifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
