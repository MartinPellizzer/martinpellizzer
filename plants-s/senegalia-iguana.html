Senegalia iguana

Senegalia iguana, commonly known as the iguana tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in dry regions.
