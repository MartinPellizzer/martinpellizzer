Shorea zeylanica

Shorea zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with a leathery texture. Its wood is highly valued for its durability, strength, and resistance to decay, making it a significant species in tropical forestry.

Shorea zeylanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
