Sauropus ochrophyllus

Sauropus ochrophyllus, commonly known as the golden-leaf sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast with its dark green stems. The plant has small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
