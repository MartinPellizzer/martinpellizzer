Streptanthus campestris var. jacobaeus

Streptanthus campestris var. jacobaeus, commonly known as Jacob's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, blooming in late spring to early summer.
