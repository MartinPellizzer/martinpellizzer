Senecio gilgii

Senecio gilgii, commonly known as the Gilgii groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio gilgii is often used in traditional medicine, though it may contain toxic compounds.
