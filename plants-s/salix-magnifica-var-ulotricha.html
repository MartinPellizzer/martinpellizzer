Salix magnifica var. ulotricha

Salix magnifica var. ulotricha, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a variant of the magnifica willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
