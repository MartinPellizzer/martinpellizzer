Stachys maweana

Stachys maweana, commonly known as the African mint or redstem deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. Stachys maweana is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
