Sparganium axilare

Sparganium axilare, commonly known as the axillary bur-reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats in North America and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, spiky flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Sparganium axilare thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
