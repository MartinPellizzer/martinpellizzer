Setaria woodii var. bechuanica

Setaria woodii var. bechuanica, commonly known as the Bechuanan lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
