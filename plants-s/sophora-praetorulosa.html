Sophora praetorulosa

Sophora praetorulosa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, flexible stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
