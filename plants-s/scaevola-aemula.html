Scaevola aemula

Scaevola aemula, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scaevola aemula is well adapted to salt spray and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats.
