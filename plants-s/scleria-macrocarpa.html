Scleria macrocarpa

Scleria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating.

Scleria macrocarpa is often found in wetlands and along the edges of rivers, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
