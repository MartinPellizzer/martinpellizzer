Stellera elliptica

Stellera elliptica, commonly known as the white sandroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly habitats. The plant features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stellera elliptica is characterized by its fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
