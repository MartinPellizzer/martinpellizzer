Streptopus roseus f. indivisus

Streptopus roseus f. indivisus, commonly known as the pink star moss, is a unique variant of the Streptopus genus.

This plant is characterized by its striking pink or purple inflorescences, which are often mistaken for flowers but are actually clusters of modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish new colonies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after specimen in horticulture and ecological studies.
