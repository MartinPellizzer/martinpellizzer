Sanguisorba officinalis subsp. longifolia

Sanguisorba officinalis subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as longleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge along the edges. This subspecies is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
