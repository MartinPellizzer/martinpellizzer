Sidotheca emarginata

Sidotheca emarginata, commonly known as the marginal sidewalk plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Sidotheca emarginata is also valued for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
