Spiraea betulifolia var. ochroleuca

Spiraea betulifolia var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the whiteleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a pale green to white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in both landscape and container settings.
