Sasa oshidensis f. flavovariegata

Sasa oshidensis f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a unique variant of the Sasa oshidensis species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and white variegated leaves, which set it apart from typical bamboo species. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.
