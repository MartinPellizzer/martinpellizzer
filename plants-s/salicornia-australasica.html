Salicornia australasica

Salicornia australasica, commonly known as Australian sea beans, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It thrives in saline environments, such as estuaries and salt marshes, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellow and appear in clusters.

Salicornia australasica is also valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional and modern culinary applications.
