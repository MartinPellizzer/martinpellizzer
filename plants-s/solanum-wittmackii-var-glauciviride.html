Solanum wittmackii var. glauciviride

Solanum wittmackii var. glauciviride, commonly known as the glaucoviride nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to yellow or red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various climates.
