Sarcopodium verruciferum var. pauciflorum

Sarcopodium verruciferum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the rare bladder fern, is a unique variant of the bladder fern genus.

It is characterized by its small, clustered fronds that resemble bladders, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

This variant is notable for its limited flowering, with only a few flowers produced per inflorescence, making it a rare and sought-after species in botanical collections.
