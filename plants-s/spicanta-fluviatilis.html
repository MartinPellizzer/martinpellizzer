Spicanta fluviatilis

Spicanta fluviatilis, commonly known as the river thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to moist riverine environments.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize riverbanks and prevent erosion. It thrives in full sun and is often found along the edges of streams and lakes.

Spicanta fluviatilis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in riparian zones.
