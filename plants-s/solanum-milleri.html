Solanum milleri

Solanum milleri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the winter months. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that ripen to a bright red color.

Solanum milleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
