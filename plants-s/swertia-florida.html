Swertia florida

Swertia florida, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Swertia florida is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
