Spatholobus auritus

Spatholobus auritus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in soil stabilization and as a food source for native wildlife.
