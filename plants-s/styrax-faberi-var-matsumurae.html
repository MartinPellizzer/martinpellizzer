Styrax faberi var. matsumurae

Styrax faberi var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
