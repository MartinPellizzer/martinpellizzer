Salvia alexeenkoi

Salvia alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.

Salvia alexeenkoi is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
