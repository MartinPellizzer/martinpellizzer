Spiradiclis repens

Spiradiclis repens, commonly known as the creeping spiradiclis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Spiradiclis repens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
