Senecio albanensis

Senecio albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
