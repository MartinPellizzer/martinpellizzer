Stereospermum bracteosum

Stereospermum bracteosum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its showy, pink to red flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
