Sapota achras var. lobata

Sapota achras var. lobata, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It produces a large, sweet, pale yellow fruit with a smooth, leathery skin. The fruit contains a creamy, custard-like pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
