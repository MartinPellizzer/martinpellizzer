Senecio auritifolius

Senecio auritifolius, commonly known as the ear-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that often have ear-like projections at the base. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio auritifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
