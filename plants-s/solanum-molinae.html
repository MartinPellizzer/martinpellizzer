Solanum molinae

Solanum molinae, commonly known as the Molina's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, and it bears red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Solanum molinae belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
