Saussurea kai-montana f. minor

Saussurea kai-montana f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf saffron plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads that resemble saffron threads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
