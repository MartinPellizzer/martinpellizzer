Syringa kamibayashii

Syringa kamibayashii, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Syringa kamibayashii is known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
