Solanum plebeium var. brachysepalum

Solanum plebeium var. brachysepalum, commonly known as the short-sepal nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with small, green leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

However, like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
