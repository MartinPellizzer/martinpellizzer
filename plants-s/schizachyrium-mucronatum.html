Schizachyrium mucronatum

Schizachyrium mucronatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and distinctive silver-gray inflorescence.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant has a clump-forming growth habit and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, while the seed heads are slender and often drooping.

Schizachyrium mucronatum plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining grassland ecosystems.
