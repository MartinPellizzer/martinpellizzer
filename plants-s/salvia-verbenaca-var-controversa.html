Salvia verbenaca var. controversa

Salvia verbenaca var. controversa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
