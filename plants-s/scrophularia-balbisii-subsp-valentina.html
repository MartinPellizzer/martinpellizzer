Scrophularia balbisii subsp. valentina

Scrophularia balbisii subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentin's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The subspecies valentina is distinguished by its specific floral morphology and geographic distribution.
