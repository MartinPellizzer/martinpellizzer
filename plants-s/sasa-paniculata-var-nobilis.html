Sasa paniculata var. nobilis

Sasa paniculata var. nobilis, commonly known as the noble bamboo, is a deciduous grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design and as a privacy screen.
