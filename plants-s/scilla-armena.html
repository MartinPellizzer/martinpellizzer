Scilla armena

Scilla armena, commonly known as Armenian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white berries that ripen in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla armena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
