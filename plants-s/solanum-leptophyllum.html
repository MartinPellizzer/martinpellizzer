Solanum leptophyllum

Solanum leptophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Solanum leptophyllum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil.
