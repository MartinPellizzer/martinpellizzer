Salacca conferta

Salacca conferta, commonly known as the sugar cane palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clusters of edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid environment to flourish.
