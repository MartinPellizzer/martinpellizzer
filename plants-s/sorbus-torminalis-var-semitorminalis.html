Sorbus torminalis var. semitorminalis

Sorbus torminalis var. semitorminalis, commonly known as the semi-torminalis whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with a rough texture and a serrated margin. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
