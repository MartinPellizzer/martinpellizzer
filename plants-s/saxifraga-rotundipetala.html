Saxifraga rotundipetala

Saxifraga rotundipetala, commonly known as the rounded-petalled saxifrage, is a herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga rotundipetala is often found in mountain meadows and is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.
