Sorbus aucuparia integerrima

Sorbus aucuparia integerrima, commonly known as the white-tailed mountain ash, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
