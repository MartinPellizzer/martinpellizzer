Scleranthus moenchiiformis

Scleranthus moenchiiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

It is adapted to thrive in arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.
