Senecio ochroleucus

Senecio ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, flat clusters, and it has hairy, often blue-green leaves.

Senecio ochroleucus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
