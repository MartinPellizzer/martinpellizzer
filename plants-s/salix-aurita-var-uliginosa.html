Salix aurita var. uliginosa

Salix aurita var. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog willow, is a variant of the willow species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is frequently found in bogs, marshes, and along stream edges. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, typically measuring up to 5 cm in length.

This variant is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
