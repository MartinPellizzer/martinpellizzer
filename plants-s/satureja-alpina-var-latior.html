Satureja alpina var. latior

Satureja alpina var. latior, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, slightly spicy flavor.

The variety latior is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Satureja alpina.
