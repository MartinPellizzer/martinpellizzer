Saxifraga adscendens subsp. oregonensis

Saxifraga adscendens subsp.

oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The species typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, demonstrating a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette-like arrangement, and the plant is adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is often found in subalpine and alpine zones, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
