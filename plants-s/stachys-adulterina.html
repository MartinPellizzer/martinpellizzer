Stachys adulterina

Stachys adulterina, commonly known as false betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys adulterina is often found in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
