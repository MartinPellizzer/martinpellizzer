Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp. fenas

Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp.

fenas, commonly known as tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for native wildlife.
