Sanguisorba muricata subsp. mushensis

Sanguisorba muricata subsp.

mushensis, commonly known as the Korean teasel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiky bracts that give it a unique appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
