Spiraea ussuriensis

Spiraea ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streamside habitats.

Spiraea ussuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
