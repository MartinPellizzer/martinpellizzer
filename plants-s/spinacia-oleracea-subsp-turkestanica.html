Spinacia oleracea subsp. turkestanica

Spinacia oleracea subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Central Asian spinach, is a cultivated variant of the common spinach. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The leaves are typically large, lobed, and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. This subspecies is well adapted to temperate climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

It is particularly valued in Central Asian countries for its resilience and culinary use.
