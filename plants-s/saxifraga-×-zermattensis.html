Saxifraga × zermattensis

Saxifraga × zermattensis, commonly known as the Zermatt saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly the Swiss Alps. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in late summer.

This species is adapted to harsh mountain environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
