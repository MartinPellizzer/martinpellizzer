Salix nigrolepis

Salix nigrolepis, commonly known as black-leaf willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage and slender, flexible branches. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Salix nigrolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
