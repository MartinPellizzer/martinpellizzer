Stoechas dentata

Stoechas dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Stoechas dentata is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
