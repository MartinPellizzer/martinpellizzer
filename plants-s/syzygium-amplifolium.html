Syzygium amplifolium

Syzygium amplifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
