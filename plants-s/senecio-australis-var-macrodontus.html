Senecio australis var. macrodontus

Senecio australis var. macrodontus, commonly known as the giant African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its robust, upright growth habit. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
