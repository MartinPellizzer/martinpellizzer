Saraca triandra

Saraca triandra, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vivid display. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its bark is smooth and light-colored. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
