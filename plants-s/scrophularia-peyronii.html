Scrophularia peyronii

Scrophularia peyronii, commonly known as the Peyron's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to pink flowers. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the Scrophulariaceae family.

Scrophularia peyronii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
