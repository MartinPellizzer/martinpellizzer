Salix gracilistyla

Salix gracilistyla, commonly known as the slender-styled willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
