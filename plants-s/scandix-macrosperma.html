Scandix macrosperma

Scandix macrosperma, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a large herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems covered in purple spots and white hairs, which can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. Its flowers are arranged in a complex, branched inflorescence and are white with yellow anthers.

Scandix macrosperma is known for its toxic sap, which can cause severe skin irritation and photodermatitis when exposed to sunlight.
