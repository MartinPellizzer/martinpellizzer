Styphelia gnaphalioides

Styphelia gnaphalioides, commonly known as the bog heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wetlands and heathlands of southeastern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and swampy areas.

Styphelia gnaphalioides is adapted to moist environments and is an important component of heathland ecosystems.
