Sporobolus indicus f. africanoides

Sporobolus indicus f. africanoides, commonly known as African speckled grass, is a variant of the species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native habitats.
