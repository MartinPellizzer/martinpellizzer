Schotia bequaertii var. bergeri

Schotia bequaertii var. bergeri, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
