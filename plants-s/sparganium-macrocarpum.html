Sparganium macrocarpum

Sparganium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited burr rush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of spikelets, which develop into spiky, spore-bearing structures. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its distinctive, burr-like fruit capsules help in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.

Sparganium macrocarpum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
