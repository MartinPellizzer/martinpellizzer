Sorindeia macrophylla

Sorindeia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant has a robust rhizome system that anchors it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving water bodies. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night and attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its size and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native environment.
