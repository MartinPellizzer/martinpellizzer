Saussurea langpoensis

Saussurea langpoensis, commonly known as the Himalayan corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine, though further research is needed to validate its therapeutic potential.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
