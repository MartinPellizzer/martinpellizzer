Sundamomum macroglossa

Sundamomum macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated floral structures that resemble tongues, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and are highly fragrant.

Sundamomum macroglossa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
