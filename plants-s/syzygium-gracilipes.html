Syzygium gracilipes

Syzygium gracilipes, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh.
