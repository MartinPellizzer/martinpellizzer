Streblus sclerophyllus

Streblus sclerophyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in small bumps. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, black berries that are a food source for various animals. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Streblus sclerophyllus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil.
