Spiraea albiflora

Spiraea albiflora, commonly known as white meadowsweet, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval and hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Spiraea albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
