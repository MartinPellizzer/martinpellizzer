Seridia solstitialis

Seridia solstitialis, commonly known as the summer solstice grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Seridia solstitialis is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
