Senna cana var. calva

Senna cana var. calva, commonly known as the white senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
