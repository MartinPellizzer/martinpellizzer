Senecio lacustrinus

Senecio lacustrinus, commonly known as water ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as stream banks, marshes, and lake shores. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio lacustrinus is often found in riparian zones and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
