Serjania oxytoma

Serjania oxytoma, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or yellow serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjania, which includes a variety of flowering plants found in the neotropics. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds.

Serjania oxytoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
