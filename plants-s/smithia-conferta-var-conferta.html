Smithia conferta var. conferta

Smithia conferta var. conferta, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Smithia conferta var. conferta is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a hardy member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
