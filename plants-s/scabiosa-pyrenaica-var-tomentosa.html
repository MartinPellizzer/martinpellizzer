Scabiosa pyrenaica var. tomentosa

Scabiosa pyrenaica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
