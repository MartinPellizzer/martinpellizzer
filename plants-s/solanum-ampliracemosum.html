Solanum ampliracemosum

Solanum ampliracemosum, commonly known as the broad-branched nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
