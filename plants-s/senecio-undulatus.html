Senecio undulatus

Senecio undulatus, commonly known as the undulating groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often undulating or wavy in shape, giving it its common name.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
