Syngonanthus pittieri

Syngonanthus pittieri, commonly known as the Pittieri's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
