Setaria violacea

Setaria violacea, commonly known as purple panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or violet hues, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Setaria violacea is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
