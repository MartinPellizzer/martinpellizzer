Sauroglossum cranichoides

Sauroglossum cranichoides, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in a spike, with a distinctive golden beard-like structure on the sepals.

It has a complex root system adapted for nutrient uptake in poor soil conditions.
