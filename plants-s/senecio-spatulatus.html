Senecio spatulatus

Senecio spatulatus, commonly known as the spoonleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spoon-shaped leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
