Sorbus mougeotii

Sorbus mougeotii, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
