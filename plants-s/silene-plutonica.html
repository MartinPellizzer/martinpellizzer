Silene plutonica

Silene plutonica, commonly known as the Plutonic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Silene plutonica is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and adaptations to harsh climates.
