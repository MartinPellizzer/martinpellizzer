Schoberia glauca

Schoberia glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, bluish-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, mist-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Schoberia glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
