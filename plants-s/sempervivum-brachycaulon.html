Sempervivum brachycaulon

Sempervivum brachycaulon, commonly known as the short-stemmed houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold winters.
