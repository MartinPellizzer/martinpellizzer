Syngonanthus drouetii var. parviceps

Syngonanthus drouetii var. parviceps, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching only a few centimeters in height. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bright green color that adds a fresh appearance to gardens. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance in humid climates.
