Solanum persicifolium var. angustifolium

Solanum persicifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features slender, angular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, typically with a bluish-gray underside. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
