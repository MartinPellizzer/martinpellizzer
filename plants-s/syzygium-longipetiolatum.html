Syzygium longipetiolatum

Syzygium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular crop in certain regions.

Syzygium longipetiolatum is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
