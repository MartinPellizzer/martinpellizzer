Swertia cuspidata

Swertia cuspidata, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations between 2,000 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Swertia cuspidata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
