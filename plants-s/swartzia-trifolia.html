Swartzia trifolia

Swartzia trifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
