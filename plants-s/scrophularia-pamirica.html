Scrophularia pamirica

Scrophularia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Scrophularia pamirica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
