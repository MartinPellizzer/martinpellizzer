Spiraea aruncus var. americana

Spiraea aruncus var. americana, commonly known as the American meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of showy, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators.
