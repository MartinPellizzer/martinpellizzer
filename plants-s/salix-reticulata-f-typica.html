Salix reticulata f. typica

Salix reticulata f. typica, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a decorative shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang like curtains, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or brown.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
