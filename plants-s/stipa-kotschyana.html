Stipa kotschyana

Stipa kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically blooms with panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Stipa kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
