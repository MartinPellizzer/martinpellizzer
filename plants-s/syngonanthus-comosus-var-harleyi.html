Syngonanthus comosus var. harleyi

Syngonanthus comosus var. harleyi, commonly known as the Harlequin grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight roughness along the edges.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
