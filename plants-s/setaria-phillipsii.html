Setaria phillipsii

Setaria phillipsii, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive yellowish-green inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
