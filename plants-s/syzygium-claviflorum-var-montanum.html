Syzygium claviflorum var. montanum

Syzygium claviflorum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain satinwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium claviflorum var. montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
