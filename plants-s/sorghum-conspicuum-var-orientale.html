Sorghum conspicuum var. orientale

Sorghum conspicuum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental millet, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in warm, arid climates and is cultivated for its edible grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is used in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
