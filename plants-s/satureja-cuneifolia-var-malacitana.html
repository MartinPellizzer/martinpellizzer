Satureja cuneifolia var. malacitana

Satureja cuneifolia var. malacitana, commonly known as Malacitana savory, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional Mediterranean cooking. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a distinctively fragrant foliage.
