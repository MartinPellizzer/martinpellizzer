Sarothamnus arboreus var. rotundatus

Sarothamnus arboreus var. rotundatus, commonly known as the round-leaved tree saxifrage, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, rounded, often hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
