Solidago cuneifolia

Solidago cuneifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow inflorescences in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Solidago cuneifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
