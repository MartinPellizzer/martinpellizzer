Senna hilariana

Senna hilariana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna hilariana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
