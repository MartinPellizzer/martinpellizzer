Sasa senanensis var. ontakensis

Sasa senanensis var. ontakensis, commonly known as the Ontake bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa senanensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming, evergreen bamboo that typically grows in mountainous regions, particularly in the Japanese Alps. The plant has slender, cylindrical culms that are often green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a waxy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
