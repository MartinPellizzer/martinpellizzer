Sorbus taurica

Sorbus taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, persistent berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sorbus taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
