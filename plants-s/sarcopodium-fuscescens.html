Sarcopodium fuscescens

Sarcopodium fuscescens, commonly known as the black root orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, tuberous roots and its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of dense forests. Its unique root structure is not only aesthetically notable but also plays a crucial role in its survival by storing nutrients and water.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in the wild.
