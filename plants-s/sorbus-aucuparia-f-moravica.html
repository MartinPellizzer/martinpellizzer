Sorbus aucuparia f. moravica

Sorbus aucuparia f. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian rowan, is a botanical variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive red berries and small, elliptical leaves. The plant is native to Central Europe, particularly the Czech Republic and surrounding regions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
