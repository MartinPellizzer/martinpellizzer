Senecio pinnatus var. divaricoides

Senecio pinnatus var. divaricoides, commonly known as the variable milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, pinnately arranged leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
