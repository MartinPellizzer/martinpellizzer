Sinapis abyssinica

Sinapis abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. Its seeds are small and can be used as a spice or in traditional medicine.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or semi-desert regions.
