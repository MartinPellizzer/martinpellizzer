Scrophularia marilandica var. viridis

Scrophularia marilandica var. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in dense, erect spikes and are typically pink to purple in color.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance to pollinators.
