Syringa persica var. pterifolia

Syringa persica var. pterifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive foliage appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
