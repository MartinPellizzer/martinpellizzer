Senecio alloeophyllus

Senecio alloeophyllus, commonly known as the silver leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
