Sanguisorba sitchensis var. pilosa

Sanguisorba sitchensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sanguisorba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name "pilosa," meaning "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
