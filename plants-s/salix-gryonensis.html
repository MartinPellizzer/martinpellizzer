Salix gryonensis

Salix gryonensis, commonly known as the Korean willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
