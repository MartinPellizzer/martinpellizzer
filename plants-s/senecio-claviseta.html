Senecio claviseta

Senecio claviseta, commonly known as the keyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
