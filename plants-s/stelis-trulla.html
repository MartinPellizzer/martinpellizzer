Stelis trulla

Stelis trulla, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.

Stelis trulla is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
