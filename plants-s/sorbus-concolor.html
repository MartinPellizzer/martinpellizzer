Sorbus concolor

Sorbus concolor, commonly known as the smooth white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and often used in jams or pies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated edges.

Sorbus concolor thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
