Schinus chilensis

Schinus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, red berries are edible and have a spicy flavor, similar to that of the pepper tree.

Schinus chilensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
