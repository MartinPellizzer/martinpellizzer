Syndesmanthus glaucus

Syndesmanthus glaucus, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers and its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adaptable to various climates.
