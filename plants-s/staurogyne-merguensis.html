Staurogyne merguensis

Staurogyne merguensis, commonly known as the Amazonian water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It possesses broad, waxy leaves that help it float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. Its roots are often submerged, absorbing nutrients from the water.

Staurogyne merguensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
