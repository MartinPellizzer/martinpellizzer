Silene divaricata var. willkommiana

Silene divaricata var. willkommiana, commonly known as the Willkomm's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

Silene divaricata var. willkommiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
