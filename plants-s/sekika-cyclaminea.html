Sekika cyclaminea

Sekika cyclaminea, commonly known as the rock cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
