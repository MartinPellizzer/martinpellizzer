Salix incana var. viridis

Salix incana var. viridis, commonly known as green willow, is a variant of the common willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and its leaves, which are typically a bright green on both sides. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

The species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland edges.
