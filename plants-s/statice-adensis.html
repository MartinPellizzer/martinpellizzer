Statice adensis

Statice adensis, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically blue-gray in color, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Statice adensis is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
