Saussurea andersonii

Saussurea andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's saffron.

It is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and India. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. Its scientific name honors the Swiss botanist Horace-Benedict de Saussure, who contributed significantly to the study of alpine flora.

The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky, mountainous terrain.
