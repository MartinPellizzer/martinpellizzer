Saxifraga micrantha var. yunnanensis

Saxifraga micrantha var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southwest China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
