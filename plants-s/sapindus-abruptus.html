Sapindus abruptus

Sapindus abruptus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a bitter, soapy substance, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
