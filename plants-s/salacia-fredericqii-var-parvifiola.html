Salacia fredericqii var. parvifiola

Salacia fredericqii var. parvifiola, commonly known as the small-fruited salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
