Salsola samanganica

Salsola samanganica, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It often grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Salsola samanganica plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
