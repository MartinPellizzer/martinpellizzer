Sida coradinii

Sida coradinii, commonly known as the yellow sida, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Sida coradinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
