Sondaria cranganoorensis

Sondaria cranganoorensis, commonly known as the Cranganoorensis orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that come in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
