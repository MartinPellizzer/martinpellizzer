Swanalloia aurantiaca

Swanalloia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and often attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Swanalloia aurantiaca prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
