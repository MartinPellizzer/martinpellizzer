Specularia holzingeri

Specularia holzingeri, commonly known as the Holzinger's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh climates.

Specularia holzingeri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
