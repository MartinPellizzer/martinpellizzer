Scabiosa lyrophylla

Scabiosa lyrophylla, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Scabiosa lyrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
