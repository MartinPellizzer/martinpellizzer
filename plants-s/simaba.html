Simaba

Simaba, commonly known as the "wild coffee" or "brazil nut tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds rich in oils. Simaba is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
