Stellaria virgata

Stellaria virgata, commonly known as the common starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, white flowers with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are usually opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy. Stellaria virgata is often found in moist, grassy areas and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
