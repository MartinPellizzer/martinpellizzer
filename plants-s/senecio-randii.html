Senecio randii

Senecio randii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that disperse easily.

Senecio randii is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
