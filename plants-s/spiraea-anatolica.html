Spiraea anatolica

Spiraea anatolica, commonly known as Turkish spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Spiraea anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
