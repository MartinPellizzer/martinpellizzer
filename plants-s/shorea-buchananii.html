Shorea buchananii

Shorea buchananii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
