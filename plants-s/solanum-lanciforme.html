Solanum lanciforme

Solanum lanciforme, commonly known as the lancifoliate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
