Scrophularia libanotica var. mesogitana

Scrophularia libanotica var. mesogitana, commonly known as the Lebanon figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
