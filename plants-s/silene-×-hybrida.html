Silene × hybrida

Silene × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Silene latifolia and Silene × australis, exhibiting a mix of traits from both parent species. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its showy, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
