Schoepfia acuminata

Schoepfia acuminata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woolly, silver-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to conserve water and withstand extreme temperatures.
