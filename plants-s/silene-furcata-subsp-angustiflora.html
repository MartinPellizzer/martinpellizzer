Silene furcata subsp. angustiflora

Silene furcata subsp.

angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often succulent stems.
