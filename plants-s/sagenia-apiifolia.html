Sagenia apiifolia

Sagenia apiifolia, commonly known as the burr sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed heads that resemble burrs. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.
