Solanum bridgesii

Solanum bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgess nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
