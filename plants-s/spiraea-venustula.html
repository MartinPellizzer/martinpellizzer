Spiraea venustula

Spiraea venustula, commonly known as the rose shrub or meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Spiraea venustula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
