Senecio trianae

Senecio trianae, commonly known as the Trianas's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent leaves that help it conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio trianae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
