Stevia mercedensis

Stevia mercedensis, commonly known as the Merced stevia, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. Stevia mercedensis thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
