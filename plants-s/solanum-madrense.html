Solanum madrense

Solanum madrense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds in their raw form.

Solanum madrense is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
