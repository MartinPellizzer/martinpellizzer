Senna truncata

Senna truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, twisted pods that split open to release the seeds.

Senna truncata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
