Schoenoxiphium leucocephalum

Schoenoxiphium leucocephalum, commonly known as white-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native flora.
