Semiarundinaria viridis

Semiarundinaria viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain ecosystems.
