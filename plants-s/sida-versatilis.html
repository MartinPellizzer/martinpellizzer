Sida versatilis

Sida versatilis, commonly known as the versatile mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, triangular stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and coastal areas, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sida versatilis is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
