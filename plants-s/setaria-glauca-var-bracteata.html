Setaria glauca var. bracteata

Setaria glauca var. bracteata, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its drought tolerance, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both a weedy species and a potential candidate for forage or erosion control.
