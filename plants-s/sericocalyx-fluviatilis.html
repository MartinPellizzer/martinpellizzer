Sericocalyx fluviatilis

Sericocalyx fluviatilis, commonly known as the river calyx plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, herbaceous stems that often grow near water bodies. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Sericocalyx fluviatilis is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil.
