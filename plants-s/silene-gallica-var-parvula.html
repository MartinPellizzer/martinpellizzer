Silene gallica var. parvula

Silene gallica var. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Silene gallica var. parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
