Satureja hortensis var. grandiflora

Satureja hortensis var. grandiflora, commonly known as sweet savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a pungent, slightly peppery scent. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
