Sedum parvum subsp. robertsianum

Sedum parvum subsp.

robertsianum, commonly known as the Roberts' stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
