Swertia perennis var. cuspidata

Swertia perennis var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
