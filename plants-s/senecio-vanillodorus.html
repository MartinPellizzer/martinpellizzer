Senecio vanillodorus

Senecio vanillodorus, commonly known as the vanilla-scented groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive vanilla-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio vanillodorus is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.
