Solidago livida

Solidago livida, commonly known as the bluegoldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Solidago livida is a valuable plant for pollinators and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
