Sorbus aria var. obtusata

Sorbus aria var. obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
