Swartzia argentea

Swartzia argentea, commonly known as silverwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Swartzia argentea prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
