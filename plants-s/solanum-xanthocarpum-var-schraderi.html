Solanum xanthocarpum var. schraderi

Solanum xanthocarpum var. schraderi, commonly known as the Schrader's yellow fruit nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are typically small and clustered. It produces small, white flowers with five petals, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
