Schisandra grandiflora var. rubriflora

Schisandra grandiflora var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color, containing a single seed. Schisandra grandiflora var. rubriflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
