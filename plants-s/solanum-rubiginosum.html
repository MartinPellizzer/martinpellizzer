Solanum rubiginosum

Solanum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, red, tomato-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white pubescence.

Solanum rubiginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
