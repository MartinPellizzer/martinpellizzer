Scirpus meyeri

Scirpus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially emergent. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpus meyeri thrives in shallow water and is often found in regions with high moisture levels.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
