Solanum paucispinum

Solanum paucispinum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

The species is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
