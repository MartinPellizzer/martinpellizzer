Silene macroclada

Silene macroclada, commonly known as the giant catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
