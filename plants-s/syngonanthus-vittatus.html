Syngonanthus vittatus

Syngonanthus vittatus, commonly known as the banded ironwood, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark that is often covered in a waxy coating. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

Syngonanthus vittatus is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental appearance.
