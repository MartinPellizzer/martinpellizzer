Swida monbeigii var. crassa

Swida monbeigii var. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved swida, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
