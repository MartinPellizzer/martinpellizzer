Solanum glaucum-fruticosum

Solanum glaucum-fruticosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, grey-green foliage and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant bears yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
