Sisymbrium assoanum

Sisymbrium assoanum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Sisymbrium assoanum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
