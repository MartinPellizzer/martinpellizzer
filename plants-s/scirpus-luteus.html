Scirpus luteus

Scirpus luteus, commonly known as yellow bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellowish to brownish flowers, which are typically found at the top of the stem. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist or aquatic environments.

Scirpus luteus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
