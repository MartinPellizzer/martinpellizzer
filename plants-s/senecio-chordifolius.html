Senecio chordifolius

Senecio chordifolius, commonly known as the wooly Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
