Stipa × manrakica

Stipa × manrakica, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Stipa capillata and Stipa krylovii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems and fine, bluish-green leaves. Its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets.

Stipa × manrakica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
