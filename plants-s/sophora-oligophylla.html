Sophora oligophylla

Sophora oligophylla, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its fragrant, cascading clusters of purple or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a distinctive silvery underside.

Sophora oligophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
