Solidago concinna

Solidago concinna, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Solidago concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
