Suberanthus pungens

Suberanthus pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pungent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Suberanthus pungens plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert regions due to its extensive root system.
