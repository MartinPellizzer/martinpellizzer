Silene lutea

Silene lutea, commonly known as the yellow campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Silene lutea has simple, opposite leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
