Senecio strictus

Senecio strictus, commonly known as the rigid senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Senecio strictus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
