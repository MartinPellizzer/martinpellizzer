Stellaria postii

Stellaria postii, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Stellaria postii is known for its ability to spread via stolons, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
