Sarcocaulon patersonii

Sarcocaulon patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's rock heath, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Sarcocaulon patersonii is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
