Scirpus × oblongus

Scirpus × oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a hybrid reed grass native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color, and it blooms in the summer months. Scirpus × oblongus is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
