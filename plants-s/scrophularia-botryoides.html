Scrophularia botryoides

Scrophularia botryoides, commonly known as the clustered figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological habitats.
