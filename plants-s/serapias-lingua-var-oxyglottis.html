Serapias lingua var. oxyglottis

Serapias lingua var. oxyglottis, commonly known as the "tongue orchid," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, tongue-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
