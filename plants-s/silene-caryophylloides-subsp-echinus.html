Silene caryophylloides subsp. echinus

Silene caryophylloides subsp.

echinus, commonly known as the hairy pincushion campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, adapted to reduce water loss.

The subspecies echinus is distinguished by its distinctive, spiny bracts that resemble a pincushion.
