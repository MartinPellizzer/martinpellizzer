Sedum ciliocaule

Sedum ciliocaule, commonly known as the threadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum ciliocaule is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
