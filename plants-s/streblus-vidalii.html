Streblus vidalii

Streblus vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's strangler fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and unique ability to climb by enveloping host trees. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are important for wildlife and local communities. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Streblus vidalii plays a significant role in forest ecosystems and is sometimes considered a invasive species in certain regions.
