Salix × macrior

Salix × macrior, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers before the leaves fully develop. Salix × macrior thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
