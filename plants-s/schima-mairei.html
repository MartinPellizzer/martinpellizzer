Schima mairei

Schima mairei, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
