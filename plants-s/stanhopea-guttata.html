Stanhopea guttata

Stanhopea guttata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a greenish-yellow color and distinctive red or orange spots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their strong, sweet fragrance.

Stanhopea guttata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
