Stipa × brevicallosa var. hissarensis

Stipa × brevicallosa var. hissarensis, commonly known as the Hissarensis needlegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that often appear gray-green due to a dense covering of hairs. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-bearing branches, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and with minimal water availability.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies and potential for use in restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
