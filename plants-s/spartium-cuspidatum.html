Spartium cuspidatum

Spartium cuspidatum, commonly known as needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Spartium cuspidatum is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various insects and birds.
