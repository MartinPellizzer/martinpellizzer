Syzygium koghianum

Syzygium koghianum, commonly known as the Koghian apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit is valued for its sweet and tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
