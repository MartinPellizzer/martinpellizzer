Solanum cevallos-tovari

Solanum cevallos-tovari, commonly known as the Cevallos-Tovar nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, specifically found in Ecuador and Peru, where it grows in humid, lowland tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
