Symplocos concolor

Symplocos concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental value.
