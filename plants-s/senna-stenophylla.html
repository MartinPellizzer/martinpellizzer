Senna stenophylla

Senna stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods and are used in traditional medicine for their laxative properties.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
