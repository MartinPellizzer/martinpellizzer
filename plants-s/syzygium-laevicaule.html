Syzygium laevicaule

Syzygium laevicaule, commonly known as the smooth-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind but have a distinct flavor. Syzygium laevicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
