Scirpus hoppei

Scirpus hoppei, commonly known as the Hoppe's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Scirpus hoppei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
