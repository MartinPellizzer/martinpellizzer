Solanum ovoideum

Solanum ovoideum, commonly known as the oval tomato or the Mexican eggplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, and it often grows in dry, rocky soils.

Solanum ovoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
