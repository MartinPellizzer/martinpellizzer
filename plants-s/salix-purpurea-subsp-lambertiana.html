Salix purpurea subsp. lambertiana

Salix purpurea subsp.

lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streams and in wet meadows.

Lambert's willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
