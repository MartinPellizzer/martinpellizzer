Solenanthus pindicus

Solenanthus pindicus, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.
