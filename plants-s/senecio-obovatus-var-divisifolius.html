Senecio obovatus var. divisifolius

Senecio obovatus var. divisifolius, commonly known as the African Daisy or African Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
