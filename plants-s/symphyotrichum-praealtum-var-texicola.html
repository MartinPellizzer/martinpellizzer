Symphyotrichum praealtum var. texicola

Symphyotrichum praealtum var. texicola, commonly known as the Texas ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of purple to pinkish-red composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a strong, aromatic odor.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
