Stachys duriaei var. ochroleuca

Stachys duriaei var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-lavender stachys, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale lavender to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
