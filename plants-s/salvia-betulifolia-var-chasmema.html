Salvia betulifolia var. chasmema

Salvia betulifolia var. chasmema, commonly known as the chasmema sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a minty fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
