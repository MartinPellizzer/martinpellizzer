Sunania virginiana

Sunania virginiana, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and brown in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible acorns that mature in the fall. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Sunania virginiana is valued for its strong wood and its role in forest ecosystems.
