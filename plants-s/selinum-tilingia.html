Selinum tilingia

Selinum tilingia, commonly known as the common fennel, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in umbels. It is often cultivated for its licorice-scented seeds and as a culinary herb.

Its botanical features include a taproot, which is edible and used in various dishes.
