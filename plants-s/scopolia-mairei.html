Scopolia mairei

Scopolia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red in color.

Scopolia mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and unique floral structure.
