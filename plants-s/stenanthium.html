Stenanthium

Stenanthium, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which often have fringed petals and are clustered in racemes. The plants are typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Stenanthium species are native to North America and have a cosmopolitan distribution in the temperate regions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
