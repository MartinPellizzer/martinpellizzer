Salix × podolica

Salix × podolica, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix viminalis and Salix schwerinii, resulting in a plant with unique morphological characteristics. The species is notable for its rapid growth rate and flexible branches, which make it suitable for various horticultural and ecological applications. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix × podolica is often used in riparian buffer zones and erosion control due to its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
