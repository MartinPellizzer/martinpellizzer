Saxifraga fragosa var. leucandra

Saxifraga fragosa var. leucandra, commonly known as the white-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often white or pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments.
