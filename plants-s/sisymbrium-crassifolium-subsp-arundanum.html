Sisymbrium crassifolium subsp. arundanum

Sisymbrium crassifolium subsp.

arundanum, commonly known as the broad-leaved shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its pinnately divided leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive triangular seed pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
