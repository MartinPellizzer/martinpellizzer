Scirpus maritimus var. ovatus

Scirpus maritimus var. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its wide, flat, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in brackish or saline water and is often found in marshes, estuaries, and along shorelines.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
