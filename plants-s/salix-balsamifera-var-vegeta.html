Salix balsamifera var. vegeta

Salix balsamifera var. vegeta, commonly known as the slender willow, is a variant of the balsam poplar native to northern regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, flexible branches and delicate, elongated leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and it typically flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a pale or yellowish hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
