Sebastiania diamantinensis

Sebastiania diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond Mountains sebastiania, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental potential.
