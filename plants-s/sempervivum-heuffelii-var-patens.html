Sempervivum heuffelii var. patens

Sempervivum heuffelii var. patens, commonly known as the spreading houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
