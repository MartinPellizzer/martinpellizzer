Sinclairia brachypus

Sinclairia brachypus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sinclairia, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
