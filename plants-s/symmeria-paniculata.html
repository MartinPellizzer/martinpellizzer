Symmeria paniculata

Symmeria paniculata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces panicles of small, clustered blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Symmeria paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
