Stachys penicillata

Stachys penicillata, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
