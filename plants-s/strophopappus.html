Strophopappus

Strophopappus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

These plants are characterized by their showy yellow flowers and are native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The leaves are typically alternate, often lance-shaped, and may have a hairy texture. Strophopappus species produce small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in their spread.

They are often found in dry, open habitats and can be used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
