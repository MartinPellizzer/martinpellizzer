Syzygium filiforme var. constrictum

Syzygium filiforme var. constrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The varietal form 'constrictum' is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical species.
