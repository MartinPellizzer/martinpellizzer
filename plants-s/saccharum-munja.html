Saccharum munja

Saccharum munja, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated leaves. The plant is known for its high sugar content in its stalks, making it a valuable resource for traditional uses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is used in both ecological restoration and as a source of natural sweeteners.
