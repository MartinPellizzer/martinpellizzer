Sophronitis crispa

Sophronitis crispa, commonly known as the crisped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Sophronitis crispa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
