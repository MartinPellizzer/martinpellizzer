Solidago virgaurea subsp. kurilensis

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of Asia, including the Kuril Islands. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The plant features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The inflorescence consists of bright yellow, composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.
