Sporobolus podotrichus

Sporobolus podotrichus, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green foliage and erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Sporobolus podotrichus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
