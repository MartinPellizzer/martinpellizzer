Stipa scabra var. elatior

Stipa scabra var. elatior, commonly known as the tall rough feathergrass, is a variant of the rough feathergrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with bristles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
