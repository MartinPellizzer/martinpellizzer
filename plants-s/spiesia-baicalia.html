Spiesia baicalia

Spiesia baicalia, commonly known as the Baikal spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Spiesia baicalia is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
