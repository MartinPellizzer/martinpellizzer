Salvia albiflora

Salvia albiflora, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and traditional herbal practices.

Salvia albiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and cultural significance among indigenous communities.
