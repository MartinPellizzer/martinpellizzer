Selaginella karowtipuensis

Selaginella karowtipuensis, commonly known as the Karowtipuens Spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. This plant exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation, through a process called poikilohydry. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are clustered at the tips of its stems and produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella karowtipuensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
