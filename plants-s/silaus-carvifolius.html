Silaus carvifolius

Silaus carvifolius, commonly known as the Siberian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the tribe Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, pale pink or white flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Silaus carvifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
