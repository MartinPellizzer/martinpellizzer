Salvia tobeyi

Salvia tobeyi, commonly known as Tobey's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Salvia tobeyi is often cultivated in xeriscapes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
