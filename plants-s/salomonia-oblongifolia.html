Salomonia oblongifolia

Salomonia oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf salomonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry climates.

It is often found in desert landscapes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
