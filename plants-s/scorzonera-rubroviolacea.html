Scorzonera rubroviolacea

Scorzonera rubroviolacea, commonly known as the red-violet scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Scorzonera rubroviolacea is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
