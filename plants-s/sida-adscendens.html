Sida adscendens

Sida adscendens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida adscendens is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability to various soil types.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
