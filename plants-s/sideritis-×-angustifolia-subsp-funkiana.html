Sideritis × angustifolia subsp. funkiana

Sideritis × angustifolia subsp.

funkiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
