Salvia albiflora var. caerulescens

Salvia albiflora var. caerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
