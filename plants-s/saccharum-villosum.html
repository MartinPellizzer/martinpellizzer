Saccharum villosum

Saccharum villosum, commonly known as hairy bamboo or wild sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and clusters of flowering spikes that produce small, white flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous stalks, which can be used for thatching or weaving. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Saccharum villosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
