Saxifraga setigera

Saxifraga setigera, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra-like habitats.

Saxifraga setigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
