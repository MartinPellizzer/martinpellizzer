Sycopsis sinensis

Sycopsis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, woolly flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. Sycopsis sinensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its rapid growth and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
