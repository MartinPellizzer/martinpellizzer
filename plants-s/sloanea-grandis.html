Sloanea grandis

Sloanea grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Sloanea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate for optimal growth.
