Sarocalamus yongdeensis

Sarocalamus yongdeensis, commonly known as the Yongde bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
