Selinum carniolicum

Selinum carniolicum, commonly known as the Carpathian wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. Selinum carniolicum is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
