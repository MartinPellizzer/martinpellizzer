Sasa kawanoyuensis var. puberula

Sasa kawanoyuensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, culm-like stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
