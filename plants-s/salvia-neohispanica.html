Salvia neohispanica

Salvia neohispanica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a minty scent.

Salvia neohispanica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
