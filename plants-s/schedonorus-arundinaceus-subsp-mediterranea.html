Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp. mediterranea

Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to temperate climates and can tolerate moderate grazing.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
