Spergularia rubra subsp. atheniensis

Spergularia rubra subsp.

atheniensis, commonly known as the Athens sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and calcareous soils.

The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environments.
