Selaginella silvestrii

Selaginella silvestrii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in whorls around the stem. Selaginella silvestrii is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its specialized water-retaining structures.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
