Salvia eichleriana

Salvia eichleriana, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage emits a strong mint-like scent when crushed, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
