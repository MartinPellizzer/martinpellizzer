Sideritis hirsuta var. minorifolia

Sideritis hirsuta var. minorifolia, commonly known as the lesser hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
