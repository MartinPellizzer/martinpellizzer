Scirpus triqueter f. macrostachys

Scirpus triqueter f. macrostachys, commonly known as the broad-stalked bulrush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its roots help in stabilizing soil and improving water quality.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
