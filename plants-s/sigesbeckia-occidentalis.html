Sigesbeckia occidentalis

Sigesbeckia occidentalis, commonly known as the western sigesbeckia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
