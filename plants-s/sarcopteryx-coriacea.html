Sarcopteryx coriacea

Sarcopteryx coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skinned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of small, fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.

The species is noted for its resilience to environmental stress, making it a hardy addition to botanical gardens.
