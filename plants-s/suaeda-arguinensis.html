Suaeda arguinensis

Suaeda arguinensis, commonly known as the Atlantic sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean, including the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, and its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is often found in salt marshes and dune habitats.

Suaeda arguinensis is an important component of the flora in arid and semi-arid coastal ecosystems.
