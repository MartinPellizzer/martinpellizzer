Saccharum narenga var. khasianum

Saccharum narenga var. khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a variant of the genus Saccharum native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a tall, clump-forming grass that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is well adapted to moist, tropical climates and is often found in wetlands and riverine areas. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Additionally, it has been used traditionally for construction and crafts in the regions where it is native.
