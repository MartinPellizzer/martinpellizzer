Solanum tuberosum var. reniforme

Solanum tuberosum var. reniforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped tubers.

This variety is native to the Andes region of South America and is valued for its unique shape and flavor. The plant is an herbaceous perennial with compound leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its tubers are typically smaller than standard potatoes but are often used in gourmet cooking.

The botanical features of this variety make it a subject of interest in both agricultural research and horticultural cultivation.
