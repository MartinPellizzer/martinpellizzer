Smilacina thyrsoidea

Smilacina thyrsoidea, commonly known as the eastern climbing fern, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and its unique, fern-like leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall. Smilacina thyrsoidea thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread and form dense colonies along tree trunks and shrubs.
