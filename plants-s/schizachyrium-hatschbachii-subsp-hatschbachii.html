Schizachyrium hatschbachii subsp. hatschbachii

Schizachyrium hatschbachii subsp.

hatschbachii, commonly known as the narrowleaf threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.

This subspecies is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
