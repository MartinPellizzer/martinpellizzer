Saussurea chitralica subsp. remotiloba

Saussurea chitralica subsp.

remotiloba, commonly known as the Chitral saffron, is a rare and high-altitude plant native to the northern regions of Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, yellowish-orange inflorescence that resembles saffron, hence its common name.

The plant thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters. It has fibrous roots and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
