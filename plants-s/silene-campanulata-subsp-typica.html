Silene campanulata subsp. typica

Silene campanulata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the bellflower catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Silene campanulata subsp.

typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
