Sphaeromorphaea russeliana var. glabrata

Sphaeromorphaea russeliana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Russell's sphaeromorphaea, is a variant of the genus Sphaeromorphaea native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical forests. Its foliage is usually dark green and densely packed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and adaptability to certain garden conditions.
