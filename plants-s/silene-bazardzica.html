Silene bazardzica

Silene bazardzica, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Silene bazardzica has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native region.
