Syndyophyllum

Syndyophyllum, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Syndyophyllum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
