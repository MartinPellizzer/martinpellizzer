Schizocodon soldanelloides var. magnus

Schizocodon soldanelloides var. magnus, commonly known as the giant marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
