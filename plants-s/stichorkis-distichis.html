Stichorkis distichis

Stichorkis distichis, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves arranged in two vertical rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique growth form and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation.
