Sonchus × decipiens

Sonchus × decipiens, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble those of true dandelions. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. It is an annual or biennial herb that spreads through seeds dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
