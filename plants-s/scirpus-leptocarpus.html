Scirpus leptocarpus

Scirpus leptocarpus, commonly known as slender-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus leptocarpus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
