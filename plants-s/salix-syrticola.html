Salix syrticola

Salix syrticola, commonly known as the Syrticola willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, often hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it is well adapted to hot, dry climates. Salix syrticola is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and preventing erosion.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitat, where it supports local wildlife and contributes to the biodiversity of arid ecosystems.
