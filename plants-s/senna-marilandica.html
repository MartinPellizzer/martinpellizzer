Senna marilandica

Senna marilandica, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Senna marilandica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
