Sideritis heraclea

Sideritis heraclea, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, particularly for its antispasmodic and digestive benefits.
