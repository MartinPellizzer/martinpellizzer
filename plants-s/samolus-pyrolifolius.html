Samolus pyrolifolius

Samolus pyrolifolius, commonly known as the lesser tamarisk, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

Samolus pyrolifolius is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
