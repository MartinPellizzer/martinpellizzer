Salsola elata

Salsola elata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in suitable environments.

Salsola elata is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water requirements.
