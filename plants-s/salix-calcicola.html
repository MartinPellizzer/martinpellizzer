Salix calcicola

Salix calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is adapted to grow in saline and alkaline soils, which are typically challenging for most plant species. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Salix calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
