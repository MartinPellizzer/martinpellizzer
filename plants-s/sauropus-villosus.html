Sauropus villosus

Sauropus villosus, commonly known as the hairy sauropus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both sides. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries.

Sauropus villosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
