Satureja pamphylica subsp. piperelloides

Satureja pamphylica subsp.

piperelloides, commonly known as Piperello's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, pungent scent when crushed.

It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
