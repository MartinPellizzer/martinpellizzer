Syringa japonica

Syringa japonica, commonly known as Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping.
