Sutera fraterna

Sutera fraterna, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes, and is often associated with spiritual symbolism in various cultures. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's unique ability to float on water surfaces adds to its ecological and aesthetic significance.
