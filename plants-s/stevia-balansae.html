Stevia balansae

Stevia balansae, commonly known as the Guatemalan sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its medicinal and culinary potential, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
