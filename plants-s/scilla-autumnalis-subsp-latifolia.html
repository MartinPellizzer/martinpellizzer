Scilla autumnalis subsp. latifolia

Scilla autumnalis subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved autumn scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in the autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other members of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
