Sporobolus serotinus

Sporobolus serotinus, commonly known as black grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. Black grama is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of grassland ecosystems.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
