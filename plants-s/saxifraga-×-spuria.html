Saxifraga × spuria

Saxifraga × spuria, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Saxifraga × spuria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and woodland settings.
