Serratula barrelieri

Serratula barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and stems that produce yellow flowers. The plant has a rough, hairy texture and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula barrelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
