Sidalcea spicata

Sidalcea spicata, commonly known as the spiculate soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Sidalcea spicata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
