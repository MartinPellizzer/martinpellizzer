Stauntonia cavalerieana

Stauntonia cavalerieana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, round, brown fruits that resemble small dates. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, sticky pulp and is used in traditional cuisines.

Stauntonia cavalerieana is also cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
