Securidaca lanceolata

Securidaca lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
