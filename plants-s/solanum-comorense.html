Solanum comorense

Solanum comorense, commonly known as the Comoros nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Solanum comorense belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.
