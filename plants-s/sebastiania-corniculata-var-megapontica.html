Sebastiania corniculata var. megapontica

Sebastiania corniculata var. megapontica, commonly known as the Greek starfruit, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to starfruits, though they are not related. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
