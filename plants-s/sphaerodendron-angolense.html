Sphaerodendron angolense

Sphaerodendron angolense, commonly known as the Angola hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has woody stems and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and elliptical in shape, with a rough texture.

Sphaerodendron angolense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
