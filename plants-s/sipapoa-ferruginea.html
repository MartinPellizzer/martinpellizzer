Sipapoa ferruginea

Sipapoa ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm-like structure for storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Sipapoa ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
