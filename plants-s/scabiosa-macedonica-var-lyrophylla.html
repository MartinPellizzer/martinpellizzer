Scabiosa macedonica var. lyrophylla

Scabiosa macedonica var. lyrophylla, commonly known as the Macedonian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
