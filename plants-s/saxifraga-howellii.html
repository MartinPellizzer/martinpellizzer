Saxifraga howellii

Saxifraga howellii, commonly known as Howell's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette shape and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Saxifraga howellii is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude regions.
