Senecio ullucosanus

Senecio ullucosanus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Senecio ullucosanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
