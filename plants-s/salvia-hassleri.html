Salvia hassleri

Salvia hassleri, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces striking violet to blue flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
