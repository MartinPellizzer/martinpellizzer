Sesleria autumnalis subsp. anatolica

Sesleria autumnalis subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian autumn sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses and related plants.
