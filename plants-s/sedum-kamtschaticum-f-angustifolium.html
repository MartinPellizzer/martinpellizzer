Sedum kamtschaticum f. angustifolium

Sedum kamtschaticum f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage and drought tolerance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
