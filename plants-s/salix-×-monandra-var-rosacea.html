Salix × monandra var. rosacea

Salix × monandra var. rosacea, commonly known as the weeping willow with rose-like flowers, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive, drooping branches and showy, pinkish-white inflorescences.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant's leaves are typically broad and oval, with a smooth margin and a silvery underside. Its floral display occurs in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
