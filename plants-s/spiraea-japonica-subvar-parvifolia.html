Spiraea japonica subvar. parvifolia

Spiraea japonica subvar. parvifolia, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
