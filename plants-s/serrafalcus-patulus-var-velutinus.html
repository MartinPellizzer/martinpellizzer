Serrafalcus patulus var. velutinus

Serrafalcus patulus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf serrafalcus, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native range, which includes parts of Southeast Asia.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in traditional medicine.
