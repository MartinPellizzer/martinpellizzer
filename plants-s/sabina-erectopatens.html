Sabina erectopatens

Sabina erectopatens, commonly known as the upright juniper, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental horticulture.
