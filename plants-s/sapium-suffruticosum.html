Sapium suffruticosum

Sapium suffruticosum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
