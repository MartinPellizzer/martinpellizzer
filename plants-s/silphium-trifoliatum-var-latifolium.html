Silphium trifoliatum var. latifolium

Silphium trifoliatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, trifoliate leaves that are broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a composite head resembling a compass. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as prairies and open woodlands.

Silphium trifoliatum var. latifolium is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
