Syzygium corymbosum

Syzygium corymbosum, commonly known as the common apple peach, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

Syzygium corymbosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
