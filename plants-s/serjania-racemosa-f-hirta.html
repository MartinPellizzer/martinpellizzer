Serjania racemosa f. hirta

Serjania racemosa f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy serjania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Serjania.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its woody structure and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and are adapted for insect pollination, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
