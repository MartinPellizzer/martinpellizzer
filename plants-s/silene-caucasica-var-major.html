Silene caucasica var. major

Silene caucasica var. major, commonly known as the Caucasian hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species with a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
