Sorghum saccharatum var. wightii

Sorghum saccharatum var. wightii, commonly known as Wight's sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and panicles of small, dense flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and heat resistance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of wax, reducing water loss.

This variety is often cultivated for its forage value and potential use in biofuel production.
