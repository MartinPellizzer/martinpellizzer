Sphaeropteris angustipinna

Sphaeropteris angustipinna, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate cycad, is a member of the cycad family, Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are narrow and pinnately compound. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. Its leaves are typically green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, with a distinctive, narrow pinnae.

The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones for reproduction.
