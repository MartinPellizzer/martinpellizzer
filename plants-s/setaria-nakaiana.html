Setaria nakaiana

Setaria nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese foxtail grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
