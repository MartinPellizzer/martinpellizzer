Setaria rudifolia

Setaria rudifolia, commonly known as rough fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, rough-textured leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Setaria rudifolia is a hardy plant that can tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
