Sideritis sipylea

Sideritis sipylea, commonly known as Sipylean Heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis sipylea thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
