Salix × hapala

Salix × hapala, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, cone-like fruits.

Salix × hapala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
