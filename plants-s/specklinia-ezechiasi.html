Specklinia ezechiasi

Specklinia ezechiasi, commonly known as the Ezechiasi fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically green with a slight whitish or silvery bloom. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous substrates to prevent root rot. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a dense layer of root hairs, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Specklinia ezechiasi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
