Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp. longespicata

Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp.

longespicata, commonly known as the longspike blue mistflower, is a member of the Verbenaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
