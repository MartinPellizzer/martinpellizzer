Silene nurensis var. haesarensis

Silene nurensis var. haesarensis, commonly known as the Haesarensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
