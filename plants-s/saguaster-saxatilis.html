Saguaster saxatilis

Saguaster saxatilis, commonly known as the rock saguaro, is a unique cactus species native to the rocky habitats of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 15 feet in height, often developing multiple branches over time. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are pollinated by various insects and bats. Its thick, fleshy stems store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Saguaster saxatilis plays a crucial role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for desert wildlife.
