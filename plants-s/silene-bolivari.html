Silene bolivari

Silene bolivari, commonly known as the Bolivian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene bolivari is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
