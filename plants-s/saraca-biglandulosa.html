Saraca biglandulosa

Saraca biglandulosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, glandular structures, which contribute to its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
