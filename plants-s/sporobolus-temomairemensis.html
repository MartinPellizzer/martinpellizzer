Sporobolus temomairemensis

Sporobolus temomairemensis, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that aids in its survival in harsh, dry environments.

Sporobolus temomairemensis is often found in disturbed soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
