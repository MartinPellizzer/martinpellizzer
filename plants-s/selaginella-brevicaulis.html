Selaginella brevicaulis

Selaginella brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed spike moss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, compact rosette of scale-like leaves and upright, spore-bearing cones. It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions.

Selaginella brevicaulis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
