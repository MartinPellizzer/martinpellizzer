Spergularia rubra var. marginata

Spergularia rubra var. marginata, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating high salinity and drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems.
