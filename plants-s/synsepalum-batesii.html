Synsepalum batesii

Synsepalum batesii, commonly known as the African date plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble plums, which are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The fruit is also used in traditional medicine and for making beverages.
