Sorbus gracilis var. maximowiczii

Sorbus gracilis var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the slender whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
