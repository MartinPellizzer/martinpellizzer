Salvia brevipes

Salvia brevipes, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia brevipes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
