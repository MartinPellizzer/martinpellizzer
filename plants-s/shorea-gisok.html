Shorea gisok

Shorea gisok, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
