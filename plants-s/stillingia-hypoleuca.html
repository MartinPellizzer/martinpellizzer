Stillingia hypoleuca

Stillingia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white stellaria, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is noted for its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
