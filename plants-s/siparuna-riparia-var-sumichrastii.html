Siparuna riparia var. sumichrastii

Siparuna riparia var. sumichrastii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and borne in clusters, often giving off a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
