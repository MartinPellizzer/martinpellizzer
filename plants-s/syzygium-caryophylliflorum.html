Syzygium caryophylliflorum

Syzygium caryophylliflorum, commonly known as the flame tree or candlewood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
