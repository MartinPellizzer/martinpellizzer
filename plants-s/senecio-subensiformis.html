Senecio subensiformis

Senecio subensiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
