Syzygium multiglandulosum

Syzygium multiglandulosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its rough, glandular leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible, acidic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Syzygium multiglandulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
