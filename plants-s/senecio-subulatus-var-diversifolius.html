Senecio subulatus var. diversifolius

Senecio subulatus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable cushionweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, spiny leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
