Senecio auricula subsp. sicoricus

Senecio auricula subsp.

sicoricus, commonly known as the Sicilian auricula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
