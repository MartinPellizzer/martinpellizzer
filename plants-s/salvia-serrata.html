Salvia serrata

Salvia serrata, commonly known as the serrated sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salvia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
