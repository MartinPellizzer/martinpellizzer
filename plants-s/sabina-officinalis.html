Sabina officinalis

Sabina officinalis, commonly known as the thuja or white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically bright green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green female cones and is known for its aromatic, resinous scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.

The botanical features of Sabina officinalis make it a popular choice for landscaping and traditional medicine.
