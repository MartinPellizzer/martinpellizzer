Sclerolaena muricata

Sclerolaena muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in rough, ridged bark, which gives it its scientific name. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

Sclerolaena muricata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
