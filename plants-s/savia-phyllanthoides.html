Savia phyllanthoides

Savia phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close by morning. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Savia phyllanthoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
