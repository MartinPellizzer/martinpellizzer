Spiesia lambertii var. sericea

Spiesia lambertii var. sericea, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

The plant is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
