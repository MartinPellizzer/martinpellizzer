Solanum sylvaticum

Solanum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild tomato or forest tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Solanum sylvaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some areas.
