Sonchus pinnatus var. canariensis

Sonchus pinnatus var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of a dandelion, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is an annual or biennial species, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in Mediterranean and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
