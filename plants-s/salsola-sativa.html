Salsola sativa

Salsola sativa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in disturbed soils.

Salsola sativa is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
