Sisyrinchium rectilineum

Sisyrinchium rectilineum, commonly known as the straight-stemmed blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. Sisyrinchium rectilineum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
