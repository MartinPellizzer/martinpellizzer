Serjania atrolineata f. genuina

Serjania atrolineata f. genuina, commonly known as the black-veined serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes several tropical trees and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, veins on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
