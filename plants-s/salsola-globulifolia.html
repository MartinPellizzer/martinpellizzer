Salsola globulifolia

Salsola globulifolia, commonly known as the globe saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing a compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that give it its common name, and its leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Salsola globulifolia is often found in deserts and along coastal dunes, contributing to stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
