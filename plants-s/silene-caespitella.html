Silene caespitella

Silene caespitella, commonly known as the tufted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and forms dense, low-growing clusters. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene caespitella is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
