Salix × heidenreichiana

Salix × heidenreichiana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix purpurea and Salix schwerinii, characterized by its slender, weeping branches and delicate, pendulous leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.

Salix × heidenreichiana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
