Senecio baccharidifolius var. angustifolius

Senecio baccharidifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that bear alternate, narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
