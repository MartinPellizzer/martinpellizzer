Stipa krascheninnikowii

Stipa krascheninnikowii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.

Stipa krascheninnikowii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
