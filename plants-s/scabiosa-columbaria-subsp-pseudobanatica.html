Scabiosa columbaria subsp. pseudobanatica

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

pseudobanatica, commonly known as the Balkan scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
