Saussurea albifolia

Saussurea albifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saussurea albifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
