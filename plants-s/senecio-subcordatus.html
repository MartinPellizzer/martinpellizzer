Senecio subcordatus

Senecio subcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to narrowly elliptic, with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
