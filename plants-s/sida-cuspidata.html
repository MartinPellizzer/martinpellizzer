Sida cuspidata

Sida cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Sida cuspidata is characterized by its slender, branched stems and the presence of small, sharp spines along the leaf edges.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
