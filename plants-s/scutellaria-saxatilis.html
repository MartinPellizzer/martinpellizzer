Scutellaria saxatilis

Scutellaria saxatilis, commonly known as the rock skullcup, is a perennial herb native to rocky habitats in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in crevices or on limestone.

Scutellaria saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
