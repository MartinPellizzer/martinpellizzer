Stevia chortiana

Stevia chortiana, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Stevia chortiana is often cultivated for its sweet leaves, which are used in herbal teas and as a sugar substitute.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for organic gardening.
