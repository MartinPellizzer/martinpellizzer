Scilla tysonii

Scilla tysonii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental and medicinal plants. Scilla tysonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
