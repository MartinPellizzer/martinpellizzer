Satureja calamintha subsp. subnuda

Satureja calamintha subsp.

subnuda, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, with a pungent, slightly minty flavor.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Satureja genus, which are known for their aromatic properties and adaptability to diverse environments.
