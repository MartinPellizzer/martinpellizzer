Sulitia longiflora

Sulitia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered sulitia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, showy flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sulitia longiflora is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
