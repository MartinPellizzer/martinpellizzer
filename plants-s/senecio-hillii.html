Senecio hillii

Senecio hillii, commonly known as the Hill's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, often found in desert regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio hillii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
