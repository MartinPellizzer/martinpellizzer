Solanum weberbaueri

Solanum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features dense, sparsely hairy foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum weberbaueri is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or semi-shaded habitats.
