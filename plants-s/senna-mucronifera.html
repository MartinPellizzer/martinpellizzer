Senna mucronifera

Senna mucronifera, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

Senna mucronifera is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye due to its rich chemical composition.
