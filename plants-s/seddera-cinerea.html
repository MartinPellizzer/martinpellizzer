Seddera cinerea

Seddera cinerea, commonly known as silver seddera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Seddera cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
