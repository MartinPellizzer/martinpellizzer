Sarcoca latbenia

Sarcoca latbenia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Sarcoca latbenia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
