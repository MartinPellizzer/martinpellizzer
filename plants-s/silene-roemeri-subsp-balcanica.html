Silene roemeri subsp. balcanica

Silene roemeri subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance in the region.
