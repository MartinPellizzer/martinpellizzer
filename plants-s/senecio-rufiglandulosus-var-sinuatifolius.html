Senecio rufiglandulosus var. sinuatifolius

Senecio rufiglandulosus var. sinuatifolius, commonly known as the Sinuate Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features woolly, glandular leaves that are often gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique foliage and bright yellow flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
