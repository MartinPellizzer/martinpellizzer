Sasaella atamiana var. kanayamensis

Sasaella atamiana var. kanayamensis, commonly known as the Kanayama bamboo, is a variant of the sasaella genus native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant with slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
