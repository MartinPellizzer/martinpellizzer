Sparganium friesii var. simplex

Sparganium friesii var. simplex, commonly known as the slender bur-reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense, grassy-like foliage at the base.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
