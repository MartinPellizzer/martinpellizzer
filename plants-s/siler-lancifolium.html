Siler lancifolium

Siler lancifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved siler, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Siler lancifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
