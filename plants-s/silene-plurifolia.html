Silene plurifolia

Silene plurifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its pink or white flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers bloom in clusters at the top of the stems.

Silene plurifolia is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is noted for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.
