Solanum brachylobum

Solanum brachylobum, commonly known as the California thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption by humans or livestock.
