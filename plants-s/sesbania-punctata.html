Sesbania punctata

Sesbania punctata, commonly known as the scarlet sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in some parts of Asia.
