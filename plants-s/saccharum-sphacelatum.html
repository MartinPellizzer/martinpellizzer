Saccharum sphacelatum

Saccharum sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or wild sugarcane, is a tall perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color and a fibrous, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces inflorescences that are often panicle-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are characterized by their stiff texture and prominent midribs, which are features common in the grass family Poaceae.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
