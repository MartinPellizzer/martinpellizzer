Salix chilensis

Salix chilensis, commonly known as the Chile willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in coastal areas.

Salix chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
