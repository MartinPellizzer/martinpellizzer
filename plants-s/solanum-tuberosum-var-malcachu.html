Solanum tuberosum var. malcachu

Solanum tuberosum var. malcachu, commonly known as the Malcachu potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is an herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller and have a unique flavor profile compared to standard potatoes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a hairy texture, and it flowers with small, yellowish blossoms.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to specific climatic conditions and its culinary versatility.
