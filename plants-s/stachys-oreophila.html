Stachys oreophila

Stachys oreophila, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys oreophila thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
