Syzygium mackinnonianum

Syzygium mackinnonianum, commonly known as the Khasi apple, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern region of India, particularly in the states of Meghalaya, Assam, and Manipur. The plant is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is typically found in dense forests.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are white and fragrant.
