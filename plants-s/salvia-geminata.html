Salvia geminata

Salvia geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia geminata is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
