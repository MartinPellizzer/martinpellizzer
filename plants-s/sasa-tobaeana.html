Sasa tobaeana

Sasa tobaeana, commonly known as the Toba bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Sasa tobaeana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes form a network that helps stabilize soil and supports the growth of other plant species.
