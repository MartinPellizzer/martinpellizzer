Silene pauciflora

Silene pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene pauciflora is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
