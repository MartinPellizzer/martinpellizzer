Silene getula var. macrotricha

Silene getula var. macrotricha, commonly known as the large-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woods and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
