Stroganowia rubtzovii

Stroganowia rubtzovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in tundra and taiga regions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, aiding in water retention.

Stroganowia rubtzovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
