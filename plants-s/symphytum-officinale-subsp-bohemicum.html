Symphytum officinale subsp. bohemicum

Symphytum officinale subsp.

bohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a coarse texture.

The plant produces tall spikes of violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its roots contain allantoin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine for its healing properties.
