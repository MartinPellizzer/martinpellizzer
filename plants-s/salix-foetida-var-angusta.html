Salix foetida var. angusta

Salix foetida var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
