Senecio incanus

Senecio incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

Senecio incanus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
