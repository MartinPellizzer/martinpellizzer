Solanum sarachioides

Solanum sarachioides, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a green, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum sarachioides is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
