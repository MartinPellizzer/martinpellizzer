Specklinia succulenta

Specklinia succulenta, commonly known as the rock cycad, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and palm-like leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem to survive drought conditions. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Despite its appearance, it is not a true cycad but a member of the Zamioculcas genus, which is closely related to cycads.
