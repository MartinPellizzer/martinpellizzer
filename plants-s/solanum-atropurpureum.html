Solanum atropurpureum

Solanum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple potato or purple nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by egg-shaped, purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

Solanum atropurpureum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique coloration.
