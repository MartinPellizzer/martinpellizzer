Saussurea × mirabilis

Saussurea × mirabilis, commonly known as the Chinese snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap and has fibrous roots that are often harvested for medicinal purposes. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal value, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
