Spiraea canescens var. canescens

Spiraea canescens var. canescens, commonly known as silver weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, arching branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive appearance and moderate maintenance requirements.
