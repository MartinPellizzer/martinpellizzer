Sideritis romana var. mutica

Sideritis romana var. mutica, commonly known as the rough-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its more robust and hairy foliage compared to the typical form of Sideritis romana.
