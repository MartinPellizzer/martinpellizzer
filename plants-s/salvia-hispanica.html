Salvia hispanica

Salvia hispanica, commonly known as chia, is an annual flowering plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy blue or purple flowers. Its seeds are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and protein, making them a popular health food.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often grown for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
