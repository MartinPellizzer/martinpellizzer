Salvia × rosuae

Salvia × rosuae, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to the United States.

It is a cross between Salvia gregaria and Salvia coccinea, resulting in a plant with vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy blooms throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
