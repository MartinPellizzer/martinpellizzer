Sorbus cyclophylla

Sorbus cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclocarpous whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern and eastern Europe, including parts of the Balkans and the Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive, round, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with serrated edges, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Sorbus cyclophylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or forested areas.
