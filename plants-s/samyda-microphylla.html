Samyda microphylla

Samyda microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved samyda, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Samyda microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
