Saxifraga erecta

Saxifraga erecta, commonly known as the upright saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, alpine environments. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Saxifraga erecta is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
