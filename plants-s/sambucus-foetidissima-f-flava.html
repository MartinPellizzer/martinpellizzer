Sambucus foetidissima f. flava

Sambucus foetidissima f. flava, commonly known as the yellow elderberry, is a variant of the common elderberry species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical white or pink blooms of the species. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, sunny habitats. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, dark berries in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
