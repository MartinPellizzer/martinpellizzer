Stellera dichotoma

Stellera dichotoma, commonly known as the forked fringe, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Stellera dichotoma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
