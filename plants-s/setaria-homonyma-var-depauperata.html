Setaria homonyma var. depauperata

Setaria homonyma var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread easily.

The botanical features of this variety make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
