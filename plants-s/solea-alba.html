Solea alba

Solea alba, commonly known as the white sole, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Soleidae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated shell that is typically white or pale in color. The shell is often covered with a thin layer of periostracum, which can vary in color depending on the environment. This species is found in marine environments across the Atlantic and Mediterranean coasts.

Its diet primarily consists of bivalves, and it plays a role in marine ecosystems as a predator.
