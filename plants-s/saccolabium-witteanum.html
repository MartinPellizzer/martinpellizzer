Saccolabium witteanum

Saccolabium witteanum, commonly known as the Witteanum burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding vibrant color to its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
