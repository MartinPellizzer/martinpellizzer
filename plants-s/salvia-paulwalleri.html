Salvia paulwalleri

Salvia paulwalleri, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

Salvia paulwalleri is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
