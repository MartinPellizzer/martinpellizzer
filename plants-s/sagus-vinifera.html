Sagus vinifera

Sagus vinifera, commonly known as the grapevine, is a perennial deciduous vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, usually green or red berries. The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking, table consumption, and juice production. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a complex root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

The grapevine plays a significant role in both agriculture and horticulture worldwide.
