Setaria pampeana

Setaria pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Setaria pampeana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.
