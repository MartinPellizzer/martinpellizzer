Sandersonia littonioides

Sandersonia littonioides, commonly known as the Litton's glory, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features tall, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette, supporting a showy inflorescence of large, white or pale pink flowers. The plant blooms in late summer, producing a dramatic display that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cool, moist environments, and it requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Sandersonia littonioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
