Shorea lissophylla

Shorea lissophylla, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and yellowish, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
