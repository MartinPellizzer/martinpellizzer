Sorbus arbutifolia var. atropurpurea

Sorbus arbutifolia var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white flowers that appear in clusters during early spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn a striking purple-red color in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
