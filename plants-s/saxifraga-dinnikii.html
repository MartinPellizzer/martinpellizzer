Saxifraga dinnikii

Saxifraga dinnikii, commonly known as the Dinnik's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually thick and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Saxifraga dinnikii is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
