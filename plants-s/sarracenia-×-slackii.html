Sarracenia × slackii

Sarracenia × slackii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, vibrant red or orange-colored pitchers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant's pitchers are designed to trap and digest insects, using a combination of sticky secretions and downward-pointing hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and carnivorous nature, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts and botanists alike.
