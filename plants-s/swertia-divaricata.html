Swertia divaricata

Swertia divaricata, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and moist environments at high altitudes. The plant features a slender stem with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue or violet, clustered in racemes, and bloom during the summer months.

Swertia divaricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
