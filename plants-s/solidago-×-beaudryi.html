Solidago × beaudryi

Solidago × beaudryi, commonly known as the Canadian goldenrod, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Solidago × beaudryi is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
