Satureja plicatula

Satureja plicatula, commonly known as the crested savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

Satureja plicatula is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
