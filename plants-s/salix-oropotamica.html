Salix oropotamica

Salix oropotamica, commonly known as the Orinoco willow, is a species of willow native to South America, particularly found in the Orinoco River basin.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to wetland environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salix oropotamica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
