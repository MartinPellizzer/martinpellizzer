Saxifraga crateriformis

Saxifraga crateriformis, commonly known as the crater-form saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
