Salvia leptophylla

Salvia leptophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Salvia leptophylla is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
