Stellaria graminea var. latifolia

Stellaria graminea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf starwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name "latifolia.". It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
