Solanum sembarto var. pubescens

Solanum sembarto var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, characterized by its toxic properties and diverse morphological features.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, often yellow or greenish flowers that are typically followed by berries that change color as they mature. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
