Sudamerlycaste fragans

Sudamerlycaste fragans, commonly known as the fragrant Amazonian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that emit a sweet, floral scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are adapted to thrive in high humidity and partial shade. Its roots are often symbiotic with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption from the nutrient-poor soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
