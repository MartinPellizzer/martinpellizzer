Solanum extusviolascens

Solanum extusviolascens, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a species belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
