Senecio cymatocrepis

Senecio cymatocrepis, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
