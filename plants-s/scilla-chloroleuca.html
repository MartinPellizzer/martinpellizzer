Scilla chloroleuca

Scilla chloroleuca, commonly known as the white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers in early spring. The plant's bulbs are adapted for storage and survival in dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms.

Scilla chloroleuca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
