Solanum coccineum

Solanum coccineum, commonly known as the red devil or scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum coccineum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
