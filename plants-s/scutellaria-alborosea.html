Scutellaria alborosea

Scutellaria alborosea, commonly known as white beardroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and often covered in a fine, white coating, giving it its common name.

Scutellaria alborosea is often found in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
