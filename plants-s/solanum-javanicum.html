Solanum javanicum

Solanum javanicum, commonly known as the Java nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum javanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
