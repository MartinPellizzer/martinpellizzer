Scirpus boliviensis

Scirpus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Scirpus boliviensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to wetland ecosystem dynamics.
