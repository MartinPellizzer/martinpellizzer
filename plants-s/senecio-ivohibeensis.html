Senecio ivohibeensis

Senecio ivohibeensis, commonly known as the Ivohibeensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
