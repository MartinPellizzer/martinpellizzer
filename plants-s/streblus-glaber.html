Streblus glaber

Streblus glaber, commonly known as the rough-leaved sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a habitat for various animals.
