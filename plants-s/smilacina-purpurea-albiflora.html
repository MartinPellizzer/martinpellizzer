Smilacina purpurea albiflora

Smilacina purpurea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory ecosystems.
