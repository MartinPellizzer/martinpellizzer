Syzygium uniflorum

Syzygium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered clove.

It is a flowering plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia, belonging to the Myrtaceae family. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a reddish, fleshy drupe.

Syzygium uniflorum is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other clove species.
