Senecio batallonensis

Senecio batallonensis, commonly known as the Batallon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
