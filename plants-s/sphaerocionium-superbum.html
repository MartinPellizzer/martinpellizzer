Sphaerocionium superbum

Sphaerocionium superbum, commonly known as the giant white lichen, is a unique foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery thallus with a white to pale yellow upper surface and a darker, more complex lower surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its nutrient acquisition and growth. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
