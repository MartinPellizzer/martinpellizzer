Syngonium mauroanum

Syngonium mauroanum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that vary in color from light green to deep red. The plant produces small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Syngonium mauroanum is also known for its ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
