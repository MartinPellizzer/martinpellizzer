Senecio oldhamianus

Senecio oldhamianus, commonly known as Oldham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
