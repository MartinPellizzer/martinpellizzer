Sasa spectabilis

Sasa spectabilis, commonly known as the showy sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, arching stems covered with narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by edible, bamboo-like shoots that are prized in Asian cuisine.

Sasa spectabilis is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
