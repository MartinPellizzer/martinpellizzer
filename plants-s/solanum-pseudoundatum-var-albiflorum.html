Solanum pseudoundatum var. albiflorum

Solanum pseudoundatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
