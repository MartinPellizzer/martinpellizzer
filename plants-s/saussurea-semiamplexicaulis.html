Saussurea semiamplexicaulis

Saussurea semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
