Spiranthes sulphurea

Spiranthes sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wetlands and forest floors. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is part of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
