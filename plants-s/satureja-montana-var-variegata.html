Satureja montana var. variegata

Satureja montana var. variegata, commonly known as variegated mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green and white or gold. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
