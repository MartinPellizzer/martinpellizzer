Scleria capillaris

Scleria capillaris, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Scleria capillaris plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
