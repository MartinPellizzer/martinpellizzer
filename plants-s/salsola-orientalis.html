Salsola orientalis

Salsola orientalis, commonly known as oriental goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water, and its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils.

Salsola orientalis is often found in dry grasslands, sandy soils, and along roadsides.
