Selinum gmelinii

Selinum gmelinii, commonly known as Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It is a low-growing perennial with dark green, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Selinum gmelinii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
