Salvia japonica

Salvia japonica, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia japonica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
