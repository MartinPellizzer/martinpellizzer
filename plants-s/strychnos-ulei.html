Strychnos ulei

Strychnos ulei, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, reddish fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. Strychnos ulei is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as strychnine and brucine, which are used in traditional medicine and as poisons.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
