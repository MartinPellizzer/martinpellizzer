Senecio subnemoralis

Senecio subnemoralis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
