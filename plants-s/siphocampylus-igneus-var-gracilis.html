Siphocampylus igneus var. gracilis

Siphocampylus igneus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender flameflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that helps retain moisture in its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
