Scorzonera lacera

Scorzonera lacera, commonly known as the slender scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

Scorzonera lacera thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
