Setaria salzmanniana

Setaria salzmanniana, commonly known as Salzmann's foxtail, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, it can become invasive in certain regions.
