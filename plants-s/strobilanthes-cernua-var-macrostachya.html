Strobilanthes cernua var. macrostachya

Strobilanthes cernua var. macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked flameflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, typically consisting of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
