Silene amurensis

Silene amurensis, commonly known as the Amur catchfly, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in forested areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
