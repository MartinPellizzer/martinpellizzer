Sideritis raeseri subsp. raeseri

Sideritis raeseri subsp.

raeseri, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
