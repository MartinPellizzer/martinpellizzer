Stellaris corymbosa

Stellaris corymbosa, commonly known as the starburst corymb, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals that radiate outward, creating a striking visual display.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
