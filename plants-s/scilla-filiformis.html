Scilla filiformis

Scilla filiformis, commonly known as the slender scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and horticulture for their ornamental value.

Scilla filiformis is well adapted to temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
