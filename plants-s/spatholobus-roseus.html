Spatholobus roseus

Spatholobus roseus, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are often visited by native bees and birds, contributing to local pollination networks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
