Sorbus boissieri

Sorbus boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. Sorbus boissieri typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies.
