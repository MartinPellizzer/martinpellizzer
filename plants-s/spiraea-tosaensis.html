Spiraea tosaensis

Spiraea tosaensis, commonly known as Tosa weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea tosaensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
