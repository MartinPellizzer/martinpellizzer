Sedum uniflorum subsp. rugosum

Sedum uniflorum subsp.

rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
