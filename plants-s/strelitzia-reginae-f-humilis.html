Strelitzia reginae f. humilis

Strelitzia reginae f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bird of paradise, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, banana-like leaves and showy, bird-of-paradise-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it a compact alternative to the larger Strelitzia reginae. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, feature a distinctive orange and blue coloration with a yellow throat, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in suitable growing conditions.
