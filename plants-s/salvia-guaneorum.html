Salvia guaneorum

Salvia guaneorum, commonly known as the Guanacaste sage, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia guaneorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
