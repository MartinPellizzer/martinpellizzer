Salix tetrasperma var. kusanoi

Salix tetrasperma var. kusanoi, commonly known as Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue on top and a silvery-white underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
