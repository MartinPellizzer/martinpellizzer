Sparganium californicum

Sparganium californicum, commonly known as the California bur reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, throughout California. The plant produces spadices, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and is notable for its distinctive, spiny, seed-bearing structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Sparganium californicum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
