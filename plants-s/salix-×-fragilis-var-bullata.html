Salix × fragilis var. bullata

Salix × fragilis var. bullata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive, flowing appearance. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
