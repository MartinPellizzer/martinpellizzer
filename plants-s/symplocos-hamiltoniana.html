Symplocos hamiltoniana

Symplocos hamiltoniana, commonly known as Hamilton's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the summer, attracting various bird species.

Symplocos hamiltoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils.
