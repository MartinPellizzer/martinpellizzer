Serruria squarrosa

Serruria squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose serruria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Serruria squarrosa is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
