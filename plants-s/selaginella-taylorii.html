Selaginella taylorii

Selaginella taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance with elongated, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Selaginella taylorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
