Scindapsus caudatus

Scindapsus caudatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which often display a mix of silver, white, and green hues. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, though it may lose some color during colder months.

Scindapsus caudatus is relatively low maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and moderate humidity.
