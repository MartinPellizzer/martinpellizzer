Silene agricola

Silene agricola, commonly known as the agricultural catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Silene agricola is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like leaves.
