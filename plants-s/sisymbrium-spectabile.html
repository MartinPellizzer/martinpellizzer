Sisymbrium spectabile

Sisymbrium spectabile, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture, and it grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall.

Sisymbrium spectabile is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and nectar for pollinators.
