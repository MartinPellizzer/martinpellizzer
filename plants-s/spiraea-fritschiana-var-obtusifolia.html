Spiraea fritschiana var. obtusifolia

Spiraea fritschiana var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching stems that cascade with clusters of white flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
