Shorea saigonensis

Shorea saigonensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a warm, humid climate with ample rainfall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also significant in traditional and contemporary woodworking.
