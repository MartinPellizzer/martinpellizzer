Scrophularia laevigata var. pubescens

Scrophularia laevigata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Scrophularia laevigata var. pubescens is often found in the wild but is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
