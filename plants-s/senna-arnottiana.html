Senna arnottiana

Senna arnottiana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Senna arnottiana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
