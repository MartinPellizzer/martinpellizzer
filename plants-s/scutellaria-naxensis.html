Scutellaria naxensis

Scutellaria naxensis, commonly known as the Naxos skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the island of Naxos in the Aegean Sea.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is square and upright.

Scutellaria naxensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
