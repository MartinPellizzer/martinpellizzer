Serronia

Serronia, commonly known as the serrated fern, is a member of the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery, serrated fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Serronia species are valued for their delicate appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
