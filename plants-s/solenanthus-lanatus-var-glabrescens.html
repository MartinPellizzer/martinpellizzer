Solenanthus lanatus var. glabrescens

Solenanthus lanatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy foliage compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
