Sasa mikamimonticola

Sasa mikamimonticola, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green color. Sasa mikamimonticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various forest organisms.
