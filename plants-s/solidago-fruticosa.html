Solidago fruticosa

Solidago fruticosa, commonly known as the goldenrod bush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago fruticosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early autumn.
