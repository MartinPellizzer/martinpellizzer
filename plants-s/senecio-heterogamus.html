Senecio heterogamus

Senecio heterogamus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers that resemble snowflakes, hence its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio heterogamus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
