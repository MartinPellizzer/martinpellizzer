Sorbus cashmiriana var. cashmiriana

Sorbus cashmiriana var. cashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir roseapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils at higher altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
