Sonderina didyma

Sonderina didyma, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring two different colors on the same flower, typically a combination of white and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The genus Sonderina is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
