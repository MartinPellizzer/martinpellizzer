Satureja varia subsp. hierrensis

Satureja varia subsp.

hierrensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are aromatic, with a distinctive savory or pine-like scent, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local herbal practices.
