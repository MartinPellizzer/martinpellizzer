Schefflera myriocarpa

Schefflera myriocarpa, commonly known as the giant umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Schefflera myriocarpa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
