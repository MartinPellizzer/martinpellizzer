Saxifraga burseriana crenata

Saxifraga burseriana crenata, commonly known as the crested saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are typically succulent and have a distinctive crenate margin, giving it a unique appearance.

Saxifraga burseriana crenata is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
