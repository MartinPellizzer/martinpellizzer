Senecio duciformis

Senecio duciformis, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a lion's tail. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Senecio duciformis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
