Stelis canae

Stelis canae, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Stelis canae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
