Solanum miniatum var. stenopetalum

Solanum miniatum var. stenopetalum, commonly known as the dwarf potato vine, is a compact, trailing perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
