Solanum brittonianum

Solanum brittonianum, commonly known as the Britton's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.
