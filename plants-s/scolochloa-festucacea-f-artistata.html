Scolochloa festucacea f. artistata

Scolochloa festucacea f. artistata, commonly known as the artist's grass, is a variant of the common festuca species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its compact form and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.
