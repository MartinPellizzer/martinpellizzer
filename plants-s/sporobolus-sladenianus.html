Sporobolus sladenianus

Sporobolus sladenianus, commonly known as the Sladen's sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It has adapted to thrive in sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Sporobolus sladenianus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
