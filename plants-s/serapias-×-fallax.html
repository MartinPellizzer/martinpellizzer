Serapias × fallax

Serapias × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the genus Serapias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, spurred flowers. Serapias × fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between species such as Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias grandiflora.
