Schedonorus × fleischeri

Schedonorus × fleischeri, commonly known as the fine fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in dense, low tufts and is often used in turfgrass applications due to its drought tolerance and shade adaptation. Schedonorus × fleischeri is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between different species of fescue, which contributes to its genetic diversity.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component in sustainable landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
