Salix camptostachya

Salix camptostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riparian zones, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Salix camptostachya plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
