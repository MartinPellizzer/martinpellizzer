Solanum elaeagnifolium var. grandiflorum

Solanum elaeagnifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
