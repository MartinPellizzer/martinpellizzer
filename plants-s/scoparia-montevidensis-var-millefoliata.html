Scoparia montevidensis var. millefoliata

Scoparia montevidensis var. millefoliata, commonly known as the Chilean thyme, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
