Stellarioides virens

Stellarioides virens, commonly known as green starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Stellarioides virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
