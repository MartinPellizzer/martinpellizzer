Sorbus javorkana

Sorbus javorkana, commonly known as the Java whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which attract various bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
