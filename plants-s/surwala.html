Surwala

Surwala, commonly known as Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries containing hard, angular seeds.

Surwala is often cultivated for its essential oil, which is used in various cosmetic and therapeutic applications.
