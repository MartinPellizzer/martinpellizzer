Stapelia tsomoensis

Stapelia tsomoensis, commonly known as the Tswalu Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insect species, such as flies and beetles.

Stapelia tsomoensis is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
