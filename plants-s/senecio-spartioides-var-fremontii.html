Senecio spartioides var. fremontii

Senecio spartioides var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. This perennial plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and it has a mat-forming growth habit.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water.
