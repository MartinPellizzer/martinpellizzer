Sonneratia × zhongcairongii

Sonneratia × zhongcairongii, commonly known as the Chinese mangrove hybrid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sonneratia alba and Sonneratia ovata.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in brackish water environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems. The plant has prop roots that help stabilize sediment and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions.

This hybrid is often found in mangrove forests along the coasts of China and Southeast Asia.
