Selaginella fissidentoides

Selaginella fissidentoides, commonly known as the zigzag spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often linear and have a distinctive zigzag pattern. Its sporophyte produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches.

Selaginella fissidentoides is known for its resilience and ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
