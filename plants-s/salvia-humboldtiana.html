Salvia humboldtiana

Salvia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia humboldtiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
