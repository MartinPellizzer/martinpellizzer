Strychnos venulosa

Strychnos venulosa, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in woodland and savanna habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red or orange berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Strychnos venulosa is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
