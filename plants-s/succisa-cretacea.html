Succisa cretacea

Succisa cretacea, commonly known as the Crete stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Succisa cretacea is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to withstand drought conditions.
