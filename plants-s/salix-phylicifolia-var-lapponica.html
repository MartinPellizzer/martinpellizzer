Salix phylicifolia var. lapponica

Salix phylicifolia var. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland willow, is a shrub native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It produces catkins during the spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

This variety is part of the Salicaceae family and is notable for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
