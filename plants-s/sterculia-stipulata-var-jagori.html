Sterculia stipulata var. jagori

Sterculia stipulata var. jagori, commonly known as the jagori or the golden sterculia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and alternate, ovate leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a moderate growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
