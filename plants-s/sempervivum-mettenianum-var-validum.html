Sempervivum mettenianum var. validum

Sempervivum mettenianum var. validum, commonly known as the "Himalayan Houseleek," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to form clumps over time, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
