Saxifraga clavistamineoides

Saxifraga clavistamineoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine habitats in Europe.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and have a dark green color with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers in early summer, which are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions where it tolerates cold temperatures and limited nutrients.

Saxifraga clavistamineoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
