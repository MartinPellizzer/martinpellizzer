Saxifraga flagellaris subsp. platysepala

Saxifraga flagellaris subsp.

platysepala, commonly known as the broad-leafed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five sepals and five petals, typical of the Saxifragaceae family. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for harsh, cold climates and high elevations.
