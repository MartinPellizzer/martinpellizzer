Selenipedium × phaedra

Selenipedium × phaedra, commonly known as the hybrid giant spider-orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Selenipedium grandiflorum and Selenipedium phaedra, characterized by its large, showy, spider-like flowers. The plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its unique morphology includes long, arching inflorescences with numerous pale green to white flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
