Satyrium membranaceum

Satyrium membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous satyrium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers display a range of colors, often with intricate patterns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
