Sida salviifolia var. submutica

Sida salviifolia var. submutica, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is a variant of the sida genus characterized by its hairy, glandular foliage.

It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture due to the dense pubescence.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a yellowish or greenish hue.
