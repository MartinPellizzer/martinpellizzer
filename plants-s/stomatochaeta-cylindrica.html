Stomatochaeta cylindrica

Stomatochaeta cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical hair plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a type of moss that exhibits a dense, cylindrical growth form, with its stems covered in fine, hair-like structures called stomatochaetae. These structures are specialized for gas exchange and moisture retention, which are essential for its survival in moist environments. The plant typically grows in shaded, damp areas, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
