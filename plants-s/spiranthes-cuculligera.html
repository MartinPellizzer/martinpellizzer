Spiranthes cuculligera

Spiranthes cuculligera, commonly known as the hooded ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
