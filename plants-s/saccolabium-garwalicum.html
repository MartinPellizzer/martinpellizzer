Saccolabium garwalicum

Saccolabium garwalicum, commonly known as the Garwal sicklebush, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Saccolabium garwalicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
