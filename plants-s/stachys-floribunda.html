Stachys floribunda

Stachys floribunda, commonly known as woodland betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink tubular flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Stachys floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
