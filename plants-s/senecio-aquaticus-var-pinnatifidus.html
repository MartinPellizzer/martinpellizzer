Senecio aquaticus var. pinnatifidus

Senecio aquaticus var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptation to aquatic environments. The plant features slender, ribbed stems and finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in slow-moving water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
