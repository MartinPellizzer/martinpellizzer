Serratula complanata

Serratula complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Serratula complanata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
