Scutellaria breviflora

Scutellaria breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

Scutellaria breviflora is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
