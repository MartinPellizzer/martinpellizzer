Senecio multicaulis subsp. stirlingensis

Senecio multicaulis subsp.

stirlingensis, commonly known as Stirling's iceplant, is a succulent perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
