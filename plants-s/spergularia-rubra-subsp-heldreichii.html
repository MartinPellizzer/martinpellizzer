Spergularia rubra subsp. heldreichii

Spergularia rubra subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, pink or red flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.
