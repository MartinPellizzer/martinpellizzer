Solanum nigrum subsp. moschatum

Solanum nigrum subsp.

moschatum, commonly known as the musk nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but have a strong, musky aroma. It typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage is characterized by ovate, hairy leaves with a distinct, slightly toxic scent.
