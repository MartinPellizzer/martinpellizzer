Saxifraga bucklandii

Saxifraga bucklandii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Saxifraga bucklandii is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
