Scirpus meyenii

Scirpus meyenii, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and dense clusters of yellowish-green spikelets at the top. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus meyenii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
