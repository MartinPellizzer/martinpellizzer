Stipa pulcherrima subsp. palatina

Stipa pulcherrima subsp.

palatina, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, fluffy panicles that are often tinged with purple or pink hues.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
