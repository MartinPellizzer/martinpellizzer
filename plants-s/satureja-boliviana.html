Satureja boliviana

Satureja boliviana, commonly known as Bolivian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional Andean cuisine and have a strong, minty aroma.
