Sorbus discolor var. paucijuga

Sorbus discolor var. paucijuga, commonly known as the variable whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a finely toothed margin. The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a bright red or orange color.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
