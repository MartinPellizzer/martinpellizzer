Salix cordata f. discolor

Salix cordata f. discolor, commonly known as the white willow or white-flowered weeping willow, is a unique cultivar of the willow tree.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale green foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its slender, weeping branches. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and graceful appearance.

However, it is susceptible to pests and diseases, requiring regular maintenance to thrive.
