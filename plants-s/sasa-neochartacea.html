Sasa neochartacea

Sasa neochartacea, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa neochartacea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create naturalistic landscapes.
