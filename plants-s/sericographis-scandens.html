Sericographis scandens

Sericographis scandens, commonly known as the climbing sericographis, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using adventitious roots along its stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sericographis scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover trellises or walls in warm climates.
