Spartium tricuspidatum

Spartium tricuspidatum, commonly known as three-lobed broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by the development of slender, three-lobed pods.
