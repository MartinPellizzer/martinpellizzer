Sium bulbosum

Sium bulbosum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, carrot-like roots and compound leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along stream banks.
