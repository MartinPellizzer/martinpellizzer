Saccharum sara

Saccharum sara, commonly known as the silver cane grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is known for its high sugar content, making it a potential resource for biofuel and sugar production. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and stabilizing wetland soils.
