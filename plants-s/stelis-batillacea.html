Stelis batillacea

Stelis batillacea, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a species of orchid belonging to the Epidendroideae subfamily.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves and produces numerous small, greenish flowers arranged in a raceme. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
