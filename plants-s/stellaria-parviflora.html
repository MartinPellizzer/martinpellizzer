Stellaria parviflora

Stellaria parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals that resemble stars, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Stellaria parviflora is often found in meadows and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
