Senecio rautaneni

Senecio rautaneni, commonly known as the Rautanen's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens.
