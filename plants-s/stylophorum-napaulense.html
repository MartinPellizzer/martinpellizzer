Stylophorum napaulense

Stylophorum napaulense, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
