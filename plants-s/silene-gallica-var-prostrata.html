Silene gallica var. prostrata

Silene gallica var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene gallica var. prostrata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
