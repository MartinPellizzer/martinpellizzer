Scaphosepalum panduratum

Scaphosepalum panduratum, commonly known as the panduratum orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, pendulous blooms that often display a mix of white and green hues. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Scaphosepalum panduratum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
