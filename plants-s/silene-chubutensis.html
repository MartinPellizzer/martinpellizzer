Silene chubutensis

Silene chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut flax or Chubut evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native environment. Silene chubutensis has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
