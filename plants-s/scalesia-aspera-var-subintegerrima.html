Scalesia aspera var. subintegerrima

Scalesia aspera var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the rough-hairy scalesia, is a variant of the Scalesia genus native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are often covered in dense, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Galápagos, where it thrives in coastal and humid forest environments.
