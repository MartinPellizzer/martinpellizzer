Sapium pallidum

Sapium pallidum, commonly known as the white sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and diverse morphological features. The tree has smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and the fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in bottomland hardwood forests.
