Stalagmitis luteus

Stalagmitis luteus, commonly known as the golden stalagmite plant, is a unique species native to the limestone caves of southern Mexico.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high mineral content in the soil, which contributes to its distinctive yellowish hue. The plant's stems grow upward from the cave floor, forming columnar structures that resemble stalagmites. Its leaves are small, waxy, and adapted to conserve water in its arid subterranean habitat.

Stalagmitis luteus plays a crucial role in the cave ecosystem by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
