Syntherisma pelagica

Syntherisma pelagica, commonly known as the oceanic spore plant, is a unique marine organism found in deep-sea environments.

It lacks traditional roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on specialized structures for nutrient absorption. Its reproductive system involves the release of buoyant spores that drift through the water column. The plant thrives in low-light conditions, utilizing symbiotic algae for photosynthesis.

Due to its habitat, it plays a crucial role in deep-sea ecosystems by providing shelter for various microorganisms.
