Scleria aromatica

Scleria aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant sedum, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant is noted for its aromatic properties, with a pleasant scent that becomes more pronounced when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Scleria aromatica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
