Solanum macrodon

Solanum macrodon, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasodine. Solanum macrodon thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
