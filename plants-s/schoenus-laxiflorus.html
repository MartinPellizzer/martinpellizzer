Schoenus laxiflorus

Schoenus laxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it forms dense stands. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on long, slender stalks and produce tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Schoenus laxiflorus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
