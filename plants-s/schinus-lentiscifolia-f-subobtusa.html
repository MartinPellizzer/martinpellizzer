Schinus lentiscifolia f. subobtusa

Schinus lentiscifolia f. subobtusa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Brazilian pepper, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are broader and more rounded compared to the typical variety. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
