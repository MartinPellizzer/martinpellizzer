Selaginella sandvicensis

Selaginella sandvicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian clubmoss, is a rare and endangered plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coastal areas and mesic forests. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
