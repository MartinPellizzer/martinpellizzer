Salix cordifolia f. tonsa

Salix cordifolia f. tonsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
