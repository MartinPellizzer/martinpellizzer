Sternbergia lutea subsp. greuteriana

Sternbergia lutea subsp.

greuteriana, commonly known as the Greuter's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking early-season color and biodiversity.
