Saraca obtusifolia

Saraca obtusifolia, commonly known as the Indian flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in spring, forming pendulous clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical to ovate leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Saraca obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
