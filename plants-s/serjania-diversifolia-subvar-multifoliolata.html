Serjania diversifolia subvar. multifoliolata

Serjania diversifolia subvar. multifoliolata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
