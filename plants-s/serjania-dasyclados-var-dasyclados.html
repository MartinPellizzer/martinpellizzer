Serjania dasyclados var. dasyclados

Serjania dasyclados var. dasyclados, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria dasyclados*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
