Salicornia stricta

Salicornia stricta, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting coastal ecosystems. Its small, green flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, edible seeds.

Salicornia stricta is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional foods and herbal remedies.
