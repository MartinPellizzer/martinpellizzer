Stellaria media var. subgymnocalyx

Stellaria media var. subgymnocalyx, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a variant of the common chickweed.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along roadsides. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, and the flowers are arranged in clusters, giving it a star-like appearance.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Stellaria genus, sharing similar botanical features and growth habits.
