Scaevola laevigata

Scaevola laevigata, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

Scaevola laevigata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
