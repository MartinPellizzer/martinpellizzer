Sedum japonicum var. humile

Sedum japonicum var. humile, commonly known as Japanese stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Japan.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
