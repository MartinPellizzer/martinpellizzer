Scirpus ferrugineus

Scirpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish-brown tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands in shallow water or damp soil.

Scirpus ferrugineus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
