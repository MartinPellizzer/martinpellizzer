Saxifraga staintonii

Saxifraga staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually rounded and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga staintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
