Salix dolichostachya

Salix dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stalked willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Salix dolichostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
