Sulcorebutia vasqueziana subsp. alba

Sulcorebutia vasqueziana subsp.

alba, commonly known as the White Sulcorebutia, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to white-colored stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
