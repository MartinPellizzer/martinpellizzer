Salvia perblanda

Salvia perblanda, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Salvia perblanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
