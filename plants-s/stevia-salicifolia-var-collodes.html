Stevia salicifolia var. collodes

Stevia salicifolia var. collodes, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its intense sweetness, derived from natural compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is often used as a natural sweetener and in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
