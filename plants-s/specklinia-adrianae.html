Specklinia adrianae

Specklinia adrianae, commonly known as Adriana's tillandsia, is a species of bromeliad native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Specklinia adrianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
