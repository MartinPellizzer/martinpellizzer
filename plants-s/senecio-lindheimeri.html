Senecio lindheimeri

Senecio lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in spring.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
