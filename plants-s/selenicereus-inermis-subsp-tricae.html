Selenicereus inermis subsp. tricae

Selenicereus inermis subsp.

tricae, commonly known as the Trichocereus tricae, is a cactus species native to parts of South America. It is a climbing cactus with elongated stems that often bear spines and are covered in areoles.

The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
