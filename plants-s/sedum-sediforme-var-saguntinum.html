Sedum sediforme var. saguntinum

Sedum sediforme var. saguntinum, commonly known as the Saguntinum stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, typical of many sedum species. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
