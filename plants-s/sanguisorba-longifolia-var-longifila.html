Sanguisorba longifolia var. longifila

Sanguisorba longifolia var. longifila, commonly known as the long-flowered sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to reddish bracteate flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Sanguisorba longifolia var. longifila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in garden landscapes for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
