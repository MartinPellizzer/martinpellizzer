Salvia scabiosae

Salvia scabiosae, commonly known as common wood sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Salvia scabiosae is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
