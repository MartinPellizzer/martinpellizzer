Sphaerocephalus villosissimus

Sphaerocephalus villosissimus, commonly known as the woolly-headed orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous or forested regions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
