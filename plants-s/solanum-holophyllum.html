Solanum holophyllum

Solanum holophyllum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Solanum holophyllum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
