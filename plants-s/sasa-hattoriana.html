Sasa hattoriana

Sasa hattoriana, commonly known as the Hattori bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for landscaping.

Sasa hattoriana is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can help prevent soil erosion.
