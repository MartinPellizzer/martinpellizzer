Stachys sieboldii

Stachys sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese violet or violet deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
