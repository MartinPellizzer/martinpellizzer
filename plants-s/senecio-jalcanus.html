Senecio jalcanus

Senecio jalcanus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio jalcanus is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
