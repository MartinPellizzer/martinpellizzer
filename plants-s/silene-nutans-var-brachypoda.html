Silene nutans var. brachypoda

Silene nutans var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its capsules are hairy and contain numerous small seeds.

It is a herbaceous perennial that is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
