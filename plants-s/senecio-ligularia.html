Senecio ligularia

Senecio ligularia, commonly known as the ice plant or snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it well-suited for arid and mountainous climates.

Senecio ligularia is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
