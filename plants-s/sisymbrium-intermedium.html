Sisymbrium intermedium

Sisymbrium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate winter cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth cycle. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
