Solidago vaseyi

Solidago vaseyi, commonly known as the vasey's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago vaseyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
