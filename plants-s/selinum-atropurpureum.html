Selinum atropurpureum

Selinum atropurpureum, commonly known as purple false onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a slightly aromatic, peppery scent.

Selinum atropurpureum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
