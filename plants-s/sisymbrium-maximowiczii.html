Sisymbrium maximowiczii

Sisymbrium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.
