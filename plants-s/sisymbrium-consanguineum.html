Sisymbrium consanguineum

Sisymbrium consanguineum, commonly known as the twinleaf mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaves and its role as a member of the Brassicaceae family.
