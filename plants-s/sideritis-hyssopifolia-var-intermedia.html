Sideritis hyssopifolia var. intermedia

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hyssop sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
