Scirpus subtilis

Scirpus subtilis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering heads are composed of small, clustered spikelets, which produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus subtilis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
