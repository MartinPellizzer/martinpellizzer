Scirpus potosiensis

Scirpus potosiensis, commonly known as the Potosi sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Scirpus potosiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
