Saxifraga iratiana

Saxifraga iratiana, commonly known as the Iratian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
