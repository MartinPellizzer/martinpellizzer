Scilla peruviana subsp. ughii

Scilla peruviana subsp.

ughii, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America. It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically evergreen throughout the year. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
