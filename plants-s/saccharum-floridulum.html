Saccharum floridulum

Saccharum floridulum, commonly known as the Florida sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of Florida, characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is adapted to the warm, humid climate of the southeastern United States. Its sugary sap was historically used by indigenous peoples for sweetening foods and beverages.

Due to its ecological significance and unique adaptations, it is considered an important species in wetland conservation efforts.
