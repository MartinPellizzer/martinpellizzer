Stipa trichoides

Stipa trichoides, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Stipa trichoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
