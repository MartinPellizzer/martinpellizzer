Spiranthes glauca

Spiranthes glauca, commonly known as the blue spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its flowers are pale blue to white in color.

Spiranthes glauca is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
