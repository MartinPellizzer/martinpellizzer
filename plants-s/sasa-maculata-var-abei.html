Sasa maculata var. abei

Sasa maculata var. abei, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green culms with distinctive purple or reddish markings along the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening and traditional landscapes.
