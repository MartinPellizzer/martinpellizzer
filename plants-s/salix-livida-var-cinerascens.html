Salix livida var. cinerascens

Salix livida var. cinerascens, commonly known as the grey willow, is a variant of the silver willow native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This willow is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
