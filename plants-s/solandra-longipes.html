Solandra longipes

Solandra longipes, commonly known as the giant yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the genus Solandra. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds.
