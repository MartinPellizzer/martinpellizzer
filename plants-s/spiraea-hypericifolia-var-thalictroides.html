Spiraea hypericifolia var. thalictroides

Spiraea hypericifolia var. thalictroides, commonly known as the thalictroides spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its attractive foliage and floral display.
