Solanum tetrapetalum

Solanum tetrapetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, rounded berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds, making them potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
