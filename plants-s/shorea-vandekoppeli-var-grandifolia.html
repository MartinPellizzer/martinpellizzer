Shorea vandekoppeli var. grandifolia

Shorea vandekoppeli var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered tamanu, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule containing winged seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in tropical rainforests.
