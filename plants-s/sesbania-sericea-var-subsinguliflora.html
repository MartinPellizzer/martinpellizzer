Sesbania sericea var. subsinguliflora

Sesbania sericea var. subsinguliflora, commonly known as the silky sesbania, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in elongated, flat pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is used in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
