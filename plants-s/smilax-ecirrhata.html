Smilax ecirrhata

Smilax ecirrhata, commonly known as the arrowhead smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, recurved spines along its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax ecirrhata is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
