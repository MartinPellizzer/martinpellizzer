Samanea inopinata

Samanea inopinata, commonly known as the blackbean tree, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flower clusters. The tree produces elongated, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Samanea inopinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in reforestation efforts.
