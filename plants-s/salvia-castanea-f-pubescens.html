Salvia castanea f. pubescens

Salvia castanea f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and showy, two-lipped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
