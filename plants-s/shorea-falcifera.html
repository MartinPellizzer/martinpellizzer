Shorea falcifera

Shorea falcifera, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
