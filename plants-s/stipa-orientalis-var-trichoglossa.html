Stipa orientalis var. trichoglossa

Stipa orientalis var. trichoglossa, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, cylindrical inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is characterized by a soft, silvery-blue color due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

This variety is well-suited for dry, well-drained soils and is often used in xeriscaping.
