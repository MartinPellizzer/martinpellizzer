Strychnos nigricans

Strychnos nigricans, commonly known as the black strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually ovate or elliptic in shape.

The plant is notable for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as strychnine and brucine, which are potent alkaloids.
