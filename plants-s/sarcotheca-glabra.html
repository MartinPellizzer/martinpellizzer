Sarcotheca glabra

Sarcotheca glabra, commonly known as the smooth sarcococca, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Sarcococaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and winter interest.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and horticultural purposes.
