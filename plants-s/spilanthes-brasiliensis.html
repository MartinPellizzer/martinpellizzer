Spilanthes brasiliensis

Spilanthes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant is known for its strong, pungent odor, which is often described as similar to that of a skunk. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

Spilanthes brasiliensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
