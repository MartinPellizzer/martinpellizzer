Senecio neomexicanus var. toumeyi

Senecio neomexicanus var. toumeyi, commonly known as the Toumey's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral coloration and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
