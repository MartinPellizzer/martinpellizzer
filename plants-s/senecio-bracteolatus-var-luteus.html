Senecio bracteolatus var. luteus

Senecio bracteolatus var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bract-like structure at the base of each flower.
