Satureja biflora var. cinereotomentosa

Satureja biflora var. cinereotomentosa, commonly known as silver-haired savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties include antiseptic and carminative effects, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
