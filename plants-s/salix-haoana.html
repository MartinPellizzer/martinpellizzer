Salix haoana

Salix haoana, commonly known as the Hawaiian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces catkins as its reproductive structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Salix haoana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
