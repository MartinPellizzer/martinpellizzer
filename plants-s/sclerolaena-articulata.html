Sclerolaena articulata

Sclerolaena articulata, commonly known as the articulated screwtree, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that are often segmented and have a distinctive, twisted appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Sclerolaena articulata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
