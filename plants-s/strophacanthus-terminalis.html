Strophacanthus terminalis

Strophacanthus terminalis, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Strophacanthus terminalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
