Senecio hercynicus subsp. hercynicus

Senecio hercynicus subsp.

hercynicus, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

The species is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience.
