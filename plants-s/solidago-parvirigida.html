Solidago parvirigida

Solidago parvirigida, commonly known as the small-stem goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Solidago parvirigida is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
