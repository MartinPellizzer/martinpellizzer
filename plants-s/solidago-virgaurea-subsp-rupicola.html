Solidago virgaurea subsp. rupicola

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows on rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
