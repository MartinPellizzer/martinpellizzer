Scrophularia sambucifolia

Scrophularia sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucus-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin, and it produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
