Symphyotrichum chihuahuense

Symphyotrichum chihuahuense, commonly known as Chihuahua heath, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
