Syngonanthus grao-mogolensis

Syngonanthus grao-mogolensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the cerrado and Atlantic Forest regions of Brazil. The plant typically grows in tall, dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Syngonanthus grao-mogolensis is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
