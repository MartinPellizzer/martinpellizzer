Syagrus sancona

Syagrus sancona, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange inflorescences, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas.

Syagrus sancona is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
