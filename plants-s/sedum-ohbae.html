Sedum ohbae

Sedum ohbae, commonly known as the Ohba's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Sedum ohbae is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
