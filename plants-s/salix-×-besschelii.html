Salix × besschelii

Salix × besschelii, commonly known as the Besschel willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus, Salix.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and delicate, narrow leaves that are typically green on top and white underneath. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The Besschel willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
