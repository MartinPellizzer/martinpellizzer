Senecio jorquerae

Senecio jorquerae, commonly known as the Jorquera's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio jorquerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
