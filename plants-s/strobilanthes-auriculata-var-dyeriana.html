Strobilanthes auriculata var. dyeriana

Strobilanthes auriculata var. dyeriana, commonly known as the Dyer's strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
