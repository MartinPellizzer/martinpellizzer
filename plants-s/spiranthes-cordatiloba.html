Spiranthes cordatiloba

Spiranthes cordatiloba, commonly known as the heartleaf spirea, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and moths.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
