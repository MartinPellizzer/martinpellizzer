Succowia fimbriata

Succowia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringeleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Succowia fimbriata is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
