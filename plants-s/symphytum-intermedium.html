Symphytum intermedium

Symphytum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of violet or blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
