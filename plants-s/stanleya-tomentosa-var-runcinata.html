Stanleya tomentosa var. runcinata

Stanleya tomentosa var. runcinata, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a hairy underside that gives the plant its "tomentosa" designation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to livestock and humans if ingested.
