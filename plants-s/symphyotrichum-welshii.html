Symphyotrichum welshii

Symphyotrichum welshii, commonly known as the Welsh's New England aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs.

Symphyotrichum welshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for late-season pollinators.
