Sempervivum radicescens

Sempervivum radicescens, commonly known as the red-scented houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive red or purple hue on the undersides. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in summer, typically on tall, branched stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its aromatic leaves have historically been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
