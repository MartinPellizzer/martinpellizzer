Scirpus tener

Scirpus tener, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, and it produces pale brown or yellowish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus tener plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
