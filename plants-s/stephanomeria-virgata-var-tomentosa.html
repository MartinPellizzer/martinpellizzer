Stephanomeria virgata var. tomentosa

Stephanomeria virgata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly stephanomeria, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
