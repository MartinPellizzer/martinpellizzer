Schnella caudigera

Schnella caudigera, commonly known as the long-tailed snakeroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Schnella caudigera thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
