Spitzelia aviorum

Spitzelia aviorum, commonly known as the golden spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Spitzelia aviorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
