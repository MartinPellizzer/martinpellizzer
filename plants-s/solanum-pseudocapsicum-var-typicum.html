Solanum pseudocapsicum var. typicum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. typicum, commonly known as the nightshade or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into green, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant often grows in dry, disturbed soils.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit that resembles a capsule, giving it its common name.
