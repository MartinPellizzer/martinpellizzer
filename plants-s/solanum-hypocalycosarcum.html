Solanum hypocalycosarcum

Solanum hypocalycosarcum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
