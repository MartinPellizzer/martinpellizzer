Sphaerolobium acuminatum

Sphaerolobium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, linear leaves and producing clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
