Sassafras parthenoxylon

Sassafras parthenoxylon, commonly known as the Chinese sassafras, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
