Senecio squalidus var. pinnatifidus

Senecio squalidus var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and deeply lobed, giving the plant a distinctive, somewhat untidy appearance. It thrives in grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This plant is also known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
