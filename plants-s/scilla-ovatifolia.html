Scilla ovatifolia

Scilla ovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are broad and ovate, giving it its common name.

Scilla ovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early bloom.
