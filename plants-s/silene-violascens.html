Silene violascens

Silene violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada, typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, and its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem. It has a short, hairy stem and small, linear leaves.

Silene violascens is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
