Stelis atrocaerulea

Stelis atrocaerulea, commonly known as the dark blue stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique dark blue or violet-colored flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its humid environment.

Stelis atrocaerulea is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
