Solanum andigenum f. magnicorollatum

Solanum andigenum f. magnicorollatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The species is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with a unique corolla structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with rich soil.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
