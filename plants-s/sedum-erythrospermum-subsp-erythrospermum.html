Sedum erythrospermum subsp. erythrospermum

Sedum erythrospermum subsp.

erythrospermum, commonly known as the red-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces clusters of bright red, star-shaped flowers in the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
