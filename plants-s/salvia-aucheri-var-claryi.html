Salvia aucheri var. claryi

Salvia aucheri var. claryi, commonly known as Clary's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Clary's sage is also valued in traditional medicine for its essential oils.
