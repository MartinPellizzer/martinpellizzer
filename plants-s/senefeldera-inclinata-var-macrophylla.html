Senefeldera inclinata var. macrophylla

Senefeldera inclinata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved senefeldera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is upright, with a tendency to lean slightly, giving it a graceful appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded woodland environments.
