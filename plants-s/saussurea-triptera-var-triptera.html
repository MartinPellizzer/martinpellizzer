Saussurea triptera var. triptera

Saussurea triptera var. triptera, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow or the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a robust, fibrous root system and a tall, branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese and Tibetan medicine.
