Salix novae-angliae cordata

Salix novae-angliae cordata, commonly known as the New England willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

The New England willow is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
