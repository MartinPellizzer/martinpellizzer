Sapium aucuparium var. hippomane

Sapium aucuparium var. hippomane, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. The plant is notable for its latex sap, which has historically been used in the production of rubber. Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and tree-like forms depending on environmental conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has applications in traditional medicine.
