Senecio moharensis

Senecio moharensis, commonly known as Moharensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
