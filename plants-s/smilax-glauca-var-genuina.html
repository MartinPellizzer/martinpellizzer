Smilax glauca var. genuina

Smilax glauca var. genuina, commonly known as the white smilax, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and trifoliate leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
