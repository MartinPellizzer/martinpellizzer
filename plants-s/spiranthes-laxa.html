Spiranthes laxa

Spiranthes laxa, commonly known as the loose spiraling orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in late summer.

Spiranthes laxa is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, reflecting its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
