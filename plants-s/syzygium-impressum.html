Syzygium impressum

Syzygium impressum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or red when ripe. Syzygium impressum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
