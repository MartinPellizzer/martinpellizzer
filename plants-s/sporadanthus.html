Sporadanthus

Sporadanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pod-like fruits. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Sporadanthus species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
