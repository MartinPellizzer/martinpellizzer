Seriola tenuifolia

Seriola tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved seriola, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Seriola tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
