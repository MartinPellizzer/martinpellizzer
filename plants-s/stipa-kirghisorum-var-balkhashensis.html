Stipa kirghisorum var. balkhashensis

Stipa kirghisorum var. balkhashensis, commonly known as the Balkhash needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and saline soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
