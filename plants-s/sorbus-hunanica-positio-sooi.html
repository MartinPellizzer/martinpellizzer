Sorbus hunanica positio sooi

Sorbus hunanica positio sooi, commonly known as the Hunan whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, particularly in the Hunan province. The plant is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
