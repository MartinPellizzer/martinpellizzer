Syzygium flosculiferum

Syzygium flosculiferum, commonly known as the white apple or white cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Syzygium flosculiferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
