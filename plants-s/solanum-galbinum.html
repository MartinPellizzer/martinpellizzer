Solanum galbinum

Solanum galbinum, commonly known as the Galbinum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum galbinum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
