Spiranthes bracteolaris

Spiranthes bracteolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually white or pale green with a subtle fragrance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and moths.
