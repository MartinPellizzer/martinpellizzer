Spiranthes grayi

Spiranthes grayi, commonly known as the gray spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tuberous roots.

Spiranthes grayi is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
