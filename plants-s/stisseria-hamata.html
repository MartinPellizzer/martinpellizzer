Stisseria hamata

Stisseria hamata, commonly known as the golden star lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
