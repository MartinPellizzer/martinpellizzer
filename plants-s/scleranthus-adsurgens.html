Scleranthus adsurgens

Scleranthus adsurgens, commonly known as the ascending sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has a slender, branched stem that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
