Scirpus fluviatilis

Scirpus fluviatilis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Scirpus fluviatilis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
