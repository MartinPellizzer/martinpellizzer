Sarracenia × mooreana

Sarracenia × mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's pitcher plant, is a hybrid species within the Sarraceniaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in wet, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright pitchers that are often red or orange in color, with intricate patterns that aid in trapping prey. These pitchers secrete a digestive enzyme-laden fluid to break down insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients.

Sarracenia × mooreana is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is a popular choice among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
