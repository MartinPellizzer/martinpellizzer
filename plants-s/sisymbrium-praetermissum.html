Sisymbrium praetermissum

Sisymbrium praetermissum, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a root system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of its host, making it an important species in ecological studies.
