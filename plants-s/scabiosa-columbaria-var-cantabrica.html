Scabiosa columbaria var. cantabrica

Scabiosa columbaria var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features delicate, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
