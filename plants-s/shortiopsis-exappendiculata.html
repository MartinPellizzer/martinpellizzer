Shortiopsis exappendiculata

Shortiopsis exappendiculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
