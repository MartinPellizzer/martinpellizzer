Syringa × dubia f. alba

Syringa × dubia f. alba, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and greyish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Syringa × dubia f. alba is also valued for its pleasant, citrus-like fragrance.
