Syzygium koghiense

Syzygium koghiense, commonly known as the Koghiense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Syzygium koghiense is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and local remedies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
