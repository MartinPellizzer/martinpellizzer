Syringodea sanguinea

Syringodea sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Syringodea sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
