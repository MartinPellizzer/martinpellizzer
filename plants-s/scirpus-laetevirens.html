Scirpus laetevirens

Scirpus laetevirens, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Scirpus laetevirens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
