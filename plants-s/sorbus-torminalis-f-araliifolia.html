Sorbus torminalis f. araliifolia

Sorbus torminalis f. araliifolia, commonly known as the white-leafed rowan, is a botanical variant of the rowan tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This form exhibits broad, ovate leaves that are typically white or pale green, contrasting with the typical red or yellow leaves of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in temperate regions of Europe. It produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn.

The unique leaf coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
