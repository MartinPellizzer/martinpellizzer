Senecio pulchellus var. squamiferus

Senecio pulchellus var. squamiferus, commonly known as the squamiferous groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its foliage is covered in fine, scale-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
