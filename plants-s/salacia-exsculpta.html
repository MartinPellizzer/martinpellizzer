Salacia exsculpta

Salacia exsculpta, commonly known as the sculpted salacia, is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, deeply ridged leaves that resemble intricate carvings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. Its roots contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, Salacia exsculpta is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
