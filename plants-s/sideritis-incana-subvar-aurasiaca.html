Sideritis incana subvar. aurasiaca

Sideritis incana subvar. aurasiaca, commonly known as the Caucasian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Sideritis incana subvar. aurasiaca is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
