Silene italica subsp. sicula

Silene italica subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant produces showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Silene italica subsp.

sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
