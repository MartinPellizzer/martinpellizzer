Sitanion × planifolium

Sitanion × planifolium, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Sitanion × planifolium is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
