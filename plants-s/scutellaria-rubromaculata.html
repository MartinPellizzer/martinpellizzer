Scutellaria rubromaculata

Scutellaria rubromaculata, commonly known as the red-spotted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive red spots on the leaves and its resemblance to the skullcap genus.

Scutellaria rubromaculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
