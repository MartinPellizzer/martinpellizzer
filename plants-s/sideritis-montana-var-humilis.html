Sideritis montana var. humilis

Sideritis montana var. humilis, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and possesses small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Sideritis montana var. humilis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
