Siliquastrum orbiculatum

Siliquastrum orbiculatum, commonly known as the Mediterranean boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by pale yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a hedge.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
