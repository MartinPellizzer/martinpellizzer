Silene kerneri

Silene kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene kerneri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
