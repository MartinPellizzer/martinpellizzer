Sphenomeris angustifolia

Sphenomeris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sphenomeris, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Sphenomeris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
