Styrax rugosus var. formosanus

Styrax rugosus var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is used in the production of incense and other aromatic products.
