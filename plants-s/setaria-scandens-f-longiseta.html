Setaria scandens f. longiseta

Setaria scandens f. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired false panicgrass, is a variant of the species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, narrow leaves that often have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly florets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
