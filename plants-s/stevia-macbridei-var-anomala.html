Stevia macbridei var. anomala

Stevia macbridei var. anomala, commonly known as the Hawaiian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique flavor and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
