Sauropus androgynus

Sauropus androgynus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
