Scorzonera adilii

Scorzonera adilii, commonly known as the Adil's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Scorzonera adilii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
