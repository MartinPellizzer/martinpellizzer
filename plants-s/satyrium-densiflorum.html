Satyrium densiflorum

Satyrium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

The orchid's unique floral structure and habitat requirements make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
