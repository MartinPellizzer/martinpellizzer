Scorzonera helodes

Scorzonera helodes, commonly known as the meadow scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features a robust, fleshy root system, which is often harvested for its edible qualities. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with five petals and a distinctive central knob.

Scorzonera helodes is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in ecological systems.
