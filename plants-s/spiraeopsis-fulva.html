Spiraeopsis fulva

Spiraeopsis fulva, commonly known as the yellow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy, flat-topped clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Spiraeopsis fulva is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
