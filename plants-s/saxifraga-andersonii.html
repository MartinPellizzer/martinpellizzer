Saxifraga andersonii

Saxifraga andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga andersonii is typically found in open areas such as meadows and talus slopes.
