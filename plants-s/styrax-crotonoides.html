Styrax crotonoides

Styrax crotonoides, commonly known as the sweet gum or Chinese sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. Its sap contains volatile oils and has been used in traditional medicine.

Styrax crotonoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
