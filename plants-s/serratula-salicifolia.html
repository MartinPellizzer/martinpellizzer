Serratula salicifolia

Serratula salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf serrata, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula salicifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
