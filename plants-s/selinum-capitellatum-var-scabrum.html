Selinum capitellatum var. scabrum

Selinum capitellatum var. scabrum, commonly known as rough false parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
