Salvia dorrii var. gilmanii

Salvia dorrii var. gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
