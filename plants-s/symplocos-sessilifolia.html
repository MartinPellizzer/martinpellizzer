Symplocos sessilifolia

Symplocos sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

Symplocos sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
