Syndesmanthus flosculosus

Syndesmanthus flosculosus, commonly known as the clustered flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Syndesmanthus flosculosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
