Spiesia gelida

Spiesia gelida, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the cold regions of Antarctica and the Southern Ocean islands.

It has a dense, mat-like growth form with fine, hair-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is highly resilient to extreme cold and strong winds.

Spiesia gelida plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microscopic organisms in its extreme habitat.
