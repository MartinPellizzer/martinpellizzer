Silene ambigua

Silene ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Silene ambigua has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often considered a rare and declining species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
