Schidiomyrtus ericacea

Schidiomyrtus ericacea, commonly known as the coastal heather, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

Schidiomyrtus ericacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
