Statice maritima var. pubigera

Statice maritima var. pubigera, commonly known as sea lavender or salt meadow grass, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal sands and preventing erosion.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.
