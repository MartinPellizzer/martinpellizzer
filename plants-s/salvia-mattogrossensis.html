Salvia mattogrossensis

Salvia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sage, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic properties. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty scent, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

Salvia mattogrossensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
