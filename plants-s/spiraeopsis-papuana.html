Spiraeopsis papuana

Spiraeopsis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan spiraeopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.

Spiraeopsis papuana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical regions.
