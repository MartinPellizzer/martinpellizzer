Sparganium nipponicum

Sparganium nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese bur reed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spiky, brownish seed heads that resemble burrs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters along the stem.

Sparganium nipponicum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
