Salix pulchroides

Salix pulchroides, commonly known as the golden willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elliptic, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
