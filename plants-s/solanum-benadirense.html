Solanum benadirense

Solanum benadirense, commonly known as the Benadire nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
