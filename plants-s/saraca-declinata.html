Saraca declinata

Saraca declinata, commonly known as the drooping flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Saraca declinata make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
