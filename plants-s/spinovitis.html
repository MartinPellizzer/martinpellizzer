Spinovitis

Spinovitis, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep purple or black color. Spinovitis is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
