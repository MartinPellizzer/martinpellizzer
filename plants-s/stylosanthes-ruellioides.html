Stylosanthes ruellioides

Stylosanthes ruellioides, commonly known as the hairy stylo, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, dehiscent pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to poor soils and is often used as forage for livestock.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in sustainable agriculture.
