Sophora tinctoria

Sophora tinctoria, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a deep blue dye from its seeds, which has been historically used in textile industries. Its growth habit includes a woody stem and a spreading root system, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The botanical features of Sophora tinctoria make it both ecologically significant and valuable for traditional dyeing practices.
