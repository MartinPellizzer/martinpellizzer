Solanum boissieri

Solanum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, featuring hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five lobed petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Solanum boissieri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
