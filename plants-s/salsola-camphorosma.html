Salsola camphorosma

Salsola camphorosma, commonly known as alkali saxifrage, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish or yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Salsola camphorosma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
