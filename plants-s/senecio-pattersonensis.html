Senecio pattersonensis

Senecio pattersonensis, commonly known as the Patterson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
