Saussurea violacea

Saussurea violacea, commonly known as the violet saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure. Its fibrous roots are adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in alpine gardens.
