Stizolobium venulosum

Stizolobium venulosum, commonly known as the veiny legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with slender, veiny leaflets, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Stizolobium venulosum is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in soil enrichment through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
