Selinum duriusculum

Selinum duriusculum, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Selinum duriusculum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
