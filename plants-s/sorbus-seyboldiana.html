Sorbus seyboldiana

Sorbus seyboldiana, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
