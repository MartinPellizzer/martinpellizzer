Shorea obtusa

Shorea obtusa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a pale to golden-yellow color. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in catkins. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Shorea obtusa is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
