Spergula tunetana var. mixta

Spergula tunetana var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed spurges, is a variant of the spurges, characterized by its distinctive floral and vegetative features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and dry habitats. The plant typically has slender stems with opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, and its genetic diversity reflects its adaptability to arid environments.
