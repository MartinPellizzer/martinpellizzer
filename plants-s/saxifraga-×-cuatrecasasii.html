Saxifraga × cuatrecasasii

Saxifraga × cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, often pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
