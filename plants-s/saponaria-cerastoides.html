Saponaria cerastoides

Saponaria cerastoides, commonly known as the white betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains saponins, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.
