Sclerochloa divaricata

Sclerochloa divaricata, commonly known as the variable wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. Sclerochloa divaricata is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, displaying drought tolerance.

Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing minute seeds that are dispersed by wind.
