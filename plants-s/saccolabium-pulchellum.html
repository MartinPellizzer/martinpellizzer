Saccolabium pulchellum

Saccolabium pulchellum, commonly known as the showy saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Saccolabium pulchellum is adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems and small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss.
