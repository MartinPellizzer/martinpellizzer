Sanguisorba cordifolia

Sanguisorba cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, thriving in well-drained to moist soils. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
