Stanhopea tigrina var. purpurea

Stanhopea tigrina var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple tigrina orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling tigers' stripes. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, fragrant blooms that appear during the dry season. Its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves are adapted to its humid, tropical environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
