Silene californica var. miniata

Silene californica var. miniata, commonly known as the California flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as chaparral and coastal bluffs. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
