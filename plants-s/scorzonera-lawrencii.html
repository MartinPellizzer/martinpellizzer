Scorzonera lawrencii

Scorzonera lawrencii, commonly known as Lawrence's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and may have some medicinal properties, though it is not widely used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera lawrencii thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
