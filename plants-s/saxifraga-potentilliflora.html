Saxifraga potentilliflora

Saxifraga potentilliflora, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are often lobed or toothed, and they are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Saxifraga potentilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
