Sinocalamus beecheyanus

Sinocalamus beecheyanus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rapid growth and hollow stems. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sinocalamus beecheyanus is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
