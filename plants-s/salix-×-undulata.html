Salix × undulata

Salix × undulata, commonly known as the wavyleaf willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Russian Far East. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside, and it produces catkin-like flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
