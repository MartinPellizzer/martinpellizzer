Sida benensis

Sida benensis, commonly known as the coastal bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in saline or coastal environments. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Sida benensis is adapted to thrive in salty soils and is often found in dune ecosystems along coastlines.
