Salsola pontica

Salsola pontica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Salsola pontica is often considered an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
