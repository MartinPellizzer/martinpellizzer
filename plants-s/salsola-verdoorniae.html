Salsola verdoorniae

Salsola verdoorniae, commonly known as the Verdoorn's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Salsola verdoorniae plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native ecosystem.
