Senna aymara

Senna aymara, commonly known as the Andean senna, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Senna aymara is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
