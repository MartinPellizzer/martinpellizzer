Stelis dromedarina

Stelis dromedarina, commonly known as the camel's hump orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that resemble a camel's hump, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the wet season.

Stelis dromedarina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
