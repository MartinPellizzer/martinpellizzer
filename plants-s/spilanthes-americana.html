Spilanthes americana

Spilanthes americana, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its common name.

Spilanthes americana is also known for its pungent odor and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
