Sterculia ampla

Sterculia ampla, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are palmately lobed, providing a distinctive appearance, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
