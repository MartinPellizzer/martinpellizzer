Solanum symphysicaulis

Solanum symphysicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge.

Solanum symphysicaulis is often found in dry, open habitats and can be cultivated as an ornamental plant.
