Symplocos puberula

Symplocos puberula, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Symplocos puberula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
