Saxifraga × geum var. ovalifolia

Saxifraga × geum var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved garden saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Saxifraga and Chrysanthemum.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to add color to garden landscapes.
