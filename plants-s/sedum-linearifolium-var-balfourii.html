Sedum linearifolium var. balfourii

Sedum linearifolium var. balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, fleshy, green to blue-green leaves that are linear and often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural use.
