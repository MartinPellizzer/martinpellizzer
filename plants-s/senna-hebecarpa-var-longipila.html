Senna hebecarpa var. longipila

Senna hebecarpa var. longipila, commonly known as the longpod senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
