Stachys pallida

Stachys pallida, commonly known as pale deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Stachys pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
