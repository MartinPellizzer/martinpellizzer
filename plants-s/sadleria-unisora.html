Sadleria unisora

Sadleria unisora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense rosette. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Sadleria unisora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
