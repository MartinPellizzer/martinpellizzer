Sida canacorum

Sida canacorum, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
