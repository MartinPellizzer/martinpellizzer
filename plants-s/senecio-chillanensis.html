Senecio chillanensis

Senecio chillanensis, commonly known as the Chillan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and has hairy, linear leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Senecio chillanensis is often found in high-altitude regions and is adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
