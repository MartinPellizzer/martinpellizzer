Selaginella fusca

Selaginella fusca, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is found in various regions, including North America and parts of Asia, often in moist, shaded environments.

Selaginella fusca plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
