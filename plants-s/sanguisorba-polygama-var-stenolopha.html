Sanguisorba polygama var. stenolopha

Sanguisorba polygama var. stenolopha, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
