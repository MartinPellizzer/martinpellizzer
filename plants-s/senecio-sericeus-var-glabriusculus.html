Senecio sericeus var. glabriusculus

Senecio sericeus var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with a soft, woolly texture due to its fine, hair-like trichomes. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid regions.
