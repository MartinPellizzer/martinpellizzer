Salix × secalliana

Salix × secalliana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that dangle from the main trunk, creating a distinctive, graceful appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green color that turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
