Salsola anatolica

Salsola anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often found in desert and steppe ecosystems.

Salsola anatolica plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
