Solanum macropilosum

Solanum macropilosum, commonly known as the large-haired nightshade, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its small, violet flowers. Its berries are typically yellow or red when ripe and are edible, though they contain toxic compounds.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.
