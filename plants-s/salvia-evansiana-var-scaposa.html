Salvia evansiana var. scaposa

Salvia evansiana var. scaposa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
