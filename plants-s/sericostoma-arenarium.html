Sericostoma arenarium

Sericostoma arenarium, commonly known as the sand flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it useful for erosion control.

Sericostoma arenarium is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor conditions.
