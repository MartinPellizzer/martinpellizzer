Statice corinthiaca

Statice corinthiaca, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in saline or coastal environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice corinthiaca is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
