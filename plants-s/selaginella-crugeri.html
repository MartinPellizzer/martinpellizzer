Selaginella crugeri

Selaginella crugeri, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. Its sporophylls are spirally arranged and bear spores on their upper surfaces.

Selaginella crugeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
