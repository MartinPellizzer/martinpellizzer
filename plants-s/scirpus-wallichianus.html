Scirpus wallichianus

Scirpus wallichianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in wetland environments such as marshes and pond margins. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it blooms during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus wallichianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
