Salix triandra f. gracilis

Salix triandra f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender weeping willow, is a horticultural form of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
