Solanum pittosporifolium var. pilosum

Solanum pittosporifolium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy pittosporifolium, is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for food. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
