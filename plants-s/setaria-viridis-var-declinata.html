Setaria viridis var. declinata

Setaria viridis var. declinata, commonly known as the drooping green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to Africa.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often drooping, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields or disturbed soils.

This plant is also valued for its potential use in sustainable agriculture and as a forage crop.
