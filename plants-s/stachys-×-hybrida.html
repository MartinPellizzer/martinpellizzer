Stachys × hybrida

Stachys × hybrida, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and woodland edges.

Stachys × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
