Strychnos andamanensis

Strychnos andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive fruit structure with a star-like appearance.

Strychnos andamanensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
