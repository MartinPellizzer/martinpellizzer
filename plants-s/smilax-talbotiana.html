Smilax talbotiana

Smilax talbotiana, commonly known as the talbot's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, recurved spines along its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Smilax talbotiana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as swamps and pine flatwoods.
