Sinapis japonica

Sinapis japonica, commonly known as Japanese mustard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a distinctive mustard-like odor when crushed.

Sinapis japonica is often grown as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and ability to suppress weeds.
