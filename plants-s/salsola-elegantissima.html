Salsola elegantissima

Salsola elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water, typical of xerophytic plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dry, winged fruits. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Salsola elegantissima is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
