Stagmaria verniciflua

Stagmaria verniciflua, commonly known as the sticky bryophyte, is a type of liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth and the production of a sticky substance on its surfaces. This secretion helps in retaining moisture and may also deter herbivores. The plant is typically found in forest floors, rock crevices, and other humid microhabitats.

Its simple morphology and unique chemical properties make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
