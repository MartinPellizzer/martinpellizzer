Scilla peruviana var. ifniensis

Scilla peruviana var. ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
