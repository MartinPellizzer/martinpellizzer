Scirpus apus

Scirpus apus, commonly known as common bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spadices, which are cylindrical flower clusters. Scirpus apus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its structure provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
