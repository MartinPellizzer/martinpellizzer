Smilax bona-nox subsp. wrightii

Smilax bona-nox subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
