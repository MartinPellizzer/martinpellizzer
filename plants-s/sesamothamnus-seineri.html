Sesamothamnus seineri

Sesamothamnus seineri, commonly known as the Mediterranean thornbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Sesamothamnus seineri plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
