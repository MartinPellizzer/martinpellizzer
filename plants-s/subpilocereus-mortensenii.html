Subpilocereus mortensenii

Subpilocereus mortensenii, commonly known as the Mortensen's night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts.
