Spiraea lobata var. angustiloba

Spiraea lobata var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing an elegant, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its graceful form and seasonal blooms.
