Synadenium glaucescens

Synadenium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue ghost lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, stocky stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

Synadenium glaucescens is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
