Salvia bella

Salvia bella, commonly known as the beauty salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Salvia bella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
