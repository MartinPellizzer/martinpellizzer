Senecio mahindae

Senecio mahindae, commonly known as the Mahindae groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to its harsh environment. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Senecio mahindae is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
