Selaginella rupestris f. angolensis

Selaginella rupestris f. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and sporophylls.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant exhibits a unique life cycle with a dominant sporophyte generation and a short, dependent gametophyte phase. Its structure includes a stem with whorled leaves and specialized structures for spore production.

This fern ally is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
