Sorbus aucuparia f. oligodens

Sorbus aucuparia f. oligodens, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, dense foliage and distinctive fruiting habits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and rocky habitats. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated margin. Its fruits are small, red berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

This form is notable for its reduced leaf density compared to the typical species, making it a striking ornamental plant in gardens.
