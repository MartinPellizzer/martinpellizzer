Syzygium cymiferum

Syzygium cymiferum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering. The fruit, which is typically red or yellow when ripe, contains a single seed and is known for its sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
