Satureja cuneifolia var. hispalensis

Satureja cuneifolia var. hispalensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.
