Spathoglottis doctersii var. emarginata

Spathoglottis doctersii var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate spathoglottis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, lanceolate leaves and bears inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticultural collections.
