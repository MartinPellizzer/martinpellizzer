Scirpus coloradoensis

Scirpus coloradoensis, commonly known as Colorado bulrush, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
