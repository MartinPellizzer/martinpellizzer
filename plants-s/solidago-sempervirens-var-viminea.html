Solidago sempervirens var. viminea

Solidago sempervirens var. viminea, commonly known as the greenstem goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, slightly hairy stems that remain green throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, bright yellow flower clusters in the late summer and early autumn, which are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, wetlands, and stream banks.
