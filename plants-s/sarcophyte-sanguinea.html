Sarcophyte sanguinea

Sarcophyte sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and a distinctive, fleshy root system. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
