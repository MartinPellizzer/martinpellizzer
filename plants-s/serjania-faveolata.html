Serjania faveolata

Serjania faveolata, commonly known as the golden serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually elliptical in shape, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Serjania faveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
