Sesleria albicans

Sesleria albicans, commonly known as the white sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Sesleria albicans is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
