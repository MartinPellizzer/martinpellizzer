Scorzonera bungei

Scorzonera bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers in composite heads, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its root system is particularly notable for being tuberous and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera bungei thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
