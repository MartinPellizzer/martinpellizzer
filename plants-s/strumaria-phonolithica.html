Strumaria phonolithica

Strumaria phonolithica, commonly known as the soundstone bellflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the mineral-rich regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and a peculiar ability to respond to sound vibrations, which it uses to attract pollinators. The plant's structure includes a hollow, bell-shaped corolla that amplifies acoustic signals, enhancing its interaction with its environment. Its roots are adapted to absorb minerals from the rocky soil, contributing to its specialized growth.

This rare species is a subject of botanical interest due to its unusual sensory adaptations and ecological niche.
