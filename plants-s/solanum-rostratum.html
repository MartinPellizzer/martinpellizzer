Solanum rostratum

Solanum rostratum, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in dry, disturbed areas.

Solanum rostratum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
