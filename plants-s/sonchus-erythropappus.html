Sonchus erythropappus

Sonchus erythropappus, commonly known as bloodleaf lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by distinctive purple-bracted pappus structures. It is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
