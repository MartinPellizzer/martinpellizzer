Solanum myosotoideum

Solanum myosotoideum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
