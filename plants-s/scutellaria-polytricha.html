Scutellaria polytricha

Scutellaria polytricha, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Scutellaria polytricha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its hairy texture and unique floral structure make it a distinctive species within the genus Scutellaria.
