Sclerocladus tenax

Sclerocladus tenax, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Sclerocladus tenax plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
