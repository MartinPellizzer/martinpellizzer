Saccolabium taiwanianum

Saccolabium taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan legume or Taiwan senna, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to the humid subtropical climate of its native region. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and educational purposes.
