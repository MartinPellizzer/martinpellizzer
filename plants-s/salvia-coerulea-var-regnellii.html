Salvia coerulea var. regnellii

Salvia coerulea var. regnellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
