Solanum subulatum

Solanum subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it its common name.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
