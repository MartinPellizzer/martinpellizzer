Stachytarpheta glazioviana

Stachytarpheta glazioviana, commonly known as Glaziov's blue mistflower, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in forest understories.

Stachytarpheta glazioviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
