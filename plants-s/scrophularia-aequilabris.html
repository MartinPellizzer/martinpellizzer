Scrophularia aequilabris

Scrophularia aequilabris, commonly known as the broad-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and grasslands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The genus Scrophularia is part of the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar morphological characteristics.
