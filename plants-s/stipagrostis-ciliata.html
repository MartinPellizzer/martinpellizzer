Stipagrostis ciliata

Stipagrostis ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Stipagrostis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
