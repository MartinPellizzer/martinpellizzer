Stillingia lineata var. laevigata

Stillingia lineata var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features smooth, hollow stems that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
