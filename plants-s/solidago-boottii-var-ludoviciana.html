Solidago boottii var. ludoviciana

Solidago boottii var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most striking characteristic is its bright yellow inflorescence, composed of numerous small, clustered flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
