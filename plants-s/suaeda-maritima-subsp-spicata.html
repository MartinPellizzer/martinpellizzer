Suaeda maritima subsp. spicata

Suaeda maritima subsp.

spicata, commonly known as sea purslane, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salinity. Its reproductive structures are typically clustered in spikes, giving it the subspecies name *spicata*.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
