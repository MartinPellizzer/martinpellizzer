Styrax sumatranus

Styrax sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran styrax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The bark and flowers are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.
