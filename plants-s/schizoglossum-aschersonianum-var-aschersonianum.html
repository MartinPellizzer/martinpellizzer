Schizoglossum aschersonianum var. aschersonianum

Schizoglossum aschersonianum var. aschersonianum, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
