Sarcopodium uniflorum

Sarcopodium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower rootplant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its single, inconspicuous flower is usually small and greenish-white in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
