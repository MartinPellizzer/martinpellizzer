Serapias cordigera var. mauritanica

Serapias cordigera var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian heartleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in semi-desert habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
