Stenogyne rugosa var. remota

Stenogyne rugosa var. remota, commonly known as the rough stenogyne, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its rough, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as on rocky slopes or in forest understories. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
