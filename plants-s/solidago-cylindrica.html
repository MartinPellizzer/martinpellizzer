Solidago cylindrica

Solidago cylindrica, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Solidago cylindrica is an important nectar source for pollinators and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
