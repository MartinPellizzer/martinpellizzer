Samara aurantiaca

Samara aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange samara or orange-winged fruit.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. It is characterized by its vibrant orange fruits, which are technically modified leaves called samaras. The fruits have a distinctive winged structure that aids in wind dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
