Saccolabium berkeleyi

Saccolabium berkeleyi, commonly known as the Berkeley's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid ecosystems.
