Schinus maurioides

Schinus maurioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red berries. The plant can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers.
