Satureja alpina var. granatensis

Satureja alpina var. granatensis, commonly known as Alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine for its pungent, peppery flavor.

Satureja alpina var. granatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
