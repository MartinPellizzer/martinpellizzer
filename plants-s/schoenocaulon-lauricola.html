Schoenocaulon lauricola

Schoenocaulon lauricola, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has a low, spreading growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Schoenocaulon lauricola plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
