Senecio lawii

Senecio lawii, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Senecio lawii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
