Scutellaria scordiifolia var. puberula

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually violet to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
