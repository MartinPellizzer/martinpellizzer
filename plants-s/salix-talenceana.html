Salix talenceana

Salix talenceana, commonly known as the Talence willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions of Spain and France. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Salix talenceana produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination and support local wildlife.
