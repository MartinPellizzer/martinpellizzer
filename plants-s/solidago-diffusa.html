Solidago diffusa

Solidago diffusa, commonly known as golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
