Senecio romeroides

Senecio romeroides, commonly known as the Romanweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.

Senecio romeroides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
