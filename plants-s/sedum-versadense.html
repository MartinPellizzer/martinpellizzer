Sedum versadense

Sedum versadense, commonly known as the variable sedum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Sedum versadense is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in gardens and rockeries.
