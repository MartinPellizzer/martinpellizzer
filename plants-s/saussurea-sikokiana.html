Saussurea sikokiana

Saussurea sikokiana, commonly known as the Bhutan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stalks allow it to withstand harsh mountainous environments.

Saussurea sikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
