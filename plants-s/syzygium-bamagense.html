Syzygium bamagense

Syzygium bamagense, commonly known as the Bamaga apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Australia, particularly in Queensland. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
