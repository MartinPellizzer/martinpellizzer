Saxifraga hirculus f. humilis

Saxifraga hirculus f. humilis, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in hardy, decorative plants.
