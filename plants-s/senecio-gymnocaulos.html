Senecio gymnocaulos

Senecio gymnocaulos, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem that is often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
