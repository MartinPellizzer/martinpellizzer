Sphaerocionium sanguinolentum

Sphaerocionium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood-red globular cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy stems that are often covered in a reddish or pinkish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the cooler months. Its thick, water-storing stems allow it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among cactus enthusiasts.
