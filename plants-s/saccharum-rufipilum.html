Saccharum rufipilum

Saccharum rufipilum, commonly known as red-stemmed sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks that range in color from red to purple, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture.

Saccharum rufipilum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
