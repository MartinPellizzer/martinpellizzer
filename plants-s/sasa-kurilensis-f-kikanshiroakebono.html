Sasa kurilensis f. kikanshiroakebono

Sasa kurilensis f. kikanshiroakebono, commonly known as the Kikanshiroakebono bamboo, is a variant of the Kuril bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, culms that are often densely packed, forming compact clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a dark green color with a slight waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
