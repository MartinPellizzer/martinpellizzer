Salomonia ramosissima

Salomonia ramosissima, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Salomonia ramosissima is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
