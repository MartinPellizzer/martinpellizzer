Sesleria argentea var. gomarica

Sesleria argentea var. gomarica, commonly known as silver grass or silver sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture to garden landscapes.
