Solanum roei

Solanum roei, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Solanum roei produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.
