Stauranthera grandifolia

Stauranthera grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved stauranthera, is a tropical perennial plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stauranthera grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
