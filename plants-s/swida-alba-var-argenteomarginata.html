Swida alba var. argenteomarginata

Swida alba var. argenteomarginata, commonly known as the silver-margin white honeysuckle, is a variant of the white honeysuckle native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers and striking silver or white marginal veins on its leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
