Stemodiacra tomentosa

Stemodiacra tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly stemodia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
