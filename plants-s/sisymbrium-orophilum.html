Sisymbrium orophilum

Sisymbrium orophilum, commonly known as the mountain mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, including parts of California and Nevada. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
