Schradera

Schradera, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae, consists of several species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants are characterized by their square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, often fragrant flowers. Many species of Schradera are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and attractive foliage. They typically thrive in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The genus includes both shrubs and herbs, with some species being used in traditional medicine.
