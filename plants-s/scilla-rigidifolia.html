Scilla rigidifolia

Scilla rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid leaf scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and rigid, giving it its common name.

Scilla rigidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
