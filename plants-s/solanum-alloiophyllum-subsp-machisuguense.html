Solanum alloiophyllum subsp. machisuguense

Solanum alloiophyllum subsp.

machisuguense, commonly known as the Machisuguense nightshade, is a species native to specific regions in South America. It is a shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
