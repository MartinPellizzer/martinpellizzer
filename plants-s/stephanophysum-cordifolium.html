Stephanophysum cordifolium

Stephanophysum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved stephanophysum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features elongated, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
