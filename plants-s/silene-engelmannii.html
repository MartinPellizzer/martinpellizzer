Silene engelmannii

Silene engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Silene engelmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
