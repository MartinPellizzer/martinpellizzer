Sideritis × puiggariana

Sideritis × puiggariana, commonly known as the hybrid Greek sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis × puiggariana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
