Sida reticulata

Sida reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined sida, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and hairy, alternate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Sida reticulata is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional weaving and textile production.
