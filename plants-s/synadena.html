Synadena

Synadena, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in dense, mist-like clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Synadena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
