Solidago gebleri

Solidago gebleri, commonly known as white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of white to pale yellow ray flowers surrounded by a yellow disk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

Solidago gebleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
