Sokinochloa bosseri

Sokinochloa bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
