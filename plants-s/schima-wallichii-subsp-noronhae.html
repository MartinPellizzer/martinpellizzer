Schima wallichii subsp. noronhae

Schima wallichii subsp.

noronhae, commonly known as the Noronha schima, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The plant is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and floral structure compared to the typical form of Schima wallichii.
