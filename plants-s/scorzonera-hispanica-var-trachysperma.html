Scorzonera hispanica var. trachysperma

Scorzonera hispanica var. trachysperma, commonly known as Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
