Senecio lyratipartitus

Senecio lyratipartitus, commonly known as the Lyrate-parted Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, lyrate-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
