Schnella outimouta

Schnella outimouta, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Schnella outimouta thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
