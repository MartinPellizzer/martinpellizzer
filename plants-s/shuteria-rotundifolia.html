Shuteria rotundifolia

Shuteria rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf shuteria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Shuteria rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
