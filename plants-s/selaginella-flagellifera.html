Selaginella flagellifera

Selaginella flagellifera, commonly known as the flagellate spore moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique reproductive structure, with spores produced on specialized structures called synangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are small and closely arranged, forming dense, carpet-like mats.

Selaginella flagellifera is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience and adaptive features.
