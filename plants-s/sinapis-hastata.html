Sinapis hastata

Sinapis hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately divided leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its capsule fruits contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Sinapis hastata is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
