Sinarundinaria mairei

Sinarundinaria mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust rhizomes. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its growth habit resembles that of bamboo, with multiple stems arising from a single base.

Sinarundinaria mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
