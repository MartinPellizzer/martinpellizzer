Scabiosa triandra subsp. agrestis

Scabiosa triandra subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the field scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small, rounded clusters.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
