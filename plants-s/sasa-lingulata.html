Sasa lingulata

Sasa lingulata, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense clumps of foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa lingulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
