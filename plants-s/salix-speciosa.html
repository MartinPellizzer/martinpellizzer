Salix speciosa

Salix speciosa, commonly known as the showy willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and delicate, bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall.

Salix speciosa plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
