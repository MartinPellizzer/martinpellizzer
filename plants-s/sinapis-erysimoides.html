Sinapis erysimoides

Sinapis erysimoides, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Sinapis erysimoides is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed due to its invasive tendencies.
