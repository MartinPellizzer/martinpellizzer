Sporobolus fimbriatus var. latifolius

Sporobolus fimbriatus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian areas, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its reproductive structures produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to spread in suitable habitats.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
