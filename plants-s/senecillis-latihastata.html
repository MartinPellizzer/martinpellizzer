Senecillis latihastata

Senecillis latihastata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of yellow and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Senecillis latihastata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
