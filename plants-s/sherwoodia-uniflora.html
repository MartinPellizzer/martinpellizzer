Sherwoodia uniflora

Sherwoodia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower woodbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
