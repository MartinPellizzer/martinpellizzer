Schrebera affinis

Schrebera affinis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The tree has a dense, rounded canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with thick, flaky bark. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Schrebera affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
