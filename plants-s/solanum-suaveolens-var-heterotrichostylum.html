Solanum suaveolens var. heterotrichostylum

Solanum suaveolens var. heterotrichostylum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible tuberous roots.
