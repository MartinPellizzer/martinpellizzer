Salix hallaisanensis var. longifolia

Salix hallaisanensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf willow, is a variant of the Hallaisan willow native to Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowering catkins appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to wetland environments.
