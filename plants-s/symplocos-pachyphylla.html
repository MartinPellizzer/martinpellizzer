Symplocos pachyphylla

Symplocos pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Symplocos pachyphylla is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
