Solanum nelsonii var. acuminatum

Solanum nelsonii var. acuminatum, commonly known as the Nelson's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, toxic berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
