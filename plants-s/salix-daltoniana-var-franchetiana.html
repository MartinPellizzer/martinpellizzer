Salix daltoniana var. franchetiana

Salix daltoniana var. franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's willow, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
