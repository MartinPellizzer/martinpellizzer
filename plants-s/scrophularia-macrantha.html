Scrophularia macrantha

Scrophularia macrantha, commonly known as the tall dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in erect spikes, with showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

Scrophularia macrantha is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
