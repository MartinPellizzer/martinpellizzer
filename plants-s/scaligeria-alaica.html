Scaligeria alaica

Scaligeria alaica, commonly known as the Alaska scaligeria, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces delicate, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Scaligeria alaica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying Arctic flora.
