Salix magnifica

Salix magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent willow, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
