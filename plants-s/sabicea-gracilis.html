Sabicea gracilis

Sabicea gracilis, commonly known as the slender sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, bean-like pods. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Sabicea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
