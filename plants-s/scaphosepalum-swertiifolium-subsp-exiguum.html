Scaphosepalum swertiifolium subsp. exiguum

Scaphosepalum swertiifolium subsp.

exiguum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed scaphosepalum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, strap-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It prefers well-drained soil and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
