Senecio glaucophyllus subsp. basinudus

Senecio glaucophyllus subsp.

basinudus, commonly known as the Basinudus Groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in heads. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent-like leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
