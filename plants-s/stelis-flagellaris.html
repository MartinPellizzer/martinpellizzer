Stelis flagellaris

Stelis flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that often have a delicate, orchid-like appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a flagellum, giving it its common name.

Stelis flagellaris is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
