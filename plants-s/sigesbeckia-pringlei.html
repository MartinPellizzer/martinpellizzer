Sigesbeckia pringlei

Sigesbeckia pringlei, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Sigesbeckia pringlei is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
