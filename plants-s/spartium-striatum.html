Spartium striatum

Spartium striatum, commonly known as narrowleaf sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
