Syzygium aqueum

Syzygium aqueum, commonly known as the water apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a bumpy, waxy skin and a juicy, sweet pulp that contains small, edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
