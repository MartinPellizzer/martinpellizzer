Syagrus evansiana

Syagrus evansiana, commonly known as the Evans' palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are edible for some bird species.

Syagrus evansiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in tropical regions.
