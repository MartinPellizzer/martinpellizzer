Seriphium gomphrenoides

Seriphium gomphrenoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically wind-pollinated flowers. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a composite head that resembles a dandelion.

Seriphium gomphrenoides is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
