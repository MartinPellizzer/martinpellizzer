Sava

Sava, commonly known as *Satureja* or savory, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, and they typically grow in dry, sunny environments. Sava species are characterized by their aromatic, often mint-scented leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Many species are used in cooking for their pungent flavor, while others have medicinal properties.

The plant's leaves and flowers are often harvested for use in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
