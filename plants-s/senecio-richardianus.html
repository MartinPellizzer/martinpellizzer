Senecio richardianus

Senecio richardianus, commonly known as the Richard's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
