Sabia floribunda

Sabia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sabia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
