Sporobolus palmeri

Sporobolus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus palmeri is adapted to withstand drought and plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing erosion.
