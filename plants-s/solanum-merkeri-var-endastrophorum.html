Solanum merkeri var. endastrophorum

Solanum merkeri var. endastrophorum, commonly known as the Merker's nightshade, is a rare and unique variant of the Solanum genus.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy, glandular leaves. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a botanically significant and endangered species.
