Sarcocephalus Cadamba

Sarcocephalus cadamba is a unique plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is known for its striking, bulbous fruits that resemble small, inflated balloons. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in the world of horticulture.
