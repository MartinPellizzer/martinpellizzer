Sphacele vesiculosa

Sphacele vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like leaves that are often inflated and contain a high volume of air. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or green.

Sphacele vesiculosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
