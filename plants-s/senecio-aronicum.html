Senecio aronicum

Senecio aronicum, commonly known as the Great Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio aronicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
