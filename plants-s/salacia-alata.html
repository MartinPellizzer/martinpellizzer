Salacia alata

Salacia alata, commonly known as the winged salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its distinctive winged branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin. Salacia alata is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
