Stachys citrina subsp. chamaesideritis

Stachys citrina subsp.

chamaesideritis, commonly known as yellow wood betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
