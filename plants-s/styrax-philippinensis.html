Styrax philippinensis

Styrax philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by hard, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark is thick and cork-like, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
