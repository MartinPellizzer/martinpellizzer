Sophora viciifolia

Sophora viciifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its seeds are encased in elongated, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Sophora viciifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has also been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
