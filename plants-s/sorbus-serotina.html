Sorbus serotina

Sorbus serotina, commonly known as the late whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, persistent berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The late whitebeam is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
