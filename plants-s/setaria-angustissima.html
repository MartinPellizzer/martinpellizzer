Setaria angustissima

Setaria angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often found in moist to mesic environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a prominent midvein, and the plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria angustissima is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various wildlife.
