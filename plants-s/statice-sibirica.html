Statice sibirica

Statice sibirica, commonly known as Siberian glassweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a hardy species adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Statice sibirica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, glass-like appearance.
