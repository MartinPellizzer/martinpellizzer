Scindapsus rumphii

Scindapsus rumphii, commonly known as the silver nettle.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking variegated foliage. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive silver or white veining pattern. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
