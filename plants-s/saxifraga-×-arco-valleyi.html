Saxifraga × arco-valleyi

Saxifraga × arco-valleyi, commonly known as the Arco Valley saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
