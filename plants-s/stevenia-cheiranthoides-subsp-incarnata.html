Stevenia cheiranthoides subsp. incarnata

Stevenia cheiranthoides subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States. It features showy, pink to violet flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to xeriscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Onagraceae and is closely related to other evening primrose species.
