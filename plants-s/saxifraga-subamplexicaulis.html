Saxifraga subamplexicaulis, commonly known as the clustered saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. Saxifraga subamplexicaulis is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for alpine gardens.
