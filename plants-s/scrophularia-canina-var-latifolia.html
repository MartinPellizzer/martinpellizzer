Scrophularia canina var. latifolia

Scrophularia canina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved dog's mouth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological preferences.

The plant is not generally considered invasive and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
