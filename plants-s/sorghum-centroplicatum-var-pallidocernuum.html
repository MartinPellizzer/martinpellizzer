Sorghum centroplicatum var. pallidocernuum

Sorghum centroplicatum var. pallidocernuum, commonly known as the pale-leaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
