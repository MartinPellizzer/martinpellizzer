Salix mairei

Salix mairei, commonly known as the Maire willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is often found in moist, mountainous areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Salix mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
