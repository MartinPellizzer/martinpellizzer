Solanum jespersenii

Solanum jespersenii, commonly known as the Jespersen's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds.
