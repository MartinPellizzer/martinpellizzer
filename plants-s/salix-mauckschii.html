Salix mauckschii

Salix mauckschii, commonly known as the Maucksch willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
