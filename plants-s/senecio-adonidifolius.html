Senecio adonidifolius

Senecio adonidifolius, commonly known as the "Blueweed," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio adonidifolius is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
