Sideritis hirsuta subsp. pourretii

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

pourretii, commonly known as the hairy ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
