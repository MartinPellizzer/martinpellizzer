Salsola dentata

Salsola dentata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are often mistaken for tumbleweeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and toothed, giving it the species name "dentata.".

Salsola dentata is well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.
