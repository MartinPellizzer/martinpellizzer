Serrafalcus billotii

Serrafalcus billotii, commonly known as the West African ironwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content, making it highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
