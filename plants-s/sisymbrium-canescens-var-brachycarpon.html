Sisymbrium canescens var. brachycarpon

Sisymbrium canescens var. brachycarpon, commonly known as the short-stalked hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces slender, hairy stems that are covered in fine, white pubescence.

The variety brachycarpon is distinguished by its shorter pedicels and compact inflorescences compared to other varieties of Sisymbrium canescens.
