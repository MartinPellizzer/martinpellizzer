Schoenus hystrix

Schoenus hystrix, commonly known as the spiked bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often have sharp, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Schoenus hystrix plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
