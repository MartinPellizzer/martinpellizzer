Spiraea densiflora

Spiraea densiflora, commonly known as denseflower spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea densiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
