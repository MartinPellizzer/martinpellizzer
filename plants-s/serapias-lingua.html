Serapias lingua

Serapias lingua, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, are often adorned with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a tongue.

Serapias lingua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
