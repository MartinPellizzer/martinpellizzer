Salacia macrocarpa var. typica

Salacia macrocarpa var. typica, commonly known as the giant salamander plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and tropical greenhouses.
