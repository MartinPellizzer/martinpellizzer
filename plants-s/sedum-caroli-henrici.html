Sedum caroli-henrici

Sedum caroli-henrici, commonly known as Caroli-Henrici's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky and arid regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil and full sun.
