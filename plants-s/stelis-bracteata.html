Stelis bracteata

Stelis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a spike. Its roots are adapted for aerial attachment to trees, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Stelis bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
