Spergula micrantha

Spergula micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and slender stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Spergula micrantha is generally short-lived and has a low-growing habit, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
