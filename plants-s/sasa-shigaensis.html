Sasa shigaensis

Sasa shigaensis, commonly known as the Shiga bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a mild, aromatic scent.

Sasa shigaensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions of central Japan.
