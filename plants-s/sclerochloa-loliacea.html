Sclerochloa loliacea

Sclerochloa loliacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pond edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often hidden among the leaves.

Sclerochloa loliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
