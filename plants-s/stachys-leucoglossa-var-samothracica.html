Stachys leucoglossa var. samothracica

Stachys leucoglossa var. samothracica, commonly known as the silver-haired betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
