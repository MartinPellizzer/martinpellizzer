Salvia lutea

Salvia lutea, commonly known as yellow sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Salvia lutea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
