Strychnos littoralis

Strychnos littoralis, commonly known as the coastal strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Strychnos littoralis is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in coastal ecosystems.
