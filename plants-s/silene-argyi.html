Silene argyi

Silene argyi, commonly known as the Mongolian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Silene argyi is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
