Sida adfinis

Sida adfinis, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Sida adfinis is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
