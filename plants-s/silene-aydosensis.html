Silene aydosensis

Silene aydosensis, commonly known as the Aydos catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene aydosensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native range.
