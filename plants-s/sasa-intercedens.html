Sasa intercedens

Sasa intercedens, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form thickets in moist, shaded environments.

Sasa intercedens is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as an ornamental plant.
