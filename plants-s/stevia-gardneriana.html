Stevia gardneriana

Stevia gardneriana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners that are much sweeter than sugar. Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds.

Stevia gardneriana is often cultivated as a herb for culinary and medicinal purposes.
