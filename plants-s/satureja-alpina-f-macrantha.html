Satureja alpina f. macrantha

Satureja alpina f. macrantha, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, green leaves and clusters of pink to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.

The essential oils derived from its leaves are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.
