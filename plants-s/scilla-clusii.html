Scilla clusii

Scilla clusii, commonly known as the purple scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Scilla clusii is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread through seed dispersal.
