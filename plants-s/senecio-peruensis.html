Senecio peruensis

Senecio peruensis, commonly known as the Peruvian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in dry climates.

Senecio peruensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
