Senecio dasyphyllus

Senecio dasyphyllus, commonly known as the Blueweed or African Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Senecio dasyphyllus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
