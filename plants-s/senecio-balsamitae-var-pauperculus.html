Senecio balsamitae var. pauperculus

Senecio balsamitae var. pauperculus, commonly known as the balsam weed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its foliage is often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
