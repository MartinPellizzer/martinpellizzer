Senecio rumicifolius

Senecio rumicifolius, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
