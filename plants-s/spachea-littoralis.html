Spachea littoralis

Spachea littoralis, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Spachea littoralis plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
