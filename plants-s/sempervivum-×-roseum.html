Sempervivum × roseum

Sempervivum × roseum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, thick leaves that range in color from green to shades of pink and red. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Sempervivum × roseum is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
