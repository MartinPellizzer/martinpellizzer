Sorbus pohuashanensis var. amurensis

Sorbus pohuashanensis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler regions.
