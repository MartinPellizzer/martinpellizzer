Scirpus setaceus var. gracillimus

Scirpus setaceus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
