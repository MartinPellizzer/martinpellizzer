Symbolanthus pascoensis

Symbolanthus pascoensis, commonly known as the Pasco symbolanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Symbolanthus pascoensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
