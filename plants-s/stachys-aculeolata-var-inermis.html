Stachys aculeolata var. inermis

Stachys aculeolata var. inermis, commonly known as velvetleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
