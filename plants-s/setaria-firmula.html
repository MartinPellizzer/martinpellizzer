Setaria firmula

Setaria firmula, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Setaria firmula is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
