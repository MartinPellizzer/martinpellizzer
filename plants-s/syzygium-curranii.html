Syzygium curranii

Syzygium curranii, commonly known as the Curran's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a round, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, and is edible, though not widely cultivated for commercial use.
