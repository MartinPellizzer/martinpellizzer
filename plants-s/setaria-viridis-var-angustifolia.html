Setaria viridis var. angustifolia

Setaria viridis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to temperate regions.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is often cultivated as a forage crop and has been used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.
