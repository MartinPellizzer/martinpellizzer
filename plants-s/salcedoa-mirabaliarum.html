Salcedoa mirabaliarum

Salcedoa mirabaliarum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Salcedoa mirabaliarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
