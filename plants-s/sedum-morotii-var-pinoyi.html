Sedum morotii var. pinoyi

Sedum morotii var. pinoyi, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
