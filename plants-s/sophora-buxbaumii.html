Sophora buxbaumii

Sophora buxbaumii, commonly known as the Buxbaum's silk tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
