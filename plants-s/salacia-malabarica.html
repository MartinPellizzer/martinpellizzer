Salacia malabarica

Salacia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar salacia, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
