Senecio pagosanus

Senecio pagosanus, commonly known as the Pagosa Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the southern Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Senecio pagosanus is a perennial herb that plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
