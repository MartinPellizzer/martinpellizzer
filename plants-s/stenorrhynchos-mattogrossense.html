Stenorrhynchos mattogrossense

Stenorrhynchos mattogrossense, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Brazilian Amazon.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense rainforest canopy. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of the region.
