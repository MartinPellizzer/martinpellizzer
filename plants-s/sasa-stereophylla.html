Sasa stereophylla

Sasa stereophylla, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are often described as having a metallic sheen. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa stereophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts and cuisine.
