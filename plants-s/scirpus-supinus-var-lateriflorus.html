Scirpus supinus var. lateriflorus

Scirpus supinus var. lateriflorus, commonly known as the upland bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush genus Scirpus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flower heads are composed of small, clustered spikes.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
