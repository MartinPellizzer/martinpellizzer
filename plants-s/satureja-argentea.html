Satureja argentea

Satureja argentea, commonly known as silver savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme and oregano, and is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Satureja argentea is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
