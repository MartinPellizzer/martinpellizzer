Spiraea eximia

Spiraea eximia, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Spiraea eximia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
