Schedonorus pratensis subsp. apenninus

Schedonorus pratensis subsp.

apenninus, commonly known as the Italian meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense, low-growing clumps.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of biodiversity in its native habitats.
