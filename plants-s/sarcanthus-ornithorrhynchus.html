Sarcanthus ornithorrhynchus

Sarcanthus ornithorrhynchus, commonly known as the bird-beak cactus, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, bird-like appearance, with a narrow, tapering stem that resembles a beak. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It has adapted to survive in harsh, low-water environments through its succulent stems, which store moisture efficiently.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar to pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
