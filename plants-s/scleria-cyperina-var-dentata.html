Scleria cyperina var. dentata

Scleria cyperina var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, three-angled stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Scleria cyperina var. dentata is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
