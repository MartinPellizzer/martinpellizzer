Sisymbrium coronopifolium

Sisymbrium coronopifolium, commonly known as crownleaf mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of western North America. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
