Stevia salicifolia var. anathera

Stevia salicifolia var. anathera, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and inconspicuous, typically blooming in late summer.

This variety is valued for its intense sweetness and is often used as a sugar substitute in culinary and medicinal applications.
