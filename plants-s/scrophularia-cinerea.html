Scrophularia cinerea

Scrophularia cinerea, commonly known as hoary scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Scrophularia cinerea is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar morphological features.
