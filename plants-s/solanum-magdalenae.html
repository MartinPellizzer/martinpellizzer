Solanum magdalenae

Solanum magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Solanum magdalenae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution in its native habitat.
