Salvia marashica

Salvia marashica, commonly known as the Marash sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia marashica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
