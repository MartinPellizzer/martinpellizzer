Spartium multicaule

Spartium multicaule, commonly known as redhead grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight hairy texture.

Spartium multicaule is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
