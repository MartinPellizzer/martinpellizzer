Salix pedicellata var. santamariae

Salix pedicellata var. santamariae, commonly known as the Santa Maria willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
