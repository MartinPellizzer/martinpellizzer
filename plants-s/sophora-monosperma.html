Sophora monosperma

Sophora monosperma, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain a single, sticky seed, giving it its scientific name *monosperma*. Its roots have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties, including treatments for inflammation and skin conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
