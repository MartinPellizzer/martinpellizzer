Solanum burtonii

Solanum burtonii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that start green and ripen to a bright orange or yellow color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum burtonii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
