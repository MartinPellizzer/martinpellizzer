Santiria fasciculata

Santiria fasciculata, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Santiria fasciculata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional herbal remedies.
