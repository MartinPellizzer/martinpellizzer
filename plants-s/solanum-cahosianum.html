Solanum cahosianum

Solanum cahosianum, commonly known as the Cahosian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

This species is characterized by its adaptability to harsh, mountainous environments and its potential medicinal properties.
