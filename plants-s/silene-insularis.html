Silene insularis

Silene insularis, commonly known as the island catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene insularis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

It is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
