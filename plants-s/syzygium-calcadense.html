Syzygium calcadense

Syzygium calcadense, commonly known as the white lily or white syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental flowers and has traditional uses in some cultures.
