Saxifraga rotundifolia subsp. rotundifolia

Saxifraga rotundifolia subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and tundra environments. Saxifraga rotundifolia subsp.

rotundifolia is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
