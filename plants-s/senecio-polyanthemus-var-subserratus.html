Senecio polyanthemus var. subserratus

Senecio polyanthemus var. subserratus, commonly known as the serrated blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, showy clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
