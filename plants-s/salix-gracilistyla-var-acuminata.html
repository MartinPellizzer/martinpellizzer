Salix gracilistyla var. acuminata

Salix gracilistyla var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
