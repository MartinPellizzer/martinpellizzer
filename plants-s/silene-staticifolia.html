Silene staticifolia

Silene staticifolia, commonly known as the sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its often arid habitat. It produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Silene staticifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in disturbed environments.
