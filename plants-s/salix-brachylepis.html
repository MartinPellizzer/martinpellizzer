Salix brachylepis

Salix brachylepis, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth margin and a pale green underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
