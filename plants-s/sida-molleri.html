Sida molleri

Sida molleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields due to its adaptability. Sida molleri is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
