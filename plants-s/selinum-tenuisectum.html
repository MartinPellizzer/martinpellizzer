Selinum tenuisectum

Selinum tenuisectum, commonly known as slender chervil, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in herbal medicine.
