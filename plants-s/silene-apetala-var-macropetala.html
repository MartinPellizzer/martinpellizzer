Silene apetala var. macropetala

Silene apetala var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces slender, branching stems. Its flowers are typically showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
