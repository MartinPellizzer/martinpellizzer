Sesamum christenhuszianum

Sesamum christenhuszianum, commonly known as the African sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing numerous seeds. These seeds are rich in oil and are used both as a food source and for medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and herbal medicine.
