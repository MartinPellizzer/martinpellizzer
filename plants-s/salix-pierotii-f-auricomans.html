Salix pierotii f. auricomans

Salix pierotii f. auricomans, commonly known as the golden willow, is a unique form of the silver willow.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast with its grayish bark. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
