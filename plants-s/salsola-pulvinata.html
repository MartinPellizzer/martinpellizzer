Salsola pulvinata

Salsola pulvinata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

Salsola pulvinata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and wastelands.
