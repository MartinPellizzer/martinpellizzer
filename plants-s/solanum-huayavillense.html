Solanum huayavillense

Solanum huayavillense, commonly known as the Huayavillense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
