Suaeda anatolica

Suaeda anatolica, commonly known as Turkish saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It is native to regions of the Mediterranean, including Turkey, Greece, and parts of the Middle East. The plant features succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in dry soils.

Suaeda anatolica plays a role in stabilizing coastal and saline soils, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
