Satureja kunthii var. nervosa

Satureja kunthii var. nervosa, commonly known as narrow-leaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive minty fragrance, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
