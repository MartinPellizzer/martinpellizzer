Syneilesis aconitifolia var. longilepis

Syneilesis aconitifolia var. longilepis, commonly known as the long-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, large, palmate leaves.

The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five lobes that curve outward. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive, waxy texture, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The species is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
