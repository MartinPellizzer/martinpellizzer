Stipagrostis foexiana

Stipagrostis foexiana, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.

Stipagrostis foexiana is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
