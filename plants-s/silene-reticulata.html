Silene reticulata

Silene reticulata, commonly known as the nettleleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, giving it its common name.

Silene reticulata is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in the summer months.
