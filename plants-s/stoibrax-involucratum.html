Stoibrax involucratum

Stoibrax involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its adaptive features that allow it to survive in harsh conditions.
