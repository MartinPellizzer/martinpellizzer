Sulcorebutia rubroaurea

Sulcorebutia rubroaurea, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a compact, low-growing species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a dense cluster of cylindrical stems covered in yellowish to reddish spines, giving it its striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique coloration and compact form make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
