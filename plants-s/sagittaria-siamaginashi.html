Sagittaria siamaginashi

Sagittaria siamaginashi, commonly known as the Siam arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom above water. This aquatic plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in marshy areas.

The plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
