Sagenia siifolia

Sagenia siifolia, commonly known as the slender sage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
