Sinapistrum triphyllum

Sinapistrum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
