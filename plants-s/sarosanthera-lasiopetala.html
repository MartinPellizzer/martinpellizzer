Sarosanthera lasiopetala

Sarosanthera lasiopetala, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
