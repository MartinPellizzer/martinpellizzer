Swartzia monachiana

Swartzia monachiana, commonly known as the monk's wood or yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Swartzia monachiana is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
