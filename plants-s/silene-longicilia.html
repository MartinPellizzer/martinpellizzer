Silene longicilia

Silene longicilia, commonly known as the long-hair catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. Silene longicilia is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
