Strobus parviflora

Strobus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fir, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cone-like structures, which contain its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Strobus parviflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
