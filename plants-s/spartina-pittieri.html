Spartina pittieri

Spartina pittieri, commonly known as saltgrass, is a halophyte native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, including tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has rhizomes that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels.

Spartina pittieri plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
