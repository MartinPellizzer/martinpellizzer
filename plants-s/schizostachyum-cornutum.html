Schizostachyum cornutum

Schizostachyum cornutum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or tall bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense, feathery appearance. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in wet, well-drained soils.

Schizostachyum cornutum is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials due to its rapid growth and durability.
