Salsola flavescens

Salsola flavescens, commonly known as bluish wormwood, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, and has adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture. It is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Salsola flavescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
