Sarcotheca laxa var. sericea

Sarcotheca laxa var. sericea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance.

This species is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
