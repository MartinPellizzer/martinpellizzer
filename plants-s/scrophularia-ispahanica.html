Scrophularia ispahanica

Scrophularia ispahanica, commonly known as the Persian figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with purple markings. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Scrophularia ispahanica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
