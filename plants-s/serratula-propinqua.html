Serratula propinqua

Serratula propinqua, commonly known as the common sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving it its scientific name.

Serratula propinqua is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
