Shorea assamica subsp. koordersii

Shorea assamica subsp.

koordersii, commonly known as the Assam teak, is a species of tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall stature and valuable timber.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.
