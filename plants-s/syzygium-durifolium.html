Syzygium durifolium

Syzygium durifolium, commonly known as the hard-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that provide drought resistance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium durifolium is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a notable species in Australian flora.
