Sorbus teodori

Sorbus teodori, commonly known as the Teodor's rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in late summer and are edible for birds and some mammals.

Sorbus teodori is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.
