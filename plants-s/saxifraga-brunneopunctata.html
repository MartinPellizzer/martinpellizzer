Saxifraga brunneopunctata

Saxifraga brunneopunctata, commonly known as the brown-spotted saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, dark green leaves that often bear brownish or reddish spots. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga brunneopunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
