Salvia congestiflora

Salvia congestiflora, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in dense clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
