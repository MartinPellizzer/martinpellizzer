Silene rubella var. bifida

Silene rubella var. bifida, commonly known as the pink-stemmed catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, clustered pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

Silene rubella var. bifida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
