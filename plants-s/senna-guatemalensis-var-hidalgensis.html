Senna guatemalensis var. hidalgensis

Senna guatemalensis var. hidalgensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are typically bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
