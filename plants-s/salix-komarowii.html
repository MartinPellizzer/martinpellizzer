Salix komarowii

Salix komarowii, commonly known as the Komarov's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its male and female flowers, and it is often found growing in rocky or alpine environments. It has a moderate growth rate and is adapted to cold climates with high altitudes.

Salix komarowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
