Spiesia leucantha

Spiesia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Spiesia leucantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
