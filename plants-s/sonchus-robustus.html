Sonchus robustus

Sonchus robustus, commonly known as common sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of spiny, hairy leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
