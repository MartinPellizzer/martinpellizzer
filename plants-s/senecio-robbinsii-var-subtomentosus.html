Senecio robbinsii var. subtomentosus

Senecio robbinsii var. subtomentosus, commonly known as the Robbin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense, soft pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
