Saxifraga porsildiana

Saxifraga porsildiana, commonly known as the Porsild's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the short summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Saxifraga porsildiana is often found in alpine and tundra habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
