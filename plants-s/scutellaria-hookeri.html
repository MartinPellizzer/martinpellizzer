Scutellaria hookeri

Scutellaria hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Scutellaria hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous medicinal practices.
