Sinia

Sinia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn silver in the autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
