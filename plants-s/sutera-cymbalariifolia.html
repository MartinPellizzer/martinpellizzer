Sutera cymbalariifolia

Sutera cymbalariifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble coral or clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and a woody, branching structure. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sutera cymbalariifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
