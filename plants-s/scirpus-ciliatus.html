Scirpus ciliatus

Scirpus ciliatus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy margin that gives it its scientific name.

Scirpus ciliatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
