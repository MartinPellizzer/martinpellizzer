Syzygium attenuatum var. ophirense

Syzygium attenuatum var. ophirense, commonly known as the Ophirense tamarind.

This plant is a variant of the tamarind tree, characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that hang in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in axillary clusters.

The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates, often found in humid, lowland areas.
