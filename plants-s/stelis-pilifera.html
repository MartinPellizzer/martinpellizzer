Stelis pilifera

Stelis pilifera, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Stelis pilifera is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
