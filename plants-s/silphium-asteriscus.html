Silphium asteriscus

Silphium asteriscus, commonly known as the eastern silphium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Silphium asteriscus is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands.

It is a perennial plant that can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking addition to native plant gardens.
