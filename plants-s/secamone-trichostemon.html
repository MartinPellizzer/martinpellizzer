Secamone trichostemon

Secamone trichostemon, commonly known as the African balm or African verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
