Solanum cutacense

Solanum cutacense, commonly known as the Cutacense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in late summer.

Solanum cutacense is often found in dry, open habitats and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.
