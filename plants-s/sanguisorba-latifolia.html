Sanguisorba latifolia

Sanguisorba latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, reddish flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a tangy, sour taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Sanguisorba latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
