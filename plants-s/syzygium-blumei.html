Syzygium blumei

Syzygium blumei, commonly known as the Blume's apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, apple-like fruits. Syzygium blumei thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a notable species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
