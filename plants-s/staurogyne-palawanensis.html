Staurogyne palawanensis

Staurogyne palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan water clover, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Palawan, Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that form a rosette-like structure, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on short stems, and it is adapted to thrive in slow-moving or still waters. Staurogyne palawanensis plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality maintenance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
