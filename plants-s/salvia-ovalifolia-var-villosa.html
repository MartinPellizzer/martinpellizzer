Salvia ovalifolia var. villosa

Salvia ovalifolia var. villosa, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that are oval in shape and often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both gardens and ecological landscapes.
