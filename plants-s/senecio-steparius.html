Senecio steparius

Senecio steparius, commonly known as the mountain ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming from spring to early summer.

Senecio steparius is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
