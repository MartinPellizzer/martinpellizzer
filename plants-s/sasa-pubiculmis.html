Sasa pubiculmis

Sasa pubiculmis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa pubiculmis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
