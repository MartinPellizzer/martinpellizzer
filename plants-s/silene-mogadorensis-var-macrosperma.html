Silene mogadorensis var. macrosperma

Silene mogadorensis var. macrosperma, commonly known as the Mogador carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is noted for its large, showy seeds, which contribute to its ornamental value.
