Solanum tovarii

Solanum tovarii, commonly known as the Tovar's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and ovate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum tovarii belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes, and it contains alkaloids that can be toxic to humans and animals.
