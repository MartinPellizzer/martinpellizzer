Salvia subincisa

Salvia subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-blue hue, while its flowers are violet to blue in color.

Salvia subincisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
