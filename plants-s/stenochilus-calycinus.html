Stenochilus calycinus

Stenochilus calycinus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stenochilus calycinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
