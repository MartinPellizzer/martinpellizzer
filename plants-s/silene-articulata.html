Silene articulata

Silene articulata, commonly known as the jointed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds.

Silene articulata is adapted to cold climates and is an important part of the local flora in its native range.
