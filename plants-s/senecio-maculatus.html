Senecio maculatus

Senecio maculatus, commonly known as the African violet or the spotted knapweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive spotted or streaked appearance.

Senecio maculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
