Secamone lenticellata

Secamone lenticellata, commonly known as the rough-seeded secamone, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The leaves and roots are used in herbal remedies to treat various ailments, including skin conditions and respiratory issues.
