Senna durangensis

Senna durangensis, commonly known as Durango senna, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Senna durangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
