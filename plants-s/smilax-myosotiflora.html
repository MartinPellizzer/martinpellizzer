Smilax myosotiflora

Smilax myosotiflora, commonly known as the blue false Solomon's seal, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a woody stem with spiny nodes and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a dark blue to black berry that contains several seeds.

Smilax myosotiflora is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
