Stereospermum cinereoviride

Stereospermum cinereoviride, commonly known as the ash-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually ash-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive reddish tint when exposed.

Stereospermum cinereoviride is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
