Stranvaesia scandens

Stranvaesia scandens, commonly known as the climbing false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows along the ground or climbs supports with its twining stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Stranvaesia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
