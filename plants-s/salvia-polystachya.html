Salvia polystachya

Salvia polystachya, commonly known as the Mexican bush sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowering stems are often branched, producing multiple spikes of flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia polystachya is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
