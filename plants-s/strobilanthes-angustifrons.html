Strobilanthes angustifrons

Strobilanthes angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved makepeace, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
