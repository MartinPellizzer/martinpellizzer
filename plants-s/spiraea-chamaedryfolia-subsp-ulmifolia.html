Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp. ulmifolia

Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp.

ulmifolia, commonly known as the European meadow spiraea, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves.

The leaves are usually dark green on top and greyish-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer, which are arranged in corymbs.

This species is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
