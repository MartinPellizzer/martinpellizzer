Stachys chamaesideritis

Stachys chamaesideritis, commonly known as creeping betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
