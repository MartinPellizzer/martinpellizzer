Syngonanthus fertilis var. huberi

Syngonanthus fertilis var. huberi, commonly known as the Huberi's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and intricate, spider-like flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
