Solanum stenotomum f. alkka-pitiquina

Solanum stenotomum f. alkka-pitiquina, commonly known as the Pitiquina nightshade, is a rare and unique botanical variant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy foliage, which are adaptations to its native environment. It thrives in humid, shaded areas and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and distinct morphological features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
