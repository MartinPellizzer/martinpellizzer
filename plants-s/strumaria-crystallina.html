Strumaria crystallina

Strumaria crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
