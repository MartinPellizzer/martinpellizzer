Salacia livingstonii

Salacia livingstonii, commonly known as the Livingston's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a reddish stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

Salacia livingstonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
