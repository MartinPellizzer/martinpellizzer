Sida rajoides

Sida rajoides, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Sida rajoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.

Its fibrous stems have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
