Smythea papuana

Smythea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan smythea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Smythea papuana is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
