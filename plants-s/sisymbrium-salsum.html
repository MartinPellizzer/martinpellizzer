Sisymbrium salsum

Sisymbrium salsum, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy, with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender, segmented pod that splits open to release the seeds.

Sisymbrium salsum is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.
