Sekika sendaica

Sekika sendaica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
