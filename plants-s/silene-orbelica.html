Silene orbelica

Silene orbelica, commonly known as the Orbelica campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Silene orbelica is often found in meadows and open woodlands at higher elevations.
