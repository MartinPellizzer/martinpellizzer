Schoenus nitens var. concinnus

Schoenus nitens var. concinnus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing shoreline sediments.
