Siphocampylus lamarckii var. sinuatus

Siphocampylus lamarckii var. sinuatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaved siphocampylus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that have a distinct wavy margin. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
