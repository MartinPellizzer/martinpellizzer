Sonerila gracilis

Sonerila gracilis, commonly known as the slender sonerila, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
