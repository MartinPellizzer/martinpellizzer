Syringa vulgaris

Syringa vulgaris, commonly known as lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 6 meters and has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Syringa vulgaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
