Scolymocephalus lepidocarpodendron

Scolymocephalus lepidocarpodendron, commonly known as the cycadeoid, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the late Paleozoic era.

It belongs to the order Scolymocephalales and is characterized by its unique, fern-like fronds and a short, trunk-like stem. The plant's reproductive structures, known as strobili, were similar to those of modern conifers, suggesting a transitional form between ferns and seed plants. Its fossils are often found in coal deposits, indicating its importance in ancient ecosystems.

The study of Scolymocephalus lepidocarpodendron provides valuable insights into the evolution of early seed plants.
