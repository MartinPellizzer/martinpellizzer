Stachys limitanea

Stachys limitanea, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Stachys limitanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
