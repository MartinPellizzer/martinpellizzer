Selaginella bella

Selaginella bella, commonly known as the pretty spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, needle-like leaves and a creeping stem that can produce upright shoots. Selaginella bella is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its capacity to retain moisture.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
