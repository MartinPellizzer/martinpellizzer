Staavia glaucescens

Staavia glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, giving the plant its common name.

Staavia glaucescens is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance.
