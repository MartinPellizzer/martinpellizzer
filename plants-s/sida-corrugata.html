Sida corrugata

Sida corrugata, commonly known as rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish and are arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
