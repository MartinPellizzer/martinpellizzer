Serratula xeranthemoides

Serratula xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert sneaker, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for xeriscaping.
