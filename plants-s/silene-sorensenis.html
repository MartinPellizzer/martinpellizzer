Silene sorensenis

Silene sorensenis, commonly known as the Sorensen's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has a low, mat-like growth form. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Silene sorensenis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying Arctic flora.
