Swertia yezo-alpina

Swertia yezo-alpina, commonly known as Yezo gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia yezo-alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
