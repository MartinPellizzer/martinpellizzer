Siphocampylus longipedunculatus var. meiopodus

Siphocampylus longipedunculatus var. meiopodus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features elongated, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
