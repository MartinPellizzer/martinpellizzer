Saxifraga oppositifolia blepharophylla

Saxifraga oppositifolia blepharophylla, commonly known as the Arctic pink flower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces small, pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, nutrient-poor soils. It has a short growing season and is often found in tundra and mountain environments.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement.
