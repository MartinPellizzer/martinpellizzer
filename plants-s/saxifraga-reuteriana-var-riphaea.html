Saxifraga reuteriana var. riphaea

Saxifraga reuteriana var. riphaea, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, mineral-rich soils. The plant features rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
