Scandix apiculata

Scandix apiculata, commonly known as the sharp-tailed mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small white to greenish flowers in clusters.

Scandix apiculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
