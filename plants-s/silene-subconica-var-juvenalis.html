Silene subconica var. juvenalis

Silene subconica var. juvenalis, commonly known as the dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky habitats in the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often folded or rolled, a characteristic feature of the genus Silene. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is distinguished by its compact size and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
