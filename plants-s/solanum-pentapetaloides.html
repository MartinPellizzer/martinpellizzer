Solanum pentapetaloides

Solanum pentapetaloides, commonly known as the five-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers with five petals, typically blooming in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
