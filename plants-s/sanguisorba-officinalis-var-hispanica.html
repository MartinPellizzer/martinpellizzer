Sanguisorba officinalis var. hispanica

Sanguisorba officinalis var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
