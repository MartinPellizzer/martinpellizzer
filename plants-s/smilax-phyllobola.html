Smilax phyllobola

Smilax phyllobola, commonly known as the broadleaf smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Smilax phyllobola is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
