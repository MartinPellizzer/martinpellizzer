Saxifraga atrata var. subcorymbosa

Saxifraga atrata var. subcorymbosa, commonly known as the black saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in exposed and harsh environments. The plant features dark green, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in low-nutrient conditions.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often found in mountainous and tundra habitats.
