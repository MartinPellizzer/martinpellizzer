Selago lepidioides

Selago lepidioides, commonly known as the silverleaf selago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Selago lepidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.
