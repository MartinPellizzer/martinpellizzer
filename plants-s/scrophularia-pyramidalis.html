Scrophularia pyramidalis

Scrophularia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces tall, sparsely branched stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy.

Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
