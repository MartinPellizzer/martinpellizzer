Selbyana velizii

Selbyana velizii, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing small, dark seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
