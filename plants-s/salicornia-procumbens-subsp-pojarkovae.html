Salicornia procumbens subsp. pojarkovae

Salicornia procumbens subsp.

pojarkovae, commonly known as the coastal glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This subspecies thrives in coastal areas, including salt marshes and tidal flats, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms. Its flowering stems are typically reddish or pinkish, bearing small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in some regions for culinary and medicinal purposes.
