Silene clavicarpa

Silene clavicarpa, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, club-shaped seed capsules that give it its scientific name. Silene clavicarpa is typically found in moist, grassy habitats and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its native environment.
