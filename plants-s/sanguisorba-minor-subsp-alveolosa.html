Sanguisorba minor subsp. alveolosa

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

alveolosa, commonly known as the alveolose dock, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.
