Senecio neoglandulosus

Senecio neoglandulosus, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color, while its flowers are yellow and resemble dandelions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
