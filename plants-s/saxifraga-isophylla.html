Saxifraga isophylla

Saxifraga isophylla, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, often blue or greenish leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga isophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
