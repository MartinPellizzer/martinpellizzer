Silene caucasica var. pamirensis

Silene caucasica var. pamirensis, commonly known as the Pamir catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Pamir Mountains.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh climate of its habitat.

Silene caucasica var. pamirensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of mountainous regions.
