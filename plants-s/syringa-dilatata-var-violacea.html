Syringa dilatata var. violacea

Syringa dilatata var. violacea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
