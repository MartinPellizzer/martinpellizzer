Swartzia discolor

Swartzia discolor, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Swartzia discolor is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
