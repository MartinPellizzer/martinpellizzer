Sullivania ferricola

Sullivania ferricola, commonly known as the ironwood plant, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Sullivania ferricola is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
