Simonisia rosea var. parviflora

Simonisia rosea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rose-scented simonisia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
