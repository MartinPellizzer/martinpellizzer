Salvia pratensis

Salvia pratensis, commonly known as meadow sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Salvia pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
