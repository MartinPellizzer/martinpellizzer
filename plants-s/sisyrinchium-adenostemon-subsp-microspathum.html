Sisyrinchium adenostemon subsp. microspathum

Sisyrinchium adenostemon subsp.

microspathum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Iridaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or violet flowers.

Native to the western United States, particularly California, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
