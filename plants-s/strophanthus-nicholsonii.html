Strophanthus nicholsonii

Strophanthus nicholsonii, commonly known as the red African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
