Syzygium suringarianum

Syzygium suringarianum, commonly known as the Sunda apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, red or yellow fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, tart flavor and is often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
