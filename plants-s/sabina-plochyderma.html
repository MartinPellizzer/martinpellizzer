Sabina plochyderma

Sabina plochyderma, commonly known as the flatleaf juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically flat and arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in temperate regions.
