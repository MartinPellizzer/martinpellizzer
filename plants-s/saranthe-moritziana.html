Saranthe moritziana

Saranthe moritziana, commonly known as the Moritzian lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, and its flowers emit a subtle fragrance.

Saranthe moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and resilience.
