Silene segetalis

Silene segetalis, commonly known as the field catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene segetalis is often found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
