Solanum spissifolium

Solanum spissifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by oval, blue-black berries. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum spissifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
