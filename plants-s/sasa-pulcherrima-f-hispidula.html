Sasa pulcherrima f. hispidula

Sasa pulcherrima f. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid velvet fern, is a variant of the velvet fern species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming evergreen plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, luxurious appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is frequently used in horticultural settings for its unique aesthetic.
