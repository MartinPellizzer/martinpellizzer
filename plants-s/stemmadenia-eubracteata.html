Stemmadenia eubracteata

Stemmadenia eubracteata, commonly known as the broad-bracted stemmadenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Its stem is woody and often branched, with a rough, bark-like surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
