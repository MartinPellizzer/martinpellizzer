Salix viminalis var. strobilacea

Salix viminalis var. strobilacea, commonly known as the strobilaceous willow, is a variant of the common osier dogwood.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, clustered catkins. This plant is typically found in moist, often swampy habitats across temperate regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
