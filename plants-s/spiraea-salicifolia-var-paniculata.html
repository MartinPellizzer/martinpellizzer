Spiraea salicifolia var. paniculata

Spiraea salicifolia var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its graceful, willow-like branches that cascade with clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
