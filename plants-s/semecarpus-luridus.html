Semecarpus luridus

Semecarpus luridus, commonly known as the yellow sterculia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
