Scrophularia duplicatoserrata

Scrophularia duplicatoserrata, commonly known as the double-toothed figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with doubly serrated margins. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia duplicatoserrata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates.
