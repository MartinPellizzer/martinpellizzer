Symphytum gussonei

Symphytum gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
