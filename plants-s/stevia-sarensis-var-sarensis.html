Stevia sarensis var. sarensis

Stevia sarensis var. sarensis, commonly known as the sweet leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are intensely sweet due to the presence of steviol glycosides. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming in clusters, and it is widely cultivated as a natural sweetener.

Stevia sarensis var. sarensis is valued for its medicinal properties and use in herbal remedies.
