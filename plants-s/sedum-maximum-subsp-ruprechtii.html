Sedum maximum subsp. ruprechtii

Sedum maximum subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
