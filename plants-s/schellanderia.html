Schellanderia

Schellanderia, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and showy yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators. Schellanderia species are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. The genus includes several species, such as Schellanderia lanata and Schellanderia saxatilis, which are native to regions in Europe and Asia.

Their botanical features make them valuable subjects for studies on plant adaptation and ecological resilience.
