Senecio vitifolius

Senecio vitifolius, commonly known as the wine-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio vitifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
