Sasa ohdana

Sasa ohdana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense growth habit and culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant forms clumps of slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Sasa ohdana thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories across Japan, Korea, and China.
