Sericolea gaultheria var. muelleri

Sericolea gaultheria var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's silverleaf, is a variant of the silverleaf plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery, velvety foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
