Salvia richardsonii

Salvia richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
