Schizostachyum capitatum var. decompositum

Schizostachyum capitatum var. decompositum, commonly known as the decomposed bamboo, is a variant of the native bamboo species found in Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its unique, loosely clustered inflorescences that resemble a decomposed or withered appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural strength. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated for landscape purposes.
