Spigelia faveolata

Spigelia faveolata, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Spigelia faveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
