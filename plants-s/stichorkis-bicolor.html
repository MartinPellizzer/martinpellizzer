Stichorkis bicolor

Stichorkis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that display two distinct color zones. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
