Senecio mweroensis f. schwartzii

Senecio mweroensis f. schwartzii, commonly known as the Mweru Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats, showcasing a compact growth form with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
