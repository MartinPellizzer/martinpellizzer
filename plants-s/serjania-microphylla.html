Serjania microphylla

Serjania microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf serjania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features simple, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Serjania microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
