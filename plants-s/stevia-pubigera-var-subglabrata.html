Stevia pubigera var. subglabrata

Stevia pubigera var. subglabrata, commonly known as the slender leaf stevia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it is often grown for its sweetening properties and potential health benefits.
