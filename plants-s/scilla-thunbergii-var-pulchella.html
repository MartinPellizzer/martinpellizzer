Scilla thunbergii var. pulchella

Scilla thunbergii var. pulchella, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.

It is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
