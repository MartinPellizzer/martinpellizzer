Serratula cardunculus

Serratula cardunculus, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of spiny, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom in the summer months.

Serratula cardunculus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
