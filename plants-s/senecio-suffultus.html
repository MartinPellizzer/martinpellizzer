Senecio suffultus

Senecio suffultus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
