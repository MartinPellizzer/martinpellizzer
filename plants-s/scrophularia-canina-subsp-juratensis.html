Scrophularia canina subsp. juratensis

Scrophularia canina subsp.

juratensis, commonly known as the dog's mouth or narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with toothed margins and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
