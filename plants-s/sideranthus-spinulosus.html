Sideranthus spinulosus

Sideranthus spinulosus, commonly known as the prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sideranthus spinulosus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
