Scirpus chilensis

Scirpus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers at the top of its stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in wetland environments, making it an important species for wetland restoration.

Scirpus chilensis is also valued for its ability to filter water and stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.
