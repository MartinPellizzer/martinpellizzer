Senecio elegans

Senecio elegans, commonly known as the Blueweed or Bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
