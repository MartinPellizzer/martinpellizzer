Spergula leptophylla

Spergula leptophylla, commonly known as the slender leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a flowering period that occurs in the spring.

Spergula leptophylla is often found in open habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
