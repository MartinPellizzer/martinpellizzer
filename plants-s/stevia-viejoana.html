Stevia viejoana

Stevia viejoana, commonly known as the old man stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stevia viejoana is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional medicine for treating ailments like diabetes and inflammation.

Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products.
