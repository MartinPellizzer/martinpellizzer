Syagrus hatschbachii

Syagrus hatschbachii, commonly known as the Pão de Açúcar palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, white flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, Syagrus hatschbachii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
