Setaria imberbis var. perennis

Setaria imberbis var. perennis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristly spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
