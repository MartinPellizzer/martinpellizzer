Scleranthus laxus

Scleranthus laxus, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils.

The plant has a hairy surface, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
