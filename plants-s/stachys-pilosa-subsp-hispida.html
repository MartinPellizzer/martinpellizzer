Stachys pilosa subsp. hispida

Stachys pilosa subsp.

hispida, commonly known as hispid stachys, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
