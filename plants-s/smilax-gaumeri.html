Smilax gaumeri

Smilax gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican catbriar, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax gaumeri is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
