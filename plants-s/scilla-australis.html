Scilla australis

Scilla australis, commonly known as the southern squill, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.
