Sipanea biflora

Sipanea biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered sipanea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Sipanea biflora is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
