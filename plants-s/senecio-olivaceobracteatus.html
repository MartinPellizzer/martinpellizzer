Senecio olivaceobracteatus

Senecio olivaceobracteatus, commonly known as the olive-bract senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
