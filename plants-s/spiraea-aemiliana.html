Spiraea aemiliana

Spiraea aemiliana, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its dense, rounded form. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath. Its most striking feature is its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Spiraea aemiliana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
