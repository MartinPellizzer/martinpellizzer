Stryphnodendron rizzinianum

Stryphnodendron rizzinianum, commonly known as Brazilian yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous covering. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
