Sararanga

Sararanga, commonly known as the "blue morning glory," is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender stems that can climb up to 10 meters. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sararanga is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
