Sauropus glaucus

Sauropus glaucus, commonly known as the silverleaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
