Satureja obovata var. hispalensis

Satureja obovata var. hispalensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their distinctive, mint-like fragrance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.
