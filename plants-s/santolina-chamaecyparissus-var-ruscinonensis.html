Santolina chamaecyparissus var. ruscinonensis

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the ruskin's santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-gray, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and drought-tolerant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
