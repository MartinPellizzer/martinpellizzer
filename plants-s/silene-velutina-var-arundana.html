Silene velutina var. arundana

Silene velutina var. arundana, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and swampy woodlands. Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

Silene velutina var. arundana is often found in association with other wetland flora and is considered an indicator species for acidic, nutrient-poor soils.
