Salix melanopsis

Salix melanopsis, commonly known as black willow, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Salix melanopsis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
