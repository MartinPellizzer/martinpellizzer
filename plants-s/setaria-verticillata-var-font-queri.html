Setaria verticillata var. font-queri

Setaria verticillata var. font-queri, commonly known as Font-Queri millet, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for marginal lands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional agriculture.
