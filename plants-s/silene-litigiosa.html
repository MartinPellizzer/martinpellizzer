Silene litigiosa

Silene litigiosa, commonly known as the battleflower, is a striking plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene litigiosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
