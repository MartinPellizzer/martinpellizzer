Schoenoplectus mucronatus f. brevisetaceus

Schoenoplectus mucronatus f. brevisetaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil and supports aquatic ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and short, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other bulrush species.

It plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
