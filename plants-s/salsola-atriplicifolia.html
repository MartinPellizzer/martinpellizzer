Salsola atriplicifolia

Salsola atriplicifolia, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.

Salsola atriplicifolia is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
