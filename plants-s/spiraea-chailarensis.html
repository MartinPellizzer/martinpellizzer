Spiraea chailarensis

Spiraea chailarensis, commonly known as the Chail river spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on the previous year's wood. Its foliage is often dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

Spiraea chailarensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
