Sideritis incana var. tomentosa

Sideritis incana var. tomentosa, commonly known as woolly hairweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
