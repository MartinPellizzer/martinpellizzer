Silene gigantea subsp. gigantea

Silene gigantea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant catchfly, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy pink or red flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue. Silene gigantea subsp.

gigantea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
