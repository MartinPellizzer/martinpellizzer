Schizogyne

Schizogyne, commonly known as the false indigo or Schizogyne stellata, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Schizogyne is often found in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
