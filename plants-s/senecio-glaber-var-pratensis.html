Senecio glaber var. pratensis

Senecio glaber var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow senecio, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
