Scilla cupanii

Scilla cupanii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact size and is often grown in rock gardens or as a container plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla cupanii is known for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
