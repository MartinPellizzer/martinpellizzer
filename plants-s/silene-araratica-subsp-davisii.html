Silene araratica subsp. davisii

Silene araratica subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's campion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often glandular and slightly sticky.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
