Sasa nipponica var. mikawana

Sasa nipponica var. mikawana, commonly known as the Mikawa bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It features slender, upright culms that are typically green, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
