Stryphnodendron guianense var. roseiflorum

Stryphnodendron guianense var. roseiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered guianense.

It is a species of legume native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its distinctive rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
