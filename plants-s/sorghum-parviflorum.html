Sorghum parviflorum

Sorghum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower sorghum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance.

Sorghum parviflorum is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
