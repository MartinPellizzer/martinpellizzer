Solanum dendrophilum

Solanum dendrophilum, commonly known as the tree tomato or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, star-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is edible, with a tart flavor and a crisp texture, often used in culinary applications.

Solanum dendrophilum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
