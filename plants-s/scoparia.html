Scoparia

Scoparia, commonly known as field scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Scoparia has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
