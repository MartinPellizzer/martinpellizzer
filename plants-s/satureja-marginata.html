Satureja marginata

Satureja marginata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Satureja marginata is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
