Styrax ovatus

Styrax ovatus, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
