Salvia cyanicephala

Salvia cyanicephala, commonly known as the blue-headed sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and aromatic foliage.
