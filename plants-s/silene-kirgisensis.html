Silene kirgisensis

Silene kirgisensis, commonly known as the Kirghiz campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Silene kirgisensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
