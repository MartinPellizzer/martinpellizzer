Sesamoides purpurascens

Sesamoides purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered sesamoid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, featuring two lips and five stamens, which are typical of the Acanthaceae family.

Sesamoides purpurascens is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.
