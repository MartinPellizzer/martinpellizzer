Spiraea teniana var. mairei

Spiraea teniana var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal interest throughout the year.
