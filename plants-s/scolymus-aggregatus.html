Scolymus aggregatus

Scolymus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with dark centers.

Scolymus aggregatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
