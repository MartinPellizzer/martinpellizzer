Sarcomphalus thyrsiflorus

Sarcomphalus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
