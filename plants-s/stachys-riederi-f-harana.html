Stachys riederi f. harana

Stachys riederi f. harana, commonly known as the Harana stachys, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
