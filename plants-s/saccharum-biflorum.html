Saccharum biflorum

Saccharum biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower sugarcane.

It is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often arranged in pairs, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to wetland environments and can thrive in muddy soils.

While not commonly cultivated for sugar production, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
