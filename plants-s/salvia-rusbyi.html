Salvia rusbyi

Salvia rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued in both horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
