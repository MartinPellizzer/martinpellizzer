Sideritis gudarica

Sideritis gudarica, commonly known as the Gudarica ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Albania and parts of southern Italy.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, small oval leaves, and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. Sideritis gudarica is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
