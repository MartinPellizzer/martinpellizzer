Sasa megalophylla f. pankensis

Sasa megalophylla f. pankensis, commonly known as the Pankenshima bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and structural form.
