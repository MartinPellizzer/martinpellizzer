Streptopus amplexifolius f. indivisus

Streptopus amplexifolius f. indivisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf starwort, is a rare and distinctive variant of the starwort genus.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often tightly clasping the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a star-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
