Sedum rhodiola var. viride

Sedum rhodiola var. viride, commonly known as the green sedum, is a variant of the sedum species characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, and it is drought-tolerant, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
