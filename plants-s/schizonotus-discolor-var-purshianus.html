Schizonotus discolor var. purshianus

Schizonotus discolor var. purshianus, commonly known as the Pursh's false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is noted for its distinctive varietal features, including its pale green to white undersides on the leaves.
