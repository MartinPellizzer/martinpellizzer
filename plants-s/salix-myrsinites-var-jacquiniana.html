Salix myrsinites var. jacquiniana

Salix myrsinites var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
