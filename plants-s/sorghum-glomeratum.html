Sorghum glomeratum

Sorghum glomeratum, commonly known as clustered sorghum, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, clustered flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Sorghum glomeratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
