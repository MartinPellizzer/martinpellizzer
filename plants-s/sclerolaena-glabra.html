Sclerolaena glabra

Sclerolaena glabra, commonly known as the smooth sclerolaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

Sclerolaena glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
