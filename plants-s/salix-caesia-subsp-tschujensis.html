Salix caesia subsp. tschujensis

Salix caesia subsp.

tschujensis, commonly known as the Tschujan willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
