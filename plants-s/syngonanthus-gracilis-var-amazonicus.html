Syngonanthus gracilis var. amazonicus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian slender fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, light green fronds that grow in a compact, upright habit. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of organic matter in the soil. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
