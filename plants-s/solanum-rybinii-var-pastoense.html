Solanum rybinii var. pastoense

Solanum rybinii var. pastoense, commonly known as the Pasto nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
