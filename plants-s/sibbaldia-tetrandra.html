Sibbaldia tetrandra

Sibbaldia tetrandra, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Sibbaldia tetrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
