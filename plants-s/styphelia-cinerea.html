Styphelia cinerea

Styphelia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern and western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that reflects light and helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Styphelia cinerea thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
