Syzygium diffusum var. purpureum

Syzygium diffusum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple mangostin, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, thick-skinned fruit that contains a segmented, white, edible aril. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are small and white with a pinkish tinge.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
