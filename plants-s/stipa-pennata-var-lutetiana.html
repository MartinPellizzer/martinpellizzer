Stipa pennata var. lutetiana

Stipa pennata var. lutetiana, commonly known as the Lutetian oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like in structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its contribution to biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
