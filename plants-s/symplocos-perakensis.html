Symplocos perakensis

Symplocos perakensis, commonly known as the Java satinwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Symplocos perakensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
