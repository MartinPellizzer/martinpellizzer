Stachys lusitanica

Stachys lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese betony, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Stachys lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
