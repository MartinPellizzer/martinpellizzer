Stachys stricta

Stachys stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys stricta is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
