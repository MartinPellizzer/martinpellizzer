Solanum orbiculatibaccatum

Solanum orbiculatibaccatum, commonly known as the red globe nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, globe-shaped berries that are highly toxic to humans and animals. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and features simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or purple, with five petals and a short corolla.

Solanum orbiculatibaccatum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas.
