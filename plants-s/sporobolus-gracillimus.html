Sporobolus gracillimus

Sporobolus gracillimus, commonly known as slender sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and compact growth habit, which allows it to thrive in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that aids in its colonization of open, disturbed habitats.

Sporobolus gracillimus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and resist invasive species.
