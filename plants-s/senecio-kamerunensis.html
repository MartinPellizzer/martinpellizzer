Senecio kamerunensis

Senecio kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and parts of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio kamerunensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
