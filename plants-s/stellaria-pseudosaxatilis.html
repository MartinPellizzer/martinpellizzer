Stellaria pseudosaxatilis

Stellaria pseudosaxatilis, commonly known as the saxifrage starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and produces tiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species often found in mountainous and coastal regions.
