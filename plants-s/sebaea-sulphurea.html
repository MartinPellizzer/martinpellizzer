Sebaea sulphurea

Sebaea sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Sebaea sulphurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
