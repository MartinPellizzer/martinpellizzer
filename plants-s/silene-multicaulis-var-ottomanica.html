Silene multicaulis var. ottomanica

Silene multicaulis var. ottomanica, commonly known as the Ottoman pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas and open fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
