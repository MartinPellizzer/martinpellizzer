Senecio hirsutulus

Senecio hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
