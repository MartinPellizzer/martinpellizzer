Solanum sparsipilum var. llallaguanianum

Solanum sparsipilum var. llallaguanianum, commonly known as the Llallagua tomato, is a wild solanaceous species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and oval, pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in high-altitude areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to botanists and plant breeders for potential use in crop improvement.
