Sinapis tournefortii

Sinapis tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sinapis tournefortii is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of oilseed.
