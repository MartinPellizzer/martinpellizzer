Sesamum grandiflorum

Sesamum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stem, opposite leaves, and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, sesame-like seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Pedaliaceae family.
