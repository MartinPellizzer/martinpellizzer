Satureja sahendica

Satureja sahendica, commonly known as Sahendic savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, ovoid fruits.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and cuisine for their pungent, aromatic properties.
