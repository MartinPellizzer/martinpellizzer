Stephanocereus leucostele

Stephanocereus leucostele, commonly known as the white-stemmed night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems that can grow quite tall. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its structure and blooming habits make it a unique and striking member of the Cactaceae family.
