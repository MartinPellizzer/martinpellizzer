Stipa caespitosa var. lilloi

Stipa caespitosa var. lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
