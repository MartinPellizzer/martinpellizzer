Sidalcea californica

Sidalcea californica, commonly known as the California checker-mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Sidalcea californica is often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats.
