Symplocos ophirensis var. pachyphylla

Symplocos ophirensis var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
