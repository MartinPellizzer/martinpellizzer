Stevia serboana

Stevia serboana, commonly known as the Serbian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies and as a natural sweetener in some cultures.
