Sorghum nigrum

Sorghum nigrum, commonly known as black sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dark purple to black leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in some cultures for food and dye.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
