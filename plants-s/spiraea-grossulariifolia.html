Spiraea grossulariifolia

Spiraea grossulariifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea grossulariifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
