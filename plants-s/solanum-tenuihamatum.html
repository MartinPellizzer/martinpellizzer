Solanum tenuihamatum

Solanum tenuihamatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, green leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum tenuihamatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
