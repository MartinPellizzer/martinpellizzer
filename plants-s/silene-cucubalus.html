Silene cucubalus

Silene cucubalus, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bladder-like, inflated seed pods that contain the seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene cucubalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
