Senecio moqueguensis

Senecio moqueguensis, commonly known as the Quechua groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of the Andean plateau. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio moqueguensis is valued for its potential traditional uses in local medicine.
