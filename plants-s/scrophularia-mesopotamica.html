Scrophularia mesopotamica

Scrophularia mesopotamica, commonly known as Mesopotamian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Scrophularia mesopotamica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
