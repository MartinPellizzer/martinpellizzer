Syzygium fergusonii

Syzygium fergusonii, commonly known as the West Australian tallowwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southwestern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey to black bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.
