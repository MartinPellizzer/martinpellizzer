Solanum septemlobum var. subintegrifolium

Solanum septemlobum var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the seven-lobed nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct seven-lobed structure. Its flowers are typically yellow with a white or greenish throat, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
