Saxifraga cespitosa var. grandiflora

Saxifraga cespitosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of dark green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
