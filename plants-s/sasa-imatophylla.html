Sasa imatophylla

Sasa imatophylla, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or matsutake bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa imatophylla is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
