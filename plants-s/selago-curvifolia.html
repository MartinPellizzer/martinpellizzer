Selago curvifolia

Selago curvifolia, commonly known as the curved-leaved selago, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled at the edges, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Selago curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
