Sedum grisebachii subsp. kostovii

Sedum grisebachii subsp.

kostovii, commonly known as the Kostov's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
