Solidago camporum var. tricostata

Solidago camporum var. tricostata, commonly known as the three-costate goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
