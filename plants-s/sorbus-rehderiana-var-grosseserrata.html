Sorbus rehderiana var. grosseserrata

Sorbus rehderiana var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the broad-toothed whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins, which are broader and more pronounced than in the typical species. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
