Siparuna cavalcantei

Siparuna cavalcantei, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly palatable to humans. Siparuna cavalcantei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable regions.
