Sutera jurassica

Sutera jurassica, commonly known as the Jurassic suture plant, is a rare and ancient species with unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that resemble those of modern suture plants, suggesting a possible evolutionary link. The plant exhibits a complex vascular system, indicating advanced adaptations for water and nutrient transport. Its flowers, though small, are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sutera jurassica is primarily found in specific fossilized layers, offering valuable insights into prehistoric flora.
