Syzygium reticulatum

Syzygium reticulatum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit exterior, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The rambutan tree is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
