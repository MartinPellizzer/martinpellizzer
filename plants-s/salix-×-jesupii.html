Salix × jesupii

Salix × jesupii, commonly known as the Jesup's willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and often have a silvery underside.

Salix × jesupii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
