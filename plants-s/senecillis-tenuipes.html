Senecillis tenuipes

Senecillis tenuipes, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its succulent stems are capable of storing water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.

Senecillis tenuipes is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
