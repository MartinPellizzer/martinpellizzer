Silene arvatica

Silene arvatica, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene arvatica is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
