Siler latifolia

Siler latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf siler, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Siler latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
