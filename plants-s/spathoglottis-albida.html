Spathoglottis albida

Spathoglottis albida, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in a dense, truss-like arrangement. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the base.

Spathoglottis albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
