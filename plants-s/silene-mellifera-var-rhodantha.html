Silene mellifera var. rhodantha

Silene mellifera var. rhodantha, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
