Sporobolus indicus f. spiciformis

Sporobolus indicus f. spiciformis, commonly known as the spike-form spikerush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
