Spiranthes romanzoffiana var. gemmipara

Spiranthes romanzoffiana var. gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmipara ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as swamps and woodlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces vegetative offshoots, known as gemmae, which aid in propagation.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
