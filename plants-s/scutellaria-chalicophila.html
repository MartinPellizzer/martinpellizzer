Scutellaria chalicophila

Scutellaria chalicophila, commonly known as the chalice skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Scutellaria chalicophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
