Stapelia juvencula

Stapelia juvencula, commonly known as the "bald man's foot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive waxy texture. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often shed during periods of drought.

Stapelia juvencula is well-suited for rock gardens or as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
