Silene disticha

Silene disticha, commonly known as the two-branched catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene disticha typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
