Setaria oplismenoides

Setaria oplismenoides, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy cereal grain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Setaria oplismenoides is cultivated for its edible grains and is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
