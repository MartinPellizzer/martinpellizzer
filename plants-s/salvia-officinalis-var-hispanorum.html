Salvia officinalis var. hispanorum

Salvia officinalis var. hispanorum, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is closely related to common sage and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, contributing to its traditional applications in herbal medicine.
