Swida iberica

Swida iberica, commonly known as the Iberian swida, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. It is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Swida iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
