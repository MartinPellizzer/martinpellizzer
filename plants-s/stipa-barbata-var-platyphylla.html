Stipa barbata var. platyphylla

Stipa barbata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, wide leaves that are typically shorter and broader than those of the typical *S. barbata*. The plant produces slender, branched inflorescences composed of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
