Solanum drymophilum

Solanum drymophilum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wet, swampy areas, often near waterways. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by bright orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, and it is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

Solanum drymophilum plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food for certain wildlife species.
