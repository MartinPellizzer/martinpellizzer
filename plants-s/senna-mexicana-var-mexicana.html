Senna mexicana var. mexicana

Senna mexicana var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican senna, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
