Sesleria uliginosa var. neifficii

Sesleria uliginosa var. neifficii, commonly known as the bog sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wet, acidic environments in Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant thrives in moist, boggy soils and is often found in peatlands and heathlands. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for low light conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
