Senecio pringlei

Senecio pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer.

Senecio pringlei is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
