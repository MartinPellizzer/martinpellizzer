Sasa basibarbigera

Sasa basibarbigera, commonly known as the broad-leaved sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa basibarbigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
