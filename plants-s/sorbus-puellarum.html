Sorbus puellarum

Sorbus puellarum, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Sorbus puellarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in British botany.
