Scleria timorensis

Scleria timorensis, commonly known as the Timor sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps.
