Sida intermedia

Sida intermedia, commonly known as Spanish thorn or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sida intermedia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
