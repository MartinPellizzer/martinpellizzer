Syzygium oblanceolatum

Syzygium oblanceolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Syzygium oblanceolatum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
