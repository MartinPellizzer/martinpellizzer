Sagenia rufescens

Sagenia rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed sage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet, clustered in whorls along the stems.

Sagenia rufescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
