Stigmaphyllon lalandianum var. grisebachianum

Stigmaphyllon lalandianum var. grisebachianum, commonly known as the gray-stemmed stigmaphyllon, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in traditional medicine.
