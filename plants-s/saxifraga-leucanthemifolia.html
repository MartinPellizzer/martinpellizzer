Saxifraga leucanthemifolia

Saxifraga leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white thistle saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, woolly leaves and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
