Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. gallica

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

gallica, commonly known as French lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in gray-green, aromatic foliage.

The plant produces compact clusters of small, pale purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to their essential oil content.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
