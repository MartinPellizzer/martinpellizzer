Symphytum microcalyx

Symphytum microcalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comfrey, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
