Selaginella laxa

Selaginella laxa, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of the stems. Selaginella laxa is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and has a slow growth rate.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
