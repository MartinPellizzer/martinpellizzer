Sesbania sesban subsp. sesban

Sesbania sesban subsp.

sesban, commonly known as the horsegram or sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is widely cultivated for its high-protein seeds and as a forage crop.

Additionally, it plays a significant role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
