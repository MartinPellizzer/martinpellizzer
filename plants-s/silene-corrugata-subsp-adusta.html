Silene corrugata subsp. adusta

Silene corrugata subsp.

adusta, commonly known as the dark red catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems that often grow in open, rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, depending on the subspecies. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
