Scutellaria tubiflora

Scutellaria tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria tubiflora typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
