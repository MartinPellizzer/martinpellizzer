Silene polypetala

Silene polypetala, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly waxy texture.

Silene polypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
