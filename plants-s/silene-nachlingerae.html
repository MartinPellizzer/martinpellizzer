Silene nachlingerae

Silene nachlingerae, commonly known as the Nachling's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
