Syringodea patersonia

Syringodea patersonia, commonly known as the Paterson's lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, urn-shaped structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Syringodea patersonia is adapted to moist, swampy environments and is often found in areas with high water tables.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing nectar for native pollinators.
