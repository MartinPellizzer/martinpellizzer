Sorbus subfusca subsp. zinserlingii

Sorbus subfusca subsp.

zinserlingii, commonly known as the Zinserling's whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions.

The plant is characterized by its white to pale yellow fruits, which are aggregate berries composed of small drupes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
