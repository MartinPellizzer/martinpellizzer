Solanum trongum var. rumphii

Solanum trongum var. rumphii, commonly known as the Rumphii Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.
