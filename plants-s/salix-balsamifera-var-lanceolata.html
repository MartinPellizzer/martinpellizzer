Salix balsamifera var. lanceolata

Salix balsamifera var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra or wetland habitats.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold climates.
