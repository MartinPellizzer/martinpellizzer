Salix × hostii

Salix × hostii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn a striking yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
