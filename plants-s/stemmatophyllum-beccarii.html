Stemmatophyllum beccarii

Stemmatophyllum beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's stemmatophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the group of liverworts.

It is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures called thallus, which are typically green and photosynthetic. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as gametophores, are distinct and contribute to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for studying evolutionary adaptations in non-vascular plants.
