Saxifraga diversifolia f. amplexifolia

Saxifraga diversifolia f. amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping saxifrage, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This succulent plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
