Syzygium attenuatum

Syzygium attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has some ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its foliage.
