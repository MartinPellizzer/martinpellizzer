Solanum patameense var. parvifolium

Solanum patameense var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
