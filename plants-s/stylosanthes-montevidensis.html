Stylosanthes montevidensis

Stylosanthes montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo stylo, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Stylosanthes montevidensis is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and climatic conditions.
