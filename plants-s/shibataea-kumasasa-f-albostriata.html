Shibataea kumasasa f. albostriata

Shibataea kumasasa f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striated Japanese maple, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the Japanese maple species.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins contrasting against the deep green leaves. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, red flowers in the spring followed by ornamental red fruits in the fall.

This cultivar is prized for its unique foliage and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
