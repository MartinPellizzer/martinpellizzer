Sida rhombifolia var. petherickii

Sida rhombifolia var. petherickii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sida, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas, grasslands, and waste places. It has a hairy stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
