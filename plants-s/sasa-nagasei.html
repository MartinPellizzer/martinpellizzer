Sasa nagasei

Sasa nagasei, commonly known as the Nagase bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually a bright green color, and it produces small, pale green flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa nagasei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
