Scabiosa sylvatica subsp. dipsacifolia

Scabiosa sylvatica subsp.

dipsacifolia, commonly known as the wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
