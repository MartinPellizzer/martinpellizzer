Solanum schenckii

Solanum schenckii, commonly known as the Schenck's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple calyx.

The berries it produces are small, red, and contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.
