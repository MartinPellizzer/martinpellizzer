Santolina microcephala

Santolina microcephala, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina microcephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
