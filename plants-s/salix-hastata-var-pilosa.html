Salix hastata var. pilosa

Salix hastata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
