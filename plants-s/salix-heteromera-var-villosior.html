Salix heteromera var. villosior

Salix heteromera var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a variant of the slender willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its catkin flowers are a key feature, contributing to its role in early spring pollination.

The species is valued for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
