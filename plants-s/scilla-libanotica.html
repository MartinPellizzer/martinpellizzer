Scilla libanotica

Scilla libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are toxic and should not be consumed.

Scilla libanotica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
