Sisymbrium leiocarpum

Sisymbrium leiocarpum, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a parasitic species that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a fibrous root system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in association with grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host species.
