Salvia ernesti-vargasii

Salvia ernesti-vargasii, commonly known as the Vargasii sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
