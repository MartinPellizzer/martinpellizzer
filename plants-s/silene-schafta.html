Silene schafta

Silene schafta, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking pink or red flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene schafta is known for its showy inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
