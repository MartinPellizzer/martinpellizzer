Satyrium odoratissimum

Satyrium odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant satyrium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
