Sisymbrium heteromallum var. dahuricum

Sisymbrium heteromallum var. dahuricum, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is an annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
