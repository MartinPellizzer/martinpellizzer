Senecio × christensenii

Senecio × christensenii, commonly known as the Christensen's groundsel, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
