Saxifraga adscendens var. blavii

Saxifraga adscendens var. blavii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
