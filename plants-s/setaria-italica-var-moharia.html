Setaria italica var. moharia

Setaria italica var. moharia, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a small-grained cereal grass native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in dry, arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the stems being sturdy and fibrous.

This variety is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and nutritional content, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
