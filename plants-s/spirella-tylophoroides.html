Spirella tylophoroides

Spirella tylophoroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf spirea, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically opposite and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Spirella tylophoroides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
