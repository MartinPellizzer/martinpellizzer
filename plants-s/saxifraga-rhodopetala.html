Saxifraga rhodopetala

Saxifraga rhodopetala, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of showy pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Saxifraga rhodopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens.
