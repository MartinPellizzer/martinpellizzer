Suaeda transoxana

Suaeda transoxana, commonly known as the Central Asian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in regions with high salinity and low precipitation, such as deserts and coastal areas.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources in nutrient-poor soils.
