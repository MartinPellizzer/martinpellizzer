Spiesia glabra

Spiesia glabra, commonly known as the smooth spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
