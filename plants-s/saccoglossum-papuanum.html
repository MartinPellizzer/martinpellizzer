Saccoglossum papuanum

Saccoglossum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan sacoglossus, is a species of sea slug found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Sacoglossidae and is characterized by its small, shell-less body and translucent appearance. The plant, more accurately a marine gastropod mollusk, feeds primarily on algae, using its radula to scrape food from surfaces. It is notable for its ability to retain chloroplasts from its algal diet, a trait that contributes to its ecological significance in marine ecosystems.

This species plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a food source for various predators in its habitat.
