Senecio drukensis

Senecio drukensis, commonly known as the Druk Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in spring.

Senecio drukensis is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
