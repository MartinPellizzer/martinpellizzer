Stipa uspallatensis

Stipa uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa uspallatensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native range.
