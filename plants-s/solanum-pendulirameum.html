Solanum pendulirameum

Solanum pendulirameum, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic compounds such as solanines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for cultivation or consumption.
