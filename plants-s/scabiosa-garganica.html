Scabiosa garganica

Scabiosa garganica, commonly known as the Garganica ladies' mantle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa garganica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
