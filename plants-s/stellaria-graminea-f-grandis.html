Stellaria graminea f. grandis

Stellaria graminea f. grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, star-shaped white flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
