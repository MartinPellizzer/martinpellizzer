Shorea assamica subsp. philippinensis

Shorea assamica subsp.

philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is valued for its high-quality timber but is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
