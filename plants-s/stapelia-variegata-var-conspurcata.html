Stapelia variegata var. conspurcata

Stapelia variegata var. conspurcata, commonly known as the spotted starfish plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that often bear a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that resemble starfish, which are typically borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collectors and indoor gardens.
