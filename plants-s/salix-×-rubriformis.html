Salix × rubriformis

Salix × rubriformis, commonly known as the red-leaf willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus, Salix.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish or bronze-colored leaves, which provide ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth margin, while the bark is often grayish-brown with a fibrous texture.

Salix × rubriformis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.
