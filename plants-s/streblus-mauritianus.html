Streblus mauritianus

Streblus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, breadfruit-like fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The plant is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
