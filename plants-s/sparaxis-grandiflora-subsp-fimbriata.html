Sparaxis grandiflora subsp. fimbriata

Sparaxis grandiflora subsp.

fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriate spear lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely fringed petals, which give it its common name.
