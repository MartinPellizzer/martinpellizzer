Symplocos mollifolia

Symplocos mollifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandpaper plant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf texture and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
