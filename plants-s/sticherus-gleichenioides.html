Sticherus gleichenioides

Sticherus gleichenioides, commonly known as the slender sticherus, is a small, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Sticherus gleichenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
