Salvia amara

Salvia amara, commonly known as bitter sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, bitter scent. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Salvia amara is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
