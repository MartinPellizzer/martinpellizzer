Setaria criniformis

Setaria criniformis, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, thread-like flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
