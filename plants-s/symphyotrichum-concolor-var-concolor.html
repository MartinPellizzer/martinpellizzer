Symphyotrichum concolor var. concolor

Symphyotrichum concolor var. concolor, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, daisy-like flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces white to pale purple ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.
