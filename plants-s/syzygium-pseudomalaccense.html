Syzygium pseudomalaccense

Syzygium pseudomalaccense, commonly known as the Malacca apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples.

Syzygium pseudomalaccense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
