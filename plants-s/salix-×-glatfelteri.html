Salix × glatfelteri

Salix × glatfelteri, commonly known as the Glatfelter's willow, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salix alba and Salix matsudana.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a silvery underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of pollination.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
