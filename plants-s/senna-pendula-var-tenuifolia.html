Senna pendula var. tenuifolia

Senna pendula var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The seeds are encased in a pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
