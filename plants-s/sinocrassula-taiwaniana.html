Sinocrassula taiwaniana

Sinocrassula taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Sinocrassula taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
