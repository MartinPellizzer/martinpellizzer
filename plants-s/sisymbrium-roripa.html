Sisymbrium roripa

Sisymbrium roripa, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are narrow and pinnately lobed. It has a taproot system and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also a source of nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
