Spiraea amabilis

Spiraea amabilis, commonly known as the sweet meadow sage, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea amabilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
