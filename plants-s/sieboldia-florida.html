Sieboldia florida

Sieboldia florida, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
