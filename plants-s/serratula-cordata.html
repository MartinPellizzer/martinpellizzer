Serratula cordata

Serratula cordata, commonly known as heart-leaved serrata, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Serratula cordata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand hot, arid conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity should be noted.
