Saussurea popovii

Saussurea popovii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, including Tibet, Mongolia, and northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cold, arid environments. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Saussurea popovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral display.
