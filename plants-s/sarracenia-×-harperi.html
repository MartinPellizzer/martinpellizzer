Sarracenia × harperi

Sarracenia × harperi, commonly known as the Harperville pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, tubular leaves that form pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green with red or purple veins, and they secrete a digestive fluid to break down prey. Sarracenia × harperi thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture and bright light for optimal growth.

It is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
