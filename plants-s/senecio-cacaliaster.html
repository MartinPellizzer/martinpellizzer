Senecio cacaliaster

Senecio cacaliaster, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Senecio cacaliaster is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun conditions.
