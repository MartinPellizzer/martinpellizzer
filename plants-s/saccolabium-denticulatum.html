Saccolabium denticulatum

Saccolabium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed labium or narrowleaf false peacock.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Saccolabium denticulatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive foliage and resilience.
