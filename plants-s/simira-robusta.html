Simira robusta

Simira robusta, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
