Solanum nakurense

Solanum nakurense, commonly known as the Nakuren mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits due to their appearance.

Solanum nakurense is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
