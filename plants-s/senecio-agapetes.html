Senecio agapetes

Senecio agapetes, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Senecio agapetes thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
