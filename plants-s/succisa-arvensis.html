Succisa arvensis

Succisa arvensis, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in gardens. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, temperate climates.

Succisa arvensis is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
