Salvia subviolacea

Salvia subviolacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Salvia subviolacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
