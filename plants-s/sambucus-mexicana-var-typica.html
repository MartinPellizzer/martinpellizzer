Sambucus mexicana var. typica

Sambucus mexicana var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and rocky areas.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for various wildlife species.
