Spiraea × nobleana

Spiraea × nobleana, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are oval and dark green, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea × nobleana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
