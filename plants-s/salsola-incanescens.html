Salsola incanescens

Salsola incanescens, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and grayish, woolly leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Salsola incanescens is often found in deserts, grasslands, and along coastal dunes.
