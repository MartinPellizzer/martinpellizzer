Sanguisorba indica

Sanguisorba indica, commonly known as redroot, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sanguisorba indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
