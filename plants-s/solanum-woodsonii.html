Solanum woodsonii

Solanum woodsonii, commonly known as the Woodson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum woodsonii is often found in woodland habitats and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.
