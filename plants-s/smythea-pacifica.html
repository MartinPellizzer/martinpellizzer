Smythea pacifica

Smythea pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific smythea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that reflects sunlight.

Smythea pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
