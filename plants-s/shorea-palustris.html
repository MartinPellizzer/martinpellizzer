Shorea palustris

Shorea palustris, commonly known as swamp ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Shorea palustris plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
