Sison arvense

Sison arvense, commonly known as field sison, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Sison arvense is an annual herb that plays a role in pollination and serves as a food source for certain insects.
