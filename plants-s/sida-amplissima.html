Sida amplissima

Sida amplissima, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, pale pink or white flowers. Its fibrous roots and stems are often used in traditional medicine.

Sida amplissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
