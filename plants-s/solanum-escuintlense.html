Solanum escuintlense

Solanum escuintlense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Solanum escuintlense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
