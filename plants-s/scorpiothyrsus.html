Scorpiothyrsus

Scorpiothyrsus, commonly known as the horned thornapple, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by orange or red berries. Scorpiothyrsus is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its toxic properties.
