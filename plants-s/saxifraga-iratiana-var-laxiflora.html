Saxifraga iratiana var. laxiflora

Saxifraga iratiana var. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to survive in rocky, arid environments.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
