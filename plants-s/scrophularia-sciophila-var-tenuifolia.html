Scrophularia sciophila var. tenuifolia

Scrophularia sciophila var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
