Sorbus aria var. arioides

Sorbus aria var. arioides, commonly known as the whitebeam or rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments.
