Solulus bibracteatus

Solulus bibracteatus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces two bracts at the base of the flower, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Solulus bibracteatus is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.
