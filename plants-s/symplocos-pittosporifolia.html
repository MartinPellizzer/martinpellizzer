Symplocos pittosporifolia

Symplocos pittosporifolia, commonly known as the Indian snowball or mountain snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Symplocos pittosporifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
