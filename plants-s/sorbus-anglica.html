Sorbus anglica

Sorbus anglica, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The bark is grey and becomes fissured with age, adding to its distinctive appearance.
