Syngonium oduberi

Syngonium oduberi, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that vary in color from light green to deep maroon, often with contrasting veins. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.

Syngonium oduberi is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
