Stemmadenia minima

Stemmadenia minima, commonly known as the small-stemmed stemmadenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its compact size and unique botanical features make it a notable species in its native habitat.
