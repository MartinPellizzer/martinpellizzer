Syndesmanthus viscosus

Syndesmanthus viscosus, commonly known as the stickyleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The sticky resin has also been utilized by local communities for various practical applications.
