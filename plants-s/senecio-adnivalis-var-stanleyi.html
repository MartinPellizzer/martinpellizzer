Senecio adnivalis var. stanleyi

Senecio adnivalis var. stanleyi, commonly known as the Stanley's snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, which helps to retain moisture and protect against harsh weather conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
