Silene cariensis subsp. cariensis

Silene cariensis subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant produces pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Silene cariensis subsp.

cariensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
