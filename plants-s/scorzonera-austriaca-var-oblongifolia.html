Scorzonera austriaca var. oblongifolia

Scorzonera austriaca var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dark green, elongated leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is tuberous and thick, contributing to its classification as a member of the Asteraceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
