Scirpus chubutensis

Scirpus chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Scirpus chubutensis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its flowering spikes are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.
