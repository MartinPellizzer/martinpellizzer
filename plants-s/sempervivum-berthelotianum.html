Sempervivum berthelotianum

Sempervivum berthelotianum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a hardy succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a resilient and low-maintenance plant for gardeners.
