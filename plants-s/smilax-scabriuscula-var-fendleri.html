Smilax scabriuscula var. fendleri

Smilax scabriuscula var. fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican devil's claw, is a member of the Smilacaceae family.

It is a shrub-like perennial vine characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
