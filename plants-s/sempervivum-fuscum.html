Sempervivum fuscum

Sempervivum fuscum, commonly known as the dark red houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a deep reddish-brown color. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers on tall stems during the summer months.

Sempervivum fuscum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
