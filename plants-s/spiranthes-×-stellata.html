Spiranthes × stellata

Spiranthes × stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped twayblade, is a hybrid orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble small stars, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is a perennial herb with a short, underground rhizome and lacks true leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
