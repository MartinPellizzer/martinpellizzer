Scirpus oryzetorum

Scirpus oryzetorum, commonly known as the rice bulrush, is a tall, reed-like plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rice paddies. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus oryzetorum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
