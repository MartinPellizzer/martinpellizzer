Steganthera suberosoalata

Steganthera suberosoalata, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
