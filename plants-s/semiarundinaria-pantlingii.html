Semiarundinaria pantlingii

Semiarundinaria pantlingii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

This species is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
