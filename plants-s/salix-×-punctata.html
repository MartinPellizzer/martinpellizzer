Salix × punctata

Salix × punctata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that drape like curtains, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, ponds, and wetlands.

Salix × punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks through its extensive root system.
