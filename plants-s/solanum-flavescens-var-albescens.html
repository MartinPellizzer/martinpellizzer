Solanum flavescens var. albescens

Solanum flavescens var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains toxic compounds, making it unsuitable for human consumption.
