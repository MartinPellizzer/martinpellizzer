Senecio palmatilobus

Senecio palmatilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
