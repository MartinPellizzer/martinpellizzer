Stellaria palustris var. imbricata

Stellaria palustris var. imbricata, commonly known as the water starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland habitats.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist or water-saturated soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
