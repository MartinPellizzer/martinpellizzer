Sarcocapnos enneaphylla var. pubescens

Sarcocapnos enneaphylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sarcocapnos, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
