Stapelia maccabeana

Stapelia maccabeana, commonly known as the Maccabean Stapelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often foul-smelling flowers. The plant produces large, star-shaped blooms that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. Its foliage consists of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

Stapelia maccabeana is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
