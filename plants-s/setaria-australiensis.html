Setaria australiensis

Setaria australiensis, commonly known as the Australian foxtail, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted spikes that bear small, bristly grains. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
