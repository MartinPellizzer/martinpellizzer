Syzygium guineense subsp. urophyllum

Syzygium guineense subsp.

urophyllum, commonly known as the African cherry or *Prunus africana*, is a member of the Myrtaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is highly durable, and for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
