Salix pentandra f. longipes

Salix pentandra f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed willow, is a variant of the common willow species.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil along waterways.
