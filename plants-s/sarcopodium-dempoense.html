Sarcopodium dempoense

Sarcopodium dempoense, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous seeds.
