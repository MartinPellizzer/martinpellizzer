Stathmostelma pachycladum

Stathmostelma pachycladum, commonly known as the thick-leaved star-tongue, is a unique species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in spring. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Stathmostelma pachycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
