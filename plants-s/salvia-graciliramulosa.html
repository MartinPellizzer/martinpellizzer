Salvia graciliramulosa

Salvia graciliramulosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long bloom period make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
