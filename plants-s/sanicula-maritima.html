Sanicula maritima

Sanicula maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and cliffs. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its flowers are often surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Sanicula maritima is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments.
