Scirpus heterocarpus

Scirpus heterocarpus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and broad, flat leaves that are often used in basket weaving. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus heterocarpus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
