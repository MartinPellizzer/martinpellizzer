Sporobolus blakei

Sporobolus blakei, commonly known as Blake's spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and riparian zones, where it thrives in saturated or waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Sporobolus blakei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to fluctuating water levels.
