Styphelia recurva

Styphelia recurva, commonly known as the pink fairybells, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Styphelia recurva thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
