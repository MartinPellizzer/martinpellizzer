Saxifraga moschata subsp. eumoschata

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

eumoschata, commonly known as the musk saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and have a distinctive musky fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
