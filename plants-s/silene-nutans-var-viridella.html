Silene nutans var. viridella

Silene nutans var. viridella, commonly known as the green-flowered silene, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The variety viridella is distinguished by its unique greenish hue, which sets it apart from the typical pink or red flowers of the species.
