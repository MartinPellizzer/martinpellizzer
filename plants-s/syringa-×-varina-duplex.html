Syringa × varina duplex

Syringa × varina duplex, commonly known as the double flowered weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, double-flowered blooms that appear in early summer. The flowers are typically pink or white, with numerous petals that give them a full, fluffy appearance. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
