Salix × basfordiana

Salix × basfordiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Salix alba (White Willow) and Salix babylonica (Weeping Willow), resulting in a plant with distinctive cascading branches. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It typically grows in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This hybrid is valued for its aesthetic beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and along waterways.
