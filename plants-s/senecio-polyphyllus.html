Senecio polyphyllus

Senecio polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Senecio polyphyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
