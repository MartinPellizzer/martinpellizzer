Scorpius falcatus

Scorpius falcatus, commonly known as the sickle scorpion plant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are often adorned with small, white tubercles that provide additional protection against intense sunlight.

Scorpius falcatus is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
