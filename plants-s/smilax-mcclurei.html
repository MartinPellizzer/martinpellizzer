Smilax mcclurei

Smilax mcclurei, commonly known as McClure's greenbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax mcclurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
