Solidago fuscata

Solidago fuscata, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a distinct serrated edge.

Solidago fuscata is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
