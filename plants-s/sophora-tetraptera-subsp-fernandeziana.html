Sophora tetraptera subsp. fernandeziana

Sophora tetraptera subsp.

fernandeziana, commonly known as the fernleaf senna, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
