Sonchus lobatiflorus

Sonchus lobatiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure.

It produces small achenes that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
