Solanum memphiticum var. abyssinicum

Solanum memphiticum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not cultivated for food but is studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
