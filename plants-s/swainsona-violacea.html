Swainsona violacea

Swainsona violacea, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Swainsona violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
