Silene nuda subsp. insectivora

Silene nuda subsp.

insectivora, commonly known as the naked catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is notable for its ability to trap and digest small insects, although it is not considered a true carnivorous plant. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often confused with other Silene species due to its similar appearance.
