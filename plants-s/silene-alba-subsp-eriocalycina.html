Silene alba subsp. eriocalycina

Silene alba subsp.

eriocalycina, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Silene alba subsp.

eriocalycina is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous insects.
