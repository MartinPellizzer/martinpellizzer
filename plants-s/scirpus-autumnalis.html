Scirpus autumnalis

Scirpus autumnalis, commonly known as purple stem bulrush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that often turn reddish or purple in color, especially during the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Scirpus autumnalis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
