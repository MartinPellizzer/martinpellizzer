Spergula pentandra var. punctata

Spergula pentandra var. punctata, commonly known as the dotted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its distinctive feature is the presence of small, dark spots on the leaves, which give it its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
