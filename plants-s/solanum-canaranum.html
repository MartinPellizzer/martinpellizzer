Solanum canaranum

Solanum canaranum, commonly known as the Canara tomato, is a flowering plant native to India.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, red berries when mature. These berries are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
