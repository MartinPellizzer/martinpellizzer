Scaphyglottis sublibera

Scaphyglottis sublibera, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
