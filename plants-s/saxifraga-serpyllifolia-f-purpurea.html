Saxifraga serpyllifolia f. purpurea

Saxifraga serpyllifolia f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine plantings.
