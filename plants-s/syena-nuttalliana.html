Syena nuttalliana

Syena nuttalliana, commonly known as the Nuttall's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Syena nuttalliana is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
