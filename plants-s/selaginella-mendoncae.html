Selaginella mendoncae

Selaginella mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendonca spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are linear and often have a waxy coating. Selaginella mendoncae is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures called strobila.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and adaptation to specific ecological niches.
