Saussurea macrota

Saussurea macrota, commonly known as the large-flowered saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Mongolia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and can be quite deep.

Saussurea macrota is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
