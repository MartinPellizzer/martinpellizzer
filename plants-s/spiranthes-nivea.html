Spiranthes nivea

Spiranthes nivea, commonly known as the white fringed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are white with intricate fringed petals, and it produces small, round seeds in elongated pods.

Spiranthes nivea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
