Silene capitata

Silene capitata, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene capitata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sandy or gravelly habitats.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
