Setaria holstii

Setaria holstii, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often hairy and contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Setaria holstii is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful plant in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
