Scaevola collina

Scaevola collina, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Scaevola collina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in gardens.
