Senegalia cockerellae

Senegalia cockerellae, commonly known as the redbrush acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets. Senegalia cockerellae is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
