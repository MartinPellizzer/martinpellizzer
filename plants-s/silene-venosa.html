Silene venosa

Silene venosa, commonly known as the veined catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or loamy soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Silene venosa is also known for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
