Silene asirensis

Silene asirensis, commonly known as the "Siren's Flower," is a rare and endangered plant species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a herbaceous annual with delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Silene asirensis is considered critically important for conservation efforts.
