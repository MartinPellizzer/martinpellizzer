Saxifraga sinomontana var. sinomontana

Saxifraga sinomontana var. sinomontana, commonly known as the Chinese mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats or cushions to withstand harsh climatic conditions. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in tight clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological niches.
