Symplocos mapirensis

Symplocos mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapire tamarind.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are drupes that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Symplocos mapirensis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
