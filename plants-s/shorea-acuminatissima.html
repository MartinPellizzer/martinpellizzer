Shorea acuminatissima

Shorea acuminatissima, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Shorea acuminatissima plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
