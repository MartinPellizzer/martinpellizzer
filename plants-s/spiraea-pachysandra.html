Spiraea pachysandra

Spiraea pachysandra, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Spiraea pachysandra is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
