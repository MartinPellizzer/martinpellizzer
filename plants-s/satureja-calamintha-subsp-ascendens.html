Satureja calamintha subsp. ascendens

Satureja calamintha subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its subspecies status reflects slight morphological differences from the nominate form, particularly in its growth habit and habitat preferences. Satureja calamintha subsp.

ascendens is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the sage family.
