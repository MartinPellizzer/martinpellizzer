Senecio pauciflorus var. atropurpureus

Senecio pauciflorus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking purple-tinged foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners in suitable climates.
