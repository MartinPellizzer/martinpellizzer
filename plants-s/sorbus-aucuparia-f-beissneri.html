Sorbus aucuparia f. beissneri

Sorbus aucuparia f. beissneri, commonly known as the Beissner's rowan, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, orange, or yellow berries that persist through winter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The foliage is typically dark green with a slight pubescence, and the leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape. It is often cultivated for its seasonal color and is adaptable to a range of soil types.

This form is particularly valued in horticulture for its visual appeal and resilience.
