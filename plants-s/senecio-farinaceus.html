Senecio farinaceus

Senecio farinaceus, commonly known as the dustymilkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a dusty appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio farinaceus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
