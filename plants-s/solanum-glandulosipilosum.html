Solanum glandulosipilosum

Solanum glandulosipilosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature, often starting green and turning red.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
