Salsola gemmascens

Salsola gemmascens, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across sandy soils. Salsola gemmascens is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
