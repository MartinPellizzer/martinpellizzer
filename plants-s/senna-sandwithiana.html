Senna sandwithiana

Senna sandwithiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Senna sandwithiana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of plant-based dye.
