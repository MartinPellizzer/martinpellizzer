Schefflera myrioneura

Schefflera myrioneura, commonly known as the New Guinea arrowroot, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Schefflera myrioneura is also valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
