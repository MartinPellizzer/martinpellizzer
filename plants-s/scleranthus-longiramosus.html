Scleranthus longiramosus

Scleranthus longiramosus, commonly known as the long-stemmed thornweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and wastelands. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Scleranthus longiramosus is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
