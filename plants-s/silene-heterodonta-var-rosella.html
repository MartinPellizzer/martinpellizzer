Silene heterodonta var. rosella

Silene heterodonta var. rosella, commonly known as the Rosella Catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
