Solidago aestivalis

Solidago aestivalis, commonly known as the early goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago aestivalis plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the summer months.
