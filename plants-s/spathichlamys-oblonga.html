Spathichlamys oblonga

Spathichlamys oblonga, commonly known as the oblong spathichlamys, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated spathaceous bract that encloses a cluster of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
