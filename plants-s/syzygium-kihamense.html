Syzygium kihamense

Syzygium kihamense, commonly known as the Kihame apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Syzygium kihamense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential horticultural use.
