Sedum mariae

Sedum mariae, commonly known as Marie's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It is often found growing in crevices or on rocky outcrops where it can receive adequate sunlight.

Sedum mariae is a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
