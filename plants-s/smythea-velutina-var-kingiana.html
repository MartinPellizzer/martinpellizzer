Smythea velutina var. kingiana

Smythea velutina var. kingiana, commonly known as the King's velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
