Saxifraga decipiens subsp. steinmannii

Saxifraga decipiens subsp.

steinmannii, commonly known as the Steinmann's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a compact cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
