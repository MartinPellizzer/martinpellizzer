Stylidium junceum var. volubile

Stylidium junceum var. volubile, commonly known as the twining mistletoe, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its twining habit, allowing it to climb and attach to host plants. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it has a unique method of seed dispersal through its sticky, fleshy fruits.

This species is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
