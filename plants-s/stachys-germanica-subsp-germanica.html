Stachys germanica subsp. germanica

Stachys germanica subsp.

germanica, commonly known as German betony, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
