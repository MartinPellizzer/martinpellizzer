Solanum jasminoides

Solanum jasminoides, commonly known as the nightshade jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum jasminoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
