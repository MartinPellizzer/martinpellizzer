Sesbania roxburghii

Sesbania roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of protein-rich seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is known for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Sesbania roxburghii is also used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
