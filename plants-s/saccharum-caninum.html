Saccharum caninum

Saccharum caninum, commonly known as the dog grass or wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough, hairy texture.

Saccharum caninum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
