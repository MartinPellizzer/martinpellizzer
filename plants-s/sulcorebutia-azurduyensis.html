Sulcorebutia azurduyensis

Sulcorebutia azurduyensis, commonly known as the Azurduy's sun rose, is a succulent plant native to the Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, woolly covering. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
