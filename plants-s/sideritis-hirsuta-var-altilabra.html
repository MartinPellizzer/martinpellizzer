Sideritis hirsuta var. altilabra

Sideritis hirsuta var. altilabra, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
