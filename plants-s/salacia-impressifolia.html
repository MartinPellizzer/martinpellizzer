Salacia impressifolia

Salacia impressifolia, commonly known as the impressifoliate salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
