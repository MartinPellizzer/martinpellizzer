Scrophularia tinantii

Scrophularia tinantii, commonly known as the Turkish figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features erect stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
