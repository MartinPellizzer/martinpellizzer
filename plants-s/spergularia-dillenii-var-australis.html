Spergularia dillenii var. australis

Spergularia dillenii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

The species is also noted for its ability to colonize disturbed areas, contributing to soil stabilization.
