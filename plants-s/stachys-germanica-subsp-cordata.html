Stachys germanica subsp. cordata

Stachys germanica subsp.

cordata, commonly known as heart-leaved hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its cordate (heart-shaped) leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
