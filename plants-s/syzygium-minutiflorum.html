Syzygium minutiflorum

Syzygium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium minutiflorum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
