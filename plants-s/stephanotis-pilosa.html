Stephanotis pilosa

Stephanotis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy stephanotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody vines and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
