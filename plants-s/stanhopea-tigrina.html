Stanhopea tigrina

Stanhopea tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple flowers with intricate yellow markings that resemble a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Stanhopea tigrina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and beautiful blooms.
