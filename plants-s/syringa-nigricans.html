Syringa nigricans

Syringa nigricans, commonly known as black honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a bushy, compact form.

Syringa nigricans is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.
