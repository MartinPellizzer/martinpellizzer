Solanum canaminense

Solanum canaminense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are yellow when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
