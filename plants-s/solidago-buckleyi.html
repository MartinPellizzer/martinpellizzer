Solidago buckleyi

Solidago buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks. It produces bright yellow inflorescences in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Buckley's goldenrod is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
