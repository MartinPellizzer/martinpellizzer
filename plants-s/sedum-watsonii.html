Sedum watsonii

Sedum watsonii, commonly known as Watson's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum watsonii is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
