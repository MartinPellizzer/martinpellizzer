Syzygium operculatum

Syzygium operculatum, commonly known as the satinwood or opal fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a glossy, waxy skin. Syzygium operculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical environments.
