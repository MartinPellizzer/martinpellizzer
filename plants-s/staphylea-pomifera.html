Staphylea pomifera

Staphylea pomifera, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny fruits called "horse chestnuts," which contain hard, shiny seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been widely cultivated in gardens across Europe and North America.
