Sutera violacea

Sutera violacea, commonly known as the violet mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Sutera violacea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
