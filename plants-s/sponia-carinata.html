Sponia carinata

Sponia carinata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Sponia carinata is a herbaceous perennial with a fibrous root system and hairy stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
