Saxifraga ferruginea

Saxifraga ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas where the climate is cool and moist. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves covered in a dense layer of rusty-brown hairs, which help to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

Saxifraga ferruginea is well adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient member of alpine flora.
