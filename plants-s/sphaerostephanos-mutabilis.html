Sphaerostephanos mutabilis, commonly known as the variable star flower, is a unique member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that change color as they mature. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
