Setaria weinmannii

Setaria weinmannii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Setaria weinmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
