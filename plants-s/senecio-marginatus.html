Senecio marginatus

Senecio marginatus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub with hairy, green stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in a cluster, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

Senecio marginatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
