Salvia membranacea var. villosula

Salvia membranacea var. villosula, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its hairy leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
