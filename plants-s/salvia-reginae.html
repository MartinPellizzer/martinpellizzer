Salvia reginae

Salvia reginae, commonly known as the Mexican mint or royal sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
