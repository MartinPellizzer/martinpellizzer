Spergula marginata var. intermedia

Spergula marginata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wall-grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by its slender, tufted stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
