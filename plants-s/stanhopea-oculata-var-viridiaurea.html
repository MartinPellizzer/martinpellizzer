Stanhopea oculata var. viridiaurea

Stanhopea oculata var. viridiaurea, commonly known as the Emerald Gold Stanhopea, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is distinguished by its vibrant greenish-yellow flowers, which feature intricate patterns and a unique, elongated shape. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
