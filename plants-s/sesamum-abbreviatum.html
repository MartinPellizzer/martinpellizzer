Sesamum abbreviatum

Sesamum abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf sesame, is a small annual herb native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, off-white seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
