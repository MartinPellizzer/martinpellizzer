Setaria verticillata var. longiseta

Setaria verticillata var. longiseta, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
