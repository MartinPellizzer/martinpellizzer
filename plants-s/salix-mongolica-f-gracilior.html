Salix mongolica f. gracilior

Salix mongolica f. gracilior, commonly known as the Mongolian willow slender form, is a shrub-like plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
