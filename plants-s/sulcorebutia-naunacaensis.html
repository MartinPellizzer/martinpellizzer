Sulcorebutia naunacaensis

Sulcorebutia naunacaensis, commonly known as the Naunacaensis moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of Peru.

It is a small, compact cactus with a short, thick stem and a variety of colorful, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a unique symbiotic relationship with a specific type of fungus, which helps it absorb nutrients from the soil. Its fleshy, ribbed structure is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Sulcorebutia naunacaensis is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
