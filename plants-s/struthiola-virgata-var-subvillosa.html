Struthiola virgata var. subvillosa

Struthiola virgata var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky struthiola, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
