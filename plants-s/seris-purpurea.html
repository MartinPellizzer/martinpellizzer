Seris purpurea

Seris purpurea, commonly known as the purple seris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Seris purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
