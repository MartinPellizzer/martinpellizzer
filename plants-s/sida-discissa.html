Sida discissa

Sida discissa, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Sida discissa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
