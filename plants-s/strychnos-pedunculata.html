Strychnos pedunculata

Strychnos pedunculata, commonly known as the blue-veined beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have distinctive blue veins, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
