Saccolabium miserum

Saccolabium miserum, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Saccolabium miserum is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
