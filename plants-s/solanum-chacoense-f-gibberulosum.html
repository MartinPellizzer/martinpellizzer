Solanum chacoense f. gibberulosum

Solanum chacoense f. gibberulosum, commonly known as the Chacoan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, contains toxic compounds and is not suitable for human consumption.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
