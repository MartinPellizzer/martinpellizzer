Styrax camporum f. lanceolatus

Styrax camporum f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate fringed woodbine, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
