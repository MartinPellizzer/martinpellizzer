Satureja fontanesii

Satureja fontanesii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, pungent aroma, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Satureja fontanesii is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
