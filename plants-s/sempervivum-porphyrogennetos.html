Sempervivum porphyrogennetos

Sempervivum porphyrogennetos, commonly known as the Byzantine rose or crown of thorns, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically red or purple in color, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
