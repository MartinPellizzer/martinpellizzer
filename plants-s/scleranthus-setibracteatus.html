Scleranthus setibracteatus

Scleranthus setibracteatus, commonly known as the bracted sicklethorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
