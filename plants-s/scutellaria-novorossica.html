Scutellaria novorossica

Scutellaria novorossica, commonly known as the Novorossiysk skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Scutellaria novorossica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
