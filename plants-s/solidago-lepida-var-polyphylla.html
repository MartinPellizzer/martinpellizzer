Solidago lepida var. polyphylla

Solidago lepida var. polyphylla, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, spiky purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
