Spelta vulgaris

Spelta vulgaris, commonly known as common spelt, is an ancient grain species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, hard grains. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Spelta vulgaris has been cultivated for thousands of years and is valued for its nutritional content and historical significance in agriculture.
