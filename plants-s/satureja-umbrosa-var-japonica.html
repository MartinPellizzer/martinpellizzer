Satureja umbrosa var. japonica

Satureja umbrosa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese woodrue, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol.
