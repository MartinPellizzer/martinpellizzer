Senecio erucifolius var. viridulus

Senecio erucifolius var. viridulus, commonly known as the greenleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections and native plant gardens.
