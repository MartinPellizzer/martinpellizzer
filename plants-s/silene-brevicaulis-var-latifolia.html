Silene brevicaulis var. latifolia

Silene brevicaulis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Silene brevicaulis var. latifolia is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
