Sinapis erucastrum

Sinapis erucastrum, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in gardens, roadsides, and fields.

Sinapis erucastrum is also noted for its potential as a forage crop and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
