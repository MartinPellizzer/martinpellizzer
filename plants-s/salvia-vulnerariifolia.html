Salvia vulnerariifolia

Salvia vulnerariifolia, commonly known as the wounded leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces vibrant purple to violet flowers in whorled clusters. Salvia vulnerariifolia is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for treating wounds and inflammatory conditions.
