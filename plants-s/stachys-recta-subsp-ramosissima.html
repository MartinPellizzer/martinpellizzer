Stachys recta subsp. ramosissima

Stachys recta subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, hairy leaves.

The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
