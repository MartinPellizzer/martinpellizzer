Stachys japonica f. intermedia

Stachys japonica f. intermedia, commonly known as Japanese hedge nectar.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia, characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins, and the plant produces spikes of pale purple to pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
