Silene densiflora var. chersonensis

Silene densiflora var. chersonensis, commonly known as the Crimean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, erect stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals, blooming from June to August. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
