Strobilanthes lanceolata

Strobilanthes lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
