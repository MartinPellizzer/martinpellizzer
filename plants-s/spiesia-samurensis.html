Spiesia samurensis

Spiesia samurensis, commonly known as the Samur pine, is a rare coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of the Pinaceae family. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Spiesia samurensis is considered a vulnerable species.
