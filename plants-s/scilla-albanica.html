Scilla albanica

Scilla albanica, commonly known as the Albanian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. Scilla albanica prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.
