Sempervivum dolomiticum

Sempervivum dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomite Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Dolomites in Italy.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina. The plant is adapted to rocky, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months.

Sempervivum dolomiticum is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
