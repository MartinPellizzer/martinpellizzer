Senecio arrabidae

Senecio arrabidae, commonly known as the Arrabida dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Senecio arrabidae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
