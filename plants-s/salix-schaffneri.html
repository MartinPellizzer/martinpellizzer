Salix schaffneri

Salix schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in cold, high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a silver-gray underside, while its catkins are a distinguishing feature.

The plant is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
