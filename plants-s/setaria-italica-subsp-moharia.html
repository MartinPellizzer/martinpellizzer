Setaria italica subsp. moharia

Setaria italica subsp.

moharia, commonly known as millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, hard grains.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence is a compact panicle, which contains the edible grains that are harvested for food and animal feed.
