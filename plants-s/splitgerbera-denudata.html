Splitgerbera denudata

Splitgerbera denudata, commonly known as the naked gerbera, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, daisy-like flowers with bright, often contrasting colors. The plant features a short, stout stem and broad, waxy leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
