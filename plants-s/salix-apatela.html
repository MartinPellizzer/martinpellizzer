Salix apatela

Salix apatela, commonly known as the ghost willow, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in arid, rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, powdery coating that gives them a ghostly appearance.

The ghost willow is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
