Schoenoplectus leucanthus

Schoenoplectus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Schoenoplectus leucanthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
