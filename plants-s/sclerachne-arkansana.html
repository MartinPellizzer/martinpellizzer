Sclerachne arkansana

Sclerachne arkansana, commonly known as Arkansas grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has tough, fibrous leaves that are adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
