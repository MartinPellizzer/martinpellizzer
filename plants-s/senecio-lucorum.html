Senecio lucorum

Senecio lucorum, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, giving it a pincushion-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio lucorum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
