Sidalcea spicata var. pedata

Sidalcea spicata var. pedata, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are pinnately compound, with slender, hairy leaflets that help distinguish it from other species. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
