Solanum melanostictocarpum

Solanum melanostictocarpum, commonly known as the black-stained fruit nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that develop from yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually ovate with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption by humans or animals.
