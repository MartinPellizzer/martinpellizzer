Stachys doerfleri

Stachys doerfleri, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Stachys doerfleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
