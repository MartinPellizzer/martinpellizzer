Sida avicularioides

Sida avicularioides, commonly known as the silky sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Sida avicularioides is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
