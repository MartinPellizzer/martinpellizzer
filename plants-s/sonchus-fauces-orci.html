Sonchus fauces-orci

Sonchus fauces-orci, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and western Asia. It has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America, where it can become invasive.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal but requires careful management due to its potential to spread rapidly.
