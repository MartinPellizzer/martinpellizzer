Spiranthes corymbosa

Spiranthes corymbosa, commonly known as the pink spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and support its growth.

Spiranthes corymbosa is a non-Showy orchid that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
