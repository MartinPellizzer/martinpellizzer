Stellaria uliginosa var. alpestris

Stellaria uliginosa var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and produces tiny, rounded seeds. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is characterized by its compact growth form.
