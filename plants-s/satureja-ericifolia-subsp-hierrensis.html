Satureja ericifolia subsp. hierrensis

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

hierrensis, commonly known as Alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is known for its pungent, thyme-like fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
