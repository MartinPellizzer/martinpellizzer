Scabiosa persica

Scabiosa persica, commonly known as Persian scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa persica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
