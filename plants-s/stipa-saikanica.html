Stipa saikanica

Stipa saikanica, commonly known as the Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and tall, cylindrical seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Stipa saikanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
