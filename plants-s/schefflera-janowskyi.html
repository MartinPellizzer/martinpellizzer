Schefflera janowskyi

Schefflera janowskyi, commonly known as the Janowsky's umbrella tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leaflets radiating from a central stem. The plant features compound, palmately arranged leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are a food source for various birds.

Schefflera janowskyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
