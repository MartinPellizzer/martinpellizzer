Schizachyrium scabriflorum

Schizachyrium scabriflorum, commonly known as roughflower grass, is a native North American grass species characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, bristly inflorescence.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often grayish-green in color, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Schizachyrium scabriflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
