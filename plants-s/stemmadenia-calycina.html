Stemmadenia calycina

Stemmadenia calycina, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its stems are woody and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
