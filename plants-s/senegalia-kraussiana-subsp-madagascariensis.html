Senegalia kraussiana subsp. madagascariensis

Senegalia kraussiana subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often reduced to small, scale-like structures in dry conditions.

The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

This subspecies is also valued for its hard wood and traditional uses in local communities.
