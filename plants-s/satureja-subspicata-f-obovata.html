Satureja subspicata f. obovata

Satureja subspicata f. obovata, commonly known as narrow-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically obovate in shape, with a slightly wrinkled surface. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in cooking for its pungent, thyme-like aroma.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary and medicinal uses.
