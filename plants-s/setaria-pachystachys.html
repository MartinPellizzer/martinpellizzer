Setaria pachystachys

Setaria pachystachys, commonly known as giant millet, is a robust annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and large, stiff leaves. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid environments. It is often used as a forage crop and in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to regenerate make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
