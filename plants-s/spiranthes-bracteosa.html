Spiranthes bracteosa

Spiranthes bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its spiral arrangement of flowers along a single stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are white or pale pink and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
