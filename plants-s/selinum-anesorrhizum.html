Selinum anesorrhizum

Selinum anesorrhizum, commonly known as the nodding globe flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silvery-gray leaves and its showy, globe-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine environments. It is known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is often found at high altitudes.

Selinum anesorrhizum is primarily grown in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
