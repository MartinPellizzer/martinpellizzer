Secale afghanicum

Secale afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
