Sida arborescens

Sida arborescens, commonly known as the tree sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its branching structure and broad leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Sida arborescens is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
