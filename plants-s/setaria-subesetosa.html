Setaria subesetosa

Setaria subesetosa, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as agricultural fields and roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
