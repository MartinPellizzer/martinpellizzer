Struthanthus giovannae

Struthanthus giovannae, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Struthanthus giovannae is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
