Saccharum cotuliferum

Saccharum cotuliferum, commonly known as the wild sugarcane or bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant thrives in wet, fertile soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its rhizomes and roots are rich in carbohydrates, making it a valuable resource for both traditional and modern uses.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
