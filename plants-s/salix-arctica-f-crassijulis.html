Salix arctica f. crassijulis

Salix arctica f. crassijulis, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of willow species. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in permafrost conditions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.

Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense mats in its natural environment.
