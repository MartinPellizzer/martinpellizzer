Sophronitis lundii f. alba

Sophronitis lundii f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

This cultivar is prized for its beauty and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
