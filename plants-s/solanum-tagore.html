Solanum tagore

Solanum tagore, commonly known as the Tagore nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which open in the evening. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum tagore is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
