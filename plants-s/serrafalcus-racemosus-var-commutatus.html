Serrafalcus racemosus var. commutatus

Serrafalcus racemosus var. commutatus, commonly known as the variable racemosus serrafalcus, is a lesser-known species within the Serrafalcus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and racemose inflorescence, which consists of a series of small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
