Saussurea compta

Saussurea compta, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions in Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh alpine conditions.
