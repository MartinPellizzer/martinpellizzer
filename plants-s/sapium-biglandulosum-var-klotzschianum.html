Sapium biglandulosum var. klotzschianum

Sapium biglandulosum var. klotzschianum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces orange to red berries containing several seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
