Sparganium asiaticum

Sparganium asiaticum, commonly known as the Asiatic burrhead, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Sparganium.

It is a wetland plant that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, and its most distinctive feature is its spiky, cone-like structure that resembles a burr. Sparganium asiaticum is also known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important part of aquatic ecosystems.

Its presence can indicate the health of wetland habitats.
