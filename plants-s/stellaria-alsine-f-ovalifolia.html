Stellaria alsine f. ovalifolia

Stellaria alsine f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing, mat-forming herbaceous plant.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and along roadsides. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through runners.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
