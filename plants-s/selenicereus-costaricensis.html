Selenicereus costaricensis

Selenicereus costaricensis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a climbing or trailing vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by moths and bats.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its preference for full sun to partial shade.
