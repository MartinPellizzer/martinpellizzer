Satureja brownei subsp. eubrownei

Satureja brownei subsp.

eubrownei, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, pink or purple flowers. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
