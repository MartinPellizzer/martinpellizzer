Swartzia tessmannii

Swartzia tessmannii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
