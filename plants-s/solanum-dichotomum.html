Solanum dichotomum

Solanum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes.
