Sorghum caucasicum

Sorghum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian sorghum, is a species of grass native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the male flowers typically located at the base of the inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib.

Sorghum caucasicum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
