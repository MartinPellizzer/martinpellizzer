Solanum elongatum

Solanum elongatum, commonly known as the elongated nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
