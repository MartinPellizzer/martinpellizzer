Smilax amaurophlebia

Smilax amaurophlebia, commonly known as the dark-stemmed smilax, is a species of climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.

Smilax amaurophlebia is known for its robust growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
