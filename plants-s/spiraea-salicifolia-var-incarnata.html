Spiraea salicifolia var. incarnata

Spiraea salicifolia var. incarnata, commonly known as the rosehip spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, willow-like leaves and produces bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
