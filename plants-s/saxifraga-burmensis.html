Saxifraga burmensis

Saxifraga burmensis, commonly known as the Burmese saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga burmensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
