Saccharum officinarum var. rubrum-altum

Saccharum officinarum var. rubrum-altum, commonly known as the red tall sugarcane.

This variety is characterized by its tall stature and distinctive reddish pigmentation in its stems and leaves. It is a variant of sugarcane, known for its unique coloration which can be attributed to higher concentrations of anthocyanin pigments. The plant is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its sugar content and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and horticultural research.
