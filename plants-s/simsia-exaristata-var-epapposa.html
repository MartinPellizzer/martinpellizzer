Simsia exaristata var. epapposa

Simsia exaristata var. epapposa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
