Swida boliviana

Swida boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian swida, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
