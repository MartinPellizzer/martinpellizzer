Senecio magellensis

Senecio magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio magellensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
