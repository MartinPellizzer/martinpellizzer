Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. hirsutus

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in a clustered inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its hairy foliage and showy flowers make it an interesting addition to native plant gardens.
