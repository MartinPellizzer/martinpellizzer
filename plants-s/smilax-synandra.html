Smilax synandra

Smilax synandra, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with thorned nodes and heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax synandra is a perennial plant with a long growing season, making it a common sight in the southeastern region.
