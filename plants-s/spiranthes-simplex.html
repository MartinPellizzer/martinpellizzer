Spiranthes simplex

Spiranthes simplex, commonly known as the grass pinks, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of small pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
