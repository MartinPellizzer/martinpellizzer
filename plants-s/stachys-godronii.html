Stachys godronii

Stachys godronii, commonly known as Godron's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, pale blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Stachys godronii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
