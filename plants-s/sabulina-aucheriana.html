Sabulina aucheriana

Sabulina aucheriana, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green. It is adapted to harsh climates and can tolerate low temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Sabulina aucheriana is often found in open, sunny habitats and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
