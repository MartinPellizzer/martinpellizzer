Senecio alloeophyllus var. glaberrimus

Senecio alloeophyllus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
