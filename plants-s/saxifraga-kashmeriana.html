Saxifraga kashmeriana

Saxifraga kashmeriana, commonly known as Kashmir saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, often found in high-altitude meadows and mountain slopes. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Saxifraga kashmeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
