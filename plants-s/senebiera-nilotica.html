Senebiera nilotica

Senebiera nilotica, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
