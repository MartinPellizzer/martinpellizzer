Suteria calycina

Suteria calycina, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Suteria calycina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sunlight.
