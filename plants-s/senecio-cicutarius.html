Senecio cicutarius

Senecio cicutarius, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of yellow disk florets, giving it a pincushion-like appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Senecio cicutarius is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
