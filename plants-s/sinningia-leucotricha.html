Sinningia leucotricha

Sinningia leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired whorled pogostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Sinningia leucotricha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in humid environments.
