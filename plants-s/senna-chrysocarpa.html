Senna chrysocarpa

Senna chrysocarpa, commonly known as golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.

Senna chrysocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
