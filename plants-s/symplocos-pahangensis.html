Symplocos pahangensis

Symplocos pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos pahangensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
