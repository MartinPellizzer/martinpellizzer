Smilax riparia var. pubescens

Smilax riparia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and produce heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, rich soils along stream banks and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it both a useful groundcover and a potential weed in certain habitats.
