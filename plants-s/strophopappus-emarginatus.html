Strophopappus emarginatus

Strophopappus emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces achenes with pappus structures, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Strophopappus emarginatus is valued for its use in soil preparation and as a forage crop in some regions.
