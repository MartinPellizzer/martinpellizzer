Silene vulgaris f. microphylla

Silene vulgaris f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its small, delicate leaves and pink to white flowers.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
