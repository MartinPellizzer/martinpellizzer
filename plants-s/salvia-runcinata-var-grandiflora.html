Salvia runcinata var. grandiflora

Salvia runcinata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and emits a strong, minty fragrance. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in both gardens and herbal settings.
