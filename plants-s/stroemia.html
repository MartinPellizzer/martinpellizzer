Stroemia

Stroemia, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange ray florets that resemble stars, often surrounded by a ring of smaller, tubular flowers. Native to the southern United States, stroemia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include opposite, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
