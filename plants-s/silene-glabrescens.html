Silene glabrescens

Silene glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene glabrescens is typically found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
