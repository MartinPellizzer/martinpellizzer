Sebastiania corniculata var. guianensis

Sebastiania corniculata var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan featherleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
