Sedum atropurpureum var. caespitosum

Sedum atropurpureum var. caespitosum, commonly known as purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of vibrant purple flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
