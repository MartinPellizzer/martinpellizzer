Synaedrys beccariana

Synaedrys beccariana, commonly known as the Beccari's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological specificity.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
