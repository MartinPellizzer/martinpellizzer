Stenocereus alamosensis f. cristatus

Stenocereus alamosensis f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate organ pipe cactus, is a unique variant of the organ pipe cactus characterized by its distinctive crest-like growth pattern.

It is native to the Sonoran Desert, primarily found in regions of Arizona and Sonora, Mexico. This plant is a columnar cactus with a ribbed, cylindrical stem that develops a crown-like structure due to the branching of its areoles. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, blooming in the spring, and it produces small, red fruits that are edible.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, waxy skin to reduce water loss and spines for protection.
