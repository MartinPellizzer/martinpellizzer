Senecio australis var. edentatus

Senecio australis var. edentatus, commonly known as the toothless African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

The variety 'edentatus' is distinguished by its lack of teeth on the leaf margins, giving it a smooth, polished look.
