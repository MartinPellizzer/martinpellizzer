Syzygium walkeri

Syzygium walkeri, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Syzygium walkeri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
