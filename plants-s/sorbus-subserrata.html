Sorbus subserrata

Sorbus subserrata, commonly known as the serrated whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the autumn. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
