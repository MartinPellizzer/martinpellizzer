Salvia × westerae

Salvia × westerae, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
