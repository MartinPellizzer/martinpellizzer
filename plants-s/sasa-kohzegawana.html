Sasa kohzegawana

Sasa kohzegawana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa kohzegawana forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, which allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
