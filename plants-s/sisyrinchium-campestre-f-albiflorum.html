Sisyrinchium campestre f. albiflorum

Sisyrinchium campestre f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers that resemble tiny bells, giving it its common name. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, often in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
