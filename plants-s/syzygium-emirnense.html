Syzygium emirnense

Syzygium emirnense, commonly known as the Emir's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Syzygium emirnense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
