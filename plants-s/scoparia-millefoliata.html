Scoparia millefoliata

Scoparia millefoliata, commonly known as silverleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Scoparia millefoliata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
