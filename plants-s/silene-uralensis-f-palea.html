Silene uralensis f. palea

Silene uralensis f. palea, commonly known as the pale-leaved bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale green to bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arctic and alpine regions.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to cold climates and its ornamental value in gardens.
