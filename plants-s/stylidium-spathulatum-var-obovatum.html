Stylidium spathulatum var. obovatum

Stylidium spathulatum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed triggerplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features a rosette of broad, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological gardens.
