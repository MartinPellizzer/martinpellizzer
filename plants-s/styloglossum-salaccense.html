Styloglossum salaccense

Styloglossum salaccense, commonly known as the Singapore starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other cucurbit species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and is valued for its sweet, tangy flavor.
