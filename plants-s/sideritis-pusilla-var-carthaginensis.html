Sideritis pusilla var. carthaginensis

Sideritis pusilla var. carthaginensis, commonly known as Carthaginian dwarf sainfoin, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oblong seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
