Siparuna manarae

Siparuna manarae, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The tree's bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
