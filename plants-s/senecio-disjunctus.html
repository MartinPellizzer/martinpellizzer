Senecio disjunctus

Senecio disjunctus, commonly known as the Disjunct Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
