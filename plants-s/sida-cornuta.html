Sida cornuta

Sida cornuta, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped bracts that encase the flowers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, and they bloom in the morning, hence the name "morning glory.".

Sida cornuta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
