Sorbus sibirica subsp. camschatcensis

Sorbus sibirica subsp.

camschatcensis, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
