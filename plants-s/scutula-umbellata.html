Scutula umbellata

Scutula umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutula umbellata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
