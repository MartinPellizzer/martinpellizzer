Struthanthus polyanthus var. gracilis

Struthanthus polyanthus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
