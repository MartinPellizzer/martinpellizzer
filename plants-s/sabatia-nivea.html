Sabatia nivea

Sabatia nivea, commonly known as the snow sabatia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

Sabatia nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
