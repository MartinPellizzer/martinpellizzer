Statice pruinosa

Statice pruinosa, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is adapted to salty, windy environments and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Statice pruinosa is often used in coastal landscaping and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
