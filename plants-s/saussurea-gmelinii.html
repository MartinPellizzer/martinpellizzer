Saussurea gmelinii

Saussurea gmelinii, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, herbaceous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Saussurea gmelinii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
