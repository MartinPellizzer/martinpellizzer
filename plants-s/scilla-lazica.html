Scilla lazica

Scilla lazica, commonly known as the Caucasian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla lazica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
