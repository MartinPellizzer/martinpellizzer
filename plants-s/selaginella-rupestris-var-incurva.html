Selaginella rupestris var. incurva

Selaginella rupestris var. incurva, commonly known as the twisted clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, twisted, spirally arranged leaves. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are typically found at the tips of its stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
