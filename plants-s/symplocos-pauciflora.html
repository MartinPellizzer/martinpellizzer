Symplocos pauciflora

Symplocos pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
