Serapias cordigera

Serapias cordigera, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Serapias cordigera is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
