Saxifraga geranioides f. edentula

Saxifraga geranioides f. edentula, commonly known as the toothless lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant characterized by its lack of teeth on the leaf margins.

This succulent perennial is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathian Mountains. The leaves are typically broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience and ornamental value in horticulture.
