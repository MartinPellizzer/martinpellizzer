Stipa longiplumosa

Stipa longiplumosa, commonly known as the long-bract grass or slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along streambanks.

Stipa longiplumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
