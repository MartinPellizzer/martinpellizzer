Sisymbrium crithmifolium

Sisymbrium crithmifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue flowers and fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Sisymbrium crithmifolium is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
