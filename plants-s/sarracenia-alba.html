Sarracenia alba

Sarracenia alba, commonly known as the white pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white, tubular leaves that form specialized pitfall traps. These traps secrete a sticky liquid that lures and traps insects, which the plant then digests to obtain nutrients. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

Its striking white coloration and unique trapping mechanism make it a distinctive and fascinating species within the Sarraceniaceae family.
