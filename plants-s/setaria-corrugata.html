Setaria corrugata

Setaria corrugata, commonly known as rough beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Setaria corrugata is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common species in many ecosystems.
