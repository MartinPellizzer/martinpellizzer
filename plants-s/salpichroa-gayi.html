Salpichroa gayi

Salpichroa gayi, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Salpichroa gayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
