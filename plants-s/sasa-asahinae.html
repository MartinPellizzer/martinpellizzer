Sasa asahinae

Sasa asahinae, commonly known as the Asahina bamboo, is a species native to Japan, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Honshu.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense thickets, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa asahinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
