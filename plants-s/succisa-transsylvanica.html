Succisa transsylvanica

Succisa transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Succisa transsylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
