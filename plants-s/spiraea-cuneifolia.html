Spiraea cuneifolia

Spiraea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elliptical and have a distinctive cuneate (wedge-shaped) base. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
