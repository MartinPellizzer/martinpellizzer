Schizoloma ferrugineum

Schizoloma ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers arranged in loose spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Schizoloma ferrugineum is valued in ecological gardens for its ability to support pollinators and its ornamental qualities.
