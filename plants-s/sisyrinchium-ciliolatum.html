Sisyrinchium ciliolatum

Sisyrinchium ciliolatum, commonly known as the silky blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to purple flowers with white centers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium ciliolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
