Senecillis vellerea

Senecillis vellerea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance.

Senecillis vellerea is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion along shorelines.
