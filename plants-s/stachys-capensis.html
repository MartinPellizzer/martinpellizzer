Stachys capensis

Stachys capensis, commonly known as the Cape betony, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and mountainous regions.

Stachys capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
