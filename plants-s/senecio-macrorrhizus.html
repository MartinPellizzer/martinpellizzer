Senecio macrorrhizus

Senecio macrorrhizus, commonly known as the giant root senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in dense, branched inflorescences, typically during the summer months.

Due to its unique root system and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
