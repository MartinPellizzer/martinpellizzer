Schoenoplectiella erecta subsp. raynalii

Schoenoplectiella erecta subsp.

raynalii, commonly known as the Raynal's bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with moderate to high water levels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense vegetation mat.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
