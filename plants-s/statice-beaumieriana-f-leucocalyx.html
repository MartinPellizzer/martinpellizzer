Statice beaumieriana f. leucocalyx

Statice beaumieriana f. leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered statice, is a cultivar of the statice genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, white, papery flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of tightly packed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
