Salix dalungensis

Salix dalungensis, commonly known as the Dalung willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and forest edges. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elongated, with a rough texture and a bluish-green upper surface.

Salix dalungensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
