Stachys bolusii

Stachys bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
