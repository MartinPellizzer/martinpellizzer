Spergularia colombiana

Spergularia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit. The plant produces pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to arid environments.

Spergularia colombiana is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in regions with low moisture.
