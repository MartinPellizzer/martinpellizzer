Sedum candollei

Sedum candollei, commonly known as the Canary Island stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, fleshy, elliptical leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum candollei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
