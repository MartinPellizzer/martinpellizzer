Solanum demissum f. perotanum

Solanum demissum f. perotanum, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are typically yellow or white with five lobes. The plant produces small, round berries that are initially green and turn red when mature.

It is native to South America and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
