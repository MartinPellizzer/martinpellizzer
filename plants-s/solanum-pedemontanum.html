Solanum pedemontanum

Solanum pedemontanum, commonly known as the Pedemontanum tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
