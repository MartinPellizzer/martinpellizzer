Sida atherophora

Sida atherophora, commonly known as the silverleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Sida atherophora is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
