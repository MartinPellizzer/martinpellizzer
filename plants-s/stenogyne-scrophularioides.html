Stenogyne scrophularioides

Stenogyne scrophularioides, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within mesic to wet forest habitats. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Stenogyne scrophularioides is known for its hard, dense wood, which has historically been used by Native Hawaiians for various tools and implements.
