Sinoleontopodium

Sinoleontopodium, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that resemble ghostly shapes.

It is native to the wetlands and slow-moving waters of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich environments. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its leaf surfaces, which are covered in a thin, waxy layer. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the water's surface.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is often considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
