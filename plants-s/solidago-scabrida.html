Solidago scabrida

Solidago scabrida, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with fine, rough hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Solidago scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
