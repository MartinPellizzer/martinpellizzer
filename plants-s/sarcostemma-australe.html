Sarcostemma australe

Sarcostemma australe, commonly known as the Australian milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal and sandy areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Sarcostemma australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
