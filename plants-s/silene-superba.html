Silene superba

Silene superba, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
