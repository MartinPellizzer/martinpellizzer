Scabiosa ateridoi

Scabiosa ateridoi, commonly known as the Sicilian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa ateridoi is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
