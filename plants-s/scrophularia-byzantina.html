Scrophularia byzantina

Scrophularia byzantina, commonly known as Byzantine figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in spikes, with tubular corollas that are usually pink to purple in color.

Scrophularia byzantina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
