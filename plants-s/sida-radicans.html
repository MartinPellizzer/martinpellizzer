Sida radicans

Sida radicans, commonly known as creeping sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom throughout the growing season.

Sida radicans is known for its ability to spread via runners, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
