Solanum myriadenium

Solanum myriadenium, commonly known as the many-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for ornamental or culinary use.
