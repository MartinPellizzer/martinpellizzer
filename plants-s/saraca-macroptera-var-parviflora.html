Saraca macroptera var. parviflora

Saraca macroptera var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
