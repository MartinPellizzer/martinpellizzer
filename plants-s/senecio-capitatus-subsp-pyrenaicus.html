Senecio capitatus subsp. pyrenaicus

Senecio capitatus subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact head. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
