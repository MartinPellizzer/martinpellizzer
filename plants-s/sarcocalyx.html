Sarcocalyx

Sarcocalyx, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple blooms that appear during the warmer months. The plant has a compact growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
