Scilla setifera

Scilla setifera, commonly known as the slender scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are small and fibrous, adapted for survival in rocky or dry soils.

Scilla setifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
