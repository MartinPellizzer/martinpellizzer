Schinus dependens

Schinus dependens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or red flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries that are highly ornamental. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes. Schinus dependens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
