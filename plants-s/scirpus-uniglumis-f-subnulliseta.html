Scirpus uniglumis f. subnulliseta

Scirpus uniglumis f. subnulliseta, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species characterized by its reduced or absent flowering structures.

It is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments, typically found in marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming dense stands. Its rhizomes are extensive, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
