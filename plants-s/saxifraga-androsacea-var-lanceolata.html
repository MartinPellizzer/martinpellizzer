Saxifraga androsacea var. lanceolata

Saxifraga androsacea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaf stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures make it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
