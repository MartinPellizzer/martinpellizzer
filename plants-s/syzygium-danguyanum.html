Syzygium danguyanum

Syzygium danguyanum, commonly known as the Danguyanum apple or the mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various parts of its native range.
