Sida excelsior

Sida excelsior, commonly known as yellow mallow, is an herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall and is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. Its fibrous stem and hairy leaves are characteristic of the Malvaceae family.

Sida excelsior is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
