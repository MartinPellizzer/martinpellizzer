Salvia officinalis subsp. lavandulifolia

Salvia officinalis subsp.

lavandulifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture and produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils rich in compounds like thujone and camphor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is closely related to common sage and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
