Stylidium septentrionale

Stylidium septentrionale, commonly known as the northern spurred orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique spurred petal that resembles a tiny tail. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive three-lobed labellum.

Stylidium septentrionale is a rare and specialized species, often found in isolated populations within its limited geographic range.
