Stelis azuayensis

Stelis azuayensis, commonly known as the Azuay orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It features a short, erect stem with several fleshy, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine roots. The flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

Stelis azuayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
