Saxifraga mucronulata subsp. sikkimensis

Saxifraga mucronulata subsp.

sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to cold climates and its ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
