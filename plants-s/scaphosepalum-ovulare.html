Scaphosepalum ovulare

Scaphosepalum ovulare, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, with the labellum being distinctly shaped and colored.

Scaphosepalum ovulare thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
