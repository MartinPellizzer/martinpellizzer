Sarothamnus maurus

Sarothamnus maurus, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
