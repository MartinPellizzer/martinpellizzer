Sidalcea asprella var. robusta

Sidalcea asprella var. robusta, commonly known as the robust mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun. Its stems are erect and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
