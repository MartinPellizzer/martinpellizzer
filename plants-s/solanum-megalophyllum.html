Solanum megalophyllum

Solanum megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in the summer. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

Solanum megalophyllum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
