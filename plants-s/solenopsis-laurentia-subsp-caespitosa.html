Solenopsis laurentia subsp. caespitosa

Solenopsis laurentia subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted antplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and elliptic, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments, typically found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
