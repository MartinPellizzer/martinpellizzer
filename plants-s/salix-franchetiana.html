Salix franchetiana

Salix franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, oval-shaped female flowers. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.
