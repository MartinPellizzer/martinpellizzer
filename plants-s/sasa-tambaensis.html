Sasa tambaensis

Sasa tambaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Tamba bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Sasa tambaensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its strong, durable shoots.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a decorative plant in gardens.
