Stryphnodendron velutinum

Stryphnodendron velutinum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elongated, trifoliate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pods containing edible, brown seeds. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
