Syzygium rostratum

Syzygium rostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and leaves are also utilized in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Syzygium rostratum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
