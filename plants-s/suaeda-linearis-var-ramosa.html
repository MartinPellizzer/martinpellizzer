Suaeda linearis var. ramosa

Suaeda linearis var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features succulent, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

Suaeda linearis var. ramosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its salt tolerance.
