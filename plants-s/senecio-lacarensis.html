Senecio lacarensis

Senecio lacarensis, commonly known as the Cape gold or Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its bright yellow flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio lacarensis is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
