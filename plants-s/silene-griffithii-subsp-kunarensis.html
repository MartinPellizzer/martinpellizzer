Silene griffithii subsp. kunarensis

Silene griffithii subsp.

kunarensis, commonly known as the Kashmir silene, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
