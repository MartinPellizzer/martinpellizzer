Syzygium brachybotryum

Syzygium brachybotryum, commonly known as the small-fruited tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, round, red or yellow fruits that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable resource in traditional cuisines.
