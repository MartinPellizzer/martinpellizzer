Sasa cochlearispatha

Sasa cochlearispatha, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
