Schrenkia pulverulenta

Schrenkia pulverulenta, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a compact, succulent-like growth habit, allowing it to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

Schrenkia pulverulenta is adapted to harsh desert environments, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.
