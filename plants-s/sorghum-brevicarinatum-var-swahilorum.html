Sorghum brevicarinatum var. swahilorum

Sorghum brevicarinatum var. swahilorum, commonly known as the Swahili sorghum, is a variant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically produces bristly, pale green to brown spikelets that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its resilience to drought and its potential use in both traditional agriculture and ornamental horticulture.
