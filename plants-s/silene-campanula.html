Silene campanula

Silene campanula, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Silene campanula is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
