Stizolobium axillare

Stizolobium axillare, commonly known as the axillary legume, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and it is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
