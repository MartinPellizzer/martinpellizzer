Salvia grahamii

Salvia grahamii, commonly known as Graham's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
