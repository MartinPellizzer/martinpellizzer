Sykesia pendula

Sykesia pendula, commonly known as the hanging sykesia, is a climbing shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous stems that hang gracefully from trees or shrubs. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Sykesia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
