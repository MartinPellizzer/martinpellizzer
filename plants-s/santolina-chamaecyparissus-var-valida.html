Santolina chamaecyparissus var. valida

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. valida, commonly known as the Spanish santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with a slight woolly texture, and its small, bright yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has a dense, rounded growth habit and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal and dry climates.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in both landscape design and herbal gardening.
