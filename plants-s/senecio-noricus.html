Senecio noricus

Senecio noricus, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Senecio noricus is often found in alpine meadows and is an important component of mountain ecosystems.
