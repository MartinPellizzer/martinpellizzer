Salmia palmifolia

Salmia palmifolia, commonly known as the palm-leaf salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its palm-like leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical classification places it within the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Salmia palmifolia is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its medicinal properties in local cultures.
