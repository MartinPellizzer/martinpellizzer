Sagittaria planipes

Sagittaria planipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike-like cluster.

Sagittaria planipes plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
