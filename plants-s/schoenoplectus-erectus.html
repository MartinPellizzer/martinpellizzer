Schoenoplectus erectus

Schoenoplectus erectus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that appear in spikes during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively in wetland environments.

Schoenoplectus erectus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
