Senecio amplifolius

Senecio amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio amplifolius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
