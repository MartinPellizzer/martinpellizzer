Spiraea japonica var. pinnatifida

Spiraea japonica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
