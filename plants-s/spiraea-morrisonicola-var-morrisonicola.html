Spiraea morrisonicola var. morrisonicola

Spiraea morrisonicola var. morrisonicola, commonly known as Morrison's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring bloom.

The species is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
