Stipagrostis proxima

Stipagrostis proxima, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fibrous leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowering heads are spike-like and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipagrostis proxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
