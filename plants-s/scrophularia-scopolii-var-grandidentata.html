Scrophularia scopolii var. grandidentata

Scrophularia scopolii var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed figwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soil. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
