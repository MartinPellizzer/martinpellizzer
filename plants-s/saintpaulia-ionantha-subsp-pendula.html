Saintpaulia ionantha subsp. pendula

Saintpaulia ionantha subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging African violet, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical Africa. It features velvety, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive purplish hue.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its pendulous growth habit makes it ideal for hanging baskets or shelves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth pattern and unique leaf morphology.
