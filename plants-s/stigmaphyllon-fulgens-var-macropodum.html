Stigmaphyllon fulgens var. macropodum

Stigmaphyllon fulgens var. macropodum, commonly known as the golden stigmaphyllon, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences, which are clustered in dense, showy spikes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
