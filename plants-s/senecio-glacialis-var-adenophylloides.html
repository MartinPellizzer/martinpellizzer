Senecio glacialis var. adenophylloides

Senecio glacialis var. adenophylloides, commonly known as the glacier senecio, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, providing insulation against freezing temperatures.

This variety is well adapted to high-altitude conditions, showcasing specialized features for survival in extreme climates.
