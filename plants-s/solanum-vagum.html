Solanum vagum

Solanum vagum, commonly known as the wandering nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually white or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
