Staurogyne macrophylla

Staurogyne macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved water starwort, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant has a branched root system that helps it anchor in muddy or sandy substrates. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Staurogyne macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
