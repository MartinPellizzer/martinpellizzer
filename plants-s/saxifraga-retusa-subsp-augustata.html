Saxifraga retusa subsp. augustata

Saxifraga retusa subsp.

augustata, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suited to extreme environments.
