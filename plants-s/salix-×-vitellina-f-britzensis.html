Salix × vitellina f. britzensis

Salix × vitellina f. britzensis, commonly known as the Britzensis willow, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are the primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
