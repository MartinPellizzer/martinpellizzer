Salix × goerffyi

Salix × goerffyi, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that drape gracefully from its slender trunk. The plant has finely toothed, bright green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
