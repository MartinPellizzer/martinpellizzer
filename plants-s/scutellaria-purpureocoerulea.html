Scutellaria purpureocoerulea

Scutellaria purpureocoerulea, commonly known as the purple-blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria purpureocoerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
