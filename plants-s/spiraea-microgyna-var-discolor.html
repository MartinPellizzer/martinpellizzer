Spiraea microgyna var. discolor

Spiraea microgyna var. discolor, commonly known as the white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a silvery or whitish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
