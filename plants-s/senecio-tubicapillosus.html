Senecio tubicapillosus

Senecio tubicapillosus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
