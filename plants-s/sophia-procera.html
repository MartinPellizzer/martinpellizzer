Sophia procera

Sophia procera, commonly known as the towering sage, is a striking member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
