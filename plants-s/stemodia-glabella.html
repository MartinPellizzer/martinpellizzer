Stemodia glabella

Stemodia glabella, commonly known as the smooth stemless daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, unbranched stem and a cluster of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
