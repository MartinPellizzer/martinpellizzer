Senecio incaicus

Senecio incaicus, commonly known as the Inca groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio incaicus is adapted to cold, arid conditions and is often used in traditional Andean medicine.
