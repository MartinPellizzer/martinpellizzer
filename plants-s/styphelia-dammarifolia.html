Styphelia dammarifolia

Styphelia dammarifolia, commonly known as the dammarifoliate heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Styphelia dammarifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and swamp environments.
