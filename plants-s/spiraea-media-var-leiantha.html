Spiraea media var. leiantha

Spiraea media var. leiantha, commonly known as the snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, compact form. Its foliage is dark green in summer and turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
