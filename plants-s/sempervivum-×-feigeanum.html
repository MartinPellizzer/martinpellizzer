Sempervivum × feigeanum

Sempervivum × feigeanum, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Sempervivum arachnoideum and Sempervivum tectorum, characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically fleshy, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often in clusters at the center of the rosette.

This plant is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
