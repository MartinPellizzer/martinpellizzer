Salvia viscidifolia

Salvia viscidifolia, commonly known as sticky sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, and its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
