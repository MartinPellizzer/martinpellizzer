Setaria geniculata var. latifolia

Setaria geniculata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
