Scirpus stenophyllus

Scirpus stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. Scirpus stenophyllus typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
