Salix × paraleuca

Salix × paraleuca, commonly known as white willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and white to pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring.

Salix × paraleuca is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
