Sida fasciculiflora

Sida fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadside areas. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Sida fasciculiflora is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
