Schedonorus gudoschnikovii

Schedonorus gudoschnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian grass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.
