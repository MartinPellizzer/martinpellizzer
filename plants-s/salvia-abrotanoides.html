Salvia abrotanoides

Salvia abrotanoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Salvia abrotanoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
