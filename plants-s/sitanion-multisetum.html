Sitanion multisetum

Sitanion multisetum, commonly known as the many-branched grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

Sitanion multisetum is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
