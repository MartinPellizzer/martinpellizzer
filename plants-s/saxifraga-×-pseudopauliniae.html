Saxifraga × pseudopauliniae

Saxifraga × pseudopauliniae, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, typically arranged in a cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
