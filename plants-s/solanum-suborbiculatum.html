Solanum suborbiculatum

Solanum suborbiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruits are typically small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

This species is often found in dry shrublands and grasslands, adapting well to arid environments.
