Solanum rubicaule

Solanum rubicaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that ripen in the summer. Solanum rubicaule contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
