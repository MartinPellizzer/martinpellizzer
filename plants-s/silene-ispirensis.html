Silene ispirensis

Silene ispirensis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
