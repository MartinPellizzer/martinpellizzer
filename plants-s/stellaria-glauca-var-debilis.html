Stellaria glauca var. debilis

Stellaria glauca var. debilis, commonly known as the weak starwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, delicate white flowers with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, rounded leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.
