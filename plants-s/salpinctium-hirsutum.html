Salpinctium hirsutum

Salpinctium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap.

Salpinctium hirsutum plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
