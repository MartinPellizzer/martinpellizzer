Serjania elongata

Serjania elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its elongated, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Serjania elongata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
