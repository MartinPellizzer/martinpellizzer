Symplocos penangiana

Symplocos penangiana, commonly known as Penang beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered, dark purple berries that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.
