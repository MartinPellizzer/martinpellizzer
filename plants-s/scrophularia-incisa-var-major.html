Scrophularia incisa var. major

Scrophularia incisa var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and pollinator habitats.
