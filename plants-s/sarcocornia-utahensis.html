Sarcocornia utahensis

Sarcocornia utahensis, commonly known as Utah pickleweed, is a halophyte native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish and bloom in clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
