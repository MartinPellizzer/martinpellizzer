Senecio sibiricus

Senecio sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Senecio sibiricus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
