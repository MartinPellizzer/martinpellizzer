Silene nelsonii

Silene nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene nelsonii is often found in alpine and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
