Solanum viscidum

Solanum viscidum, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Solanum viscidum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
