Salix × tamagawaensis

Salix × tamagawaensis, commonly known as the Tamagawa willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and delicate, bright green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and adaptability.

Salix × tamagawaensis is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
