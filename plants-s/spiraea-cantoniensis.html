Spiraea cantoniensis

Spiraea cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea cantoniensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate floral display and compact growth habit.
