Stachytarpheta incana var. angustibracteata

Stachytarpheta incana var. angustibracteata, commonly known as narrow-bract blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
