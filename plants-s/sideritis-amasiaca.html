Sideritis amasiaca

Sideritis amasiaca, commonly known as Armenian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Sideritis amasiaca is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
