Swertia obtusa f. leucantha

Swertia obtusa f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered swertia, is a variant of the Swertia genus native to the Himalayan regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, distinguishing it from other species in the genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable subject for studies in alpine flora and traditional medicine.
