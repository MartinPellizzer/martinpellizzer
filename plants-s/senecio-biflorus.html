Senecio biflorus

Senecio biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio biflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
