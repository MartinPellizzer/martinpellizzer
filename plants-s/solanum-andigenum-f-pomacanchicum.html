Solanum andigenum f. pomacanchicum

Solanum andigenum f. pomacanchicum, commonly known as the Andean tomato, is a botanical variant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. This plant is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
