Sparaxis atropurpurea

Sparaxis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple grass gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sparaxis atropurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
