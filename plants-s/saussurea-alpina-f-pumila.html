Saussurea alpina f. pumila

Saussurea alpina f. pumila, commonly known as the alpine saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
