Scleroxylon

Scleroxylon, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark and produces a valuable fruit with a hard, oily seed. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a rough texture, and it flowers in clusters.

The plant is highly valued for its shea butter, which is used in cosmetics and food products.
