Stironeurum stipulatum

Stironeurum stipulatum, commonly known as the stipule leafcup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
