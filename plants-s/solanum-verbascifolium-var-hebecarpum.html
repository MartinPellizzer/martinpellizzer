Solanum verbascifolium var. hebecarpum

Solanum verbascifolium var. hebecarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are violet to purple, and it produces small, elongated fruits that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
