Setaria cordobensis

Setaria cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba Panicgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Setaria cordobensis is adapted to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
