Spiraea nayarii

Spiraea nayarii, commonly known as the Mexican spirea, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, often arching stems. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Spiraea nayarii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
