Sorbus hajastana

Sorbus hajastana, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
