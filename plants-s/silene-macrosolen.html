Silene macrosolen

Silene macrosolen, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Silene macrosolen is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
