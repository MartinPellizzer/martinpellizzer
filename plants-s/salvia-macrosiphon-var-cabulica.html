Salvia macrosiphon var. cabulica

Salvia macrosiphon var. cabulica, commonly known as Afghan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice among gardeners.
