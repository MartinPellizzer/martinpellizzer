Solanum macolae

Solanum macolae, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Solanum macolae belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
