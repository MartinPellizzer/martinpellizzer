Solidago macrophylla f. ramosissima

Solidago macrophylla f. ramosissima, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in panicles.

This variety is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
