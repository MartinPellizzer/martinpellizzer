Silene bocinoi

Silene bocinoi, commonly known as the Bocino's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Silene bocinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to harsh mountainous conditions.
