Sabina grandis

Sabina grandis, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a species of evergreen conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Sabina grandis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a hedge.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
