Sida nudiflora

Sida nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, pale green or white, and are borne in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Sida nudiflora is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
