Selaginella setosa

Selaginella setosa, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are released into the air for dispersal.

Selaginella setosa is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in various ecosystems.
