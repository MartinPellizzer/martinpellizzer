Solanum dulcamara f. rupestre

Solanum dulcamara f. rupestre, commonly known as red bittersweet, is a variant of the common bittersweet plant.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, showy flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are usually yellow with orange stamens, and the fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, and it has a historical association with traditional herbal medicine.
