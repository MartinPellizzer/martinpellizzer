Salvia greggii

Salvia greggii, commonly known as Gregg's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to red. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia greggii is also known for its fragrant foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
