Solidago pulchra

Solidago pulchra, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago pulchra plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
