Syngonium occidentale

Syngonium occidentale, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive arrowhead pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red berries. Syngonium occidentale thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in the right conditions.
