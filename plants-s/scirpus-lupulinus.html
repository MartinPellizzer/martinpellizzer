Scirpus lupulinus

Scirpus lupulinus, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Scirpus lupulinus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
