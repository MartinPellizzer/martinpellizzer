Seddera arabica var. microphylla

Seddera arabica var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Arabian seddera, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its fibrous roots allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in local communities.
