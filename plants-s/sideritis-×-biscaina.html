Sideritis × biscaina

Sideritis × biscaina, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. Sideritis × biscaina is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory effects.
