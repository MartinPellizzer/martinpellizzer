Salicornia disarticulata

Salicornia disarticulata, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, where it thrives in high-salinity conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Salicornia disarticulata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
