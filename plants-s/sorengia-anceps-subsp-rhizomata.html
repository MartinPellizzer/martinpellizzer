Sorengia anceps subsp. rhizomata

Sorengia anceps subsp.

rhizomata, commonly known as the rhizomatous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is native to parts of Asia.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient and often invasive species in suitable habitats.

The botanical features of this subspecies are well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
