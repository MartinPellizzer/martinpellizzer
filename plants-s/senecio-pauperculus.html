Senecio pauperculus

Senecio pauperculus, commonly known as the poor man's spider plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
