Sherardia muralis

Sherardia muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry environments, often along walls or in crevices. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Sherardia muralis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
