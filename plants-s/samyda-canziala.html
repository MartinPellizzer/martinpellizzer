Samyda canziala

Samyda canziala, commonly known as the Canebrake or Canebrake Grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has long, slender blades and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

Samyda canziala plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
