Selaginella shabaensis

Selaginella shabaensis, commonly known as the Shaba spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often needle-like in appearance. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
