Smilax reticulata

Smilax reticulata, commonly known as the greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Smilax reticulata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its aggressive growth habit makes it both a valuable ecological component and a potential invasive species in certain areas.
