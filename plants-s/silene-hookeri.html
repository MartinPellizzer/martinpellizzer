Silene hookeri

Silene hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene hookeri is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance to pollinators.
