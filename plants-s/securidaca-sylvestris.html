Securidaca sylvestris

Securidaca sylvestris, commonly known as the wild bean or African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
