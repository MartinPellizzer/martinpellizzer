Sisymbrium lasiolobum

Sisymbrium lasiolobum, commonly known as the woolly brookweed, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as streambanks and wet meadows.
