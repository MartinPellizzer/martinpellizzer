Silene tatarica var. wilfordii

Silene tatarica var. wilfordii, commonly known as the Tatarian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Silene tatarica var. wilfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
