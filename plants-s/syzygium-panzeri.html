Syzygium panzeri

Syzygium panzeri, commonly known as the Java apple or wild durian, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant, and often spiky fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by the distinctive, fleshy, and sometimes odorous fruit. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Syzygium panzeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
