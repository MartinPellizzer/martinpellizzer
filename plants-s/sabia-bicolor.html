Sabia bicolor

Sabia bicolor, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are slender and often hairy.

Sabia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique flower shape.
