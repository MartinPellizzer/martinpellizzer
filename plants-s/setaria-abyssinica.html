Setaria abyssinica

Setaria abyssinica, commonly known as African millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop in arid regions. Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used for food and animal feed.

The plant also plays a role in soil conservation due to its extensive root system.
