Spiraea tomentosa var. pumila

Spiraea tomentosa var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf snowball bush, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has grayish-white, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blooms. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it suitable for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
