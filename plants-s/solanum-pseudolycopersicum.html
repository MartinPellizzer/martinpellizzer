Solanum pseudolycopersicum

Solanum pseudolycopersicum, commonly known as the tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to western South America and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that mature from green to red, containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary use and is rich in nutrients like vitamin C and lycopene.

Botanically, it is a dicot with a taproot system and undergoes complete flower development.
