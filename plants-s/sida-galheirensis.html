Sida galheirensis

Sida galheirensis, commonly known as Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sida galheirensis is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
