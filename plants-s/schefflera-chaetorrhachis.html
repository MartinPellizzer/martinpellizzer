Schefflera chaetorrhachis

Schefflera chaetorrhachis, commonly known as the hairy-veined umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that radiate from a single point, resembling an umbrella. The leaves are typically dark green with a rough, textured surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
