Samyda ilicifolia

Samyda ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits. Samyda ilicifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its growth habit and leaf structure make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
