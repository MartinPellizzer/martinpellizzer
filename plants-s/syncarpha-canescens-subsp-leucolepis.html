Syncarpha canescens subsp. leucolepis

Syncarpha canescens subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the white-haired synkarpa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
