Smilax diversifolia

Smilax diversifolia, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically elliptical and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Smilax diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
