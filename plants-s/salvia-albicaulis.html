Salvia albicaulis

Salvia albicaulis, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and spikes of pale blue to white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Salvia albicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
