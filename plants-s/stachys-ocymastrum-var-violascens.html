Stachys ocymastrum var. violascens

Stachys ocymastrum var. violascens, commonly known as violet wood betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
