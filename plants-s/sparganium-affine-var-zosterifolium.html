Sparganium affine var. zosterifolium

Sparganium affine var. zosterifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bur-reed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as pond margins and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense stands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs, and its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface. Its most distinctive feature is its spike-like inflorescence, which consists of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by bracts.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
