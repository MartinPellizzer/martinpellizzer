Sorbus maderensis

Sorbus maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Alps. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

Sorbus maderensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
