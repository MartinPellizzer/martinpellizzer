Sorghum pratense

Sorghum pratense, commonly known as purpletop tridens, is a warm-season grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking purple-tinted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
