Sarcoglottis sarthouae

Sarcoglottis sarthouae, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and striking coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
