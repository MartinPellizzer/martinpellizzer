Sedum alfredi var. bulbiferum

Sedum alfredi var. bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbous stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
