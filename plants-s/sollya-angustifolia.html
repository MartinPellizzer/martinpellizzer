Sollya angustifolia

Sollya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sollya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, opposite leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
