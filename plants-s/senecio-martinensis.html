Senecio martinensis

Senecio martinensis, commonly known as the Martin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
