Silene italica var. arenicola

Silene italica var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand pink, is a flowering plant native to Mediterranean regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene italica var. arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
