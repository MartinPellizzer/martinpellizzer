Spiraea

Spiraea, commonly known as meadow spirea, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These shrubs are characterized by their clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge. Spiraea species are often found in moist or well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

They are valued in gardens for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
