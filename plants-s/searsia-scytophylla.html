Searsia scytophylla

Searsia scytophylla, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, bright yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Searsia scytophylla is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects.
