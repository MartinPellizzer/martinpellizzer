Sigesbeckia formosana

Sigesbeckia formosana, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
