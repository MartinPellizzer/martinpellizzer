Stixis suaveolens var. cochinsinensis

Stixis suaveolens var. cochinsinensis, commonly known as the sweet-scented stixis, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its flowers are white to pale yellow in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
