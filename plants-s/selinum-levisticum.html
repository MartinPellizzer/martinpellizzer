Selinum levisticum

Selinum levisticum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial or annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into small, round, black seeds. Its leaves and seeds are rich in essential oils, contributing to its culinary and medicinal uses.

Selinum levisticum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular garden plant.
