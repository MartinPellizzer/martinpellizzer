Sphaeropteris stipitipinnula

Sphaeropteris stipitipinnula, commonly known as the slender-leafed cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds that are typically slender and gracefully curved. The plant has a short, stout trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark, and its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern. Sphaeropteris stipitipinnula is known for its slow growth and preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
