Schistocarpha velutina

Schistocarpha velutina, commonly known as the velvethead bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Schistocarpha velutina is a perennial herb that thrives in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in alpine and arid ecosystems.
