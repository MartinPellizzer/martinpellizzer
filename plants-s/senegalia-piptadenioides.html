Senegalia piptadenioides

Senegalia piptadenioides, commonly known as the screwbean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually reduced to small, stiff, spirally arranged stipules, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiral-shaped pods. It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of fodder.

Senegalia piptadenioides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
