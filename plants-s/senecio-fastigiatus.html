Senecio fastigiatus

Senecio fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
