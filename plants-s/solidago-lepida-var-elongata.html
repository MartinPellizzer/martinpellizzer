Solidago lepida var. elongata

Solidago lepida var. elongata, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
