Syncephalum candidum

Syncephalum candidum, commonly known as the white-headed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of white disk florets. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
