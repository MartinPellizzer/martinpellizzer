Saxifraga robusta

Saxifraga robusta, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and come in shades of pink, red, or white.

Saxifraga robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.
