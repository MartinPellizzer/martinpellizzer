Spiranthes rupestris var. parviflora

Spiranthes rupestris var. parviflora, commonly known as the rock twayblade, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true leaves and its specialized root system.
