Serapias fasciculata

Serapias fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces racemes of flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Serapias fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
