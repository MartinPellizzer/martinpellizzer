Syngonanthus eriophyllus var. glandulifera

Syngonanthus eriophyllus var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically glandular-pubescent, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
