Salix alba f. chermesina

Salix alba f. chermesina, commonly known as the white willow with red leaves, is a cultivar of the white willow tree.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts vividly with its pale green shoots and white bark. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a serrated margin. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
