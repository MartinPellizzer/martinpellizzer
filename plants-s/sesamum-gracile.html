Sesamum gracile

Sesamum gracile, commonly known as slender sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. Sesamum gracile is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
