Samolus valerandi var. americanus

Samolus valerandi var. americanus, commonly known as American marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
