Senecio pinachensis

Senecio pinachensis, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Senecio pinachensis has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that appear in late spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
