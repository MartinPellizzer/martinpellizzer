Scleromitrion verticillatum

Scleromitrion verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks.

Scleromitrion verticillatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
