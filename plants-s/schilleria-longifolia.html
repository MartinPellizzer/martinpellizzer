Schilleria longifolia

Schilleria longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf schilleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Schilleria longifolia is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
