Saussurea semifasciata

Saussurea semifasciata, commonly known as the veined arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
