Serianthes petitiana

Serianthes petitiana, commonly known as the "ghost tree," is a rare and endangered species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, producing pale yellow blooms that are not particularly showy.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
