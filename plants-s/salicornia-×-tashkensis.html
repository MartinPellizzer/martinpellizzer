Salicornia × tashkensis

Salicornia × tashkensis, commonly known as the Tashkent glasswort, is a halophyte plant native to Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal and inland salt marshes. The plant exhibits succulent stems and leaves that store water, aiding its survival in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in pink or red hues.

Salicornia × tashkensis is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional foods and as a source of bioactive compounds.
