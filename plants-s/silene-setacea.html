Silene setacea

Silene setacea, commonly known as the tufted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene setacea is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.
