Solena scandens

Solena scandens, commonly known as the climbing solena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solena scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
