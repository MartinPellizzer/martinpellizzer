Solanum nudum var. pseudoindigoferum

Solanum nudum var. pseudoindigoferum, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, smooth, green capsule that matures into a dry, woody structure.
