Senecio purpureus

Senecio purpureus, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple-pink flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio purpureus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
