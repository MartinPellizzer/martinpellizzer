Salix × grahamii var. moorei

Salix × grahamii var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist soils near water sources and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
