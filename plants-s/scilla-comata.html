Scilla comata

Scilla comata, commonly known as the woolly squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its dense, white, woolly coating on the leaves and stems, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Scilla comata is valued in gardens for its early spring blooms and ornamental appeal.
