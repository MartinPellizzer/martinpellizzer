Salvia leclerei

Salvia leclerei, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia leclerei is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and drought tolerance.
