Swertia wattii

Swertia wattii, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Swertia wattii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.
