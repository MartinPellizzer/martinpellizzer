Sorbus luristanica

Sorbus luristanica, commonly known as the Luristan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent fruits that are aggregate berries. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a degree of drought tolerance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
