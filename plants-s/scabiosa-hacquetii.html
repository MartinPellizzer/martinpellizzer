Scabiosa hacquetii

Scabiosa hacquetii, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to cold climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
