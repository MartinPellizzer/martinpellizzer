Salvia officinalis variegata

Salvia officinalis variegata, commonly known as variegated sage, is a cultivar of the common sage plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring shades of green, white, and cream. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.

Its ornamental value and aromatic leaves make it both a decorative and functional plant.
