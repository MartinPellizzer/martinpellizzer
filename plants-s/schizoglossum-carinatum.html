Schizoglossum carinatum

Schizoglossum carinatum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structure with a prominent central ridge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and are designed to attract specific pollinators.

Schizoglossum carinatum is noted for its intricate floral morphology, which reflects adaptations for specialized pollination strategies.
