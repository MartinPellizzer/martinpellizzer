Setaria glauca var. ambigua

Setaria glauca var. ambigua, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
