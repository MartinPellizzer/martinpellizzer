Sagina × scotica

Sagina × scotica, commonly known as the common sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. Sagina × scotica thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
