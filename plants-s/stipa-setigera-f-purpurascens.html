Stipa setigera f. purpurascens

Stipa setigera f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often bluish-green in color. This variety is distinguished by its vibrant purple tinge in the inflorescences, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
