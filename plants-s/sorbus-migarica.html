Sorbus migarica

Sorbus migarica, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

Sorbus migarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
