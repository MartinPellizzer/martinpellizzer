Salvia microcalyx

Salvia microcalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance contribute to its value in both ornamental and ecological gardens.
