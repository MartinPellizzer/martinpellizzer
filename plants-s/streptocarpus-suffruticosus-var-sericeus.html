Streptocarpus suffruticosus var. sericeus

Streptocarpus suffruticosus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
