Stipa × gnezdilloi

Stipa × gnezdilloi, commonly known as the hybrid false oat, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Stipa capillata and Stipa robusta.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in temperate regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its morphology reflects a combination of traits from both parental species, making it a notable example of interspecific hybridization in the grass family.

Stipa × gnezdilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
