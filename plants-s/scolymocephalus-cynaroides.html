Scolymocephalus cynaroides

Scolymocephalus cynaroides, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Scolymocephalus cynaroides is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
