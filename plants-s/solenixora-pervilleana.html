Solenixora pervilleana

Solenixora pervilleana, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its unique floral structure and coloration attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solenixora pervilleana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xeriscaping.
