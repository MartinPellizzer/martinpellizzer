Scindapsus pteropodus

Scindapsus pteropodus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The leaves often have a distinctive silvery or variegated pattern, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
