Silene invisa

Silene invisa, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Silene invisa is known for its ephemeral blooming period, making it a rare and delicate sight in its natural environment.
