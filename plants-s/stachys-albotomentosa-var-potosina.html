Stachys albotomentosa var. potosina

Stachys albotomentosa var. potosina, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

It is commonly found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
