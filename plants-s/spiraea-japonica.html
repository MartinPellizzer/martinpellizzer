Spiraea japonica

Spiraea japonica, commonly known as Japanese spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
