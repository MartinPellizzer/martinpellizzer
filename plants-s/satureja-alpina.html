Satureja alpina

Satureja alpina, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic leaves that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Satureja alpina is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties, particularly for its antimicrobial and antioxidant compounds.
