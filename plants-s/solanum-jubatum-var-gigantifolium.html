Solanum jubatum var. gigantifolium

Solanum jubatum var. gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant-leaved nightshade, is a striking plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
