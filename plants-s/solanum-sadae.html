Solanum sadae

Solanum sadae, commonly known as the Sadae nightshade, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a dark purple hue.

Solanum sadae is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often lobed or deeply toothed.
