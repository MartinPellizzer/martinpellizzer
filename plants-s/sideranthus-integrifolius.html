Sideranthus integrifolius

Sideranthus integrifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. The flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and have five petals that are usually pink to purple in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
