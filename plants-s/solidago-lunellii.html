Solidago lunellii

Solidago lunellii, commonly known as the moonflower goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, woodland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in panicles.

Solidago lunellii is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp environments where it contributes to local biodiversity.
