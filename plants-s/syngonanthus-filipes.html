Syngonanthus filipes

Syngonanthus filipes, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing tufts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Syngonanthus filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
