Scorzonera praetuberosa

Scorzonera praetuberosa, commonly known as the mountain scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. Its root system is tuberous, and the roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera praetuberosa thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
