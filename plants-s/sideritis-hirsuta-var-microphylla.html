Sideritis hirsuta var. microphylla

Sideritis hirsuta var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
