Sterculia helicteres

Sterculia helicteres, commonly known as the silky oaks or the Australian silky oak, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its hard, durable wood.
