Senecio pogonias

Senecio pogonias, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio pogonias is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
