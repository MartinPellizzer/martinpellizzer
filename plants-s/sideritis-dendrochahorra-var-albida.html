Sideritis dendrochahorra var. albida

Sideritis dendrochahorra var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and arranged in dense spikes, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
