Schefflera alongensis

Schefflera alongensis, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are divided into numerous leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a distinctive shield-like shape at the base of each leaflet.

Schefflera alongensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
