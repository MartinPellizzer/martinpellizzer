Senecio badilloi

Senecio badilloi, commonly known as the Chihuahuan Desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Senecio badilloi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
