Salvia colonica

Salvia colonica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Salvia colonica is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
