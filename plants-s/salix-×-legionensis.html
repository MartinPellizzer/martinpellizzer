Salix × legionensis

Salix × legionensis, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Salix × legionensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
