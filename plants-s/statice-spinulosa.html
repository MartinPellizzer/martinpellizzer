Statice spinulosa

Statice spinulosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like flower heads and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Statice spinulosa is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental value.
