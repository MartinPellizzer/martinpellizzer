Saxifraga laciniata var. takedana

Saxifraga laciniata var. takedana, commonly known as the Takeda's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
