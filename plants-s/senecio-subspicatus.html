Senecio subspicatus

Senecio subspicatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in dense spikes. Senecio subspicatus is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
