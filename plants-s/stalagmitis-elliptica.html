Stalagmitis elliptica

Stalagmitis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic stalagmite plant, is a unique species native to the limestone caves of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that grow in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with consistent moisture levels. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the mineral-rich cave floor.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
