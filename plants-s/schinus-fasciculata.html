Schinus fasciculata

Schinus fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a reddish or brownish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other plants like cashew and poison ivy.
