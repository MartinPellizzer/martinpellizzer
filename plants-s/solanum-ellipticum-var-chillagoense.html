Solanum ellipticum var. chillagoense

Solanum ellipticum var. chillagoense, commonly known as the Chillago nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming in spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.
