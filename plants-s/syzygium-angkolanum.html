Syzygium angkolanum

Syzygium angkolanum, commonly known as the Kali Kumbhong or Malabar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Syzygium angkolanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.
