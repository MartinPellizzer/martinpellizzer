Solenocarpus philippinensis

Solenocarpus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Solenocarpus philippinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in lowland forests.
