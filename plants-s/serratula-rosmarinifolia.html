Serratula rosmarinifolia

Serratula rosmarinifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is perennial, often growing in rocky or dry soils, and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and have a mild, camomile-like scent.

Serratula rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
