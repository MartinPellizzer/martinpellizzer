Scirpus atrocinctus

Scirpus atrocinctus, commonly known as black-stem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense stands.

Scirpus atrocinctus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and wetland organisms.
