Saccus glaucescens

Saccus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue sac fungus, is a unique species of fungus characterized by its distinctive sac-like structure.

It belongs to the family Sarcoscygaceae and is notable for its glaucous, bluish-gray appearance. The fruiting body of Saccus glaucescens is typically small, with a smooth, membranous surface that becomes more pronounced as it matures. It thrives in damp, shaded environments, often found in forested areas with rich organic soil.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a specialized and less commonly encountered fungus.
