Shorea macrantha

Shorea macrantha, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a capsule containing winged seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its strength, durability, and rich golden color, making it a significant timber species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native forests, contributing to biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
