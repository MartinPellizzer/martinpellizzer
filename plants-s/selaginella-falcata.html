Selaginella falcata

Selaginella falcata, commonly known as the sickle spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its creeping stems and dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Its ability to survive extreme conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
