Salix matsudana f. rubriflora

Salix matsudana f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered weeping willow, is a unique cultivar of the weeping willow tree.

It is characterized by its striking red-brown catkins that appear in early spring, adding a distinctive visual appeal. The plant has long, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental willow is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and ability to provide shade.
