Scutellaria uliginosa

Scutellaria uliginosa, commonly known as swamp skullcap, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scutellaria uliginosa is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous peoples for its purported healing benefits.
