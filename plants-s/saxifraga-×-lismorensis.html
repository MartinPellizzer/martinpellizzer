Saxifraga × lismorensis

Saxifraga × lismorensis, commonly known as the Lismore saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga myrtilloides and Saxifraga oppositifolia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, a characteristic adaptation to cold and arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Saxifraga × lismorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
