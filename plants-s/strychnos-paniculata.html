Strychnos paniculata

Strychnos paniculata, commonly known as the Indian strychnos, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing yellowish seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
