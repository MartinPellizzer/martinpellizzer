Sedum quitense

Sedum quitense, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum quitense is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
