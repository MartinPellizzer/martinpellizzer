Sagittaria andina

Sagittaria andina, commonly known as the Andean arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow water and wetlands, with its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves floating on the water surface. The plant has submerged and floating leaves, and it produces small white or pink flowers that bloom above the water. It is often used in water gardens and can help in water filtration.

Sagittaria andina is also a popular choice for aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
