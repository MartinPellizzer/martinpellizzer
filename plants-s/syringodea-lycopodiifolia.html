Syringodea lycopodiifolia

Syringodea lycopodiifolia, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores rather than seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots form dense networks that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Syringodea lycopodiifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
