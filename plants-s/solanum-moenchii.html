Solanum moenchii

Solanum moenchii, commonly known as the redcurrant plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are white or pale purple with five petals.

Solanum moenchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
