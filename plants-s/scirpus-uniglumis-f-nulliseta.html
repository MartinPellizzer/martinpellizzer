Scirpus uniglumis f. nulliseta

Scirpus uniglumis f. nulliseta, commonly known as the barren-stem bulrush, is a rare and distinctive variant of the bulrush genus.

It is characterized by its reduced inflorescence, which lacks the typical dense spikes of its parent species. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense stands that contribute to wetland vegetation structure.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
