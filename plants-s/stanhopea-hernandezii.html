Stanhopea hernandezii

Stanhopea hernandezii, commonly known as the Hernandez's stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
