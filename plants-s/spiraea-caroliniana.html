Spiraea caroliniana

Spiraea caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with a smooth, greyish bark and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and other shaded to partially shaded habitats.
