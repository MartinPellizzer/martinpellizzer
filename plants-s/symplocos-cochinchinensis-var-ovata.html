Symplocos cochinchinensis var. ovata

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often pubescent underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
