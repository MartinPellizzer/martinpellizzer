Senecio tomasianus

Senecio tomasianus, commonly known as the Tomasi's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio tomasianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
