Solanum filamentum

Solanum filamentum, commonly known as the threadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

The berries that follow the flowers are small and yellow, containing the plant's toxic seeds.
