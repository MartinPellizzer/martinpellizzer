Scutellaria cyrtopoda

Scutellaria cyrtopoda, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Scutellaria cyrtopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
