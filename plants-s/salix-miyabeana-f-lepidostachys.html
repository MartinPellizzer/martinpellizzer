Salix miyabeana f. lepidostachys

Salix miyabeana f. lepidostachys, commonly known as the silver-stem willow, is a unique form of the Miyabe willow.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and slender, drooping branches. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its graceful appearance.

The foliage is typically bright green, with a soft texture that adds visual interest to landscapes.
