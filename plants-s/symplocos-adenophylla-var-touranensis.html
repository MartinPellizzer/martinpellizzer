Symplocos adenophylla var. touranensis

Symplocos adenophylla var. touranensis, commonly known as the Touranese waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, with a glossy appearance, and the plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
