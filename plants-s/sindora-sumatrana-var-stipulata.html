Sindora sumatrana var. stipulata

Sindora sumatrana var. stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate sindora, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
