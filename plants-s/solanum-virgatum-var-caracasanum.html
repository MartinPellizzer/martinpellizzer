Solanum virgatum var. caracasanum

Solanum virgatum var. caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracas nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
