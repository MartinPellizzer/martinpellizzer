Scirpus fimbrisetus

Scirpus fimbrisetus, commonly known as the soft-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the plant's ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus fimbrisetus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
