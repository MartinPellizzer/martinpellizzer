Swartzia apiculata

Swartzia apiculata, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Swartzia apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
