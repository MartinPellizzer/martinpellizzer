Solanum recurvum

Solanum recurvum, commonly known as the curly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange berries that mature in late summer.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
