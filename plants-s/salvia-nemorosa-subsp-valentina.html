Salvia nemorosa subsp. valentina

Salvia nemorosa subsp.

valentina, commonly known as Woodland sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
