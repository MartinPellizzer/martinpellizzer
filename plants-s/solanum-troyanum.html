Solanum troyanum

Solanum troyanum, commonly known as the Troyanum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Solanum troyanum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

It is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes but is of interest to botanists due to its unique characteristics.
