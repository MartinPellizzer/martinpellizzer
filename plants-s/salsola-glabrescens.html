Salsola glabrescens

Salsola glabrescens, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments, and its roots are adapted to stabilize sandy soils. It is often found in dunes and disturbed areas, playing a role in preventing soil erosion.

Salsola glabrescens is also known for its ability to thrive in extreme temperatures and low-nutrient conditions.
