Solanum nilagiricum

Solanum nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, cherry-like berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine pubescence.

Solanum nilagiricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
