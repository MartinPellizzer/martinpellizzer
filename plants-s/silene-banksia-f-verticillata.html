Silene banksia f. verticillata

Silene banksia f. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered banksia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of tightly clustered, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
