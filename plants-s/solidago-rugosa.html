Solidago rugosa

Solidago rugosa, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Solidago rugosa is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
