Stachygynandrum subcordatum

Stachygynandrum subcordatum, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a fleshy, lobed calyx, resembling tiny tomatoes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and edible fruits.
