Syzygium acutangulum

Syzygium acutangulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sour taste, similar to tamarind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium acutangulum is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
