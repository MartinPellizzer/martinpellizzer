Sasa hiugensis

Sasa hiugensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Sasa hiugensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
