Scabiosa columbaria subsp. affinis

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the lesser dropwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive, rounded, woolly leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in grassy areas and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
