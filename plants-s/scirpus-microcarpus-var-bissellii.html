Scirpus microcarpus var. bissellii

Scirpus microcarpus var. bissellii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in its survival in wet environments.
