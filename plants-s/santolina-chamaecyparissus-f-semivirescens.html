Santolina chamaecyparissus f. semivirescens

Santolina chamaecyparissus f. semivirescens, commonly known as the Silver Santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is densely packed and highly ornamental. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or borders.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
