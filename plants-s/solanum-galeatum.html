Solanum galeatum

Solanum galeatum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
