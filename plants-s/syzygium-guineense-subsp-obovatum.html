Syzygium guineense subsp. obovatum

Syzygium guineense subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as the African cherry or *Prunus africana*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Myrtaceae family, sharing similar botanical features.
