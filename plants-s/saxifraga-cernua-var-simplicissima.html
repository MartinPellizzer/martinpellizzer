Saxifraga cernua var. simplicissima

Saxifraga cernua var. simplicissima, commonly known as the prostrate saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, basal leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, alpine or subalpine environments.
