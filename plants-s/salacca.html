Salacca

Salacca, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sugar. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties.

Salacca thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
