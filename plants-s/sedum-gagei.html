Sedum gagei

Sedum gagei, commonly known as the Gage's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, Sedum gagei is often used in landscaping for its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
