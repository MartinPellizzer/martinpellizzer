Sambucus mexicana subsp. velutina

Sambucus mexicana subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet elderberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers.

The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional uses.
