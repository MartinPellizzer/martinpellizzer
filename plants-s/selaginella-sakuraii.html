Selaginella sakuraii

Selaginella sakuraii, commonly known as the Japanese clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged along its stems. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Selaginella sakuraii is notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
