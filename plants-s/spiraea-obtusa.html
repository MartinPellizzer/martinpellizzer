Spiraea obtusa

Spiraea obtusa, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea obtusa is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and long-lasting floral display.
