Salix muriei

Salix muriei, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.
