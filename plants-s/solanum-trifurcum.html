Solanum trifurcum

Solanum trifurcum, commonly known as the three-branched nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is generally avoided for consumption by humans and animals.
