Sedum rhodiola var. elongatum

Sedum rhodiola var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments in the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often branching at the base. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
