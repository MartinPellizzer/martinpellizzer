Siphocampylus sonchifolius var. laciniatus

Siphocampylus sonchifolius var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaf siphocampylus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed or cut leaves, which give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
