Sedum robustum

Sedum robustum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun.

Sedum robustum is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance requirements.
