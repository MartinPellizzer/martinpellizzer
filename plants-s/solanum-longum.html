Solanum longum

Solanum longum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries contain numerous small, black seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

Solanum longum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
