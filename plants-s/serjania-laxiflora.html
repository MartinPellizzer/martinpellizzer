Serjania laxiflora

Serjania laxiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
