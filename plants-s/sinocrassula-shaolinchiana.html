Sinocrassula shaolinchiana

Sinocrassula shaolinchiana, commonly known as the Shaolin rock rose, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent leaves. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
