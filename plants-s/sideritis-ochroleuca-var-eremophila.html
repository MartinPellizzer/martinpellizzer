Sideritis ochroleuca var. eremophila

Sideritis ochroleuca var. eremophila, commonly known as the desert germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild antispasmodic properties.
