Synaphea polypodioides

Synaphea polypodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is evergreen and can be found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Synaphea polypodioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate moisture.
