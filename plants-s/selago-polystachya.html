Selago polystachya

Selago polystachya, commonly known as the hairy selago, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, producing yellowish to brownish inflorescences.

Selago polystachya is often found in rocky or sandy soils, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
