Silene pulchra

Silene pulchra, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 5,000 feet. Its leaves are linear and opposite, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Silene pulchra is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
