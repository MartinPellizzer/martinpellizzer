Saxifraga × petraschii

Saxifraga × petraschii, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × petraschii is often found in high-altitude regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
