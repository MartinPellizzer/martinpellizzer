Scabiosa communis subsp. sylvatica

Scabiosa communis subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
