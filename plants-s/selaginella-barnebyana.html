Selaginella barnebyana

Selaginella barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's spikemoss, is a rare and endangered vascular plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including drought and fire, due to its specialized adaptations.

Its conservation status highlights the importance of preserving its natural habitat, which is under threat from habitat loss and climate change.
