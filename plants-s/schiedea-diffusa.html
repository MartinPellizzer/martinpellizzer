Schiedea diffusa

Schiedea diffusa, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, tall stem that bears a dense cluster of yellow flowers. It thrives in the high-elevation volcanic soils of Haleakalā and other Hawaiian summits.

Due to its restricted habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is classified as endangered.
