Silene depressa var. meyeri

Silene depressa var. meyeri, commonly known as the Meadow Cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is adapted to moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
