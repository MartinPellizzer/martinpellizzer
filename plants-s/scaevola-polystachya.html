Scaevola polystachya

Scaevola polystachya, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola polystachya is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
