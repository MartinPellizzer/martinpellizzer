Stachys albereana

Stachys albereana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys albereana is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
