Silene pedicellata

Silene pedicellata, commonly known as the pink-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene pedicellata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
