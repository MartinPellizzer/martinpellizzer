Sedum acre var. morbifugum

Sedum acre var. morbifugum, commonly known as the medicinal stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has earned its common name.
