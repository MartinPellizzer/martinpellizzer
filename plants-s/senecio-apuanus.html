Senecio apuanus

Senecio apuanus, commonly known as the Apuan Alps groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Italy.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright stems and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio apuanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
