Stachys schimperi

Stachys schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian betony, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Stachys schimperi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
