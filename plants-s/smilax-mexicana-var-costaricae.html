Smilax mexicana var. costaricae

Smilax mexicana var. costaricae, commonly known as the Costa Rican smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Central America.

It features woody stems with sharp spines and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its berries are typically orange-red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
