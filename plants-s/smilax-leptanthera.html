Smilax leptanthera

Smilax leptanthera, commonly known as the slender-stalked catbrier, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp, curved spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a heart-shaped base.

Smilax leptanthera is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
