Scrophularia balbisii subsp. cinerea

Scrophularia balbisii subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the pale scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features erect, branched stems covered in a dense layer of pale grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pale pink to almost white, with five lobed petals and a distinctive spur.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique floral structure.
