Saxifraga rivularis var. purpurascens

Saxifraga rivularis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions.

It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold climates.
