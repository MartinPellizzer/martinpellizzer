Seguieria macrophylla

Seguieria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its fronds are divided into small, delicate pinnae that help in photosynthesis and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Seguieria macrophylla plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
