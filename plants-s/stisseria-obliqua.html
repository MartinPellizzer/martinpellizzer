Stisseria obliqua

Stisseria obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland and marshy habitats, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant's unique zigzagging stem is a distinguishing feature, contributing to its common name.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
