Satureja microphylla subsp. filiformis

Satureja microphylla subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as narrow-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine due to its pungent flavor and potential health benefits.
