Spiraea reevesiana var. florepleno

Spiraea reevesiana var. florepleno, commonly known as the doubleflower weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading branches that create a weeping effect, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of small, white, double-flowered blooms in early spring, adding a delicate and showy display. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the flowers.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders and landscape design.
