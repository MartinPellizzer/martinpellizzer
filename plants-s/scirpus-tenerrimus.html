Scirpus tenerrimus

Scirpus tenerrimus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, three-parted flowers.

Scirpus tenerrimus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
