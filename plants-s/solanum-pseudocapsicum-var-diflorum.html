Solanum pseudocapsicum var. diflorum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. diflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescence, which sets it apart from other species in the genus. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a small, globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
