Sphaeralcea ambigua var. versicolor

Sphaeralcea ambigua var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its variability in flower color, which contributes to its common name.
