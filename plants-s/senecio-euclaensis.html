Senecio euclaensis

Senecio euclaensis, commonly known as the Eucla Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
