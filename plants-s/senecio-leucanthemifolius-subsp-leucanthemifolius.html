Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. leucanthemifolius

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

leucanthemifolius, commonly known as the white-flowered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period usually occurs during the winter months, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
