Solanum berthaultii f. zudanense

Solanum berthaultii f. zudanense, commonly known as the Zudanese nightshade, is a rare and flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its limited distribution and unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
