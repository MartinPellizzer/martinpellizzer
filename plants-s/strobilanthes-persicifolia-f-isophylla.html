Strobilanthes persicifolia f. isophylla

Strobilanthes persicifolia f. isophylla, commonly known as the rose-leaf strobilanthes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, glabrous leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to white. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
