Scabiosa arvensis f. collina

Scabiosa arvensis f. collina, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
