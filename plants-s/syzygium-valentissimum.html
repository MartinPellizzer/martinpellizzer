Syzygium valentissimum

Syzygium valentissimum, commonly known as the Christmas cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The tree produces small, red, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
