Spadonia polymorpha var. obtusifolia

Spadonia polymorpha var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spadonia, is a variant of the spadonia plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and slightly waxy in texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
