Stemodia heterophylla

Stemodia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves that vary in shape depending on the environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
