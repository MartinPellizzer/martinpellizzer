Sphondylium lanatum

Sphondylium lanatum, commonly known as the hairy leafcrown, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
