Solanum stenotomum f. koso-nahui

Solanum stenotomum f. koso-nahui, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, green, egg-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
