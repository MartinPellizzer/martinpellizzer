Solanum aphananthum

Solanum aphananthum, commonly known as the small-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, pale green flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for human consumption.
