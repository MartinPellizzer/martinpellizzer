Sedum thirkeanum

Sedum thirkeanum, commonly known as the Thirkean stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It blooms in late summer, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.

Sedum thirkeanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
