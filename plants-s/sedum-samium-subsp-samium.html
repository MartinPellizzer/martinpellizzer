Sedum samium subsp. samium

Sedum samium subsp.

samium, commonly known as stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are usually green or bluish-gray, and it produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the summer. Sedum samium subsp.

samium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
