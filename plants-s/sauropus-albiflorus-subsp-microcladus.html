Sauropus albiflorus subsp. microcladus

Sauropus albiflorus subsp.

microcladus, commonly known as the whiteflower sauropus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique foliage and floral display.
