Sanguisorba hakusanensis var. coreana

Sanguisorba hakusanensis var. coreana, commonly known as Korean narrowleaf teaflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
