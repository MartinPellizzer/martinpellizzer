Styrax kotoensis

Styrax kotoensis, commonly known as Koto styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense-making.
