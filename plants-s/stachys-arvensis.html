Stachys arvensis

Stachys arvensis, commonly known as field betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys arvensis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
