Saracha umbellata

Saracha umbellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Saracha umbellata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
