Salacia maingayi

Salacia maingayi, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
