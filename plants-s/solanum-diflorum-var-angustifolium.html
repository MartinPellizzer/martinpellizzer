Solanum diflorum var. angustifolium

Solanum diflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features simple, alternate, narrow leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
