Seridia straminea

Seridia straminea, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green spikelets that are clustered along the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Seridia straminea is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop for livestock.
