Salvia akiensis

Salvia akiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and tubular flowers.
