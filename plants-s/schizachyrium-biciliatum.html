Schizachyrium biciliatum

Schizachyrium biciliatum, commonly known as eastern grass or little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are usually brown or reddish in color.

Schizachyrium biciliatum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
