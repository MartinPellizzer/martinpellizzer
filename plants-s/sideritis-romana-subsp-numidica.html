Sideritis romana subsp. numidica

Sideritis romana subsp.

numidica, commonly known as Numidian rockrose, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
