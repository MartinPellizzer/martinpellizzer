Spiraea kamaonensis

Spiraea kamaonensis, commonly known as the Kamaon spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Spiraea kamaonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its compact growth and seasonal flowering.
