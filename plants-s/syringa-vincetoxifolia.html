Syringa vincetoxifolia

Syringa vincetoxifolia, commonly known as the weeping bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that produce fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Syringa vincetoxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
