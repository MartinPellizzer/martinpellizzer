Syzygium guineense subsp. littorale

Syzygium guineense subsp.

littorale, commonly known as the mangosteen or mangostan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant white flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits with a segmented, juicy pulp. The fruit is renowned for its high vitamin C content and distinctive sweet-tart flavor.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
