Saxifraga perporosa

Saxifraga perporosa, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive purple hue. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Saxifraga perporosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
