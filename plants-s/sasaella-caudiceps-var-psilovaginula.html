Sasaella caudiceps var. psilovaginula

Sasaella caudiceps var. psilovaginula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sasa, is a variant of the sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often forming dense mats or clumps. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
