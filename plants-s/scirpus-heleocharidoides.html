Scirpus heleocharidoides

Scirpus heleocharidoides, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the plant's ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus heleocharidoides plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
