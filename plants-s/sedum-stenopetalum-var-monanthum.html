Sedum stenopetalum var. monanthum

Sedum stenopetalum var. monanthum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The leaves are typically blue-green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is notable for its compact growth and drought tolerance, making it ideal for low-maintenance landscaping.
