Salvia hevanda

Salvia hevanda, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Salvia hevanda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
