Scirpus fohaiensis

Scirpus fohaiensis, commonly known as the Fohai bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus fohaiensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
