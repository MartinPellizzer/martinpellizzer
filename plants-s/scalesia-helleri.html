Scalesia helleri

Scalesia helleri, commonly known as the Heller's tree heather, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scalesia helleri thrives in the humid, volcanic soils of the Galápagos, often found in coastal and lowland habitats.
