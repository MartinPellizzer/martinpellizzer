Saxifraga hederacea var. libanotica

Saxifraga hederacea var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon ivy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
