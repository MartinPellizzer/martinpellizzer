Saxifraga ranunculoides

Saxifraga ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright green, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a raceme, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
