Sedum lungtsuanense

Sedum lungtsuanense, commonly known as the Lungtsuan sedum, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, usually in shades of yellow or orange.

Due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
