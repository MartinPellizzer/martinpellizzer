Sorghum caudatum

Sorghum caudatum, commonly known as the weeping sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping, arching stems that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale to reddish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Sorghum caudatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
