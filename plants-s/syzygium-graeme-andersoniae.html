Syzygium graeme-andersoniae

Syzygium graeme-andersoniae, commonly known as the Anderson's Syzygium, is a rare tropical rainforest tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
