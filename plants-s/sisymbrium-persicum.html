Sisymbrium persicum

Sisymbrium persicum, commonly known as Persian mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are edible and have a pungent flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Sisymbrium persicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
