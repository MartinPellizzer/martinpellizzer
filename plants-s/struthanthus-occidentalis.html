Struthanthus occidentalis

Struthanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or reddish in color and bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
