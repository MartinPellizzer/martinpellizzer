Strophanthus perakensis

Strophanthus perakensis, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

However, it contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
