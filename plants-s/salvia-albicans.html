Salvia albicans

Salvia albicans, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of fragrant, pale blue to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its essential oils, particularly those containing sclareol, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Salvia albicans is often used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant in various cultural practices.
