Scirpus acaulis

Scirpus acaulis, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense, upright clusters of leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpus acaulis thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
