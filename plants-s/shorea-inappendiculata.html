Shorea inappendiculata

Shorea inappendiculata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
