Suaeda moquinii

Suaeda moquinii, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in dry soils.

Suaeda moquinii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal and desert soils.
