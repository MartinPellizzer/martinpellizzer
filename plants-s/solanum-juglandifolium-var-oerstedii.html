Solanum juglandifolium var. oerstedii

Solanum juglandifolium var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's hollyleaf nightshade, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree with thick, waxy leaves that resemble holly leaves, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes both economically important crops and toxic plants.
