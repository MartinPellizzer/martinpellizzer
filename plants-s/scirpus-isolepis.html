Scirpus isolepis

Scirpus isolepis, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flowering spikes that emerge from water or moist soil. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds, marshes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant.

Scirpus isolepis plays a vital role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
