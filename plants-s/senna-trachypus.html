Senna trachypus

Senna trachypus, commonly known as the slender senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Senna trachypus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
