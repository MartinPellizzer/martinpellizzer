Senecio pilotus

Senecio pilotus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in mountainous regions.

Senecio pilotus is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
