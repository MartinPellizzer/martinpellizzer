Sphaerocionium gracile

Sphaerocionium gracile, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh, dry environment. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, ensuring pollination by insects.

Sphaerocionium gracile plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
