Sesleria caerulea subsp. angustifolia

Sesleria caerulea subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flower spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
