Sorghum × almum var. parvispiculum

Sorghum × almum var. parvispiculum, commonly known as dwarf finger millet, is a hybrid grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and small, finger-like grains.

It is native to the semi-arid regions of Africa and thrives in warm, dry climates with low rainfall. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with dense, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both human consumption and animal feed.

The variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soils, contributing to food security in arid regions.
