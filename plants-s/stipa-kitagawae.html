Stipa kitagawae

Stipa kitagawae, commonly known as the Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Stipa kitagawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
