Sedum × fuereri

Sedum × fuereri, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, aiding in water retention.

Sedum × fuereri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
