Scabiosa longiseta

Scabiosa longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Scabiosa longiseta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
