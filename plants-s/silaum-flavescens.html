Silaum flavescens

Silaum flavescens, commonly known as yellow salsify, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces a long, swollen root that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head.

Silaum flavescens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
