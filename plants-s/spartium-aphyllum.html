Spartium aphyllum

Spartium aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless spartium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its unique morphology. The plant is notable for its lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present at the base of the stems. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain seeds.

Spartium aphyllum is often found in rocky or dry habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
