Silaus saxatilis

Silaus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on steep slopes or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Silaus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
