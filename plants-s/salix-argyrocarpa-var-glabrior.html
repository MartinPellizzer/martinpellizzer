Salix argyrocarpa var. glabrior

Salix argyrocarpa var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth silver willow, is a variant of the silver willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, pale yellow catkins. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

This willow is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
