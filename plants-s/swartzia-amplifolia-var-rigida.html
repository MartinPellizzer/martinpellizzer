Swartzia amplifolia var. rigida

Swartzia amplifolia var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid swamp bean, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is typically found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
