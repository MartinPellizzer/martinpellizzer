Sarcostemma andongense

Sarcostemma andongense, commonly known as the Andong milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
