Saussurea calcicola

Saussurea calcicola, commonly known as the rock saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flower heads. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme climatic conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
