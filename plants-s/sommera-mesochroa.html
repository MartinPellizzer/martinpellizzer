Sommera mesochroa

Sommera mesochroa, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sommera mesochroa is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it thrives in full sun.
