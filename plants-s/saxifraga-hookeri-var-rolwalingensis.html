Saxifraga hookeri var. rolwalingensis

Saxifraga hookeri var. rolwalingensis, commonly known as the Rolwaling saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial species characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh mountain conditions and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
