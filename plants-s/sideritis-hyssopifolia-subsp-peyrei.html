Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. peyrei

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

peyrei, commonly known as the Balkan sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowering stems have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
