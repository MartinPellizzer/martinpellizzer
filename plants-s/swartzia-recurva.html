Swartzia recurva

Swartzia recurva, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Swartzia recurva is often found in dry forests and woodlands, where it plays an important ecological role.
