Sasa magnifica var. igaensis

Sasa magnifica var. igaensis, commonly known as the Igaensis bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often tinged with red or purple at the nodes. The leaves are narrow, lanceolate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and texture.
