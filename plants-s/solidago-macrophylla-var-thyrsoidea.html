Solidago macrophylla var. thyrsoidea

Solidago macrophylla var. thyrsoidea, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with dense, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Its showy flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological landscapes.
