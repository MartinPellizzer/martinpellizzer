Silene olympica var. stenophylla

Silene olympica var. stenophylla, commonly known as the Olympic narrow-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
