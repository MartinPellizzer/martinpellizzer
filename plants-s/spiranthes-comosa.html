Spiranthes comosa

Spiranthes comosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Spiranthes comosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, intricate floral structure.
