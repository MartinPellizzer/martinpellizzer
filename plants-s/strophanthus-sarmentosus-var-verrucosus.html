Strophanthus sarmentosus var. verrucosus

Strophanthus sarmentosus var. verrucosus, commonly known as the African milkweed or velvet milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is known for containing cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine and have potential pharmaceutical applications.
