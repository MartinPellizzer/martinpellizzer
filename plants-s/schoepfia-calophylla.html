Schoepfia calophylla

Schoepfia calophylla, commonly known as the California sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species in its native range.
