Sagittaria aquatica var. minor

Sagittaria aquatica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser water arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike-like cluster.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
