Sempervivum ligulare

Sempervivum ligulare, commonly known as the pointed houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum ligulare is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
