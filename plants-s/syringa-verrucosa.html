Syringa verrucosa

Syringa verrucosa, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Syringa verrucosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
