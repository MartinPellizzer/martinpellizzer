Senecio sagittatus var. lancifolia

Senecio sagittatus var. lancifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a conical inflorescence, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
