Sagina belonophylla

Sagina belonophylla, commonly known as the mossy pearlwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Sagina belonophylla is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil stabilization.
