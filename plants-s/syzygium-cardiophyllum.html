Syzygium cardiophyllum

Syzygium cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
