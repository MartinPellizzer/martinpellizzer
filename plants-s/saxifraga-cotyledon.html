Saxifraga cotyledon

Saxifraga cotyledon, commonly known as the baby's breath saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green with a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
