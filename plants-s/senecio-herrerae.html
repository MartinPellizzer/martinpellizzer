Senecio herrerae

Senecio herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its delicate, yellow-centered flowers. Senecio herrerae has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid conditions.
