Senecio actinella

Senecio actinella, commonly known as the silverleaf sedum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to dense cobweb-like hairs covering its leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio actinella is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
