Silene bryoides

Silene bryoides, commonly known as the mossy catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, including parts of the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Silene bryoides is often found in association with other low-growing flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
