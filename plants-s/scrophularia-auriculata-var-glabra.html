Scrophularia auriculata var. glabra

Scrophularia auriculata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth auriculate scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange-red flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
