Salicornia leiosperma

Salicornia leiosperma, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it has a unique ability to store water in its tissues. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Salicornia leiosperma plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
