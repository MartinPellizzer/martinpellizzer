Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. maritimum

Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. maritimum, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces feathery, golden-brown inflorescences in late summer. The plant is highly adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in dune and tidal marsh environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
