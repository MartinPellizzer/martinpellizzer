Scutellaria condensata subsp. pycnotricha

Scutellaria condensata subsp.

pycnotricha, commonly known as the thick-hair scullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple, forming spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
