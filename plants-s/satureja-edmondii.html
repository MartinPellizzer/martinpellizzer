Satureja edmondii

Satureja edmondii, commonly known as Edmond's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and used in culinary applications, similar to other members of the sage family.

Satureja edmondii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
