Stellera tarton-raira

Stellera tarton-raira, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stellera tarton-raira is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
