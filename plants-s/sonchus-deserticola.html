Sonchus deserticola

Sonchus deserticola, commonly known as the desert sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Sonchus deserticola is often found in disturbed areas and can be a persistent weed in agricultural settings.
