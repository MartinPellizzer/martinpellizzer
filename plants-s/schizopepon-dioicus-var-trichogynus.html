Schizopepon dioicus var. trichogynus

Schizopepon dioicus var. trichogynus, commonly known as the hairy female gourd, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct male and female flowers, a feature typical of dioecious plants. The female flowers are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that mature to a yellowish-orange color.

It is valued in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native regions.
