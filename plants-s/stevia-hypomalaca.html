Stevia hypomalaca

Stevia hypomalaca, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet leaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are highly aromatic and sweet-tasting. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Stevia hypomalaca is valued for its natural sweetening properties, making it a popular alternative to sugar in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are responsible for its intense sweetness.
