Scorzonera kirghisorum

Scorzonera kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Scorzonera kirghisorum is valued for its edible roots, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
