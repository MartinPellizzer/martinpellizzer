Senecio scapiformis

Senecio scapiformis, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with low maintenance requirements.
