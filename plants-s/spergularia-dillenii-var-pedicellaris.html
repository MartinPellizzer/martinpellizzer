Spergularia dillenii var. pedicellaris

Spergularia dillenii var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the pink sand spurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy coastal environments.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically linear and pointed. It thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is often found in dunes or coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
