Salvia chorianthos

Salvia chorianthos, commonly known as the yellow salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia chorianthos thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
