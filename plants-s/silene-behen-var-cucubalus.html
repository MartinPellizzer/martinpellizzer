Silene behen var. cucubalus

Silene behen var. cucubalus, commonly known as the African evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
