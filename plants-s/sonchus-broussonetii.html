Sonchus broussonetii

Sonchus broussonetii, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
