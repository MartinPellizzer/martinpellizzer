Scirpus acicularis f. submersus

Scirpus acicularis f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged rush, is a variant of the narrowleaf bulrush that thrives in aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically submerged in water, adapted for underwater growth. It forms dense stands in shallow water, contributing to wetland vegetation structure. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often partially or fully submerged, aiding in wind pollination.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
