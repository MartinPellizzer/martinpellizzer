Swainsona villosa

Swainsona villosa, commonly known as the silky swainsona, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Swainsona villosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant blooms.
