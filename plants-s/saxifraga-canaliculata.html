Saxifraga canaliculata

Saxifraga canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive channel or groove along the central vein. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh growing conditions.
