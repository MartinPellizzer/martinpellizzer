Scilla chinensis var. mounsei

Scilla chinensis var. mounsei, commonly known as Chinese squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, often preceding the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
