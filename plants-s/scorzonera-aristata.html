Scorzonera aristata

Scorzonera aristata, commonly known as the mountain scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera aristata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
