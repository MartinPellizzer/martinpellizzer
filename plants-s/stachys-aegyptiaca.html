Stachys aegyptiaca

Stachys aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
