Salix × pentandroides

Salix × pentandroides, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage.

It is native to the northern hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features long, slender branches that droop gracefully, creating a striking visual effect. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Salix × pentandroides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways.
