Silene campanulata var. angustifolia

Silene campanulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, bell-shaped flowers.

It typically blooms with pink or purple petals, which are often tinged with white at the base. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall delicate appearance. This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often succulent stems.
