Saxifraga × crawfordii

Saxifraga × crawfordii, commonly known as the Crawford's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, mat-forming foliage that features rounded, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
