Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. glomerata

Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. glomerata, commonly known as snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall, which are edible for wildlife but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and riparian areas.

The species is known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
