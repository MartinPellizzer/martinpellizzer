Solaenacanthus longiflorus

Solaenacanthus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered sea hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in mangrove ecosystems and tidal zones. Its flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Solaenacanthus longiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
