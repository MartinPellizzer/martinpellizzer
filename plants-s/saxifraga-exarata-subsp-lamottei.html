Saxifraga exarata subsp. lamottei

Saxifraga exarata subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-forming, with lanceolate or ovate shapes and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found at high altitudes.
