Sinapis cheiranthos subsp. rectangularis

Sinapis cheiranthos subsp.

rectangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces slender, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in the Mediterranean region and is adapted to arid conditions.
