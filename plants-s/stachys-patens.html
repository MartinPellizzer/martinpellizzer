Stachys patens

Stachys patens, commonly known as spreading betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that often spread horizontally, giving it a carpet-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Stachys patens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
