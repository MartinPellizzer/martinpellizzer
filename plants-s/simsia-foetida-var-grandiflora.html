Simsia foetida var. grandiflora

Simsia foetida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora variant of the fetid simsa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
