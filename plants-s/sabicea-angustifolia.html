Sabicea angustifolia

Sabicea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various insects and birds.
