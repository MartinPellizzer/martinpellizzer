Spiraea betulifolia f. angustifolia

Spiraea betulifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf silverweed, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver-white underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
