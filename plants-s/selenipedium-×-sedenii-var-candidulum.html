Selenipedium × sedenii var. candidulum

Selenipedium × sedenii var. candidulum, commonly known as the white-flowered giant orchid, is a hybrid species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often reaching several feet in length.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
