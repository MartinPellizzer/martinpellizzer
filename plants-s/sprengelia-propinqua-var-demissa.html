Sprengelia propinqua var. demissa

Sprengelia propinqua var. demissa, commonly known as the hanging spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flower spikes that bear small, spider-like blossoms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
