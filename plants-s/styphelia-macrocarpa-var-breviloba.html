Styphelia macrocarpa var. breviloba

Styphelia macrocarpa var. breviloba, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
