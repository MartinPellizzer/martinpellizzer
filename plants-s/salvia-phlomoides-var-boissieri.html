Salvia phlomoides var. boissieri

Salvia phlomoides var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and aromatic, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
