Solidago sciaphila

Solidago sciaphila, commonly known as the shade goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Solidago sciaphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
