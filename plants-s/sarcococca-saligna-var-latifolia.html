Sarcococca saligna var. latifolia

Sarcococca saligna var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sweetbox, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its dense growth habit and winter interest make it a valuable addition to ornamental landscapes.
