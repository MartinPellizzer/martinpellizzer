Smilax japonica

Smilax japonica, commonly known as Japanese climbing smilax, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

Smilax japonica is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and gardens.
