Scutellaria ovata subsp. calcarea

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, reflecting its subspecies name.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet, borne in whorled clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
