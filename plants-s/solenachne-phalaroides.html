Solenachne phalaroides

Solenachne phalaroides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Solenachne phalaroides is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
