Syringa palibiniana var. kamibayashii

Syringa palibiniana var. kamibayashii, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
