Salix divaricata subsp. pulchra

Salix divaricata subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the pink willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of showy pink to reddish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its early blooming habit and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
