Solanum simplex

Solanum simplex, commonly known as the simple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. Solanum simplex is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
