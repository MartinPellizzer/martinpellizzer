Senghasia chocoensis

Senghasia chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocó wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Chocó region of Colombia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and its ability to produce a natural wax-like substance from its leaves. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Senghasia chocoensis is valued for its unique botanical properties and is often found in humid, lowland rainforests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
