Silene sedoides subsp. sedoides

Silene sedoides subsp.

sedoides, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

The plant features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
