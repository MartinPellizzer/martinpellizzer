Senecio viscidus

Senecio viscidus, commonly known as sticky groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features hairy, often sticky leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense mats in suitable conditions.

Senecio viscidus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
