Sedum album subsp. serpentini

Sedum album subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
