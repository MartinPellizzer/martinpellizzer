Sabicea cruciata

Sabicea cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaf sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a cross-shaped venation pattern that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying a combination of white and yellow hues.

Sabicea cruciata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
