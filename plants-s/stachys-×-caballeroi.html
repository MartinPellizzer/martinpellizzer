Stachys × caballeroi

Stachys × caballeroi, commonly known as the hybrid betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
