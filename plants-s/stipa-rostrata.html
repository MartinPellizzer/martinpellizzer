Stipa rostrata

Stipa rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Stipa rostrata is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
