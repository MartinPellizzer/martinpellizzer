Sparaxis blanda

Sparaxis blanda, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces numerous small, white, spherical berries after flowering. Sparaxis blanda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
