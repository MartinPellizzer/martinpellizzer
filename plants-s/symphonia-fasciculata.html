Symphonia fasciculata

Symphonia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered symphonia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
