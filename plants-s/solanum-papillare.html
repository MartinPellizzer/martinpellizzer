Solanum papillare

Solanum papillare, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum papillare is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
