Smallanthus obscurus

Smallanthus obscurus, commonly known as the Andean false potato, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
