Sida elegans

Sida elegans, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Sida elegans is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
