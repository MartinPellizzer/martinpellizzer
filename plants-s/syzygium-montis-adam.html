Syzygium montis-adam

Syzygium montis-adam, commonly known as the Adam's mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, red or yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium montis-adam is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
