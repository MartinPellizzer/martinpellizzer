Salvia savannarum

Salvia savannarum, commonly known as the Florida sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia savannarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
