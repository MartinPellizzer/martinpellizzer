Senecio minutus

Senecio minutus, commonly known as the dwarf groundsels, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio minutus is often used in rock gardens due to its compact size and drought tolerance.
