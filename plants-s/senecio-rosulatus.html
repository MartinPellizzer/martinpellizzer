Senecio rosulatus

Senecio rosulatus, commonly known as the Rosulate Senecio, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a showy, daisy-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
