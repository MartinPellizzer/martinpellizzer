Stanleya pinnatifida var. integrifolia

Stanleya pinnatifida var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Western globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. Its leaves are usually entire, giving it the species name *integrifolia*.
