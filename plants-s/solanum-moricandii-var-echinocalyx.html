Solanum moricandii var. echinocalyx

Solanum moricandii var. echinocalyx, commonly known as the spiny-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
