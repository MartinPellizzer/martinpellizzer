Scirpus purpurascens

Scirpus purpurascens, commonly known as purple bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often display a reddish-purple hue, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for aquatic life.
