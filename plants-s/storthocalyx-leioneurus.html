Storthocalyx leioneurus

Storthocalyx leioneurus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
