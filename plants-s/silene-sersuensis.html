Silene sersuensis

Silene sersuensis, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well-adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Silene sersuensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
