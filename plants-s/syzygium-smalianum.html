Syzygium smalianum

Syzygium smalianum, commonly known as the Java apple or red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium smalianum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
