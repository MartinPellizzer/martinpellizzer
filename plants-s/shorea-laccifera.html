Shorea laccifera

Shorea laccifera, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is known for its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. Its fruit is a small, round, and often edible drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Shorea laccifera plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
