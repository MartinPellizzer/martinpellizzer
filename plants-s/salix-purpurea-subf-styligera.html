Salix purpurea subf. styligera

Salix purpurea subf.

styligera, commonly known as purple willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and distinctive purple-tinged catkins.

The leaves are typically oval, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, streams, or wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
