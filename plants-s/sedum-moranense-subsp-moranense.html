Sedum moranense subsp. moranense

Sedum moranense subsp.

moranense, commonly known as Moran's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, with thick leaves that store water. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging growing conditions.
