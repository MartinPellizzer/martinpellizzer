Solanum dubium var. brevipetiolatum

Solanum dubium var. brevipetiolatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed nightshade, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, oval-shaped fruits.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and potential toxicity, making it a plant of both ecological and cautionary interest.
