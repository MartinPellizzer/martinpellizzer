Spiraea pubescens f. leiocarpa

Spiraea pubescens f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters along the stems in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving the plant its "smooth" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
