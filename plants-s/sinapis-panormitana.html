Sinapis panormitana

Sinapis panormitana, commonly known as the Mediterranean mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated, pod-like fruits.

Sinapis panormitana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
