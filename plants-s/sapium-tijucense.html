Sapium tijucense

Sapium tijucense, commonly known as Brazilian cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Sapium tijucense also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
