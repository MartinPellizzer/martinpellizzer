Silene linophila

Silene linophila, commonly known as the alpine silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene linophila is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
