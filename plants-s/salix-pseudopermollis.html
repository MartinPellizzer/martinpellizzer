Salix pseudopermollis

Salix pseudopermollis, commonly known as the Mongolian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Salix pseudopermollis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
