Sphaeralcea vidalii

Sphaeralcea vidalii, commonly known as Vidal's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

Sphaeralcea vidalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in xeriscaped gardens.
