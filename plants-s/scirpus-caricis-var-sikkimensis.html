Scirpus caricis var. sikkimensis

Scirpus caricis var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often submerged or floating in water.

This variant is adapted to moist to aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
