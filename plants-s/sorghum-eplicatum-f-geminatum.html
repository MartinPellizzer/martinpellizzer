Sorghum eplicatum f. geminatum

Sorghum eplicatum f. geminatum, commonly known as the twin-stemmed sorghum, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique growth pattern.

This plant exhibits gemination, where the stem splits into two distinct shoots at the base, creating a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
