Solidago virgaurea subsp. macrorhiza

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

macrorhiza, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with abundant yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular ornamental in gardens.
