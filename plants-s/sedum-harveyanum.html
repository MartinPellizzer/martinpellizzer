Sedum harveyanum

Sedum harveyanum, commonly known as the Harvey's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners in dry climates.
