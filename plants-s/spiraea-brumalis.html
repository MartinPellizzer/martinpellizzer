Spiraea brumalis

Spiraea brumalis, commonly known as the winter spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Spiraea brumalis is often used in gardens for its early blooming habit and ornamental value.
