Schoenorchis pachyglossa

Schoenorchis pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, broad lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
