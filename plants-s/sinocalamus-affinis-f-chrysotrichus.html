Sinocalamus affinis f. chrysotrichus

Sinocalamus affinis f. chrysotrichus, commonly known as the golden-haired bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-colored bracts that cover the inflorescence, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in subtropical regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its growth habit includes tall, slender culms that can reach heights of several meters.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
