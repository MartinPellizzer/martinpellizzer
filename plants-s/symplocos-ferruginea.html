Symplocos ferruginea

Symplocos ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
