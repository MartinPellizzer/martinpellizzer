Satyrium calceatum

Satyrium calceatum, commonly known as the lacey orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate floral structure, featuring a tall, slender stem with a single, showy flower. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in high-altitude grasslands and cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
