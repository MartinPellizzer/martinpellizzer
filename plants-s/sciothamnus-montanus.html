Sciothamnus montanus

Sciothamnus montanus, commonly known as the mountain blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a fibrous root system and upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Sciothamnus montanus is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
