Salacia gabunensis

Salacia gabunensis, commonly known as the Gabon salicine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to Central and West Africa, particularly found in the tropical rainforests of Gabon and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
