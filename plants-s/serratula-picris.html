Serratula picris

Serratula picris, commonly known as the common sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are serrated and gray-green, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Serratula picris is often considered a weed but can provide nectar for pollinators in its native range.
