Steiractinia glandulosa subsp. nevadensis

Steiractinia glandulosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada leek, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the mountainous regions of Nevada. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are essential to protect this unique botanical species.
