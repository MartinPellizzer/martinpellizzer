Scrophularia mellifera

Scrophularia mellifera, commonly known as honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often glandular and aromatic.

Scrophularia mellifera is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
