Solidago nemoralis var. incana

Solidago nemoralis var. incana, commonly known as the gray goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and moist soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, radiate flowers with both ray and disk florets.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
