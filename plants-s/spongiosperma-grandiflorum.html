Spongiosperma grandiflorum

Spongiosperma grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant sponge tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, woody stems and distinctive, spongy-textured bark. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically large, broad, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Spongiosperma grandiflorum is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
