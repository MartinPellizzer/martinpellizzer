Sinocalamus triramus

Sinocalamus triramus, commonly known as the three-branched bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in temperate and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sinocalamus triramus is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
