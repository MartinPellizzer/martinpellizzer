Salvia maximowicziana var. maximowicziana

Salvia maximowicziana var. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
