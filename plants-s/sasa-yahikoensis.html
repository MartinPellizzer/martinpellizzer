Sasa yahikoensis

Sasa yahikoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating a thick undergrowth in its natural habitat.

Sasa yahikoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
