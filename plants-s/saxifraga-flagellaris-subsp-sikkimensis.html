Saxifraga flagellaris subsp. sikkimensis

Saxifraga flagellaris subsp.

sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at high elevations, often on slopes and ridges.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive, serrated edge. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
