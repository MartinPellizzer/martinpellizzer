Steironema revolutum

Steironema revolutum, commonly known as the spiral leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling leaves that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are long and narrow, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Steironema revolutum is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indirect light conditions.
