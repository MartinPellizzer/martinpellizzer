Stipa plumosula

Stipa plumosula, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a soft, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa plumosula is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
