Salvia sordida

Salvia sordida, commonly known as the dirty sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia sordida is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
