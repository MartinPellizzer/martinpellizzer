Salpinga secunda var. parviflora

Salpinga secunda var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered salpinga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
