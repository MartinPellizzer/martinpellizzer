Senecio subcoriaceus

Senecio subcoriaceus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio subcoriaceus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
