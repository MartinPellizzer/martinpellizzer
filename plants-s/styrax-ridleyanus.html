Styrax ridleyanus

Styrax ridleyanus, commonly known as the Malayan styrax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces fragrant white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The bark and flowers of Styrax ridleyanus have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
