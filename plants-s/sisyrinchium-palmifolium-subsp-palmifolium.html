Sisyrinchium palmifolium subsp. palmifolium

Sisyrinchium palmifolium subsp.

palmifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often broad at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
