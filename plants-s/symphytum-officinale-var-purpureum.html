Symphytum officinale var. purpureum

Symphytum officinale var. purpureum, commonly known as purple comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside, and it produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is known for its thick, fleshy roots and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Purple comfrey is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for skin healing and as a remedy for various ailments.
