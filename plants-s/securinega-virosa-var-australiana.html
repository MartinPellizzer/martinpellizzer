Securinega virosa var. australiana

Securinega virosa var. australiana, commonly known as the Australian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

The species is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic and cause skin irritation.
