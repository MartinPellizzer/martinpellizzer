Salix viminalis var. pallida

Salix viminalis var. pallida, commonly known as white willow.

This variety of willow is characterized by its pale, almost white bark, which distinguishes it from other Salix species. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces catkins and small, inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Salicaceae family.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth margin, often turning yellow in the fall.
