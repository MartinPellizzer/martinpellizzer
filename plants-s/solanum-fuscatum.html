Solanum fuscatum

Solanum fuscatum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a small, shiny berry, turns from green to black as it matures.

The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes.
