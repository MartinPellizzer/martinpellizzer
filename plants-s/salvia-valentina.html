Salvia valentina

Salvia valentina, commonly known as the Valentine sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often with a minty scent.

Salvia valentina is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
