Solanum hidetaroi

Solanum hidetaroi, commonly known as the Hidetaro's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
