Silene pratensis

Silene pratensis, commonly known as the meadow campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
