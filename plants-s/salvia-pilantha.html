Salvia pilantha

Salvia pilantha, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other well-known herbs like mint and lavender.
