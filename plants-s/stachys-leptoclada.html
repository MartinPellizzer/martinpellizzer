Stachys leptoclada

Stachys leptoclada, commonly known as the slender betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, growing in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Stachys leptoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
