Sapium sebiferum var. dabeshanense

Sapium sebiferum var. dabeshanense, commonly known as the Dabeshan Chinese tallowtree, is a variant of the Chinese tallowtree native to southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by orange to red drupes that contain a single seed. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, oily aril, which is a key feature of the genus Sapium.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest margins.
