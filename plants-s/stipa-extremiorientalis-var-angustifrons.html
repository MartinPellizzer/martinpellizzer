Stipa extremiorientalis var. angustifrons

Stipa extremiorientalis var. angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-fruited feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that turns a striking golden hue in autumn. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical spikelets that are adorned with small, bristle-like florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
