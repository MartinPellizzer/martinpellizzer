Senacia

Senacia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its rosette growth habit and silver-gray foliage.

It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Senacia species are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its succulent leaves store water, making it adaptable to arid environments.
