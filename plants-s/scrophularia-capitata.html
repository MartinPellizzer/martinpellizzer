Scrophularia capitata

Scrophularia capitata, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

Scrophularia capitata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
