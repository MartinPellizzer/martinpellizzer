Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. polycephalum

Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. polycephalum, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flower heads. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
