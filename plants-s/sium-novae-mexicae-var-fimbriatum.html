Sium novae-mexicae var. fimbriatum

Sium novae-mexicae var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the New Mexico waterparsley, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to moist environments, often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

This variant is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and is considered a rare and specialized member of the Apiaceae family.
