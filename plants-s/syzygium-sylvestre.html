Syzygium sylvestre

Syzygium sylvestre, commonly known as wild clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Syzygium sylvestre thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
