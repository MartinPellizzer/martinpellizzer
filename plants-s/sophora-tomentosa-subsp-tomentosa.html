Sophora tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces showy, pale yellow to greenish flowers in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical, dark brown seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.
