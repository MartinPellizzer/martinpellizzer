Sophora genistoides

Sophora genistoides, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its fragrant, pendulous clusters of purple or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Sophora genistoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
