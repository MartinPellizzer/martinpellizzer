Stipularia fallax

Stipularia fallax, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Stipularia fallax is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
