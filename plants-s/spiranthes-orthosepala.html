Spiranthes orthosepala

Spiranthes orthosepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and moths.
