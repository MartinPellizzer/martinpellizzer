Sesleria uliginosa var. rohlenae

Sesleria uliginosa var. rohlenae, commonly known as the bog sesleria, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Europe.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in bog gardens or water features.
