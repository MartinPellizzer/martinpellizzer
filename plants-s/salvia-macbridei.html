Salvia macbridei

Salvia macbridei, commonly known as MacBride's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and striking violet to blue-purplish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
