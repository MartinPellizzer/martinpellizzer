Staurogyne ophiorrhizoides

Staurogyne ophiorrhizoides, commonly known as the creeping water primrose, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Staurogyne ophiorrhizoides is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
