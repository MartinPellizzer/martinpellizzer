Sloanea picapica var. hirsuta

Sloanea picapica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sloanea, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and dark green color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
