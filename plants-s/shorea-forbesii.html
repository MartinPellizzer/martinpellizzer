Shorea forbesii

Shorea forbesii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and fragrance industries. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, often reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
