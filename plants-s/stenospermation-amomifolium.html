Stenospermation amomifolium

Stenospermation amomifolium, commonly known as the narrow-sperma amomum leaf plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that resemble those of the ginger plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
