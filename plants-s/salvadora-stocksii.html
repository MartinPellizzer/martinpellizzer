Salvadora stocksii

Salvadora stocksii, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its bark and leaves are valued for their astringent properties and are used in various herbal remedies.
