Serratula odoratissima

Serratula odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Serratula odoratissima is also known for its aromatic foliage, which gives it its common name.
