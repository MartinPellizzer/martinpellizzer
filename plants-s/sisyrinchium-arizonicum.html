Sisyrinchium arizonicum

Sisyrinchium arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy plains. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in desert and semi-desert regions.

Sisyrinchium arizonicum is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
