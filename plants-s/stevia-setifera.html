Stevia setifera

Stevia setifera, commonly known as the slender leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia setifera is often cultivated for its sweetening properties and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
