Selaginella illecebrosa

Selaginella illecebrosa, commonly known as the "spider moss," is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in various habitats.

Its reproductive structures, including microspores and megaspores, are contained within specialized leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
