Sidalcea asprella subsp. nana

Sidalcea asprella subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf checkerbloom, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features small, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
