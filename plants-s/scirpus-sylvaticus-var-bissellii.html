Scirpus sylvaticus var. bissellii

Scirpus sylvaticus var. bissellii, commonly known as the Bissell's bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush found in wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
