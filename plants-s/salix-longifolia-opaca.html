Salix longifolia opaca

Salix longifolia opaca, commonly known as the eastern silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a soft, silky texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, and other water sources.

This species plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds, and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
