Syzygium thumra

Syzygium thumra, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped fruit, which is typically five-lobed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are small and white, often clustered in groups.

The fruit is commonly consumed fresh or used in culinary applications due to its tart flavor and high vitamin C content.
