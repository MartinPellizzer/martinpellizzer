Solanum kieslingii

Solanum kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum kieslingii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
