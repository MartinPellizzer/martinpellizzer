Sclerodactylon

Sclerodactylon, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood and thick, waxy leaves. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

The species is valued for its durable timber but is also threatened by overharvesting and habitat loss.
