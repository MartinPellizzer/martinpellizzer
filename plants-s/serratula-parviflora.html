Serratula parviflora

Serratula parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered serrata, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Serratula parviflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
