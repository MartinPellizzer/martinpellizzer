Sloanea sterculiacea var. sterculiacea

Sloanea sterculiacea var. sterculiacea, commonly known as the Andean sloanea, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in terminal panicles, often blooming in spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
