Stevia semperflorens

Stevia semperflorens, commonly known as sweetleaf or honey plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Stevia semperflorens is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its medicinal properties.
