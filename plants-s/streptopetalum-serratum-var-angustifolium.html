Streptopetalum serratum var. angustifolium

Streptopetalum serratum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
