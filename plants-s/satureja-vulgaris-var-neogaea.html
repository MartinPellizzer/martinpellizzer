Satureja vulgaris var. neogaea

Satureja vulgaris var. neogaea, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pungent scent reminiscent of thyme and oregano. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
