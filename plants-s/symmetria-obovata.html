Symmetria obovata

Symmetria obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate symmetry plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive obovate leaves that are symmetrical in shape.

The plant typically features smooth, waxy foliage that reflects light, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually small and clustered, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
