Spathoglottis regneri

Spathoglottis regneri, commonly known as the Regner's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically produces a single, showy spike of flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Spathoglottis regneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
