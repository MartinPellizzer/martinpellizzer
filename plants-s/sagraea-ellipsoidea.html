Sagraea ellipsoidea

Sagraea ellipsoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sagraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sagraea ellipsoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and delicate blooms.
