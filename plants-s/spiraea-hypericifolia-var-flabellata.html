Spiraea hypericifolia var. flabellata

Spiraea hypericifolia var. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, providing a delicate texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its refined appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
