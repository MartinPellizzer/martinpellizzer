Sokinochloa chapelieri

Sokinochloa chapelieri, commonly known as the chapelieri grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
