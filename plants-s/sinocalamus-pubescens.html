Sinocalamus pubescens

Sinocalamus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and river valleys. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sinocalamus pubescens is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a source of sustainable materials.
