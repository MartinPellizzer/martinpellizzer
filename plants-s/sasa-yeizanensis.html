Sasa yeizanensis

Sasa yeizanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that form dense clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa yeizanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
