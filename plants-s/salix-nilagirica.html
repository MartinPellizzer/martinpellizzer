Salix nilagirica

Salix nilagirica, commonly known as Nilagiri willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces catkins as its primary floral structure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Salix nilagirica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
