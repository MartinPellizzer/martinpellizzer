Sinodolichos lagopus

Sinodolichos lagopus, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in late summer. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
