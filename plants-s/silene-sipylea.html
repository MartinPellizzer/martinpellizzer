Silene sipylea

Silene sipylea, commonly known as the Cyprus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Silene sipylea is notable for its delicate appearance and is considered a symbol of Cyprus, often associated with local folklore and traditions.
