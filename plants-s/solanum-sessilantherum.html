Solanum sessilantherum

Solanum sessilantherum, commonly known as the sessile-hair nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is occasionally cultivated for its ornamental value.
