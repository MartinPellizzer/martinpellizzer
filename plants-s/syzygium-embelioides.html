Syzygium embelioides

Syzygium embelioides, commonly known as the flame apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Syzygium embelioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and fruiting patterns make it a notable species in tropical horticulture.
