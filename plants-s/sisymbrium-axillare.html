Sisymbrium axillare

Sisymbrium axillare, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is an annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant lacks true leaves, instead having reduced, scale-like structures, and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on host plants for water and nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on parasitic plant ecology and adaptation.
