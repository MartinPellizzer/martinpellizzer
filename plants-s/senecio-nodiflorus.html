Senecio nodiflorus

Senecio nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

Senecio nodiflorus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
