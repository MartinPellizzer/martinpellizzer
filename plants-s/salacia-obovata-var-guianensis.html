Salacia obovata var. guianensis

Salacia obovata var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese salacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the rainforests of Guyana and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
