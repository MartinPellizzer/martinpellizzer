Serratula aspera

Serratula aspera, commonly known as the rough serratula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Serratula aspera is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
