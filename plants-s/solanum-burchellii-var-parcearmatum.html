Solanum burchellii var. parcearmatum

Solanum burchellii var. parcearmatum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, oval berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
