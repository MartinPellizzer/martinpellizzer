Silene velenovskyana

Silene velenovskyana, commonly known as the Velenovsky's campion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Silene velenovskyana is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
