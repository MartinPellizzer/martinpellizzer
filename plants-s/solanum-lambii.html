Solanum lambii

Solanum lambii, commonly known as the purple passionfruit vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, tangy pulp containing edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
