Sedum japonicum var. senanense

Sedum japonicum var. senanense, commonly known as the Senanese stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Japan.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to thrive in arid conditions.
