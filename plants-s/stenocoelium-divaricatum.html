Stenocoelium divaricatum

Stenocoelium divaricatum, commonly known as the African wild fig, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible, greenish-yellow fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and supporting local biodiversity.
