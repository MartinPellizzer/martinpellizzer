Sacosperma paniculatum var. pubescens

Sacosperma paniculatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sago palm, is a variant of the sago palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately arranged leaves that are covered in a dense layer of short, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which contain the toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in tropical climates.

However, its seeds contain cycasin, a toxin that can be harmful to humans and animals.
