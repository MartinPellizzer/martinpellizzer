Syzygium guineense var. littorale

Syzygium guineense var. littorale, commonly known as the mangosteen or mangostan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, reddish-purple rind and a segmented, white, sweet and tangy pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found near coastal areas and riverbanks.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits.
