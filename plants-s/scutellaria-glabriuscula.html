Scutellaria glabriuscula

Scutellaria glabriuscula, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Scutellaria glabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
