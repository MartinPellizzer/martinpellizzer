Saxifraga egregia

Saxifraga egregia, commonly known as the showy saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga egregia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
