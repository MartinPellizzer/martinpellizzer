Scleroxylum melanophloeum

Scleroxylum melanophloeum, commonly known as the black-flanged ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, dark bark. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its durability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
