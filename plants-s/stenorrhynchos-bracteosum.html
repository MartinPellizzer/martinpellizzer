Stenorrhynchos bracteosum

Stenorrhynchos bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
