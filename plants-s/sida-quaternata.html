Sida quaternata

Sida quaternata, commonly known as four-angle sida, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular seed pods. Sida quaternata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
