Spiraea × prattii

Spiraea × prattii, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters, with a spread of up to 2 meters, forming a dense, rounded shape. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, turning yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea × prattii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
