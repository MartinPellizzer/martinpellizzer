Sclerolobium physophorum

Sclerolobium physophorum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody trunk and is known for its compound leaves composed of multiple small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The golden wattle is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
