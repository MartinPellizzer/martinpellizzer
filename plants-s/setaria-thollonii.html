Setaria thollonii

Setaria thollonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein.

Setaria thollonii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in arid conditions.
