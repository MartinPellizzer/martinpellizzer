Solanum acroleucum

Solanum acroleucum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and can be toxic if ingested.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
