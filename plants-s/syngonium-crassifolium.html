Syngonium crassifolium

Syngonium crassifolium, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that vary in color from bright green to deep red, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Syngonium crassifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
