Spergularia balearica

Spergularia balearica, commonly known as the Balearic sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Spergularia balearica is well adapted to arid conditions and is considered a characteristic species of maritime ecosystems.
