Sebaea rhomboidea

Sebaea rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamond leaf or rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are rhomboid in shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, where it stores water in its leaves and stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.

Sebaea rhomboidea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
