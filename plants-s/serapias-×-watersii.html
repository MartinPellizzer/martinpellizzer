Serapias × watersii

Serapias × watersii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a natural hybrid between Serapias acaulis and Serapias microchila.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Orchidaceae family.

Serapias × watersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with proper drainage and humidity.
