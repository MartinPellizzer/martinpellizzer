Solidago canadensis subsp. salebrosa

Solidago canadensis subsp.

salebrosa, commonly known as Canada goldenrod subspecies salebrosa, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer season.
