Stenostephanus citrinus

Stenostephanus citrinus, commonly known as the yellow starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
