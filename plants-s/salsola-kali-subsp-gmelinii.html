Salsola kali subsp. gmelinii

Salsola kali subsp.

gmelinii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a perennial shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

This species is often invasive in non-native regions, where it can outcompete native vegetation.
