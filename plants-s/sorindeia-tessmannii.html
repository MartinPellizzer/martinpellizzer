Sorindeia tessmannii

Sorindeia tessmannii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a small shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
