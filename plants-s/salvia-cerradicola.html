Salvia cerradicola

Salvia cerradicola, commonly known as the Cerrado sage, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the seasonal climate of the Cerrado biome.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
