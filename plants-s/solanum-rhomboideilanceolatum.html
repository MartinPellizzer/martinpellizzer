Solanum rhomboideilanceolatum

Solanum rhomboideilanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, typically white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are usually red or yellow when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
