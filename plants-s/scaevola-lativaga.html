Scaevola lativaga

Scaevola lativaga, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Scaevola lativaga is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
