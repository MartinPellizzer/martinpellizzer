Salsola platyphylla

Salsola platyphylla, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in summer. Salsola platyphylla is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in sandy and saline soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
