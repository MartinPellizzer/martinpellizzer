Senecio chenopodioides

Senecio chenopodioides, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit and adaptations make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in dry environments.
