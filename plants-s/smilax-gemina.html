Smilax gemina

Smilax gemina, commonly known as the twinberry vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spiny nodes along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Smilax gemina is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
