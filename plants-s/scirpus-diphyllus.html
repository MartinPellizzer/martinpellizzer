Scirpus diphyllus

Scirpus diphyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered brownish flowers that appear in late summer.

Scirpus diphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
