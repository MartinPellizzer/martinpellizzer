Stereospermum longiflorum

Stereospermum longiflorum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Stereospermum longiflorum make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
