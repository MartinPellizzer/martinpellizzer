Sesamum imperatricis

Sesamum imperatricis, commonly known as the imperial sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sesamum imperatricis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in certain cultures.
