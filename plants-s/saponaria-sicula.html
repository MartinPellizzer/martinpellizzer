Saponaria sicula

Saponaria sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features succulent, cylindrical stems and toothed, lance-shaped leaves. Saponaria sicula is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its common name derives from the saponin compounds found in its roots, which produce a soapy lather when crushed.
