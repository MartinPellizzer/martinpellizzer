Silene turcomanica

Silene turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene turcomanica is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
