Selaginella frondosa var. splendida

Selaginella frondosa var. splendida, commonly known as the splendour spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, compact foliage and distinctive, needle-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
