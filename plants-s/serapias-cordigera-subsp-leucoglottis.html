Serapias cordigera subsp. leucoglottis

Serapias cordigera subsp.

leucoglottis, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with white petals and a yellow lip.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale green to white flowers, which are adorned with prominent yellow veins.
