Sarcomphalus carolinianus

Sarcomphalus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
