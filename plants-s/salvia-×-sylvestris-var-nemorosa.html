Salvia × sylvestris var. nemorosa

Salvia × sylvestris var. nemorosa, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
