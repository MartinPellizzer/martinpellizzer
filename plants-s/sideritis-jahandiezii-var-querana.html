Sideritis jahandiezii var. querana

Sideritis jahandiezii var. querana, commonly known as the Persian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
