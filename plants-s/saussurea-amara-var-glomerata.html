Saussurea amara var. glomerata

Saussurea amara var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for use in traditional medicine.
