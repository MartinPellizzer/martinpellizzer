Solanum swartzianum subsp. chrysophyllum

Solanum swartzianum subsp.

chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental, star-shaped yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and edible fruits.
