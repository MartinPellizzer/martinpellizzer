Sterculia guineensis

Sterculia guineensis, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and roots are also valued for their medicinal properties, including antimalarial and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
