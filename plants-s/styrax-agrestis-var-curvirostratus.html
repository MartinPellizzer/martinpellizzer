Styrax agrestis var. curvirostratus

Styrax agrestis var. curvirostratus, commonly known as the curved-beak styrax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
