Stenochilus bignoniiflorus

Stenochilus bignoniiflorus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
