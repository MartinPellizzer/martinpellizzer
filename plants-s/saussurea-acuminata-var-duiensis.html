Saussurea acuminata var. duiensis

Saussurea acuminata var. duiensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
