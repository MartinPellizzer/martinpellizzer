Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. elodes

Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. elodes, commonly known as the New York ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in moist, well-drained habitats.
