Symplocos lucida subsp. howii

Symplocos lucida subsp.

howii, commonly known as the How's symplocos, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and southern China. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
