Saxifraga × furcata

Saxifraga × furcata, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, often pink or white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Saxifraga × furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a hardy garden plant.
