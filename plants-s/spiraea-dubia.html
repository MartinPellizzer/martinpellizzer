Spiraea dubia

Spiraea dubia, commonly known as the western spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems covered in small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
