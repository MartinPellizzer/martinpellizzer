Stevia scabrella var. venosa

Stevia scabrella var. venosa, commonly known as the woolly stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herbal gardens.

Its unique appearance and mild flavor make it both ornamental and potentially useful in natural sweeteners.
