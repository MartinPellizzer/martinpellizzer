Symplocos macrophylla var. glandulifera

Symplocos macrophylla var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leafed evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves with prominent glandular dots on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
