Selaginella mnioides var. major

Selaginella mnioides var. major, commonly known as the giant spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stems, with sporangia developing on specialized structures called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This variety is notable for its size and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
