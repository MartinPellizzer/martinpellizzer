Sideritis scordioides subsp. fruticulosa

Sideritis scordioides subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the shrub yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Sideritis scordioides subsp.

fruticulosa is also an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
