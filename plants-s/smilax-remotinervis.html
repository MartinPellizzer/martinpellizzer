Smilax remotinervis

Smilax remotinervis, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-veined leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential weed in certain habitats.
