Sasa uyetsuensis

Sasa uyetsuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Uyetsu bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa uyetsuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
