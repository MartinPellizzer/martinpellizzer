Silene sclerocarpa

Silene sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Silene sclerocarpa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in disturbed soils.
