Sweetia dasycarpa var. torta

Sweetia dasycarpa var. torta, commonly known as the twisted sweetia, is a unique variant of the sweetia plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted fruiting bodies, which set it apart from other members of the genus. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphological features and potential ecological significance.
