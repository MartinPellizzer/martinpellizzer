Silene sartorii

Silene sartorii, commonly known as the "sower's bonnet," is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Silene sartorii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.

Its name derives from the historical belief that it was used to aid in seed dispersal, though this practice is no longer widely practiced.
