Senna apsidoneura

Senna apsidoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Senna apsidoneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which contributes to its survival in harsh climates.
