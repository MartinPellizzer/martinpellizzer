Scutellaria columnae var. sibthorpii

Scutellaria columnae var. sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorpi's beardroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habits.
