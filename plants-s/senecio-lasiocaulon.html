Senecio lasiocaulon

Senecio lasiocaulon, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features a branched stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
