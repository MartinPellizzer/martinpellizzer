Smilax corbularia var. hypoglauca

Smilax corbularia var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
