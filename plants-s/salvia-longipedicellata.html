Salvia longipedicellata

Salvia longipedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
