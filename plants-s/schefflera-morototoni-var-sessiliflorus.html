Schefflera morototoni var. sessiliflorus

Schefflera morototoni var. sessiliflorus, commonly known as the Madagascar umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is popular in tropical and subtropical regions.
