Silene mollissima subsp. auriculifolia

Silene mollissima subsp.

auriculifolia, commonly known as the auriculate silene, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of North America. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy pink to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is often found in the northern United States and southern Canada, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
