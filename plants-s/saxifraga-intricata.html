Saxifraga intricata

Saxifraga intricata, commonly known as the intricate saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, often covered in fine hairs. Saxifraga intricata thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
