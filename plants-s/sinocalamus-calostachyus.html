Sinocalamus calostachyus

Sinocalamus calostachyus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine. Sinocalamus calostachyus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
