Selago phyllopodioides

Selago phyllopodioides, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
