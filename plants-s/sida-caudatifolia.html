Sida caudatifolia

Sida caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

The plant is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
