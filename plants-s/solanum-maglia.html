Solanum maglia

Solanum maglia, commonly known as the Maglia nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Solanum maglia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
