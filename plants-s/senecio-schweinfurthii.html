Senecio schweinfurthii

Senecio schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schreiber's groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of blue-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a terminal inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy succulent.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
