Schismatoglottis calyptrata f. resupinata

Schismatoglottis calyptrata f. resupinata, commonly known as the Resupinate Schismatoglottis, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, hood-like structure known as a calyptra. It is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly sought after by botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
