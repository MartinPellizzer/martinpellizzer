Spananthe huanaca

Spananthe huanaca, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
