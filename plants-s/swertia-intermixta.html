Swertia intermixta

Swertia intermixta, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Swertia intermixta is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses in herbal medicine.
