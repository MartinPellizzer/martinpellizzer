Spathodea alternifolia

Spathodea alternifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
