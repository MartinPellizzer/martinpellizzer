Solanum swartzianum

Solanum swartzianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are hairy as well.

Solanum swartzianum is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
