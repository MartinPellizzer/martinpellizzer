Salix eriocephala var. watsonii

Salix eriocephala var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in the early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
