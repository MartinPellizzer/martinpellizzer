Salvia azurea var. elata

Salvia azurea var. elata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
