Schizostachyum burmanicum

Schizostachyum burmanicum, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brown flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Schizostachyum burmanicum is also valued for its strong, durable culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
