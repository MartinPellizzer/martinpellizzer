Selaginella pallescens var. acutifolia

Selaginella pallescens var. acutifolia, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs. It is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
