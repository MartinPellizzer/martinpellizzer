Sisyrinchium schaffneri

Sisyrinchium schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Sisyrinchium, which includes various species of blue-eyed grasses characterized by their showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in early spring, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom in challenging conditions.
