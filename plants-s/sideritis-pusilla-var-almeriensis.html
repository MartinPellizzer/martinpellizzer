Sideritis pusilla var. almeriensis

Sideritis pusilla var. almeriensis, commonly known as Almerian dwarf sainfoin, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hairy leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
