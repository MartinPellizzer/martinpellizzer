Silene mollissima subsp. pseudovelutina

Silene mollissima subsp.

pseudovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with a mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
