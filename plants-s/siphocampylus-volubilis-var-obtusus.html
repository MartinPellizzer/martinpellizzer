Siphocampylus volubilis var. obtusus

Siphocampylus volubilis var. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved climbing bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often hairy, while the stems are hairy and can become woody with age. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
