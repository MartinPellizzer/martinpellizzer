Stevia triangularis

Stevia triangularis, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. Stevia triangularis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.

Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds, offering a natural alternative to sugar.
