Siphocampylus hazenii

Siphocampylus hazenii, commonly known as the Arizona mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to pale purple.

Siphocampylus hazenii is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
