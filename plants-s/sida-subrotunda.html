Sida subrotunda

Sida subrotunda, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pale purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida subrotunda is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
