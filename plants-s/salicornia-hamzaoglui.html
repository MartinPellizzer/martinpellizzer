Salicornia hamzaoglui

Salicornia hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Hamzaoglu glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. This species is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, green or reddish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by wind or water.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content and potential use in agriculture and food production.
