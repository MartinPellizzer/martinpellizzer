Sempervivum × vaccarii

Sempervivum × vaccarii, commonly known as the common stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating called a cuticle. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Sempervivum × vaccarii is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.
