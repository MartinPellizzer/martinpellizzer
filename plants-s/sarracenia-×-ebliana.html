Sarracenia × ebliana

Sarracenia × ebliana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, tubular pitchers that are often red or orange with intricate patterns. These pitchers secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for bog gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological function make it a captivating subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical researchers.
