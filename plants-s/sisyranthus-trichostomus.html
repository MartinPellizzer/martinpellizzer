Sisyranthus trichostomus

Sisyranthus trichostomus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive mist-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
