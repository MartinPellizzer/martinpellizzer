Sideritis saetabensis

Sideritis saetabensis, commonly known as the serrated rock sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Sideritis saetabensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
