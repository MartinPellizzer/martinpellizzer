Salvia confinis

Salvia confinis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia confinis is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
