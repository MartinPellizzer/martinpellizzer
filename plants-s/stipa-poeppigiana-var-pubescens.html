Stipa poeppigiana var. pubescens

Stipa poeppigiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of soft, pubescent (hairy) leaves. The plant produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to form low, spreading mats.
