Scirpus mucronatus var. tataranus

Scirpus mucronatus var. tataranus, commonly known as the Tatarian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in northern regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This species is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and as a habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
