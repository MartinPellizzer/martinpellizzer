Sisymbrium tucumanense

Sisymbrium tucumanense, commonly known as the Tucuman mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and has pinnately divided leaves. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

The seeds of Sisymbrium tucumanense are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
