Scutellaria versicolor var. bracteata

Scutellaria versicolor var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
