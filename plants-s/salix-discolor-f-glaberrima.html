Salix discolor f. glaberrima

Salix discolor f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a botanical variant of the common willow.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

Its flowers appear in catkins, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological systems.
