Sphaerostephanos humilis

Sphaerostephanos humilis, commonly known as the low-growing starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals that are slightly recurved.

Sphaerostephanos humilis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
