Syringa hirsuta var. formosissima

Syringa hirsuta var. formosissima, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has greyish-green foliage that turns yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its pleasant fragrance.
