Sida dictyocarpa var. esperanzae

Sida dictyocarpa var. esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza Sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
