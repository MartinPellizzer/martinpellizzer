Sambucus callicarpa f. bicolor

Sambucus callicarpa f. bicolor, commonly known as the variegated elderberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries, while edible, are typically used in culinary and medicinal applications.
