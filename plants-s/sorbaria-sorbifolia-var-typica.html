Sorbaria sorbifolia var. typica

Sorbaria sorbifolia var. typica, commonly known as the typical leafed whitebeard, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a dry, winged capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
