Stelis vinosa

Stelis vinosa, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption.

Stelis vinosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
