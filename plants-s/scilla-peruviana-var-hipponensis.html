Scilla peruviana var. hipponensis

Scilla peruviana var. hipponensis, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of 3 to 6. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
