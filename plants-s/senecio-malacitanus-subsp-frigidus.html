Senecio malacitanus subsp. frigidus

Senecio malacitanus subsp.

frigidus, commonly known as the cold mountain groundsel, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are typically pale yellow and bloom during the cooler months.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
