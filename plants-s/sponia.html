Sponia

Sponia, commonly known as the spongy moss, is a type of bryophyte belonging to the family Sphagnaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spongy growth form and the ability to absorb and retain large amounts of water. Sponia typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as peat bogs and forest floors. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in low-nutrient conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant's rhizoids help anchor it to the substrate, while its leafy stems contribute to its overall sponge-like appearance.
