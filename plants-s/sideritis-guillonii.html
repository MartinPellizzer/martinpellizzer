Sideritis guillonii

Sideritis guillonii, commonly known as the Greek heather, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Sideritis guillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
