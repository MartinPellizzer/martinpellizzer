Sapium mannianum

Sapium mannianum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native regions.
