Sebastiania discolor var. subconcolor

Sebastiania discolor var. subconcolor, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on both sides. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
