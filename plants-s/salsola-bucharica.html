Salsola bucharica

Salsola bucharica, commonly known as sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Salsola bucharica is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in sandy soils and can play a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
