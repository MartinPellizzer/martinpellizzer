Salvia dorystaechas

Salvia dorystaechas, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, sky-blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia dorystaechas is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
