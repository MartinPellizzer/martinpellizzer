Salvia hirsuta

Salvia hirsuta, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia hirsuta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal and ornamental uses have made it a valued plant in both traditional and modern horticulture.
