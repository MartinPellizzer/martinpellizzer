Solanum ramizii

Solanum ramizii, commonly known as the Ramizii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers and red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
