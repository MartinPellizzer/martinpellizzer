Solanum dunalianum var. lanceolatum

Solanum dunalianum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and potential medicinal significance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
