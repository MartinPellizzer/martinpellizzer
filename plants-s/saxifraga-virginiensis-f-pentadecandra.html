Saxifraga virginiensis f. pentadecandra

Saxifraga virginiensis f. pentadecandra, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
