Stanhopea fregeana

Stanhopea fregeana, commonly known as the Fregeana orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
