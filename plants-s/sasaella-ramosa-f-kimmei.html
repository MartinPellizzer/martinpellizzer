Sasaella ramosa f. kimmei

Sasaella ramosa f. kimmei, commonly known as the Kimmei sasa, is a variant of the sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
