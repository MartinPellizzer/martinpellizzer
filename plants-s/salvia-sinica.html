Salvia sinica

Salvia sinica, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a minty aroma.

Salvia sinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
