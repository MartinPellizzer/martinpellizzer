Sclerocarya birrea subsp. birrea

Sclerocarya birrea subsp.

birrea, commonly known as the African bullthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
