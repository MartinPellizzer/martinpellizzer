Spiraea magellanica

Spiraea magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea magellanica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
