Senegalia gageana

Senegalia gageana, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces hard, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species.

Senegalia gageana is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in erosion control and as a forage plant.
