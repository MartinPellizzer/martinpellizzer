Swertia columbiana

Swertia columbiana, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The genus Swertia belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
