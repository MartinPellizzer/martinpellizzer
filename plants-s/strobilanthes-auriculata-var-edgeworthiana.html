Strobilanthes auriculata var. edgeworthiana

Strobilanthes auriculata var. edgeworthiana, commonly known as the golden flame or edgeworth's flame flower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, elongated, and often brightly colored bracts that resemble small flames. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is known for its rapid growth cycle, flowering within a few months of germination. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is particularly noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
