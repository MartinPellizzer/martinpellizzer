Solanum loretoanum

Solanum loretoanum, commonly known as the Loreto nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.
