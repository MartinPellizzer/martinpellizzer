Stipa nervosa var. neutralis

Stipa nervosa var. neutralis, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, cylindrical seed heads that are often adorned with silky hairs. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and open woodlands. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Its delicate appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
