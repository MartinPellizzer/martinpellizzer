Sagittaria pugioniformis

Sagittaria pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive narrow, pointed form. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters above the water surface.

Sagittaria pugioniformis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
