Solanum maglia var. collinum

Solanum maglia var. collinum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and unique morphological traits.
