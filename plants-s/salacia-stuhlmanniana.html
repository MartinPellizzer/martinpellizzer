Salacia stuhlmanniana

Salacia stuhlmanniana, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its woody vine structure and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of primary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
