Spongiosperma cataractarum

Spongiosperma cataractarum, commonly known as the water spongy fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its spongy, porous fronds that aid in water absorption.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's structure includes specialized root-like rhizomes and leaf-like fronds that are adapted for underwater life. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, typical of ferns.

The botanical features of Spongiosperma cataractarum make it an important indicator species for water quality and ecosystem health.
