Sesbania sesban subsp. punctata

Sesbania sesban subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the spotted sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded stipules and yellow flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable forage crop in certain agricultural systems.
