Styrax magnus

Styrax magnus, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Styrax magnus thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.
