Spiraea japonica var. tomentosa

Spiraea japonica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Japanese spirea or tomentosa spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage that becomes more prominent in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
