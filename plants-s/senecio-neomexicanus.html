Senecio neomexicanus

Senecio neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It features basal leaves that are usually gray-green in color and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Senecio neomexicanus is often found in disturbed sites and has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
