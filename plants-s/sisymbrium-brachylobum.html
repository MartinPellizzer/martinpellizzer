Sisymbrium brachylobum

Sisymbrium brachylobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional medicine.
