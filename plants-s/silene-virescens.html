Silene virescens

Silene virescens, commonly known as the green willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purplish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene virescens typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a relatively rare species in some regions.
