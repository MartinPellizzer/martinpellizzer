Schlumbergera × graeseri

Schlumbergera × graeseri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, flattened stems that form a branching structure, often with colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces showy blooms during the holiday season, typically from November to January, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schlumbergera × graeseri is prized for its long-lasting flowers and ability to bloom reliably under proper care.
