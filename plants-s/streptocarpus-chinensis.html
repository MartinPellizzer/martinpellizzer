Streptocarpus chinensis

Streptocarpus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Streptocarpus chinensis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
