Solanum indicum var. modicearmatum

Solanum indicum var. modicearmatum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
