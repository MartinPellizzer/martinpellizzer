Silene caroliniana var. wherryi

Silene caroliniana var. wherryi, commonly known as the Carolina leek, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Silene caroliniana var. wherryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
