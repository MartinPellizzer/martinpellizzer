Symplocos jucunda

Symplocos jucunda, commonly known as the small-fruited wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that are initially green and turn red when mature. Symplocos jucunda thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
