Senecio argophyllus

Senecio argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. It produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Senecio argophyllus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
