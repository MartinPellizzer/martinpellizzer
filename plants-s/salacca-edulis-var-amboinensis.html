Salacca edulis var. amboinensis

Salacca edulis var. amboinensis, commonly known as the sugar palm or burasari, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling small pineapples. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and is often consumed fresh or processed into syrups.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native regions.
