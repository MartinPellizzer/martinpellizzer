Saxifraga macrostigma subvar. macrantha

Saxifraga macrostigma subvar. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy. Its most distinguishing characteristic is its large, showy white or pinkish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
