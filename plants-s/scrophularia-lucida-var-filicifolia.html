Scrophularia lucida var. filicifolia

Scrophularia lucida var. filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
