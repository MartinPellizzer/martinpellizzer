Senecio latilobus

Senecio latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have distinctive lobed margins. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio latilobus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
