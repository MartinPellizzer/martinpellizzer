Saxifraga pavonii

Saxifraga pavonii, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of showy pink or purple flowers in early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga pavonii is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
