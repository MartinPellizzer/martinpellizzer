Siphocampylus erianthus

Siphocampylus erianthus, commonly known as the woolly phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
