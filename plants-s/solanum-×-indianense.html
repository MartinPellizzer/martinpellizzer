Solanum × indianense

Solanum × indianense, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that transition from green to red as they mature. Native to regions of India and Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
