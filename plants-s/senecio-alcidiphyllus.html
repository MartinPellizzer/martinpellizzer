Senecio alcidiphyllus

Senecio alcidiphyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio alcidiphyllus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
