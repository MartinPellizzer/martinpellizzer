Simlera

Simlera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Simlera is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
