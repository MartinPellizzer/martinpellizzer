Silene ciliata var. pyrenaica

Silene ciliata var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Silene ciliata var. pyrenaica is often found in meadows and mountain slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters.
