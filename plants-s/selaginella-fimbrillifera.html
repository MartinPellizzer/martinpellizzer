Selaginella fimbrillifera

Selaginella fimbrillifera, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a rosette-like structure with needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stem. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.
