Stipa ventanicola

Stipa ventanicola, commonly known as the coastal needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand saline and drought conditions.

Stipa ventanicola plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
