Schwabea ecbolioides

Schwabea ecbolioides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental species suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
