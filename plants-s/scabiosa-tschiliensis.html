Scabiosa tschiliensis

Scabiosa tschiliensis, commonly known as the Siberian scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, pink or purple flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
