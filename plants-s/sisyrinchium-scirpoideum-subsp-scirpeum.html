Sisyrinchium scirpoideum subsp. scirpeum

Sisyrinchium scirpoideum subsp.

scirpeum, commonly known as the slender zigzag flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to purple flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
