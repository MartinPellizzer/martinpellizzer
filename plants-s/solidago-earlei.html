Solidago earlei

Solidago earlei, commonly known as the Earley's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Solidago earlei plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
