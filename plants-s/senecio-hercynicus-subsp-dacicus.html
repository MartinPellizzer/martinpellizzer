Senecio hercynicus subsp. dacicus

Senecio hercynicus subsp.

dacicus, commonly known as the Dacian Senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are generally lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in compact clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
