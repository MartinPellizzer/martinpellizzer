Salvia longispicata

Salvia longispicata, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces fluffy, cone-like seed heads that persist into autumn, adding visual interest. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
