Scutellaria rupicola

Scutellaria rupicola, commonly known as the rock skullcup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and slightly square in cross-section.

Scutellaria rupicola is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
