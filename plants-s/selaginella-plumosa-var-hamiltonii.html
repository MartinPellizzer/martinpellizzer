Selaginella plumosa var. hamiltonii

Selaginella plumosa var. hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's clubmoss, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage, with long, slender leaves that give it a soft, moss-like appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in whorls, and it produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and botanical study.
