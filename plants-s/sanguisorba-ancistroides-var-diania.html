Sanguisorba ancistroides var. diania

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. diania, commonly known as the Korean dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
