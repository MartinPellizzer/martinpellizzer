Sida borbonica

Sida borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar mallow, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface. Its showy flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
