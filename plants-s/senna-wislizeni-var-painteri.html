Senna wislizeni var. painteri

Senna wislizeni var. painteri, commonly known as the painter's senna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
