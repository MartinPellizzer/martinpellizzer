Sasaella glabra f. albostriata

Sasaella glabra f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striated bamboo, is a variant of the smooth bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with a distinctive white stripe along the nodes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a smooth, glabrous surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique visual characteristics.
