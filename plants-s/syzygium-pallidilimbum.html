Syzygium pallidilimbum

Syzygium pallidilimbum, commonly known as the pale-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with pale veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Syzygium pallidilimbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
