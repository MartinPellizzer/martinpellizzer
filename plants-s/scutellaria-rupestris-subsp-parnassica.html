Scutellaria rupestris subsp. parnassica

Scutellaria rupestris subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, nutlet-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological adaptations.
