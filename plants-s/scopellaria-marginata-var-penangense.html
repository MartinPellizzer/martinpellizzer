Scopellaria marginata var. penangense

Scopellaria marginata var. penangense, commonly known as Penang scopellaria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or muddy soils along tidal zones, often found in intertidal habitats. Its leaves are linear and somewhat succulent, adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Scopellaria marginata var. penangense plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for small marine and terrestrial organisms.
