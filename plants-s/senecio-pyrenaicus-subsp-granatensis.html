Senecio pyrenaicus subsp. granatensis

Senecio pyrenaicus subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
