Schizachyrium bimucronatum

Schizachyrium bimucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sidebladder, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
