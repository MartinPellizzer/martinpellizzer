Struthanthus emarginatus

Struthanthus emarginatus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a woody stem and hairy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its small, round seed pods contain numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Struthanthus emarginatus is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
