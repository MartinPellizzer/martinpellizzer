Senecio flintii

Senecio flintii, commonly known as Flint's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
