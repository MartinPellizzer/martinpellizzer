Sedum sieboldii

Sedum sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
