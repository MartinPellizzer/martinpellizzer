Statice limonium proles longidentata

Statice limonium proles longidentata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts.

It is characterized by its compact, spiky inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dune environments. Its flowers, typically pink or purple, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice limonium proles longidentata is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
