Setaria paractaenoides

Setaria paractaenoides, commonly known as narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical seed heads that resemble a foxtail, typically appearing in late summer. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

It is often considered a weed in cultivated areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
