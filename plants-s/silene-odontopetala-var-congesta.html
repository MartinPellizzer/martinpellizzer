Silene odontopetala var. congesta

Silene odontopetala var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its dense inflorescence and is often found in shaded to partially shaded habitats.
