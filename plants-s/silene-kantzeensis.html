Silene kantzeensis

Silene kantzeensis, commonly known as Kantze's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene kantzeensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native range.
