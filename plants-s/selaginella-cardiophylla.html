Selaginella cardiophylla

Selaginella cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest floors. Selaginella cardiophylla exhibits a unique reproductive system, producing both microspores and megaspores on the same plant.

Its structure includes a short stem with spirally arranged leaves and a cluster of sporophylls at the tip.
