Sceptridium officinale

Sceptridium officinale, commonly known as the common spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sceptridium officinale plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
