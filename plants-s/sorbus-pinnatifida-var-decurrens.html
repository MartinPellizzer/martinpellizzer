Sorbus pinnatifida var. decurrens

Sorbus pinnatifida var. decurrens, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
