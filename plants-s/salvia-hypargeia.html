Salvia hypargeia

Salvia hypargeia, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in whorls along the upper stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia hypargeia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
