Sagittaria palifolia f. pubescens

Sagittaria palifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its hairy foliage.

It is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America. The leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a distinctive pubescent texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface.

This plant is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality.
