Senecio volckmannii var. pinohachense

Senecio volckmannii var. pinohachense, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, blue-green leaves with a woolly texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces distinctive, elongated bracts.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
