Sasamorpha purpurascens f. subpubescens

Sasamorpha purpurascens f. subpubescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered sasamorpha, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple blooms and hairy, velvety foliage, which give it its subspecies name "subpubescens.". The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
