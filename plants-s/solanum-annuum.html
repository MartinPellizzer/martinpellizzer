Solanum annuum

Solanum annuum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum annuum is known for its alkaloid content, which can be toxic in high quantities.
