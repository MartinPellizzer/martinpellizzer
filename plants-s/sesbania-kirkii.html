Sesbania kirkii

Sesbania kirkii, commonly known as the African sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and livestock forage.

Sesbania kirkii plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural systems.
