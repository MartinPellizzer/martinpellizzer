Sida periplocifolia var. caribaea

Sida periplocifolia var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its slender stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in disturbed habitats along the shorelines of the Caribbean islands.
