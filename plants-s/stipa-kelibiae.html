Stipa kelibiae

Stipa kelibiae, commonly known as the Kelibia needlegrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy component of desert ecosystems. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Stipa kelibiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
