Senecio laetevirens

Senecio laetevirens, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Senecio laetevirens is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
