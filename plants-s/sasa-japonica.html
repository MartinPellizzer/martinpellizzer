Sasa japonica

Sasa japonica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by the development of dark purple to black berries. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense thickets, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

Sasa japonica is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Japanese culture.
