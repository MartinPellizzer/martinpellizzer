Scilla hispanica var. patula

Scilla hispanica var. patula, commonly known as Spanish squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
