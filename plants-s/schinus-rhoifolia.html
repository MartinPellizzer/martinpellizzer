Schinus rhoifolia

Schinus rhoifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and consist of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to native ecosystems.
