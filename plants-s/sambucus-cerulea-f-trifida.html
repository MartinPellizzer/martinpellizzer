Sambucus cerulea f. trifida

Sambucus cerulea f. trifida, commonly known as the dwarf elderberry, is a compact variant of the common elderberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, hardy plant.
