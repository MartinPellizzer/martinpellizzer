Swida macrophylla var. longipedunculata

Swida macrophylla var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate swida, is a variant of the swida plant characterized by its elongated peduncles.

It is a deciduous shrub native to specific regions of East Asia, particularly China. The plant features large, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
