Spryginia gracilis

Spryginia gracilis, commonly known as the slender spryginia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
