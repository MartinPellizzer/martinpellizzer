Simsia anethifolia

Simsia anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features finely divided, anise-scented leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Simsia anethifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
