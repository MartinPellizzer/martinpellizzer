Solanum barbeyanum

Solanum barbeyanum, commonly known as the Barbey's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Solanum barbeyanum is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
