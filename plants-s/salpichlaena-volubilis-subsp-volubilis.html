Salpichlaena volubilis subsp. volubilis

Salpichlaena volubilis subsp.

volubilis, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of aerial roots that allow it to attach to surfaces.

The plant has feather-like fronds with a distinctive, glossy green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
