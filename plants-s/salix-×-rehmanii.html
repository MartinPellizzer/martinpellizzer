Salix × rehmanii

Salix × rehmanii, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

Salix × rehmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
