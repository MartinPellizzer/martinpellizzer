Sanicula liberta

Sanicula liberta, commonly known as the freedom anise, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are dry, two-lobed schizocarps that release tiny seeds when mature.

Sanicula liberta is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
