Schizaea elegans var. flabellum

Schizaea elegans var. flabellum, commonly known as the flabellate spleenwort, is a rare and specialized fern belonging to the family Schizaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched fronds that resemble a fan or a fan-like structure, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in rocky crevices or on the trunks of trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaf-like formations called sporophylls.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
