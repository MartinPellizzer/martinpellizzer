Solanum ledermannii

Solanum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum ledermannii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped fruits.
