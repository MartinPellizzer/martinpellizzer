Spiraea callosa var. semperflorens

Spiraea callosa var. semperflorens, commonly known as everblooming weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that produce clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant features small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and long blooming period.
