Syzygium siamense

Syzygium siamense, commonly known as the Siam tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, sour fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.

Syzygium siamense thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
