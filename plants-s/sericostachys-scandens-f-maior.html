Sericostachys scandens f. maior

Sericostachys scandens f. maior, commonly known as the giant climbing bamboo, is a variant of the sericostachys species.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, flexible stems that can climb and twine around supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne along the upper portions of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to create dense, vertical coverage in gardens.
