Sabicea calycina var. hirsutiflora

Sabicea calycina var. hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, hairy flowers that bloom in yellow or greenish-yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination networks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
