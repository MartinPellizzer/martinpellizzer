Salix exigua subsp. interior

Salix exigua subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
