Sicyocarya saltuaria

Sicyocarya saltuaria, commonly known as the salt marsh aster, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in marshes and tidal flats. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding.

Sicyocarya saltuaria plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various pollinators.
