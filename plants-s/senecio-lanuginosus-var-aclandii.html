Senecio lanuginosus var. aclandii

Senecio lanuginosus var. aclandii, commonly known as the Acland's wooly aster, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
