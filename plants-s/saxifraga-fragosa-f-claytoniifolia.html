Saxifraga fragosa f. claytoniifolia

Saxifraga fragosa f. claytoniifolia, commonly known as the Clayton's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to silver hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Saxifragaceae family.
