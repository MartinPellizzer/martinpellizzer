Sasa albomarginata

Sasa albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It forms dense clumps with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive white margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and sustainable use in papermaking and weaving.
