Sideritis × liantei

Sideritis × liantei, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Sideritis × liantei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
