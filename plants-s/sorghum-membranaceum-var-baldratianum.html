Sorghum membranaceum var. baldratianum

Sorghum membranaceum var. baldratianum, commonly known as the Baldratian grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
