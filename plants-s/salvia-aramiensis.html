Salvia aramiensis

Salvia aramiensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent.

Salvia aramiensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
