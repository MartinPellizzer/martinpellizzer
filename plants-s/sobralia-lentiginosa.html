Sobralia lentiginosa

Sobralia lentiginosa, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Sobralia lentiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
