Senecio gynura

Senecio gynura, commonly known as the blueweed or blue senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring grey-green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are small and blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio gynura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
