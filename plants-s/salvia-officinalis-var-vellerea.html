Salvia officinalis var. vellerea

Salvia officinalis var. vellerea, commonly known as wooly sage, is a cultivar of the common sage plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its unique texture and appearance. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.

It produces purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators and adding visual interest to gardens.
