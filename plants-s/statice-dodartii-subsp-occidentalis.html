Statice dodartii subsp. occidentalis

Statice dodartii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western glass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
