Senecio crocatus

Senecio crocatus, commonly known as the Orange ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, somewhat succulent leaves. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio crocatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
