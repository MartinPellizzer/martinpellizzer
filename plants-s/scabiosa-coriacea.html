Scabiosa coriacea

Scabiosa coriacea, commonly known as the hardy scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Scabiosa coriacea is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
