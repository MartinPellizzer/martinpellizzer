Salvia urticifolia

Salvia urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has serrated, hairy leaves that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact, similar to stinging nettle. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Salvia urticifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
