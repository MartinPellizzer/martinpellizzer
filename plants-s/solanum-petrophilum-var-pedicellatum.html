Solanum petrophilum var. pedicellatum

Solanum petrophilum var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the rock pepper, is a perennial shrub native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, providing some protection against harsh weather conditions.

The fruit, which is a small, yellowish berry, contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine.
