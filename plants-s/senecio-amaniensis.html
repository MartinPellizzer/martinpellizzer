Senecio amaniensis

Senecio amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani groundsels, is a flowering plant native to the East African highlands, particularly in Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
