Syzygium wolfii

Syzygium wolfii, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Syzygium wolfii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
