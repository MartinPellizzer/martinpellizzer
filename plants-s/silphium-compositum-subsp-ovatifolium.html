Silphium compositum subsp. ovatifolium

Silphium compositum subsp.

ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaved compass plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the central United States. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant produces seeds in small, disk-like structures. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, and is adapted to full sun.

The plant has historical significance as a medicinal and food source for indigenous peoples.
