Sambucus ebulus var. africana

Sambucus ebulus var. africana, commonly known as the African dogwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
