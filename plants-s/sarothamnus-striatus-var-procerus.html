Sarothamnus striatus var. procerus

Sarothamnus striatus var. procerus, commonly known as the hairy thornstep, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

This variety is distinguished by its more elongated and hairy stems compared to the typical form of Sarothamnus striatus.
