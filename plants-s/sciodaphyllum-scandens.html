Sciodaphyllum scandens

Sciodaphyllum scandens, commonly known as the climbing mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Sciodaphyllum scandens is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forest canopies where it thrives in shaded conditions.
