Syzygium surigaense

Syzygium surigaense, commonly known as the Surigaon mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellow-orange drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
