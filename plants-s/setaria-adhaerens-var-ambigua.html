Setaria adhaerens var. ambigua

Setaria adhaerens var. ambigua, commonly known as the sticky foxtail, is a variant of the foxtail millet species.

It is a herbaceous annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind or attached to animals.

This plant is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
