Syzygium campanellum

Syzygium campanellum, commonly known as the bell-shaped clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and it produces small, dark red to black berries.

Syzygium campanellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
