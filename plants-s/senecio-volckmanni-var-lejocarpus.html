Senecio volckmanni var. lejocarpus

Senecio volckmanni var. lejocarpus, commonly known as the yellow stonecress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to desert conditions, showcasing features typical of xerophytic plants.
