Sagittaria montevidensis f. flaviflora

Sagittaria montevidensis f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. This aquatic or semi-aquatic plant thrives in wetlands, marshes, and shallow water environments. Its roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for wetland restoration.

The plant is also popular in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to purify water.
