Sisyrinchium solstitiale

Sisyrinchium solstitiale, commonly known as the summer blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Its compact growth habit and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower landscapes.
