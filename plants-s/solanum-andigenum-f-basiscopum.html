Solanum andigenum f. basiscopum

Solanum andigenum f. basiscopum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. This particular form is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered together.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
