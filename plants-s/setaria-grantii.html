Setaria grantii

Setaria grantii, commonly known as the African dogstail, is a grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein.

Setaria grantii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
