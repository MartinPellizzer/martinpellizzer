Sideritis argosphacelus

Sideritis argosphacelus, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture.

Sideritis argosphacelus is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
