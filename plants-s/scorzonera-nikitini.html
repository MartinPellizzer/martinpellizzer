Scorzonera nikitini

Scorzonera nikitini, commonly known as the Nikitin's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a thick, tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Scorzonera nikitini thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
