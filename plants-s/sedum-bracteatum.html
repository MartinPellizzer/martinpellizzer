Sedum bracteatum

Sedum bracteatum, commonly known as the "Blue Spruce Sedum," is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
