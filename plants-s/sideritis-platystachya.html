Sideritis platystachya

Sideritis platystachya, commonly known as broad-stalked ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis platystachya is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
