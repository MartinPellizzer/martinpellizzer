Sageretia apiculata

Sageretia apiculata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental species often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
