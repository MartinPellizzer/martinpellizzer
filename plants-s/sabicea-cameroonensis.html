Sabicea cameroonensis

Sabicea cameroonensis, commonly known as Cameroon sabelia, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions in Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid tropical climates. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in the wild.

Sabicea cameroonensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
