Syzygium affine

Syzygium affine, commonly known as the white apple or small-fruited clove, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Syzygium affine thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
