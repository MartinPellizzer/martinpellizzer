Strophanthus tchabe

Strophanthus tchabe, commonly known as the African bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, which have historically been used in traditional medicine and as a source of digitalis-like compounds.
