Sabatia macrophylla var. macrophylla

Sabatia macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of bright yellow, ray-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The showy goldenrod is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
