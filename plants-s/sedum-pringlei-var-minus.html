Sedum pringlei var. minus

Sedum pringlei var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
