Swertia warackensis

Swertia warackensis, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia warackensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.
