Smilax simulans

Smilax simulans, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax simulans is often found in woodlands and thickets, thriving in partial to full shade.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
