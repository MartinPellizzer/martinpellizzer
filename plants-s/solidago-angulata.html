Solidago angulata

Solidago angulata, commonly known as angular goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Angular goldenrod is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
