Scleria striatonux var. lacunosa

Scleria striatonux var. lacunosa, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Scleria.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, three-lobed flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are often striated, giving it the common name "narrow-ribbed.".

The plant is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
