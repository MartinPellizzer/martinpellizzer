Stipa caucasica subsp. glareosa

Stipa caucasica subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as Caucasian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-green panicles that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that enhances its visual appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
