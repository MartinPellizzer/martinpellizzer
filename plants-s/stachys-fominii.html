Stachys fominii

Stachys fominii, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Stachys fominii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
