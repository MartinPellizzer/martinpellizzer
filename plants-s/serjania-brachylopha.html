Serjania brachylopha

Serjania brachylopha, commonly known as the short-stalked serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.
