Solanum melongena var. esculentum

Solanum melongena var. esculentum, commonly known as the eggplant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, purple-colored fruit, which can vary in shape and size depending on the cultivar. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and is widely used in various cuisines around the world.
