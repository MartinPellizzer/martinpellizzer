Scaligeria napiformis

Scaligeria napiformis, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic animals. The plant has no true leaves, relying instead on its specialized bladders for nutrient absorption. It thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, using its carnivorous adaptations to supplement its diet.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
