Solanum corneliomulleri

Solanum corneliomulleri, commonly known as the Corneliomuller's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, rounded berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Solanum corneliomulleri belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
