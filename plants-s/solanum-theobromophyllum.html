Solanum theobromophyllum

Solanum theobromophyllum, commonly known as the chocolate berry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that resemble chocolate candies. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces fruit that is edible when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties.
