Sterculia cauliflora

Sterculia cauliflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, a feature known as cauliflory. The plant produces woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Its leaves are usually large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Sterculia cauliflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
