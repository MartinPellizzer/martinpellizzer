Stobaea echinacea

Stobaea echinacea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky central cone composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Stobaea echinacea is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
