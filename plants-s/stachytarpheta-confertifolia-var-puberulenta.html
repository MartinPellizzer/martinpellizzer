Stachytarpheta confertifolia var. puberulenta

Stachytarpheta confertifolia var. puberulenta, commonly known as the roughleaf blue mistflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
