Struthiopteris germanica var. pubescens

Struthiopteris germanica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy field shield fern, is a variant of the broad buckwheat fern.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant features large, triangular fronds with a distinctive hairy underside, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The fronds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
