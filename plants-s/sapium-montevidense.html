Sapium montevidense

Sapium montevidense, commonly known as the red swamp water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, fibrous stems. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red calyx, blooming during the warmer months.

Sapium montevidense plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
