Sorbus prunifolia

Sorbus prunifolia, commonly known as the redleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red leaves, which provide striking visual interest in the autumn. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
