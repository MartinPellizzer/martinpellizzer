Solanum umbelliferum var. hoffmannii

Solanum umbelliferum var. hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation despite its ornamental potential.
