Syncolostemon eriocephalus

Syncolostemon eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, giving it a woolly appearance. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Syncolostemon eriocephalus is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
