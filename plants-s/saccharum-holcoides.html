Saccharum holcoides

Saccharum holcoides, commonly known as the grassy bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is adapted to wetland environments and can thrive in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Due to its hardiness, it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in some regions.
