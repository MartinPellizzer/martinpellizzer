Streptachne verticillata

Streptachne verticillata, commonly known as the clustered fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, whorled arrangement of leaf blades. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the nodes along the stem. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Streptachne verticillata is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid environments.
