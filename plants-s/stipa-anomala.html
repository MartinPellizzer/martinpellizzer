Stipa anomala

Stipa anomala, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Stipa anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
