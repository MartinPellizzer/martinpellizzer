Schizocodon soldanelloides var. longifolius

Schizocodon soldanelloides var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved schizocodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are characterized by their elongated shape and are often aromatic.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
