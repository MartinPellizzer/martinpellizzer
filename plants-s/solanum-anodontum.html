Solanum anodontum

Solanum anodontum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and arid environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.
