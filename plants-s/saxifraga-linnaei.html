Saxifraga linnaei

Saxifraga linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga linnaei is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
