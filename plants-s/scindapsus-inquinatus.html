Scindapsus inquinatus

Scindapsus inquinatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of silver, green, and white. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus inquinatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
