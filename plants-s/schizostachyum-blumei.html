Schizostachyum blumei

Schizostachyum blumei, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Schizostachyum blumei is valued for its strong, durable culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species.
