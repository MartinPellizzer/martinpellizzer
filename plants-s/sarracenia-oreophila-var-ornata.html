Sarracenia oreophila var. ornata

Sarracenia oreophila var. ornata, commonly known as the ornate pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, colorful pitchers to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, and they secrete a sticky liquid to lure prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

This variant is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique ecological adaptations.
