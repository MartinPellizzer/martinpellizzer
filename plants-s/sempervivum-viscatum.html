Sempervivum viscatum

Sempervivum viscatum, commonly known as the woolly houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Sempervivum viscatum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.
