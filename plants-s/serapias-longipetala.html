Serapias longipetala

Serapias longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces racemes of vivid orange or yellow flowers with intricate petal structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Serapias longipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
