Senecio thapsoides subsp. visianianus

Senecio thapsoides subsp.

visianianus, commonly known as the visianian ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
