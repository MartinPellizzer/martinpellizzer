Silene unilocularis

Silene unilocularis, commonly known as the single-chambered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Silene unilocularis is known for its short flowering period, usually occurring in late spring to early summer.
