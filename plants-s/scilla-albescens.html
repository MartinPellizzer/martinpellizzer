Scilla albescens

Scilla albescens, commonly known as the white scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Scilla albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
