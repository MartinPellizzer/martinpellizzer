Solidago neglecta var. simulata

Solidago neglecta var. simulata, commonly known as the plains goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry to moist soils in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have hairy surfaces.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
