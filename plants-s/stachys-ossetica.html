Stachys ossetica

Stachys ossetica, commonly known as Ossetian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinct net-like venation, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Stachys ossetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
