Solanum tuberosum f. barmacata

Solanum tuberosum f. barmacata, commonly known as the Barmacata potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato characterized by its unique tuber morphology.

It exhibits a distinctive elongated shape and often displays a range of skin colors, including purple, red, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other nightshade species. Its tubers are typically smaller than standard potatoes and have a higher starch content.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
