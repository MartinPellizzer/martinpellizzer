Schoepfia lucida

Schoepfia lucida, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open plains.

Schoepfia lucida is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
