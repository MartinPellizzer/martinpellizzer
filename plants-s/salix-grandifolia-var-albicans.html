Salix grandifolia var. albicans

Salix grandifolia var. albicans, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its striking white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elongated, serrated, and typically have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along streams.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
