Syzygium iteophyllum

Syzygium iteophyllum, commonly known as the silky mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Syzygium iteophyllum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
