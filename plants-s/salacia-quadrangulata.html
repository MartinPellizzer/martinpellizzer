Salacia quadrangulata

Salacia quadrangulata, commonly known as the quadrangular salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Salacia quadrangulata is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
