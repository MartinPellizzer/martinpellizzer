Saxifraga platyloba

Saxifraga platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Saxifraga platyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
