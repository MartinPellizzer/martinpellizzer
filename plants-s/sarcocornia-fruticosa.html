Sarcocornia fruticosa

Sarcocornia fruticosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern United States. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in wet, salty soils and is often found in marshes and estuaries.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
