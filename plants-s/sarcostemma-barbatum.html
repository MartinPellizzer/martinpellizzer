Sarcostemma barbatum

Sarcostemma barbatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to southern and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, toxic berries that are a source of concern in some regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution during handling.
