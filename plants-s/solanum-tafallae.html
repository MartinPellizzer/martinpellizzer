Solanum tafallae

Solanum tafallae, commonly known as the Tafalla nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Solanum tafallae is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its island habitat.
