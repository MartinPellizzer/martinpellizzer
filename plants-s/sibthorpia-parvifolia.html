Sibthorpia parvifolia

Sibthorpia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sibthorpia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Sibthorpia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
