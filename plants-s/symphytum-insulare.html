Symphytum insulare

Symphytum insulare, commonly known as the island comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of violet-blue flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
