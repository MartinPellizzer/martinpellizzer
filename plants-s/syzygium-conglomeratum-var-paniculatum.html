Syzygium conglomeratum var. paniculatum

Syzygium conglomeratum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the paniculate clove or fragrant clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are glossy, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a strong, clove-like scent when crushed.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in the global spice trade.
