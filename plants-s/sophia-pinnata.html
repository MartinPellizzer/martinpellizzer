Sophia pinnata

Sophia pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sophia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
