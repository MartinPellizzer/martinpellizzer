Scilla poluninii

Scilla poluninii, commonly known as the Caucasian scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette.

Scilla poluninii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
