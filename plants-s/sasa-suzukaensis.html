Sasa suzukaensis

Sasa suzukaensis, commonly known as the Suzuka bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and preventing erosion.

Sasa suzukaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in Japanese culture.
