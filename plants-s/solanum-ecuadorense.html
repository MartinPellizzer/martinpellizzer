Solanum ecuadorense

Solanum ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorean nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
