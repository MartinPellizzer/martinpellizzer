Syzygium handelii

Syzygium handelii, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium handelii thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
