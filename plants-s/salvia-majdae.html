Salvia majdae

Salvia majdae, commonly known as the Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia majdae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
