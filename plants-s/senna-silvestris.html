Senna silvestris

Senna silvestris, commonly known as wild senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna silvestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
