Sedum brachypetalum

Sedum brachypetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
