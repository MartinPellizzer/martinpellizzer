Schistostephium radicale

Schistostephium radicale, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head. The plant has a taproot system and is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun.

Schistostephium radicale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
