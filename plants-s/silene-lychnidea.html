Silene lychnidea

Silene lychnidea, commonly known as the moonflower of the Alps, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain environment.

Silene lychnidea is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
