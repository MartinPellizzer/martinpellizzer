Serjania meridionalis var. odonellii

Serjania meridionalis var. odonellii, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
