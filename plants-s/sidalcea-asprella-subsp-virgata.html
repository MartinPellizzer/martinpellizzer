Sidalcea asprella subsp. virgata

Sidalcea asprella subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
