Sonchus crispus

Sonchus crispus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, wavy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stems. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

It is considered a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
