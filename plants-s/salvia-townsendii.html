Salvia townsendii

Salvia townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia townsendii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
