Scirpus thyrsiflorus

Scirpus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the spike-flowered bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along rivers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, typically growing in dense stands.

Scirpus thyrsiflorus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland species.
