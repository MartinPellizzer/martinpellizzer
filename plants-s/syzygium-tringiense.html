Syzygium tringiense

Syzygium tringiense, commonly known as the Bornean apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium tringiense is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
