Syringodea pellucida

Syringodea pellucida, commonly known as the glassy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and small, fragrant flowers that often display a striking contrast of colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its unique appearance and rarity make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Syringodea pellucida is also noted for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient absorption from the soil.
