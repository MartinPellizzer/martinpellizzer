Senecio chassanicus

Senecio chassanicus, commonly known as the Chassan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grey-green, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Senecio chassanicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
