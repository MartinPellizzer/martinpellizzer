Spiesia triphylla

Spiesia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Spiesia triphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
