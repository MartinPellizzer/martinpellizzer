Seaforthia sylvestris

Seaforthia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest seaforthia, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Seaforthia sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate moisture and partial shade.
