Setaria clementii

Setaria clementii, commonly known as the clementii fingergrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Setaria clementii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open fields.
