Stelis inversa

Stelis inversa, commonly known as the inverted stelis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its flowers facing downward, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Stelis. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Stelis inversa thrives in humid environments with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
