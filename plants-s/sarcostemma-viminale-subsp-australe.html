Sarcostemma viminale subsp. australe

Sarcostemma viminale subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian yellow milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Australia. It features slender, woody stems that can twine around supports, often reaching lengths of several meters.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some drought tolerance.
