Smilax rotundifolia var. parviflora

Smilax rotundifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
