Senecio oppositicordius

Senecio oppositicordius, commonly known as the opposite-heart senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features distinctive, opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
