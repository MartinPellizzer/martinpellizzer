Salix alba subsp. vitellina

Salix alba subsp.

vitellina, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically yellow-green in color.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers early in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near watercourses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
