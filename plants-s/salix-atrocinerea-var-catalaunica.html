Salix atrocinerea var. catalaunica

Salix atrocinerea var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica black willow, is a variant of the black willow native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its dark, fissured bark and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
