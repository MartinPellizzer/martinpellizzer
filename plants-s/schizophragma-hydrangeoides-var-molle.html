Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. molle

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. molle, commonly known as the softleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, during the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
