Silene scouleri var. typica

Silene scouleri var. typica, commonly known as the Pacific silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance.

Silene scouleri var. typica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
