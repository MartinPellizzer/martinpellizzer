Stephania micrantha

Stephania micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese herb "Fang Ji," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody berries. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, containing various alkaloids with pharmacological properties.

Stephania micrantha is often found in moist, shaded environments in forests and mountainous regions.
