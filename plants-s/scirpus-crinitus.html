Scirpus crinitus

Scirpus crinitus, commonly known as the silky bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshes in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus crinitus plays an important role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
