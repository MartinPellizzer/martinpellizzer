Scleranthus singuliflorus

Scleranthus singuliflorus, commonly known as the single-flower sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of pink or white, which blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are adapted for insect pollination.

Scleranthus singuliflorus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
