Salix × besseri

Salix × besseri, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix laevigata and Salix discolor, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its slender, zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves are distinctive features.

It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, contributing to its ecological importance for pollinators.
