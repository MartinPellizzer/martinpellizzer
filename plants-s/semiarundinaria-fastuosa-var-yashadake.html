Semiarundinaria fastuosa var. yashadake

Semiarundinaria fastuosa var. yashadake, commonly known as the Yashadake bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color, growing in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded along the midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in both horticultural and traditional settings.
