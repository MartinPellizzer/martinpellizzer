Stipa subsessiliflora subsp. basiplumosa

Stipa subsessiliflora subsp.

basiplumosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for use in xeriscaping.
