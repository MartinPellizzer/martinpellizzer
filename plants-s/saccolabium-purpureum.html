Saccolabium purpureum

Saccolabium purpureum, commonly known as the purple thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
