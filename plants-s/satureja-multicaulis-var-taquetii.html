Satureja multicaulis var. taquetii

Satureja multicaulis var. taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing a compact growth habit. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and possibly antiseptic properties.
