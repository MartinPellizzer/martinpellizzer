Solanum ligustrinum

Solanum ligustrinum, commonly known as the nightshade or wild tomato, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, green, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It is native to South America and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
