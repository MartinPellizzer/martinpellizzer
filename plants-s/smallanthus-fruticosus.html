Smallanthus fruticosus

Smallanthus fruticosus, commonly known as the Andean false potato, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Smallanthus fruticosus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and agricultural applications.
