Sorbus domestica var. obtinui

Sorbus domestica var. obtinui, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.
