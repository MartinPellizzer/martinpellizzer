Scorzonera latifolia

Scorzonera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and tuberous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera latifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
