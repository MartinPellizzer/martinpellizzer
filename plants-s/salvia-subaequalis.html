Salvia subaequalis

Salvia subaequalis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
