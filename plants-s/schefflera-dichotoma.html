Schefflera dichotoma

Schefflera dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like foliage with large, compound leaves that radiate from a central stem. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

Schefflera dichotoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
