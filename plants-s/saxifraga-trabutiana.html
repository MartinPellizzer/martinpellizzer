Saxifraga trabutiana

Saxifraga trabutiana, commonly known as the Armenian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Saxifraga trabutiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
