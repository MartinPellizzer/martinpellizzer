Sarcochilus recurvus

Sarcochilus recurvus, commonly known as the Hooked Sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, pendulous flowers with bright yellow sepals and a dark red labellum. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist conditions and is known for its unique floral structure.

Sarcochilus recurvus is a rare and endangered orchid, requiring specific care to cultivate successfully.
