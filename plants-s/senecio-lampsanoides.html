Senecio lampsanoides

Senecio lampsanoides, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to arid environments.

Senecio lampsanoides is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
