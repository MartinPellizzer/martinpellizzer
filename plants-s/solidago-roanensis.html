Solidago roanensis

Solidago roanensis, commonly known as Roan mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago roanensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in native plant gardens.
