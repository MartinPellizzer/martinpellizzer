Sarothamnus albus

Sarothamnus albus, commonly known as white thorned broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has spiny stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Sarothamnus albus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
