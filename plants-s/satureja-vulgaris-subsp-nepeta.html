Satureja vulgaris subsp. nepeta

Satureja vulgaris subsp.

nepeta, commonly known as common wood sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and range in color from purple to pink.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
