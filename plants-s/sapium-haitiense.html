Sapium haitiense

Sapium haitiense, commonly known as Haitian sycamore, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, flaky bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional remedies.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
