Semecarpus oblongifolius

Semecarpus oblongifolius, commonly known as the Indian beech or long-leaved beech, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests.
