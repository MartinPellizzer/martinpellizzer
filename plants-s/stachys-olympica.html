Stachys olympica

Stachys olympica, commonly known as the Olympic hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Stachys olympica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
