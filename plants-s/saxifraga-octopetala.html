Saxifraga octopetala

Saxifraga octopetala, commonly known as the eight-petaled saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves and its clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas and tundra environments.

Saxifraga octopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
