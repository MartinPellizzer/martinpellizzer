Solanum pygmaeum var. hastatum

Solanum pygmaeum var. hastatum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, green berries, which turn red when mature. It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
