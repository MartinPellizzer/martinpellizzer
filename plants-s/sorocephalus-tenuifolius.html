Sorocephalus tenuifolius

Sorocephalus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorocephalus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, globular inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
