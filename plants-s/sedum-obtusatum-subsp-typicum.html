Sedum obtusatum subsp. typicum

Sedum obtusatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the typical stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
