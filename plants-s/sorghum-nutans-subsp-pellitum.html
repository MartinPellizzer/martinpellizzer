Sorghum nutans subsp. pellitum

Sorghum nutans subsp.

pellitum, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other sorghum varieties.

The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental horticulture and as a forage crop in certain regions.
