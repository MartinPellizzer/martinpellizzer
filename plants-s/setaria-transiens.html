Setaria transiens

Setaria transiens, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Setaria transiens is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
