Senebiera figariana

Senebiera figariana, commonly known as the Brazilian fig tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
