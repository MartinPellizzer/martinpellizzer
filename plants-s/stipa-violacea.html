Stipa violacea

Stipa violacea, commonly known as violet grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a violet hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Stipa violacea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
