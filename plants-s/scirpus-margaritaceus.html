Scirpus margaritaceus

Scirpus margaritaceus, commonly known as the pearl rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that resemble pearls, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help stabilize soil.

Scirpus margaritaceus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
