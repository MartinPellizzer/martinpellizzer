The plant produces large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Silene gigantea subsp.

rhodopea is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
