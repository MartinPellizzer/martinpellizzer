Schoenoplectus lacustris

Schoenoplectus lacustris, commonly known as the lake sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes.

Schoenoplectus lacustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
