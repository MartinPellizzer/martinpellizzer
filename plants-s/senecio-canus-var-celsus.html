Senecio canus var. celsus

Senecio canus var. celsus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its silvery, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
