Spodiopogon cuspidatus

Spodiopogon cuspidatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Spodiopogon cuspidatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and taxonomic research.
