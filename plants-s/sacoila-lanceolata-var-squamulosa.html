Sacoila lanceolata var. squamulosa

Sacoila lanceolata var. squamulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are lance-shaped and covered with fine, silvery scales, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces dark brown, three-angled berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
