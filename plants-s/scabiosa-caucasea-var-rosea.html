Scabiosa caucasea var. rosea

Scabiosa caucasea var. rosea, commonly known as the Caucasian pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, rocky conditions.
