Sorbus torminalis var. kissii

Sorbus torminalis var. kissii, commonly known as the Kishii whitebeam, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, fuzzy fruit clusters that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
