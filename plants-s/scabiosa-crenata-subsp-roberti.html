Scabiosa crenata subsp. roberti

Scabiosa crenata subsp.

roberti, commonly known as the purple scabious, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features upright stems covered with soft, woolly hairs, and its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in clustered inflorescences.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
