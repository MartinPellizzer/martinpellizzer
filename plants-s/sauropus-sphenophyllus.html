Sauropus sphenophyllus

Sauropus sphenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sander, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
