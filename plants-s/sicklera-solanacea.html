Sicklera solanacea

Sicklera solanacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
