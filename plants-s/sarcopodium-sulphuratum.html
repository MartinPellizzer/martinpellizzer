Sarcopodium sulphuratum

Sarcopodium sulphuratum, commonly known as the sulfur plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking yellowish-green to orange-colored inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sarcopodium sulphuratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
