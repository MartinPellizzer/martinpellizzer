Senecio lapeyrousei

Senecio lapeyrousei, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
