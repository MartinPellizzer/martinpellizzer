Stipa subsessiliflora

Stipa subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
