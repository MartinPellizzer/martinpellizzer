Santiria caudata

Santiria caudata, commonly known as wild senna, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a slender, twisted pod that contains several seeds.
