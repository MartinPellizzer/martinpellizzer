Spiraea salicifolia var. lanceolata

Spiraea salicifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
