Scrophularia variegata var. rupestris

Scrophularia variegata var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to rocky habitats in Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture, adapted to withstand arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in limestone or chalky environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
