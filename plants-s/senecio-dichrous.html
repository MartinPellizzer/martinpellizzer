Senecio dichrous

Senecio dichrous, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Senecio dichrous is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
