Stelis viridibrunnea

Stelis viridibrunnea, commonly known as the green-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in forest understories.

Stelis viridibrunnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
