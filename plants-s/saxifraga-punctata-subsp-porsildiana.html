Saxifraga punctata subsp. porsildiana

Saxifraga punctata subsp.

porsildiana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves with prominent white or pinkish markings.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes at high elevations.
