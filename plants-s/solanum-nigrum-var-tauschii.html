Solanum nigrum var. tauschii

Solanum nigrum var. tauschii, commonly known as the wild black nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces yellowish-white flowers.

The berries contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
