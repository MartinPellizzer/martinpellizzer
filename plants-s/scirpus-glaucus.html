Scirpus glaucus

Scirpus glaucus, commonly known as blue joint grass, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Scirpus glaucus thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
