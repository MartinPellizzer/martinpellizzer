Scleria schiedeana

Scleria schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces distinctive, three-angled seed heads.

Scleria schiedeana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
