Silene sobolifera

Silene sobolifera, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene sobolifera is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
