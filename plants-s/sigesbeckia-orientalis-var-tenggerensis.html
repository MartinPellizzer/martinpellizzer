Sigesbeckia orientalis var. tenggerensis

Sigesbeckia orientalis var. tenggerensis, commonly known as the Tengger cowplant, is a perennial herb native to the Tengger Desert region of China.

It features a robust stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh desert conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
