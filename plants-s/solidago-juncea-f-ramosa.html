Solidago juncea f. ramosa

Solidago juncea f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
