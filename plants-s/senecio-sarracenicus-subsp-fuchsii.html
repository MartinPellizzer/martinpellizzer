Senecio sarracenicus subsp. fuchsii

Senecio sarracenicus subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs's ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
