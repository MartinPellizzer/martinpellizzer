Syzygium acmenoides

Syzygium acmenoides, commonly known as the glossy apple or mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature and is edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

Syzygium acmenoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
