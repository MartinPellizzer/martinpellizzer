Saxifraga decora

Saxifraga decora, commonly known as the Decorative Saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in cooler climates.
