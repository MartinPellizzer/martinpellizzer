Selaginella intacta

Selaginella intacta, commonly known as the cliff brake, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen, spore-producing plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, scale-like leaves that form dense mats. It is characterized by its ability to survive in harsh environments due to its thick, waxy cuticle.

Selaginella intacta is often found in Mediterranean climates and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
