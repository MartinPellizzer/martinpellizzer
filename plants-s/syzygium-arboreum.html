Syzygium arboreum

Syzygium arboreum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

Syzygium arboreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
