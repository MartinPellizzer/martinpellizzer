Schizostachyum mampouw

Schizostachyum mampouw, commonly known as the Mampouw palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is also cultivated for horticultural purposes.
