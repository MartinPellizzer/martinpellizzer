Salisburia adiantifolia var. pendula

Salisburia adiantifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping salisburia, is a rare and unique plant native to the wetlands of southern China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that hang in cascading clusters, giving it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments. Its leaves are typically light green and have a feathery texture, while its stems are slender and arching.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
