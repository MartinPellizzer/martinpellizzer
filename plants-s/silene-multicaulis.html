Silene multicaulis

Silene multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene multicaulis is also known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude regions, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
