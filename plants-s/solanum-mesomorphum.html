Solanum mesomorphum

Solanum mesomorphum, commonly known as the mesomorphous nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by berries that change color as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Solanum mesomorphum is notable for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
