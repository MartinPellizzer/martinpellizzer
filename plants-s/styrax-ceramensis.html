Styrax ceramensis

Styrax ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram styrax, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The bark of Styrax ceramensis is thick and often has a rough, fissured texture, which is characteristic of many species in the Styracaceae family.
