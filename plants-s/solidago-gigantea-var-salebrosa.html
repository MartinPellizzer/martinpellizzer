Solidago gigantea var. salebrosa

Solidago gigantea var. salebrosa, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, bright yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its showy blooms appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a slightly hairy texture.
