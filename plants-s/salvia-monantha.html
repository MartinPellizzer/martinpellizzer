Salvia monantha

Salvia monantha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia monantha is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
