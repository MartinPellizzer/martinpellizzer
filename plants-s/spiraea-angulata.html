Spiraea angulata

Spiraea angulata, commonly known as the angular spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Spiraea angulata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
