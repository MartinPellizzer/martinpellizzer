Sidalcea diploscypha var. minor

Sidalcea diploscypha var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf checkerberry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pea-like blooms that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This variant is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
