Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. austrotexanus

Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. austrotexanus, commonly known as the Texas burr sage, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, grayish-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single, hard-seeded capsule. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
