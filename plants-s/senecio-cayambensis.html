Senecio cayambensis

Senecio cayambensis, commonly known as Cayambensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Senecio cayambensis produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators.
