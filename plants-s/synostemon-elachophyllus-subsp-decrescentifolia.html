Synostemon elachophyllus subsp. decrescentifolia

Synostemon elachophyllus subsp.

decrescentifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf synostemon, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The subspecies decrescentifolia is distinguished by its gradually decreasing leaf size along the stems, a unique morphological feature.
