Senecio argophylloides

Senecio argophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
