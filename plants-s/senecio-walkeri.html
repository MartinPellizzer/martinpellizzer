Senecio walkeri

Senecio walkeri, commonly known as the Walker's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio walkeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
