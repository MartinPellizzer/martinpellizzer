Saussurea melanotricha

Saussurea melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dark, hairy foliage. The plant produces yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying alpine flora.
