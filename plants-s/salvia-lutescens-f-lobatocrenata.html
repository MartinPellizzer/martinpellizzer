Salvia lutescens f. lobatocrenata

Salvia lutescens f. lobatocrenata, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
