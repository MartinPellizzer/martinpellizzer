Solanum costaricense

Solanum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum costaricense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
