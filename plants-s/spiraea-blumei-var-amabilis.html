Spiraea blumei var. amabilis

Spiraea blumei var. amabilis, commonly known as the sweet spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
