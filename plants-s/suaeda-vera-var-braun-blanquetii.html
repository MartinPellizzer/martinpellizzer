Suaeda vera var. braun-blanquetii

Suaeda vera var. braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. This plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, demonstrating remarkable tolerance to high salinity and drought. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Suaeda vera var. braun-blanquetii plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in harsh environments.
