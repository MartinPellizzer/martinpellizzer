Schoenus deustus

Schoenus deustus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Schoenus deustus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
