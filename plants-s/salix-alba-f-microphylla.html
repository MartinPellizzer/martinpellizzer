Salix alba f. microphylla

Salix alba f. microphylla, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a decorative cultivar of the white willow.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features or in wetland areas.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
