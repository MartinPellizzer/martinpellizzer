Salvia amarissima

Salvia amarissima, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist, hence its common name. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. Salvia amarissima thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
