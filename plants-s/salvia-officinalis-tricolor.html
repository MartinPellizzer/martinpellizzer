Salvia officinalis tricolor

Salvia officinalis tricolor, commonly known as garden sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking tricolor foliage with purple, white, and green hues, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.
