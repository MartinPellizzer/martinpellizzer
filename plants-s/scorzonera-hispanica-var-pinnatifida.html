Scorzonera hispanica var. pinnatifida

Scorzonera hispanica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible, tuberous roots. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central style.

Scorzonera hispanica var. pinnatifida is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
