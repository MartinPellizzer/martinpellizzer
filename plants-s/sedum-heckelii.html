Sedum heckelii

Sedum heckelii, commonly known as the Heckel's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum heckelii is also valued for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
