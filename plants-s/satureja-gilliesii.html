Satureja gilliesii

Satureja gilliesii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja gilliesii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Andean cultures.
