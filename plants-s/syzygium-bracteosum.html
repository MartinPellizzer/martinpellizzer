Syzygium bracteosum

Syzygium bracteosum, commonly known as the bractean syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bract-like leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

Syzygium bracteosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
