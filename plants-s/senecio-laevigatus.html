Senecio laevigatus

Senecio laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and basal leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
