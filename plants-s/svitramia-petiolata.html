Svitramia petiolata

Svitramia petiolata, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Svitramia petiolata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
