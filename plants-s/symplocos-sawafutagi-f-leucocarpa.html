Symplocos sawafutagi f. leucocarpa

Symplocos sawafutagi f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited evergreen holly, is a botanical variant of the Symplocos genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, white or pale yellow berries in the fall. Its fruit is particularly notable for its unique color and mild flavor, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and culinary contexts.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
