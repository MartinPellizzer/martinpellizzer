Succisa angustula

Succisa angustula, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Succisa angustula is known for its resilience and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ecological value.
