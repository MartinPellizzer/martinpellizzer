Saxifraga filifolia f. angustipetala

Saxifraga filifolia f. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, often arranged in a raceme, during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
