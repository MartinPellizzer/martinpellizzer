Smilax griffithii var. pallescens

Smilax griffithii var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale smilax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is a vine or shrub characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow when mature and are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
