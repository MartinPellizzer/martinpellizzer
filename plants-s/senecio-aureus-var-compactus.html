Senecio aureus var. compactus

Senecio aureus var. compactus, commonly known as the Compact Golden Groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its compact form and showy blooms, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
