Scaligeria hirtula

Scaligeria hirtula, commonly known as the hairy scalerie, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its hairy texture, from which it gets its common name, is a distinguishing feature.

Scaligeria hirtula is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
