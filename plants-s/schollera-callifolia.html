Schollera callifolia

Schollera callifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in dry, coastal environments. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Schollera callifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
