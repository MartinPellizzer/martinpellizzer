Semecarpus mangifera

Semecarpus mangifera, commonly known as the Indian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, spiny fruits. The plant produces achenes enclosed in a woody, bumpy capsule, which ripens to a yellowish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
