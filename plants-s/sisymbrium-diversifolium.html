Sisymbrium diversifolium

Sisymbrium diversifolium, commonly known as the variable shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, triangular seed pods that resemble a shepherd's purse, hence its common name. It typically grows in dry, open areas and has small, alternate leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its species name "diversifolium.".

The flowers are usually yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
