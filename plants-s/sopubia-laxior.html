Sopubia laxior

Sopubia laxior, commonly known as the woolly spongeplant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Sopubia laxior is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
