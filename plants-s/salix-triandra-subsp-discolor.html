Salix triandra subsp. discolor

Salix triandra subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

The plant is notable for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
