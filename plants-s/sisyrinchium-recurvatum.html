Sisyrinchium recurvatum

Sisyrinchium recurvatum, commonly known as the curly blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically 1 to 2 inches in diameter, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

Sisyrinchium recurvatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
