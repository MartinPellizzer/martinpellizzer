Stenoseris leptantha

Stenoseris leptantha, commonly known as the slender-stemmed serissa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, often blooming in late summer.

Stenoseris leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
