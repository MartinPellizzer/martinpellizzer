Stachyurus praecox var. lancifolius

Stachyurus praecox var. lancifolius, commonly known as the lancolate stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features distinctive, finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its seasonal interest and adaptability in garden settings.
