Syzygium pterophorum

Syzygium pterophorum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

Syzygium pterophorum is often found in lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
