Smilax herbacea var. heterophylla

Smilax herbacea var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variegated smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often variegated leaves that can range from green to yellow and white. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
