Solanum indicum subsp. grandifrons

Solanum indicum subsp.

grandifrons, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn from green to red or yellow when mature, containing numerous small seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
