Syzygium archboldianum

Syzygium archboldianum, commonly known as the Archbold's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

Syzygium archboldianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
