Salvia viridis var. violacea

Salvia viridis var. violacea, commonly known as violetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
