Sobralia decora var. aerata

Sobralia decora var. aerata, commonly known as the aerated sobralia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a visually appealing structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
