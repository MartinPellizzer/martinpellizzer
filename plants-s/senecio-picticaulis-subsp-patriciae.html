Senecio picticaulis subsp. patriciae

Senecio picticaulis subsp.

patriciae, commonly known as the Patricia's iceplant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which store water and help it survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
