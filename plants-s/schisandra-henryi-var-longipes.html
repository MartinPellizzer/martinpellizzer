Schisandra henryi var. longipes

Schisandra henryi var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed five-flavor vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces red berries that mature in the autumn, which are rich in aromatic compounds and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
