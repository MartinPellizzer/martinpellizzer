Sinapis tuberosa

Sinapis tuberosa, commonly known as the turnip mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features a thick, fleshy root that is typically white or yellow in color, and its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs. Sinapis tuberosa produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its edible root and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
