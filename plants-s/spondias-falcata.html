Spondias falcata

Spondias falcata, commonly known as the sickle mango, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
