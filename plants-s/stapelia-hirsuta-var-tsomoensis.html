Stapelia hirsuta var. tsomoensis

Stapelia hirsuta var. tsomoensis, commonly known as the Tsomo's hairy stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces striking, waxy flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are small, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
