Shawia chrysophylla

Shawia chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Shawia chrysophylla has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers feature intricate patterns of white and yellow.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
