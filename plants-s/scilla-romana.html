Scilla romana

Scilla romana, commonly known as Roman squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust bulb that stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scilla romana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
