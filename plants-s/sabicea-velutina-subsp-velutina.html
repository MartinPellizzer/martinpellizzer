Sabicea velutina subsp. velutina

Sabicea velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with serrated margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
