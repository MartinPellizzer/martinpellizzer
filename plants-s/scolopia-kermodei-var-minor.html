Scolopia kermodei var. minor

Scolopia kermodei var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf scolopia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale green, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain several seeds.
