Symplocos japonica

Symplocos japonica, commonly known as Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Symplocos japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
