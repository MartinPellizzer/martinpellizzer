Sabicea diversifolia

Sabicea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable sabicea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
