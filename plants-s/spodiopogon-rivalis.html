Spodiopogon rivalis

Spodiopogon rivalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in wet meadows, along stream banks, and in other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
