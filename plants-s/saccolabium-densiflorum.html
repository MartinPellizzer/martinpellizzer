Saccolabium densiflorum

Saccolabium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower saccolabium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Saccolabium densiflorum is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
