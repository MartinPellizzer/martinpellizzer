Salix myrtilloides var. mandshurica

Salix myrtilloides var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian willow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
