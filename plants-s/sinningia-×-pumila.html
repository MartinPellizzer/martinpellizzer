Sinningia × pumila

Sinningia × pumila, commonly known as the silver mounding toad lily, is a hybrid plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive silver or pale green hue on the petals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia × pumila is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
