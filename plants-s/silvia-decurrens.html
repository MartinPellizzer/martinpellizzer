Silvia decurrens

Silvia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping silvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features spreading, mat-forming growth with slender, wiry stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silvia decurrens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
