Shorea javanica

Shorea javanica, commonly known as the Javan rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins.

Shorea javanica produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
