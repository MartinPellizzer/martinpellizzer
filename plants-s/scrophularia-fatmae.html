Scrophularia fatmae

Scrophularia fatmae, commonly known as Fatma's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
