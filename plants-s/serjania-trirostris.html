Serjania trirostris

Serjania trirostris, commonly known as the three-beaked serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Serjania trirostris is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
