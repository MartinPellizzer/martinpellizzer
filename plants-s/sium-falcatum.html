Sium falcatum

Sium falcatum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and stream banks in North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, carrot-like roots and heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Sium falcatum is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
