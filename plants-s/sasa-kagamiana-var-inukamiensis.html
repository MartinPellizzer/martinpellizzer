Sasa kagamiana var. inukamiensis

Sasa kagamiana var. inukamiensis, commonly known as the Inukamiensis bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
