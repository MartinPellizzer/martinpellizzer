Solanum concinnum

Solanum concinnum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum concinnum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
