Sasaella epitricha

Sasaella epitricha, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Sasaella epitricha is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
