Sedum cooperi

Sedum cooperi, commonly known as the Blue Spruce Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, Sedum cooperi is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9.
