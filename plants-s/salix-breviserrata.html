Salix breviserrata

Salix breviserrata, commonly known as the short-toothed willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with short, blunt teeth along the edges.

Salix breviserrata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
