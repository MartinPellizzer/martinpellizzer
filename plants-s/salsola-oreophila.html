Salsola oreophila

Salsola oreophila, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or erect, depending on environmental conditions.

Salsola oreophila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
