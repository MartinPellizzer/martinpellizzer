Scleropyrum wallichianum

Scleropyrum wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem with persistent bark and a unique ability to retain moisture in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
