Silene nutans var. dunensis

Silene nutans var. dunensis, commonly known as the Duneland catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
