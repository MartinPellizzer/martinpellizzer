Stanhopea oculata var. minor

Stanhopea oculata var. minor, commonly known as the miniature stanhopea, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is distinguished by its compact size and vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting the plant's growth and storing water.

The flowers feature intricate patterns and are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.
