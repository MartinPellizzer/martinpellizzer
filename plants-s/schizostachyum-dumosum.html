Schizostachyum dumosum

Schizostachyum dumosum, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that form dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight sheen. Schizostachyum dumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and structural qualities.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
