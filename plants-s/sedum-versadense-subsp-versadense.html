Sedum versadense subsp. versadense

Sedum versadense subsp.

versadense, commonly known as the silver rose sedum, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and easy to care for. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
