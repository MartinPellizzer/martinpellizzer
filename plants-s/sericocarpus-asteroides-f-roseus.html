Sericocarpus asteroides f. roseus

Sericocarpus asteroides f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered silverweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of silvery, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its unique sericocarpous fruits, which are dry, winged seeds that detach easily. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in gardens.
