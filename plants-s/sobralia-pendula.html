Sobralia pendula

Sobralia pendula, commonly known as the hanging sobralia, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used for propagation. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
