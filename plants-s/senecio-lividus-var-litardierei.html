Senecio lividus var. litardierei

Senecio lividus var. litardierei, commonly known as the Litardiere's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
