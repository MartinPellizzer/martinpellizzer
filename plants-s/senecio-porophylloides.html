Senecio porophylloides

Senecio porophylloides, commonly known as the yellow groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio porophylloides is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
