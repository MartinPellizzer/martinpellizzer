Selaginella gardneri

Selaginella gardneri, commonly known as the garden spider moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense, carpet-like mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. It is characterized by its ability to survive in arid environments due to its efficient water retention mechanisms.

Selaginella gardneri is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
