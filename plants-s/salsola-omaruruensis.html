Salsola omaruruensis

Salsola omaruruensis, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It has small, spineless leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Salsola omaruruensis is a halophyte, meaning it can tolerate high levels of salt in its environment.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
