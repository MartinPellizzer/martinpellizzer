Stillingia prostrata

Stillingia prostrata, commonly known as creeping stillingia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its prostrate stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Stillingia prostrata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
