Salvia chrysadenia

Salvia chrysadenia, commonly known as the golden sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia chrysadenia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
