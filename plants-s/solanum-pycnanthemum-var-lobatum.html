Solanum pycnanthemum var. lobatum

Solanum pycnanthemum var. lobatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers range in color from purple to pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
