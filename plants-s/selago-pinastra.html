Selago pinastra

Selago pinastra, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Selago pinastra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
