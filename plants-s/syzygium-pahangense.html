Syzygium pahangense

Syzygium pahangense, commonly known as the Pahang tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium pahangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
