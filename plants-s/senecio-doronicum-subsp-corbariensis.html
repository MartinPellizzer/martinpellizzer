Senecio doronicum subsp. corbariensis

Senecio doronicum subsp.

corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbariense Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
