Solanum saruwagedense

Solanum saruwagedense, commonly known as the Saruwagede nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the lowland forests of eastern Indonesia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum saruwagedense is often found in shaded, humid environments and is adapted to thrive in the understory of tropical rainforests.
