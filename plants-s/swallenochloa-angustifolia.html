Swallenochloa angustifolia

Swallenochloa angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Swallenochloa angustifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
