Syzygium owariense

Syzygium owariense, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Otaru cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and attractive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Syzygium owariense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
