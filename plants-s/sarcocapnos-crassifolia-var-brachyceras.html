Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. brachyceras

Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in rock gardens.
