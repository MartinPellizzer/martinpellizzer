Sideritis grandiflora

Sideritis grandiflora, commonly known as largeflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, mint-like scent and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Sideritis grandiflora is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
