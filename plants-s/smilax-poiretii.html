Smilax poiretii

Smilax poiretii, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Smilax poiretii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
