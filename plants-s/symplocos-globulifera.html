Symplocos globulifera

Symplocos globulifera, commonly known as the globe-leaf waterthrift, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, globe-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
