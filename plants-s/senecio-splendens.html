Senecio splendens

Senecio splendens, commonly known as the splendour groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio splendens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
