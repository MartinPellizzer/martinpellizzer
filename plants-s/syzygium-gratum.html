Syzygium gratum

Syzygium gratum, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Syzygium gratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its fragrant blooms and attractive foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
