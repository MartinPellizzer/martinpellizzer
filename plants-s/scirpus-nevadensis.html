Scirpus nevadensis

Scirpus nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in areas with standing water.

Scirpus nevadensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
