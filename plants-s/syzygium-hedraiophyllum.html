Syzygium hedraiophyllum

Syzygium hedraiophyllum, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine systems.
