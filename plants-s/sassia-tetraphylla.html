Sassia tetraphylla

Sassia tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, often hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with four petals, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Sassia tetraphylla thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
