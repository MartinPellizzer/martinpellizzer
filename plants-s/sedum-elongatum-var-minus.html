Sedum elongatum var. minus

Sedum elongatum var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
