Schoenoplectus pungens subsp. longispicatus

Schoenoplectus pungens subsp.

longispicatus, commonly known as the long-spined bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

This subspecies is adapted to wetland environments and plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
