Spiraea salicifolia var. grandiflora

Spiraea salicifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that cascade with clusters of white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
