Sisyrinchium micranthum f. purpureum

Sisyrinchium micranthum f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers with blue or purple petals and a distinctive yellow center, which are typically clustered in racemes. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens for its vibrant color and easy maintenance.
