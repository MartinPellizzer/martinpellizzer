Scrophularia anagae

Scrophularia anagae, commonly known as the Mediterranean figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring. Scrophularia anagae thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
