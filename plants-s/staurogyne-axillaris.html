Staurogyne axillaris

Staurogyne axillaris, commonly known as the creeping waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.

Staurogyne axillaris plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
