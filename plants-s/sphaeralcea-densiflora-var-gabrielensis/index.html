Sphaeralcea Densiflora Var. Gabrielensis

Sphaeralcea densiflora var. gabrielensis is a rare and striking plant native to the southwestern United States. This variant of the common globemallow is known for its dense clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant is named after Gabriel, a region in New Mexico, highlighting its local significance. Due to its limited range and ecological importance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
