Scutellaria microviolacea

Scutellaria microviolacea, commonly known as the small viola scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in whorls along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
