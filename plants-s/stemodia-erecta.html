Stemodia erecta

Stemodia erecta, commonly known as the upright stemodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
