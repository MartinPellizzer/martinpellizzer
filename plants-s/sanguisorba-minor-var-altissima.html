Sanguisorba minor var. altissima

Sanguisorba minor var. altissima, commonly known as the tall narrowleaf burnet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with small, clustered, reddish-brown flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Sanguisorba minor var. altissima is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
