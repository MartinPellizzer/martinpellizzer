Spiraea japonica var. angulata

Spiraea japonica var. angulata, commonly known as Japanese spirea or angular spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, angular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal foliage changes.
