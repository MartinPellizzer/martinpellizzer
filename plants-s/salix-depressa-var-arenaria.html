Salix depressa var. arenaria

Salix depressa var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing form, with multiple stems that often spread along the ground. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This hardy species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
