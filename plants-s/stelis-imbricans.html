Stelis imbricans

Stelis imbricans, commonly known as the overlapping orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive overlapping arrangement of petals.

Stelis imbricans prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
