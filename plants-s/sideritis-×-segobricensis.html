Sideritis × segobricensis

Sideritis × segobricensis, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, pink to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, typical of the genus Sideritis.

It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
