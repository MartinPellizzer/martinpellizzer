Syzygium hebephyllum

Syzygium hebephyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
