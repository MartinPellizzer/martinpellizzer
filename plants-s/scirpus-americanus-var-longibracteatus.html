Scirpus americanus var. longibracteatus

Scirpus americanus var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
