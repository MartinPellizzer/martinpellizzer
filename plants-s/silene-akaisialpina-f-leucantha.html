Silene akaisialpina f. leucantha

Silene akaisialpina f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the European Alps.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals that are slightly notched at the edges. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
