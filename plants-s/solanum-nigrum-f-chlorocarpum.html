Solanum nigrum f. chlorocarpum

Solanum nigrum f. chlorocarpum, commonly known as the green-fruited nightshade, is a botanical variant of the common nightshade.

It is characterized by its distinctive green berries, which contrast with the typical black fruits of the species. This form is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its foliage is typically dark green with a hairy texture, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer.
