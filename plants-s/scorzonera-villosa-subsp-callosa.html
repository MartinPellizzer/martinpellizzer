Scorzonera villosa subsp. callosa

Scorzonera villosa subsp.

callosa, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, stout stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is robust, and the roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera villosa subsp.

callosa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
