Stachys galanderi

Stachys galanderi, commonly known as Galander's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and aromatic scent are notable botanical features.
