Seriphidium czukavinae

Seriphidium czukavinae, commonly known as the Czukavina's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Seriphidium czukavinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
