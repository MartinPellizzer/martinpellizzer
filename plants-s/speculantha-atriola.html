Speculantha atriola

Speculantha atriola, commonly known as the black-flowered speculantha, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark violet to almost black petals, which contrast sharply with its pale green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Speculantha atriola is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
