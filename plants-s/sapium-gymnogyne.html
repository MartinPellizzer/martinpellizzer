Sapium gymnogyne

Sapium gymnogyne, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
