Saxifraga × drudei

Saxifraga × drudei, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its compact form and attractive foliage.
