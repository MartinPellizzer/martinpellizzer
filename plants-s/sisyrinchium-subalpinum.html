Sisyrinchium subalpinum

Sisyrinchium subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States and South America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium subalpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
