Sageretia trinervia

Sageretia trinervia, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and have three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are highly ornamental. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Sageretia trinervia is valued for its aromatic foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
