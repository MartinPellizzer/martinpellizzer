Statice denticulata

Statice denticulata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, linear leaves are adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Statice denticulata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
