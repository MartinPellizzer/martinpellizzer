Seriphidium barrelieri

Seriphidium barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's tansy, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and feathery leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Its resilience to drought makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
