Salvia karelinii

Salvia karelinii, commonly known as the Karakul sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Salvia karelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
