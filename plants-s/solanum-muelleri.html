Solanum muelleri

Solanum muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of southern Australia. It produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

Solanum muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
