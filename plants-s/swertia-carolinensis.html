Swertia carolinensis

Swertia carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various ailments.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
