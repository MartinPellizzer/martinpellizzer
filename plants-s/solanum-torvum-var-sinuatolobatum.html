Solanum torvum var. sinuatolobatum

Solanum torvum var. sinuatolobatum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and alternate, ovate to cordate leaves that have wavy or sinuate margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats and is often found in agricultural areas where it can become a weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its glandular hairs and toxic alkaloids.
