Smilax havanensis f. inermis

Smilax havanensis f. inermis, commonly known as the Cuban smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems and the presence of sharp spines along its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a waxy coating.

This plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth in forests.
