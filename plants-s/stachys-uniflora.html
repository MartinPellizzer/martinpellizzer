Stachys uniflora

Stachys uniflora, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
