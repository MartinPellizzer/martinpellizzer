Sida scabra

Sida scabra, commonly known as rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it often grows in disturbed soils.

Sida scabra is sometimes considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
