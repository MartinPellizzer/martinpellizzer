Sparaxis grandiflora var. lineata

Sparaxis grandiflora var. lineata, commonly known as the stripe daffodil, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The varietal name "lineata" refers to the distinctive, striped pattern on its petals. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
