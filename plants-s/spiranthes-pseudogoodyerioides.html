Spiranthes pseudogoodyerioides

Spiranthes pseudogoodyerioides, commonly known as the Florida lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological importance as a pollinator attractant and its role in wetland ecosystems.
