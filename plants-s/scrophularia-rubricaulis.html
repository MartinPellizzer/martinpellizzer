Scrophularia rubricaulis

Scrophularia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas. The plant features erect, slender stems that are reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scrophularia rubricaulis is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
