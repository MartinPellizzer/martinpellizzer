Silene geminiflora

Silene geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Silene geminiflora is known for its unique floral structure, with two rows of flowers on each stem, giving it its common name.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
