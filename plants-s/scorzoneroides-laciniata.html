Scorzoneroides laciniata

Scorzoneroides laciniata, commonly known as the shredded rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and often appear shredded, giving it its common name.
