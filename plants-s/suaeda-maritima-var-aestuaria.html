Suaeda maritima var. aestuaria

Suaeda maritima var. aestuaria, commonly known as saltmarsh sandmat, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, particularly in salt marshes and estuaries, where it tolerates high salinity levels. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid and salty conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Suaeda maritima var. aestuaria plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
