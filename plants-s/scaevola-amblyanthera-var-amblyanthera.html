Scaevola amblyanthera var. amblyanthera

Scaevola amblyanthera var. amblyanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, fan-shaped clusters of blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are typically erect and covered in fine pubescence. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
