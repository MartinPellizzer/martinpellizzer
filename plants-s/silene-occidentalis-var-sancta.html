Silene occidentalis var. sancta

Silene occidentalis var. sancta, commonly known as the sacred evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
