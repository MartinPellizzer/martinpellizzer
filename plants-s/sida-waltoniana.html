Sida waltoniana

Sida waltoniana, commonly known as the golden sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a pale green color.

Sida waltoniana is known for its adaptability to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.
