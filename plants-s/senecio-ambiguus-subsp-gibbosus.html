Senecio ambiguus subsp. gibbosus

Senecio ambiguus subsp.

gibbosus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early fall, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water availability.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
