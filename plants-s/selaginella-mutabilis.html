Selaginella mutabilis

Selaginella mutabilis, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a creeping stem with whorls of needle-like leaves, which are often a striking green or reddish color. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper part of the stem and contain spores.

Selaginella mutabilis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
