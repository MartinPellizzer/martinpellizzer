Scutellaria oaxacana

Scutellaria oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
