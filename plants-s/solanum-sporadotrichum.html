Solanum sporadotrichum

Solanum sporadotrichum, commonly known as the red-seeded nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its berries, which are typically red when mature, contain toxic compounds and are not suitable for human consumption.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
