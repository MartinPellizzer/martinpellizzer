Senecio pachycarpus

Senecio pachycarpus, commonly known as the Cape rosebay, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio pachycarpus is known for its hardy nature and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
