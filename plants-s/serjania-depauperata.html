Serjania depauperata

Serjania depauperata, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Serjania depauperata is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
