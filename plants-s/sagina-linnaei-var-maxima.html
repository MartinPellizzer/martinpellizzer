Sagina linnaei var. maxima

Sagina linnaei var. maxima, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in coastal areas, dunes, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover.
