Stephania Japonica

Stephania japonica is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia known for its distinctive blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. This species belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties. Its roots contain alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Despite its beauty, it can be toxic if ingested, making it both fascinating and cautionary.
