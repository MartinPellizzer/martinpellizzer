Stipa speciosa f. atuelensis

Stipa speciosa f. atuelensis, commonly known as the Atuel ornamental grass, is a botanical variant of the silky wheatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of Argentina, particularly in the Province of San Juan. The plant features slender, silver-green blades with a feathery appearance, and it produces delicate, airy inflorescences during the growing season. It is adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its graceful form and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
