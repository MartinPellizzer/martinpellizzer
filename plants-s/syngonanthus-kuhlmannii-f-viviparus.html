Syngonanthus kuhlmannii f. viviparus

Syngonanthus kuhlmannii f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous mistletoe, is a unique plant with specialized botanical features.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, drawing nutrients and water through its haustoria. The plant exhibits vivipary, a reproductive trait where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant, forming a small shoot that later detaches. This characteristic allows for efficient propagation and establishment on new hosts.

Its morphology includes small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or yellowish flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds.
