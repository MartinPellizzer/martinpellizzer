Scirpus kielmeyeri

Scirpus kielmeyeri, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Scirpus kielmeyeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species in wetland ecosystems.
