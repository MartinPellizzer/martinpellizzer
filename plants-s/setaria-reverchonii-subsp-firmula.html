Setaria reverchonii subsp. firmula

Setaria reverchonii subsp.

firmula, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Poaceae family, adapted to arid environments.
