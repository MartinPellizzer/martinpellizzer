Syzygium sambiranense

Syzygium sambiranense, commonly known as the Sambiranense apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to Madagascar. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
