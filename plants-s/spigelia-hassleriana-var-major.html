Spigelia hassleriana var. major

Spigelia hassleriana var. major, commonly known as the redroot dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
