Sarcochilus argochilus

Sarcochilus argochilus, commonly known as the golden helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, erect leaf and produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive helmet-shaped lip. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rainforest understories. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
