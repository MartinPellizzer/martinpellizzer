Sagina apetala var. depressa

Sagina apetala var. depressa, commonly known as the cushion sagina, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving it a cushion-like appearance. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and typically appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

It thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
