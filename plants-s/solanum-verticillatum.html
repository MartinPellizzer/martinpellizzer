Solanum verticillatum

Solanum verticillatum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or purple, which bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are prostrate and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
