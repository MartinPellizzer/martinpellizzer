Stapelia verrucosa var. pulchra

Stapelia verrucosa var. pulchra, commonly known as the "Coral Stapelia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are often yellow or orange with a distinctive, waxy texture. These flowers are pollinated by flies and emit a strong, unpleasant odor to attract them.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
