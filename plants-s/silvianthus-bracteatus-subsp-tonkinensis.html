Silvianthus bracteatus subsp. tonkinensis

Silvianthus bracteatus subsp.

tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin silvianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
