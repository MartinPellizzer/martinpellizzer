Silene gallica var. cantabrica

Silene gallica var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
