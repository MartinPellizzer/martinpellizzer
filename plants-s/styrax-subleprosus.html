Styrax subleprosus

Styrax subleprosus, commonly known as the leprose styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, woody capsules that contain glossy, black seeds.

Styrax subleprosus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
