Sempervivum cantabricum subsp. guadarramense

Sempervivum cantabricum subsp.

guadarramense, commonly known as the Guadarramense houseleek, is a succulent plant native to parts of Spain and Portugal. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
