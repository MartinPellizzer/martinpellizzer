Spergularia macrotheca var. leucantha

Spergularia macrotheca var. leucantha, commonly known as white sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal areas of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in dunes or coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other varieties of Spergularia macrotheca.
