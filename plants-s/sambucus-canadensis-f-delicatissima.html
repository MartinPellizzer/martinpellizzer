Sambucus canadensis f. delicatissima

Sambucus canadensis f. delicatissima, commonly known as the Canada elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 3 to 6 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with 5 to 7 ovate leaflets, and the berries are edible when fully ripe.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
