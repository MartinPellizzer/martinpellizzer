Scilla collina

Scilla collina, commonly known as the sea scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Scilla collina is often found in maritime climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
