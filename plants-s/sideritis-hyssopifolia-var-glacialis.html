Sideritis hyssopifolia var. glacialis

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. glacialis, commonly known as alpine heather, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, narrow leaves arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.
