Senegalia kelloggiana

Senegalia kelloggiana, commonly known as the Kellogg's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and is well adapted to hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in small heads, and its leaves are typically small and feathery. Senegalia kelloggiana has small, hard, angular pods that contain seeds, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
