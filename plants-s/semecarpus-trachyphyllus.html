Semecarpus trachyphyllus

Semecarpus trachyphyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaved satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Syllepteae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The species is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and carving, and it also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
