Symphytum ibiricum

Symphytum ibiricum, commonly known as the Iberian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and tall spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant has a thick, fleshy root system that stores nutrients, contributing to its hardy nature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its healing capabilities.
