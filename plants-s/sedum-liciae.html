Sedum liciae

Sedum liciae, commonly known as the stonecrop or liveforever, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple during the summer months. Sedum liciae is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in well-drained soils, making it a hardy and low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
