Silphium asteriscus var. simpsonii

Silphium asteriscus var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's compassplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Simpson's compassplant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
