Senecio chubutensis

Senecio chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the dry, arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio chubutensis is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
