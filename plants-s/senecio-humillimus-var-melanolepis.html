Senecio humillimus var. melanolepis

Senecio humillimus var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking dark foliage and delicate floral display.
