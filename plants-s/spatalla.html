Spatalla

Spatalla, commonly known as the spider plant, is a popular ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible for humans but toxic to pets. Spatalla is easy to care for, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardens and hanging baskets.

It is also known for its ability to purify the air and thrive in a variety of light conditions.
