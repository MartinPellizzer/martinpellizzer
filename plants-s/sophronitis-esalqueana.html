Sophronitis esalqueana

Sophronitis esalqueana, commonly known as the Esalquean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in the spring, with flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
