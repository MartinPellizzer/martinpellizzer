Silene italica var. amurensis

Silene italica var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur campion, is a flowering plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northeastern China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as forest edges and stream banks. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
