Symphytum euboicum

Symphytum euboicum, commonly known as Euboian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and tall spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant is known for its thick, fleshy roots that store nutrients, contributing to its robust growth. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
