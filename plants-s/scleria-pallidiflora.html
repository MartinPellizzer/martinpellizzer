Scleria pallidiflora

Scleria pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bulbous sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a short rhizomatous stem and produces bulb-like structures that aid in propagation.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
