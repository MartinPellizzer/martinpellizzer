Setaria laeta

Setaria laeta, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
