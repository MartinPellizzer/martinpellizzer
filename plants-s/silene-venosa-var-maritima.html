Silene venosa var. maritima

Silene venosa var. maritima, commonly known as sea bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Silene venosa var. maritima plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
