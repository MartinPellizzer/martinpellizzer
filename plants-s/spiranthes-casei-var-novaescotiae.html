Spiranthes casei var. novaescotiae

Spiranthes casei var. novaescotiae, commonly known as the Nova Scotia lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as bogs and cedar swamps. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for nutrient uptake.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
