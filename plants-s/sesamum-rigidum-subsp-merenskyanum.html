Sesamum rigidum subsp. merenskyanum

Sesamum rigidum subsp.

merenskyanum, commonly known as the stiff sesame or Merensky's sesame, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on long peduncles. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule fruits, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
