Senecio alsophilus

Senecio alsophilus, commonly known as the Alsop's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio alsophilus is often found in coastal and inland regions, displaying adaptability to various environmental conditions.
