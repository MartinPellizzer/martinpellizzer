Stachys major

Stachys major, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring square stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Stachys major is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
