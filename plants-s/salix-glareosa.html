Salix glareosa

Salix glareosa, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and finely toothed leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in alpine environments.

Salix glareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
