Senecio sinuatidens

Senecio sinuatidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio sinuatidens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
