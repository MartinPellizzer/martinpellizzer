Sophora microphylla subsp. macnabiana

Sophora microphylla subsp.

macnabiana, commonly known as the MacNab's small-leaf Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in forest margins and open woodlands.
