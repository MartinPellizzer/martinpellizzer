Silene laciniata var. latifolia

Silene laciniata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
