Scutellaria salvia

Scutellaria salvia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Scutellaria salvia is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
