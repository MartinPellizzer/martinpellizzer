Smilax darrisii

Smilax darrisii, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and hammocks.

Smilax darrisii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
