Stachys schweinitzii

Stachys schweinitzii, commonly known as Schweinitz's betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys schweinitzii is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
