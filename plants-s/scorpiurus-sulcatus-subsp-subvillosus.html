Scorpiurus sulcatus subsp. subvillosus

Scorpiurus sulcatus subsp.

subvillosus, commonly known as the hairy scorpionvetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which provides a distinctive texture and appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various insect species.
