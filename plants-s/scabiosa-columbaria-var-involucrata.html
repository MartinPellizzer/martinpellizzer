Scabiosa columbaria var. involucrata

Scabiosa columbaria var. involucrata, commonly known as the snowball scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that resemble small snowballs, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
