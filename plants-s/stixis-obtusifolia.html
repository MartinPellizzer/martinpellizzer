Stixis obtusifolia

Stixis obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stixis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its robust, branching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Stixis obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
