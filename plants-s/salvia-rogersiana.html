Salvia rogersiana

Salvia rogersiana, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its widespread use in landscaping.
