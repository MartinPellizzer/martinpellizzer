Sloanea garckeana

Sloanea garckeana, commonly known as the yellow sloanea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Sloanea garckeana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
