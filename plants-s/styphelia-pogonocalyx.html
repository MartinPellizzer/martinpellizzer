Styphelia pogonocalyx

Styphelia pogonocalyx, commonly known as the pink snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round coverage.

Styphelia pogonocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
