Spiraea obovata

Spiraea obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that droop gracefully, creating a weeping appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

Spiraea obovata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
