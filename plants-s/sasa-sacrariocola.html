Sasa sacrariocola

Sasa sacrariocola, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa sacrariocola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
