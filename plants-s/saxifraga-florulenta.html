Saxifraga florulenta

Saxifraga florulenta, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga florulenta is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
