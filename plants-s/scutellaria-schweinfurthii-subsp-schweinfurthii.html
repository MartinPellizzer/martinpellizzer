Scutellaria schweinfurthii subsp. schweinfurthii

Scutellaria schweinfurthii subsp.

schweinfurthii, commonly known as Schweinfurth's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native garden landscaping.
