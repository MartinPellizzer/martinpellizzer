Seriphidium caerulescens var. sipontinum

Seriphidium caerulescens var. sipontinum, commonly known as the Sipontine tansy.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to southern Europe. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
