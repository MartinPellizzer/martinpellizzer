Shorea macrobalanos

Shorea macrobalanos, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a glossy surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
