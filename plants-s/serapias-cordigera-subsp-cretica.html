Serapias cordigera subsp. cretica

Serapias cordigera subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan heartleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Crete in Greece. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and delicate, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
