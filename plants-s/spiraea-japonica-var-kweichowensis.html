Spiraea japonica var. kweichowensis

Spiraea japonica var. kweichowensis, commonly known as the Kweichow weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fine, dark green foliage that turns red in the autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
