Sapium acetosella var. elatius

Sapium acetosella var. elatius, commonly known as the tall Chinese lantern plant, is a variant of the Chinese lantern plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its unique appearance and seasonal color display make it a popular choice for autumn gardens.
