Scirpus atrovirens f. angustispicatus

Scirpus atrovirens f. angustispicatus, commonly known as narrow-spike bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
