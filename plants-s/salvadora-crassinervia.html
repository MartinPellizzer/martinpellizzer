Salvadora crassinervia

Salvadora crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined Salvadora, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water.

Salvadora crassinervia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
