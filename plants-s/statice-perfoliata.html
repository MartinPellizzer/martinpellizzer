Statice perfoliata

Statice perfoliata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of blue to purple flowers and its ability to thrive in salty, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often fused at the base, giving it a distinctive perfoliata appearance. It is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Statice perfoliata is widely used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and ornamental value.
