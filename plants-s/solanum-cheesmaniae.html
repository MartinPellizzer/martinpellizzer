Solanum cheesmaniae

Solanum cheesmaniae, commonly known as the Cheesman's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum cheesmaniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and dramatic appearance.
