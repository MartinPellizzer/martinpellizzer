Saccharum angustifolium

Saccharum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and robust rhizomes, which contribute to its extensive root system. The plant is closely related to other sugarcane species and is often cultivated for its high sugar content in its stems. Its growth is typically favored in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a significant role in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
