Silene douglasii var. viscida

Silene douglasii var. viscida, commonly known as sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its stems are often erect and can grow up to several feet tall.

The species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
