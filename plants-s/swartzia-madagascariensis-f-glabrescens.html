Swartzia madagascariensis f. glabrescens

Swartzia madagascariensis f. glabrescens, commonly known as the Madagascan swartzia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive spurred structure, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its smooth, glabrous bark and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
