Salvia lavandulifolia var. spicata

Salvia lavandulifolia var. spicata, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, mint-like fragrance. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
