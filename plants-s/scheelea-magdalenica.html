Scheelea magdalenica

Scheelea magdalenica, commonly known as the Magdalena palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Scheelea magdalenica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
