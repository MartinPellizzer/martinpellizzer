Swertia subspeciosa

Swertia subspeciosa, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Swertia subspeciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in some Asian cultures.
