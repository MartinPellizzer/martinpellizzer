Staberoha aemula

Staberoha aemula, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with a faintly fragrant scent, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Staberoha aemula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
