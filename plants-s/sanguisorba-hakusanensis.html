Sanguisorba hakusanensis

Sanguisorba hakusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in early summer, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
