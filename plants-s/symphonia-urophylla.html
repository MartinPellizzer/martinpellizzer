Symphonia urophylla

Symphonia urophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing edible seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
