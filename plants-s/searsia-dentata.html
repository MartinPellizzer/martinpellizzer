Searsia dentata

Searsia dentata, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are often sour in taste. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Searsia dentata is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
