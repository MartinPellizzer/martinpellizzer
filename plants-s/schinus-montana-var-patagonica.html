Schinus montana var. patagonica

Schinus montana var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
