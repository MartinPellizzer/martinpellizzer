Spiraea digitata var. latiloba

Spiraea digitata var. latiloba, commonly known as broad-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a broad, rounded base and a serrated margin. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature, suitable for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
