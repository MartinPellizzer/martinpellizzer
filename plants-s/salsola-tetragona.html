Salsola tetragona

Salsola tetragona, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and coastal areas. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in pink or purple hues.

Salsola tetragona is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in many regions.
