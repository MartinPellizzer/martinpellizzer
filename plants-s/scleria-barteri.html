Scleria barteri

Scleria barteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speargrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Scleria barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
