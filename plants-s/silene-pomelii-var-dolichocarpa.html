Silene pomelii var. dolichocarpa

Silene pomelii var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.
