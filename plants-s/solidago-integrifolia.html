Solidago integrifolia

Solidago integrifolia, commonly known as the entire-leaved goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The leaves are lance-shaped and have smooth, entire margins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

This species is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
