Solanum indicum var. uollense

Solanum indicum var. uollense, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may be used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
