Stizolobium sempervirens

Stizolobium sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen acacia, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
