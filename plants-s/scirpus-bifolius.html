Scirpus bifolius

Scirpus bifolius, commonly known as the two-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Scirpus bifolius plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
