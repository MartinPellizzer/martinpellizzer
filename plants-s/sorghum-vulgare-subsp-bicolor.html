Sorghum vulgare subsp. bicolor

Sorghum vulgare subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, clustered spikelets that produce edible grains.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its grains are used for food, animal feed, and even as a source of biofuel.

The plant also has cultural significance in many parts of the world, particularly in traditional agriculture.
