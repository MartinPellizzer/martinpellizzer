Salvia consobrina

Salvia consobrina, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia consobrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
