Stachys albotomentosa var. albotomentosa

Stachys albotomentosa var. albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-haired betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fine layer of white pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
