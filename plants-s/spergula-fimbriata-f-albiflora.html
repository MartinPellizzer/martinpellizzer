Spergula fimbriata f. albiflora

Spergula fimbriata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered silverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
