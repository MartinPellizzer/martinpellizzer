Scleropus amaranthoides

Scleropus amaranthoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed false amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for dry environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
