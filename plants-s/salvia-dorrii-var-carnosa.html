Salvia dorrii var. carnosa

Salvia dorrii var. carnosa, commonly known as the Sierra foothill salvia, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Salvia genus, sharing similar structural and botanical characteristics.
