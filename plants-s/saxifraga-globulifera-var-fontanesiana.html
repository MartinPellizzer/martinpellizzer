Saxifraga globulifera var. fontanesiana

Saxifraga globulifera var. fontanesiana, commonly known as the Alpine snowbell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
