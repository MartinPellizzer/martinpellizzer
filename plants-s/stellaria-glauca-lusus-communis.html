Stellaria glauca lusus communis

Stellaria glauca lusus communis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval with a hairy texture. The plant has a fibrous root system and spreads through both seeds and stolons, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in gardens, fields, and along pathways.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its simple leaves and clustered inflorescences.
