Styrax subcrenatus

Styrax subcrenatus, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth or slightly lobed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Styrax subcrenatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
