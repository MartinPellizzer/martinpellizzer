Senecio schultzii subsp. schultzii

Senecio schultzii subsp.

schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fibrous root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to South Africa and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
