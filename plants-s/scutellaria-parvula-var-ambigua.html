Scutellaria parvula var. ambigua

Scutellaria parvula var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its flowering period occurs in the spring to early summer, and it is often found in the eastern United States.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
