Schizanthus incanus

Schizanthus incanus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Schizanthus incanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
