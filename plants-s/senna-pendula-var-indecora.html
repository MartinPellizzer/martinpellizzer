Senna pendula var. indecora

Senna pendula var. indecora, commonly known as the hanging senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are yellow with prominent orange veins. It produces pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
