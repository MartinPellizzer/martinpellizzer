Sasaella akiensis

Sasaella akiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella akiensis is often used in traditional landscaping and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
