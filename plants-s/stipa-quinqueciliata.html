Stipa quinqueciliata

Stipa quinqueciliata, commonly known as the five-ribbed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces feathery plumes of light yellow to golden flowers, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators. Stipa quinqueciliata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and use in traditional landscaping.
