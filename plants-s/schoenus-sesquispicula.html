Schoenus sesquispicula

Schoenus sesquispicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
