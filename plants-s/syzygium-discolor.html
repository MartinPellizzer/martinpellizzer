Syzygium discolor

Syzygium discolor, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium discolor is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
