Semecarpus thyrsoideus

Semecarpus thyrsoideus, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Saraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
