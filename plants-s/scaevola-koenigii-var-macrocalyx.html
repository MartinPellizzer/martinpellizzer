Scaevola koenigii var. macrocalyx

Scaevola koenigii var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered fanflower, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has linear, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
