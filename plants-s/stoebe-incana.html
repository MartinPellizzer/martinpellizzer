Stoebe incana

Stoebe incana, commonly known as hoary bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stoebe incana is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
