Scutellaria coleifolia

Scutellaria coleifolia, commonly known as broadleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Scutellaria coleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
