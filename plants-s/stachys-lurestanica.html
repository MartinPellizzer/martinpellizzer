Stachys lurestanica

Stachys lurestanica, commonly known as Persian betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
