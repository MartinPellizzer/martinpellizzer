Scorpius clusii

Scorpius clusii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.

Scorpius clusii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
