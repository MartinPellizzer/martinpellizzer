Shorea rugosa var. uliginosa

Shorea rugosa var. uliginosa, commonly known as the mangrove shorea, is a variant of the Shorea rugosa species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tree that typically grows in swampy or brackish water environments, adapting to high salinity and waterlogged soils. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize coastal areas and prevent erosion. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and resistance to decay.

This species plays a vital role in the ecological balance of mangrove ecosystems.
