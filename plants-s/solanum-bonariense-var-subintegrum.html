Solanum bonariense var. subintegrum

Solanum bonariense var. subintegrum, commonly known as the Argentine nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features elliptical, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant bears purple-black berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family.
