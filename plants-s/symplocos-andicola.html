Symplocos andicola

Symplocos andicola, commonly known as the Andean waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Symplocos andicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
