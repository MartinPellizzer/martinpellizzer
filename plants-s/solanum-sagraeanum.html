Solanum sagraeanum

Solanum sagraeanum, commonly known as the sacred tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
