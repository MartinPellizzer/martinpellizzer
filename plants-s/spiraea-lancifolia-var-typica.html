Spiraea lancifolia var. typica

Spiraea lancifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a pointed tip and a finely toothed margin, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its graceful form and showy floral display.
