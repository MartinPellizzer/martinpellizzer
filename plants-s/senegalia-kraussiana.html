Senegalia kraussiana

Senegalia kraussiana, commonly known as the Krauss acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and compound leaves that are often reduced to small spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Senegalia kraussiana is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
