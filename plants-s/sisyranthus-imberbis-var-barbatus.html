Sisyranthus imberbis var. barbatus

Sisyranthus imberbis var. barbatus, commonly known as the barbed sisyranthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescence. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
