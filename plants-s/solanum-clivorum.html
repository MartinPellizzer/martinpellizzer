Solanum clivorum

Solanum clivorum, commonly known as the cliff toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found on cliffs and slopes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to poor soil conditions.

Solanum clivorum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
