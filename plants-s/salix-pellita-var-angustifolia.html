Salix pellita var. angustifolia

Salix pellita var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams, wetlands, and forest edges. The plant features slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its catkins are a key feature, with male and female flowers occurring on separate stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
