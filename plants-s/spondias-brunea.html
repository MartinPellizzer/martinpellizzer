Spondias brunea

Spondias brunea, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its flowers are small, green, and clustered in panicles, and the fruit is known for its sweet, aromatic pulp.

Spondias brunea is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its medicinal properties.
