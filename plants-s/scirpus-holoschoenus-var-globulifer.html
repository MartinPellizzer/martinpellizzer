Scirpus holoschoenus var. globulifer

Scirpus holoschoenus var. globulifer, commonly known as the globular bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
