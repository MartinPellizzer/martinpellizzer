Spondias aurantiaca

Spondias aurantiaca, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange or red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Its fruits are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high vitamin C content and medicinal properties.
