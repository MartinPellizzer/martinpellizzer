Silene amoena var. angustifolia

Silene amoena var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and stems that are typically branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a distinctive central stamen that is often longer than the petals.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.
