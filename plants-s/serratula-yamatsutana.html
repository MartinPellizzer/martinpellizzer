Serratula yamatsutana

Serratula yamatsutana, commonly known as the Japanese serrata, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Serratula yamatsutana typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its leaves are serrated, hence the species name, and are often used in traditional Japanese herbal medicine.
