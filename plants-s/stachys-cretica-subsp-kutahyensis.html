Stachys cretica subsp. kutahyensis

Stachys cretica subsp.

kutahyensis, commonly known as Kutahyensis betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
