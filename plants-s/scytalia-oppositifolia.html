Scytalia oppositifolia

Scytalia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed scytalia, is a unique plant species characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spring, and it produces distinctive, elongated fruits.

Scytalia oppositifolia is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and ecological niche.
