Saxifraga fragosa subsp. claytoniifolia

Saxifraga fragosa subsp.

claytoniifolia, commonly known as Clayton's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically features dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are generally ovate to elliptic, with a waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
