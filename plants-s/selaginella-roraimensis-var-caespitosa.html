Selaginella roraimensis var. caespitosa

The plant thrives in the humid, shaded environments of high-altitude rock formations, where it forms dense mats on rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its role in the unique ecosystems of the tepuis.
