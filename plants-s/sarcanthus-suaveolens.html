Sarcanthus suaveolens

Sarcanthus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
