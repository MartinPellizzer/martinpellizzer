Senecio caranianus

Senecio caranianus, commonly known as the Cape Verde blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
