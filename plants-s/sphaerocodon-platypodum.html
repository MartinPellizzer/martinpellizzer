Sphaerocodon platypodum

Sphaerocodon platypodum, commonly known as the flat-footed sponge spore, is a unique fungal plant that belongs to the class Sphaerocodons.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-producing structures called spherocysts, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant's morphology includes a central stalk with a broad, flattened base that supports its reproductive structures. Its spores are dispersed through wind and water, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient cycling.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
