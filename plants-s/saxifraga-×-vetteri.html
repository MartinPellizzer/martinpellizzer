Saxifraga × vetteri

Saxifraga × vetteri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has dark green, succulent-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Saxifraga × vetteri is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
