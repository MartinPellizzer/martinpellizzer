Sarcostemma pubescens

Sarcostemma pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
