Solanum stenotomum f. kehuillo

Solanum stenotomum f. kehuillo, commonly known as the Kehuillo tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant exhibits a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, typical of many Solanum species. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its flavor and versatility in culinary applications.
