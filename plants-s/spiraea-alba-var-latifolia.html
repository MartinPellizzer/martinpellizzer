Spiraea alba var. latifolia

Spiraea alba var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of white, flat-topped flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
