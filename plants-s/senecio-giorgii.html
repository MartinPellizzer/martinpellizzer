Senecio giorgii

Senecio giorgii, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with silver-gray foliage adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, are pollinated by insects and birds.

Senecio giorgii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and extreme temperatures, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
