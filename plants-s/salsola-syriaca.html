Salsola syriaca

Salsola syriaca, commonly known as Syrian glassworts, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline or alkaline environments, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

Salsola syriaca is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
