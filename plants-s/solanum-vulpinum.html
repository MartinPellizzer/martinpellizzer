Solanum vulpinum

Solanum vulpinum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant red berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Solanum vulpinum is adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
