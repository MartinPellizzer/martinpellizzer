Salvia hypoleuca

Salvia hypoleuca, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

White sage is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices.
