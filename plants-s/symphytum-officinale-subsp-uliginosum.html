Symphytum officinale subsp. uliginosum

Symphytum officinale subsp.

uliginosum, commonly known as marsh comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance.

The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, typically found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for skin wounds and respiratory ailments.
