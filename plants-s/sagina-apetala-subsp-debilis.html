Sagina apetala subsp. debilis

Sagina apetala subsp.

debilis, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of pioneer vegetation in disturbed or barren landscapes.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
