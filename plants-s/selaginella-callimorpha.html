Selaginella callimorpha

Selaginella callimorpha, commonly known as the running-moss spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a branched stem covered with tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include strobili, or cone-like structures, that produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella callimorpha is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
