Soroseris hookeriana subsp. gillii

Soroseris hookeriana subsp.

gillii, commonly known as the Hooker's soroseris, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish in color.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
