Silene potaninii

Silene potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Silene potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
