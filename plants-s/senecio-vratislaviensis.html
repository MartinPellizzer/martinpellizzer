Senecio vratislaviensis

Senecio vratislaviensis, commonly known as the Vratislaviensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
