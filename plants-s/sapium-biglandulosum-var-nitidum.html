Sapium biglandulosum var. nitidum

Sapium biglandulosum var. nitidum, commonly known as the glossy sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that split open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
