Spilanthes mauritiana

Spilanthes mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius cactus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
