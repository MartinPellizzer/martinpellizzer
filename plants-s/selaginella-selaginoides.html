Selaginella selaginoides

Selaginella selaginoides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage.

Selaginella selaginoides is also noted for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
