Silphium brachiatum

Silphium brachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved compass plant, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and distinctive, palmately compound leaves that resemble a compass. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, open habitats like prairies and rocky soils.

Silphium brachiatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
