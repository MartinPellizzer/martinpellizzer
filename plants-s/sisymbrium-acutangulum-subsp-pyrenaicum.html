Sisymbrium acutangulum subsp. pyrenaicum

Sisymbrium acutangulum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean tansy mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and pods that split open to release seeds.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other subspecies of Sisymbrium acutangulum.
