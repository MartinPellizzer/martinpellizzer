Silene fauriei

Silene fauriei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene fauriei is an important species for pollinators and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
