Sedum eriocarpum subsp. apertiflorum

Sedum eriocarpum subsp.

apertiflorum, commonly known as the open-flower stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
