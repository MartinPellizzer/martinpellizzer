Sium cicutifolium var. heterophyllum

Sium cicutifolium var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the yellow water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
