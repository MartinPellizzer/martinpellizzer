It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct, often fragrant aroma.
