Silene meyeri subsp. persica

Silene meyeri subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are often arranged in racemes.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
