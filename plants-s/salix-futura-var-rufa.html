Salix futura var. rufa

Salix futura var. rufa, commonly known as the red-leaf willow, is a variant of the willow species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which becomes more prominent during the growing season. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands. Its slender, flexible branches and catkin-like flowers are typical of the Salix genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
