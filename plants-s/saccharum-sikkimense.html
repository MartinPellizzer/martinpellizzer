Saccharum sikkimense

Saccharum sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters and is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Saccharum sikkimense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
