Scleria macrophylla

Scleria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes.

Scleria macrophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
