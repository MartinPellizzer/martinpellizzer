Senegalia lankaensis

Senegalia lankaensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
