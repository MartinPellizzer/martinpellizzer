Scleria georgiana var. filiculmis

Scleria georgiana var. filiculmis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of small, three-sided spikelets that are usually green or reddish in color.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
