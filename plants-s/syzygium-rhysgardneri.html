Syzygium rhysgardneri

Syzygium rhysgardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
