Saussurea catharinae

Saussurea catharinae, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, feathery inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange-colored ray florets. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and it thrives in cold, arid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
