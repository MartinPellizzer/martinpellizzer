Smilax lanceifolia var. lanceolata

Smilax lanceifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
