Solanum sessiliflorum

Solanum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the nightshade or desert tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and its fruits are small, green berries that turn red when ripe.

Solanum sessiliflorum contains various alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
