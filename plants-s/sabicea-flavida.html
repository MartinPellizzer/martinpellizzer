Sabicea flavida

Sabicea flavida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sabicea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Sabicea flavida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
