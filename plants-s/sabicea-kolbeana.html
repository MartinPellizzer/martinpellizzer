Sabicea kolbeana

Sabicea kolbeana, commonly known as the Kolbe's acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are pods that release seeds through explosive dehiscence, aiding in seed dispersal.

Sabicea kolbeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
