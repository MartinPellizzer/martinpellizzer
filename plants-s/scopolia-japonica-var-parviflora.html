Scopolia japonica var. parviflora

Scopolia japonica var. parviflora, commonly known as Japanese bellflower or parviflora scopolia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and graceful foliage.
