Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. formosum

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
