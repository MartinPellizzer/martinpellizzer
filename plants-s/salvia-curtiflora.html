Salvia curtiflora

Salvia curtiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
