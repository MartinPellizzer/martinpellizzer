Schoenoplectus lupulinus

Schoenoplectus lupulinus, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually yellow or reddish, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
