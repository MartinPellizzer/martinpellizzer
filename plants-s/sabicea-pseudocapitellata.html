Sabicea pseudocapitellata

Sabicea pseudocapitellata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
