Smilax magnifolia

Smilax magnifolia, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax magnifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and swamps.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its native environment.
