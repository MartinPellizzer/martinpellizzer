Smilax petiolatumida

Smilax petiolatumida, commonly known as the trailing smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
