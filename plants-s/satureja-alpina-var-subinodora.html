Satureja alpina var. subinodora

Satureja alpina var. subinodora, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its pungent, slightly minty flavor.
