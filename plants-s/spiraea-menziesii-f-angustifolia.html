Spiraea menziesii f. angustifolia

Spiraea menziesii f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spiraea, is a flowering shrub characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of white to pink flowers.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are elongated and narrow, giving the plant a graceful, weeping appearance. It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to gardens and natural landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to partial shade to full sun conditions.
