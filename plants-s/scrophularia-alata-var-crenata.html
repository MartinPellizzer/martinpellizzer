Scrophularia alata var. crenata

Scrophularia alata var. crenata, commonly known as the hairy dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have wavy or toothed edges. It produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers in the summer, which are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
