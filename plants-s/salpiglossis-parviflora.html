Salpiglossis parviflora

Salpiglossis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lollipop flower, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and ease of cultivation have made it a favored plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
