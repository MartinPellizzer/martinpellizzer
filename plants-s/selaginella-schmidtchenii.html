Selaginella schmidtchenii

Selaginella schmidtchenii, commonly known as the Schmidtchen's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with delicate, needle-like leaves arranged in spiral patterns along its stems. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Selaginella schmidtchenii is valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental appeal.
