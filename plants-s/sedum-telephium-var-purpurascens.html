Sedum telephium var. purpurascens

Sedum telephium var. purpurascens, commonly known as Blush Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, fleshy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in late summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in both cultivated and natural settings.
