Serjania fuscifolia

Serjania fuscifolia, commonly known as the black-leaved serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by yellowish or reddish berries. Its leaves are dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it survive in the humid, shaded environments of its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
