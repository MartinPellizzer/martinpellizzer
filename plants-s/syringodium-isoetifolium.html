Syringodium isoetifolium

Syringodium isoetifolium, commonly known as the shoalgrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the western Atlantic Ocean.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms extensive underwater meadows. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, contributing to its role in stabilizing sediments in shallow marine environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the air above the water surface.

Syringodium isoetifolium plays a vital ecological role in supporting marine biodiversity and coastal protection.
