Selaginella vestita

Selaginella vestita, commonly known as the naked spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to regions of North America, including the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, underground stem with upright, needle-like leaves that are usually pale green to reddish in color. Selaginella vestita is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing adaptations such as reduced water loss and efficient photosynthesis.
