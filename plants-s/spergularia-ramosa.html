Spergularia ramosa

Spergularia ramosa, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Spergularia ramosa is often found in dunes and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand and preventing erosion.
