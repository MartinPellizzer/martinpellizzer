Sorbus turkestanica

Sorbus turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
