Scilla cyrenaica

Scilla cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to dry conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scilla cyrenaica is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
