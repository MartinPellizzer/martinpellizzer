Stelis sclerophylla

Stelis sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Stelis sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
