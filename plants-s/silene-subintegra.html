Silene subintegra

Silene subintegra, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at high elevations. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Silene subintegra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
