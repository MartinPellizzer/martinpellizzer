Succisa radiata

Succisa radiata, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has fleshy, lobed leaves that are adapted to retain moisture, typical of succulent species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens with limited water resources.
