Stipa arabica subsp. koenigii

Stipa arabica subsp.

koenigii, commonly known as Persian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in arid landscapes.
