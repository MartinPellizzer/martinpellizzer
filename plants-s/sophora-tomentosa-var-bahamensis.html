Sophora tomentosa var. bahamensis

Sophora tomentosa var. bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it resilient to salt and wind. Its fruit is a woody pod containing edible seeds, which have been used traditionally for food and medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in coastal ecosystems.
