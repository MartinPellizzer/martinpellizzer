Salvia raveniana

Salvia raveniana, commonly known as Raven's sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and its stems are often branched and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
