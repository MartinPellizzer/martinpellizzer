Sciadotenia ramiflora var. colombiana

Sciadotenia ramiflora var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
