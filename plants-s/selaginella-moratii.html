Selaginella moratii

Selaginella moratii, commonly known as the tropical spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has creeping stems that produce erect, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a glossy appearance.

Selaginella moratii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
