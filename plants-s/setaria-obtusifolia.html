Setaria obtusifolia

Setaria obtusifolia, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa but now widespread in various parts of the world.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed blades and dense, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a competitive species in diverse environments.

Botanically, it is noted for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and climatic conditions.
