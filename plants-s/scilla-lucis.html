Scilla lucis

Scilla lucis, commonly known as the "light scilla," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Scilla lucis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
