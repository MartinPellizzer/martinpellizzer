Salvia veronicifolia

Salvia veronicifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
