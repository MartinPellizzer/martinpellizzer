Scirpus novae-zealandiae

Scirpus novae-zealandiae, commonly known as New Zealand bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, lake margins, and riverbanks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus novae-zealandiae plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
