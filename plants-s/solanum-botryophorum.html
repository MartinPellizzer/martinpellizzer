Solanum botryophorum

Solanum botryophorum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
