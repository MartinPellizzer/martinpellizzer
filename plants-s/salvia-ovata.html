Salvia ovata

Salvia ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its roots are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia ovata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
