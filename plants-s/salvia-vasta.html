Salvia vasta

Salvia vasta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a minty fragrance when crushed.

Salvia vasta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
