Salvia paniculata

Salvia paniculata, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. Salvia paniculata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
