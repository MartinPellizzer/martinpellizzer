Solanum stuckertii var. atrichostylum

Solanum stuckertii var. atrichostylum, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is typically avoided by livestock and wildlife.
