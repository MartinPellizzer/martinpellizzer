Salvia spielmanniana

Salvia spielmanniana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
