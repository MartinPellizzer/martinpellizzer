Senites latifolius

Senites latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf senita, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and broad, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senites latifolius is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system and a slow growth rate to conserve resources.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
