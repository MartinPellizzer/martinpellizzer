Silene pruinosa

Silene pruinosa, commonly known as the downy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Silene pruinosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
