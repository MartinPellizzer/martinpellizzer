Salix × argusii

Salix × argusii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and a lighter green on the underside. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, streams, and wetlands.

Salix × argusii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
