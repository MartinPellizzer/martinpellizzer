Syzygium cleistocalyx

Syzygium cleistocalyx, commonly known as the white syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits known as cloves, which are highly valued for their aromatic properties. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
