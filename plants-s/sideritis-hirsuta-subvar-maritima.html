Sideritis hirsuta subvar. maritima

Sideritis hirsuta subvar. maritima, commonly known as the coastal sideritis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, pale blue or violet flowers. This plant thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in well-drained soils. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, maritime conditions.
