Sidalcea hickmanii subsp. parishii

Sidalcea hickmanii subsp.

parishii, commonly known as Hickman's catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
