Selinocarpus chenopodioides

Selinocarpus chenopodioides, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a valuable food source in regions where traditional crops struggle to grow.

The plant's ability to thrive in poor soils and high temperatures makes it an important species for ecological resilience and human nutrition in harsh climates.
