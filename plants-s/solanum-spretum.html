Solanum spretum

Solanum spretum, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, purple-veined leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Solanum spretum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
