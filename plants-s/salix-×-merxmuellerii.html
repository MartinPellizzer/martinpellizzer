Salix × merxmuellerii

Salix × merxmuellerii, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its slender, gray-barked trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It produces small, inconspicuous catkins in the spring, which are followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal.
