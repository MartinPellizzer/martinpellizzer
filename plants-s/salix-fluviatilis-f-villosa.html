Salix fluviatilis f. villosa

Salix fluviatilis f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a shrub native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows along streams, rivers, and other water bodies, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its catkins are an important source of pollen for early-season pollinators.

The hairy willow is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
