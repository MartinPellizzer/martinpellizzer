Solanum capsicastrum variegatum

Solanum capsicastrum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated nightshade, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that turns red when ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
