Stachys zappanioides

Stachys zappanioides, commonly known as the Zappan-like hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Stachys zappanioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
