Salix guebriantiana

Salix guebriantiana, commonly known as the Guebriant willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
