Shorea andulensis

Shorea andulensis, commonly known as the Andulensian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
