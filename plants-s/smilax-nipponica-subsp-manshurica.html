Smilax nipponica subsp. manshurica

Smilax nipponica subsp.

manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian arrowroot, is a flowering plant species native to eastern Asia. It is a perennial vine characterized by its robust, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
