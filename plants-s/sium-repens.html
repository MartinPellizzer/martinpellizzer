Sium repens

Sium repens, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and aromatic. Small white flowers are borne in clusters, and the plant produces rounded, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
