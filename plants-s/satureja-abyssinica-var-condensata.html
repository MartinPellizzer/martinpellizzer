Satureja abyssinica var. condensata

Satureja abyssinica var. condensata, commonly known as Ethiopian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
