Sideritis arborescens f. lazari

Sideritis arborescens f. lazari, commonly known as the Lazari shrub, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Sideritis arborescens f. lazari is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
