Stipa columbiana var. nelsonii

Stipa columbiana var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's blue grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense tufts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or blue hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
