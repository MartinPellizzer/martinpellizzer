Senna demissa var. demissa

Senna demissa var. demissa, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye due to its chemical compounds.
