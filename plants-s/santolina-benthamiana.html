Santolina benthamiana

Santolina benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's santolina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Santolina benthamiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
