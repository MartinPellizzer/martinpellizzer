Sphaeranthus epigaeus

Sphaeranthus epigaeus, commonly known as the groundsel or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small annual herb with a distinctive basal rosette of pale green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, spherical seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Sphaeranthus epigaeus is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
