Scilla siberica var. alba

Scilla siberica var. alba, commonly known as the Siberian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
