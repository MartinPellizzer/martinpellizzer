Senecio kolenatianus subsp. pseudoorientalis

Senecio kolenatianus subsp.

pseudoorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through a unique mechanism involving a specialized structure called a pappus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
