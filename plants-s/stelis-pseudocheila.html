Stelis pseudocheila

Stelis pseudocheila, commonly known as the violet-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stelis pseudocheila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
