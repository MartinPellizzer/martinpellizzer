Stizolobium bracteatum

Stizolobium bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
