Senecio badaroi

Senecio badaroi, commonly known as the Namib Desert Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a small, mat-forming succulent that thrives in arid conditions, showcasing adaptations such as thick, fleshy leaves for water storage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the sparse rainfall.

Senecio badaroi plays a crucial role in stabilizing the desert soil and supporting local biodiversity.
