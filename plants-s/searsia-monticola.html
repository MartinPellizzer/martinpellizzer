Searsia monticola

Searsia monticola, commonly known as the mountain sugarbush, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Searsia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
