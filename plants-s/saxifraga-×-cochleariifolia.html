Saxifraga × cochleariifolia

Saxifraga × cochleariifolia, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, woolly texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga × cochleariifolia is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
