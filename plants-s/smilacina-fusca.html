Smilacina fusca

Smilacina fusca, commonly known as greenbrier, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the summer. Smilacina fusca thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in the understory of forests.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
