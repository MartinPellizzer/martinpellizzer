Salvia prunelloides

Salvia prunelloides, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia prunelloides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
