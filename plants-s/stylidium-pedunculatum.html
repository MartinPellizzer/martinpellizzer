Stylidium pedunculatum

Stylidium pedunculatum, commonly known as the clubbed sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive club-shaped spur. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforest or heathland habitats.

Stylidium pedunculatum is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves the movement of the flower's column in response to touch.
