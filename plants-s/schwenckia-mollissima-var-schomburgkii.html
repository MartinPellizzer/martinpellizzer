Schwenckia mollissima var. schomburgkii

Schwenckia mollissima var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's thistle, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners interested in native plants.
