Scutellaria angustifolia

Scutellaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Scutellaria angustifolia is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive calyx and corolla structure.
