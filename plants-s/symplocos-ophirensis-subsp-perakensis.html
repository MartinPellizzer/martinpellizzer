Symplocos ophirensis subsp. perakensis

Symplocos ophirensis subsp.

perakensis, commonly known as the Perakensis wax tree, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
