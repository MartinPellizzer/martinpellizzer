Stipa baicalensis var. macrocarpa

Stipa baicalensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and long, narrow, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds to its ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
