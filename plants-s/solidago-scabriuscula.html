Solidago scabriuscula

Solidago scabriuscula, commonly known as roughleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators including bees and butterflies.

Solidago scabriuscula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
