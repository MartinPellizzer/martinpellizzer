Senecio isatideus

Senecio isatideus, commonly known as the blueweed or blueweed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Senecio isatideus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
