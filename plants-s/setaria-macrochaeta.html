Setaria macrochaeta

Setaria macrochaeta, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows.

Setaria macrochaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
