Sempervivum validum

Sempervivum validum, commonly known as the hen-and-chicks plant, is a succulent species belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It produces upright flowering stalks that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

Sempervivum validum is highly drought-tolerant and requires well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
