Satyrium longissimum

Satyrium longissimum, commonly known as the long-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The orchid produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, making it a rare and specialized species in the botanical world.
