Stylidium streptocarpum var. macrocarpum

Stylidium streptocarpum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited spurred gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features a short, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are notable for their unique, spurred shape and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions of Australia.
