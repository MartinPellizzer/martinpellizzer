Solanum stenotomum var. pitiquina

Solanum stenotomum var. pitiquina, commonly known as the pitiquina nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a glossy green surface and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright orange or red color, containing several seeds.

This variety is part of the Solanaceae family, known for its diverse array of economically important plants.
