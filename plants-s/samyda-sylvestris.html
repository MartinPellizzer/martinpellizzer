Samyda sylvestris

Samyda sylvestris, commonly known as the wild silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale pink or white, arranged in clusters. Samyda sylvestris produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
