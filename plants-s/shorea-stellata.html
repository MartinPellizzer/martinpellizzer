Shorea stellata

Shorea stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaf scars. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in construction and furniture.
