Spergularia bocconei

Spergularia bocconei, commonly known as the Mediterranean sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in sandy and coastal environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Spergularia bocconei is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
