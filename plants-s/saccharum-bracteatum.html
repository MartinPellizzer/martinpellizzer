Saccharum bracteatum

Saccharum bracteatum, commonly known as the hooded cane or hooded grass, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive hood-like bracts at the base of its inflorescence. The plant is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in marshy or swampy environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green.

Saccharum bracteatum is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
