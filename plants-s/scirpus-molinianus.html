Scirpus molinianus

Scirpus molinianus, commonly known as Molin's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often emerging from dense clusters of rhizomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and tapering, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Scirpus molinianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
