Stachys latipes

Stachys latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Stachys latipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
