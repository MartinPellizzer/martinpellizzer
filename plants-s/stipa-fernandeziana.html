Stipa fernandeziana

Stipa fernandeziana, commonly known as the Fernandez's grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.

Stipa fernandeziana is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
