Streptopus streptopoides var. brevipes

Streptopus streptopoides var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed star-nut, is a rare and unique plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle floral element to its appearance. Its stems are typically shorter and more compact compared to other varieties in the genus, contributing to its common name.
