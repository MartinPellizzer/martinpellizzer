Sipanea limnophila

Sipanea limnophila, commonly known as the water sipanea, is a rare aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It possesses long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant has specialized root systems that anchor it to submerged substrates and absorb nutrients. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by water currents.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sipanea limnophila is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality.
