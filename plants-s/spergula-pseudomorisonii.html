Spergula pseudomorisonii

Spergula pseudomorisonii, commonly known as the false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
