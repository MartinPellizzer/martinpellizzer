Saccus nitidus

Saccus nitidus, commonly known as the shiny sac plant, is a unique species within the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, sac-like structures that develop from the base of its leaves. These sacs, which are often filled with water, serve as a habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
