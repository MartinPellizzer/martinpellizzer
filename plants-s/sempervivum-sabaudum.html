Sempervivum sabaudum

Sempervivum sabaudum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are densely packed and compact. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Sempervivum sabaudum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
