Sphaeropteris macrocarpa

Sphaeropteris macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the tree-like structure.

Sphaeropteris macrocarpa plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
