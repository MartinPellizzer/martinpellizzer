Schefflera albida

Schefflera albida, commonly known as the white schefflera, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-shaped cluster of leaves, known as a compound umbel, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Schefflera albida is relatively low-maintenance and adaptable to various indoor growing conditions.
