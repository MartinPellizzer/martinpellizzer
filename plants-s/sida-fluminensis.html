Sida fluminensis

Sida fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in sandy soils and can tolerate saline conditions, making it adaptable to coastal environments.

Sida fluminensis is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
