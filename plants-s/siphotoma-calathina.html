Siphotoma calathina

Siphotoma calathina, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Siphotoma calathina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
