Seruneum

Seruneum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers have five petals and a prominent yellow center.

Seruneum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
