Styrax hypargyreus

Styrax hypargyreus, commonly known as the golden styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Styrax hypargyreus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
