Saxifraga strigosa f. simplex

Saxifraga strigosa f. simplex, commonly known as the simple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant among gardeners seeking alpine-style landscapes.
