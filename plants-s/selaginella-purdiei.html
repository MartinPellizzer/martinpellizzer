Selaginella purdiei

Selaginella purdiei, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Selaginella purdiei is known for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
