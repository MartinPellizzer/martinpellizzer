Spiraea diversifolia

Spiraea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is upright and compact, making it suitable for small gardens or as a hedge.

Spiraea diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
