Sempervivum canariense subsp. christii

Sempervivum canariense subsp.

christii, commonly known as the Canary Island houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky, arid environments and requires minimal water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
