Siphanthera paludosa

Siphanthera paludosa, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments, making it ecologically significant.

Siphanthera paludosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
