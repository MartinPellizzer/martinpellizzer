Sideritis sierrarafolsiana

Sideritis sierrarafolsiana, commonly known as Sierra Rafa's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
