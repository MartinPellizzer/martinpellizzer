Solanum caumii

Solanum caumii, commonly known as the Caumii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round berries that contain the toxic seeds.

Due to its toxicity, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
