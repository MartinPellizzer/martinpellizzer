Sauromatum angolense

Sauromatum angolense, commonly known as the Angola arum, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around a fleshy, yellowish spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens.
