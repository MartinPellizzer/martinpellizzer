Swartzia latifolia

Swartzia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Swartzia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
