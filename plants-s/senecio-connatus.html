Senecio connatus

Senecio connatus, commonly known as the Cape mountain fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio connatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
