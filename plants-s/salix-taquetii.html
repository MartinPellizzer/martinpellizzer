Salix taquetii

Salix taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's willow, is a species of willow native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant has slender, often pendulous branches and produces catkin-like flowers, typical of the Salicaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that reflects sunlight.

Salix taquetii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
