Satureja stolonifera

Satureja stolonifera, commonly known as creeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces small, pink or white flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils, giving it a distinct minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Satureja stolonifera is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
