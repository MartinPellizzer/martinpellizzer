Sambucus glauca var. neomexicana

Sambucus glauca var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically grayish-green and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
