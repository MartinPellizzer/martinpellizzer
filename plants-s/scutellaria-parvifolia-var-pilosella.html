Scutellaria parvifolia var. pilosella

Scutellaria parvifolia var. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy blue skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
