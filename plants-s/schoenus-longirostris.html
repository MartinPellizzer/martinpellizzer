Schoenus longirostris

Schoenus longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that consist of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
