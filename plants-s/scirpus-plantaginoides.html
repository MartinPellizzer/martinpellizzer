Scirpus plantaginoides

Scirpus plantaginoides, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in phytoremediation due to their ability to absorb heavy metals.

Scirpus plantaginoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
