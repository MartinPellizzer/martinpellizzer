Spiraea blumei var. hayatae

Spiraea blumei var. hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance in landscaping.
