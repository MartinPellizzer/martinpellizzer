Sasa magninoda

Sasa magninoda, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sasa magninoda thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
