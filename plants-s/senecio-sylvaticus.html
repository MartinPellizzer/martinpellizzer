Senecio sylvaticus

Senecio sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens with suitable conditions.
