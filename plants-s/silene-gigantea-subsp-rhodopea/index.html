Silene Gigantea Subsp. Rhodopea

Silene gigantea subsp. rhodopea is a striking plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria. It is known for its tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments. Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species.
