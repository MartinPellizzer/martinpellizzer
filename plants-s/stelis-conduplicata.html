Stelis conduplicata

Stelis conduplicata, commonly known as the clustered stelis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers, which are often white or pale pink, bloom in late summer or early autumn.
