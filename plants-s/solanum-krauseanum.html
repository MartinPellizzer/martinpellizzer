Solanum krauseanum

Solanum krauseanum, commonly known as the Krause's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
