Spatholobus pottingeri

Spatholobus pottingeri, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and heathlands. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Spatholobus pottingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
