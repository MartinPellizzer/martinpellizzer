Scabiosa atropurpurea var. paui

Scabiosa atropurpurea var. paui, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spiky purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with gray-green foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The variety 'paui' is noted for its dense, conical flower heads and robust growth, making it suitable for both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
