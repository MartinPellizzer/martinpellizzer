Streptocarpus thompsonii var. thompsonii

Streptocarpus thompsonii var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and compact growth form.
