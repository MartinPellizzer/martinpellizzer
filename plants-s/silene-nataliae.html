Silene nataliae

Silene nataliae, commonly known as Natal's campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene nataliae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
