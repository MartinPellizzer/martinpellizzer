Satureja majoranifolia f. ozrensis

Satureja majoranifolia f. ozrensis, commonly known as the Ozrensky savory, is a subspecies of the savory plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a low-growing herb with aromatic, small, oval leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is known for its strong, pungent aroma, similar to oregano and thyme.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and herbal collections.
