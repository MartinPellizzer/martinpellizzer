Solanum orbignianum

Solanum orbignianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Solanum orbignianum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It contains various alkaloids and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
