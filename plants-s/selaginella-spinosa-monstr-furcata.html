Selaginella spinosa monstr. furcata

Selaginella spinosa monstr.

furcata, commonly known as the forked spikemoss, is a unique vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae. It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of spirally arranged, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, cone-like strobila that bear microspores and megaspores, facilitating its reproduction. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its distinctive forked structure and resilience make it a notable species in botanical studies.
