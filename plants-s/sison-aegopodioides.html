Sison aegopodioides

Sison aegopodioides, commonly known as the false agrimony, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple centers.

Sison aegopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
