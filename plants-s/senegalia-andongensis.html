Senegalia andongensis

Senegalia andongensis, commonly known as the Andong acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Senegalia andongensis is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
