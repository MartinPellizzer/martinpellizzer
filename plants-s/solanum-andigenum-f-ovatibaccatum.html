Solanum andigenum f. ovatibaccatum

Solanum andigenum f. ovatibaccatum, commonly known as the Andean eggplant, is a variant of the Andean tomato.

It is characterized by its ovate, slightly hairy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly the Andes.

This cultivar is valued for its unique flavor and adaptability to cooler climates.
