Senecio pauciflorus

Senecio pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant has a compact, shrub-like appearance.

Senecio pauciflorus is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
