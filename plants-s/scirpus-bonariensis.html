Scirpus bonariensis

Scirpus bonariensis, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of the stem. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
