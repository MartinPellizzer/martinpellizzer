Sorbus aria var. denticulata

Sorbus aria var. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
