Serjania marginata f. paucidentata

Serjania marginata f. paucidentata, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Serjania.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, lanceolate leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
