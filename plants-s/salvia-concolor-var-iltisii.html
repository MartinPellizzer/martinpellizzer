Salvia concolor var. iltisii

Salvia concolor var. iltisii, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
