Sida antillensis

Sida antillensis, commonly known as the seaside cotton or sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, adapting well to coastal environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

Sida antillensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
