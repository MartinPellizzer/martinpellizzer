Sassafridium macrophyllum

Sassafridium macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf sassafrid, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it easily distinguishable from other plants in its habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is notable for containing aromatic compounds, similar to those found in traditional sassafras plants.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
