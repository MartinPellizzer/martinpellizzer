Strychnos pachycarpa

Strychnos pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, thick, fleshy fruits that are often orange or red in color. Its seeds are enclosed within the fleshy, berry-like fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
