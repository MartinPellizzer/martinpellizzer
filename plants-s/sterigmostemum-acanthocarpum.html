Sterigmostemum acanthocarpum

Sterigmostemum acanthocarpum, commonly known as the hairy stemmed acanthocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.
