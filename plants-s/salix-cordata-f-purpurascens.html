Salix cordata f. purpurascens

Salix cordata f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf willow, is a unique cultivar of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which adds a dramatic visual contrast to its slender, cascading branches. The plant thrives in moist environments, such as along riverbanks or in wetlands, and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, feathery texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in landscaping for its visual impact.
