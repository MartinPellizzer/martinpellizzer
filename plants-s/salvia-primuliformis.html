Salvia primuliformis

Salvia primuliformis, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia primuliformis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
