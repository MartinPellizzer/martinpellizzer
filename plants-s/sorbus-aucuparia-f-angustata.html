Sorbus aucuparia f. angustata

Sorbus aucuparia f. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rowan, is a botanical variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and smaller, yellowish fruits compared to the typical species. The plant is native to northern Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in forested areas and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The narrowleaf rowan is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
