Syzygium kingii

Syzygium kingii, commonly known as the King's satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Syzygium kingii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
