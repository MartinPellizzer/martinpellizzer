Salacia alveolata

Salacia alveolata, commonly known as the hollow vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains multiple seeds.

Salacia alveolata is often found in moist, shaded environments within dense forest understories.
