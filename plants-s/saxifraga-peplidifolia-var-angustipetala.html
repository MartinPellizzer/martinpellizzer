Saxifraga peplidifolia var. angustipetala

Saxifraga peplidifolia var. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions.
