Salvia ochrantha

Salvia ochrantha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spire-like flower heads that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Salvia ochrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
