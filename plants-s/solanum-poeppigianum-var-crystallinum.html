Solanum poeppigianum var. crystallinum

Solanum poeppigianum var. crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue or black color. One of its most distinctive features is the presence of a crystalline substance on its leaves and stems, which gives it its common name.

This unique botanical trait makes it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
