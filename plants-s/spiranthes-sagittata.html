Spiranthes sagittata

Spiranthes sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and they bloom from late summer into early autumn.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
