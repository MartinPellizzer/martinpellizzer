Sorghum nitidum var. majus

Sorghum nitidum var. majus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to thrive in warm climates with well-drained soils.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
