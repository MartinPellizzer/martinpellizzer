Smallia

Smallia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind that contains a high concentration of essential oils. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Smallia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic compounds it emits.
