Senecio nigrostagnosus

Senecio nigrostagnosus, commonly known as the black-stained groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
