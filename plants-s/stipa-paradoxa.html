Stipa paradoxa

Stipa paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Stipa paradoxa thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
