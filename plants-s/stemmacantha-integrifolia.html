Stemmacantha integrifolia

Stemmacantha integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-edged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
