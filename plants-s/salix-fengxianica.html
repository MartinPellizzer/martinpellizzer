Salix fengxianica

Salix fengxianica, commonly known as the Fengxian poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, typical of the willow genus, and is adapted to thrive in moist, temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
