Sorghum centroplicatum var. alborubrum

Sorghum centroplicatum var. alborubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and distinctive reddish hues in its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional agricultural practices in certain regions.
