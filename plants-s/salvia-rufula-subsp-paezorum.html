Salvia rufula subsp. paezorum

Salvia rufula subsp.

paezorum, commonly known as the Paez's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of showy, violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
