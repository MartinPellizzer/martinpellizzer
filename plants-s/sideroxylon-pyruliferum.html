Sideroxylon pyruliferum

Sideroxylon pyruliferum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically grows in dry, seasonal forests and is known for its slow growth rate, which contributes to its valuable timber. Its leaves are opposite and simple, often with a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Sideroxylon pyruliferum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
