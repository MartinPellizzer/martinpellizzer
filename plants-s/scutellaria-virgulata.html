Scutellaria virgulata

Scutellaria virgulata, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria virgulata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
