Saussurea kubotae var. kubotae

Saussurea kubotae var. kubotae, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This species is well adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
