Sambucus glauca var. fontenayensis

Sambucus glauca var. fontenayensis, commonly known as the Fontenay dogwood, is a variant of the common dogwood native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its berries, which are typically black or blue, are edible but often have a bitter taste.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
