Salvia macrochlamys

Salvia macrochlamys, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional herbal medicine.
