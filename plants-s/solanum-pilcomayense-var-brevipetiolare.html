Solanum pilcomayense var. brevipetiolare

Solanum pilcomayense var. brevipetiolare, commonly known as the short-stalked nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves with short petioles, and its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when immature and turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.
