Semonvillea punctata

Semonvillea punctata, commonly known as the spotted semonvillea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically dark green with distinctive white or yellow spots, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
