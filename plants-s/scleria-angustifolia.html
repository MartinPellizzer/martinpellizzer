Scleria angustifolia

Scleria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Scleria angustifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
