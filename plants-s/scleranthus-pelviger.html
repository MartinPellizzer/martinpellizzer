Scleranthus pelviger

Scleranthus pelviger, commonly known as the common thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Scleranthus pelviger is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
