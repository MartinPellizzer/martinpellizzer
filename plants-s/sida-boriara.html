Sida boriara

Sida boriara, commonly known as the coastal sida, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Sida boriara thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime environments.

It is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
