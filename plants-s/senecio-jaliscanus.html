Senecio jaliscanus

Senecio jaliscanus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Jalisco, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Senecio jaliscanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
