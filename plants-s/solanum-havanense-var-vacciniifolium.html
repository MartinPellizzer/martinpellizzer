Solanum havanense var. vacciniifolium

Solanum havanense var. vacciniifolium, commonly known as the red-berry nightshade, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries, which are typically red when mature, are toxic and should not be consumed.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
