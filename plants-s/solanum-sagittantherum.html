Solanum sagittantherum

Solanum sagittantherum, commonly known as the arrowhead nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that change color as they mature, and the plant is often found in moist, shady habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle it with care.
