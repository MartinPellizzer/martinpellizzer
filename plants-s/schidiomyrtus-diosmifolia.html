Schidiomyrtus diosmifolia

Schidiomyrtus diosmifolia, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Schidiomyrtus diosmifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
