Salix chaenomeloides var. pilosa

Salix chaenomeloides var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
