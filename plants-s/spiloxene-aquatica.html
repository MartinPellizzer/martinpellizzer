Spiloxene aquatica

Spiloxene aquatica, commonly known as water spurge, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas.

It is characterized by its erect stem and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has milky sap that can cause skin irritation, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams and ponds.

Spiloxene aquatica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
