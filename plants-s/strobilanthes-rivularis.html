Strobilanthes rivularis

Strobilanthes rivularis, commonly known as the river gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Strobilanthes rivularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
