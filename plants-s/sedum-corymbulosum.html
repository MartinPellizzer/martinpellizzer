Sedum corymbulosum

Sedum corymbulosum, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
