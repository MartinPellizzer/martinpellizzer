Salvia gattefossei

Salvia gattefossei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Salvia gattefossei is also noted for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
