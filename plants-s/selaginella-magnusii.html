Selaginella magnusii

Selaginella magnusii, commonly known as the Magnus's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. It produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the spores for reproduction.

Selaginella magnusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
