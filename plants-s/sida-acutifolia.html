Sida acutifolia

Sida acutifolia, commonly known as Mexican hairless or velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
