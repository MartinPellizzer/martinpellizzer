Setaria gracilis f. purpurascens

Setaria gracilis f. purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of the grass species Setaria gracilis.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and the presence of purplish pigmentation in its inflorescence and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
