Sorghum margaritiferum

Sorghum margaritiferum, commonly known as the bead sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and small, bead-like spikelets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sorghum margaritiferum is also used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
