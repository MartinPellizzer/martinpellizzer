Salix × ehrhartiana

Salix × ehrhartiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the United Kingdom.

It is a hybrid between Salix alba (White Willow) and Salix triandra (Caucasian Willow), resulting in a plant with distinctive weeping branches. The tree has long, slender, pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a cascading effect. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water bodies for its ornamental value.
