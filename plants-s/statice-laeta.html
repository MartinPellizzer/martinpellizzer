Statice laeta

Statice laeta, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to coastal environments.

Statice laeta is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental appeal.
