Scleranthus wilkommii

Scleranthus wilkommii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus wilkommii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
