Sambucus gaudichaudiana

Sambucus gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian elderberry, is a species of flowering plant in the honeysuckle family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Sambucus gaudichaudiana plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is also valued for its ornamental features.
