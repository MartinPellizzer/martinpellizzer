Sinosidus albescens var. limprichtii

Sinosidus albescens var. limprichtii, commonly known as the white-flowered sinosidus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution.
