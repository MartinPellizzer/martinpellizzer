Secamone lagenifera

Secamone lagenifera, commonly known as the bottlebrush secamone, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.
