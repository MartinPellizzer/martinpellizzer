Scorzonera hispanica subsp. crispatula

Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

crispatula, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided.

The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, hairy corolla, which bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
