Spergularia glaucophylla

Spergularia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Spergularia glaucophylla is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
