Saxifraga ferruginea var. typica

Saxifraga ferruginea var. typica, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with reddish-brown markings, which help in identifying the species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Saxifraga ferruginea var. typica is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
