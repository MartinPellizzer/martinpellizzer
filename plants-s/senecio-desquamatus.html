Senecio desquamatus

Senecio desquamatus, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio desquamatus is adapted to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
