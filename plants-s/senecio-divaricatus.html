Senecio divaricatus

Senecio divaricatus, commonly known as the Blueweed or Bluecup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio divaricatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
