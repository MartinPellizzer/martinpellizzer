Silene khasiana

Silene khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Silene khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
