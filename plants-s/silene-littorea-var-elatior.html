Silene littorea var. elatior

Silene littorea var. elatior, commonly known as the greater sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along the edges of the sea. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene littorea var. elatior is an important component of coastal vegetation, contributing to stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various insects.
