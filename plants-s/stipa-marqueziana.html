Stipa marqueziana

Stipa marqueziana, commonly known as the Marquez's feathergrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-gray hue that helps it conserve water in arid conditions.

Stipa marqueziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant, wind-blown foliage.
