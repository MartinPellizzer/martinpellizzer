Sideroxylon oppositifolium

Sideroxylon oppositifolium, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves. The leaves are opposite in arrangement, hence the species name, and are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

This plant is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
