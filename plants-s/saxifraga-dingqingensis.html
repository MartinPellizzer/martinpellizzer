Saxifraga dingqingensis

Saxifraga dingqingensis, commonly known as the Dingqing saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and small, often white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude, rocky environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically succulent, aiding in water retention in arid settings.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
