Scirpus cyperinus f. andrewsii

Scirpus cyperinus f. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrew's bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to wetland environments, this plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
