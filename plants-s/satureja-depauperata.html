Satureja depauperata

Satureja depauperata, commonly known as poor satureja, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense terminal spikes, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Satureja depauperata is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
