Syzygium zeylanicum var. magamalayanum

Syzygium zeylanicum var. magamalayanum, commonly known as the Magamalaya clove.

It is a variant of the clove tree, native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, reddish-brown flowers that give way to edible, fragrant fruits. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctively fragrant scent.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
