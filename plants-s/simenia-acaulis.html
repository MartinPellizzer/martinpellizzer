Simenia acaulis

Simenia acaulis, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has a compact growth habit with no distinct stem, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species often used in rock gardens.
