Salix × luiensis

Salix × luiensis, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
