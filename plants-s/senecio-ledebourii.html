Senecio ledebourii

Senecio ledebourii, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is a low-growing species, typically reaching heights of only a few inches, and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
