Salicornia persica subsp. iranica

Salicornia persica subsp.

iranica, commonly known as Persian glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is native to regions of Iran and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
