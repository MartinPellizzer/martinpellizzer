Syringa cordifolia var. alba

Syringa cordifolia var. alba, commonly known as white heartleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually opposite and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
