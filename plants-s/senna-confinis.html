Senna confinis

Senna confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the genus Senna.

Senna confinis is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
