Sorbus cretica var. typica

Sorbus cretica var. typica, commonly known as the common whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
