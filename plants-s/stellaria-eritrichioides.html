Stellaria eritrichioides

Stellaria eritrichioides, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers that resemble stars, giving it its species name "eritrichioides," meaning "resembling Eritrichium.". The plant has a basal rosette of ovate, hairy leaves and produces numerous tiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Stellaria eritrichioides is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
