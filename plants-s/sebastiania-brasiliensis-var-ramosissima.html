Sebastiania brasiliensis var. ramosissima

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, star-shaped fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
