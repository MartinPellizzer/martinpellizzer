Scutellaria picta

Scutellaria picta, commonly known as the painted skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria picta is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
