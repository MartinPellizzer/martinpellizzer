Swainsona coronillifolia var. purpurea

Swainsona coronillifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as purple crownworts, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
