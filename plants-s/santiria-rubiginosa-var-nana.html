Santiria rubiginosa var. nana

Santiria rubiginosa var. nana, commonly known as dwarf red santiria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compact, rounded foliage with dark green leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
