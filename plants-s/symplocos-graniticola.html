Symplocos graniticola

Symplocos graniticola, commonly known as the granite alder, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Symplocos graniticola is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
