Silene pusilla

Silene pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Silene pusilla is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in moist, open habitats.
