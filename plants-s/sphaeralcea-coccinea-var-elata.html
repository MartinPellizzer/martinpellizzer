Sphaeralcea coccinea var. elata

Sphaeralcea coccinea var. elata, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces spherical, fuzzy seed pods that mature into a bright red color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
