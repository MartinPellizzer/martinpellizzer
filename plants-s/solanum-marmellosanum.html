Solanum marmellosanum

Solanum marmellosanum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Solanum marmellosanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
