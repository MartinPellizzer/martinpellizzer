Scaphyglottis unguiculata

Scaphyglottis unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spurred flowers, which are often pollinated by insects.

Scaphyglottis unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
