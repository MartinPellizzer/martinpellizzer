Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. violacea

Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, violet-hued leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with hairy stems and is often found in open grasslands and chaparral.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
