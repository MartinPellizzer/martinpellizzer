Scrophularia eggersii

Scrophularia eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' figwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to poor soils.

Scrophularia eggersii is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar structural features.
