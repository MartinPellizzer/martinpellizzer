Syzygium lilacinum

Syzygium lilacinum, commonly known as the pink apple or lilac apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Syzygium lilacinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
