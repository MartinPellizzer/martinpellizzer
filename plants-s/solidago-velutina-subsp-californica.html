Solidago velutina subsp. californica

Solidago velutina subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
