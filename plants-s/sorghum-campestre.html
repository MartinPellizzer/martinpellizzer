Sorghum campestre

Sorghum campestre, commonly known as yellow cupid, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sorghum campestre is often found in the eastern and central United States.
