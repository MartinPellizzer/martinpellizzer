Setaria megaphylla

Setaria megaphylla, commonly known as giant foxtail, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and distinctive bristly seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its rapid growth and high seed production make it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
