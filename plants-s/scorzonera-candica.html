Scorzonera candica

Scorzonera candica, commonly known as the white scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated tubers that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera candica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
