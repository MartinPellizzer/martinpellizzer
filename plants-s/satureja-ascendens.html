Satureja ascendens

Satureja ascendens, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja ascendens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
