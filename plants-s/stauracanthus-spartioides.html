Stauracanthus spartioides

Stauracanthus spartioides, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in dry conditions.

The plant's latex sap contains milky substances, which can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
