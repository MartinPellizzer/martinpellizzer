Senecio libertatis

Senecio libertatis, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio libertatis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
