Suaeda iranshahrii var. arabica

Suaeda iranshahrii var. arabica, commonly known as the Persian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to extreme environmental conditions. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in dry climates.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
