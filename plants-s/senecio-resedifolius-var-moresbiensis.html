Senecio resedifolius var. moresbiensis

Senecio resedifolius var. moresbiensis, commonly known as the Moresby's ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or wetland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
