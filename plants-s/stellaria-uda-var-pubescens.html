Stellaria uda var. pubescens

Stellaria uda var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy starwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name and help in identifying it in the field. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often hairy foliage.
