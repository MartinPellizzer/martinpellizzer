Solanum calochroma

Solanum calochroma, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bisexual and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which matures to a dark purple color, is edible and has a slightly bitter taste.
