Senecio neobakeri

Senecio neobakeri, commonly known as Baker's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, seed-filled structures.
