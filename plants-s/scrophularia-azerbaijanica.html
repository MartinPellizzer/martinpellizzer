Scrophularia azerbaijanica

Scrophularia azerbaijanica, commonly known as Azerbaijani figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia azerbaijanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
