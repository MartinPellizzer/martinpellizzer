Senna stipulacea

Senna stipulacea, commonly known as the golden senna, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, leaf-like stipules, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Senna stipulacea is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
