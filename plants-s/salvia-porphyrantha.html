Salvia porphyrantha

Salvia porphyrantha, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia porphyrantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
