Scorzonera austriaca var. curvata

Scorzonera austriaca var. curvata, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which are arranged in a composite head. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera austriaca var. curvata thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
