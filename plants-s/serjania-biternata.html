Serjania biternata

Serjania biternata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces yellow, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to starfruit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
