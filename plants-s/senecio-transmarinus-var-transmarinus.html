Senecio transmarinus var. transmarinus

Senecio transmarinus var. transmarinus, commonly known as the Transmarine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
