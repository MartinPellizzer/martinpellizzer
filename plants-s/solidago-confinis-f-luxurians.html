Solidago confinis f. luxurians

Solidago confinis f. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant goldenrod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
