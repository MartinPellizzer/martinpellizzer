Senecio integerrimus var. exaltatus

Senecio integerrimus var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall yellow gentian, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it one of the most visually prominent plants in its habitat. The plant features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes, often in arid or semi-arid environments.
