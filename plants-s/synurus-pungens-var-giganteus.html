Synurus pungens var. giganteus

Synurus pungens var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant spiky plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, spiky leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an attractive feature. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
