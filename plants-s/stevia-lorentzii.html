Stevia lorentzii

Stevia lorentzii, commonly known as Paraguay tea, is a perennial shrub native to the Parana River basin in South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Stevia lorentzii is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
