Scrophularia erzincanica

Scrophularia erzincanica, commonly known as the Erzincan scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
