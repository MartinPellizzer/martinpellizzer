Sparganium japonicum

Sparganium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese bur reed, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines, which help in anchoring it in muddy substrates. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces distinctive, spiny bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Sparganium japonicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
