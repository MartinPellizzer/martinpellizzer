Schoepfia quintuplinervis

Schoepfia quintuplinervis, commonly known as the five-veined spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic, a common feature among members of this family.
