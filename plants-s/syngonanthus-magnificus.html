Syngonanthus magnificus

Syngonanthus magnificus, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and provide a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms. Syngonanthus magnificus thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
