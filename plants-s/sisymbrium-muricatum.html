Sisymbrium muricatum

Sisymbrium muricatum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged stems and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
