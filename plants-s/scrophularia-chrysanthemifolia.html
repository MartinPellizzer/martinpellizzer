Scrophularia chrysanthemifolia

Scrophularia chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the chrysanthemum scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia chrysanthemifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
