Stylidium albolilacinum

Stylidium albolilacinum, commonly known as the white and pink spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Stylidium albolilacinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
