Solanum bonariense var. villaricense

Solanum bonariense var. villaricense, commonly known as the Argentine nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
