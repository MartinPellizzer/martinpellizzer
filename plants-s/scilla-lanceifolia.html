Scilla lanceifolia

Scilla lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and rock garden settings. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, lanceolate leaves. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Scilla lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
