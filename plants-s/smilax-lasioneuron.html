Smilax lasioneuron

Smilax lasioneuron, commonly known as the red-tipped smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red-veined underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
