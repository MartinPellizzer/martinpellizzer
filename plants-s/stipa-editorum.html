Stipa editorum

Stipa editorum, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tall, cylindrical seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its silvery sheen.

Stipa editorum is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
