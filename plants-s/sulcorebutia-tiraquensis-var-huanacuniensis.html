Sulcorebutia tiraquensis var. huanacuniensis

Sulcorebutia tiraquensis var. huanacuniensis, commonly known as the Huanacuniensis Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange, during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
