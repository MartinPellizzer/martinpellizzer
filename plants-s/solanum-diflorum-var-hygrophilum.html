Solanum diflorum var. hygrophilum

Solanum diflorum var. hygrophilum, commonly known as the water tomato, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet to purple in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
