Sedum luchuanicum

Sedum luchuanicum, commonly known as the Luchuan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in mountainous regions, where it adapts well to poor soil conditions.

Sedum luchuanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
