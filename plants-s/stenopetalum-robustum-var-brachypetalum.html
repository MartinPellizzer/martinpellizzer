Stenopetalum robustum var. brachypetalum

Stenopetalum robustum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled rock fig, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive, fleshy, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.
