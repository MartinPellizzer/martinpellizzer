Satureja matthewsii

Satureja matthewsii, commonly known as Matthew's savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its aromatic foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other members of the sage family.

Satureja matthewsii is valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its ornamental appeal.
