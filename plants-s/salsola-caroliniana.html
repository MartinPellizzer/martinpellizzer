Salsola caroliniana

Salsola caroliniana, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its extensive root system allows it to survive in poor soil conditions and recover quickly after disturbances.

Salsola caroliniana is often considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread prolifically.
