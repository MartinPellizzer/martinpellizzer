Salvia amasiaca

Salvia amasiaca, commonly known as the "Mexican sage," is a perennial herb native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. Salvia amasiaca is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
