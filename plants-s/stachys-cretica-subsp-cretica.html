Stachys cretica subsp. cretica

Stachys cretica subsp.

cretica, commonly known as redstem betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that often turn reddish in color.

The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
