Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pedicellata

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered sandpaper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
