Stevenorchis satyrioides

Stevenorchis satyrioides, commonly known as the satyr orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark veins, aiding in pollination by bees and other insects.

Stevenorchis satyrioides is a rare and protected species, valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
