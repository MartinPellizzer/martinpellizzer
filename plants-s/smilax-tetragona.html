Smilax tetragona

Smilax tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Smilax tetragona is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
