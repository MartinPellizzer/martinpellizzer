Stevia incanescens

Stevia incanescens, commonly known as the white stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested and dried for use in food and beverages.

Stevia incanescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
