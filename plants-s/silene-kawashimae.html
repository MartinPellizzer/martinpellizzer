Silene kawashimae

Silene kawashimae, commonly known as the Kawashima's campion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene kawashimae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
