Sorbus salwinensis

Sorbus salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in Myanmar, Thailand, and China. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

The tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters and is often found in mixed forests and along stream banks.
