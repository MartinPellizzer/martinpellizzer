Symphyotrichum × priceae

Symphyotrichum × priceae, commonly known as the eastern coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It has hairy, alternate leaves and produces a conical cluster of seeds at the center of each flower.

Symphyotrichum × priceae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
