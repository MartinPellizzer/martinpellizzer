Saugetia fasciculata

Saugetia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in spike-like clusters.

Saugetia fasciculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
