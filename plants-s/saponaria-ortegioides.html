Saponaria ortegioides

Saponaria ortegioides, commonly known as the Ortegian soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant contains saponins, which give it its name and make it useful in traditional herbal remedies.
