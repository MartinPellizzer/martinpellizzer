Selinum caucasicum

Selinum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Its bulbous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.
