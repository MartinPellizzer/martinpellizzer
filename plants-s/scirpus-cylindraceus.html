Scirpus cylindraceus

Scirpus cylindraceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base.

Scirpus cylindraceus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
