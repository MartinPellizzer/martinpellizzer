Solanum hystrix

Solanum hystrix, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Solanum hystrix belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
