Syzygium hooglandii

Syzygium hooglandii, commonly known as the Hoogland's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance. Syzygium hooglandii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
