Statice macloviana

Statice macloviana, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Statice macloviana is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
