Seruneum hispidum

Seruneum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy seruneum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "hispidum," meaning hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
