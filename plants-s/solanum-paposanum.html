Solanum paposanum

Solanum paposanum, commonly known as the Paposanum nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
