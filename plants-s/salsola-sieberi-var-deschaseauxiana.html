Salsola sieberi var. deschaseauxiana

Salsola sieberi var. deschaseauxiana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are extensive, allowing it to anchor in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

This species is well adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.
