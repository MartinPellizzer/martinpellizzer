Senecio alatus var. nudicaulis

Senecio alatus var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed lion's ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairless, giving it its varietal name "nudicaulis.". The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
