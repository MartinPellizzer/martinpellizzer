Sasa kurilensis f. kiakebono

Sasa kurilensis f. kiakebono, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a structural material. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
