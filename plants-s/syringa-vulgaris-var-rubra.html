Syringa vulgaris var. rubra

Syringa vulgaris var. rubra, commonly known as the red-blossom lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscaping.
