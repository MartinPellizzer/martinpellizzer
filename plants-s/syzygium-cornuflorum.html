Syzygium cornuflorum

Syzygium cornuflorum, commonly known as the horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped bracts that surround its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

Syzygium cornuflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
