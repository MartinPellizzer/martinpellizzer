Spergularia fimbriata var. glabrifolia

Spergularia fimbriata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-fringed sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows low to the ground, with succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers have five petals and are often surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils.
