Senecio nudicaulis

Senecio nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise directly from the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
