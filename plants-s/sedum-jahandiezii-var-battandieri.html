Sedum jahandiezii var. battandieri

Sedum jahandiezii var. battandieri, commonly known as the Persian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically blue-green and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
