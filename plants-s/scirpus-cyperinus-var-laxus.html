Scirpus cyperinus var. laxus

Scirpus cyperinus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus cyperinus var. laxus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
