Senecio steudelioides

Senecio steudelioides, commonly known as the blue-green groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, blue-green flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Senecio steudelioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
