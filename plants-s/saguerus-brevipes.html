Saguerus brevipes

Saguerus brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed saguaro, is a unique cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems that grow in a more compact form compared to the typical saguaro. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it relies on its extensive root system to store water.

Saguerus brevipes plays an important ecological role in its arid habitat, providing shelter and food for desert wildlife.
