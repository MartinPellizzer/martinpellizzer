Salvia jacalana

Salvia jacalana, commonly known as red sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia jacalana is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
