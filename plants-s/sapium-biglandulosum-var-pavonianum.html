Sapium biglandulosum var. pavonianum

Sapium biglandulosum var. pavonianum, commonly known as the Pavonian sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red, spiky seed pods.

It is often found in moist, bottomland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
