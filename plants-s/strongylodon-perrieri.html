Strongylodon perrieri

Strongylodon perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often brightly colored fruits. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing supports with its aerial roots. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

The fruit, which is edible, plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various animals.
