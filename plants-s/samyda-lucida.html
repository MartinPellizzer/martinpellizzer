Samyda lucida

Samyda lucida, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a luminous appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Samyda lucida is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
