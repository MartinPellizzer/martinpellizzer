Smilax rubens

Smilax rubens, commonly known as the red smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves that are typically light green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax rubens thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its hardy nature and distinctive appearance make it a notable plant in the botanical landscape of its native habitat.
