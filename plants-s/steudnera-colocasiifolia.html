Steudnera colocasiifolia

Steudnera colocasiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of edible, starchy tubers. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Steudnera colocasiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in certain regions.
