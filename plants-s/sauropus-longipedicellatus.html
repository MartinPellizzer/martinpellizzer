Sauropus longipedicellatus

Sauropus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-fruited sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
