Sedum dasyphyllum subsp. granatense

Sedum dasyphyllum subsp.

granatense, commonly known as the granate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
