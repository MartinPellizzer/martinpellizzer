Spiraea muliensis

Spiraea muliensis, commonly known as the Muli spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers, which range from white to pink, are borne in cylindrical inflorescences, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Spiraea muliensis is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
