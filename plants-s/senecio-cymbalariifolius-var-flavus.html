Senecio cymbalariifolius var. flavus

Senecio cymbalariifolius var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
