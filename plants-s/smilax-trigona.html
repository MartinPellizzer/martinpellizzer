Smilax trigona

Smilax trigona, commonly known as the three-lobed smilax, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax trigona is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species.
