Stipa readeri

Stipa readeri, commonly known as the Reader's needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Stipa readeri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
