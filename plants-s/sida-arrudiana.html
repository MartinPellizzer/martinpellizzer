Sida arrudiana

Sida arrudiana, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.

Sida arrudiana is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
