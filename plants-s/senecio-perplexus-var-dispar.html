Senecio perplexus var. dispar

Senecio perplexus var. dispar, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are usually yellow, with ray florets that may vary in color depending on the specific variety.
