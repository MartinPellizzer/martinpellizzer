Stachys × delgadoi

Stachys × delgadoi, commonly known as the hybrid mint or Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Stachys × delgadoi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental plant.
