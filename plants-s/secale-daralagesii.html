Secale daralagesii

Secale daralagesii, commonly known as the Armenian rye.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the Caucasus region. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
