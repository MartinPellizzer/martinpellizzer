Saussurea auriculata

Saussurea auriculata, commonly known as the Auriculate Hawk's Beard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structure. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately divided with auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
