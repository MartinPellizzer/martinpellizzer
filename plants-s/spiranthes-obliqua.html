Spiranthes obliqua

Spiranthes obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spiral pattern that gives it its common name.

Spiranthes obliqua is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
