Selaginella stenostachya

Selaginella stenostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often narrow and linear. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the ends of the stems and produce spores.

Selaginella stenostachya is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
