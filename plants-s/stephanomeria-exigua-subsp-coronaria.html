Stephanomeria exigua subsp. coronaria

Stephanomeria exigua subsp.

coronaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitecrown, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a narrow, cylindrical shape.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
