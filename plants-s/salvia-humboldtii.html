Salvia humboldtii

Salvia humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's sage, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
