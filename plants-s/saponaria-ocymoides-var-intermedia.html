Saponaria ocymoides var. intermedia

Saponaria ocymoides var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate soaproot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that produce clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and sessile. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its mild sap, which has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
