Solanum ulei

Solanum ulei, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for cultivation or consumption.
