Senecio diversidentatus

Senecio diversidentatus, commonly known as the toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and distinctive, toothed leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
