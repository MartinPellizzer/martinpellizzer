Selaginella smithii

Selaginella smithii, commonly known as the Smith's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in the southeastern United States.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella smithii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
