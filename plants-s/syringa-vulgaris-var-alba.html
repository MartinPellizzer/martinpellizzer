Syringa vulgaris var. alba

Syringa vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The white lilac is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
