Saussurea bodinieri

Saussurea bodinieri, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibet.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine. Its roots are often harvested for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
