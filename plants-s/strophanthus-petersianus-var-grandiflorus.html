Strophanthus petersianus var. grandiflorus

Strophanthus petersianus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and has a woody stem, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The flowers are also known for their medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
