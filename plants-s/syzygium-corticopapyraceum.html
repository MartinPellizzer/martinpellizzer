Syzygium corticopapyraceum

Syzygium corticopapyraceum, commonly known as the paperbark apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
