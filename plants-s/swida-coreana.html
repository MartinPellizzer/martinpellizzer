Swida coreana

Swida coreana, commonly known as Korean swida, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
