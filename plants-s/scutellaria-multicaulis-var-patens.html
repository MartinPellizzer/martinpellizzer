Scutellaria multicaulis var. patens

Scutellaria multicaulis var. patens, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
