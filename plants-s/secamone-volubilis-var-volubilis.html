Secamone volubilis var. volubilis

Secamone volubilis var. volubilis, commonly known as the creeping secamone, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and trail along the ground or other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
