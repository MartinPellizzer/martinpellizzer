Senecio fruticulosus

Senecio fruticulosus, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
