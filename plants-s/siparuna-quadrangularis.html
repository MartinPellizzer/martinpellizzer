Siparuna quadrangularis

Siparuna quadrangularis, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Siparuna quadrangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
