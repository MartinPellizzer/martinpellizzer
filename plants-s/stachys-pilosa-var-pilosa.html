Stachys pilosa var. pilosa

Stachys pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves provide a unique texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
