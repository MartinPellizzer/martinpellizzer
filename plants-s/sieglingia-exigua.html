Sieglingia exigua

Sieglingia exigua, commonly known as the slender sieve-plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. Sieglingia exigua is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species of healthy wetland ecosystems.
