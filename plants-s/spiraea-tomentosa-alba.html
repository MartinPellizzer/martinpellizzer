Spiraea tomentosa alba

Spiraea tomentosa alba, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea tomentosa alba is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
