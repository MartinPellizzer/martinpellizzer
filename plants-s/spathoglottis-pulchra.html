Spathoglottis pulchra

Spathoglottis pulchra, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its striking, pinkish-white flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, which is a distinctive feature of the orchid family.

Spathoglottis pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
