Sorbus austriaca subsp. serpentini

Sorbus austriaca subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's bone or serpent's thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, thorn-like fruits that resemble the bones of a serpent, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
