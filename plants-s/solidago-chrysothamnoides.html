Solidago chrysothamnoides

Solidago chrysothamnoides, commonly known as goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago chrysothamnoides plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
