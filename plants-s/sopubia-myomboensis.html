Sopubia myomboensis

Sopubia myomboensis, commonly known as the myombo woodland plant, is a shrub native to the miombo woodlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to dry, seasonal climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Sopubia myomboensis is often found in association with other drought-tolerant flora in its ecosystem.
