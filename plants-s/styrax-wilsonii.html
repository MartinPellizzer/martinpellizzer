Styrax wilsonii

Styrax wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Styrax wilsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
