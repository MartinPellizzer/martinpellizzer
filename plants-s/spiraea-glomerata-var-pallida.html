Spiraea glomerata var. pallida

Spiraea glomerata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue during the growing season. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and early blooming habit make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
