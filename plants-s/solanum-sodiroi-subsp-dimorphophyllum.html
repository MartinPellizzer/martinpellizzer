Solanum sodiroi subsp. dimorphophyllum

Solanum sodiroi subsp.

dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the two-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are usually elliptical and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cooler, shaded environments.
