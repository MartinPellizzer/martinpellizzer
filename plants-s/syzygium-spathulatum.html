Syzygium spathulatum

Syzygium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sour fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Syzygium spathulatum is also valued for its timber, which is durable and suitable for construction and furniture-making.
