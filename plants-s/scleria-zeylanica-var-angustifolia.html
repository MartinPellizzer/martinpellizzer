Scleria zeylanica var. angustifolia

Scleria zeylanica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, bearing small, white or pale blue flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
