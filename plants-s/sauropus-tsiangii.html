Sauropus tsiangii

Sauropus tsiangii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by greenish-yellow capsules containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
