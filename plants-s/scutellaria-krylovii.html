Scutellaria krylovii

Scutellaria krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
