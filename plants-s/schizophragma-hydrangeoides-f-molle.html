Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. molle

Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaf hydrangea, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by showy, blue or pink berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.
