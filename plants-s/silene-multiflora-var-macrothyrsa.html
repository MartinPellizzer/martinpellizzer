Silene multiflora var. macrothyrsa

Silene multiflora var. macrothyrsa, commonly known as the large-thrummed catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features hairy, branched stems that grow up to 30 inches tall, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
