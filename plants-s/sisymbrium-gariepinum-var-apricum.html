Sisymbrium gariepinum var. apricum

Sisymbrium gariepinum var. apricum, commonly known as the apricot mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated pods typical of the mustard family.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
