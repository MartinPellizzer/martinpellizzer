Silene carnosa

Silene carnosa, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Silene carnosa is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
