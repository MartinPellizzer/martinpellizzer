Spartina leiantha

Spartina leiantha, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding. Its rhizomes form dense mats that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Spartina leiantha plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
