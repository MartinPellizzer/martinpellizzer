Silphium laciniatum

Silphium laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf silphium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow to orange flowers in the summer. Silphium laciniatum is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
