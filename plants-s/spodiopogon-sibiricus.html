Spodiopogon sibiricus

Spodiopogon sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It is adapted to harsh environments with low temperatures and limited moisture.

Spodiopogon sibiricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
