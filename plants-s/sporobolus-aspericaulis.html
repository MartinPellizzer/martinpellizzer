Sporobolus aspericaulis

Sporobolus aspericaulis, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a grass species native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and rough, bristly leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Sporobolus aspericaulis is drought-tolerant and is used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
