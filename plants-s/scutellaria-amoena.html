Scutellaria amoena

Scutellaria amoena, commonly known as the blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria amoena is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
