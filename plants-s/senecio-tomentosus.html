Senecio tomentosus

Senecio tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
