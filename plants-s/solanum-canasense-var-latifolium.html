Solanum canasense var. latifolium

Solanum canasense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.

This species is often found in humid, lowland areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
