Schoenoxiphium filiforme

Schoenoxiphium filiforme, commonly known as the slender false foxglove, is a member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Schoenoxiphium filiforme is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
