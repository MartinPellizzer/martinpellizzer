Sycomorus antiquorum

Sycomorus antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient sycamore, is a member of the Moraceae family, characterized by its unique morphology and ecological significance.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and a distinctive bark that exfoliates in patches. The tree produces small, fig-like fruits enclosed in a syconium, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sycomorus antiquorum is native to parts of Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
