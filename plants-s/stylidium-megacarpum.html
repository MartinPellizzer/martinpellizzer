Stylidium megacarpum

Stylidium megacarpum, commonly known as the giant spurred orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant features a short, fleshy stem and produces large, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat.

Stylidium megacarpum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient and visually distinctive plant.
