Sida verticillata

Sida verticillata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including agricultural fields.

Sida verticillata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
