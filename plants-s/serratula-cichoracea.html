Serratula cichoracea

Serratula cichoracea, commonly known as the bitter cichory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

Serratula cichoracea is known for its bitter taste and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
