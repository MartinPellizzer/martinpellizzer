Solanum ellipsoideibaccatum var. ficilobum

Solanum ellipsoideibaccatum var. ficilobum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, elongated berries, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
