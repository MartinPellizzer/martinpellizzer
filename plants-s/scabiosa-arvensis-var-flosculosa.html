Scabiosa arvensis var. flosculosa

Scabiosa arvensis var. flosculosa, commonly known as the clustered scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
