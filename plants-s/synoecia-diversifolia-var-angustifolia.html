Synoecia diversifolia var. angustifolia

Synoecia diversifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf synoecia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale green, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
