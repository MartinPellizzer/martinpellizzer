Strychnos depauperata

Strychnos depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's strychnos, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and berries.
