Stryphnodendron glandulosum

Stryphnodendron glandulosum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, glandular hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences.

It produces legume pods that contain small, hard seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
