Sloanea pseudogranulosa

Sloanea pseudogranulosa, commonly known as the granulated sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and thrives in partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
