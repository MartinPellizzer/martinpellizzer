Salvia officinalis var. crispa

Salvia officinalis var. crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled sage, is a variety of the common sage plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.

This variety is often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
