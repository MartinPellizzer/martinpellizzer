Senecio mollendoensis

Senecio mollendoensis, commonly known as the Bolivian groundseller, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive shape that resembles a small dandelion.

Senecio mollendoensis is often used in traditional medicine by local communities for its purported health benefits.
