Solanum syringifolium

Solanum syringifolium, commonly known as the blue bell solanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Solanum syringifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
