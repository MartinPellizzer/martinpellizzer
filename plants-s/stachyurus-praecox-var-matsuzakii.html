Stachyurus praecox var. matsuzakii

Stachyurus praecox var. matsuzakii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, especially in the spring. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow in the autumn.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant form and seasonal display, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
