Solanum mapiriense

Solanum mapiriense, commonly known as the Mapiri nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries, which are typically edible but may contain toxic compounds.

Solanum mapiriense is often found in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in shaded undergrowth.
