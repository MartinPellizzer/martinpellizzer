Sida maura

Sida maura, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline or brackish environments. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida maura is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in coastal ecosystems.
