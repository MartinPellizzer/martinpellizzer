Santolina chamaecyparissus var. diversifolia

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated santolina, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage with distinctive variegation, often displaying white and yellow markings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
