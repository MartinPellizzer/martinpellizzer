Salvia Pratensis Subsp. Haematodes

Salvia pratensis subsp. haematodes is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant known for its vivid red and purple blooms. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections. The plant's name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively rare in the wild, making conservation efforts all the more important.
