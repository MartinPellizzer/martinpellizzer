Sonchus neglectus

Sonchus neglectus, commonly known as common sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are rough to the touch and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, appearing in the summer months.

Sonchus neglectus is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
