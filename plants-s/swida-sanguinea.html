Swida sanguinea

Swida sanguinea, commonly known as the red swida, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Swida sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
