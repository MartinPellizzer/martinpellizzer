Sickingia ecuadorensis

Sickingia ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorean ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is appreciated both for ornamental purposes and its resilience in challenging environments.
