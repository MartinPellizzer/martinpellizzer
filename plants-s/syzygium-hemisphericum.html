Syzygium hemisphericum

Syzygium hemisphericum, commonly known as the hemispherical starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, five-lobed, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often blooming in clusters.
