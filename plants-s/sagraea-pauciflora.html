Sagraea pauciflora

Sagraea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sagraea, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sagraea pauciflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
