Saccoloma acuminatum

Saccoloma acuminatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and its whorled arrangement of tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
