Schizoglossum bidens subsp. galpinii

Schizoglossum bidens subsp.

galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's false dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
