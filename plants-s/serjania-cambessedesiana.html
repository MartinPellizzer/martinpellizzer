Serjania cambessedesiana

Serjania cambessedesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
