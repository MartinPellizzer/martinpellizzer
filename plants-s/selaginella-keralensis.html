Selaginella keralensis

Selaginella keralensis, commonly known as the Keralan spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. The plant features a distinctive stem with spirally arranged leaves, and its sporophylls are elongated and bear spores on their upper surfaces. Selaginella keralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It plays a role in maintaining soil stability and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
