Salvia vazquezii subsp. tancitaroensis

Salvia vazquezii subsp.

tancitaroensis, commonly known as the Tancitaro sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
