Sida corynocarpa

Sida corynocarpa, commonly known as the silky sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pale green flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round, green to brown seeds.

Sida corynocarpa is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.
