Senecio lanceus

Senecio lanceus, commonly known as the lanceleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio lanceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
