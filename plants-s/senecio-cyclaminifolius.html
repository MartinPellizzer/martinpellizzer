Senecio cyclaminifolius

Senecio cyclaminifolius, commonly known as the cyclamen leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that resemble those of cyclamen, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
