Scleria diffusa

Scleria diffusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Scleria diffusa is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
