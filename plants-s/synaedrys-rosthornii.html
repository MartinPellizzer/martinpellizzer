Synaedrys rosthornii

Synaedrys rosthornii, commonly known as the red-stemmed thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often bear red or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Synaedrys rosthornii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
