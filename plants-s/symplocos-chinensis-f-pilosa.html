Symplocos chinensis f. pilosa

Symplocos chinensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Chinese waxberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
