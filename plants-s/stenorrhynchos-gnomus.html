Stenorrhynchos gnomus

Stenorrhynchos gnomus, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a few leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a unique floral structure with a narrow, spurred lip.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
