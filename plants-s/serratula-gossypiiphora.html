Serratula gossypiiphora

Serratula gossypiiphora, commonly known as the cotton thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Serratula gossypiiphora is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Asteraceae family.
