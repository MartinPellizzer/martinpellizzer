Sorbus taishanensis

Sorbus taishanensis, commonly known as the Taishan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by small, red berries that are an important food source for birds.

It is typically found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
