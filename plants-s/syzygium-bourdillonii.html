Syzygium bourdillonii

Syzygium bourdillonii, commonly known as the Bourdillon's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium bourdillonii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
