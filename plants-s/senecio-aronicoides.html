Senecio aronicoides

Senecio aronicoides, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
