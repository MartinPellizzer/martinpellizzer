Salvia intercedens

Salvia intercedens, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is typically gray-green and has a distinctive fuzzy texture.

Salvia intercedens is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
