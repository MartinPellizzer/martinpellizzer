Scutellaria viarum

Scutellaria viarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine meadows.

Scutellaria viarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
