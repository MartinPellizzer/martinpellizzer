Sicydium tamnifolium

Sicydium tamnifolium, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a rounded, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

Sicydium tamnifolium is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
