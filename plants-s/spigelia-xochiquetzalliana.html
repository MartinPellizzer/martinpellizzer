Spigelia xochiquetzalliana

Spigelia xochiquetzalliana, commonly known as the Mexican candlewood, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Spigelia anthelmia and Spigelia splendens.

It is native to the southeastern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly waxy texture, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Spigelia xochiquetzalliana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
