Sarcoyucca treculiana

Sarcoyucca treculiana, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
