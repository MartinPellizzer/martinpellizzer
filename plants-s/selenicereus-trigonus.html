Selenicereus trigonus

Selenicereus trigonus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bats and moths, which are active during the evening.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking, ephemeral blooms and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
