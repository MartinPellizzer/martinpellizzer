Sorbus hardeggensis

Sorbus hardeggensis, commonly known as the Hardegg mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in rocky or mountainous environments and is often found at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
