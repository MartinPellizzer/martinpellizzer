Strobilanthes attenuata subsp. nepalensis

Strobilanthes attenuata subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal flameflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, often in forest understories or along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
