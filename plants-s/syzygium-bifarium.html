Syzygium bifarium

Syzygium bifarium, commonly known as the white tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling tamarinds. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
