Stenomesson chilense

Stenomesson chilense, commonly known as the Chilean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and produce seeds with parachute-like structures.

Stenomesson chilense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
