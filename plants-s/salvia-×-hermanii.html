Salvia × hermanii

Salvia × hermanii, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically tubular, adapted for pollination by bees and other insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Salvia × hermanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators to the garden.
