Salvia rhytidea

Salvia rhytidea, commonly known as the silver sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and tall spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and drought-tolerant gardens.
