Scandix pecten

Scandix pecten, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in saline environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Scandix pecten is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
