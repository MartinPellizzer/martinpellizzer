Sonchus esperanzae

Sonchus esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for insects and serving as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
