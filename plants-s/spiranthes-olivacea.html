Spiranthes olivacea

Spiranthes olivacea, commonly known as the olive swamp twayblade, is a species of orchid native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a slender, erect stem and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant lacks true leaves, instead having reduced, scale-like structures called bracts. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, giving the plant its characteristic name.
