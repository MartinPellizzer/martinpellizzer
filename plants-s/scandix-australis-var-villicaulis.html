Scandix australis var. villicaulis

Scandix australis var. villicaulis, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
