Sulcorebutia tarijensis subsp. samaensis

Sulcorebutia tarijensis subsp.

samaensis, commonly known as the Tarija sun rose, is a succulent plant native to Bolivia. It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
