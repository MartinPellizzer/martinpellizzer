Scirpus cartilagineus

Scirpus cartilagineus, commonly known as the cartilaginous bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in two tiers. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus cartilagineus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
