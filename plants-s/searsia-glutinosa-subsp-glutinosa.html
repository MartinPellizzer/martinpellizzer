Searsia glutinosa subsp. glutinosa

Searsia glutinosa subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky serissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are sticky to the touch due to a resinous substance.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
