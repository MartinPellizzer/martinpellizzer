Solidago porteri

Solidago porteri, commonly known as the Porteri's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Solidago porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
