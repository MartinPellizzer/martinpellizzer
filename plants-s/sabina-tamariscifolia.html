Sabina tamariscifolia

Sabina tamariscifolia, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and blue-gray foliage, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is well adapted to poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry landscapes.
