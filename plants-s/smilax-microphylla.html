Smilax microphylla

Smilax microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf catbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax microphylla is known for its prickly stems and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
