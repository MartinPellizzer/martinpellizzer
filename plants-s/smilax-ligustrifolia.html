Smilax ligustrifolia

Smilax ligustrifolia, commonly known as the Virginia arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its roots are thick and tuberous, which help it survive in various soil conditions.

Smilax ligustrifolia is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
