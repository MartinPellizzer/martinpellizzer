Staphylea bumalda var. glabra

Staphylea bumalda var. glabra, commonly known as smooth staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in panicle clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
