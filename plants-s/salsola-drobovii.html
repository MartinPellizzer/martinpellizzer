Salsola drobovii

Salsola drobovii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Salsola drobovii is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
