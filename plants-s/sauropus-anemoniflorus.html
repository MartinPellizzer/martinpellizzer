Sauropus anemoniflorus

Sauropus anemoniflorus, commonly known as the flame tree or the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic visual appearance. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
