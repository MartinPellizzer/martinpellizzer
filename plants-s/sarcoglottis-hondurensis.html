Sarcoglottis hondurensis

Sarcoglottis hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras sarcoglottis, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
