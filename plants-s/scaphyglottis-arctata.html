Scaphyglottis arctata

Scaphyglottis arctata, commonly known as the bear's paw orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive, fleshy, heart-shaped leaf that resembles a bear's paw, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scaphyglottis arctata is valued for its unique appearance and is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
