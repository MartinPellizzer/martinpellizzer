Strumaria chilensis

Strumaria chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

Strumaria chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
