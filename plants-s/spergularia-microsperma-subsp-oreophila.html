Spergularia microsperma subsp. oreophila

Spergularia microsperma subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the alpine sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and features tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid and high-altitude areas.
