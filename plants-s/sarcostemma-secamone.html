Sarcostemma secamone

Sarcostemma secamone, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and milky sap. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are woody and can become branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Sarcostemma secamone is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
