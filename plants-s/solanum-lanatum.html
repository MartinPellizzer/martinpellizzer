Solanum lanatum

Solanum lanatum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically white or pale green when mature. Solanum lanatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.
