Senecio lautus var. raoulii

Senecio lautus var. raoulii, commonly known as the Raouli's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
