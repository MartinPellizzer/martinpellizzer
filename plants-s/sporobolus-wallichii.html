Sporobolus wallichii

Sporobolus wallichii, commonly known as the Indian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Sporobolus wallichii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its use in traditional medicine.
