Senecio pimpinellifolius

Senecio pimpinellifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Senecio pimpinellifolius is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
