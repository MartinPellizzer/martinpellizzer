Sasa suwekoana

Sasa suwekoana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Sasa suwekoana is valued for its strong, durable culms, which are used in traditional crafts and construction.
