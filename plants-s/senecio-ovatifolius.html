Senecio ovatifolius

Senecio ovatifolius, commonly known as the ovate-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate, with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive, often aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassland habitats.
