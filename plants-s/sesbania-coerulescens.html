Sesbania coerulescens

Sesbania coerulescens, commonly known as the blue sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Sesbania coerulescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
