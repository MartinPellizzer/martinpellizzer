Styrax vestitus

Styrax vestitus, commonly known as the woolly styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Styrax vestitus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
