Solanum angustiflorum

Solanum angustiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum angustiflorum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
