Salvia rubescens subsp. truxillensis

Salvia rubescens subsp.

truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis sage, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet to red flowers in spikes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
