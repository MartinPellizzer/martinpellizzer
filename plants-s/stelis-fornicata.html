Stelis fornicata

Stelis fornicata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stelis fornicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
