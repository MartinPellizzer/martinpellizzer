Stipa carettei

Stipa carettei, commonly known as Caretta's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa carettei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
