Satureja ericifolia subsp. meridialis

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

meridialis, commonly known as southern mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.
