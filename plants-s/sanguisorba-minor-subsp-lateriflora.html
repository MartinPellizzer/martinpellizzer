Sanguisorba minor subsp. lateriflora

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

lateriflora, commonly known as redroot filaree, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features upright, slender stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a reddish tinge on the underside.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
