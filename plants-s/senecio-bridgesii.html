Senecio bridgesii

Senecio bridgesii, commonly known as the Orange River Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Senecio bridgesii is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
