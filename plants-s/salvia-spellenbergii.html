Salvia spellenbergii

Salvia spellenbergii, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Salvia spellenbergii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
