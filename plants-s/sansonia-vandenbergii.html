Sansonia vandenbergii

Sansonia vandenbergii, commonly known as the Vandenberg's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Sansonia vandenbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
