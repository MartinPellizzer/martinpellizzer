Scirpus diffusus

Scirpus diffusus, commonly known as soft-stemmed bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots help stabilize soil in marshy environments, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus diffusus is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
