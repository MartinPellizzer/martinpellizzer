Saraca indica

Saraca indica, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Saraca indica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
