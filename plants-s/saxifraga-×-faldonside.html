Saxifraga × faldonside

Saxifraga × faldonside, commonly known as the Faldonside saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus.

It is native to the Scottish Highlands and is typically found in rocky, alpine environments. The plant features compact, cushion-like growth with bright green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga × faldonside is well adapted to harsh climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in rock gardens.
