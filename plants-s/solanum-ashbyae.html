Solanum ashbyae

Solanum ashbyae, commonly known as Ashby's nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum ashbyae thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
