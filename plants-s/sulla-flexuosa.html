Sulla flexuosa

Sulla flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Sulla flexuosa is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
