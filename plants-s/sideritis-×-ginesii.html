Sideritis × ginesii

Sideritis × ginesii, commonly known as the hybrid Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Sideritis × ginesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
