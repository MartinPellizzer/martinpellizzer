Stenodon gracilis

Stenodon gracilis, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the muddy bottom of ponds and lakes.

Stenodon gracilis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
