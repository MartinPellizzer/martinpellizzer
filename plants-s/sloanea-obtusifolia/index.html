Sloanea Obtusifolia

Sloanea ash, scientifically known as Sloanea obtusifolia, is a unique and lesser-known plant species native to the cloud forests of South America. This evergreen shrub or small tree is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for biodiversity conservation. However, habitat loss and deforestation pose serious threats to its survival.
