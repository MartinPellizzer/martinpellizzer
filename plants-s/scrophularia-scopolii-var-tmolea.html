Scrophularia scopolii var. tmolea

Scrophularia scopolii var. tmolea, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features erect, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
