Sansevieria conduplicata

Sansevieria conduplicata, commonly known as the "Mother-in-Law's Tongue," is a species of snake plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with prominent, lighter green transverse bands. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are stiff and fibrous, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Sansevieria conduplicata is also noted for its air-purifying properties, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
