Senecio riddellii

Senecio riddellii, commonly known as the Riddell's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
