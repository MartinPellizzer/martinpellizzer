Siparuna grandiflora

Siparuna grandiflora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and emit a sweet fragrance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a dark green color. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
