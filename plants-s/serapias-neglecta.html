Serapias neglecta

Serapias neglecta, commonly known as the neglected orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces raceme-like inflorescences with yellow-orange petals and a prominent lip. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Serapias neglecta is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
