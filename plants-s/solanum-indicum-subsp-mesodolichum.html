Solanum indicum subsp. mesodolichum

Solanum indicum subsp.

mesodolichum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, violet flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it has simple, alternate leaves and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
