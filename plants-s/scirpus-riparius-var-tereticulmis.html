Scirpus riparius var. tereticulmis

Scirpus riparius var. tereticulmis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush found in wetland environments.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming part of the vegetation in marshes and along lake shores.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
