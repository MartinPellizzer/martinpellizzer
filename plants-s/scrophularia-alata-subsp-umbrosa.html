Scrophularia alata subsp. umbrosa

Scrophularia alata subsp.

umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
