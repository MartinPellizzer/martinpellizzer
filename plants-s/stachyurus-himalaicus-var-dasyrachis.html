Stachyurus himalaicus var. dasyrachis

Stachyurus himalaicus var. dasyrachis, commonly known as the Himalayan stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, raceme-like clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
