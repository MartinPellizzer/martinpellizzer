Sparganium minimum var. flaccidum

Sparganium minimum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the slender bur reed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, seed-bearing structures called burrs, which are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
