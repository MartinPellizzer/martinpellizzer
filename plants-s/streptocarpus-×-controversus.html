Streptocarpus × controversus

Streptocarpus × controversus, commonly known as the hybrid streptocarpus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Streptocarpus section Streptocarpus and Streptocarpus section Lepidosperma.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant typically produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This hybrid is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
