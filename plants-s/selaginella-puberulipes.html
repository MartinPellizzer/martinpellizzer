Selaginella puberulipes

Selaginella puberulipes, commonly known as the hairy spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila that contain spores for reproduction.

Selaginella puberulipes is valued for its resilience and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
