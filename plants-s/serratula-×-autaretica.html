Serratula × autaretica

Serratula × autaretica, commonly known as the hybrid ragwort, is a composite plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, often branching stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
