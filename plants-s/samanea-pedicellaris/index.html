Samanea Pedicellaris

Cupuaçu tree, scientifically known as Samanea pedicellaris, is a tropical species native to the Amazon basin of South America. This tree is renowned for producing the cupuaçu fruit, which is prized for its rich flavor and high nutritional value. Growing up to 20 meters tall, it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. Its distinctive appearance features broad leaves and fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. The tree plays a vital role in both ecological systems and local economies.
