Sphaerocionium myriocarpum

Sphaerocionium myriocarpum, commonly known as the many-seeded globular acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, hard seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange sarcocarp, which is adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are small, phyllodes-like structures that reduce water loss, and it has a deep root system for accessing groundwater.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
