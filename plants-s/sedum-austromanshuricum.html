Sedum austromanshuricum

Sedum austromanshuricum, commonly known as the Korean stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, usually in late summer. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
