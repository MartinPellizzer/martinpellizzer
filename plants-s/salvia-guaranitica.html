Salvia guaranitica

Salvia guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian sage or purple sage, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of dark violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia guaranitica is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
