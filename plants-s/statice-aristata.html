Statice aristata

Statice aristata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes.

Statice aristata is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
