Schedonorus erectus var. suberectus

Schedonorus erectus var. suberectus, commonly known as the upright meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are usually green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
