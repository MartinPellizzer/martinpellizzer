Salvia azurea subsp. pitcheri

Salvia azurea subsp.

pitcheri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
