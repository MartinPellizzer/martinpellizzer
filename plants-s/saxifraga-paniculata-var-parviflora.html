Saxifraga paniculata var. parviflora

Saxifraga paniculata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a succulent texture, adapted to withstand harsh conditions. Its showy, clustered inflorescence consists of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga paniculata var. parviflora is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive floral display.
