Statice auriculae-ursifolia

Statice auriculae-ursifolia, commonly known as bear-ear statice, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often resembling bear ears, which is reflected in its common name.

Statice auriculae-ursifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
