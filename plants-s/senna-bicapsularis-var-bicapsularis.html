Senna bicapsularis var. bicapsularis

Senna bicapsularis var. bicapsularis, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a slender, two-valved pod that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
