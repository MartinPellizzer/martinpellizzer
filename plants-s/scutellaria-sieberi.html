Scutellaria sieberi

Scutellaria sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Scutellaria sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
