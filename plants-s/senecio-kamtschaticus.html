Senecio kamtschaticus

Senecio kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Kamtschaticus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
