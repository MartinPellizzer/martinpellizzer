Sabicea globifera

Sabicea globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its distinctive globular inflorescences. The plant features pinnate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The seeds are encased in hairy pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
