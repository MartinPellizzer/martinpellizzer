Spiraea fritschiana var. microgyna

Spiraea fritschiana var. microgyna, commonly known as the small-fruited spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, forming dense clusters along the stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to rock gardens and borders.
