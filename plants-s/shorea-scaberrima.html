Shorea scaberrima

Shorea scaberrima, commonly known as the mangrove ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The plant thrives in coastal and brackish water environments, often forming part of mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits.

Shorea scaberrima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
