Sasa megalophylla f. aureovariegata

Sasa megalophylla f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a distinctive yellow and green variegation pattern on its leaves. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, with tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for decorative purposes.
