Sorghum exothecum

Sorghum exothecum, commonly known as the outer protective layer of the sorghum seed, is a specialized structure composed of lignified cells.

It serves as a barrier against environmental stressors and pathogens, contributing to the seed's durability. The exothecum is part of the pericarp, which develops from the outermost layer of the ovary. It plays a crucial role in the dispersal and germination process of the sorghum plant.

Its thick, fibrous nature provides structural support and aids in water retention.
