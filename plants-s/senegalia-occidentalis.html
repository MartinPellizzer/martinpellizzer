Senegalia occidentalis

Senegalia occidentalis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds, and it is well adapted to drought conditions. It has deep roots that allow it to access groundwater, making it a valuable species in desert ecosystems.

Senegalia occidentalis is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
