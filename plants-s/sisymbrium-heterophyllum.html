Sisymbrium heterophyllum

Sisymbrium heterophyllum, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
