Simocheilus puberulus

Simocheilus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy water-plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, with elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic habitats.

Simocheilus puberulus plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
