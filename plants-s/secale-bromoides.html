Secale bromoides

Secale bromoides, commonly known as false rye or bromegrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to yellowish flowers. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
