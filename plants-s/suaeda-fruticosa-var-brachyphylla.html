Suaeda fruticosa var. brachyphylla

Suaeda fruticosa var. brachyphylla, commonly known as dwarf saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It possesses succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration, aiding survival in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

The species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in phytoremediation to reclaim degraded lands.
