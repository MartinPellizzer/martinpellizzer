Scrophularia orientalis

Scrophularia orientalis, commonly known as oriental figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
