Stachys subnuda

Stachys subnuda, commonly known as the naked stachys, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Stachys subnuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
