Solanum muricatum

Solanum muricatum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red or purple berries that resemble a pineapple in appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thick, hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, containing numerous small, edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
