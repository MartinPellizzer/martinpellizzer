Senecio vegetus

Senecio vegetus, commonly known as the upright groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Senecio vegetus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
