Solidago canadensis f. procera

Solidago canadensis f. procera, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces abundant yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called panicles. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and other open habitats across the eastern United States.
