Syringa microphylla var. flavoanthera

Syringa microphylla var. flavoanthera, commonly known as the golden anther lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers have distinctive golden anthers, giving it its common name. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and is hardy in USDA zones 5 through 8.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
