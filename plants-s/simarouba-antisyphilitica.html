Simarouba antisyphilitica

Simarouba antisyphilitica, commonly known as the antisyphilitic tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Simaroubaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
