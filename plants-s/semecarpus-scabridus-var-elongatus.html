Semecarpus scabridus var. elongatus

Semecarpus scabridus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-fruited Indian chestnut, is a species native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
