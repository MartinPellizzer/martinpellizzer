Saccolabium acutilabrum

Saccolabium acutilabrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod, which aids in seed dispersal.

Saccolabium acutilabrum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
