Salvia linguifolia

Salvia linguifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet and purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Salvia linguifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
