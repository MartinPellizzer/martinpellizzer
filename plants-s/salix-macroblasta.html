Salix macroblasta

Salix macroblasta, commonly known as the giant weeping willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
