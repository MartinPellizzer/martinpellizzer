Sabicea hirta

Sabicea hirta, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, smooth seeds.

Sabicea hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
