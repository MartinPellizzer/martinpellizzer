Syzygium papillosum

Syzygium papillosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Syzygium papillosum is also valued for its medicinal properties in various herbal remedies.
