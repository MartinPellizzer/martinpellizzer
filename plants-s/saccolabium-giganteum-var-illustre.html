Saccolabium giganteum var. illustre

Saccolabium giganteum var. illustre, commonly known as the yellow glory bower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Due to its vibrant color and unique structure, it is often used in landscaping for its visual impact.
