Scabiosa bipinnata var. calcarea

Scabiosa bipinnata var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
