Styrax sipapoanus

Styrax sipapoanus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre styrax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and can be reddish-brown in color, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Styrax sipapoanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
