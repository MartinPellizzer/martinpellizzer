Sambucus sieboldiana var. pubescens

Sambucus sieboldiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed with caution due to potential toxicity. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its varietal name "pubescens.".

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
