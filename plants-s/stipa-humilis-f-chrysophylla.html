Stipa humilis f. chrysophylla

Stipa humilis f. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by its dense, tufted clumps of slender, golden-yellow foliage. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with a soft, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking color and texture, which add visual interest to landscapes throughout the growing season.
