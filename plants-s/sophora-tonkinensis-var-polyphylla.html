Sophora tonkinensis var. polyphylla

Sophora tonkinensis var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Vietnamese acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
