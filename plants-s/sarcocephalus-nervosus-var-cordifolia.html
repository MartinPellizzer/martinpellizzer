Sarcocephalus nervosus var. cordifolia

Sarcocephalus nervosus var. cordifolia, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small, round oranges. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
