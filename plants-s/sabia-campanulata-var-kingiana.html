Sabia campanulata var. kingiana

Sabia campanulata var. kingiana, commonly known as the King's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and colorful foliage and blooms.
