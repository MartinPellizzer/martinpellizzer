Senecio latecorymbosus

Senecio latecorymbosus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy succulent.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
