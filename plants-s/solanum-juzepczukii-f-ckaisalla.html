Solanum juzepczukii f. ckaisalla

Solanum juzepczukii f. ckaisalla, commonly known as the Ckaisalla tomato, is a botanical variant of the Solanum juzepczukii species.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, orange-red fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as potatoes and peppers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary use, though it is less commonly cultivated than other tomato species.
