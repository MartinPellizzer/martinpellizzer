Selaginella chrysospora

Selaginella chrysospora, commonly known as the golden spore clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia that are yellowish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

Selaginella chrysospora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
