Scirpus jingmenensis

Scirpus jingmenensis, commonly known as Jingmen bulrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical clusters of pale brown flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Scirpus jingmenensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
