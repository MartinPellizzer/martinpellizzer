Solanum torvum f. gardneri

Solanum torvum f. gardneri, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a variant of the common morning glory, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is native to tropical regions and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
