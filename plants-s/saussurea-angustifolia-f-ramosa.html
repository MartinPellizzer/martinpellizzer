Saussurea angustifolia f. ramosa

Saussurea angustifolia f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched saussurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its branched inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other members of the species.
