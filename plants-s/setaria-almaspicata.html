Setaria almaspicata

Setaria almaspicata, commonly known as the spikeless foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist to wet environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
