Solanum jasminoides subvar. pilosum

Solanum jasminoides subvar. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a faint fragrance. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

This subspecies is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
