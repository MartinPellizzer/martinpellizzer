Salaxis octandra var. artemisioides

Salaxis octandra var. artemisioides, commonly known as the Artemisia-like salaxis, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves with a distinct, fragrant scent reminiscent of wormwood. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
