Smilax papuana

Smilax papuana, commonly known as the Papuan smilax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a bright red berry containing several seeds.

Smilax papuana is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
