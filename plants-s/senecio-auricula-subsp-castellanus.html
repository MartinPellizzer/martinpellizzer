Senecio auricula subsp. castellanus

Senecio auricula subsp.

castellanus, commonly known as the Castellan's auricula, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
