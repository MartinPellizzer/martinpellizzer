Salvia pomifera subsp. calycina

Salvia pomifera subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the California sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
