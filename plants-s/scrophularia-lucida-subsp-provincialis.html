Scrophularia lucida subsp. provincialis

Scrophularia lucida subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the Alpine Scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high elevations where conditions are harsh.

The plant features lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are showy, with tubular corollas in shades of pink or purple. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
