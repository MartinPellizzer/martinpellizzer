Saxifraga mazanderanica

Saxifraga mazanderanica, commonly known as the Mazanderan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in a terminal inflorescence during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
