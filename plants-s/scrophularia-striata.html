Scrophularia striata

Scrophularia striata, commonly known as the striped figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in whorls, usually pink or purple, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia striata is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
