Sapium putumayense

Sapium putumayense, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
