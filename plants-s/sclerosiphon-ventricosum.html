Sclerosiphon ventricosum

Sclerosiphon ventricosum, commonly known as the bladder wort, is a type of aquatic plant belonging to the order Coleochaetales.

It is characterized by its green, branched thallus that often resembles a cluster of bladders, hence its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It reproduces through the release of motile zoospores, which are important for its propagation and distribution.

Sclerosiphon ventricosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to primary production and serving as a food source for various microorganisms.
