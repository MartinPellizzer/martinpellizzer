Sorbus suecica

Sorbus suecica, commonly known as the Swedish whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Sorbus suecica is often found in woodland edges, rocky areas, and along streams, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
