Specklinia alexii

Specklinia alexii, commonly known as the Alexander's speckled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually occur in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Specklinia alexii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
