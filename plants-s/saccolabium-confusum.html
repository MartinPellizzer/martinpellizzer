Saccolabium confusum

Saccolabium confusum, commonly known as the confused saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
