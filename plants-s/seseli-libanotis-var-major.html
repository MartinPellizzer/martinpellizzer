Seseli libanotis var. major

Seseli libanotis var. major, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers, which are arranged in a dense, spherical head. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Seseli libanotis var. major is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
