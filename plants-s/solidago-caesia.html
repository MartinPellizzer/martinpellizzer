Solidago caesia

Solidago caesia, commonly known as the bluegoldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Solidago caesia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Its foliage is generally dark green and lance-shaped, with a slight silvery or bluish hue on the underside.
