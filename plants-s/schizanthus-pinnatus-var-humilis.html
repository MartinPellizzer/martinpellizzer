Schizanthus pinnatus var. humilis

Schizanthus pinnatus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf garden bean, is a low-growing annual plant native to South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem, compound leaves, and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
