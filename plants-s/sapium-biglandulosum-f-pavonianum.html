Sapium biglandulosum f. pavonianum

Sapium biglandulosum f. pavonianum, commonly known as the Pavonian Chinese lantern, is a variant of the Chinese lantern plant characterized by its distinctive red, inflated fruit capsules.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces showy, orange-yellow cyathia that attract pollinators.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, lantern-like fruits that persist into winter, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
