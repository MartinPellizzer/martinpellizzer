Sieglingia latifolia

Sieglingia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sieglingia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.

Sieglingia latifolia is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
