Sorbus megalocarpa var. cuneata

Sorbus megalocarpa var. cuneata, commonly known as the large-seeded whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, elongated fruits that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which appear before the leaves in early spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical or cuneate in shape, giving the variety its name.

The tree is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
