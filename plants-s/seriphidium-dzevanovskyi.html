Seriphidium dzevanovskyi

Seriphidium dzevanovskyi, commonly known as the Dzevanovsky's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers appear in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
