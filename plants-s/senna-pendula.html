Senna pendula

Senna pendula, commonly known as the weeping senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Senna pendula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
