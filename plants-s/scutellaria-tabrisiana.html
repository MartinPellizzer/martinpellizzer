Scutellaria tabrisiana

Scutellaria tabrisiana, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine pubescence, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scutellaria tabrisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
