Solanum chrysiflorum

Solanum chrysiflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and the plant can grow as an annual or perennial depending on the environment.

Solanum chrysiflorum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
