Saussurea kitamurana

Saussurea kitamurana, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
