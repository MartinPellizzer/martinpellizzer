Styrax hookeri

Styrax hookeri, commonly known as the hooker's styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

Styrax hookeri is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
