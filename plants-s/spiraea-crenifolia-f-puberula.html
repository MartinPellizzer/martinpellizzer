Spiraea crenifolia f. puberula

Spiraea crenifolia f. puberula, commonly known as the downy meadow spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
