Seriphidium spiciforme

Seriphidium spiciforme, commonly known as the spicate tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spire-like clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Seriphidium spiciforme is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
