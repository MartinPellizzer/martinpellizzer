Sabicea brachycalyx

Sabicea brachycalyx, commonly known as the short calyx sabelia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
