Sisymbrium asperum var. minus

Sisymbrium asperum var. minus, commonly known as lesser field pennycress, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often pubescent, giving the plant a rough texture. It produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically oval and slightly ribbed.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
