Scrophularia aquatica subsp. oblongifolia

Scrophularia aquatica subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as water figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and adapted to aquatic or moist environments.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it a characteristic species of marshes and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
