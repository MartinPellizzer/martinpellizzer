Salsola robinsonii

Salsola robinsonii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure that helps it survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Salsola robinsonii is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
