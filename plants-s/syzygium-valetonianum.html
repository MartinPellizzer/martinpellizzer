Syzygium valetonianum

Syzygium valetonianum, commonly known as the West African rose apple, is a tropical tree native to regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits resembling small apples.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, juicy pulp, making it a valuable food source in its native habitat.
