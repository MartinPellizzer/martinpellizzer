Saussurea bullata

Saussurea bullata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine. Its flowers are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Saussurea bullata thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme weather conditions.
