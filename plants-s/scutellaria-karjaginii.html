Scutellaria karjaginii

Scutellaria karjaginii, commonly known as Karjagin's skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
