Samara robusta

Samara robusta, commonly known as the robust samara, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive samara fruits, which are dry, winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Samara robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and fruit structure.
