Sloanea malayana

Sloanea malayana, commonly known as the Malay sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
