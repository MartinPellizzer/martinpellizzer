Sasa kagamiana

Sasa kagamiana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a grass-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa kagamiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
