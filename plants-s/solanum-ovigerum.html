Solanum ovigerum

Solanum ovigerum, commonly known as the "eggplant vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Solanum ovigerum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
