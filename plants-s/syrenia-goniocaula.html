Syrenia goniocaula

Syrenia goniocaula, commonly known as the spiral-cylinder sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves along its cylindrical stem. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Syrenia goniocaula thrives in coastal and brackish water environments, often growing in shallow tidal zones.

Its botanical features make it an important species in marine botany and ecological studies.
