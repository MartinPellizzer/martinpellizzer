Salvia capitata

Salvia capitata, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compact, dense clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and fragrant foliage.
