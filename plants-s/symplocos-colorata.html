Symplocos colorata

Symplocos colorata, commonly known as the red-flowered inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, which contains a single seed.

Symplocos colorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
