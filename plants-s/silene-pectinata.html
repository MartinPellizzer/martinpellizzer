Silene pectinata

Silene pectinata, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Silene pectinata is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
