Stenotus acaulis var. glabratus

Stenotus acaulis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed stenotus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, glabrous (hairless) stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its compact and upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
