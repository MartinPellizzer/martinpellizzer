Solanum sodiroi

Solanum sodiroi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color, and the plant is often found in cloud forests and montane habitats.

Solanum sodiroi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
