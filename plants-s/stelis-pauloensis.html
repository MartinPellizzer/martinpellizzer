Stelis pauloensis

Stelis pauloensis, commonly known as the Paulo's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, pink, or purple, and are fragrant.

Stelis pauloensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
