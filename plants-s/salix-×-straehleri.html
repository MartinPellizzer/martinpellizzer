Salix × straehleri

Salix × straehleri, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that produce long, slender leaves. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often planted near water bodies due to its ability to tolerate wet conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in landscaping and garden design.
