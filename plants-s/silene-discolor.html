Silene discolor

Silene discolor, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that open in the evening. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Silene discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
