Salix serpillifolia

Salix serpillifolia, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous environments, often near streams or in alpine meadows. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
