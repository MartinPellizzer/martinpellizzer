Swertia chirayita

Swertia chirayita, commonly known as Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 2,500 and 5,000 meters above sea level. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Swertia chirayita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
