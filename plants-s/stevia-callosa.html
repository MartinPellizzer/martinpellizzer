Stevia callosa

Stevia callosa, commonly known as the hairy stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stevia callosa is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain natural glycosides used as a sugar substitute.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herbal gardens.
