Saxifraga altaica

Saxifraga altaica, commonly known as the high alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically rosette-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga altaica is valued for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
