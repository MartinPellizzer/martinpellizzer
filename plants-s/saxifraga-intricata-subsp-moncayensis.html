Saxifraga intricata subsp. moncayensis

Saxifraga intricata subsp.

moncayensis, commonly known as the Andean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments, where it forms a mat-like structure to anchor itself in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are usually succulent and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
