Sisymbrium mongolicum

Sisymbrium mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Sisymbrium mongolicum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
