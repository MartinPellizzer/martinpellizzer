Spondias xerophila

Spondias xerophila, commonly known as the red sand apple, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round, red or yellow fruits with a gritty texture. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Spondias xerophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
