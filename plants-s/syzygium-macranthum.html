Syzygium macranthum

Syzygium macranthum, commonly known as the large-fruited apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium macranthum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a valued species in both horticulture and local cuisines.
