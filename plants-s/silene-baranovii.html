Silene baranovii

Silene baranovii, commonly known as the Baranov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene baranovii is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
