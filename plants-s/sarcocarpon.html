Sarcocarpon

Sarcocarpon, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique, often fleshy, and sometimes scented flowers. The plants typically grow in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh conditions. Sarcocarpon species are known for their small, clustered growth habit and their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
