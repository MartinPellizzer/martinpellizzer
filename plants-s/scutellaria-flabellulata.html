Scutellaria flabellulata

Scutellaria flabellulata, commonly known as the bellflower scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scutellaria flabellulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
