Saussurea topkegolensis

Saussurea topkegolensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh mountainous environments. Its fibrous roots help it anchor in rocky soils, while its leaves are adapted to reduce water loss in cold, arid conditions.

Due to its unique ecological role and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
