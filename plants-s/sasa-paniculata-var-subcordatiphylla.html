Sasa paniculata var. subcordatiphylla

Sasa paniculata var. subcordatiphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf bamboo, is a variant of the sasa bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating a thick understory in forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in panicles, and it produces edible shoots in early spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
