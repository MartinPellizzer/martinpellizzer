Scirpus olneyi var. longisetis

Scirpus olneyi var. longisetis, commonly known as the long-haired bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
