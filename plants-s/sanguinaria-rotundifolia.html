Sanguinaria rotundifolia

Sanguinaria rotundifolia, commonly known as the red puccoon, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a reddish sap, which has historically been used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Sanguinaria rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
