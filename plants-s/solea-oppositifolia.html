Solea oppositifolia

Solea oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green in color and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Solea oppositifolia is primarily used in ornamental aquatic gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
