Solanum piceum

Solanum piceum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum piceum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids and showy fruit.
