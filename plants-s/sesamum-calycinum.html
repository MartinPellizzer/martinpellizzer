Sesamum calycinum

Sesamum calycinum, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain small, hard seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
