Selenicereus calcaratus

Selenicereus calcaratus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a swollen, cactus-like stem known as a "pseudostem" that supports its showy, night-blooming flowers. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
