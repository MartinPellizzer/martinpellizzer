Solanum dasypodum

Solanum dasypodum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem, glandular hairs, and a preference for warm, humid climates.

Solanum dasypodum is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
