Stiptanthus polystachyus

Stiptanthus polystachyus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences, typically during the summer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces woody, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
