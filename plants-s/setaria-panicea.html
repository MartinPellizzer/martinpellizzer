Setaria panicea

Setaria panicea, commonly known as panicled millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria panicea is also used as a forage crop and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
