Symmetria

Symmetria, commonly known as the symmetrical beauty plant, is a rare flowering species native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its perfectly balanced, mirror-image leaves and flowers, which give it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its blooming season typically occurs in late spring, producing clusters of small, fragrant blossoms.

Due to its unique symmetry, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific value.
