Syzygium tayabense

Syzygium tayabense, commonly known as the Philippine apple or tayabas apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
