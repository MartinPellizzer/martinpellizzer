Solanum boergeri

Solanum boergeri, commonly known as the Boerger's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Solanum boergeri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
