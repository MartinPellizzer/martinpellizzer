Selaginella burchellii

Selaginella burchellii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, creeping plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella burchellii is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
