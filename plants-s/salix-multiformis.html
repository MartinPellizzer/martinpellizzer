Salix multiformis

Salix multiformis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and elegant look. The tree produces catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically male and wind-pollinated. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Salix multiformis is often found near water sources and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
