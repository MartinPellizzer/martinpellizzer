Scrophularia aquatica f. appendiculata

Scrophularia aquatica f. appendiculata, commonly known as water scrophularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to wet habitats and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
