Sabicea aspera var. rotundifolia

Sabicea aspera var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaved sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has rough, hairy foliage, which contributes to its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
