Stachys glabrata

Stachys glabrata, commonly known as the smooth betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its showy, two-lipped flowers are usually blue to violet in hue, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Stachys glabrata is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
