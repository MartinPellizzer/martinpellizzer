Syngonanthus eriophyllus

Syngonanthus eriophyllus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Syngonanthus eriophyllus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.
