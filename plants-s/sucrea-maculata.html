Sucrea maculata

Sucrea maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted petals that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sucrea maculata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
