Solanum leucanthum

Solanum leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
