Senecio ladakhensis

Senecio ladakhensis, commonly known as the Ladakh Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Ladakh.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well-suited to cold, harsh climates. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to the rugged landscape.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in alpine environments.
