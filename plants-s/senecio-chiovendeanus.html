Senecio chiovendeanus

Senecio chiovendeanus, commonly known as the Chiovenda's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.
