Senna foetidissima var. grandiflora

Senna foetidissima var. grandiflora, commonly known as the stinking senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Due to its strong odor, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
