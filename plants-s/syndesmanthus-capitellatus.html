Syndesmanthus capitellatus

Syndesmanthus capitellatus, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has silver-green, fuzzy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
