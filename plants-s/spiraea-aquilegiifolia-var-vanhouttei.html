Spiraea aquilegiifolia var. vanhouttei

Spiraea aquilegiifolia var. vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slightly hairy texture, and turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
