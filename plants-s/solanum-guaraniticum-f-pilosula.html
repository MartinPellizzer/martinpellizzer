Solanum guaraniticum f. pilosula

Solanum guaraniticum f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy Guarani tomato, is a variant of the Guarani tomato native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, red, tomato-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and potential culinary uses, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
