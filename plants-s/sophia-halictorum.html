Sophia halictorum

Sophia halictorum, commonly known as the yellow squash bee, is a species of halictine bee native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow and black striped body and is often found pollinating squash and pumpkin plants. The plant it pollinates, Cucurbita pepo, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes various edible gourds and melons. Sophia halictorum plays a crucial role in the reproductive success of these crops through its pollination activities.

Its ecological importance highlights the interdependence between specific plant species and their pollinators.
