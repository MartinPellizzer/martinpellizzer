Samyda affinis

Samyda affinis, commonly known as the silky camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. Samyda affinis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
