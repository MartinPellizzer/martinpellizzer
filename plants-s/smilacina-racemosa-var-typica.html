Smilacina racemosa var. typica

Smilacina racemosa var. typica, commonly known as greenbrier, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, waxy, yellowish-green stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Smilacina racemosa var. typica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
