Saxifraga pallida var. geoides

Saxifraga pallida var. geoides, commonly known as the pale saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, often pale green or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, toothed leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
