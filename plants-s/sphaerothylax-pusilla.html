Sphaerothylax pusilla

Sphaerothylax pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf moon cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a globular stem with a smooth, green surface and is often covered in short, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
