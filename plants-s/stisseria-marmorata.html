Stisseria marmorata

Stisseria marmorata, commonly known as the marble leaf plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern of cream and green. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching up to 3 meters in height. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
