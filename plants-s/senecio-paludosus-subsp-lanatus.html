Senecio paludosus subsp. lanatus

Senecio paludosus subsp.

lanatus, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
