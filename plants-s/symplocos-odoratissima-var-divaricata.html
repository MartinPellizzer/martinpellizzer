Symplocos odoratissima var. divaricata

Symplocos odoratissima var. divaricata, commonly known as the fragrant wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are woody drupes that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
