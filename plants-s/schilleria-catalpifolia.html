Schilleria catalpifolia

Schilleria catalpifolia, commonly known as the catalpa leaf spurge, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Schilleria catalpifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
