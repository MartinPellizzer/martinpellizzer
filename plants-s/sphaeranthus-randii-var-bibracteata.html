Sphaeranthus randii var. bibracteata

Sphaeranthus randii var. bibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bracted globe flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety bibracteata is distinguished by its enlarged bracts, which give it a more pronounced appearance compared to other varieties.
