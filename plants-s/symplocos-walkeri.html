Symplocos walkeri

Symplocos walkeri, commonly known as the Himalayan wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate regions.
