Silene villosa var. erecta

Silene villosa var. erecta, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene villosa var. erecta thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
