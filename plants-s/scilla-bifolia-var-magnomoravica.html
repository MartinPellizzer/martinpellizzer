Scilla bifolia var. magnomoravica

Scilla bifolia var. magnomoravica, commonly known as the Moravian squill, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is native to Central Europe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This variety is noted for its larger flowers compared to the typical form of Scilla bifolia.
