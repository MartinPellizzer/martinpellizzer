Stipa occidentalis var. montana

Stipa occidentalis var. montana, commonly known as western wheatgrass mountain form, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan to brown in color. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
