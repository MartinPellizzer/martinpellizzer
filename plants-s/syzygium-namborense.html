Syzygium namborense

Syzygium namborense, commonly known as the Nambore rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its wood is highly valued for its rich aroma and density, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts.

Syzygium namborense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
