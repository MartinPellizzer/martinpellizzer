Sorbus mougeotii var. anglica

Sorbus mougeotii var. anglica, commonly known as the English whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. The plant produces small, red-orange berries that are edible for birds but generally unpalatable to humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
