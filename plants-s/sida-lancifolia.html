Sida lancifolia

Sida lancifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Sida lancifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a branching stem that supports its foliage and floral display.
