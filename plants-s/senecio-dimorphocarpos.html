Senecio dimorphocarpos

Senecio dimorphocarpos, commonly known as the two-seeded groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
