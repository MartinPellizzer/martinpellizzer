Saxifraga exarata var. intricata

Saxifraga exarata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive, intricate vein structure that gives it its varietal name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
