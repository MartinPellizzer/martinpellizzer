Symphoricarpos oreophilus var. utahensis

Symphoricarpos oreophilus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its white, clustered berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The Utah snowberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
