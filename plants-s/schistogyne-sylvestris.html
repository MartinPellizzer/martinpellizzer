Schistogyne sylvestris

Schistogyne sylvestris, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Schistogyne sylvestris has a hairy stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
