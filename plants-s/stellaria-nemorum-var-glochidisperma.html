Stellaria nemorum var. glochidisperma

Stellaria nemorum var. glochidisperma, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a bluish-gray appearance due to dense pubescence. Its small, white or pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
