Staphylea pyramidalis

Staphylea pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid spurge, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in a sticky, milky sap. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue, waxy berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Staphylea pyramidalis is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
