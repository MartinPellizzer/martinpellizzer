Swertia angustifolia var. pulchella

Swertia angustifolia var. pulchella, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
