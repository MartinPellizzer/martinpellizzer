Shorea longipetala

Shorea longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its durable, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Shorea longipetala plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
