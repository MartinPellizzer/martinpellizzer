Solanum pachyandrum

Solanum pachyandrum, commonly known as the thick-leaved nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and often hairy leaves, which are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by berries that change color as they mature. Solanum pachyandrum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
