Scrophularia mellifera var. vidalii

Scrophularia mellifera var. vidalii, commonly known as the honeyflower scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
