Solidago canadensis var. bartramiana

Solidago canadensis var. bartramiana, commonly known as Bartram's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along streambanks. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Bartram's goldenrod is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
