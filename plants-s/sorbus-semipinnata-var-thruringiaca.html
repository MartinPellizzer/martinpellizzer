Sorbus semipinnata var. thruringiaca

Sorbus semipinnata var. thruringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are alternately arranged along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit display.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
