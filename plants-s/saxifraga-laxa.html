Saxifraga laxa

Saxifraga laxa, commonly known as the loose saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, usually in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Saxifraga laxa is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
