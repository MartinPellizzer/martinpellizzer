Saponaria hybrida

Saponaria hybrida, commonly known as the soapwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Saponaria hybrida is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its common name comes from the soapy suds it produces when its leaves are crushed.
