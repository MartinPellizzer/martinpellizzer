Sphaeranthus africanus

Sphaeranthus africanus, commonly known as the African globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.
