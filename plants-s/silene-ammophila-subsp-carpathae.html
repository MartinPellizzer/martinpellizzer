Silene ammophila subsp. carpathae

Silene ammophila subsp.

carpathae, commonly known as the Carpathian sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils, often in coastal or dune environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
