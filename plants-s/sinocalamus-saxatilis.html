Sinocalamus saxatilis

Sinocalamus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock bamboo, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sinocalamus saxatilis is typically found growing in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
