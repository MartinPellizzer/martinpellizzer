Sericocarpus bifoliatus var. collinsii

Sericocarpus bifoliatus var. collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' two-leaved silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
