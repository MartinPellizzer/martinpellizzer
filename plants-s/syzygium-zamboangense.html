Syzygium zamboangense

Syzygium zamboangense, commonly known as the Zamboanga apple or Zambanapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the Zamboanga Peninsula, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, round, red or yellow fruits that resemble apples, hence its common name. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slight aromatic scent.

Syzygium zamboangense is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruits, which are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
