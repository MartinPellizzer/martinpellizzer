Sobralia mandonii f. coerulea

Sobralia mandonii f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered sobralia, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in both greenhouse and garden settings.
