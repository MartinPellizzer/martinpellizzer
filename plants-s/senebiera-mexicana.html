Senebiera mexicana

Senebiera mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
