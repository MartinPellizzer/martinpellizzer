Sorbus neuillyi

Sorbus neuillyi, commonly known as the Neully's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to France.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Sorbus neuillyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive form and seasonal interest.
