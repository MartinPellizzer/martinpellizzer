Schinopsis heterophylla

Schinopsis heterophylla, commonly known as the huillpe or huillpe tree, is a deciduous tree native to the dry forests of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in local cuisine.

Its ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important component of the regional ecosystem.
