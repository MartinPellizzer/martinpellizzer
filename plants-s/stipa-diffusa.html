Stipa diffusa

Stipa diffusa, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
