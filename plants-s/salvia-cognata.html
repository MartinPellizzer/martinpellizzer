Salvia cognata

Salvia cognata, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Salvia cognata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
