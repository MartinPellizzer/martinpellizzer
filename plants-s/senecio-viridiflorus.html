Senecio viridiflorus

Senecio viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique floral display and compact growth habit.
