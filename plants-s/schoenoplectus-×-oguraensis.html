Schoenoplectus × oguraensis

Schoenoplectus × oguraensis, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Schoenoplectus mucronatus and Schoenoplectus californicus. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its stems are tall and cylindrical, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and it produces small, clustered flowering spikes.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and improving water quality in wetland ecosystems.
