Serapias laxiflora f. hellenica

Serapias laxiflora f. hellenica, commonly known as the Hellenic looseflower orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure over the reproductive organs. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its native environment.
