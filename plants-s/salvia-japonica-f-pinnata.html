Salvia japonica f. pinnata

Salvia japonica f. pinnata, commonly known as the Japanese forest sage, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, upright growth habit and vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features pinnately divided leaves, which give it a feathery appearance and contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
