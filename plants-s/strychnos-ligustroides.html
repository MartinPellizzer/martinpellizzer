Strychnos ligustroides

Strychnos ligustroides, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is part of the Loganiaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of strychnine and brucine.
