Solanum jamesii var. michoacanum

Solanum jamesii var. michoacanum, commonly known as the Michoacan nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by smooth, fleshy berries. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
