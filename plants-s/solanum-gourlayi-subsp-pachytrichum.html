Solanum gourlayi subsp. pachytrichum

Solanum gourlayi subsp.

pachytrichum, commonly known as the thick-haired nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, pubescent foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
