Silene graminifolia var. paucifolia

Silene graminifolia var. paucifolia, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Silene graminifolia var. paucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
