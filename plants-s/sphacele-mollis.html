Sphacele mollis

Sphacele mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Sphacele mollis is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.
