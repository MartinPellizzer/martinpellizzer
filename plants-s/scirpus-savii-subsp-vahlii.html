Scirpus savii subsp. vahlii

Scirpus savii subsp.

vahlii, commonly known as the Vahl's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers at the top of the stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
