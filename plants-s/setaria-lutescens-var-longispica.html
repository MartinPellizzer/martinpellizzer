Setaria lutescens var. longispica

Setaria lutescens var. longispica, commonly known as yellow beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of spikelets that produce small, yellowish to golden florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence is elongated and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
