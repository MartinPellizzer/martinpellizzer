Spraguea pulcherrima

Spraguea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
