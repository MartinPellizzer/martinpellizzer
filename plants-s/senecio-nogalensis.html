Senecio nogalensis

Senecio nogalensis, commonly known as the Sonoran Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Senecio nogalensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in scrubland and chaparral habitats.
