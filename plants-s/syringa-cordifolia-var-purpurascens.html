Syringa cordifolia var. purpurascens

Syringa cordifolia var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a valuable addition to landscapes.
