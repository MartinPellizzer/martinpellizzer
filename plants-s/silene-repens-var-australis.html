Silene repens var. australis

Silene repens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bladdercamp, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and inland dunes.
