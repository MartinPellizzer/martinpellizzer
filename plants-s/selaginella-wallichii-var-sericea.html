Selaginella wallichii var. sericea

Selaginella wallichii var. sericea, commonly known as the silver spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the Himalayas.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance, and it produces cone-like structures for reproduction. It is often found in rocky crevices or on forest floors, where it thrives in humid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
