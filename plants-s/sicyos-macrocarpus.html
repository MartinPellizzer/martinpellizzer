Sicyos macrocarpus

Sicyos macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant horsehoe vine, is a vigorous, climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, curved tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

However, it can be invasive if not managed properly.
