Sorbaria grandiflora subsp. rhoifolia

Sorbaria grandiflora subsp.

rhoifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by edible, red drupes in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
