Sersalisia arnhemica

Sersalisia arnhemica, commonly known as the Arnhem sandstone lichen, is a unique lichen species found in the arid regions of northern Australia.

It thrives in rocky, sandy soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a cyanobacterium, enabling it to survive in nutrient-poor environments. Its morphology includes a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to rock surfaces.

Due to its restricted distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
