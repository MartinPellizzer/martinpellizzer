Schraderia hastata

Schraderia hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed schraderia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Schraderia hastata is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

It is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe environments.
