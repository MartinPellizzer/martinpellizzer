Syzygium rubescens

Syzygium rubescens, commonly known as the red syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
