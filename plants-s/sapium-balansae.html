Sapium balansae

Sapium balansae, commonly known as the Balansae soapberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, smooth, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a toxic seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
