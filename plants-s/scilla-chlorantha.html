Scilla chlorantha

Scilla chlorantha, commonly known as the greenbloom scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens. Its flowers are typically 3 to 5 centimeters in diameter, and the plant is known for its early blooming habit.

Scilla chlorantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can naturalize easily in suitable climates.
