Senecio smithii

Senecio smithii, commonly known as Smith's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to white.

Senecio smithii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
