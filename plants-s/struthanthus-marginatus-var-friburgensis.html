Struthanthus marginatus var. friburgensis

Struthanthus marginatus var. friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburg dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
