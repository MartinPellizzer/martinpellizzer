Selaginella goetzei

Selaginella goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella goetzei is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
