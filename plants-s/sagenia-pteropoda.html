Sagenia pteropoda

Sagenia pteropoda, commonly known as the common sagewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually greenish-yellow in color.

Sagenia pteropoda is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
