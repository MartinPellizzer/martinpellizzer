Schellolepis persicifolia

Schellolepis persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and resemble those of peaches, hence its common name.

Schellolepis persicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
