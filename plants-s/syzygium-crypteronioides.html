Syzygium crypteronioides

Syzygium crypteronioides, commonly known as the wild durian or Malaysian durian, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and fragrant, spiky fruits. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are often pollinated by insects. Its fruit is highly valued for its unique flavor and aroma, though it is less commercially cultivated than other durian species.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
