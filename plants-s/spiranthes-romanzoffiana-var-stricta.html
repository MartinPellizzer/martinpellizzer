Spiranthes romanzoffiana var. stricta

Spiranthes romanzoffiana var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces a single, erect flowering stem that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
