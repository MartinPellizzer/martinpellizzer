Saccolabium myosurus

Saccolabium myosurus, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spherical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Saccolabium myosurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
