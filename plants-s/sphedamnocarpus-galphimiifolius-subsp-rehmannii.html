Sphedamnocarpus galphimiifolius subsp. rehmannii

Sphedamnocarpus galphimiifolius subsp.

rehmannii, commonly known as the Rehmann's sphaerocarpus, is a rare and little-known species within the Sphaerocarpaceae family. It is characterized by its small, spherical, fleshy fruits that remain attached to the plant for extended periods.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a leathery texture, adapted to retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically significant but understudied species.
