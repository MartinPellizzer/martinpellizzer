Syzygium velutinum

Syzygium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, woody pods containing sweet, sticky seeds. Syzygium velutinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient addition to tropical landscapes.
