Saxifraga planifolia

Saxifraga planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Saxifraga planifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
