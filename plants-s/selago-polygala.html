Selago polygala

Selago polygala, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often forming low, spreading mats. Its leaves are small, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are wiry and often branching.

Selago polygala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping due to its salt tolerance.
