Senecio parrasianus

Senecio parrasianus, commonly known as the Parrasian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio parrasianus is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
