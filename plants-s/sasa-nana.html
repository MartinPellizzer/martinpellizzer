Sasa nana

Sasa nana, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It forms dense clumps of slender, hollow stems that grow close to the ground, typically reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color and are arranged in a fan-like pattern. Sasa nana is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded, forested areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form thick mats, making it a common understory plant in temperate forests.
