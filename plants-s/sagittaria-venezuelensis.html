Sagittaria venezuelensis

Sagittaria venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow waters and wetlands, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are usually submerged, with some leaves emerging above the water surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a spike, and it produces berries that are dispersed by water.

Sagittaria venezuelensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
