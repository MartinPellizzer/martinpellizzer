Salix exigua var. hindsiana

Salix exigua var. hindsiana, commonly known as Hinds' willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in arid environments.

Hinds' willow is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in canyons and along streambanks.
