Symplocos costata

Symplocos costata, commonly known as the serrated waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
