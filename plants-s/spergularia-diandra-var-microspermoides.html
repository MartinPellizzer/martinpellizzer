Spergularia diandra var. microspermoides

Spergularia diandra var. microspermoides, commonly known as the small-sperm seaside sandplant, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
