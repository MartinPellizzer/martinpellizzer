Solanum grandiflorum var. setosum

Solanum grandiflorum var. setosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming at night. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties.

While it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes, caution is advised due to its potentially harmful effects.
