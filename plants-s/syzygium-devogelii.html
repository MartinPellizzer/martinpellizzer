Syzygium devogelii

Syzygium devogelii, commonly known as the Java apple or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, though they are not edible for humans. Syzygium devogelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species in horticultural and ecological studies.
