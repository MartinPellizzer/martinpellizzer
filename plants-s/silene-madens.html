Silene madens

Silene madens, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.
