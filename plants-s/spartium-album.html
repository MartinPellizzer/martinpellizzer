Spartium album

Spartium album, commonly known as white sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Spartium album is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
