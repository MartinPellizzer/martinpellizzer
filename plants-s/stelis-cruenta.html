Stelis cruenta

Stelis cruenta, commonly known as the Crimson Stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a pseudobulbous stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of white tomentum. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of tropical orchids.
