Shorea albida

Shorea albida, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Shorea albida plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
