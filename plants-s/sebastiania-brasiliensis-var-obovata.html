Sebastiania brasiliensis var. obovata

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The leaves are typically obovate in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
