Sobralia oroana

Sobralia oroana, commonly known as the golden sobralia, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in canopy environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Sobralia oroana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
