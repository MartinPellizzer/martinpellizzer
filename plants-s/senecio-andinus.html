Senecio andinus

Senecio andinus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Senecio andinus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andes.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
