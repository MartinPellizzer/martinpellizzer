Solanum salsum

Solanum salsum, commonly known as the saltbush or sea holly, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in saline and arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Solanum salsum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
