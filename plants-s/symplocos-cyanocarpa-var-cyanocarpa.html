Symplocos cyanocarpa var. cyanocarpa

Symplocos cyanocarpa var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit color.
