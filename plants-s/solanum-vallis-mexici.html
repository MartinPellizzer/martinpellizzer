Solanum vallis-mexici

Solanum vallis-mexici, commonly known as Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments.

Solanum vallis-mexici is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
