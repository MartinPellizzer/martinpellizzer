Sida cambuiensis

Sida cambuiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Sida cambuiensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
