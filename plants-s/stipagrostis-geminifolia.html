Stipagrostis geminifolia

Stipagrostis geminifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in pairs, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Stipagrostis geminifolia is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
