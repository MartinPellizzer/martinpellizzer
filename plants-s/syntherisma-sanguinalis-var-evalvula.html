Syntherisma sanguinalis var. evalvula

Syntherisma sanguinalis var. evalvula, commonly known as the scarlet valvular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
