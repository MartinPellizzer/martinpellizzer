Sempervivum globiferum subsp. globiferum

Sempervivum globiferum subsp.

globiferum, commonly known as the Globeflower Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

The plant produces tall, flowering stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
