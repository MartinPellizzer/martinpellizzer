Smilax obliquata

Smilax obliquata, commonly known as the zigzagged smilax, is a perennial climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Smilax obliquata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
