Serratula coronata var. manshurica

Serratula coronata var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian crown thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
