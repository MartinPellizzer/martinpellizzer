Scrophularia betonicifolia

Scrophularia betonicifolia, commonly known as the betonyleaf figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pale purple or pink, with five petals.

Scrophularia betonicifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
