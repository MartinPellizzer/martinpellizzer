Senecio biserratus

Senecio biserratus, commonly known as the serrated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
