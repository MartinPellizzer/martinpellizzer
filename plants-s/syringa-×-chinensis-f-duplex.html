Syringa × chinensis f. duplex

Syringa × chinensis f. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered Chinese lilac, is a cultivar of the lilac plant characterized by its dense, double blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dark green, ovate leaves. The flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters, ranging in color from white to pale pink. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its showy spring display.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
