Sedella atrata

Sedella atrata, commonly known as the black sedella, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique coloration and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Sedella atrata is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
