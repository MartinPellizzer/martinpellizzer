Symplocos chloroleuca

Symplocos chloroleuca, commonly known as the whiteleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in clusters during late spring. Its fruit is a dark purple berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for some wildlife.

Symplocos chloroleuca is valued in landscaping for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
