Sterculia multiovula

Sterculia multiovula, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain smooth, silky seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
