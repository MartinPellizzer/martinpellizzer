Schultesia crenuliflora

Schultesia crenuliflora, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Schultesia crenuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
