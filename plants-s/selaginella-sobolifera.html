Selaginella sobolifera

Selaginella sobolifera, commonly known as the Appalachian clubmoss, is a vascular fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and creeping rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Selaginella sobolifera is an important species in ecological studies due to its ancient lineage and role in forest ecosystems.
