Sarcanthus castaneus

Sarcanthus castaneus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, aromatic trunk and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, serrated edge.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically in construction and craftsmanship.
