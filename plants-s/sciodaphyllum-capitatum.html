Sciodaphyllum capitatum

Sciodaphyllum capitatum, commonly known as the sun-veined fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with finely divided, glossy leaves that exhibit a striking pattern of light and dark green veins. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Sciodaphyllum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
