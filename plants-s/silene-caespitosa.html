Silene caespitosa

Silene caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically blooms from June to August and is often found in grassy areas, rocky soils, and disturbed sites. Silene caespitosa has hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and adaptations to various environmental conditions.
