Senecio variifolius

Senecio variifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and serrated, lobed leaves that vary in shape, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
