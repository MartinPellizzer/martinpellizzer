Sideritis mascaensis

Sideritis mascaensis, commonly known as the Maltese ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Malta and surrounding islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. Sideritis mascaensis typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand the harsh Mediterranean climate.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
