Salix reticulata f. subrotunda

Salix reticulata f. subrotunda, commonly known as the broad-leaf willow, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves with a finely toothed margin.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green on the upper surface and paler beneath, often with a waxy coating. It produces catkins in early spring, which are male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
