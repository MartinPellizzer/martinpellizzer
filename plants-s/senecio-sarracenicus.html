Senecio sarracenicus

Senecio sarracenicus, commonly known as the banana leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that resemble banana leaves in shape and texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
