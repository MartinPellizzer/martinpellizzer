Setaria grisebachii var. ampla

Setaria grisebachii var. ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along waterways. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

The species is often noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
