Spiraea koreana var. rosea

Spiraea koreana var. rosea, commonly known as the Korean weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has fragrant, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
