Stachys revoluta

Stachys revoluta, commonly known as velvetleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Stachys revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
