Sayeria dendrocolloides

Sayeria dendrocolloides, commonly known as the tree-dwelling sponge plant, is a unique species of liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, sponge-like thallus that absorbs moisture from the air and substrate. This plant typically grows on tree trunks and branches, forming intricate, cushion-like structures. Its reproductive structures, called gametophores, are often visible on the surface of its thallus.

Sayeria dendrocolloides plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
