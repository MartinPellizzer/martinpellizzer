Salvia multifida var. delicatula

Salvia multifida var. delicatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often softly hairy, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both native and cultivated landscapes.
