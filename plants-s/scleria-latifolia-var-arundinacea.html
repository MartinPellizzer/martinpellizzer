Scleria latifolia var. arundinacea

Scleria latifolia var. arundinacea, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist or aquatic conditions and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
