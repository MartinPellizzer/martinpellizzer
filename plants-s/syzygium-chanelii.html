Syzygium chanelii

Syzygium chanelii, commonly known as the Chanel's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Syzygium chanelii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
