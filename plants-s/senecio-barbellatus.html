Senecio barbellatus

Senecio barbellatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
