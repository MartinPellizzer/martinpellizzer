Silene colorata var. stenophylla

Silene colorata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Silene colorata var. stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
