Sterculia columnaris

Sterculia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
