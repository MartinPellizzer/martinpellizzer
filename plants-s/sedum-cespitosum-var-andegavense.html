Sedum cespitosum var. andegavense

Sedum cespitosum var. andegavense, commonly known as the Andegavense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense clusters of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
