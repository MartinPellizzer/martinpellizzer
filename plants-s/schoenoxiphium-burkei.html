Schoenoxiphium burkei

Schoenoxiphium burkei, commonly known as the Burke's goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schoenoxiphium burkei is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
