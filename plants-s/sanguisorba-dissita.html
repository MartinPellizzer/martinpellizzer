Sanguisorba dissita

Sanguisorba dissita, commonly known as the Japanese dropwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Sanguisorba dissita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
