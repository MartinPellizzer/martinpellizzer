Sehima spathiflora

Sehima spathiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, white, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Sehima spathiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
