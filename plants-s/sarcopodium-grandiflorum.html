Sarcopodium grandiflorum

Sarcopodium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a central spadix, blooming in the late afternoon and closing at night. Its rhizomes grow underwater, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water.

Sarcopodium grandiflorum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
