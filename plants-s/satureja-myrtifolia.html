Satureja myrtifolia

Satureja myrtifolia, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.
