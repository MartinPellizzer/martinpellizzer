Stipagrostis anomala

Stipagrostis anomala, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Stipagrostis anomala is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
