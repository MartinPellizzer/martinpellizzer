Solanum lyratum var. leucanthum

Solanum lyratum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The flowers typically bloom in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative vine.
