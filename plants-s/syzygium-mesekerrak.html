Syzygium mesekerrak

Syzygium mesekerrak, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thick, leathery skin. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and cabinetry.

The plant is also sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
