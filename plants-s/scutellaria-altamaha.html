Scutellaria altamaha

Scutellaria altamaha, commonly known as Altamaha blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Scutellaria altamaha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
