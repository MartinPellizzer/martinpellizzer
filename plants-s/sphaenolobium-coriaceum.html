Sphaenolobium coriaceum

Sphaenolobium coriaceum, commonly known as the rough everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Sphaenolobium coriaceum is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
