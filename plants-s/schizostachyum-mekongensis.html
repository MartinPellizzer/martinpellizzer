Schizostachyum mekongensis

Schizostachyum mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for weaving. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by dark brown, finger-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that provide shade and are used in traditional crafts.

Schizostachyum mekongensis is valued for its durability and is often harvested for its rattan-like material.
