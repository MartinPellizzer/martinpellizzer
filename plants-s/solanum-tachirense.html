Solanum tachirense

Solanum tachirense, commonly known as the Tachira tomato, is a species of nightshade native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Solanum tachirense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
