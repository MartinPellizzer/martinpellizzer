Sabicea ingrata var. insularis

Sabicea ingrata var. insularis, commonly known as the island sabicea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically features compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often in shaded, moist habitats.
