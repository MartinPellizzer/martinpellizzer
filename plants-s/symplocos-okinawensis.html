Symplocos okinawensis

Symplocos okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa stonecrop, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.

Symplocos okinawensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
