Salvia nervosa

Salvia nervosa, commonly known as slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Salvia nervosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous communities.
