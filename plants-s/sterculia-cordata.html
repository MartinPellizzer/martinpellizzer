Sterculia cordata

Sterculia cordata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
