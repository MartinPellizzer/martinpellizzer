Saxifraga simulata

Saxifraga simulata, commonly known as the rockfoil saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, succulent leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga simulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, rocky conditions.
