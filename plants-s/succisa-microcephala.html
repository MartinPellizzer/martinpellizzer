Succisa microcephala

Succisa microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
