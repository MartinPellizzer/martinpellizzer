Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. eynensis

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

eynensis, commonly known as the Eynen mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
