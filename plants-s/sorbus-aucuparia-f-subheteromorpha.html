Sorbus aucuparia f. subheteromorpha

Sorbus aucuparia f. subheteromorpha, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its smaller stature and distinct morphological features compared to the typical form. The plant exhibits a unique combination of leaf shapes, with some leaves being lobed and others entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its morphological variation and potential ecological significance.
