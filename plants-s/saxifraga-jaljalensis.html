Saxifraga jaljalensis

Saxifraga jaljalensis, commonly known as the Jordanian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of Jordan and Saudi Arabia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on limestone or rocky substrates. It has a low-growing habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Saxifraga jaljalensis is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient and unique species in its native range.
