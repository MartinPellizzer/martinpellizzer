Senna multifoliolata var. metaxi

Senna multifoliolata var. metaxi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and have multiple small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the warm season. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
