Sophora tomentosa f. polyphylla

Sophora tomentosa f. polyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese honey locust, is a variant of the honey locust tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically longer and more densely arranged compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
