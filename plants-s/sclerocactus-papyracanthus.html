Sclerocactus papyracanthus

Sclerocactus papyracanthus, commonly known as the papery spiny cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in white, papery spines that provide insulation and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its fleshy stems make it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
