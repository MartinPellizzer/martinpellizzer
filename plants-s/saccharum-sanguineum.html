Saccharum sanguineum

Saccharum sanguineum, commonly known as the red cane grass, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, red-brown flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Saccharum sanguineum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking color and graceful growth habit.
