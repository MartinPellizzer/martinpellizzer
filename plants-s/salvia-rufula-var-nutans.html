Salvia rufula var. nutans

Salvia rufula var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and striking flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
