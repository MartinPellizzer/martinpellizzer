Sisymbrium columnae var. altissimum

Sisymbrium columnae var. altissimum, commonly known as the tall hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its tolerance of disturbed soils and its role in ecological succession.
