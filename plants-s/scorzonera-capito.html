Scorzonera capito

Scorzonera capito, commonly known as the mountain garlic or the Italian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in Mediterranean countries. Scorzonera capito thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
