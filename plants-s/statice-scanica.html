Statice scanica

Statice scanica, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

Statice scanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried flower arrangements.
