Senecio cordato-rotundus

Senecio cordato-rotundus, commonly known as the broad-leaved groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped and rounded, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient species suited to semi-arid climates.
