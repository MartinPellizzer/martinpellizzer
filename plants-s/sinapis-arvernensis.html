Sinapis arvernensis

Sinapis arvernensis, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Sinapis arvernensis is known for its rapid growth cycle, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
