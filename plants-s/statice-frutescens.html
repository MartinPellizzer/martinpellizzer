Statice frutescens

Statice frutescens, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to sandy and saline soils.

Statice frutescens is often used in ornamental gardening and is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
