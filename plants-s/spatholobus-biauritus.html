Spatholobus biauritus

Spatholobus biauritus, commonly known as the two-lobed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Spatholobus biauritus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
