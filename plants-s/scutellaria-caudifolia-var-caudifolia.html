Scutellaria caudifolia var. caudifolia

Scutellaria caudifolia var. caudifolia, commonly known as the long-stalked skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips, and are usually blue to purple in color.

The genus Scutellaria is known for its medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception.
