Sideroxylon cinereum

Sideroxylon cinereum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
