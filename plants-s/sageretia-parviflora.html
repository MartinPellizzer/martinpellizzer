Sageretia parviflora

Sageretia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sageretia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sageretia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
