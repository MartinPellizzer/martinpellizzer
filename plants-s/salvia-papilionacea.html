Salvia papilionacea

Salvia papilionacea, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia papilionacea is often cultivated for its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
