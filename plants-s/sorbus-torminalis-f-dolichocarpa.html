Sorbus torminalis f. dolichocarpa

Sorbus torminalis f. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruit whitebeams, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruits that differ from the typical spherical fruits of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in catkins, typical of the Rosaceae family.
