Sponia andaresa

Sponia andaresa, commonly known as the Andes spongeplant, is a unique species native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, spongy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant exhibits a rosette growth pattern, which helps it withstand strong winds and intense sunlight. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors to attract pollinators.

Sponia andaresa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
