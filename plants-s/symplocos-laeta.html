Symplocos laeta

Symplocos laeta, commonly known as the shining evergreen or silverleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Symplocos laeta is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
