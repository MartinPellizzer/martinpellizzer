Salix glauca var. acutifolia

Salix glauca var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, and riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
