Syringa villosa var. hirsuta

Syringa villosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem covered in dense, fuzzy hairs, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture on both sides.

Syringa villosa var. hirsuta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
