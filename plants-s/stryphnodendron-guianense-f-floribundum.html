Stryphnodendron guianense f. floribundum

Stryphnodendron guianense f. floribundum, commonly known as the Guianese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with trifoliate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.

The plant also has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
