Specklinia blepharoglossa

Specklinia blepharoglossa, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Specklinia blepharoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
