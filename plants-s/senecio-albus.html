Senecio albus

Senecio albus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and hairy, often bluish-gray leaves. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a tall, sparsely branched stem.

Senecio albus is often considered a weed but can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
