Schima monticola

Schima monticola, commonly known as the mountain schima, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Schima monticola is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
