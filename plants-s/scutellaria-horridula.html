Scutellaria horridula

Scutellaria horridula, commonly known as the rough scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage.

Scutellaria horridula is often found in open, sunny habitats and is relatively drought-tolerant.
