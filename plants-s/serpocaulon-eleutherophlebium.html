Serpocaulon eleutherophlebium

Serpocaulon eleutherophlebium, commonly known as the slender serpocaulon, is a rare and specialized fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feathered structure. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. It is typically found in temperate regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
