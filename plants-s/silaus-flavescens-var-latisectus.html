Silaus flavescens var. latisectus

Silaus flavescens var. latisectus, commonly known as the broad-leafed silaus, is a variant of the silaus plant characterized by its distinctive broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is known for its yellowish-green foliage and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers that produce capsule-like fruits.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptations.
