Sorbus foliolosa var. wenzigiana

Sorbus foliolosa var. wenzigiana, commonly known as the Wenzig's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into narrow, elongated leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
