Sedum allantoides

Sedum allantoides, commonly known as the silver rose or white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
