Scolymocephalus scabridus

Scolymocephalus scabridus, commonly known as the rough scolymus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, rough-textured stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scolymocephalus scabridus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
