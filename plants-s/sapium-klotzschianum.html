Sapium klotzschianum

Sapium klotzschianum, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in some regions.
