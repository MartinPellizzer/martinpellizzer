Senecio chilensis f. discodeus

Senecio chilensis f. discodeus, commonly known as the Chilean Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
