Senecio integrifolius subsp. czernjaewii

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

czernjaewii, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are entire, with a smooth, waxy surface, and stems that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments.
