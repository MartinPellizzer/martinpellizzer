Syagrus petraea var. alpina

Syagrus petraea var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine palm, is a rare and unique variant of the royal palm.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more clustered fronds compared to the standard species. This variety thrives in high-altitude environments, typically found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are usually a deep green and exhibit a more rugged texture than those of its relatives.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized collections.
