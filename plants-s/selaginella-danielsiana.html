Selaginella danielsiana

Selaginella danielsiana, commonly known as the Daniels' spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are closely arranged along its stems. It is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures called strobila, which are borne on specialized branches.

Selaginella danielsiana is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained, mineral-rich soils.
