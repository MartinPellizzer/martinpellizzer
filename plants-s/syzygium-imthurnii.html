Syzygium imthurnii

Syzygium imthurnii, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Syzygium imthurnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Its fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
