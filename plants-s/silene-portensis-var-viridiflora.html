Silene portensis var. viridiflora

Silene portensis var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is noted for its unique floral coloration and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
