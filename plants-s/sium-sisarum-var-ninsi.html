Sium sisarum var. ninsi

Sium sisarum var. ninsi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterparsley, is a variant of the waterparsley species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal practices, though its medicinal applications are not well-documented in modern botanical literature.
