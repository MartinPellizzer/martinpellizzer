Salvia domenechii

Salvia domenechii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
