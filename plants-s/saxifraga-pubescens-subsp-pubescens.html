Saxifraga pubescens subsp. pubescens

Saxifraga pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, often reddish stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and parts of Asia.
