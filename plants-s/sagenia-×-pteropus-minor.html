Sagenia × pteropus-minor

Sagenia × pteropus-minor, commonly known as the hybrid fig tree, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Sagenia pteropus and Sagenia minor.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to produce edible figs, making it a valuable species in horticulture. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, which can influence its cultivation and use.
