Sebaea involucrata

Sebaea involucrata, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sebaea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
