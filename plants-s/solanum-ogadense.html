Solanum ogadense

Solanum ogadense, commonly known as the Ogaden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions. It produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The fruit is a small, green berry that ripens to a yellowish color, often containing several seeds.
