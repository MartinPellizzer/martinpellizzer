Syzygium winitii

Syzygium winitii, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, and the fruit develops from a single flower, maturing to a yellow or orange color when ripe.
