Solanum tundalomense

Solanum tundalomense, commonly known as the Tundal mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
