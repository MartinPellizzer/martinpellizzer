Scrophularia pyrrolopha

Scrophularia pyrrolopha, commonly known as the purple scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia pyrrolopha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
