Solanum andigenum var. imilla

Solanum andigenum var. imilla, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
