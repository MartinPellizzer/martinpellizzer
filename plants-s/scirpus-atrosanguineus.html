Scirpus atrosanguineus

Scirpus atrosanguineus, commonly known as black blood sedge, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelike cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

Scirpus atrosanguineus plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
