Sabazia obtusata

Sabazia obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf sabazia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a blunt tip, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Sabazia obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
