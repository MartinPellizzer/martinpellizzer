Sicyos microcarpus var. kamoaensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. kamoaensis, commonly known as the Kamoa bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to Central and Southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruit with a smooth, pale green surface. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and for its ornamental appeal.
