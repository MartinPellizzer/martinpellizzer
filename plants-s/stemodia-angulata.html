Stemodia angulata

Stemodia angulata, commonly known as the angular stemodia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its angular, ridged stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Stemodia angulata is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
