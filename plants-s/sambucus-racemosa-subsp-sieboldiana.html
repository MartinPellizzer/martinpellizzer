Sambucus racemosa subsp. sieboldiana

Sambucus racemosa subsp.

sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia. It typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by dark purple berries in late summer. These berries are edible but should be consumed in moderation due to their high acidity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
