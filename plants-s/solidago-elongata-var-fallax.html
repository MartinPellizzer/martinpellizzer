Solidago elongata var. fallax

Solidago elongata var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are golden-yellow, arranged in dense, terminal panicles, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
