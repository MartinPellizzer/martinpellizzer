Solenanthus tournefortii

Solenanthus tournefortii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Solenanthus tournefortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
