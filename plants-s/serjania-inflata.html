Serjania inflata

Serjania inflata, commonly known as the inflated serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, fleshy fruits that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. Its unique fruit structure is adapted for seed dispersal by animals, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Serjania inflata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
