Sapium ciliatum

Sapium ciliatum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that contain sticky seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climate conditions.
