Saccharifera

Saccharifera, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of natural sugars in its leaves, which can be harvested and processed into a sweetener. Saccharifera thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
