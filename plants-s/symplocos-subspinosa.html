Symplocos subspinosa

Symplocos subspinosa, commonly known as the prickly evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered with fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos subspinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
