Scilla monanthos

Scilla monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive harsh conditions. Scilla monanthos thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate floral display.
