Stipa viridula

Stipa viridula, commonly known as green wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa viridula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
