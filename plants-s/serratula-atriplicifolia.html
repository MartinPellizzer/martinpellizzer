Serratula atriplicifolia

Serratula atriplicifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Serratula atriplicifolia has a taproot system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.

It is also considered a potential weed in agricultural settings.
