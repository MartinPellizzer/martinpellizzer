Senecio nigrescens

Senecio nigrescens, commonly known as the blackfoot daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow with a dark purple or black center, giving it its common name.

Senecio nigrescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
