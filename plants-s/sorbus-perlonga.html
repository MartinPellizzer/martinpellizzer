Sorbus perlonga

Sorbus perlonga, commonly known as the long-fruited whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Sorbus perlonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
