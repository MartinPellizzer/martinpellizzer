Stephania ramosii

Stephania ramosii, commonly known as the climbing arrowroot, is a member of the Menispermaceae family.

It is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if not handled properly.
