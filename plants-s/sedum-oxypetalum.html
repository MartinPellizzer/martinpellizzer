Sedum oxypetalum

Sedum oxypetalum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky and arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
