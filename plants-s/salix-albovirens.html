Salix albovirens

Salix albovirens, commonly known as the white-green willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and is often found in moist, temperate environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellow-green catkins. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth, pale green underside.

Salix albovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
