Sophora japonica f. pendula

Sophora japonica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping pagoda tree, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to China. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its tolerance to urban environments.
