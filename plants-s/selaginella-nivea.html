Selaginella nivea

Selaginella nivea, commonly known as the silver spruce or white spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its delicate, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila. It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its ability to retain water in its tissues, which helps it survive in humid conditions.

Selaginella nivea plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
