Swartzia schomburgkii var. schomburgkii

Swartzia schomburgkii var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the yellow wood or African yellowwood, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its dark, heavy timber and yellowish sap. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, ovate leaflets, and the flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It is native to tropical Africa and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
