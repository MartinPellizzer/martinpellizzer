Stipagrostis acutiflora

Stipagrostis acutiflora, commonly known as the sharpflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in dry to semi-arid conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Stipagrostis acutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
