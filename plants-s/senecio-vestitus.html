Senecio vestitus

Senecio vestitus, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio vestitus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
