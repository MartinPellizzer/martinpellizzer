Solanum portoricense

Solanum portoricense, commonly known as the Puerto Rican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Solanum portoricense belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem as a food source for certain insects and birds.
