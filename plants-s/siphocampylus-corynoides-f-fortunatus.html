Siphocampylus corynoides f. fortunatus

Siphocampylus corynoides f. fortunatus, commonly known as the Fortunatus form of the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a rare and unique variant of this flowering plant.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small, tubular, and pale blue or white in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. This form is distinguished by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral morphology.
