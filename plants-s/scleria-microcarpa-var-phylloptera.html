Scleria microcarpa var. phylloptera

Scleria microcarpa var. phylloptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in shallow water or moist soil.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shoreline sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
