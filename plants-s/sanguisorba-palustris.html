Sanguisorba palustris

Sanguisorba palustris, commonly known as marsh tea, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, rose-like flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it is adapted to thrive in moist or swampy environments. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Sanguisorba palustris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
