Sapium populifolium

Sapium populifolium, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features alternate, palmately compound leaves that are broad and often lobed, resembling the leaves of poplar trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
