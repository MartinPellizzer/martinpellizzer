Soldanella alpina var. rotundifolia

Soldanella alpina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Alpine soldanella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to cold and windy environments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to harsh alpine conditions, making it a characteristic component of alpine flora.
