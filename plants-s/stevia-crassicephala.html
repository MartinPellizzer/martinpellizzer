Stevia crassicephala

Stevia crassicephala, commonly known as the giant stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener alternative to sugar.
