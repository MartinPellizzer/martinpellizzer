Siphonochilus longitubus

Siphonochilus longitubus, commonly known as the long-tubed siphonochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique structure, featuring long, tubular petals and a specialized lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by horticulturists.
