Saxifraga × richteri

Saxifraga × richteri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink or white petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga × richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and vibrant floral display.
