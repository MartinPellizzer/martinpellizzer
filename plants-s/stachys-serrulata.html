Stachys serrulata

Stachys serrulata, commonly known as the serrated betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer. Stachys serrulata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming and healing effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
