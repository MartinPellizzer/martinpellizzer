Struthanthus syringifolius var. paniculatus

Struthanthus syringifolius var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled struthanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle, giving it its common name.

This species is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
