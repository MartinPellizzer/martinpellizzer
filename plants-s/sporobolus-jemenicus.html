Sporobolus jemenicus

Sporobolus jemenicus, commonly known as the Yemen spikerush, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often borne on long, slender stalks. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sporobolus jemenicus plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
