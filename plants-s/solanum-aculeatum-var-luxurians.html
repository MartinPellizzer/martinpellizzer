Solanum aculeatum var. luxurians

Solanum aculeatum var. luxurians, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features spiny stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that transition into berries, typically green turning to yellow or red when mature. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
