Solanum sessile

Solanum sessile, commonly known as the sessile nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Solanum sessile belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture and often have a distinct, spicy scent.
