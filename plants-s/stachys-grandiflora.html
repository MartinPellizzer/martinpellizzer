Stachys grandiflora

Stachys grandiflora, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers with two lips, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
