Scirpus flaccifolius

Scirpus flaccifolius, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often droop under the weight of its fluffy, white seed heads. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its hollow stems and fibrous roots contribute to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.

Scirpus flaccifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket-making.
