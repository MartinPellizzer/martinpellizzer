Scirpus crassiculmis

Scirpus crassiculmis, commonly known as broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and broad, flat leaves that are often used in basket-making. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically found at the top of the stems. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus crassiculmis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
