Syagrus × cipoensis

Syagrus × cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo palm, is a hybrid species belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a yellowish-green color, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Syagrus × cipoensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and providing resources for local fauna.
