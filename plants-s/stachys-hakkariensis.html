Stachys hakkariensis

Stachys hakkariensis, commonly known as Hakkari betony, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and often have a violet or purple hue.

Stachys hakkariensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
