Sinapis glauca

Sinapis glauca, commonly known as hoary stock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Sinapis glauca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
