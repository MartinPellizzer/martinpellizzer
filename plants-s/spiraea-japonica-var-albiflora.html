Spiraea japonica var. albiflora

Spiraea japonica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2 meters and has finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its delicate appearance and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to landscape design.
