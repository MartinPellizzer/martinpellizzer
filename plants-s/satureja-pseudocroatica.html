Satureja pseudocroatica

Satureja pseudocroatica, commonly known as the Croatian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pinkish to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja pseudocroatica is valued for its pungent, savory scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
