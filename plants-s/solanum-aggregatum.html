Solanum aggregatum

Solanum aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture.

Solanum aggregatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
