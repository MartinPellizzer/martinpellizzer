Silene vespertina var. parviflora

Silene vespertina var. parviflora, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene vespertina var. parviflora is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is a short-lived perennial.
