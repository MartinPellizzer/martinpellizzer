Smilax planipeduncula

Smilax planipeduncula, commonly known as the Florida arrowhead vine, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It has a climbing habit and features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its roots are tuberous and often used in traditional medicine.

Smilax planipeduncula is adapted to wetland and disturbed habitats, showcasing its resilience in moist environments.
