Senecio rosulatus var. coryphocola

Senecio rosulatus var. coryphocola, commonly known as the Rosulate Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
