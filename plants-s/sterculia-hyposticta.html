Sterculia hyposticta

Sterculia hyposticta, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds with tufted hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and habitat for various organisms.
