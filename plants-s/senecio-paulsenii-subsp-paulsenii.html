Senecio paulsenii subsp. paulsenii

Senecio paulsenii subsp.

paulsenii, commonly known as the Paulsen's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth form and silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
