Salvia zaragozana

Salvia zaragozana, commonly known as Zaragoza sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
