Stachys spectabiliformis

Stachys spectabiliformis, commonly known as the showy stachys, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Stachys spectabiliformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
