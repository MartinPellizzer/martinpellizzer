Schizostachyum auriculatum

Schizostachyum auriculatum, commonly known as the golden bamboo or auriculate bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base. This plant is typically found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Schizostachyum auriculatum is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for landscaping in warm climates.
