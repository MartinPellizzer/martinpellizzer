Scirpus conspersus

Scirpus conspersus, commonly known as the tufted bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted clusters of grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus conspersus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
