Sedum carinatifolium

Sedum carinatifolium, commonly known as the ridge-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright leaves that are often ribbed or ridged, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
