Stachys saturejoides var. burgsdorffioides

Stachys saturejoides var. burgsdorffioides, commonly known as the Burgsdorff's betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
