Sanicula europaea

Sanicula europaea, commonly known as European sanicle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, branched stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose umbels, appearing in early summer.

The root system is fibrous, and the plant is known for its mild, aromatic scent.
