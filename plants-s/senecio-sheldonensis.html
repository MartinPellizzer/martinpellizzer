Senecio sheldonensis

Senecio sheldonensis, commonly known as Sheldon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. This species is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive involucral bract that resembles a leaf.

Sheldon's groundsel is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
