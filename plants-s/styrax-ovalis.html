Styrax ovalis

Styrax ovalis, commonly known as the oval styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Styrax ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
