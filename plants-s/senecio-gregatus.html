Senecio gregatus

Senecio gregatus, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by bracts.

Senecio gregatus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
