Solanum stenotomum f. pacajes

Solanum stenotomum f. pacajes, commonly known as the Pacajes tomato, is a botanical variant of the wild tomato species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and culinary uses.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
