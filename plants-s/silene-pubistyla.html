Silene pubistyla

Silene pubistyla, commonly known as the hairy-styled catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Silene pubistyla is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
