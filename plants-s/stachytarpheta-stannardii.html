Stachytarpheta stannardii

Stachytarpheta stannardii, commonly known as the St. John's Wort of the Caribbean, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of bright blue or purple flowers, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
