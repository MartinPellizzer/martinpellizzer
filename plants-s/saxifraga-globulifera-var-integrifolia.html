Saxifraga globulifera var. integrifolia

Saxifraga globulifera var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Greenland saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features compact, rosette-like growth with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
