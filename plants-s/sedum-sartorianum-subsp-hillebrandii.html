Sedum sartorianum subsp. hillebrandii

Sedum sartorianum subsp.

hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
