Scirpus ancistrochaetus

Scirpus ancistrochaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus ancistrochaetus plays an important role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
