Stachys galeottii

Stachys galeottii, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys galeottii is known for its hairy foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
