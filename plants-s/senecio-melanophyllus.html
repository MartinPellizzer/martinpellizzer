Senecio melanophyllus

Senecio melanophyllus, commonly known as the dark-leaved groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal.

Senecio melanophyllus is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
