Schedonorus scaber

Schedonorus scaber, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, pastures, and disturbed sites. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Schedonorus scaber is also used in landscaping and forage production due to its hardy nature.
