Sideritis fruticulosa subsp. ferreriana

Sideritis fruticulosa subsp.

ferreriana, commonly known as the Corsican ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and pink to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
