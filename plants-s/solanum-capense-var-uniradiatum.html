Solanum capense var. uniradiatum

Solanum capense var. uniradiatum, commonly known as the Cape nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, often at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant produces yellow berries that are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
