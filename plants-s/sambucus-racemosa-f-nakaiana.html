Sambucus racemosa f. nakaiana

Sambucus racemosa f. nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaian elderberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It is a form of the common elderberry, characterized by its smaller size and dense growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
