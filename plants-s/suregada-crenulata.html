Suregada crenulata

Suregada crenulata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Suregada crenulata is adapted to saline and windy conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
