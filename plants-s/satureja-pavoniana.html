Satureja pavoniana

Satureja pavoniana, commonly known as the purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent aroma, while its small purple flowers bloom in summer.

Satureja pavoniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
