Silene delavayi

Silene delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
