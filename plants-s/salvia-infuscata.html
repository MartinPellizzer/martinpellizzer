Salvia infuscata

Salvia infuscata, commonly known as desert sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its drought-tolerant nature. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia infuscata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
