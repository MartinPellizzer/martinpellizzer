Sesleria autumnalis var. voronovii

Sesleria autumnalis var. voronovii, commonly known as the autumn sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer to autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.

The variety voronovii is distinguished by its specific floral characteristics and geographic distribution.
