Suaeda divaricata

Suaeda divaricata, commonly known as the saltbush or coastal saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, thriving in harsh environments with high salt content. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters. Suaeda divaricata is well adapted to drought and salt stress, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in coastal and desert areas.
