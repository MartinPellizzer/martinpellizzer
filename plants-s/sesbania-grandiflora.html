Sesbania grandiflora

Sesbania grandiflora, commonly known as the Crown Flower, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

Sesbania grandiflora also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
