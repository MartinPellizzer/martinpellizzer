Sophronitis xanthina

Sophronitis xanthina, commonly known as the Yellow Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, narrow flower spike. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure and fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sophronitis xanthina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
