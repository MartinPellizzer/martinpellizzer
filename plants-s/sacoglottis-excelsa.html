Sacoglottis excelsa

Sacoglottis excelsa, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring large, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Sacoglottis excelsa make it a notable species in the Araceae family.
