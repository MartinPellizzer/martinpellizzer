Stipa borysthenica subsp. germanica

Stipa borysthenica subsp.

germanica, commonly known as the German feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
