Spiraea arunachalensis

Spiraea arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal spiraea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Spiraea arunachalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
