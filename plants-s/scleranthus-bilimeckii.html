Scleranthus bilimeckii

Scleranthus bilimeckii, commonly known as the Balkan sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, ribbed fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
