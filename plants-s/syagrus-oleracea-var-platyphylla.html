Syagrus oleracea var. platyphylla

Syagrus oleracea var. platyphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian fan palm, is a striking palm species native to northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits resembling small coconuts. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.

Syagrus oleracea var. platyphylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
