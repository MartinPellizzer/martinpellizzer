Stanhopea velata

Stanhopea velata, commonly known as the white-lipped night orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive orange lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stanhopea velata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
