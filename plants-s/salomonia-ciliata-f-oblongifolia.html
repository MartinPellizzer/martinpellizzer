Salomonia ciliata f. oblongifolia

Salomonia ciliata f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pink or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
