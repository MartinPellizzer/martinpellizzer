Sesleria varia var. albicans

Sesleria varia var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Sesleria varia var. albicans is valued in horticulture for its fine texture and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
