Sonchus wightianus subsp. wallichianus

Sonchus wightianus subsp.

wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, bitter taste, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
