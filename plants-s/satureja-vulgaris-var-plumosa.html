Satureja vulgaris var. plumosa

Satureja vulgaris var. plumosa, commonly known as woolly savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
