Senecio petraeus

Senecio petraeus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Senecio petraeus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
