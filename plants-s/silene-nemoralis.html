Silene nemoralis

Silene nemoralis, commonly known as the woodcranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in woodland areas and along hedgerows. The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Silene nemoralis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
