Spiraea crataegifolia

Spiraea crataegifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical and have a distinctive rose-like appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
