Senecio succisifolius

Senecio succisifolius, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly white hairs covering its leaves and stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted for water storage, typical of succulent species.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
