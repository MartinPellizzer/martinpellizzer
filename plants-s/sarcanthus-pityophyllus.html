Sarcanthus pityophyllus

Sarcanthus pityophyllus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, woodland environments. The plant features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Sarcanthus pityophyllus is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in partial shade.
