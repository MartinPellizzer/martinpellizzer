Schefflera lechleri

Schefflera lechleri, commonly known as the Sacha Inchi or "Peru nut," is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into numerous leaflets arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by unique, egg-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in South American medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high omega-3 fatty acid profile.
