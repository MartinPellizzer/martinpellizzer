Siloxerus brachypappus

Siloxerus brachypappus, commonly known as the narrow-pappus siloxerus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Its fruit is a dry capsule that releases tiny, feathery pappus hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Siloxerus brachypappus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
