Selaginella valdepilosa subsp. tricholoma

Selaginella valdepilosa subsp.

tricholoma, commonly known as the tricholoma spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves. It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, producing both microspores and megaspores on separate structures. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

This subspecies is notable for its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
