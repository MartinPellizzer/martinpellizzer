Smilax cissoides var. laeviuscula

Smilax cissoides var. laeviuscula, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed catbriar, is a species of vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
