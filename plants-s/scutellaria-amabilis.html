Scutellaria amabilis

Scutellaria amabilis, commonly known as the pretty skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its small, nutlet-like fruits are typical of the Scutellaria genus.

Scutellaria amabilis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
