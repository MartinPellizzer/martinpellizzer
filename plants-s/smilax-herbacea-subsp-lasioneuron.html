Smilax herbacea subsp. lasioneuron

Smilax herbacea subsp.

lasioneuron, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
