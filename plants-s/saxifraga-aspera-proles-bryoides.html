Saxifraga aspera proles bryoides

Saxifraga aspera proles bryoides, commonly known as the mossy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga aspera proles bryoides thrives in moist, rocky or shaded environments, making it a common sight in alpine and subalpine habitats.
