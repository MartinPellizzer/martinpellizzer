Symphytum sepulcrale

Symphytum sepulcrale, commonly known as the sepulchral comfrey, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
