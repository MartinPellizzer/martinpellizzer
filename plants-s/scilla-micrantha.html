Scilla micrantha

Scilla micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
