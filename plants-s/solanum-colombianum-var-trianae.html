Solanum colombianum var. trianae

Solanum colombianum var. trianae, commonly known as the Colombian nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, often green or purple berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features include simple leaves with serrated edges and small, white or pale yellow flowers.
