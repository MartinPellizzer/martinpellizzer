Staurogyne paniculata var. glabrior

Staurogyne paniculata var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, floating mats of small, oval leaves that form a lush green canopy on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge above the water. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquatic gardens for its ornamental value.

Its smooth, hairless stems and leaves distinguish it from other varieties within the Staurogyne genus.
