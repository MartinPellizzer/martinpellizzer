Solanum corymbosum

Solanum corymbosum, commonly known as the potato, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces tuberous roots as its primary edible part. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The fruit is a berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.
