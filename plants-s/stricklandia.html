Stricklandia

Stricklandia, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Stricklandia, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to yellow and orange, with intricate patterns.

Stricklandia species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
