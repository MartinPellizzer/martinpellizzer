Sida cordifolia subsp. maculata

Sida cordifolia subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are used as a leguminous crop in some parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
