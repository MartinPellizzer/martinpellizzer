Stylidium paludicola

Stylidium paludicola, commonly known as the marsh stylidium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Stylidium paludicola thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems and its unique adaptations to waterlogged environments.
