Strychnos bifurcata

Strychnos bifurcata, commonly known as the two-branched strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for their medicinal and pharmacological potential.
