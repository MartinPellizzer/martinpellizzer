Senecio carpathicus

Senecio carpathicus, commonly known as the Carpathian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Senecio carpathicus is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows.
