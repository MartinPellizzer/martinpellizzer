Salvia pratensis var. caucasica

Salvia pratensis var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
