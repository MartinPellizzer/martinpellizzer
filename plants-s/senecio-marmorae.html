Senecio marmorae

Senecio marmorae, commonly known as the marble cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in small, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
