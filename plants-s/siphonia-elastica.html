Siphonia elastica

Siphonia elastica, commonly known as the elastic siphonia, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Siphonaceae.

It is characterized by its flexible, ribbon-like leaves that can bend and twist in response to water currents. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds. Its reproductive structures consist of small, elongated spores that are released into the water for dispersal.

Siphonia elastica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
