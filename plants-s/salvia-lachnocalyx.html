Salvia lachnocalyx

Salvia lachnocalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and striking violet to purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
