Sorbus latifolia

Sorbus latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
