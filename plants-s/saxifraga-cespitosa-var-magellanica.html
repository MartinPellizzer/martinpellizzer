Saxifraga cespitosa var. magellanica

Saxifraga cespitosa var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features dense, compact rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
