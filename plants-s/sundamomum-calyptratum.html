Sundamomum calyptratum

Sundamomum calyptratum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
