Sorbus aria var. carpinifolia

Sorbus aria var. carpinifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in autumn.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.
