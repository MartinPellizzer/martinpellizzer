Senecio crithmifolius

Senecio crithmifolius, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central yellow disk, which make it visually distinctive. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Senecio crithmifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
