Sida rhombifolia var. minor

Sida rhombifolia var. minor, commonly known as the smallleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, rhombic-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
