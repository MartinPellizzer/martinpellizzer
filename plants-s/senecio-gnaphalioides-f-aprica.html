Senecio gnaphalioides f. aprica

Senecio gnaphalioides f. aprica, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, with a mat-like form that helps it colonize disturbed sites.
