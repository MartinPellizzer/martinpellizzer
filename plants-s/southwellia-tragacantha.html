Southwellia tragacantha

Southwellia tragacantha, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Southwellia tragacantha is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
