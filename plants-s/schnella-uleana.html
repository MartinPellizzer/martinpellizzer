Schnella uleana

Schnella uleana, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Schnella uleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
