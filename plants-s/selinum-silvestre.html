Selinum silvestre

Selinum silvestre, commonly known as wild celery, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which are white or pale green, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Selinum silvestre is valued for its mild, celery-like flavor and potential medicinal properties.
