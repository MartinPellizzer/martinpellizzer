Solanum malmeanum

Solanum malmeanum, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are typically red or orange when mature and contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum malmeanum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
