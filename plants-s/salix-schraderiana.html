Salix schraderiana

Salix schraderiana, commonly known as Schrader's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant has slender, often twisted branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a silver-gray underside.

Salix schraderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
