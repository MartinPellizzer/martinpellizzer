Sambucus loureiroana

Sambucus loureiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climatic conditions.

Sambucus loureiroana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
