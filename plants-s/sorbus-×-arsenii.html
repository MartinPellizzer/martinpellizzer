Sorbus × arsenii

Sorbus × arsenii, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the genus Sorbus, which includes over 1,400 species of shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits that typically turn red or orange in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive bark and foliage.

The hybrid nature of Sorbus × arsenii results from crosses between different Sorbus species, contributing to its adaptability and horticultural interest.
