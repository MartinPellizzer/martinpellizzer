Sedum erythrostictum f. variegatum

Sedum erythrostictum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which enhances its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and colorful foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
