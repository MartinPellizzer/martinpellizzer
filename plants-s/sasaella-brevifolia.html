Sasaella brevifolia

Sasaella brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved bamboo, is a clump-forming evergreen plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes contribute to its ability to spread vegetatively.

Sasaella brevifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
