Statice limonium var. puberula

Statice limonium var. puberula, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, linear leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Statice limonium var. puberula is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
