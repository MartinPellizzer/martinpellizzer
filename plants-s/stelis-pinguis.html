Stelis pinguis

Stelis pinguis, commonly known as the fat stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Stelis pinguis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
