Sophora tetragonocarpa

Sophora tetragonocarpa, commonly known as the four-angled sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically four-sided in cross-section, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
