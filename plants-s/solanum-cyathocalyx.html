Solanum cyathocalyx

Solanum cyathocalyx, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and glandular leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
