Saussurea angustifolia subsp. yukonensis

Saussurea angustifolia subsp.

yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon narrowleaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the Yukon region of Canada. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
