Schizocalyx syriacus

Schizocalyx syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Middle East.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental purposes. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Schizocalyx syriacus is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand urban conditions.
