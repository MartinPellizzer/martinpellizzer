Stachys scordiifolia

Stachys scordiifolia, commonly known as broadleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys scordiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
