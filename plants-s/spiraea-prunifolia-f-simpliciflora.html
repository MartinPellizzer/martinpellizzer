Spiraea prunifolia f. simpliciflora

Spiraea prunifolia f. simpliciflora, commonly known as the simple-flowered weeping spirea, is a compact, shrub-like plant with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are small, white, and clustered in cylindrical inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
