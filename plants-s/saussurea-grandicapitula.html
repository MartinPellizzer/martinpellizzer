Saussurea grandicapitula

Saussurea grandicapitula, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower or large-headed safflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy yellow flower head, which can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

Saussurea grandicapitula is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
