Syzygium gyrostemoneum

Syzygium gyrostemoneum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a large, segmented fruit that turns from green to yellow or red when mature. The fruit's pericarp contains a white, juicy aril that is often consumed for its unique flavor and nutritional value.

Syzygium gyrostemoneum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
