Sansevieria gentilis

Sansevieria gentilis, commonly known as the snake plant, is a succulent plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with light green or yellowish variegation. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for indoor environments with low light and minimal watering. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks during the summer months.

The leaves are also known for their ability to absorb and neutralize indoor pollutants, contributing to air purification.
