Steinchisma laxum

Steinchisma laxum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Steinchisma laxum is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid landscapes.
