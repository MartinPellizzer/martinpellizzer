Senecio productus

Senecio productus, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features fleshy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its succulent leaves help it store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Senecio productus is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
