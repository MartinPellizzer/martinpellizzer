Sesamum integribracteatum subsp. integribracteatum

Sesamum integribracteatum subsp.

integribracteatum, commonly known as the African sesame, is a species native to tropical Africa. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its seeds, encased in a hard shell, are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique bract structure and is often found in dry, open habitats.
