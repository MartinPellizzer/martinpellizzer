Spiraea callosa var. alba

Spiraea callosa var. alba, commonly known as white meadowsweet, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a soft, hairy texture, and they turn yellow in the fall.

Spiraea callosa var. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
