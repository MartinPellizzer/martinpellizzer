Sesquicella involucrata

Sesquicella involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Sesquicella involucrata is an annual herb that plays a role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
