Sideritis × aragonensis

Sideritis × aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon sainfoin, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

Sideritis × aragonensis is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
