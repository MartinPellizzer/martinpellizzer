Stenophyllus stenophyllus

Stenophyllus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable microhabitats. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Stenophyllus stenophyllus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a lush, green texture to gardens.
