Schrenkia

Schrenkia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Schrenkia species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. They are often found growing in rocky soils or along riverbanks in their native habitats.

The genus includes several species, such as Schrenkia grandiflora and Schrenkia taurica, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
