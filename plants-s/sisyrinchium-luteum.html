Sisyrinchium luteum

Sisyrinchium luteum, commonly known as the yellow gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, often resembling a candle-like shape.

Sisyrinchium luteum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
