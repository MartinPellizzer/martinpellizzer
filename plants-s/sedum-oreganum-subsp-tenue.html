Sedum oreganum subsp. tenue

Sedum oreganum subsp.

tenue, commonly known as narrowleaf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
