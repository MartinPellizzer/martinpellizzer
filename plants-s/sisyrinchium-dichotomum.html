Sisyrinchium dichotomum

Sisyrinchium dichotomum, commonly known as the forked blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, which gives it its common name.
