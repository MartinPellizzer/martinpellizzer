Scleranthus herbichii

Scleranthus herbichii, commonly known as the herbich's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Scleranthus herbichii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
