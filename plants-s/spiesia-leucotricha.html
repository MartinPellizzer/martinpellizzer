Spiesia leucotricha

Spiesia leucotricha, commonly known as the silky tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
