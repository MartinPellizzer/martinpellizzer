Silene bamianica

Silene bamianica, commonly known as the Bamian Pass catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Silene bamianica plays a role in its local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
