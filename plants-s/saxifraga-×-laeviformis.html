Saxifraga × laeviformis

Saxifraga × laeviformis, commonly known as the smooth saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually rounded and slightly succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × laeviformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden settings.
