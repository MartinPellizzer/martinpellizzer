Satureja parnassica subsp. sipylea

Satureja parnassica subsp.

sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oblong fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent, peppery aroma.
