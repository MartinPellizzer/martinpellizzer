Sesbania emerus

Sesbania emerus, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, bursy covering. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it useful for soil enrichment and erosion control.

Sesbania emerus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for agricultural and ecological purposes.
