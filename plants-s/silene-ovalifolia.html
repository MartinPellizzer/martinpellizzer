Silene ovalifolia

Silene ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval-shaped leaves and stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene ovalifolia is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
