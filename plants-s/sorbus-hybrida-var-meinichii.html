Sorbus hybrida var. meinichii

Sorbus hybrida var. meinichii, commonly known as the Meinhof's whitebeam, is a hybrid species of the whitebeam genus Sorbus.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This ornamental tree is valued for its attractive bark and seasonal foliage changes, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
