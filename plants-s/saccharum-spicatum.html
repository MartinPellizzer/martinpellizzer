Saccharum spicatum

Saccharum spicatum, commonly known as purple bamboo or tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to wetland and marshy areas of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, tufted clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Saccharum spicatum plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes can spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
