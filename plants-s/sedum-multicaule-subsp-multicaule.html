Sedum multicaule subsp. multicaule

Sedum multicaule subsp.

multicaule, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems that often form dense clusters, and its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water, and it is known for its hardiness in temperate climates.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
