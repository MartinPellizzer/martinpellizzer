Satureja cordata

Satureja cordata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their carminative and antiseptic properties.

Satureja cordata is also valued in culinary applications for its pungent, thyme-like aroma.
