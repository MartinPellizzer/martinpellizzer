Senecio pseudostigophlebius

Senecio pseudostigophlebius, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish or greyish hue, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
