Salpinga cristata

Salpinga cristata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salpinga cristata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
