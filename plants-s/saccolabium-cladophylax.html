Saccolabium cladophylax

Saccolabium cladophylax, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockheath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats.
