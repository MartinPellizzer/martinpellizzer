Salvia ambigens

Salvia ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia ambigens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
