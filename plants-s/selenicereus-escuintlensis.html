Selenicereus escuintlensis

Selenicereus escuintlensis, commonly known as the Escuintla night bloomer, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Selenicereus escuintlensis is valued for its ornamental flowers and ecological role in its native habitat.
