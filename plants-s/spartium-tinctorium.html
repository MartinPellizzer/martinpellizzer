Spartium tinctorium

Spartium tinctorium, commonly known as redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are notable for their reddish color and have been historically used for their dyeing properties.

Spartium tinctorium is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
