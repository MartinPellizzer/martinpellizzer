Samyda

Samyda, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common mulberry. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as syconia, which are typically green when immature and turn red or black when ripe.

The bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive aromatic scent.
