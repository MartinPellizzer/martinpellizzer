Scutellaria aurantiaca

Scutellaria aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of orange and red, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria aurantiaca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
