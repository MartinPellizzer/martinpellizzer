Senecio lophophilus

Senecio lophophilus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
