Solanum nigrum var. stylosum

Solanum nigrum var. stylosum, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels.

The species is notable for its toxic properties, containing solanine and other alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
