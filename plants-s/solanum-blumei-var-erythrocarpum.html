Solanum blumei var. erythrocarpum

Solanum blumei var. erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The species is characterized by its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and colorful fruits.
