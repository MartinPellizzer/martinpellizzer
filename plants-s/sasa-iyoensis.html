Sasa iyoensis

Sasa iyoensis, commonly known as the Iyo bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to several meters in height. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa iyoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
