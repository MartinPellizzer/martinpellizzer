Spiraea salicifolia f. grandiflora

Spiraea salicifolia f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically bright green, turning yellow in the fall, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is noted for its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
