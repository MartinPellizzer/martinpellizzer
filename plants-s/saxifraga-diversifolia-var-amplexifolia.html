Saxifraga diversifolia var. amplexifolia

Saxifraga diversifolia var. amplexifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are broad and often clasping, giving it the varietal name "amplexifolia.". Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually display shades of pink or white.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
