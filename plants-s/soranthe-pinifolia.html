Soranthe pinifolia

Soranthe pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf soranthe, is a unique plant species characterized by its needle-like leaves that resemble those of coniferous trees.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant's foliage is dense and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast against its woody stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters along the stems.

Soranthe pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract various pollinators.
