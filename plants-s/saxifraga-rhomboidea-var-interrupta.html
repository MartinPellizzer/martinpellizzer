Saxifraga rhomboidea var. interrupta

Saxifraga rhomboidea var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sand saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

This species is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
