Sarcocornia perennis subsp. alpini

Sarcocornia perennis subsp.

alpini, commonly known as the alpine pickleweed, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments. It is a shrub-like perennial with succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal and inland saline soils, often found in salt marshes and dunes. Its fleshy stems and leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert and coastal species.
