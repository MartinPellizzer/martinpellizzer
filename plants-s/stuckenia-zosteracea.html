Stuckenia zosteracea

Stuckenia zosteracea, commonly known as narrow-leaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Stuckenia zosteracea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
