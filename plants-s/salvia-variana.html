Salvia variana

Salvia variana, commonly known as the variable sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia variana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
