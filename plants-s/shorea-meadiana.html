Shorea meadiana

Shorea meadiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

Shorea meadiana plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
