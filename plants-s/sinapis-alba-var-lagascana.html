Sinapis alba var. lagascana

Sinapis alba var. lagascana, commonly known as white mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a branched stem covered in fine hairs, with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its rapid growth and early maturation make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
