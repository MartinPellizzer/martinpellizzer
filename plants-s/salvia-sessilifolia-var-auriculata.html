Salvia sessilifolia var. auriculata

Salvia sessilifolia var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with oval-shaped leaves that have distinctive ear-like lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and showy two-lipped flowers.
