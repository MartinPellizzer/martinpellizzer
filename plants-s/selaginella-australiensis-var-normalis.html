Selaginella australiensis var. normalis

Selaginella australiensis var. normalis, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its historical significance in botanical studies.
