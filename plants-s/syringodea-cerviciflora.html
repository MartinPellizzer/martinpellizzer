Syringodea cerviciflora

Syringodea cerviciflora, commonly known as the neck-flowered orchid, is a rare and distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with flowers arranged in a cluster that resembles a neck or collar. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, providing a striking contrast to its intricate floral display.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Syringodea cerviciflora is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
