Stethoma comata

Stethoma comata, commonly known as the velvety moth orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, violet-purple flowers with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Stethoma comata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral appearance.
