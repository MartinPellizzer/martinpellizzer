Spiraeanthemum meridionale

Spiraeanthemum meridionale, commonly known as the southern spiraea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Spiraeanthemum meridionale is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
