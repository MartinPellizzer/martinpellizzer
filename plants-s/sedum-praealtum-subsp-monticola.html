Sedum praealtum subsp. monticola

Sedum praealtum subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine garden environments.

Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in harsh, arid conditions.
