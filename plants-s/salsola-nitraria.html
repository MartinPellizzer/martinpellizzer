Salsola nitraria

Salsola nitraria, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in deserts and saline soils. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in disturbed areas.

Salsola nitraria is known for its ability to tolerate high levels of salt and drought, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
