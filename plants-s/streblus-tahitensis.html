Streblus tahitensis

Streblus tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is sometimes used in local cuisines.
