Searsia longipes var. elgonensis

Searsia longipes var. elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon yellowwood, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its yellowish bark and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
