Scrophularia ebulifolia var. schousboei

Scrophularia ebulifolia var. schousboei, commonly known as the Schousboe's beardroot, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes, which attract various pollinators.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
