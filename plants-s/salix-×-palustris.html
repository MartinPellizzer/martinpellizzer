Salix × palustris

Salix × palustris, commonly known as the swamp willow, is a hybrid deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, arching branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down. Its catkins, which are its flowers, appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Salix × palustris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
