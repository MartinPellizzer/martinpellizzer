Sideritis christii

Sideritis christii, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis christii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
