Sanguisorba officinalis var. sabauda

Sanguisorba officinalis var. sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are rhombic or ovate with serrated edges, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
