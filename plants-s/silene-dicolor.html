Silene dicolor

Silene dicolor, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and rocky areas.

Silene dicolor is an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes due to its ornamental value and role in supporting local wildlife.
