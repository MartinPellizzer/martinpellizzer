Sterculia pallidiflora

Sterculia pallidiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
