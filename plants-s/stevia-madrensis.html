Stevia madrensis

Stevia madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia madrensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
