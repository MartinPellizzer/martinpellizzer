Smilax campestris var. spruceana

Smilax campestris var. spruceana, commonly known as the spruce smilax, is a variant of the common smilax that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial vine or shrub with stout, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

This variant is often found in shaded environments and is notable for its persistent, waxy leaf surfaces.
