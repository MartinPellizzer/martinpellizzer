Salvia clandestina var. hiemalis

Salvia clandestina var. hiemalis, commonly known as winter sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscapes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is notable for its ability to bloom during colder months, providing color when most gardens are dormant.
