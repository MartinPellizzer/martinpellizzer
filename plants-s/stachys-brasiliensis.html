Stachys brasiliensis

Stachys brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian betony, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Stachys brasiliensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
