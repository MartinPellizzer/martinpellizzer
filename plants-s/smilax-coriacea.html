Smilax coriacea

Smilax coriacea, commonly known as the rough smilax, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Smilax coriacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
