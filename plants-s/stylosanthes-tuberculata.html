Stylosanthes tuberculata

Stylosanthes tuberculata, commonly known as the African stylo, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. It is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
