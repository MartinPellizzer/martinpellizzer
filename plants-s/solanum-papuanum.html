Solanum papuanum

Solanum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Papua and surrounding regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Solanum papuanum produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by berries that ripen to a bright red color.
