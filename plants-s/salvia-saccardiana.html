Salvia saccardiana

Salvia saccardiana, commonly known as the Saccardian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia saccardiana is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
