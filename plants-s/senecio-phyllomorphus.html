Senecio phyllomorphus

Senecio phyllomorphus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a robust stem and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
