Spergularia fimbriata

Spergularia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are linear and often have a fringe of fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Spergularia fimbriata is a perennial species that is well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
