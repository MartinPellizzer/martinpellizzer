Salsola praecox

Salsola praecox, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and widely naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Salsola praecox plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in certain areas.
