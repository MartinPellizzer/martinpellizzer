Stenaria nigricans var. gypsophila

Stenaria nigricans var. gypsophila, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive choice for ornamental gardening.
