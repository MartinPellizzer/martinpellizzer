Saraca palembanica

Saraca palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral bracts that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive aromatic scent.
