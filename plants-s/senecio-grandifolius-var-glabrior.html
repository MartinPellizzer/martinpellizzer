Senecio grandifolius var. glabrior

Senecio grandifolius var. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky slopes and open grasslands.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
