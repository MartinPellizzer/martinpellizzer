Salvia varia

Salvia varia, commonly known as the variable sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia varia is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
