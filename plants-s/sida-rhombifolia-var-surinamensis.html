Sida rhombifolia var. surinamensis

Sida rhombifolia var. surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist, sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.
