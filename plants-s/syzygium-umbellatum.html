Syzygium umbellatum

Syzygium umbellatum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in corymbs. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Syzygium umbellatum is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
