Saussurea leontodontoides

Saussurea leontodontoides, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes with cold climates. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
