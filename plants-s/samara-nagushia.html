Samara nagushia

Samara nagushia, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons, particularly in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the tree has a graceful, often spreading growth habit.

The botanical features of Samara nagushia make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
