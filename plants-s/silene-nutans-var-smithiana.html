Silene nutans var. smithiana

Silene nutans var. smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's drooping campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a herbaceous perennial that is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
