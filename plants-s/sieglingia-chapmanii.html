Sieglingia chapmanii

Sieglingia chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's sieve-bush, is a rare shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, spherical fruits. Sieglingia chapmanii is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a species of conservation concern due to its limited distribution.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important subject for botanical study.
