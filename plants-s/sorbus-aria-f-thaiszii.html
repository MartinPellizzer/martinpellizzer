Sorbus aria f. thaiszii

Sorbus aria f. thaiszii, commonly known as the Thaiszii whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a red or orange color. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Its genetic variation contributes to the biodiversity of the Sorbus genus.
