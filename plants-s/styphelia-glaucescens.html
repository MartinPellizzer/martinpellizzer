Styphelia glaucescens

Styphelia glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to the presence of fine, waxy hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Styphelia glaucescens thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

It is an important component of heathland ecosystems and is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
