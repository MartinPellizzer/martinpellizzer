Stisseria normalis

Stisseria normalis, commonly known as the common stisseria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Stisseriaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance.

The botanical interest of Stisseria normalis lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
