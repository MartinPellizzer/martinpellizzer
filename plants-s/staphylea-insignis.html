Staphylea insignis

Staphylea insignis, commonly known as the sweet flag or sweet reed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Staphylea insignis is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild, pleasant fragrance that contributes to its ornamental value.
