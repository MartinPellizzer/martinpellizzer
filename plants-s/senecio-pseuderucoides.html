Senecio pseuderucoides

Senecio pseuderucoides, commonly known as the false African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Senecio pseuderucoides is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
