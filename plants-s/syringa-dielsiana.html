Syringa dielsiana

Syringa dielsiana, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Syringa dielsiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
