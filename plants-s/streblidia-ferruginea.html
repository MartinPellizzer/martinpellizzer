Streblidia ferruginea

Streblidia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically a dull green with a reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain several seeds.

Streblidia ferruginea plays a role in the ecological system by providing food for native birds and insects.
