Syzygium lakshnakarae

Syzygium lakshnakarae, commonly known as the Lakshnakarae apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
