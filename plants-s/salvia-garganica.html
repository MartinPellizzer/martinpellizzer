Salvia garganica

Salvia garganica, commonly known as Garganica sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its fragrant properties and medicinal uses.
