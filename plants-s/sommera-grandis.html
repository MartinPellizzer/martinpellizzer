Sommera grandis

Sommera grandis, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
