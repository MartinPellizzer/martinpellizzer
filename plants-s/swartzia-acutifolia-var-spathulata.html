Swartzia acutifolia var. spathulata

Swartzia acutifolia var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved swartzia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. It produces woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
