Sideritis spinosa var. subspinosa

Sideritis spinosa var. subspinosa, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.
