Serratula gmelinii subsp. tanaitica

Serratula gmelinii subsp.

tanaitica, commonly known as the Caucasian serratula, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
