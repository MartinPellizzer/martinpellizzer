Sclerostemma scopolii

Sclerostemma scopolii, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in sunny locations. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often hairy.

Sclerostemma scopolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
