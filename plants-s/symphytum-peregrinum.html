Symphytum peregrinum

Symphytum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering symphytum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems and produces violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Its name reflects its widespread distribution across different regions.
