Salvia dryophila

Salvia dryophila, commonly known as the dry prairie sage, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to prairie and woodland habitats. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia dryophila plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
