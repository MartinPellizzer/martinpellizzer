Sceptrum carolinum

Sceptrum carolinum, commonly known as the Carolina false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white vein pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

The leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
