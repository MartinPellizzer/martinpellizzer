Spiranthes australis var. pudica

Spiranthes australis var. pudica, commonly known as the slender ladies' tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spirally arranged flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are typically arranged in a helical pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.
