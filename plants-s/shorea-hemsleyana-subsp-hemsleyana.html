Shorea hemsleyana subsp. hemsleyana

Shorea hemsleyana subsp.

hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's rosewood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a high oil content.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its timber but is also of ecological importance in its native habitats.
