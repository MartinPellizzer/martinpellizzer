Saundersia paniculata

Saundersia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branched, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Saundersia paniculata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
