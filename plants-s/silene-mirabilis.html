Silene mirabilis

Silene mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ephemeral blooming period. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene mirabilis is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and moths.
