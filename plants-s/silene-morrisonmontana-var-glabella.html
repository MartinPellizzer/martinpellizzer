Silene morrisonmontana var. glabella

Silene morrisonmontana var. glabella, commonly known as the Morrison Mountains sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is particularly rare and is often found in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous areas.
