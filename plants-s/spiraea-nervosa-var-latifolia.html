Spiraea nervosa var. latifolia

Spiraea nervosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are broad and ovate, with a serrated edge, and small white to pinkish flowers that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

The species is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various soil types.
