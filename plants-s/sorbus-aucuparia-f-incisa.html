Sorbus aucuparia f. incisa

Sorbus aucuparia f. incisa, commonly known as the serrated rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
