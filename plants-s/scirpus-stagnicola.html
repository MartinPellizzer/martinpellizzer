Scirpus stagnicola

Scirpus stagnicola, commonly known as marsh clubmoss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that form a rosette at the base, with small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist to submerged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus stagnicola plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
