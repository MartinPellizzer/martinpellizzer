Stylosanthes setosa

Stylosanthes setosa, commonly known as slender stylosanthe, is a low-growing perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, and it produces trifoliate leaves with three small leaflets. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in dryland agriculture. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Stylosanthes setosa is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
