Salsola tragus var. pseudocollina

Salsola tragus var. pseudocollina, commonly known as sandmat or Russian thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to Eurasia and has been introduced to various parts of the world, often becoming invasive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ability to spread rapidly makes it a significant weed in agricultural and natural areas.
