Sanicula chinensis

Sanicula chinensis, commonly known as Chinese sanicle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sanicula chinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
