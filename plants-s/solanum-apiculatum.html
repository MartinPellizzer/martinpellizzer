Solanum apiculatum

Solanum apiculatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep red, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum apiculatum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
