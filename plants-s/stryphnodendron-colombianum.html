Stryphnodendron colombianum

Stryphnodendron colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.
