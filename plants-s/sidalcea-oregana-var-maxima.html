Sidalcea oregana var. maxima

Sidalcea oregana var. maxima, commonly known as the Oregon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and palmately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
