Scorzonera curvata

Scorzonera curvata, commonly known as the bent-stem scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Scorzonera curvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
