Salvia albiflora f. caerulescens

Salvia albiflora f. caerulescens, commonly known as the Azure-flowered sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking, sky-blue to lavender flowers in whorled clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its ornamental value lies in its vivid floral display and drought tolerance.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
