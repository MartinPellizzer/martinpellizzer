Silene aetolica

Silene aetolica, commonly known as the Aeolian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene aetolica is often found in areas with limited moisture, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
