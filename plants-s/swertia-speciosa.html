Swertia speciosa

Swertia speciosa, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and is often found growing in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. Swertia speciosa belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
