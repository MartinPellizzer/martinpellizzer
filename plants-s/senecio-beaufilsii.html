Senecio beaufilsii

Senecio beaufilsii, commonly known as the Beaufort's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
