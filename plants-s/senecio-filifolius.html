Senecio filifolius

Senecio filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio filifolius is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
