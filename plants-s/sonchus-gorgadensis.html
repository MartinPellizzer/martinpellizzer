Sonchus gorgadensis

Sonchus gorgadensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are edible when young, though they may become bitter with age, and it has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it both a potential weed and a resource in certain regions.
