Senecio novenlepis

Senecio novenlepis, commonly known as the nine-ribbed groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with a rosette of leaves.

Senecio novenlepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
