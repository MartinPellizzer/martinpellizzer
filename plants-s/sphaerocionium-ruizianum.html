Sphaerocionium ruizianum

Sphaerocionium ruizianum, commonly known as the Ruiz's globular cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
