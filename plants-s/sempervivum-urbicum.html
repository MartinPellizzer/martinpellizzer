Sempervivum urbicum

Sempervivum urbicum, commonly known as the common houseleek, is a low-growing, rosette-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in rocky or urban environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Sempervivum urbicum is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
