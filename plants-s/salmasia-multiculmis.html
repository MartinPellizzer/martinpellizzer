Salmasia multiculmis

Salmasia multiculmis, commonly known as the multibract fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its unique morphology includes bract-like structures that surround the inflorescence, giving it its common name.

Salmasia multiculmis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
