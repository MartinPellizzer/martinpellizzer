Syzygium brachyantherum

Syzygium brachyantherum, commonly known as the white apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible, white, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a fragrant aroma when crushed.

Syzygium brachyantherum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
