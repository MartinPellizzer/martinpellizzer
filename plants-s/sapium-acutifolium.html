Sapium acutifolium

Sapium acutifolium, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
