Salix wendtii

Salix wendtii, commonly known as the Wendt's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salix wendtii is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.
