Sphenogyne anthemoides

Sphenogyne anthemoides, commonly known as the slender rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and moths.

Sphenogyne anthemoides is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.
