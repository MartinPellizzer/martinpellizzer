Sasa kurilensis f. kimmei

Sasa kurilensis f. kimmei, commonly known as the Kimmei sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
