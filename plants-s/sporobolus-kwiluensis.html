Sporobolus kwiluensis

Sporobolus kwiluensis, commonly known as the Kwiluensis grass, is a species native to Central Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry blades. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a compact panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Sporobolus kwiluensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
