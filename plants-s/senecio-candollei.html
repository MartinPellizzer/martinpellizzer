Senecio candollei

Senecio candollei, commonly known as the Cape gold or golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are softly hairy. Its bright yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late winter to early spring.

Senecio candollei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
