Salvia barbata

Salvia barbata, commonly known as the beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia barbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
