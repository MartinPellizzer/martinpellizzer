Sedum rosea var. polygamum

Sedum rosea var. polygamum, commonly known as the rose-colored stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically rose-pink to reddish in color, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
