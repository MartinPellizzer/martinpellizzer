Salvia chinensis var. intermedia

Salvia chinensis var. intermedia, commonly known as Chinese sage or purple flower sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
