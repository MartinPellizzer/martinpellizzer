Silene quadriloba

Silene quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has four-lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene quadriloba is an important species for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in native plant gardens.
