Scrophularia kiriloviana

Scrophularia kiriloviana, commonly known as Kirilov's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, characteristic of the Scrophulariaceae family. Its flowers are usually tubular with two lips, and it produces capsule fruits containing small, black seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
