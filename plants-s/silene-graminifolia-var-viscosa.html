Silene graminifolia var. viscosa

Silene graminifolia var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky grass pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes. Its variety name, viscosa, refers to the sticky or glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
