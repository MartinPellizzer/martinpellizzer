Sinapis retrohirsuta

Sinapis retrohirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas and is native to regions in Europe and Asia. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its fruits are short pods containing several seeds.

Sinapis retrohirsuta is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
