Scabiosa gigantea

Scabiosa gigantea, commonly known as the giant scabious, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower meadows.

Scabiosa gigantea is also known for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
