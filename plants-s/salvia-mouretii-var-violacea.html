Salvia mouretii var. violacea

Salvia mouretii var. violacea, commonly known as violet-flowered sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically gray-green, hairy, and aromatic, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
