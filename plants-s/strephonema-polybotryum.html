Strephonema polybotryum

Strephonema polybotryum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable resource in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.

Strephonema polybotryum is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in ecological succession.
