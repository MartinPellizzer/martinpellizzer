Stelis hiatilabia

Stelis hiatilabia, commonly known as the white-lipped stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, where it thrives in partial shade. The plant features a short, erect stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a raceme, often with a faintly fragrant scent.

Stelis hiatilabia is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
