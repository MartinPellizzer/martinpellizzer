Sideritis approximata

Sideritis approximata, commonly known as mountain tea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes herbs and shrubs characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis approximata is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for its calming and antispasmodic effects.

Its leaves are harvested and dried to make teas, which are valued for their soothing properties.
