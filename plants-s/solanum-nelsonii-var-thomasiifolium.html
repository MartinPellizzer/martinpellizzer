Solanum nelsonii var. thomasiifolium

Solanum nelsonii var. thomasiifolium, commonly known as the Thomasiifolium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often described as resembling the leaves of the Thomasiis genus. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
