Setaria viridis f. japonica

Setaria viridis f. japonica, commonly known as Japanese green millet, is a variant of green millet native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or for seed production.
