Sclerolobium duckei

Sclerolobium duckei, commonly known as the African screwtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Sclerolobium duckei is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
