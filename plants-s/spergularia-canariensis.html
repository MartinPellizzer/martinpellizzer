Spergularia canariensis

Spergularia canariensis, commonly known as Canary Island sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky coastal environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Spergularia canariensis is often found in dunes and coastal vegetation, playing a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
