Sida teneriffae

Sida teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island sida, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features small, pale green leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually pale blue or white. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Sida teneriffae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
