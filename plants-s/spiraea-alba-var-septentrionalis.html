Spiraea alba var. septentrionalis

Spiraea alba var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern white spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of white, flat-topped flowers in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
