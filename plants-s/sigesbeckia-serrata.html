Sigesbeckia serrata

Sigesbeckia serrata, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, hairy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Sigesbeckia serrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
