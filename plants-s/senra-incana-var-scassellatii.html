Senra incana var. scassellatii

Senra incana var. scassellatii, commonly known as the woolly senra, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
