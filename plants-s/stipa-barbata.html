Stipa barbata

Stipa barbata, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces airy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Stipa barbata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
