Sida acrantha

Sida acrantha, commonly known as the smallleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features small, pale green leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sida acrantha is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and other open habitats.
