Stemodia viscosa var. grandiflora

Stemodia viscosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the sticky stemodia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
