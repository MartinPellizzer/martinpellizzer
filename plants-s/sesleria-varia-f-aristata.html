Sesleria varia f. aristata

Sesleria varia f. aristata, commonly known as the tufted wood sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated leaf tips, which give it its specific epithet "aristata.".
