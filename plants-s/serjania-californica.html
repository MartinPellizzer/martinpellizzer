Serjania californica

Serjania californica, commonly known as the California serjania, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Serjania californica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and chaparral habitats.
