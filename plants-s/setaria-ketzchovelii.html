Setaria ketzchovelii

Setaria ketzchovelii, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system for water absorption.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
