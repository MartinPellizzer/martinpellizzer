Scutellaria botbaevae

Scutellaria botbaevae, commonly known as the Botbaev's skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
