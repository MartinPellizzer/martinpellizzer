Stevia fastigiata

Stevia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, making it a popular natural sweetener.

Stevia fastigiata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and commercial use.
