Senecio leucanthemus

Senecio leucanthemus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which are typical of the genus Senecio. It grows in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and dry grasslands, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Senecio leucanthemus is also known for its toxic properties, making it a plant to avoid in gardens and natural areas.
