Specularia perfoliata

Specularia perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
