Sinomalus mandshurica

Sinomalus mandshurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sinomalus mandshurica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
