Solanum zuccagnianum

Solanum zuccagnianum, commonly known as the Zuccagnia nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, and it bears berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are hairy as well.

Solanum zuccagnianum is often found in humid, shaded environments within the tropical rainforests of its native region.
