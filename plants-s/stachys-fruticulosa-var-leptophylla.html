Stachys fruticulosa var. leptophylla

Stachys fruticulosa var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
