Spiraea acuminata

Spiraea acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea acuminata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
