Serratula albida

Serratula albida, commonly known as the white serrata, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Serratula albida is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
