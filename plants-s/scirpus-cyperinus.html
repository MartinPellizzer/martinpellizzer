Scirpus cyperinus

Scirpus cyperinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, where it thrives in saturated or waterlogged soils. Scirpus cyperinus has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the sedge family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
