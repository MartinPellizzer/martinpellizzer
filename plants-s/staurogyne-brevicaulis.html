Staurogyne brevicaulis

Staurogyne brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pollinated by insects. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Staurogyne brevicaulis is often found in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.
