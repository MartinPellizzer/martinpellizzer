Sclerolobium odoratissimum

Sclerolobium odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods containing flattened, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
