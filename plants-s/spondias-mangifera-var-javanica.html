Spondias mangifera var. javanica

Spondias mangifera var. javanica, commonly known as the Java mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a round, segmented berry that turns from green to bright red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and the edible fruit, which is rich in antioxidants.
