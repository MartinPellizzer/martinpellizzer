Sphaerothylax algiformis

Sphaerothylax algiformis, commonly known as the algae-like spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble algae in appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
