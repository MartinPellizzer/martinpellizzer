Solanum tumulicola

Solanum tumulicola, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellowish color and contain numerous seeds.

Solanum tumulicola is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
