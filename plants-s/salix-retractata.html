Salix retractata

Salix retractata, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
