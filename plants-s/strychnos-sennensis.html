Strychnos sennensis

Strychnos sennensis, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, though it is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
