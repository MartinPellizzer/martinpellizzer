Salix caprea var. sphacelata

Salix caprea var. sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a variant of the common willow that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks. Its catkins are a notable feature, with male and female flowers appearing on separate stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife and stabilizing riverbanks.
