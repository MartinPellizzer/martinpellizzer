Stalagmitis indica

Stalagmitis indica, commonly known as the Indian stalagmite plant, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of India.

It is characterized by its distinctive upward-growing, column-like structures that resemble stalagmites, forming from the accumulation of calcium carbonate deposits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in limestone caves and rocky crevices. Its leaves are typically small, waxy, and adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

While it has limited commercial use, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological rarity and unique morphology.
