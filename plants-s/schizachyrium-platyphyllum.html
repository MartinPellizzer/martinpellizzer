Schizachyrium platyphyllum

Schizachyrium platyphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are flat and often have a bluish tinge, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy ornamental and ecological plant.

Its foliage provides food for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
