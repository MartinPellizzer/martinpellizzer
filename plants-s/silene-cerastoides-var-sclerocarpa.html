Silene cerastoides var. sclerocarpa

Silene cerastoides var. sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-seeded catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain hard, dark seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species in harsh environmental conditions.
