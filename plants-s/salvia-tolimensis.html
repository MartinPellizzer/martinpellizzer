Salvia tolimensis

Salvia tolimensis, commonly known as Tolima sage, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
