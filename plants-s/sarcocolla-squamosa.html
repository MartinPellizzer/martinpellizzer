Sarcocolla squamosa

Sarcocolla squamosa, commonly known as the scaly sarcocolla, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.
