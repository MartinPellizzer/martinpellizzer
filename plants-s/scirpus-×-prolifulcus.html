Scirpus × prolifulcus

Scirpus × prolifulcus, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense clusters of cylindrical inflorescences. This species thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow ponds, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and filtering water. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant's ability to tolerate a wide range of water depths makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
