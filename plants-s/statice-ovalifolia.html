Statice ovalifolia

Statice ovalifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces spikes of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Statice ovalifolia is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
