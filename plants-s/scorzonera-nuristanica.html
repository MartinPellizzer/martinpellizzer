Scorzonera nuristanica

Scorzonera nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan mountain tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in northern Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant is known for its yellow to orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold and arid environments. Scorzonera nuristanica is often found in rocky slopes and alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique habitat and potential pharmacological uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
