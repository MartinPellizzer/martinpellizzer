Suaeda ramosissima

Suaeda ramosissima, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Suaeda ramosissima plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
