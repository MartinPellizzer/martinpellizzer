Sisyrinchium elegans

Sisyrinchium elegans, commonly known as the elegant blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
