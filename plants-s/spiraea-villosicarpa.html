Spiraea villosicarpa

Spiraea villosicarpa, commonly known as the woolly spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea villosicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
