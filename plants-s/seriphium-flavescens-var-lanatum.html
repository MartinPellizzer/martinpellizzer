Seriphium flavescens var. lanatum

Seriphium flavescens var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
