Sphaerocarya moschifera

Sphaerocarya moschifera, commonly known as the musk persimmon, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, orange to red berries that are highly aromatic and contain a high concentration of essential oils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are clustered and typically appear before the leaves in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
