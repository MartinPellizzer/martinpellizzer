Scorzonera aucheriana

Scorzonera aucheriana, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

Scorzonera aucheriana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
