Salvia derasa

Salvia derasa, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Salvia derasa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
