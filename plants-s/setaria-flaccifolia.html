Setaria flaccifolia

Setaria flaccifolia, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a herbaceous annual grass species native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute seeds. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is often used as a forage crop in some regions.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
