Syzygium ludovicii

Syzygium ludovicii, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Syzygium ludovicii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
