Smallanthus latisquamus

Smallanthus latisquamus, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators. Smallanthus latisquamus is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
