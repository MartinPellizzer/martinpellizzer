Senecio schlechtendahlii

Senecio schlechtendahlii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.
