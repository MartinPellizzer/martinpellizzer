Symphyotrichum falcatum var. commutatum

Symphyotrichum falcatum var. commutatum, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
