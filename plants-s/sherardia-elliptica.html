Sherardia elliptica

Sherardia elliptica, commonly known as the red sanders, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
