Syzygium filiforme var. clavimyrtus

Syzygium filiforme var. clavimyrtus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple or clavimyrtus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
