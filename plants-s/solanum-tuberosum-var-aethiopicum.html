Solanum tuberosum var. aethiopicum

Solanum tuberosum var. aethiopicum, commonly known as the Ethiopian potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinct botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces tubers with a unique, often purple or red skin and a starchy, slightly sweet flesh. Native to Ethiopia, this variety thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped and hairy.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other potato species.
