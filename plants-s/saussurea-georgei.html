Saussurea georgei

Saussurea georgei, commonly known as the George's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, typical of its alpine habitat. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
