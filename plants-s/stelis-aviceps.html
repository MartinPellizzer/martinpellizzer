Stelis aviceps

Stelis aviceps, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower that resembles a bird's beak, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Stelis aviceps thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
