Salvia natalensis

Salvia natalensis, commonly known as Natal sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Natal sage is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
