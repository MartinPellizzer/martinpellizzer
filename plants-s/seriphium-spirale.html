Seriphium spirale

Seriphium spirale, commonly known as the spiral hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a spiral pattern.

Seriphium spirale is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
