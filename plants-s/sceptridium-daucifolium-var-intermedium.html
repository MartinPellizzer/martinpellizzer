Sceptridium daucifolium var. intermedium

Sceptridium daucifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
