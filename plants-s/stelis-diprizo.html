Stelis diprizo

Stelis diprizo, commonly known as the orchid with pendant flowers, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that hang from the plant. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it produces showy, white or pale pink blooms.

Stelis diprizo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
