Sporobolus geminatus

Sporobolus geminatus, commonly known as the bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and its ability to form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

Sporobolus geminatus is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
