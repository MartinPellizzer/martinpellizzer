Specularia falcata subsp. castellana

Specularia falcata subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, silver-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring, arranged in terminal racemes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
