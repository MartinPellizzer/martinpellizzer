Sieruela viscosa

Sieruela viscosa, commonly known as sticky sieruela, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
