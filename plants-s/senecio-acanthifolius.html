Senecio acanthifolius

Senecio acanthifolius, commonly known as the spinyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, spiny leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
