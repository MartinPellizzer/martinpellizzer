Specklinia periodica

Specklinia periodica, commonly known as the periodic speckled orchid, is a rare and fascinating epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique speckled foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in a cyclical pattern, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for its natural habitat on tree trunks and branches.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Specklinia periodica is considered a challenging species to cultivate outside its native environment.
