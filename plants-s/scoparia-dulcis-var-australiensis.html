Scoparia dulcis var. australiensis

Scoparia dulcis var. australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and aromatic, slightly sweet-smelling leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
