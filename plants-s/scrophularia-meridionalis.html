Scrophularia meridionalis

Scrophularia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
