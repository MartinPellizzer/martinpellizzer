Silene drummondii

Silene drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
