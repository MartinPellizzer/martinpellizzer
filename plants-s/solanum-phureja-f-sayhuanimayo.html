Solanum phureja f. sayhuanimayo

Solanum phureja f. sayhuanimayo, commonly known as the Sayhuanimayo potato, is a wild potato species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are typically small and irregular in shape. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, known for its diverse array of flowering plants, including tomatoes and peppers. It exhibits a semi-shrub growth habit with hairy stems and lobed leaves.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic diversity and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
