Silene inflata var. colorata

Silene inflata var. colorata, commonly known as the showy bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, inflated seed pods that range in color from red to purple, which give the plant its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers are pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
