Saxifraga ledebouriana

Saxifraga ledebouriana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Saxifraga ledebouriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
