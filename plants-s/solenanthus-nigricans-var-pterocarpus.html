Solenanthus nigricans var. pterocarpus

Solenanthus nigricans var. pterocarpus, commonly known as the black-flowered solenanthus, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with distinctive dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically clustered in small inflorescences, and it is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

The variety pterocarpus is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, making it a notable species within the Solenanthus genus.
