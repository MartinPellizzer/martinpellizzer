Specklinia daviesii

Specklinia daviesii, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
