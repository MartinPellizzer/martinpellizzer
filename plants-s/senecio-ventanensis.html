Senecio ventanensis

Senecio ventanensis, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.

Senecio ventanensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.
