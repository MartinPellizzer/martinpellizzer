Salvia ramamoorthyana

Salvia ramamoorthyana, commonly known as Ramamoorthy's sage, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in moist, shaded forest areas and thrives in humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
