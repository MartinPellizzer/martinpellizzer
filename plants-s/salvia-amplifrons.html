Salvia amplifrons

Salvia amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia amplifrons is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
