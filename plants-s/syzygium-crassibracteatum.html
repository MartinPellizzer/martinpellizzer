Syzygium crassibracteatum

Syzygium crassibracteatum, commonly known as the broad-bract syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
