Stillingia lineata subsp. pacifica

Stillingia lineata subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as Pacific stelleria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the Pacific coast.

The plant features slender stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
