Scabiosa humilis

Scabiosa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa humilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
