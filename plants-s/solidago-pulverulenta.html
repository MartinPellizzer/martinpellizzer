Solidago pulverulenta

Solidago pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago pulverulenta is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
