Stevia mitopoda

Stevia mitopoda, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested for use in herbal teas and as a sugar substitute.

Stevia mitopoda thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
