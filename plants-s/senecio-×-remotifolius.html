Senecio × remotifolius

Senecio × remotifolius, commonly known as the African Violet, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Senecio × remotifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
