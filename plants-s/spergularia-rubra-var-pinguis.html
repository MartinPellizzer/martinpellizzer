Spergularia rubra var. pinguis

Spergularia rubra var. pinguis, commonly known as the fatstem sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
