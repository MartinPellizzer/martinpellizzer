Scleria trisulcata

Scleria trisulcata, commonly known as the three-ridged sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Scleria trisulcata has three distinct longitudinal ridges on its stem, which is a key identifying feature.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
