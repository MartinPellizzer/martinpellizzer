Solanum brusquense

Solanum brusquense, commonly known as the Brusquense nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
