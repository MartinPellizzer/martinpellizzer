Salix arguta var. alpigena

Salix arguta var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine willow, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
