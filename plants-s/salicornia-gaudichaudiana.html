Salicornia gaudichaudiana

Salicornia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as sea bean or glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in coastal environments with high salinity.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and the plant is often found in salt marshes and tidal flats.

Salicornia gaudichaudiana plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
