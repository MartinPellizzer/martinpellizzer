Salix caprea var. sinica

Salix caprea var. sinica, commonly known as Chinese pussy willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and its distinctive catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and forest edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
