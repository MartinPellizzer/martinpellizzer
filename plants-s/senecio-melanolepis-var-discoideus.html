Senecio melanolepis var. discoideus

Senecio melanolepis var. discoideus, commonly known as the "Cushion Senecio," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that form a compact cushion-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
