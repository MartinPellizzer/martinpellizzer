Sterculia pruriens var. parviflora

Sterculia pruriens var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant is often found in moist lowland forests and is adapted to humid, warm climates. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has traditional uses in local communities.
