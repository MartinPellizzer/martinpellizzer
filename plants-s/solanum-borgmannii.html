Solanum borgmannii

Solanum borgmannii, commonly known as the Borgia nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Argentina and Chile.
