Stachys harleyana

Stachys harleyana, commonly known as Harley's violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
