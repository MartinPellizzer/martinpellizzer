Schismus calycinus var. genuinus

Schismus calycinus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a succulent perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
