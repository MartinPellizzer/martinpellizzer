Santolina pectinata

Santolina pectinata, commonly known as silver santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
