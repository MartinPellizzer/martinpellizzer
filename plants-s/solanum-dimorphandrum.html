Solanum dimorphandrum

Solanum dimorphandrum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
