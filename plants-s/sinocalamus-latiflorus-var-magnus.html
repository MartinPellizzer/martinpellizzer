Sinocalamus latiflorus var. magnus

Sinocalamus latiflorus var. magnus, commonly known as the broadflower bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowering is relatively rare, occurring only once in its lifetime, which makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in traditional Chinese gardens.
