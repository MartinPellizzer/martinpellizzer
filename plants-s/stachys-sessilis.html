Stachys sessilis

Stachys sessilis, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Stachys sessilis is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
