Symplocos juiyenensis

Symplocos juiyenensis, commonly known as the Juiyen's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright purple berries that are a striking contrast to its green foliage. These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.

Symplocos juiyenensis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
