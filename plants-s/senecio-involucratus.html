Senecio involucratus

Senecio involucratus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Senecio involucratus is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
