Salvia cadmica var. cadmica

Salvia cadmica var. cadmica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
