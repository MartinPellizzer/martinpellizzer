Sabicea tchapensis

Sabicea tchapensis, commonly known as the Cameroon flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange floral display. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds coated in a hairy, fibrous covering. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Sabicea tchapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
