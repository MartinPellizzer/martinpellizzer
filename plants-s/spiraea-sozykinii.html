Spiraea sozykinii

Spiraea sozykinii, commonly known as the Siberian spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, providing a textured appearance.

Spiraea sozykinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
