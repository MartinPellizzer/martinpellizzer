Scaphosepalum beluosum

Scaphosepalum beluosum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a unique shape with a prominent lip petal.
