Solanum praealtum

Solanum praealtum, commonly known as the high mountain nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, oval-shaped leaves, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, and the plant is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Solanum praealtum is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
