Somphoxylon magnifolium

Somphoxylon magnifolium, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad leaves that are a deep emerald green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers during the rainy season, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is now considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
