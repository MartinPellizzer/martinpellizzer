Schoenus crinitus

Schoenus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Schoenus crinitus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
