Strychnos spinosa var. pubescens

Strychnos spinosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy strobilanthes, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. The plant features pubescent (hairy) leaves and stems, which give it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
