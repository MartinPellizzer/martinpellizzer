Schizaea fistulosa var. australis

Schizaea fistulosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear spirally arranged, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters called strobili, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

This variety is native to Australia and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in its habitat.
