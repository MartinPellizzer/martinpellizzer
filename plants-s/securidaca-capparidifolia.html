Securidaca capparidifolia

Securidaca capparidifolia, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pubescent and have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
