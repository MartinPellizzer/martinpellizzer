Salvia camphorata

Salvia camphorata, commonly known as the camphor sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant produces aromatic essential oils, particularly in its leaves and stems, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia camphorata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
