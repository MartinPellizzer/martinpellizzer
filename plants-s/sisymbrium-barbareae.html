Sisymbrium barbareae

Sisymbrium barbareae, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and lobed. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, making it both a weed and a subject of ecological study.
