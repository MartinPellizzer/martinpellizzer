Serapias × meridionalis

Serapias × meridionalis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Serapias, which includes several species of orchids characterized by their distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near streams or in damp meadows. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a long, slender spur that houses the reproductive structures.

Serapias × meridionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
