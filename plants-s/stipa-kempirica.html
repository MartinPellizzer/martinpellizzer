Stipa kempirica

Stipa kempirica, commonly known as the Kempirica needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Stipa kempirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
