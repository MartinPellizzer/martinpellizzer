Satureja kilimandschari

Satureja kilimandschari, commonly known as Kilimanjaro thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Satureja kilimandschari thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
