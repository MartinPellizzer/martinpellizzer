Solidago capitata

Solidago capitata, commonly known as the purpletop blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
