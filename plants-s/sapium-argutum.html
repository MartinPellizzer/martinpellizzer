Sapium argutum

Sapium argutum, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a three-winged samara that disperses by wind.

The tree is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
