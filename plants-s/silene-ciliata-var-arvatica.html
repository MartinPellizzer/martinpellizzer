Silene ciliata var. arvatica

Silene ciliata var. arvatica, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
