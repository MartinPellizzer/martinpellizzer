Scrophularia bornmuelleri

Scrophularia bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia bornmuelleri is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
