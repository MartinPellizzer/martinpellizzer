Senecio semperflorens

Senecio semperflorens, commonly known as the African Violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces bright purple, pink, or white blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Senecio semperflorens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
