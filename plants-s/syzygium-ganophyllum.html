Syzygium ganophyllum

Syzygium ganophyllum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
