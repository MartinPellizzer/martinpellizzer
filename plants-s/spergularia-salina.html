Spergularia salina

Spergularia salina, commonly known as saltwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it thrives under high salt concentrations. Spergularia salina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
