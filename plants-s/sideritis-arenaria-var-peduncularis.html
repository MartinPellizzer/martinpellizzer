Sideritis arenaria var. peduncularis

Sideritis arenaria var. peduncularis, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Sideritis arenaria var. peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
