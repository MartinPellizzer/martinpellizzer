Syzygium zippelianum

Syzygium zippelianum, commonly known as the Zippel's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium zippelianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
