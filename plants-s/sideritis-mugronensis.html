Sideritis mugronensis

Sideritis mugronensis, commonly known as Mugron mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. Sideritis mugronensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
