Saguaster seemannii

Saguaster seemannii, commonly known as the Mexican saguaro, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that develop a tree-like structure as it matures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations to desert environments include thick, waxy skin to reduce water loss and spines for protection.

Saguaster seemannii plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
