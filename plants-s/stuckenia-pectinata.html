Stuckenia pectinata

Stuckenia pectinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and slow-moving freshwater environments.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

Stuckenia pectinata is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
