Salvia eremophila

Salvia eremophila, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia eremophila is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
