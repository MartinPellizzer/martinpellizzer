Stevia sanguinea

Stevia sanguinea, commonly known as red stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia sanguinea is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of natural sweetener.
