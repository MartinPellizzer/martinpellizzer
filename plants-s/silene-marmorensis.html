Silene marmorensis

Silene marmorensis, commonly known as the marble plant, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive marbled pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Silene marmorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
