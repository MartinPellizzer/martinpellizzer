Swartzia lamellata

Swartzia lamellata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species in some regions.
