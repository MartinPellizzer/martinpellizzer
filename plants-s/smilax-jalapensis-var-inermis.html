Smilax jalapensis var. inermis

Smilax jalapensis var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a species of climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
