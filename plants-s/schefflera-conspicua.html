Schefflera conspicua

Schefflera conspicua, commonly known as the Hawaiian Schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are usually a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Schefflera conspicua is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique appearance.
