Sorbus sibirica var. sawurensis

Sorbus sibirica var. sawurensis, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
