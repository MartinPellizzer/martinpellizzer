Symphyotrichum fendleri

Symphyotrichum fendleri, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes, where it has adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils. Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
