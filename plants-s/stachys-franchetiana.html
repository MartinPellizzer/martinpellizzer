Stachys franchetiana

Stachys franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese betony, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Stachys franchetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
