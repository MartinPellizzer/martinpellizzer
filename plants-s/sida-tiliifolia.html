Sida tiliifolia

Sida tiliifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Sida tiliifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
