Strobilanthes madagascariensis var. calvescens

Strobilanthes madagascariensis var. calvescens, commonly known as the Madagascar flame flower, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
