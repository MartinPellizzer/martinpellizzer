Schefflera mathewsii

Schefflera mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leaflets radiating from a central stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically palmately compound, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly leathery.

Schefflera mathewsii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
