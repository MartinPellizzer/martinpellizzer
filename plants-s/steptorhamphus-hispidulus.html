Steptorhamphus hispidulus

Steptorhamphus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance along the stem and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
