Statice arabica

Statice arabica, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used traditionally for their strong fibers, similar to flax.

Statice arabica plays a role in stabilizing soil in sandy environments and supports pollinators with its nectar-rich flowers.
