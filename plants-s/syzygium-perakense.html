Syzygium perakense

Syzygium perakense, commonly known as the Perak tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature. It is valued for its edible pulp, which has a tart, acidic flavor similar to tamarind.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
