Solanum phoberum

Solanum phoberum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or bhut jolokia, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that develop into bright red, elongated fruits. The plant is known for its extremely high capsaicin content, making it one of the hottest chili peppers in the world. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
