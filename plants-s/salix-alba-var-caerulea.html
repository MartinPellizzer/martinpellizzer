Salix alba var. caerulea

Salix alba var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue poplar, is a variant of the white willow that exhibits distinctive blue-gray foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-blue hue due to dense pubescence. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its unique color and graceful form.
