Sarcanthus crucicallus

Sarcanthus crucicallus, commonly known as the cross-leaved sarcanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive cross-shaped venation pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
