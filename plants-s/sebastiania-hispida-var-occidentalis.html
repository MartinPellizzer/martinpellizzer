Sebastiania hispida var. occidentalis

Sebastiania hispida var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Sebaceous Bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and dry landscapes.
