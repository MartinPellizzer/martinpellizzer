Solidago rigida var. laevicaulis

Solidago rigida var. laevicaulis, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, rigid stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
