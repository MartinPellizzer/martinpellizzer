Senecio gramineus

Senecio gramineus, commonly known as the green everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces numerous narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio gramineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
