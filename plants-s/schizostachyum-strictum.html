Schizostachyum strictum

Schizostachyum strictum, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, feathery foliage. The plant features a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern at the top of the plant.

Schizostachyum strictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
