Senecio sotoanus

Senecio sotoanus, commonly known as Soto's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.
