Sarcochilus zamboangensis

Sarcochilus zamboangensis, commonly known as the Zamboanga sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant blooms with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with red or orange markings. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
