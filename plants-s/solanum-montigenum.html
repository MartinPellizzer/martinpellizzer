Solanum montigenum

Solanum montigenum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
