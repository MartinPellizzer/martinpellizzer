Senecio carthagenensis

Senecio carthagenensis, commonly known as the Cape York dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by tubular florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Senecio carthagenensis is known for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
