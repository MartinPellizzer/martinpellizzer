Scleria ciliata var. ciliata

Scleria ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the threadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in damp soil near water sources. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with finely ciliated margins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, three-lobed flowers.

Scleria ciliata var. ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist conditions.
