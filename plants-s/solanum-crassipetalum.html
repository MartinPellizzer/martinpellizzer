Solanum crassipetalum

Solanum crassipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are ovate and often have a rough texture, while its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum crassipetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
