Serapias × semilingua

Serapias × semilingua, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the genus Serapias, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias lingua and Serapias sanguinea.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of orange, yellow, and red hues. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It features a compact growth habit with fleshy, linear leaves and produces multiple flower spikes during the spring and summer months.

Serapias × semilingua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in sunny, dry conditions.
