Sanicula simulans

Sanicula simulans, commonly known as the false buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels, which attract various pollinators.

Sanicula simulans thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
