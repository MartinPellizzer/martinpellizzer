Stylidium elachophyllum

Stylidium elachophyllum, commonly known as the dwarf sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
