Selinum verticillatum

Selinum verticillatum, commonly known as threadleaf filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Selinum verticillatum is a low-growing perennial that is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
