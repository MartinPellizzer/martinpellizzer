Stellaria triangularis

Stellaria triangularis, commonly known as the three-angled starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers that are arranged in clusters, giving it a star-like appearance. The plant has three-angled stems and leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
