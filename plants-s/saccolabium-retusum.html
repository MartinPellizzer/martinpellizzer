Saccolabium retusum

Saccolabium retusum, commonly known as the smooth saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
