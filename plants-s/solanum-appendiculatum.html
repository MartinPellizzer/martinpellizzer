Solanum appendiculatum

Solanum appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Solanum appendiculatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
