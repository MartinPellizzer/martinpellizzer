Salix × meinshausenii

Salix × meinshausenii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species known for its distinctive cascading foliage.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a bright green color during the growing season. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways.
