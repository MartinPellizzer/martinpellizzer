Serratula phrygia

Serratula phrygia, commonly known as the Armenian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Serratula phrygia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
