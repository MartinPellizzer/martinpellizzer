Syzygium niviferum

Syzygium niviferum, commonly known as the snow apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
