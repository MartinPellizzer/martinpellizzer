Senna davidsonii

Senna davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Senna davidsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
