Silene paktiensis

Silene paktiensis, commonly known as the Paktian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene paktiensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
