Smilax divaricata

Smilax divaricata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with woody stems that often have sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Smilax divaricata is an important plant in native ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
