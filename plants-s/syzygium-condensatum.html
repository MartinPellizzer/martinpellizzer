Syzygium condensatum

Syzygium condensatum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia and is characterized by its dense, showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

Syzygium condensatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
