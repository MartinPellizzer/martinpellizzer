Stipa parishii var. depauperata

Stipa parishii var. depauperata, commonly known as the poor grass or sparse feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, open panicle that produces small, fluffy spikelets.

Stipa parishii var. depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
