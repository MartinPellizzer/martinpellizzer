Stachytarpheta subincisa

Stachytarpheta subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Stachytarpheta subincisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
