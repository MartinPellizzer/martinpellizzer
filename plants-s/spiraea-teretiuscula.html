Spiraea teretiuscula

Spiraea teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Spiraea teretiuscula is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
