Satyrium macrostachyum

Satyrium macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant orchid or the mountain orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or a lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
