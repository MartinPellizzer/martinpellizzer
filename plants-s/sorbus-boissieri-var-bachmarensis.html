Sorbus boissieri var. bachmarensis

Sorbus boissieri var. bachmarensis, commonly known as the Bachmaren rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils and is often found in forested habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
