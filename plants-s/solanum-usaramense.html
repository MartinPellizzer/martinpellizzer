Solanum usaramense

Solanum usaramense, commonly known as the Usaramense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, ovate leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
