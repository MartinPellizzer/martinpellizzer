Salvia × hegelmaieri nothosubsp.

hegelmaieri, commonly known as the German sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long flowering period.
