Syzygium subdecussatum

Syzygium subdecussatum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, musky flavor. Syzygium subdecussatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes a broad, spreading canopy and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.
