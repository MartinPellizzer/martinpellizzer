Sabicea sanguinosa

Sabicea sanguinosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed sabelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
