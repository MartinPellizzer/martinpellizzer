Solanum muricatum var. popayanum

Solanum muricatum var. popayanum, commonly known as the Popayan gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other economically important crops like tomatoes and potatoes. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor.

The plant thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
