Sapium guaricense

Sapium guaricense, commonly known as Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and trifoliate. The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to a variety of soil types.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in full sun.
