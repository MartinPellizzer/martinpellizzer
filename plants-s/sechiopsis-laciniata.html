Sechiopsis laciniata

Sechiopsis laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Sechiopsis laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
