Spilanthes melissifolia

Spilanthes melissifolia, commonly known as honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Spilanthes melissifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
