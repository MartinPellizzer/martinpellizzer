Sempervivum corymbosum

Sempervivum corymbosum, commonly known as the common houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the nickname "everlasting.".
