Stipa iberica var. pygmaea

Stipa iberica var. pygmaea, commonly known as the Iberian needlegrass, is a small perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces fluffy, pale green to yellowish inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to dry and nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
