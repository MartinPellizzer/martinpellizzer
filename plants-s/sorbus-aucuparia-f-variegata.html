Sorbus aucuparia f. variegata

Sorbus aucuparia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated rowan or variegated whitebeam, is a cultivar of the European rowan tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and yellow margins on its leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
