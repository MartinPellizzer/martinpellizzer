Sideritis incana var. angustifolia

Sideritis incana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
