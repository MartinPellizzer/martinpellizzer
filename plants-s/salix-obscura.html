Salix obscura

Salix obscura, commonly known as the obscure willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salix obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil along waterways.
