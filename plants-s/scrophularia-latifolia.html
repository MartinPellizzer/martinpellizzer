Scrophularia latifolia

Scrophularia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia latifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological roles.
