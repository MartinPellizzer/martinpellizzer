Silene rubella subsp. bergiana

Silene rubella subsp.

bergiana, commonly known as the Bergian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Silene rubella subsp.

bergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
