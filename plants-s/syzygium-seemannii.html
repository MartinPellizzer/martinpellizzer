Syzygium seemannii

Syzygium seemannii, commonly known as the yellow rose of China, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and a reddish-brown bark. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium seemannii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
