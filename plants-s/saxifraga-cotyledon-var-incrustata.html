Saxifraga cotyledon var. incrustata

Saxifraga cotyledon var. incrustata, commonly known as the crinkled saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, crinkled leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
