Swainsona stipularis var. purpurea

Swainsona stipularis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple stipple bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, stipular structures that give it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
