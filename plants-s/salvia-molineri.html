Salvia molineri

Salvia molineri, commonly known as the Chilean sage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and produces vibrant violet or blue flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Salvia molineri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
