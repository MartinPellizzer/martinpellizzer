Shorea peltata

Shorea peltata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, often with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and fruits that are important for local wildlife and ecological balance.
