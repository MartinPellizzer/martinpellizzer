Scleria tenuis

Scleria tenuis, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, three-parted flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or reddish in color. Scleria tenuis thrives in moist, often waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
