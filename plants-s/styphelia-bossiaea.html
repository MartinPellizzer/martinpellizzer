Styphelia bossiaea

Styphelia bossiaea, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wet heathlands of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
