Senecio aureus f. ecoronatus

Senecio aureus f. ecoronatus, commonly known as the golden crown senecio, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically yellow-green in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on tall, branched stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
