Sapranthus ligularis

Sapranthus ligularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Sapranthus ligularis is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other bellflowers.
