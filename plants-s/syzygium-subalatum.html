Syzygium subalatum

Syzygium subalatum, commonly known as the white apple or white syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and the fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe.

Syzygium subalatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
