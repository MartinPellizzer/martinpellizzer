Setaria longiflora

Setaria longiflora, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, thread-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Setaria longiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate poor soil conditions.
