Scrophularia spicata

Scrophularia spicata, commonly known as greater spikenard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in spiky inflorescences, producing showy, tubular blooms in shades of purple and pink.

Scrophularia spicata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
