Sideritis spinulosa var. hirsuta

Sideritis spinulosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy sickle mint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems gives it its common name and contributes to its distinctive appearance.
