Syrenia macrocarpa

Syrenia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited syrenia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Syrenia macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
