Serratula caspica

Serratula caspica, commonly known as Caspian serrata, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert regions. Its leaves are serrated and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Serratula caspica is adapted to arid environments and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
