Sisymbrium monense

Sisymbrium monense, commonly known as the Mexican winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to parts of North America. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sisymbrium monense produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
