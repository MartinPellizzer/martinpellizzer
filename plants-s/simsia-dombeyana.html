Simsia dombeyana

Simsia dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, giving it a sunflower-like appearance. It typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Simsia dombeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
