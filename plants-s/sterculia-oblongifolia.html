Sterculia oblongifolia

Sterculia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional practices.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some uses in local medicine and crafts.
