Stelis santiagoi

Stelis santiagoi, commonly known as the Santiago's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
