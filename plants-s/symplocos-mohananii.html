Symplocos mohananii

Symplocos mohananii, commonly known as Mohanan's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos mohananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
