Schliebenia secunda

Schliebenia secunda, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits that split open to release seeds. Schliebenia secunda is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
