Sarcanthus parishii

Sarcanthus parishii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Sarcanthus parishii is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
