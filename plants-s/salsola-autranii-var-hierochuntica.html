Salsola autranii var. hierochuntica

Salsola autranii var. hierochuntica, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed and sandy soils.

Salsola autranii var. hierochuntica is well adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.
