Scirpus tuberculosus

Scirpus tuberculosus, commonly known as the tuberculate bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize wetland soils, making it an important species for erosion control.

Scirpus tuberculosus thrives in marshy areas and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
