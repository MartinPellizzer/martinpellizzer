Solanum miniatum var. villosissimum

Solanum miniatum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy dwarf tomato, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosissimum.". The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and resemble tiny tomatoes. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its unique fruit.

Due to its compact size and distinctive appearance, it is a popular choice for container gardening.
