Senecio jacobaea var. subumbellatus

Senecio jacobaea var. subumbellatus, commonly known as the lesser ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that are harmful to livestock.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
