Sapium aubrevillei

Sapium aubrevillei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Sapium aubrevillei is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
