Solanum multiinterruptum f. longipilosum

Solanum multiinterruptum f. longipilosum, commonly known as the long-hairy potato bush, is a unique variant of the Solanum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
