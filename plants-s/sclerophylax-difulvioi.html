Sclerophylax difulvioi

Sclerophylax difulvioi, commonly known as the golden-leaved rock orchid, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
