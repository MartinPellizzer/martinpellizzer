Sideritis peyrei var. vidalii

Sideritis peyrei var. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis species are often valued for their ability to thrive in poor soils and their historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
