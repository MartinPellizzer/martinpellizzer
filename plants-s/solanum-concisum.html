Solanum concisum

Solanum concisum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
