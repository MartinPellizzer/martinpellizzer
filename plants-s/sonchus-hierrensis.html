Sonchus hierrensis

Sonchus hierrensis, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Sonchus hierrensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
