Saxifraga carpetana subsp. carpetana

Saxifraga carpetana subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and visually appealing plant for alpine gardens.
