Silene corsica

Silene corsica, commonly known as the Corsican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Silene corsica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.
