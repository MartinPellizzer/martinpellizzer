Serjania bahiana

Serjania bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
