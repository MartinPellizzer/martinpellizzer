Sorocea ilicifolia var. grandifolia

Sorocea ilicifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved holly, is a variant of the holly genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with spines along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
