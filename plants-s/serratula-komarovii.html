Serratula komarovii

Serratula komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a fibrous root system. It is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.

Serratula komarovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
