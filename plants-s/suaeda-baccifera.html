Suaeda baccifera

Suaeda baccifera, commonly known as sea lavender or desert spurge, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and coastal regions.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and extreme temperatures. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals.

Suaeda baccifera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
