Scaevola macrodonta

Scaevola macrodonta, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in dense, fan-like clusters. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Scaevola macrodonta is a hardy perennial that is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
