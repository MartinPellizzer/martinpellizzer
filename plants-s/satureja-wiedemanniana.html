Satureja wiedemanniana

Satureja wiedemanniana, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their distinctive aromatic properties.
