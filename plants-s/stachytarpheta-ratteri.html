Stachytarpheta ratteri

Stachytarpheta ratteri, commonly known as the Ratter's blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
