Salvia keerlii

Salvia keerlii, commonly known as the Keerli sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in dense, fuzzy foliage. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Salvia keerlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
