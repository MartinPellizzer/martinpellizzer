Smilax schomburgkiana

Smilax schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax schomburgkiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
