Senecio bucharicus

Senecio bucharicus, commonly known as the Bukharan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, hairy leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.

Senecio bucharicus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
