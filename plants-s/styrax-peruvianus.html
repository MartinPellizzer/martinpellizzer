Styrax peruvianus

Styrax peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by hard, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its resin, known as copal, has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
