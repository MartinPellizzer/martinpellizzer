Scrophularia tortuosissima

Scrophularia tortuosissima, commonly known as the zigzag scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Scrophularia tortuosissima is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
