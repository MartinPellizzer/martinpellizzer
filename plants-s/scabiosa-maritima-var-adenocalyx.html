Scabiosa maritima var. adenocalyx

Scabiosa maritima var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by narrow, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt-laden environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are typically linear and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.
