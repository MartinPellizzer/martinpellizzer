Senecio mauritanicus

Senecio mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, cushion-like structure, adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio mauritanicus is often used in xerophytic gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
