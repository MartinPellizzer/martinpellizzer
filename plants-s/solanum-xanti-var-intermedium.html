Solanum xanti var. intermedium

Solanum xanti var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
