Saxifraga hirculus var. tafeliana

Saxifraga hirculus var. tafeliana, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It typically grows in crevices and on slopes, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

This variety is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
