Selaginella magnafornensis

This species is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills of Peru and Bolivia. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which are arranged on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

Selaginella magnafornensis is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and its role in studying the adaptation of plants to high-altitude environments.
