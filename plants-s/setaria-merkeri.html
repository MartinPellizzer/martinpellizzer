Setaria merkeri

Setaria merkeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Setaria merkeri is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
