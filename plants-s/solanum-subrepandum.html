Solanum subrepandum

Solanum subrepandum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with thin, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale violet, followed by yellowish berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence.

Solanum subrepandum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
