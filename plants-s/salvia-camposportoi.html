Salvia camposportoi

Salvia camposportoi, commonly known as the golden salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange to yellow in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
