Swartzia acuminata var. puberula

Swartzia acuminata var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetwood or African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds.

This species is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.
