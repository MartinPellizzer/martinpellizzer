Solanum aculeastrum var. parceaculeatum

Solanum aculeastrum var. parceaculeatum, commonly known as the lowland nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white to purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
