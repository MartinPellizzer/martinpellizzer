Schraderanthus viscosus

Schraderanthus viscosus, commonly known as sticky schraderanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.
