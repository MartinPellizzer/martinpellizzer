Spiranthes steigeri

Spiranthes steigeri, commonly known as the Steiger's spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a fibrous root system.

Spiranthes steigeri is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
