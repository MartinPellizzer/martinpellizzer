Sempervivum × barbulatum var. noricum

Sempervivum × barbulatum var. noricum, commonly known as the Carpathian houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a powdery white coating. The plant produces tall flowering stems with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact clumps over time.
