Sabia nervosa

Sabia nervosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sabia nervosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
