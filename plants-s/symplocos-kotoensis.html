Symplocos kotoensis

Symplocos kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto elderberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Symplocos kotoensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
