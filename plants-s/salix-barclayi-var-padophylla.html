Salix barclayi var. padophylla

Salix barclayi var. padophylla, commonly known as the Padophylla willow, is a rare and specialized shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers, typical of the Salicaceae family, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

This variant is particularly adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a key component of desert ecosystems.
