Sicyos barbatus

Sicyos barbatus, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens.
