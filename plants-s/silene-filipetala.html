Silene filipetala

Silene filipetala, commonly known as the Filipetala catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Silene filipetala thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms.
