Sorbus pseudolatifolia

Sorbus pseudolatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
