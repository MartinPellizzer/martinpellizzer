Sagenia subtriphylla

Sagenia subtriphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often divided into three parts, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Sagenia subtriphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
