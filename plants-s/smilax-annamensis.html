Smilax annamensis

Smilax annamensis, commonly known as the Annam arrowroot or Annam smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax annamensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
