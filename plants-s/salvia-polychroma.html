Salvia polychroma

Salvia polychroma, commonly known as the rainbow sage, is a striking perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multi-colored flowers that range from purple to blue, pink, and red, creating a visually stunning display. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its long bloom period, from late spring to early autumn, ensures it remains a focal point in the landscape for several months.
