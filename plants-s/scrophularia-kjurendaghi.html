Scrophularia kjurendaghi

Scrophularia kjurendaghi, commonly known as the Kjurendagh scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes plants known for their diverse morphological adaptations. The plant typically features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
