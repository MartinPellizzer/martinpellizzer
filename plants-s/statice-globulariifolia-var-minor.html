Statice globulariifolia var. minor

Statice globulariifolia var. minor, commonly known as dwarf sea thrift, is a compact, low-growing plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powder, known as bloom. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and wind.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping and as a hardy ornamental plant.
