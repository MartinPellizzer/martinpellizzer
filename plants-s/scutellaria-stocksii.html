Scutellaria stocksii

Scutellaria stocksii, commonly known as Stocks' skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

Scutellaria stocksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
