Scleranthus annuus subsp. alpestris

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in alpine ecosystems.
