Spergula salina var. halophila

Spergula salina var. halophila, commonly known as saltgrass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes where soil salinity is high. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that reduce water loss and help it survive in arid or salty conditions. Its root system is capable of absorbing and tolerating high concentrations of salt.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
