Saxifraga × stormonthii

Saxifraga × stormonthii, commonly known as the Stormonth's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Native to the British Isles, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and its flowers are borne on slender, branching stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable microclimates.
