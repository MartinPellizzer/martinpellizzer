Stipa parodii

Stipa parodii, commonly known as the Parodi's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically pale green to golden in color.

Stipa parodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
