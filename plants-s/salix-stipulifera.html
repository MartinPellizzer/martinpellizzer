Salix stipulifera

Salix stipulifera, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
