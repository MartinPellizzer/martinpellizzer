Sondera tubistylis

Sondera tubistylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in sedges.

Sondera tubistylis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
