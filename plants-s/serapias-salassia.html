Serapias salassia

Serapias salassia, commonly known as the Salassian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces numerous small, clustered flowers during the spring and early summer.

Serapias salassia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and botanical collections.
