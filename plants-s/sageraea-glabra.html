Sageraea glabra

Sageraea glabra, commonly known as the smooth sageretia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Sageraea glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
