Sacoglottis mattogrossensis var. subintegra

Sacoglottis mattogrossensis var. subintegra, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a short, stout rhizome. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizome is rich in starch and has been traditionally used for its nutritional value.

The species is adapted to thrive in wet, tropical environments with high humidity and nutrient-rich soils.
