Sigmatostalix cuculligera

Sigmatostalix cuculligera, commonly known as the hooded tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
