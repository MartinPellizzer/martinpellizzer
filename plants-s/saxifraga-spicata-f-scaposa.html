Saxifraga spicata f. scaposa

Saxifraga spicata f. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems that bear clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

This frost-tolerant species is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
