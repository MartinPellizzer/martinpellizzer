Samyda rubra

Samyda rubra, commonly known as the red samyda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Samyda rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
