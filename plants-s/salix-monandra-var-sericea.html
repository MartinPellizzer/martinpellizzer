Salix monandra var. sericea

Salix monandra var. sericea, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and long, silky, grayish-white catkins that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
