Setaria italica subsp. macrochaeta

Setaria italica subsp.

macrochaeta, commonly known as giant millet, is a grass species native to Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both forage and seed production.
