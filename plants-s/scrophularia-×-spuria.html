Scrophularia × spuria

Scrophularia × spuria, commonly known as the hybrid common figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have serrated edges. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Scrophularia × spuria is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
