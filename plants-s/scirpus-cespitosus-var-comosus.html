Scirpus cespitosus var. comosus

Scirpus cespitosus var. comosus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, triangular stems that form dense tussocks, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus cespitosus var. comosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
