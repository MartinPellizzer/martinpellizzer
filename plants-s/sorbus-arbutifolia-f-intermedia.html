Sorbus arbutifolia f. intermedia

Sorbus arbutifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rowan, is a hybrid form of the rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring broad, leathery leaves that are intermediate in shape between those of its parent species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which give way to orange-red berries in the fall. It is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
