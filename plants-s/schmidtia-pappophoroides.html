Schmidtia pappophoroides

Schmidtia pappophoroides, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and the subantarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in harsh, cold climates with limited nutrients. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in silky, hair-like structures called pappus, aiding in its survival in remote areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Schmidtia pappophoroides is an important component of the vegetation in its native habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for microorganisms.
