Smilax renifolia

Smilax renifolia, commonly known as the creeping smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax renifolia is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent and sometimes invasive species in certain habitats.
