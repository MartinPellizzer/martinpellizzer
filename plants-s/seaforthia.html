Seaforthia

Seaforthia, commonly known as the seaforthia orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Seaforthia species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
