Senecio bellidioides var. glabratus

Senecio bellidioides var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
