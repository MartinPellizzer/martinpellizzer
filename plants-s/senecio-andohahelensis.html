Senecio andohahelensis

Senecio andohahelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
