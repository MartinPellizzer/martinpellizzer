Synstemon linearifolius

Synstemon linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved synstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are usually borne on short pedicels and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
