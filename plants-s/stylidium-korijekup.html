Stylidium korijekup

Stylidium korijekup, commonly known as the "Cape York jewel orchid," is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange, often resembling jewels. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its unique structure includes a short, erect stem with a single, showy flower that opens in the early morning.

Stylidium korijekup is also noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, where the flower's structure facilitates the transfer of pollen to specific insect pollinators.
