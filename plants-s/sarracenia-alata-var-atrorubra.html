Sarracenia alata var. atrorubra

Sarracenia alata var. atrorubra, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-like structures used to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green with red or orange markings, especially at the base, which helps attract prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarracenia alata var. atrorubra is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
