Sisymbrium × anceps

Sisymbrium × anceps, commonly known as the field pennycress, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions.
