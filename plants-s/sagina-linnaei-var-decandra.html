Sagina linnaei var. decandra

Sagina linnaei var. decandra, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with its compact growth form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland deserts.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in harsh environments.
