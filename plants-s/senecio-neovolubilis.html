Senecio neovolubilis

Senecio neovolubilis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Senecio neovolubilis is often used in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
