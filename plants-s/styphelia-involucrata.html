Styphelia involucrata

Styphelia involucrata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils found in forested areas. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature in shaded environments.
