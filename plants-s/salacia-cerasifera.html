Salacia cerasifera

Salacia cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry apple tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and large, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are red when ripe. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content and mild flavor.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
