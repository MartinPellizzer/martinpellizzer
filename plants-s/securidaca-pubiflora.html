Securidaca pubiflora

Securidaca pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or savanna habitats.

The hairy senna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
