Solanum aripoense

Solanum aripoense, commonly known as the Aripo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Guyana.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are generally ovate or elliptic in shape, with a rough texture.

Solanum aripoense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
