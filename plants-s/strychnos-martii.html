Strychnos martii

Strychnos martii, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. Strychnos martii is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
