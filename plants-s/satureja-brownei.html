Satureja brownei

Satureja brownei, commonly known as Brown's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Satureja brownei is valued for its fragrance and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
