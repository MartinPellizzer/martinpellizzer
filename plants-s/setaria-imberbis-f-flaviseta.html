Setaria imberbis f. flaviseta

Setaria imberbis f. flaviseta, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to warm climates.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with a golden hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or disturbed areas.

The golden panicgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
