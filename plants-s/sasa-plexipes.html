Sasa plexipes

Sasa plexipes, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Sasa plexipes is often used in traditional landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
