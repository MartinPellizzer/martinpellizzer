Setaria poiretiana

Setaria poiretiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a flowering plant species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, bristle-tipped florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
