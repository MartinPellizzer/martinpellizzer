Syzygium hendersonii

Syzygium hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically reddish when mature, and contains a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or on limestone soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
