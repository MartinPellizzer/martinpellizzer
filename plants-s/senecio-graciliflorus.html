Senecio graciliflorus

Senecio graciliflorus, commonly known as slender-flowered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
