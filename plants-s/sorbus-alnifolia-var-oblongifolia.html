Sorbus alnifolia var. oblongifolia

Sorbus alnifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically white or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
