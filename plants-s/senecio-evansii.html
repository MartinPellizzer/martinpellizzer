Senecio evansii

Senecio evansii, commonly known as the Evans' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio evansii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
