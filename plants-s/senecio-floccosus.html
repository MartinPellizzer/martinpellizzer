Senecio floccosus

Senecio floccosus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique texture and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
