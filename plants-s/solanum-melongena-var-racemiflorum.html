Solanum melongena var. racemiflorum

Solanum melongena var. racemiflorum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the common eggplant characterized by its unique floral structure.

This plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. It produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in racemes, giving it its varietal name. The fruit is typically smaller and more elongated than standard eggplants, with a deep purple color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and decorative purposes.
