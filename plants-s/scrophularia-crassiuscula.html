Scrophularia crassiuscula

Scrophularia crassiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers, which are usually pale purple or pink, are arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia crassiuscula is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
