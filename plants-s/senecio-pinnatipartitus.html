Senecio pinnatipartitus

Senecio pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the featherleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio pinnatipartitus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
