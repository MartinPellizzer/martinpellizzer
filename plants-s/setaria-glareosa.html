Setaria glareosa

Setaria glareosa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Setaria glareosa is used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
