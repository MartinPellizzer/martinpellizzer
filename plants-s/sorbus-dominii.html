Sorbus dominii

Sorbus dominii, commonly known as the Canary Island whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in mild, temperate climates.

Sorbus dominii is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the unique flora of the Canary Islands.
