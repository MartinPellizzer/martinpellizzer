Stubendorffia pterocarpa

Stubendorffia pterocarpa, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinct aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
