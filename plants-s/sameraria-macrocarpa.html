Sameraria macrocarpa

Sameraria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sameraria, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, inflated fruits. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
