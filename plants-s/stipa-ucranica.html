Stipa ucranica

Stipa ucranica, commonly known as the Ukrainian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Stipa ucranica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along the edges of forests.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
