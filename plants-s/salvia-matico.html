Salvia matico

Salvia matico, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, making it popular in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Salvia matico is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
