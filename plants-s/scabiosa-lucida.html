Scabiosa lucida

Scabiosa lucida, commonly known as the shining scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scabiosa lucida is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
