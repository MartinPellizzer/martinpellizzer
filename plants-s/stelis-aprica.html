Stelis aprica

Stelis aprica, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments.

Stelis aprica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific environmental requirements.
