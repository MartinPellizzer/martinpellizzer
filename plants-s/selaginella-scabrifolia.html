Selaginella scabrifolia

Selaginella scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in tiny, rough scales, giving it its common name. It reproduces via spores and is often found in crevices or on rock surfaces where it can retain moisture.

Selaginella scabrifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
