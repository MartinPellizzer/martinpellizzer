Schedonorus asper subsp. serotinus

Schedonorus asper subsp.

serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom later in the growing season, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
