Symplocos topica

Symplocos topica, commonly known as the roughleaf waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch and have a waxy, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Symplocos topica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
