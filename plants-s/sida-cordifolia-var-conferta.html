Sida cordifolia var. conferta

Sida cordifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the clustered heartleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

The leaves are often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
