Satureja graeca subsp. laxiflora

Satureja graeca subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as Greek oregano or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
