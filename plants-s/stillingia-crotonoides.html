Stillingia crotonoides

Stillingia crotonoides, commonly known as the redroot stillingia, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown roots and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded areas along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
