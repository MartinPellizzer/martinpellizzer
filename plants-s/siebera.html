Siebera

Siebera, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the Rosaceae family.

It consists of several species native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees with simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. They produce small, red or orange berries that are often edible.

Siebera species are valued for their ornamental qualities and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
