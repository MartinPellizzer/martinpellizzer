Sulcorebutia mariana var. laui

Sulcorebutia mariana var. laui, commonly known as the Laui's desert rose, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Central America.

It features a short, thick stem with a woolly coating that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its succulent leaves are adapted for water storage, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
