Salvia dorrii var. argentea

Salvia dorrii var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms.
