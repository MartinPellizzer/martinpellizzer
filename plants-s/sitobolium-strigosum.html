Sitobolium strigosum

Sitobolium strigosum, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sitobolium strigosum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
