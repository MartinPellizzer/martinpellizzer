Staurogyne flexicaulis

Staurogyne flexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pollinated by insects. Its roots help stabilize sediment in aquatic environments, contributing to water clarity.

Staurogyne flexicaulis is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.
