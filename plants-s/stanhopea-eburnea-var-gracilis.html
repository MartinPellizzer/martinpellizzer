Stanhopea eburnea var. gracilis

Stanhopea eburnea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender white orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender pseudobulbs and gracefully drooping inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure and requires well-draining, loose potting mix. Its flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for their elegance and fragrance.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
