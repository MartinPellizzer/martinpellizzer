Salvia scorodonia

Salvia scorodonia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia scorodonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
