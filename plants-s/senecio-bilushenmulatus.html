Senecio bilushenmulatus

Senecio bilushenmulatus, commonly known as the Bluebell Senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South Africa and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue and purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

However, it is important to note that some species within the Senecio genus can be invasive in certain regions.
