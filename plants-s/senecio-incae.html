Senecio incae

Senecio incae, commonly known as the Inca Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio incae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
