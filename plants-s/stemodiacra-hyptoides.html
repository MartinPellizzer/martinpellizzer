Stemodiacra hyptoides

Stemodiacra hyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to parts of Africa and is typically found in forested areas where it thrives in partial shade. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually greenish-white in color. It produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
