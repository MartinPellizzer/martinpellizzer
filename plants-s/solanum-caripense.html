Solanum caripense

Solanum caripense, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals, often in shades of purple and white. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum caripense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
