Salvia oroana

Salvia oroana, commonly known as the golden salvia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia oroana typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
