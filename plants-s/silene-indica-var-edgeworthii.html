Silene indica var. edgeworthii

Silene indica var. edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene indica var. edgeworthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
