Schoenus viscosus

Schoenus viscosus, commonly known as sticky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a sticky, waxy substance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, typical of the sedge family.

Schoenus viscosus thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along waterways.
