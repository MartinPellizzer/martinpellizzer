Salvia schmalhausenii

Salvia schmalhausenii, commonly known as Schmalhausen's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
