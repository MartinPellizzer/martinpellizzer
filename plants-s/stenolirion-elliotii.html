Stenolirion elliotii

Stenolirion elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's ghostflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry, open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Stenolirion elliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
