Salix lutea var. watsonii

Salix lutea var. watsonii, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a variant of the yellow willow native to specific regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, weeping branches and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
