Senecio laciniatus var. maritimus

Senecio laciniatus var. maritimus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a hardy perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-blue, lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to withstand salt spray make it a popular choice for seaside gardens.
