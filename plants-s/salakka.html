Salakka

Salakka, commonly known as the "snake fruit" or "salak," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, spathiflorous inflorescence. The fruit is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or red when mature, with a thick, fibrous rind. Inside, it contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, creamy pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
