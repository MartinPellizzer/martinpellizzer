Stilpnolepis centiflora var. pilifera

Stilpnolepis centiflora var. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy centiflora, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety 'pilifera' is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy covering compared to the typical form.
