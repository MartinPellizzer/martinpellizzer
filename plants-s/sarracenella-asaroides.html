Sarracenella asaroides

Sarracenella asaroides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow, which bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sarracenella asaroides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
