Sabicea brasiliensis

Sabicea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
