Salvia japonica f. crenata

Salvia japonica f. crenata, commonly known as Japanese sage or narrow-leaf sage, is a compact, ornamental plant native to Japan.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering display in gardens.

Salvia japonica f. crenata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
