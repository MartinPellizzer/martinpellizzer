Senecio sacramentanus

Senecio sacramentanus, commonly known as the sacred senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers are yellow and often appear in clusters, while its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
