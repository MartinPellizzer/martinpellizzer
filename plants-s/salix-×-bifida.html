Salix × bifida

Salix × bifida, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has slender, elongated leaves that are typically bright green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
