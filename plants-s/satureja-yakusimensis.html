Satureja yakusimensis

Satureja yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakutian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Satureja yakusimensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
