Sidalcea gigantea

Sidalcea gigantea, commonly known as the giant checkermallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and palmately divided leaves with a distinctive, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Sidalcea gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
