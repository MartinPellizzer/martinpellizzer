Saccharum spontaneum subsp. indicum

Saccharum spontaneum subsp.

indicum, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions. It plays an important role in ecological systems as a source of forage for livestock and as a potential raw material for biofuel production.

Additionally, it is considered a wild relative of cultivated sugarcane, offering genetic diversity for breeding programs.
