Saccolabium squamulosum

Saccolabium squamulosum, commonly known as the woolly saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often glandular foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes, and the pods are usually slender and woody.

Saccolabium squamulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
