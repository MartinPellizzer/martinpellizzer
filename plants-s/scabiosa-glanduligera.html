Scabiosa glanduligera

Scabiosa glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa glanduligera is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
