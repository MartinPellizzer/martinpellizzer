Symplocos ovatilobata

Symplocos ovatilobata, commonly known as the broad-lobed wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

Symplocos ovatilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
