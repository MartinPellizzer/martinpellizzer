Sphacele chamaedryoides

Sphacele chamaedryoides, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps it survive in harsh environments.

Sphacele chamaedryoides is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
