Sapindus papuanus

Sapindus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan soapnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. Its bark and leaves contain saponins, which have cleansing properties and are utilized in natural detergents.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
