Schoenoplectiella saximontana

Schoenoplectiella saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to its wetland habitat, with specialized structures for water retention and nutrient absorption.
