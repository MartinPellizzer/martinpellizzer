Symphoricarpos symphoricarpos

Symphoricarpos symphoricarpos, commonly known as snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces a fragrant, aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Snowberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
