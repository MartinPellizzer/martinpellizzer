Senecio chavanilloensis

Senecio chavanilloensis, commonly known as the Chavanillo's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and botanical collections.
