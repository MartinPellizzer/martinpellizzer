Spilanthes iodiscaea f. leucaena

Spilanthes iodiscaea f. leucaena, commonly known as the white spilanthes, is a variant of the spilanthes plant characterized by its pale or white floral structures.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant is notable for its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a mild, spicy scent. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This botanical form is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
