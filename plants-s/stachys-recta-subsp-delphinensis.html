Stachys recta subsp. delphinensis

Stachys recta subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as Delphin's betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
