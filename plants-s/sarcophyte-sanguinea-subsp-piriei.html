Sarcophyte sanguinea subsp. piriei

Sarcophyte sanguinea subsp.

piriei, commonly known as the red sarcophyte, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful management due to its toxicity.
