Solanum coconilla

Solanum coconilla, commonly known as the purple eggplant or coconilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically purple or red in color. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Solanum coconilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
