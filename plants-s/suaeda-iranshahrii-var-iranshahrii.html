Suaeda iranshahrii var. iranshahrii

Suaeda iranshahrii var. iranshahrii, commonly known as the Iranian sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity. Its leaves are often covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is native to Iran and is of interest for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
