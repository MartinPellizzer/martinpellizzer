Symplocos martinicensis

Symplocos martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy, violet-black berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

Symplocos martinicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
