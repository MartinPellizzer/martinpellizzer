Solanum ankazobe

Solanum ankazobe, commonly known as the Ankazobe nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
