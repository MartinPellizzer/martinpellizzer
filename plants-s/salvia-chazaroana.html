Salvia chazaroana

Salvia chazaroana, commonly known as the Chazaroan sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia chazaroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
