Saccolabium insectiferum

Saccolabium insectiferum, commonly known as the insect-bearing saccolabium, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its unique floral structure, which resembles a cluster of insect-like structures, possibly serving as an adaptation for pollination. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
