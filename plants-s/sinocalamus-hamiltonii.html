Sinocalamus hamiltonii

Sinocalamus hamiltonii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sinocalamus hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
