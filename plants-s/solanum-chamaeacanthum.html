Solanum chamaeacanthum

Solanum chamaeacanthum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
