Senecio comosus var. comosus

Senecio comosus var. comosus, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It prefers full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
