Silene sinensis f. verticillata

Silene sinensis f. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that bear small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks, and is native to regions of East Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
