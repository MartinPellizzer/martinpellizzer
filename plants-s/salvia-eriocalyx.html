Salvia eriocalyx

Salvia eriocalyx, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Salvia eriocalyx is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
