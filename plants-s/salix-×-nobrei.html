Salix × nobrei

Salix × nobrei, commonly known as the noble willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands. Its foliage is typically bright green in summer and turns yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Salix × nobrei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
