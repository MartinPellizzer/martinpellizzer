Scleria pergracilis var. brachystachys

Scleria pergracilis var. brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

It is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its use in traditional medicine.
