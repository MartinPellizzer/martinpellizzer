Solanum inaequale

Solanum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically orange to red in color and contain several seeds.

Solanum inaequale is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
