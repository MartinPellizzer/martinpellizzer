Spiraea laucheana

Spiraea laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its profuse display of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, arching branches covered in fine, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are borne in cylindrical clusters at the ends of the branches, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Spiraea laucheana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
