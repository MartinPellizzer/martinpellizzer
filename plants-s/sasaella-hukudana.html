Sasaella hukudana

Sasaella hukudana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

Sasaella hukudana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
