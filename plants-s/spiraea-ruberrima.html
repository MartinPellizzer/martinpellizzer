Spiraea ruberrima

Spiraea ruberrima, commonly known as the redleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy, clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that persist into the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its foliage turns a striking orange-red in the autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Spiraea ruberrima prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
