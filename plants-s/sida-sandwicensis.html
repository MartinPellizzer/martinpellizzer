Sida sandwicensis

Sida sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian sida, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in coastal and disturbed areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Sida sandwicensis is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii, including high humidity and nutrient-poor soils.
