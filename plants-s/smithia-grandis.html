Smithia grandis

Smithia grandis, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to two meters in height, making it a dominant species in its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Smithia grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and high-altitude landscapes.
