Senecio douglasii var. normalis

Senecio douglasii var. normalis, commonly known as Douglas's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that produces seeds through its composite flowers, contributing to its ecological role in its native range.
