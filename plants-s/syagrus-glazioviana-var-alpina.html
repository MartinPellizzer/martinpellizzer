Syagrus glazioviana var. alpina

Syagrus glazioviana var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpina Pindo palm, is a rare and striking palm species native to the high-altitude regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a warm, humid climate with moderate to high light exposure. Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and researchers.
