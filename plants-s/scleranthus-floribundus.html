Scleranthus floribundus

Scleranthus floribundus, commonly known as the common thimbleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain a single seed.
