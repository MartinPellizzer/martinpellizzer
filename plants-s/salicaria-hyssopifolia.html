Salicaria hyssopifolia

Salicaria hyssopifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers with four petals. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Salicaria hyssopifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
