Senecio pururu

Senecio pururu, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its striking purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio pururu thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
