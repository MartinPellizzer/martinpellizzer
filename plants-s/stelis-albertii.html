Stelis albertii

Stelis albertii, commonly known as the Albert's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, spider-like floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Stelis albertii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
