Spiraeopsis brassii

Spiraeopsis brassii, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy clusters of white to yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Spiraeopsis brassii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
