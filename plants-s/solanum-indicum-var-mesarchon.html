Solanum indicum var. mesarchon

Solanum indicum var. mesarchon, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems, glandular leaves, and alkaloid-rich berries.
