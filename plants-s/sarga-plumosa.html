Sarga plumosa

Sarga plumosa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and feathery, soft foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. Sarga plumosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in aquatic or wetland gardens.
