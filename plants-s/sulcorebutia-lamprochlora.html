Sulcorebutia lamprochlora

Sulcorebutia lamprochlora, commonly known as the silver-leafed rock cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in dense, silver-gray spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
