Serapias orientalis subsp. carica

Serapias orientalis subsp.

carica, commonly known as the Carpathian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, spurred orchid flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
