Silene Morrisonmontana Var. Transalpina

Alpine campion, scientifically known as Silene morrisonmontana var. transalpina, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the European Alps. This delicate species is characterized by its vibrant pink or white flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and alpine meadows where few plants can survive. Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, the Alpine campion is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and human activity. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical gem for future generations.
