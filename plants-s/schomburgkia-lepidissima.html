Schomburgkia lepidissima

Schomburgkia lepidissima, commonly known as the red-tailed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange bracts that resemble a tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Schomburgkia lepidissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
