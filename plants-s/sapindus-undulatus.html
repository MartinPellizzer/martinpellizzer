Sapindus undulatus

Sapindus undulatus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that encase a single seed, and the fruit's outer layer can be used to make a natural soap.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
