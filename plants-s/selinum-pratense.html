Selinum pratense

Selinum pratense, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Selinum pratense is often used in herbal medicine and is a valuable plant for pollinators.
