Saxifraga aizoon rex

Saxifraga aizoon rex, commonly known as the Royal Saxifrage, is a striking alpine plant native to the high elevations of the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
