Syngonanthus wahlbergii var. wahlbergii

Syngonanthus wahlbergii var. wahlbergii, commonly known as Wahlberg's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in sandy or rocky habitats.
