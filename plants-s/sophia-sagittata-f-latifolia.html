Sophia sagittata f. latifolia

Sophia sagittata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
