Scutellaria formosana var. pubescens

Scutellaria formosana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Taiwanese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its hairy leaves and bracts are distinctive features, contributing to its varietal name "pubescens," meaning hairy.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
