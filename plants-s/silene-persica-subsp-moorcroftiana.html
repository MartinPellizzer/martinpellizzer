Silene persica subsp. moorcroftiana

Silene persica subsp.

moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
