Sasa miyazawae

Sasa miyazawae, commonly known as the Miyazawa bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer, followed by the development of three-sided, pointed seed pods.

Sasa miyazawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
