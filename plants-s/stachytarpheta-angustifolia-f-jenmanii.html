Stachytarpheta angustifolia f. jenmanii

Stachytarpheta angustifolia f. jenmanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
