Salvia qimenensis

Salvia qimenensis, commonly known as the Qimen sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

Salvia qimenensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
