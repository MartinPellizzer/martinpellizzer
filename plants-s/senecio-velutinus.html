Senecio velutinus

Senecio velutinus, commonly known as the velvet senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio velutinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
