Syngonium connatum

Syngonium connatum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that vary in color from bright green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in water features.

Syngonium connatum is known for its rapid growth and is popular in aquascaping due to its ability to thrive in submerged conditions.
