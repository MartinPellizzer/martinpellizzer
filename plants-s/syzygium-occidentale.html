Syzygium occidentale

Syzygium occidentale, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or acerola, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, bright red berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritional benefits.
