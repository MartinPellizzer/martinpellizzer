Senna pilifera

Senna pilifera, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna pilifera is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmaceutical contexts.
