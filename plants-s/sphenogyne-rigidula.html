Sphenogyne rigidula

Sphenogyne rigidula, commonly known as the rigid lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil while facilitating nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
