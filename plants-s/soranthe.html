Soranthe

Soranthe, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its aromatic, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Soranthe is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for digestive issues.

Its rhizomes contain compounds that contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic uses.
