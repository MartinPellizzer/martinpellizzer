Solanum pancheri

Solanum pancheri, commonly known as the Pancheri nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
