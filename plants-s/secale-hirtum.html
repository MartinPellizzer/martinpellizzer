Secale hirtum

Secale hirtum, commonly known as hairy barley, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers, typically in shades of green or brown. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
