Senecio pachyphyllus

Senecio pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick, and often blue-green colored leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
