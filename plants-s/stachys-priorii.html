Stachys priorii

Stachys priorii, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features square, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Stachys priorii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
