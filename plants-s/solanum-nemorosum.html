Solanum nemorosum

Solanum nemorosum, commonly known as the wild nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland areas and has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, yellowish berries. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum nemorosum is often considered a weed but can provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
