Scorzonera humilis var. ramosa

Scorzonera humilis var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
