Silene gracilicaulis var. longipedicellata

Silene gracilicaulis var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ascending stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
