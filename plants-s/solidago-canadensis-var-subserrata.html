Solidago canadensis var. subserrata

Solidago canadensis var. subserrata, commonly known as the rough-leaved goldenrod, is a variant of the common goldenrod native to North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its showy flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
