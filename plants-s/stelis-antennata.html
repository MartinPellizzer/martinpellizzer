Stelis antennata

Stelis antennata, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive antennae-like appendages, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and feature a unique coloration that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Stelis antennata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
