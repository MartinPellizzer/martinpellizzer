Senecio napaeifolius

Senecio napaeifolius, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Senecio napaeifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
