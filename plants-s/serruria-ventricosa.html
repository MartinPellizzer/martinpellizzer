Serruria ventricosa

Serruria ventricosa, commonly known as the bubble bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bubbles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Serruria ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
