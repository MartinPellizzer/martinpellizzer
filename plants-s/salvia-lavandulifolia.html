Salvia lavandulifolia

Salvia lavandulifolia, commonly known as French lavender, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage and spikes of purple or blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant leaves. Its leaves are often used in aromatherapy and as a culinary herb due to their mild lavender scent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
