Scirpus laciniatus

Scirpus laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The leaves are long, narrow, and deeply lobed, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along lake shores.

The plant produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.
