Silene caroliniana subsp. typica

Silene caroliniana subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Carolina lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually slender and branched.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
