Serpicula verticillata var. angustifolia

Serpicula verticillata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf serpicula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the stem tips. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland environments.

Its foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a distinct, slightly aromatic scent.
