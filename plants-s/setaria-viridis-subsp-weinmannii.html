Setaria viridis subsp. weinmannii

Setaria viridis subsp.

weinmannii, commonly known as green millet or weinmann's green millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, making it efficient in hot and dry climates.

This subspecies is sometimes used in agricultural or ecological studies due to its adaptability and growth characteristics.
