Silene tachtensis

Silene tachtensis, commonly known as the Tachtan silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene tachtensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
