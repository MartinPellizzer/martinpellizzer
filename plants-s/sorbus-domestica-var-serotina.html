Sorbus domestica var. serotina

Sorbus domestica var. serotina, commonly known as the late-fruiting whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white berries that mature later in the season compared to the typical variety. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
