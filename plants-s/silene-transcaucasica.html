Silene transcaucasica

Silene transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
