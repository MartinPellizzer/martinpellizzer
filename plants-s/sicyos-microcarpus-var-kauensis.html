Sicyos microcarpus var. kauensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. kauensis, commonly known as the Kauaʻi bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to the Hawaiian island of Kauaʻi.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, spherical, bottle-shaped fruits with a smooth, green rind. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This variety is notable for its historical and cultural significance in Hawaiian traditional agriculture.
