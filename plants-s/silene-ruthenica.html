Silene ruthenica

Silene ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Silene ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
