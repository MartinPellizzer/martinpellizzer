Sophora microphylla var. longicarinata

Sophora microphylla var. longicarinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often twining stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of pale blue to violet, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a pod containing flat, winged seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
