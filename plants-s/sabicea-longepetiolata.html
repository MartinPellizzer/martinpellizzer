Sabicea longepetiolata

Sabicea longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated petioles. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its woody structure and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments make it a notable species in understory vegetation.
