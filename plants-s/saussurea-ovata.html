Saussurea ovata

Saussurea ovata, commonly known as the Tibetan costus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. The plant has feathery, grass-like leaves that are adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. It is often found growing in rocky or mountainous regions at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Saussurea ovata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
