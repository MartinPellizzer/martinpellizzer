Stachys pilosa var. homotricha

Stachys pilosa var. homotricha, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
