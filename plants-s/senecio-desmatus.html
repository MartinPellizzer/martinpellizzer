Senecio desmatus

Senecio desmatus, commonly known as the blueweed or desert blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and sandy plains. It has a compact growth habit with a short, branching stem and small, linear leaves.

Senecio desmatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
