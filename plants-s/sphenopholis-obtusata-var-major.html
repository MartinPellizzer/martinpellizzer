Sphenopholis obtusata var. major

Sphenopholis obtusata var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved sphenophyll, is a member of the family Sphenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

The species is notable for its primitive morphology, resembling ancient vascular plants.
