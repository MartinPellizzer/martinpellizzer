Scabiosa succisa var. arenaria

Scabiosa succisa var. arenaria, commonly known as sand scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Sand scabious is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
