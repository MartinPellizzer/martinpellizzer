Senecillis subspicata

Senecillis subspicata, commonly known as the slender everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and other nitrogen-fixing plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It produces small, rounded seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Senecillis subspicata is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
