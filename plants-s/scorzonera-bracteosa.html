Scorzonera bracteosa

Scorzonera bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant features a taproot system and produces yellow or orange flowers with five petals. Its leaves are usually basal, broad, and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Scorzonera bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
