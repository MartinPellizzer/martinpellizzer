Sedum rubrum var. louisii

Sedum rubrum var. louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana sedum, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically green with a slight reddish tinge, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
