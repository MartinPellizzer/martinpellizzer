Salix poronaica

Salix poronaica, commonly known as the Poronai willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This shrub typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near water sources such as rivers and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and catkin-like, typical of the Salix genus.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
