Senegalia tanganyikensis

Senegalia tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanzania acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Senegalia tanganyikensis is also used traditionally for its wood and as a source of fodder for livestock.
