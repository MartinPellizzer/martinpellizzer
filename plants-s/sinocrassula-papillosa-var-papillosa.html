Sinocrassula papillosa var. papillosa

Sinocrassula papillosa var. papillosa, commonly known as the woolly sinocrassula, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues. It produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in rock gardens and indoor collections.
