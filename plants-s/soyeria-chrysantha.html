Soyeria chrysantha

Soyeria chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-filled capsules after flowering.

Soyeria chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
