Solanum hastatilobum subsp. brachyphyllum

Solanum hastatilobum subsp.

brachyphyllum, commonly known as the short-lobed nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant typically features small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated but is of interest in botanical studies.
