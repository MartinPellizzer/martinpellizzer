Silene shehbazii

Silene shehbazii, commonly known as the Kashmiri campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene shehbazii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
