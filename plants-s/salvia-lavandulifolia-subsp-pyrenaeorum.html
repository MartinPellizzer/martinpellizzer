Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. pyrenaeorum

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

pyrenaeorum, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for xeriscaping.

The essential oils derived from its leaves are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
