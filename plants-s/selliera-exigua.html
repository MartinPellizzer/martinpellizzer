Selliera exigua

Selliera exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selliera, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
