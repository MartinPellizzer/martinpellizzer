Senecio serratulifolius

Senecio serratulifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, displaying a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
