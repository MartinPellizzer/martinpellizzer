Scilla polyantha var. angustifolia

Scilla polyantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, with its foliage emerging from a bulbous base. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
