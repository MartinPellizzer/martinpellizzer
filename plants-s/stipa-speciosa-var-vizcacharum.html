Stipa speciosa var. vizcacharum

Stipa speciosa var. vizcacharum, commonly known as the vizcacharum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, silver-tipped bracts. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it withstand arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
