Sanguisorba officinalis f. angustifolia

Sanguisorba officinalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade make it a popular choice for garden planting.
