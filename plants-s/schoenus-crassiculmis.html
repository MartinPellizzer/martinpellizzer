Schoenus crassiculmis

Schoenus crassiculmis, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedgeland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, forming a dense mat or tussock-like structure. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus crassiculmis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
