Salvia gomezpompae

Salvia gomezpompae, commonly known as the Pompae sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Salvia gomezpompae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
