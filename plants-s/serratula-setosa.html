Serratula setosa

Serratula setosa, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Serratula setosa is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a weed in agricultural settings.
