Selaginella tenerrima

Selaginella tenerrima, commonly known as the "spiky clubmoss," is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions, often found in rocky or shaded environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including drought and poor soil, due to its specialized adaptations.

Selaginella tenerrima is also notable for its spore-producing structures, which contribute to its reproductive strategy.
