Satureja cylindristachys

Satureja cylindristachys, commonly known as narrow-stalked savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Satureja cylindristachys is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
