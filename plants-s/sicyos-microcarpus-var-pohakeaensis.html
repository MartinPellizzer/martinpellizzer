Sicyos microcarpus var. pohakeaensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. pohakeaensis, commonly known as the Pohakea bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to Hawaii.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, smooth, bottle-shaped fruits with a pale green to white color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and historical use by indigenous Hawaiian communities.
