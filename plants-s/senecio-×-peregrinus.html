Senecio × peregrinus

Senecio × peregrinus, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy, succulent stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
