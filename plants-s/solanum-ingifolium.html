Solanum ingifolium

Solanum ingifolium, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a bright red color. These berries contain capsaicin, which gives them their spicy heat.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
