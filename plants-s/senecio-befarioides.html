Senecio befarioides

Senecio befarioides, commonly known as the Cape bloom or Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
