Smilax celastroides

Smilax celastroides, commonly known as the red-stemmed smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its sharp, spiny stems and its ability to climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Smilax celastroides is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
