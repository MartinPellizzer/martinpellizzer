Senegalia ferruginea

Senegalia ferruginea, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by long, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable plant in desert ecosystems.

Senegalia ferruginea also plays an important role in preventing soil erosion and providing forage for livestock.
