Strophanthus alterniflorus

Strophanthus alterniflorus, commonly known as the African milk vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. The plant has woody vines that can climb or trail along the ground, often reaching several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
