Senna nitida

Senna nitida, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant in reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
