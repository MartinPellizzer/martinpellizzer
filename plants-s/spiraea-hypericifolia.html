Spiraea hypericifolia

Spiraea hypericifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
