Simaba salubris

Simaba salubris, commonly known as the sweetgum tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, spiky fruit clusters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in providing habitat for local wildlife.
