Saxifraga × geum subsp. hirsuta

Saxifraga × geum subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features rosettes of hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy and attractive appearance.
