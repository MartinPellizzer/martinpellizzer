Senecio pectinatus var. ochroleucus

Senecio pectinatus var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and fynbos areas. It has compound leaves with a pinnate structure, and its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This variant is distinguished by its pale yellow coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Senecio pectinatus.
