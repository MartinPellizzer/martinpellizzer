Solanum tuberosum var. pearcei

Solanum tuberosum var. pearcei, commonly known as the Pearce's potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller and have a unique skin coloration compared to standard potato varieties. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it flowers with small, yellowish-green blossoms.

This variety is notable for its potential use in breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and adaptability to different growing conditions.
