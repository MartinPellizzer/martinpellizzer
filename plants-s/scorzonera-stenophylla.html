Scorzonera stenophylla

Scorzonera stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Scorzonera stenophylla is valued for its edible roots, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
