Sorbus aucuparia var. americana

Sorbus aucuparia var. americana, commonly known as the American whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

The bark is typically smooth and grayish, with shallow furrows as the plant matures.
