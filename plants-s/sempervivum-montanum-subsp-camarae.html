Sempervivum montanum subsp. camarae

Sempervivum montanum subsp.

camarae, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features rosettes of fleshy, green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for alpine and Mediterranean gardens.
