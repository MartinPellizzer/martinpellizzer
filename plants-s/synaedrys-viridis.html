Synaedrys viridis

Synaedrys viridis, commonly known as the green synaedrys, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Synaedrys viridis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
