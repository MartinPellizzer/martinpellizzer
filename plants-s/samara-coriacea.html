Samara coriacea

Samara coriacea, commonly known as the hard-seeded samara, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dry, and woody fruits called samaras, which are typically winged and adapted for wind dispersal. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Samara coriacea is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
