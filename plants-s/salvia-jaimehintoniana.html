Salvia jaimehintoniana

Salvia jaimehintoniana, commonly known as Hinton's sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
