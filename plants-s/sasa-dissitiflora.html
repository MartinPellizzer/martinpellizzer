Sasa dissitiflora

Sasa dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flowered bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa dissitiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
