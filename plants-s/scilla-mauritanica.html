Scilla mauritanica

Scilla mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.
