Symplocos elephantis

Symplocos elephantis, commonly known as the elephant's tusk or elephant's ear, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, ovate leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Symplocos elephantis is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and traditional medicinal uses.
