Salvia koru

Salvia koru, commonly known as the New Zealand native sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. Salvia koru thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
