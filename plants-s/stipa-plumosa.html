Stipa plumosa

Stipa plumosa, commonly known as the plumose feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery plumes that give it a soft, flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in early summer.

Stipa plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, airy foliage.
