Solanum heteromorphum

Solanum heteromorphum, commonly known as the rainbow plant or heteromorph, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant is known for its ability to produce a wide range of flower colors, often changing with the seasons, which contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive appearance.
