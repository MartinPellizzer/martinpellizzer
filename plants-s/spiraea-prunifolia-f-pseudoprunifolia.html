Spiraea prunifolia f. pseudoprunifolia

Spiraea prunifolia f. pseudoprunifolia, commonly known as the false cherry spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, and the plant has a dense, rounded growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance in garden settings.
