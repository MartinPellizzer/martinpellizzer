Senecio argentinus

Senecio argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
