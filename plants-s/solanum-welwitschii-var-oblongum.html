Solanum welwitschii var. oblongum

Solanum welwitschii var. oblongum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant bears berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
