Swartzia amplifolia

Swartzia amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved swartzia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

Swartzia amplifolia is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
