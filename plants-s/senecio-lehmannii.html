Senecio lehmannii

Senecio lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio lehmannii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
