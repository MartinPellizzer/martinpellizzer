Sclerolaena diacantha

Sclerolaena diacantha, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly pubescent surface.

Sclerolaena diacantha is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus.
