Sidneya tenuifolia var. tenuifolia

Sidneya tenuifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf sidneya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath family, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
