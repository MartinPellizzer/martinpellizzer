Silene gracilis

Silene gracilis, commonly known as slender campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its stems are slender and often hairy. Silene gracilis is characterized by its delicate appearance and is adapted to cold climates.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
