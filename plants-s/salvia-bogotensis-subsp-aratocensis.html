Salvia bogotensis subsp. aratocensis

Salvia bogotensis subsp.

aratocensis, commonly known as the Aratoca sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of Colombia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in rocky or alpine habitats. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
