Scutellaria theobromina

Scutellaria theobromina, commonly known as chocolate scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent, while its root system is fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Scutellaria theobromina is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, where it thrives in partial shade.
