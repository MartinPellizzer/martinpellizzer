Senecio fendleri var. molestus

Senecio fendleri var. molestus, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with hairy, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often found in rocky soils and is considered an important component of desert ecosystems.
