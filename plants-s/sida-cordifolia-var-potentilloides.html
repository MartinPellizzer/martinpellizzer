Sida cordifolia var. potentilloides

Sida cordifolia var. potentilloides, commonly known as the heartleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is often found in swamps or along the edges of ponds. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 feet, with a fibrous root system.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
