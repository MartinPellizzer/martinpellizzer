Silene inflata var. aetnensis

Silene inflata var. aetnensis, commonly known as the Etna bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated seed pods, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to the harsh volcanic environment.
