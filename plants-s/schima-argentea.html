Schima argentea

Schima argentea, commonly known as silver flame or silver schima, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Schima argentea thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
