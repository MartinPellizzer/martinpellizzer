Syzygium aphanomyrtoides

Syzygium aphanomyrtoides, commonly known as the satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.

Syzygium aphanomyrtoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
