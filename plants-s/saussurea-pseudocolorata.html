Saussurea pseudocolorata

Saussurea pseudocolorata, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cold, arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to reddish-orange, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Saussurea pseudocolorata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
