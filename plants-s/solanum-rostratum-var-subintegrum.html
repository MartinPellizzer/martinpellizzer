Solanum rostratum var. subintegrum

Solanum rostratum var. subintegrum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.

The species is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
