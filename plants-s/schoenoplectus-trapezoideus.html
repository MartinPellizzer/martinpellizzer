Schoenoplectus trapezoideus

Schoenoplectus trapezoideus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland habitats.

The plant plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
