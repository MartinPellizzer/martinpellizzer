Syringa amurensis var. japonica

Syringa amurensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters in early summer. The plant has ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its showy blossoms and graceful form.
