Syagrus schizophylla

Syagrus schizophylla, commonly known as the golden palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish flowers that eventually develop into orange or red fruits, which are edible for some bird species. Syagrus schizophylla thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
