Solanum arboreum

Solanum arboreum, commonly known as the tree tomato or purple tomato, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its branching structure and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round to oval fruits that are typically purple or red when mature, with a tart, tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Solanum arboreum is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
