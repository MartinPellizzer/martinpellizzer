Senecio pauperculus var. firmifolius

Senecio pauperculus var. firmifolius, commonly known as the stiff-leaved senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically South Africa, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

This succulent is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
