Smilacina racemosa var. cylindrata

Smilacina racemosa var. cylindrata, commonly known as the Southern false Solomon's seal, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in the understory of forests.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
