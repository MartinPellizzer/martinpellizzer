Setaria nigrirostris var. pallida

Setaria nigrirostris var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, pale green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale brown seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for erosion control in suitable environments.
