Spathiphyllum

Spathiphyllum, commonly known as the peace lily, is a popular ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and its showy, white spathes that enclose a cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements. It is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.

Spathiphyllum belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies.
