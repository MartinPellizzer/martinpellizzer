Salvia lavandulifolia var. aurasiaca

Salvia lavandulifolia var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the aurora sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
