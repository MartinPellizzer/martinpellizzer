Sabia javanica

Sabia javanica, commonly known as the Java sabia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Sabia javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
