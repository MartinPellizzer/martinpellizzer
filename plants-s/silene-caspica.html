Silene caspica

Silene caspica, commonly known as the Caspian campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly serrated edge.

Silene caspica is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
