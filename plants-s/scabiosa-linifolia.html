Scabiosa linifolia

Scabiosa linifolia, commonly known as the slender scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
