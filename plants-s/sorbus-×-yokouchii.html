Sorbus × yokouchii

Sorbus × yokouchii, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in forested areas, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, persistent fruits that provide food for birds.

Sorbus × yokouchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
