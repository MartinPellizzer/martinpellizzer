Scrophularia bosniaca

Scrophularia bosniaca, commonly known as Bosnian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are tubular, clustered in whorls, and range in color from purple to pink, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
