Salvia bazmanica

Salvia bazmanica, commonly known as the Persian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia bazmanica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
