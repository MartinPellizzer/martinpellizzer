Senecio cajonensis

Senecio cajonensis, commonly known as the Cahuenga groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio cajonensis is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
