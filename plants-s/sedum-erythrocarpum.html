Sedum erythrocarpum

Sedum erythrocarpum, commonly known as red-fruited stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or blue-gray in color. The plant produces vibrant red flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum erythrocarpum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom in harsh environmental conditions.
