Stylarthropus stuhlmannii

Stylarthropus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's stylar plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming succulent with fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts.
