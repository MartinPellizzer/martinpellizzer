Saxifraga pseudosancta

Saxifraga pseudosancta, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, often pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture adapted to cold and windy environments.

Saxifraga pseudosancta thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine tundra and mountain meadows.
