Schlegelia parviflora var. trichandra

Schlegelia parviflora var. trichandra, commonly known as the hairy schlegelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
