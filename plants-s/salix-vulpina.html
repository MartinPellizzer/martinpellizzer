Salix vulpina

Salix vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, often swampy areas, and is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and pointed. It has a shallow root system and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to wetland environments.
