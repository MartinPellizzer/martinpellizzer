Sedum daigremontianum

Sedum daigremontianum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments and rock gardens. Its unique appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves, gives it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
