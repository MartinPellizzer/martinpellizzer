Sanvitalia procumbens

Sanvitalia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping zinnia, is a low-growing annual plant native to Mexico.

It features bright, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to reseed itself makes it a hardy and resilient addition to many landscapes.
