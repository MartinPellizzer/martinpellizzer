Silene macrorhiza

Silene macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust root system, which contributes to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability.

Silene macrorhiza is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
