Seidlitzia rosmarinus

Seidlitzia rosmarinus, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which are needle-like and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Rosemary thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy, cooking, and traditional medicine.
