Saussurea reniformis

Saussurea reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and basal leaves that are broadly kidney-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry climates.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
