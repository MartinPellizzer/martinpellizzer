Solanum herthae

Solanum herthae, commonly known as Herth's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright yellow berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solanum herthae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
