Salicornia corticosa

Salicornia corticosa, commonly known as sea bean or marsh glasswort, is a halophyte species that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are reduced to scales, and it is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Salicornia corticosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
