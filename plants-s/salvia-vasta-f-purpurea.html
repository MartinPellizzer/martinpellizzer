Salvia vasta f. purpurea

Salvia vasta f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple flowering sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ability to reseed make it a resilient addition to gardens.
