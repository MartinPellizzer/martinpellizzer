Smilax leucophylla

Smilax leucophylla, commonly known as the whiteleaf arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, with a distinctive white or pale green underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
