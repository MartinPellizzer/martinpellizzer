Sulettaria multiflora

Sulettaria multiflora, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty sea air, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Sulettaria multiflora is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
