Sempervivum marmoreum subsp. blandum

Sempervivum marmoreum subsp.

blandum, commonly known as the Blanched Rosette Plant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and its ability to store water in thick, fleshy leaves.

The leaves are typically pale green to bluish-gray, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
