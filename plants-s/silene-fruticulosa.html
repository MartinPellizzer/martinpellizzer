Silene fruticulosa

Silene fruticulosa, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear. Silene fruticulosa is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
