Sinarundinaria kunishii

Sinarundinaria kunishii, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a species of conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and slender, upright growth habit. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals around the stems. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of coniferous plants.

Sinarundinaria kunishii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various environmental conditions.
