Sapium madagascariense

Sapium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
