Sporobolus senegalensis

Sporobolus senegalensis, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soils in arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its ability to spread vegetatively and produce large quantities of seeds contributes to its invasive potential in some regions.
