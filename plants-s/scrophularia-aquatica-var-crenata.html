Scrophularia aquatica var. crenata

Scrophularia aquatica var. crenata, commonly known as water figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
