Swertia piloglandulosa

Swertia piloglandulosa, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its scientific name.

Swertia piloglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
