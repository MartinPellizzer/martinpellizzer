Scorzonera marschalliana

Scorzonera marschalliana, commonly known as the marsh scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Scorzonera marschalliana is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
