Silene alsinoides

Silene alsinoides, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and northern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species with hairy, succulent stems and small, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene alsinoides is often found in dunes and along shorelines, playing a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
