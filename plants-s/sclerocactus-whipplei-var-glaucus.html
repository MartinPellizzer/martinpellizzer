Sclerocactus whipplei var. glaucus

Sclerocactus whipplei var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue whipplei, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, blue-gray spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique coloration and compact growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
