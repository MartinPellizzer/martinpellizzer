Salix americana f. barrattiana

Salix americana f. barrattiana, commonly known as the Barratt's willow, is a unique form of the American willow.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found along stream banks and in wetland areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it is less commonly cultivated than other willow species.
