Shorea paludosa

Shorea paludosa, commonly known as the swamp ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, adapting to waterlogged soils with its extensive root system. Its leaves are usually alternate and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

Shorea paludosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
