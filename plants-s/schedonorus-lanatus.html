Schedonorus lanatus

Schedonorus lanatus, commonly known as the silky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, soft blades that are covered with fine hairs, giving it a silky texture. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Schedonorus lanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
