Silene mellifera

Silene mellifera, commonly known as the honeyflower catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its scientific name reflects its association with honey, as the flowers emit a sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Silene mellifera is a herbaceous perennial that reaches heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
