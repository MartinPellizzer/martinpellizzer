Salix × kawamurana

Salix × kawamurana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits striking ornamental features.

It is characterized by its pendulous, slender branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Its rapid growth rate and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
