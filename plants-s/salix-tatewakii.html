Salix tatewakii

Salix tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a species of willow native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins during the spring.

Salix tatewakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
