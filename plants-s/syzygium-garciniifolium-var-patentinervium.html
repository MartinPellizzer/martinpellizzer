Syzygium garciniifolium var. patentinervium

Syzygium garciniifolium var. patentinervium, commonly known as the patent-veined sand ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and lanceolate leaves with distinctive, prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain aromatic, reddish seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
