Synodon montanus

Synodon montanus, commonly known as the mountain synodon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Synodon montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
