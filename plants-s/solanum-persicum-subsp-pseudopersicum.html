Solanum persicum subsp. pseudopersicum

Solanum persicum subsp.

pseudopersicum, commonly known as the Persian nightshade, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae). It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a slight pubescence.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for both its foliage and fruit in some regions.
