Spilanthes litoralis

Spilanthes litoralis, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Spilanthes litoralis is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy coastal species.
