Saxifraga mixta var. prostiana

Saxifraga mixta var. prostiana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are characterized by their serrated edges and hairy texture.

Saxifraga mixta var. prostiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
