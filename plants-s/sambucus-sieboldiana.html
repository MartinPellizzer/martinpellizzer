Sambucus sieboldiana

Sambucus sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Sambucus sieboldiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
