Suberanthus stellatus

Suberanthus stellatus, commonly known as the star-flowered suberanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Suberanthus stellatus is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
