Semonvillea portulacoides

Semonvillea portulacoides, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Semonvillea portulacoides is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
