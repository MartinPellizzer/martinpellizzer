Senecio flanaganii

Senecio flanaganii, commonly known as Flanagan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
