Stephania longa

Stephania longa, commonly known as the longleaf stephania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
