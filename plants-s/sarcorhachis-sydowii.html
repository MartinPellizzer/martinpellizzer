Sarcorhachis sydowii

Sarcorhachis sydowii, commonly known as the sydow's ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved floral structure, which resembles a ghostly figure, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of markings.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Sarcorhachis sydowii is considered a delicate and endangered species.
