Stemona gloriosa

Stemona gloriosa, commonly known as the golden stemona, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stemona gloriosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
