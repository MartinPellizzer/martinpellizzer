Salvia × sylvestris var. barcinonensis

Salvia × sylvestris var. barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.
