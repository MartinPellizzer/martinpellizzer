Scindapsus poilanei

Scindapsus poilanei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a species of climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus poilanei is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
