Sinapis cheiranthos subsp. recurvata

Sinapis cheiranthos subsp.

recurvata, commonly known as the curly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a distinct, pungent aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
