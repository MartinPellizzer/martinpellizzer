Sebastiania hispida var. subpatula

Sebastiania hispida var. subpatula, commonly known as the smooth-sepal sebastiania, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by spherical, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
