Silene apetala var. lagascae

The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system, which help it retain water. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene apetala var. lagascae is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and minimal moisture conditions.
