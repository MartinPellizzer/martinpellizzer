Sideritis × angustifolia f. brevispicata

Sideritis × angustifolia f. brevispicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and gravelly areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
