Sesbania muelleri

Sesbania muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's sesbania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces elongated, flat pods containing seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sesbania muelleri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry.
