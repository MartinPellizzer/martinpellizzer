Sisyrinchium scirpoideum subsp. luridum

Sisyrinchium scirpoideum subsp.

luridum, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vivid coloration and compact growth habit.
