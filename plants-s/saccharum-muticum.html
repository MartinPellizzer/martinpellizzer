Saccharum muticum

Saccharum muticum, commonly known as the wild sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features long, narrow leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. Its stems are fibrous and contain a high concentration of sucrose, making it a source of natural sugar.

It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshlands and riverbanks.
