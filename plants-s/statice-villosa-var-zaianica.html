Statice villosa var. zaianica

Statice villosa var. zaianica, commonly known as the Zaianica glassflower, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple, giving it a glass-like appearance. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its visual appeal in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.

This variety is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators.
