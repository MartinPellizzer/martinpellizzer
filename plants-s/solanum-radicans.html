Solanum radicans

Solanum radicans, commonly known as creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial vine that produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to spread rapidly along the ground. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are typically yellow or red when mature.

Solanum radicans is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
