Syzygium parameswaranii

Syzygium parameswaranii, commonly known as the Parameswaranii apple or the Malabar apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a unique, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
