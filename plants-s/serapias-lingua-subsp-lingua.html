Serapias lingua subsp. lingua

Serapias lingua subsp.

lingua, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble a tongue, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive purple or red spur, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
