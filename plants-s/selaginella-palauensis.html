Selaginella palauensis

Selaginella palauensis, commonly known as the Palau spikeweed, is a species of spikeweed native to the island nation of Palau.

It is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas and forest floors.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of the local flora.
