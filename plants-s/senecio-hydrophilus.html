Senecio hydrophilus

Senecio hydrophilus, commonly known as the water-loving groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Senecio hydrophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
