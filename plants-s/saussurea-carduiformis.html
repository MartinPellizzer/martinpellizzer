Saussurea carduiformis

Saussurea carduiformis, commonly known as the carduiform saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Saussurea carduiformis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
