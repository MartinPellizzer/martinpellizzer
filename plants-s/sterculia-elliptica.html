Sterculia elliptica

Sterculia elliptica, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural and medicinal significance in many regions.
