Silene pringlei

Silene pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's campion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to drought conditions.

Silene pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
