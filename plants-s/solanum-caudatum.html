Solanum caudatum

Solanum caudatum, commonly known as the trailing nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Solanum caudatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.
