Strychnos nova

Strychnos nova, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree with compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and the presence of milky sap.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
