Syzygium philippinense

Syzygium philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Syzygium philippinense thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its fruits are valued for their flavor and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
