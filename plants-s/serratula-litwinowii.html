Serratula litwinowii

Serratula litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a tall, upright stem covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula litwinowii is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
