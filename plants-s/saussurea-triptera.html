Saussurea triptera

Saussurea triptera, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the Chinese saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where its roots are used to treat various ailments. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh mountainous conditions.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
