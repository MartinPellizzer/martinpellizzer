Senecio lugens var. megalocephalus

Senecio lugens var. megalocephalus, commonly known as the giant-headed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has sturdy, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
