Spiraea sibirica

Spiraea sibirica, commonly known as Siberian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and become reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea sibirica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
