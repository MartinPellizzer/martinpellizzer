Synoum

Synoum, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid climates. Its fruit, known as the sweetleaf, contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous husk.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
