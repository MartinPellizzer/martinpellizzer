Salvia bolleana

Salvia bolleana, commonly known as the Bolleana sage, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and aromatic foliage.
