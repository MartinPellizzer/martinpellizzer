Scirpus yagara

Scirpus yagara, commonly known as the bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. Scirpus yagara thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for making thatching materials.
