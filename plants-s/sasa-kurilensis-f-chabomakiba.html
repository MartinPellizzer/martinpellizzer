Sasa kurilensis f. chabomakiba

Sasa kurilensis f. chabomakiba, commonly known as the Chabomakiba sasa, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming compact colonies.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of florets, which are usually inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.
