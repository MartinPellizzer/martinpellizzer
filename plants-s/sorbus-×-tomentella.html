Sorbus × tomentella

Sorbus × tomentella, commonly known as the silverleaf whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically eaten by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
