Scirpus aciformis

Scirpus aciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in wetland environments.

Scirpus aciformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
