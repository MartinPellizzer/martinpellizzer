Stylidium xanthellum

Stylidium xanthellum, commonly known as the yellow sun-orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

This herbaceous perennial features vivid yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its structure includes a short, hairy stem and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Stylidium xanthellum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
