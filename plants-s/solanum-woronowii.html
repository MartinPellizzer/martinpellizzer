Solanum woronowii

Solanum woronowii, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum woronowii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
