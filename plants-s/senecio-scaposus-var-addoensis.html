Senecio scaposus var. addoensis

Senecio scaposus var. addoensis, commonly known as Addo senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
