Synaedrys clathrata

Synaedrys clathrata, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, almost translucent fronds that give it a unique, ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense tropical forests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to habitat loss, Synaedrys clathrata is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
