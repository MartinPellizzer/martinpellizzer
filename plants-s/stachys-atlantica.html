Stachys atlantica

Stachys atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys atlantica is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.
