Sedum tarokoense

Sedum tarokoense, commonly known as the Taroko sedum, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it forms dense mats or small clusters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Sedum tarokoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
