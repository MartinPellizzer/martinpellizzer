Syntherisma argillacea

Syntherisma argillacea, commonly known as the clayroot plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and adapted to extract moisture from deep within the soil.

Syntherisma argillacea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soils.
