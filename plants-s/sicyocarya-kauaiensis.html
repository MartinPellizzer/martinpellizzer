Sicyocarya kauaiensis

Sicyocarya kauaiensis, commonly known as the Kauai water spinach, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Kauai.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots dangle beneath, absorbing nutrients from the water. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine, highlighting its cultural significance.

Sicyocarya kauaiensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through natural filtration.
