Sida paniculata var. normalis

Sida paniculata var. normalis, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Sida paniculata var. normalis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
