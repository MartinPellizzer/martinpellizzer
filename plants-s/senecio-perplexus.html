Senecio perplexus

Senecio perplexus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. It has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Senecio perplexus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
