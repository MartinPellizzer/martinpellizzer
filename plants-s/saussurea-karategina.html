Saussurea karategina

Saussurea karategina, commonly known as the Karategina thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
