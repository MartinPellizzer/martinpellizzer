Sabia campanulata subsp. campanulata

Sabia campanulata subsp.

campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower sabia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
