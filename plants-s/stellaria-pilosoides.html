Stellaria pilosoides

Stellaria pilosoides, commonly known as hairy starwort, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Stellaria pilosoides is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
