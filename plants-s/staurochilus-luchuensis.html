Staurochilus luchuensis

Staurochilus luchuensis, commonly known as the Luchuan starry orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that resemble stars. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Staurochilus luchuensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
