Sambucus dimidiata

Sambucus dimidiata, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that turn dark purple when ripe. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–6 feet and features compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets. Its flowers are white and appear in umbels, followed by the showy berries that are a key feature of the plant.

The berries are toxic when raw but can be used in cooking after proper preparation.
