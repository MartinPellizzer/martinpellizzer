Senefeldera multiflora var. intermedia

Senefeldera multiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate senefeldera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
