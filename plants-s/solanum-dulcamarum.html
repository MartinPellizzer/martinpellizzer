Solanum dulcamarum

Solanum dulcamarum, commonly known as bindweed, is a climbing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often considered a noxious weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to smother other vegetation.

Solanum dulcamarum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine, though its toxic properties limit its modern applications.
