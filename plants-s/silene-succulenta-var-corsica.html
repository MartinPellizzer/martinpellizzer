Silene succulenta var. corsica

Silene succulenta var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a perennial species characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking pink to magenta flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats or cushions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms and often succulent characteristics.
