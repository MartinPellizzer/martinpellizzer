Stachys rigida subsp. lanata

Stachys rigida subsp.

lanata, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
