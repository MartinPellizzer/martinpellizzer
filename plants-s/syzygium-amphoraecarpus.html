Syzygium amphoraecarpus

Syzygium amphoraecarpus, commonly known as the bottle-shaped apple or bottle-fruit, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped fruit that turns red when mature. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
