Senecio integerrimus var. major

Senecio integerrimus var. major, commonly known as the Big Globe Flower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
