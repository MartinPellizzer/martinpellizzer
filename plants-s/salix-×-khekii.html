Salix × khekii

Salix × khekii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that hang gracefully from the main trunk, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and typically have a silvery-green underside, contributing to its visual appeal. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, ponds, or in wetlands.

This species is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and ornamental horticulture.
