Scrophularia sphaerocarpa

Scrophularia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Scrophularia sphaerocarpa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
