Setaria imberbis f. longiseta

Setaria imberbis f. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired foxtail, is a variant of the foxtail millet species.

It is a herbaceous annual grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces long, slender bristles along the stem and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or agricultural fields.

This grass is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
