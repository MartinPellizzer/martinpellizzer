Salvia orgyalis

Salvia orgyalis, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia orgyalis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
