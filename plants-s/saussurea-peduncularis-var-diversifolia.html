Saussurea peduncularis var. diversifolia

Saussurea peduncularis var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan spirea, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cold, arid environments.
