Setaria sphacelata

Setaria sphacelata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
