Schomburgkia galeottiana

Schomburgkia galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a fragrant display that attracts pollinators.

Schomburgkia galeottiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
