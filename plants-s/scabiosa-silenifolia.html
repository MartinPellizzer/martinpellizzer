Scabiosa silenifolia

Scabiosa silenifolia, commonly known as the silvery scabious, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a central cluster of pale yellow to reddish-brown stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

Scabiosa silenifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
