Stevia potosina

Stevia potosina, commonly known as Mexican stevia, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. Stevia potosina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary use.

Its adaptability and low maintenance make it a popular choice for gardeners and herbalists alike.
