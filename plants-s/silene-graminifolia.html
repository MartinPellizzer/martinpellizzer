Silene graminifolia

Silene graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name, while its stems are often hairy.

Silene graminifolia blooms from June to August, producing small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
