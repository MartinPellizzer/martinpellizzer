Stellaria gypsophiloides

Stellaria gypsophiloides, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and arid environments.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss, adapting it to its harsh habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or limestone regions.

Stellaria gypsophiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky areas.
