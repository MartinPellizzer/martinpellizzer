Stoibrax dichotomum

Stoibrax dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually with five petals.

It is adapted to arid environments and exhibits a unique branching pattern that gives it its common name.
