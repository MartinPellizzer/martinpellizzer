Senecio flammeus var. rufus

Senecio flammeus var. rufus, commonly known as the red-flowered senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
