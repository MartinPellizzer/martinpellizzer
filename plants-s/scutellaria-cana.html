Scutellaria cana

Scutellaria cana, commonly known as blue scutellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, often clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
