Scleria toksanensis

Scleria toksanensis, commonly known as the Toksan grassveld sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grassland or shrubland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scleria toksanensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
