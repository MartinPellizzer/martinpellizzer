Sedum villosum subsp. nevadense

Sedum villosum subsp.

nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada sedum, is a succulent plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
