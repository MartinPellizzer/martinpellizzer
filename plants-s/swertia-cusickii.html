Swertia cusickii

Swertia cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

Swertia cusickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local pollinators.
