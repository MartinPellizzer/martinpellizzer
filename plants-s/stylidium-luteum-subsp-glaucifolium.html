Stylidium luteum subsp. glaucifolium

Stylidium luteum subsp.

glaucifolium, commonly known as the yellowhood sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, bluish-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is a perennial herb with a short, erect stem and small, clustered leaves.

The species is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its distinctive floral structure and ecological adaptations.
