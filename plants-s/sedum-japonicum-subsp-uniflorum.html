Sedum japonicum subsp. uniflorum

Sedum japonicum subsp.

uniflorum, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
