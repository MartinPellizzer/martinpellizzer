Sesbania cannabina

Sesbania cannabina, commonly known as wild hemp, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Sesbania cannabina is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
