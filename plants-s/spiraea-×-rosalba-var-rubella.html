Spiraea × rosalba var. rubella

Spiraea × rosalba var. rubella, commonly known as the redleaf weeping spirea, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms, it is a popular choice for landscape design.
