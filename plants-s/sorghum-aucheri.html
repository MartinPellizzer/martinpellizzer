Sorghum aucheri

Sorghum aucheri, commonly known as the silverleaf sorghum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or reddish in color. Sorghum aucheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable plant for arid and semi-arid climates.
