Salix rehderiana var. lasiogyna

Salix rehderiana var. lasiogyna, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a variant of the willow tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide shade in gardens and along waterways.
