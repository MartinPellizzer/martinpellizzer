Sonchus asper var. littoralis

Sonchus asper var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle native to coastal regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is often found in maritime environments. Its growth is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of coastal areas.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
