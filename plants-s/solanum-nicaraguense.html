Solanum nicaraguense

Solanum nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
