Syzygium rowlandii

Syzygium rowlandii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the holiday season. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Syzygium rowlandii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
