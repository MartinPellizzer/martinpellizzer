Sphaeromorphaea australis

Sphaeromorphaea australis, commonly known as the Southern Globe Flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Sphaeromorphaea australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
