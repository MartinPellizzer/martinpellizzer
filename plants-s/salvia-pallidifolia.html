Salvia pallidifolia

Salvia pallidifolia, commonly known as paleleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both gardens and ecological landscapes.
