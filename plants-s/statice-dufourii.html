Statice dufourii

Statice dufourii, commonly known as the Dufour's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its salt tolerance makes it well adapted to maritime environments.

Statice dufourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
