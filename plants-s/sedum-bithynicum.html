Sedum bithynicum

Sedum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, helping it store water efficiently.

Sedum bithynicum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
