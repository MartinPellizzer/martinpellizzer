Scirpus miliaceus

Scirpus miliaceus, commonly known as spike rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus miliaceus is also an important food source for various waterfowl and other wetland wildlife.
