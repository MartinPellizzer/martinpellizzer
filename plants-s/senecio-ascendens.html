Senecio ascendens

Senecio ascendens, commonly known as the ascending groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.

Senecio ascendens is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
