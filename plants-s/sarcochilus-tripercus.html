Sarcochilus tripercus

Sarcochilus tripercus, commonly known as the three-lobed hothouse, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that are often covered in a dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

This species is well-suited for cultivation in warm, humid environments with partial shade.
