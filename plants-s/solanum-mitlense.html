Solanum mitlense

Solanum mitlense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
