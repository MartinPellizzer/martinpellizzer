Sorbus amabilis

Sorbus amabilis, commonly known as the sweet-scented whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Sorbus amabilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
