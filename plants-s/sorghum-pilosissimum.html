Sorghum pilosissimum

Sorghum pilosissimum, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage, erosion control, and ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
