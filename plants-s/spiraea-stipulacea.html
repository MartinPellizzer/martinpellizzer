Spiraea stipulacea

Spiraea stipulacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Spiraea stipulacea is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
