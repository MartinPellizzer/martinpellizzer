Sasa tangoana

Sasa tangoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant forms a thick mat of foliage, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa tangoana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has cultural significance in some Asian countries.
