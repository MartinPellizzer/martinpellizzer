Saussurea fauriei

Saussurea fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fibrous roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Saussurea fauriei is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
