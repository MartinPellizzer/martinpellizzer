Saussurea deltoidea var. polycephala

Saussurea deltoidea var. polycephala, commonly known as the Tibetan blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibet.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and hairy, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in certain regions of Asia.
