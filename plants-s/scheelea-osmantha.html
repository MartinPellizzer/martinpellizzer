Scheelea osmantha

Scheelea osmantha, commonly known as the Osmantha palm, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The Osmantha palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in its native habitats.
