Streptopus roseus f. giganteus

Streptopus roseus f. giganteus, commonly known as the giant starwort, is a striking variant of the starwort genus characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

This plant features elongated, spiraling stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The species is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
