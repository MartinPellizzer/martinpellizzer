Scabiosa fumariifolia

Scabiosa fumariifolia, commonly known as the torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa fumariifolia is valued for its long blooming period and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
