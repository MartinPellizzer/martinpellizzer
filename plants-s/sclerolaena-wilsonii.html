Sclerolaena wilsonii

Sclerolaena wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's brittlestem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
