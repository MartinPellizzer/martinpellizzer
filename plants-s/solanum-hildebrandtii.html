Solanum hildebrandtii

Solanum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
