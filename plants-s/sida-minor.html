Sida minor

Sida minor, commonly known as lesser sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months.

Sida minor is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
