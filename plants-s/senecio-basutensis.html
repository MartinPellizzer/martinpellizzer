Senecio basutensis

Senecio basutensis, commonly known as the Basutu groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is found in grassland and mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio basutensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
