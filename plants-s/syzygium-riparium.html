Syzygium riparium

Syzygium riparium, commonly known as the river syzygium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist lowland areas, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits.

Syzygium riparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
