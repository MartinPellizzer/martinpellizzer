Satureja menthifolia var. hirta

Satureja menthifolia var. hirta, commonly known as hairy mint sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
