Sphaeropteris dissimilis

Sphaeropteris dissimilis, commonly known as the broad-leafed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it a valuable subject in botanical studies.
