Sparaxis auriculata

Sparaxis auriculata, commonly known as the golden candle or auriculate sparaxis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble candles. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sparaxis auriculata is known for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
