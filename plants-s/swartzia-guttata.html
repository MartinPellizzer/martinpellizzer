Swartzia guttata

Swartzia guttata, commonly known as the streaked ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood with distinctive streaks, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing flat, elongated seeds.

Swartzia guttata is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
