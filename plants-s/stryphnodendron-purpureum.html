Stryphnodendron purpureum

Stryphnodendron purpureum, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in various crafts and construction.
