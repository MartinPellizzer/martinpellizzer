Senecio doronicum subsp. orientalis

Senecio doronicum subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
