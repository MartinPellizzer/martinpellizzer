Solanum ottonis

Solanum ottonis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum ottonis belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.
