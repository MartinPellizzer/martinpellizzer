Silene eremicana

Silene eremicana, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation.

Silene eremicana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
