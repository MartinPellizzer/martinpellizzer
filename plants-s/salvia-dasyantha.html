Salvia dasyantha

Salvia dasyantha, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia dasyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
