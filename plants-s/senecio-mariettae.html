Senecio mariettae

Senecio mariettae, commonly known as Marietta's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
