Senecio longiradiatus

Senecio longiradiatus, commonly known as the long-rayed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for Mediterranean climate gardens.
