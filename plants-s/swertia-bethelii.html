Swertia bethelii

Swertia bethelii, commonly known as Bethel's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia bethelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
