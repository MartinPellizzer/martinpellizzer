Salix × praegravis

Salix × praegravis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly found in wetland areas and along water bodies.
