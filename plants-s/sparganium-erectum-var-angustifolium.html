Sparganium erectum var. angustifolium

Sparganium erectum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bur-reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces yellowish-green to brownish inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered spikelets. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

This species is characterized by its ability to form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of wetland habitats.
