Silene bupleuroides subsp. bupleuroides

Silene bupleuroides subsp.

bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebell catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
