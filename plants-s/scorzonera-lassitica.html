Scorzonera lassitica

Scorzonera lassitica, commonly known as the Lassiter's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root system, which is often harvested for its edible qualities. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Scorzonera lassitica is valued both for its culinary uses and its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
