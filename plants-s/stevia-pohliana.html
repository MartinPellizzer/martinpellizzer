Stevia pohliana

Stevia pohliana, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Stevia pohliana is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
