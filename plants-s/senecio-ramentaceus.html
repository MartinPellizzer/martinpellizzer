Senecio ramentaceus

Senecio ramentaceus, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
