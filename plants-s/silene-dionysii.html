Silene dionysii

Silene dionysii, commonly known as the Dionysus catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, hairy leaves. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered flowers.
