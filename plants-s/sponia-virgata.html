Sponia virgata

Sponia virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's spire, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas or along cliffs.

Sponia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
