Syzygium horsfieldii

Syzygium horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to apples.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
