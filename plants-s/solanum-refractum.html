Solanum refractum

Solanum refractum, commonly known as the brittle nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it is generally avoided for consumption or medicinal use.
