Scirpus wichurae f. concolor

Scirpus wichurae f. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-flowered bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, narrow leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, pale brown flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and filter water, making it ecologically significant in aquatic ecosystems.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to waterlogged soils, with a robust rhizome system supporting growth in fluctuating water conditions.
