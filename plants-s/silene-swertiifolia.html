Silene swertiifolia

Silene swertiifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in a raceme. Silene swertiifolia is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of high-altitude flora.
