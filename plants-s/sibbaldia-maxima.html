Sibbaldia maxima

Sibbaldia maxima, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Sibbaldia maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
