Solanum bodinieri

Solanum bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct, often aromatic scent.

Solanum bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
