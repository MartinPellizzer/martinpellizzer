Solanum vespertilio subsp. doramae

Solanum vespertilio subsp.

doramae, commonly known as the evening bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

It produces small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple, containing the plant's seeds.
