Smilax hispida

Smilax hispida, commonly known as rough smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in stiff, bristly spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries are red or yellow when ripe and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Smilax hispida is often found in moist woodlands and thickets, where it thrives in partial shade.
