Stachys ajugifolia

Stachys ajugifolia, commonly known as blueweed or narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Stachys ajugifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
