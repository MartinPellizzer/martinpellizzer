Silene scaposa var. typica

Silene scaposa var. typica, commonly known as the common mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its small, pink to white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender, flowering scapes.

Silene scaposa var. typica is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
