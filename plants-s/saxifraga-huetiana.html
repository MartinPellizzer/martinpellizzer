Saxifraga huetiana

Saxifraga huetiana, commonly known as the Huet's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette formation that helps conserve moisture.

Saxifraga huetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
