Saxifraga lamottei

Saxifraga lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to protect itself from harsh weather conditions.

Saxifraga lamottei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
