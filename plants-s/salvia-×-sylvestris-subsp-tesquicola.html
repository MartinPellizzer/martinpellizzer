Salvia × sylvestris subsp. tesquicola

Salvia × sylvestris subsp.

tesquicola, commonly known as the blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems.

The subspecies tesquicola is distinguished by its compact growth habit and intense floral coloration.
