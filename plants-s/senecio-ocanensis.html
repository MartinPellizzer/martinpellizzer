Senecio ocanensis

Senecio ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio ocanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
