Sedum lenophylloides

Sedum lenophylloides, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent species, characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of yellow or orange, often in late summer or early autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sedum lenophylloides is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
