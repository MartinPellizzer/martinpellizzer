Sorbaria kirilowii var. arborea

Sorbaria kirilowii var. arborea, commonly known as the tree sorbaria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, red-brown berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
