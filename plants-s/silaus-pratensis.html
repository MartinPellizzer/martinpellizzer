Silaus pratensis

Silaus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow grass or meadow sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. This plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and wet meadows, preferring temperate climates. Silaus pratensis is often found in nutrient-rich soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
