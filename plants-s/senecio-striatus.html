Senecio striatus

Senecio striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct striated pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Senecio striatus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
