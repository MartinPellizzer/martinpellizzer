Salix silesiaca var. lancifolia

Salix silesiaca var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Silesian willow, is a variety of the willow tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
