Solidago nemoralis subsp. decemflora

Solidago nemoralis subsp.

decemflora, commonly known as the early goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flower heads, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are generally smooth and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
