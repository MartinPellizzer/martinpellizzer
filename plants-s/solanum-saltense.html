Solanum saltense

Solanum saltense, commonly known as the saltbush tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to saline soils. Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly salty taste, which is why it is sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The fruit, though small, can be used in culinary applications similar to other tomato species.
