Silene barbata

Silene barbata, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Silene barbata thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and historical significance.
