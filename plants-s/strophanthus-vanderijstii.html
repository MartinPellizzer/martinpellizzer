Strophanthus vanderijstii

Strophanthus vanderijstii, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Strophanthus vanderijstii is also noted for its toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that have historically been used in traditional medicine.
