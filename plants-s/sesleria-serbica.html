Sesleria serbica

Sesleria serbica, commonly known as the Serbian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sesleria serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
