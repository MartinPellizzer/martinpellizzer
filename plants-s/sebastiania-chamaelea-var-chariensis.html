Sebastiania chamaelea var. chariensis

Sebastiania chamaelea var. chariensis, commonly known as the red-seeded sebastiania, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various bird and mammal species.
