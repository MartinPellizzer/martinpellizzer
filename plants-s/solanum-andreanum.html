Solanum andreanum

Solanum andreanum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Solanum andreanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, colorful blooms.
