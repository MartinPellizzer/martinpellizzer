Symphyotrichum × longulum

Symphyotrichum × longulum, commonly known as the eastern blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Symphyotrichum × longulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
