Syzygium rhamphiphyllum

Syzygium rhamphiphyllum, commonly known as the golden apple or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy, spiny fruit that resembles a hairy coconut. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by the showy, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and the edible, sweet-tart fruit it produces.
