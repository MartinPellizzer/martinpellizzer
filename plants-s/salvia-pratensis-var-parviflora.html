Salvia pratensis var. parviflora

Salvia pratensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic rugged appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
