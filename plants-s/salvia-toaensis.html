Salvia toaensis

Salvia toaensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, while the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Salvia toaensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
