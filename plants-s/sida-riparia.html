Sida riparia

Sida riparia, commonly known as the river sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is typically found growing along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in wetland environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used historically for their strength and durability.

Sida riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
