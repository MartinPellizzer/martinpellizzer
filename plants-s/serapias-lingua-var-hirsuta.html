Serapias lingua var. hirsuta

Serapias lingua var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tongue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Serapias, which is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive tongue-like shape. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to its common name.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, typically found in sunny, open habitats.
