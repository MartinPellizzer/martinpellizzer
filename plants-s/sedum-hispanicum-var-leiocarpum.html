Sedum hispanicum var. leiocarpum

Sedum hispanicum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the Spanish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
