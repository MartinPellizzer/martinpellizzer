Symphyotrichum retroflexum

Symphyotrichum retroflexum, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its pale blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
