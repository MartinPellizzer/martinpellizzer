Syringa debelderorum

Syringa debelderorum, commonly known as the Himalayan lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Syringa debelderorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
