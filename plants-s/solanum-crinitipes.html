Solanum crinitipes

Solanum crinitipes, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically orange or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
