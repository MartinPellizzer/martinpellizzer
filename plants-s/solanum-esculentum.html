Solanum esculentum

Solanum esculentum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces fruits that are typically red when ripe, though other colors like yellow, orange, and purple also exist.

The fruit is a fleshy berry containing numerous seeds, and it is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.
