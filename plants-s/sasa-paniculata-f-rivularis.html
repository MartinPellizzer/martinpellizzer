Sasa paniculata f. rivularis

Sasa paniculata f. rivularis, commonly known as the river sasa, is a variant of the sasa bamboo genus native to eastern Asia.

It is a clump-forming plant characterized by its slender, arching culms that often grow in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy green appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. This plant is well adapted to wetland conditions and is often found along stream banks and in marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and horticultural use in humid climates.
