Sonchus ludovicianus

Sonchus ludovicianus, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are edible when young but become bitter and tough as the plant matures.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
