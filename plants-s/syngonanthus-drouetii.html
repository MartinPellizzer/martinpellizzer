Syngonanthus drouetii

Syngonanthus drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the high altitudes of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts by local communities. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Syngonanthus drouetii is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
