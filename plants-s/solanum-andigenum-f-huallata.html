Solanum andigenum f. huallata

Solanum andigenum f. huallata, commonly known as the Huallata potato, is a botanical variety native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a tuberous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its high-altitude adaptation. The plant produces edible tubers that are valued for their unique flavor and nutritional content. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, while the flowers are usually white or purple.

This variety is often cultivated for both subsistence and commercial purposes in local communities.
