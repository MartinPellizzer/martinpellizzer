Salisia pallidiflora

Salisia pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower salix, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Salisia pallidiflora is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky and arid habitats.
