Sorbus latifolia var. remensis

Sorbus latifolia var. remensis, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
