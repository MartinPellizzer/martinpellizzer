Solanum huanucense

Solanum huanucense, commonly known as the Huanuco nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum huanucense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
