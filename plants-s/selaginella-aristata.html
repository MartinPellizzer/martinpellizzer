Selaginella aristata

Selaginella aristata, commonly known as the feather moss or the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, moss-like mats. Selaginella aristata is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its capacity for vegetative propagation through rhizomes.

It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its natural habitat.
