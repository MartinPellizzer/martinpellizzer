Symplocos paniculata var. glabra

Symplocos paniculata var. glabra, commonly known as smooth symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and oval, glossy leaves with a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
