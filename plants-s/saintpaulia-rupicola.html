Saintpaulia rupicola

Saintpaulia rupicola, commonly known as the rock Saintpaulia, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often used in rock gardens and as a potted plant.
