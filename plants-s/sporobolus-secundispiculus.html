Sporobolus secundispiculus

Sporobolus secundispiculus, commonly known as the slender sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss.

Sporobolus secundispiculus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
