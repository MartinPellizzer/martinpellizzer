Siphonanthus strictus

Siphonanthus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf siphonanthus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits that contain seeds.

Siphonanthus strictus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.
