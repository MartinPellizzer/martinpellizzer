Scutellaria hyssopifolia var. hispida

Scutellaria hyssopifolia var. hispida, commonly known as rough scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
