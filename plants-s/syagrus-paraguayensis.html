Syagrus paraguayensis

Syagrus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish bark and produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual, and it produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into small, round fruits. Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and resilience.
