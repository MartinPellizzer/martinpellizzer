Symphytum sylvaticum subsp. sepulcrale

Symphytum sylvaticum subsp.

sepulcrale, commonly known as the woodland comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet-blue flowers.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in partial to full shade. It has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine for its healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its mucilaginous roots.
