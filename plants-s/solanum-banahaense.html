Solanum banahaense

Solanum banahaense, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
