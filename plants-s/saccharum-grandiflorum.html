Saccharum grandiflorum

Saccharum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems and large, feathery panicles of flowers. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a fibrous texture, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in bioenergy production.
