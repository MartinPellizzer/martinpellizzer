Sidalcea malviflora subsp. purpurea

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple flaxflower, is a species native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus.

The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
