Smilax saulensis

Smilax saulensis, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax saulensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
