Sasaella sikokiana

Sasaella sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source.

Sasaella sikokiana thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common component of mountainous and forested regions.
