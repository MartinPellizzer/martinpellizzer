Satureja pallaryi

Satureja pallaryi, commonly known as Pallary's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and thyme. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, slightly minty aroma.
