Seddera welwitschii var. bakeri

Seddera welwitschii var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's seddera, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has adapted to extreme conditions with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.

It is listed as endangered due to habitat destruction and over-collection.
