Sophia heterotricha

Sophia heterotricha, commonly known as the hairy sophia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy texture, which gives it its common name and helps in reducing water loss in arid environments. The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
