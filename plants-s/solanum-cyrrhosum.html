Solanum cyrrhosum

Solanum cyrrhosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its ornamental value.

However, like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
