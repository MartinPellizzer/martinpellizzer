Scirpus scariosus

Scirpus scariosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a fibrous, leaf-like sheath. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which are typically green to brown in color. Its rhizomes are extensive and help in stabilizing the soil in wetland habitats.

Scirpus scariosus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
