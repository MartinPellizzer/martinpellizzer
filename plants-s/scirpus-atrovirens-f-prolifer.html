Scirpus atrovirens f. prolifer

Scirpus atrovirens f. prolifer, commonly known as the purple rush, is a variant of the purple rush plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive purplish hue, particularly in the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming compact clumps that can spread through rhizomes. Its flowering stems are slender and often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
