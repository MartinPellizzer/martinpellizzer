Salix × duchasseintii

Salix × duchasseintii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
