Smilacina bifolia var. canadensis

Smilacina bifolia var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its berries are red and edible. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
