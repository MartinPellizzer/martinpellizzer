Sambucus nigra var. decussata

Sambucus nigra var. decussata, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often arranged in a decussate pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by dark purple berries in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
