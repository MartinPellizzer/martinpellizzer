Senecio powellii

Senecio powellii, commonly known as Powell's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with feathery, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
