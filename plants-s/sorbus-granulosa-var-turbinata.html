Sorbus granulosa var. turbinata

Sorbus granulosa var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often hairy leaves that are grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
