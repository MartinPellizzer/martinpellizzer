Senecio pentaphyllus var. navarri

Senecio pentaphyllus var. navarri, commonly known as Navarre's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
