Silene linnaeana

Silene linnaeana, commonly known as the Linnaean catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene linnaeana is also notable for its historical significance as it was named in honor of Carl Linnaeus, the father of modern taxonomy.
