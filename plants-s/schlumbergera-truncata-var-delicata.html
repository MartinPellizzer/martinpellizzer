Schlumbergera truncata var. delicata

Schlumbergera truncata var. delicata, commonly known as the Thanksgiving cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often ribbed and have a distinctive, claw-like appearance at the ends. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
