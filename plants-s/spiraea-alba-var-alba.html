Spiraea alba var. alba

Spiraea alba var. alba, commonly known as white spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea alba var. alba is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive floral display and hardy nature.
