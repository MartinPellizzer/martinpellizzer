Smilax maculata

Smilax maculata, commonly known as the spotted smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown stippling. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spiny, red berries. Smilax maculata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
