Sarcochilus aphyllus

Sarcochilus aphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flower and narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique, elongated labellum.

Sarcochilus aphyllus is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions for successful cultivation and conservation.
