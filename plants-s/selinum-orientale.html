Selinum orientale

Selinum orientale, commonly known as the common fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive anise-like fragrance.

Selinum orientale is valued both for its culinary uses and its medicinal properties.
