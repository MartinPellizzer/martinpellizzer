Senecio heliophytoides

Senecio heliophytoides, commonly known as the sun-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
