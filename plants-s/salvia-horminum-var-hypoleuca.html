Salvia horminum var. hypoleuca

Salvia horminum var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white wood sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, mint-like aroma when crushed.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
