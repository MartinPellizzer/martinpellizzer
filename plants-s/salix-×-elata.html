Salix × elata

Salix × elata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree that is native to the northern regions of the Palearctic.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
