Scutellaria baicalensis

Scutellaria baicalensis, commonly known as Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant contains several bioactive compounds, including flavonoids such as baicalin and wogonin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its medicinal properties have made it a significant plant in traditional Chinese medicine.
