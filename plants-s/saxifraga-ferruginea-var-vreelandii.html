Saxifraga ferruginea var. vreelandii

Saxifraga ferruginea var. vreelandii, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, slightly hairy leaves that have a distinctive rust-colored edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
