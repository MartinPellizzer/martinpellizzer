Senecio farfugium

Senecio farfugium, commonly known as the "Hawksbeard," is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, hairy, gray-green leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a single, showy head, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
