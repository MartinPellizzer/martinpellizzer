Satureja alpina var. erecta

Satureja alpina var. erecta, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.
