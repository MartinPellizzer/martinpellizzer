Sinningia gesnerifolia

Sinningia gesnerifolia, commonly known as the violet plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, often purple or violet-colored blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia gesnerifolia is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
