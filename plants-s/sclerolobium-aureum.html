Sclerolobium aureum

Sclerolobium aureum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in clusters and bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Sclerolobium aureum is a key species in the Australian flora and is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
