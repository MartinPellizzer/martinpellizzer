Symplocos verticillifolia

Symplocos verticillifolia, commonly known as the whorled snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in whorled clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Symplocos verticillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
