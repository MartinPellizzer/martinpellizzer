Scleria liebmannii

Scleria liebmannii, commonly known as the knobbed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Scleria liebmannii thrives in wetland environments, often growing in marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
