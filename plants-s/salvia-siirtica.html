Salvia siirtica

Salvia siirtica, commonly known as Siirt sage, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia siirtica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
