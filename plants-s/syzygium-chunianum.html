Syzygium chunianum

Syzygium chunianum, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small tamarinds. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous, acidic pulp, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
