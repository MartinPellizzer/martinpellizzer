Semecarpus australiensis var. macrophyllus

Semecarpus australiensis var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal or riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its dramatic foliage and seasonal blooms.
