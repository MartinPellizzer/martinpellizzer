Syringa josikaea var. eximia

Syringa josikaea var. eximia, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and long blooming period.
