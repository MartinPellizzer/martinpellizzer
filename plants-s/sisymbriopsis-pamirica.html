Sisymbriopsis pamirica

Sisymbriopsis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces slender, elongated pods containing seeds.

Sisymbriopsis pamirica is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
