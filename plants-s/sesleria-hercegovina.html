Sesleria hercegovina

Sesleria hercegovina, commonly known as the Hercegovina pink grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, making it a valuable ground cover in shaded gardens.

Sesleria hercegovina is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
