Sebastiania glandulosa var. campestris

Sebastiania glandulosa var. campestris, commonly known as the field sebastiania, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glandular-dotted leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and prairies.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
