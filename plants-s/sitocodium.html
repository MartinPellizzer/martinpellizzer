Sitocodium

Sitocodium, commonly known as the false sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. The plants are typically shrubs or subshrubs with hairy, often spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale in color.

Sitocodium species are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
