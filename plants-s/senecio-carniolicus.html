Senecio carniolicus

Senecio carniolicus, commonly known as the Carniolan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Senecio carniolicus is often found in meadows and slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
