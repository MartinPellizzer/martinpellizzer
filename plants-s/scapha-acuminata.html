Scapha acuminata

Scapha acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Scapha acuminata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
