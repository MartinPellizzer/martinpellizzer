Stephania capitata

Stephania capitata, commonly known as the clustered stephania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
