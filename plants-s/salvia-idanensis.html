Salvia idanensis

Salvia idanensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian sage, is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia idanensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
