Secale cereale var. setosohepaticum

Secale cereale var. setosohepaticum, commonly known as the hairy liver wheat, is a variant of rye characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and stems.

This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It exhibits a compact growth habit with narrow, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential for genetic research.
