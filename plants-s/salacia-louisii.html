Salacia louisii

Salacia louisii, commonly known as the Florida salacia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Salaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
