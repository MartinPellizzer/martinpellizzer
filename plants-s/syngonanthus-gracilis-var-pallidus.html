Syngonanthus gracilis var. pallidus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by red berries that are a source of food for birds. It is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, forested areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
