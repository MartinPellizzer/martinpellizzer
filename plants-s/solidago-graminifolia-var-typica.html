Solidago graminifolia var. typica

Solidago graminifolia var. typica, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Solidago graminifolia var. typica plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
