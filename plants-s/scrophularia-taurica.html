Scrophularia taurica

Scrophularia taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in alpine or subalpine zones. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia taurica is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
