Solanum indicum subsp. adoense

Solanum indicum subsp.

adoense, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
