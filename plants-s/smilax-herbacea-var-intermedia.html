Smilax herbacea var. intermedia

Smilax herbacea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and berries are used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
