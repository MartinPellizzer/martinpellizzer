Sarothamnus carlierus

Sarothamnus carlierus, commonly known as Carlier's thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Sarothamnus carlierus is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
