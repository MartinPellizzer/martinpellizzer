Sesquicella pedata

Sesquicella pedata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, herbaceous structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its unique floral morphology, including spurred petals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
