Schizachyrium tenerum var. muticum

Schizachyrium tenerum var. muticum, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in shaded to partially shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often pinkish to reddish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a useful plant for erosion control.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the grass family, Poaceae.
