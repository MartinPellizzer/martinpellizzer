Solanum cerasiferum var. grandiflorum

Solanum cerasiferum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower cherry tomato, is a variant of the cherry tomato plant characterized by its larger, more robust fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as potatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, producing clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into red, round tomatoes. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and a compact growth habit suitable for container gardening.

This variety is prized for its sweet flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
