Spergularia brevifolia

Spergularia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh conditions, with thick, succulent stems that store water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Spergularia brevifolia is often found in coastal dunes and dry grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
