Sarcoyucca endlichiana

Sarcoyucca endlichiana, commonly known as the Mexican yucca, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces striking, white or pale yellow flowers that emerge from a tall, branched inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid environments.
