Spiranthes aguacatensis

Spiranthes aguacatensis, commonly known as the Mexican ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
