Soliva macrocephala

Soliva macrocephala, commonly known as giant false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that resemble dandelions, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Soliva macrocephala thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
