Sageretia spicata

Sageretia spicata, commonly known as the spicate sageretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in spiculate inflorescences, giving the plant its common name.
