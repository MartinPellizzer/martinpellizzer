Salvia kochiana

Salvia kochiana, commonly known as the Mexican rock sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia kochiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
