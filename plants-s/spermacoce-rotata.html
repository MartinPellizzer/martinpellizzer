Spermacoce rotata

Spermacoce rotata, commonly known as the small cowpea or small ladies' fingers, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and capsule fruits that split open to release its seeds. The plant typically grows in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
