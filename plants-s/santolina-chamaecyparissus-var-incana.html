Santolina chamaecyparissus var. incana

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. incana, commonly known as silver santolina, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal remedies and as a companion plant in gardens.

This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
