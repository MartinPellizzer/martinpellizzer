Silene ceccariana

Silene ceccariana, commonly known as the Ceccaria catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene ceccariana is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
