Sutera argentea

Sutera argentea, commonly known as silver sutera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Sutera argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
