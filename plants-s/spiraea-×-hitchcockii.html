Spiraea × hitchcockii

Spiraea × hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is prized for its showy, cone-shaped clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its foliage is dark green and turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
