Solanum dealbatum

Solanum dealbatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically yellow or red when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.
