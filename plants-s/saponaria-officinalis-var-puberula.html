Saponaria officinalis var. puberula

Saponaria officinalis var. puberula, commonly known as woolly soaproot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall textured look.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
