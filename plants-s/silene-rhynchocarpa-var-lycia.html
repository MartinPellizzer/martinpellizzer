Silene rhynchocarpa var. lycia

Silene rhynchocarpa var. lycia, commonly known as the Lyceum catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
