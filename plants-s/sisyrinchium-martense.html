Sisyrinchium martense

Sisyrinchium martense, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to arid landscapes.

Sisyrinchium martense is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
