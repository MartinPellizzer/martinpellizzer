Stachyurus praecox subsp. matsuzakii

Stachyurus praecox subsp.

matsuzakii, commonly known as the Japanese stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant appearance and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.
