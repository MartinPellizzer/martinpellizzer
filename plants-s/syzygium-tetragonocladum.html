Syzygium tetragonocladum

Syzygium tetragonocladum, commonly known as the four-angled clove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and unique morphology make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
