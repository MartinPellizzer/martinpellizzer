Stachys germanica subsp. tymphaea

Stachys germanica subsp.

tymphaea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland margins and meadows.

The plant features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in whorls, producing a striking blue or violet color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
