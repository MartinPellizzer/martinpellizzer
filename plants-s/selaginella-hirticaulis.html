Selaginella hirticaulis

Selaginella hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy strobilanthes, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its short, erect stems and dense, overlapping leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Selaginella hirticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
