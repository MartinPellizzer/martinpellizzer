Syzygium cerasiforme

Syzygium cerasiforme, commonly known as the cherry apple or red apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Syzygium cerasiforme thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
