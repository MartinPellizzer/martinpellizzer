Stevia soratensis var. typica

Stevia soratensis var. typica, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its leaves are harvested and used in various culinary and medicinal applications due to their intense sweetness and low caloric content.
