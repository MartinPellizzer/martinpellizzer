Silene rosulata subsp. reeseana

Silene rosulata subsp.

reeseana, commonly known as the Reese's silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and striking pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are succulent and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
