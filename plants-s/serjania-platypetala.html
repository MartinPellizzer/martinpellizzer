Serjania platypetala

Serjania platypetala, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
