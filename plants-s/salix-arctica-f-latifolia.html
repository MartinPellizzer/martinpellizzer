Salix arctica f. latifolia

Salix arctica f. latifolia, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and small, catkin-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, with a silvery underside that helps reflect sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for arctic wildlife.
