Senecio wernerioides var. scaposus

Senecio wernerioides var. scaposus, commonly known as the Cape mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, greenish to yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
