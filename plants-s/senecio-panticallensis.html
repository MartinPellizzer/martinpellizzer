Senecio panticallensis

Senecio panticallensis, commonly known as the Panticalla Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Senecio panticallensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
