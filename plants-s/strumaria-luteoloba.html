Strumaria luteoloba

Strumaria luteoloba, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
