Spathelia fruticosa

Spathelia fruticosa, commonly known as the shrubby spathelia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a spathaceous structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Spathelia fruticosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests and coastal areas.
