Solidago virgaurea subsp. pinetorum

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barren goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often in open woodlands or pine barrens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
