Salvia botschantzevii

Salvia botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
