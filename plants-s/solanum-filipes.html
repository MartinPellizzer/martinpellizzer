Solanum filipes

Solanum filipes, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, oval leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
