Sidalcea asprella subsp. asprella

Sidalcea asprella subsp.

asprella, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
