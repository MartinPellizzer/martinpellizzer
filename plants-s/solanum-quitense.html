Solanum quitense

Solanum quitense, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white in color. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum quitense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
