Satureja ciliata

Satureja ciliata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja ciliata is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
