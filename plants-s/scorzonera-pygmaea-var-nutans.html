Scorzonera pygmaea var. nutans

Scorzonera pygmaea var. nutans, commonly known as the dwarf scorzonera, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rosette growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and relatively short, adapted to the harsh, rocky environments it inhabits.

Scorzonera pygmaea var. nutans is often found in alpine meadows and dry slopes, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
