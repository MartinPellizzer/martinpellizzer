Schinus pilifera

Schinus pilifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red berries. The plant can grow as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound.

Schinus pilifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
