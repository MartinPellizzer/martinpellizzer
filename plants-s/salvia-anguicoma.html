Salvia anguicoma

Salvia anguicoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
