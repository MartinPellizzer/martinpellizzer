Sanguisorba rubra

Sanguisorba rubra, commonly known as red dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and sessile. Sanguisorba rubra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
