Salvia simulans

Salvia simulans, commonly known as violet salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Salvia simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
