Sanilum

Sanilum, commonly known as the Sanilum tree, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is thick and fibrous, making it resistant to pests and diseases.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, the Sanilum tree is considered a valuable species in conservation efforts.
