Syzygium nigrans

Syzygium nigrans, commonly known as the black-berried apple or black apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Syzygium nigrans thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it both a horticultural and culinary interest.
