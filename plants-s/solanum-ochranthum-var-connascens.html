Solanum ochranthum var. connascens

Solanum ochranthum var. connascens, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper plant, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and small, round fruits that mature to a deep red color. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and a compact growth habit.
