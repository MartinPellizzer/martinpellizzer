Senecio cervariifolius

Senecio cervariifolius, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often found in xerophytic vegetation types.
