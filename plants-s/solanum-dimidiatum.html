Solanum dimidiatum

Solanum dimidiatum, commonly known as the half-leaf nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or deeply notched. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Solanum dimidiatum is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology.
