Staurogyne alboviolacea

Staurogyne alboviolacea, commonly known as the white violet water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale violet flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its leaves are elongated and often float on the water surface, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Staurogyne alboviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.
