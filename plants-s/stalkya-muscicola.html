Stalkya muscicola

Stalkya muscicola, commonly known as the mossy stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in dense vegetation or on the forest floor. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and decaying organic matter.

Stalkya muscicola plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
