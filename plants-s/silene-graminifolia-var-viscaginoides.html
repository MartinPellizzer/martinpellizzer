Silene graminifolia var. viscaginoides

Silene graminifolia var. viscaginoides, commonly known as the sticky leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
