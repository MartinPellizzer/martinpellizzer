Silene gynodioica subsp. glandulosa

Silene gynodioica subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular silene, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers and glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces both male and female flowers on the same individual, a feature known as andromonoecy.

The subspecies glandulosa is distinguished by its more pronounced glandular structures compared to other subspecies of Silene gynodioica.
