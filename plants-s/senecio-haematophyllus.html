Senecio haematophyllus

Senecio haematophyllus, commonly known as the bloodleaf senecio, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange bracts that resemble bloodstains, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and features a compact, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in dense clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique and colorful appearance.
