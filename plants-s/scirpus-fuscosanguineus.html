Scirpus fuscosanguineus

Scirpus fuscosanguineus, commonly known as the red-stem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reddish-brown stems that often have a purplish tinge, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Scirpus fuscosanguineus is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
