Silene graminifolia lusus tenuis

Silene graminifolia lusus tenuis, commonly known as the slender grass pink, is a species of flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant is typically found in sandy or loamy soils and is often associated with disturbed or early successional environments.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and fragmentation.
