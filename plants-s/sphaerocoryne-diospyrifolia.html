Sphaerocoryne diospyrifolia

Sphaerocoryne diospyrifolia, commonly known as the Indian star gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, fleshy berries that resemble small, inflated capsules. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
