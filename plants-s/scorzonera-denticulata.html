Scorzonera denticulata

Scorzonera denticulata, commonly known as the toothed scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped tubers that are edible and have a nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Scorzonera denticulata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
