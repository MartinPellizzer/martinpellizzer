Scirpus rosthornii

Scirpus rosthornii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late summer.

Scirpus rosthornii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
