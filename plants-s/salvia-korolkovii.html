Salvia korolkovii

Salvia korolkovii, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, fuzzy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers.
