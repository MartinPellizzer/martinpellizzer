Salix × krausei

Salix × krausei, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue on the upper surface and a silvery underside.

Salix × krausei thrives in moist environments, making it a popular choice for riparian areas and water gardens.
