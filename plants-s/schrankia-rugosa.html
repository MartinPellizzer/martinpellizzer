Schrankia rugosa

Schrankia rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf schrankia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Schrankia rugosa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
