Syzygium nigricans

Syzygium nigricans, commonly known as black satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically black when mature, hence its common name. Syzygium nigricans is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and carving.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
