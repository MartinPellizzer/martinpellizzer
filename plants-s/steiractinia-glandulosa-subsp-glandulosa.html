Steiractinia glandulosa subsp. glandulosa

Steiractinia glandulosa subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular steiractinia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its floral structure includes ray florets and disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
