Saxifraga sichotensis

Saxifraga sichotensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and on mountain slopes at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, Saxifraga sichotensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
