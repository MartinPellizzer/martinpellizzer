Satureja cubensis

Satureja cubensis, commonly known as Cuban oregano, is a perennial herb native to Cuba and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, minty aroma.

Satureja cubensis is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional medicine.
