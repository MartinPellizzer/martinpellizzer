Syagrus glaucescens

Syagrus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue palm or blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by orange-red fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Syagrus glaucescens is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
