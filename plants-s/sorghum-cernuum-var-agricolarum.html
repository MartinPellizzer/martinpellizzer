Sorghum cernuum var. agricolarum

Sorghum cernuum var. agricolarum, commonly known as the tallgrass sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, often reaching heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping and has historical significance in agricultural practices.
