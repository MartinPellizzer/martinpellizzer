Saccharum spontaneum subsp. biflorum

Saccharum spontaneum subsp.

biflorum, commonly known as the wild sugarcane with two flowers, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers.

This subspecies is known for its high sugar content in its stalks, making it a potential resource for commercial sugarcane cultivation. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
