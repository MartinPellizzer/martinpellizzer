Stegania

Stegania, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plants typically have showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pink petals. Stegania species are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in dry to semi-arid conditions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
