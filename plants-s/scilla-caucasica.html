Scilla caucasica

Scilla caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically in clusters of 4 to 6. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is closely related to other members of the Amaryllidaceae family.

Scilla caucasica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
