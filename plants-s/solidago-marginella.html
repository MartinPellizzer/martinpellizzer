Solidago marginella

Solidago marginella, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago marginella is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
