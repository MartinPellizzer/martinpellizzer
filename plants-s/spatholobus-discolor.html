Spatholobus discolor

Spatholobus discolor, commonly known as the slender wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
