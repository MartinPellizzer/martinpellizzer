Sambucus barbinervis

Sambucus barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed unripe.
