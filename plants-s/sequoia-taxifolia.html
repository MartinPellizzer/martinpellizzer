Sequoia taxifolia

Sequoia taxifolia, commonly known as the lacebark pine, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, peeling bark that reveals a pattern of white, yellow, and orange underneath. The tree has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a flat, feathery manner, giving it a unique appearance. It produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into seed-bearing structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
