Syzygium lanceolarium

Syzygium lanceolarium, commonly known as the narrow leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisine. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its glossy foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Syzygium lanceolarium also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
