Senecio albodentatus

Senecio albodentatus, commonly known as the white-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and leaves with a distinctive white border. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
