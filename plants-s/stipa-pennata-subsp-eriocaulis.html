Stipa pennata subsp. eriocaulis

Stipa pennata subsp.

eriocaulis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silvery-green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
