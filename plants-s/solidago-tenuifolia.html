Solidago tenuifolia

Solidago tenuifolia, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago tenuifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
