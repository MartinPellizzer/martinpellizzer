Sarinia

Sarinia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Sarinia is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
