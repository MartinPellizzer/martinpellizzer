Sabicea pannosa

Sabicea pannosa, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Sabicea pannosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
