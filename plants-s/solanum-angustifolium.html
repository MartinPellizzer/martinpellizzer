Solanum angustifolium

Solanum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, and it is part of the nightshade family, which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
