Sanguisorba officinalis var. alba

Sanguisorba officinalis var. alba, commonly known as white sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.
