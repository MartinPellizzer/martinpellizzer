Solanum basilobum

Solanum basilobum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to berries containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than its edible parts.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and texture to landscapes.
