Syringa alborosea

Syringa alborosea, commonly known as the white meadow hyacinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Syringa alborosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
