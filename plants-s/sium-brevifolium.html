Sium brevifolium

Sium brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has small, alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in a raceme.

The plant is known for its peppery, aromatic leaves that are sometimes used in culinary applications.
