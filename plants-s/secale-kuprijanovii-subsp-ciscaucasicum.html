Secale kuprijanovii subsp. ciscaucasicum

Secale kuprijanovii subsp.

ciscaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential agricultural uses.
