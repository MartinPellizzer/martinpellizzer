Stevia origanifolia

Stevia origanifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweet herb, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are highly aromatic and intensely sweet. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia origanifolia is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
