Sclerorhachis platyrachis

Sclerorhachis platyrachis, commonly known as the broad-rachis fern, is a member of the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its large, robust fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome that helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous pinnae arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern.

This fern is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.
