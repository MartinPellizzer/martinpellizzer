Scorzonera edulis

Scorzonera edulis, commonly known as the edible scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are rich in nutrients and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

Scorzonera edulis is often cultivated for its culinary value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
