Solanum carense

Solanum carense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum carense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
