Sphacele cordifolia

Sphacele cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf sphacele, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Sphacele cordifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
