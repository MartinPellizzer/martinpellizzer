Selinum canadense

Selinum canadense, commonly known as Canada mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, blooming in white to pale purple hues.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
