Solanum stenotomum var. huicu

Solanum stenotomum var. huicu, commonly known as the Huicu nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
