Stenorrhynchos apetalum

Stenorrhynchos apetalum, commonly known as the naked flowered orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it its scientific name "apetalum," which translates to "without petals.". The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often resembling leaves, which helps it blend into its surroundings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
