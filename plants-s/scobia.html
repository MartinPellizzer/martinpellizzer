Scobia

Scobia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions in the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette. They produce yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, puffball-like seed heads.

Scobia species are often found in open, disturbed areas and are adapted to a variety of soil types.
