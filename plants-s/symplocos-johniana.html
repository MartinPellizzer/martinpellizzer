Symplocos johniana

Symplocos johniana, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
