Stipa coronata var. depauperata

Stipa coronata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is tolerant of cold and arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
