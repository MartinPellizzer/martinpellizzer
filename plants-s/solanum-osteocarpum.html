Solanum osteocarpum

Solanum osteocarpum, commonly known as the bonefruit plant, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum osteocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
