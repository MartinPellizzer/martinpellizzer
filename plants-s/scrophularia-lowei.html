Scrophularia lowei

Scrophularia lowei, commonly known as the Low's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia lowei is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
