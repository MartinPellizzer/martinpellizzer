Sarcomphalus laurinus

Sarcomphalus laurinus, commonly known as the laurel spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has a milky sap that can be toxic, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sarcomphalus laurinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
