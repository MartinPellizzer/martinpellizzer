Syzygium fusiforme

Syzygium fusiforme, commonly known as the narrow-fruit clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic flowers and elliptical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Syzygium fusiforme is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves.
