Scabiosa gumbetica

Scabiosa gumbetica, commonly known as the Gumbetica ladies' mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scabiosa gumbetica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
