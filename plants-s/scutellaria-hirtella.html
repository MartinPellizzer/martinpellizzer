Scutellaria hirtella

Scutellaria hirtella, commonly known as hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, often arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Scutellaria hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
