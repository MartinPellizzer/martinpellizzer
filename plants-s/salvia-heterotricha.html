Salvia heterotricha

Salvia heterotricha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia heterotricha is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
