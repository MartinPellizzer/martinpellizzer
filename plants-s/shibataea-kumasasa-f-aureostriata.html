Shibataea kumasasa f. aureostriata

Shibataea kumasasa f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-stripped Japanese maple, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Japanese maple species.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow veins on its leaves, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique leaf patterns and seasonal color changes make it a prized addition to botanical gardens and private collections.
