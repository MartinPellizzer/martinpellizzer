Sophia carolina

Sophia carolina, commonly known as Carolina jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
