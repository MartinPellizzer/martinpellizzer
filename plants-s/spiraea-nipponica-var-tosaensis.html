Spiraea nipponica var. tosaensis

Spiraea nipponica var. tosaensis, commonly known as Tosa spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and they turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
