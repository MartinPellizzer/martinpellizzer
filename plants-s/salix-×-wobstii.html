Salix × wobstii

Salix × wobstii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins as its primary reproductive structure, typical of willow species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval in shape, with a serrated margin.

It thrives in moist soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
