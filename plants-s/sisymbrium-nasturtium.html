Sisymbrium nasturtium

Sisymbrium nasturtium, commonly known as garden mustards, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its flowers and leaves are edible, with a peppery flavor similar to that of mustard.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.
