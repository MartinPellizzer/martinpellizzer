Sapindus forsythii

Sapindus forsythii, commonly known as Chinese soapnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that encase a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is also known for its natural soap-like properties, making it useful in herbal cleansing products.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
