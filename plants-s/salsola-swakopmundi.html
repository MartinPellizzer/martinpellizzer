Salsola swakopmundi

Salsola swakopmundi, commonly known as the Namib Desert goosefoot, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is well adapted to extreme aridity, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert fauna.

Salsola swakopmundi is a resilient species that exemplifies the adaptive strategies of plants in harsh, arid environments.
