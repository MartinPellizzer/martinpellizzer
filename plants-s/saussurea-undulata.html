Saussurea undulata

Saussurea undulata, commonly known as the Himalayan cat's paw, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a long flowering period, typically from June to September, and thrives in cold, arid environments. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Saussurea undulata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
