Sonchus pinnatus

Sonchus pinnatus, commonly known as common sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.

The species is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions.
