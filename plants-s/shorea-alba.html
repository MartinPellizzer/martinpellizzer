Shorea alba

Shorea alba, commonly known as white ironwood, is a species of tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

Shorea alba plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
