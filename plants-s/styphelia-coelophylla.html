Styphelia coelophylla

Styphelia coelophylla, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.
