Salvia querceticola

Salvia querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that resemble the leaves of oak trees, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically purple or violet and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Salvia querceticola is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.
