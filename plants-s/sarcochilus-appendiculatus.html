Sarcochilus appendiculatus

Sarcochilus appendiculatus, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive appendage on the lip. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in shaded, moist areas of forests or woodlands.
