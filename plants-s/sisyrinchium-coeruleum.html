Sisyrinchium coeruleum

Sisyrinchium coeruleum, commonly known as blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
