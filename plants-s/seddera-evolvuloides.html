Seddera evolvuloides

Seddera evolvuloides, commonly known as the spiral seddera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
