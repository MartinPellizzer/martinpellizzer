Senecio simplicissimus

Senecio simplicissimus, commonly known as the simpleleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
