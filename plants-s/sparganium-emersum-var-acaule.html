Sparganium emersum var. acaule

Sparganium emersum var. acaule, commonly known as the slender bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that rise directly from the base without a distinct stem base, hence the name "acaule.". The plant produces spiky, cylindrical inflorescences that are often mistaken for flowering structures, though they are actually modified stems called spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often submerged or floating at the water's surface.

This variant is adapted to still or slow-moving freshwater environments and plays a role in aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
