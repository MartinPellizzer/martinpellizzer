Swartzia colombiana

Swartzia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features a straight trunk with a rough, fissured bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
