Styrax americanus var. pulverulentus

Styrax americanus var. pulverulentus, commonly known as the powdered styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, dark purple berries. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
