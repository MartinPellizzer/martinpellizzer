Scutellaria darvasica

Scutellaria darvasica, commonly known as the Darvasica skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
