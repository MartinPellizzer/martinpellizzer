Swertia covillei

Swertia covillei, commonly known as Coville's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Mexico and Arizona in the United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in open woodlands or alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are tubular with prominent blue or purple corollas.

Swertia covillei is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
