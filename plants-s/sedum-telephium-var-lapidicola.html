Sedum telephium var. lapidicola

Sedum telephium var. lapidicola, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed, forming a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
