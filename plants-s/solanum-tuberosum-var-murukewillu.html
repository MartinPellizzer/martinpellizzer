Solanum tuberosum var. murukewillu

Solanum tuberosum var. murukewillu, commonly known as the Murukewillu potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes a high starch content and a distinct flavor profile compared to other potato varieties. The plant typically grows in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its tubers are often used in traditional Andean cuisine and are valued for their nutritional properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits genetic traits that make it resilient to certain environmental conditions.
