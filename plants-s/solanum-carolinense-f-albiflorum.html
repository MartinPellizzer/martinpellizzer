Solanum carolinense f. albiflorum

Solanum carolinense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. The plant features showy white flowers that bloom in the summer, distinguishing it from the typical purple-flowered form. Its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, green berry that turns red when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids.
