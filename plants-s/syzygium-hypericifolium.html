Syzygium hypericifolium

Syzygium hypericifolium, commonly known as the flame tree or torchwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium hypericifolium can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy that provides shade and habitat for various wildlife.
