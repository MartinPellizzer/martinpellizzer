Symplocos glomerata

Symplocos glomerata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodlands.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
