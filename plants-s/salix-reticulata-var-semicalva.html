Salix reticulata var. semicalva

Salix reticulata var. semicalva, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, elongated leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hairs. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. Its growth habit makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.
