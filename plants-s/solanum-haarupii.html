Solanum haarupii

Solanum haarupii, commonly known as the Harup's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and potential medicinal significance.

Solanum haarupii is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.
