Saxifraga granulata subsp. glaucescens

Saxifraga granulata subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
