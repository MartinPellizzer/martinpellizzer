Stemona rupestris

Stemona rupestris, commonly known as the rock stemona, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features a slender, woody stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
