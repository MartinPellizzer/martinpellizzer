Saxifraga tricuspidata

Saxifraga tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in crevices or on rock faces.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
