Saxifraga exarata var. integrifolia

Saxifraga exarata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
