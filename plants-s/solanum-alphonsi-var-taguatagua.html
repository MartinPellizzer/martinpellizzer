Solanum alphonsi var. taguatagua

Solanum alphonsi var. taguatagua, commonly known as the Taguatagua nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along forest edges, where it can spread rapidly.
