Solanum macrocarpon var. parcesetosum

Solanum macrocarpon var. parcesetosum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple veins, and it produces small, orange berries that are edible when ripe.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
