Scindapsus havilandii

Scindapsus havilandii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of silver, white, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Scindapsus havilandii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
