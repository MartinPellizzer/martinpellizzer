Sedum sediforme

Sedum sediforme, commonly known as the "buttercup sedum," is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that bear small, star-shaped yellow flowers resembling buttercups. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Sedum sediforme is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
