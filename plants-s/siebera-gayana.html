Siebera gayana

Siebera gayana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Siebera gayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
