Suaeda intermedia

Suaeda intermedia, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and the Middle East. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in drought conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Suaeda intermedia is often used in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock in saline soils.
