Sorbus aucuparia var. hortorum

Sorbus aucuparia var. hortorum, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called "sorbs," which are typically red or orange in color and contain several small seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
