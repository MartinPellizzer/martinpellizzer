Solanum ochroleucum var. flavum

Solanum ochroleucum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
