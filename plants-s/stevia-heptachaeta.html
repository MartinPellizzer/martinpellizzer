Stevia heptachaeta

Stevia heptachaeta, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its sweetening properties, it is often used as a natural alternative to sugar in various food products.
