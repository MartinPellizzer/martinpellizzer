Salsola vermiculata var. glabrescens

Salsola vermiculata var. glabrescens, commonly known as smooth sandmat, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Salsola vermiculata var. glabrescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in regions with limited water availability.
