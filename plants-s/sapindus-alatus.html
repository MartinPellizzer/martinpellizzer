Sapindus alatus

Sapindus alatus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, edible seed. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit's outer layer can be used as a natural soap.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
