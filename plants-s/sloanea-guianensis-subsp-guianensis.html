Sloanea guianensis subsp. guianensis

Sloanea guianensis subsp.

guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
