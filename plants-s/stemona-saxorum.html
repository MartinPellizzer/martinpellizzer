Stemona saxorum

Stemona saxorum, commonly known as the rock stemona, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stemona saxorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
