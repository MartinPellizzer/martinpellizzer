Stachys recta subsp. grandiflora

Stachys recta subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its purported calming and digestive properties.
