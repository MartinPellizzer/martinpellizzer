Salsola baranovii

Salsola baranovii, commonly known as Russian thorn, is a perennial shrub native to the steppes and deserts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by fleshy, bead-like fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Salsola baranovii is also known for its ability to tolerate extreme temperatures and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
