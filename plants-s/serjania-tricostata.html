Serjania tricostata

Serjania tricostata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain hard, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
