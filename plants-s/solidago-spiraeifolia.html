Solidago spiraeifolia

Solidago spiraeifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Solidago spiraeifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
