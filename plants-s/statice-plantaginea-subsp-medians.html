Statice plantaginea subsp. medians

Statice plantaginea subsp.

medians, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spiked flowers and silver-gray foliage.

The plant is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
