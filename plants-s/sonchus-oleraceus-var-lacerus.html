Sonchus oleraceus var. lacerus

Sonchus oleraceus var. lacerus, commonly known as the lacerate sowthistle, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of pale green, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, jagged appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, short stem, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
