Scrophularia mesogitana

Scrophularia mesogitana, commonly known as the Greek figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Scrophularia mesogitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
