Strychnos quadrangularis

Strychnos quadrangularis, commonly known as the African angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has angular, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some cultures.
