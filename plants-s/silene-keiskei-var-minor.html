Silene keiskei var. minor

Silene keiskei var. minor, commonly known as the "pincushion flower," is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rock crevices.

Silene keiskei var. minor is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its unique, cushion-like growth form.
