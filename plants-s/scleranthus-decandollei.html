Scleranthus decandollei

Scleranthus decandollei, commonly known as the Decandolle's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Scleranthus decandollei is known for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid conditions.
