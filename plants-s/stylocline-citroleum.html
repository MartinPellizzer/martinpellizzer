Stylocline citroleum

Stylocline citroleum, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Stylocline citroleum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
