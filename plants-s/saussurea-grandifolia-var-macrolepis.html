Saussurea grandifolia var. macrolepis

Saussurea grandifolia var. macrolepis, commonly known as the large-seeded saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The variety macrolepis is distinguished by its larger, more prominent seeds compared to the typical form of S. grandifolia.
