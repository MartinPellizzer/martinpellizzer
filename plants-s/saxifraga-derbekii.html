Saxifraga derbekii

Saxifraga derbekii, commonly known as the Derbek's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

Saxifraga derbekii is a hardy species that demonstrates adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
