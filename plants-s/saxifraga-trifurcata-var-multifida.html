Saxifraga trifurcata var. multifida

Saxifraga trifurcata var. multifida, commonly known as the forked saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens and high-altitude environments.
