Silene anatolica

Silene anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
