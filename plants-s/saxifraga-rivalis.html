Saxifraga rivalis

Saxifraga rivalis, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Saxifraga rivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
