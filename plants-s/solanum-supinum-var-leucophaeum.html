Solanum supinum var. leucophaeum

Solanum supinum var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with hairy, often prostrate stems. The leaves are typically oval or ovate, with a pale green to white underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The flowers are usually small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences.

This plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
