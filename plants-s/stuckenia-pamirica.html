Stuckenia pamirica

Stuckenia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments such as lakes, rivers, and wet meadows. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are submerged or floating, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. Stuckenia pamirica is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in cold, nutrient-rich waters.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
