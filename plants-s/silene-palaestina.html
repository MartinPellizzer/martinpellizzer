Silene palaestina

Silene palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel, Palestine, and Lebanon.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Silene palaestina is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
