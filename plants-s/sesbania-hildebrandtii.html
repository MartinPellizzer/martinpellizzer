Sesbania hildebrandtii

Sesbania hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. Its pods contain small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Sesbania hildebrandtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical regions.
