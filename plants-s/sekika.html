Sekika

Sekika, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in various colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Sekika is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
