Syzygium polycephalum

Syzygium polycephalum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a woody, elongated pod containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Syzygium polycephalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
