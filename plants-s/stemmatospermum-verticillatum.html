Stemmatospermum verticillatum

Stemmatospermum verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled star sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, a common feature among sedges. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
