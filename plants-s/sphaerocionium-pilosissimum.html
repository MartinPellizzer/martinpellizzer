Sphaerocionium pilosissimum

Sphaerocionium pilosissimum, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name and help reduce water loss in arid environments. It is typically found in coastal dunes and other dry, open habitats.

Sphaerocionium pilosissimum is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils.
