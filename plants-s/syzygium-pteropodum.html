Syzygium pteropodum

Syzygium pteropodum, commonly known as the winged tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged fruit pods that give it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the characteristic woody pods.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
