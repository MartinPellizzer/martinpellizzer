Scottellia mimfiensis

Scottellia mimfiensis, commonly known as the Mimfiensis ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Scottellia mimfiensis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
