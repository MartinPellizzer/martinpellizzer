Smilax nipponica

Smilax nipponica, commonly known as Japanese devil's bit, is a woody vine native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
