Stellaria borealis f. subcorollina

Stellaria borealis f. subcorollina, commonly known as the Arctic starwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically has slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
