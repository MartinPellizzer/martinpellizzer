Sarcopyramis napalensis var. viridiflora

Sarcopyramis napalensis var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered sarcopyramis, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a rosette pattern to minimize water loss.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
