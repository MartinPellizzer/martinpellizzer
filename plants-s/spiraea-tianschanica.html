Spiraea tianschanica

Spiraea tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features multiple stems with dense, rounded clusters of small white or pink flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
