Solanum coronopoideum

Solanum coronopoideum, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, green to red berries. It is often cultivated as a ground cover due to its ability to prevent soil erosion.

Solanum coronopoideum is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
