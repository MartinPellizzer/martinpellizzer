Stylypus vernus

Stylypus vernus, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Stylypus vernus plays an important role in early pollination by attracting bees and other insects.
