Sida hederacea var. parvifolia

Sida hederacea var. parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaf sida, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It features small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The plant is characterized by its soft, succulent stems and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
