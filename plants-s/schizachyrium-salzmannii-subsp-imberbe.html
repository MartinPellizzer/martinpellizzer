Schizachyrium salzmannii subsp. imberbe

Schizachyrium salzmannii subsp.

imberbe, commonly known as northern grassland panicum, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to golden in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
