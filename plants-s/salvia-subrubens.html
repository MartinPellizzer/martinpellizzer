Salvia subrubens

Salvia subrubens, commonly known as the redstem sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia subrubens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by Native American tribes for various healing purposes.
