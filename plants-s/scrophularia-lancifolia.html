Scrophularia lancifolia

Scrophularia lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct veining pattern. Its flowers are tubular, ranging in color from pink to purple, and are arranged in spikes.

Scrophularia lancifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
