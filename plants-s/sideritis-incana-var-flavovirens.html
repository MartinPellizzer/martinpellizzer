Sideritis incana var. flavovirens

Sideritis incana var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green mountain sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and calcareous soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in open, sunny environments with well-drained soil.
