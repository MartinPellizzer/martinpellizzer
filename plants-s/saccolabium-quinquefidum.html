Saccolabium quinquefidum

Saccolabium quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-lobed saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five lobes. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
