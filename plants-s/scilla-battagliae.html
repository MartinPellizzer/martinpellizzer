Scilla battagliae

Scilla battagliae, commonly known as the Battaglia's scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact, bulbous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla battagliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
