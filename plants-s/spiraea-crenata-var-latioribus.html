Spiraea crenata var. latioribus

Spiraea crenata var. latioribus, commonly known as the broad-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its arching branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated edge and a glossy green hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its cascading form and seasonal interest.
