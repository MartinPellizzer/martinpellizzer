Senecio pterocaulis

Senecio pterocaulis, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a striking addition to arid and semi-arid landscapes.
