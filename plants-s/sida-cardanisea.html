Sida cardanisea

Sida cardanisea, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, pale blue or white flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Sida cardanisea is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
