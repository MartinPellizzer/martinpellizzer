Sparganium americanum var. androcladum

Sparganium americanum var. androcladum, commonly known as the American bur reed, is a wetland plant characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that disperse via water, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a rhizomatous root system.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for aquatic and avian wildlife.
