Semidopsis viridis

Semidopsis viridis, commonly known as the green semidopsis, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in spikes, producing a striking display of blue or purple hues.

Semidopsis viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
