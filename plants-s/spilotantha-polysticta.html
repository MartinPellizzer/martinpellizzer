Spilotantha polysticta

Spilotantha polysticta, commonly known as the bluebell of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows from a short, fibrous rootstock and has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Spilotantha polysticta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
