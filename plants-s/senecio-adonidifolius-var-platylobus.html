Senecio adonidifolius var. platylobus

Senecio adonidifolius var. platylobus, commonly known as the broad-leaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
