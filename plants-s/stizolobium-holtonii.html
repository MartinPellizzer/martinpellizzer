Stizolobium holtonii

Stizolobium holtonii, commonly known as the holtonii legume, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Stizolobium holtonii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
