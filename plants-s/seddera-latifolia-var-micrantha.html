Seddera latifolia var. micrantha

Seddera latifolia var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-flowered seddera, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy or open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native environment.
