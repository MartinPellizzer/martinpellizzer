Symplocarpus nipponicus f. variegatus

Symplocarpus nipponicus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated Japanese arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The variegation in its foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
