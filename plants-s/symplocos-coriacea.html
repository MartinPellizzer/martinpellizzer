Symplocos coriacea

Symplocos coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Symplocos coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
