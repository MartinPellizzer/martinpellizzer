Saturnia littoralis

Saturnia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal moon moth, is a species of moth found primarily in coastal regions.

It belongs to the family Saturniidae, which includes large, often strikingly colored moths. The adult Saturnia littoralis has distinctive, broad wings that are usually pale with intricate patterns. Its larvae feed on a variety of plants, including shrubs and trees near the coast.

This species plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat through its life cycle and interactions with local flora.
