Saxifraga planifolia var. citrina

Saxifraga planifolia var. citrina, commonly known as the lemon saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
