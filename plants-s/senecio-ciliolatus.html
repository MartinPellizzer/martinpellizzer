Senecio ciliolatus

Senecio ciliolatus, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving them a silvery appearance.

Senecio ciliolatus produces small blue or purple flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
