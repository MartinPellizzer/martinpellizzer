Salsola glabella

Salsola glabella, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Salsola glabella plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
