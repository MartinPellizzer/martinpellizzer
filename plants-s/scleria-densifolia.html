Scleria densifolia

Scleria densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground rhizome and produces dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Scleria densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
