Sesamum indicum var. subindivisum

Sesamum indicum var. subindivisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sesame, is a variant of the sesame plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its growth habit is upright, with branching occurring from the base, and it typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its oil-rich seeds and is cultivated for both culinary and industrial purposes.
