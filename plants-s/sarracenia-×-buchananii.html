Sarracenia × buchananii

Sarracenia × buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped structures used to trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to thrive. Its flowers are usually red or orange with yellow throats, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Sarracenia × buchananii is valued by horticulturists for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
