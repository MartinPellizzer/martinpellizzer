Scaevola angustifolia

Scaevola angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in a rosette pattern. This species is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
