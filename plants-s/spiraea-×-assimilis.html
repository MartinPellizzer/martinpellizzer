Spiraea × assimilis

Spiraea × assimilis, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, upright form. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to the flowering display.

Spiraea × assimilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
