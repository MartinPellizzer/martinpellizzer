Sorbus aria var. acutifolia

Sorbus aria var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, acuminate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
