Saxifraga gautieri

Saxifraga gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Saxifraga gautieri thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
