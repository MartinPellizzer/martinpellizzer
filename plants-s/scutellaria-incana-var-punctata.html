Scutellaria incana var. punctata

Scutellaria incana var. punctata, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
