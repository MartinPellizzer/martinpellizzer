Statice tschurjukiensis

Statice tschurjukiensis, commonly known as the Tschurjukian sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the rocky coastal areas of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, saline environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
