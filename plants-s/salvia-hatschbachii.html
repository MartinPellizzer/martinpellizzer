Salvia hatschbachii

Salvia hatschbachii, commonly known as the desert sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, fuzzy leaves. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
