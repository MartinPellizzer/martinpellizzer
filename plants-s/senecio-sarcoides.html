Senecio sarcoides

Senecio sarcoides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like appearance with fleshy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Senecio sarcoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
