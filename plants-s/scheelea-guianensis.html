Scheelea guianensis

Scheelea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries such as Guyana, Venezuela, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The Guianan palm produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
