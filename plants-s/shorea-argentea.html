Shorea argentea

Shorea argentea, commonly known as silverwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The tree is characterized by its large, smooth, silver-gray bark and can grow up to 40 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing a single seed.
