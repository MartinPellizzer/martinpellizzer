Sasa kesenensis

Sasa kesenensis, commonly known as the Kesen bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa kesenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in woodland areas.
