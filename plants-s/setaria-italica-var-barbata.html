Setaria italica var. barbata

Setaria italica var. barbata, commonly known as Italian millet, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, hard grains that are often used as a grain crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in soil stabilization.
