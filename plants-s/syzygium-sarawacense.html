Syzygium sarawacense

Syzygium sarawacense, commonly known as the Sarawak apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor, often compared to a mix of apple and pear. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, contributing to its ornamental value.

Syzygium sarawacense is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
