Streptopus amplexifolius

Streptopus amplexifolius, commonly known as the clasping starwort, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers and its ability to form dense, low-growing mats. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that often encircle the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
