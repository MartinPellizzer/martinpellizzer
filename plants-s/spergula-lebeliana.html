Spergula lebeliana

Spergula lebeliana, commonly known as the Lebel's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Spergula lebeliana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
