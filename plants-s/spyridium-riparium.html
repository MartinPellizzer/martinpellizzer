Spyridium riparium

Spyridium riparium, commonly known as the river spyridium, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often found along the edges of rivers and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spikes. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Spyridium riparium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
