Sterculia cinerea

Sterculia cinerea, commonly known as the grey sterculia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark that peels off in strips. The plant produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or for its hardy nature.
