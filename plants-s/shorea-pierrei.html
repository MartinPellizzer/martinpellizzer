Shorea pierrei

Shorea pierrei, commonly known as the Sarawak ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in spikes, and it produces heavy, woody fruits with spiny capsules.

Shorea pierrei plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
