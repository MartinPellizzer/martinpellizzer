Senecio amblyphyllus

Senecio amblyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio amblyphyllus is often found in fynbos vegetation, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
