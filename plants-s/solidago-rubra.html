Solidago rubra

Solidago rubra, commonly known as the redstem goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a reddish tint on the underside.

Solidago rubra plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
