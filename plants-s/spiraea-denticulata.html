Spiraea denticulata

Spiraea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and finely toothed leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Spiraea denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens and natural landscapes.
