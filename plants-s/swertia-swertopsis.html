Swertia swertopsis

Swertia swertopsis, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Swertia swertopsis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective effects.
