Sclerolobium tinctorium var. uleanum

Sclerolobium tinctorium var. uleanum, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that ranges from reddish-brown to gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
