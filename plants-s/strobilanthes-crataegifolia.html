Strobilanthes crataegifolia

Strobilanthes crataegifolia, commonly known as the "Christmas flower," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Strobilanthes crataegifolia is known for its seasonal blooming, usually during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental plant.
