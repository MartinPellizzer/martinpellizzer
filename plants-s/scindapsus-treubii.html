Scindapsus treubii

Scindapsus treubii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus treubii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
