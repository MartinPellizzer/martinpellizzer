Salacia letouzeyana

Salacia letouzeyana, commonly known as the Madagascar starvine, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
