Sasa glaucissima

Sasa glaucissima, commonly known as the silver sasa, is a perennial evergreen bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa glaucissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
