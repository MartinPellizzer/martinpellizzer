Styloma pericularum

Styloma pericularum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Styloma pericularum is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
