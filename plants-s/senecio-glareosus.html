Senecio glareosus

Senecio glareosus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dense, woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio glareosus is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
