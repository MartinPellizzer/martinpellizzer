Schizophragma tomentellum

Schizophragma tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Schizophragma tomentellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
