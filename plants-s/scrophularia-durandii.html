Scrophularia durandii

Scrophularia durandii, commonly known as the Durand's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia durandii is part of the Plantaginaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
