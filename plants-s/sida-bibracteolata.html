Sida bibracteolata

Sida bibracteolata, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Sida bibracteolata is known for its fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
