Salisia flava

Salisia flava, commonly known as the yellow salisphere, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salisia flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
