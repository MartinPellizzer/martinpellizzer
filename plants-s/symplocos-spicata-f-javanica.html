Symplocos spicata f. javanica

Symplocos spicata f. javanica, commonly known as the Javanese symplocos, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne in dense spikes. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
