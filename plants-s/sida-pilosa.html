Sida pilosa

Sida pilosa, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers and hairy, often grayish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sida pilosa is used in traditional medicine and is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
