Sebastiania corniculata var. sclerophylla

Sebastiania corniculata var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
