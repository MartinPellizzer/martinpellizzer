Scirpus kasachstanicus

Scirpus kasachstanicus, commonly known as the Kazakhstan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Scirpus kasachstanicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
