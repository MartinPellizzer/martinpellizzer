Sphaeranthus oppositifolius

Sphaeranthus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaf globeflower, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite in arrangement, giving the plant its scientific name, and are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
