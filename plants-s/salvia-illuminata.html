Salvia illuminata

Salvia illuminata, commonly known as the golden beard or sun salvia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a bright splash of color to gardens. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a shimmering appearance in the sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
