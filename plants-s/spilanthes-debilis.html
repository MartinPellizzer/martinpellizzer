Spilanthes debilis

Spilanthes debilis, commonly known as the weak spilanthes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the genus Spilanthes, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
