Silene nutans f. rubens

Silene nutans f. rubens, commonly known as the red-flowered evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking red petals that bloom in the evening.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals that curve inward, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is noted for its ephemeral blooming period, often lasting only a few days.
