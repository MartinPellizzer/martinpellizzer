Senecio serrurioides

Senecio serrurioides, commonly known as the serrated senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens in arid regions.
