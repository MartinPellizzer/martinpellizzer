Saxifraga pilopetala

Saxifraga pilopetala, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps protect it from harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Saxifraga pilopetala is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold climates and high-altitude environments.
