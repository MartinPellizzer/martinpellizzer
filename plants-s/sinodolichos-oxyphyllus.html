Sinodolichos oxyphyllus

Sinodolichos oxyphyllus, commonly known as the Chinese cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Sinodolichos oxyphyllus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
