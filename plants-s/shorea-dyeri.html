Shorea dyeri

Shorea dyeri, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy upper surface.

Shorea dyeri plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
