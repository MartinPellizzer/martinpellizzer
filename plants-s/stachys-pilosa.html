Stachys pilosa

Stachys pilosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage.

Stachys pilosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
