Sedum takesimense

Sedum takesimense, commonly known as the Take Island stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky coastal areas of Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Sedum takesimense is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh, maritime climates.
