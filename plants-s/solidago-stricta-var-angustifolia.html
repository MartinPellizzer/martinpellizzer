Solidago stricta var. angustifolia

Solidago stricta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are linear and narrow, with a distinctive yellowish-green color and serrated edges.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming dense, spherical clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
