Smilax brevipes

Smilax brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed catbrier, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, recurved spines. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Smilax brevipes is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
