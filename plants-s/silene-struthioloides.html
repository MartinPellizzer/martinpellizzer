Silene struthioloides

Silene struthioloides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant's flowers are tubular and have a unique, ghostly appearance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, often found in desert environments.

Silene struthioloides is pollinated primarily by moths and has a short flowering period during the summer months.
