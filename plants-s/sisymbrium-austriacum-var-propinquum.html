Sisymbrium austriacum var. propinquum

Sisymbrium austriacum var. propinquum, commonly known as the Austrian babylon wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and weed management.
