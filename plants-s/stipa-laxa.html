Stipa laxa

Stipa laxa, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. Stipa laxa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
