Syzygium cruriflorum

Syzygium cruriflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a unique, sweet flavor. Syzygium cruriflorum thrives in wet tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
