Saussurea gebleriana

Saussurea gebleriana, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and traditional herbal practices.
