Scrophularia ehrhartii var. crenata

Scrophularia ehrhartii var. crenata, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly toothed or wavy margin. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, displaying tubular shapes in shades of pink to purple.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
