Syzygium firmum

Syzygium firmum, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Syzygium firmum is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
