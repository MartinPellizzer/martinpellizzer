Stachys hispidula

Stachys hispidula, commonly known as hairy betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
