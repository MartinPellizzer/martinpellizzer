Salvia dasycalyx

Salvia dasycalyx, commonly known as the wooly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia dasycalyx is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
