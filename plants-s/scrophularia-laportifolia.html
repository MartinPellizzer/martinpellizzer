Scrophularia laportifolia

Scrophularia laportifolia, commonly known as purple toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Scrophularia laportifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
