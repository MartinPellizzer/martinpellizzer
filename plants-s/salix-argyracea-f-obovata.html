Salix argyracea f. obovata

Salix argyracea f. obovata, commonly known as the silver willow, is a variant of the silver willow species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are obovate in shape, broad at the tip and tapering towards the base. This plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils, thriving in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
