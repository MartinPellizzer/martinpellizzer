Staurogyne subcordata

Staurogyne subcordata, commonly known as the broadleaf water clover, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are oval to heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it remain buoyant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Staurogyne subcordata is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.

Its dense growth habit also makes it effective for water filtration and oxygenation in pond ecosystems.
