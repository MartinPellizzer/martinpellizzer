Sebastiania glandulosa var. scabra

Sebastiania glandulosa var. scabra, commonly known as rough-seborrhea, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
