Stachys philippiana

Stachys philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Stachys philippiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
