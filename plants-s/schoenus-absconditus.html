Schoenus absconditus

Schoenus absconditus, commonly known as the hidden sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it forms dense, mat-like colonies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
