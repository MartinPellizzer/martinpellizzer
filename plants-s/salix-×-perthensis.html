Salix × perthensis

Salix × perthensis, commonly known as the Perth river willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant typically grows near water sources, such as rivers and lakes, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it is primarily pollinated by wind.

Salix × perthensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
