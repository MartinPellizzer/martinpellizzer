Synostemon gracilis

Synostemon gracilis, commonly known as the slender synostemon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Synostemon gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
