Saponaria thessala

Saponaria thessala, commonly known as the Balkan soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces a milky sap that can be soapy when broken.

Saponaria thessala is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
