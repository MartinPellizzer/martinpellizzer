Scutellaria indica f. leucantha

Scutellaria indica f. leucantha, commonly known as white skullcap, is a variant of the Scutellaria indica species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue to white, with two lips, and are arranged in whorled clusters. This plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
