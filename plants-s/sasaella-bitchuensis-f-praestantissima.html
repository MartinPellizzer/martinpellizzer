Sasaella bitchuensis f. praestantissima

Sasaella bitchuensis f. praestantissima, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a variant of the Sasaella genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing a striking visual contrast against its grayish-brown stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
