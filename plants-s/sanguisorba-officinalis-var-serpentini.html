Sanguisorba officinalis var. serpentini

Sanguisorba officinalis var. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, translucent, red-tinged leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
