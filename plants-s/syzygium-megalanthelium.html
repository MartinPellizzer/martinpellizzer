Syzygium megalanthelium

Syzygium megalanthelium, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its bark is often smooth and pale in color.

Syzygium megalanthelium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
