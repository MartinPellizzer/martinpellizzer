Solanum stenotomum f. huamanpa-uman

Solanum stenotomum f. huamanpa-uman, commonly known as the Huamanpa-Uman tomato, is a rare botanical variant of the tomato species.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, specifically in Peru. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic traits and potential for cultivation in specific climates.
