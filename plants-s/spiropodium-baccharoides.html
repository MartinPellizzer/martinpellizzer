Spiropodium baccharoides

Spiropodium baccharoides, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Spiropodium baccharoides is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for xeriscaping.
