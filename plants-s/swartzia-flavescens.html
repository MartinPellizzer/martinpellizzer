Swartzia flavescens

Swartzia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
