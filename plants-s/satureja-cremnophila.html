Satureja cremnophila

Satureja cremnophila, commonly known as rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces aromatic, mint-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Satureja cremnophila is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a culinary herb in some regions.
