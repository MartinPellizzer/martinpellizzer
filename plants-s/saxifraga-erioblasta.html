Saxifraga erioblasta

Saxifraga erioblasta, commonly known as the red-sandstone saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers in the summer. Saxifraga erioblasta is well adapted to harsh alpine environments due to its low water requirements and frost resistance.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
