Strepsiphus

Strepsiphus, commonly known as the false holly or Christmas tree fern, is a type of fern belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common sight in forest understories.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush, green appearance throughout the year.
