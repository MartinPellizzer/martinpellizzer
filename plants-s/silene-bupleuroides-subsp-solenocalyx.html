Silene bupleuroides subsp. solenocalyx

Silene bupleuroides subsp.

solenocalyx, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
