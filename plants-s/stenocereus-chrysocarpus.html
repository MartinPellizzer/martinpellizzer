Stenocereus chrysocarpus

Stenocereus chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden fruit cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, spiny stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. The plant produces yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans in desert ecosystems.
