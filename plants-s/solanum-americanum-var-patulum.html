Solanum americanum var. patulum

Solanum americanum var. patulum, commonly known as the wild tomato or large-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its fruit contains numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
