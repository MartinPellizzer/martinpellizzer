Salvia univerticillata

Salvia univerticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological gardens.
