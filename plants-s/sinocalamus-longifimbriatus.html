Sinocalamus longifimbriatus

Sinocalamus longifimbriatus, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sinocalamus longifimbriatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
