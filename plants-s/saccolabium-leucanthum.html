Saccolabium leucanthum

Saccolabium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Saccolabium leucanthum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
