Strophanthus ledienii

Strophanthus ledienii, commonly known as the red-stemmed African milkvine, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a distinctive red stem and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy appearance. Its latex contains cardiac glycosides, making it both toxic and historically significant in traditional medicine.

Strophanthus ledienii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
