Sedum cryptomerioides

Sedum cryptomerioides, commonly known as the alpine stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in June and July. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens with cold climates.
