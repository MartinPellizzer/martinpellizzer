Scabiosa japonica f. albiflora

Scabiosa japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower Japanese scabious, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a central yellow disc, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
