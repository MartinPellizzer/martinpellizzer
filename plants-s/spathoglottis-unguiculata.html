Spathoglottis unguiculata

Spathoglottis unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
