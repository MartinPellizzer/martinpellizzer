Silene paradoxa var. angustifolia

Silene paradoxa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
