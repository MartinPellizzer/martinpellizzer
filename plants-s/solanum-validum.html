Solanum validum

Solanum validum, commonly known as the validum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution when handling.
