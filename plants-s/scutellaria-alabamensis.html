Scutellaria alabamensis

Scutellaria alabamensis, commonly known as Alabama blueflag, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Scutellaria alabamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
