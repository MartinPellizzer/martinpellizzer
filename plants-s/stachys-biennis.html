Stachys biennis

Stachys biennis, commonly known as betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

Stachys biennis is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
