Senecio pericaulis

Senecio pericaulis, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and Mediterranean climates.
