Stevia micrantha

Stevia micrantha, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia micrantha is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its sweet-tasting foliage.
