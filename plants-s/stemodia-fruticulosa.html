Stemodia fruticulosa

Stemodia fruticulosa, commonly known as the small-stemmed broom, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features wiry, often branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and opposite, often with a slight waxy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
