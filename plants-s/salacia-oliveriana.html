Salacia oliveriana

Salacia oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest undergrowth.

Salacia oliveriana is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its possible medicinal properties.
