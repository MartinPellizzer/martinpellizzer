Setaria paniculifera

Setaria paniculifera, commonly known as the panicled fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria paniculifera is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil quality.
