Saraca hullettii

Saraca hullettii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Saraca hullettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
