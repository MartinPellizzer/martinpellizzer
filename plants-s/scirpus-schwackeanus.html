Scirpus schwackeanus

Scirpus schwackeanus, commonly known as the swamp bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, ponds, and along the edges of lakes, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with dense clusters of small, brownish flowers. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus schwackeanus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
