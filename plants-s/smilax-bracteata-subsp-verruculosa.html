Smilax bracteata subsp. verruculosa

Smilax bracteata subsp.

verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
