Silene othryana

Silene othryana, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Silene othryana is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Its delicate appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in alpine botany.
