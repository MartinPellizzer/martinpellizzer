Soemmeringia psittacorhyncha

Soemmeringia psittacorhyncha, commonly known as the parrot-billed water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, usually greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
