Serapias × godferyi

Serapias × godferyi, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Serapias acaulis and Serapias orchidifera.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, showcasing its preference for moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, often resembling a bee in shape. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a dense cluster, and it blooms during the late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
