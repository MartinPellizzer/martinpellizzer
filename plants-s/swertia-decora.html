Swertia decora

Swertia decora, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Swertia decora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
