Sasa sugimotoi

Sasa sugimotoi, commonly known as the Sugimoto bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

Sasa sugimotoi is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in its natural habitat.
