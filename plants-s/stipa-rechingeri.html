Stipa rechingeri

Stipa rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in rocky and degraded landscapes.

Stipa rechingeri plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
