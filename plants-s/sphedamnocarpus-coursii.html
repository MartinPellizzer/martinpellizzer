Sphedamnocarpus coursii

Sphedamnocarpus coursii, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sphedamnocarpus coursii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
