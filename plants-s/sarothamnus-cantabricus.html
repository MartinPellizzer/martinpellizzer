Sarothamnus cantabricus

Sarothamnus cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Sarothamnus cantabricus is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
