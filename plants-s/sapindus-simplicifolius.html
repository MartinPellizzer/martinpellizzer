Sapindus simplicifolius

Sapindus simplicifolius, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. The bark is smooth and grayish, and the leaves turn yellow in autumn before shedding.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
