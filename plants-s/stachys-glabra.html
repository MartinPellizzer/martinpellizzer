Stachys glabra

Stachys glabra, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
