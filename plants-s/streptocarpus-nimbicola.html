Streptocarpus nimbicola

Streptocarpus nimbicola, commonly known as the cloud streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a velvety texture, and it has a short flowering period during the rainy season.

Streptocarpus nimbicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens for its unique floral display.
