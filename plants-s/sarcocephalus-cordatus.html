Sarcocephalus cordatus

Sarcocephalus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
