Sagittaria palifolia f. paniculata

Sagittaria palifolia f. paniculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are arranged in a spike or panicle, which is a notable feature of this particular form. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often floating on the water surface or partially submerged.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
