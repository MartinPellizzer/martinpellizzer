Selaginella spinulosovena

Selaginella spinulosovena, commonly known as the spinulose spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, needle-like leaves that are often covered in tiny, spiny projections, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Selaginella spinulosovena plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
