Sebastiania mucronata

Sebastiania mucronata, commonly known as the red-berried sebastiania, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, red or orange berries. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are a key feature, attracting birds and contributing to seed dispersal.
