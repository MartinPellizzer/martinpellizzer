Sorbus aria var. ellipticifolia

Sorbus aria var. ellipticifolia, commonly known as the ellipticifoliate whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting.
