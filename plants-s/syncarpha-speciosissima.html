Syncarpha speciosissima

Syncarpha speciosissima, commonly known as the showy synkarpa, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant produces bright yellow to orange ray flowers, which are often showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Syncarpha speciosissima typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of mountain flora.
