Solidago uliginosa

Solidago uliginosa, commonly known as the yellow blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It produces showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that range from yellow to orange in color, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in bogs, marshes, and other wet habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Solidago uliginosa is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
