Silene uniflora subsp. petraea

Silene uniflora subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the rock campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant produces solitary, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
