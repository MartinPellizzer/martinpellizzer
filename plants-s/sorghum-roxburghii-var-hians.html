Sorghum roxburghii var. hians

Sorghum roxburghii var. hians, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
