Smilax vaginata var. stans

Smilax vaginata var. stans, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological restoration projects.
