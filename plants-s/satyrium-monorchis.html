Satyrium monorchis

Satyrium monorchis, commonly known as the single-testis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, prominent pollen sac, which is a distinguishing trait among orchids. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex arrangement of sepals and petals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
