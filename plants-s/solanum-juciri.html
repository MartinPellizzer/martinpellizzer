Solanum juciri

Solanum juciri, commonly known as the Juciri nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds, and the plant is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
