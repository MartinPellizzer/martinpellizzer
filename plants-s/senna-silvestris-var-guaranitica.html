Senna silvestris var. guaranitica

Senna silvestris var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
