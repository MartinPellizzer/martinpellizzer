Scilla veneris

Scilla veneris, commonly known as the Sacred Flower or Star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value.

Scilla veneris is also associated with religious and cultural symbolism in various ancient traditions.
