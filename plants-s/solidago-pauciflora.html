Solidago pauciflora

Solidago pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Solidago pauciflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
