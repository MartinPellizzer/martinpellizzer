Spiropetalum klainianum

Spiropetalum klainianum, commonly known as the golden spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
