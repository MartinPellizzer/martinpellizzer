Scrophularia aquatica var. ampla

Scrophularia aquatica var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved water figwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, adapted to retain moisture in moist environments. Its flowers are typically pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
