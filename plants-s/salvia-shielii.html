Salvia shielii

Salvia shielii, commonly known as Shiel's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia shielii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
