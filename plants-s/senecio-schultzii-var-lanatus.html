Senecio schultzii var. lanatus

Senecio schultzii var. lanatus, commonly known as the Woolly Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
