Stemona moluccana var. ternatensis

Stemona moluccana var. ternatensis, commonly known as the Ternate stemona, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
