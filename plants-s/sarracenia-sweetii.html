Sarracenia sweetii

Sarracenia sweetii, commonly known as the Sweet's pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded pitchers that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps, where it can absorb nutrients from the soil. Its leaves are typically green with a red or purple hue, and the pitchers vary in size depending on the plant's age and environmental conditions.

Sarracenia sweetii is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific care to cultivate successfully.
