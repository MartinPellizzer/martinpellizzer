Stevia suaveolens

Stevia suaveolens, commonly known as sweetleaf or honey plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern applications.
