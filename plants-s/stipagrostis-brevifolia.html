Stipagrostis brevifolia

Stipagrostis brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with poor soil, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Stipagrostis brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
