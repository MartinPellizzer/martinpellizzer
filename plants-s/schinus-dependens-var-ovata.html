Schinus dependens var. ovata

Schinus dependens var. ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or red mite tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a glossy dark green surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
