Scutellaria pseudotenax f. brevipelta

Scutellaria pseudotenax f. brevipelta, commonly known as the short-peltate scutellaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with two-lipped corollas typical of the genus. This plant is native to specific regions in China and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
