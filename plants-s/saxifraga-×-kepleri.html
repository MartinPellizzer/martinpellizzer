Saxifraga × kepleri

Saxifraga × kepleri, commonly known as the rockfoil or the frost plant, is a hybrid plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate frost, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and visually appealing plant for alpine gardens.
