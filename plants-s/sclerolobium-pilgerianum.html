Sclerolobium pilgerianum

Sclerolobium pilgerianum, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its woody, evergreen structure and produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The yellow trumpet tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blooms.
