Stipa acuta

Stipa acuta, commonly known as sharp wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Stipa acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
