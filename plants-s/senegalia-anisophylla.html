Senegalia anisophylla

Senegalia anisophylla, commonly known as the burro bristlebush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody pods containing several seeds. Senegalia anisophylla is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
