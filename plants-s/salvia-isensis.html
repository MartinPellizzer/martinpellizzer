Salvia isensis

Salvia isensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia isensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
