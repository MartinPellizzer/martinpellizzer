Solanum pterocaulon var. heterogonum

Solanum pterocaulon var. heterogonum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
