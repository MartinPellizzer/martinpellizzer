Satyrium gigas

Satyrium gigas, commonly known as the giant satyrium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a prominent lip and spurred ovary, typical of many orchids.

Satyrium gigas is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
