Sorghum vulgare var. bicolor

Sorghum vulgare var. bicolor, commonly known as millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and its distinctive two-colored grain, which typically features a red or purple hue on the outside and a white or yellow interior. This variety is native to Africa and has been cultivated for thousands of years as a staple food crop. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant's botanical features include narrow leaves, spike-like inflorescences, and small, wind-pollinated flowers.
