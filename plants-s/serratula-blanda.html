Serratula blanda

Serratula blanda, commonly known as the common serrated everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically arranged in a flat, disk-like structure. Serratula blanda is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact for extended periods, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
