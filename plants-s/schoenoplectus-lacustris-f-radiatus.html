Schoenoplectus lacustris f. radiatus

Schoenoplectus lacustris f. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate marsh arrowhead, is a variant of the marsh arrowhead plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, radiating appearance, which gives it its common name.
