Stevia macbridei var. typica

Stevia macbridei var. typica, commonly known as the Hawaiian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has gained attention for potential use in natural sweetener production.
