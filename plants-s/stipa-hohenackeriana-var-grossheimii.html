Stipa hohenackeriana var. grossheimii

Stipa hohenackeriana var. grossheimii, commonly known as the Hohenacker's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a delicate, airy appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
