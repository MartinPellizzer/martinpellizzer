Scirpus monophyllus

Scirpus monophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Scirpus monophyllus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
