Spiranthes richardi

Spiranthes richardi, commonly known as the richard's spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its intricate, spiral-like arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and often have a subtle fragrance.

Spiranthes richardi is known for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
