Senecio gibbosus subsp. bicolor

Senecio gibbosus subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The subspecies bicolor is distinguished by its two-toned flowers, which have a darker center than the outer petals.
