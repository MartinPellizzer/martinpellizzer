Shorea warburgii

Shorea warburgii, commonly known as black mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, dark bark and broad, elliptical leaves. It plays an important role in coastal ecosystems, helping to stabilize shorelines and prevent erosion.

Shorea warburgii is also known for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it well adapted to mangrove environments.
