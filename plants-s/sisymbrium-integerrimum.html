Sisymbrium integerrimum

Sisymbrium integerrimum, commonly known as hoary crowsfoot, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, low-growing stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions.
