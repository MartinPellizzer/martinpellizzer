Schisandra wilsoniana

Schisandra wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's five-flavor vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red berries that are highly aromatic. Schisandra wilsoniana is valued for its medicinal properties, with the berries containing various bioactive compounds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
