Shorea gibbosa

Shorea gibbosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood with a pale to reddish-brown color. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall stature and buttressed roots. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Shorea gibbosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
