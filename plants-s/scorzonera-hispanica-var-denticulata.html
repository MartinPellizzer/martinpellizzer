Scorzonera hispanica var. denticulata

Scorzonera hispanica var. denticulata, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often harvested for culinary use, similar to parsnips. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera hispanica var. denticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
