Schoberia biebersteiniana

Schoberia biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to dry conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
