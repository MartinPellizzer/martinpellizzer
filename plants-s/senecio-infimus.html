Senecio infimus

Senecio infimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
