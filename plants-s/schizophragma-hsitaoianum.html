Schizophragma hsitaoianum

Schizophragma hsitaoianum, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern China.

It features compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to yellow. The plant's flowers are pollinated by insects, and it relies on birds for seed dispersal. Schizophragma hsitaoianum thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
