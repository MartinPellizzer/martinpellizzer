Sasa togashiana

Sasa togashiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the tribe Sino-Nemoralae and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit with slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa togashiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
