Silene moldavica

Silene moldavica, commonly known as Moldavian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals that are often tinged with red.

Silene moldavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
