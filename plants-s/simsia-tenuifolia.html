Simsia tenuifolia

Simsia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf simsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Simsia tenuifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
