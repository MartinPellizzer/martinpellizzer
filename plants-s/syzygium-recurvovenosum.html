Syzygium recurvovenosum

Syzygium recurvovenosum, commonly known as the narrow-veined tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with prominent, curved veins. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible, sour fruits that are similar to tamarind.

The botanical features of Syzygium recurvovenosum make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
