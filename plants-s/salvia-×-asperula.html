Salvia × asperula

Salvia × asperula, commonly known as meadow sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia × asperula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
