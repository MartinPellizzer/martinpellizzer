Saldinia platyclada

Saldinia platyclada, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Saldinia platyclada is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland conservation and ecological studies.
