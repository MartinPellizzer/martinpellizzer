Sambucus ebulus var. viridis

Sambucus ebulus var. viridis, commonly known as green elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, often reddish stem and clusters of small, clustered flowers.

The plant is part of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
