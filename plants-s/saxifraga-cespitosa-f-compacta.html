Saxifraga cespitosa f. compacta

Saxifraga cespitosa f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are relatively small but numerous. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
