Silene erysimifolia

Silene erysimifolia, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and its clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and the flowers are borne on short pedicels.

Silene erysimifolia is an important component of its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
