Salvia amplexicaulis var. minutiflora

Salvia amplexicaulis var. minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf hummingbird sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, velvety gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and bees, adding both ecological value and visual interest to gardens.
