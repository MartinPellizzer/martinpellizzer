Stachys obtusifolia

Stachys obtusifolia, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys obtusifolia is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
