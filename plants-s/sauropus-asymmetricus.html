Sauropus asymmetricus

Sauropus asymmetricus, commonly known as the asymmetric sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, round berries.
