Senecio cassinioides

Senecio cassinioides, commonly known as the Cassini's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio cassinioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
