Solanum zamorense

Solanum zamorense, commonly known as the Zamora nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
