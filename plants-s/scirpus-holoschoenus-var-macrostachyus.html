Scirpus holoschoenus var. macrostachyus

Scirpus holoschoenus var. macrostachyus, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale brown flowers at the top of its stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands in moist soils, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
