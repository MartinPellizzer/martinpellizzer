Solanum leptanthum var. parvifolium

Solanum leptanthum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped, violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when mature and contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
