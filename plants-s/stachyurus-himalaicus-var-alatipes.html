Stachyurus himalaicus var. alatipes

Stachyurus himalaicus var. alatipes, commonly known as the Himalayan weeping buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
