Solidago arguta var. arguta

Solidago arguta var. arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flower clusters in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Solidago arguta var. arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
