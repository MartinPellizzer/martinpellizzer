Scutellaria granatensis

Scutellaria granatensis, commonly known as the granate skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria granatensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
