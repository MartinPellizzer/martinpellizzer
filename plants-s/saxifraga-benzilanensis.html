Saxifraga benzilanensis

Saxifraga benzilanensis, commonly known as the Benin saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to parts of West Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the wet season.

Saxifraga benzilanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
