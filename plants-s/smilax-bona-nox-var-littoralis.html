Smilax bona-nox var. littoralis

Smilax bona-nox var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal smilax, is a variant of the common smilax species native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and is often found in wetlands and dunes.

Its adaptability to coastal environments makes it a resilient and ecologically significant plant in these habitats.
