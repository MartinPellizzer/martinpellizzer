Silene fabaria subsp. fabaria

Silene fabaria subsp.

fabaria, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. It is an annual herb with erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its showy blooms and has been used in traditional medicine.
