Stigmaphyllon richardianum

Stigmaphyllon richardianum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines, and its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid garden environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its adaptations to harsh climates.
