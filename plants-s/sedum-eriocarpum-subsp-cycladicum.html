Sedum eriocarpum subsp. cycladicum

Sedum eriocarpum subsp.

cycladicum, commonly known as the Cycladic sedum, is a perennial succulent native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
