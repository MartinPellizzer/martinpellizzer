Salix barclayi var. resurrectionis

Salix barclayi var. resurrectionis, commonly known as the resurrection willow, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its ability to survive in extreme drought conditions, which is reflected in its common name. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its grayish bark and long, drooping branches that give it a weeping appearance. Botanically, it is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring.
