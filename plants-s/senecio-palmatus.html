Senecio palmatus

Senecio palmatus, commonly known as the palmate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Senecio palmatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
