Stilifolium

Stilifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its compound leaves that are often arranged in a distinctive, feathery pattern. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a pea-like shape typical of the Fabaceae family. Stilifolium species are native to various regions in Africa and are often found in dry, arid environments.
