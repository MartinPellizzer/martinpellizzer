Schoenus lepidosperma

Schoenus lepidosperma, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and forms extensive tussocks that are often found in wetland and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the wetland ecosystems in its native range.
