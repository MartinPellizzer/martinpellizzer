Saldinia obovatifolia

Saldinia obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape, adapted to store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Saldinia obovatifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
