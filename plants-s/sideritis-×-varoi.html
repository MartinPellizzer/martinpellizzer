Sideritis × varoi

Sideritis × varoi, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sideritis × varoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, slightly minty fragrance.
