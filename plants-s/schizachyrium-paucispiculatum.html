Schizachyrium paucispiculatum

Schizachyrium paucispiculatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open prairies and dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature to a reddish or brown color in the fall. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a valuable component of native plant landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
