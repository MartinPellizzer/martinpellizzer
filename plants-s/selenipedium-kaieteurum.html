Selenipedium kaieteurum

Selenipedium kaieteurum, commonly known as the Kaieteurum spike orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, spiraling inflorescence that can reach several meters in length, bearing numerous showy, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Selenipedium kaieteurum is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
