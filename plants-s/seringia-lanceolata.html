Seringia lanceolata

Seringia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved seringia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Seringia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
