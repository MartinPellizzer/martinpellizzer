Senecio aureus var. ashei

Senecio aureus var. ashei, commonly known as the Ashely's golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
