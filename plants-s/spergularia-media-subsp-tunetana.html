Spergularia media subsp. tunetana

Spergularia media subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Mediterranean sand-spurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or calcareous soils.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in coastal or inland dune habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a characteristic species of Mediterranean ecosystems.
