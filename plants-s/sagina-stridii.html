Sagina stridii

Sagina stridii, commonly known as the sandparsley, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its succulent leaves. It often grows in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sagina stridii is typically found in the Mediterranean region and parts of western North America.
