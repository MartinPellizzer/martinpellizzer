Senecio imparipinnatus

Senecio imparipinnatus, commonly known as the blueweed or the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Senecio imparipinnatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
