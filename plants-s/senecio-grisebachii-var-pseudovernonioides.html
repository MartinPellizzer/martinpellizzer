Senecio grisebachii var. pseudovernonioides

Senecio grisebachii var. pseudovernonioides, commonly known as the gray-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
