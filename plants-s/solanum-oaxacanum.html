Solanum oaxacanum

Solanum oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxacan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in dry forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
