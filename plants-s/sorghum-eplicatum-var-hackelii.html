Sorghum eplicatum var. hackelii

Sorghum eplicatum var. hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's sorghum, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the sorghum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage and unique flowering structure, which differ from more common sorghum species. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
