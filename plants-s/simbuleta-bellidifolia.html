Simbuleta bellidifolia

Simbuleta bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Simbuleta bellidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
