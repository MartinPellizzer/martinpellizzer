Sedum telephium var. album

Sedum telephium var. album, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners.
