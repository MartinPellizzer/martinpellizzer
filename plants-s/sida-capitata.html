Sida capitata

Sida capitata, commonly known as the "velvetleaf" or "capitata sida," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Sida capitata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
