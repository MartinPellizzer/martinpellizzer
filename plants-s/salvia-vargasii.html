Salvia vargasii

Salvia vargasii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
