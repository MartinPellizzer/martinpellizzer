Sisymbrianthus pusillus

Sisymbrianthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, attaching itself to the roots of grasses or other plants. Its small, yellowish flowers are often inconspicuous, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and survival of its host plants.
