Seriphidium fedorovii

Seriphidium fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant features linear, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
