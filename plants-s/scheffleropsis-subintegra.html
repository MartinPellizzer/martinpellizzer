Scheffleropsis subintegra

Scheffleropsis subintegra, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing structures. Its leaves are typically dark green with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Scheffleropsis subintegra thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in indoor gardening.
