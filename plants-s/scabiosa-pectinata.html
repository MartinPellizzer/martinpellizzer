Scabiosa pectinata

Scabiosa pectinata, commonly known as the cloud flower or pink scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that resemble clouds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
