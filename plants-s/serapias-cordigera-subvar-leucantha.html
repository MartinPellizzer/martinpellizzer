Serapias cordigera subvar. leucantha

Serapias cordigera subvar. leucantha, commonly known as the white-heart orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces racemes of flowers with intricate, lip-like petals. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
