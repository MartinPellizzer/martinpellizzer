Serjania serrata

Serjania serrata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or seringa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
