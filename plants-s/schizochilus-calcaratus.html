Schizochilus calcaratus

Schizochilus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred orchid flowers that are often pale green or yellow in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Schizochilus calcaratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
