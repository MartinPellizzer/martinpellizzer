Salvia brasiliensis

Salvia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sage, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia brasiliensis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
