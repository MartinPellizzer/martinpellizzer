Sasa tobagenzoana

Sasa tobagenzoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact shrub-like structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across temperate regions.
