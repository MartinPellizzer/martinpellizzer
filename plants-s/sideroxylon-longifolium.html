Sideroxylon longifolium

Sideroxylon longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved ironwood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, dark bark and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

Its hard, heavy wood is valued for its durability and has been used in traditional construction and tool-making.
