Sedum laconicum f. elongatum

Sedum laconicum f. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves.

It is native to arid and rocky environments, typically found in regions with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fleshy leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

This cultivar is popular in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
