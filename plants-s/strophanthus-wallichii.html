Strophanthus wallichii

Strophanthus wallichii, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with tufted hairs.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it both dangerous and valuable in traditional medicine.
