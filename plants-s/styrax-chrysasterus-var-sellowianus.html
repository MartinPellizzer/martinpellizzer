Styrax chrysasterus var. sellowianus

Styrax chrysasterus var. sellowianus, commonly known as the golden styrax, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the family Styracaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and subtle scent. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The wood of this species is lightweight and has been historically used in the production of incense and other aromatic products.
