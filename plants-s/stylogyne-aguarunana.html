Stylogyne aguarunana

Stylogyne aguarunana, commonly known as the Amazonian leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Stylogyne aguarunana is often found growing along the forest floor or on tree trunks in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in Amazonian ecosystems.
