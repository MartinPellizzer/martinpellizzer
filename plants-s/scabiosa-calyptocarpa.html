Scabiosa calyptocarpa

Scabiosa calyptocarpa, commonly known as the shieldflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, shield-like calyx that encloses the base of the petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Scabiosa calyptocarpa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
