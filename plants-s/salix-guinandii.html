Salix guinandii

Salix guinandii, commonly known as Guianan willow, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction.

Salix guinandii is adapted to wetland and riparian environments, making it an important component of these ecosystems.
