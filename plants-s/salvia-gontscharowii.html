Salvia gontscharowii

Salvia gontscharowii, commonly known as the Gontscharow's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia gontscharowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
