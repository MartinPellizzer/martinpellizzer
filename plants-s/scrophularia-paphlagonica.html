Scrophularia paphlagonica

Scrophularia paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, clustered in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia paphlagonica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
