Sinocrassula indica var. maculosa

Sinocrassula indica var. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted jade plant, is a succulent native to China.

It features dense, compact growth with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that have distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
