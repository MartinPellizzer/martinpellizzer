Silene mairei

Silene mairei, commonly known as the Maire's campion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
