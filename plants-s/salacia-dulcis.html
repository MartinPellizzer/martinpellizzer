Salacia dulcis

Salacia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet-scented salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
