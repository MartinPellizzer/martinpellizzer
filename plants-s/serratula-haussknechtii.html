Serratula haussknechtii

Serratula haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually spiny and deeply lobed, while its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer.

Serratula haussknechtii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
