Sedum parvistamineum

Sedum parvistamineum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Sedum parvistamineum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact size and drought tolerance.
