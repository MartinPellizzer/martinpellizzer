Spyridium mucronatum subsp. recurvum

Spyridium mucronatum subsp.

recurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spyridium, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, oval leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

The subspecies is distinguished by its recurved leaf tips and compact growth habit.
