Saxifraga paddoensis

Saxifraga paddoensis, commonly known as the Padoes saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga paddoensis is adapted to harsh conditions and is an important component of New Zealand's alpine flora.
