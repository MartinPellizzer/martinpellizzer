Saussurea xinjiangensis

Saussurea xinjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang saffron plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense, yellowish-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically arranged in a composite flower head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environments of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
