Sedum anhuiense

Sedum anhuiense, commonly known as the Anhui stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in the summer months.

Sedum anhuiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
