Sickingia calderoniana

Sickingia calderoniana, commonly known as the Mexican ice plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
