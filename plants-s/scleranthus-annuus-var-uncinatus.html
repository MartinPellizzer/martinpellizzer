Scleranthus annuus var. uncinatus

Scleranthus annuus var. uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked evening-primrose, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically opposite and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, hook-shaped seeds. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
