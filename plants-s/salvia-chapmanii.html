Salvia chapmanii

Salvia chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia chapmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
