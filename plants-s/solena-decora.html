Solena decora

Solena decora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Solena decora is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
