Salix × spathulata

Salix × spathulata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that hang in delicate, drooping clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and turn a striking yellow in the autumn.

Native to the Northern Hemisphere, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
