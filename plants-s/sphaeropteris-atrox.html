Sphaeropteris atrox

Sphaeropteris atrox, commonly known as the giant cliff fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the fern family, characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rocky or cliff faces. Its rhizomes are thick and robust, allowing it to anchor itself in difficult terrain.

Sphaeropteris atrox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
