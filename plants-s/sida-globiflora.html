Sida globiflora

Sida globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique floral appearance.

Sida globiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
