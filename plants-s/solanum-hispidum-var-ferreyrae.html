Solanum hispidum var. ferreyrae

Solanum hispidum var. ferreyrae, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
