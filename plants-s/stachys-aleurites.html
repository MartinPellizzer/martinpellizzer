Stachys aleurites

Stachys aleurites, commonly known as the silverweed or woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, silver-gray foliage and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Stachys aleurites is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
