Symplocos attenuata

Symplocos attenuata, commonly known as the slender waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos attenuata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
