Strelitzia humilis

Strelitzia humilis, commonly known as the common bird-of-paradise, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, banana-like leaves and dramatic, bird-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers full sun to partial shade. Strelitzia humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
