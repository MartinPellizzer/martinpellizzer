Schefflera octostyla

Schefflera octostyla, commonly known as the giant umbrella plant, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, making it a dramatic addition to any landscape. The plant features multiple stems that branch out from the base, supporting its expansive foliage. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white, and it produces berries that are dark blue when mature.

Schefflera octostyla thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
