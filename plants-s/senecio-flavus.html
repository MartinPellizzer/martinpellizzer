Senecio flavus

Senecio flavus, commonly known as the yellow groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio flavus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
