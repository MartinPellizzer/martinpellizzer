Staurogyne polybotrya

Staurogyne polybotrya, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, floating mats of narrow, bright green leaves that form a thick canopy on the water surface. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its rhizomes spread horizontally beneath the water, allowing it to colonize large areas of still or slow-moving water.

Staurogyne polybotrya plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
