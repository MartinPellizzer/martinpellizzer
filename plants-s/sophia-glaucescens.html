Sophia glaucescens

Sophia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue sophia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The botanical features of Sophia glaucescens make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
