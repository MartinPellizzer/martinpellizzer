Senecio vaginatus

Senecio vaginatus, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, white layer of trichomes, giving them a silvery appearance.

Senecio vaginatus is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
