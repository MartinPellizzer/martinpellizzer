Salvia arizonica var. huachucana

Salvia arizonica var. huachucana, commonly known as Arizona sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh desert conditions, which contributes to its ecological significance in its native range.
