Sarracenia × diesneriana

Sarracenia × diesneriana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wet, acidic soils along the edges of bogs and swamps. The plant produces striking, tubular pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its vibrant red and green coloration makes it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical studies.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not found in the wild and is typically cultivated by enthusiasts and researchers.
