Strophanthus hypoleucos

Strophanthus hypoleucos, commonly known as the white-stemmed strophanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces striking yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is notable for containing cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine.
