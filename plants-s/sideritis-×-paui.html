Sideritis × paui

Sideritis × paui, commonly known as the hybrid sidereal herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scordata and Sideritis taurica, characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in regions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sideritis × paui is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
