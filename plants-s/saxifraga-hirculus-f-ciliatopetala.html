Saxifraga hirculus f. ciliatopetala

Saxifraga hirculus f. ciliatopetala, commonly known as the ciliate-petaled saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy cilia, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
