Schizeilema pallidum, commonly known as the pale stinkhorn, is a unique fungi that belongs to the Phallaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often foul-smelling cap, which is covered in a slimy, whitish to yellowish spore mass. Schizeilema pallidum plays an important ecological role in decomposing organic matter and recycling nutrients in forest ecosystems.
