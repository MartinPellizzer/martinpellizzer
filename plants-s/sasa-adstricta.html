Sasa adstricta

Sasa adstricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Sasa adstricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in basketry and papermaking.
