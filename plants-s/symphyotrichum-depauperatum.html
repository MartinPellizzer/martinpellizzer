Symphyotrichum depauperatum

Symphyotrichum depauperatum, commonly known as the slender white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
