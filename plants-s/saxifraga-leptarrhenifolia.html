Saxifraga leptarrhenifolia

Saxifraga leptarrhenifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats or cushions. The plant features small, often pale green or silver-gray leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga leptarrhenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
