Sideritis conferta

Sideritis conferta, commonly known as Greek burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Sideritis conferta thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Its aromatic properties and medicinal uses have made it a valued plant in both horticulture and herbal remedies.
