Sempervivum arachnoideum subsp. tomentosum

Sempervivum arachnoideum subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the fuzzy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
