Silene italica var. brevipes

Silene italica var. brevipes, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Silene italica var. brevipes is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
