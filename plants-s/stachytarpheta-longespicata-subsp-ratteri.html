Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp. ratteri

Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp.

ratteri, commonly known as the Ratter's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
