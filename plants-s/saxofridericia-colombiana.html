Saxofridericia colombiana

Saxofridericia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky soils and high-altitude areas, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
