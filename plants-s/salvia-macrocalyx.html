Salvia macrocalyx

Salvia macrocalyx, commonly known as the largeflower sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia macrocalyx is also valued for its aromatic foliage and resilience to drought conditions.
