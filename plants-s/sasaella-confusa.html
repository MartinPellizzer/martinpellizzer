Sasaella confusa

Sasaella confusa, commonly known as the cloud forest bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the humid mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and forms dense clumps through rhizome expansion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it thrive in low-light conditions.

Sasaella confusa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various forest organisms.
