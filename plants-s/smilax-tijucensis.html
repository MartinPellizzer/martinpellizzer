Smilax tijucensis

Smilax tijucensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or Tijuca smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a robust, woody stem with thorned tendrils that help it climb and spread through its environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, bitter scent.

Smilax tijucensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
