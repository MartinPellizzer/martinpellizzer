Symphyotrichum sagittifolium

Symphyotrichum sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
