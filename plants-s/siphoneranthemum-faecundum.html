Siphoneranthemum faecundum

Siphoneranthemum faecundum, commonly known as the golden rain plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, pendulous clusters, resembling small raindrops. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Siphoneranthemum faecundum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
