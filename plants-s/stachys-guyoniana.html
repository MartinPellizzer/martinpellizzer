Stachys guyoniana

Stachys guyoniana, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Stachys guyoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
