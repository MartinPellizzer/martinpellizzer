Salvia anglica

Salvia anglica, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Salvia anglica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
