Salvia sclareoides f. lusitanica

Salvia sclareoides f. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
