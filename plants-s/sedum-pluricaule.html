Sedum pluricaule

Sedum pluricaule, commonly known as the many-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Sedum pluricaule is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
