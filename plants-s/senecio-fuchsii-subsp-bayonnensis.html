Senecio fuchsii subsp. bayonnensis

Senecio fuchsii subsp.

bayonnensis, commonly known as the Bayonne Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
