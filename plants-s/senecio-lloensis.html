Senecio lloensis

Senecio lloensis, commonly known as the Lloens Island groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Lloen in the southern Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio lloensis is notable for its yellowish-pink flowers, which bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.
