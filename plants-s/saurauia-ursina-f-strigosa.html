Saurauia ursina f. strigosa

Saurauia ursina f. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy bearberry, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It produces clusters of bright red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. The plant is native to regions of eastern North America, where it thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate acidic soils and partial sunlight.
