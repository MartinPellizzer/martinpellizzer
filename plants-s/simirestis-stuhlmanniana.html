Simirestis stuhlmanniana

Simirestis stuhlmanniana, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's mimosa, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
