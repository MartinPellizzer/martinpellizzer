Sanguinaria vernalis

Sanguinaria vernalis, commonly known as the spring bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, single flowers with five petal-like sepals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and appear after the flowers.

Sanguinaria vernalis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
