Solanum vescum var. davidii

Solanum vescum var. davidii, commonly known as the David's nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
