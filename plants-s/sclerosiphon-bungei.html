Sclerosiphon bungei

Sclerosiphon bungei, commonly known as the rock siltweed, is a freshwater green alga that thrives in still or slow-moving waters.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure, consisting of long, unbranched filaments that are often coated with a gelatinous sheath. The plant is typically found in substrates such as sand, silt, or clay, where it can anchor itself. Its morphology allows it to withstand turbulent conditions, making it a resilient species in aquatic environments.

Sclerosiphon bungei plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a food source for various aquatic organisms.
