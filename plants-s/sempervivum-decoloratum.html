Sempervivum decoloratum

Sempervivum decoloratum, commonly known as the stonecrop or houseleek, is a low-growing succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green, red, or variegated, depending on the variety. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the common name "everlasting," reflecting its resilience and longevity.
