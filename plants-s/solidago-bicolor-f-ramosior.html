Solidago bicolor f. ramosior

Solidago bicolor f. ramosior, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
