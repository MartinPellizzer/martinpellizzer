Scutellaria quadrilobulata var. pilosa

Scutellaria quadrilobulata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy four-lobed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
