Schidiomyrtus sieberi

Schidiomyrtus sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Schidiomyrtus sieberi typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
