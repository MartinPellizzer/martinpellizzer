Schedonorus arundinaceus var. aristatus

Schedonorus arundinaceus var. aristatus, commonly known as the tufted fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its ability to thrive in moderate climates makes it a popular choice for ground cover and erosion control.
