Schizachyrium schottii

Schizachyrium schottii, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its role in stabilizing soil in such areas.

Schizachyrium schottii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
