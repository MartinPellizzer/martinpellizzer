Saxifraga veronicifolia

Saxifraga veronicifolia, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a mantle. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Saxifraga veronicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
