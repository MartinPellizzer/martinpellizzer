Scorzoneroides cantabrica

Scorzoneroides cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
