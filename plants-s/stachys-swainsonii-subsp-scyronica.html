Stachys swainsonii subsp. scyronica

Stachys swainsonii subsp.

scyronica, commonly known as Swainson's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, violet-colored flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its distinctive stachys (whorled) inflorescence, which is a key feature in the genus.
