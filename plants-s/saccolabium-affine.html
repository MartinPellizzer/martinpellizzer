Saccolabium affine

Saccolabium affine, commonly known as the narrowleaf false senna, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Saccolabium affine is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
