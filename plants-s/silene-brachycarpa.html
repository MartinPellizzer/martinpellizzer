Silene brachycarpa

Silene brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Silene brachycarpa is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
