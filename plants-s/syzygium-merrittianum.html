Syzygium merrittianum

Syzygium merrittianum, commonly known as the Katteri or Malabar plum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or yellow when mature.

Syzygium merrittianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
