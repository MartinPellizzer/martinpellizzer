Sedum papillicaulum

Sedum papillicaulum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in tiny white bumps, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
