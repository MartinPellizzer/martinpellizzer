Symplocos pulgarensis

Symplocos pulgarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Symplocos pulgarensis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with visual interest.
