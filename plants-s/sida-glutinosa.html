Sida glutinosa

Sida glutinosa, commonly known as sticky sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Sida glutinosa is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
