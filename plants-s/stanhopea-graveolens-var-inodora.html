Stanhopea graveolens var. inodora

Stanhopea graveolens var. inodora, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive, sweet fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique aroma and aesthetic appeal.
