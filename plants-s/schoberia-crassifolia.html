Schoberia crassifolia

Schoberia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces striking blue or purple star-shaped flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens across regions with similar climates.
