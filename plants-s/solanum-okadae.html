Solanum okadae

Solanum okadae, commonly known as Okada's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a smooth, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Solanum okadae is considered a rare and endangered species.
