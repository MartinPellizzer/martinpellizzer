Salaxis axillaris

Salaxis axillaris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe.

Salaxis axillaris is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant suited to dry conditions.
