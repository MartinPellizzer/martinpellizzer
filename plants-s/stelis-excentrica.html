Stelis excentrica

Stelis excentrica, commonly known as the eccentric stelis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom during the wet season and attract various pollinators.
