Syzygium guineense var. parvifolium

Syzygium guineense var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple drupes. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
