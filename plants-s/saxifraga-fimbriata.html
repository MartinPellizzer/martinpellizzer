Saxifraga fimbriata

Saxifraga fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring cool, moist conditions.

Saxifraga fimbriata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden settings.
