Seriphidium maritimum var. salinum

Seriphidium maritimum var. salinum, commonly known as saltbush or sea holly, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and arid regions with high salt content in the soil. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and salt absorption.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
