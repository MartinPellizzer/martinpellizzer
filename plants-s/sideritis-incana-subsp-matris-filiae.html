Sideritis incana subsp. matris-filiae

Sideritis incana subsp.

matris-filiae, commonly known as mother's-milk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
