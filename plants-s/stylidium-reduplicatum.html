Stylidium reduplicatum

Stylidium reduplicatum, commonly known as the double-styled sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes two distinct staminodes that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in a spike, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Stylidium reduplicatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky areas.
