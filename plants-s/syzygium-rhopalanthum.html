Syzygium rhopalanthum

Syzygium rhopalanthum, commonly known as the red-fruit tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits that are rich in tannins. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
