Setaria melinis

Setaria melinis, commonly known as slender drop-seed, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, open environments.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
