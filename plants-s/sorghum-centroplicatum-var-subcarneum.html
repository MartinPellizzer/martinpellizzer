Sorghum centroplicatum var. subcarneum

Sorghum centroplicatum var. subcarneum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces panicles that are typically red or purple in color, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and as a source of forage for livestock.
