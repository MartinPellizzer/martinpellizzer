Sorghum somaliense

Sorghum somaliense, commonly known as Somali sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing high drought tolerance.

Its fibrous stalks are used for thatching and as a source of fodder for livestock.
