Sehima elegans

Sehima elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Sehima elegans is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to various parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
