Scorzonera lamellata

Scorzonera lamellata, commonly known as the leafy scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. Scorzonera lamellata is known for its edible roots, which have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
