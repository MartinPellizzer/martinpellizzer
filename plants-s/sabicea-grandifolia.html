Sabicea grandifolia

Sabicea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered sabicea, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are fleshy berries that mature to a red color, containing several seeds.

Sabicea grandifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
