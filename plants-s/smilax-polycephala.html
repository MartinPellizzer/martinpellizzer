Smilax polycephala

Smilax polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed greenbriar, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny thorns and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax polycephala is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be invasive in some regions.
