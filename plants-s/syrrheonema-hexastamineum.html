Syrrheonema hexastamineum

Syrrheonema hexastamineum, commonly known as the six-stamen orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring six stamens that are often fused with the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

Syrrheonema hexastamineum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
