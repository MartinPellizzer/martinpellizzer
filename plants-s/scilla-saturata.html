Scilla saturata

Scilla saturata, commonly known as the blue scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.

Scilla saturata is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread over time.
