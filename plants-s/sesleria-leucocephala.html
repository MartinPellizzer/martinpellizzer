Sesleria leucocephala

Sesleria leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flower clusters that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Sesleria leucocephala is also noted for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
