Sedum fusiforme

Sedum fusiforme, commonly known as the spindle-shaped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Sedum fusiforme is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to withstand harsh climates.
