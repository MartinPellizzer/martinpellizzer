Scrophularia smyrnaea

Scrophularia smyrnaea, commonly known as the Smyrna figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms from spring to early summer, and its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects.

Scrophularia smyrnaea is often found in coastal areas and is considered a hardy plant with medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
