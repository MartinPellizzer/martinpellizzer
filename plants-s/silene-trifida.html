Silene trifida

Silene trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and can be found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Silene trifida is known for its short life cycle and is often considered a rare or endangered species in certain regions.
