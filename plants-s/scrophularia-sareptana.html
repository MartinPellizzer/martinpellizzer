Scrophularia sareptana

Scrophularia sareptana, commonly known as the Sareptan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, clustered in whorls along the stems.

Scrophularia sareptana is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
