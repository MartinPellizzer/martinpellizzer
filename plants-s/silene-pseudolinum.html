Silene pseudolinum

Silene pseudolinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene pseudolinum is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its short blooming period in the spring.
