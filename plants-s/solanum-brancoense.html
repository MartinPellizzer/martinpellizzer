Solanum brancoense

Solanum brancoense, commonly known as the White Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or moist environments. Its fruits are small berries that can be toxic if ingested, similar to other members of its family.

Solanum brancoense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
