Salvia pusilla

Salvia pusilla, commonly known as dwarf sage, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, hairy stems and produces clusters of tiny, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, downy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
