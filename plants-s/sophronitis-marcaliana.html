Sophronitis marcaliana

Sophronitis marcaliana, commonly known as the Marcalia orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized orchid with pendulous, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant features a slender, wiry stem and produces clustered flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in rocky or humid forest environments.

The orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its delicate appearance and subtle fragrance.
