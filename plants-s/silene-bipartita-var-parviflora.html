Silene bipartita var. parviflora

Silene bipartita var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered two-parted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woods or along stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in racemes.

This species is often found in shaded or partially shaded habitats and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
