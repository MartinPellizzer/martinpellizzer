Senecio nemorensis subsp. fuchsii

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
