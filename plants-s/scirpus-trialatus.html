Scirpus trialatus

Scirpus trialatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus trialatus plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
