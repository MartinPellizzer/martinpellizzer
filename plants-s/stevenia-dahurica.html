Stevenia dahurica

Stevenia dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian stevia, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which bloom in late summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
