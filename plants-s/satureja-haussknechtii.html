Satureja haussknechtii

Satureja haussknechtii, commonly known as Hausknecht's savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Satureja haussknechtii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
