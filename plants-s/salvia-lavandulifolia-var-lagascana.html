Salvia lavandulifolia var. lagascana

Salvia lavandulifolia var. lagascana, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like thymol and linalool, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal properties.
