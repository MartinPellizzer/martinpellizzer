Sinningia leopoldii

Sinningia leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's gloxinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are typically borne on upright stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Sinningia leopoldii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
