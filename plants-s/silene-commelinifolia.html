Silene commelinifolia

Silene commelinifolia, commonly known as the swamp bladder campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, bogs, and other moist environments, where it thrives in acidic soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene commelinifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
