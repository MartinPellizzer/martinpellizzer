Scabiosa columbaria f. norica

Scabiosa columbaria f. norica, commonly known as the Alpine scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its charming, dainty appearance.
