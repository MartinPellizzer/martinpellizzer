Sorbus majestica

Sorbus majestica, commonly known as the royal rowan, is a striking deciduous tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its showy clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that appear in the autumn. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
