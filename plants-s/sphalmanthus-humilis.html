Sphalmanthus humilis

Sphalmanthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf spongeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sphalmanthus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
