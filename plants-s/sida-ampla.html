Sida ampla

Sida ampla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Sida ampla is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
