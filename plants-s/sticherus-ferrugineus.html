Sticherus ferrugineus

Sticherus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty sticherus, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or rust-colored hue, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in early spring.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
