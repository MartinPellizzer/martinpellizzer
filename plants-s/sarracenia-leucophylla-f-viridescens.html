Sarracenia leucophylla f. viridescens

Sarracenia leucophylla f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered pitcher plant, is a unique variant of the white-winged sarracenia.

This cultivar is characterized by its pale green to yellowish pitchers, which differ from the typical red and orange hues of the species. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in wet, acidic soils. It is a popular choice among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The foliage and pitchers are not only visually appealing but also serve as efficient traps for insects.
