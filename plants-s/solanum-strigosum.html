Solanum strigosum

Solanum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. Solanum strigosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
