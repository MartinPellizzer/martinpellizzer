Salsola chaudharyi

Salsola chaudharyi, commonly known as the Chaudhary's saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly found in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Salsola chaudharyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
