Serapias apulica subsp. messapica

Serapias apulica subsp.

messapica, commonly known as the Apulian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained habitats. It has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers during the spring season.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in Mediterranean regions.
