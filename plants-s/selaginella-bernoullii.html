Selaginella bernoullii

Selaginella bernoullii, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, low-growing plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with erect, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It is characterized by its ability to reproduce through spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella bernoullii is well adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
