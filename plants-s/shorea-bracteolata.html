Shorea bracteolata

Shorea bracteolata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
