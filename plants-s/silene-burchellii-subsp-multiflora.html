Silene burchellii subsp. multiflora

Silene burchellii subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Silene burchellii subsp.

multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
