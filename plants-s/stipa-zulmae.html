Stipa zulmae

Stipa zulmae, commonly known as Zulma's needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. Stipa zulmae is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
