Sedum engleri var. dentatum

Sedum engleri var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern China.

It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
