Senecio minutifolius

Senecio minutifolius, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
