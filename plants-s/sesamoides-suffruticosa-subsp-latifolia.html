Sesamoides suffruticosa subsp. latifolia

Sesamoides suffruticosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sesamoid, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
