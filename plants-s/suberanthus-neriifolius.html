Suberanthus neriifolius

Suberanthus neriifolius, commonly known as the blackleaf suberanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
