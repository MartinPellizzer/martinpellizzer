Sanamunda grandiflora

Sanamunda grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are dark green and often have a slight sheen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Sanamunda grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
