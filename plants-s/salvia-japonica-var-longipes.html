Salvia japonica var. longipes

Salvia japonica var. longipes, commonly known as the Japanese scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and the elongated nature of its flower spikes.
