Sapium triste

Sapium triste, commonly known as bitter sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing dark seeds. Its latex is milky and toxic, containing compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streams and in wetlands.
