Schoenoplectus lacustris subsp. acutus

Schoenoplectus lacustris subsp.

acutus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and lake shores. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while its flowering spikes are usually unbranched and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
