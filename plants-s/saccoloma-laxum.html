Saccoloma laxum

Saccoloma laxum, commonly known as the slender saccoloma, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows on moist, shaded rock surfaces or tree trunks, thriving in humid environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Saccoloma laxum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
