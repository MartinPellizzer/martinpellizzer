Senecio argophylloides var. argophylloides

Senecio argophylloides var. argophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
