Saccharum polydactylum

Saccharum polydactylum, commonly known as the nectar cane or honey cane, is a tropical grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that produce a sweet, sticky nectar. The plant is often cultivated for its nectar, which is harvested by bees and used in traditional honey production. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique properties, it holds cultural and economic significance in certain regions.
