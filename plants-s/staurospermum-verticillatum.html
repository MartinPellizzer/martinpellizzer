Staurospermum verticillatum

Staurospermum verticillatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
