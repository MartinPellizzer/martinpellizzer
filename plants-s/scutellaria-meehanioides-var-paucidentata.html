Scutellaria meehanioides var. paucidentata

Scutellaria meehanioides var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
