Saccolabium discolor

Saccolabium discolor, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its leguminous structure and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seed pods are slender, hairy, and contain several reniform seeds.

Saccolabium discolor is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
