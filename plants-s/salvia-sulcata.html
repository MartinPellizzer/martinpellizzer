Salvia sulcata

Salvia sulcata, commonly known as the African sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia sulcata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
