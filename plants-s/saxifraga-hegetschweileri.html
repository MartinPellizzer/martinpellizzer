Saxifraga hegetschweileri

Saxifraga hegetschweileri, commonly known as the Hegetschweiler's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure, and the stems are short and often hairy.

Saxifraga hegetschweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
