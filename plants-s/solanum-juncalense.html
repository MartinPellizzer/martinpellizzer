Solanum juncalense

Solanum juncalense, commonly known as the swamp nightshade, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a slight purple tinge. Its fruits are globose berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum juncalense is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.
