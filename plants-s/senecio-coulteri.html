Senecio coulteri

Senecio coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, dry fruits.

Senecio coulteri is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
