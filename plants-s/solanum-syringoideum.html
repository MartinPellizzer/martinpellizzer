Solanum syringoideum

Solanum syringoideum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that resemble small bells and are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Solanum syringoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
