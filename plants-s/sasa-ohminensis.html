Sasa ohminensis

Sasa ohminensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Ohmi bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Sasa ohminensis is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
