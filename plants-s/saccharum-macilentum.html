Saccharum macilentum

Saccharum macilentum, commonly known as the silvergrass or slender bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands.

Saccharum macilentum is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel due to its high biomass yield.
