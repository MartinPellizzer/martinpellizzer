Senecio humboldtianus

Senecio humboldtianus, commonly known as Humboldt's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This species is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
