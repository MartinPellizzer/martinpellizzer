Scleranthus leucocarpus

Scleranthus leucocarpus, commonly known as white-fruited sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Scleranthus leucocarpus is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
