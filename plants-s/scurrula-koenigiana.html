Scurrula koenigiana

Scurrula koenigiana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly faces, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unusual appearance and drought tolerance.
