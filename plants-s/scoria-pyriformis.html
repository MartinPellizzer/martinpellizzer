Scoria pyriformis

Scoria pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped scoria, is a unique mineral formation rather than a true plant.

It is a type of volcanic rock characterized by its porous, lightweight structure and distinctive pear-shaped morphology. This rock is often found in areas with a history of volcanic activity, where it forms as a result of gas bubbles trapped during the cooling of lava. Scoria pyriformis is not photosynthetic and does not participate in biological processes like plant life, but it can provide habitat for certain microorganisms.

Its appearance and texture make it a popular specimen for geological collections and educational purposes.
