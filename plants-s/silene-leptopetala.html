Silene leptopetala

Silene leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene leptopetala is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
