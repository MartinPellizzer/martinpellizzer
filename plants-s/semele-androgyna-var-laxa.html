Semele androgyna var. laxa

Semele androgyna var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a basal rosette and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively. It thrives in environments with high water tables and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession within wetland ecosystems.
