Smilax calocardia

Smilax calocardia, commonly known as the red-veined smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and climbing habit, often found in moist woodlands and thickets. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with prominent veins, and the plant is known for its spiny stems.

Smilax calocardia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
