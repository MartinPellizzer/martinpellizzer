Salvia occlusa

Salvia occlusa, commonly known as Mexican beebalm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia occlusa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
