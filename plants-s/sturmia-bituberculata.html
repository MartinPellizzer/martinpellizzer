Sturmia bituberculata

Sturmia bituberculata, commonly known as the two-ridged stromboid, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Strombidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive shell, which features two prominent ridges running from the base to the apex. The shell is typically white or light-colored with a smooth surface and a conical shape. This species is found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, often inhabiting shallow waters.

Its anatomy includes a well-developed radula and a muscular foot, which aid in its herbivorous feeding habits.
