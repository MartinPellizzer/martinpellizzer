Spergularia salina var. australis

Spergularia salina var. australis, commonly known as salt marsh sandspurry, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salt concentrations in the soil and water. The plant has small, yellow flowers and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are often succulent, helping to store water in arid conditions.

Spergularia salina var. australis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
