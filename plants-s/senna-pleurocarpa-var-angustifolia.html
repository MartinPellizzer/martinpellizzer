Senna pleurocarpa var. angustifolia

Senna pleurocarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Australia and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a woody stem and produces pods that contain small, flat seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
