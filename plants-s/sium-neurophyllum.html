Sium neurophyllum

Sium neurophyllum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic if ingested.

The species is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
