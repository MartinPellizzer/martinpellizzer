Steyerbromelia ramosa

Steyerbromelia ramosa, commonly known as the ramose bromeliad, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

Steyerbromelia ramosa is often found growing on trees or in shaded understory areas of tropical forests.
