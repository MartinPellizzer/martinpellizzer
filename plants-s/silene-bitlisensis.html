Silene bitlisensis

Silene bitlisensis, commonly known as the Bitlis catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene bitlisensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
