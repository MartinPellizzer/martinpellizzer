Solanum tuberosum var. leonhardianum

Solanum tuberosum var. leonhardianum, commonly known as the Leonhardi potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its unique tuber morphology.

It produces elongated, narrow tubers that are often red or purple in color, distinguishing it from the typical white or yellow potatoes. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves that have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
