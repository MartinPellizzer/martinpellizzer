Solanum daturifolium var. albescens

Solanum daturifolium var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a species native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
