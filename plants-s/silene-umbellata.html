Silene umbellata

Silene umbellata, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the flowers are arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene umbellata is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
