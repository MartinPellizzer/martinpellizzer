Sesleria nitida f. macrostachya

Sesleria nitida f. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked sesleria, is a botanical variant of the sesleria genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive, elongated inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant in cultivated landscapes.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
