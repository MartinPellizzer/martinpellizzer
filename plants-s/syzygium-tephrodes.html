Syzygium tephrodes

Syzygium tephrodes, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, aromatic flowers. The plant produces small, black, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Syzygium tephrodes is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
