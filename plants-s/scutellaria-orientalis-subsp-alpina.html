Scutellaria orientalis subsp. alpina

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a compact, upright growth habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal medicine.
