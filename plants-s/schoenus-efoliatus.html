Schoenus efoliatus

Schoenus efoliatus, commonly known as the leafless sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its reduced leaves, which are often mistaken for stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important species in wetland ecosystems.
