Solanum interius

Solanum interius, commonly known as the purple eggplant or wild eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged fruits that resemble small eggs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and the fruit matures to a dark purple color.

Solanum interius is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
