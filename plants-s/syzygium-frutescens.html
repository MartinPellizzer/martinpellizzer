Syzygium frutescens

Syzygium frutescens, commonly known as the satinwood or white corymbia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in corymbs, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Syzygium frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Indigenous practices.
