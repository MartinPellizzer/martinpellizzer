Saussurea mucronulata

Saussurea mucronulata, commonly known as the Tibetan corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Saussurea mucronulata is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
