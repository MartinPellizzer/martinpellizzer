Stachys fruticulosa

Stachys fruticulosa, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys fruticulosa is known for its hairy foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
