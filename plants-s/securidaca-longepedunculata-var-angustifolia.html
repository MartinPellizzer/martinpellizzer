Securidaca longepedunculata var. angustifolia

Securidaca longepedunculata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved devil's claw, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, claw-like seed pod. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The seeds are dispersed by the explosive release of the pod, a characteristic feature of the genus Securidaca.
