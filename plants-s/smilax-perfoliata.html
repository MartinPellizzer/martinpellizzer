Smilax perfoliata

Smilax perfoliata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often described as arrowhead-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its stems are covered in sharp, curved spines, which serve as a defense mechanism.

Smilax perfoliata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
