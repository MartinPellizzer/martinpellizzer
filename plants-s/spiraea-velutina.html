Spiraea velutina

Spiraea velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense, silky white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
