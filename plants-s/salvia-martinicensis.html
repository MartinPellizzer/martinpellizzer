Salvia martinicensis

Salvia martinicensis, commonly known as the scarlet salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
