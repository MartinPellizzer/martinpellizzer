Smilax aspera

Smilax aspera, commonly known as the rough smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Smilax aspera is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
