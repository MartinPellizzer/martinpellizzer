Sinarundinaria suberecta

Sinarundinaria suberecta, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, glossy appearance and is often found growing on rocky or cliff faces. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to temperate climates.

Sinarundinaria suberecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
