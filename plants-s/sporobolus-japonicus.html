Sporobolus japonicus

Sporobolus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese grass or wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, with small, awned lemmas. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Sporobolus japonicus is often found in disturbed areas and can become invasive in certain regions.
