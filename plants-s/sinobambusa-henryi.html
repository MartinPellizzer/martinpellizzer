Sinobambusa henryi

Sinobambusa henryi, commonly known as Henry's bamboo, is a species of grass-like bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sinobambusa henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
