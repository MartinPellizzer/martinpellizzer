Saussurea pohlei

Saussurea pohlei, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant is distinguished by its tall, slender stems and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits.

Saussurea pohlei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
