Sacciolepis turgida var. repens

Sacciolepis turgida var. repens, commonly known as the creeping sacciolepis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem and leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
