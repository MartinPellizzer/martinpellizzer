Scrophularia reuteri

Scrophularia reuteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and arranged in spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia reuteri is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
