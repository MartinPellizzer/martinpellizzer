Senecio stellatopilosus

Senecio stellatopilosus, commonly known as the starry pilose groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, star-like hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
