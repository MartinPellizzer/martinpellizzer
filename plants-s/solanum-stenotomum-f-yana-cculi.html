Solanum stenotomum f. yana-cculi

Solanum stenotomum f. yana-cculi, commonly known as the Yana-cculi tomato, is a botanical variant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits unique morphological characteristics compared to its parent species. The plant typically produces small, round fruits with a distinctive red to orange coloration. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to cooler temperatures and lower oxygen levels.

Its cultivation is of interest for both agricultural research and potential use in local food systems.
