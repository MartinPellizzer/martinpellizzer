Swintonia glauca

Swintonia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, mist-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Swintonia glauca thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
