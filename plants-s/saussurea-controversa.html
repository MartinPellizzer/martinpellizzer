Saussurea controversa

Saussurea controversa, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green to brown bracts. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Saussurea controversa is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
