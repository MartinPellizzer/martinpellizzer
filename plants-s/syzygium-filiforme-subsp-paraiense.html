Syzygium filiforme subsp. paraiense

Syzygium filiforme subsp.

paraiense, commonly known as the Paraiense rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny rambutan fruits that are typically orange-red in color.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and the edible, hairy fruits it produces.
