Struthanthus corymbifer

Struthanthus corymbifer, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and shrub-like growth habit. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
