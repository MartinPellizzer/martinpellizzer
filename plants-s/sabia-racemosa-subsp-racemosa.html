Sabia racemosa subsp. racemosa

Sabia racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the clustered sabia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
