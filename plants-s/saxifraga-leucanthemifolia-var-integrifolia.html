Saxifraga leucanthemifolia var. integrifolia

Saxifraga leucanthemifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves that are typically broad and toothed. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with yellow centers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
