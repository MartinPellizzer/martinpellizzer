Spiraea brahuica var. hindukushiensis

Spiraea brahuica var. hindukushiensis, commonly known as the Hindukush spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright, slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its showy, clustered inflorescences produce pink to red flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
