Spiraea sargentiana

Spiraea sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, flat-topped clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea sargentiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
