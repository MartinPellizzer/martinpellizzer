Scilla paradoxa

Scilla paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of many bulbous plants. Scilla paradoxa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
