Seringia arborescens

Seringia arborescens, commonly known as the tree seringia, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fibrous roots make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Seringia arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in landscaping.
