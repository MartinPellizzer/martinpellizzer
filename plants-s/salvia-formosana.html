Salvia formosana

Salvia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan sage, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically violet to blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches.

Salvia formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
