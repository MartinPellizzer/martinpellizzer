Sphondylococcum

Sphondylococcum, commonly known as the "cup-shaped lichen," is a genus of lichens characterized by their unique thallus structure.

These organisms are typically composed of a fungal partner and a photosynthetic partner, usually a green alga or cyanobacterium. The thallus forms a crust-like layer on rocks or soil, often resembling a cup or shell in shape. Sphondylococcum species are found in various habitats, including arid and temperate regions, and are often used as indicators of environmental conditions.
