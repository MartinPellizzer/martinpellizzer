Scutellaria saslayensis

Scutellaria saslayensis, commonly known as Saslay's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Scutellaria saslayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
