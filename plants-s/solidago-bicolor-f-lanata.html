Solidago bicolor f. lanata

Solidago bicolor f. lanata, commonly known as the Showy Goldenrod.

This variant of Solidago bicolor is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy texture. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands or meadows.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in the northeastern United States.
