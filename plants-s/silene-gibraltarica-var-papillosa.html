Silene gibraltarica var. papillosa

Silene gibraltarica var. papillosa, commonly known as the Gibraltar rockflower, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rocky habitats of Gibraltar.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to the unique microclimates of the Rock of Gibraltar.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
