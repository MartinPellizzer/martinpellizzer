Solanum spirale var. tetrasepalum

Solanum spirale var. tetrasepalum, commonly known as the four-lobed nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and unique foliage.

However, like many members of the nightshade family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
