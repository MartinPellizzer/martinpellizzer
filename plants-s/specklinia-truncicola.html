Specklinia truncicola

Specklinia truncicola, commonly known as the banded fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often marked with dark, wavy bands. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself securely.

The foliage is evergreen, and the plant is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening.
