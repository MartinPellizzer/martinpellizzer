Sciothamnus virgatus

Sciothamnus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Sciothamnus virgatus is drought-tolerant and has historical uses by indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
