Silene rudbarica

Silene rudbarica, commonly known as the Rudbar's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Silene rudbarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
