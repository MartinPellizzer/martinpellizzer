Sedum acutifolium

Sedum acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and narrow, pointed leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum acutifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
