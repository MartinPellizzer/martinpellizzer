Sarcococca philippinensis

Sarcococca philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine boxwood, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of small, leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
