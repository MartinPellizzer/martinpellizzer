Senecio araneosus var. walkeri

Senecio araneosus var. walkeri, commonly known as the spider plant or woolly senecio, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
