Syagrus graminifolia subsp. glazioviana

Syagrus graminifolia subsp.

glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil. It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish hairs.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, red fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
