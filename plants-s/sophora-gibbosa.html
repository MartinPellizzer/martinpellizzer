Sophora gibbosa

Sophora gibbosa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces long, pendulous racemes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals.

Sophora gibbosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
