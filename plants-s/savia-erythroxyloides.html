Savia erythroxyloides

Savia erythroxyloides, commonly known as the redwood saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

Savia erythroxyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
