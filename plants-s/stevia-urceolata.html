Stevia urceolata

Stevia urceolata, commonly known as the spoonleaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is noted for its lance-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine for their sweet-tasting properties.

Stevia urceolata is also valued for its potential as a natural sweetener and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
