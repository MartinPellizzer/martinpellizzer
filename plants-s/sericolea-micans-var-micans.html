Sericolea micans var. micans

Sericolea micans var. micans, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The leaves and fruits of this plant contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
