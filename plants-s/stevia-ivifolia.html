Stevia ivifolia

Stevia ivifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as alternatives to sugar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens and as a medicinal plant.

Stevia ivifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
