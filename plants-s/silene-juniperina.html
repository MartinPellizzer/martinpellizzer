Silene juniperina

Silene juniperina, commonly known as the juniper bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland areas. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, and it produces small, bladder-like structures that can trap small aquatic organisms.

Silene juniperina is a semi-aquatic plant, often found near streams or in wetlands.
