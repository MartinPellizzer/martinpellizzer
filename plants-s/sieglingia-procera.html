Sieglingia procera

Sieglingia procera, commonly known as the tall sieglingia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of up to 2 meters, with erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
