Statice leptophylla var. iranica

Statice leptophylla var. iranica, commonly known as the Persian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, papery bracts that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in Mediterranean and semi-arid climates.
