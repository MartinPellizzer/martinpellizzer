Salvia verbascifolia var. lancifolia

Salvia verbascifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters atop tall, upright stems. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, arid environments.
