Sonerila maculata var. emaculata

Sonerila maculata var. emaculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

The flowers display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.
