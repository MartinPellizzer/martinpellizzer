Saxifraga scardica var. obtusa

Saxifraga scardica var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense cushions or low mats. The plant features broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
