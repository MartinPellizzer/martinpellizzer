Salvia pseudopendula

Salvia pseudopendula, commonly known as the Chinese sage or purple flower sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the upper stems. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Salvia pseudopendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
