Schefflera chimantensis subsp. rugosifolia

Schefflera chimantensis subsp.

rugosifolia, commonly known as the rugged-leaf schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Ecuador. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems.

The leaves are usually thick and leathery, with a rough, textured surface that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
