Senecio crispus

Senecio crispus, commonly known as the Woolly Bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is typically found in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and in wet meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its distinctive leaf structure.

Senecio crispus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
