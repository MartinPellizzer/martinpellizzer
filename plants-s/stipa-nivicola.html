Stipa nivicola

Stipa nivicola, commonly known as the snow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cold, alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Stipa nivicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
