Scrophularia koraiensis

Scrophularia koraiensis, commonly known as Korean foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, often in shades of purple or pink, and are arranged in whorled clusters.

Scrophularia koraiensis is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
