Sium angustifolium var. polyphylla

Sium angustifolium var. polyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterparsley, is a variant of the waterparsley species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
