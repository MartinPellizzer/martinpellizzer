Saxifraga magellanica f. scaposa

Saxifraga magellanica f. scaposa, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapted to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
