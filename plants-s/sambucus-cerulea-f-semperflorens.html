Sambucus cerulea f. semperflorens

Sambucus cerulea f. semperflorens, commonly known as the Eastern red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
