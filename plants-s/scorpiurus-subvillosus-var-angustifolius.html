Scorpiurus subvillosus var. angustifolius

Scorpiurus subvillosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved scorpiurus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
