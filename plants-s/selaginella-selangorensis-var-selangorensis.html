Selaginella selangorensis var. selangorensis

Selaginella selangorensis var. selangorensis, commonly known as the Selangor spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found primarily in Southeast Asia.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and strobilus structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as strobili, contain sporangia that release spores for propagation.

This species is notable for its resilience and adaptability to humid tropical climates.
