Sidalcea robusta

Sidalcea robusta, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Sidalcea robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
