Scirpus muricatus

Scirpus muricatus, commonly known as the rough bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that gives it its common name.

Scirpus muricatus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
