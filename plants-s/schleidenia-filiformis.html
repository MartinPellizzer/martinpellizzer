Schleidenia filiformis

Schleidenia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairless stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are inconspicuous, clustered in umbels, and are pollinated by insects.
