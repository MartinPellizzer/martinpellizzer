Salvia argentea var. pomelii

Salvia argentea var. pomelii, commonly known as silver sage or silverstem sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
