Siphocampylus retrorsus

Siphocampylus retrorsus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Siphocampylus retrorsus is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
