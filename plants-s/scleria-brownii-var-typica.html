Scleria brownii var. typica

Scleria brownii var. typica, commonly known as the Brown's bulbous grass, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Scleria brownii var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
