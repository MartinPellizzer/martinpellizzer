Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. compactum

Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. compactum, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically less than 1 meter in height, making it ideal for small garden spaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often followed by tiny, dry seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for landscape design.
