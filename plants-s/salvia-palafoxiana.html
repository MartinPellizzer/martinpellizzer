Salvia palafoxiana

Salvia palafoxiana, commonly known as Palafox's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant purple to pink flower spikes that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
