Saccolabium macphersonii

Saccolabium macphersonii, commonly known as the Macpherson's saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
