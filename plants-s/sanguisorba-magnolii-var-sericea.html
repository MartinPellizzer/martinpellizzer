Sanguisorba magnolii var. sericea

Sanguisorba magnolii var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in silvery, woolly hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
