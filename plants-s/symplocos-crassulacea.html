Symplocos crassulacea

Symplocos crassulacea, commonly known as the thick-leaved wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos crassulacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
