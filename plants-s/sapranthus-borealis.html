Sapranthus borealis

Sapranthus borealis, commonly known as the northern sapranthus, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cold, temperate climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
