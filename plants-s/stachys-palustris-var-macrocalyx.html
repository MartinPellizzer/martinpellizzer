Stachys palustris var. macrocalyx

Stachys palustris var. macrocalyx, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a serrated margin. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in shades of purple or pink during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found along stream banks and in marshy areas.
