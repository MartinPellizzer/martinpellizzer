Solanum stephanodes

Solanum stephanodes, commonly known as the blue potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum stephanodes is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
