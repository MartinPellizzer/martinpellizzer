Salvia cavaleriei

Salvia cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese sage or mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia cavaleriei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in partial shade.
