Schumeria kurdica

Schumeria kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the surrounding soil, contributing to wetland ecosystems.

Schumeria kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
