Sorbus subochracea

Sorbus subochracea, commonly known as the yellow rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
