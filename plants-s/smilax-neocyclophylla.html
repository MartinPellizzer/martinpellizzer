Smilax neocyclophylla

Smilax neocyclophylla, commonly known as the red-tipped smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and can vary in size.

Smilax neocyclophylla is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
