Syngonanthus chrysanthus var. castrensis

Syngonanthus chrysanthus var. castrensis, commonly known as the Castren's goldenbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native habitat.
