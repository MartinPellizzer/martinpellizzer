Scirpus maculosus

Scirpus maculosus, commonly known as the spotted bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often bear reddish or purple spots, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually found in clusters at the top of the stems.

Scirpus maculosus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
