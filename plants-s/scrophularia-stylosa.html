Scrophularia stylosa

Scrophularia stylosa, commonly known as the purple-stemmed scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia stylosa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
