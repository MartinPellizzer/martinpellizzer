Syzygium stipitatum

Syzygium stipitatum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible, though they are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium stipitatum thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
