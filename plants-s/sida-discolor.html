Sida discolor

Sida discolor, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. The leaves are ovate to heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively in certain environments.
