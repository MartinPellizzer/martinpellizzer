Solanum ternifolium

Solanum ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum ternifolium is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
