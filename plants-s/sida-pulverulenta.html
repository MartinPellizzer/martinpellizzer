Sida pulverulenta

Sida pulverulenta, commonly known as dustflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, powdery white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom throughout the growing season.
