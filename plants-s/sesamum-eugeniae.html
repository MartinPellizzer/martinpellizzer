Sesamum eugeniae

Sesamum eugeniae, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Sesamum eugeniae is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
