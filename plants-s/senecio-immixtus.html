Senecio immixtus

Senecio immixtus, commonly known as the mixed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with dark green, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
