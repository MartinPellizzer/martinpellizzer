Salvia aurea

Salvia aurea, commonly known as golden sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Salvia aurea is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
