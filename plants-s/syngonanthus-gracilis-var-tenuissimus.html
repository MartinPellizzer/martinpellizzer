Syngonanthus gracilis var. tenuissimus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
