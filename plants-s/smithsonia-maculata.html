Smithsonia maculata

Smithsonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Smithsonia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
