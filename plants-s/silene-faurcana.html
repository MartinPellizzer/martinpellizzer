Silene faurcana

Silene faurcana, commonly known as the Faurcana campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene faurcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
