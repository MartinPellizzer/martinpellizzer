Saxifraga sullivantii

Saxifraga sullivantii, commonly known as the Sullivant's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or streambanks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga sullivantii is characterized by its ability to thrive in challenging habitats, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
