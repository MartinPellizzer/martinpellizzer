Scrophularia auriculata subsp. major

Scrophularia auriculata subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater earwax plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has a branched stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The flowers are showy, with a distinctive bilabiate structure, and are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
