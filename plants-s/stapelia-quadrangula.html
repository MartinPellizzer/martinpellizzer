Stapelia quadrangula

Stapelia quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces unique, meaty flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers are designed to attract pollinators such as flies and beetles.

Stapelia quadrangula is a hardy species that thrives in arid conditions and requires minimal watering.
