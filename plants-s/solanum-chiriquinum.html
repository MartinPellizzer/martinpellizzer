Solanum chiriquinum

Solanum chiriquinum, commonly known as the goldenberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit develops in a capsule and is often harvested when fully mature for culinary use.
