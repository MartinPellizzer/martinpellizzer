Salsola gmelinii

Salsola gmelinii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments due to its efficient water retention mechanisms. It produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Salsola gmelinii is often found in disturbed soils and can play a role in stabilizing sandy or degraded landscapes.
