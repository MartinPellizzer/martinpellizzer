Silene taquetii

Silene taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
