Simonisia

Simonisia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry environments, making it well adapted to desert conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Simonisia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
