Spathium crispum

Spathium crispum, commonly known as the crinkled spathium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, crinkled leaves that give it its common name, and its small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its botanical features include a short stem, lance-shaped leaves, and a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators.

Spathium crispum is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
