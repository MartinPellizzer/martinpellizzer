Sedum kingdonii

Sedum kingdonii, commonly known as Kingdon's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, Sedum kingdonii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
