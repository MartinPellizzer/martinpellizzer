Solanum moscopanum

Solanum moscopanum, commonly known as the Moscopan tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its foliage and fruit in certain regions.

Due to its hardiness, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
