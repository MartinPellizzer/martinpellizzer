Silene biflora

Silene biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of about 20 to 40 centimeters. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

Silene biflora is known for its short blooming period and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.
