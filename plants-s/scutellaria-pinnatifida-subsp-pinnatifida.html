Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp. pinnatifida

Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
