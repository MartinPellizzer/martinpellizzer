Sempervivum royanum

Sempervivum royanum, commonly known as the rock groundcover or alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Sempervivum royanum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.
