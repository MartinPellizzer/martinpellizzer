Stellaria pinvalliaca

Stellaria pinvalliaca, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are heart-shaped with a velvety texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Stellaria pinvalliaca is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
