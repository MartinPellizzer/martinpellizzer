Solanum setaceum var. irakuanum

Solanum setaceum var. irakuanum, commonly known as the Irakuanum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and bamboo thickets.
