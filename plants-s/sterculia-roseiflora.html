Sterculia roseiflora

Sterculia roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
