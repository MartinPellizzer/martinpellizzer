Symphonia oligantha

Symphonia oligantha, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in coastal and inland forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
