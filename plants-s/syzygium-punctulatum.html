Syzygium punctulatum

Syzygium punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a tart flavor.

Syzygium punctulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
