Sirmuellera ericifolia

Sirmuellera ericifolia, commonly known as the heath shrub, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a delicate pink or white color.

Sirmuellera ericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
