Stadtmannia paniculata

Stadtmannia paniculata, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Stadtmannia paniculata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
