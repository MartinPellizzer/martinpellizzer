Scutellaria scutellarioides

Scutellaria scutellarioides, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, two-lipped, and violet to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported sedative and antispasmodic properties.
