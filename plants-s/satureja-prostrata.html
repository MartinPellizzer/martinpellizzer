Satureja prostrata

Satureja prostrata, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a strong, peppery scent. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Satureja prostrata is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
