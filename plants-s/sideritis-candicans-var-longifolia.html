Sideritis candicans var. longifolia

Sideritis candicans var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, commonly found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
