Suaeda maritima subsp. salsa

Suaeda maritima subsp.

salsa, commonly known as salt marsh spinach, is a halophyte shrub adapted to saline environments. It thrives in coastal areas, including salt marshes and tidal flats, where it tolerates high salinity levels.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid and salty conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round seeds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
