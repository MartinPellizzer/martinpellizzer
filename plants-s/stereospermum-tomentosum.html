Stereospermum tomentosum

Stereospermum tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic uses.
