Scandix pecten-veneris subsp. hispanica

Scandix pecten-veneris subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, spiky, and often blue or purple flower heads that resemble the shell of the Venus shell, giving it its scientific name.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high degree of salt tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are sturdy and erect.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
