Senecio fastigiatus subsp. macounii

Senecio fastigiatus subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's upright groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often branching towards the top.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
