Syringa pubescens var. patula

Syringa pubescens var. patula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or patula lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its graceful appearance and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
