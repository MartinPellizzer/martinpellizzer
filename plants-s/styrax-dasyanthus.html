Styrax dasyanthus

Styrax dasyanthus, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense-making. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Styrax dasyanthus thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
