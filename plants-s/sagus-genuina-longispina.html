Sagus genuina longispina

Sagus genuina longispina, commonly known as the long-spined saguaro, is a distinctive cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that develop a rugged, spiny appearance as it matures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are pollinated by various insects and bats. Its adaptations include a deep root system for water retention and a slow growth rate to survive in arid conditions.

The longispina species is notable for its long, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.
