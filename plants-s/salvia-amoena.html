Salvia amoena

Salvia amoena, commonly known as the pretty sage or narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
