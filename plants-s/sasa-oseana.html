Sasa oseana

Sasa oseana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, grassy texture. Sasa oseana thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories and mountainous regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive mats or clumps over time.
