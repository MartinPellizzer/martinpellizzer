Salix sericeocinerea

Salix sericeocinerea, commonly known as silverleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
