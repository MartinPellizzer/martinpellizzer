Senecio dryadeus var. macrodontus

Senecio dryadeus var. macrodontus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

This variant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, upright growth habit. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
