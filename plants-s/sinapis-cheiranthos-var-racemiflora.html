Sinapis cheiranthos var. racemiflora

Sinapis cheiranthos var. racemiflora, commonly known as the racemiflora wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and easy cultivation make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
