Syagrus rupicola

Syagrus rupicola, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking golden-colored fronds that contrast with its dark green trunk. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible fruits.

Syagrus rupicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
