Solanum psidiifolium

Solanum psidiifolium, commonly known as the "Papaya Leaf Nightshade," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
