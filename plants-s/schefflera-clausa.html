Schefflera clausa

Schefflera clausa, commonly known as the Philippine umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, palmately compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a thick, woody trunk and a broad, rounded shape. Its leaves are usually dark green, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

Schefflera clausa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
