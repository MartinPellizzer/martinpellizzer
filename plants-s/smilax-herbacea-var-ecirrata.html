Smilax herbacea var. ecirrata

Smilax herbacea var. ecirrata, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a woody stem with spines and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant's leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the summer and is an important food source for wildlife.
