Stollaea

Stollaea, commonly known as the coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant is native to various regions in Africa, Madagascar, and parts of Asia. Stollaea species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, featuring compound leaves and pods that contain seeds.

These plants are often valued for their ornamental value and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
