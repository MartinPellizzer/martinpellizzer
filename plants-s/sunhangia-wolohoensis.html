Sunhangia wolohoensis

Sunhangia wolohoensis, commonly known as the Woloho sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and dark brown central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a striking visual display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
