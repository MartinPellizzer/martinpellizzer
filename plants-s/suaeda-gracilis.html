Suaeda gracilis

Suaeda gracilis, commonly known as slender saltbush, is a halophyte native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in compact clusters.

Suaeda gracilis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal and desert soils due to its deep root system.
