Spiraea betulifolia var. rosea

Spiraea betulifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf willow-leaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and clusters of small, rose-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically bright green, with a slight reddish tinge in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
