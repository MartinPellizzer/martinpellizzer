Securidaca angustifolia

Securidaca angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a fleshy, sticky appendage, which aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in both botanical studies and traditional medicine.
