Stachys anisochila

Stachys anisochila, commonly known as the variable betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys anisochila is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
