Stelis zamorae

Stelis zamorae, commonly known as the Zamora's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Stelis zamorae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
