Sasa fallax

Sasa fallax, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
