Suaeda edulis

Suaeda edulis, commonly known as saltbush or desert spinach, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, green to red flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Suaeda edulis is also valued for its nutritional content, as it is rich in minerals and can be consumed as a leafy vegetable in some regions.
