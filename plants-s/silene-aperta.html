Silene aperta

Silene aperta, commonly known as the open silene, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, often pink or white-flowered species. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene aperta is often found in meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
