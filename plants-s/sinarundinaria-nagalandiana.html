Sinarundinaria nagalandiana

Sinarundinaria nagalandiana, commonly known as the Nagaland bamboo, is a rare and endemic species native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and is often found growing in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
