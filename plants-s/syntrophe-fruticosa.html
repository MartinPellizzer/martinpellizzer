Syntrophe fruticosa

Syntrophe fruticosa, commonly known as the red-flowered shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Syntrophe fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
