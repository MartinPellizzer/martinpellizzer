Sideritis montana

Sideritis montana, commonly known as mountain ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma.

Sideritis montana is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
