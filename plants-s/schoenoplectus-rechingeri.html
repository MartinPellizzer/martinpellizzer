Schoenoplectus rechingeri

Schoenoplectus rechingeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological succession. It thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
