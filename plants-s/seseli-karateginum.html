Seseli karateginum

Seseli karateginum, commonly known as Turkish seseli, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Seseli karateginum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
