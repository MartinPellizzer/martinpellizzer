Semiarundinaria lima

Semiarundinaria lima, commonly known as the Lima bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The bamboo is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and construction.
