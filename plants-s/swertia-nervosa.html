Swertia nervosa

Swertia nervosa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of East Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have prominent veins, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Swertia nervosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
