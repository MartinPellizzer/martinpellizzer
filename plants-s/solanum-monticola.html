Solanum monticola

Solanum monticola, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cooler and more humid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
