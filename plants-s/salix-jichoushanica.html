Salix jichoushanica

Salix jichoushanica, commonly known as the Jichoushan willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
