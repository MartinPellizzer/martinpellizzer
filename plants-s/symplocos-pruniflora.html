Symplocos pruniflora

Symplocos pruniflora, commonly known as the cherry-leafed evergreen, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos pruniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
