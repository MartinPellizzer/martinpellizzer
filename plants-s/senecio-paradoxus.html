Senecio paradoxus

Senecio paradoxus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, pale blue to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a distinctive rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that form a dense, compact structure. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and intriguing botanical specimen.
