Sium sulcatum

Sium sulcatum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Sium sulcatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
