Syzygium erythrodoxum

Syzygium erythrodoxum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Syzygium erythrodoxum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
