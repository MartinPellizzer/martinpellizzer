Symplocos nervosa

Symplocos nervosa, commonly known as the veined waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Symplocos nervosa is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
