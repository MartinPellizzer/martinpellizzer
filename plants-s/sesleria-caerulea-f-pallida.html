Sesleria caerulea f. pallida

Sesleria caerulea f. pallida, commonly known as the pale blue sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a pale blue-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
