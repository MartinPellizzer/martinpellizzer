Senna tomentosa

Senna tomentosa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna tomentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
