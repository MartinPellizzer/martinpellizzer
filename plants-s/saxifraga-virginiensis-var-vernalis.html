Saxifraga virginiensis var. vernalis

Saxifraga virginiensis var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or rocky habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of oval, dark green leaves with toothed margins. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, often before the leaves are fully expanded.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
