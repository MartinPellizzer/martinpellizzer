Saussurea laxmannii

Saussurea laxmannii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Saussurea laxmannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
