Senecio otiphyllus

Senecio otiphyllus, commonly known as the Orange Glories, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features vibrant orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Senecio otiphyllus is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and drought-resistant properties.
