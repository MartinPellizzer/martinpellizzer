Sanguinella tripsacoides

Sanguinella tripsacoides, commonly known as the red grass or blood grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish or purple coloration in its inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sanguinella tripsacoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
