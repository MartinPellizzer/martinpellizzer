Strobilanthes eriocephala

Strobilanthes eriocephala, commonly known as the "Cape York morning glory," is a flowering plant native to northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and wetland areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
