Solidago juncea var. neobohemica

Solidago juncea var. neobohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it ecologically significant.

The species is often found in dry meadows and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
