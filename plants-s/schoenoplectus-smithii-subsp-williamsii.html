Schoenoplectus smithii subsp. williamsii

Schoenoplectus smithii subsp.

williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
