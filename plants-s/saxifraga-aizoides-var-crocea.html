Saxifraga aizoides var. crocea

Saxifraga aizoides var. crocea, commonly known as the red saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a reddish or purplish hue, especially under stress. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late summer.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a hardy and resilient species.
