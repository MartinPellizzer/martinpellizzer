Salvia rhyacophila

Salvia rhyacophila, commonly known as the stream sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Salvia rhyacophila is often found near streams and in rocky habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
