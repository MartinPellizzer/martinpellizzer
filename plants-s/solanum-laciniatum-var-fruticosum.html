Solanum laciniatum var. fruticosum

Solanum laciniatum var. fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It features woody stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
