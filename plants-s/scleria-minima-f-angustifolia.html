Scleria minima f. angustifolia

Scleria minima f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbous grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along streams.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
