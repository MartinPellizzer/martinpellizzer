Sclerolaena drummondii var. hispida

Sclerolaena drummondii var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy sclerolaena, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study and potential use in xeriscaping.
