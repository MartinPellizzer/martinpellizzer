Satureja hispida

Satureja hispida, commonly known as rough savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its fragrant flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in summer.

Satureja hispida is often used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning due to its pungent, spicy aroma.
