Strychnos hypogyna

Strychnos hypogyna, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a poison.
