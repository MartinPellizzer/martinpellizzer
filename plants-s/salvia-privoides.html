Salvia privoides

Salvia privoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
