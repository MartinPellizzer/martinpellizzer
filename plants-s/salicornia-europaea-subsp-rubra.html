Salicornia europaea subsp. rubra

Salicornia europaea subsp.

rubra, commonly known as red glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and coastal areas. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are typically red or pink in color, which help in reducing water loss in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often used in culinary applications for its salty, crunchy texture. Its ability to accumulate salt makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems. Salicornia europaea subsp.

rubra is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its capacity to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
