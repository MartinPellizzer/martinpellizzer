Sorbus chengii

Sorbus chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a small, edible berry that turns red when mature. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
