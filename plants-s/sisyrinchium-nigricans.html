Sisyrinchium nigricans

Sisyrinchium nigricans, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and dark centers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sisyrinchium nigricans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
