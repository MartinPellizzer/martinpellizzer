Salix atopantha var. glabra

Salix atopantha var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a variant of the willow genus native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and slender, weeping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats, such as riverbanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in well-drained but consistently damp soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a pale green underside, and it produces catkins that are essential for its reproductive cycle.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil along waterways.
