Saussurea odontolepis

Saussurea odontolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan giant gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and often grows in rocky or alpine regions. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Tibetan and Chinese medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
