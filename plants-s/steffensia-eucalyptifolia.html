Steffensia eucalyptifolia

Steffensia eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus leaf steffensia, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of eucalyptus trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its succulent-like leaves store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Steffensia eucalyptifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
