Sobralia dissimilis

Sobralia dissimilis, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers with prominent green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Sobralia dissimilis is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral display.
