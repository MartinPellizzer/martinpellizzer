Salvia subobscura

Salvia subobscura, commonly known as the pale sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces striking violet to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
