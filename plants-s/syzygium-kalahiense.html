Syzygium kalahiense

Syzygium kalahiense, commonly known as the Kalahari apple, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
