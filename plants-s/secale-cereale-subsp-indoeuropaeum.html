Secale cereale subsp. indoeuropaeum

Secale cereale subsp.

indoeuropaeum, commonly known as the European rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
