Selaginella valdepilosa

Selaginella valdepilosa, commonly known as the Mexican spikemoss, is a terrestrial fern ally native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often arranged in whorls and are covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Selaginella valdepilosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
