Solanum cladotrichodes

Solanum cladotrichodes, commonly known as the hairy leaf nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
