Sorbus vertesensis

Sorbus vertesensis, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are typically consumed by birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Sorbus vertesensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
