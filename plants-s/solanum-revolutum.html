Solanum revolutum

Solanum revolutum, commonly known as the purple potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum revolutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
