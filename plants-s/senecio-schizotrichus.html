Senecio schizotrichus

Senecio schizotrichus, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
