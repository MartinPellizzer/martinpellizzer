Salvia miltiorrhiza var. charbonnelii

Salvia miltiorrhiza var. charbonnelii, commonly known as Chinese sage or red sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

This variant is closely related to other Salvia species and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
