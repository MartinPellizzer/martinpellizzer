Senecio pellucidinervis

Senecio pellucidinervis, commonly known as the translucent-veined groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent, translucent veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many common garden flowers.
