Silene squamigera

Silene squamigera, commonly known as the squamose campion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene squamigera is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
