Saxifraga tricuspidata var. micrantha

Saxifraga tricuspidata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold climates with full sun exposure. The plant features upright stems with alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white or pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
