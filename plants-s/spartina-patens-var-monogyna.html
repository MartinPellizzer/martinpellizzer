Spartina patens var. monogyna

Spartina patens var. monogyna, commonly known as saltmeadow cordgrass, is a perennial grass found in coastal salt marshes.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, white flowers. This plant is highly salt-tolerant and plays a crucial role in stabilizing tidal wetland soils. Its rhizomes form extensive networks that help bind sediments and reduce erosion.

Spartina patens var. monogyna is an important component of estuarine ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
