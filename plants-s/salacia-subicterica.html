Salacia subicterica

Salacia subicterica, commonly known as the yellow salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
