Setaria hassleri var. aequalis

Setaria hassleri var. aequalis, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
