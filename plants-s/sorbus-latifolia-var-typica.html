Sorbus latifolia var. typica

Sorbus latifolia var. typica, commonly known as the broad-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
