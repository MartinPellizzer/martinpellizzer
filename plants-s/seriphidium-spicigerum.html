Seriphidium spicigerum

Seriphidium spicigerum, commonly known as the spiked tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Seriphidium spicigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
