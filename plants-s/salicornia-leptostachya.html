Salicornia leptostachya

Salicornia leptostachya, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of salt crystals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is native to coastal regions and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Salicornia leptostachya is also valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional cuisines.
