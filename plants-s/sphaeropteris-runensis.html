Sphaeropteris runensis

Sphaeropteris runensis, commonly known as the Run Island cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces both male and female cones, typical of cycads, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its unique morphology and evolutionary history make it an important subject in botanical research.
