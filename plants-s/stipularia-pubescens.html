Stipularia pubescens

Stipularia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stipularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Stipularia pubescens is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
