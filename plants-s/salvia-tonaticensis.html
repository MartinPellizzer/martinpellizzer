Salvia tonaticensis

Salvia tonaticensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia tonaticensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
