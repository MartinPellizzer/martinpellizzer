Sida rhombifolia var. rhomboidea

Sida rhombifolia var. rhomboidea, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The species is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
