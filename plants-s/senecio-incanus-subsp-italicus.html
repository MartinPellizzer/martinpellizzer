Senecio incanus subsp. italicus

Senecio incanus subsp.

italicus, commonly known as the Italian hairweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations compared to other Senecio species.
