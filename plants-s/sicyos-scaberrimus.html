Sicyos scaberrimus

Sicyos scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough sicyos, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its hairy, often prickly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
