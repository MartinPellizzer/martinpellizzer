Sapium integrifolium

Sapium integrifolium, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features alternate, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, each with a smooth, entire margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that contain sticky seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The tree has a shallow root system and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
