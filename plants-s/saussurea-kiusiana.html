Saussurea kiusiana

Saussurea kiusiana, commonly known as the Himalayan swamp saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust, herbaceous stem system adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Saussurea kiusiana is often found in wet meadows and alpine tundra, where it plays a significant ecological role.
