Syzygium frodinii

Syzygium frodinii, commonly known as the Frodin's star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped yellow fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit is edible, with a tangy flavor, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary use.
