Serruria fallax

Serruria fallax, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Serruria fallax is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
