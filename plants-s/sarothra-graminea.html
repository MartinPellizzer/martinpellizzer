Sarothra graminea

Sarothra graminea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Sarothra graminea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
