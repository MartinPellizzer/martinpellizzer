Sedum kurdistanicum

Sedum kurdistanicum, commonly known as Kurdish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange, during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in dry, well-drained soils.

Sedum kurdistanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
