Scaevola ovalifolia var. media

Scaevola ovalifolia var. media, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fanflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and oval-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salty conditions, which enhances its ornamental value in coastal regions.
