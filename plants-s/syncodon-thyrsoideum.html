Syncodon thyrsoideum

Syncodon thyrsoideum, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or arid regions. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, clustered yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Syncodon thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
