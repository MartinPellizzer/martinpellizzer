Schizachyrium tuberculatum

Schizachyrium tuberculatum, commonly known as roughside grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
