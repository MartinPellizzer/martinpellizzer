Salvia lemmonii

Salvia lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and striking spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Salvia lemmonii is also an important plant for pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
