Silene campanulata subsp. glandulosa

Silene campanulata subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
