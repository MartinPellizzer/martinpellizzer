Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. nocedoi

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

nocedoi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
