Solidago minor

Solidago minor, commonly known as lesser goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Solidago minor is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
