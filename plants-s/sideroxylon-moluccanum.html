Sideroxylon moluccanum

Sideroxylon moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it plays an important ecological role.
