Symphytum davisii subsp. davisii

Symphytum davisii subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly lobed, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used for its healing qualities in traditional herbal remedies.
