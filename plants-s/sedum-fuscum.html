Sedum fuscum

Sedum fuscum, commonly known as the dark sedum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, typically growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features compact, fleshy leaves that are usually a shade of green to reddish-brown, depending on light exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Sedum fuscum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
