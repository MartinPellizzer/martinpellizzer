Silene mogadorensis var. gibraltarica

Silene mogadorensis var. gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar rockflower, is a flowering plant native to the Rock of Gibraltar.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to the Mediterranean climate, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

Its unique habitat and limited distribution make it a notable species in the region's flora.
