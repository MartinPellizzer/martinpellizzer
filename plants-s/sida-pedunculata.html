Sida pedunculata

Sida pedunculata, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that thrives in sandy or saline soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval with a hairy texture. Sida pedunculata is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a common species in coastal dunes.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used historically for various practical purposes.
