Scirpus eriophorum

Scirpus eriophorum, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and bogs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form tussocks, and its flowers are clustered in spikelets at the tips of the stems. Scirpus eriophorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience in waterlogged conditions.
