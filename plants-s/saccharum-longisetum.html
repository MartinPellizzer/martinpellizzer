Saccharum longisetum

Saccharum longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair sugarcane.

It is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense foliage. The plant is notable for its long, stiff hairs that cover its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is primarily valued for its potential in biofuel production and as a source of sugar.
