Saccharum atrorubens

Saccharum atrorubens, commonly known as black sugar cane, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black stems, which give it its common name. The plant is known for its high sugar content, making it a valuable resource for traditional sugar production. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Saccharum atrorubens is often cultivated for both its economic and medicinal properties.
