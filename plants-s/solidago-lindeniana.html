Solidago lindeniana

Solidago lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Solidago lindeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
