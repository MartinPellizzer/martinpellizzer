Shorea polysperma

Shorea polysperma, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tall evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds.

Shorea polysperma plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
