Solanum nigrum var. hebecaulon

Solanum nigrum var. hebecaulon, commonly known as the slender black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. These berries are toxic to humans and animals but are used in some traditional medicines.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
