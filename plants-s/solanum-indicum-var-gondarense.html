Solanum indicum var. gondarense

Solanum indicum var. gondarense, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds such as solanine.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its use is limited due to its potential toxicity.
