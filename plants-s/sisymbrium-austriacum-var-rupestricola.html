Sisymbrium austriacum var. rupestricola

Sisymbrium austriacum var. rupestricola, commonly known as the Austrian poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
