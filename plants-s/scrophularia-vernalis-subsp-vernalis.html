Scrophularia vernalis subsp. vernalis

Scrophularia vernalis subsp.

vernalis, commonly known as spring scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in early spring.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance to pollinators.
