Senecio karelinioides

Senecio karelinioides, commonly known as the Karolinian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
