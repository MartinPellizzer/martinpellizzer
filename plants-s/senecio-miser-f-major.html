Senecio miser f. major

Senecio miser f. major, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a variant of the ragwort genus native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its robust growth and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
