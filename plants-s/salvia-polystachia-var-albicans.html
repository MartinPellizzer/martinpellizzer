Salvia polystachia var. albicans

Salvia polystachia var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale violet flowers that bloom in dense, whorled clusters along the stems. The plant features lance-shaped, grey-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique floral display and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
