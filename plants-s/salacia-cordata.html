Salacia cordata

Salacia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf salacia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. Salacia cordata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
