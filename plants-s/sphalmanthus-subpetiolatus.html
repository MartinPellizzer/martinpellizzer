Sphalmanthus subpetiolatus

Sphalmanthus subpetiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
