Schizostachyum sharmae

Schizostachyum sharmae, commonly known as the Sharma's bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense, fibrous structure that makes it suitable for various industrial applications. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib and a smooth, waxy surface.

Schizostachyum sharmae is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
