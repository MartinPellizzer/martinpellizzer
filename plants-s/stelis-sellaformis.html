Stelis sellaformis

Stelis sellaformis, commonly known as the saddle-shaped stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, wiry stem with clustered, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and leathery. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters of white or pale yellow.

Stelis sellaformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
