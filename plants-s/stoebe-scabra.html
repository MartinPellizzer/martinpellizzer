Stoebe scabra

Stoebe scabra, commonly known as rough puccoon, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Stoebe scabra thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
