Saussurea stenolepis

Saussurea stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes, often growing in mountain meadows and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
