Syzygium koumacense

Syzygium koumacense, commonly known as the Kourou mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of New Caledonia, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, red or orange fruits resembling apples. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinct scent.

Syzygium koumacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
