Solanum suaveolens var. pliophyllidium

Solanum suaveolens var. pliophyllidium, commonly known as the sweet-smelling nightshade, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
