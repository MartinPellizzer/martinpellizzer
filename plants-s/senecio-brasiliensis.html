Senecio brasiliensis

Senecio brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that are clustered in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. Senecio brasiliensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
