Silene multicaulis subsp. genistifolia

Silene multicaulis subsp.

genistifolia, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and branching.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
