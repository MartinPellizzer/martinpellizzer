Saussurea yanagitae

Saussurea yanagitae, commonly known as the Japanese snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in cold and dry conditions. Its root system is thick and fleshy, adapted to store moisture and nutrients in harsh climates.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and traditional medicinal uses.
