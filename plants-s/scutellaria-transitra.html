Scutellaria transitra

Scutellaria transitra, commonly known as the wandering scullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Scutellaria transitra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
