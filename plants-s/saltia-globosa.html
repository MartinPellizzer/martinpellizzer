Saltia globosa

Saltia globosa, commonly known as the globe saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Saltia globosa is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it an important component of dryland ecosystems.
