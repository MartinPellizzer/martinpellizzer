Syneilesis aconitifolia

Syneilesis aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Syneilesis aconitifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
