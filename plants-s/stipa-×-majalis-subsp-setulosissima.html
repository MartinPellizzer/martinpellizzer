Stipa × majalis subsp. setulosissima

Stipa × majalis subsp.

setulosissima, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in ecological restoration projects.
