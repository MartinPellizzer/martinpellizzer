Sabicea floribunda

Sabicea floribunda, commonly known as the showy sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sabicea floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
