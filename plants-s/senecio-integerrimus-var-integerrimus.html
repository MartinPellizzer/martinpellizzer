Senecio integerrimus var. integerrimus

Senecio integerrimus var. integerrimus, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a whitish bloom, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in desert ecosystems.
