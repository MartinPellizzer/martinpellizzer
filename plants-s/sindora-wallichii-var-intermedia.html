Sindora wallichii var. intermedia

Sindora wallichii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
