Spergula maxima

Spergula maxima, commonly known as the giant mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. The plant features basal rosettes of pale green, hairy leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Spergula maxima is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
