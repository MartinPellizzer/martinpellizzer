Silphium integrifolium var. gattingeri

Silphium integrifolium var. gattingeri, commonly known as the Gattinger's compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a single, showy head. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
