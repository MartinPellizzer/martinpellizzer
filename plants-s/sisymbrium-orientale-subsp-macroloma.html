Sisymbrium orientale subsp. macroloma

Sisymbrium orientale subsp.

macroloma, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemate inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
