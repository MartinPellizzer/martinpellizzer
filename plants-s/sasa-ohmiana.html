Sasa ohmiana

Sasa ohmiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa ohmiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.

Its rhizomes are particularly notable for their ability to form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
