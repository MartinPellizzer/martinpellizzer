Saxifraga punctata subsp. redowskyana

Saxifraga punctata subsp.

redowskyana, commonly known as the redowsky's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or pinkish margin.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
