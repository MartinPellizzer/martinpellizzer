Scrophularia congesta

Scrophularia congesta, commonly known as the clustered figwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its erect stems are often branched, and it produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.
