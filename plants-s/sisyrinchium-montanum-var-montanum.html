Sisyrinchium montanum var. montanum

Sisyrinchium montanum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
