Simbuleta pedata

Simbuleta pedata, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the ground.

It features long, arching fronds with a distinctive, upright spore-bearing structure called a sorus. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its ability to "walk" through the release of spores and rhizome growth makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color depending on the environment.
