Saussurea grandifolioides

Saussurea grandifolioides, commonly known as the broad-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
