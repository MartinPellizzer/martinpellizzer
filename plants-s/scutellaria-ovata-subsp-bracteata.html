Scutellaria ovata subsp. bracteata

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern North America. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded, rich soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
