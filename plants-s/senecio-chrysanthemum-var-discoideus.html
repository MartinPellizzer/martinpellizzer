Senecio chrysanthemum var. discoideus

Senecio chrysanthemum var. discoideus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Senecio chrysanthemum var. discoideus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
