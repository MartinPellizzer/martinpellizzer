Strigilia punctata

Strigilia punctata, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom in the spring. Strigilia punctata is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.
