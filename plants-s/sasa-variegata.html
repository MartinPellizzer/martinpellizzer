Sasa variegata

Sasa variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a deciduous shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Sasa variegata is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
