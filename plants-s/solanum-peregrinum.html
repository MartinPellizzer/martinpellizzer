Solanum peregrinum

Solanum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red berries resembling tomatoes. Its fruits are edible when ripe but contain toxic alkaloids in their raw state.

Solanum peregrinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of ornamental berries in gardens.
