Sparganium simplex var. gracile

Sparganium simplex var. gracile, commonly known as slender bur-reed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, bur-like fruits. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in wetland environments.

Sparganium simplex var. gracile plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
