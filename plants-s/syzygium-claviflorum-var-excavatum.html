Syzygium claviflorum var. excavatum

Syzygium claviflorum var. excavatum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, showy flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
