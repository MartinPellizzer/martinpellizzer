Schlegelia organensis

Schlegelia organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Schlegelia organensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
