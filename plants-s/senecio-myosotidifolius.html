Senecio myosotidifolius

Senecio myosotidifolius, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Senecio myosotidifolius is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
