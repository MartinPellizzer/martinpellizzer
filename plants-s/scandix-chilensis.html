Scandix chilensis

Scandix chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its fragrant leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
