Smilax bauhinioides

Smilax bauhinioides, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in branched cymes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Smilax bauhinioides is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories.
