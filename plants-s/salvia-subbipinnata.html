Salvia subbipinnata

Salvia subbipinnata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that resemble mist. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
