Sessilibulbum

Sessilibulbum, commonly known as the sessile bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with flowers that are often sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. Its bulbs serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

Sessilibulbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
