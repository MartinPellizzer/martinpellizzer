Scirpus setifolius

Scirpus setifolius, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpus setifolius thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
