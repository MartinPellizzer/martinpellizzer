Staurogyne capillipes

Staurogyne capillipes, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has thin, thread-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its reproductive strategy. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Staurogyne capillipes is often used in water gardens and pond ecosystems due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
