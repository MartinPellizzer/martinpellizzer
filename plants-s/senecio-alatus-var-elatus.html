Senecio alatus var. elatus

Senecio alatus var. elatus, commonly known as the Great Spurflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
