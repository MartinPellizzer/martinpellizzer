Senecio comarapensis

Senecio comarapensis, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Senecio comarapensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
