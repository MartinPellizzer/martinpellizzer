Schinus polygama f. crenata

Schinus polygama f. crenata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or cipó-do-paraíba, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with rounded or slightly toothed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Schinus polygama f. crenata is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
