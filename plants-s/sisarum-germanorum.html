Sisarum germanorum

Sisarum germanorum, commonly known as the German salsify, is a biennial plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by long, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Sisarum germanorum is also known for its edible roots, which have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.
