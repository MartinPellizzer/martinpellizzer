Strychnos moandaensis

Strychnos moandaensis, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

The plant belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is known for its compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.
