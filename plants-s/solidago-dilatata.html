Solidago dilatata

Solidago dilatata, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, disk-like flowers surrounded by bracts.

Solidago dilatata plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
