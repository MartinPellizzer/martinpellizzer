Stellaria pubera subsp. homotricha

Stellaria pubera subsp.

homotricha, commonly known as hairy starwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, white or pale blue flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
