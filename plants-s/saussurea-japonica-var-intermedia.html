Saussurea japonica var. intermedia

Saussurea japonica var. intermedia, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a tall, branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
