Spiranthes × simpsonii

Spiranthes × simpsonii, commonly known as the Simpson's spike-rush, is a hybrid orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wetlands and forest floors. It blooms in late summer to early fall, producing a delicate, fragrant floral display.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
