Solanum ipomoea

Solanum ipomoea, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Solanum ipomoea is cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
