Saussurea maximowiczii

Saussurea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Mongolian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a single terminal head. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
