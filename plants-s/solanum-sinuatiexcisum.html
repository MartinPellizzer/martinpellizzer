Solanum sinuatiexcisum

Solanum sinuatiexcisum, commonly known as the sinuately lobed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves with deeply sinuate and excised margins, giving it a distinctive, intricate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
