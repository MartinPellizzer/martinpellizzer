Solanum schuchii

Solanum schuchii, commonly known as the Schuch's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

Its foliage and berries contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
