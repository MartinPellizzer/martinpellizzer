Stachys ciliata

Stachys ciliata, commonly known as velvetleaf betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a fuzzy underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stachys ciliata is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
