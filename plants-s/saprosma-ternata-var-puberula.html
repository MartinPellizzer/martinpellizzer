Saprosma ternata var. puberula

Saprosma ternata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy saprosma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
