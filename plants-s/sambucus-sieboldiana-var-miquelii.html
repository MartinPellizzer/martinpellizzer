Sambucus sieboldiana var. miquelii

Sambucus sieboldiana var. miquelii, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
