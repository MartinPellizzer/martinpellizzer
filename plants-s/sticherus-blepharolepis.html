Sticherus blepharolepis

Sticherus blepharolepis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a shimmering appearance. Native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
