Senecio oxyriifolius subsp. tropaeolifolius

Senecio oxyriifolius subsp.

tropaeolifolius, commonly known as the "tansy-leaved groundsel," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the genus Senecio.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
