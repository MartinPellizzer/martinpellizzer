Scutellaria subcaespitosa

Scutellaria subcaespitosa, commonly known as the tufted skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
