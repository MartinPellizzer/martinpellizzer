Salvia verbenaca var. bicolor

Salvia verbenaca var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature purple upper petals and white lower petals. The plant produces aromatic leaves with a slight minty scent, making it popular in herbal gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its visual appeal and fragrance.
