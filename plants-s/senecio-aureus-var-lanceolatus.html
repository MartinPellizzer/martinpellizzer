Senecio aureus var. lanceolatus

Senecio aureus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, hence the variety name, and are covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
