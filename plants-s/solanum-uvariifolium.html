Solanum uvariifolium

Solanum uvariifolium, commonly known as the warty nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its berries are usually red or orange when ripe and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important species.
