Solidago arguta var. strigosa

Solidago arguta var. strigosa, commonly known as the stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, stiff stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
