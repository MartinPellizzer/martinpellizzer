Salvia × velutina

Salvia × velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Salvia × velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
