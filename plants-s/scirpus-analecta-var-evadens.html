Scirpus analecta var. evadens

Scirpus analecta var. evadens, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it thrive in moist or aquatic environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
