Specklinia simmleriana

Specklinia simmleriana, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
