Scleria melanotricha var. grata

Scleria melanotricha var. grata, commonly known as the purple spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with damp conditions.
