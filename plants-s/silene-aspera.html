Silene aspera

Silene aspera, commonly known as rough silene, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Silene aspera produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
