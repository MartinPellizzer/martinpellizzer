Seriphidium thomsonianum

Seriphidium thomsonianum, commonly known as the Thomson's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, with a strong, pungent odor.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
