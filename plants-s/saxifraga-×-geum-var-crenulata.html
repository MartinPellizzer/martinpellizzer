Saxifraga × geum var. crenulata

Saxifraga × geum var. crenulata, commonly known as the rounded-leaved saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically rounded and toothed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add color to shaded landscapes.
