Sesamum radiatum

Sesamum radiatum, commonly known as the radiate sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sesamum radiatum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
