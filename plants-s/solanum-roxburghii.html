Solanum roxburghii

Solanum roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian bell pepper or wild tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale green, with five lobes, and the fruit develops in clusters.
