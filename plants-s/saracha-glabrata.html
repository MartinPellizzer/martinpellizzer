Saracha glabrata

Saracha glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf saracha, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Saracha glabrata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Apiaceae family.
