Salvia caduca

Salvia caduca, commonly known as the annual sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia caduca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
