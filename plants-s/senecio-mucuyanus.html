Senecio mucuyanus

Senecio mucuyanus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid climates.

Senecio mucuyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
