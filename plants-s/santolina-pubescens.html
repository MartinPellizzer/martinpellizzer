Santolina pubescens

Santolina pubescens, commonly known as silver woolly lavender, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina pubescens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
