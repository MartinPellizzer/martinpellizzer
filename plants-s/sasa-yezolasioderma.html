Sasa yezolasioderma

Sasa yezolasioderma, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight curl at the edges.

Sasa yezolasioderma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
