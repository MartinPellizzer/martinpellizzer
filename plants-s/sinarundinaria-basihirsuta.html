Sinarundinaria basihirsuta

Sinarundinaria basihirsuta, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, robust fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and colonize rocky or exposed areas. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, serrated leaflets.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
