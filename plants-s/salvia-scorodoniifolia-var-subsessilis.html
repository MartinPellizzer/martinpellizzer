Salvia scorodoniifolia var. subsessilis

Salvia scorodoniifolia var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal practices.

The botanical features of this variety make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
