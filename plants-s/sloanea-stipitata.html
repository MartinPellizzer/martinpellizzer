Sloanea stipitata

Sloanea stipitata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sloanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Sloanea stipitata is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
