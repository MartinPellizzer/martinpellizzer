Senecio benaventianus

Senecio benaventianus, commonly known as the Benaventian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in composite heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
