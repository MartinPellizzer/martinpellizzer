Stapelia hirsuta var. unguipetala

Stapelia hirsuta var. unguipetala, commonly known as the hairy stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
