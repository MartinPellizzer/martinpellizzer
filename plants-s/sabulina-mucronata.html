Sabulina mucronata

Sabulina mucronata, commonly known as the sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Sabulina mucronata thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.
