Salvia maximowicziana

Salvia maximowicziana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
