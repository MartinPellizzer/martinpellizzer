Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. schottii

Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. schottii, commonly known as scarlet grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright red to orange panicles that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
