Solanum acanthocarpum

Solanum acanthocarpum, commonly known as the spiny fruit morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Solanum acanthocarpum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
