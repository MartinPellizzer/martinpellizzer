Sterculia bidwillii

Sterculia bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's sterculia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are large, ovate, and glossy green. It produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
