Solanum rybinii f. cuencanum

Solanum rybinii f. cuencanum, commonly known as the Cuencanum nightshade, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
