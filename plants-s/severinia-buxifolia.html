Severinia buxifolia

Severinia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the fall. Its foliage is dense and evergreen in some climates, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
