Sasa kobemontana

Sasa kobemontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa kobemontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and traditional Japanese garden design.
