Solanum infundibuliforme

Solanum infundibuliforme, commonly known as the bell-shaped nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
