Senecio patersonianus

Senecio patersonianus, commonly known as the Paterson's weed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
