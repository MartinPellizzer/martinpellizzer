Senecio pollicaris

Senecio pollicaris, commonly known as the "Thimbleweed," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Senecio pollicaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
