Spiranthes × australis

Spiranthes × australis, commonly known as the Australian ladies' tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, spiraled inflorescence of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It has strap-like leaves and a rhizomatous root system, which helps it thrive in wet conditions.

Spiranthes × australis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
