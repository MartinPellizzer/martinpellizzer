Silene patagonica

Silene patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian campion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Silene patagonica has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit type.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
