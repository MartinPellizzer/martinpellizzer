Sida gracilis

Sida gracilis, commonly known as slender sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are pale blue to white and are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators.

Sida gracilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
