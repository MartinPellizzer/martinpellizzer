Sesleria flexuosa

Sesleria flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive zigzagging inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Sesleria flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
