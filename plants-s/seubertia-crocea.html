Seubertia crocea

Seubertia crocea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
