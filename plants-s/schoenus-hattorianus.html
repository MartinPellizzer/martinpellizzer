Schoenus hattorianus

Schoenus hattorianus, commonly known as the Hattori grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in grassland and wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
