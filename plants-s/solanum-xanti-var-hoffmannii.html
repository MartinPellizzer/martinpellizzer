Solanum xanti var. hoffmannii

Solanum xanti var. hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.
