Saguerus rumphii

Saguerus rumphii, commonly known as the Rumphii Sago Palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The trunk is composed of a fibrous material similar to sago, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
