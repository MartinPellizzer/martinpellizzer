Statice anceps

Statice anceps, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments and coastal meadows. Its salt tolerance makes it a valuable species for stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Statice anceps is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
