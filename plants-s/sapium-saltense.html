Sapium saltense

Sapium saltense, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It has compound leaves with three to seven ovate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The tree is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with a plume-like structure.

The plant has been introduced to other parts of the world and is sometimes considered an invasive species.
