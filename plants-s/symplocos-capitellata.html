Symplocos capitellata

Symplocos capitellata, commonly known as the clustered snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Symplocos capitellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
