Setulocarya diffusa

Setulocarya diffusa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
