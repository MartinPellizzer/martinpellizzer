Sorbus barthae

Sorbus barthae, commonly known as the Barth's whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Sorbus barthae is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
