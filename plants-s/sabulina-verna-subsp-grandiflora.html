Sabulina verna subsp. grandiflora

Sabulina verna subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the spring sandwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
