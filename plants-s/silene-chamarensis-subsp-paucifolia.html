Silene chamarensis subsp. paucifolia

Silene chamarensis subsp.

paucifolia, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent stems. Silene chamarensis subsp.

paucifolia is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and coastal conditions.
