Sorbus aria f. matrensis

Sorbus aria f. matrensis, commonly known as the Matrenskaya rowan, is a rare and ornamental variant of the rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red fruits that persist through the winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant is native to the Ural Mountains and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This form is highly valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
