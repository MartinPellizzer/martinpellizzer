Solms-laubachia carnosifolia

Solms-laubachia carnosifolia, commonly known as the fleshy-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often hairy leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
