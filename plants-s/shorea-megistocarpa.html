Shorea megistocarpa

Shorea megistocarpa, commonly known as the giant ironwood, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and thick, leathery leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
