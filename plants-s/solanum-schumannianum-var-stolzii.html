Solanum schumannianum var. stolzii

Solanum schumannianum var. stolzii, commonly known as the Schumann's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
