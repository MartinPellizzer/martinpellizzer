Sorbus apicidens

Sorbus apicidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange-red in color, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
