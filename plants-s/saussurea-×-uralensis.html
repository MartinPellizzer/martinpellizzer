Saussurea × uralensis

Saussurea × uralensis, commonly known as the Uralian safflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust stem and large, feathery leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

Saussurea × uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
