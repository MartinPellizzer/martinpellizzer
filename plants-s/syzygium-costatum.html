Syzygium costatum

Syzygium costatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, ridged bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium costatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
