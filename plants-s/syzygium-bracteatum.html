Syzygium bracteatum

Syzygium bracteatum, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth bark, opposite leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Syzygium bracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
