Saussurea pseudoleontodon

Saussurea pseudoleontodon, commonly known as the Himalayan arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
