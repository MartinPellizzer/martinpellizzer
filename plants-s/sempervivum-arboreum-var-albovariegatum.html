Sempervivum arboreum var. albovariegatum

Sempervivum arboreum var. albovariegatum, commonly known as the White Variegated Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils and requires minimal water. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

This variety is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
