Senecio bergianus

Senecio bergianus, commonly known as the Bergian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio bergianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
