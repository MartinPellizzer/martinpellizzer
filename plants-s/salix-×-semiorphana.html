Salix × semiorphana

Salix × semiorphana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits a range of morphological characteristics.

It typically has slender, zigzagging branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it blooms in early spring. Salix × semiorphana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
