Sophora velutina var. multifoliolata

Sophora velutina var. multifoliolata, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in racemes, followed by legume pods containing dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
