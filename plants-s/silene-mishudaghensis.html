Silene mishudaghensis

Silene mishudaghensis, commonly known as the Mishudagh catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
