Stachyurus obovatus

Stachyurus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features attractive, showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
