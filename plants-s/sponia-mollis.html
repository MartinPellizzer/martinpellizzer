Sponia mollis

Sponia mollis, commonly known as the soft sponge plant, is a unique species characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that resemble a sponge.

It belongs to the family Sponiaceae and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant's leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Sponia mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
