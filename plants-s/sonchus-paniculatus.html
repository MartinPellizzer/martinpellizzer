Sonchus paniculatus

Sonchus paniculatus, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces a tall, branched stem topped with a composite flower head. The flowers are typically yellow and are surrounded by a series of green bracts. It is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and fields, and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
