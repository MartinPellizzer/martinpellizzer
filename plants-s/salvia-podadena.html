Salvia podadena

Salvia podadena, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Salvia podadena is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
