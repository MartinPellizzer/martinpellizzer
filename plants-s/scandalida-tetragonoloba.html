Scandalida tetragonoloba

Scandalida tetragonoloba, commonly known as the four-angled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, often containing toxic compounds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
