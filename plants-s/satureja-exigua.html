Satureja exigua

Satureja exigua, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a strong, minty aroma, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine and culinary uses.

Satureja exigua thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
