Suaeda glauca

Suaeda glauca, commonly known as saltbush or desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, dry climates. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access underground water sources.

Suaeda glauca is also valued for its nutritional content, as its young leaves are edible and rich in minerals.
