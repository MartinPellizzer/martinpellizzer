Stelis hymenantha

Stelis hymenantha, commonly known as the lacey stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, velvety hairs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive, hooded lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses and botanical gardens.
