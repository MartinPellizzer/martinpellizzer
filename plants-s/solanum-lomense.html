Solanum lomense

Solanum lomense, commonly known as the Lomense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
