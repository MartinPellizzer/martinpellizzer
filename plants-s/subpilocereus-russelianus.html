Subpilocereus russelianus

Subpilocereus russelianus, commonly known as the red-caped night-blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a shrub-like cactus with short, cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of pink or red. Its flowers are tubular and have a strong, sweet scent to attract pollinators such as moths.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
