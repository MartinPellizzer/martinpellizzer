Semele maderensis

Semele maderensis, commonly known as the Madeiran sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Semele maderensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its adaptability to humid, temperate climates.
