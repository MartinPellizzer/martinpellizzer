Sapium yutajense

Sapium yutajense, commonly known as the Brazilian sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
