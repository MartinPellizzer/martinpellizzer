Stachys rugosa var. longiflora

Stachys rugosa var. longiflora, commonly known as hairy betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Stachys rugosa var. longiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be found in wildflower meadows.
