Satureja graeca subsp. longiflora

Satureja graeca subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong aroma and medicinal properties.
