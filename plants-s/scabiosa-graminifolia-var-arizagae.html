Scabiosa graminifolia var. arizagae

Scabiosa graminifolia var. arizagae, commonly known as Arizona grass scabious, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small, showy blooms that attract pollinators.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
