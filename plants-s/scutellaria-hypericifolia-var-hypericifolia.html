Scutellaria hypericifolia var. hypericifolia

Scutellaria hypericifolia var. hypericifolia, commonly known as blue beakviolet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable plant for native gardens.
