Salsola roborowskii

Salsola roborowskii, commonly known as Russian thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is adapted to withstand extreme drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
