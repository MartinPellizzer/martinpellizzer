Symbolanthus rosmarinifolius

Symbolanthus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy due to their calming properties.

Symbolanthus rosmarinifolius is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
