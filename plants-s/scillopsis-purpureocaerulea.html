Scillopsis purpureocaerulea

Scillopsis purpureocaerulea, commonly known as the purple-blue scillopsis, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its unique coloration and elegant form, it is valued in horticulture for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
