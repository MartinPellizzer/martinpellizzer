Saxifraga lolaensis

Saxifraga lolaensis, commonly known as the Lolaensis saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually small, clustered in inflorescences, and come in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Saxifraga lolaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
