Spergula levis

Spergula levis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
