Senecio elongatus

Senecio elongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features linear, narrow leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio elongatus is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
