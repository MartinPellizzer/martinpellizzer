Saxifraga rivularis f. purpurascens

Saxifraga rivularis f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed water saxifrage, is a compact perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically a deep purple or reddish hue, distinguishing it from the typical green foliage of the species. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
