Salvia ophiocephala

Salvia ophiocephala, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Salvia ophiocephala is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
