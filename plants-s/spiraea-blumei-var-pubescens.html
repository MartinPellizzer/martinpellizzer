Spiraea blumei var. pubescens

Spiraea blumei var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and profuse display of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
