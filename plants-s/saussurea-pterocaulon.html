Saussurea pterocaulon

Saussurea pterocaulon, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant's flowers are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental qualities.
