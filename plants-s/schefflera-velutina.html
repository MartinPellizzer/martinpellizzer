Schefflera velutina

Schefflera velutina, commonly known as the velvet schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered leaves that are dark green and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
