Solanum pendulum

Solanum pendulum, commonly known as the weeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with pendulous clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually white or pale purple and bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing toxic seeds.

It is often found in moist, shady areas and is part of the nightshade family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
