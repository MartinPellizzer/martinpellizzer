Statice ciliata

Statice ciliata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Statice ciliata is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower.
