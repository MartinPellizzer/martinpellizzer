Serratula caspia

Serratula caspia, commonly known as Caspian serrata, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has basal leaves that are serrated and often covered in fine hairs.

Serratula caspia is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its safety and efficacy are not well established.
