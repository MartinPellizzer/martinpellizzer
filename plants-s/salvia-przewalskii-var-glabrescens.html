Salvia przewalskii var. glabrescens

Salvia przewalskii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Przewalski's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
