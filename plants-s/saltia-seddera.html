Saltia seddera

Saltia seddera, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered together. Saltia seddera is well adapted to salty and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing degraded land.

Its resilience to drought and high salinity makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
