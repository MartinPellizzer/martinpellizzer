Sempervivum tatrense

Sempervivum tatrense, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and can survive in poor soil conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum tatrense is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
