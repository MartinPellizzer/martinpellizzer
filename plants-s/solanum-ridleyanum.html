Solanum ridleyanum

Solanum ridleyanum, commonly known as the Ridley's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while the fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum ridleyanum is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
