Sinarundinaria sparsiflora

Sinarundinaria sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
