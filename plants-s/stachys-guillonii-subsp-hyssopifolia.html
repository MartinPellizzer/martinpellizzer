Stachys guillonii subsp. hyssopifolia

Stachys guillonii subsp.

hyssopifolia, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae). It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. It produces small, nutlet-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. Stachys guillonii subsp.

hyssopifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
