Solanum pimpinelloides

Solanum pimpinelloides, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, featuring small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
