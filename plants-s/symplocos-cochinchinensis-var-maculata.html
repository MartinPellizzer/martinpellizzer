Symplocos cochinchinensis var. maculata

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often adorned with red or purple spots. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
