Sciaphila atroviolacea

Sciaphila atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to blackish petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in low light conditions. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Sciaphila atroviolacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
