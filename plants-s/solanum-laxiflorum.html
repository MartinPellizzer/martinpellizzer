Solanum laxiflorum

Solanum laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it bears berries that mature to a yellow or orange color. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
