Silene physalodes

Silene physalodes, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 60 to 100 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Silene physalodes is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
