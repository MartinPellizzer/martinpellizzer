Shorea penicillata

Shorea penicillata, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a pale yellow to white color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
