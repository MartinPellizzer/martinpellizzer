Scolymus theophrasti

Scolymus theophrasti, commonly known as common wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its slender stems and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.

Scolymus theophrasti is also known for its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
