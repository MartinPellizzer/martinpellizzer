Salix paraplesia var. subintegra

Salix paraplesia var. subintegra, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams and in mountainous regions. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-elevation habitats.
