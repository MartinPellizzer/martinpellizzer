Saxifraga gemmipara var. siamensis

Saxifraga gemmipara var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its ability to propagate through vegetative means, such as gemmae, which are tiny plantlets that can detach and grow independently.

This variety is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
