Senecio delfinii

Senecio delfinii, commonly known as the skyblue daisy or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Senecio delfinii is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
