Saxifraga Nelsoniana Subsp. Pacifica

Saxifraga nelsoniana subsp. pacifica is a rare and delicate plant native to the coastal regions of Oregon and Washington. It thrives in moist, shaded environments along rocky shores and tidal zones. This subspecies is known for its small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its unique adaptations allow it to survive in harsh, salt-laden conditions. Due to its limited range and habitat fragility, it is considered a vulnerable species.
