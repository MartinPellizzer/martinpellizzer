Sedum ettyuense

Sedum ettyuense, commonly known as the Ettyuense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
