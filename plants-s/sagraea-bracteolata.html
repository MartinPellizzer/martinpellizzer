Sagraea bracteolata

Sagraea bracteolata, commonly known as the golden bell or golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Sagraea bracteolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adaptable to well-drained soils.
