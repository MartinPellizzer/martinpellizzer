Sigmatostalix hymenantha

Sigmatostalix hymenantha, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarack, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its seeds are enclosed in small, woody capsules that split open to release the seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and ecological role in its native habitat.
