Scirpus hokkaidoensis

Scirpus hokkaidoensis, commonly known as Hokkaido bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, fibrous foliage. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus hokkaidoensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
