Silene koreana

Silene koreana, commonly known as Korean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
