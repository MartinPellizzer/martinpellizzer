Stanhopea grandiflora odorata

Stanhopea grandiflora odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, with a distinctive fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and yellow, with a prominent lip that aids in pollination.

Stanhopea grandiflora odorata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and visual appeal.
