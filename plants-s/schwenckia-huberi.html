Schwenckia huberi

Schwenckia huberi, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a short, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Schwenckia huberi is known for its rapid blooming cycle, often producing flowers in the early morning and closing them by midday.
