Solenanthus circinnatus

Solenanthus circinnatus, commonly known as the spiral herb, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
