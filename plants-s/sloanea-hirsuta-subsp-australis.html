Sloanea hirsuta subsp. australis

Sloanea hirsuta subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian hirsute sloanea, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats. Sloanea hirsuta subsp.

australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
