Secale cereale var. brunneum

Secale cereale var. brunneum, commonly known as red rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its reddish grain color.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are used for flour production and have a distinct, slightly bitter flavor compared to common rye.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
