Senecio thunbergianus

Senecio thunbergianus, commonly known as the blueweed or Thunberg's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking floral display.
