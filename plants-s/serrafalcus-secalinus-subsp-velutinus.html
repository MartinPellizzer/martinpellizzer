Serrafalcus secalinus subsp. velutinus

Serrafalcus secalinus subsp.

velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. Its flowering spike is usually short and bears small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
