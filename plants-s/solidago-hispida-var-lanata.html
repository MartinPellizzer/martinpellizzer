Solidago hispida var. lanata

Solidago hispida var. lanata, commonly known as the wooly goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along streambanks. Its fluffy appearance and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
