Satureja hyssopifolia

Satureja hyssopifolia, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctively sharp and peppery scent. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
