Senecio erici-rosenii var. alticola

Senecio erici-rosenii var. alticola, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in harsh, arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
