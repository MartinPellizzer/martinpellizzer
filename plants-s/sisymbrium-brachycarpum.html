Sisymbrium brachycarpum

Sisymbrium brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrowpod wild radish, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some regions but also has potential as a forage plant.
