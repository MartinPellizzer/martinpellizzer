Schefflera heterophylla var. biternata

Schefflera heterophylla var. biternata, commonly known as the Three-lobed Umbrella Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like foliage composed of three to five lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability to various growing conditions contribute to its ornamental appeal.
