Senecio gilliesii var. dasycarpus

Senecio gilliesii var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
