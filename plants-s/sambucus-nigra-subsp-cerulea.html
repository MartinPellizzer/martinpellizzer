Sambucus nigra subsp. cerulea

Sambucus nigra subsp.

cerulea, commonly known as blue elderberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-black berries that ripen in the fall.

The plant produces compound leaves with a serrated margin and emits a mild, sweet scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for wildlife.
