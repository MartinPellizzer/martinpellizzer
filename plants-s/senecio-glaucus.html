Senecio glaucus

Senecio glaucus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and desert margins.

Senecio glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
