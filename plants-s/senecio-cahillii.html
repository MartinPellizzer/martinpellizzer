Senecio cahillii

Senecio cahillii, commonly known as Cahill's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a grayish or blue-green hue.

Senecio cahillii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
