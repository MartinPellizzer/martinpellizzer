Solanum andigenum f. ancacpomacanchicum

Solanum andigenum f. ancacpomacanchicum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a botanical variant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is adapted to cool, mountainous climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
