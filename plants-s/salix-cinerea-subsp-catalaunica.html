Salix cinerea subsp. catalaunica

Salix cinerea subsp.

catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica willow, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and grayish-barked stems that exude a sticky sap.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture on both sides. It thrives in moist, often wetland habitats, such as streambanks and marshes.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
