Syzygium elephantinum

Syzygium elephantinum, commonly known as the elephant apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, waxy skin and a soft, edible pulp containing numerous seeds. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in its native regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
