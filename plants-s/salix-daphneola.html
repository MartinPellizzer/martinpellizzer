Salix daphneola

Salix daphneola, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender branches and catkin-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Salix daphneola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
