Solidago anisata

Solidago anisata, commonly known as the anise goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinct aromatic scent.

The flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
