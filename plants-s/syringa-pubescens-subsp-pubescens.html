Syringa pubescens subsp. pubescens

Syringa pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate with a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
