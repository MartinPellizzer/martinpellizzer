Salvia jaliscana

Salvia jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a horticultural and ecological asset.
