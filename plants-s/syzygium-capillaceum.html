Syzygium capillaceum

Syzygium capillaceum, commonly known as the silky apple or kapok tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Syzygium capillaceum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of fiber.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
