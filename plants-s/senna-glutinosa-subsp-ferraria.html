Senna glutinosa subsp. ferraria

Senna glutinosa subsp.

ferraria, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
