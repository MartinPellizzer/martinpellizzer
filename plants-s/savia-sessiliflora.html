Savia sessiliflora

Savia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Savia sessiliflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
