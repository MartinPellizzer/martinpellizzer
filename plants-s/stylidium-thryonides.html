Stylidium thryonides

Stylidium thryonides, commonly known as the purple-stemmed sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, erect stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, fleshy leaves and has a unique structure where the flower stem elongates after flowering.

Stylidium thryonides is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
