Stipa uruguaycola

Stipa uruguaycola, commonly known as the Uruguay needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
