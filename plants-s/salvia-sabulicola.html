Salvia sabulicola

Salvia sabulicola, commonly known as sand sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Salvia sabulicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
