Salsola ugabica

Salsola ugabica, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, with a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Salsola ugabica is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
