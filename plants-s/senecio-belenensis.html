Senecio belenensis

Senecio belenensis, commonly known as the Bolivian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio belenensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
