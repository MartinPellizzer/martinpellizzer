Solidago retrorsa

Solidago retrorsa, commonly known as the reverseface goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall, consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by yellow to orange ray florets.
