Silene tomentosa var. arundana

Silene tomentosa var. arundana, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Silene tomentosa var. arundana is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
