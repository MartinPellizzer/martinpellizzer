Sorbus futakiana

Sorbus futakiana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
