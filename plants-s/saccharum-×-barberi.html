Saccharum × barberi

Saccharum × barberi, commonly known as hybrid sugarcane.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Saccharum spontaneum and Saccharum officinarum. This plant is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for commercial sugarcane production. It exhibits vigorous growth and is often used in breeding programs to improve cane yield and disease resistance.

The botanical features of Saccharum × barberi include its fibrous root system and the presence of specialized cells for sugar storage.
