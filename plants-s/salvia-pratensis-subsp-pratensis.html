Salvia pratensis subsp. pratensis

Salvia pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as meadow sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and showy spikes of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Salvia pratensis subsp.

pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
