Syzygium griffithii

Syzygium griffithii, commonly known as the white clydesdale or white lily of the valley, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in early spring. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
