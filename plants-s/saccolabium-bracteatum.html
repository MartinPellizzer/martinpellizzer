Saccolabium bracteatum

Saccolabium bracteatum, commonly known as the hairy bract saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its adaptations to dry environments include deep root systems and the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria.

Saccolabium bracteatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
