Silene excedens

Silene excedens, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Silene excedens thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

It plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and flies.
