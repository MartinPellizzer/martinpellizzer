Salvia arida

Salvia arida, commonly known as the Brazilian sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Salvia arida is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Brazilian medicine.
