Senecio fresenii

Senecio fresenii, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with silvery-green, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio fresenii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
