Sideritis ambigua

Sideritis ambigua, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, hairy leaves. It produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months.

Sideritis ambigua is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
