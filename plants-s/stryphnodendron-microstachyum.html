Stryphnodendron microstachyum

Stryphnodendron microstachyum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small leaflets, and the flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, elongated seeds.

It is valued for its hard, dark wood, which has been historically used in musical instruments and furniture.
