Salix phylicifolia var. hibernica

Salix phylicifolia var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish willow, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of willow species, and it produces small, winged seeds for dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
