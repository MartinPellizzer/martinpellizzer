Sorbus × uzenensis

Sorbus × uzenensis, commonly known as the Uzen whitebeam, is a hybrid species of the whitebeam genus Sorbus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and greyish in color.

The Uzen whitebeam is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruiting characteristics.
