Schizachyrium sanguineum

Schizachyrium sanguineum, commonly known as blood grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves that turn coppery in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or brown in color.

Blood grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
