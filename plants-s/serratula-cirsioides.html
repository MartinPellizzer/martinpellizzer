Serratula cirsioides

Serratula cirsioides, commonly known as yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Serratula cirsioides is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
