Swertia quadricornis

Swertia quadricornis, commonly known as the four-horned gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. It has a compact growth habit with four prominent bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name.

Swertia quadricornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
