Saxifraga corsica var. cossoniana

Saxifraga corsica var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Corsican saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, white or pinkish flowers bloom in clusters during the spring or early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a hardy ground cover.
