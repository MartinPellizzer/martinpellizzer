Salix depressa subsp. rostrata

Salix depressa subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and flexible, often drooping branches.

The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and along streambanks.
