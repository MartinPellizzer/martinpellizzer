Senecio ionophyllus var. stygius

Senecio ionophyllus var. stygius, commonly known as the Stygian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
