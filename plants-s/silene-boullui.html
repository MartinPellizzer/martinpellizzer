Silene boullui

Silene boullui, commonly known as the French catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals that are often reflexed.

Silene boullui is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
