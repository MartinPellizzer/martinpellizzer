Steveniella satyrioides f. longibracteata

Steveniella satyrioides f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract Steveniella, is a unique variant of the Steveniella genus native to specific regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its elongated bracts, which are modified leaves that often resemble floral structures, adding to its ornamental appeal. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. The inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
