Syzygium afromontanum

Syzygium afromontanum, commonly known as the African star fruit or *Carambola*, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. Syzygium afromontanum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its fruit is widely cultivated for its tart flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
