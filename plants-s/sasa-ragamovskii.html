Sasa ragamovskii

Sasa ragamovskii, commonly known as black bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often covered with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa ragamovskii is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
