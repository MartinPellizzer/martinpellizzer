Spilanthes grisea var. intermedia

Spilanthes grisea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate yellow root, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. The plant features a stout, hairy stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
