Schefflera planchoniana

Schefflera planchoniana, commonly known as the Madagascar umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Schefflera planchoniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
