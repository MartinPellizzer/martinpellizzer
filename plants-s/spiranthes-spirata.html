Spiranthes spirata

Spiranthes spirata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette at the base of the plant.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
