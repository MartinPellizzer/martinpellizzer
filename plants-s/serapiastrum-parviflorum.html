Serapiastrum parviflorum

Serapiastrum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered serapiastrum, is a member of the Ericaceae family, typically found in moist, acidic soils of temperate regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
