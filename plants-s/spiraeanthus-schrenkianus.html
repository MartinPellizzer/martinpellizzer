Spiraeanthus schrenkianus

Spiraeanthus schrenkianus, commonly known as the Schrenk's spirea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a grayish or silver underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
