Salvia dorrii subsp. mearnsii

Salvia dorrii subsp.

mearnsii, commonly known as Mearns' sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
