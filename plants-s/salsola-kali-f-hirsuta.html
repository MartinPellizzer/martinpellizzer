Salsola kali f. hirsuta

Salsola kali f. hirsuta, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline or coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny, sprawling growth habit and small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are typically succulent and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.
