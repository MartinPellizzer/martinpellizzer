Sinapis pubescens var. circinata

Sinapis pubescens var. circinata, commonly known as the curly mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are small and oval, typically containing compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
