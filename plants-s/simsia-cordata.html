Simsia cordata

Simsia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces spiny, bracted inflorescences that bear small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Simsia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
