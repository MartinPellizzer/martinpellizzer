Solidago graminifolia var. media

Solidago graminifolia var. media, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are alternate along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Solidago graminifolia var. media is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
