Solanum minutiflorum

Solanum minutiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, branched stems and small, egg-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature, often containing several seeds.

Solanum minutiflorum is part of the nightshade family, which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
