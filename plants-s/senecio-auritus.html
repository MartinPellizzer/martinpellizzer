Senecio auritus

Senecio auritus, commonly known as the ear-shaped groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Senecio auritus is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
