Sagina inconspicua

Sagina inconspicua, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of tiny, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Sagina inconspicua is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh, well-drained conditions.
