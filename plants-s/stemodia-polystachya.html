Stemodia polystachya

Stemodia polystachya, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
