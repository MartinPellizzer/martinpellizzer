Strumaria spiralis

Strumaria spiralis, commonly known as the spiral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Strumaria spiralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
