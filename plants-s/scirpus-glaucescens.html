Scirpus glaucescens

Scirpus glaucescens, commonly known as blue stem bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features tall, slender, blue-green stems that are often hollow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus glaucescens plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
