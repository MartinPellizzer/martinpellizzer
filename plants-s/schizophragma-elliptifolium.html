Schizophragma elliptifolium

Schizophragma elliptifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which give it its common name due to their appearance resembling a mist. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Schizophragma elliptifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
