Spinacia oleracea var. gaudinii

Spinacia oleracea var. gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's spinach, is a cultivated variety of spinach characterized by its compact growth habit and early maturity.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its tender, flavorful leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamins. The plant typically thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often grown as a leafy vegetable and can be harvested multiple times during the growing season.

Its botanical features include serrated leaves and a short, sturdy stem, making it suitable for both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
