Saccolabium violaceum var. harrisonianum

Saccolabium violaceum var. harrisonianum, commonly known as the Harrison's bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its showy blooms.
