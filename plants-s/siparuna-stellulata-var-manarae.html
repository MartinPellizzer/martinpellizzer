Siparuna stellulata var. manarae

Siparuna stellulata var. manarae, commonly known as the Manarae beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bright blue to purple berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the colorful fruits.

Siparuna stellulata var. manarae is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
