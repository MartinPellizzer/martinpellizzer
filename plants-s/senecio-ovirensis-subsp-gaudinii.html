Senecio ovirensis subsp. gaudinii

Senecio ovirensis subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
