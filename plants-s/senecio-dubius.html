Senecio dubius

Senecio dubius, commonly known as the blueweed or blue devil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Senecio dubius is known for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
