Satureja kuegleri

Satureja kuegleri, commonly known as Kuegler's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, thyme-like flavor.

Satureja kuegleri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and herb gardens.
