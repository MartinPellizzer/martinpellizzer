Schedonorus mazzettianus

Schedonorus mazzettianus, commonly known as the Italian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil stabilization.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in cultivated fields.
