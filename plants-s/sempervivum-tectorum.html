Sempervivum tectorum

Sempervivum tectorum, commonly known as the roof plant or hen and chicks, is a succulent species native to the Alps and surrounding regions.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a wide range of climates, from temperate to alpine environments.
