Salvia lavandulifolia var. latifolia

Salvia lavandulifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies for its soothing and antiseptic qualities.
