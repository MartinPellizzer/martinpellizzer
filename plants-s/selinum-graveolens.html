Selinum graveolens

Selinum graveolens, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a biennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant is also noted for its strong, aromatic odor and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
