Spiraeanthemum pedunculatum

Spiraeanthemum pedunculatum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
