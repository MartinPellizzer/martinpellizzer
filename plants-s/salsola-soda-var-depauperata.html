Salsola soda var. depauperata

Salsola soda var. depauperata, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in saline soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
