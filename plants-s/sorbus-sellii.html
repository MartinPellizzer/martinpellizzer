Sorbus sellii

Sorbus sellii, commonly known as the Kashmir snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the maturity of the fruit. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly serrated edge.

Sorbus sellii is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
