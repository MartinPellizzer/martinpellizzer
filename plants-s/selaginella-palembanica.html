Selaginella palembanica

Selaginella palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces strobila, which are clusters of sporangia, typically located at the tips of specialized branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
