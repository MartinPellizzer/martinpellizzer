Suaeda novae-zelandiae

Suaeda novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to coastal regions of New Zealand.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves to store water. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss and are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Suaeda novae-zelandiae plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and is an important component of New Zealand's unique flora.
