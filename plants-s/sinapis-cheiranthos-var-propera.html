Sinapis cheiranthos var. propera

Sinapis cheiranthos var. propera, commonly known as the common mustard, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and a flowering stem that bears racemose inflorescences of yellow flowers. The plant produces siliques containing numerous small, triangular seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

The species is known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of oilseed.
