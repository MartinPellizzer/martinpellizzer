Sabina turcomanica

Sabina turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals.

Sabina turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
