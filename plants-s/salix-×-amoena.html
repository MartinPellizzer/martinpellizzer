Salix × amoena

Salix × amoena, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically light green on top and pale white underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

Salix × amoena is often planted for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
