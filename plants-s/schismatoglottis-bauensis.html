Schismatoglottis bauensis

Schismatoglottis bauensis, commonly known as the Bauens Bower Vine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of showy, orchid-like flowers that are typically greenish-white with intricate patterns. The plant features elongated, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Schismatoglottis bauensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
