Syzygium nanum

Syzygium nanum, commonly known as the small apple or dwarf apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red or yellow berries. Syzygium nanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its compact size makes it suitable for small gardens and container planting.
