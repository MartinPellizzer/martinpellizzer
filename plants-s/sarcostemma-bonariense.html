Sarcostemma bonariense

Sarcostemma bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian starvine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiral-shaped fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
