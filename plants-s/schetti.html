Schetti

Schetti, commonly known as the Italian wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions and garlic, and is characterized by its fragrant, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Schetti is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in local cuisines.
