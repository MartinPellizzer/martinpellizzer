Symplocos reginae

Symplocos reginae, commonly known as the royal honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, reddish-brown seeds.

Symplocos reginae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
