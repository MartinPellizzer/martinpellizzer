Smilax beyrichii

Smilax beyrichii, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the genus Smilax, known for its adaptability and ecological significance.
