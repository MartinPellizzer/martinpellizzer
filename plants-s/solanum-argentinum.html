Solanum argentinum

Solanum argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. It is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
