Spodiopogon sibiricus f. purpurascens

Spodiopogon sibiricus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Siberian spikerush, is a variant of the spikerush genus native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems that often bear a reddish tinge, especially in the inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and boreal ecosystems.
