Senecio hoehnei

Senecio hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
