Schoenus filiformis

Schoenus filiformis, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats in Australia and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
