Sophora moorcroftiana var. viciifolia

Sophora moorcroftiana var. viciifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and its striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
