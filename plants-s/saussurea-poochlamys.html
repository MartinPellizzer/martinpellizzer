Saussurea poochlamys

Saussurea poochlamys, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellow to orange ray florets. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and has been used in traditional medicine.
