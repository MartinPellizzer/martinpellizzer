Scleria microdiscus

Scleria microdiscus, commonly known as the small disk sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. Scleria microdiscus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
