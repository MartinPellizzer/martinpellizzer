Scabiosa japonica var. littoralis

Scabiosa japonica var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in salty, windy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
