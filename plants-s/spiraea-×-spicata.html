Spiraea × spicata

Spiraea × spicata, commonly known as weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage turns golden in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Spiraea × spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
