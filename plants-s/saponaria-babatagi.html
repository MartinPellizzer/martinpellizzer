Saponaria babatagi

Saponaria babatagi, commonly known as the Babatagi soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Saponaria babatagi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
