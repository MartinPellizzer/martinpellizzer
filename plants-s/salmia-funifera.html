Salmia funifera

Salmia funifera, commonly known as the hanging salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its drooping inflorescences of tubular flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
