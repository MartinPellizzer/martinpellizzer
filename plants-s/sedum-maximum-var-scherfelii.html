Sedum maximum var. scherfelii

Sedum maximum var. scherfelii, commonly known as the Scherfel's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
