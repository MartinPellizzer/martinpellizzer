Styrax argenteus var. argenteus

Styrax argenteus var. argenteus, commonly known as silver styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a valued addition to landscape design.
