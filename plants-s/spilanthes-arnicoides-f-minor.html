Spilanthes arnicoides f. minor

Spilanthes arnicoides f. minor, commonly known as the lesser Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
