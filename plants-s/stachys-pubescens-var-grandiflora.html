Stachys pubescens var. grandiflora

Stachys pubescens var. grandiflora, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and healing properties.
