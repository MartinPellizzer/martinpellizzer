Sambucus javanica subsp. javanica

Sambucus javanica subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Java elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds that should be avoided.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
