Symplocos pulvinata

Symplocos pulvinata, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos pulvinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
