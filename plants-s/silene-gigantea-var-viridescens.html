Silene gigantea var. viridescens

Silene gigantea var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered catchfly, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting clusters of showy, pinkish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene gigantea var. viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
