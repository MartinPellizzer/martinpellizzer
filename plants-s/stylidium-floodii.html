Stylidium floodii

Stylidium floodii, commonly known as the floodplain devil's-bit, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland or floodplain habitats, adapting to periodic inundation. The plant features a slender, often reddish stem with a single, showy, violet or purple flower that blooms during the wet season. Its roots are specialized for water absorption, allowing it to thrive in saturated soils.

The species is notable for its ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, making it an important indicator of wetland health.
