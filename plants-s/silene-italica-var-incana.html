Silene italica var. incana

Silene italica var. incana, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene italica var. incana is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
