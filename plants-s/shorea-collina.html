Shorea collina

Shorea collina, commonly known as hill teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

Shorea collina plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
