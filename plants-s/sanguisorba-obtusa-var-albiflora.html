Sanguisorba obtusa var. albiflora

Sanguisorba obtusa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower dropwort, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
