Schinus terebinthifolia var. acutifolia

Schinus terebinthifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy fruit.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, impacting local ecosystems.
