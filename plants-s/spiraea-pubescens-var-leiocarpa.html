Spiraea pubescens var. leiocarpa

Spiraea pubescens var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture that gives the plant its species name. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate floral display and easy maintenance.
