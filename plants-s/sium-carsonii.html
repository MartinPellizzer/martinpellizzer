Sium carsonii

Sium carsonii, commonly known as Carson's water parsnip, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils near streams and wetlands.
