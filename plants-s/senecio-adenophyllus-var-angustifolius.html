Senecio adenophyllus var. angustifolius

Senecio adenophyllus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent perennial with a rosette growth habit, characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
