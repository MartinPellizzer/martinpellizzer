Syzygium artense

Syzygium artense, commonly known as the Java apple or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny, hairy pericarp that encases its sweet, edible fruit. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pink and are followed by the unique, hairy fruit that develops over several months.

Syzygium artense is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
