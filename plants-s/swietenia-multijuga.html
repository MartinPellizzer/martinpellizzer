Swietenia multijuga

Swietenia multijuga, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its heartwood is highly valued for its rich reddish-brown color, fine grain, and durability.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List.
