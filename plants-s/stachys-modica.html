Stachys modica

Stachys modica, commonly known as the Sicilian hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that bear small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Stachys modica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
