Solidago subviscosa

Solidago subviscosa, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

The sticky secretions on its leaves are a distinctive feature that helps deter herbivores.
