Simsia caucana

Simsia caucana, commonly known as the Peruvian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble sunflowers, with bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Simsia caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
