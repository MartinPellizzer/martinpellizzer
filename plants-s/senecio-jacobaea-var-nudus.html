Senecio jacobaea var. nudus

Senecio jacobaea var. nudus, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
