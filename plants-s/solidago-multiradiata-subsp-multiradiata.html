Solidago multiradiata subsp. multiradiata

Solidago multiradiata subsp.

multiradiata, commonly known as the multibract yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flowering heads. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
