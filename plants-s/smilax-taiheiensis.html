Smilax taiheiensis

Smilax taiheiensis, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or Japanese smilax, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and thick, tuberous roots. The plant features dark green, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, spadix-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its berries are usually red or black when mature and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax taiheiensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
