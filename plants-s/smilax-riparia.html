Smilax riparia

Smilax riparia, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax riparia is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in moist woodlands.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common sight in these habitats.
