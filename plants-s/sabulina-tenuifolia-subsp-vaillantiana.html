Sabulina tenuifolia subsp. vaillantiana

Sabulina tenuifolia subsp.

vaillantiana, commonly known as the slenderleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically features small, clustered stems that produce delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy environments.
