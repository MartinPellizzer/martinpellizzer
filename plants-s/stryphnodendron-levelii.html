Stryphnodendron levelii

Stryphnodendron levelii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pataua, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
