Sedum celatum f. calcaratum

Sedum celatum f. calcaratum, commonly known as the rock sedum or dwarf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to tolerate drought conditions further enhance its appeal as a decorative and resilient plant.
