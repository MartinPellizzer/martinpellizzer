Schudia chilensis

Schudia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Schudia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
