Salvia pseudoincisa

Salvia pseudoincisa, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
