Saxifraga hypnoides var. robusta

Saxifraga hypnoides var. robusta, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

Saxifraga hypnoides var. robusta is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
