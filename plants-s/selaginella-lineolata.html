Selaginella lineolata

Selaginella lineolata, commonly known as the zigzag spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive, branched stem with small, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern. It produces spores in capsules that are borne on specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella lineolata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
