Sanguisorba applanata var. villosa

Sanguisorba applanata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sanguisorba applanata var. villosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
