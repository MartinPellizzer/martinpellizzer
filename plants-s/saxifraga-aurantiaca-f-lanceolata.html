Saxifraga aurantiaca f. lanceolata

Saxifraga aurantiaca f. lanceolata, commonly known as the orange saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
