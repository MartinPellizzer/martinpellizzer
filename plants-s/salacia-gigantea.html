Salacia gigantea

Salacia gigantea, commonly known as the giant salacia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, providing a unique visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Salacia gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic foliage and unusual fruit.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for large gardens or as a houseplant in warm climates.
