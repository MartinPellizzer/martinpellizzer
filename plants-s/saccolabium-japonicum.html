Saccolabium japonicum

Saccolabium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese burrweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits known as burrs. These burrs are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
