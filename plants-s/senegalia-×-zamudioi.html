Senegalia × zamudioi

Senegalia × zamudioi, commonly known as the Mexican mesquite, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pods that contain edible seeds.

Senegalia × zamudioi is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and role in stabilizing soils in dry regions.
