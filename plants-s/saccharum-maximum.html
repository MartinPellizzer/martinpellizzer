Saccharum maximum

Saccharum maximum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its jointed stems and long, narrow leaves that are often coated in a waxy layer. The plant is cultivated for its high sugar content in the stalks, which are processed into sugar and biofuel. Saccharum maximum thrives in warm climates with abundant sunlight and water.

Its growth cycle involves rapid vegetative development followed by flowering and seed production.
