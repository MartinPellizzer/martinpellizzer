Sporobolus hildebrandtii

Sporobolus hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Sporobolus hildebrandtii is an important component of native plant communities and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
