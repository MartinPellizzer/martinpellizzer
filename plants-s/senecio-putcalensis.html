Senecio putcalensis

Senecio putcalensis, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.
