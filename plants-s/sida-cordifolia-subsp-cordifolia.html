Sida cordifolia subsp. cordifolia

Sida cordifolia subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf sida, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
