Sideritis scordioides var. crispata

Sideritis scordioides var. crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
