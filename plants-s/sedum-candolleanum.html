Sedum candolleanum

Sedum candolleanum, commonly known as the Canary Island sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
