Syzygium albiflorum

Syzygium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and pale in color.

Syzygium albiflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential pharmacological properties.
