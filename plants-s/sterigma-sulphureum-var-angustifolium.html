Sterigma sulphureum var. angustifolium

Sterigma sulphureum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sulphur plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive sulfur-yellow flowers.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color.
