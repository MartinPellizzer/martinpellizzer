Sapium taburu

Sapium taburu, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds.

Sapium taburu is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
