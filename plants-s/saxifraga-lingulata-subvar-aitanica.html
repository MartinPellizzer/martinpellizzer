Saxifraga lingulata subvar. aitanica

Saxifraga lingulata subvar. aitanica, commonly known as the Aitanic saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine zones. The plant features a rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a terminal cyme.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
