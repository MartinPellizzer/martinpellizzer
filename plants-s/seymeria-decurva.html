Seymeria decurva

Seymeria decurva, commonly known as the creeping seymaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Seymeria decurva is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
