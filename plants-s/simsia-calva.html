Simsia calva

Simsia calva, commonly known as the hairy beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Simsia calva is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
