Salvia × auriculata

Salvia × auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it often grows in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Salvia × auriculata is popular in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
