Selaginella dregei var. petersiana

Selaginella dregei var. petersiana, commonly known as the Peters' spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to southern Africa.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae, characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and sporangia that produce spores. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded, moist environments. Its morphology includes upright stems with whorled leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This variety is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
