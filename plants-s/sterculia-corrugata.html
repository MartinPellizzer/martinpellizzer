Sterculia corrugata

Sterculia corrugata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark. The leaves are large, alternate, and palmately lobed, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or pale green, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental potential.
