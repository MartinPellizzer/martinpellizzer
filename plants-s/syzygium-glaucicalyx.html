Syzygium glaucicalyx

Syzygium glaucicalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Syzygium glaucicalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
