Saxifraga flagellaris subsp. mucronulata

Saxifraga flagellaris subsp.

mucronulata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the European Alps. It features small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the alpine flora diversity.
