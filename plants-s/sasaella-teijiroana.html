Sasaella teijiroana

Sasaella teijiroana, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses and reeds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.
