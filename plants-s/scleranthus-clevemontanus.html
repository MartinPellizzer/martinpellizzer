Scleranthus clevemontanus

Scleranthus clevemontanus, commonly known as the Clevemontan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
