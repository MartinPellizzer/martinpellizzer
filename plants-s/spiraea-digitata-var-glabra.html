Spiraea digitata var. glabra

Spiraea digitata var. glabra, commonly known as smooth meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and often slightly hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
