Senecio helenitis var. discoideus

Senecio helenitis var. discoideus, commonly known as the wooly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
