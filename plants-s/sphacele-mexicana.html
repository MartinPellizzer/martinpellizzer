Sphacele mexicana

Sphacele mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican globe flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its globe-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months.

Sphacele mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
