Silene cartilaginea

Silene cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often pink or reddish stems and leaves, which are adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments.

Silene cartilaginea is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitats.
