Spiraea cana var. mollis

Spiraea cana var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that cascade with clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, turning yellow in the fall, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
