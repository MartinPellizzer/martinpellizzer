Saccharum arundinaceum var. trichophyllum

Saccharum arundinaceum var. trichophyllum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo grass, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that distinguishes it from other members of the Saccharum genus. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies.

Botanically, it is valued for its potential in biofuel production and ecological restoration due to its high biomass yield and adaptability.
