Silaus afghanicus

Silaus afghanicus, commonly known as Afghan thyme, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Silaus afghanicus is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in harsh climates.
