Senegalia emilioana

Senegalia emilioana, commonly known as the Emilioan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically reduced to spines in mature plants, and its yellow, globular flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Senegalia emilioana is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in harsh environmental conditions.
