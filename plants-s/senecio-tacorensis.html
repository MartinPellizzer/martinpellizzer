Senecio tacorensis

Senecio tacorensis, commonly known as the Tacorensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio tacorensis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
