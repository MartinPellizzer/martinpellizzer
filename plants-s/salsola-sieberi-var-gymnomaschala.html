Salsola sieberi var. gymnomaschala

Salsola sieberi var. gymnomaschala, commonly known as the smoothleaf Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The leaves are typically smooth and fleshy, which helps the plant conserve water in harsh conditions.
