Symphyotrichum unctuosum

Symphyotrichum unctuosum, commonly known as the sticky goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It thrives in full sun and is often found in the eastern and central United States.

The sticky secretions on its stems and leaves are a distinctive botanical feature that aids in its identification.
