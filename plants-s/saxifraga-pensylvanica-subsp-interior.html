Saxifraga pensylvanica subsp. interior

Saxifraga pensylvanica subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior rockfoam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine or subalpine environments.
