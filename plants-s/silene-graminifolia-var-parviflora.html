Silene graminifolia var. parviflora

Silene graminifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grass pink, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
