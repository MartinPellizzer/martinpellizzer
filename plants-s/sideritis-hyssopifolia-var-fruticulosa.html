Sideritis hyssopifolia var. fruticulosa

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. fruticulosa, commonly known as the creeping ironwort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both gardens and ecological restoration projects.
