Stachys recta var. longiflora

Stachys recta var. longiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers along the upper stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
