Saxifraga moschata

Saxifraga moschata, commonly known as the musk saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle musky scent. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Saxifraga moschata is known for its hardy nature and is often used in rock gardens or alpine displays.
