Salsola brachiata

Salsola brachiata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, cone-like structures. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Salsola brachiata is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly.
