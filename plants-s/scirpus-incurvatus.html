Scirpus incurvatus

Scirpus incurvatus, commonly known as the curled bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus incurvatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
