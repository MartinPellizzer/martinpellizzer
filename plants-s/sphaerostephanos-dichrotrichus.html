Sphaerostephanos dichrotrichus

Sphaerostephanos dichrotrichus, commonly known as the two-colored star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in dense, star-like inflorescences. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
