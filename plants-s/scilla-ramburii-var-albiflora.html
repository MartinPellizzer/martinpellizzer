Scilla ramburii var. albiflora

Scilla ramburii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rambur scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Due to its delicate appearance, it requires careful attention to avoid overwatering and frost damage.
