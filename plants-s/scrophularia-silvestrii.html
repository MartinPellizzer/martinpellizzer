Scrophularia silvestrii

Scrophularia silvestrii, commonly known as the wild scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is a perennial herb with a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves.

Scrophularia silvestrii is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some species.
