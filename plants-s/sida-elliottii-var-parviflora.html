Sida elliottii var. parviflora

Sida elliottii var. parviflora, commonly known as Elliott's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are oval-shaped with toothed edges. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
