Smilax aspericaulis

Smilax aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swamps or along stream banks.

Smilax aspericaulis is a hardy plant that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
