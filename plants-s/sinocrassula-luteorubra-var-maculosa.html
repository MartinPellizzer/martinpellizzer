Sinocrassula luteorubra var. maculosa

Sinocrassula luteorubra var. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted jade plant, is a succulent species native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in both indoor and outdoor settings.
