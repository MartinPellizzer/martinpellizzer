Senecio amplectens

Senecio amplectens, commonly known as the woolly lion's ear, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
