Sambucus sieboldiana var. coreana

Sambucus sieboldiana var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 7 serrated leaflets, and its flowers are small, clustered, and white or pale green. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they can be toxic if unripe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
