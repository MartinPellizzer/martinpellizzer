Sasa yosaensis

Sasa yosaensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or yosaensis bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense, compact colonies. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Sasa yosaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.
