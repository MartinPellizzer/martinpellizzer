Sebaea intermedia

Sebaea intermedia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or creeping sebaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sebaea intermedia is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
