Stachys tubulosa

Stachys tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Stachys tubulosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
