Spiraea fritschiana var. latifolia

Spiraea fritschiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green color that turns yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is prized for its graceful appearance and ability to provide seasonal interest throughout the growing season.
