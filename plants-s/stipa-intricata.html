Stipa intricata

Stipa intricata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Stipa intricata is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
