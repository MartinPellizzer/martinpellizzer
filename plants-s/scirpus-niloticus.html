Scirpus niloticus

Scirpus niloticus, commonly known as the nilotic sedge, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It typically grows in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Scirpus niloticus is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching in some regions.
