Sorghastrum brunneum

Sorghastrum brunneum, commonly known as blackgrass or sudangrass, is a warm-season grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, dark green leaves and sturdy, upright stems that can grow up to 3 to 5 feet tall. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are clustered along the stems, and it is often used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value. Sorghastrum brunneum thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Its resilience and rapid growth make it a valuable plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
