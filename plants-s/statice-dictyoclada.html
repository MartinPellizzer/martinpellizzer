Statice dictyoclada

Statice dictyoclada, commonly known as the sea lavender or desert statice, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Statice dictyoclada is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
