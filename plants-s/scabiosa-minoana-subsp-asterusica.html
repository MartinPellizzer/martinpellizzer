Scabiosa minoana subsp. asterusica

Scabiosa minoana subsp.

asterusica, commonly known as the Sicilian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, daisy-like appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
