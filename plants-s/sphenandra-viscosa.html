Sphenandra viscosa

Sphenandra viscosa, commonly known as the sticky sphenandra, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often covered in a sticky substance, which aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
