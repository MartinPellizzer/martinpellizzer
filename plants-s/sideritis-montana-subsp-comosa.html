Sideritis montana subsp. comosa

Sideritis montana subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
