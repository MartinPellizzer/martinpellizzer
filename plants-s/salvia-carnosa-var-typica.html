Salvia carnosa var. typica

Salvia carnosa var. typica, commonly known as the purple beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are opposite and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

Salvia carnosa var. typica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
