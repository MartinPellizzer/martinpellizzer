Stipa karataviensis

Stipa karataviensis, commonly known as Karatav grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Stipa karataviensis is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and as a forage plant for grazing animals.
