Sasa hibaconuca

Sasa hibaconuca, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa hibaconuca forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating a mat-like structure that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.
