Sambucus nigra f. pendula

Sambucus nigra f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping elderberry, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its cascading clusters of white, fragrant flowers.

It is a flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family. The plant produces small, dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its unique, drooping form makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
