Symphyotrichum × finkii

Symphyotrichum × finkii, commonly known as the Fink's New England aster, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as bogs and marshes, and thrives in full sun. It produces numerous ray florets that are usually purple to pink in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Symphyotrichum × finkii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
