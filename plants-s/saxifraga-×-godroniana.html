Saxifraga × godroniana

Saxifraga × godroniana, commonly known as the Godron's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
