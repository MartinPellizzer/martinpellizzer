Saussurea phyllocephala

Saussurea phyllocephala, commonly known as the leaf-headed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf-like bracts that enclose the flower head, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals, and it blooms during the summer months.

Saussurea phyllocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
