Santiria rubiginosa var. pedicellata

Santiria rubiginosa var. pedicellata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and distinctive reddish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species in many regions.
