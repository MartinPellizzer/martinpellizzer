Sakakia morii

Sakakia morii, commonly known as the Japanese rose or Morii's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has pinnately compound leaves. Its botanical features include thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Sakakia morii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
