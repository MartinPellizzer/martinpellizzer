Selaginella nicaraguensis

Selaginella nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a short, upright stem with spirally arranged leaves, and it produces spores in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella nicaraguensis is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
