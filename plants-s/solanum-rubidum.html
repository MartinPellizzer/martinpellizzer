Solanum rubidum

Solanum rubidum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its compact shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible when ripe, though they can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Solanum rubidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its potential culinary uses.
