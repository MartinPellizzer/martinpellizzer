Santolina chamaemelifolia

Santolina chamaemelifolia, commonly known as silver santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the spring, which are borne on short, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina chamaemelifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and resistance to drought and pests.
