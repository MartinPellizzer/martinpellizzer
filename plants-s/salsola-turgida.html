Salsola turgida

Salsola turgida, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often wind-pollinated. Salsola turgida is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry climates.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
