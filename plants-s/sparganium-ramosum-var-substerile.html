Sparganium ramosum var. substerile

Sparganium ramosum var. substerile, commonly known as the sterile bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiky inflorescences that lack functional seeds, making it less viable for propagation. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help stabilize the surrounding habitat.

This variant is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems and its unique reproductive characteristics.
