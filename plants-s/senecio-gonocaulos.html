Senecio gonocaulos

Senecio gonocaulos, commonly known as the purple stem senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive floral structure and composite flowers.

It is native to southern Africa and typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a hairy, purple-tinged stem and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in compact heads, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Senecio gonocaulos is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
