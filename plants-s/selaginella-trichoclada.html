Selaginella trichoclada

Selaginella trichoclada, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, creeping plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged leaves and a short, upright stem that bears sporophylls. Selaginella trichoclada reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
