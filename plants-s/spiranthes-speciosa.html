Spiranthes speciosa

Spiranthes speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and prairies. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Spiranthes speciosa relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, reflecting its specialized ecological relationships.
