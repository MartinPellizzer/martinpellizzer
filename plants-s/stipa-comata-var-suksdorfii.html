Stipa comata var. suksdorfii

Stipa comata var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksaywaman hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender, hair-like leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
