Scorzonera glandulosa

Scorzonera glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in glandular dots, giving it its scientific name.

Scorzonera glandulosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
