Sampacca montana

Sampacca montana, commonly known as the mountain sampacca, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Sampacca montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
