Semiarundinaria nitida

Semiarundinaria nitida, commonly known as the silver bamboo or silver reed, is a grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant grows in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands that help stabilize soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Semiarundinaria nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
