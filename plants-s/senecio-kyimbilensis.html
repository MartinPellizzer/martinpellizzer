Senecio kyimbilensis

Senecio kyimbilensis, commonly known as the Kyimbilensis groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
