Sida schweinfurthii

Sida schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African mallow, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots and stems have been used traditionally for their textile properties.

Sida schweinfurthii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
