Symphytum × ferrariense

Symphytum × ferrariense, commonly known as the Italian comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its healing qualities.
