Sedum corynephyllum

Sedum corynephyllum, commonly known as the coral sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
