Solanum chiquidenum

Solanum chiquidenum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a small, green berry that turns red when mature, containing seeds inside.

The species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in warm, arid environments.
