Senegaria

Senegaria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to various regions across Africa, including South Africa, Kenya, and Tanzania. The plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a range of colors such as yellow, orange, and red. Senegaria species are often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
