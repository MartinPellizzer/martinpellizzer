Scaphochlamys tenuis

Scaphochlamys tenuis, commonly known as the slender-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its roots are adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Scaphochlamys tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
