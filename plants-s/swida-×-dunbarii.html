Swida × dunbarii

Swida × dunbarii, commonly known as the hybrid dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Swida mexicana and Swida mariana, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species features showy white or pink bracts that surround small, inconspicuous true flowers. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and it produces red or black berries in the fall.

Swida × dunbarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
