Stipa mucronata

Stipa mucronata, commonly known as the spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Stipa mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
