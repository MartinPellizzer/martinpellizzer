Sphenogyne punctata

Sphenogyne punctata, commonly known as the spotted sphenogyne, is a member of the family Sphenogyneaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, featuring distinct sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

The plant's unique leaf patterns and floral structure make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
