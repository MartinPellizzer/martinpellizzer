Stachys floridana

Stachys floridana, commonly known as Florida betony, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Stachys floridana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
