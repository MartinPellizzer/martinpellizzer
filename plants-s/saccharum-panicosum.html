Saccharum panicosum

Saccharum panicosum, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features long, slender leaves and produces dense, fluffy panicles of small, pale flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, sweet scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Saccharum panicosum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
