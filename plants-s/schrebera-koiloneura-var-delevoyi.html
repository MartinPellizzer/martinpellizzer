Schrebera koiloneura var. delevoyi

Schrebera koiloneura var. delevoyi, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a small, fleshy capsule. Its growth habit is typically as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
