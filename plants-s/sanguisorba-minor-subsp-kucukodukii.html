Sanguisorba minor subsp. kucukodukii

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

kucukodukii, commonly known as the Turkish bloodwort, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces dark purple berries that are often consumed by birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
