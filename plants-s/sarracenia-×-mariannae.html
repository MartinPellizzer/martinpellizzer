Sarracenia × mariannae

Sarracenia × mariannae, commonly known as the Marianna swamp pitcherplant, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, trumpet-shaped pitchers that can reach up to 20 inches in height. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its pitchers are designed to trap and digest insects, making it a unique carnivorous plant.

It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture and partial sunlight for optimal growth.
