Selago quadrangularis

Selago quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle grass or slender grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are typically four-sided, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Selago quadrangularis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
