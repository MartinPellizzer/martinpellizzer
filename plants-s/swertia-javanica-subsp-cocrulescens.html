Swertia javanica subsp. cocrulescens

Swertia javanica subsp.

cocrulescens, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.
