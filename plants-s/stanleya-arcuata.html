Stanleya arcuata

Stanleya arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Stanleya arcuata is an important plant for local wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
