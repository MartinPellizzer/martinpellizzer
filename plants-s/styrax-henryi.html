Styrax henryi

Styrax henryi, commonly known as Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exuding a sticky resin when damaged.

Styrax henryi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
