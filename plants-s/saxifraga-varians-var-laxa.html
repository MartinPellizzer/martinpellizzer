Saxifraga varians var. laxa

Saxifraga varians var. laxa, commonly known as the loose saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
