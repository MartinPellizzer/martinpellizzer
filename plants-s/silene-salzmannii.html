Silene salzmannii

Silene salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy.

Silene salzmannii is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
