Selaginella floridana

Selaginella floridana, commonly known as the Florida spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, spore-bearing cones and scale-like leaves. Selaginella floridana is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
