Selaginella christii

Selaginella christii, commonly known as the Christ's spikemoss, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, cone-like structures called strobila that produce spores. Native to arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in crevices or on rock surfaces. Its ability to survive in extreme conditions makes it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Selaginella christii is also noted for its slow growth and unique reproductive features, contributing to its ecological significance.
