Securidaca spinosa

Securidaca spinosa, commonly known as the spiked securidaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
