Salvia atlantica

Salvia atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic sage, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems that bear pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Salvia atlantica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to Mediterranean climates.
