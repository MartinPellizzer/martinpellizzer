Sicklera cuspidata

Sicklera cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that are often described as resembling a sickle in shape. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
