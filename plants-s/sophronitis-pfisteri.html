Sophronitis pfisteri

Sophronitis pfisteri, commonly known as the Pfister's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are often pale pink to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sophronitis pfisteri is considered a rare and endangered orchid.
