Sunaptea rynchocarpa

Sunaptea rynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, curved seed pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The seeds are encased in a hard, horn-like shell, which gives the plant its common name.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
