Silene graminifolia lusus elata

Silene graminifolia lusus elata, commonly known as the tall meadow catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, hence the species name *graminifolia*, and it is often found in temperate regions.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
