Solanum sinclairii

Solanum sinclairii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its yellowish flowers and dark purple berries. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum sinclairii is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
