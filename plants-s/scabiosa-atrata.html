Scabiosa atrata

Scabiosa atrata, commonly known as the black scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a bushy growth habit with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Scabiosa atrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
