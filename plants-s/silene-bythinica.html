Silene bythinica

Silene bythinica, commonly known as the Bythine campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually upright and branching.

Silene bythinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
