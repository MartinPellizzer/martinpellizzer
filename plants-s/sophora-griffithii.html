Sophora griffithii

Sophora griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces striking purple-pink flowers in pendulous clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The seeds of Sophora griffithii contain toxic compounds and require proper processing before use.
