Solidago foliosa

Solidago foliosa, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine hairs, and the flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Solidago foliosa plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
