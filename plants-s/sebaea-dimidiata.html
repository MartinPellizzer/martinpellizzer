Sebaea dimidiata

Sebaea dimidiata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
