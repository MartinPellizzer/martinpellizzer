Senghasia costaricensis

Senghasia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican senghasia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
