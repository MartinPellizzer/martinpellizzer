Sinocalamus parishii

Sinocalamus parishii, commonly known as the Yunnan bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, linear leaves. This species is often found in moist, mountainous regions and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its growth habit makes it a valuable resource for both traditional uses and ecological restoration projects.

The plant plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
