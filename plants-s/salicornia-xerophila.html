Salicornia xerophila

Salicornia xerophila, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically inconspicuous. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Salicornia xerophila thrives in coastal regions and salt marshes, showcasing remarkable resilience to harsh, arid conditions.
