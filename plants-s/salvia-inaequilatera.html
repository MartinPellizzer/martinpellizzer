Salvia inaequilatera

Salvia inaequilatera, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage. Salvia inaequilatera typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
