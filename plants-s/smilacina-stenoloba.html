Smilacina stenoloba

Smilacina stenoloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils that help it ascend vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinct, narrow shape.

Smilacina stenoloba thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
