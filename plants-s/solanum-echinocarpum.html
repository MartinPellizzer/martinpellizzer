Solanum echinocarpum

Solanum echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, and its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
