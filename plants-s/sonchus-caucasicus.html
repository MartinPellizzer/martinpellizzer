Sonchus caucasicus

Sonchus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Europe. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head and bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
