Stachys hirtula

Stachys hirtula, commonly known as woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys hirtula is also sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
