Salix myrsinites var. arbuscula

Salix myrsinites var. arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is an important component of the vegetation in cold, northern environments.
