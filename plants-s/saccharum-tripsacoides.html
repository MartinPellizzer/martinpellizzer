Saccharum tripsacoides

Saccharum tripsacoides, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is closely related to cultivated sugarcane and is considered a valuable genetic resource for plant breeding programs.
