Scilla siberica subsp. caucasica

Scilla siberica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It produces striking blue to violet flowers in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats. It is closely related to other members of the Hyacinthaceae family.

Its bulbs are toxic and should not be consumed.
