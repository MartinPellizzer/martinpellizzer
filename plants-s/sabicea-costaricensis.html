Sabicea costaricensis

Sabicea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
