Scilla neglecta

Scilla neglecta, commonly known as the bluebells, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Scilla neglecta is known for its ornamental value and is often used in naturalized landscapes.
