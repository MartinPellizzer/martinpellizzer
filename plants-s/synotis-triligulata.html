Synotis triligulata

Synotis triligulata, commonly known as the three-lobed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, triangular leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
