Syzygium peregrinum

Syzygium peregrinum, commonly known as the golden apple or the wild apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Syzygium peregrinum is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its medicinal properties.
