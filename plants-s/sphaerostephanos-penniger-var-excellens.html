Sphaerostephanos penniger var. excellens

Sphaerostephanos penniger var. excellens, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy inflorescences that feature bright yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, star-shaped blossoms that are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts. It prefers cool, shaded environments with high humidity and well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized greenhouse conditions.
