Saccolabium turneri

Saccolabium turneri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow composite flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Saccolabium turneri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
