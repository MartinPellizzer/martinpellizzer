Sirium

Sirium, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants are characterized by their alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers with greenish or reddish sepals and white or yellow petals.

Sirium species are generally herbaceous, with some varieties growing as perennials or annuals.
