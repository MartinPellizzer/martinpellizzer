Serapias orientalis var. cordigeroides

Serapias orientalis var. cordigeroides, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and orange.

This variant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
