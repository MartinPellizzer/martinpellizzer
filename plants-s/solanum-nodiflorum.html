Solanum nodiflorum

Solanum nodiflorum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white or purple flowers that bloom during the day. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum nodiflorum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
