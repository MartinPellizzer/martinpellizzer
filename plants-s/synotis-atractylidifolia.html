Synotis atractylidifolia

Synotis atractylidifolia, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Synotis atractylidifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
