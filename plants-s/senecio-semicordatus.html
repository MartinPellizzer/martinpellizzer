Senecio semicordatus

Senecio semicordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio semicordatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
