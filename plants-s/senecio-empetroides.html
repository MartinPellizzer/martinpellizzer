Senecio empetroides

Senecio empetroides, commonly known as the blueweed or blueweed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with a fibrous root system and a sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Senecio empetroides is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
