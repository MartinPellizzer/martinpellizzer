Systemon fischeri

Systemon fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's systemon, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral arrangement of leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
