Saxofridericia pandanoides

Saxofridericia pandanoides, commonly known as the pandan-like plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional cooking and crafts. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage emits a subtle, pleasant aroma, which is attributed to the presence of volatile compounds.

While it is not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it holds cultural significance in various regions.
