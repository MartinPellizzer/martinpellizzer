Spergularia rubra var. marina

Spergularia rubra var. marina, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in maritime environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent and adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

This variety is notable for its ability to colonize disturbed or barren coastal areas, contributing to soil stabilization.
