Salix heterochroma var. concolor

Salix heterochroma var. concolor, commonly known as the white-barked willow, is a variant of the white willow species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale bark, which is smooth and often peeling in thin layers. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas.
