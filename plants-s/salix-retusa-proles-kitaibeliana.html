Salix retusa proles kitaibeliana

Salix retusa proles kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a light green color that turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil along water bodies.
