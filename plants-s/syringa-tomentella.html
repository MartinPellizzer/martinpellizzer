Syringa tomentella

Syringa tomentella, commonly known as the wooly bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage, which gives it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Syringa tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh growing conditions.
