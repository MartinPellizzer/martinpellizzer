Saussurea leucophylla

Saussurea leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it its common name.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
