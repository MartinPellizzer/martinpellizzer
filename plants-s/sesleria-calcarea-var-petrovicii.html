Sesleria calcarea var. petrovicii

Sesleria calcarea var. petrovicii, commonly known as the mountain sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
