Solanum nodiflorum var. acuminatum

Solanum nodiflorum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are yellow when mature, containing seeds within a gelatinous pulp.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
