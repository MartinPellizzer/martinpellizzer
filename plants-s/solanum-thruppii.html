Solanum thruppii

Solanum thruppii, commonly known as the Thrupps nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
