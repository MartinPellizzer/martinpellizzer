Salvia basilii

Salvia basilii, commonly known as sacred basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties.

Salvia basilii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
