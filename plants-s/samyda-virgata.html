Samyda virgata

Samyda virgata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Samyda virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
