Syringa villosa subsp. wolfii

Syringa villosa subsp.

wolfii, commonly known as the silky lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Syringa villosa subsp.

wolfii is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
