Stillingia angustifolia

Stillingia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stellaria, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from 30 to 90 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stillingia angustifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
