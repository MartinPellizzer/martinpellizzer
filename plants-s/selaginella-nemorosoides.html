Selaginella nemorosoides

Selaginella nemorosoides, commonly known as the mountain clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a prostrate or upright stem and small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. It produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are often clustered at the tips of the branches.

Selaginella nemorosoides is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
