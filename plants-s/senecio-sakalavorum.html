Senecio sakalavorum

Senecio sakalavorum, commonly known as the Sakalavo's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
