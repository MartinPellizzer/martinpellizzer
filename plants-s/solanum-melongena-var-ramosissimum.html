Solanum melongena var. ramosissimum

Solanum melongena var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its highly branched structure.

It produces deep purple, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is valued for its culinary uses in various cuisines.

Its foliage is broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
