Salix chaenomeloides f. obtusa

Salix chaenomeloides f. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved willow, is a variant of the willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.
