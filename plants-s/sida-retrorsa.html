Sida retrorsa

Sida retrorsa, commonly known as the backward sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Sida retrorsa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
