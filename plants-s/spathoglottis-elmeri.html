Spathoglottis elmeri

Spathoglottis elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender leaves and a single, showy flower that resembles a spider. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Spathoglottis elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
