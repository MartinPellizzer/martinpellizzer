Sasa horribilis

Sasa horribilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in panicles, and its rhizomes are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

Sasa horribilis is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
