Sida carandana

Sida carandana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

Sida carandana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
