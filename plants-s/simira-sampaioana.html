Simira sampaioana

Simira sampaioana, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dark, glossy bark. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Simira sampaioana plays an important ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
