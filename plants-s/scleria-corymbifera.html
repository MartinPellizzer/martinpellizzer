Scleria corymbifera

Scleria corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the top. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Scleria corymbifera is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
