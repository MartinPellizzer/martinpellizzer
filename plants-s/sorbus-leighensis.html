Sorbus leighensis

Sorbus leighensis, commonly known as the Leigh's whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the UK and is characterized by its white, fluffy flowers and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
