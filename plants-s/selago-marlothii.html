Selago marlothii

Selago marlothii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selago, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Selago marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
