Solanum boselliae

Solanum boselliae, commonly known as the Boselli's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
