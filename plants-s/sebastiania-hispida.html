Sebastiania hispida

Sebastiania hispida, commonly known as the rough-seaberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and mangrove habitats. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, with five petals, and are often white or pale yellow in color.
