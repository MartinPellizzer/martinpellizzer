Silene viscidula

Silene viscidula, commonly known as sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Silene viscidula is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
