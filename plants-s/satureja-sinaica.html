Satureja sinaica

Satureja sinaica, commonly known as Sinai thyme, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

Satureja sinaica is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, known for their antimicrobial properties.
