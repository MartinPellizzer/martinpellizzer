Scorzonera dichotoma

Scorzonera dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a taproot system, which allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deep water sources. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

Scorzonera dichotoma is also known for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
