Stenorrhynchos ventricosum

Stenorrhynchos ventricosum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often hairy foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, are borne on slender stems and attract various pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
