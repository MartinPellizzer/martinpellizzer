Staurogyne diantheroides

Staurogyne diantheroides, commonly known as the pink water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically pink or reddish in color, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread along the water's surface, forming dense mats that can help stabilize sediments.

Staurogyne diantheroides is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.
