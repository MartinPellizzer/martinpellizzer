Salix × gracilistyloides

Salix × gracilistyloides, commonly known as the narrow-styled willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth upper surface with a hairy underside.

Salix × gracilistyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
