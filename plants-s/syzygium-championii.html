Syzygium championii

Syzygium championii, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Syzygium championii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
