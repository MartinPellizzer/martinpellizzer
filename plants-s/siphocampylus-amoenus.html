Siphocampylus amoenus

Siphocampylus amoenus, commonly known as the elegant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
