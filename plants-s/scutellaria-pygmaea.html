Scutellaria pygmaea

Scutellaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf skullcap, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Scutellaria pygmaea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies.
