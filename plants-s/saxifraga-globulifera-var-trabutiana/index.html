Saxifraga Globulifera Var. Trabutiana

Saxifraga globulifera var. trabutiana is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region. It is known for its compact, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. This variety is a subspecies of Saxifraga globulifera, which is part of the Saxifragaceae family. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts. Due to habitat loss and climate change, this plant faces increasing threats to its survival.
