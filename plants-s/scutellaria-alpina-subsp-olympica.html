Scutellaria alpina subsp. olympica

Scutellaria alpina subsp.

olympica, commonly known as the Olympic skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often clustered in whorls.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
