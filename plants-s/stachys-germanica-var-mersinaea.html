Stachys germanica var. mersinaea

Stachys germanica var. mersinaea, commonly known as the Mersinaea betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
