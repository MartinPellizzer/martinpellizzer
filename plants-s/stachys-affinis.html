Stachys affinis

Stachys affinis, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
