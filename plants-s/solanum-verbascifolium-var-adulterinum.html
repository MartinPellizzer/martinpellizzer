Solanum verbascifolium var. adulterinum

Solanum verbascifolium var. adulterinum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, woolly leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
