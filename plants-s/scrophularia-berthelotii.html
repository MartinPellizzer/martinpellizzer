Scrophularia berthelotii

Scrophularia berthelotii, commonly known as the Berthelot's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
