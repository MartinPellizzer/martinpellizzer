Satureja hungarica

Satureja hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Satureja hungarica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
