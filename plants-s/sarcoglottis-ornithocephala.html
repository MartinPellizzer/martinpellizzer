Sarcoglottis ornithocephala

Sarcoglottis ornithocephala, commonly known as the bird's head sarcoglottis, is a climbing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence that resembles a bird's head, giving it its common name. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a spathe that encloses the spadix, a feature common in the Araceae family.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
