Spiraea digitata

Spiraea digitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, providing an attractive texture throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
