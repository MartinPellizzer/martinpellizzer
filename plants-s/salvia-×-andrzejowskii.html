Salvia × andrzejowskii

Salvia × andrzejowskii, commonly known as Andrzejowski's sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
