Sorghastrum flexuosum

Sorghastrum flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible gramina or flexible bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often curved stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Sorghastrum flexuosum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
