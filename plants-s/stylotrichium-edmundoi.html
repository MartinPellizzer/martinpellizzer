Stylotrichium edmundoi

Stylotrichium edmundoi, commonly known as the Edmund's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in soft, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its showy purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stylotrichium edmundoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
