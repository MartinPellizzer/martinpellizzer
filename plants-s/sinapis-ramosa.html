Sinapis ramosa

Sinapis ramosa, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its branched stems, alternate leaves, and small yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. The plant produces spherical pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sinapis ramosa is a common weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
