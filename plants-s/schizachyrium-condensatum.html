Schizachyrium condensatum

Schizachyrium condensatum, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottle brush, composed of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Schizachyrium condensatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
