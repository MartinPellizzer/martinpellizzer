Schismatoglottis clarae

Schismatoglottis clarae, commonly known as the Clara's slit-leaf orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive split at the base of the petals.

Schismatoglottis clarae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
