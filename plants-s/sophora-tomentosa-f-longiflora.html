Sophora tomentosa f. longiflora

Sophora tomentosa f. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered milkvetch, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and elongated, clustered inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces legume pods that contain several elliptical, brown seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
