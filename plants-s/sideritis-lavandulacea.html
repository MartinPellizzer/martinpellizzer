Sideritis lavandulacea

Sideritis lavandulacea, commonly known as lavender sideritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

Sideritis lavandulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild essential oils.
