Serjania schunkei

Serjania schunkei, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and often marked with lenticels.

Serjania schunkei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
