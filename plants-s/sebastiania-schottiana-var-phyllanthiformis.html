Sebastiania schottiana var. phyllanthiformis

Sebastiania schottiana var. phyllanthiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, often with a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
