Sabulina helmii var. helmii

Sabulina helmii var. helmii, commonly known as the sand saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features a rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra or mountainous areas.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
