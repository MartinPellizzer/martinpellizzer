Scrophularia micradenia

Scrophularia micradenia, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters, with tubular corollas that are usually pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
