Solanum polytrichum var. enoplocalyx

Solanum polytrichum var. enoplocalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems, with small, alternate leaves that are usually glandular-dotted. The flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and stamens that are clustered around the pistil. This variant is native to parts of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its berries, which are small and usually green or red, contain toxic alkaloids and are not typically consumed by humans.
