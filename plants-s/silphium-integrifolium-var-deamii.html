Silphium integrifolium var. deamii

Silphium integrifolium var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's compassplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tallgrass prairies of the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy composite flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, often thriving in open, disturbed habitats.

Silphium integrifolium var. deamii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
