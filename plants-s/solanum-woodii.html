Solanum woodii

Solanum woodii, commonly known as the Woodii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing numerous seeds.

Solanum woodii is characterized by its woody stems and hairy leaves, which are typical features of many species in the genus.
