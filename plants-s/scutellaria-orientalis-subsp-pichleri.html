Scutellaria orientalis subsp. pichleri

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

pichleri, commonly known as the eastern skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.
