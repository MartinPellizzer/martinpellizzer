Stachyococcus

Stachyococcus, commonly known as the "moss-like" plant, is a genus of small, green, leafless plants that belong to the family Stachyococcaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as on rocks or tree trunks. They lack true leaves and instead have structures called rhizoids that anchor them to surfaces. Stachyococcus species are often mistaken for mosses due to their similar appearance.

They play a minor role in ecological systems but are of interest in botanical studies due to their unique morphology and growth habits.
