Spathicarpa gardneri

Spathicarpa gardneri, commonly known as the desert fig, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy fruits that resemble figs, which are typically green when immature and turn red or purple when ripe. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are clustered in spathaceous structures, giving the plant its genus name.

Spathicarpa gardneri plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
