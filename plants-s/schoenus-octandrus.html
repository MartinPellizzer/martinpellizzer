Schoenus octandrus

Schoenus octandrus, commonly known as the eight-stamen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, three-nerved seeds.

Schoenus octandrus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
