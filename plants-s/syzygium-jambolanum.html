Syzygium jambolanum

Syzygium jambolanum, commonly known as the Java apple or bastard cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin that turns from green to red as it ripens. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, acidic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine.
