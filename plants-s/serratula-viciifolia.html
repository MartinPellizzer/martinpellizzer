Serratula viciifolia

Serratula viciifolia, commonly known as the common sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, followed by small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Serratula viciifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
