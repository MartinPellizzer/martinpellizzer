Satyrium perrieri

Satyrium perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, stout rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. Its flowers are borne on a slender stem and are often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Satyrium perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
