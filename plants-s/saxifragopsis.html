Saxifragopsis

Saxifragopsis, commonly known as the rockfoil, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or rosettes. The plants typically thrive in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Saxifragopsis species are native to regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.
