Sporobolus airoides subsp. regis

Sporobolus airoides subsp.

regis, commonly known as the purple threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and spikelets that often have a purple tinge.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of three to five spikelets, which are usually borne on short, ascending branches.

The leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture, and the plant is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
