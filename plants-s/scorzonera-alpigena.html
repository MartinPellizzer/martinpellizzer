Scorzonera alpigena

Scorzonera alpigena, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often contains a thick, edible tuberous root.

Scorzonera alpigena thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
