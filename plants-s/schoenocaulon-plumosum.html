Schoenocaulon plumosum

Schoenocaulon plumosum, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with a distinctive rosette of silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle floral display. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The silky texture of its leaves and the unique structure of its flowers make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
