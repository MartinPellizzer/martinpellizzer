Solidago simplex var. racemosa

Solidago simplex var. racemosa, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish-brown and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
