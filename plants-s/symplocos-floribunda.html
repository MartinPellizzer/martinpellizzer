Symplocos floribunda

Symplocos floribunda, commonly known as the clustered sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its botanical features include a woody stem with smooth bark and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Symplocos floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
