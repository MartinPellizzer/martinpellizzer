Solanum trilobatum

Solanum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have three lobes, giving it its common name.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
