Salvia natalis

Salvia natalis, commonly known as Natal sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia natalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
