Stachyurus duclouxii

Stachyurus duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils.

Stachyurus duclouxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
