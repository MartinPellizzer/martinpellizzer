Sorghum miliiforme var. sikkimense

Sorghum miliiforme var. sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim millet, is a grass species native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its small, triangular grains and is typically grown in temperate climates. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
