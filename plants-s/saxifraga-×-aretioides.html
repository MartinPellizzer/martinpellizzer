Saxifraga × aretioides

Saxifraga × aretioides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga × aretioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh, cold climates.
