Sisyrinchium alpestre

Sisyrinchium alpestre, commonly known as alpine blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flowers clustered at the tip.

Sisyrinchium alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with rocky or alpine themes.
