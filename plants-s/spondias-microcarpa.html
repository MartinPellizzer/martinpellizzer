Spondias microcarpa

Spondias microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited satinash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like mango and cashew. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually leathery in texture.

Its small, yellowish-green fruits are edible and often used in local cuisines, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
