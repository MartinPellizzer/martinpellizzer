Solanum andigenum subsp. australiperuvianum

Solanum andigenum subsp.

australiperuvianum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant typically grows in altitudes ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its flowers are small and usually white or purple in color.

The fruit, which is a berry, contains toxic compounds and is not typically consumed by humans.
