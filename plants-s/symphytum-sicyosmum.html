Symphytum sicyosmum

Symphytum sicyosmum, commonly known as the Turkish comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a distinctive white or gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
