Silene involucrata

Silene involucrata, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Silene involucrata is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
