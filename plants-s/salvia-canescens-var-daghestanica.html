Salvia canescens var. daghestanica

Salvia canescens var. daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
