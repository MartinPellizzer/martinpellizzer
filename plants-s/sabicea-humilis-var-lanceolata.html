Sabicea humilis var. lanceolata

Sabicea humilis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sabicea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
