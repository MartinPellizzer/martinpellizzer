Sida rubifolia

Sida rubifolia, commonly known as the redstem sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Sida rubifolia is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
