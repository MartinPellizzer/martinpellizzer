Schizachyrium glaziovii

Schizachyrium glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that are often pink or purple, adding visual interest in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Schizachyrium glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and improving soil stability.
