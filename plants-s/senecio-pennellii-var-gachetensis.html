Senecio pennellii var. gachetensis

Senecio pennellii var. gachetensis, commonly known as the Gachet's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance.
