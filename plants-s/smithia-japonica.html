Smithia japonica

Smithia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rock rose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are short and sturdy.

Smithia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
