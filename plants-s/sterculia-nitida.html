Sterculia nitida

Sterculia nitida, commonly known as the silky nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential economic value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
