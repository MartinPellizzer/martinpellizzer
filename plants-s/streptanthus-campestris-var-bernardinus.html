Streptanthus campestris var. bernardinus

Streptanthus campestris var. bernardinus, commonly known as the desert streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, while its flowers are often white or pale yellow.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
