Salacia fruticosa

Salacia fruticosa, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, sour fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
