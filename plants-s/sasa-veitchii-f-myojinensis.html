Sasa veitchii f. myojinensis

Sasa veitchii f. myojinensis, commonly known as the Myojin bamboo, is a variant of the Veitch bamboo native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense, upright clusters, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
