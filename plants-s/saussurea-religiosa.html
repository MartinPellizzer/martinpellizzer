Saussurea religiosa

Saussurea religiosa, commonly known as the sacred saffron, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and it is particularly noted for its aromatic, saffron-like seeds. Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in alpine environments.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
