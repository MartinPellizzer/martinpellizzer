Saxifraga candollei

Saxifraga candollei, commonly known as the Andes saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features rosettes of fleshy, basal leaves that help it survive in cold, arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom during the warmer months.

Saxifraga candollei is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
