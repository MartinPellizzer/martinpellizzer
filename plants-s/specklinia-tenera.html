Specklinia tenera

Specklinia tenera, commonly known as the slender vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, and they bloom in clusters.

Specklinia tenera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
