Spiraea bella var. coccinea

Spiraea bella var. coccinea, commonly known as the red spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is dark green and turns reddish in the fall, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Spiraea bella var. coccinea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
