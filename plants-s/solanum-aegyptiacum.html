Solanum aegyptiacum

Solanum aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, green berries, which turn red when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids.
