Senecio yunguyensis

Senecio yunguyensis, commonly known as the Yunguya Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Andean regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Senecio yunguyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
