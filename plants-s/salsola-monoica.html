Salsola monoica

Salsola monoica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline or alkaline soils, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has a spiny, prostrate growth habit with small, gray-green leaves and produces fluffy, white to pinkish flowers. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in many areas.

Salsola monoica is also notable for its role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in certain conditions.
