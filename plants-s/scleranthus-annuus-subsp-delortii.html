Scleranthus annuus subsp. delortii

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

delortii, commonly known as the Delort's common thornweed, is an annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early autumn. This species thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
