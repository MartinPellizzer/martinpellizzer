Saxifraga intricata f. major

Saxifraga intricata f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a slightly toothed margin and a waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
