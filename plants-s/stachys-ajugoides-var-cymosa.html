Stachys ajugoides var. cymosa

Stachys ajugoides var. cymosa, commonly known as silverweed or woolly betony, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and ecological restoration projects.
