Satureja bulgarica

Satureja bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Satureja bulgarica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
