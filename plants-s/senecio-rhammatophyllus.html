Senecio rhammatophyllus

Senecio rhammatophyllus, commonly known as the rock lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a drought-tolerant garden plant.
