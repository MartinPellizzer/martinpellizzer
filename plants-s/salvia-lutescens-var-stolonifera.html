Salvia lutescens var. stolonifera

Salvia lutescens var. stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, sprawling stems that form a low, mat-like growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover. The plant produces clusters of small, bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
