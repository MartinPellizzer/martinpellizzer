Saxifraga rosacea subsp. hartii

Saxifraga rosacea subsp.

hartii, commonly known as the Hart's pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces showy pink or red flowers in late summer.

The leaves are typically succulent, with a bluish-green hue and a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens.
