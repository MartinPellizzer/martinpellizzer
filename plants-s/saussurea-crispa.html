Saussurea crispa

Saussurea crispa, commonly known as the Alpine gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing at high elevations with poor soil conditions.

Saussurea crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
