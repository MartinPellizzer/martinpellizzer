Saxifraga sarmentosa var. leuconeura

Saxifraga sarmentosa var. leuconeura, commonly known as the white-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, branching stems that are often tinged with white or pink. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
