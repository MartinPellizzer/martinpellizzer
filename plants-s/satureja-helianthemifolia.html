Satureja helianthemifolia

Satureja helianthemifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.

Satureja helianthemifolia is valued both for its fragrance and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
