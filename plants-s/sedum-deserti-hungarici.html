Sedum deserti-hungarici

Sedum deserti-hungarici, commonly known as the Hungarian desert sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Hungary and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in full sun. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Sedum deserti-hungarici is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
