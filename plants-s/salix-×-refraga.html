Salix × refraga

Salix × refraga, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Salix × refraga thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.
