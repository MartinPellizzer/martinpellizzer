Schoenoplectus californicus var. tereticulmis

Schoenoplectus californicus var. tereticulmis, commonly known as the slender tule, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

The plant is adapted to moist or waterlogged conditions, making it a key component of wetland ecosystems.
