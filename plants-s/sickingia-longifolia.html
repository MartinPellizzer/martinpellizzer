Sickingia longifolia

Sickingia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf sickingia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Sickingia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
