Sabicea umbellata var. paraensis

Sabicea umbellata var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Sabicea, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
