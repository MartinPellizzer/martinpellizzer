Satureja ericifolia subsp. gomerensis

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

gomerensis, commonly known as Gomer's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, ovoid seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Satureja ericifolia.
