Sinapis cheiranthos var. montana

Sinapis cheiranthos var. montana, commonly known as the mountain mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
