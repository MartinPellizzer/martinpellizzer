Saxifraga eschholzii

Saxifraga eschholzii, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga eschholzii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine tundras and high-altitude meadows.
