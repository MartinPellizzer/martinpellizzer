Sedum dyvrandae

Sedum dyvrandae, commonly known as the Dyvrand's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
