Sorghastrum minarum

Sorghastrum minarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
