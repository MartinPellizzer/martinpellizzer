Salacia papuana

Salacia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan salacia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands, particularly New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological diversity.
