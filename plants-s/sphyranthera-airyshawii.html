Sphyranthera airyshawii

Sphyranthera airyshawii, commonly known as the Queensland bottle tree, is a unique species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, aiding in survival in arid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, flaky bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

This plant is well adapted to drought and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and woodlands.
