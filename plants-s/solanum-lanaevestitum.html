Solanum lanaevestitum

Solanum lanaevestitum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellowish or orange hue.

Solanum lanaevestitum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
