Selinum alpestre

Selinum alpestre, commonly known as alpine wormwood, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Selinum alpestre thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
