Syzygium myrtillus var. borneense

Syzygium myrtillus var. borneense, commonly known as the Bornean myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Borneo and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
