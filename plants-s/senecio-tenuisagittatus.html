Senecio tenuisagittatus

Senecio tenuisagittatus, commonly known as the narrow arrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
