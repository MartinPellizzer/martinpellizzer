Stelleropsis turcomanica

Stelleropsis turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Stelleropsis turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
