Sasa auricoma

Sasa auricoma, commonly known as the auriculate bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, green culms that are often covered with a distinctive golden-brown sheath. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa auricoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
