Serratula latifolia

Serratula latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sow thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features rosettes of broad, wavy-edged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray flowers surrounded by tubular florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Serratula latifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
