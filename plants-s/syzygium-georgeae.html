Syzygium georgeae

Syzygium georgeae, commonly known as the George's apple or George's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, smooth, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

Syzygium georgeae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
