Senecio sublyratus

Senecio sublyratus, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces seeds in achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Senecio sublyratus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
