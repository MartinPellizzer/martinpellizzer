Sceptridium nipponicum var. minus

Sceptridium nipponicum var. minus, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf moss, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and the plant lacks a distinct stem. It is a cryptogamic plant, reproducing via spores rather than seeds, and plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.

This variety is notable for its compact size and adaptability to humid, shaded conditions.
