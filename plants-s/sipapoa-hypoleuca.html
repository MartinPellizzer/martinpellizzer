Sipapoa hypoleuca

Sipapoa hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, showy blooms that typically feature white petals with subtle veining. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers that are often pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Sipapoa hypoleuca is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
