Syringa oblata var. hupehensis

Syringa oblata var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside, and it produces small, woody fruits known as follicles. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in urban environments and its resistance to pests and diseases.
