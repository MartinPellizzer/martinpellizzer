Salvia × sakuensis

Salvia × sakuensis, commonly known as the Saku hybrid sage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Salvia leucantha and Salvia splendens.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
