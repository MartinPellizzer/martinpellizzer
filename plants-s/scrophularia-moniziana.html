Scrophularia moniziana

Scrophularia moniziana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Scrophularia moniziana is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
