Sticherus penniger

Sticherus penniger, commonly known as the feather moss, is a small, mat-forming evergreen moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, cup-like structures called gametophores, which bear the sex organs for reproduction. Sticherus penniger is often found in forest floors, rock crevices, and on tree trunks in temperate regions.

Its ability to retain moisture makes it an important component of soil stabilization and moisture regulation in its habitat.
