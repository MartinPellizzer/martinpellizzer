Schoenus spilocarpus

Schoenus spilocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Schoenus spilocarpus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
