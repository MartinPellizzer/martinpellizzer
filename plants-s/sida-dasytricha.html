Sida dasytricha

Sida dasytricha, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Sida dasytricha is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also considered a weed in many regions due to its competitive nature and invasive tendencies.
