Scaevola kallophylla

Scaevola kallophylla, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.

Scaevola kallophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
