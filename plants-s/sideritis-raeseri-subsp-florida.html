Sideritis raeseri subsp. florida

Sideritis raeseri subsp.

florida, commonly known as the Florida sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
