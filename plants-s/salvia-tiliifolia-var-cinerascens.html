Salvia tiliifolia var. cinerascens

Salvia tiliifolia var. cinerascens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or gray on the underside. The plant produces spikes of small, showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
