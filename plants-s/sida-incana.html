Sida incana

Sida incana, commonly known as hoary sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white pubescence, which gives it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

Sida incana is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
