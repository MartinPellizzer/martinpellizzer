Saussurea carduicephala

Saussurea carduicephala, commonly known as the Tibetan yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to the harsh, cold climate of its habitat.

Saussurea carduicephala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
