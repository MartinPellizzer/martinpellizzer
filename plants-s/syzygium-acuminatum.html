Syzygium acuminatum

Syzygium acuminatum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny exterior covering its fruits, which are typically bright red or yellow. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its edible, hairy fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
