Smithia coerulescens

Smithia coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
