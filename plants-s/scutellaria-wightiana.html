Scutellaria wightiana

Scutellaria wightiana, commonly known as Wight's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scutellaria wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
