Stylagrostis eminens

Stylagrostis eminens, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
