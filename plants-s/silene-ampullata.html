Silene ampullata

Silene ampullata, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and its distinctive bladder-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Silene ampullata is an annual or biennial herb that reaches heights of up to 60 cm.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
