Succisa sylvatica

Succisa sylvatica, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is herbaceous, with a short, often hairy stem that grows from the base. Wood sorrel thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
