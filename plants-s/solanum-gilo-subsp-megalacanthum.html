Solanum gilo subsp. megalacanthum

Solanum gilo subsp.

megalacanthum, commonly known as the large-fruited gooseberry, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, egg-shaped berries that are often green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a traditional food source in some indigenous communities.
