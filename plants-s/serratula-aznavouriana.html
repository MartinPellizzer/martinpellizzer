Serratula aznavouriana

Serratula aznavouriana, commonly known as the Armenian sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a loose inflorescence.

Serratula aznavouriana is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
