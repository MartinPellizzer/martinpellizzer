Smilax tijucensis var. pohliana

Smilax tijucensis var. pohliana, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or Brazilian smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a thick, woody stem covered in spines and possesses compound leaves with long, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
