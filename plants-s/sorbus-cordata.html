Sorbus cordata

Sorbus cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Sorbus cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
