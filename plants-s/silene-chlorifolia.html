Silene chlorifolia

Silene chlorifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are coated in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. Silene chlorifolia is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
