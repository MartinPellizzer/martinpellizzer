Santolina chamaecyparissus var. mariolensis

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. mariolensis, commonly known as the Spanish santolina, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features silver-gray foliage with a fine, velvety texture, and small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous scent and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its botanical classification places it within the family Asteraceae, highlighting its relationship to other members of the sunflower family.
