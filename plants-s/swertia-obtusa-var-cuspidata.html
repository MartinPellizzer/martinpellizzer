Swertia obtusa var. cuspidata

Swertia obtusa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
