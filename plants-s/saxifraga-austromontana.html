Saxifraga austromontana

Saxifraga austromontana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga austromontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
