Solena trinitatis

Solena trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Solena trinitatis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
