Seetzenia prostrata

Seetzenia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate setaria, is a low-growing annual grass species native to parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, branching stems that lie close to the soil surface. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it can be a persistent weed in certain environments.
