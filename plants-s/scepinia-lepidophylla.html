Scepinia lepidophylla

Scepinia lepidophylla, commonly known as the "fairy fan flower," is a unique desert plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble small fans, which open in response to moisture. The plant is a member of the aster family and is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions by storing water in its succulent leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Scepinia lepidophylla is also notable for its ability to curl up and appear dormant when dry, only to unfurl when water becomes available.
