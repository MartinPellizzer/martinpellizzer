Salvia ocimifolia

Salvia ocimifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils are often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
