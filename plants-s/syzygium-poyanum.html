Syzygium poyanum

Syzygium poyanum, commonly known as the Poyanum apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. Syzygium poyanum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
