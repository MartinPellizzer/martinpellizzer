Sadiria boweri

Sadiria boweri, commonly known as the Bower's lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Sadiria boweri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
