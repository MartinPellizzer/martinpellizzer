Scutellaria nervosa

Scutellaria nervosa, commonly known as the veined skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria nervosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
