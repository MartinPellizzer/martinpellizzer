Sclerochaetium spirale

Sclerochaetium spirale, commonly known as the spiral spore plant, is a unique member of the Lepidocarpaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of spore-bearing structures, which are typically found on the surface of its stems. The plant produces spores in specialized clusters called sporangia, which are often arranged in a helical pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and spore development.
