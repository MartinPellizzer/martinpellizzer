Sorghum durra var. elongatum

Sorghum durra var. elongatum, commonly known as long-stalk sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated stalks and is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, arid climates, making it an important crop in regions with limited water resources. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a robust root system that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant's versatility and adaptability contribute to its significance in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
