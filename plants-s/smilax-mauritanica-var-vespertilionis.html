Smilax mauritanica var. vespertilionis

Smilax mauritanica var. vespertilionis, commonly known as the evening bat smilax, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny projections that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

This species is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
