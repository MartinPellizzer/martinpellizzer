Sabicea aspera var. latifolia

Sabicea aspera var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
