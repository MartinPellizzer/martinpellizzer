Sorbus × arnoldiana

Sorbus × arnoldiana, commonly known as the Arnold whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow berries that ripen in the autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–15 meters and has a rounded, sometimes irregular crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
