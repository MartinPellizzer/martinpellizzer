Sideritis peyrei subsp. castellana

Sideritis peyrei subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly found in Spain and Portugal. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
