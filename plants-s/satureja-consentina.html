Satureja consentina

Satureja consentina, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Satureja consentina is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, with a strong, thyme-like aroma.
