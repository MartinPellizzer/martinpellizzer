Silene inflata var. intricata

Silene inflata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that serve as specialized organs for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, where it thrives in the presence of standing water. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape.

This variant is notable for its intricate floral morphology and its unique adaptation to aquatic habitats.
