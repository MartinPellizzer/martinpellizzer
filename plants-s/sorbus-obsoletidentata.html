Sorbus obsoletidentata

Sorbus obsoletidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often narrowly elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes.
