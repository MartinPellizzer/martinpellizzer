Senecio quinquelobus

Senecio quinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio quinquelobus is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
