Sphalmanthus

Sphalmanthus, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble chains, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and often thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Sphalmanthus species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value and others for their medicinal properties.
