Sorbus cochleariformis

Sorbus cochleariformis, commonly known as the ear-shaped rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, and it can grow as either a shrub or a small tree.

Sorbus cochleariformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
