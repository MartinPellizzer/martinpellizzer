Sagina maxima f. crassicaulis

Sagina maxima f. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its dense, prostrate growth habit.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its compact structure and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
