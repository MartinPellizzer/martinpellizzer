Spiraea alba var. typica

Spiraea alba var. typica, commonly known as white spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea alba var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance.
