Silene mundiana

Silene mundiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene mundiana is often found in disturbed or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
