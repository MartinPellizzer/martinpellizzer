Scleria cyperina

Scleria cyperina, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, three-sided spikelets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, with the lower portion being male and the upper portion female. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its sedge-like morphology.

Scleria cyperina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in wetland ecosystems.
