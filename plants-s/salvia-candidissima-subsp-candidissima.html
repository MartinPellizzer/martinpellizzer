Salvia candidissima subsp. candidissima

Salvia candidissima subsp.

candidissima, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, grey-green leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
