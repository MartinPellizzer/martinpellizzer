Symplocos claussenii

Symplocos claussenii, commonly known as the Clausen's wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and small, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Symplocos claussenii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
