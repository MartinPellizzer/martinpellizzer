Scirpus micranthus

Scirpus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. Scirpus micranthus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along lake shores.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic wildlife and contributing to water filtration in wetland ecosystems.
