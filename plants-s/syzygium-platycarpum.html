Syzygium platycarpum

Syzygium platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its distinctive, broad, elliptical fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Syzygium platycarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential use in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
