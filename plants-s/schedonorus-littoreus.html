Schedonorus littoreus

Schedonorus littoreus, commonly known as sea oats, is a coastal grass native to the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and long, drooping seed heads that sway with the wind. This plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is highly adapted to salt spray and tidal conditions. Its extensive root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

Schedonorus littoreus plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of coastal ecosystems.
