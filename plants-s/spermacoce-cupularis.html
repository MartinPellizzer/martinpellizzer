Spermacoce cupularis

Spermacoce cupularis, commonly known as the cupular spurred amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Spermacoce cupularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
