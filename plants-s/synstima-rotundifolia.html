Synstima rotundifolia

Synstima rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf synstima, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color, blooming during the cooler months. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
