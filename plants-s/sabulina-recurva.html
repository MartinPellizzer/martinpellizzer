Sabulina recurva

Sabulina recurva, commonly known as the bentleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

Sabulina recurva plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
