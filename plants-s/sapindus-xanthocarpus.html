Sapindus xanthocarpus

Sapindus xanthocarpus, commonly known as the golden fruit soapberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn yellow when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a starchy pulp. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a natural soap due to its saponin content.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
