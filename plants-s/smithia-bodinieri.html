Smithia bodinieri

Smithia bodinieri, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Smithia bodinieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
