Serapias ionica

Serapias ionica, commonly known as the Ionian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy layer to reduce water loss.

Serapias ionica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
