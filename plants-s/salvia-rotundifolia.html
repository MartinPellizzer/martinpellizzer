Salvia rotundifolia

Salvia rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both gardens and botanical collections.
