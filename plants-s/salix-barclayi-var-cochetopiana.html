Salix barclayi var. cochetopiana

Salix barclayi var. cochetopiana, commonly known as the Cochetopa willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkins, which are typical of willow species. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and mountainous regions. Its bark is typically gray to reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The Cochetopa willow plays an important ecological role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
