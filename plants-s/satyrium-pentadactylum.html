Satyrium pentadactylum

Satyrium pentadactylum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid blooms during the rainy season, showcasing its vibrant colors and playing a role in local ecosystems.
