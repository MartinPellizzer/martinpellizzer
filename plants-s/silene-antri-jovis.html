Silene antri-jovis

Silene antri-jovis, commonly known as the cave of Jupiter's flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the limestone caves of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vivid red or pink flowers, which bloom in the spring and are adapted to thrive in low-light, humid environments. The plant's morphology includes slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve moisture. Its unique habitat makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptations to subterranean ecosystems.

Due to its restricted range and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
