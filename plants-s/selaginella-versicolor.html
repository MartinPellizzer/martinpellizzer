Selaginella versicolor

Selaginella versicolor, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often variable foliage.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in challenging conditions. Its leaves are usually elongated and may display a range of colors, contributing to its common name.

Selaginella versicolor plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
