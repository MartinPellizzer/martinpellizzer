Sanguisorba hakusanensis f. coreana

Sanguisorba hakusanensis f. coreana, commonly known as the Korean dropwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
