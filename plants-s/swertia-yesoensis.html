Swertia yesoensis

Swertia yesoensis, commonly known as the Yesso Swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Swertia yesoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
