Saxifraga qinghaiensis

Saxifraga qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Qinghai Province in China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in the harsh, arid conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
