Salvia charpini

Salvia charpini, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Salvia charpini thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
