Solanum minax

Solanum minax, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are highly toxic and contain alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
