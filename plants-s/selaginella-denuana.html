Selaginella denuana

Selaginella denuana, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, low-growing mat. Selaginella denuana is characterized by its ability to reproduce through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

This species is also notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
