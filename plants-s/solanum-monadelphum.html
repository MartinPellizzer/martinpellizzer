Solanum monadelphum

Solanum monadelphum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red bush tomato, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its reddish-orange flowers and small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Solanum monadelphum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
