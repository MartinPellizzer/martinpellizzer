Solanum ovigerum var. luteum

Solanum ovigerum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, and it produces small, orange berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
