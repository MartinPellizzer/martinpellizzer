Solanum acanthodapis

Solanum acanthodapis, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum acanthodapis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
