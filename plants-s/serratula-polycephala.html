Serratula polycephala

Serratula polycephala, commonly known as the clustered sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of dark green, toothed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Serratula polycephala is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
