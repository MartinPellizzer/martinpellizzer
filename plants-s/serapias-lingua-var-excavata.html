Serapias lingua var. excavata

Serapias lingua var. excavata, commonly known as the "hollow-tongued orchid," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Serapias, which is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The variety 'excavata' is distinguished by its deeply grooved or excavated flower petals, which give it its specific epithet.
