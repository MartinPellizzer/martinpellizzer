Sprengelia incarnata f. propinqua

Sprengelia incarnata f. propinqua, commonly known as the pink-flowered sprengelia, is a hybrid form of the genus Sprengelia, native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.
