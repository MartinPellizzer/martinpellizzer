Sapota latifolia

Sapota latifolia, commonly known as the greater soapnut, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is well-known for its fruit, which has a rough, spiky exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antifungal and antibacterial properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
