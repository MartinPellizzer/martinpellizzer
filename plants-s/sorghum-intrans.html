Sorghum intrans

Sorghum intrans, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is often cultivated for its edible grains, which are used in food and animal feed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
