Silene scouleri subsp. pringlei

Silene scouleri subsp.

pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
