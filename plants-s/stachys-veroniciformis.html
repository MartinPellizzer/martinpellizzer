Stachys veroniciformis

Stachys veroniciformis, commonly known as velvetleaf stachys, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Stachys veroniciformis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
