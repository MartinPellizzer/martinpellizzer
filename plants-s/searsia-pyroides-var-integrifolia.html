Searsia pyroides var. integrifolia

Searsia pyroides var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are smooth and glossy, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
