Solanum tuberosum var. corsicanum

Solanum tuberosum var. corsicanum, commonly known as Corsican potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, and is characterized by its distinctive tuber morphology. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits a herbaceous growth habit. Its tubers are typically smaller and have a more complex flavor profile compared to standard potatoes.

This variety is valued for its unique taste and potential use in regional cuisines.
