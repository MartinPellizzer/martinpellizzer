Sonchus radicatus

Sonchus radicatus, commonly known as common sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, with both ray and disk florets.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is considered a weed in many regions.
