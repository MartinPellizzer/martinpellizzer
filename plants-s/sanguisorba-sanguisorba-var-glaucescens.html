Sanguisorba sanguisorba var. glaucescens

Sanguisorba sanguisorba var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive bluish-green hue on top.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
