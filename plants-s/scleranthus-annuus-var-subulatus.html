Scleranthus annuus var. subulatus

Scleranthus annuus var. subulatus, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
