Salvia tenuior

Salvia tenuior, commonly known as slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Salvia tenuior is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
