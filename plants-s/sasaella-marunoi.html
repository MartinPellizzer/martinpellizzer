Sasaella marunoi

Sasaella marunoi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sasaella marunoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
