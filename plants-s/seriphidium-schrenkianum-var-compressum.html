Seriphidium schrenkianum var. compressum

Seriphidium schrenkianum var. compressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, compressed stems and small, grey-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in dryland gardens.
