Sasa asagishiana

Sasa asagishiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Sasa asagishiana is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an effective groundcover in natural settings.
