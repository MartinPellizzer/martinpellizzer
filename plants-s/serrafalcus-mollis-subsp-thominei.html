Serrafalcus mollis subsp. thominei

Serrafalcus mollis subsp.

thominei, commonly known as the Thomine's hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer months, which are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance plants.
