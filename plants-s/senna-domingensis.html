Senna domingensis

Senna domingensis, commonly known as the golden senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry to moist tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Senna domingensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported laxative effects.
