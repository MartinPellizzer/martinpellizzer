Seruneum pulchellum

Seruneum pulchellum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The flowers are typically pale blue or white, with intricate patterns that mimic the shape of insects, aiding in pollination by attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its beauty and rarity, Seruneum pulchellum is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
