Salix babylonica var. japonica

Salix babylonica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
