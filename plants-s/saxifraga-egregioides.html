Saxifraga egregioides

Saxifraga egregioides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Saxifraga egregioides is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, showcasing resilience to cold and drought.
