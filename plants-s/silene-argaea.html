Silene argaea

Silene argaea, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Silene argaea is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological zones.
