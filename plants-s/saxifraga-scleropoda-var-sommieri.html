Saxifraga scleropoda var. sommieri

Saxifraga scleropoda var. sommieri, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically in June or July. It is adapted to harsh climates with high altitudes and low temperatures, showcasing resilience to cold and wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
