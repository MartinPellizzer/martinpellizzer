Stisseria × mutabilis

Stisseria × mutabilis, commonly known as the variable stisseria, is a hybrid plant species within the Stisseriaceae family.

It is characterized by its variable morphology, including changes in leaf shape and flower color depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
