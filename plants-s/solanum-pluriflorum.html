Solanum pluriflorum

Solanum pluriflorum, commonly known as the everlasting tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to retain its flowers for extended periods, giving it the common name. Its fruit, which resembles a tomato, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

Solanum pluriflorum thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
