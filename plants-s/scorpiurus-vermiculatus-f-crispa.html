Scorpiurus vermiculatus f. crispa

Scorpiurus vermiculatus f. crispa, commonly known as the crisped sandwort, is a succulent plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, waxy leaves.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant's leaves are typically bluish-gray, with a distinctive crinkled or curled texture that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to rocky or sandy landscapes.

This variety is prized for its drought tolerance and ability to form low-growing mats, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
