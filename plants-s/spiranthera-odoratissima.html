Spiranthera odoratissima

Spiranthera odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its clustered, fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its use in traditional medicine.
