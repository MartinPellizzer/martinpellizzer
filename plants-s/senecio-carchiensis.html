Senecio carchiensis

Senecio carchiensis, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio carchiensis is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
