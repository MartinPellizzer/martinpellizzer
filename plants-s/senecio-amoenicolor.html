Senecio amoenicolor

Senecio amoenicolor, commonly known as the blue-eared cyanea, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in various regions.
