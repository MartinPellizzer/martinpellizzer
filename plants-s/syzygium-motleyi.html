Syzygium motleyi

Syzygium motleyi, commonly known as the Motley’s apple or Motley’s tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium motleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in traditional medicine.
