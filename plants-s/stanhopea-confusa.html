Stanhopea confusa

Stanhopea confusa, commonly known as the yellow stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flower spikes, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by specific insects, such as beetles.

Stanhopea confusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
