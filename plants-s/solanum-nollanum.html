Solanum nollanum

Solanum nollanum, commonly known as the Nollan's nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
