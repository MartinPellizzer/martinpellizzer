Salsola chinensis

Salsola chinensis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Salsola chinensis is known for its ability to spread rapidly, sometimes becoming an invasive species in new environments.
