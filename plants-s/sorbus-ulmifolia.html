Sorbus ulmifolia

Sorbus ulmifolia, commonly known as the common whitebeams, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish in younger trees.

Sorbus ulmifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
