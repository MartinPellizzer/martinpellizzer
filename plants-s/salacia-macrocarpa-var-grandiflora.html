Salacia macrocarpa var. grandiflora

Salacia macrocarpa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant salacia, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
