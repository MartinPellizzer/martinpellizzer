Sideritis penzigii

Sideritis penzigii, commonly known as the Balkan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Sideritis penzigii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
