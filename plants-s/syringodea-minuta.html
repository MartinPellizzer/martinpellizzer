Syringodea minuta

Syringodea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf syringodea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces delicate white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes.

Syringodea minuta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodland environments.
