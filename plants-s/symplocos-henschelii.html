Symplocos henschelii

Symplocos henschelii, commonly known as the Henschel's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are small, round berries that mature to a dark red or black color.

Symplocos henschelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
