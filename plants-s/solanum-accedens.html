Solanum accedens

Solanum accedens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum accedens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
