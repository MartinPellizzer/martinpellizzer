Smilax jalapensis var. botterii

Smilax jalapensis var. botterii, commonly known as the Mexican catclaw, is a vine species native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to both woodland and disturbed habitats.
