Scrophularia diversifolia

Scrophularia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive two-lipped structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

Scrophularia diversifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
