Syzygium idanum

Syzygium idanum, commonly known as the Idanum apple or mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor.

Syzygium idanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
