Seruneum populifolium

Seruneum populifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down on the underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

Seruneum populifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
