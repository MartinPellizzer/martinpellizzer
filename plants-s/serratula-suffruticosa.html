Serratula suffruticosa

Serratula suffruticosa, commonly known as the shrubby sicklewort, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Serratula suffruticosa is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
