Streblus solomonensis

Streblus solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's banyan, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by fig-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
