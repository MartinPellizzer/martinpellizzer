Scabiosa ochroleuca var. balcanica

Scabiosa ochroleuca var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and pale blue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
