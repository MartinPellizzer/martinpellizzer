Sansevieria burmanica

Sansevieria burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese snake plant, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with subtle variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it highly adaptable to various indoor environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clusters over time.

Sansevieria burmanica is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
