Senecio squalidus var. bipinnatifidus

Senecio squalidus var. bipinnatifidus, commonly known as the ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and deeply lobed, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
