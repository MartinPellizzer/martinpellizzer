Sasa guangdongensis

Sasa guangdongensis, commonly known as Guangdong bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa guangdongensis is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
