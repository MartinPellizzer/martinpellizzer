Scleranthus annuus subsp. aetnensis

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.

The plant has slender, branched stems covered with fine hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The subspecies is characterized by its adaptation to the unique environmental conditions of the volcanic soil and climate of Mount Etna.
