Sorghum centroplicatum var. sabderatense

Sorghum centroplicatum var. sabderatense, commonly known as the Sabderatense sorghum, is a wild species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often studied for its potential in forage and as a source of genetic diversity for agricultural improvement.
