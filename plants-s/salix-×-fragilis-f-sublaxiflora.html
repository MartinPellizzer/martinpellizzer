Salix × fragilis f. sublaxiflora

Salix × fragilis f. sublaxiflora, commonly known as the silky willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its delicate, silky-haired leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are often hairy on the upper surface, giving it a soft, silvery appearance.

It produces catkins in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
