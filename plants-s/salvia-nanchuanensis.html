Salvia nanchuanensis

Salvia nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Salvia nanchuanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
