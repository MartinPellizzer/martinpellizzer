Sterculia cinnamomifolia

Sterculia cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or sweet gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing oval seeds. Its fruit, known as a "cinnamon apple," is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
