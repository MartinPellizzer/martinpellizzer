Swertia plantaginea

Swertia plantaginea, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Swertia plantaginea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in Tibetan and Chinese herbal medicine.
