Scirpus atrocinctus f. grandis

Scirpus atrocinctus f. grandis, commonly known as the giant tussock sedge, is a variant of the tussock sedge species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and dense, grass-like foliage, which can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
