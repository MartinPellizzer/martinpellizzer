Suaeda corniculata

Suaeda corniculata, commonly known as the horned evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant with succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to water conservation. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal areas.

Suaeda corniculata is also known for its ability to tolerate extreme drought and high salt concentrations.
