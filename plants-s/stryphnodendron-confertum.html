Stryphnodendron confertum

Stryphnodendron confertum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color, which has made it highly valued in the musical instrument industry. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species, prompting conservation efforts to protect its population.
