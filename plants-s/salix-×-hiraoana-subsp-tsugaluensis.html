Salix × hiraoana subsp. tsugaluensis

Salix × hiraoana subsp.

tsugaluensis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a pale green underside.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, which contributes to its ecological importance in riparian zones.
