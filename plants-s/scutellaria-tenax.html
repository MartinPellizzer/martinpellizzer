Scutellaria tenax

Scutellaria tenax, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Scutellaria tenax is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential use in traditional herbal remedies.
