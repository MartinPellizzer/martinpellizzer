Salix lemmonii var. macrostachya

Salix lemmonii var. macrostachya, commonly known as the Lemmon willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. Its growth habit is generally upright, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The leaves are usually gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
