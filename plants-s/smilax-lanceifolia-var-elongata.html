Smilax lanceifolia var. elongata

Smilax lanceifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed arrowwood, is a variant of the arrowwood plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
