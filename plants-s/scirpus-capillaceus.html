Scirpus capillaceus

Scirpus capillaceus, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a fibrous, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and capable of stabilizing soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus capillaceus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
