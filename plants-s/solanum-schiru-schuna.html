Solanum schiru-schuna

Solanum schiru-schuna, commonly known as the Schiru-schuna nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its berries are typically small and yellow when ripe, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
