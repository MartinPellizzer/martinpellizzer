Scaevola scabrida

Scaevola scabrida, commonly known as the rough scabious, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
