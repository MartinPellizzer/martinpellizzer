Serapias dupuyana

Serapias dupuyana, commonly known as the Dupuy's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
