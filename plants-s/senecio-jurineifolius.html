Senecio jurineifolius

Senecio jurineifolius, commonly known as the Jurine's groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow daisy-like flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
