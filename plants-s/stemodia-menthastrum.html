Stemodia menthastrum

Stemodia menthastrum, commonly known as lemon-scented herb or lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. The plant has slender, erect stems that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal cultivation.

Its essential oils contain compounds like citral, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.
