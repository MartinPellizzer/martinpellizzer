Serichonus

Serichonus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
