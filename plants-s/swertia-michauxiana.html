Swertia michauxiana

Swertia michauxiana, commonly known as the Indian skyblue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Swertia michauxiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
