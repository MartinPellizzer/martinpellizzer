Senecio hirtifolius

Senecio hirtifolius, commonly known as the hairyleaf senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

Senecio hirtifolius is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
