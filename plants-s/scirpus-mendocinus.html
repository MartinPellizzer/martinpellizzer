Scirpus mendocinus

Scirpus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to California.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually yellow-green and appear at the top of the stems, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus mendocinus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
