Stenochlaena juglandifolia

Stenochlaena juglandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.

Stenochlaena juglandifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
