Silene rubella subsp. turbinata

Silene rubella subsp.

turbinata, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often succulent foliage.
