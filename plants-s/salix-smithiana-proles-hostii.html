Salix smithiana proles hostii

Salix smithiana proles hostii, commonly known as the Hostii Weeping Willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree known for its distinctive cascading branches.

It is a deciduous tree that features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. Native to the United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun. This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to create a dramatic visual effect.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for pond edges or as a specimen tree in gardens.
