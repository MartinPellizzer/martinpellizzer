Sapium baccatum

Sapium baccatum, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or red root, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces distinctive, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, red aril. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sapium baccatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of climates.
