Stipa grafiana var. paradoxa

Stipa grafiana var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Stipa, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds visual interest to its habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
