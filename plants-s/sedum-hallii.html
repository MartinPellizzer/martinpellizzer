Sedum hallii

Sedum hallii, commonly known as the Hall's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, typically from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum hallii is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in arid environments.
