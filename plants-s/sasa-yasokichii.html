Sasa yasokichii

Sasa yasokichii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its culms are slender and often have a smooth, slightly woody texture.

Sasa yasokichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
