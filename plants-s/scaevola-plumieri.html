Scaevola plumieri

Scaevola plumieri, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and salt spray. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scaevola plumieri is well adapted to harsh coastal environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its resilience and beauty.
