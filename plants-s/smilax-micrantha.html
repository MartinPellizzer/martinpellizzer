Smilax micrantha

Smilax micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often consumed by birds.

Smilax micrantha is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
