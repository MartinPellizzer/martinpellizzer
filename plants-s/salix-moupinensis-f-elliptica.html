Salix moupinensis f. elliptica

Salix moupinensis f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic weeping willow, is a unique form of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often a striking green, with a silvery underside. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its graceful, drooping branches make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
