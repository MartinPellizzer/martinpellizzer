Selago walpersii

Selago walpersii, commonly known as the silverleaf lily.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

Selago walpersii is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
