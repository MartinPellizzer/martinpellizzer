Selinum podagraria

Selinum podagraria, commonly known as ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features spreading rhizomes and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lawns and along pathways.

Selinum podagraria is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
