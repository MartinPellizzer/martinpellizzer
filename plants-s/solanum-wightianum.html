Solanum wightianum

Solanum wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum wightianum is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
