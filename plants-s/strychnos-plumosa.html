Strychnos plumosa

Strychnos plumosa, commonly known as the plumose strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are often feather-like in appearance, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
