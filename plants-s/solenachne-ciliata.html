Solenachne ciliata

Solenachne ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of pollen. Solenachne ciliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
