Syzygium jambos var. sylvaticum

Syzygium jambos var. sylvaticum, commonly known as wild rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, clustered fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The fruit is typically small, hairy, and contains a single seed, with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
