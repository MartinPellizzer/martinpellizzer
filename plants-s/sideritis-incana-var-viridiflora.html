Sideritis incana var. viridiflora

Sideritis incana var. viridiflora, commonly known as greenflower hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
