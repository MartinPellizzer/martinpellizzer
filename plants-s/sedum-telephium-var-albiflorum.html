Sedum telephium var. albiflorum

Sedum telephium var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of white flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically blue-green, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The species is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom reliably in mild climates.
