Solanum pseudogeminifolium

Solanum pseudogeminifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, oval leaves that are often arranged in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically yellow or red, are toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum pseudogeminifolium is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
