Spiraeanthemum macgillivrayi

Spiraeanthemum macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's spirea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
