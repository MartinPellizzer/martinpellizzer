Strychnos tepicensis

Strychnos tepicensis, commonly known as the Mexican strychnos, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain small, black seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
