Styrax odoratissimus

Styrax odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, pale green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces hard, spiky fruits that contain small, dark seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
