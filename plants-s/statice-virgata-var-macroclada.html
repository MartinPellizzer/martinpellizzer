Statice virgata var. macroclada

Statice virgata var. macroclada, commonly known as sea lavender or desert lavender, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for dry, sandy environments. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in harsh, saline conditions.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
