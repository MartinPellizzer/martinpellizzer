Simaba glabra var. emarginata

Simaba glabra var. emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian sand cherry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by cherry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
