Styrax parviflorus

Styrax parviflorus, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Styrax parviflorus thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
