Syzygium curvistylum

Syzygium curvistylum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sour taste.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes in its native habitat.
