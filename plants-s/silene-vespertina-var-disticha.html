Silene vespertina var. disticha

Silene vespertina var. disticha, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
