Sporobolus michauxianus

Sporobolus michauxianus, commonly known as Michaux's sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
