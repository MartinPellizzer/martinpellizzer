Scabiosa monspeliaca

Scabiosa monspeliaca, commonly known as the mountain scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Scabiosa monspeliaca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
