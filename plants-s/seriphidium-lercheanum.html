Seriphidium lercheanum

Seriphidium lercheanum, commonly known as the desert chicory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Seriphidium lercheanum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
