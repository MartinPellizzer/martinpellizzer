Stemonurus umbellatus

Stemonurus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stemonurus umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
