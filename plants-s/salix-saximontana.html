Salix saximontana

Salix saximontana, commonly known as the saximontana willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and has small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
