Sesleria uliginosa f. micrantha

Sesleria uliginosa f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered bog sedge, is a compact, clump-forming perennial grassland plant native to wet and acidic habitats in Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and heathlands. Its foliage is typically dense and low-growing, contributing to the structure of its ecosystem.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for invertebrates.
