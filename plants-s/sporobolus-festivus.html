Sporobolus festivus

Sporobolus festivus, commonly known as festive spikerush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, and is adapted to saturated soil conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that contributes to its ability to colonize new areas.

Sporobolus festivus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
