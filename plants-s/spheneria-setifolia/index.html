Spheneria Setifolia

Spheneria setifolia is a small, delicate liverwort commonly found in moist, shaded environments. This plant belongs to the family Porellaceae and is known for its intricate leaf structures. Its name, meaning "bristly" in Latin, refers to the fine, hair-like structures on its leaves. Spheneria setifolia plays an important role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms. Despite its modest size, it is a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
