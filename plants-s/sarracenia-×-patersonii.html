Sarracenia × patersonii

Sarracenia × patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped traps to capture insects. The plant's red or orange-red coloration, often with green veins, is due to the presence of anthocyanins. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × patersonii is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
