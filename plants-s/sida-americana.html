Sida americana

Sida americana, commonly known as American sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
