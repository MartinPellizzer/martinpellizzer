Solanum stenotomum f. cuchipacon

Solanum stenotomum f. cuchipacon, commonly known as the Cuchipacon tomato, is a botanical variant of the Solanum stenotomum species.

It is native to South America, particularly Peru, and is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and the fruits mature to a bright red color.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
