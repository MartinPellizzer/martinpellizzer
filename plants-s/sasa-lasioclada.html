Sasa lasioclada

Sasa lasioclada, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Sasa lasioclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
