Solanum physalidicalyx var. plurilobulatum

Solanum physalidicalyx var. plurilobulatum, commonly known as the multilobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically open in the morning. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
