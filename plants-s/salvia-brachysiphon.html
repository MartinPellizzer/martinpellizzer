Salvia brachysiphon

Salvia brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia brachysiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
