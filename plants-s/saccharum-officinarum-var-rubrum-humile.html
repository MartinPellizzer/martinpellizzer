Saccharum officinarum var. rubrum-humile

Saccharum officinarum var. rubrum-humile, commonly known as the red dwarf sugarcane, is a variant of the sugarcane species characterized by its smaller stature and unique coloration.

This plant typically exhibits reddish hues in its leaves and stems, which distinguish it from the more common green varieties of sugarcane. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and abundant sunlight for optimal growth. The plant's compact size makes it suitable for smaller cultivation spaces, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and genetic research.
