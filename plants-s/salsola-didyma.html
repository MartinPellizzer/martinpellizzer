Salsola didyma

Salsola didyma, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and disturbed soils, and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Salsola didyma is an annual plant that produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.

Its succulent leaves help it conserve water, making it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions.
