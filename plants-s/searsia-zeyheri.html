Searsia zeyheri

Searsia zeyheri, commonly known as the yellow cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.

Searsia zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
