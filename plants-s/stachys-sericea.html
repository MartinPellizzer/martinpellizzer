Stachys sericea

Stachys sericea, commonly known as silver hair vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
