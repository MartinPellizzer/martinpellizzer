Spiraea veitchii

Spiraea veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea veitchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
