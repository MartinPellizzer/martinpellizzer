Sedum sukaczevii

Sedum sukaczevii, commonly known as the Sukaczev's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
