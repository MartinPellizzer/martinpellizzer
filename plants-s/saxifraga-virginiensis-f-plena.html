Saxifraga virginiensis f. plena

Saxifraga virginiensis f. plena, commonly known as the Virgin's bride or the white saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a silvery appearance. Its showy, clustered inflorescences consist of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
