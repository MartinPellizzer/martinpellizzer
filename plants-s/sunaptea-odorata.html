Sunaptea odorata

Sunaptea odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented sunaptea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a mild, pleasant aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
