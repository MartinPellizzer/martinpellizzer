Salsola inermis

Salsola inermis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a hardy, annual or biennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits. It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.

Salsola inermis is adapted to poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
