Scutellaria siphocampyloides

Scutellaria siphocampyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Scutellaria siphocampyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
