Strychnos mitis

Strychnos mitis, commonly known as the African angel's trumpets, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange pod, which is often used in traditional medicine.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
