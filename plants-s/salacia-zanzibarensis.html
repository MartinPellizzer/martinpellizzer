Salacia zanzibarensis

Salacia zanzibarensis, commonly known as Zanzibar salacia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa, particularly Zanzibar.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a glossy surface, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Salacia zanzibarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
