Sambucus racemosa f. hiraii

Sambucus racemosa f. hiraii, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a botanical variant of the common elderberry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
