Sylphia

Sylphia, commonly known as the meadow beauty, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring well-drained soil. Sylphia species are perennial herbs with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

They are valued in gardens for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
