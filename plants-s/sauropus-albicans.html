Sauropus albicans

Sauropus albicans, commonly known as the white-veined sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins. Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
