Schoenus megacarpus

Schoenus megacarpus, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, cylindrical inflorescences that contain numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and form dense mats that stabilize wetland soils.

Schoenus megacarpus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
