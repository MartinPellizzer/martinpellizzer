Syringa vulgaris f. coerulea

Syringa vulgaris f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac that is prized for its striking blue-violet flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its fragrant, clustered blooms that appear in early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
