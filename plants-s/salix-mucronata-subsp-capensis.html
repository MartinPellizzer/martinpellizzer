Salix mucronata subsp. capensis

Salix mucronata subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
