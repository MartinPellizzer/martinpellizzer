Salsola giessii

Salsola giessii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and pale green to bluish-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Salsola giessii is well adapted to harsh environments, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.

It often thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is commonly found in desert ecosystems.
