Solanum bromoense

Solanum bromoense, commonly known as the Bromo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may be used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
