Sedum pauciflorum

Sedum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
