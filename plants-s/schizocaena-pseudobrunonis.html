Schizocaena pseudobrunonis

Schizocaena pseudobrunonis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved schizocaena, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.
