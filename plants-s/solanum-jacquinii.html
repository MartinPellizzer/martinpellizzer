Solanum jacquinii

Solanum jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
