Salvia byzantina

Salvia byzantina, commonly known as Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems and produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia byzantina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
