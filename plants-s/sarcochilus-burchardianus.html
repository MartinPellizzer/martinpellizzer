Sarcochilus burchardianus

Sarcochilus burchardianus, commonly known as the Burchard's ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered epiphytic orchid native to the subtropical rainforests of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to yellowish flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of leaves. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, showy inflorescence with several small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
