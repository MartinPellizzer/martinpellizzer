Saponaria sicula subsp. intermedia

Saponaria sicula subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Sicilian soapwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a mild soap-like substance from its roots, which has historical uses in traditional medicine.
