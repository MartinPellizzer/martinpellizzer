Scirpus albus

Scirpus albus, commonly known as white bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus albus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
