Senecio scaphiformis

Senecio scaphiformis, commonly known as the arrowhead lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are arrowhead-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a head-like structure, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio scaphiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
