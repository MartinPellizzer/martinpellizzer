Syzygium adelphicum var. adenanthum

Syzygium adelphicum var. adenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple or adenanthum apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to yellow or red when ripe.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
