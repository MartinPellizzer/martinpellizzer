Sidalcea hickmanii subsp. viridis

Sidalcea hickmanii subsp.

viridis, commonly known as Hickman's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
