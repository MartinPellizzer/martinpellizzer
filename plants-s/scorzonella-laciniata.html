Scorzonella laciniata

Scorzonella laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved scorzonera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Scorzonella laciniata is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
