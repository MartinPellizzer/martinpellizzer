Serapias parviflora var. lutescens

Serapias parviflora var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange-yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, sun-like inflorescences.
