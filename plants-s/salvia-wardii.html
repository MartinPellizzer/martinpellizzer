Salvia wardii

Salvia wardii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, minty scent when crushed.

Salvia wardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
