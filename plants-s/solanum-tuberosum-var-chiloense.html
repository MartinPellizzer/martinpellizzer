Solanum tuberosum var. chiloense

Solanum tuberosum var. chiloense, commonly known as the Chilean potato, is a variant of the common potato native to South America.

It is characterized by its tubers, which are typically smaller and have a higher starch content compared to other potato varieties. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and features compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and has been cultivated for centuries in regions like Chile and Peru.
