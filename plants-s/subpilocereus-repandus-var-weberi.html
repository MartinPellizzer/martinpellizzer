Subpilocereus repandus var. weberi

Subpilocereus repandus var. weberi, commonly known as the Weber's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
