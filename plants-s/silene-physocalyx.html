Silene physocalyx

Silene physocalyx, commonly known as the showy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene physocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
