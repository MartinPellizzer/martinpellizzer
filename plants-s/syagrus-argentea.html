Syagrus argentea

Syagrus argentea, commonly known as the queen of the palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that eventually develop into ornamental fruits resembling small pineapples. Syagrus argentea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
