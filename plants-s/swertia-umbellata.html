Swertia umbellata

Swertia umbellata, commonly known as the Indian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Swertia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
