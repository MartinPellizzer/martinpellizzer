Syzygium arcanum

Syzygium arcanum, commonly known as the golden apple gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and attractive, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, yellowish-red fruits that are similar in appearance to apples. Syzygium arcanum is often found in coastal and riverine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role, providing food for various native animals and birds.
