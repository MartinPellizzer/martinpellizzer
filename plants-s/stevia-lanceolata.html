Stevia lanceolata

Stevia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia lanceolata is often used in herbal teas and as a natural sweetener alternative.
