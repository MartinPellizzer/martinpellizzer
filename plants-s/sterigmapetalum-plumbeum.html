Sterigmapetalum plumbeum

Sterigmapetalum plumbeum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its most distinctive feature is the clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant is well adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
