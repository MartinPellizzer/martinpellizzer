Stevia petiolata

Stevia petiolata, commonly known as the leafy stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Stevia petiolata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
