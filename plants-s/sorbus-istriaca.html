Sorbus istriaca

Sorbus istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
