Syzygium laeve

Syzygium laeve, commonly known as the satinwood or white sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium laeve is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both landscaping and agroforestry in tropical regions.
