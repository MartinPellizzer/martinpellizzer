Selago venosa

Selago venosa, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Selago venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
