Selaginella intermedia

Selaginella intermedia, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing plant with a rosette-like structure and produces spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are typically scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Selaginella intermedia is known for its ability to survive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
