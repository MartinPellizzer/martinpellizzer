Salvia blancoana subsp. maurorum

Salvia blancoana subsp.

maurorum, commonly known as the Maurorian blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and aromatic foliage.
