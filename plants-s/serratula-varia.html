Serratula varia

Serratula varia, commonly known as the variable sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and wastelands. Its leaves are serrated and alternate, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a clustered head.

Serratula varia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
