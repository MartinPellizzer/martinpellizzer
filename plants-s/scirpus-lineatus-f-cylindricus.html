Scirpus lineatus f. cylindricus

Scirpus lineatus f. cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus Scirpus.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its cylindrical, narrow leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Scirpus lineatus f. cylindricus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
