Sideritis hyssopifolia var. phyllantha

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. phyllantha, commonly known as the leafy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
