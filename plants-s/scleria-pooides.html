Scleria pooides

Scleria pooides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scleria pooides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
