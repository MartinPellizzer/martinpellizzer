Silene mollissima subsp. velutina

Silene mollissima subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
