Sedum pallidum subsp. bithynicum

Sedum pallidum subsp.

bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
