Scirpus gracilis

Scirpus gracilis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-white flowers.

Scirpus gracilis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
