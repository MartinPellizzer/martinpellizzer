Seseli gummiferum subsp. gummiferum

Seseli gummiferum subsp.

gummiferum, commonly known as the gum arabic sesame, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

The subspecies is notable for its latex-rich stems, which have been historically used in the production of gum arabic.
