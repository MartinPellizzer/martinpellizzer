Scilla monstrosa

Scilla monstrosa, commonly known as the giant scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, nodding blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Scilla monstrosa is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to naturalize over time.
