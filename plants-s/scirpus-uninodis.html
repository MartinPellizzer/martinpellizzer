Scirpus uninodis

Scirpus uninodis, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered brown flowers that are typically found at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a single node, giving it its scientific name.

Scirpus uninodis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
