Schanginia trigyna

Schanginia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in humid climates. Its roots are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates, while its aerial roots absorb moisture from the air.

Schanginia trigyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
