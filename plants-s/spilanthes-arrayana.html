Spilanthes arrayana

Spilanthes arrayana, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its pungent, peppery scent and the presence of active compounds such as spilanthol, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and the plant thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Spilanthes arrayana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
