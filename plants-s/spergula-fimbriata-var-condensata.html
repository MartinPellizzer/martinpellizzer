Spergula fimbriata var. condensata

Spergula fimbriata var. condensata, commonly known as the dense-fringed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting insect populations.
