Solanum nigrum subsp. ochroleucum

Solanum nigrum subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant features hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are usually green when immature and turn red or yellow when ripe.

The berries contain toxic alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful if ingested.
