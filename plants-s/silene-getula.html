Silene getula

Silene getula, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Silene getula has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
