Samaipaticereus inquisivensis

Samaipaticereus inquisivensis, commonly known as the Inquisitive Cactus, is a rare and striking species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach several meters in height, covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the dry season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
