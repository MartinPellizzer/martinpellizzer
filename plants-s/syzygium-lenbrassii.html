Syzygium lenbrassii

Syzygium lenbrassii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped red or orange flowers that appear during the holiday season. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy texture, while its fruit is a small, edible drupe.
