Spiranthes pachychila

Spiranthes pachychila, commonly known as the broad-leaf ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has long, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and forest floors. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
