Sideritis incana var. occidentalis

Sideritis incana var. occidentalis, commonly known as western hoary sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its grayish-blue, hairy leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Sideritis species are valued for their potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
