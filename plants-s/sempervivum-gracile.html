Sempervivum gracile

Sempervivum gracile, commonly known as the dwarf houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the common name "everlasting," reflecting its resilience and longevity.
