Spiraea zuccarinii

Spiraea zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarini weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its foliage turns yellow in autumn, enhancing its seasonal appeal.

Spiraea zuccarinii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
