Spergularia succulenta

Spergularia succulenta, commonly known as the succulent sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is typically found growing in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or coastal environments. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture in dry conditions, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Spergularia succulenta is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
