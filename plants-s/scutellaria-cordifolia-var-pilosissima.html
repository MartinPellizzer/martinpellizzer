Scutellaria cordifolia var. pilosissima

Scutellaria cordifolia var. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
