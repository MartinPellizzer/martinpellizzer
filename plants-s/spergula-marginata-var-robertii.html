Spergula marginata var. robertii

Spergula marginata var. robertii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive marginal toothed edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
