Smallanthus quichensis

Smallanthus quichensis, commonly known as the Andean quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and branched stems. The plant is adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments with poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often used as a vegetable, while the seeds are a nutrient-rich pseudocereal.

Smallanthus quichensis is an important crop for local communities and has gained global recognition for its nutritional value.
