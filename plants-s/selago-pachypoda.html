Selago pachypoda

Selago pachypoda, commonly known as the broadleaf selago, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Selago pachypoda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
