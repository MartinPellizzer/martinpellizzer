Sauvagesia longifolia

Sauvagesia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered sauvagesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sauvagesia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
