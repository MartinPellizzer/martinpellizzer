Stachys cretica subsp. bulgarica

Stachys cretica subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
