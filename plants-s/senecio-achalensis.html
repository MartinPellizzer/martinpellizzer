Senecio achalensis

Senecio achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis groundsels, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio achalensis is adapted to survive in dry conditions, showcasing features like reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems.
