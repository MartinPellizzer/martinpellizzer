Schizocodon soldanelloides f. niveus

Schizocodon soldanelloides f. niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Schizocodon genus.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and compact, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and unique appearance.
