Selinum hippomarathrum

Selinum hippomarathrum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to coastal environments. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Selinum hippomarathrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils.
