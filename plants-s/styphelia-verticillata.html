Styphelia verticillata

Styphelia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, evergreen, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Styphelia verticillata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal heath communities.
