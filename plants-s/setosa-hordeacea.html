Setosa hordeacea

Setosa hordeacea, commonly known as the slender barley, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellow flowers. Its seeds are typically small and oval, adapted for dispersal by wind.

Setosa hordeacea is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
