Stelis casanaensis

Stelis casanaensis, commonly known as the Casanensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, and the inflorescence bears a cluster of small, colorful flowers.

Stelis casanaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
