Seriphidium pauciflorum

Seriphidium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves.

Seriphidium pauciflorum is often found in open habitats such as wastelands and along roadsides.
