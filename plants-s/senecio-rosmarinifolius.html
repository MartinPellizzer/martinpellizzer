Senecio rosmarinifolius

Senecio rosmarinifolius, commonly known as blueweed or blue fennel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Senecio rosmarinifolius is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
