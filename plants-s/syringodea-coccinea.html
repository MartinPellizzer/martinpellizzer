Syringodea coccinea

Syringodea coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.
