Saxifraga virginiensis

Saxifraga virginiensis, commonly known as Virginia saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and shaded woodlands. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with lance-shaped, toothed leaves. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Saxifraga virginiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
