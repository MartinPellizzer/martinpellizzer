Salvia rumicifolia var. minor

Salvia rumicifolia var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense whorls along the stems. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
