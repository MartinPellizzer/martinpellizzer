Selaginella sechellarum

Selaginella sechellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles spikemoss, is a rare and endangered species native to the Seychelles.

It is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and exhibits unique adaptations to its tropical island environment. The plant has a distinctive morphology with small, scale-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats, often found in rocky crevices or forest floors.

Due to habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
