Schizophragma integrifolium var. fauriei

Schizophragma integrifolium var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, dark blue to black, and edible, often attracting birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
