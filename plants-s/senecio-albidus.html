Senecio albidus

Senecio albidus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
