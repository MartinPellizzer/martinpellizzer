Stenaria mullerae

Stenaria mullerae, commonly known as the Mexican fairy grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
