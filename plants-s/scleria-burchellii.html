Scleria burchellii

Scleria burchellii, commonly known as the spear grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed stigma, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scleria burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
