Strychnos longisepala

Strychnos longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled strychnos, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
