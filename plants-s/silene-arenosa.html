Silene arenosa

Silene arenosa, commonly known as sand catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and sandy regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky areas.

Silene arenosa is a perennial species that thrives in low-nutrient, well-drained soils.
