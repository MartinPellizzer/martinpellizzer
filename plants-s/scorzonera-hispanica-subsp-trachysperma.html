Scorzonera hispanica subsp. trachysperma

Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

trachysperma, commonly known as Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features a robust root system, with fleshy, tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary use.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central style.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
