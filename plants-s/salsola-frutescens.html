Salsola frutescens

Salsola frutescens, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand extreme temperatures.

Salsola frutescens is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
