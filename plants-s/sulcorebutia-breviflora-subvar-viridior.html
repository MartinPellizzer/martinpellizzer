Sulcorebutia breviflora subvar. viridior

Sulcorebutia breviflora subvar. viridior, commonly known as the green-flowered stonecress, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, fleshy stems that often form a rosette shape, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on short, hairy stalks. Its leaves are usually thick and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
