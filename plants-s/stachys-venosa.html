Stachys venosa

Stachys venosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Stachys venosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
