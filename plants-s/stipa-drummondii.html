Stipa drummondii

Stipa drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet that is usually adorned with small, bristly flowers.

Stipa drummondii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
