Serapias neretina

Serapias neretina, commonly known as the Neretina orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branching stems that bear the showy blooms. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine meadows.

Serapias neretina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
