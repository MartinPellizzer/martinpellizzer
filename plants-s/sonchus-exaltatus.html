Sonchus exaltatus

Sonchus exaltatus, commonly known as the tall sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sonchus exaltatus is an annual plant that can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
