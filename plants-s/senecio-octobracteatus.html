Senecio octobracteatus

Senecio octobracteatus, commonly known as the eight-bract senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
