Solanum coelocalyx

Solanum coelocalyx, commonly known as the purple star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Solanum coelocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
