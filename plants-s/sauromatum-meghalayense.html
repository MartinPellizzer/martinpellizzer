Sauromatum meghalayense

Sauromatum meghalayense, commonly known as the Meghalaya arrowhead, is a striking plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and dramatic, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in dense forests. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
