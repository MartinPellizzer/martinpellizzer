Sambucus pubens f. rosiflora

Sambucus pubens f. rosiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered elderberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like inflorescences that appear in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark berries in the fall.

The botanical features of this variety make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
