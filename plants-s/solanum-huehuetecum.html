Solanum huehuetecum

Solanum huehuetecum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
