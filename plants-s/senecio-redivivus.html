Senecio redivivus

Senecio redivivus, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that can survive extreme desiccation, appearing dead but reviving when water becomes available. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often curled and grayish-green in color. It produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique ability to survive drought makes it an interesting subject for botanical research and a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
