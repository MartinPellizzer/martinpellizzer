Salix miyabeana subsp. gymnolepis

Salix miyabeana subsp.

gymnolepis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, and its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, greenish female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.
