Smilax oldhamii f. angustifolia

Smilax oldhamii f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and spiny projections. The plant features simple, alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and thickets.
