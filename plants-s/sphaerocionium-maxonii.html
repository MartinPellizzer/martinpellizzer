Sphaerocionium maxonii

Sphaerocionium maxonii, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by the development of red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Sphaerocionium maxonii plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
