Salacia bayakensis

Salacia bayakensis, commonly known as the Bayakensis Salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its striking, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are often pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.
