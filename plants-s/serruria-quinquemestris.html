Serruria quinquemestris

Serruria quinquemestris, commonly known as the five-month serruria, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, serrated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils in the Western Cape region.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
