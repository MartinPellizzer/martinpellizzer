Sorbus alnoides

Sorbus alnoides, commonly known as the alderleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that resemble those of alder trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are a food source for birds.

Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream valleys.
