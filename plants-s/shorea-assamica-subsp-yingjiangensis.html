Shorea assamica subsp. yingjiangensis

Shorea assamica subsp.

yingjiangensis, commonly known as the Yingjiang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in China's Yunnan Province. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The species is known for its tall stature and can reach heights of up to 40 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
