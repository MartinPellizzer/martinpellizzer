Solanum santolallae

Solanum santolallae, commonly known as the Lantana nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
