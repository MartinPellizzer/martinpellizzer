Sapium marginatum var. paraguariense

Sapium marginatum var. paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
