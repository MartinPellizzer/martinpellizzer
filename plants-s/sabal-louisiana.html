Sabal louisiana

Sabal louisiana, commonly known as the Louisiana palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to moist, swampy environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that develop into dark blue berries.

Sabal louisiana is an important component of the wetland ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
