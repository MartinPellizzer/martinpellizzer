Sisyrinchium junceum subsp. colchaguense

Sisyrinchium junceum subsp.

colchaguense, commonly known as the Chilean blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Iridaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

The plant features grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
