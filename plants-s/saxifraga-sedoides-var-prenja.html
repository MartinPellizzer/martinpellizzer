Saxifraga sedoides var. prenja

Saxifraga sedoides var. prenja, commonly known as the Prenja saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and upright stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
