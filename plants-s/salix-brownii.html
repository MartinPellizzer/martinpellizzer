Salix brownii

Salix brownii, commonly known as the brown willow, is a species of willow native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, riparian habitats. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Salix brownii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
