Sida cumingii

Sida cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fibrous stems and serrated leaves make it identifiable within the Malvaceae family.

Sida cumingii is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
