Senecio anacletus

Senecio anacletus, commonly known as the Anacletus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio anacletus is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
