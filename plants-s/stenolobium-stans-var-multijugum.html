Stenolobium stans var. multijugum

Stenolobium stans var. multijugum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets arranged in pairs. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and fine grain, though it is also used in traditional medicine.
