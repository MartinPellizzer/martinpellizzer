Senna aurantia

Senna aurantia, commonly known as golden senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna aurantia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
