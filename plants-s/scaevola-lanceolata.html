Scaevola lanceolata

Scaevola lanceolata, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Scaevola lanceolata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
