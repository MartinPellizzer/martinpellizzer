Saussurea spinulifera

Saussurea spinulifera, commonly known as the spinyl leafed saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its habitat in remote and fragile ecosystems, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
