Styrax youngiae

Styrax youngiae, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Styrax youngiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
