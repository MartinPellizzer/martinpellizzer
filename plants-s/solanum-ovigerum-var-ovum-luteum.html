Solanum ovigerum var. ovum-luteum

Solanum ovigerum var. ovum-luteum, commonly known as the yellow eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-colored fruits, which are typically oval in shape and somewhat similar in appearance to traditional eggplants. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. It prefers warm climates and well-drained soil, often grown in gardens for both culinary and decorative purposes.

The flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the plant can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
