Spilanthes sinuata

Spilanthes sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Spilanthes sinuata is also known for its milky sap, which contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
