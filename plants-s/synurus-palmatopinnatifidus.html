Synurus palmatopinnatifidus

Synurus palmatopinnatifidus, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a distinctive tropical plant characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves.

The foliage consists of pinnately compound fronds that are palmately divided, giving it a unique, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant's vascular system is well-developed, supporting its tall, upright growth habit.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, facilitating its propagation in suitable habitats.
