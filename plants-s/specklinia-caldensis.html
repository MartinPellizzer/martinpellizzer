Specklinia caldensis

Specklinia caldensis, commonly known as the Calden's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Specklinia caldensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests.
