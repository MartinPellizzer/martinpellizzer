Solanum andigenum subsp. bolivianum

Solanum andigenum subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
