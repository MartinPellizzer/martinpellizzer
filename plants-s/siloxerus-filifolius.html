Siloxerus filifolius

Siloxerus filifolius, commonly known as the thread-leaved lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the class Lecanoromycetes.

It is characterized by its intricate, crust-like growth form, which allows it to adhere tightly to surfaces such as rocks and tree bark. The plant's thallus is typically pale to dark gray, with a dense, leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It reproduces through the release of airborne spores, which are dispersed by wind to colonize new substrates.

Siloxerus filifolius is often found in temperate regions and is an indicator species for clean, unpolluted environments.
