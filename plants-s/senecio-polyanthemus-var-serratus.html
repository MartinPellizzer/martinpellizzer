Senecio polyanthemus var. serratus

Senecio polyanthemus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
