Scalia longipedata

Scalia longipedata, commonly known as the long-leaved scalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
