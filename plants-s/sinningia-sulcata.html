Sinningia sulcata

Sinningia sulcata, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with a unique waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia sulcata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
