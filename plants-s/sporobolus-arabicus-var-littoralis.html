Sporobolus arabicus var. littoralis

Sporobolus arabicus var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found along shorelines and in wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
