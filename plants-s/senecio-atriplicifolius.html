Senecio atriplicifolius

Senecio atriplicifolius, commonly known as the African iron plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed heads. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
