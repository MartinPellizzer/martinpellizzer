Scleranthus minusculus

Scleranthus minusculus, commonly known as the lesser sicklegrass, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, greenish or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds readily.

Scleranthus minusculus is often considered a weed but has some ornamental value in certain garden settings.
