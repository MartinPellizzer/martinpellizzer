Sida simpsonii

Sida simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in a fluffy pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

Sida simpsonii is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
