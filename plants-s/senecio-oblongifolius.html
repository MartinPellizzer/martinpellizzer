Senecio oblongifolius

Senecio oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
