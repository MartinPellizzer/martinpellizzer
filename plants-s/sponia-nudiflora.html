Sponia nudiflora

Sponia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower sponia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often unbranched stems and small, clustered flowers that appear directly on the stems without leaf axils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Sponia nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
