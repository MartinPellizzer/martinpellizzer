Sedum greggii subsp. angustifolium

Sedum greggii subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features upright, branching stems covered in fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
