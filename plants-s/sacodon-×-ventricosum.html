Sacodon × ventricosum

Sacodon × ventricosum, commonly known as the hybrid sacodons, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between Sacodon sphaerocephalus and Sacodon ventricosus.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including a swollen, bulbous stem and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in regions with limited rainfall. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually displaying shades of purple or pink, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Sacodon × ventricosum is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
