Spiraea tatakaensis

Spiraea tatakaensis, commonly known as the Tataka spiraea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Spiraeaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Spiraea tatakaensis is often found in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
