Salacia madagascariensis subsp. sambiranensis

Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambirana salacia, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
