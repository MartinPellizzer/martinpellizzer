Scirpus nodulosus

Scirpus nodulosus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus nodulosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
