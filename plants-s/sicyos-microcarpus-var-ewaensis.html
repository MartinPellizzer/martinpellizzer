Sicyos microcarpus var. ewaensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. ewaensis, commonly known as the Ewa bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to Hawaii.

It is a climbing vine with hairy, lobed leaves and produces large, smooth, bottle-shaped fruits. The plant is adapted to the tropical climate of the Hawaiian Islands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and it relies on pollinators for reproduction.

This variety is notable for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Hawaiian agriculture.
