Schoenorchis roseus

Schoenorchis roseus, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense mats.

Schoenorchis roseus is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
