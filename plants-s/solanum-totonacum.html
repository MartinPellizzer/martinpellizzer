Solanum totonacum

Solanum totonacum, commonly known as the Totonac nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
