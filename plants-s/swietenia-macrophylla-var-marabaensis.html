Swietenia macrophylla var. marabaensis

Swietenia macrophylla var. marabaensis, commonly known as the Maraba Mahogany, is a tropical hardwood tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also significant in traditional timber industries.
