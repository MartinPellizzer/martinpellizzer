Solanum subbiflorum

Solanum subbiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that start green and turn red when ripe. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Solanum subbiflorum is part of the nightshade family, which includes many plants with medicinal and toxic properties.
