Statice corymbulosa

Statice corymbulosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically blue-green in color.

Statice corymbulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
