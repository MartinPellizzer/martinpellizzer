Syzygium bellutta

Syzygium bellutta, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by round, yellow fruits resembling apples. Syzygium bellutta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is edible and used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both landscaping and agroforestry in suitable climates.
