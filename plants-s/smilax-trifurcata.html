Smilax trifurcata

Smilax trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall.

Smilax trifurcata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground.
