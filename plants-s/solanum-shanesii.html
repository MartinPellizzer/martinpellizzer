Solanum shanesii

Solanum shanesii, commonly known as the Shanesii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, though they are not typically consumed by humans.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
