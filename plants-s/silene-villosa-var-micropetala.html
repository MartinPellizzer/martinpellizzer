Silene villosa var. micropetala

Silene villosa var. micropetala, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene villosa var. micropetala is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
