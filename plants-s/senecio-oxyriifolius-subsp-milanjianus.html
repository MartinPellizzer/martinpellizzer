Senecio oxyriifolius subsp. milanjianus

Senecio oxyriifolius subsp.

milanjianus, commonly known as the Milanjian's blueweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
