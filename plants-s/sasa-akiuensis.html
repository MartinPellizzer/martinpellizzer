Sasa akiuensis

Sasa akiuensis, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that often exhibit a reddish hue, especially in younger shoots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Sasa akiuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
