Solidago ambigua

Solidago ambigua, commonly known as the yellow gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to wetland landscapes.
