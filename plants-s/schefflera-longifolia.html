Schefflera longifolia

Schefflera longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Schefflera longifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
