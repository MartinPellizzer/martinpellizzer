Scorzonera sativa

Scorzonera sativa, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a rough texture.

Scorzonera sativa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
