Senecio lewisrosei

Senecio lewisrosei, commonly known as Lewis's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio lewisrosei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
