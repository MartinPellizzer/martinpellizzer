Scutellaria violacea var. floribunda

Scutellaria violacea var. floribunda, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite leaves which are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
