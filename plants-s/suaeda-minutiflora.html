Suaeda minutiflora

Suaeda minutiflora, commonly known as dwarf sea-blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity. Its structure includes fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding survival in arid conditions.

Suaeda minutiflora plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is often found in regions with high salt content in the soil.
