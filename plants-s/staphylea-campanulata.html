Staphylea campanulata

Staphylea campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Staphylea campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
