Sophronitis nutans

Sophronitis nutans, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Sophronitis nutans is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and relatively compact growth habit.
