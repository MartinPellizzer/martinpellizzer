Smelowskia pectinata

Smelowskia pectinata, commonly known as the woolly smelowskia, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Smelowskia pectinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture.
