Solanum verbascifolium var. caducum

Solanum verbascifolium var. caducum, commonly known as the caducous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several small, yellowish seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its early leaf drop, which occurs before the plant enters its dormant phase.
