Sempervivum pallidum

Sempervivum pallidum, commonly known as the silver cushion plant, is a species of succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces small, pink or red flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sempervivum pallidum is a hardy plant that can withstand cold temperatures and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
