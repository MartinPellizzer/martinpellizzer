Syringa emodi var. variegata

Syringa emodi var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink during late spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
