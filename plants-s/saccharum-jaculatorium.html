Saccharum jaculatorium

Saccharum jaculatorium, commonly known as the wild sugarcane or dwarf sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fibrous leaves, which are adapted to thrive in wetland and marshy environments. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are less developed compared to cultivated sugarcane, making it less suitable for commercial sugar production.

Despite its limited economic use, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
