Salvia thymiflora

Salvia thymiflora, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in teas and culinary applications due to their pleasant aroma.

Salvia thymiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
