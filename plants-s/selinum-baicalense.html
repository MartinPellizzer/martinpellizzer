Selinum baicalense

Selinum baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its dark blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin, as "Baikal" refers to Lake Baikal in Russia.
