Scirpus cubensis var. paraguayensis

Scirpus cubensis var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
