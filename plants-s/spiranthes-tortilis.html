Spiranthes tortilis

Spiranthes tortilis, commonly known as the twisted spike orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its slender, spiraling inflorescence of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, and the plant is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
