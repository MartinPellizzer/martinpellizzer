Sophora rhynchocarpa

Sophora rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain flattened, leathery seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

The plant also has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
