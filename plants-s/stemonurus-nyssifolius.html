Stemonurus nyssifolius

Stemonurus nyssifolius, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in the summer months. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The species is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
