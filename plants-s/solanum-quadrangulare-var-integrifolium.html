Solanum quadrangulare var. integrifolium

Solanum quadrangulare var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and along roadsides, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
