Syngonanthus multicaulis

Syngonanthus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces bright yellow blooms that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Syngonanthus multicaulis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its persistent, showy flowers.
