Sorbus syrmiensis

Sorbus syrmiensis, commonly known as the Sirmium rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange, containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
