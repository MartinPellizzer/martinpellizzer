Strychnos diplotricha

Strychnos diplotricha, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is part of the Loganiaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of strychnine and brucine in its seeds.
