Stipagrostis uniplumis var. neesii

Stipagrostis uniplumis var. neesii, commonly known as the Nees' single-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear few, elongated spikelets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat.
