Senecio emirnensis subsp. angavonensis

Senecio emirnensis subsp.

angavonensis, commonly known as the Angavonensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Iran. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and succulent, aiding in water retention.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
