Solanum muricatum var. protogenum

Solanum muricatum var. protogenum, commonly known as the "Dragon fruit" or "pitahaya," is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers that range from white to pink and yellow. Its fruit is typically large, with a thick rind and a segmented interior containing numerous seeds.

This variety is native to South America and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
