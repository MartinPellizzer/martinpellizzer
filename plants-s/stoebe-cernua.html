Stoebe cernua

Stoebe cernua, commonly known as narrowleaf sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Stoebe cernua is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
