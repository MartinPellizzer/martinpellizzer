Serratula chaetocarpa

Serratula chaetocarpa, commonly known as the roughhead serratula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are serrated and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.
