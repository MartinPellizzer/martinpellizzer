Salvia aucheri var. maurorum

Salvia aucheri var. maurorum, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
