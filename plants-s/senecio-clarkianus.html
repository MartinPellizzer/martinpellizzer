Senecio clarkianus

Senecio clarkianus, commonly known as Clark's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio clarkianus is often found in open habitats like grasslands and slopes, where it thrives in full sun.
