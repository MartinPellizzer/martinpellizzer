Sisymbrium lagascae var. glabrum

Sisymbrium lagascae var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and lack of leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, attaching itself to the roots of grasses or shrubs. Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health and growth of its host plants.
