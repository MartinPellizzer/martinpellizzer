Stipagrostis plumosa var. alexandrina

Stipagrostis plumosa var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrine feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, hair-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its elegant, low-maintenance appearance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in dryland environments.
