Sarcophyton crassifolium

Sarcophyton crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved sarcophyton, is a species of green algae belonging to the family Sarcophytonaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, branching thallus with thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a gelatinous sheath. The plant thrives in shallow, warm waters, typically found in the Indo-Pacific region. Its growth form allows it to form dense underwater mats, contributing to reef ecosystem structure.

Sarcophyton crassifolium plays a role in providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
