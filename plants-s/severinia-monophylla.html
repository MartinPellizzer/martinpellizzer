Severinia monophylla

Severinia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a single, large, palmate leaf per stem, which is a distinguishing botanical trait. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and seasonal floral display.
