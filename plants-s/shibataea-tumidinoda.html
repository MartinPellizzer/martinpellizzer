Shibataea tumidinoda

Shibataea tumidinoda, commonly known as the Japanese mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural purposes in gardens and landscapes.
