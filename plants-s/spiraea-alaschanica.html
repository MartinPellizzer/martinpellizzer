Spiraea alaschanica

Spiraea alaschanica, commonly known as the Alaska spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is typically dark green and becomes reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Spiraea alaschanica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
