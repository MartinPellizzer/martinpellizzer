Salvia piasezkii

Salvia piasezkii, commonly known as the Piasezkii sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia piasezkii is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and resilience in harsh climates.
