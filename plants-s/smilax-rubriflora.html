Smilax rubriflora

Smilax rubriflora, commonly known as red-flowered smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Smilax rubriflora is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
