Solanum mccannii

Solanum mccannii, commonly known as the McCann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers and berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
