Senna organensis var. friburgensis

Senna organensis var. friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburg senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and emit a faintly aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
