Salix iseriensis

Salix iseriensis, commonly known as the Iseri willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its slender branches and narrow leaves are adapted to withstand strong winds and cold temperatures.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, a common trait among willow species.
