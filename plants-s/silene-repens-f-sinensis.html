Silene repens f. sinensis

Silene repens f. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is native to China and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
