Synaptantha tillaeacea

Synaptantha tillaeacea, commonly known as the Tillaeacea rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Synaptantha tillaeacea is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy species for xerophytic landscapes.
