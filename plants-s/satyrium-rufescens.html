Satyrium rufescens

Satyrium rufescens, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

Due to habitat loss, Satyrium rufescens is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
