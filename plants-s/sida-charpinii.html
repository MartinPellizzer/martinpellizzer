Sida charpinii

Sida charpinii, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Sida charpinii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
