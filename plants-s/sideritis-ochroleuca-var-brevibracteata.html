Sideritis ochroleuca var. brevibracteata

Sideritis ochroleuca var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bracted bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
