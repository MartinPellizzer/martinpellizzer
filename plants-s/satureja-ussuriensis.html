Satureja ussuriensis

Satureja ussuriensis, commonly known as Siberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its essential oils.

The leaves are often used as a seasoning, similar to oregano or thyme.
