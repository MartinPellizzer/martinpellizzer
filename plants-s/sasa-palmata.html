Sasa palmata

Sasa palmata, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and cuisine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa palmata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
