Selaginella basipilosa

Selaginella basipilosa, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant exhibits a distinct life cycle with a gametophyte stage that is short-lived and dependent on the sporophyte. Its morphology includes a creeping stem with dense clusters of spore-bearing structures at the tips of its branches.

Selaginella basipilosa is notable for its ability to survive in adverse conditions, making it an important species in ecological studies.
