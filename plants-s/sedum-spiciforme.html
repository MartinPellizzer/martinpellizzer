Sedum spiciforme

Sedum spiciforme, commonly known as the spike sedum, is a succulent plant native to northern regions of North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, typical of the Crassulaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.
