Senecio dunedinensis

Senecio dunedinensis, commonly known as the Dunedin groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio dunedinensis is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is often found in coastal scrub and grassland ecosystems.
