Sedum koyuncui

Sedum koyuncui, commonly known as the Turkish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration.

Sedum koyuncui is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
