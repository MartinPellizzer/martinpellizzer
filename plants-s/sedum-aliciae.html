Sedum aliciae

Sedum aliciae, commonly known as Alice's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It blooms in late summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

Sedum aliciae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
