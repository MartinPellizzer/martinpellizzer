Satureja graeca subsp. imperica

Satureja graeca subsp.

imperica, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong aroma and medicinal properties.
