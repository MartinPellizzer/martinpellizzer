Sporobolus glaucifolius

Sporobolus glaucifolius, commonly known as blueleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Sporobolus glaucifolius is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
