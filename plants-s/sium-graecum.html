Sium graecum

Sium graecum, commonly known as wild celery or water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Sium graecum thrives in moist, aquatic or wetland habitats, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
