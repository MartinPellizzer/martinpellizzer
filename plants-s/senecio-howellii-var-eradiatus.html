Senecio howellii var. eradiatus

Senecio howellii var. eradiatus, commonly known as Howell's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub environments.
