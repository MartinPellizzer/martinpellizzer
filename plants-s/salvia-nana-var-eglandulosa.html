Salvia nana var. eglandulosa

Salvia nana var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, woolly gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
