Sesquicella papaver

Sesquicella papaver, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae, which includes other well-known species like the common poppy. The plant features striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
