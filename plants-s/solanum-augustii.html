Solanum augustii

Solanum augustii, commonly known as the Augustus nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum augustii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
