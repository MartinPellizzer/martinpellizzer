Swainsona brachycarpa

Swainsona brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited swainsona, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated pods. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Swainsona brachycarpa is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional Aboriginal practices.
