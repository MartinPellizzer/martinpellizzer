Shorea ciliata

Shorea ciliata, commonly known as the mangrove or white mangrove, is a tropical tree belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with ciliate margins, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in brackish water environments, often found in coastal areas and estuaries. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

Shorea ciliata is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
