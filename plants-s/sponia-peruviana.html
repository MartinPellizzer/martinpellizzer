Sponia peruviana

Sponia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sponge plant, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spongy, and often fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its growth habit is usually low and mat-forming, with a compact structure.

Sponia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a succulent in dry climates.
