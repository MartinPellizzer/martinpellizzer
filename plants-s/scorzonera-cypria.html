Scorzonera cypria

Scorzonera cypria, commonly known as Cyprus scorzonera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system that produces edible tubers, which are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Scorzonera cypria is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
