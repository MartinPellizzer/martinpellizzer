Solidago maya

Solidago maya, commonly known as the maya goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
