Senecio oleifolius

Senecio oleifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Senecio oleifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
