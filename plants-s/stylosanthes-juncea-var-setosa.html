Stylosanthes juncea var. setosa

Stylosanthes juncea var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy stylosanthes, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
