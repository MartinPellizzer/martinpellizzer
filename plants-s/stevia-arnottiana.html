Stevia arnottiana

Stevia arnottiana, commonly known as the New Zealand stevia, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible leaves that contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food products. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its sweet leaves and ornamental value.

Stevia arnottiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
