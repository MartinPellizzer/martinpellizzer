Spiraea pallasii

Spiraea pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features dense clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea pallasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
