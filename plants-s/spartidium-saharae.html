Spartidium saharae

Spartidium saharae, commonly known as the Saharan spartidium, is a rare and resilient plant native to the arid regions of the Sahara Desert.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptive structures for survival in harsh environments. The plant exhibits a low-growing, succulent morphology, allowing it to store water and reduce surface area exposed to extreme temperatures. Its small, narrow leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Spartidium saharae plays a minor but ecologically significant role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert insects.
