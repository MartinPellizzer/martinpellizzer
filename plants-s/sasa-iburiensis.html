Sasa iburiensis

Sasa iburiensis, commonly known as the Kuma bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal medicine.

Sasa iburiensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
