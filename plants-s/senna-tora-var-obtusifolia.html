Senna tora var. obtusifolia

Senna tora var. obtusifolia, commonly known as Indian Senna, is a perennial shrub native to South Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with rounded or obtuse leaflets, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal spikes, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, twisted pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant due to its hardy nature and nutritional value.
