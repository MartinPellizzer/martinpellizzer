Senna punoensis

Senna punoensis, commonly known as the Puno senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
