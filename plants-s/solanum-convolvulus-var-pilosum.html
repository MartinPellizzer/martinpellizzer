Solanum convolvulus var. pilosum

Solanum convolvulus var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, yellowish seeds.
