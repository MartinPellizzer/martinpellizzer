Stipa rudolphii

Stipa rudolphii, commonly known as the Rudolph's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain the flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. Stipa rudolphii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species in its native range.
