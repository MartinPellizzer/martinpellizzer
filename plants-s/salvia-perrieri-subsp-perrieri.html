Salvia perrieri subsp. perrieri

Salvia perrieri subsp.

perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
