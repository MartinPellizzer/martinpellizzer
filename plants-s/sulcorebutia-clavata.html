Sulcorebutia clavata

Sulcorebutia clavata, commonly known as the Clavate Pincushion Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, woolly covering of light green to grayish-blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
