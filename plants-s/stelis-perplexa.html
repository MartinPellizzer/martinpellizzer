Stelis perplexa

Stelis perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its unique appearance.

Stelis perplexa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
