Sisyrinchium filifolium subsp. junceum

Sisyrinchium filifolium subsp.

junceum, commonly known as slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. It has a basal rosette growth habit and is part of the Iridaceae family.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
