Salvia discolor

Salvia discolor, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, typically blooming from spring to summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Salvia discolor is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
