Schizostachyum glaucocladum

Schizostachyum glaucocladum, commonly known as the silver-cloaked bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a distinctive silver-blue sheen on its leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often used in traditional crafts and construction due to their durability.

Schizostachyum glaucocladum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
