Sideritis arborescens subsp. pauli

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

pauli, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
