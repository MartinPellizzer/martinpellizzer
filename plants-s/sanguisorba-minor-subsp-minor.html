Sanguisorba minor subsp. minor

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

minor, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear whorled clusters of small, reddish-brown to pinkish flowers.

The plant has basal and opposite leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. Sanguisorba minor subsp.

minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
