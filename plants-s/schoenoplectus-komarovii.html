Schoenoplectus komarovii

Schoenoplectus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Schoenoplectus komarovii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
