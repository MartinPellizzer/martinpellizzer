Sublimia palmicaulis

Sublimia palmicaulis, commonly known as the skyward palm, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, waxy, strap-like leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. Its flowers, which appear at the top of the plant, are small and clustered, often in shades of red or orange.

Sublimia palmicaulis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
