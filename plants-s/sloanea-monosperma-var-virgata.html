Sloanea monosperma var. virgata

Sloanea monosperma var. virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf sloanea, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and silvery-white on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential medicinal properties.
