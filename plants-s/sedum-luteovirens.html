Sedum luteovirens

Sedum luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to central and southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tint. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum luteovirens is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
