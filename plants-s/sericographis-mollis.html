Sericographis mollis

Sericographis mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is native to regions in the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Sericographis mollis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
