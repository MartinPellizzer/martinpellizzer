Salix kossoviensis

Salix kossoviensis, commonly known as the Kossoviensis willow, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. It is often found in river valleys and moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in riparian zones.
