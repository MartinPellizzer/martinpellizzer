Stevia purpurea

Stevia purpurea, commonly known as purple stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its violet to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Due to its low caloric content, it is often used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages.
