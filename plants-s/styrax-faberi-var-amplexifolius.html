Styrax faberi var. amplexifolius

Styrax faberi var. amplexifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense-making.
