Solanum setigeroides

Solanum setigeroides, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum setigeroides is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
