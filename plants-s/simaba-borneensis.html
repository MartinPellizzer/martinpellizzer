Simaba borneensis

Simaba borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean simaba, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The species plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
