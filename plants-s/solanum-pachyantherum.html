Solanum pachyantherum

Solanum pachyantherum, commonly known as the purple false tomato, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its attractive, showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that changes color from green to yellow as it matures.

Solanum pachyantherum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
