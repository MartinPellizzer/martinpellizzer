Silene saxifraga var. pungens

Silene saxifraga var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked catchfly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Silene saxifraga var. pungens is often found in mountainous areas and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
