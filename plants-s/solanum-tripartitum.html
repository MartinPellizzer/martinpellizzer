Solanum tripartitum

Solanum tripartitum, commonly known as the three-parted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, round berries that contain the toxic seeds.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes.
