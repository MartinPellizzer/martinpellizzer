Sporobolus modestus

Sporobolus modestus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient to harsh conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, often in disturbed or open habitats.

Sporobolus modestus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
