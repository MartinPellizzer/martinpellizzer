Smilacina racemosa var. amplexicaulis

Smilacina racemosa var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that often wrap around stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
