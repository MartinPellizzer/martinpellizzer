Senecio lugens var. megacephalus

Senecio lugens var. megacephalus, commonly known as the Giant Senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant features sturdy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires well-drained soil and full sun to flourish.
