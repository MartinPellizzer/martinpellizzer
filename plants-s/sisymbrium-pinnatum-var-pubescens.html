Sisymbrium pinnatum var. pubescens

Sisymbrium pinnatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartwater, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its varietal name. Its small white or yellow flowers are typically arranged in racemes, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
