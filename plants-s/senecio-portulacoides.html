Senecio portulacoides

Senecio portulacoides, commonly known as the stonecress or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, exhibiting a low, cushion-like growth form. The plant has fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

Senecio portulacoides is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
