Spiraea betulifolia

Spiraea betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge and a bluish-green hue, resembling the texture of birch leaves, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate floral display.
