Schomburgkia lueddemannii var. costaricana

Schomburgkia lueddemannii var. costaricana, commonly known as the Costa Rican spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific care requirements.
