Steireya angustifolia

Steireya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
