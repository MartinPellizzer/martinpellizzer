Scutia buxifolia var. acutifolia

Scutia buxifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scutia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "acutifolia.". The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
