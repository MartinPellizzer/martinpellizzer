Saxifraga magellanica f. laxa

Saxifraga magellanica f. laxa, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery or woolly coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value.
