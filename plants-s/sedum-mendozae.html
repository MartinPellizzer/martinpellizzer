Sedum mendozae

Sedum mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Sedum mendozae is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
