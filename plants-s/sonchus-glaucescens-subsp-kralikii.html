Sonchus glaucescens subsp. kralikii

Sonchus glaucescens subsp.

kralikii, commonly known as Kralik's sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glaucous leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of basal leaves.

The plant is generally considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
