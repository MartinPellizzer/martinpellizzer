Senecio donianus

Senecio donianus, commonly known as the Donian's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
