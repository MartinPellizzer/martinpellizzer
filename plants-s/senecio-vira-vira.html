Senecio vira-vira

Senecio vira-vira, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with slender stems and small, narrow leaves. It produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by the showy, multicolored bracts.

Senecio vira-vira is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique visual appeal.
