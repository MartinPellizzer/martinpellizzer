Sapindus attenuatus

Sapindus attenuatus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red when mature. Its fruit contains a bitter, soapy substance that has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
