Statice sinuata var. integrifolia

Statice sinuata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry landscapes. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
